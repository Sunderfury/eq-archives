      Inspired by the crappy connectivity to the servers lately (namely the ones on cerf.net, which of course are the only ones I play on) the ability to ping and perform trace routing on servers is being added to the server status section. Read on if you don't know what pinging and trace routing are.

      Pinging a server is a way of finding out how quickly (or slowly) information is being relayed between your computer and the server you play on. This transmission speed of data is generally measured in milliseconds, so "slowly" is a relative term, but it's still very noticeable when you're playing. The more milliseconds it takes for you and the server to talk to each other, the worse your connection is. A high ping makes it difficult to do things in EQ like timing your spells to go off perfectly between mob attacks or to follow someone through a dungeon. On the EverQuest Lag-O-Meter, your ping is the number furthest to the left. In EQu, your relative ping to a server will be represented by an appropriately colored box next to the server name which you will be able to hover your mouse over for an exact number.

      Trace routing is a way of discovering just what exactly is giving you a high ping, or a loss of connectivity, etc. The information that goes between your computer and verant's servers can get slowed down by the networks it has to travel through on the internet during it's journey. Trace routing is a method of seeing what networks your data is traveling through and getting a little bit of info on those networks to see where on the internet you're running into trouble. For example, I'm personally getting real crappy ping to the Torvonnilous server, so I run a trace route to Torvonnilous and see this:
