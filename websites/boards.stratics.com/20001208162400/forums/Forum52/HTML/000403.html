I've posted this also in General Trade Skills, but am a tailor, and have most of my experience with tailoring when it comes to the Norrath economy. So I hope it's not a bad breach in etiquette to repeat the post here.

---

This is a ramble regarding the harsh economy for trade skills in Norrath, and how some people are a part of the problem.
Before I make some people feel unfairly mis-represented by this post, I'll disclaimer the following now instead of later: while some rich folk – both twinks and some wealthy higher level characters – help to ruin the trade economy in Norrath for those who “work for a living,” this is of course not always the case. Furthermore, I have nothing against twinking in itself (though haven’t done so personally), and there are many wealthy characters (high level types and even twinked ones) who are extremely charitable people, who enjoy nothing more than the imagined glee on the face of a newbie who suddenly has been given the means for payback against the many critters that have been killing him/her for so long. I applaud generosity. And it may even be with the intention of generosity that some richer folk pay out a lot of money to someone selling, say, an overpriced MQ/HQ pelt (I’m a tailor, so much of my thoughts originate from tailor issues), with the idea that they’re helping someone get some much-needed cash.

But what richer characters must understand in this case is that, while they may be giving a small burst of wealth to one character, they are simultaneously helping to financially ruin many more; their fellow tailors. I don’t mind how you got your money, that’s all up to you; it’s how you use it which might well affect me, and others.

I don’t think there should be any rules against these ruinous practices, of people buying their way quickly through a trade skill – people should be able to do what they want, just like in real life. In real life we’ve also got people who have inherited money, or been born into better situations (twinks), and people who have clawed their way up out of the newbie ghettoes through a lot of patient work (the route I’m personally in now, in both worlds – and it should be mentioned, the route a twink most likely originally took in order to gain all the stuff with which to twink).

It’s common in real life for companies with larger financial power to conduct themselves with practices that serve themselves first with disregard to the smaller companies (the people who are struggling to “make a living”) that they’re intentionally or unintentionally injuring or destroying. Because this happens in real life, I think it’s fine to have it be “legal” in Norrath as well. What I’d ask is that richer folks who are serving themselves and ruining the economy for those trade skill types that “work for a living” please consider the impact of paying bloated prices for necessary items (such as pelts, etc.) on the economy; and if you are well aware of the effects of your actions on those of us who aren’t rich, well, stop expecting us to like you, and understand that we’re not going to stop complaining about it.

I’ve heard way too many people – whether they realized these effects of their actions or not – complain about our complaints. They sometimes like to refer to is as “whining,” which is pretty funny if you imagine a real life comparison to the situation. Say a promising young software developer is getting screwed by the latest monopolistic move of an industry giant that wields enormous power and resources (example: Microsoft). Now, Bill Gates is incredibly talented, did a ton of work, and got where he did for doing some pretty big things (I have no interest in listing or debating the many faults of some of his products, I’m painfully aware of them, thank you). But it’s one thing to wield that enormous power, and another to wield it in a way that makes the trade much more difficult for others in the same field. If that person’s hard work and livelihood is being ruined by the practices of this powerful corporate giant, are you also going to tell him to “stop whining?”

A consequent phrase often given then is “get a life, it’s only a game,” which to me is just a way people often try to escape being called on their own negative behavior. Of course it’s only a game. It is, however, a great, fun, complex game that people put a ton of time and energy into, and it simulates a lot of things from real life, like the trade economy, the complexity of which Verant might not have fully intended or even realized from the outset. My character and I have put a lot of time and effort and simulated expense into this simulated trade, so you can expect some simulated hostility when someone simulates some ruinous business practices. That’s all. No one died in real life, it ain’t no biggie, but within the game, sure it’s a big issue!

All I’m asking is that people who have been shelling out lots of cash in easily buying their way through a trade, please be considerate of the effects of your actions and who they’re affecting. To any richer types that have been sticking to only the going rates for the various much-needed resources, I applaud you, and for anyone who is charitable and generous, I applaud you as well.
