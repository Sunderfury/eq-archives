Above is the basic map to the first section of  Sol B (Nagafens Lair). Take a second to look at the zone to Lavastorm because most of your entrances will be from there. Now get a good idea where the zones to Sol A are because there will be times when a zone "up" to Sol A will be the safest and/or quickest route out. The area to the left of the Lava zone is know as "the window room" due to the fact that there is a large hole cut out of the stone that acts as a window to the room below. May parties try to hold either the bottom or top of the window room and manage pulls between there and the Sol A zones. The area in the center with the red is the "Royals" area, named for the spawns you will kind here: King, Priest, Champion and to a lesser extent the noble. If you find the "stone block" trap you have found the hall that leads to deeper into the bats and bugs. This area can easily support you from lvl 40-46, after that you need to move up to bat/bugs, imps, fire giants, etc. I believe the spawn is at around 24 min per location. In addition to the static mob's each all has a patrol that covers a wide loop. To break this zone pull the 2 front static ones back deep by zone line and root there. After that fight hold the intersection until the first patrol comes. Be warned that if you fight at the first intersection instead of back by the zone line you run the risk of having up to 3 shamans healing the mobs. To the left there are 2 kobolds, just behind the wall. If either of them are shaman you may have to deal with that first.

 

 

The Enemy:

Koblds

 

Types:

Greater kobolds (gk)

Can hit for dammage over 100 an attack. Med amount of HP.

Greater Shaman kobolds

Fewer HP than gk. Given the time, all shamans will make a 30's pet. Primary problem =healer.

Solusek kobolds (SK)

Hit hearder than gk and have more HP.

Solusek Shaman kobolds

More HP than their greater shaman kobold relatives.

Champion

Massive HP. Drops Shield of the Slain Unicorn and Painbringer.

Priest

Healer for the royal circle. Priority kill. Drops carnal pauldrons and mith BP I think?

King

High level Shadow Knight with a painful harm touch. Tons of HP. Drops Fleshripper and mith bracer.

Noble

High lvl Enchanter. Lots of HP, can and will charm. Usually has a damage shield and a pet. Drops a staff of writhing or a heart neck piece for casters.

Experience:

All kobolds offer exp up to 46. After 46 all Solusek kobolds offer exp up to 49. Named Kobolds offer exp through 50.

THE WINDOW ROOM

 

 

To the left is the lower level of the window room. In here you will find 3 local spawns and a patrol. If you go up the ramp you will find the area where most of your pulls will come from. On the right is the view out of the window from above. For those with the flair for the dramatic, may I suggest jumping through the window when pulling through? First off, it's fun as hell, and second, the pathing on the kobolds is such that you will gain a distance advantage if you have to pull further down.

 

The Royals

 

 

 

IF you wander up around the back you will see an area that looks like the one above, with lots of halls and murals on the walls. It is guarded by 3 hall spawns and a patrol primarily Solusek kobolds. This is the choke point for all the royal areas. You can opt to stay here and try to manage them all, or you can advance into an area and camp one. Usually there is another party around so be considerate and figure out who is doing what so there is no confusion later. Looking at the picture above there are 3 sections. The priest is directly in front behind the fake wall dead ahead. Just walk right through it. The champ is to the right and down the hall. To the left and up the ramp is the king and his entourage. If you decide to try to manage all of the spawns there are a few words of warning. There is an order that must be followed to take them all down successfully. Kill the priest and it's room first, it has a nasty heal that will wear you down if not dealt with. Then kill the champ and its room to the right. You must kill the champ because once you attack the king anyone left standing in very large radius will come to assist the king in the fight. If you have not killed the champ room or the hall spawns you may find yourself fighting 10+ mobs. Always account for the patroller before the next big room pull. One extra mob can mess timing up.

 

Below is a look at each room from the inside. Each picture is looking back toward the mail hall. Behind me in each shot is a pool of lava. Each room is exposed in the back, looking on to each other.

 

 

 

 

Champ Room

4-5 Kobols in here. Champ is rare and usually drops the shild of the slain unicorn over the Painbringer. Took 1 month to get the Painbringer.

Priest Room

4-5 Kobolds in here. Priest seems to pop a lot but the Mith BP is rare. The priest will heal anything you fight if you are to close so mind you don't nudge too far back when you are breaking in.

King Room

5-6 Kobolds here plus 1 patrol. The king or his place holder will agro any living dog in a large radius into the fight when you attack.

 

 

 

 

 

Standing in the champ room looking across the lava into the other rooms. The one below and to the left is the back section of the priest room. Above and back is the king room. Carful about pulling across the pool of lava with arrows or spells as the mob will run down and around and sometimes bring more than you had anticipated. If you fall into the lava pool don't panic, calmly swim to the king area and climb up from there. For the brave of spirit there are 2 thrill seaking activities here. 1) if you jump into the lava pool and swim below, you will notice a tub that leads under the royals. If you swim under and up you will come to a small chamber safe from lava. In a perfect world I hope this will be a zone to deeper into the underworld or something, but for now it's just an interesting factoid. 2) you can jump from the king to the priest and the champ to the priest rooms. If you are in the Kings room and the priest room is in trouble it makes for a daring rescue if you jump from your post and fly through the air into combat. The jump from the King platform is the more difficult of the 2.

 

 

Once in a while Targin the Rock will also stop by to help the King out in his room. He is a moderately difficult monk and drops a monk quest item, or so I'm told.
