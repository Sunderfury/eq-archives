System Shock bits
Whew, VN keeps the System Shock info flowing like a monkey with bowl problems. Only difference is that they don't spray the news all over the walls. Read this fat little update. - Sacrates

on game levels: We've honored that System Shock tradition, for better than worse. The game is broken into levels (actually, decks), however, the world is persistent and you may freely move between decks. You can drop an ammo clip on deck one, come back hours later and it will still be there...

on 3D hardware discouraging multi-function displays and multiple windows: Yes, what you say about 3D hardware is true, but MFDs are so critical to Shock's RPG gameplay that we bit the bullet and made it work. Like Shock 1, there's plenty of cyber gadgets to play with. One of our core goals, however, was to make sure the interface for using them was simpler and more intuitive. In other words, you can do the same cool stuff, but without the complicated interface...

on crouching, ducking, leaning sideways, etc.: It wouldn't be Shock 2 without them...

on outdoor terrain: Not currently planned. We really wanted to focus our energies on a game that gave that great System Shock sci-fi horror vibe and the indoors suited that better.

on models and animation: Polygonal motion captured.

on multi-player: Can't say much about multiplayer now except that we are working on it.

on control configuration: The Dark engine is intended to have keyboard remapping and therefore, so will we.

There certainly was a philosophical predeliction at LG against such things before, but I think that feeling has changed somewhat.

I think you'll be pleased with the system we've come up with mouselook/cursor control. Let me just say, we've got the best of both worlds (more on this later).

on whether the RPG elements will be similar to Ultima Underworld: It will be rather serious to Underworld, actually, in terms of the RPG elements. There will be character generation (your "character class" is based on which branch of the military you enter) and character development (stats, skills, traits, the whole magilla)...

In many ways Shock 2 is sort of Shock meets Underworld in a hot 3D engine...

on requiring a 3D accelerator card: Yes, this is true. We felt this was the only way to really take advantage of the 3d hardware out there. By the time of our release date, 3D hardware will simply be a reality. Currently, there are sites on the web you can get a 3dfx card for 50 bucks! Any gamer should have one...

on the interface compared to Descent Freespace and X-Wing vs Tie-Fighter: Our interface is somewhere in between the two, perhaps closer to freepace...

on time limits: Time limits are a tricky thing to pull off right, esp. in a game like Shock where exploration is so important. We don't want people whipping through the levels, because they'll miss so much re: miniquests, story, content, and general coolness. So, I don't think (though not saying for sure) we'll have much in this area...

on re-spawning: Foes won't respawn per se, but we will have a wandering monster ecology...the system will determine (based on designer specs) how crowded with monstery-goodness a level should be and try to keep it that way (with some randomness). Of course the player himself can impact this (e.g. hacking the security system to shut down respawning).

on AI and the security system: We will be using Thief's AI system. Security cameras behave differently in Shock 2 than Shock 1. Can't say much, but their behavior is far more direct now.

on the voices for Shock 2: Currently, no plans to cast the "Talking Moose" in Shock 2 (if you know what I'm talking about, then you know what I'm talking about... However, Teri Brosius will be back as Shodan, though she's asking her usual 20 million dollar fee. But we didn't bat an eye, we must have the real SHODAN!

More BG blah
My woman is still off studying in my Tent O' love at the moment so I thought I would pass this on to ya's. This is for those BG druggies out there who have to have that next fix of Baldur's gate info. Check out this post: - Sacrates

on a suggestion to remove the experience cap: The main problem with your scenario is that it is only applicable in single-player. Since you can export a character to a multi-player game and play through again, if there were no cap you would continue gaining experience with no benefit. Warriors are reasonably easy to implement as far as plain stat growth, but what about the other characters and the stats/skills/spells they could not get? This would also present problems when you installed the mission packs and sequels. They will be designed with a reasonable starting level in mind. It throws everything out of whack if your character is already 14th level in a scenario made for levels 8-14 (or whatever it turns out to be).

Basically, the cap encompasses your suggestion (because you will have to do pretty much everything to hit the cap!) and solves the above problems as well.

The main reason why the game is capped where it is is because it is designed for characters of levels one through 6/7. We are following traditional AD&D rules as best we can, and that means paying attention to the changes that characters undergo as they reach higher levels. If you are unfamiliar with AD&D, character development in the game is not all straight stat advancement. Once level 10 hits, most classes have special abilities or perks from then on, and we are planning on addressing them as best we can. No ignoring important character elements that make AD&D characters what they are. Of course these will take resources and planning that we simply could not incorporate into BG in the time allotted.

on alignment shifts and loss of Paladin or Ranger status during the game: As it stands, alignment change is possible. Basically, if you chose a good alignment at character creation and you run around doing evil things, your alignment will shift downward eventually. You'll have to do a LOT of unmistakably evil things, but it could happen. As well, if you coose evil alignment at creation and are the consumate goody two-shoes, your alignment can shift upwards eventually. If you are neutral, you pretty much stay the same.

The loss of paladin or Ranger abilities WILL occur a long time before your actual alignment shifts.

on what happens to fallen Paladins and Rangers: 1. they become pseudo-fighters that can't grandmaster. Other than that they progress as fighters from then on. I believe their character sheet says "Fallen Paladin" or "Fallen Ranger" so the disgrace is plainly evident.

2. warning? Nope, though you will not lose your status killing people that have obviously wronged you (like stealing from you). The things that are evil will seem so from the start. You'll have made the conscious decision to be bad.

3. no voluntary change of alignment(aside from a killing spree, that is).

4. Can you regain lost paladin or ranger status? Not in the first BG. We'll be addressing that more closely in BG2, where it will be a major event in the character's development.

on not using Chapter 1 as the interactive demo: This is the reason that an interactive demo has to be a stand alone story, and not a cropped version of the game. "Chapter one" is not a limited area, or a strict story arc. It ends when you arrive at a certain location, and this is marked as a milestone for your character, but where you go before arriving there can span a hell of a lot of space. The story is pretty much imbedded in the areas as well, so to take sensitive stuff out would be just as much of pain as making all new triggers and content for one area or so.
