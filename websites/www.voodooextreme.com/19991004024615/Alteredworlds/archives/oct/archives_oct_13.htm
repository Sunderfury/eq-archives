The clock continues ticking and the team players working on 'Destiny' constantly complaint that there is not enough time to fit everything they want to do into such a short time. I asked the guys what else they could show me and they whisked me away to a HUGE ancient mansion atop a mountain. They said that this is where a grand martial arts competition would be... Hmm... Can't wait for it to happen.. all those hunky guys flex their muscles.

There are more locations to come which when I have visited and seen them all, I will give you an insight of what I have seen. Deal?

Weekly WoT goodness
Glen also made his weekly WoT development update that we have come to love. It's a biggun so sit back grab a pair of eyes and enjoy. - Sacrates

This has been a busy week (but when are they not?).  First, I'm sure you're wondering what/if I'll have anything to say about the rumors flying all over the net, specifically BitchX's latest claims about GT and Unreal 2.  And as you suspected, I can't say much.  Let me tell you what I can say: BitchX made the assertion that GT had signed Legend to create Unreal 2.  Mark Rein wrote in to correct him/her/it that, in fact, Epic had signed Legend to write said game, not GT.   Given that in doing so, Mark has confirmed this rumor, the bag is catless.  We have, in fact, signed a contract to produce the sequel to the amazing game Unreal. 

I can't say much more, and I certainly can't answer your questions--so please don't write me asking about it.  I will give you my impression, however.  Legend has gone through a transformation in the last couple of years.  We always had smart and talented people--game designers, programmers, artists, etc.--but we applied them to rather unrewarding genres (commercially, at least).  Now, we've changed our course.   We've attacked the new frontier, combining our skills at creating content with cutting edge technology, and adding talented people to the pipeline.  Although I keep touting our progress to you, I think this new deal is a clear indication of the behind-the-scenes reaction to what we've achieved with The Wheel of Time. Naturally, I'm very excited about the prospect of Unreal 2 coming from Legend, but I'm also excited about what this says about WoT and Legend in general.

Today is a milestone day, so this might be shorter than normal.  We're delivering tons of maps, including a new multi-player prototype that screams (it's optimized for lower end machines), a set of Arena levels (these are tons of fun), and a couple of Shadar Logoth citadel variants.  Also, we're delivering animation for the Minion, the Black Ajah Boss, the Warder, and the Whitecloak Archer.  The level design team and Steve B. deserve a lot of credit for all of the effort they've put in to make this milestone happen.

WoT's lead artist Paul Mock showed up today, computer in tow.  He's set up in our conference room to help our new texture artist Marc Tetrault come up to speed.   Although he doesn't have the sheaf of sketches for the White Tower primitives with him (his dog ate them), he has spent some time establishing the architectural feel to the place.  This week, he should be able to pump out a few drawings so that the lds can start building the primitives.  I'm really looking forward to seeing these.

Our AI dynamic duo (Ryan and Mike) are hard at work.  Ryan is almost done laying down the basic architecture for a class of troop--to the point where they can actually start implementing specific cases.  They've got their work cut out for them for the next milestone--including getting the Minion up and running.  I really can't wait to see him do his thing (also included are the Trolloc, the Whitecloak Soldier, and the Warder).  Aaron and Sam have spent a ton of time together testing and tweaking the Artifacts.  Already Aaron has implemented some major improvements to their functionality that has made our "testing" sessions a lot more fun.  These improvements are actually part of the multi-player prototype milestone deliverable.   Thanks go to these guys for putting in some long hours making it happen.

Everything looks wonderful, but as we approach the release date for this game (yes, I know it's months away, but it seems like days), I'm becoming increasingly focused on making sure we have a polished product at the end.  I'd rather have something a little smaller that's absolutely done right than something that could collapse under its own weight.  Right now I'm in the process of figuring all of this out.  It's making my head hurt.  Maybe I'll just go play--ah, test--some more WoT. :-)

Until next time!

U:A Faq
Noticed over at VE that Sith's U:A Faq has been updated with more U:A goodies. Hope Billy doesn't mind but I am gonna cut and paste like a monkey boy since I am so lazy this morning. - Sacrates

4.1 Will Ascension use the same engine as UVIII?

No. Holding with Origin tradition, each new Ultima involves new technology. Every new numeral has a totally new engine (U7SI is not considered a new numeral). Ascension will feature a 3-D polygon-based engine with a movable point of view, or "camera", that will allow the player to customize the viewpoint in the game to his/her liking.

5.2.1 Will Ascension utilize 3-D acceleration?

U:A will definitely support 3D acceleration, but will no longer require it to run. Of course, not having a 3D accelerator means that the game will play much more slowly.

5.2.2 Will Ascension be 3DFX native?

Yes. Ascension is planned to be a 3DFX native program. However, other 3D chipsets will also be supported.

Third World clippage
Here's another brief spot of Third World from Producer Adam Maxwell. Nice name, sounds very "Espionage". Maybe he works for the CIA in his past time? Check it - Sacrates

on gang size: We chose a 5 person gang for its tactical value, polygon counts and the like were considered after the gang size was determined. A 5 person squad offers you enough flexiblity to try most, if not all, of the small unit tactics that have already been devised by militants the world over. It also allows you enough flexibility to create your own tactics and strategies. 5 people is also small enough that you don't just play the game you would Dark Reign or Warcraft. This is not an RTS and we wanted a gang size that would guarantee that it wasn't played that way.

on a comparison with Jagged Alliance 2: there really isn't a comparison between JA2 and Third World that's fair to either game. They have similiarities (small unit tactics, etc.) but they have too many differences to be considered in teh same genre. JA2, at least the demo, seems to be centered around combat. Third World shares the focus between adventuring with your gang and combat equally.
