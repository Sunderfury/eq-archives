Everquest Week 1: Addressing the Issues
by: Sacrates

This game is horrible!!

Now that i've gotten your attention let me explain why this game is so horrible. If your a Crack dealer read this article, you've got a new Drug to deal out. If you read magazines like Cat Fancy often then you don't want to read this article. If you play computer games often then it's time to sit up in your chair, perk up your ears and read on. If you have any form of Heart disease, high blood pressure, or often have spastic convulsions when you get too excited you may not want to read this either. Just a warning.
Before I recieved my copy of Beta 3, I had absorbed every piece of information on this game as possible. I dreamed the dreams that everyone else dreams about before they get a game (Not always about naked big breasted female elves) they've never played before. Usually in these cases we end up underwhelmed when we finally get the game. So here I am after finally downloading the client on my PC just sitting there staring at the Icon on my desktop wondering what lay beyond it. Little did I know.....
With all that out of the way lets start looking at what makes EQ tick. I sign on and have a Character made up within minutes. You enter the world for your first time thinking things like UO etc only to get bitch slapped back to reality and realizing this is not UO. After my first 2 days of testing out the Wizard class I quickly decide that it isn't what i'm looking for. Why, after only 2 days have I deduced this you may wonder? Well, like all the other articles have stated, Everquest is extremely intuitive to use and navigate in. Therefore easier to get accustomed to with what you want to do.
This game is all about you. It gives you the opportunity to do and be all you can be. (No, i've never been in the Army) The Races and Classes are EXTREMELY well balanced therefore you choose what you would want to choose not out of necessity but out of personal preference. In UO we were forced to powergame at times just to stay alive. In EQ this is not the case. You choose the Class or Race not because it has inherent advantages over other Race/Classes but because it fits your style of play.
So here I am, not wanting to use the Wizard anymore so I switch over to the Cleric which is what I am looking for. A fairly defensive class that uses mostly assistance spells and defensive spells, yet can wear armor and use pretty good weapons. I am a High Elf, so must start out in the city of Felwithe located in the Greater Faydark. Faydark, like the name implies is very dark, foggy, wet, and nasty, (Please don't read between the lines with that analogy, I know what your thinking you perv!) but hey, I'm an Elf so I call it home.
To address a few concerns I have recently noticed on the boards etc. First starting out with the repetition or monotony of simply killing Rats and Bats to make money. Personally I think the lower levels are just as much and maybe even more fun than the higher levels. Now admittedly i'm not exactly high level yet but am strong enough to handle my own in a battle against the weak creatures. You automatically know when you've graduated from one creature to another. You can almost feel your character become stronger and more powerfull as he/she gains levels. Believe me, gaining levels is a slow process also.
There is nothing boring or monotonous about this game. And that leads me back to my original statement. This game is horrible. You can't stop playing once you start. Your first experience won't be earth shattering, but when you wake up the next day and play another session you realize something. You can't stop thinking about the game. Even when I am off playing Tennis or doing something else my mind is still on EQ and what I can do and what I will do next.
Personally I don't like to be overwhelmed with a game at first anymore because I know it will lose it's luster down the road. Half life and Unreal are good examples of this. Half Life is a brilliant example of making a good game but didn't have replayability. Unreal was Purdy to look at in the beginning but later you think, been there done that. With a game like EQ you aren't completely overwhelmed with the graphics at first. Not until you actually start exploring then things become all too clear. You want to see more, you want to meet more people and creatures.
Basically you do what the programmers want you to do. Socialize and be in a world that is massive in size. And Adventure, which is why this game is so horrible. You want to keep on going and see new sites and be in big epic Fantastical battles. This game is so horrible because it is so good! That is the great secret the guys at 989 have been hiding so long. Ahem...sorry got a little sidetracked there, they don't call me Sacrates (With an A) for nothing!
The graphics are, like I said earlier, not earth shattering until you start exploring. Then things start getting impressive. To actually see the front of my home in Felwithe you'd understand. It's a massive Medevil castle. Seeing the sunset in the Steamfront Mountains (Home of the Gnomes) is literally breathtaking. Seeing Akanon (Again home of the Gnomes) your first time is pretty crazy. A very dark, underground place with mechanized spiders and Gnomes walking around. I also watched as they maliciously killed a Half Elf that had gotten too close to their city. Obviously they don't like Half Elves for some reason.
I cover a lot of games here at AW, but to this point nothing comes even close to what this game does for me. I've seen the glory of Baldur's Gate, i've played hours and hours of Diablo, and have been addicted to UO once upon a time. But those experiences just don't come close to this. It actually "feels" like a real world. And all you want to do is explore it. Keep checking our news section and i'll keep you updated on it's progress. I'll see you all in Beta 4 hopefully!
