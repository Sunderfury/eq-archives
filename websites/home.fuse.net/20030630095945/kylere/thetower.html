    I heard a scream from far above,
    and turned to size the tower's height.
    She stood on high, a wicked dream,
    her lust engaged in sating night.
   
    What fate had kept her there for me,
    had only I the eyes to see.
    Her elvin form, her spectral streams,
    Oh, what were she and I to be?
   
    There visions drew me forth,
    was she a love from lives before?
    Down at my feet, an iron key,
    and in my path a sterling door.
   
    Into the tower my will was bound,
    in darkness was the spiral found.
    Ascending those stone steps in fear,
    that I had forever lost the ground.
   
    I cringed in care of my destiny,
    but could not break my hastened pace.
    My soul sobbed mute, my breath in wait,
    impaled by the ghostly vision of her face.
   
    Would I climb for eternity,
    amid these million blocks of stone?
    Was she alongside healing me,
    or was this pain felt all alone?
   
    The heights grew near, I heard her voice,
    mine eyes sought for her desperately.
    Found nought but moonglow filtered down,
    from where she stood in wait for me.
