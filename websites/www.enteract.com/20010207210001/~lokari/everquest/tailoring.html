General Tailoring Notes

  • Verant is somewhat inconsistent in their use of "skin" and "pelt". For the most part they are interchageable terms - if you have a "skin", but a recipe calls for a "pelt", generally you can just go ahead and use it.

  • When combining stackable items (such as studs) in a sewing kit, don't stack them! Put each item in its own slot.

  • A smith can make an item called a "skinning knife" for you. You can use this knife to convert a high quality skin to medium quality, medium to low, and low to ruined. Just place the skin and the knife in your kit together and hit combine. The knife is not used up in the process. Note that this does not work with the named skins (like lion skin, or shadow wolf skin) - the knife is destroyed when you try an illegal combination.

    For those inclined towards smithing, the recipe for the skinning knife is - 1 small brick of ore + 1 dagger mold + 1 hilt mold + 1 water.

Trivial Guide

The "trivial" levels for items made in Everquest indicate the point at which you can no longer increase your skills by making that item. You will still experience failures making them, though. Some of these trivial levels are pretty certain, some are just estimates, based on conflicting information. Unfortunately, I can't test out trivial levels without starting up a new tailor every time an item is in question! Just bear in mind that these are close estimates, and some items may be off a few points.
  • Silk Thread - 15
  • Silk Swatches - 17
  • Silk Bandages - 21
  • Silk Cord - 26
  • Patchwork Armor - 26
  • Tailored Wrist Pouch - 26
  • Leather Padding - 31

Leather Armor

Tailors can create three kinds of leather armor – patchwork, studded and reinforced.
The size of the armor made depends upon the kind of pelt used, not the size of the sewing kit.

Silk Armor

Ingredients

All silk-based items require at least some of the following ingredients:

  • Silk Swatch

    Tailoring - These are made by combining two spider (not spiderling) silks in a sewing kit. Swatches become trivial around skill 17; however, they never fail.

  • Heady Kiola

    Brewing - Combine two kiola saps, one water flask, and one bottle in a brewers barrel. This item requires only a low skill at brewing, so any tailor should be able to make them as needed. The cost to make them is just under 3 gold each. Heady Kiola becomes trivial at brewing skill 46.

  • Viscous Mana

    Enchanter - It uses the 12th level enchanter spell Thicken Mana, which is available in the Brownie village in Lesser Faydark, and on an island in the Ocean of Tears. Casting the spell requires the material components of a poison vial (made by a potter) and a pearl.

Raw Silk Armor

Raw silk armor is wearable by Monks, Mages, Necromancers, Wizards, and Enchanters, of any race. Raw silk armor is made by combining silk swatches with the appropriate pattern. All raw silk items have a weight of 0.4. Raw silk becomes trivial to create at skill 36.

Cured Silk Armor

Cured silk armor is wearable only by monks. Cured silk armor is made by combining silk swatches and heady kiolas with the appropriate pattern. Remember that the heady kiolas and the silk swatches must not be stacked. All cured silk items have a weight of 0.1. Cured silk becomes trivial to create at skill 82.

Wu's Fighting Gauntlets

These magical glowing silk gloves are Monk only, AC 4, with a weight of 0.1, and give off light as a greater lightstone. The recipe is 1 glove pattern, 1 silk swatch, 1 greater lightstone, 1 vial of viscous mana, and 4 heady kiolas. Do not stack the heady kiolas. These become trivial to make at skill 88.

Quest Recipes

  • Shadow Silk

    1 Shadow Wolf Pelt, 1 Silk Swatch and 1 Scroll of Gather Shadows.

  • Large Shadow Silk

    2 Shadow Wolf Pelts, 1 Silk Swatch and 1 Scroll of Gather Shadows.

  • Inky Shadow Silk

    1 Shadow Wolf Pelt, 1 Silk Swatch and; 2 Scrolls of Gather Shadows.

  • Cabilis Necromancer Quest - Second Rank Skullcap

    Combine a "torn tapestry" and a "ripped tapestry", both found on Sarnak Hatchlings in the Lake of Ill Omen, in a sewing kit. Creates a "mended tapestry" which is Lore, No Drop.

  • Cabilis Monk Quest - Monk Training Bag

    Combine three granite pebbles and a "prepared leech husk" in a sewing kit.

  • Robe of the Lost Circle - Monk Quest

    This is a fairly high level monk quest. Combine one swatch of shadow silk and one bard song (Jonathan's Whistling Warsong) and one Needle of the Void (from Brother Qwinn in South Karana) and one Rare Robe Pattern (from Brother Zephyl in the Rathe Mountains). This yeilds the Robe of the Lost Circle (stats unknown). To get the Needle, you need a Purple Monk Headband and The Code of the Whistling Fist. To get the pattern you need a Red Monk Sash and the Idol of Zan Fi.

  • Patched Gnoll Fur Bundle

    On the second floor of the Halas bank, hail Cappi Mctarnigal. He will ask if you're a member of the White Rose. Tell him you are - he will ask you for mammoth calf hides but at the same time his fellow trainer, Vsanna MacGibbons, mentions gnoll fur. Ignore Cappi after this. Ask Vsanna "What gnoll fur", and she tells you to take four patches of gnoll fur and sew them together, then take four of this product and sew them together, and finally take four of that and sew them together. Yes, that's right, a total of 64 patches of gnoll fur are required. This creates the Patched Gnoll Fur Bundle, which you can either turn into Vsanna for the Fang of the Wolf, or keep it to wear - the bundle serves as a cloak, weight 3.0, AC +4, Save vs Cold +5, wearable by all.

  • Blanket

    This is a Velious quest that takes place in the Eastern Wastes. Combine two High Quality Tundra Kodiak Hides and two High Quality Snow Cougar Skins.

Other Items

  • Bearskin Potion Bag

    One grizzly bear skin and one silk cord. A bearskin potion bag has a combine button and holds 10 items of small size only. When you put 5 or 10 shaman-made potions into the bag and click the combine button, they will be stacked. Becomes trivial at skill 68.

  • Fleeting Quiver

    Recipe unknown.

  • Handmade Backpack

    One high quality bear skin and one backpack pattern. Handmade backpacks weigh the same as regular backpacks (3 pounds), but have 10 slots. Becomes trivial at skill 88.

  • Leather Padding

    One silk thread, and one low quality skin (wolf, cat or bear). Creates a Leather Padding, which is a stackable item used in smithing. It doesn't matter which kind of skin you start with - the end results will always be the same. Becomes trivial at skill 31.

  • Silk Bandage

    Two silk threads, unstacked. Silk bandages weigh 0.2 each, as opposed to a normal bandage at 0.5. Becomes trivial at skill 21.

  • Silk Cord

    Three silk threads, unstacked. Becomes trivial at skill 26.

  • Silk Thread

    Two spiderling (not spider) silks. Becomes trivial at skill 15, but like Silk Swatches, they never fail.

  • Tailored Quiver

    One high quality cat pelt and one quiver pattern. The tailored quiver has six slots, weighs 0.4, and holds only arrows. Becomes trivial at skill 115.

  • Tailored Large Bag

    One silk cord, and one low quality bear skin. Tailored large bags have six slots, and weigh 0.8 pounds. These bags reduce weight by about 5%. Becomes trivial at skill 41.

  • Tailored Large Belt Pouch

    One silk cord, and one low quality cat skin. Tailored large belt pouches have 6 slots (they cannot hold large items), and weigh 0.6. Becomes trivial at skill 37.

  • Tailored Small Bag

    One silk cord, and one low quality wolf pelt. Tailored small bags have four slots, and weigh 0.5 pounds. Trivial level at skill 33.

  • Tailored Whip

    One medium quality wolf pelt, four heady kiolas (unstacked) and one pattern. A tailor made whip has damage 7 and delay 33. Becomes trivial at skill 36.

  • Tailored Wrist Pouch

    One ruined wolf pelt and one silk cord. A tailored wrist pouch has four slots and weighs 0.3. Becomes trivial at skill 26.
