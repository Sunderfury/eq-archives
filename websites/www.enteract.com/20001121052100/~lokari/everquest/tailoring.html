General Tailoring Notes

  • Verant is somewhat inconsistent in their use of "skin" and "pelt". In general, they are interchageable terms - if you have a "skin", but a recipe calls for a "pelt", go ahead and use it.

  • When combining stackable items (such as studs) in a sewing kit, don't stack them! Put each item in its own slot.

  • A smith can make an item called a "skinning knife" for you. You can use this knife to convert a high quality skin to medium quality, medium to low, and low to ruined. Just place the skin and the knife in your kit together and hit combine. The knife is not used up in the process. Note that this does not work with the named skins (like lion skin, or shadow wolf skin) - the knife is destroyed when you try an illegal combination.

    For those inclined towards smithing, the recipe for the skinning knife is - 1 small brick of ore + 1 dagger mold + 1 hilt mold + 1 water.

Leather Armor

Tailors can create three kinds of leather armor – patchwork, studded and reinforced.
The size of the armor made depends upon the kind of pelt used, not the size of the sewing kit.

Silk Armor

Ingredients

All silk-based items require at least some of the following ingredients:

  • Silk Swatch

    These are made by combining two spider (not spiderling) silks in a sewing kit. Swatches become trivial at skill 15; however, they never fail.

  • Heady Kiola

    Brewing skill - Combine two kiola saps, one water flask, and one bottle in a brewers barrel. This item requires only a low skill at brewing, so any tailor should be able to make them as needed. The cost to make them is just under 3 gold each. Heady Kiola becomes trivial at brewing skill 46.

  • Viscous Mana

    Enchanter made - It uses the 12th level enchanter spell Thicken Mana, which is available in the Brownie village in Lesser Faydark, and on an island in the Ocean of Tears. Casting the spell requires the material components of a poison vial (made by a potter) and a pearl.

Raw Silk Armor

Raw silk armor is wearable by Monks, Mages, Necromancers, Wizards, and Enchanters, of any race. Raw silk armor is made by combining silk swatches with the appropriate pattern. All raw silk items have a weight of 0.4. Raw silk becomes trivial to create at skill 36.

Cured Silk Armor

Cured silk armor is wearable only by monks. Cured silk armor is made by combining silk swatches and heady kiolas with the appropriate pattern. Remember that the heady kiolas and the silk swatches must not be stacked. All cured silk items have a weight of 0.1. Cured silk becomes trivial to create at skill 82.

Wu's Fighting Gauntlets

These magical glowing silk gloves are Monk only, AC 4, with a weight of 0.1, and give off light as a greater lightstone. The recipe is 1 glove pattern, 1 silk swatch, 1 greater lightstone, 1 vial of viscous mana, and 4 heady kiolas. Do not stack the heady kiolas. These become trivial to make at skill 88.

Halfling Cultural Armor

Ingredients

Halfling cultural armor requires the following special ingredients:

  • High Quality Metal Bits

    Smithing skill - recipe unknown.

  • Vale Sewing Kit

    Smithing skill - High quality metal bits, needle mold, thimble mold, and a flask of water. Combine in the Rivervale forge. Recipe unconfirmed.

  • Acorn Oil

    Brewing skill - A flask of water and a Misty Acorn, combined in a brewing barrel. The acorns spawn on the ground in the Misty Thisket. Recipe unconfirmed.

Wood Elf Cultural Armor

Ingredients

Wood Elf cultural armor requires the following special ingredients:

  • Mithril Bits

    Smithing skill - 2 small pieces mithril ore, morning dew. Recipe unconfirmed.

  • Mithril Boning

    Smithing skill - small brick mithril ore, file, morning dew. Recipe unconfirmed.

  • Mithril Studs

    Smithing skill - 3 mithril bits, file, morning dew. Recipe unconfirmed.

  • Fier'dal Sewing Kit

    Smithing skill - mithril bits, needle mold, thimble mold, flask water. This recipe is currently bugged.

  • Oak Bark Tanin

    Brewing skill - A flask of water and a piece of Oak Bark, combined in a bewing barrel. The Oak Bark can be foraged in Kithicor and Warslik's Woods, and also occurs as a ground spawn in Butcherblock. This recipe is currently bugged.

Mithril Studded Leather

Mithril studded leather armor is created by combining the appropriate tailoring pattern with oak bark tanin, some quantity of mithril studs, and a Sabertooth Tiger Hide. Quest Recipes

  • Shadow Silk

    1 Shadow Wolf Pelt, 1 Silk Swatch and 1 Scroll of Gather Shadows.

  • Large Shadow Silk

    2 Shadow Wolf Pelts, 1 Silk Swatch and 1 Scroll of Gather Shadows.

  • Inky Shadow Silk

    1 Shadow Wolf Pelt, 1 Silk Swatch and; 2 Scrolls of Gather Shadows.

  • Cabilis Necromancer Quest - Second Rank Skullcap

    Combine a "torn tapestry" and a "ripped tapestry", both found on Sarnak Hatchlings in the Lake of Ill Omen, in a sewing kit. Creates a "mended tapestry" which is Lore, No Drop.

  • Cabilis Monk Quest - Monk Training Bag

    Combine three granite pebbles and a "prepared leech husk" in a sewing kit.

Other Items

  • Bearskin Potion Bag

    One grizzly bear skin and one silk cord. A bearskin potion bag has a combine button and holds 10 items of small size only. When you put 5 or 10 shaman-made potions into the bag and click the combine button, they will be stacked. Becomes trivial somewhere between skill 70 and 90.

  • Fleeting Quiver

    Recipe unknown.

  • Handmade Backpack

    One high quality bear skin and one backpack pattern. Handmade backpacks weigh the same as regular backpacks (3 pounds), but have 10 slots. Becomes trivial at skill 88.

  • Leather Padding

    One silk thread, and one low quality skin (wolf, cat or bear). Creates a Leather Padding, which is a stackable item used in smithing. Becomes trivial at skill 31.

  • Silk Bandage

    Two silk threads, unstacked. Silk bandages weigh 0.2 each, as opposed to a normal bandage at 0.5. Becomes trivial at skill 21.

  • Silk Cord

    Three silk threads, unstacked. Becomes trivial at skill 26.

  • Silk Thread

    Two spiderling (not spider) silks. Becomes trivial at skill 15, but like Silk Swatches, they never fail.

  • Tailored Quiver

    One high quality cat pelt and one quiver pattern. The tailored quiver has six slots, weighs 0.4, and holds only arrows. Becomes trivial at skill 115.

  • Tailored Large Bag

    One silk cord, and one low quality bear skin. Tailored large bags have six slots, and weigh 0.8 pounds. Becomes trivial at skill 41.

  • Tailored Large Belt Pouch

    One silk cord, and one low quality cat skin. Tailored large belt pouches have 6 slots (they cannot hold large items), and weigh 0.6. Becomes trivial at skill 37.

  • Tailored Small Bag

    One silk cord, and one low quality wolf pelt. Tailored small bags have four slots, and weigh 0.5 pounds. Trivial level unknown.

  • Tailored Whip

    One medium quality wolf pelt, four heady kiolas (unstacked) and one pattern. A tailor made whip has damage 7 and delay 33. Becomes trivial at skill 36.

  • Tailored Wrist Pouch

    One ruined wolf pelt and one silk cord. A tailored wrist pouch has four slots and weighs 0.3. Trivial level unknown.
