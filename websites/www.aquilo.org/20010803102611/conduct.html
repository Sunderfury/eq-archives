Aquilo Phoenix Rules of Conduct

Membership in Aquilo Phoenix means that you agree to follow some basic rules of conduct. Most of these are common sense, and should take no effort for any honorable person to follow.

As a general rule of thumb, remember the following principles:

  1. All of Verant's rules of conduct are also Aquilo Phoenix rules of conduct. These are viewable here.
  2. Disputes with other guilds or players will be handled by our guild officers, not by individual members.
  3. Be polite to other Aquilo Phoenix members and treat them with the same respect that you wish to be treated.
  4. Be mature in your words and deeds, and let your conduct be an example for others to follow.

If a guild member is accused of violating any of our rules, that member will be considered innocent until proven guilty of the charges. Guild officers will investigate any claims of wrongdoing. Should the party in question be proven to be in violation of our rules, guild officers will decide what punishment is appropriate. Please note, however, that some actions are considered to be so serious that the only recourse is removal from the guild.

Some specific rules of conduct are examined below.


Guild Tags Must Be Shown At All Times

We want our members to be proud that they are a part of the Aquilo Phoenix family, and to always remember that the actions they take reflect upon all of us. As such, we require that guild members always display their guild tag. Using /anon to mask your class, race, level, and guild is unacceptable. However, members can use the /roleplay command in situations where they don't want other players to know their class, race, or level. This is useful, of course, to avoid constant requests for teleports or resurrections.

Alternate Characters (Alts) Cannot Join Other Guilds

Members of Aquilo Phoenix may choose to play more than one character. Usually, the player will have one character that they play most often (referred to as their "main") and other characters that they play less often (referred to as their "alts"). The player may choose to have alts guilded, or may choose to have them remain outside the guild. However, alts are not permitted to join any other guild - they must be members of Aquilo Phoenix, or else remain guildless. Further, alts are expected to obey the same standards of conduct as mains. If it is found that the alt of a member, whether wearing our guild tag or not, has broken the Aquilo Phoenix rules of conduct, then all characters of that player are subject to removal from the guild. By joining Aquilo Phoenix, you agree to behave according to our rules. This doesn't just apply to characters on screen; it applies to the person behind the keyboard.

Do Not Kill Steal

When an individual or group has engaged an NPC in combat or is camping a spawn, it is unacceptable to deliberately attempt to take the kill from them. If a group appears to be in trouble, ask if they want your assistance before entering combat (unless, of course, it is obvious that they will die without your help). In situations where an individual or group is monopolizing an area and will not allow you to get on a waiting list or otherwise participate, you are expected to follow Verant's "play nice" rules and take the matter to a GM.

Example: You are logged in with your low-level character and go to fight at the orc camps. The group that is there is hostile towards you and tells you that you can't be in the area. You should file a /petition with a GM to resolve this. It is not acceptable for you to log in a higher-level character (or ask for the assistance of a higher-level character) to come to the camp and steal the kills from the group that is there. This is considered kill stealing, and proven cases will result 1 in removal from the guild.


Do Not Deliberately Train Other Players

Trains of NPC's, especially in dungeon environments, are a fact of life in Norrath. Accidental trains happen all the time, and while these are unfortunate when they cause harm to innocent players, trains are sometimes unavoidable. However, less scrupulous players may intentionally bring a train upon other players to drive them away from an area or to exact revenge for a perceived wrong done to them. Deliberately bringing a train of NPC's onto another player or players is considered a bannable offense by Verant, and Aquilo Phoenix looks upon this action no less harshly. Training is never justified, and any confirmed report of it being done by a guild member will result in removal from Aquilo Phoenix.


Do Not Bash Other Guilds

If you feel that members of another guild have wronged you, report the specifics of the incident immediately to one of our guild officers. They will attempt to resolve the situation. It is not acceptable for you to insult, badger, provoke, or otherwise engage members of another guild. Nor is it acceptable behavior for you to insult or belittle another guild in any public forum, including /say, /ooc, /shout, message boards, or guildchat.

Remember, guilds don't behave poorly; people do. Don't assume that the actions of one individual, or even a group, represent the beliefs or practices of an entire guild. We wouldn't want Aquilo Phoenix to be judged by something a single member did, and we should afford the same courtesy to other guilds.


Do Not Ninja Loot

"Ninja" looting means to sneak into an area and loot the kills of other parties. This term is most often applied to situations where high-level NPC's drop rare items. Ninja looting is despised by every honorable player in the game, and any guild member proven to participate in such an activity will be immediately removed from our ranks.


Do Not Farm Plane Breaks

Note: This mostly applies to Plane of Fear.

"Breaking" a plane means to enter when the plane is fully spawned, kill the roamers, and secure the entrance area for other members of your raid to join you. Once your raid members have killed every spawn (or as many as they intended to kill), the raid is ended and everyone leaves.

"Farming" is defined as staying in the plane after everything is dead and killing mobs as they respawn. This is considered dishonorable by most guilds in the game, as the real challenge in a plane raid is to break in and take control of the zone. Staying to kill respawns is much, much easier, and is done merely to farm items.

Even worse than farming your own break is to farm the breaks of others. This happens when the raiding party that breaks the plane leaves, and another party enters the plane and begins farming the respawns.

Aquilo Phoenix will not farm its own breaks, and members of Aquilo Phoenix are not allowed to participate in farming the breaks of another guild. Any member invited to farm a plane is expected to turn the invitation down. Because of the onus associated with farming a break, members who participate in such activity face removal from the guild.

Do Not Beg For Handouts

It is unacceptable, in guild chat or via /tell, to pester other guild members for donations of items or cash, teleports, resurrections, or to plead for help camping an item. While members are encouraged to help others in the guild, no one should feel pressured to give things away just because a guildmate asks for them. Members can ask for help completing quests, but they should do so in a polite and non-invasive manner.

Unacceptable: "Hey, can one of you level 50's come kill stuff in Mistmoore for me? I really really want my Armor of Ro!"

Acceptable: "I'm about to go to Mistmoore to camp a couple items for my Armor of Ro quests. Is there anyone who's free who could lend me a hand?"

Membership in Aquilo Phoenix is not about power leveling or "twinking," it is about being part of a family that helps and encourages one another to have fun and be successful. Beggars do not help the guild be successful, and thus will not be welcome.

Be Respectful and Courteous To Verant Staff Members

It is our desire that Aquilo Phoenix have a positive reputation with everyone in the game, including the Verant staff. Guild members should always be courteous to GM's and guides, even when they don't give us the answers we're looking for. At GM events, guild members are expected to either roleplay appropriately or observe quietly. Spamming, engaging in disruptive combat, spell casting, or otherwise interfering with GM events will not be tolerated.


Tolerate All Beliefs, Races, and Practices

Aquilo Phoenix does not allow its members to make comments, jokes, or attacks, in any public or private forum, against the race, religion, sexual orientation, or economic status of another player. We do not require that you agree with or even accept beliefs or practices that you find objectionable; we simply expect that you will keep your opinions private.

Please note that it is perfectly acceptable to make roleplaying comments about the races of other in-game characters. For example, it is fine for a human to refer to dark elves as "inkies," or for a dwarf to say that trolls are "oversized swamp rats." Such comments generally enhance the gaming experience when they are used in the proper context. It is when negativity is directed at the people behind the keyboards that action must be taken.
