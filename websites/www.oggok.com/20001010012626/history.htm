The history of Oggok and the Ogres themselves is a tale of war, triumph, treachery, and eventual defeat. During the second age the Ogres were a vast empire, unstoppable in might, with over eight separate strongholds, now only Oggok remains. Very few ogres remember their former glory, but those that do seek to restore it. Below is a breif passage from an Elven book of history. It breifly describes the events that lead up to the current state of the ogres and their society. Below that is a player submitted history section.
HISTORY

The Fall

It was during the second age of Norrath, when the gods still seeded the lands with life, that Rallos Zek, the warlord, did create the Orcs on Faydwer, and the giants and ogres on Tunaria (now called Antonica) The giants, being one people at that time, flourished in the Northlands, vast and fertile grasslands that stretched from the ocean to the great mountains both to the east and south. In seclusion they made huge cities and castles, and built a civilization rivaled at the time by very few of the other intelligent races. They were a strong nation, and worshipped their creator, the Warlord. Armies and outposts guarded the passes leading into the Northlands, and the few times their home was attacked, the invaders were utterly crushed.

Far to the south, another civilization grew to great power as well. The Ogres also worshipped the Warlord, even more fervently than the Giants. Their empire constantly expanded, eventually even to other dimensions, and upon invading the Plane of Earth they did do battle with the Council of Rathe. Initially the Ogres had victory over thes twelve mighty beings, but as one of these gods fell, soon another rose from the earth to take his place. The Ogres could do little against an eternal foe, and were eventually defeated, their armies forced to fall back through the portals that had brought them to that plane. Hoards of earth elementals and flesh eating plants followed them, even into Norrath, and southern Tunaria fell victum to the wrath of the Twelve Gods.

Rallos Zek looked upon the Ogres, and in seeing their defeat, grew angry. And while he was a lesser deity, he was still the god of war, and in his arrogance he too opened a portal to the Plane of Earth and led his forces to invade it. An inconceivable battle ensued, one of which history says little other than the heavens themselves shook, and in the end even the Warlord was defeated by the Council of Rathe. He and his minions were cast back into the Planes of Influence, and the greater gods came together, creating a barrier such that no lesser god or creature could ever pass into the Planes of Power again. They also saw if fit to punish Rallos Zek for his treachery and arrogance.

The Ogres, being already dispersed and defeated, were cursed. Thousand died instantly, all but the most basic of knowledges was removed from their midst and their empire was scattered. The surviving Ogres then began to dwell in small villages, warring on themselves over petty things, their former glory gone forever. The Orcs and Giants, also the Warlord's creation, were to feel their wrath as well. The fate of the Orcs is almost totally unknown, for they are not a people to keep history, concerned only for the present. But the Giants remember...

- Elven Book Of History
