Here's a quick rundown on the way to determine another mobs attitude towards you is with /con.

 
XXXX scowls at you, ready to attack. [REALLY hated]
XXXX glares at you threateningly [hates you, but *might* not attack if you don't hang around too long]
XXXX glowers at you dubiously [won't sell to you or talk with you except insults, but won't attack either]
XXXX glances at you apprehensively [you aren't their favorite person, but they will sell to and deal with you]
XXXX regards you indifferently [just what it sounds like]
XXXX regards you [they like you]
XXXX regards you warmly [they REALLY like you]
XXXX regards you abmerably [they REALLY REALLY like you, Best Prices]

 

Command Name

Command Description
Emote Commands
/nod You nod to the highlighted person
/panic You scream in panic.
/rofl You roll on the floor laughing.
/wave Your character waves. An animated emote.
/cry Your character crys. An animated emote.
/cheer Your character cheers. An animated emote
/bow Your character bows. An animated emote.
/panic Your character Screams in Panic.

Pet Commands
/pet as you were Makes pet forget all other commands including follow
/pet attack (with NPC targeted) Pet Will attack target if with in levels to fight (note Pet will not attack magical unless it has the ability too)
/pet back off Tells pet to quit attacking target (if you are he is attack he will continue to swing)
/pet follow me Pet's will start following you (cancels any other guard commands)
/pet guard here Makes your pet guard one location and only moves to and from to attack a target that is near him (NPC only Targets)
/pet guard me Pet will attack anything around you that is a NPC (Not good in Towns near Good NPC's)
/pet sit down Causes pet to sit in one place and rest much like you sitting down.
/pet stand up Causes pet to Stand.

Miscellaneous Commands
/played Gives you the birthdate & hours played w/ your character
/random (x) Not used a whole lot. Generates a random number between 1 and x. This can be used to decide things randomly, for instance, who gets a recently acquired treasure.
/shownames Turns on or off visible names above PC's and NPC's heads.
/split # # # # Splits a defined amount of money with your group: plat, gold, silver, & copper respectively...i.e. /split 4 3 2 0 splits 4 plat, 3 gold, 2 silver, and 0 copper with the rest of the group. All numbers must be present, use 0 for coins to not be split. Make sure you put the zeros in there or you will split more thatn you intended.
/time Gives you the time of day in Norrath. This is another command you may want to put in your hot keys, especially if you are a human and need to know how long it is untill it gets dark.
/who [filter] use filter by following command with "all" for the entire server (all can be used in conjunction with the other filters), a class (first letter of class will do) for members of that class, a race for members of that race, a name for characters with that name, "corpse" to list the number of corpses you have in the zone, or friends for a list of friends online] -- Brings up a list of characters in your zone or on the server per your filter. You should get familiar with using this command, since it is very useful. If someone invites you to join type /who his name to see his race, class and level. If you're missing a corpse, try /who b or /who n to find a bard or necromancer to ask for help. If you're not sure you have a corpse in a zone, /who corpse will tell you. Looking for a Cleric, try /who c. Wondering if your friend is in the game yet, try /who all his name and it will tell you.
/petition <msg> Used to request assistance for a GM. The more detailed <msg> is, the quicker the assistance can be rendered.
Speech Commands
/reply Sends a /tell to the last person who sent you a /tell message.
/ooc Makes an out of character statement to everyone in the zone.
/shout Shouts to all of the zone. Please don't use this for private conversations
/group to talk to your group.
/tell [name] Sends a message to the named character.
