1.0 General questions.

1.1 What is a FAQ?

FAQ stands for Frequently Asked Questions. FAQs are designed to cover the most common questions and answers regarding a product.

1.2 What is EverQuest?

EverQuest is a real 3D massively multiplayer online RPG. Another equally accurate way of describing EverQuest is to call it a graphical MUD.

1.3 What is an RPG?

RPG stands for Role-Playing Game. People often debate what this really means, so we'll attempt to clarify what we mean. When you play EverQuest you'll first create a character. This creation of yours can be any one of 12 races and 14 classes, male or female, and you even have the opportunity to adjust your attributes (for instance, how strong you are, or how intelligent you aren't). You'll then immerse this character of yours into our virtual world and make of him or her whatever you wish. You define your role in the lands of EverQuest and how well you play that role it is up to you. People also often assume that RPG means an certain theme or setting. While we don't necessarily agree that this is always true, EverQuest is a traditional RPG. Our world is one of high fantasy, of sorcery and dragons, of great wizards and marauding hoards of orcs. There are quests and puzzles to solve aplenty, but it's up to you decide which ones, if any, to pursue. You might choose to become a warrior bent on wanton destruction, or perhaps a merchant making his profit selling goods from one land to another. Essentially, while we've defined the setting, you create the world.

1.4 What do you mean by 3D?

We realize that the term 3D is loosely used almost everywhere today. In reference to EverQuest, however, we mean it quite literally. Our engine is truly 3D -- it is not a raycaster, nor does it use a pseudo-3D isometric view. You can walk completely around a house or any structure and then enter it, all the while adjusting the camera view between first person and any number of top-down or 3rd person viewpoints.

1.5 What's a "massively multiplayer online game"?

While many modern computer games support multiplayer gaming, few are designed to support well over 1,000 simultaneous players. With EverQuest, you don't start the server program on a local network and invite a few of your friends over to play. Instead, you run the EverQuest client program and use the Internet to connect to our World Server. This server will run 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, creating a truly persistent environment. Call or email your friends and tell them in what city or dungeon to meet you, or better yet log on and encounter hundreds of other people from all over the world and make a slew of new acquaintances.

That's EverQuest, and this is what massively multiplayer really means.

1.6 What's a MUD?

MUDs (Multi-User Dungeons/Dimensions) are online text-based RPGs that have been around for several years. EverQuest is similar to these MUDs, except it was designed from the ground up to depict our world using real-time 16bit color fully texture mapped graphics, as opposed to merely a well written paragraph.

Also, while most of these MUDs are run by a couple of people as a hobby in their spare time, EverQuest is a professional and commercial product -- our staff will be on hand 24 hours a day to meet your needs both as a player and as a customer.

1.7 Who are you guys? Where did EverQuest come from?

The EverQuest team is part of SISA (Sony Interactive Studios America), the same studio that's brought you PSX hits like Rally Cross and NFL Gameday '97.

We've taken the console world by storm, and now we're setting our sights on online gaming. EverQuest will be the second online title to be released by SISA, with many more to follow. Our first online game, Tanarus, has been released and is in stores now.. It's a multiplayer futuristic tactical tank combat game, and it's hot! Check out the web site, http://www.tanarus.com and download the latest version of the client software now.

1.8 What kind of hardware and software will be required to play EverQuest?

The EverQuest client program requires a fast Pentium, with a Direct 3D compliant 3D card and 32 megabytes of RAM. The program runs under Windows 95 or Windows NT. We recommend a 3D card at least as fast as the Voodoo 3DFX. Note that this is estimate, and is subject to change as game development progresses.

1.9 Which 3D cards will you support?

EverQuest will use Direct 3D, which will allow us to support most 3D cards currently on the market and those coming out.

3.0 Features.

3.1 What's the screen resolution for the game and number of colors?

640x480 16bit color (that's 64,000 colors).

3.2 Can we run the game at higher resolution if we have the horsepower for it?

Not sure about this yet.

3.3 How big is the game world?

Huge. It should take a very long time to walk from one end to the other.

3.4 What kind of communication will there be in the game? Will we be able to send messages privately or to broadcast to the entire server?

You'll be able to do the following:

1. Say things to people in your general area

2. Shout things across the zone you are in

3. Tell someone a message privately

4. Say things such that only people grouped with you can hear.

3.5 The game will be server based -- can I switch servers to go meet friends on another server?

Yes.

3.6 If I switch servers, will my character keep his levels and equipment or will I start new? And can I switch back and forth between servers?

We're still working this out -- off the top of my head, I'd say that level and items should transfer over, but certain unique artifacts may not. Also, we probably won't be able to allow constant switching between servers.

3.7 Will there be some other ways to travel than walking? Horses, boats or teleport spell?

Ships and teleport spells, yes. Horses we hope, if we have the room and can keep the poly count down. There will also be a few other more exotic ways to traverse the lands of Norrath.

3.7.1 When traveling in the wilderness between towns, is the player's view going to be first person the entire duration of travel, or will there be an overhead view of the map of the region?

Travelling long distances is exactly the same as walking around a city or dungeon. The views themselves are the same throughout the game -- you can choose from first person or from several third person viewpoints. As for the time it takes to walk from one zone to another, depending on the distance, it could take a while. Now that doesn't mean there won't be high level spells and perhaps some other ways to 'shorten' those distances.

3.8 How will fighting work? Real time or turned based?

Real time, but I must stress that this is not a 'twitch' game. It won't matter how quick you can click that mouse button. Weapons have speeds, and they will 'go off' (i.e. swing, fire, etc.) at that speed. It's like playing the piano to a metronome -- it's real time, but you can't exceed a set pace.

3.9 Will someone with a better connection have an advantage over someone with a poor connection?

Not during combat or other critical times. Certainly having a better connection will reduce lag and some warping, but it won't put you at a disadvantage necessarily in terms of game play.

3.10 Will there be night and day and how long does an EverQuest day last in real time?

Yes, there is a night and day. We are still tweaking how long each period will last -- I imagine this will be determined in the betas.

3.11 How dark is night? Do I need a spell or torch to see at night?

Well, many areas are lighted... but if you're talking about whether you can see with no torch or spells in the bottom of a cave, I'd say no. Unless you have infravision...

3.12 Will there be an active economy in the game?

Yes, there is an economy in EverQuest. Price will change according to supply and demand. If an item is offered more often then bought the price will go down.

3.13 Will there be weather in the game?

Yes, you will see rain, fog, wind, and this will affect gameplay (i.e. visibility, slippery surfaces, etc.).

3.14 How many cities and other zones will there be in the game?

There should be between 6 and 12 cities, and well over 20 adventure zones. And remember, the world will continue to expand after the initial release.

3.15 EverQuest is supposedly playable from different viewpoints. Has any thought been put into ensuring that no one viewpoint has an undue advantage over another?

It's pretty hard to look down a hall or a tunnel or a street in the top down view. Also, there's a lot of information and detail in the background textures that you might miss if you're not in 1st person mode. By their natures, different views have different advantages and disadvantages.

3.15.1 Is the EverQuest development team doing what they can to ensure a player won't be "unduly" hampered (or favored) by his or her choice of view?

If a person were to stick to any one view they would be at a disadvantage. The plan is to have several different user definable camera views that are toggled between depending on the situation. "Hmm, I can't tell whether or not I can jump across this ravine... (toggle to top down view) Oh man! That is farther than it looked in first person view.. I better find another way." Or (in first person view) "Get ready! There are a band of Orcs coming out of the forest!" (I would not have seen them if I were in top down view until they we almost upon my party and me.) So yes, if you stick to one view and only one view more than likely you will not live as long as someone who takes advantage of all the possible views.

3.16 As I recall, no existent screenshot of the adventure window shows a character from a first-person-perspective viewpoint equipped with either weapon or shield, a la Daggerfall, Meridian, and countless Doom clones. Why?

The latest screen shots (August) show swords in just about all the character's hands. Different weapons and shields will indeed appear wielded or worn by the character. Also, all the screen shots from August were taken from first person perspective mode. But in any case, the weapons and shields and such will show regardless of where the camera is.

3.16.1 The August screenshots (or most of them) show characters being looked at with swords in hand. However, the character that is doing the looking doesn't appear to be holding anything. Why?

The sword just isn't visible from the first person view because of where it is (kind of to your side). Were you to swing it or do one of many other animations, you would see it as well as your hand, arm, etc.

Keyboard or mouse – we will support mouselook.

11.0 Pkilling and death.

11.1 Will Pkilling be allowed in the game?

Yes and no. See #11.2.

11.2 I heard about a pk switch? How will this work? Is it something you select at the beginning when creating your character or is it something one can toggle on and off at will?

You select it when you create your character. If you choose to abstain from PK, only NPCs can kill you. Certain actions can switch you from non-PK to PK, and it may be possible to switch back, although if we do allow this, it will be a long and hard series of tasks one must complete before switching back to non-PK.

11.3 If I have my pk switch set to no-pkill, can I heal a friend while he fights another player?

Yes, but that will most likely result in you switching to PK.

11.3.1 If I have my pk switch set to no-pkill, can I heal a friend while he fights a monster without turning PK?

I believe a non-PK cleric will become PK anytime they heal a PK. (They knew the job was dangerous when they took it).

11.4 Can I steal stuff, or pickpocket with the switch set to no-pkill? That would make it impossible for people to get back at me.

You may not steal from another player if you are non-PK. You can, however, steal from NPCs. If you are PK, you may steal from both PK players and NPCs.

11.4.1 If I have my switch set to no-pkill and go adventuring with my friend who has his switch on and another player kills him, can I take his stuff before the PKer steals it?

A non-PKer will not be able to loot a PK corpse.

11.5 Are there negative consequences to Pkilling? Like getting arrested in cities, not being able to shop, or stuff like that?

Yes.

11.6 Will some areas be safe-zones or can you be killed anywhere even in shops?

No safe-zones, but there will be consequences if you do it in civilized areas.

11.7 Will there be guards in the cities?

Yes.

11.8 Can I log off from a fight to survive?

If you log off, your character will still be in the game for a few minutes. He will defend himself, but I'd say it's certainly not without significant risk.

11.9 If I lose connection during a fight, will my character defend himself or will he remain there doing nothing?

Yes, see #11.8.

11.10 In Tanarus, if you hit the ALT-Q, you actually send a signal to the server indicating you want to willingly leave the game. But if you lose your connection (local brownout, blackout, rodent chews through power cord, etc.) this signal is not sent. In this situation, couldn't you simply be listed as "XXXXX has been disconnected" without any penalty and without your character remaining in the game for a few minutes?

We would have to handle both cases in the same manner to avoid abuse by the less than honorable who would intentionally drop signal (ctrl-alt-del, reset, etc.) to avoid defeat.

11.11 Will there be any environments where a Non-PKer can challenge another player? Perhaps in a non-lethal way?

Yes, there will be Arenas and other locations where Non-Pkill characters can fight one another -- the fight is not to the death, but rather to unconsciousness, and the victor will be unable to loot the loser's corpse.

11.12 How will you deal with death? Is it permanent or do you just lose some stats, skills and stuff?

It is not permanent, however there are serious penalties. You will want to seriously avoid death, but if you do screw up, it doesn't mean that your character you've worked hard on for six months is gone.

11.13 Is there a limit to the number of times you can die?

No official limit, but given the penalties, you won't want to do it often.

11.14 When monsters die, will they remain as sprites or fade away slowly like in Quake?

Corpses deteriorate over time.
