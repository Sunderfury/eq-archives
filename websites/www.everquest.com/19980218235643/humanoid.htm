Humanoids

(human-like creatures, dwelling in the lands of Norrath and elsewhere).

 Goblin

Goblins are nasty scavengers. Everything they own or work with they either discovered or stole from somebody. Because of their lack of ingenuity, goblins are either found raiding towns, or in their lairs, which are typically primitive villages or damp natural caves. A solitary goblin is rarely a threat to even the most novice warrior, but their nature is to form clans and strike in groups. Wise adventurers, wary of that fact, avoid goblin settlements unless their numbers are also great.
Froglok

Step carefully in the swamps of Norrath for they are the domain of the Frogloks. Decades of slavery and persecution have hardened these once timid amphibious creatures into fierce guerrilla warriors. Many Troll war parties have fallen to swift and vicious Froglok ambushes. However, if treated with respect and well being, the Frogloks can be the greatest ally an adventurer can have while traveling the dangerous swamplands of Norrath.
Gargoyle

Carved from stone and then animated by powerful magic, the gargoyle is a fearsome foe indeed. Often mistaken for statues until it's too late, gargoyles will strike suddenly. Their skin being virtually impenetrable, combined with their ability to fly, makes them almost impossible to damage much less hit at all.

Orc

Bred by the gods for battle, Orcs are single-minded in their desire for conquest. They abuse the weak and quickly cower beneath the strong. Ideas and objects unrelated to war have no value to an Orc. They are the mortal enemies of Dwarves and Elves, and seek to slay them whenever possible.
Werebat

Several of these small, burly, creatures have been spotted deep in the caverns beneath Kaladim, lurking about in the shadows, and stalking the mining crews. Many a Dwarven Miner's disappearance has been attributed to these foul beings, whose only fear seems to be bright light. Rumor has it that these creatures may actually be Dwarves -- perhaps even the missing Miners -- that were bitten by strange bats that live in the deep mines and caverns, inflicting their victims with the curse of Lycanthropy. Or, maybe the sharp claws and long, dripping, fangs of the Werebats themselves are responsible for this curse.
Djinn

Sharing the Plane of Sky with the great dragons and drakes, the djinn are mages of immense power. But while they are both intelligent and strong willed, they all possess an unexplained and curious weakness -- experienced wizards and sorcerers of Norrath have been known to summon them from their native plane and imprison them in sealed rooms and smaller devices. Some legends speak of fantastic rewards for those who release a djinn, yet others speak of the violent deaths of those who unleash them.
Minotaur
Sand Giant

Of the myriad of giants that dwell in Norrath, Sand Giants are perhaps the most curious. They, unlike other giant-kin, are nomadic and dwell almost always in the desert or other dry and dusty locales. And while they are not overtly evil in their nature (one would never find Sand Giants raiding towns or other civilized areas), they are fiercely xenophobic. Their distrust of other races borders on the paranoid and many adventurers have sealed their fates in death by accidentally coming across a Sand Giant settlement while exploring. The fact that these settlements move and could be anywhere makes desert questing all the more perilous.
