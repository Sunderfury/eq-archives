Introduction

This page tries to describe the various tricks that can be done with a kendama.

The correct way to hold the kendama will depend on your preference, and the tricks you are doing. There are two main methods for holding the Kendama:
  • Pencil Grip: Hold the Kendama as if it were a pencil.
  • Sword Grip: Hold the Kendama as if it were a sword.
I prefer the pencil grip for most tricks, but the sword grip works better for other tricks.

There are three categories of tricks. The first is catches and involve holding the kendama with the ball dangling on the string, then catching the ball on some part of the kendama in some way. The second involves starting with the ball on the kendama, and tossing it from one part of the kendama to another. The third category includes stuff that does not really fit in the first two :-)

Catches

There are two ways to do most of the catches described here. The difference is in the way you bring up the ball.

  • Pull up: This method involves pulling the ball straight up and placing the kendama under it and catching it as it reaches it's highest point.
  • Swing up: In this method you swing the ball through 180 degrees and have it land on top of the kendama.
I describe the pull up version in most cases as I find that the easiest method.

CupCatch
Video Pull up the ball and catch it on one of the cups.


SpikeCatch1
Video For this catch pull up the ball (trying to keep the hole pointing down) and quickly move the kendama under it to catch the ball on the spike.


Spike Catch 2
Video For this catch hold the kendama horizontal (the two side cups will be facing sideways). Pull up the ball and catch it balanced on the side of the spike and the edges of the two cups.


Spike Catch 3
Video This one is tricky. Hold the kendama horizontal, with either side cup facing up. Pull up the ball and catch it balanced on the side of the spike and against the edge of the upper cup!!


Spike To Ball Catch
Video Hold the ball with the hole up and the kendama dangling. Swing the kendama up and catch it with the spike in the hole.


End Cup To Ball Catch
Video Hole the ball with the kendama dangling. Pull up the kendama and catch it on the ball, on the end cup. This trick is hard if the ball is slippery.


Large Cup To Ball Catch
Video As above but catch the kendama on the large cup. You have to swing the kendama to that it rotates enough.


Side Pull
Video Hole the kendama in the right hand, the ball in the left with the string stretched out in front of you. Pull the spike down wards so that the ball arcs across and lands on the end cup. Toss the ball back to the left hand.


Transfers

Cup To Cup
Video Toss the ball from one cup to another. I find with the pencil grip, large cup up, the small cup is the hardest to catch on as the hand has to be twisted right around.


Around The World
Video Start with the ball on the large cup. Toss it up. Move the kendama in a circle around the ball and catch it back on the kendama.

Double Around the World:
As above but move the kendama around the ball twice.



Cup To Spike
Video Hold the kendama like a sword with the spike pointing away and the larg cup facing up. Start with the ball on the large cup with the hole pointing up and back at a 45 degree angle (towards your face). Toss the ball up, and make the hole spin downwards and catch the ball on the spike.


Cup To Spike 2
Video Hold the kendama as above but with the two side cups horizontal. Start with the ball balanced on the side of the spike and the edges of the two side cups. The hole should be facing down. Pull the kendama back slightly, then push it forwards so that the spike goes in the hole.


Cup On Ball To Spike
Video Start by holding the ball and balancing the kendama on the ball with the end cup to the bottom. Toss the kendama of the ball, allow it to rotate 180 degrees to be caught with the spike in the hole.


Miscellaneous

Double Swing To Catch
Video Swing the ball up in a 180 degree arc. Release the kendama, catch the ball as it reaches it's highest point, swing the kendama up in the same arc, release the ball, catch the kendama, swing the ball up into a catch on the end cup.


Ball Drop To Spike Catch
Video Hold the ball, with the kendama dangling, spike pointing up. Release the ball, grab the kendama and let the ball land on the spike.
