Micro Juggling

This page describes the manipulation of small objects such as coins, pencils, cards, and others. Examples of the types of tricks described include walking a coin across the back of the knuckles, spinning a pencil through the fingers, and others.

Notation

There is a common set of notation and terms used throughout these pages.

In all descriptions the fingers are numbered 1-4 with the index finger being finger 1 and the pinkie being finger 4. The thumb is the thumb :-)

The joints are numbered 1-3 with 1 being the innermost joint and 3 being the outermost joint.

Outwards is in the direction of the fingertips.

For some tricks I describe the two faces of the coin as 'heads' and 'tails'. If you don't have a coin with heads and tails you'll have to improvise :-) Coins can also be held flatwise by holding the faces of the coin, or edgewise by holding the edges of the coin.

Dynamic - Dynamic moves involve imparting on the object a certain amount of spin so that it spins around the fingers in various ways until caught again.

Static - Static moves are ones in which firm control of the object is always maintained.

Tip - The bit of the pen you write with.

End - The other end of the pen.
