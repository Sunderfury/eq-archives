K.C.'s Guide To Making Banded Armor




K.C.'s Banded Guide
Last updated: 05/13/2000

Now that you are gaining lots of skill points in blacksmithing, you want to do something useful with your skill, right? Making banded armor is the main profit potential, i.e. the "Bread and Butter" of our trade.

Everybody has different advice of when to start making banded armor. Since higher skill level = more success, I recomend that you wait as long as possible to start. At least 90 for 1 sheet items, 105 for 2 sheet, and 125 for 3 sheet items. Banded is NOT a very good way to raise skill level. It goes up very slow, and is extremely expensive. Make banded to make yourself some money.

While you are making skewers, pots, or sewing kits, auction your wares. I always make these items in East Freeport, so when someone wants banded, I go to the armor shop, buy the stuff, and make the armor on the spot.

There are three sizes of armor: small, medium, and large. Keep this in mind when someone asks for armor, make sure you ask them what size they want. There is nothing more annoying than going through the trouble of making a piece of armor, only to find out that it doesn't fit!

I am going to list the items bye the amount of sheets of metal they require. *NOTE* Boots, Mantles, Cloaks, and Gauntlets are a LOT harder than the rest of the armor in the same sheet range. If anyone has any information as to when banded items become trivial, please SUBMIT it.

All of these recipes are found in the ARMOR & WEAPONS GUIDE.

1 SHEET ITEMS The following items require 1 sheet of metal:

  • Gorget
  • Bracer
  • Boots

They are all made the same way, 1 sheet of metal, 1 flask of water, and 1 mold. Gorget molds are used to make gorgets, boot molds are used to make boots, etc...
