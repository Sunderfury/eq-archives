As you request Syfe, here it is:


Quote:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In working with the people doing the strategy guide for Kunark we decided to provide them with general information as to what the stats do.
I'm going to post them here.

This is a change in the sense that we were not going to discuss them or release more info. We changed our minds.

That said, we're not posting any formulas, and it's absolutely true that some stats have considerably more direct impact on gameplay than others. 

We're posting this for clarification, and because previous explanations have not been accurate (manual, etc). We will not, however, engage in debate and our stance on how much some stats affect game play is that it's by design to inhibit some aspects of twinking. Are there better attribute systems out there? Yeah, but we're not going to change something as fundamental as a stat system in EQ after the game's been out for more than a year.

Anyway, here you go:

DEX: how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills; how hard you hit with bows

STR: determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills

STA: affects how many hit points you have, how long you can hold your breath

INT: directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights

WIS: affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT.

CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular)

AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit

-Brad

Here's something for you str geeks to look at. The MN formula :

Magic Number (MN) = (2 x wpn dmg) + lvl bonus

This is used for ALL weapons, yada yada. Magic number is basically the number you're going to hit for ALOT of times. This number does NOT change due to str, DOES NOT change. The only things that affect this are level & weapon damage. Not even weapon skill has anything to do with this.

The level bonus formula for two handers (which has been found to change at 51) is as follows :

2H level bonus (51+) = (Level -7) / 3




That was parsed at level 52 using a 2hb weapon with a 25 dmg rating (Rod Of Lamentation), 128 str, and 205 skill. Those red bars judge how often I hit for a certain number of damage. The more the red the more times I hit for that.

Now...
My level bonus : (52-7)/3 = 45/3 = 15
My Magic Number : (2 x 25) + 15 = 50 + 15 = 65

Anyone care to notice what number the tall red bar hovers over? oh, look : 65. Notice where the shortest red bar is? The one I hit for like 0.01% of the time? Hey look, its at 99, my MAX dmg.

Oh, and what does STR affect? Max dmg. and how is max dmg calculated? let's see...

Max Damage (MAX) = (((Str + Wpn Skill)/100) x Wpn Dmg) + level bonus

NOTE : I think there is a small flaw with this formula, you shall see in a moment, but it MINORLY affects the outcome therefore is still viable

So my max would be :

Max = (((128 + 205)/100) x 25) + 15 = (3.33 x 25) + 15 = 82.5 + 15 = 97.5, round that on up to 98.

Well sheesh, I hit for 99? Damn.. that str may have tapped me up an extra point, but then again maybe not. Just look at how many times I hit for MAX. Boy, I bet that REALLY made a difference in my taunting abilities & Average damage.

What if I stacked another hundred str in? anyone care to guess what my max hit would be? and how often I'd hit for that or any numbers near it? Let's see.

Max = (((228 + 205)/100) x 25) + 15 = (4.33 x 25) + 15 = 123.25, round that off to 123.

Keep in mind your MN will NOT change due to str. How many times you think you're gonna hit anywhere near 123? You are not changing the MN, but you are adding more numbers you can hit, therefore, YES, str does affect average dmg, but in 3000 swings and you hit for over 100 say 50 times, you really think that's gonna change much of any outcome in the battle?

I should also mention that your hit % DOES NOT get affected by str, it is a complete standalone attribute fixated on your level, skill, and mob level and there ac. If you think ATK means you're gonna hit more, you're wrong.
