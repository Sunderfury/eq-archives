Well, I disagree. The stacking of this spell is going to have to be kept in line with the other spells. Making it stack with everything just to make it ‘special’ for paladins isn’t a good reason.

I’m thinking that adding more disciplines isn’t a great idea. I’m reluctant to start stuffing in disciplines to fix things. But I’ll add it to the list, assuming that we do not do something like your number 3.

>”5) Reduce the reuse time of LOH post level 50. Make it gradually less until level 60. Make it a Complete Heal at level 60.”<

No. Look, LoH is good as it is. Perhaps an increase in its healing power is in order. But reducing the reuse time isn’t appropriate (it’s kind of the basis power for recast times), and Complete Heal is out of the question. I’ll add a suggestion for an increase in the healing power of LoH, but I think it’s fine as is.

>”6) Add Yaulp IV to our spells at level 59.”<

Hmmm... I’ll add it. But there is probably a reason it wasn’t added with the last changes.

>”7) Raise our TAUNT skill. Again, we are defensive tanks. It is currently too hard to taunt mobs off of classes which deal much more damage than us.”<

Every fighting class seems to want a better ability to taunt. Folks, taunt is a group thing, not an individual thing. Your group needs to be controlling their aggro. And we just can’t be setting it up so that any character can flip a switch to gain the attention of their enemy.

Again, I’ll add it to the list. But we’ll certainly have to look at this is relationship to everything else.

Paladins and SKs are the only two melee classes that use shields the majority of the time; so much so tank looking types with shields are automatically assumed to be a hybrid since ?real? tanks don?t use them. Clerics, Shamans and/or Druids sometimes use shields, but also sometimes use books or other items in the off hand. A maxed Bash skill makes sense for Paladins.
7) Disarm and Archery; both skills cap low enough to make them effectively useless. Spells
1) Move the Armor line down: Guard to 39, Armor of Faith to 49.
2) Move the Yaulp line down: Yaulp II to 30, Yaulp III to 39, Yaulp IV to 49.
3) Move Superior Healing from 57 to 53-54. Reduce fizzle rate in line with other previously noted comments on SH fizzles.
4) Add Remedy at 57-58.
5) Move Revive to 39, Resuscitate to 49, Resurrection to 56, Reviviscence to 59. The reason?s pretty basic. Travel spells are shared by two classes (Wizards and Druids). Damage Shields are shared by two classes (Druids and Mages). Primary Pets are shared by two classes (Mages and Necromancers). Speed spells (SoW) are shared by two classes (Druids and Shamans). All the ?market spells? are available from at least two separate class sources, those spells which are generally being sought after. Except Resurrections (specifically what people want when they want a Res, which is some of their lost time back in the form of returned death XP); Clerics are always preferred since they have the highest Res. Doubly so since 59+ Paladins are in such short supply. Granting the hybrid this ability of the parent (Cleric) would not impact the parent?s tactical abilities, only the support slot. And it brings parity to the market of spells we all eternally have varying needs for as we play the game. I haven't met a cleric yet that'd object to help in taking care of the eternal cries for 'Can anyone XP Rez?'

1) Add a ?Lichbane? 45-50+ version of Ghoulbane, appropriate for the level range it would be used in. Basically, an upgraded version of the popular mid level Paladin sword. 4) Reexamine the Paladin Epic; is it comparable to other tank epic weapons? Why is it 2hS when many Paladins are using shields? Combat Problems:

To make an encounter difficult and require challenging use of skill is one thing; to make an encounter unalterable through any means is another.
