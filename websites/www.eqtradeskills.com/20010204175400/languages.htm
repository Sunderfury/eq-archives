Now, on to the nitty gritty.

How to Train and Learn Languages in Norrath


Let's say that Raul the Half Elf and Meurtos the Gnome want to meet and trade languages. They get together in the same zone, preferably a very safe place such as a city, and group. (I did a lot of my lessons near the Greater Faydark and Crushbone zone). They go to a zone boundary and, without crossing it, sit down facing each other next to the zone line. 

Now, Raul must set up an "auto-spam" macro. He does this by picking one of the buttons on the Socials menu and right clicking on it. That brings up a new window with a name (which Raul can change to "auto-spam" or whatever name he prefers) and five lines. Raul should fill up all 5 of those lines with /groupsay statements such as: 

/g Let us begin our language lesson. 
/g my name is Raul and I'm a Half-elf. 
/g I get the girlies like no one else!. 
/g The woodies, the high ones, the dark ones too, 
/g And I'll leave the ogres and trolls to you.. 

(a small poem by Raul) 

Then Raul should drag this new Social button over to his hotkey box and drop it in an available slot. Raul now has a hotkey which will /groupsay those five lines whenever he activates it. 

Next, while he is in windowed mode, Raul right-clicks on the chat window. This brings up a new window which includes such information as which language Raul  is speaking. Raul left-clicks on the language until it cycles to "Elvish." He then right-clicks on it to dismiss that new window. 

Meanwhile, Meurtos has been doing the exact same thing, except that he has selected "Gnomish" as the language he will speak. 

Now the lesson begins. Meurtos and Raul begin to user their hotkeys (either clicking with the mouse or pressing the right numeric key, whichever you find more convenient) to "auto-spam" each other with their respective language. Meurtos yammers at Raul in Gnomish, and Raul chatters right back at Meurtos in Elvish. While this is happening, lines of gibberish are filling up their chat windows so fast that there is no possibility of successfully using the /say or /tell commands, or hearing anything shouted or told or said to them. This is why they need to be in a safe place (like a city); they cannot hear any warnings anyone tries to tell them, and moreover, if a monster does run up and start hitting them, they will not even notice until they see the change on their health bar. 

There is a way around this, however. Simply go to the "chat filters" submenu of the "options" menu, and turn group chat off. The you will not see the other person's auto-spam, though your skill in that language will still improve. 

While the lesson is underway, each should see the message "Your language skills have improved!" appear in their chat window from time to time. It is necessary to watch closely, as it scrolls off very quickly. Eventually Meurtos or Raul will stop seeing that message. When that happens, he should stand up. Standing up is the signal that the language lesson needs to stop at least temporarily. Now both of them should cross the zone line. Zoning is necessary because otherwise you will not be able to judge your progress. If you check your skill level in the language window, it will still appear to be at the same level UNTIL you zone.

Once both of them are in the new zone, Meurtos and Raul should check their skill list to see how good they are at their new languages. If Meurtos's Elvish is now "Excellent" and Raul's Gnomish is also now "Excellent," then the language trade has been a complete success. If that is not the case, then they need to sit down and repeat the process until they both speak the other's language Excellently.  You can of course do this with more than two people.  You can have an entire group and each learn 5 new languages at once!
