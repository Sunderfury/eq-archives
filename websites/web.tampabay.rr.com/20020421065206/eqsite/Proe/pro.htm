Daelin


Daelin, Half-Elven Bard, worships Rallos Zek.


Will be changing alignment to Discordant Neutral.

Daelin grew up in Kelethin.  His late mother was a Human Bard whose travels led her across the face of Norrath, and while studying with the Songweavers guild of the tree-city, she came to fancy a Wood Elven Warrior, a reserve member of the Guard.  Their son, a charming and witty young half-elf, joined the Songweavers, inspired by tales his mother would tell him as a child, but also because he was so very moved by a recital by that guild -- a telling of an epic battle.  The glory of combat, struggling through the strife of enemies for joyous victory:  these feelings stirred a fervent passion within Daelin, whose adopted name means "Warrior," and he found himself praying to Rallos Zek.

He would break away from his studies of songs and swordsmanship to attend many recitals and events staged within Kelethin, most of which revolved around tales of epic heroism.  During this period of his idealistic studies, he met Deilya, a lovely but troubled young Wood Elf. She was orphaned, he learned, thanks to the Crushbone Orcs, and they shared many a strangely intimate evening discussing combat tactics and her main goal in life -- the destruction of every Orc, everywhere.  They shared many journeys of learning to Ak'anon, learning from their zoo and appreciating the Tinkering of the gnomes, and made many friends among the smaller folk, but journeys to Kaladim had to be undertaken only by Deilya, as the Dwarven Guards refused to allow passage to Daelin, because of rumors of his fealty to Rallos Zek.

During his studies of lore, Daelin discovered that the Orcs claim their creator was Rallos Zek himself, and he  worried that this fact could damage his relationship with Deilya.  The subject, however, has never come up, perhaps because both of them were avoiding it.

Now that the pair have both officially met the requirements of their trainers, teachers, and guildmasters, they travel almost exclusively together. Daelin seeks out the glorious conquest of battle, carving his destiny as a warrior poet.

My eyes glazing with the brewing scents,
The heat of battle wafting like incense;
Slowing time as my song cries aloud,
Swinging swords pierce the hazing shroud.

Muscles carry me through the raging sea
Of bodies and weapons and magery,
Chanting my dissonance, striking sounds
Across yielding and crumpling enemy mounds.
