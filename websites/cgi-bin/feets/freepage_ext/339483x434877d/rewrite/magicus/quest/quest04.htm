Chalice of Conquest

This is a long involved quest that results in the Holy Partisan of Underfoot, and perhaps an even better weapon, since there is an implication that there is more than what I have here. For those who wish to just know how to start the quest, read below. Dru Razbind who is fishing just north of Port Faydwer tells you he can no longer venture into Kaladim because of a shame he suffered. He used to be a respected Paladin of the Church of Underfoot, until he failed to complete a very [important task] for Lord Datur Nightseer, who then striped Dru of his Cape of Underfoot, without which he can't enter Kaladim anymore. When you ask him what the important task was he says... "I was ordered by Lord Nightseer to carry the [Chalice of Conquest] to a noble band of Paladins within the mountains of Rathe. While there, the entire camp was overtaken by a band of orcs. During the battle there was a [strange occurrence]. " When you ask him what the Chalice of Conquest is he says... "The Chalice of Conquest is said to be delivered to the Church of Underfoot from Brell himself. It is said whoever drinks from the chalice is assured victory in battle. It is also said that only good natured creatures can safely drink from it and only a state of comatose is assured to any evil creature who dares to sip from it." When you ask him about the strange occurrence he says... "A large fray raged. Brave, valiant paladins fought vile green skinned Orcs and while this happened I spied the [Chalice of Conquest] being taken away from the camp. I was then distracted by a well placed Orcish blade. After I defeated the Orcs within my way I turned to see a blue Orc running towards the horizon. I gave chase to no avail. I lost the chalice and all respect. If only a [noble Paladin of Underfoot] would assist me." If you tell him that you are a noble Paladin of Underfoot he replies..."Yes, you shall do noble <name>. Take this. This is the Chalice Case. It is intended for the Chalice of Conquest and for the [Candle of Bravery]. If you should get both then be sure to place them within the Chalice Case and combine it. Take the full Chalice Case to Lord Nightseer in Kaladim and I shall be redeemed and you shall surely earn a fine paladin's reward. Let your quest begin in the mountains of Rathe." When you ask him about the Candle of Bravery he replies... "The Candle of Bravery is ceremonial and is lit when one sips from the [Chalice of Conquest]. The candlestick was left with a fellow paladin of Underfoot within the Rathe Mountains. The candle itself was lost, and I do not know where Priestess Ghalea purchases them."

Ogre Heads

In the warrior guild in Kaladim there is a dwarf male named Byzar Bloodforge. He is looking for the heads of the ogres Zarchoomi and Corflunk who roam the countryside of Butcherblock. If you ask Byzar "What about Corflunk?" He responds with, don't mention those names, I hate those ogres and would pay handsomely for their heads. He does the same if you ask about Zarchoomi. You must give him both heads, and then he will say he underestimated you and give you experience, faction, some coin and a decent item (This used to be blackened mail, but now you seem to only get various pieces of bloodforge mail, which generally has the same stats, but can only be worn by dwarf clerics, paladins or warriors). Your faction with Stormguard, Kazon Stormhammer, MinersGuild 249 and Merchants of Kaladim will all get better, and your faction with CrakenkWarriors gets worse. He will then give you the Trumpy Irontoe Quest.

Orc Runner No 2

This quest is given by Dill Fireshine after you do the orc hatchet quest. You need to kill the orc runner and return his damaged pouch to Dill Fireshine. He will also ask you for a note which can sometimes be found on the orc runner. The orc runner can be found in butcherblock. He runs around a lot, but the easiest place to find him is by the two cabins hunting area. The faction changes are as follows: Faction with Faydarks Champions, King Tearisthex, Clerics of Tunare and Soldiers of Tunare gets better, and Crushbone Orcs gets worse. You sometimes get a pretty nice reward for this (sometimes worth several platinum), and a decent experience boost.
