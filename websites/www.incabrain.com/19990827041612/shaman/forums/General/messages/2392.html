Well, I guess I am uniquely qualified to answer this one. I played a mage (Zevon Von Zevon) to level 22, then switched to Shaman (currently Grun at Lvl 18).

Don't let the grass-is-greener syndrome get to you. Sure, a mage can take down yellow mobs a shaman can't. But as soon as two mobs appear you are doomed! The pet can only tank one at a time, and at higher levels the mobs come after you once you start to help your pet.

If you want to take on solo mobs one at a time, the mage is better. But this guy can't live in a dungeon or crowded area. He's dead meat in any place with multiple mobs, even in a group. And Shaman are invited into a lot more groups than mages.

Also, this Shaman has lived in many situations (SoW) where the mage would be dead.

As a Shaman I miss taking out those white's and yellows solo. The Shaman must settle for blues or whites solo. But the Shaman goes up in level faster. Zevon hit lvl 20 in 9 days 16 hours. Grun is at level 18 at 6 days and a few hours. I play both about the same level of "power-gaming". Remember the mage can beat yellows but shares the XP.

Also, Shaman does much better in mobile groups. In moving and zoning groups, mages can't keep a pet up or near, and can't med, so is pretty useless.

Finally, keep in mind Shaman IS a pet class! Heard thos pets get pretty good at higher level -- our lvl 34 pet cons blue to my lvl 35 necro friend.

That's why I switched to Shaman... pets and nuke, and actually able to survive two mobs. I miss those 150 bolt spells and elemental, but not the inabilty to go anywhere there are multiple targets.

Finally, remeber mages get in big trouble after disconnects! They lose their pets and often summoned stuff... they re-appear surrounded by baddies without their main weapon (pet).

When Grun hits 22 I'll give a better comparison, but so far Grun is better. The mages also had little to look forward too while Grun's goal is to get a pet.
