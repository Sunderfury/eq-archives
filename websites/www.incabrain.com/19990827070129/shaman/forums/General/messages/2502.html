When Greyweasel mentions how the number of hits vs. misses factors into the equation, he's referring to the fact that most comparisons between slow and fast weapons of proportional damage take the theoretical case that you hit every single time for full damage. Here's an example:

Let's say you have

-Fast Weapon: dmg 4, delay 20
-Slow Weapon: dmg 12, delay 60

(where every 10pts of delay are roughly equal to one second of real-time)

Theoretically, they should do about the same damage, assuming you always hit, and each time for maximum damage. Let's say you have a 18-second melee (or 180 ticks):

-with Fast Weapon, you'll get (9 attacks) x (4 dmg) = 36 dmg
-with Slow Weapon, you'll get (3 attacks) x (12 dmg) = 36 dmg

(I know my math isn't holding exactly to formula here, but it's consistent with this example.)

Now let's take the slightly more realistic case of an 18-second melee where your weapon skill is such that you only miss once (but hit every single other time, and for full damage):

-with Fast Weapon, you'll get (8 attacks) x (4 dmg) = 32 dmg
-with Slow Weapon, you'll get (2 attacks) x (12 dmg) = 24 dmg

See the difference? In most melee combats (especially if you're a non-melee class like a shaman), you won't always hit with every swing. You'll miss. This reduced total damage in the event of a miss is part of the tradeoff of slower, more powerful weapons vs. faster, weaker weapons (and for the most part, part of the tradeoff between 2H vs. 1H, except for some of the more insanely low delay/high dmg weapons in Lower Guk, Cazic, Kedge, etc.). Many newbie tanks will jump on the rusty halberd (dmg 10, delay 51, I think) over the less impressive rusty longsword (dmg 5, delay 35, I think), but the smart ones will change their tune the first few times they miss in combat and realize how agonizing it is to wait for that next swing.

No matter how long you swing for, or how many swings you take, each shot is only going to hit on a 1 in 10 chance. The more swings you take, the more likely your average is going to come out to actually hitting about 10% of the time. The less you take, the more random the case is, and the less likely you get that 10% average. The formula for calculating damage ratio is based on the ideal that you hit constantly and do average damage each time.
