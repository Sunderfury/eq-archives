Ranger Guide

Summary

Well what can be said about the Ranger Class? First of all the facts, from the Everquest site...
"The Ranger is a meld between a warior and a druid, sharing some of the skills of both. They are at home in the outdoors"
To elaborate, the Ranger is a druid basically, or a cousin of the druids, if you will. The difference is that they don't have the spellcasting ability of the druids. In it's place, though, they are pretty good warriors. By this I mean that they are able to wear all kinds of armor and weild almost any weapon. They also have a wonderful skill called track. No one else has this skill (except Druids, but they don't get it until level 20). This one skill makes Rangers very useful and highly sought after for groups. This is for a couple of reasons. One: they can quickly track and find the monsters good for the group. Two: they can help the group avoid monsters they aren't ready to fight just yet.

Rangers are a pretty well rounded charcter. Fairly good fighters, archers, they have good skills, and they are able to cast magic. They can be humans, wood elves, or Half elves. At level 9 they gain the spellcasting abilities. I highly enjoy playing rangers, and in my humble opinion Rangers are one of the best and most versitile classes. ;-)

Other Information

The Ranger if played right is a great class. In my experience it's best to group right around level 7; before then is pretty well useless. I'd take the time from 1 till 7 to work on the Tracking skill, sense heading, and maybe something like tailoring. At level 3 you get forage, make good use of this skill it'll save you some money. As soon as you get level 5 grab that kick and start useing it as much as possible. Also, take advantage of dodge when you get level 8. When it's time to start grouping you should be well on your way to being a good character. I suggest grouping with Clerics. Mostly because I feel that this is an ideal group. A cleric and a ranger can take on 'bout near anything. Also, remember that you are a ranger and how does everyone see rangers (or at least what's the usual way people look at Rangers)? Well, they are guides, they know the land, and they can hunt monsters pretty easily. So it's a good idea to explore and get to know an area that you intend to go to. That way you're not totally lost (i.e. you track an orc, run off into the wilderness, and POOF you're lost). That's no fun at all and can lead to lots of frustration. So have an idea of the layout of the land you're in when you're grouping.
