I play both a necro and a mage... (Actually, I have each a character of each class a Dark Elf can be, save rogue. Yes, I know, I'm sick.) Anyways, that said, bear in mind that neither is my primary character (my cleric is) so take what I'm saying with a grain (or whole shaker-full) of salt.

Here's how I see it: Mages are the game's master summoners, so it would make sense that their pets would be more powerful. But it seems there should have been a way around nerfing necro pets in the process of improving mage pets.

Magicians are more mana efficient when it comes to pets, buffing, shielding, and equipping them -- but necros are capable of the most powerful increase in pet performance (granted, their buff requires more mana to maintain and has a shorter duration than magician pet buffs). I believe necro pets still attack faster, and in the right circumstances, are easier to keep alive (using darkness and fear to keep things from beating on the pet, and if need be, they can pull health from the mob and use pact spells to keep their pets alive).

Using darkness and fear, a necro can sort of... reverse kite, making it so their pet takes less damage. Magicians don't have that ability as they have no way of slowing down a mob (aside from having an earth pet root it. Heh). Since magicians can't root, they have to rely on being able to resummon new pets quickly to replace the ones they lose during combat. (And, yes, I know mage pets have more HP than necro ones. I sort of see mage pets as damage absorbers, akin to warriors, and necro ones as being damage dealers like monks, but that's a whole other stupid spiel. )

I'll agree with Pithello in that necros can be more adept at soloing than mages, primarily because soloing (to me) is safer with a necro than with a mage. Necros gain mana back faster, if there's something nice and green around they gain HP back faster, a couple tricks a magician can't touch... I also agree with Sevok: I think a necro's (stackable) DoTs can ultimately do more damage than a mage; a mage just get all of his damage off in one big BOOM instead of over a period of time.

I don't really think the patch made necro's weak, but it was a downgrade in their performance...
