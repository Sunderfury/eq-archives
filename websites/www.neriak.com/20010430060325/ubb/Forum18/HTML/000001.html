I play an evil human cleric named Thundarbreath. He is not an overt murder or thief. Instead, this pathetic little worm prefers a more subtle way to serve his master, the Lord of Disease, Bertoxxulous.
Thundarbreath desires treasure and power. The fewer people around, the more cash and prizes for Thundarbreath and the more cash and prizes, the greater the power.

As a cleric and master of disquise, thundarbreath sneaks his way into the camps of his enemies posing as a traveling "do-gooder" and offering free healing services. Usually the need for healers is great following a battle with the armies of the undead. This is usually when he makes his move.

Bertoxxulous, as we all know, is the plaguebringer and seeks to spread plague and disease. His followers are the Bloodsabers. Thundarbreath, being a loyal follower, enjoys making his enemies suffer a long painful death and obediently helps to spread the disease.

How does he do this, you ask? When he is tending to the wounds of his enemies he applies just a dab of plague or disease to their wounds using a dirty bandage (which he has prepared beforehand). These enemies then become very sick and die a slow death. Much to Bertoxxulous' delight not only has Thundarbreath managed to kill an enemy of the Bloodsabers but has also most likely managed to spread the plague and disease to the entire opposing army. Bravo Thundarbreath!

Many of you may be wondering if the name Thundarbreath has any inherent evilness. Well, you see, Thundarbreath also seems to have a sweet tooth for eating rotting flesh, a habit he picked up while locked up for over a year by the Priests of Life in one of their "salvation" prisons.

Some say that eating the rotting flesh of a powerful enemy can produce visions of great meaning. By performing a ritual chant, casting some bones and eating the flesh of one who you have killed you can get visions that will answer almost any question. Some say that Bertoxxulous himself sometimes speaks to Thundarbreath directly through these visions.

Where were we, oh yes, back to the name....As a young cleric, Thundarbreath had a name like other humans, can't recall what that name was now, doesnt matter though...not many know him well enough to call him by name. All those that new him by name died from plague (snicker). How strange. Maybe, in Thundarbreaths line of work, you don't want to be recognized by alot of people, better to keep a low profile.

So how come the name? Apparently eating rotting human flesh makes your breath smell like burnt ashes. Thundarbreath has eaten so many of his enemies that his breath really began to stink. Someone once joked that his breath had the smell of cow manuer cooked by lightning. His voice was so deep and hoarse that it sounded like thunder. People started to refer to him as thunderbreath. Somehow over the years this became thundarbreath. Sad that no one is left alive to tell how the transition took place.

How does your character live and practice his/her craft? What rewards does he/she receive? What makes him/her so evil?
