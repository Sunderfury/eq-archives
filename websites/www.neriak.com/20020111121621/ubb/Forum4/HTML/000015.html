Greetings from Quellious. I've come not for the purposes of creating / participating in a discussion of roleplaying, but rather to seek advice for an evil character which I'm planning to create...

However I could not help but attempt to appease my curiosity with a post. My intent is not to point fingers or to be judgemental; rather, I'm just interested in getting an idea of the general mindset of the majority of the evil inhabitants of Norrath.

That said, my question is this: Where is the line drawn? At what point does this cease to be a roleplaying game and become personal, in the opinion(s) of whomever wishes to share their views with the board?

There is a Dark Elven guild on my server which takes roleplaying to an extreme. They are beyond such petty things as (mentioned in an earlier post) retaliation for annoying, persistent beg-tells for ports, Clarity etc.. <Insert class gripe here>; Those who lack grace often deserve what they get, regardless of their alignment.

The guild I bring to attention has made a habit of doing quite "evil" things, such as training high level MOBs or NPCs on lower level "good" characters, intentionally kill-stealing with no thought given to self-gain, only to pain inflicted, (I'd just assumed that the traditional 'evil' view prioritizes self gain, regardless of if and to what extent others are affected, please feel free to your own views concerning this), porting polite people who /auc for a port to a destination other than the one they want, and other acts of similar or greater 'evilness'. I can only begin to imagine the impact this would have on their target in a high level keyed dungeon or a Plane....

If you prey upon a good character, that good *character* has what may well be a very long CR... and so does the person at the keyboard, as well as potentially all of the time that they've invsted in their character if they're under level 6, or if their corpse is in an exquisitely difficult place to get to.

Please respond with any thoughts on this i.e. Is it roleplay, the way of the world, or what? Specifically, a previous poster mentioned that she / he feels as though the Tier'Dal tend to be more mature players - Are actions such as these a part of good roleplaying? Are evil characters, because of the hardships they've been through gaining levels, entitled to this behavior? Or is this guild (and indeed many, many players who aren't a part of this specific guild, but who seem to have the same mindset) an exception to the rule or is it the job of 'good' characters to be wary of such deeds, and how so?

In a perfect world, in a perfect game, such things would be accepted and understood as "good roleplaying".

Why WOULDN'T, in "real life", an evil Dark Elf lure a really powerful creature over to a Wood Elf, for the purpose of getting that Wood Elf killed? Well, the Dark Elf WOULD do that. It's TOTALLY in character.

HOWEVER... This ISN'T a perfect world, and EverQuest (and indeed, no current MMORPG) is NOT a perfect game, so these kinds of things are not understood or accepted as anything other than "being an ass" or "bannable".

Unfortunately, not everyone roleplays. Hell, not everyone even UNDERSTANDS roleplaying. Yes, it's a shame... EQ/UO/AC/etc are "Massively Multiplayer Online ROLE PLAYING GAMES", after all.

But they are also BUSINESS VENTURES and therein lies both the problem and their very reason for exsiting.

MUDs have always been around. They're text-based MMORPGS, essentially. But text doesn't do it for most people. So people want graphical MUDs... And they want hundreds or thousands of people playing with them. This costs huge amounts of cash, both to develop and to maintain.

ONLY a business venture can produce this kind of thing, because of both development costs and maintenance costs.

Of course, that business venture needs to be profitable, or else it will close down.

So it has to cater to EVERYONE willing to pay the fee. Which includes non roleplayers.

Sooooo... We can't train MOBs onto people because it's bannable. And it's bannable because the business venture behind the game wants to retain paying customers (who DO NOT like having MOBs trained on them by other players)...

What's the solution?

There isn't one. We have to live with limited roleplaying, or become millionaires and make our own game where roleplaying, no matter what it entails, is allowed.
