The wind blew gently through the trees of Nektulos carrying the scents of Neriak throughout the forest. Dark forces worked feverously trying to master their skills to better honor Bertoxxulous and Innoruuk. Life was continuing as normal, at its evil pace.

The Necromancer initiate Salfereet feeling the twinges of pain in her abdomen sat to rest near the river that ran through the forest, her ever protective pet made of bone chips handed to her by a friend paced anxiously awaiting her command. Things began to dim, slowly fading to the embrace of her beloved, darkness.

Kuff watched the scene with much trepidation. Having made himself invisible to the undead he did not fear the pet. He knew that if he used his healing magic to save the evil creature he would quickly find the unappreciative pet trying to take the last bit of life he had left. Kuff sat and prayed to Brell, asking what would be the right thing to do. As usual, there was no answer. He could feel that he might have enough mana left within him to destroy the pet and still aid the pregnant drow.

Her skin now a pale blue reflecting what little moonlight made its way through the forest canopy the young necromancer's life slowly drained. Something began tickling her senses, she felt fear from her pet, and pain. A loud noise interrupted what would have been her death sleep. Inching her way up to a seated position Salfereet saw a Dwarf putting the finishing blows on her pet. She did not recognize the language he spoke but saw nothing but hatred in his eyes as he tore through the last of the strength of her summoned creature. It was not fear that motivated her. It was not anger over her pet. It was her love of darkness that compelled the dying Drow as she drove her dagger into the thigh of the dwarf. It was her last effort to please the Plague Bringer.

Kuff looked down into the eyes of the smiling Drow. He knew at once that it was too late to save her. She had used the last of her energy to do him harm. He felt the dagger in his thigh but the pain did not register. Tears were streaming down the face of the sad dwarf recognizing the fact that it would take longer for him to heal her than she had left alive. He watched as the eyes lost there joy, the smile still on her face as she slumped to the ground. Placing his hands upon her he began to utter a prayer for her. A motion under his hand nearly stopped his heart, and abruptly ended his prayer. Shocked into utter silence Kuff quickly went to work attempting to do what this young dark elf had died trying to avoid.

On a cool and cloudless night, as the wind brought scents of Neriak throughout the forest, a Dwarven cleric pulled a dark elf away from death, and into life.
