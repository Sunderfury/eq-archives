It would be difficult to tell what I looked like when I first entered Norrath.  I did not have my smooth dark skin or silk-like white hair.  Instead I had the ruddy complexion and coarse dark hair of a Wood Elf. 

Ever since I was young, I was angry.  I was filled with a rage I couldn't comprehend.  I hated everything from the way the birds sang to the way I was forced to become a tree hugging druid.  I couldn't tell who I hated more: Tunare for making me this way, my father for making me become a druid or Innoruuk for not making me a child of his, a powerful Teir'dal.

One day after another argument with my father I left home.  I took nothing.  As I stalked off into the forest known as the Faydark I could hear my father's voice in the early evening "Indifferent, do not return until you can accept your responsibility."

I walked for days.  I destroyed everything I touched.  Eventually I realized that with every thing I destroyed I caused my skin to turn a shade darker, till I looked every inch a grand Dark Elf.  Well, everything except my hair.

Months after leaving home that fateful night I arrived in Neriak.  My journey there was long, hard and fraught with danger.  I had to avoid Dwarven guards, spend weeks at see hidden in the bottom of a boat and then elude angry humans in the tunnels below Freeport. 

I immediately went to the Priests of Innoruuk, where I found out due to my unfortunate beginnings I could no longer claim any god as my own.  When I was told this I went into a rage like the Priests had never seen.  I destroyed everything in the room where I was, using magic's I didn't know I possessed.  I even caused the Priests to obey my commands and even caused them to be mesmerized.  When I was done the remaining Priests just looked at me and smiled.  When I demanded to know what they were smiling at they just pointed to the mirror.  I was elated with what I saw.  My hair had turned white and with this came the revelation that I would become an Enchanter.  Changing my form and causing others to obey my will.  Soon I will be strong enough to face my father....

                -- Indifferent
