Zariu strolls into the Blind Fish, keeping her dagger handy - one never knows what may occur in a tavern, especially not one in Neriak.

Memories of people that she knew that she once called 'friend' and 'family' flood her, but she shakes her head to clear the unpleasant thoughts from her mind. Her, Sevok and Sevok's brother, Sylad are her family now, she reminds herself.

She motions for service to the serving wench who has taken the opportunity during the lull in business to practice her skills on the lute. Zariu cringes at the music, and when her red wine is served, skillfully flips the girl some platinum. "Buy yourself a better instrument, that one does your skill no justice"

Zariu chuckles as she remembers a friend who once called her a thief and the squeak he let out when he felt her dagger pointing warningly into his spine. But, this brings back memories of those who never shared nor understood her ambition..

Staring out the window she thinks on the new lands and wonders when her and Sevok shall be adventuring to them....

The narrow streets in the city were quiet, relatively empty of passers by. Thanks be to whatever god still guides my steps, she thought. The absence of others to torment her as once when she was a child was the first reprieve she had in a long time.

Miserikordi felt a thousand years older for having to rely on a staff to hobble about on. A wound on her left leg, while partially sealed over and dressed in bandages, throbbed with searing pain. A fire beetle had bitten her in Freeport when her bodyguard had let his vigil down. That earned her a weeks' stay in the nearest Teir'Dal settlement while the fever dreams passed.

Miserikordi paused in her walk when she head the shuffling steps of one of the guards. Ages had passed since she visited her birthplace, and she no longer was in such good standing with the citizens of Neriak. The shuffling stopped; a silent, awkwards moment passed. Blood rushed to her ears and heated the sides of her face as she waited.

The sound of walking guards continued past. She clenched her teeth and took her first few steps into the street. The popping and crackling of joints torn from bones told her she was in the Ogre dominated area of Neriak. A moment to visualize the area, and she was on her way to the Commons.

She was going back home.
