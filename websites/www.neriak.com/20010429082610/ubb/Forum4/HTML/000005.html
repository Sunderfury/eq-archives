Here's a hot topic... Seems no one is neutral on this subject.

Generally, one trait atributed to those who are "evil" is power-hungriness. This comes into play in EQ when you start thinking about getting your character items which are way beyond his or her level.

Personally, I don't have any problem with this. My level 25 Necromancer has items ABSURDLY beyond his level - his INT is currently 177! And you know what? I still die all the time... It really doesn't make you a god, trust me. Yes, it's a BIT easier, but that's about it. The game is still a challenge, and I like it that way.

These items come from a few sources: [1] some really good trades (buy real low, sell real high; cashed in on the Shadow Wolf Pelt craze when SolTemple came out) [2] GENEROUS gifts from friends and Guildmates [3] some things off characters I don't play anymore.

It's easy to justify this in roleplay. My character is a power hungry Necromancer, why WOULDN'T he seek out things that make him more powerful? It'd be insane to expect him not to.

What's the consensus on this? Am I somehow a horrible player for having done this? Is it fine? Not that I'm going to get rid of the items, but I'm curious to discuss this...
