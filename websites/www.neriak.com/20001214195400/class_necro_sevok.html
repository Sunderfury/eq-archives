Introduction

Among the four "pure" casting classes, the Necromancer is perhaps the most unusual, with the Enchanter being a close second. Wizards are pretty straightforward -- they beat the crap out of things in very short order; Magicians are basically a weakened Wizard with a pet to make up for it; Enchanters get some awesome buffs and debuffs, and a near-worthless animation as a pet that they can't even control; Necromancers get some incredibly wicked Damage Over Time (DOT) spells, a very powerful pet, the ability to charm the undead or ward them back into the grave, and some very good personal buff spells. Playing a Necromancer is not like playing any other casting class.

This guide is meant to be used in conjunction with the Necromancer spell listing at Casters Realm [clickable]. That site contains an exact, detailed list of all spells you receive, with accurate damage statistics and so forth. I won't list the names of each pet spell, for instance; you can instead can that information from Casters Realm.

Creating your Corpse-hugger :)

If druids are tree-huggers, then Necromancers are corpse-huggers. Or corpse-makers, depending on how you look at it. As you grow in levels you will see how quickly we can turn a snarling, pissed off MOB into easy EXP and loot.

Anyway, when creating a Necromancer you have a few choices to make -- those being Race, stat bonus distribution, and in some cases, which God you will follow.

The Race choice essentially comes down to personal taste, though there are a few benefits and drawbacks to each choice. I'm partial to Dark Elves of course (being one myself), but others strongly like Gnomes, Humans, Erudites, or Iksar.

Dark Elves have the second highest unmodified starting INT of any Necromancer race and they also have Ultravision, the night best vision in the game. Their starting hometown, Neriak, is in a great location, and Necromancers are loved there. Without any faction work (and assuming you don't do anything that lowers base faction), we can freely travel to Neriak (of course), Oggok (the city of the Ogres), Grobb (Trolls), The Overthere (the Kunark outpost for evil races), and East/West Freeport (and E/W commonlands, as they are guarded by E/W Freeport faction guards). We are also not KOS at Druid Rings (nice for TPs and Evacs). Do not travel to North Freeport without faction work, nor to Highhold, Rivervale, Qeynos, Kelethin, Felwithe, Ak'anon, Kaladim, Erudin, Firiona Vie, Paineel, or Cabilis either. This sounds like a lot of towns to be KOS in, but keep in mind most of those factions can be fixed with some work. Also keep in mind that most of those towns are essentially uneeded. Most of the important non-Kunark dungeons are located near evil territory, so you will have easy access to SolA, SolB, Guk, and Cazic-Thule. It is also very easy to get to Unrest and if you're careful Mistmoore is easy to get to as well.

Erudites have the highest starting unmodified base INT for Necromancers. They are also blind as a bat at night, though for a Necromancer that is easy to fix early on. Erudite Necromancers begin in Paineel, the evil counterpart to Erudin, where they are well-loved, and which has a fairly excellent newbie area with very high spawn rates (especially compared to the horrid normal Erudite newbie area, Tox Forest). You would not be KOS in Erudin itself, but no merchant there will deal with you -- just be thankful you're not KOS as the only way off Odus is via Teleport or via the ship from the Erudin docks, deep within the city. You're KOS in Qeynos, which is a fairly Bad Thing(tm) since once you leave Odus, it is Qeynos you will travel through and you would need to use their sewers. I am told Erudite Necromancers are not KOS (but not well-liked though) in Neriak, and I'm positive you would be fine in E/W Freeport as well. The rest of the world I'm unsure on (Erudite Necros: EMAIL ME with faction info).

Gnomes, aka Walking Footstools, have decent starting stats and decent night vision. They begin in Ak'anon where they are NOT well-liked, but at least you're not KOS. Lots of your own hometown vendors will refuse to deal with you. The upside is that Gnomes are the least KOSed Necromancers. I'm told they can go virtually anywhere (Gnome Necros: EMAIL ME with this info), including Neriak, Freeport, Qeynos, and even Erudin. Not well-liked mind you, but hey, at least not KOS.

Iksar, the newest playable race, are lizardman like creatures who begin in their city of Cabilis located deep with the continent of Kunark. By far, Iksar newbie areas make the old world newbie areas look like a total joke, and the progression in levels available in the zones that connect to Cabilis is a masterpiece of game design. Necromancers are loved in Cabilis and all vendors will deal with you, and indeed give you great prices. Understatement Alert. Iksar have some, ummm, well, faction "issues". Like for example, they are KOS, well, EVERYWHERE. Yup, only town you're not KOS in by default is Cabilis. =) I've heard it's fixable but not without a lot of work. However, if you play an Iksar Necro and get to high levels, you'll be unique and to a lot of people that's a reward in itself.

Humans may also become Necromancers, though in my personal opinion I cannot understand why ANYONE would play a human ANYTHING. All their stats are extremely mediocre and they are blind at night. I've seen accurate numeric EXP charts and I can tell you that the EXP difference between a Human Necro and say a DE Necro is minimal. And the benefits far outweigh it. Unless you have a personal reason to choose a Human Necro (roleplaying, or what have you), don't do it! There is no game-mechanics reason to.

The stat allotment choice is fairly easy for Necromancers. You'll have 30 points to add to whatever stats you want. As a Caster, our most important stat is INT, and that is where you need to put most of the 30 points. Most. It will let you put up to 25 into INT (the game won't let you put the whole thing in one stat), though I'd advise against it. Why? At early levels the effect of INT on Mana isn't much -- this is to reduce the impact of twinking. It's EASY for a Necro (or any INT caster) to get to 200 INT (200 is a magic number after which additional INT is useless as it has almost no effect on mana). So, if they allowed INT to have a large effect on mana at early levels, a twink Necro could level much faster than they can now. So, at later levels, a lot of your gear will naturally be INT gear and you'll have plenty INT. But you'll also want to carry loot, pet weapons, and so on, for which you need STR. I would therefore advise 20 INT and 10 STR. The choice is up to you but I do believe the extra STR to be helpful, especially early on.

After picking your race and setting your starting stats, you'll be presented with a Deity selection. Most of them will be greyed out meaning you can't choose them for your character to worship. If you're a Dark Elf, Iksar, or Erudite, you'll only have one choice (Innoruuk or Cazic-Thule respectively). Humans and Gnomes I believe have a few choices (Bertoxx? Cazic? Innoruuk?). The only deity choice that would really help you as a Necromancer would be AGNOSTIC and you don't have that choice so just take the only choice you have, or pick one that you like if you have a couple choices.

After doing all of that and logging in as your Necromancer, do the normal new character stuff -- your guild quest, scribing and memming your first two spells, and so forth. This isn't a beginner's EQ guide, you should already know these steps, if not, then read a more basic guide to EQ.

Early Strategies

Once you earn enough money killing the standard newbie creatures and selling their loot, start buying up your level 1 spells. First buy the level 1 pet spell (Cavorting Bones), then the rest of the spells. Your level 1 pet frankly sucks but it's better than nothing and will train you on how to use a pet, which you're going to be doing for the rest of your life, so use it at all times.

Pets are pretty easy to use -- there are only a few commands they respond to, and you will use even fewer. The commands are: /pet guard me - tell the pet to guard you - redundant, because the pet is always guarding you; /pet follow me - tell the pet to follow you; /pet sit down - has the pet sit down, causing it to regain HP faster; /pet stand up - has the pet stand up again after sitting down; /pet guard here - tells the pet to stay in one place, guarding; /pet taunt - tells the pet to taunt what it's fighting (the default); /pet notaunt - tells the pet to not taunt the things it's fighting; /pet back off - tells the pet to stop fighting what it's currently fighting - note that if the MOB hits you or the pet, forget it, the pet is going to continue fighting; /pet attack - duh; /pet get lost - kills the pet. Most Necromancers make a hotkey bank with the 6 most used pet commands -- these vary from player to player, over time you'll find out what works best for you.

If you hand your pet a weapon (just like trading with a player), it will wield it. Note that there is no way to get it back, so don't give it anything good. Unlike players, the only thing weapons do for pets is possibly make them attack faster. Each pet spell has a maximum damage the pet will do, regardless of weapon. So the only reason to give it a weapon is to make it attack faster. A pet's fists attack at a speed of 30, so any weapon with a speed of 30 or better will help the pet, whereas a weapon with a speed over 30 will hurt the pet because it will make him attack slower. Also note that later on, your pet will gain the ability to dual wield -- so giving him two weapons will be desirable.

The above paragraph on giving weapons to pets has been left in for historical reasons. It is now completely untrue. The only reason to give a pet weapons is to get it to dual wield, as pets won't dual wield their fists; they need two weapons to DW. Giving a pet a high damage weapon won't increase his damage -- each level of pet has a set max damage. Giving a pet a fast weapon won't increase his speed -- each level of pet has a set speed. Only your pet buffs and the haste spells of Enchanters and Shamen can increase your pet's attack speed.

From level 1 thru level 4, you basically are going to make sure your shielding spell is active, then pull a beast to you with a Lifetap or some other offensive spell, and then beat on the thing in melee along with your pet, and cast more Lifetaps here and there until the thing dies. You are insanely weak at these levels. Do not worry -- it's more than made up for by the power you will soon start to feel

Once you reach level 4, you get Meditate, which will make your life a LOT easier, and more new spells. Get the pet spell first again, and then Clinging Darkness, then the rest. The level 4 pet is a huge upgrade and will serve you quite well. Clinging Darkness is the first spell in the Darkness "line", line being a term for a group of spells that you receive updates to throughout your career. The Darkness line is a great group of spells because they reduce your target's movement speed, allowing you to get off more spells before they reach melee range. The Darkness line also does Damage Over Time (DOT).

Typical level 4 strategy goes like this: pull with Darkness, send pet, hit with poison bolt, maybe then drop a disease cloud and/or siphon strength, then help out the pet with melee or maybe a few lifetaps. This is a pretty sound, easy, and safe kill strategy. You'll probably use all your mana, but at these levels you don't have much so medding back up to full shouldn't be a big deal. The medding time will also give your pet time to heal back to full. Make sure you don't start a fight unless your pet is at full health -- you can check this at any time with /pet report health.

If you get into trouble, you can also use your Fear spell to make the monster run away in fear. This is not a permanent solution -- the spell will wear off and when it does the monster will come back, possibly bringing his friends to kill you. So you'll need to either zone, or find a way to kill it when it comes back. Still, Fear can save your life.

This type of strategy will continue pretty much up until level 12. Level 8 brings new spells again -- as always get your pet spell first, then the rest. There are a few things to note at level 8.

The first is that you get Ward Undead. This will allow you to do (for the level) a high amount of damage to undead creatures. It's not mana-light, it uses quite a bit of mana, but if you are fighting the undead you will be more efficient than most other classes because of this spell.

You also get Dark Pact, the first in a line of fairly unique spells. What it does is trade some of your HP for Mana over a period of time. It's not a 1:1 trade, you get more Mana than you lose HP. To some players, especially those who have experience with melee classes, this spell seems terrifying. They can't understand why you would want to trade HP for Mana. Well, it's quite simple -- you are a Caster, and Casters are NOT supposed to get hit much, if at all. It is FAR more important that you have Mana, because as long as you have Mana you can cast spells and do damage. Dark Pact is also great to cast right before you sit down to meditate as it will greatly reduce the time it takes to med up to full.

Finally, you get Dark Empathy. This may not be useful to you at this level because you are likely exclusively soloing, but if you do want to help others, you can finally do so. The spell transfers some of your HP to another character. So, at the cost of your own health, you can heal others. Just something to take note of.

Level 12

Level 12 brings another change in your power, and probably in your killing strategy. You get the first in another line of DOT spells -- Heat Blood. This is a VERY nice DOT, and you will be using it a lot. Please note that it is heat-based and DOES NOT work in Lavastorm. You also get an upgrade to the Darkness line, Engulfing Darkness. Also of note you get Bind Affinity, so you can finally Bind yourself anywhere you like.

Another new spell type we see at this level [when I was level 12, this spell didn't exist] is Leach, the first in a line of spells that continues with Vampiric Curse and finally Bond of Death at 49. It is like a Lifetap in that it transfers life from your target to yourself, but this line does it over time. This ends up being more efficient -- since you have to wait for the transfer, it gives you more than a same-level direct lifetap. It also tends to cause less aggression from the MOB since he doesn't notice what's happening until it's too late. =) Another good thing about this line is that you can drop the spell on a MOB one second before it dies and you STILL get the health over the duration of the spell, even after the MOB is dead. This is great to lessen the health drain of Dark Pact.

You will probably want to change your tactics at this point. You can continue killing Blue-con creatures and possibly some White/Blacks with your same tactic, but if you like, you can take on some much tougher beasts, including yellows and reds, but you have to change how you fight. Your pet can no longer stay in melee with these beasts long enough for the MOB to die, so you'll have to make the creature not hit your pet.

The way to do this is with Fear. What you do is this, find a yellow or JUST red beast (i.e., just barely red... not a spectre for instance), pull it with Darkness, send the pet, hit it with Heat Blood and then FEAR IT. It will turn and run, the DOTs will be doing full damage because it's fleeing, and also the pet will be following it and beating on it. If the Fear breaks, just re-fear it. If the DOTs wear off before it's dead, just re-apply them or just use Lifedraws to finish it. You need to use the Fear tactic to do this because MOBs of yellow/red con will kill your pet long before the DOTs kill it, and then it's coming for YOU. For example, Kodiaks (ADULT kodiaks, not young kodiaks) con yellow and red (it varies) at 13 and 14. They hit very hard (high 30s) and pretty fast. They'll shred your pet pretty quick -- if you don't believe me try it. :) But, I can solo them with this tactic at 13 and up.

Save a small amount of Mana in case something goes wrong. If your pet dies or you run way too low on Mana to finish the thing, just Shadowstep a few times to get far away from the MOB and then run for the zone. You may still occasionally die but this will seriously cut down on it. Shadowstep uses very little Mana and has a very short casting time, so it's great for putting quick distance between you and something that's beating you up bad. The beast will still come for you, so you need to either run for the zone or gate. Don't try to cast gate while in melee, it will never work. Shadowstep 1-2 times first.

Level 16

As with all previous spell groups, the level 16 spells bring yet another huge jump in power. As before, buy your pet spell (Restless Bones) first, and then the rest. Most especially Heart Flutter. Heart Flutter is a new DOT -- and it is a good one! Not only does it do pretty decent damage, it also drops the target's Strength and AC as well. It makes your pet and any other melee fighter much more effective, and makes them take less damage due to the MOB's reduced STR.

You also get a huge amount of new buffs, most of which are as always self-only. You get Shielding, an upgrade to your personal shield spells (starts at level 1 with minor shielding -- you've gotten 2 or 3 upgrades to this series already, you should have been buying them all along). This should be kept active on yourself at all times. It adds Magic Resist, Hit Points, and AC. Another new spell is Shieldskin -- this is a strange sort of spell that doesn't add to any stats, instead it adds a 100-point or so damage sink. The first 100 points of damage you take while this spell is active simply sink into the shield; you take no damage. It's expensive to cast, requiring a Cat's Eye Agate at 0.2.5.3 per stone, but it's well worth it. And finally, you get Spirit Armor. This is a targetable (believe it or not!) armor class (AC) buff that you can cast on anyone -- including your pet! It adds 15 AC, which doesn't seem like much, but it's at least a little helpful. And it's targetable. :)

Another spell you receive at this level, Feign Death, is quite controversial. What it does it allow you to "play dead", hopefully tricking MOBs into thinking you're dead and hence leaving you alone. Opinions on this spell's usefulness and safety are extremely varied, but in the right situation it's a great spell. I'm working on a separate guide on FD tactics that should be available soon.

Also of note is Cancel Magic. This is a great spell, especially if you hunt in Lavastorm. Tired of those "YOU are burned!" or "Jabartik was burned." messages? Those are from the fireshields that a lot of Lavastorm (and other zones as well, Lavastorm just seems to be really bad for this) MOBs have on them. These shields can be taken down with Cancel Magic and then you will no longer get damaged each time you hit the MOB. You may have to cast it 2, sometimes even 3 times to remove the fireshield, but when you no longer get burned when you hit the MOB, you'll be very thankful you took down the shield. This spell can also remove buffs from MOBs, and you can even use it to remove harmful spells (such as DOTs) from yourself, your pet, and your group members. It doesn't work (on non-PvP servers) on anyone but MOBs, yourself, your pet, and those in your group. This is to prevent jerks from running around taking buffs off people.

Level 20

Ah, at last! Level 20, the age of Norrathian majority, where you can choose your surname. Choose it with pride, and use the /surname command to add it, or have a Senior Guide do it if it contains an accent mark. :)

This is another great level for Necromancers. We get a new pet, as with all new spell circles. This one is another great upgrade, it summons a powerful pet indeed. You are of course /con'ing your pets and making sure they are the most powerful available, right? Generally, at the level of the spell itself (i.e., level 20 for the level 20 summon spell), the strongest pet will /con with "looks kind of dangerous". Make sure you keep using /pet get lost and re-summoning until you get the powerful one or you could find yourself in a heap of trouble.

This level brings us another ability we share with all pure casting classes (and priest classes as well) - Specialization. This allows us to specialize in a certain casting skill (Alteration, Abjuration, Evocation, Divination, Conjuration), thereby reducing mana usage, casting time, fizzle rate, and resist rate for spells of that type. What you do is, at level 20, go to your guild and train ALL FIVE specialization skills. Just put one point into each, this will make your skill 20 in all of them. THEN, you cast a spell of the type you want to primarily specialize in until that Specialization skill is 51. That sets your primary specialization. DO NOT leave this to chance! Mem a level 1 spell of the type you want to have your primary specialization in, and cast until you get 51 in that specialization. Most Necromancers pick Alteration and this is the logical choice - our lifetaps, and most of our DOT spells use Alteration, so reducing mana usage, cast time, and resist/fizzle rate of these spells is a Good Thing(tm).

At level 20, we also finally get a new lifetap spell. This is a nice upgrade, as we can now steal 65hp of life (at level 20) from our foes. This is a good thing, because we also get Allure of Death, with trades 8 hp for 8 mana each tick for quite some time. This, as with the lower power spell Dark Pact, is the true power of a Necromancer - converting life into mana and then stealing that life back from our foes. No other class can do this and it is the secret of ultra-successful Necromancers.

If you like to hunt Undead, this is a great level also! We get Expulse Undead, which does a large amount of damage to Undead - it's a great upgrade to our level 8 Ward Undead spell. We also get Dominate Undead, which allows us to charm an undead monster for a period of time. You can't have a summoned pet at the same time, but clearing out an Undead camp using one of the undead from the camp as your pet (especially when there's a bunch of zombies and a ghoul - charm the ghoul!) to kill the rest, then killing the pet when the charm wears off is great fun. Be warned that the charm WILL wear off, and when it does, your former pet is going to be VERY MAD at you. Be prepared to kill it quick.

If you are doing any grouping (some Necromancers never group, some always do...), at level 20 you get a pretty good spell to use in groups. It's called Shadow Compact and it's a sort of reverse lifetap. It transfers some of your HP to the target over a short period of time. Your pet is a valid target also so it's not only a grouping spell; it can work great when soloing as well. It transfers a little over 100 HP from you to the target over around 20 seconds.

Also of note is Shadow Vortex, which lowers the target's AC. This is great, especially at these lower levels, as it allows your pet (and your melee class groupmates if you have them) to hit more often and for more damage. Use it, love it. :)

Level 24

Ahhhh... What a true level of power this is for a Necromancer! So many spells we receive at this level are truly powerful, it's very hard to explain how strong you will feel when you finally get this level!

You get an upgrade to your personal shield, Major Shielding, that gives a nice boost to your standard shielding. Also, Leatherskin is now available, which is an upgrade to Shieldskin and will damage-sink around 200 damage -- a very nice benefit! The spell requires a bloodstone and is well worth it.

Of course, as always, you receive a new pet spell (Haunting Corpse), though this time it is research only, so you will need to obtain the Words and create the spell via research, or purchase it from another player. Mythiran Tower (click here) has all the research information. Your pet at this level appears very dark, a nice visual upgrade.

Another research only spell, Intensify Death, is a pet-only buff that truly makes your pet a death machine! It only lasts 5 minutes, so what you do is cast the spell on your pet right before a tough battle and then send him in to kill. You will really notice a difference in effectiveness using this spell.

Now we come to the true surprise of this level. Shock of Poison. Poison? A Necromancer spell? Must be another DOT, right? Nope. 146 direct damage at level 24. No waiting, 146 damage instantly. Wow... It's hard to describe how wonderful it is to finally have this spell! Basically you will: buff pet, send pet, darkness, wait a while, then SOP SOP, dead. Easy fights. This spell is really effective at this level -- 146 damage is a lot at level 24, especially for Necromancers who are used to having to wait 60 seconds or more to see the full damage of their spells. You will use this spell A LOT.

You also get a few utility spells at this level: Breath of the Dead, a self-only underwater breathing spell; Resist Cold, a cold resistance buff; Rest the Dead, an undead-only lull spell; and Screaming Terror, which is SUPPOSED to cause a stun effect/mesmerize effect, but I have yet to see it actually do ANYTHING on the MOBs I've tried it on.

All in all, level 24 is a true power level for the Necromancer. Enjoy it!

Beyond

Whew! It's been ages since I've updated this guide! I last updated this guide shortly after I made 24, and now I've been at Level 44 for 3 days! A lot of power is given Necromancers between 24 and 44 -- a new hp-to-mana conversion spell, upgrades to all major DOTs lines, 2 very fast acting and very high damage DOTs that stack, a new pet buff, a new Fear spell, the list goes on! We'll tackle them spell-level-by-spell-level.

Level 29

As always, another big upgrade in power. Most of your pets summoned with the Level 29 pet spell will dual wield -- make sure you have one that dual wields before fighting as the ones that don't will be too weak. You also get a great number of other spells.

29 brings an upgrade in the Darkness line - Dooming Darkness. I'm a rather controversial Necromancer in that even now at level 44 I use the highest level Darkness (this one - 49 is the next upgrade). Most of our brethren say to use the lowest (L4 Clinging) or second-lowest (L12 Engulfing) due to high resists. Yes, Darkness gets resisted. A lot. But it's not that bad and using the higher level Darkness has it's benefits.

Firstly, it lasts longer. Darknesses are not high-damage DOTs; most of us use them for the Snare effect so we can Fear-kite (and in dungeons, to prevent runners). Clinging has a real short duration and Engulfing isn't a lot better. Dooming lasts a good long time. Secondly, the higher level spells seem to slow the MOBs down more, which can be a really good thing. =)

So - I say use your judgement as to which Darkness to use. I use the higher level one, a lot of people don't. Test it both ways and find out what works best for YOU. Everyone's a little different.

We also get upgrades to the Heat Blood line (Boil Blood), the Leach/Vamp Curse line (Vampiric Curse - 150 or so HP from MOB to you over about a minute even if the MOB dies 1 second after casting), the pet heal line (Renew Bones), and the Undead DD line (Dismiss Undead). To round the spells out, we get a DD-vs-Plant (*cough*useless*cough*) spell (Defoliate), a new lifetap (Spirit Tap, get it, use it, love it), and a point-blank AOE (read: suicide) spell (Word of Spirit).

Level 34

This is often considered a major level for us Necromancers. You've made it to the mid-thirties. You've made it past your first Hell Level and Post Hell Level (personally I hate the post-hell EXP loss MUCH more than the in-hell slow EXP gain). Now you get Level 34 and you get some truly awesome spells!

The first I'll mention (I don't usually mention pets at these levels because you know by now you need the new pet) is Call of Bones. This is an upgrade to the Allure of Death line. It converts HP to mana at a pretty decent rate. It's almost as good as Clarity (Enchanter spell). It also turns you into a skeleton for the duration of the spell, making you non-KOS to some things while KOS to others (don't have COB on near Nektulos guards, for instance). The spell lasts a good while and IT WILL KILL YOU if you don't pay attention to your health. What I like to do is cast a Vampiric Curse right before a MOB dies so I get the 175 or so health while I post-fight Meditate. You will use this spell a lot.

Another awesome spell is Venom of the Snake, a poison based 40 second DOT that does an initial 40 points of direct damage. You may think that being only 40 seconds long it doesn't do much damage. Wrong. It does around 540 damage in that 40 seconds making for a GREAT DOT! Of course this is not without a price. Once the spell is 1/4 or 1/2 way through you can FORGET about anyone getting a MOB off you. This spell makes them frenzy on you like mad. Then again, you're probably fear-kiting at this level anyway so no worry. =)

Other must-haves are Greater Shielding (upgrade to personal AC/HP/MR spell), Steelskin (upgrade to damage-absorbing shield line, uses a Jasper [ouch!]), new undead Charm (Beguile Undead), Root (gee, thanks, other casters only got it at level 4!), and Invoke Fear (a really nice upgrade to good old Fear).

Enjoy the power!

Level 39

You've made it through Hell-2 and PostHell-2! Congrats! More new spells and yet again more power! By now you probably have your spell effect graphics (Particle Density = low or off) turned off due to graphic draw lag during heavy fighting, but when you level to 39, turn it back on for a while. =) You'll enjoy the treat when you cast.

Augment Death is an upgrade to Intensify Death, the pet buffing line. It adds HP, AC, and more importantly HASTE to your pet. It makes it into a true killing machine. Keep this on your pet at all times! You'll notice a huge difference.

You thought VoTS was great at level 34? Well it was, and at level 39 we get yet another spell that basically does the same thing. 540 damage over 40 seconds with 40 points of initial direct damage. It's called Scourge, and it's disease based and STACKS WITH VENOM OF THE SNAKE! I really don't think I need to elaborate. Revel in your class choice. =)

We also get a new lifetap (Drain Spirit), a new disease cure (Counteract Disease), a new magic cancel (Nullify Magic), new vs. undead DD (Expel Undead), and an upgrade to the Scent line of resistance debuffs (Scent of Darkness).

At 39, you could also scribe and cast the spell Summon Corpse but acquiring the spell is a very long camp in the dungeon of Paw. Additionally, the reagent you need to cast it costs a lot of cash (175pp or so), weighs 10, and can't be placed into any container. As you can see, at 39, this is not a useful spell unless you have rich friends who don't know any higher level Necromancers. Get it now if you want or need it, but most Necromancers wait quite some time -- I have a Level 54 friend who still doesn't have it.

Level 44

Muahahaha... Only one spell group between you and extreme power! While I was going through 43, friends kept telling me how much I'd love finally being 44, and they were absolutely right! The 44 spells are insane!

It's hard to decide which spell to talk about first. So I'll start with the obvious - the new pet. It hits for a lot of damage, is pretty fast (especially when you slap Augment Death on it), and it randomly procs a lifetap effect during fights. This is one mean pet! Give him two weapons and let him go to town!

For a long time now, you've probably been enjoying the raw power of your DOTs. Maybe sometimes loading on all 6 lines and Fearing a MOB just to watch it keel over with nothing touching it -- just because you can (though doing so is a scary waste of mana). But the whole time, deep down inside, there's been a gnawing, aching feeling... Wizzy envy! Sure you can do 540 damage over 40 seconds with only one spell. But Wizards can do it in 2 seconds with a single spell. And keep doing it until the MOB frenzies on them and they die... But still. You wish you had some DD power -- Shock of Poison is pretty useless by 44. Enter Ignite Bones. 494 damage. Instantly. BAM! Sure Wizzies can do more at this level. Sure Ingnite Bones isn't chain castable (you have to wait for the secondary effect of it to wear off - 18 seconds). But still, it's a powerful spell and you're probably going to use it a lot. It does 494 direct damage to any target that doesn't resist it (grrrrr) and turns that target into a skeleton for 18 seconds. During that time their AC is severely reduced (some say it's dropped to 0 others say to 1).

A lot of people love DMF, or Dead Man Floating. Well at 44 you'll finally be able to give them what they want! Hell I entered Lower Guk the other night and instantly got 8 tells asking for it! It's no wonder. It has a lot of effects: Levitate, See Invisible, +60-70 or so to Poison Resist, Underwater Breathing, and Ultravision. And it lasts a LONG and more importantly PREDICTABLE time. No more having Levitate pop off when you need it most. DMF always lasts for the same amount of time (long - I haven't measured it; it's around a game day or so).

You thought that Call of Bones and Shadow Compact was going to kill you someday when you weren't paying enough attention? Well at 44 you get a new way to kill yourself in a scarily short period of time. Pact of Shadow is an upgrade to Shadow Compact and transfers around 300 hp from you to the target over only 24 seconds. Ouch. Note that you can heal your pet with this also. Also be aware that you can have POS active on 2 or more targets. Be doubly aware that until you get Bond of Death at 49, POSing 2 or more targets will probably kill you. =) Still it's a good way to heal a party member when needed, just so long as you can Lifetap and/or Vamp Curse your health back.

At 44, you also get upgrades to your personal shielding spell (Arch Shielding), the damage absorbing shield line (Diamondskin - uses Peridot (!!!) so only use it for super tough fights and stick to Steelskin for normal use), the Heart Flutter line (Asystole, a fairly high damage debuffing DOT), and the mana transfer line (Covetous Subversion).

Level 49

As I'm writing this (on November 8, 2000), I've been past 49 for quite some time (actually I'm a bubble into level 51) and I can honestly tell you, it is every bit as good as you've been told, and better! Wow.

The first thing is the pet. Finally, after all these levels, a pet that can take out certain blue-con MOBs without help! Great for when you accidentally end up with two MOBs - he can kill one and you can fear/DOT the other. The highest-level pet from the 49 pet spell (Invoke Death) can quad-attack for 55 damage allowing for a whopping 220 per round if he gets all four attacks. Plus kick and bash. Plus his Lifetap proc. Invoke Death is MEAN.

By now Call of Bones is seeming kinda old... It doesn't really kick up the mana like it used to, because your levels and your equipment upgrades have given you huge amounts of mana over what you had at 34 when Call of Bones was new. Well now you get Lich and it makes a huge difference! 20 mana for 31 HP per tick. Keep your eye on your health though, or it will kill you quickly.

At 44, you got Pact of Shadow, a 300 point DOT heal over 24 seconds. You may have used it once or twice then frantically Lifetapped to get it back. The spell becomes infinitely more useful once you achieve 49 and get Bond of Death. It's a 720 damage Lifetap DOT over one minute. It will totally counteract 2 Pacts of Shadow (you can have POS on multiple targets at once but will get only one icon for it). It also counteracts Lich which is a good thing. You'll be using it a lot.

Perhaps the coolest named spell in the game is Drain Soul, and at 49 you get this fairly nice Lifetap. It's got the same long cast time you're used to for a Lifetap but it heals you right quick when it's needed.

49 also gives you a Heat Blood upgrade (Ignite Blood), a Darkness upgrade (Cascading Darkness - sick casting time and mana cost, I never use it =( ), a Charm Undead upgrade (Cajole Undead), an Undead DD upgrade (Banish Undead - extremely good damage on this spell!), and a Root spell (Paralyzing Earth - no one uses it; supposedly very short-lasting).

Level 51

Ahhh, 51. You've passed the pre-Kunark-release level cap. Your title now shows as Heretic instead of Necromancer in /who lists, a fact that REALLY annoys me. I have not gone against the teachings and customs of my society and religion. I am not a damn Heretic! Anyway. You're getting a lot of tells from lower level Necros (assuming you're not /role or /anon all the time) asking for advice, spells, equipment, etc; and from other people asking you to summon their corpses. And now you see that level 45, while being a Hell, was only training wheels for post-50. Yes, everything you've heard is true. All levels post-50 are hells, with 54 and 59 being super-uber-hells from what I hear. But at least you get new spells EVERY level instead of every 5. Downside is one level FEELS like 5!

We share our high-level Poison and Disease DOTs (Venom of the Snake and Scourge) with Shamans, and at 51 we get the ultimate one: Envenomed Bolt. It's not a wizard-style bolt spell, it's simply a great upgrade to VOTS. It does an initial 110 points of damage then 1022 more over 42 seconds. Yes, 1132 damage in 42 seconds. This is a spell you'll use a LOT. You can purchase it from the Troll vendor on the stone hand in Innothule Swamp.

A new type of DOT is also available at 51. Splurt is a long term DOT - 2 minutes - and it does a LOT of damage. No one is sure exactly how much but pretty much everyone agrees it's right around 1800. It's a new style DOT in that it doesn't do a consistent amount of damage per tick. It starts out very very low and with each tick it's on the target, it the damage increases. This is a good thing because it doesn't aggro a MOB right away like a fast-damage DOT like EB does. It's great for fighting high-HP stuff like Wurms in Skyfire, where the MOB is going to live a long time anyway. It's not as good for stuff that dies really fast since it's a 2-minute DOT. This spell, like most post-50 spells, is a random drop from level 45+ MOBs, but of course you can also purchase it or trade for it from other players who have it but don't need it. Mine was given to me by my guild which I am very grateful for. =)

You also get an Undead-only Fear spell (Dread of Night) which can be purchased in Overthere. Also available at level 51 is Sacrifice, a spell which KILLS a level 45 or over character (who must be grouped with you, and click Yes to accept the death and who CAN NOT BE RESURRECTED) and turns a regular emerald into an Essence Emerald which is a component for 3 higher level spells. Sacrifice is a dropped spell (random off of level 45+ MOBs) and I wouldn't worry about getting it. =)

Beyond...

I'm a almost a bubble into 51, and spending time hunting in Skyfire, and doing Fear and Hate raids, and I can tell you 51 goes VERY slow. Much slower than 45 but being high level is pretty fun nonetheless. New spells at 52, and I'll update this when I get there. =)

Until then...
