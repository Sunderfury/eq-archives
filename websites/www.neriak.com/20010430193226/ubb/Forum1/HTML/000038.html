I've been reading through the many posts and helpful links in this site, and I do have to say that it has been a most helpful and informative site. However, I must admit that I have one major point I feel I'd like to make.

It concerns role-playing.

I, for one, as a player, have always done things different than the norm, and have pretty much done so for 21 years. My characters have been, to say the least, unique. (Like the vampire in V:tM that was afraid of the dark... had to sleep with a night light in his coffin.) I have always stood by my personal right, as a RP'er, to play my characters however I wish to, regardless of racial tensions, class angst, social standing, and the like.

Thus was born Daveris, my Dark Elf ShadowKnight on The Rathe server. the personal history I have for her goes something like this: She was forced into SK training by a very cruel father. Humans killed her mother when she was still in her teens. She had the teachings of Innoruuk rammed down her throat since almost Day One.

However, instead of it sticking to her, she felt resentful towards both Innoruuk and her father. She felt even more so when a captive human priest of Erollisi was brought in to be summarily executed. Curious, she asked him why. When she found out it was merely because he was a human, and a priest of Erollisi, she was horrified. Destroying both the Book of Discord and the Guild letter she was given, she left and hasn't been back since.

Since then, she has strove to try and be better than Innoruuk, better than her father, and better than most people that (in her mind) blindly hate everyone for what they are, who they follow, and who they associate with. She'll help anyone in need if she at all can.

Pretty lengthy? This is how much detail I put into my characters. And to think that was all off the cuff.

This is why I assert my personal right to RP however I want to: Where's the individuality in it if everyone follows the stereotype? Hell, on Tholuxae Paellis, I play a Human Cleric that was essentially shielded by no less than 3 Dark Elves. You're trying to tell me that the suggestions (and that's all that they are, suggestions) printed on this site should be taken as The Role-Players Guide to Dark Elves?

Somehow, I don't think so.

At any rate, I think I've rambled on for far too long about my character, and why I can, should, and will play them as individuals and not something that any blacksmith can make from a mold.

Thank you.

We *strongly dislike* site and pages that tell people what they should and should not do as Dark Elves (and are actually fascistly strict about it), we laugh at sites that say that all Dark Elves or other evil races must not EVER be happy, must always be tortured inside etc.

We have never ever written anything that we think is "Roleplaying Law" and would *never* do so.

I do *think*, for example, character histories should be thought out and written down - but I'm not the RP Police and I'm not going to log onto your server and insist that you give me the URL of your bio. =)
