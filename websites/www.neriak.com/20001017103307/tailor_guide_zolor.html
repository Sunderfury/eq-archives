Teir'Dal Tailoring Guide, by Zolor of The Nameless

Tailoring is a great money-making skill for the Teir'dal, especially for lower levels. You can easily make a fairly good income with this skill and there's not a huge investment required to get started as in smithing or jewelcraft, since some of the raw components (pelts and silks) can be looted from monsters you hunt. I started tailoring around level 4, and by level 9 I had earned enough platinum to buy myself almost an entire suit of small banded armor as well as a Shiny Brass Halberd (WELL over 150pp in value). I also helped finance my partner Aundrea (a Teir'dal Enchantress) getting started making jewelery, which is a real money pit at low levels.

First of all, in order to get started, you obviously need to put some practice points into the Tailoring skill in your guild. The more you put in at first, the less likely your chance of failures. I started tailoring at 4th level (as a Shadow Knight) and from fourth to about sixth level all my practice points went into Tailoring. I've heard of a trick (mentioned below) that could possibly allow you to raise your tailoring skill up to 17 without expending ANY practice points, but I have not verified that it works yet.

To start out with, I suggest you purchase a small sewing kit and some patterns. These can be purchased from several locations (listed below), with the small sewing kit selling for approximately 5gp and the patterns around 5-6sp each.

At this time you cannot buy large sewing kits or all of the sewing patterns in Neriak. The closest place I've found to buy these items is from from Spoolie Gee at the Inn closest to Freeport in Northern Ro. Be careful traveling into the human Inns...Dark Elves are usually KOS (Kill-On-Sight) until they've raised enough faction with the merchants around the Freeport and Commonlands area. This can be done by killing LOTS of Orcs and performing assorted quests to gain favor with the humans. Even if you are not KOS, they still will not sell to you unless you con "apprehensively" or above.

If you don't want to wait until your faction is good enough to purchase from the humans, one option is to have a non-evil character or a Dark Elf Enchanter of 8th level using an "Illusion: Half-Elf" spell (or something similar) to purchase the patterns and large sewing kit for you. If you DO get these patterns and the large sewing kit you will have a DISTINCT advantage over your other would-be Teir'dal competitors, who don't have them, and perhaps don't even know WHERE to get them. :-)

At lower levels, whenever you kill a black wolf or shadow wolf, keep those pelts! The "ruined wolf pelts" are used for making small patchwork armor, and the "shadow wolf pelts" are used for making shadow silk (for a Necromancer quest in Solusek). Also, once you start killing large spiders, KEEP THOSE SPIDER SILKS! The spiderling silk is worthless for tailoring, but the large spider silks are used for making raw and cured silk armor.

Whenever you get a ruined wolf pelt, put the pelt and a sewing pattern into your sewing kit and click "Combine". If you are successful you will get a piece of small patchwork armor. These can be sold for around 2-3gp per piece, or you can do what I usually do and just give them to newbies sitting around the newbie log. There isn't much profit to be made making patchwork, so you're just basically getting your skill up so you can start making raw silk armor. Also, by giving away free patchwork armor, you're losing maybe 6-7 sp per piece for the pattern (which isn't very much) but you're getting your tailoring skill up and you are helping another player, generating goodwill and publicity for yourself. Those low-level characters you're helping might someday be high level characters, and then whenever they need some tailoring done, they will remember that helpful tailor in Nektulos Forest! The ruined bear pelts can be used for making patchwork armor for our Ogre and Troll brothers and sisters who sometimes visit our lands, and once again it's great for gaining goodwill with other players. Ruined cat pelts (found in East and West Commonlands, among other places) can be used for making medium-sized armor, which can be worn by humans as well as elves.

I try to keep a large supply of ruined pelts pelts in the bank, since occasionally I will get a request for an entire set of patchwork armor. I usually charge 5pp for an entire set of patchwork armor, which consists of 13 pieces. If you don't keep a supply of pelts, it's very time consuming to run around madly killing wolves or bears in order to fill an order, especially after you've risen high enough in level that the wolves and bears con green and seldom drop loot.

While you're collecting pelts and making patchwork, anytime you get two large spider silks put them in your sewing kit and click "Combine" and if successful you will get a silk swatch. These swatches are stackable, and you definitely want to save them for making silk armor. I wouldn't try making raw silk until your skill level is high enough that patchwork is trivial, since each failure early on is one less swatch of silk you could be making a profit on later!

Making silk swatches becomes trivial around skill level 17, and making patchwork armor becomes trivial around skill level 26 or so. When you hit this level you are ready to (FINALLY!) start making raw silk armor and realizing some real profit from all of this.

Raw silk armor is made by combining the right amounts of silk swatches with the appropriate pattern in your sewing kit. Raw silk robes and leggings require two swatches of silk, while all other pieces only require one. For the robes and leggings, make sure you don't put the two silk swatches in your sewing kit stacked! It will not work if you do! I normally sell the robes and leggings for 4pp each, and all other pieces sell for 2pp each. If you do the math, you will see that this comes out to 30pp for a full set of raw silk armor, which is pretty good money for a player below level 10! The raw silk armor is very popular with mid-level spell-casters and monks, who want something better than cloth armor and have the funds to buy it.

I usually carry my large sewing kit and two backpacks full of all of the assorted patterns with me at all times. I also try to keep an ample supply of silk swatches on me at all times. Whenever I'm sitting down and healing, I do an "auction" that I am selling raw silk armor, and if I find a buyer I can make it on the spot and make a quick and tidy profit while I am resting.

Once the large spiders in Nektulos Forest start conning green to you, you have other options for obtaining more silk. You can either go to other places to hunt tougher spiders, or you can pay lower level characters to "harvest" it for you. The Giant Spiders in the Commonlands and the Desert Tarantulas in Northern/Southern Ro and Oasis drop silk, and the Carrion and Crag spiders in the Karanas drop silk as well. The Desert Tarantulas and Carrion Spiders seem to be around level 12-15 or so, but the Crag Spiders in the Karanas go up to over level 20! I have seen these Crag spiders drop as much as EIGHT silks from a single kill, so once you're powerful enough it might be worth a trip over there to harvest silk.

If you prefer to buy your silk, the going rate for large spider silk seems to be 3-5gp each. You can still kill spiders even though they con green, and occasionally get silk, but once again it's EXTREMELY time consuming!

Once your tailoring skill hits around 36 or so, making raw silk armor will become trivial, and you will need to move onto Studded Leather Armor in order to advance your skill. Making Studded Leather Armor is a nuisance because there is no real market for it. However, to do this you need to make or buy some studs from a blacksmith, and collect some Medium-Quality pelts. The only place I know of that has MQ wolf pelts is Qeynos Hills, so it'll probably be easier to just use MQ cat and bear pelts. If you're going to try studded, I recommend that you just make studded masks and sell them to the vendor, until it trivials out around skill level 56 or so.

What I've done, however, is give up on studded, since it is WAY too time-consuming and expensive just to get my smithing skill high enough to make the studs. I've moved straight from raw silk to cured silk armor, and although I still get a lot of failures my skill is slowly moving up.

To make cured silk armor, you will probably want to take up the Brewing skill. You will need this skill to make the Heady Kiolas used for curing the silk. This is a tailoring guide, so I won't go into a lot of detail about brewing here, but basically you take two Kiola saps, one flask of water, and a bottle and you combine them in the Brewing Barrel (similar to a Forge or Oven) to make a Heady Kiola. Brewing is cheap and easy to learn, and actually my brewing skill is already higher than my tailoring, and all I've done is make Heady Kiolas!

Once you have made a bunch of Heady Kiolas, combine one Heady Kiola, one swatch of silk, and one mask pattern in your sewing kit to (hopefully!) make a Cured Silk Veil. You can try to sell them to other players, (2pp per AC seems to be a fair price for cured silk) or give them away to monks in East Commonlands to (once again!) get a good reputation as a friendly and generous tailor. Cured Silk doesn't trivial out until around skill level 82 or so (I'm not there yet!), so you will get a LOT of failures if you are going straight to cured silk from raw silk. I'm going to stick with this method and see how it works, I will continue to update this guide as I progress.

I haven't progressed beyond Cured Silk yet, so I cannot comment much on making 10-item backpacks or Wu's Fighting Guantlets. However, I am including the recipes below for completeness.

One final note...I read this on another guide when I was starting out, and it's very good advice so I pass it along as well. ALWAYS be polite and friendly to customers. You will occasionally get people who accuse you of having outrageous prices, or are down-right insulting. I always strive to be polite to them, to the point of absurdity. You will also get people who want to haggle with you on prices (either buying armor or selling you silk). Use your best judgement on this, but always remember you can say "no" to a deal. The only real concession I will ever make is that I will usually make raw silk for half-price, if they provide all the silk. That way I'm not dipping into my own stock, and they're paying me almost a platinum piece just for my time and a 6sp pattern. If you're friendly and courteous, your reputation will spread, and you will find yourself getting tells from people you've never met asking you if you can make them some armor.

Trivial Skill Levels

The tailoring skill level at which an item becomes "trivial" to make.
  • Silk Swatches: 17
  • Patchwork Armor: 26
  • Raw Silk Armor: 36
  • Studded Armor: 56
  • Cured Silk Armor: 82
  • Backpacks: 82
  • Wu's Fighting Guantlets: 90
  • Reinforced Armor: 100+ ?

Recipes

Patchwork Armor

  • Small Patchwork:  1 ruined wolf pelt + pattern
  • Medium Patchwork:  1 ruined cat pelt + pattern
  • Large Patchwork:  1 ruined bear pelt + pattern

Raw Silk Armor

  • Raw silk robe/leggings:  2 swatches of silk (unstacked) + pattern
  • Everything else:  1 swatch of silk + pattern

Studded Leather Armor
(Use appropriate Medium-Quality pelts: wolf for small, cat for medium, and bear for large.)

  • Mask:  MQ pelt + pattern + 1 stud
  • Cap:  MQ pelt + pattern + 3 studs
  • Gorget:  MQ pelt + pattern + 2 studs
  • Shoulderpads:  MQ pelt + pattern + 3 studs
  • Cloak:  MQ pelt + pattern + 4 studs
  • Sleeves:  MQ pelt + pattern + 3 studs
  • Gloves:  MQ pelt + pattern + 4 studs
  • Boots:  MQ pelt + pattern + 4 studs
  • Belt:  MQ pelt + pattern + 2 studs
  • Tunic:  MQ pelt + pattern + 5 studs
  • Pants:  MQ pelt + pattern + 4 studs
  • Bracer:  MQ pelt + pattern + 2 studs

Cured Silk Armor
Monk only armor--sells well in East Commonlands, etc.

  • Veil (mask):  1 silk swatch + 1 Heady Kiola + pattern
  • Cap:  1 silk swatch + 3 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Collar (gorget):  1 silk swatch + 2 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Mantle (shoulderpads):  2 silk swatches + 2 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Cloak:  2 silk swatches + 2 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Sleeves:  2 silk swatches + 2 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Gloves:  2 silk swatches + 2 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Sandals (boots):  1 silk swatch + 2 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Sash (belt):  1 silk swatch + 2 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Gi (tunic):  3 silk swatches + 2 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Leggings (pants):  2 silk swatches + 3 Heady Kiolas + pattern
  • Wristband (bracer):  1 silk swatch + 2 Heady Kiolas + pattern

Reinforced Leather Armor
(Use appropriate High-Quality pelts: wolf for small, cat for medium, and bear for large.)

  • Mask:  HQ pelt + pattern + 1 steel bore
  • Helmet:  HQ pelt + pattern + 2 steel bores
  • Gorget:  HQ pelt + pattern + 1 steel bore
  • Shoulderpads:  HQ pelt + pattern + 2 steel bores
  • Cloak:  HQ pelt + pattern + 2 steel bores
  • Sleeves:  HQ pelt + pattern + 2 steel bores
  • Gloves:  HQ pelt + pattern + 2 steel bores
  • Boots:  HQ pelt + pattern + 3 steel bores
  • Belt:  HQ pelt + pattern + 1 steel bore
  • Tunic:  HQ pelt + pattern + 4 steel bores
  • Pants:  HQ pelt + pattern + 3 steel bores
  • Bracer:  HQ pelt + pattern + 1 steel bore

Shadow Silk
(Used for Necromancer Shadowbound boots quest)

  • 1 silk swatch + 1 Shadow Wolf Pelt + 1 scroll of "Gather Shadows"

Large Shadow Silk
(Used for Necromancer Robe of Enshroudment quest)

  • 1 silk swatch + 2 Shadow Wolf Pelts + 1 scroll of "Gather Shadows"

Inky Shadow Silk
(Used for Necromancer Shadowbound gloves quest)

  • 1 silk swatch + 1 Shadow Wolf Pelt + 2 scrolls of "Gather Shadows"

Wu's Fighting Guantlets
Monk-only item, magic weapon for hands and also a lightsource. Viscous Mana is created by an Enchanter, using the "Thicken Mana" spell, which apparently can only be bought from the Brownie village in Lesser Faydark. The components for the spell are a pearl and a poison vial (created via Pottery skill).

  • 1 silk swatch + 4 Heady Kiola + glove pattern + Greater Lightstone + Vial of Viscous Mana

Tailor-Made Backpack
A ten-item backpack, sells for a LOT of money to high-level players.

  • 1 High-Quality Bear Pelt + 1 Backpack pattern

Anyway, hopefully this guide will be helpful to a beginning Dark Elf tailor!
