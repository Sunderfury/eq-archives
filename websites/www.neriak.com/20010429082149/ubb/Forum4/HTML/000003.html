There's is an ongoing debate about the logistics of how the Teir'Dal worship their patron deity. Some argue as Winnd does, saying that it becomes a cutthroat backstabbing society inwhich the strongest survive through a torrent of constant political chaos. The second theory is the one I prefer and, indeed, is the one that I roleplay on the Rallos Zek server.

The idea is that the Teir'Dal are a fierce caste society. Every Dark Elf knows his or her place within the whole of society, much like a colony of ants. Depending on class, individuals are met out with roles to which they adhere. They may become the best of their class, but they will never truly be able to challenge the next heighest tier in Dark Elf society. This formed a debate amongst the players about which caste was most dominant. Immediatly, the following were discarded: The 'Outcast' mages, namely anything but the Necromancers. So too were the Rogue Assassins and Warrior Guards of Indigo Brotherhood discarded. This left Shadow Knights, Necromancers and Clerics. Clerics seemed ill suited according to the masses, speaking the word and providing advisory to those in power. It took little exploring through Neriak to reveal that the real power behind the throne existed in the Shadow Knights as the palace honour guard and the Necromancers as the object focussed power of darkness. No other race truly welcomes and seeks the dark powers of necomancy like the Teir'Dal. In all other societies, they're outcasts. Not so here. They're leaders.

So a fierce loyalty begins to devellope with this cast system. Each relies upon the other because simply, it's the Teir'Dal against the world. Yes, the Ogres and Trolls are their allies, but how far can they be relied upon? Some conversions have been made among the ranks of Trolls and Ogres, swaying them to the side of Innoruuk, but not all. Out of a common goal to ruin the world and shape it for themselves as their Father, Innoruuk, shaped the Elvin King and Queen, the Dark Elves must band together.

Do marriages happen? Perhaps. Treaties, favours, and yes, even undying loyalty can form lasting partnerships. By our standards, it probably might even seem like love. Honour, devotion, even a trust could be observed. Children born of such unions might have the advantage of class depending on parentage. The son of a Priestess and Necomancer might indeed enjoy a fullfilled life in either role, as their Lords dictate.

Roleplaying isn't about finding out what *can't* happen and what *shouldn't* happen. It's about exploring possibilities and making discoveries. Shutting down ideas is the wrong way to go. Flesh out those first inspirations and see where they take you.
