(strictly out of character)

I have some very real problems with people who take roleplaying to this degree on a non-roleplay dedicated server.

Now, on a 'perfect' server where everyone roleplayed you'd take your chances when dealing with a wizard who had a different religion than you did. Or one who was a different race. Because, this would be your *character* mistrusting them, and your *character* probably would rather just walk then get the teleport. =)

But, when people ask for teleports, they're very likely asking out of character. To do this to someone who does *not* roleplay and who made it clear that they asked someone out of character in a /tell to teleport them is very clearly not right to me.

Even if one plays a soloable classes we all depend on others on our server to aid us, especially out of character. By pulling this trick, and pulling it too often, you are running the risk of having very few people to group with at the higher levels - and by having the word go around that *you* (not your character, You) are a jerk. (this is not my opinion of you, I don't know you, just my perception of how the teleportee may find you)

There is a large difference between a character and the person behind the keyboard. When you do things that have very negative repercussions on someone's enjoyment of the game, you have stepped beyond the boundaries of roleplaying.

Because, if I need an experience resurrection and none of my guildmates or friends are online, I wouldn't want the only option to be the Cleric I dropped into the middle of CT when he was level 14...

Aye, Keiote, it has been my observation as well.

For this phenomenon, I offer a possible explanation.

Before my wife and I began playing Teir'dal, we played several light race characters, and when in groups, groupspeak such as "ack mom says get off computer, gotta go" was fairly common. Now, playing, Teir'dal, "afk for a sec baby crying" is VERY common -- denoting to me at least, that people who play Teir'dal (or at least those who stick with it) seem to be older, more mature.

That's not to say that younger people can't or don't roleplay, but the desire for instant gratification and easy play is certainly there among the younger ones. Sticking with the restrictions and such of a Teir'dal is difficult and generally takes someone more mature...

And since those who stick with the Teir'dal characters tend to be more mature, the desire for roleplay seems to also be there, and hence you will see a lot more roleplaying Dark Elves...

I do, however, run into a decent number of light race roleplayers - usually to a limited degree -- but roleplaying with them plants the seed, and hopefully they enjoy it and continue to do it.

Hello from Tarew Marr! It has been my experience that most rp'ers are from the evil races. Most people that rp love the challenge of playing an evil or shunned race/class. It adds some excitement to the game, in my humble opinion. I have played many different race/classes, and seen and slain almost all that EQ has to offer. After doing it all I settled on a DE cleric. I am having the most fun with this character out of them all. While it is true I am having more trouble raising and questing with this character, but I think the rp aspects more than make up for these short comings. The character interaction that comes with being an rp'er is to me, more fun than mindlessly slaying everything that walks.

Ok, with that in mind, what exactly does rp'ing mean? EQ has laid out a story in the manuel of how the world was created and how the different races came to be. While the story is short and full of gaps, it does mention the basics of race interaction. DE's are racists and seperratists, believing all others races or inferior, and other gods false. Does not mean you have to play your character that way? HELL NO! It is just a guideline of how the creators of the game invisioned their world. Many tend to follow these guidelines and have great fun with it. To each his own. I think one of the underutilized abilities in the game is to type in a description others can read if they inspect you. If you are rp'ing, type in something that lets others know HOW you are rp'ing. This will cut down on others missed expections of you. I have no problem letting others inspect me to see my description. It cuts down on people badgering me for help or guidenece. If a "good" character, who I don't know, asks me for help, I tell them to inspect me. They read the following: "I am Lilythe K'nightengale of the Clan Rath N'Karrion. I am a priestess of Innoruuk, and sworn protector of the Teir'Dal. If I can be of any assistance to fellow Teir'Dal, just ask! All others I will not tolerate and I give you to the count of 3 to leave! 1....2...."
Most people say thanks and walk away. I usually send them a /tell saying "its nothing personal, I am just trying to RP!". Again most people send another tell say "np, good job at rp'ing" and go about their merry way. Sure, some people are jerks about it, but most don't mind.

On to the subject of inflicting harm or teleporting to dangerous areas as a means of rp'ing. Again, to each his own! Just don't bitch and moan when you are faced with the repercusions of your actions. To me, that sounds like a reportable offense. Others may not see it as rp'ing, just your mean nature showing through. Please people, this is just a game! A highly addictive and extremly fun one, but just a game. It is an excape from reality for an hour or two ( unless your me, then its more like 4-5 hrs. a night). People have the same right to be idiots and wonder around asking for help, just as you have the right carry out your rp'ing to the extent you see fit. But just as in RL, there is a system in place to make sure your rights don't interfer with the rights of others.

Well, thats just my 2cp worth! Have fun and enjoy! =)
