Proposed changes to bylaws

Article I. League Defined

Section 1.01 The league name will be Falstaff Spacesuit Fantasy Football League. The charter year is 1992.

Section 1.02 The league will be comprised of fourteen teams, separated into three divisions. Two of the divisions will consist of five teams each. The remaining division will consist of four teams. Teams will be assigned to the divisions based on their drafting order. The first five teams shall be in one division, the next four teams into another division, and the last five teams into the remaining division.

Article II. Franchise Ownership

Section 2.01 Franchise ownership fee shall be $50.00 for all charter teams.

Section 2.02 New teams requesting admission into the league will be required to pay a first year entry fee of $25.00 in addition to the franchise cost.

Section 2.03 Ownership can be split among as many individuals as desired. Teams with multiple ownership must specify a managing partner. All weekly playoff and prize money will be paid to managing partner and it will be his responsibility to distribute the winnings. Any teams desiring to take on new co-owners must have the approval of two-thirds majority vote of the league members.

Section 2.04 Franchise ownership is non-transferable. Any team owner wanting to drop out of the league may not transfer ownership to another party.

Section 2.05 The commissioner shall maintain a Franchise Acquisition Waiting List. Available franchises shall be awarded to the first person on the acquisition waiting list. Persons interested must be nominated by a league member and approved by two-thirds majority vote to gain the next available position on the Waiting List.

Section 2.06 Ownership rights may be revoked by two-thirds majority vote of all league members.

Article III. Draft

Section 3.01 The player pool will consist of every current NFL player. Once a team selects a player, he is removed from the pool of available players. For drafting purposes, an NFL Team Defense will be referred to as a player.

Section 3.02 Draft order will be determined prior to draft night by lottery. There will be 14 rounds of drafting. Round numbers 1,3,5,7,9,11, and 13 will draft in order of first to last (teams 1 through 14). Round numbers 2,4,6,8,10,12, and 14 will draft in order of last to first (teams 14 through 1).

Section 3.03 Each team will have a total of one and a half minutes per round to draft a player. Teams will be notified when their time is down to the final ten seconds of their allotted time. If a selection has not been made upon the expiration of their allotted time, their pick will move to the end of that round. Disputes as to whether or not a team made a selection within the appropriate time will be decided immediately by a majority vote of all teams present.

Section 3.04 Each team must have a representative present at the draft. A person associated with one team may not draft for another team. Any team owner not present for a round on draft day shall select its players for the rounds missed from the remaining pool of players following the draft.

Section 3.05 Each team will draft players in accordance with the roster defined under Article IV, Rosters. If a team drafts a player who is determined to be in violation of the defined roster, that team shall immediately adjust their roster to comply with roster guidelines. Players will be dropped and new players selected from the available pool of players. This must be done prior to that team issuing a new starting lineup.

Article IV. Rosters

Section 4.01 Defense and Special Teams are combined and fall under the category of Team Defense.

Section 4.02 Wide Receiver and Tight End are combined and fall under the category of Wide Receiver.

Section 4.03 Each team roster must consist of the following:
(a) Two (2) Quarterbacks.
(b) Two (2) Place-kickers.
(c) Two (2) Team Defenses.
(d) Two (2) Runningbacks.
(e) Three (3) Wide Receivers.
(f) The remaining three (3) positions may consist of any combination of Runningbacks or Wide Receivers.

Section 4.04 Weekly starting lineups will consist of seven (7) players and one (1) team defense. The remaining players on that roster shall make up the reserves or bench. Starting lineups must include the following:
(a) One (1) Quarterback.
(b) One (1) Place-kicker.
(c) One (1) Team Defense.
(d) One (1) Runningback.
(e) Three (3) Wide Receivers.
(f) Either one additional Runningback OR Wide Receiver.

Section 4.05 Deadline for turning in a starting lineup is prior to the opening kickoff of the first game of the NFL week. If the first game of the week is on a Thursday, ALL starting lineups for the week must be in prior to kickoff of that game.

Section 4.06 If a team does not turn in a starting lineup for the first game of the year following the draft, a default lineup will be selected based on the order of players drafted.

Section 4.07 If a team does not submit changes to the starting lineup, the lineup of the previous week will be used.

Article V. Transactions

Section 5.01 Moves, Defined
(a) Trades between two teams made at the same time count as a trading set. Each trading set counts as one move.
(b) Each team will be allowed three (3) free moves per season. Each move thereafter must be paid for in accordance with the appropriate fees.
(c) Each drop and add combination counts as one move.

Section 5.02 Deadlines
(a) Players added to the roster become active at Midnight on Friday and are available for play in the next starting lineup.
(b) Trading deadline for the season shall be set to Midnight Friday prior to the eleventh (11th) week of play in the NFL season.
(c) Any team mathematically eliminated from the playoffs prior to the trading deadline will not be allowed to make trades.
(d) Any player dropped from the roster of a team mathematically eliminated from the playoffs prior to the trading deadline will not be put into the player pool.

Section 5.03 Trades
(a) Once a move is completed and certified by the commissioner, the players become active to their respective new rosters and are available for play in the next starting lineup.
(b) Free trades will be allowed on draft day and at the Midyear social gathering.
(c) Once a team trades a player away, that team cannot trade back for that player. However, if said player becomes available via the player pool, said team will be allowed to pick that player up.

Section 5.04 Drops and Adds
(a) Teams may only drop and add players from the available pool of players. A player being dropped does not become part of the player pool until midnight Friday of that week.
(b) When requesting a move to drop/add players, a team may include alternate picks in the event that another team picks up their selection.
(c) Teams may drop and add players to their roster during the week with a transaction deadline for the week dependant upon when the first NFL game of the NFL week is played. When the first NFL game of the week is played on:
     (i) Sunday: deadline is Midnight Friday.
     (ii) Saturday: deadline is Midnight Thursday.
     (iii) Friday: deadline is Midnight Wednesday.
     (iv) Thursday: deadline is Midnight Wednesday.
(d) In the event that two separate teams request to add the same player to their rosters, the team with the worst record shall be awarded that player. Tiebreakers will be the least points scored. If this fails to break the tie, a second tie breaker shall be least total points scored.
(e) Teams may continue to drop and add players until the end of the season.
(f) Any player dropped by a team following the trading deadline will not be put into the player pool. Once a player is dropped from a team after this deadline said player would become unavailable for pickup.

Article VI. Scoring

Section 6.01 Teams competing receive points based on their starting lineups. Winner is the team with the most points scored in one NFL weekend. In the event of a tie, points scored by non-starting players shall be used.

Section 6.02 The following defines the offensive player scoring:
(a) Throwing a touchdown pass, 4 points.
(b) Receiving a touchdown pass, 6 points.
(c) Running for a touching down, 6 points.
(d) Kicking a Field Goal, 3 points.
(e) Kicking an Extra Point, 1 points.
(f) Running for Extra Points, 2 points.
(g) Passing for Extra Points, 1 points.
(h) Receiving for Extra Points, 2 points.

Section 6.03 The following defines the defensive player scoring:
(a) Kickoff returned for a touchdown, 6 points.
(b) Punt returned for a touchdown, 6 points.
(c) Interception returned for a touchdown, 6 points.
(d) Fumble returned for a touchdown, 6 points.
(e) Blocked Punt returned for a touchdown, 6 points.
(f) Blocked Field Goal return for a touchdown, 6 points.
(g) Safety, 2 points.
(h) Intercepting a pass, 1 points.
(i) Recovering a fumble, 1 points.

Section 6.04 The following defines special Bonus point scoring:
(a) Passing for 300 or more yards, 2 points.
     (i) 1 additional point will be awarded for each 100 yards passing beyond 300 yards.
(b) Rushing for 100 or more yards, 2 points.
     (i) 1 additional point will be awarded for each 100 yards rushing beyond 100 yards.
(c) Receiving for 100 or more yards, 2 points.
     (i) 1 additional point will be awarded for each 100 yards receiving beyond 100 yards.
(d) Kicking a field goal for 50 yards or more 2 points.

Article VII. Regular Season

Section 7.01 Teams will play a fourteen-week schedule. The remaining three weeks shall be reserved for playoffs and bowls.

Section 7.02 Regular Season will coincide with that of the NFL. The first NFL week of the NFL season will mark the first week of the League regular season.

Article VIII. Playoffs

Section 8.01 There will be two separate playoffs for teams. One will be for the Schaeffer Cup and one for the Super Bowl Championship.

Section 8.02 Each division will produce one division winner. Of the remaining teams, the top three will gain a wildcard berth.

Section 8.03 The two top division winners will gain a BYE for the first playoff game. The remaining division winner will play against the lowest of the three-wildcard teams. The other two-wildcard teams will play each other.
(a) The winner of the two-wildcard teams will play against the top division winner. The winner of the third division winner and the third wild card team will play against the second best division winner.
(b) The winners of these two games advance to play in the Falstaff Spacesuit Super Bowl.

Section 8.04 The remaining eight teams not competing in the playoffs for the Super Bowl will compete for the Schaeffer Cup.
(a) These teams will be ranked in order from 1 through 8 with ties broken in accordance with section VIII.6.
(b) Teams will be pitted against each other based on their records.
     (i) Game 1 will be team 1 vs. team 8
     (ii) Game 2 will be team 2 vs. team 7
     (iii) Game 3 will be team 3 vs. team 6
     (iv) Game 4 will be team 4 vs. team 5
(c) Winner of game 1 will play winner of game 4. Winner of game 2 will play winner of game 3
(d) The two remaining teams will then advance to the Schaefer Cup.

Section 8.05 Tiebreakers for winner of the Division shall be as follows:
(a) Overall record
(b) Divisional record
(c) Head to head competition
(d) Points Scored
(e) Total points scored
(f) Points Against

Section 8.06 Tiebreakers for Wildcard teams shall be as follows:
(a) Overall record
(b) Points Scored
(c) Total points scored
(d) Points Against

Article IX. Fees & Money Distribution

Section 9.01 Franchise Fee: 14 teams, $50.00 per team.

Section 9.02 Pay Out shall be as follows:
(a) 16 Weekly High Point Winners, $20.00 each.
(b) 3 Division Winners, $25.00 each.
(c) 3 Wild Card Teams, $15.00 each.
(d) 2 Round 1 Playoff winners, $15.00 each.
(e) 2 Round 2 Playoff winners, $30.00 each.
(f) Super Bowl Champion, $75.00
(g) Super Bowl Loser, $25.00
(h) Schaeffer Cup Winner, $15.00
(i) Handicap bowl winner, $25.00
(j) League Expenses, $30.00

Section 9.03 Total Pay out, $700.00

Section 9.04 All monies gained from transaction fees shall be applied to league expenses.

Article X. Commissioner's Job Description

Section 10.01 A League Commissioner shall be appointed annually by a majority vote of team owners.

Section 10.02 Commissioner shall enforce the League Bylaws as they are written.

Section 10.03 Commissioner shall appoint a league treasurer. (a) Treasurer will handle all league monetary business which include the following:
     (i) Collection of all franchise fees prior to Draft Night.
     (ii) Collection of all transaction fees throughout the year.
     (iii) Distribution of all league money as directed by the Commissioner.

Section 10.04 Commissioner shall appoint a league secretary.
(a) Secretary will handle all aspects involved in the writing, publication, and distribution of a weekly newsletter which must include (but not restricted to) the following:
     (i) Past week's game results.
     (ii) Current standings and total points
     (iii) Next week's schedule.
     (iv) Newsletter can be distribute via mail or facsimile, or e-mail.
     (v) Members who request hard copies by mail must provide the league with the appropriate funds to cover mailing expenses.

Section 10.05 Commissioner shall resolve all disputes in accordance with league bylaws. Disputed items not covered under written league bylaws will be entirely up to the commissioner to rule on.

Section 10.06 A Commissioner ruling can be overturned by a three-quarter-majority vote of the league (approximately 10 of 14 vote).

Section 10.07 Commissioner must maintain statistics involving league play. Statistics involving past week's game results, current standings, total points, and next week's schedule will be transferred to the league secretary for publication in a timely manner.

Article XI. Changes in Bylaws

Section 11.01 League Bylaws should be annually ratified by a majority vote of all team owners before the beginning of the draft.

Section 11.02 Majority vote of all teams shall be required to change any section of the league bylaws.

Section 11.03 Any changes, if not directly specified, become effective immediately.
