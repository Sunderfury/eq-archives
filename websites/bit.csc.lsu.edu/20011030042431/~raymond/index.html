Luck Factor Index

Consider a 14 team league. On any given NFL weekend, 7 of those teams will score in the top half while the other 7 teams score in the bottom half. The chances for a top 7 team to register a win are better than that for the lower 7 teams. The top 4 teams have an even greater chance to win because there are 10 other teams that scored less points than them.

The top 2 teams are expected to have the best odds of winning since there are 12 other teams that scored less than them. Suppose your team finished 2nd in overall points scored. You would expect a win because 12 out of the remaining 13 teams rank below you. However, the question is, which of those other 13 teams did you play this week? Only one of them could beat you. You'd win if you played any of the other 12. This is what my Luck Factor is based on.

Rank all teams based on their score. Your team will finish somewhere from 1-14 leaving each of the 13 other teams to finish in one of the remaining positions. If you finished 1st, then the probability that another team could beat you is 0/13 or 0% chance because none of the remaining 13 teams could finish ahead of yours. If you finished 2nd, the probability that another team could finish ahead of yours is 1/13 or 0.077 giving you a 92.3% chance to win. So, if you happen to lose when you finished 2nd in the rankings, you just had tough scheduling luck. Fortunately, however, everything has a way of evening out. If you lose when you should have won, more than likely, you'll win a game you should have lost. But some people are snake bitten. Their scales in the odds of balance are skewed. My chart will reveal these wrongly weighted misalignments of chance.

I have converted the probability table into a simple luck factor chart. Rank the teams from 1st to last. The top 7 teams should win and the bottom 7 teams should lose. Since positions 7 and 8 are very close to 50%, I have assigned them both a luck factor of 0. To determine what your luck factor is, note what your ranking was for the week. If you lose when you finished in the top half you get a negative luck factor rating. If you win when you finish in the bottom half, you get a positive luck factor rating. Ratings from week to week are added. A negative factor is unlucky; a positive factor is lucky. If you finished 4th and your opponent finished 2nd, you lost when you should have won because you finished in the top half. Additionally, if you lost to a divisional opponent when you should have won, add a modifier of -1. If you beat a divisional opponent when you should have lost, add a modifier of +1. Calculate your luck factor for any given week based on the chart.
