An Unexpected Pilgrimmage
by Simon DeMontfort

"This is of great importance for him", said Tasslehof Windrunner, eyeing his friend's frowning face. "The path of the warrior is what he has chosen, quite like the Aluvian Hero". Zaphod the Great reluctantly took out a strange key from beneath his tunic and unlocked the glowing doorway to Thorsten Cragstone's crypt. "Enter, Paladin Simon", again said Tasslehof with a warm and kindly voice. The two friends moved out of the way to let the heavily armored figure in.

At first, all was blindness, as if the Shard of Light itself was contained in this very chamber. As his eyes slowly grew accustomed, Simon de Monfort stared in awe at the polished stone coffin, quietly sitting in the center. His heaume fell with a loud clanking sound on the cold hard floor. Soon, both of the knight's knees followed the object in its descent A long prayer and meditation to Thorsten began.

Other adventurers, hunting for beasts and treasures in the Underground city, peeked into the tomb and came in. They started asking questions about the place and who was buried there. Tasslehof answered their demands with a wondrous story. He told them of a time when the Empyreans were forced to leave Dereth through Asheron's portals before the Olthoi menace. He explained to them how the Aluvian, coming into Dereth, were made slaves by the Olthoi for many many years. He recounted the rebellion that followed and the feats of the two Aluvian leaders, Elysa Strathelar and Thorsten Cragstone. Tasslehof ended with the death of Thorsten, fighting the Olthoi Queen and liberating the humans from the insect's claws.

Enthralled by the story, the adventurers listened with great curiosity. But soon they reverted to their more. usual nature. "Blast! Let's pry the thing open and see what's inside!" shouted one of them. Others started running and jumping about the chamber, while a most daring one climbed on the coffin. "Sacrilege!", yelled Simon de Monfort, standing suddenly high on his legs. "How dare you think of pillage after learning the deeds of such a courageous man?" In his rage, his fist landed directly on the coffin. There was a stunning flash of holy light. Simon saw it. But most of it he saw not with his eyes. The light had burst elsewhere.

All was white. Nothing but pure light. Light so bright Simon could not see his own body. A deep voice came, resounding in what must have been his head. "Sad indeed is it to see that so many humans already forgot what occurred merely ten years ago. They act as if the menace was gone forever. It has not. I have seen the hive, I have fought it for so long. Many still learn to use a blade and conjure fire. But very few keep in mind why." The voice paused and started again: "A Queen will return. The land shall bleed. Never lose the focus."

When Simon came back to reality, he was walking out of the crypt with Tass and Zaphod. His head ached, a sharp pain only worsened by the shouts and laughter coming out of the now overly crowded tomb chamber. "That is quite a bump you have gained there Paladin. You almost opened your head there on that stone corner. Had us real worried", Tass told him, smiling. "Aye, quite", briefly replied Simon. He was lost in his thoughts, wondering if the prophetic voice really had existed. Rounding a corner, Tasslehof turned and faced him. "Did I tell you I saw an Olthoi real close lately? T'was a frightening site! Of course I had to leave in quite a hurry!", laughed he. He looked Simon intently in the eyes and added: "Battling an Olthoi one on one is the utmost challenge a warrior could dream of. The time will come for you to face it my lad. You might as well prepare for it right away!" The Akalabethian leader winked and darted up a flight of stairs, leaving there a very dumbfounded paladin.
