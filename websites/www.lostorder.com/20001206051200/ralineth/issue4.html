"FRRROOOOOOOOAK!"
 The mighty LOA stood assembled and in great numbers all pledging their services to the Paladins Zirc and Dargo. We were here to get a legendary sword, Ghoul's Bane, off the lord of the dank dungeon of Guk. We descended for many miles searching for the throne room of the Shin Lord and battled a vast array of frogs and fungis men. Wherever the Order went battles were won and lives lost yet still we bravely ventured onward. The courage that I saw!
 
It seems apparent to mention that the comradarie that comes with the Order brings an honor all alone. To fight and die by such valiant members is indeed a blessing from Tunare. We never found the Shin Lord. We waited for many days for the fiend to return but he never did. Even so, the LOA rallied together and fought the Lord's minions claiming a major victory for the Order. 
 
Pickled Froglock anyone?
 
Perth Treeskin

BARBARIANS TIE THE KNOT
Rumours that have been spreading recently about a possible union between that pair of unwashed barbarians Waran and Azlana were recently confirmed. A translator for the pair announced that yes the two were engaged to be married, however he did not go as far as stating when the union would actually take nor did he deny the rumour that the father of the bride to be had to bribe the burly barbarian with tinkets and copious amounts of ale to actually wed his daughter.

Early Sunday morning Dearg, Gildan, Woozle, Aillean, and Grogor consented to take a mere
babe-in-arms named Argil with them into the depths of the Goblin fortress. The young mage reportedly comported himself quite well, alternating valiant meditation with courageously fleeing from the larger goblins at every opportunity.  Many times the high-pitched shriek "Help! There's one on me! Get it off, get it off!!!" was heard, and every time the group responded by
covering their ears and taunting the goblins off the white-robed neophyte. Occasionally, the lanky High Elf valiantly stabbed a weaker goblin in the back with his miniscule dagger.  Rumors of his robe having been soiled in terror, however, were completely baseless.  That was... rust, from a rusty short sword.

Ahem.  In any case, the group was quite successful in their assault.  Once the "King's Room" was gained, the group settled into a rhythm which proved to be quite effective.  Killing goblins and goblin workers at every opportunity seemed to cause the group's main target, the Evil Eye Borxx, to appear more frequently.  In fact, by the end of the adventure, every member of the group had possession of at least one of the foul lenses.  While Woozle the Balding Gnome insisted on having his lens crafted into a beannie (+10 to saves vs Hair Loss), he did consent to assist other group members in their acquisition of the coveted Staff of the Observers (unlimited charges of Voyeurism).  All hail Woozle!

Sadly, these gains did not come without price.  The group was victimized twice by huge trains brought to them coutesy of some other groups of... questionable ethics.  Gildan fell twice, defending the rear and ensuring that the rest of the group made it to safety.  Rumor has it that he was last
seem in Steamfont wearing some sharp-looking black leggings.. but those may have just been sweatpants.

Argil Silverlock

THE CENATURS OF SOUTH KARANA
As part of our ongoing efforts to vainly drag our readers out of an intellectual mire that appears be bogging down the populace of Norrath, we present another thought-provoking and totally unsubstantiated view of the world...
For eons centaurs have trotted across the grassy plains of the South Karanas living in harmony with it's other major inhabitants, the Gnolls and the Aviaks. But just what are these creatures that 
(continued next column....)		 appear to be a cross between a man and some other beast unkown to the world? At first glance centaurs are merely peaceful sentient creatures that carry bows and adorn themselves with armour, but underneath this lies a strong social culture, and a well-defined ordering within them from the weakest foals to the strongest charger. To harass one is to invite the wrath of the entire village, although if done in secrecy naught will be done to the perpretator. The reasons for this are as yet unknown - perhaps they believe the centaur to be taken by one of the many cyclopses that roam the area or mayhap they simply do not care. Whatever the the reason is a matter for another debate as now this space is filled.

A RESPONSE TO THE HISTORY OF THE ELVEN RACE...
I am writing in response to your recent article implying that High Elves and Wood Elves are in fact one race.  Your suggestion that the noble High Elves of Felwithe are related in anything more than name to the animal-like Wood Elves of Kelethin is shocking and disgusting.  An slobbrering Ogre could tell us apart (just from looking at us I mean... I have it on good faith that Wood Elf tastes "just like chicken", but I know of no Ogre who has
attempted to eat a High Elf and survived)  But I  digress...
Let me present to you, in a lowbrow manner I hope will not excede your cognitive capacity, a few ways to tell the difference between a High Elf and a Wood Elf:
1)  High Elves honor both trees and our cousins the Naiads, eternal spirits of our beloved trees.  Wood Elves build cities out of them.
2)  High Elves assist the process of natural selection through carefully arranged marriages between our highest families.  Wood Elves perform this function after birth, by allowing their slowest and stupidest  children (relatively speaking, of course) to fall to their deaths.
3)  Young High Elven maidens wear the latest in fashionable evening wear out
of Felwithe, and always look prim and proper.  Young Wood Elf wenches look
as if they should be dancing on stage in a sleazy Freeport tavern.
4)  High Elf young men look like Spock; Wood Elf boys look like Johnny Depp.
5)  Lastly, one of these two races is disparagingly called "Woodies"... and it's NOT the High Elves.
High Elves: we're not snobs, we're just better than them.

Argil Silverlock
