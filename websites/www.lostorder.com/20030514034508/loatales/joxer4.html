The Origin of Joxer
by Joxer

Lord Joxer the Mighty, Grandmaster Swordsman & Captain of the Charon's Pride

The Brewman was busy tonight. The clatter of tankards and aging wood rang throughout the festive pub. Yule celebrations were in full effect with holly wreathes and garland draperies strung about the soot covered rafters. The place was quite packed, as it usually was this time of year; a fire, freshly made, burned brightly - casting misshapen shadows across the main room. The Tavern was alive with chatter.

Joxer the Mighty sat quite drunk on a bench near the hearth. He gripped a large mug of mead in his thin left hand as he stared deeply into the crimson glow. The Lord nervously played with his thick mustache as he gazed - obviously deep in thought about some past event. A small child, about the age of seven, walked cautiously up to the thoughtful warrior carrying a small toy ship carved out of the finest Yew.

"My lord," the young child said, "Brother Brimstone said ye would tell me of the sea.. he said ye knew of far off lands and magical folk."

Joxer, startled out of the drunken pondering, turned his gaze down to the peasant. As he gazed at the child, a tear welled up in his eye and slowly dripped down his cheek - a distant pain being caressed with care.

"Aye lad, I do. Come here and let me tell ye my story." The child, a poor boy named Merrel from the neighboring Abbey, climbed into Joxer's lap, attentive to every word.

"I was born in a small town known as Illwood, located on the southern shores or Brittannia near the Great Jungle. Aye, son, many moons ago that Jungle was taken care of and inhabitants of isolated villages grew wealthy off the various rare plants and exotic birds that once lived in abundance there. Fair Trinsic, just a few days ride to the north, was the majestic jewel of the south. I can still remember the way the jungle mists softly embrace that great city in the mornings. Aye, what a sight indeed."

"This was, of course, before the wicked slavers came. We were kind and hospitable to the many trappers that came from Trinsic, but once news of a possible iron shortage near Destard reached us, many armed men entered the jungles looking to enslave the once peaceful lizardfolk for use as cheap labor in the work camps. The lizardfolk, being mighty in their own right, fought back with an unexpected vengeance."

"Soon after the villages began to burn. I can still remember the day the lizardmen attacked Illwood. I was baking a Dewberry Pie for my wife and child, the smell of which still haunts me to this day. It was about midday I believe, the jungle was hot and humid, very typical this time of year..the screams.hissing.."

"I awoke much later on a merchant ship headed for Serpent's Hold. The Greel Major was her name. I had been severely wounded in the attack and left for dead, a large gash cut deep into my chest - the victim of a savage spear thrust. Captain Greel, during a routine run of the coastline, spotted the burning village just after sunset. He and his men rescued several of us that were left for dead - the lizardmen having long since vanished back into the deep Jungle. My wife, Laurell, and son Geoffrey were not so lucky...I can still hear her sometimes in the middle of the night.gently whispering my name or singing sweet Geoffrey to sleep..."

"I spent many months recovering at Serpent's Hold.the thought of my lost friends and family weighing heavy on my spirit. Captain Greel and I had become good friends during this time as he was often traveling to and from the Hold and Trinsic. He was a burly man, not even six stones tall, but with the demeanor of a giant. A good friend he was and in my days of confusion, he gave me purpose aboard his ship. We sailed for many moons together - Greel teaching me the ways of the Great Oceans so that I may perhaps command my own ship and assist him in his trades."

"We came across several pirates in our travels, among them included the Dread Baert the Grim himself! It was that foul pirate's ship, the Charon's Pride, which we conquered! Oh the sight of her on the horizon was incredible! Her freshly painted ebony hull appearing as a demon on the dark waters. But the skill of Captain Greel and his well-trained guards were too much for the battle weary Baert. 'Twas I, Joxer the Mighty, who bested him in combat - the ship being my true reward. I sailed that shackled pirate back to the Serpent's Hold where to this day he rots, lamenting his past greatness."

"My travels are wide, young friend, wide indeed. I have sailed the whole of the world, seeing many strange lands and wondrous peoples. I have fought with the greatest of Lords, sailed with the virtuous of men. I even gave the one known as the Avatar a ride to Buccaneer's Den once - he was on some mission of great import that he would not disclose. It was on one of my many sea going journeys to Deceit that I met a most noble of men, Lord Unforgiven. He spoke to me of a great guild that had the noblest of citizenry that served to uphold the sacred virtues. I learned of the Shard and Tome and pledged my heart to that cause. Aye lad, now ye find me still here in their gracious company."

"That is my story, or a small part of it.now go, take yer ship to the water and set it afloat. Only then will it take ye where ye need to be. Free, with virtue, but free nonetheless."
