Greyhawk's Vision

Greyhawk stared stoically into the dancing flames. He felt the heat from the blaze on his face – an odd contrast to the icy chill of the winter wind. The fire seemed alive to him, as if a living being had been summoned out of dry kindling. With their hissing and crackling, the flames spoke to him, in a language older than words.

Nearby, Bolgar fidgeted uncomfortably. It was getting dark, and the frozen wastes are a dangerous place to be after the sun has fallen. It would not be long before the wolves started to hunt – and there were worse things than wolves out here. He knew better to disturb Greyhawk, though. He did not understand these vision quests, but he knew that they required a lot of concentration.

He had agreed to accompany his older brother out into the plains of Everfrost, but the truth was that he would rather have stayed home. Bolgar was a simple man. He liked to believe that the world was a simple place. He did not like mysteries, nor magic. He preferred to deal with real things, things he could see and touch. When he first learned that Greyhawk had chosen the path of the Shaman, he was filled with dismay. Why chase after ghosts and visions? Was it not enough to lead a simple life?

When at last Greyhawk stirred and rose, Bolgar breathed a sigh of relief. "What did you see?" he asked.

"Darkness."

Bolgar laughed. "I can see darkness. It is night, now. I don’t need to stare into a fire for an hour to see it."

Greyhawk shook his head. "You do not understand. I saw a world of darkness. A world covered forever with night. Our world."

Bolgar shivered. "That makes no sense. The sun will come up tomorrow. You can bet your last copper on it. Tomorrow, there will be sun."

"Aye, perhaps tomorrow. And perhaps the day after that. But there is a great darkness coming, a darkness which will end all light."

"If that is true…and I do not think it is…then we will need many torches."
