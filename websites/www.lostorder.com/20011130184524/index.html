Sunday, November 18, 2001
Another patch is on the way!

From the Front: (and you know its a mad house at CRS)

Version 140 will be released this week, probably before Wednesday. Note that how they version the program has changed. The second number now denotes a version that does not significantly change to graphics or game engine. That being said, here's whats in the new version, straight from the mouth of Mo.

-We re-enabled the J key for riding trucks. troops can jump off at any time again, however, we also added a velocity check when they do, and lets just say it can be "dangerous" to leave a moving vehicle. Troops can also be "thrown" from trucks and tanks if those vehicles impact buildings or trees etc at speed. And again, being in a vehicle mishap can be very nasty for riders. As well, troops can be run over and killed by vehicles again.
-We redid all of the map icons with less saturation. The map should look a lot better now. We also gave the in-game map a transparency setting (you can change the setting using "settings.exe" and the "misc" tab). In that same tab in the settings app you can select if you'd like the mouse on or off at spawn as a default.
-We redid all in-vehicle screen display text to a font that is much less "horrible".
-We redid all the icon display technology, they should look sharper and function better, and aircraft types or player Ids won't show until the IFF color draws. This will make ID'ing planes a bit more fun.
-We fixed the bomb collider to detect high altitude drops more effectively.
-Crew members on multi crewed planes can now red/black out.
-We added an in-game clock, tied to the game time of day (use "numlock" to view)
-The Bf109/Bf110 elevator trim changes are now visible in the 3d model, also aileron droop on flap deployment is modeled.
-We improved the mobility on the 47mm AT gun, it should be movable over most terrain now.
-The P38(t)'s machinegun is now aligned with gunsight
-The P38(t) and PzIIIf's ammo loads were revised to match historical loads
-The Char B1's hull machine gun has been realigned
-We added new interior main gun sounds for most tanks.
-We added separate color for squad-member icons, these will show white for now, and we will eventually allow players to set this color in the settings app.
-We added a forced exit after death. This basically shift esc's for you after a timeout.
-We changed the red-out at wounding effect to be less opaque, so unless you are almost dead, you'll still be able to see a bit.
-We enhanced the "Death Cam" feature to be a bit more dramatic.
-We hunted down another cause of the yellow flags, these should now be fixed.

Also mentioned in a different post, is a major crash bug fixed. Apparently data was not matching from the player to the server (the sever wasn't getting what it thought it was getting). This has been "tortured to death" as Mo was quoted as saying, and should make the game a lot more stable.

Tuesday, October 09, 2001

The cell is going on a diet...

Gryf has dropped a ton of wiz bang neato update info on the production notes forum. I'll give a brief rundown and translation:

The code for bombers is done. The test build can now handle multiple engines, as well as variable bomb load outs. Also, a counter has been added to keep track of clips for guns that use them. (Thats clips, not bullets.)

AI auto guns can now be selective of the targets they engage (choosing a truck full of soldiers over a tank). Plane convergence has been tweaked, and is now set at 200 meters. (This applies to planes with guns on the wings. Guns don't shot straight on the wing, rather they aim slightly in toward the propeller to allow the bullets to all focus in on one particular point in front of the plane. This allowed maximum hits to the pilot if he could get an enemy plane in that spot.)
The front end is modified with the addition of a radio channel that reports whenever a CP is captured.
[This is a big one]
The game engine has dropped the super cell in favor of an octet cell. For the mortals amoung us, a super cell means that while in the game, the engine draws a huge area around you and keeps it in memory. AC uses this method, it allows for easy tracking of objects that could affect a player, but more importantly, its what makes moveing from server to server seem seamless. This is why the game can take up so much RAM. With the new octet-radius system only a slice is kept in memory, namely, what the player can see. The game then constantly scans the situation to load what you are about to see, and forget what you no longer see. This system should reduce RAM usage a good bit, but the trade off will be higher CPU usage. Shouldn't be a problem for anything over 1k htz. (Thats a semi educated guess though, read the post on Support Systems and Terrain for details.

And finally, (this is a really good bit here) troopers no longer suffer from the 'get a scratch, fail the mission' syndrom. For those who don't know what this is, currently the mission scoring system in game is very simple. If you kill something, return to base and despawn without damage you gain mission points. Do this about 30 times and you gain a rank. Obviously this is very difficult to do, even in a tank, because despite having armor, a scratch on ANY of the crew results in mission failure. The community thinks this is a pile of gerbil droppings as a whole, and fortunatly, CRS agrees.

Basically the new system awards a mission success if you haven't lost a set amount of blood or gotten hit in the brain. (This is called a critical system, I tend to agree with the name.) In a vehicle, you not only have to worry about you and your crewmens bodies losing too much blood and brains, but also you have to worry about what systems on the tank have been hit. Some systems (criticalprimary, or critical systems) knock you out of the mission immediatly... such as losing your engine. Less important systems like a gun are non critical (criticalsecondary) and won't knock you out of a mission unless you lose to many of them.

Again head over to the forums for detailed reading.
