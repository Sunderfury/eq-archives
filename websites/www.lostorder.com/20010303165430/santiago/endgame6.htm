Three Little Elves

"Well, this is just great," said Jarlaxle. "I thought you said you knew the way."

"I do," said Kileyan. "This just isn’t it."

"So we’re lost, is that it? How can we be lost? You’re a druid, aren’t you? At home in the woods, and all that?"

"I am at home in the woods, but that does not mean that I never get lost. We simply took a wrong turn, that’s all. Just relax."

"Relax? We were supposed to be in Kaladim yesterday. But no…we just had to stop at that wine shop, didn’t we?"

"Ah, but it was fine wine," said Penaden, who had sat down against a tree and was tuning his lute. He strummed the strings, and lifted his gaze up to the leaves of the trees.

"We are three little elves
Who have lost our way
Will we ever find Kaladim?
Who can say?"

"Penaden, put away that damn thing before I beat you to death with it," warned Jarlaxle. "I’m not in a good mood."

"Half an elf
With half a heart
Jarlaxle’s turning into
A grouchy old fart."

"Oh, now that does it!" Jarlaxle began to chase Penaden, who ran through the woods, laughing. "Come here, you little goblin!" shouted Jarlaxle. "I’m going to use you for spear practice!"

Kileyan sighed and leaned against a massive oak tree.

"We’re never going to get to Kaladim," he muttered.

"Yes you will," said the tree.

Kileyan cried out in surprise, and jumped away from the great oak.

"Don’t worry," said the tree. "My bark is worse than my bite."

"A tree that talks, and tells bad jokes?" whispered Kileyan in amazement. "What manner of creature is this?"

The tree began to change. The branches became arms, and the trunk divided into two legs. In seconds, the great oak had transformed into a venerable elf who wore a simple brown robe and carried a wooden staff carved with many glowing sigils.

"Ralineth!" whispered Kileyan, bowing deeply.

"Greetings, young one," said the archdruid. "Tell me, why are you not in Kaladim?"

"We…um…well, we ran into difficulties, milord." He glanced at the trees nearby, where Jarlaxle was still chasing Penaden.

"Those two are at it again, I see," said Ralineth. "Fortunately, the others have not yet arrived, either. But you must make haste, and get there by nightfall tomorrow. There is trouble coming."

"Trouble? Of what sort, milord?"

Ralineth shook his head. "All will be explained at Kaladim. Until then, there is much I must yet learn. Farewell, Kileyan, and may the Goddess protect you."

"Wait! Milord?"

"Yes?"

"Which way is it to Kaladim?"
