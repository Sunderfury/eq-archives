Greetings one and all,

I agree that I notice nobody ever going to the shrines anymore. I could say almost the
same of myself but for the occasional trip to a dust covered relic of the old days. And how
to bring back the need of the shrines to return virtue to the land is indeed a good and
quite tough question. After reading other peoples thoughts on it, and quietly musing over
the idea for a short time, I feel that perhaps it could be best done by using a combination
of all the things suggested by people. Yes, it would be nice if there was in-game code to
support it all, but as soon as that happened I can foresee people sweating over it day and
night to find an exploit. So in-game code should perhaps only be a part of it all, it could
also come back to other players 'honoring' you or your guild somehow as well as GM's
(or seer, counselor or whoever) taking notice of your actions and giving a 'virtue point' or
whatever the system may involve...

This all could even bring in other things into the different towns, such as more beggars or
even a poor house where food and/or gold can be given... just little things to make the
places more complete, more involved and give those who wish to ( and enjoy ) roleplay a
chance to do just that.

Personally, I like the idea of gaining 'something' apon gaining full understanding of all 8
virtues. Even as simple as having 'Avatar' in your title or name bar. Hmm... concept - what
if there was only to be allowed a specified number of 'avatars' per shard at any one time?
This way you would have to work very hard to retain your faith in the virtues lest someone
else who is trying harder gain the title as soon as you lose it through not paying enough
attention. If this was the case, with only a few avatars allowed in game, then giving out
a reward of some sort to them becomes more valuable. Not an item that is all powerful, but
something that has meaning in game, perhaps through quests.

And why limit this to just being 'good' in game? In the earlier ultima's, wasnt there an
oposite virtue for each shrine? The only one I recall was pride being the focus of Magincia,
till it was destroyed by daemons which was the reason they changed to humility. Players
could easily decide to try becoming an 'anti-avatar', or whatever title it would be given,
and work to cause strife within the land in the same concept as Mondain and Minax. Again,
with a set limit on how many of these people can be within the game allows for rp'ing,
especially when considering quests that require an evil avatar to desecrate a shrine, then
a pure avatar to rectify it and so on... It all allows for more quests and in-game activity.

I know I'm wandering off the topic here, but I'm just trying to throw more angles into the
subject to hopefully spark yet more ideas from people.

Fare the all well, and may the cramp in my back soon go away...
Sindaer Roze, Bard of Oceania.
