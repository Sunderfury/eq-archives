A Farewell to Halas

"You have enough food and water?" asked Bolgar.

Greyhawk smiled. His little brother’s voice was gruff, but he could sense his sorrow.

"Yes. I will be fine, Bolgar."

"So you say. What if you never return? What if you die chasing after this ‘vision’, or whatever it is?"

"Then I shall die," said Greyhawk. He turned and looked into the worried face of his brother. "Bolgar, you do not understand why I chose the way of the Shaman, do you?"

Bolgar shook his head. "Because you are crazy?"

Greyhawk laughed. "Aye, perhaps. I would like to think otherwise." He sighed, and looked across the dark lake. "Most men live their lives in darkness," he said. "They never know where they are going, nor where they have been. They never truly see the world around them." He glanced at his brother. "I do not wish to live in such blindness. I cannot. In choosing to follow the path of the spirits, I am following my own heart."

Bolgar nodded. "I still think you are crazy."

"Then wish your crazy brother luck, for here comes the raft," Greyhawk smiled, and embraced his brother. "Farewell, Bolgar. May the Ancestors watch over you, and keep you from harm."

"Farewell, Greyhawk. Safe journey."

Greyhawk stepped onto the raft. "I shall return," he said, smiling. "The spirits have told me so."

"Well, the spirits had better be right," said Bolgar.

The raft began to drift out into the lake. Bolgar watched as it carried his brother off into the darkness.

Greyhawk strode through the familiar icy canyons with a light heart. To outsiders, the paths of Everfrost were an incomprehensible labyrinth. Occasionally, the morning hunting parties would discover the bodies of outsiders who had gotten lost, and either froze to death or got attacked by wolves, or both. Everfrost was not a place for the unwary, nor for those who have no sense of direction.

The young warriors were gathered, as usual, outside the main entrance to Halas. Several of them ran up to him, asking if he would like to accompany them for a hunt. He smiled and shook his head.

"I am going on a journey," he said.
