Posted by Talesn of Wodclf, Bard o' Pacific on November 01, 2000 at 12:49:14:

As some may recall, Erin and I are attending the Ultima Online World Faire, set for November 10 - 11. We'll be leaving in about a week. We are looking forward to this event, as not only should the Faire be fun, but we have friends from Houston and Austin we plan to spend some time with.

If anyone has discovered they are going since I last mentioned this let me know and we will try to find ye in the crowd. We made reservations in Candle Wood Suites Motel in Austin, and the telephone number there is 512-444-8882. You can leave a message for Jerry Suchan and we will follow up on it. So far only Amber has mentioned she is going. Hi Amber!

If anyone has any issues or thoughts that might be brought up at the Faire let me know. I plan to attend one of the Tamer sessions which they reconstituted to include Bards. I duno why. Most Tamer's don't even talk to Bards. As for me, tame Dragons make me nervous. At least the gods (OSI) didn't create Dragon poop.

Another interesting development, which I was not aware of when we signed up for the Faire, is it is said that the Faire participants have first preference for UO2 Beta. Well, actually the quote is, "All UO World Faire attendees will be automatically included in an early stage of external testing, as a special bonus for attending the Faire." I duno what that really means either. A chance to play a barely running game that is hardly related to my favorite game out of the box, Ultima VI. Actually, I am sure that UO2 will eventually overcome UO, in the long run.

I have posted info about UO2 both to the Bards here and to the Smiths at the UBB board. The response has been universal. Disinterest, denial, lack of interest, distaste. "No one" currently playing UO thinks much about starting over again. Then again, there are all the people who caught the Bored of Sosaria Affliction and left.

What can I say? There will almost certainly be Bards in UO2, but who knows what they will be like? The skill tree approach being developed for UO2 is completely different from the approach in UO, and will lead to … what? Well, since we are not giving the developers any of our ideas, as experienced Bards with UO, well they will do … WHAT!? We will see. It is probably too late to have any seminal impact on any of this, but further input is possible.

Anyone interested in looking deeper into UO2 can check out UWO: ORIGIN website

Tal
