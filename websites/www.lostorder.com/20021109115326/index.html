Saturday, October 05, 2002

Ya gotta keep rooting for them.

Yes, the newest WWIIOnline public beta is underway allowing the players to run amok amid the bountiful new patch. The players rejoiced! Until that is, 2000 and XP users relised that everything in their root C directory had been deleted including critical system files. Me? I use 98 thanks, and the patch runs great. I'm loving it.

The Rats have taken it in stride, and a "CRS wiped my harddrive and all I got was this lousy T-shirt" banner now displays prominently on the HQ forums page.

The players have also taken it in stride, and the patch seems to be worth the frustration. Indeed in a move that truly showed how close the developers are to the community, the sheep statues [To get that reffrence, you have to be an online sim nut, and have played Warbirds 1.x extensivly... its a deep and long running subject. Mostly humorus... mostly.] are now being put to use in very appreciated way. Carrot, was a prominent member of the community during the beginning. Recently he was overcome by cancer and now his username is memorialized upon the plaque on one of those statues. Other members who have died while a part of the community have likewise been remembered, and others are under consideration. I know other online games have permanent memorials to significant players, but ask any member of the WWIIOnline community and they'll all say that nothing but nothing beats the style laid upon our game like what CRS has done.
