I question that one profession is more noble than another one -- is is the individual player(s) and the usage that make a profession noble. The tamer's reputation currently suffers from twinks dominating spawn points and people that use pets to be anti-social in various ways, but bards have been in that situation as well. I vividly remember the complaints and negative feelings towards bards when we could provoke thru walls.

Taming is now easier than provocation, however, prior to UO:R provocation could be GM'd in a few nights with two bunnies trapped in a house. Taming on the flipside had been extremely hard (no gains from previously tamed animals, etc.) and it used to be a great achievement to be a grandmaster tamer. The tide has turned ... but that does not make taming less noble or barding more noble.

- Cirander
