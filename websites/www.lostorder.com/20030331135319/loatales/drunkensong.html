Drunken Verses of Gratitude
by Orbison

Things seemed rather bleak for me,
As I died in Paw just recently
I nodded off there, among my friends
But awoke much later to meet my end

Then I was lost for many days
Emerging slowly from my haze
I found myself in Rivervale
Brought back to life by the smell of ale

I quickly ran through many zones
Past Kithicor Woods, and Highhold's stones
And down the pass into the plain
Evading griffins with disdain

And back at Paw, I made my plea
For any guildmate to aid me
And though he was about to rest
Aanlain answered my request

He ventured deep into that hole
Fending off some deadly gnolls
And pulled my corpse back up to me
Allowing for my recovery

I gave him an ale, in gratitude
Applauded his helpful attitude
But that taste of ale made him think
Of going to Halas for a drink

So with a wave of his arms he cast a spell
That took us from that gnollish hell
And sent us back out to the plains
My head was dizzy from the strain

Once my mind was no longer numb
He bade me to equip my drum
And as I sang my song of speed
We departed for Halas, him in the lead

Through many zones, we crossed quite fast
Emerging in the north at last
I stood there, awestruck by the sight
Of mountains and valleys, covered in white

He lead me then, up to a town
A city with tall people gathered around
Twas Halas at last, after coming so far
I was indeed ready to locate a bar

Once in the tavern, he again honored me
With many bottles of warm brandy
And soon enough my speech was slurred
My stance was stumbling and vision blurred

And as I played a drunken rhyme
He noticed a woman named Thadres Thyme
But as he inquired of her past and her quest
She suddenly became distressed

She attacked us both, and we had to run
Suddenly the brandy wasn't much fun
I slipped in the snow and often fell down
Scrambling to reach the edge of town

Across the lake once again, Aanlain and I
Breathed an icy collective sigh
That woman, it seemed, had no use for jest
So he bid me farewell and sat down to rest

I then waved goodbye, and thanked him again
Our little adventure had come to an end
I found peace at last, by that icy cold lake
But dreaded the hangover with which I'd awake.
