Friday, June 07, 2002
Asheron's Call 1 Developers Chat Log

Warcry's Crossroads of Dereth has posted a log of last night's Asheron's Call 1 developer's chat. In the chat the developers covered a bit more detail on the upcomming changes to the magic system, when we could expect tinkering to go in and a rough date for the next patch.

As reported by: Enosh at 12:08:33 PM

Wednesday, June 05, 2002

Letter to the Players: June Magic System Changes

Microsoft and Turbine have announced the upcomming changes to Dereth's magic system in a new letter to the players. These changes will effect everyone that plays AC and has ever picked up a wand. I Strongly suggested you go read the Letter to the Players for all the details. Here's a snippet:

How Players Cast Spells
Players will no longer need the typical array of components in order to cast spells. Instead, they will need three separate ingredients.
How Players Learn Spells
With the June Event, players can no longer learn spells by researching them in the old fashion. Instead, players must purchase and use scrolls for each spell they wish to learn. You must have a certain skill level in the spell skill of the spell you wish to learn.
The New Length of Spells
With the June Event, all buffs of level 1-5 now last 30 minutes exactly. All level 6 buffs last 45 minutes. All level 7 buffs last 60 minutes. We are not planning on revisiting buff lengths after this update.
The Removal of Spell Economy
As we designed and planned these changes to the magic system in Asheron's Call, we often discussed the risk that we were taking with the most powerful and flexible game system in AC, and making it one of the easiest and simplest to use as well. In a game where a character using magic is extremely more powerful than a character who isn't, and where most vestiges of tedium and needless complexity have been removed from the magic system, and where enchantments at the highest level of power last an hour, why would anyone not have a magic-casting character in AC?

The full letter goes into great detail on these and other changes to the magic system in AC1. I personally think these changes are going to be for the good of AC althrough I personally will losed almost 30 points of combat effective bow skill.

Sunday, June 02, 2002

Goings on in Camelot


Must post more often, the Enosh-spam is just getting too intense... Anyway, here's some news from the land of Albion.


Well, not much has been going on in the way of major events recently, people have been ill, been away, been to the gathering, etc etc so we haven't had anything like a big guild-event like last report. However, we are coming along very nicely as a guild, with a large number of up-and-coming members soon to be reaching the higher levels so we can field a decent force of combatants for Realm vs. Realm combat. Currently I'm trying to organise more events that the Alliance as a whole can take part in. We've got a good community spirit going in the Alliance, but we need to start working together a bit more in mine, and others, opinions.


As to DAoC as a whole, well Mythic will be introducing the Holy Grail of player crafters soon, by finally allowing crafters to enchant their items and make them vastly superior to the dropped stuff. Of course, there is still ongoing 'tweaks' to the game, or nerfs if you listen to some people, that are changing the game. I think we're all having a lot of fun in the DAoC Chapter, and we've really got some good friendships going on, both in and out of game. We're a good bunch of people, honest, even if Cazz smells and doesn't shave, and Phyleus keeps wanting his bottom smited...
