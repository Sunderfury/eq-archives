Memec ... (Ramble alert.)

1977 ... Clear Creek ... Well I used to canoe Clear Creek and Armand Bayou and parts there about. Saw an alligator there and a number of other notable near monsters. Did some things with computers, too, but my home computer was a long wayyy off. Um, yesss, 23 years ago it was.

*eyes stare off in distance*

Actually the story really begins more like this, as Richard Garriott puts it: "I'm one of the industry old timers. I actually started writing games back in 1974 at the age of 14." (From an interview with Richard at www.zdnet.com/zdtv/gamespottv/interviews/story/0,3776,25 but that is long gone. It had a cool picture of Richard, too.)

1979 …. Akalabeth Never played it. I think some Ultima Fanatics, such as some Ultima Dragons, have. People will go to any extreme. My experience is the early Ultima Worlds are true antiques. I think of Akalabeth as the Ultima World Zero (i.e., Ultima 0).

1980 … Society for Creative Anachronism. Well now, what a surprise. Who would have thought the RW (RW?) had anything to do with Ultima.

1981 … so Richard Garriott and his friends copied the maps used in the Time Bandits, and use the idea in the design of Ultima II. That is indeed interesting. Maybe I should look at those maps again. As I recall the map in question applies not only to all of The Universe, but describes time as well. Might have been a little tricky to copy the real thing.

1990 … Ultima VI is released for PC. A great great Ultima World. Well, I visited VI, VII, VIII and have IX around here somewhere. Yes, Ultima VI is something of an antique, but it is a wonderful antique, and quite playable. Richard was really rolling by then.

1992 … Ultima VII is released, the last Ultima released independently by Origin. That explains everything about Ultima VIII. Not that VIII isn't great fun, but there are no Virtues in it. Indeed, the standard thing for The Avatar to do is muck around in the houses of the local inhabitants looking for clues and other "useful things".

1996 … Ultima Online beta test … over 50,000 people sign up and pay $2 to receive a copy of the beta CD. WOW! Those must be collector's items.

1997 … In September, Origin … officially releases the commercial version of UO. Well, my older brother, Vulcan of Wodclf was born on 29 January 1999, so the world was already old by then. Back in 1977 I heard rumors about UO. Began to read the newsgroup (rec.games.computer.ultima.series ) on it. There were some really interesting tales published there. Not all the Ultima Freaks approved of UO, of course. Considered it Origin's cash cow and the reason that Ultima IX wasn't published at the time.

1998 … Summer … Approximate expected release date for Ultima IX: Ascension.

HAR! Har!! har!!!

*wipes tears of laughter from eyes*

Well, Lord British is dead. As was said in the 18 June 2000 interview, Lord British's maker Richard Garriott … left Origin, the Austin, Texas-based company that he founded nearly 20 years ago. He talked a little about leaving the company, and how since Electronic Arts purchased it, it has become increasingly clear that Origin is "the Ultima company," even when Garriott is pressing to do other things.

So, the Ultima worlds live on, or so "they" say, and we see UO2 on the horizon. Ultima Freaks everywhere fret though. How can this be an Ultima World without Lord British? Will there be any Virtues in it, or like Ultima VIII, will the Avatar be reduced to rummaging about in other people's houses, looking for clues and other "useful things"? Ask "just about" anyone in UO Sosaria about The Avatar.

Avatar? What's that?

One thing is clear, though. UO Sosaria is not just a game out of a box. IMHO it is a world in it's own right. A small one, to be sure. (Plenty of room for discussion and agreement on this one.) Further the Virtues are there too. Not in the Shrines, not in Lord British, but in the wonderful people who visit this world from time to time. Those are the true Avatars.

May the Virtues be with ye, my friends.
