Captain's Log: Harvestgrain 23-24
by Enosh Flam

Captain's Log: Harvestgrain 23, 10 P.Y.

Don't know why I'm still writin' this bloody log, I've not the foggiest idea what happened to me ship after that blasted purple lightin' hit it. One minute I'm fighting a storm, the next layin' in the middle of a grove of trees. At any rate I may as well keep this log, just in case it becomes important.

When I came too I was standing just on the out skirts of a town named Holtburg. Not a bad little town, but she's nothing compared to Skara Brea if ye ask me. Still a nice place, nice and open and spread out. I sought out the local eatery and spent the last of me gold on a fine mug of stout. Some of the best I've had in a long time. Where ever I am, they sure know a thing or two about brewin' a good drink.

This place is strange though, I feel like things work different here. It actually took me a few minutes to get me land legs back (or was it the ale workin' on me, I don't rightly know) Still, I've been usin' a bow almost as long as I've been holdin' a tiller, and it took me a good few minutes to get a bloody arrow notched in me bow again. Looks like I'm going to have to spend some time huntin' rabbits again before I wander to far into trouble.

I heard some rumors today that there's a city to the south of here where there might be some more LOA. I'll have to start there after I finish another one of these stouts.

Captain's Log: Harvestgrain 24, 10 P.Y.

This place is a little stranger than I thought. Today I started to make me way south along the road. I managed to "aquire" a map of the lands from a fellow customer at Fispur's Foodstuffs. Poor fellow can't 'old his ale to save his life.

Along the way I ran into the local welcome party. A little group of what I later learned were called Drudges. The first thing I thought when I saw one was, "ohmygod, all the cats I killed as a lad have come back on two legs to kill me." An that's what they did all right, twice I found myself back in the same bloody clearin' near Holtburg, till I finnaly figured out which end of the arrow goes on the sting (reminder: pointy end in the other man) After a good deal of runnin' and a full quiver of arrows, I finally made it to the village of Glenden Wood.

This was a much nicer town in me humble opinion, as the ale was of an even more astounding quality. I lingered here a while taking in the view. This village is nestled in a tree covered valley at the interceton of the west, north and south roads. It has everything a well to do adventurer might want, including the tavern, two bowyers and even a mage.

I tried to head south again, but found myself doging all sorts of beasties, such as giant wasps of the like I've never seen, beasties that look a cross between a cat and a troll, and bloody land sharks! Needless to say I'll be staying the night here before i head on to the next village in me way, er, along me path. Tomorrow its on to Cragstone.
