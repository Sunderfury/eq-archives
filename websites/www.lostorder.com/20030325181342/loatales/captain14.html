Captain's Log: Solclaim 30, 11 P.Y.
by Enosh Flam

Captain's Log: Solclaim 30

It’s been almost a full fortnight since I last braved the wilds of Dereth, and I was still feelin’ the effects of me last encounter with a mud golem when I left Hebian-To. I’d been trying to rest and recover from such an adventure when my unfailing wander lust too over. I do enjoy good sake, but there is nothing to compare with a fine stout from an Aluvian pub.

With that in mind I gathered me gear and struck out for Shoushi. I was determined to find my way back to Aluvian territory one way or another. Shoushi, I knew had a portal to Yanshi, which would put me within a day’s walk of Lytelthorpe.

Portal travel is starting to get easier to handle for me, but it still will never be like walkin’ or sailin’ for me. This time when I arrived in Yanshi, I was able to hold onto the contents of my stomach, but I still needed a bit of time before I could do much of anything. I rested leanin’ again that giant boulder in the middle of the town.

I guess the townsfolk think that boulder to be something important, since they were givin’ me the dirtiest looks I’ve seen this side of a banderling ball. This was unbelievably helpful in clearin’ my head so I could set off through the wild lands between Yanshi and Lytelthorpe. Something about angry locals always stirs that wanderlust in me legs and gives me the motivation to move on.

With that I decided to move on before I caused a real stir in Yanshi. I set off to the north, hoping to find a road or maybe just stumble onto Lytelthorpe itself. It’s always humbling to think of how hard it is fer me to navigate this world. On the seas I was unmatched in me navigation skills (when sober) but here the land is so alien to me yet, that I have a hard time findin’ me way around sometimes.

Still I set off in the direction I hoped would lead me to familiar Aluvian ale. Not too far north of town, I stumbled across one of those Sho tower in the wilds. The place was under attack by a small clan of drudges. Being who I am, I immediately set about perforating that pack of putrid cats and liberated the hour.

One I finished off the last of those pesky kittens I checked the tower out. Seems the drudges had already killed the occupant of the house, which saddened me greatly. I said a few prayers fer the soul of the poor bugger that passed on, and decided to move on.

The rest of my trip to Lytelthorpe was uneventful, which I don’t mind all that much. Seems though that I didn’t remember that certain “friends” in Lytelthorpe might not be so happy to see me. I wound up camping for the night at the eastern outpost. I need to stop makin’ a name fer myself one of these days.
