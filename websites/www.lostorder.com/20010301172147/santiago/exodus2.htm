The Scars of Britain

Once upon a time, Britain had been the golden center of a magical kingdom. Valiant knights had patrolled the roads in search of villainy to conquer. Mages and scholars had congregated here to share ideas and to discuss the mysteries of the world. Intrepid adventures had sailed forth from its harbor, their eyes aglow with the hope of new discoveries. The Realm had been young and alive, and Britain was its very heart.

Things have changed since then.

"’Ey, mister! Can ye spare any coins fer a poor soul in need?"

Sarangoulis stopped and stared at the filthy urchin. The youth could not have been more than sixteen years old. The robe that he wore was old and tattered, perhaps a gift from some kind-hearted healer. The boy’s face was covered with scratches and bruises, and he walked with a limp.

"Have you been in a fight, young one?" asked Sarangoulis.

"Aye, lord," said the boy. "I was out in the woods this mornin’, tryin’ to catch some rabbits when three blokes did me in. Took everythin’ I had, they did…even me clothes. I ain’t got nothin’ no more, no food, no clothes…nothin’."

"Did you get a good look at these villains?"

"Well, they was all tricked out in metal armor, so I couldn’t see their faces. They was chargin’ at me like I was some bloody dragon! They had great big swords, but they didn’t even use ‘em. They started castin’ spells at me, an’ that’s the last bit I remember. I would o’ died if it weren’t for a kindly ol’ healer who happened to pass by. He bandaged me wounds an’ pointed me back to town. So here I am, beggin’ fer money to buy food. Only no one wants to help me. They all think I’m some sort o’ thief." He sighed. "I dunno what to do."

Sarangoulis gazed around the bank plaza. Everywhere he looked, he saw wealthy men and women milling about, clad in expensive clothing and armor. Then he looked back at the boy in the dirty old robe, a boy who had been crushed today, in both body and spirit. He looked at the boy’s face, a face too young to bear such scars. Looking into the eyes of the youth was like looking at a fire that has gone out, leaving only the bitter ashes of a hope cruelly extinguished. It was the face of today’s Britain.

Sarangoulis bent down and placed a hand on the boy’s shoulder. "You see that tower over there?" he said, pointing toward the Sorceror’s Guild. "Go there. Tell them that Sarangoulis sent you. They will take you in, give you food, clothing and shelter. They will teach you how to make a living for yourself."

"Thank ye, yer lordship!" The boy bowed deeply, and ran off in the direction of the Tower.

"It’s the least I can do," said Sarangoulis.

Once upon a time, Britain was known as the City of Compassion. Now, no one has time or desire to help those in need. There are so many villains in the world that we don’t trust anyone anymore. How can we? The land is full of dark-hearted people who murder and steal, day after day, simply because they can. Thieves and killers grow rich and powerful at the expense of the innocent. They do not care about the misery they inflict – in fact, they relish it. Virtue and kindness have been replaced by greed and cruelty. There are no kings or gods – or if there are, they are silent and do not interfere. These are Britain’s darkest days.

No wonder that so many have been enticed by the rumors of Norrath. Paradise or not, how can it possibly be worse than Britain?
