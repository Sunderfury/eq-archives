Ode to Akalabeth
by Pasquat

'Twas the night of the festival and all through the keep.
There was merriment and music….no one could sleep.
The banners were strung from the walls with care.
In hopes that Lord British would even be there.

Brim stood at the doors beaming with pride.
He shook hands and gave hugs as he sent them inside.
Come one and come all to the our Sage Keep!
He even welcomed the one known as Gleep.

Inside the great hall, 'twas truly a scene.
A paladin, a bard and yes a Blood Queen.
There was Littlewode and Jade, in her popular greens.
Why there were even some dastardly OES fiends.

Crawling through dungeons for a new spell.
Running from demons and monsters from hell
They stood and they marveled at all the old scrolls
The tomes and the books all looted from trolls.

Said Nivek …Why I remember the first orc that I killed.
I was a quite a young lad…fairly unskilled.
I fireballed and and arrowed but orc he fought back
This cabbage and book I found in his pack.

Now it's Corp Por and Vas Flam
Not much to be said
A much bigger flamestrike
And then they're quite dead.

Enough of this talk, there's food and there's drink.
Lichbane glanced at the door …and my lady I think
She smiled as she entered…"Was I gone too long?
He shouted to the bard…Lad give us a song!
Jediit sang and strummed the great harp.
Fair Gwenneth charmed a young mongbat, naming it Yarp.
Hawkeye laughed and grabbed Brigadier.
Rhi lifted her mug and let out a great cheer.

They sang and the danced all night in the keep.
Till the wee mornin' hours called them to sleep.
At long last, there remained just a few.
The rest had since made their way back to Yew.

Gwen in her gown and Brew in his kilt.
Marveled at the ale the sailors had spilt.
Santiago, Tara!… Mae called them by name.
I'll tell Enosh, that ye are to blame.

Ar! Mae, ye do that, my magical wench.
Santi reached for her rear, for one last pinch.
Tara giggled and staggered toward the keep's door.
Pinch that one matey and ye'll be kissin' the floor.

Stubby leaned back…a big smile on his face.
It did his heart good…they all loved the place.
Its fame would spread both here and away.
May the light shine forever on beloved LOA
