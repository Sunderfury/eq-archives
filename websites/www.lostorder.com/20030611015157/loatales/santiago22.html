Captain's Log: Nujel'm
by Santiago

Now this is what I’m talkin’ about. Barefoot in the warm sand, listenin’ to the sound o’ the waves rollin’ in. Let other folks have their cities, their dungeons. I’ll just have me breakfast under the shade o’ this quiet palm tree, thankee very much.

Only problem is, thar ain’t many folks here. Just like I always thought, Heaven is empty. Everyone’s down in the other place, I s’pose. Now sometimes it’s nice, havin’ a whole isle to meself. But sometimes ye just want someone to talk to, ye know? Parrots just don’t count.

Still, thar’s a warm breeze blowin’ in from the south. I ain’t wearin’ nothin’ except pants an’ skullcap. Life is lazy, sunny an’ peaceful fer once, an’ I don’t mind it one bit. Oh, thar be battles an’ blood on the horizon, to be sure – but not until after I’ve had a nice long siesta here in the shady sand, on this isle which right now belongs to me alone.
