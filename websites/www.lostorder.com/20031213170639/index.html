FFXI Wins Best Online Game!

By: Shancy @ 11:47

LOS ANGELES, Calif., December 3, 2003 – Square Enix U.S.A., Inc. ("Square Enix U.S.A."), the publisher of Square Enix products in North America, announced that FINAL FANTASY XI won the accolade of "Best Online Game" at Spike TV's first annual Video Game Awards. The winners were announced last night at the awards ceremony held at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas, Nevada.

FINAL FANTASY XI was selected for the interactive entertainment industry's top online game honor by gaming industry experts, the network's editorial board and public opinion via Spike TV's website Spike TV. FINAL FANTASY XI for Windows, released on October 28, 2003, was a finalist in two categories, "Best Online Game" and "Best Fantasy Game." The game is the first online title in the world-renowned FINAL FANTASY series, which to date has sold over 45 million units worldwide. The first cross-platform, cross-continent massively multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG), FINAL FANTASY XI allows gamers to connect to the same exact world regardless of whether they are playing on a personal computer (PC) or PlayStation®2 computer entertainment system and whether they are playing in North America or Japan. FINAL FANTASY XI for the PlayStation 2 is scheduled for release in March 2004.

The first annual Spike TV "Video Game Awards" (VGAs) premieres with a two-hour fete on Thursday, December 4 (9:00-11:00 PM, ET/PT). Hosted by actor/comedian David Spade celebrates the many accomplishments of the past year in video "gamedom" while also looking to the future of games in 2004 and beyond. Awards in 16 wide-ranging categories were presented during Spike's "VGAs" which was held at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas. For all Spike "VGA" results, log on to Spike TV.

Another Instant Messenger Application

By: Freejack @ 16:06

I bumped into this application yesterday. It's similar to the well-known Trillian in that it condenses all the major IM protocals into a single lightweight application. It seems to support all the major necessary features. I think the only major features it lacks that Trillian Pro has is video conferencing, but really, if I want to do that, I'm still going to use Yahoo! Messenger.

The program is called Gaim. It's supported on the *nix platforms and recently Windows. It functions on a plugin basis and there are lots of plugins out there. It seems most are experimentary at this point, but I expect to see more/better plugins as the application continues to mature.

Quoting a bit of text from the about_:

"Gaim is a modular Instant Messaging client capable of using AIM, ICQ, Yahoo!, MSN, IRC, Jabber, Napster, Zephyr, and Gadu-Gadu all at once. It is written using Gtk+ and is licensed under the GPL."

Browse over to Gaim and take a look.
