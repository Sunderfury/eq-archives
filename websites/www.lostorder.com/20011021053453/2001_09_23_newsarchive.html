Pool Of Radiance Warning

This is important enough to report here, as I get the feeling that several LOA members still play CRPGs.

From Voodoo Extreme, who got it from Adrenaline Vault, there is a report that uninstalling POR will delete critical windows files.

A Quote:

We have uncovered a probable bug in the installation program for PoR that could wipe out a user's Windows 9x system files during the uninstallation process. During our installation of PoR, the install program locked up the computer, forcing a reboot. After this, the PoR installation program would only give the option to uninstall the game; at this time, the program also wiped out our Win98 SE system files and would not reinstall the game. A further bug was uncovered in the installation program that prevents the user from installing to any directory other than the default.

Tuesday, September 25, 2001

Appropriate IRC behavior seems to be a big issue these days, so it would probably be good if I took a few moments to review this with everyone.

The primary rule above all others is respect for members and visitors to the channels that the LOA operates. That means, if someone is taking offense at something that you are saying and indicates such either in channel or privately, please respect their wishes and cease the discussion or move it to another channel or private messages. Beyond that, the rules are pretty simple: avoid swearing whenever possible; try to avoid the sensitive topics that are known to incite "flame wars" (everyone should know what these are); as much as we welcome public discussion of the LOA and its operation, we would ask that extremely negative discussions pertaining to the guild and its management be held in private channels or private messages.

The enforcement of these rules and others not mentioned (because they might often be situation dependent) are up to the discretion of the currently active Ops.

OPS: This seems to be becoming an issue again. There is a reason certain individuals have been granted AOPs (Auto OPS) in our channels, either they are chapter or guild leaders or have shown themselves to be respectful and intelligent in the enforcement of channel policies. PLEASE DO NOT OP ANYONE NOT ON THE AOPS LIST. There are cases where leaders are not currently oped in a channel, of course, it would be OK
to op them, but if someone is not in a position to receive ops, do not give it to them. There's no reason to have every person in a channel oped. In a channel with 15 people, there is no reason to have 8 ops, three or four are PLENTY. Having ops is something I take very seriously, and anyone seen to be messing around as an op or oping people 'willy nilly' will answer to me.

If you have any questions about these policies, feel free to contact myself, Brimstone or the High Council list (loa-council@lostorder.com).

Thanks everyone,

Opinions are like as... nevermind
Hey everyone!! Just wanted to say that LOA, just like any other guild or organization, has people with varying thoughts and opinions on a huge range of topics. Sometimes a person makes a post that either comes off the wrong way, or perhaps offends intentionally. When this happens, try to consider the source and remember that one person's ramblings/thoughts/opinions are not those of the LOA overall. I think that sometimes we forget this important fact, thus leading to flame wars, dissention in the ranks or hurt feelings.

If someone says something that really upsets you, approach them about it. Perhaps it was a misunderstanding and the typed "word" wasn't what the person REALLY meant. This happens all the time, as it did yesterday.

We all share so much in common... we are mostly friends here, there is no reason to elevate things to negative level.

God bless America.
