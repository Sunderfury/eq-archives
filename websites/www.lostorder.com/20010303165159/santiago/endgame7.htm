A Fish Tale

It was a gray morning in the Butcherblok mountains. The sulking skies were the color of lead, and a light drizzle had begun to fall.

The beach was mostly deserted. The only sounds to be heard were the crashing of the waves and the plaintive cries of the seagulls which circled in the air.

"I don’t see why we have to be here," grumbled Walterus.

"’Cause the King asked us to," said Bierfreund.

"But why us? We are warriors, not bloody escorts. I want to fight!"

"Aye, so do I. But the King wouldn’t have sent us if he didn’t think it was important. Hey…is that Morgf over there?"

The two dwarves walked toward the pier, where a lone dwarf sat, his fishing pole dangling out over the water.

"Hullo, Morgf!" Bierfreund called out, approaching the old fisherman.

"Blimey! If it ain’t Bierfreund an’ Walterus. Come to pay ol’ Morgf a visit, have ye? Well, have a sit. I’d offer ye some fish, but the bucket be empty."

"Fish aren’t biting today?"

"Not a one. Either this bait’s gone south, or they all been scared away. I seen a shark swimmin’ around not too long ago."

"How soon do you figure the next boat will be here?"

Morgf shrugged. "Should be here any minute. Ye goin’ for a sail?"

Bierfreund laughed. "Not me. I’ll keep to solid ground if I can. Naw, we’re waiting for some folks to get here from Freeport."

"Tall folk?" Morgf spat into the water. "Hell, why can’t they just stay on their side o’ the pond? I keep trippin’ over their dead bodies in the woods. Ye think they’d learn to stay out o’ trouble."

"Speaking of trouble…have you heard the rumors lately?" asked Bierfreund. "They say there are dark days ahead."

"Aye. Thar’s a storm comin’, an’ it’s gonna be a rough one. Me, I just roll with the punches, same as always." Morgf nodded his head out toward the sea. "Here comes yer boat."

Bierfreund gazed in awe at the galleon as it drew near. The sheer size of the mighty sea vessels, with their masts as tall as trees, never failed to impress him. He felt dwarfed in comparison.

"Quite a sight, eh, Walterus?" Bierfreund glanced around. "Where did Walterus go?"

They heard splashing in the water, and ran to the edge of the pier. Walterus was standing in the shallow water, wrestling with a shark that was twice his size. He had grabbed it by the back fin, and was in the process of repeatedly punching it in the head with his meaty fist.

"Walterus, what in the blazes do you think you’re doing?!" cried Bierfreund.

The bloody dwarf looked up and hoisted the now-unconscious shark into the air. "Breakfast!" he shouted, a wide grin on his face.

Bierfreund sighed and shook his head.

"That thar is the toughest damn dwarf I ever did see," said Morgf.
