The Akalabethian Glossary of Terminology and Phrases:

As with any community, we have established our own words, phrases and definitions over the years. In addition to OUR terms, there are other non-LOA specific terms that are used by the gaming community in general. We will try to define some of these things on this page. It is our belief that understanding our terms and definitions will help our applicants during the process of joining the Lost Order of Akalabeth.

Chapter:

Used to describe a group of LOA members who are involved in a game/world that our guild has fully embraced. A Chapter receives a mailing list, forum/s, chat room, news page, website, etc. -- unlike nonofficial games that we play together.

CORE:

Term used to describe the entire guild, despite chapter or individual status; "CORE" is also used in the phrase, "LOA to the CORE!". This statement is often attached to member email/posts to show their pride and loyalty in the LOA.

Faction:

Old term used to describe a group of LOA members who were involved in a game/world that our guild fully embraced (i.e. Ultima Online, EverQuest, Asheron's Call, etc.). We retired this word due to it's negative undertone, in favor of a new word, "Chapter".

Governing Bodies of LOA:

Founders: The official title granted to the first group of people who were involved in the birth of the LOA. This title is often abbreviated in chat room nicknames and/or posts by using the following suffix: [f] Founders have a major say in the direction and overall goal of the Lost Order of Akalabeth. Each Founder is given a seat on the Grand Council of the LOA. Founders who are inactive are asked to reserve their votes, due to being uninformed about the current issues. The very first founders were Brimstone and Freejack. More information about the "birth" of LOA can be found on our site.

Grand Council: The Grand Council is the highest governing body of the LOA. It consists of all the Founders, as well as the Chapter Leaders. The Founders represent the original goals and ideas of LOA, as well as the vision we set forth to establish so many years ago. The Chapter Leaders represent their chapter of members, as well as new ideas, new goals, policy updates, debates, etc. Together, high level decisions are discussed and voted upon. If an issue requires a 'Tie Breaker', Freejack and Brimstone reserve the right to make a final decision based on the feedback received, as well as the overall 'good of the guild'.

High Council: Each chapter of LOA consists of a Chapter Leader, as well as a host of members responsible for units and/or tasks associated with the management of the chapter. Together, the Chapter Leader and official members of the management team for that chapter, make up the "High Council". The High Council is responsible for game specific management issues and policies that must align themselves with the overall guild goals and direction.

#Hall of Akalabeth:

Name given to a chat room that we use for central, guildwide meetings that are hosted every Wednesday night, as well as in times of emergency or importance. During our meetings, we formally address the guild members using roleplaying elements such as a podium, seating and more. Our speakers actually roleplaying their approach to the podium, as well as other atmospherical things taking place around them during the discussion. The Hall of Akalabeth is a "Moderated" chatroom in IRC, meaning that only authorized (Voiced) people can speak and have their text viewed by others.

How We Got Our Name:

Akalabeth: Richard Garriot, creator of Ultima Online and the previous Ultima series, was inspired by the Tolkien story entitled, "The Hobbit". Within this story, the name "Aklabeth" was used. Richard Garriot used that name for the title of his first CRPG called, "Akalabeth". This game earned Richard enough exposure in the gaming industry that it launched his career and gave birth to Ultima. When looking for a name for our guild, we wanted something that would catch the attention of Richard Garriot (as well as Origin), and we also wanted a name that would attract die-hard fans of the Ultima series. Thus, "Akalabeth" was chosen as a portion of our name.

The Lost Order of: If you have already read the description of the term, "Akalabeth", as it applies to us -- then I can easily explain why we added, "The Lost Order of" to our name. With the game Akalabeth being so ancient, we wanted to make our guild appear to be a band of prophets and seers sent from the old world of "Akalabeth" -- to the new world of "Sosaria". We felt that the complete name, "Lost Order of Akalabeth" would have an air of mystery and intrigue about it, thus appealing to fellow gamers and Ultima fans. The fact that we jumped from one world (Akalabeth) to another (Sosaria) opened the door to the possibility that our members had powerful magiks at their control (Roleplaying is SO fun!). This roleplay fact about our Order allowed us to easily explain how and why we were able to enter OTHER worlds (i.e. Norrath, Dereth, etc.) using the same name, "LOA".

Membership Titles (Based on Seniority)

Senior Member: Unofficial title assigned to members who have served with LOA for more than 1 YEAR. This title is often abbreviated in chat room nicknames and/or posts by using the following suffix: [s] For each additional year, members can add a number to this suffix to denote how many years they have been a FULL member of LOA.

This is displayed as follows: [s] or [s1] = ONE YEAR; [s2] = TWO YEARS; [s3] = THREE YEARS, etc.

Veteran Member: Unofficial title assigned to members who have served with LOA for more than 6 MONTHS, yet less than 1 YEAR. This title is often abbreviated in chat room nicknames or posts by using the following suffix: [v]

Apprentice Member: Unofficial title assigned to new members in the LOA. This title is given to members who have been fully accepted into LOA and served no more than 6 MONTHS of service. This title is often abbreviated in chat room nicknames or posts by using the following suffix: [a]

Unit:

Each chapter has the option to establish specialized units that members can join to persue a common goal or cause (i.e. Mage Unit, Merchant Unit, War Unit, etc.)

Unit Leader:

If a unit is established, a Unit Leader must be assigned to manage that group of people and see to it that there is a real purpose and activity that justifies the unit. Unit leaders often have a seat on the Chapter "High Council" and relay information/ideas to the Chapter Leader. Unit Leaders are also responsible for providing reports regarding the unit during our weekly meetings.

Various Pre-member Titles/Ranks:

Petitioner of "Petitioner of Akalabeth": Once an application is submitted, the applicant is thereby referred to as a "Petitioner" of the LOA. This phase consists of 7 days whereby the Full Members of LOA review the application submitted, discuss any issues they may have regarding the applicant, and finally vote whether to accept or deny the application. Petitioners require three votes of confidence for their application to make it to the next phase of membership which is entitled, "The Initiate Phase".

Initiate, INIT or "Initiate of the Order": Once an applicant has passed the Petitioner Phase (having received three votes of confidence), he/she becomes an Initiate. The Initiate Phase consists of 30 days where the applicant must spend time learning our rules/policies, meeting our members (both of that chapter and overall) and participating in our community (both ingame and out of game). As Initiates spend time posting on our forums, talking with us in the chat rooms and being involved with us "in-game", they will become noticed by our members. Either a positive or negative impression will be made, and our Full Members will keep this in mind during our private voting. Full Members of LOA are encourage to vote at ANY TIME during the 30 days of the Initiation Phase. If the Initiate receives (6) or more votes during that time, they will progress to the final phase of the application process, also known as "Commoner of Akalabeth".

Commoner, CMNR or "Commoner of Akalabeth": The final phase of the LOA Application Process, just prior to being named a "Full Member of Akalabeth". The Commoner Phase consists of 30 days, at which point, Full Members give a final vote of confidence on the applicant. Commoners must receive (8) or more votes to become a full member of the LOA. Commoners are highly respected and have usually proven their worthiness and maturity by this time. The Commoner Phase is more of a tradition/formality that prepares people for full membership.

Voting System:

The voting system is fairly easy to use. It is a web-based application that requires a password and login ID to use (see your Chapter Lord/Lady of Initiation). Each Full Member has the option to vote on every single applicant throughout every phase. During the Petitioner Phase, Full Members can cast a vote either "For" or "Against" the applicant. During the Initiate and Commoner phases, our members are given a range of votes that they can use to judge an applicant, and they are: +1 [Exceptional], + [Good], 0 [No Opinion], - [Bad], -1 [Horrible]. Only +1 votes are used to determine if someone will move on to the next phase. + votes are very positive and show us that an applicant is doing well and making progress within that Chapter. 0 votes simply mean that a given Full Member has not had an opportunity to meet with or form an opinion of a candidate. This vote does not hinder the applicant at all, unless it is an overwhelming number of '0' votes. The - vote simply means that a candidate has shown characteristics that are unbecoming. Either immature, uncaring, reckless, etc. Oftentimes we will notify a candidate that there are problems that need to be addressed. The -1 vote is the absolute worst vote a player can receive. -1 votes negate +1 votes and send a clear message to the guild that this candidate has done something to really upset a member of the guild. The way our system works, a few -1 votes will not automatically remove someone from the guild. Instead, we will tally up the +1 and -1 votes and make a decision based on the overall perception of the candidate. We feel that this is a very fair system of checks and balances, ensuring that nobody will be ousted over one member who is upset.

Village:

The name given to our Ultima Online (Sosarian) Chapter's 'Home'. We have a village consisting of many shops and buildings, as well as a decorative fountain/gathering area. The village has been a central gathering point for not only LOA, but many other guilds/players as well.
