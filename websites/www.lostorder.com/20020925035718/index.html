Monday, September 16, 2002

News from AC2

There's lots of news from the Asheron's Call 2 Beta, where to begin. First we've started the process of building the guild allegiance. If you're not yet in the allegiance let me know (IM, e-mail, PM, /tell) and I'll do my best to get you in. There are currently some bugs with the in game chat system which are making grouping and gathering rather difficult so bear with me while we build the allegiance. I fully expect this process will be faster when retail hits.

Beta world Green is in the process of moving to the Redmond data center, a new beta world, Blue, is online on the Canyon Park data center. The rules for play on Green will remain the same but Blue should go without a wipe for the remainder of the beta.

The message boards at the Asheron's Call 2 beta community have been taken offline. In their place, use the official (and moderated) beta feedback forums. Please use those forums to submit bugs, suggestions and comments. You should also head over to various fan sites such as AC2 Warcry and join in the discussion (however I warn you, you'll find mostly negative posts on most fan sites. Gamer seem to have become much more bitter of late

I think that's all I have for now, hope to see you all in Dereth! (in portal year 422???)

As reported by: Enosh at 8:40:25 PM

Ugh, I hate anatomy. I think if I were to become a doctor I'd go for the holistic yank the chicken liver tumor out of the patients belly when he's not looking approach.

Good looking women give me the willies. I'm not talking about the implanted drop dead gorgeous ones you see on certain web sites, I'm talking about truly good looking women. Escpecially when they are friendly toward me. I don't know why, I just shut down. Fortunatly I have resisted the temptation to clasp their legs and whine "Date me!", but I certainly do a lot of self evaluation if they give me a smile. Damn biology. (theres a point to this I swear)

Take the local EB game store, I dread going there now because whenever a certain clerk asks if she can help me the only thing I can think to do is grab the nearest box and stammer something about 'no, no, I've found it its right here thanks'. My credit cards hate me. Well there's a cure for that, I'll ask her for her Email tommorrow. Rejection is a great cure for the jitters, though it may not help in the future.

Last thursdays grab and go game fortunatly was a real gem. Mafia. If you haven't picked this up your missing a classic. Yes its like GTA but...

Mafia is one of those games that you can't really have explained to you. It's not a "Its like Warcraft but with tanks" type of explination. Now I've gone and contradicted myself haven't I? Better sort this out. First off let me say I loved GTA, and I'll be grabbing Vice City when hit hits. Second, Mafia is like GTA in just about every way. You steal cars, you run around the city, you blow away cops, and run over little old ladies and an "I am the law!" Judge Dread type never shows up to stop you. However where GTA let you run willy nilly all over the city and only take missions when you felt like it, Mafia follows a step by step movie style format that can't be deviated from. Where GTA is about action, Mafia is about style. This is where the can't really explain it part comes in. Mafia nails the time period and the situations that one would expect exactly right. Because it follows a set path, Mafia can set a mission at night, with a certain lighting in a certain place no problem. Standing in the mud while the rain pounds on your face and the only thing you can see is a distinctly unfriendly looking set of porch lights on a farm house can be an out and out creepy experience. That colt 1911 in your hand just doesn't give the secure feel it would in any other situations and you'll find yourself wishing for the warm stock of a tommy gun in your hands. Or standing on the tarmac of the airport watching a plane come in for a landing in the twilight. And the story, what a story! I keep saying style and I mean STYLE. Cool Hand Lukes got nothing. The cut scenes are very well done, proffessionally voiced and (to point out how good the engine is) done in game. The scripting is nothing short of astronomical. If you get to a certain point and your supposed to be beat to hell, you better believe you will be feeling beat to hell. I'm still not convinced a couple of guys name Louie and Greedo didn't slip in and work me over when I wasn't looking. I could go one but by this point your either in or out, the outs probably left the second GTA was mentioned.

Now I suppose I should list some specific cons, but I can only mention one right now that won't be seen as a direct comparison to GTA. I've gotten several nasty crashes on a few missions, I advise running a healthy and fairly clean computer during this game. No NWN's before hand.

In summary, this games a classic and if your the type that just plain likes a good game, then this is on the must have list.
