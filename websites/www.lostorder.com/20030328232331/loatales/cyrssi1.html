And So It Goes...
by Cyrssi

Dear Elandel,

It is hard to go on without you, but I know that it is what you would want me to do. I am going to be keeping this journal from this day forth until we shall see each other again. Each entry will be addressed to you, so that you may know what I have been doing in the event that I do not hand this book to you myself.

When I awoke and found you missing, I was terribly disturbed. I took the ship back to Faydwer and went to our hometown of Kelethin in order to find some peace. Once I had found that peace, I returned to Antonica determined to do what we had been doing all along...striving to earn the right to carry the name Treeskin.

At first I tried to go back to the desert and oasis, but I couldn't stay there long because memories began to cloud my spirits and I knew that survival depended on my clearheadedness and quick thinking. I instead travelled to the West Commonlands. After seeing only a few bears worthy of my skills, I happened upon a Dervish Cutthroat encampment. They did not attack me when I approached, but they did not seem to be too thrilled that I was there. I asked a passing monk by the name of Fengtui if he wanted to do something about the Dervs, and he agreed to help me.

We proceeded to rid the West Commonlands of many a Dervish; mainly the thugs and the mages of the group. We would also engage passing wisps. When the whole fight ended it was pretty late. All the monk asked for was a large bag, 3 of the many rings we had collected, and a third of the money we had collected. I was amazed, but he explained that he had little use for money, although what I gave him would feed him for many a day. He was a nice and fun person to fight alongside. I hope that if you see him you show him the kindness he showed me.

Shortly after we parted ways, I realized that I was extremely exhausted. I walked over to the druid ruins and set up camp here. I feel safe among fellow druids. I am now about to fall asleep writing this, so I will bring this to a close. I shall write again tomorrow night before I sleep.

Goodnight, brother.
