The Goblins Hatch a Plan

King Kazon Stormhammer was miserable.

He did not like outsiders. The humans were tall, smelly and rude. The elves were too damn happy all the time. And the gnomes…he didn’t even want to think about the gnomes.

But now his audience hall was full of these outsiders. They had traveled great distances to come here, though Kazon wished that they would have stayed at home where they belonged.

Just when he thought it couldn’t get any worse, one of the elves began to play a song on his lute. The king was about to order his immediate execution, but one of the other elves seized the instrument and threw it across the hall.

The king’s herald entered the chamber, followed closely by Bierfreund and the tallest human that Kazon had ever seen. Bierfreund approached the throne and bowed deeply. The barbarian followed suit, though his attempt at a bow was clumsy and awkward. Kazon nodded curtly at both of them, and they rose and retreated back into the crowd.

"Everyone shut up now," said the king. The room became silent.

"If you haven’t heard the rumors, then you’ve either been asleep or dead. There’s trouble coming…big trouble. Ralineth, fill them in."

The hall was filled with whispering as a shadowy figure stepped forward from the shadows.

"Some of you know me," said the archdruid. "Many do not. I am a guardian of the land. I have set myself to the task of protecting the Faydark from those who seek to disrupt the natural order of things. To this end, I have brought all of you here this night. I have learned of a threat to our realm, the single greatest threat this world has ever known."

"You mean…we’re out of ale?" Kherghon said, growing pale.

"No," said Ralineth. "Worse than that."

"Don’t see how things could get worse than that," muttered Walterus.

"The goblins are plotting to destroy the world," said the druid.

"Goblins?" laughed Dargo the dwarf. "I chops them with me axe! I eats them fer breakfast! Goblins ain’t no threat."

"Normally, I would agree with you," said the druid calmly. "But these goblins have discovered something which makes them very dangerous. They have in their possession a dragon egg, in which lies one of Veeshan’s brood."

"Who’s Veeshan?" asked Bierfreund.

"Bloody big dragon," whispered Kherghon.

"The goblin shamans are preparing a dark spell," continued Ralineth. "At midnight tonight, the egg will hatch, and a new dragon will be born. As a result of the goblin magic, the new dragon will quickly grow to such a size that our very world will seem small in comparison. The goblins are insanely fanatical; they believe that this dragon will favor them, and grant them power over all their enemies."

"Fine," said the king. "I’ll send an army, and put all the goblins to death."

Ralineth shook his head. "The goblins have posted scouts on all the roads. An army would be detected, and the goblins would have time to evacuate their camp and move the egg elsewhere. What we need is a small band – about a dozen or so – to assault the camp and put an end to their insane plan."

Kazon stood up and approached the assembly. "So what we need here is some volunteers. That means you step forward and volunteer before I have you executed."

From the dwarves, Walterus, Kherghon, Dargo and Morgf stepped forward.

From the elves, Kileyan, Jarlaxle and Penaden stepped forward.

From the humans, Greyhawk, Rothan and Eshay stepped forward.

"We’re gettin’ paid for this, right?" asked Bierfreund, who had brazenly propped himself up on the throne.

"You have about two seconds to get off my throne before I chop you in half," said Kazon, hefting his axe in one hand. Bierfreund grinned mischievously and scurried into the crowd.

"Now get out of here, the lot of you!" roared the king. "Don’t come back until you’ve killed every single goblin in the Butcherbloks!"

The king watched the motley crowd depart. "We’re doomed, aren’t we?" he asked the druid.

"Pretty much," said Ralineth.
