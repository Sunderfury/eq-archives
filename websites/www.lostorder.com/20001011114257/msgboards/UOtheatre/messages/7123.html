Consider Amber's post below. In the case of the Animal Tamers we have a large and popular community, able to persuade the gods (OSI) to waver. They changing their mind from their initial weakening (i.e., nerfing) of 2 stabled animals per character to 5, then to what? There are better ideas, I suppose, but my point is the gods (OSI) change their mind because the Animal Tamer customer base is large enough to affect their profits.

As for the Bards, when some twink players figure out how to use a Bard skill to make grief to so many players that the gods (OSI) profit base is endangered, then the they weaken that Bard skill, and never do give any thought to adjusting it. Promises are so many instances of letting wind. Why? Because there are not enough real Bards to affect the profit base. Yes indeed, Amber, ye remember the great Line of Sight Bard (LOS) weakening, eh?

This is why I say the gods fear the Bards. Bard skills only make work for them, not profits.

Amber, where did Sage say 5 animals stabled per Tamer? The latest information I have is available on Stratics, but was taken from a very long thread on the UO OSI DEV Board at the link I have supplied below. The length and volume and depth of this response illustrates the point I am make above. The Animal Taming community is HUGE and active compared to the "Real Bard" community. Of course, one must look elsewhere than the CBA to get an idea of a representative Bard community as the gods (OSI) might look at it.

Not that we should ever get it, but I propose that Bards get increased benefits similar to those suggested by Sage for tamers. The more Bard skills one has, the more effective they would be. Thus, for example, a twink using only Provocation and Musicianship would be much less successful at their Provocation than a Real Bard that has Musicianship, Provocation, and Peacemaking, based on the total number of points in these skills. Even for these three skills, a GM in two gives a total of 200 points while a GM in three is 300 points, 1.5 times as many skills. Thus the effectiveness of Provocation would be 1.5 times as much for the Real Bard as compared to the twink. Further, if a Real Bard chooses to take Enticement, making 4 Bard skills, and somehow was able to GM those, then the Real Bard would be 2 times as successful at Provocation (and Peacemaking and Enticement) as the twink. This at least makes some purpose for Enticement. Not that I would take it anyway. I propose it be remover altogether instead.

Of course this idea would need some adjusting. First of all, make it possible for a Real Bard to GM some Bard skills. I still only have about 91 in Provocation and Peacemaking despite the fact that I have a GM in Musicianship. These other skills just are not moving above 90.

Second of all, either get rid of Enticement or make it useful, since we don't want the twink using an easy 70 points in it to make their Provocation better.

Or, perhaps only let the effect I describe come into play after a skill has, say 80 points in it. Thus no one would ever take Enticement for this or any other purpose. Just like now.

Virtues b' with ye all, me friends o' th' CBA!
