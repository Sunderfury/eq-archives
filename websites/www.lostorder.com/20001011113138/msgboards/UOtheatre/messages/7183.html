I admit, I've never been a PK bard, but I have known a few. I've also known more than a couple twink-NPK bards who would drag in a hoard of monsters then peacemake when more of the monsters would target the "victim."

One of my last incarnations of Dedigan in UO was an anti-pk bard. With barely 50 strength and very high magic resist, I did very well against PK's in spawn areas. With the help of a friendly wyvern or terathan or demon, most would-be player-killers would fall under my first magical assault (even with my low EI). The smart ones would recall to their nearest point then start their combo's right before I'm visible to them as they rush in. I had about a 50/50 chance of surviving a powerful combination if I precast (cast before they did) heal. The less than smart ones ran away or died.

My best (bragging time :P) victory was a 5 on me in the terathan dungeon. They were silly enough to attack me in the balron room. I didn't even provoke, but I knew exactly where the balrons were going next at all times, so I take credit for their win.
My other best was a 3 on me in the desert close to the oasis. This was the more impressive win in my eyes since the 3 were better coordinated than the previous 5 and they survived long enough that the fight would best be considered a draw. With my weak self, I felt lucky to survive at all.

Dedigan was never killed by anyone who used a melee weapon in combat (even sparring). She'd leave too quickly when it was obvious that the dex-monkey or melee-mage was out for blood. Pure magery with a touch of provoke isn't a good combination against the dex-types.

And with regards to the anti-tamer bard which was proposed, there are enough anti-tamer tactics for PvP now to make taming in combat kind of rough. There isn't any real need to add another method of PvP-nerfing tamers.

Regards,
Ded
