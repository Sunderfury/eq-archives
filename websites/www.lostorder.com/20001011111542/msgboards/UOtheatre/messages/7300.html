Well Dave c., I probably missed it, but I didn't catch your shard. Seems to me that the degree of Bard Role Playing, and Role Playing in general, is claimed to be a characteristic of the particular shard, and indeed facet of the shard. Further I think, this depends on the friends one keeps, not the general rif-raff encountered on the street. My shard is not renown for it's role playing. Not to say it is not here because it is. Other shards have a more Role Playing reputation, and rather than speak for them, maybe others will say how well their own homes are suited for it.

A useful thought to keep in mind is that UO Sosaria is but a shadow of the Real World. Indeed, how many playful performers and provocative tricksters, begging street players do we know in the Real World? Probably none for the most of us, but I say, this depends on the friends we keep.

I'd say you would have great fun "attempting to sing for me dinner, compose and recite some poetry and what have you" if you can find a companion to share your adventures with. For adventures they are indeed.

Another thought to contemplate is that the purpose of UO Sosaria is for us to have fun, for if we are not having fun, what are we doing here? Not that I am suggesting there is any flaw in the solitary Performing Bard, other than the chance of the "Bored of Sosaria" disease setting in is made that much greater.

Let me say some further things to ye, Dave c, and anyone else with this idea. Everyone knows my posts are a bit long and rambling, so stay with me if ye can.

As all know, I am Guild Master of Steel & Magic of Pacific. It is not a small guild and though I aspire to grow the Performing Bard side of myself, I find I am limited perhaps due to my leadership as Guild Master. My interest is in the people of the land, and most especially the Virtuous. This limits my chances to stay in character, and so on and so fourth, not that I don't play my Sosaian self as often as I am able. Yet I have aspired to find people on Pacific interested in Role Playing. Now this is not just the Bard, but also the adventurer, Mages, Smiths and others. Indeed, think of the challenge of the Swordsman role playing. I recently offended, and may have lost a wonderful Role Player, a great Swordsman steeped in Dragons Lore, by failing to understand his purposes. Aye, 'twa a tragedy as he attempted to protect an Elder Wyrm, "stuck" in a defenseless place, from the wrath of attacking Mages, and he felt abandoned and perhaps even betrayed. I know not if I can heal this wound.

*sigh*

Well, my point here is the possibilities of having fun playing the Performing Bard can be greatly increased by finding like minded companions. How can this be accomplished? Let me suggest some ideas.

Of course, finding, or even building a guild of like minded people to join with is a way. Is there a chapter of the CBA on your shard? Possibly not, for we have been notoriously weak in actually developing this. If your shard is Pacific, this can be accomplished by becoming the CBA guild master yourself, or if you are not up to being a Guild Master (not always easy), you could join my guild and help my efforts to do this. I have continued to grow a kind of CBA within Steel & Magic, and even now have had some small success.

Strangely, many Bards are not interested in guilds and other organization. They are an enigmatic crew, seeking to be solitary and independent, and at the same time wishing to entertain the random stranger on the street corners. What to do? One approach is to make friends. An excellent way to do this is to befriend newbys, usually known as "young ones" now a days. I have found that newbys make excellent friends and companions and further are willing to join their mentors in the most outrageous of adventures. They have no preconception of what is right or wrong for them. They want to have fun as well as learn skills, so here is your chance.

Of course, there is the off chance that someone matured in the ways of the land can be encountered in yer wanderings. Perhaps the one in a hundred you meet while playing on the street corner who shows an interest or empathy? Perhaps ye can have a ready adventure ready, and offer it up to such a chance acquaintance? "Thank you my friend for your gracious comments. Would ye, by chance, be interested in visiting the local tavern with me? Or perhaps ye would care to walk with me to the great city of Britania?"
Then again, you never know who you might meet here in the Conservatory of Bardic Arts.

Just some thoughts.

May the Virtues be with ye all.
