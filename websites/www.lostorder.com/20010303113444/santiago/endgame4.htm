Beware of Giants

"Master, I am cold! Can we not leave this place?"

"Not until we find what we’re looking for," said Rothan, calmly scanning the wintry horizon.

"Master, I am a fire elemental. FIRE elemental. Fire is warm. Fire likes being in places that are warm. This place is not warm. This place is COLD!"

Ignoring the whining of his familiar, Rothan walked over to where a young woman kneeled, examining the ground. His eyes drifted over her graceful form with undisguised admiration. She was the most beautiful creature he had ever seen.

"And just what do you think you’re looking at?" she asked playfully, without turning toward him.

"Oh, nothing," Rothan smiled and walked to her side. "Find anything yet?"

Eshay shook her head. "Wolves, polar bears, spiders – not much else out here." She stood up. "Rothan, are you sure you want to go through with this? Halas could be days away, yet."

The mage nodded. "I have to find the Shaman. If what I’ve been told is true, he may be the only one who can help. I just hope we find him before we freeze out here."

"Are you cold?" she asked with mock incredulity. She drew closer to him. "Is there anything I might be able to do that would warm you up?"

"Well, I can think of one or two things…"

She smiled, and kissed her husband.

"Oh, this is ridiculous," said the elemental. "Here I am, out in the middle of nowhere, freezing to death. And they’re kissing! Well, that does it. That’s the last straw. I’m leaving. Good-bye, Master!"

Rothan was still kissing Eshay, and took no notice of the elemental.

"I said, ‘Good-bye, Master!’"

Rothan waved in farewell."

The elemental floated away, sulking and muttering to himself. "Worthless, two-bit mage. Not even powerful enough to summon a real elemental. We’ll see how well he does without me. I bet he’ll be calling my name within seconds."

He stopped and listened, but there was no call.

"Well, maybe not yet, but soon. He’ll realize what a fool he was to let me go. And then he’ll come crawling back to me. And you know what I’ll do? I’ll laugh. I’ll laugh at him! I’ll…aaaaaaaaaaaiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiieeeeeeeeeeeeeeee!!!!!!!!!"

Rothan pulled away from Eshay. "Did you hear that?" he asked.

"It was nothing," she said. "Only the wind." She began to kiss him again.

"Master!" cried the elemental, floating toward Rothan as fast as he could. "Master!"

"I thought you left," said Rothan.

"Master, there’s a giant out there!"

"A giant?" asked Eshay, drawing her sword.

"Yes! What did I just say? Am I speaking Common?"

"What did this giant look like?"

"Well, he was tall, for one thing. He carried a great big club, and had a giant polar bear skull on his head! What in the blazes does it matter what he looked like…we have to get out of here, now!"

"All right, all right," said Rothan. "Just calm down."

"Too late!" screamed the elemental. "There he is!"

All eyes turned in terror as the creature approached them.

"Hello, there," said the giant. "My name is Greyhawk. Um, is everything all right?"
