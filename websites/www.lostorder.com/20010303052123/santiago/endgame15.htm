The Gnomes Blow Up the World

Deep beneath the roots of the Steamfont Mountains lies the gnomish city of Ak’Anon. Many outsiders travel great distances to behold its mechanical wonders. The city is famous for the various robots and machines that stir and whir and click and tick all day long. But beware…some say that prolonged exposure to the incessant noises can result in utter insanity. While this has never been conclusively proven, many point to the often erratic behavior of gnomes as an example. Though it has never officially been authorized or authenticated as an official word, the term ‘wonky’ is often used colloquially as a reference to gnomish madness.

‘What does this have to do with anything?’ you might ask. It is common knowledge that, with respect to mental stability, gnomes are not the strongest of all the known races. But what most people do not know is that Ak’Anon hides a great and terrible secret.

Those who reveal this secret are subject to terrible punishment – perhaps you have seen their decaying skeletons roaming mindlessly across the desolate wilderness.

Hidden deep beneath the city, deeper than even its deepest dungeons, lies a vast chamber. Within this chamber, a small band of ancient gnomes toil endlessly. Picked from among the most intellectually advanced members of their species, these few gnomes are held in the highest esteem by the other members of their community. For these elite few hold the safety of the entire world in their tiny hands.

However, the fact remains that they are gnomes. And such being the case, it is inevitable that one of them will occasionally go ‘wonky’.

McQuaid’s wizened features contorted into a scowl as he gazed up at the giant viewscreen. Displayed upon the screen’s surface was an image of a world – his world. Normally, the view was that of a serene planet, spinning happily upon its axis. But now there was one minor blemish: a gargantuan dragon had wrapped its wings over most of the planet’s surface, and was threatening to destroy it entirely.

"I hate that bloody creature," muttered the gnome.

The command center was in chaos. Everywhere he looked, there were gnomes running around with worried expressions on their diminutive faces. Light blinked on and off, papers flew through the air, and there was a general sense of impending doom that made McQuaid want to jump into the document shredder.

"Commander on deck!" cried one of the gnomes. All of the tiny eyes turned and watched as Commander Smedley entered the command center. For a moment, the commander simply stood there, looking as dignified and regal as a three-foot-tall gnome can look. Then he spotted McQuaid at one of the consoles and marched toward him.

"McQuaid…update!"

"Um…well, sir…it appears that things have…um…well, I mean…circumstances have…erm…"

"Out with it!"

"We’re screwed, sir."

Smedley glanced at the overhead screen. "Ah, I see. The egg finally hatched."

McQuaid blinked a couple of times. "Sir? You mean…you knew about this?"

There was a strange, wild expression in Smedley’s eyes as he smiled at the chief engineer. "Of course I knew about it. All according to my plan."

"What plan? What are you talking about?"

"This world is flawed, McQuaid. Imperfect. The best thing to do with an imperfect machine is to destroy it and start over again from scratch."

"You’re mad! I won’t let you destroy the world!"

Smedley shook his head. "It’s already too late, my friend." He gazed up at the image of the dragon on the display. "He is splendid, is he not? The perfect harbinger of destruction." Smedley began to laugh – an insane high-pitched giggle that made McQuaid’s skin crawl.

Smedley was laughing still even as the guards carried him out of the room.

One of the engineers timidly approached McQuaid. "Excuse me, sir…is it true? Are we all doomed?"

"Not by a long shot," said McQuaid. "Prepare to launch the Final Release."

The engineer staggered. "But sir…! Do you realize what that will do?"

"It will charge the planet’s atmosphere into a state of highly reactive volatility. Then we’ll light a match, and blow that dragon to kingdom come."

"But what about the people?"

"We can’t save them," said McQuaid. "There’s no time. But we do have their data patterns in our computer banks. We’ll be able to recreate them once the planet’s surface is again inhabitable. We’ll even give them food and water…maybe even a weapon or some spell scrolls. Look, I don’t like this any more than you do. But I don’t see any other way out of this, do you?"

The engineer sighed and shook his head.

"Right, then," said McQuaid. "Battle stations."

The peak of highest mountain in the Steamfonts opened up, and a gigantic mechanical eye emerged, slowly rising up into the air. The awesome Red Eye engine had been built ages ago by the gnomish military as a ‘last defense’ measure for the planet – a device that would turn the planet itself into a weapon. Many had argued against its creation, doubting that it would ever be needed. But the military advocates eventually won the favor of the council. None of the council members could resist the idea that the gnomes would be in control of the most powerful weapon in the world. Many scholars believe that the gnomes attempt to compensate for their tiny size by seeking incredible destructive power.

As the gnomes donned their tinted goggles, McQuaid gave the order. The chief engineer pressed a big red button, and the world blew up.

"Damage report!" shouted McQuaid.

"Everything’s gone to hell, sir," said a technician.

"Um, can you be a little more specific?"

"All of our systems are down. Stability is less than 5 percent. We’re losing packets left and right."

"Can you identify the source?"

The gnome fiddled with the buttons on his console. "Ah, hell. It’s UUNet, sir."

"Dammit! Where is he?"

The two gnomes ran across the chamber and climbed down a ladder leading to an area known as "The Pit", where they kept all of the robots. In one of the dark corners, they found an old, rusty robot that was lying on the floor, twitching spasmodically.

"Poor UUNet," said the technician. "I should have known…the strain was just too much for him."

"How long will it take to repair him?"

"Ah, hell, sir…this is an ancient model. It could take days just to get the right parts."

McQuaid thought of all the red lights blinking upstairs, lights representing lives that were on hold, all because an old robot had gone to pieces at the worst possible time.

"Do the best you can," he told the technician. With a sigh, he turned and began to climb the ladder.

"Where are you going, sir?"

"To the bar," said the gnome. "I need a stiff drink."

Smedley did eventually recover his sanity. Living underground with robots and technicians for long periods of time can place a terrible strain upon even the strongest mind. But he was right, the world is not perfect. On the other hand, it could be much worse.

So, when faced with the noisome problems of lag or packet loss, you may rest assured that the gnomes are on it. When your world crashes, when your life gets locked up, you may take comfort in the fact that the world is in the hands of a bunch of tired gnomes on the verge of insanity, struggling day and night to make sure that the world keeps working.
