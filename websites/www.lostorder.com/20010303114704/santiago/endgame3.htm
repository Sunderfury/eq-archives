The Howling Wind

Nightfall in Everfrost gives new meaning to the word ‘cold’. When the bitter wind howls through the mountaintops, it is brutal and merciless, murdering all warmth.

Greyhawk heard the wind, but it did not frighten him. He knew how long it would be before the sun went down, and he knew that he would arrive at the shelter before then. He knew how to build a warm campfire, and he knew that he would survive this winter night.

What worried him was the vision. What did it mean? Never-ending night. For a moment, he wondered if Bolgar was right. What if it was a false vision, one that would lead him to doom? But no, the spirits had never once lied to him. But what was he to do? Where was he to go?

He stopped to rest for a moment. Far below, he saw a giant woolly mammoth trudge through the valley. Even from way up here, he could hear its mighty footsteps. He closed his eyes, and listened to the sound. After the mammoth had gone, there was only the wind.

His people had lived in Everfrost for many, many years. His father’s father’s father had perhaps walked these very mountains, once. And though they were long dead, it is said that their spirits still wander the earth, still watching over their children. Were the ghosts of his ancestors watching him now? Was it their ghostly voices he heard in the howling wind?

How he wished that they could guide him now, for he did not know what to do. What he needed was a sign. But there were no signs, no instructions. There was only the wind.

He opened his eyes and gazed out across the icy landscape. Was that a fire he saw, in the distance?
