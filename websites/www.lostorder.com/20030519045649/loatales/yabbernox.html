Personal Demons
by Yabbernox Flamebeard

The old dwarf hefted his axe and glanced at each of the oncoming orcs, weighing his chances. The centurion on the right would be the first to fall, he decided as he dropped into his fighting stance. "Do not chase the spider and let the mosquito feed," his father was often heard saying...Aye he would follow that advice even here, and leave the larger of the two, a Legionnaire, for last.

The orcs began to run as they drew nearer, and moved as if they wished to flank the dwarf. Feigning towards the larger Legionnaire, the dwarf swung his great-axe in an arc toward the Centurion, catching him squarely across the middle of the back, severing his spine. The axe, however, became lodged and as the orc fell dead the haft of the axe was twisted from the dwarf's grip. As he futilely scrambled to free his axe, the dwarf was suddenly knocked sprawling in the dirt by the force of the Legionnaire's fearsome bash.

Blows rained down on the dwarf as he struggled groggily to his feet. Turning towards his assailant, the dwarf let loose a mighty kick, catching the orc mid-thigh and sending it off-balance. As the orc regained his footing, the dwarf drew his long sword and once again settled into his fighting stance. Realizing its foe was no longer at a disadvantage, the orc beat a hasty retreat and soon disappeared over a nearby hill.

"Such a fight would only have been a warm up in me youth," the dwarf muttered to himself. "I am getting too old for this!"

After freeing his axe and ripping away a portion of the dead centurion's tunic, the dwarf cleaned his blades. Glancing down at himself, he noticed a small gash in the calf of his right leg...a gash he strangely hadn't even felt. The dwarf pulled a bottle from his back and doused the wound with dwarven spirits. Shrugging to himself, he then doused his throat and sucked wind through his teeth at the potent yet satisfying burn that settled in his belly before spreading outward in fiery tendrils. As he lowered the bottle, a glint of light on the nearby hilltop caught his eye...

There was a familiar form standing up there...all too familiar.

"Bloody, damned elf!"

The form on the hilltop raised its sword in mock-salute to the dwarf and then vanished over the other side.

"Would that I could douse my heart with this as well," the dwarf sighed as he finished off the spirits. "I am getting too old for this."
