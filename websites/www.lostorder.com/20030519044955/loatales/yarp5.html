Setting Sun
by Yarp

As the puppet dances all about
The Master controls his strings
But the Master's world stretches far and wide
Beyond the reach of the marionnette's wings.

And so, with sadness, the Master retires
As the puppet's strings go slack
With a heavy heart and a tear in his eye
He quietly turns his back and so,

The hearth grows cold as the fire goes out
But yet, an ember remains.
Glowing with hope that one day soon,
The fire will rage and burn once again.
