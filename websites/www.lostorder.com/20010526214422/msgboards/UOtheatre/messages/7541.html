Aye, barding is rather fun, especially when you get to the upper level things and have enough supplies in reserve to shrug off a death or three.

The one left over is rarely a problem for me, but my two main bards can handle virtually anything in game. The provoking and peace are just added bonusses to an already kick-tail warrior or mage. The trick for me is to make sure the most powerful monster in a dance is severely weakened by the time it decides I'm lunch. With Dedigan-Napa, I use flame strikes (for magery gain mostly) to balance the scale. For Path-Lake Superior, I use my halberd or katana to balance the scale. That way I can one or two hit the survivor of the first bout and get the gold and fame.

For music and peacemaking, get on the boat (or run around) and make it move. You can only gain in some skills when you have different locations. You can only gain in provoke when you use different targets.

And I honestly don't think provoke is affected by power hours. A few months ago, I was doing some testing with it on LS, and I would routinely gain just as much in my second hour that I would in my first hour.

Again, welcome, enjoy,
Dedigan
