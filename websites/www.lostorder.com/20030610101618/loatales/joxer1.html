Knight of Virtue
by Joxer

Lord Joxer clutched the jeweled hilt of Soulforce, the legendary silver Scimitar of Vanquishing. Peering deep into the thick mists, Joxer stared and waited for the next wave to come. His mithril plate glistened under the early morning light - revealing crimson smears of fresh blood.

The pain in his leg was intense and the bandages that covered the deep spear wound hung loosely about his knee; but Joxer would not leave the front. In the distance he heard the screaming of children - painful memories from a time long past when his village was destroyed; when the place of his birth was burned and every beast, man, woman and child were slain. His children. His wife.

He had chose the life of the sea for a time. Having made his name in the great oceans of Britannia, in his time he was the mightiest of all the pirates - rivaling the infamy of dastardly Baert the Black. It was toward the ends of these dark times for Joxer that he sought solace and an easing of the pain that plagued him. This was when he found the Order of Akalabeth.

The snap of a twig roused Joxer from his thoughts. Gazing steadfast into the dim distance, Joxer heard the moaning sounds of an undead servant.

"Arr. So the Necromancer now sends her servants to fight. So be it, let them come."

Shambling forth out of the mist came the liche lord. Dark black with eyes of fire the beast lunged forward toward Joxer. But the Soulforce was true. Joxer swung the mighty sword deep into the side of the abomination - black jelly and rotting flesh spattered his shield as the blade cut deep. Staggered, the Liche stepped back and summoned words of power to smite Joxer. Feeling the hairs on his head rising, the Admiral raised his magic kite shield and spoke a prayer. A long crackle and the smell of sulfur - a tremendous force of energy and flame slammed into the Paladin's raised shield. The shield began to emit a low hum and the Liche's spell was suddenly reflected back at the monster. The Liche was powerful, but not powerful enough to withstand its own spell; bone, flesh and fragments of the staff the beast held exploded in a dazzling display of fire and lightning.

Joxer grinned, then wiped the gore off his magic plate.

It was a fellow named Zael who warned Joxer of an approaching darkness. He Even heard rumors through his contacts within Castle British of evil deeds afoot In Moonglow, led by a dark mage well versed in the arts of necromancy.

His suspicions were confirmed when he found the body of his beloved friend, Rahyl, nailed to a large tree near Shame - a personal challenge to Joxer from this dark fiend. He had grown well versed in the arts of combat against the evil murderers and thieves of the land - this is undoubtedly what brought the necromancer to know him. He would be no trophy for this witch.

In the distance further down the ridge, Joxer heard the battle cry of LOA. He was brave, surely, but he was not stupid. He knew that the witch had recruited many evil humans to assist her in her quest and this is why he had brought three of his closest guildmates: Affliction, Palehorse and Littlewode. They were undoubtedly engaged in the same combats and he wondered how they fared. The price of glory is high, but the reward of knighthood is higher.

And Joxer waited for the next wave of enemy soldiers. Dug behind a wall, the brave LOA defended what remained of the farming village on the outskirts of Yew. And to the last man and woman they would stay.
