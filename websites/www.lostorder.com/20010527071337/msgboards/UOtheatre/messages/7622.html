I read yer thoughts with interest.

Ye say, "wherever I go and there is a bard at work … but you almost never see anyone enticing or peacemaking anymore.

As I have studies th' Bardic enchantments for near two years, I have taken a keen interest in them. Truly th' gods (OSI) have made th' learning of th' Bardic enchantments more important than th' learning of true understanding, more important than th' sharing of these things with others, they have!

Th' gods have, for all time into th' murky past, mostly failed th' Bards enchantment of Enticement. While 'tis not truly useless it has few great uses, such as Provocation and Peacemaking have. Lately some uses of Enticement have emerged, such as practicing yer Musicianship in unsuspecting Royal NPCs an' such. Othes have remarked they have "noticed that monsters quit attacking went enticed".

Yet another has said this: "As for Enticement, I realize many adventuring bards feel it has little use, but I find it very helpful. My rationale it this: as a healer, I can use Enticement to further assist my fellow adventurers. When the going gets rough and some poor soul has a pack of monsters swarming all over him or her, a little Enticement goes a long way toward giving them some breathing room."

I myself see that this obscure enchantment has it's value, but as the gods have decreed we may learn only so much formal skills, then I have chosen to leave it be.

As for yer comment, Sindaer Roze, that "you almost never see anyone … peacemaking anymore", well with this I must disagree. Ah yes, many who casually pick up th' Musicianship do so for th' purpose of learning Provocation never learn th' subtle Enchantment of Peacemaking. Some have said these are not Bards at all but instead are Warriors or Mages seeking to learn a bit o' something to help them on their way. Indeed it has been said that a "Real Bard" is never one who merely dabbles in Musicianship to improve their agility and then maybe add a bit of Provocation to tweak th' Monster's noses.

Truly I no longer entertain such thinking. Take ol' Santiago th' Pirate for example. To th best o' me understanding he can barely pick up a lute and tune it. Yet he is without doubt a Bards' Bard if there were ever one. Th' truth is whether one is a Bard or not stands not on the skills known in UO Sosaria, but instead flows from one's true actions and deeds. Musicianship and such is only a path.

As far as Peacemaking, however, this enchantment is perhaps th' most powerful in all of th' land. Those who say otherwise fail in their understanding. I speak not of the contests that Warriors and Mages have with each other, such as in th' guild wars or th' conflicts of Order an' Chaos. Nay, I speak only of Monsters. A great Bard can, using only a simple harp and the skill of Peacemaking, wander to th' depths of many a dangerous Dungeon, travel across the foulest swamp, and pass through th' fiendish realms of Fire an' Ice with little harm to themselves or their companion. If they are clever enough. Th' skill of Peacemaking is a mystery and a puzzle to all but those who have mastered it. Mastered it in the god's (OSI) skill and in their avatar's maker's mind as well.

May th Virtues bless ye, Sindaer Roze!
Please do share yer thoughts with us more often.
