Captain's Log: Morningthaw 9, 11 P.Y.
by Enosh Flam

Captain's Log: Morningthaw 9

You know I'm startin' to wonder about this place. You'd figure that most places, when spring arives and the new year starts, the weather starts getting warmer. Today when I awoke it was probably the coldest day yet since I found myself walking the forests of Dereth. Still I've got more imediate concers, like how I'm going to pay me tab here at the Royal Firkin.

To that end I figure I'll take a stab at the rumored Rithwic crypts. That is if I can manage to find them. I'd do just about anything fer a few pyreal at this point (anything that is except werk fer Ricetha cleanin' mugs and washin' tables)

(later in the day)

Well I didn't find those cypts though I did find meself back in Lytelthotpe again. I searched all over the west bank of the river north of town and didn't find a bloody thing but trouble. First I happened across a camp of drudges and moswarts. That wasn't a problem fer me and I layed them all out in a line. Next it was that red phyntos wasps. Again not much of a problem.

Once I dispatched the red wasps, I set about tryin' to pry their wings off with out damage. I know of a collector in Lytelthorpe that was lookin' fer a set of wings to add to her collection. Well wouldn't you know it that as soon as I got one good pair of wings off that wasp that a squadrin of blue wasps snuck up behind me. I didn't know they were there until I felt the air around me explode in a fire ball. Now I want to know what god's sick sence of humor gave the blue wasps the fire magic in the first place. The bloody wasp nearly roasted me alive before I knew what I was doing. Well I did what any harded adventure would do when roasted to within an inch of his life.

I ran.

Some how I managed to run far enough and fast enough that the blue wasps got bored and gave up tryin' to turn me into "salty sailor shishkabob". Needless to say, when I made it back to Rithwic propor I needed a drink! I walked into the Royal Firkin just in time to see Ricetha talkin' to a couple of rough lookin' mercinaries. She pointed one finger at me and said "That's the pirate!" Once again I did what any hardened adventure would do in such a situation.

I ran, again.

This time though I through a small pouch of pyreal and smacked good ole Ricetha right in the forhead before I high tailed it out of there. Those mercs were persistant to say the least. I managed to beat them to the Lytelthorpe portal by no more than 3 steps. This was of course right before I remeber why I left Lytelthorpe to begin with. I really need to stop "makin' a name fer meself" in some of these towns, afore I have no place to go get me ale anymore.

Still I was lucky when I made my way into Lytelthorpe, seems all by Selflora's forgotten me already. That and the ax wieldin' brute of a husband was gone travelin' again too. So fer the time bein' I settled down, ordered meself a good stout and watched the rain fall fer a while. Mayhaps tomorrow I'll head out again fer some more trouble makein', er I mean adventuring.
