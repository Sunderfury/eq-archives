Gradishar's Night of Talons Report
Guest Report by Gradishar Tregaron, The Long-Winded Bard of Shadowbania
June 11, 2002 / Shadowbane Closed Beta Test

The evening of June 9, 2002, shall henceforth be known as the Night of Talons. The House Tregaron, led by its stalwart Lord Protector, the Minotaur Kronok Boneapart, prepared to re-plant the Tree of Life for the Tregaron ancestral home, Akalabethia. For days, Lord Kronok was actively engaged in diplomatic plottings with both friends and historic foes alike. Patron of Wrath, a one time ally but more recently a hated adversary, proposed that both Wrath and Akalabethia be planted simultaneously... thus rendering the dark forces of Compton with a dilemma of which sapling to attack. Lord Kronok, judged that Patron and his Crimson Empire were much more hated than the brave and honorable citizens of Akalabethia, and thus suspected that Akalabethia would be spared the brunt of Comptonian aggression. However, nothing could be further from the truth.

The plans were proceeding as anticipated. The House Tregaron's bannermen were being rallied from both Navarre and King's Cross. No level of experience was too low for a defender of Akalabethia...every man or woman who could lift a sword was encouraged to participate. The Immortal Vengeance, the Prothoe Tribe, the Tribunal... each sent word that their sword arms were ready to defend Akalabethia and her new source of life. The time was set... midnight would be the planting hour... and all eyes turned to the northern reaches, past the ruins of Estragoth, waiting for Kronok to give the word.

Kronok himself was as impatient as the rest. None knew of his agreement with Emperor Patron... that Patron would plant first (presumably to be the "2nd" city planted) and that Kronok would only plant once Wrath was visible on the "World Map." As more and more bannermen arrived... the tension grew. "Tells" flew through the night air like swarms of bees. Scouting parties were dispatched and deeds were arranged... and finally... it was bellowed... "Wrath is Planted!"

Upon hearing this news, Kronok set out to make Akalabethia the third, but greatest city of Aerynth. For those present, and watching the World Map, it was a tense moment. We of the Inner Council had remembered that there was a "Name" interface for planting which was given AFTER the clock started on the planting [a Tree of Life is particularly susceptible to attack during its first hour of life, when it is unranked and incapable of healing itself or any other structure]. Thus, we of the House Tregaron's Council had hoped that if we didn't "name" our Tree for an hour, perhaps we would not show up on the World Map and be able to evade the prying eyes (and sharpened swords) of our enemies. Alas, our plans were for naught. Name or no name, our city was visible to all of Aerynth immediately upon planting and within moments shouts were heard of a Compton scouting party to the southwest. We quickly formed up into two groups (of 13 apiece) and set each group to guarding our two assets... an Inn and a Church. The idea was that the Inn was our forward position and the group most likely to engage the enemy first. This group was led by Lord Kronok himself and would take the brunt of the Comptonian attack. The second group was our reserve force, led by Sir Tearen, once of the Final Haven contingency. His group was to wait and guard the tree with their lives if necessary.

While these two formidable forces waited to meet our enemies on the glorious field of battle, our scouts and rangers hunted through the neighboring snow covered forests, looking for signs of attackers or their siege equipment.

It did not take long for the enemy to arrive.

The forces of Compton barreled in like a tidal wave. Their archers sending a volley of arrows at us like rain. Their mages casting incantations that filled the sky like fireworks. And at their lead was the dared Templar Bone Dancer, his "debuffs" crippling our front line of attackers almost immediately. However... the combined forces of Akalabethia did not waiver, nor did we flinch. The forces of Compton are battle hardened... having cut their teeth on many an unsuspecting camper. We were largely untested and unproven... and yet... we showed the courage of true warriors that night. While the brunt of the Inn Group centered our efforts on the dred Bone Dancer, the rest of us began to take strikes at his supporting crew. At our sides were Cinder's Covenant of Swords and Vorgen's Immortal Vengeance... and thus we had three parties of attackers to their one... all engaged in bloody glorious war. The battle waged on... and then... all of a sudden... a few of the Comptonites began to fall... and then they began to run... we were winning! [Winning with still a full reserve guarding our tree!] The forces of Compton were being either slain or scattered. Akalabethia was VICTORIOUS!

The celebration was immediate! Akalabethia had survived the greatest onslaught ever seen in Aerynth! Ours was not a battle of guards or barracks... but of warriors and wizards... of blades and spells... and it was a beautiful thing to behold!

Of course, we could not rest on our laurels... we immediately turned our attention to the next order of business... "When will they return?" and "will they have trebuchet?" Unfortunately, a second battle was not our destiny... as the gods expelled us from Aerynth while they cleaned the Comptonian blood we had drenched it with. When we returned... we found our sapling on the verge of being a true Tree of Life... and with that graduation... the security of Akalabethia was assured. Akalabethia had survived the Night of Talons... and had proven her citizens worthy of being her defenders.

To all those who bled on the battlefield... on either side... I commend you. To those who stood in reserve, itching to engage our enemy... but instead following our orders... I commend you. To those who helped purchase our Tree of Life, our walls and our barracks... I commend you. To those who have pledged their blood and their steel to the survival of Akalabethia... I commend you. The Night of Talons was a wonder to behold... and I was honored to be a part of it.

Thank you all!

Signed: Gradishar
LOA SB Chapter Leader
