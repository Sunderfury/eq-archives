There's an old saying: "Virtue is its own reward." I happen to agree with it.

I don't think of UO as a game...but rather an environment. People that travel there bring their own personalities. It's kinda like the scene in Empire Strikes Back where Luke is about to enter that dark cave in the swamp. He asks Yoda, "What's in there?" Yoda replies, "Only what you take with you."

If Virtue is absent from UO, I think that just means it's absent from most of the people that play it. The main purpose of the game for many is to rake in lots of gold and magic items, and become as powerful as possible. That's the way a lot of computer games are designed - and we enjoy them, because it's not quite as easy to become rich and powerful in the 'real' world. But for a lot of people, wealth and power are the only things worth seeking.

While it would be nice to have some sort of game system that encouraged or rewarded acts of virtue, I really don't know how it would happen. People would always exploit it, for one thing; there's not a whole lot anyone can do to prevent that.

But then, when you save someone from certain death...when you give money or knowledge to a fellow player...doesn't that make you feel good? To me, there's a certain sense of satisfaction in knowing that you've brightened someone else's day, that you've brought a smile to someone's face. For some people, that feeling is better than gold, or fame, or any other worldly 'reward'.

For other people, virtue is pretty much pointless. Why sacrifice yourself to save someone else? Why give money away, when you could spend it for yourself? Why, etc. etc. You can't 'change' these people; you can't just make them virtuous. To try to do so would be a source of certain disappointment. It would be like trying to make a fish grow legs; it just doesn't work that way.

The world is not full of virtuous people, alas. I guess the trick is to live your own life as best you can. Keep your own candle burning, so that others can see that the world isn't entirely dark.
