Ambush at Kaladim

"I can’t believe you’re going to carry that thing all the way back to Kaladim," said Bierfreund, staring at the dead shark that Walterus was dragging along behind him.

"Why not?" asked Walterus. "I caught it, didn’t I? I aim to make a meal out of it."

Bierfreund glanced back at the three humans whom they were now leading to Kaladim. "They probably think you’re crazy."

"Crazy? Me? I’m not the one wearing a polar bear skull on my head."

"Well, you’ve got a point there."

They walked for a while in silence. The overcast skies made the forest seem darker than usual. The woods were strangely quiet, aside from the occasional sound of fluttering wings as a bat flew overhead.

"We’re being watched," said Bierfreund.

Walterus continued walking, but his eyes darted to the trees of the forest. "Goblins?"

"Aye," said Bierfreund. "Up on that hill to our right. Several of them. What do you think we should do?"

"Attack!" screamed Walterus, charging up the hill.

"Brilliant," muttered Bierfreund. He turned to face the three humans. "We’ve been caught in an ambush. Walterus and I will hold them off. You folks run as fast as you can along this path. It will take you straight to Kaladim. Be careful!" He turned and charged up the hill after Walterus.

"What did he say?" asked Greyhawk.

Rothan shrugged. "I don’t know. I think he was speaking the dwarven tongue."

"I think he wants us to follow him," said Eshay.

By the time the three travelers reached the top of the hill, the two dwarves had already defeated one of the goblins. But they were badly outnumbered, and both of them were bleeding badly from several wounds. Some of the goblins spotted the newcomers, and charged toward them. The battle was joined, and what followed was a chaotic frenzy of clashing weapons and spell explosions. More goblins fell to the ground, but there were still too many of them.

"Fall back!" shouted Bierfreund, as he deflected a goblin blade aimed at his throat.

"I’m not done yet!" grunted Walterus, hacking the legs off of one of his foes.

Suddenly, there was a brilliant flash of a light, followed by a tremendous explosion as a bolt of lightning fell from the sky, searing their foes. Many goblins fell, and the rest were beginning to rout.

"Good work, Rothan!" shouted Greyhawk, as he crushed the skull of a goblin that was trying to run away.

"It wasn’t me," said the mage. "It was them." He pointed to three elves who had just arrived.

"Greetings, elf friends!" said Bierfreund. "We are grateful for your assistance."

"Greetings, good dwarf," said Kileyan. "I am glad we could be of help against these foul creatures. There were quite a few of them!"

"Bah!" muttered Walterus, wiping the blood from his forehead. "We could have handled them."

"A glorious victory!" said Penaden. "I think I’ll sing a song!"

"Wait," said Jarlaxle. "Let me get my spear ready."
