You ask, "Does provoke still not stick ... ever?"

Provocation seems to work very well for me. Of course I am essentially unable to raise it above about 91. It raises only about 0.1 per week through "just playing the game". But since I am GM Musicianship, Provocation works for me about 9 out of 10 times and it seems to "stick" well now. I must, however, be sure to step away from the provoked monsters as they are usually standing on me by the time I provoke them. Also it is necessary to provoke each against the other if one wants to be sure to be eligible for the treasures they carry.

You ask, "If you attack a provoked monster 5 minutes after you provoke it, does it still retarget you? "

Yes this can happen. Well, I am also a Mage, and I can mana dump on it with no retargeting. However if a melee artist runs up and lays on they might take notice. For example, I provoked my EV on a stone gargoyle the other day. My consort then attacked the Gargoyle and the EV took notice of her and then she was dead. But then the EV went back to the Gargoyle.


You comment that, "Enticement is of course useless, but the only solution is to completely change the skill or make it go away."

On this one I posted "Suggested Improvements" below at http://www.lostorder.com/msgboards/UOtheatre/messages/7149.html Why not take a look and post your response. Yeow! I got flamed on it already.

You say, "Peacemaking isn't useful enough to warrant using 100 (or even 20) skill points on it. It would be better if the peace lasted for more than a second, if the peace worked on people, including the bard who uses it(no offensive spells or attacking while peace is in effect)... but that would mean increasing the skill delay time again."

Actually I find Peacemaking to be a great Bardic skill, but this seems to be a point on which there is a great diversity of opinion. Again, this skill does not raise significantly above the low 90's, so I only have 92 Peacemaking. So I am successful 9 out of 10 times. I would like to be GM in that skill since that would mean I am successful "nearly all" the time. I hope.

Peacemaking has a lot of good uses, taking into consideration it is a defensive, not offensive skill. I can move about pretty effectively by making peace and running out of sight, or even by continually peacemaking (but beware when it fails). Whatever happens I do not wish for any more delay between the successes, yet I understand the value of that for the less accomplished. It has a lot of other uses, but I understand the diversity of opinion too. Each to their own. I don't want to be anyone's clone, thank you.
