WALARYN BASTARDS
It has come to the attention of King Ak'anon that numerous novice gnomes have been frequenting the grand mechanical city of Ak'anon claiming to be of blood relation to the famous/infamous Walaryn Clan.  It is believed that this new generation of gnomes with names such as Wyloon, Wyleddyn and Wynwood are actually the bastard sons of Wyntrok the Dashing, a rogue of some repute who disappeared near the northern city of Halas many seasons ago.  Nevertheless, these new Walaryns, combined with the sons of Wynston the Brave and Wyndsor the Pious now turn up under every rock or shrub in Steamfont, much to the dismay of the local indigenous populations of Kobolds, Harpies and Minotaur Slavers.  Furthermore, it is rumoured that the Walaryns may have "underworld" connections, but this reporter could neither confirm nor deny such allegations.  Woozle Walaryn, the alleged patriarch of the family, responded to such allegations by stating that "the members of the Walaryn Clan endeavour to advance the causes of the family, by all means at their disposal."  This cryptic response can only lead one to question the motivations of this mysterious family, and wonder how they will influence future Ak'anonian affairs.

OGRE COMMITS ATROCITY IN SUREFALL GLADES
There is mourning in Surefall Glades as the community lays to rest one of the most controversial of it's inhabitants. Mammoth, a grizzled old bear recently accused of killing a Surefall resident, was slaughtered overnight by an Ogre. Authorities are unsure if this was revenge for Mammoths prior actions of just the whim of a blood-crazed Shadow Knight. Reports also implicated a female Barbarian and a local Ranger in the execution.
INNOCENT CYCLOPS HUNTING PARTY DIES IN CAZIC CATASTROPHE
What was planned to be a simple exercise in Cyclops hunting recently turned to disaster after a group comprising noted paladin Zirc, his betrothed, the cleric Osyra, long-nosed patriarch of the Walaryn Clan, Woozle, and a unnamed wolf decided that Cyclops hunting lacked a challenge and set off to the legendary temple of Cazic-Thule. After finding a secure position near the entrance to the temple the group initially had some success in slaying the evil reptiles, until a large group of the creatures rallied and charged the hapless adventurers en-mass. Osyra and Zirc quickly fell to the onslaught of lizard beasties, and only quick thinking by the Gnome and an excellent ability to follow short creatures by the wolf saved the remaining two party members.

YET ANOTHER PALADIN INVOLVED IN SENSELESS MASSACRE
Hot on the heels of our previous exposé on misdoings by Paladins, comes reports from an unnamed source (rumoured to be in league with the extremist animal protection society the unkempt preservers) of another paladin in-training engaging in some rather dubious activity. It appears that Valdox slaughtered a trio of those cuddly creatures of the forest, kodiak bears, after they had been baited by a mob of Aradani poachers. Sources close to the upper echelons of the Surefall Glade and Kelethin Councils indicate that they are extremely unhappy with the blind-eye that is being turned to these incidents by the Royal Palace in Felwithe. What comes of this can only be speculated, but needless to say that relations between these groups are somewhat strained at present.

FURTOG OGREBANE REJOICES - MUDTOE OGRES ELIMINATED
Reports today from Kaladim say that son of the famed Burtog Ogrebane handed the heads of the last two Mudtoe Ogres to the King today. Further investigation indicates that Furtog did not kill the remaining Mudtoes, Boog and Goob, but that a bounty hunter tracked them to their hide-out in the Ocean of Tears and returned the heads to Furtog for a reward. Furtog has been tight-lipped over these accusations, insisting that he tracked them himself.
CYCLOPS MURDERED IN EASTERN KARANA - FAMILY IN MOURNING
Long-time bully and odorous malcontent, Broon the Cyclops was discovered today lying in a pool of blood and covered with insect stings. The cause of his death is unknown at present, but Proon, his distraught father and his brother Droon have sworn to crush all the 'little-uns' until they feel better about it. Reports of a mystery wolf being seen in the area before the murder are currently unsubstantiated, but druids from the local druid circle were not entirely unhappy with the outcome.

HISTORY OF THE ELVEN RACE (part 1)
As part of our ongoing task of educating the unwashed, we present to you a brief history of the Elven race.
We all know that Tunare placed the Elves upon the surface of  Norrath in the beginning of the Elder Age, and that soon after this Innoruuk, the Prince of Hate kidnapped the first Elvish King and Queen and bent and twisted them to his will to create the Teir'Dal. But how did the Elves become two races? We know from our history that Tunare only created one race, yet we also know that by the end of the Age of Blood the elves had split into two groupings, the woodland loving Wood Elves and the High Elves who lived in cities built of stone and magic. Is it possible that somewhere between these Ages the elves became two separate races or is there another explanation? Recent research into this question leads to the interesting proposition that the elves are still one race, although this is contested by both Wood and High Elves alike. However a number of disturbing facts remain. The offspring from a union of either Wood or High Elves and Humans is a Half-Elf and it is impossible to tell the race of it's Elven parents. Also the offspring from a union between Wood and High Elves takes on the characteristics of the region they were brought up in. These facts have lead keen-minded researches to propose that there is still only one Elven race and that the differences between are related to the environment they were brought up in. Whether this is true not, it is sure to spark controversy over which of the two groupings have remained true to their heritage.
