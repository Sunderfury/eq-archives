LOA Frequently Asked Questions:

We spend quite a bit of time answering questions that are asked every time someone becomes a part of our community, either as a member or a guest. Rather than repeating this cycle, we decided to place all of the FAQs on this page. If you cannot find the answer to something, simply send our Council (links on the menu) a message with your question. We will very quickly respond via email.

What is the LOA?
LOA is a community of online gamers. We do not make the games, we simply play them and try to make them a little more fun for our members and those around us.
return to top

How long has the LOA been in existence?
The LOA has been around since November 1997.
return to top

How was the LOA formed?
Brimstone was a member of the GAMES-L mailing list. Prior to the release of Ultima Online, he sent a message asking for people who might be interested in forming a guild. Freejack and several others responded and the LOA was born. Great effort was spent deciding on a name, drafting policies and procedures, marketing the LOA, advertising and recruiting.
return to top

How do I join the LOA?
You simply fill out an application form and submit it. We highly recommend that you read our policies FIRST. Then make sure that you feel the LOA is for you. We also suggest that you spend time working on your application and put some thought into it. Additionally, you should spend lots of time getting to know our community, history and tales, as well as going out of your way to be noticed on our forums and in our chatroom.
return to top

Who is responsible for allowing new members into the guild?
Every full member of LOA is given the opportunity to vote on all new applicants that apply to the LOA. Therefor, the entire LOA is tasked with reviewing applications, getting to know the applicants and voting on each person.
return to top

How long does it normally take to join the LOA?
On average, it takes about 2 to 3 months to become a full member. The petitioner phase lasts for 2 weeks. The Initiate phase lasts 30 days. The Commoner  phase (final phase) lasts 30 days as well.
return to top

What alignment is your guild (i.e. good, evil, chaotic, etc.)?
The LOA, out of game, is ALWAYS considered a "good aligned guild". We do NOT engage in harmful or immature acts. We are not hackerdewdz, we are not stalkers, we are not out to hinder the enjoyment of games for other players. "IN GAME", our alignments vary depending on the game. In Ultima Online, the majority of our guild members are Lawful or Chaotic Good. Some of our members in UO are Neutral or Evil, all for roleplay purposes only. Our EverQuest chapter allows all playable alignments. The AC Chapter is primarily good aligned. Future games may have rules regarding what alignments are acceptable for our members, based on our theme or purpose in that game.
return to top

Does the LOA roleplay, PK or PVP?
The LOA offers a diverse selection of play-styles. We strongly encourage roleplaying, however, we also want our non-RP members to feel appreciated as well. Therefor, PVP is also encouraged so long as the fighting is either: 1.) agreed upon (organized warfare) or 2.) used to defend ourselves and/or wage war against an enemy of the guild. We WILL kick members out of our guild, forever, if they are found "Player Killing" people who were not willing combatants. Period.
return to top

How does LOA decide what games to enter "Officially" as a guild?
We try to monitor games as they are announced. Usually one or more members become interested in a given game, they then discuss the game via our forums/mailing lists and attract a larger audience. Once beta is opened, we try to have our members review the game to see if it suits our needs and has an interest amongst our members. If enough people are interested, we declare an official presence in that game. Official LOA chapters receive a web page, news page, forums, mailing list and chat rooms, as well as promotional and admin support from the LOA leadership.
