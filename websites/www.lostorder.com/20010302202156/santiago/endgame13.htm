The Wrong Spell

From the safety of a nearby hilltop, Blogbog watched the battle below. A smile of satisfaction crossed his lips as he watched his goblin brethren perish at the hands of the dwarves, elves and humans.

"Fools!" the goblin chieftain hissed. "They did not listen to me! They thought I was crazy! Well, they have paid the price for their lack of faith!"

Blogbog heard a cracking noise behind him, and jumped to his feet. "At last, at last!" he cried.

Over the past few days, aided by the dark magicks of the goblin shamans, the egg had grown to the size of a large boulder. Dozens of tiny cracks now appeared, as the newborn dragon struggled to break of its shell. Blogbog eagerly rubbed his hands together and jumped up and down.

"Yes!" cried the goblin. "Emerge, and be born!"

Finally, the dragon’s head emerged, and its black forked tongue flickered into the air. The dragon’s yellow eyes peered at the goblin.

"Greetings, brood of Veeshan! I am Blogbog, king of all goblins. You will be my servant, my agent of destruction. Together, we will rule this world!"

With a sudden movement, the egg shattered. The dragon stretched its wings out into the air.

"My, but you are a large dragon! Well, let’s not waste time. I want you to begin by slaying all the dwarves, elves and humans in that valley down there. Do you understand?"

Apparently the dragon did not understand, for it lunged forward and bit the goblin’s head off.

"Tell me that wasn’t a dragon’s roar," said Rothan.

"That wasn’t a dragon’s roar," said Bierfreund.

"Liar," said Kileyan. "I can see it from here." The druid pointed to a nearby mountaintop.

"That ain’t no dragon," said Dargo. "That’s just a bat."

"I am an elf," said Kileyan haughtily. "My eyesight is as sharp as an eagle’s, and I see as well during the darkest night as I do on a sunny afternoon. You, however, are a dwarf. Your sight is clouded by one too many ales, and by overly bushy eyebrows."

"Look, elf, if ye wanna fight, just say so!" growled the dwarf.

"I simply state the truth," said the elf.

"And I’m simply gonna break yer legs if ye don’t shut up!"

Kileyan was about to respond with an angry retort, but he was interrupted by Penaden. All eyes turned toward the elven bard who sat cross-legged on the grass, strumming the strings of his lute.

As the end draws near
The dwarves fight the elves
Instead of saving the world
We fight amongst ourselves

"Bloody elf makes sense for once," muttered Kherghon.

"Aye, he does at that," said Kileyan. The druid extended his hand to Dargo. "Let’s put our differences aside, Dargo. Tonight we face a common enemy."

Dargo grunted reluctantly and shook the elf’s hand. "Fine fer now," he muttered, "but after tonight’s over I’m gonna kick yer elven arse all the way back to Felwithe."

"To arms!" cried Jarlaxle. "The dragon approaches!"

Weapons were drawn and spells were readied. They watched as the creature glided gracefully toward them, like an angel of death.

"I take it back," said Dargo. "That ain’t no bloody bat."

Rothan hurled the first spell, and a brilliant burst of fire erupted from the dragon’s side. It did not seem to hurt the dragon, but they had definitely gained its attention. As the dragon swooped in to attack, the heroes threw everything they had at the beast. Axes, spears, arrows and spells all flew through the air – some of the missiles bounced harmlessly away, but others found their mark, and the creature was soon bleeding from a dozen different wounds. The dragon flew up into the air, beyond reach of their blades.

Rothan threw another spell of blue fire at the monster as it retreated, and this time it seemed more effective – perhaps too effective, for the enraged dragon whirled about and flew directly toward the mage. Rothan grew pale as he realized he would not survive the attack. But just as the dragon flew past, Eshay leaped up and drove her sword into the creature’s flank. The creature reared back and shrieked in pain. Before the others could attack, the dragon had soared up into the air again. Its flight was unsteady – it had been heavily wounded.

"He’s trying to get away!" shouted Walterus.

"We must not let him escape!" shouted Rothan. "He is growing in size by the minute!"

"Well cast a spell at him or something!" cried Jarlaxle.

"I can’t…my magic has been exhausted!"

Greyhawk watched as the dragon flew away. Time seemed to stop, and he realized that this was his chance, his single chance to save the world.

"Ancestral spirits, guide my hands." He began to cast a spell, a powerful spell that would lift him into the air and allow him to pursue the dragon and finish it off. But his mind was still foggy from the dwarven ale – would he be able to successfully complete the spell?

Taking a deep breath and pushing his fear aside, he began to trace mystical patterns in the air with his fingers. Blue sparks of light began to appear around his body, floating and swirling like a cloud of insects. But wait…something was wrong! With a gasp of disbelief, he realized that he was casting the wrong spell!

But it was too late. The blue lights coalesced into a solid sphere of magical energy that slowly faded away. When the magical haze had dissipated, he found himself back in Halas.

"Greyhawk!" shouted Bolgar with surprise. "You’ve returned! I trust your quest was successful? It must have been, otherwise you would not be here. Is everything all right? You look tired. You must have had quite a journey! Come on, let’s go have a drink, and you can tell me all about it."
