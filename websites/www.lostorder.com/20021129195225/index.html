Monday, November 11, 2002

Veterans Day

Today is a very difficult 'holiday' for me. (It's probably why I'm awake past 1am...) Many people simply don't understand the what or why of Veterans Day. They can't comprehend it...honestly, I didn't either until I became a disabled Vet myself. Here is the abridged version, skip to the (END STORY) if you aren't interested:

During the time I was in the military, I was hurt fairly badly. One afternoon I was working in a G.P. Hardback tent (picture a tent with wooden walls with all of the canvas flaps shut) across the flightline from the S.P. (that's M.P. to you Army guys and Police to you civvies) squadron's hangar. (The base was so run down that they had abandoned some buildings and had begun to take over flightline hangars rather than repair them due to lack of funding) The SPs were getting a new Commanding Officer, whom was receiving the rank of Colonel at the same time as taking this new command posting. For added effect (this is all what I was told after my recovery) they were exploding GBS or Ground Burst Simulators (think 1/2 stick of dynamite) at the same time that the new commander took the flag from the outgoing commander. A neat effect...it damned near killed me.

Without regard to safety, one TSgt and one SSgt (that's Tech and Staff Sergeant...been in about 8-10 years) SP were thinking that it might be a good idea to set off the GBS in a trashcan and roll it into this tent that nobody seemed to be using. It would deaden the sound a bit and reduce the echo through the metal hangar! Flash forward 5 minutes, I'm gathering up some gear in the tent when I see a metal trash can roll towards me. I hear "FIRE IN THE HOLE!!"....it's the very last thing I've heard clearly in my life.

My boss tells me that it had thrown me out the back of the tent through the solid wall. I just knew that I was bleeding from my nose, I couldn't hear what he was saying, my eyes hurt, and I couldn't stand up. When I impacted the ground it was in an almost tip-toes fashion which tore the tendons in my ankles to roughly 180 degrees to the rear and about 80 degrees down. My left knee took serious twist damage as well, I shoved my way up and tried to run to the bathroom to assess the damage. As soon as I was helped to my feet I lost my balance and my lunch. I felt like the whole damned hangar was spinning, and I couldn't hear anything but a high pitched whine. I don't remember much after that for about the next week.

The final tally thus far is massive tearing to both ankles (for those who are familiar, the Occipital drawer is open 9 degrees and should only be open less than 1/2 a degree.) as well as minor damage to my knee. I've lost 75% of my hearing bilaterally, as well as picked up a .5 correction in my left eye. On top of that (and far worse) are the 3-4 a month migraines and nosebleeds. The doctors can't explain them, nor can they treat them...all I've been told is that I'm lucky to be alive. (END STORY)

So, here I am 4 years later...a little better off mentally, a little worse off physically. I've recovered partially from my nosebleeds but the migraines and ankle/knee damage are still greatly a part of my life. The hearing is no better and thankfully no worse, but the glasses make me look more 'studious' so my wife says. *grin* People ask me if I'm angry at the military for all of this pain that I will endure for the rest of my life. Honestly? No. Has there been times that I've been angry...you are damned right! But then it happens...

The other day I was walking through the halls at school when the V.A. Representative from A.S.U. makes a wide detour to catch up with me. She pulls me aside and tells me that she appreciates all of the sacrifices that I've made, and for all the crosses I'll have to bear for the rest of my life so that she can feel safe in her home. Man...how do you respond to that? Anything you say would cheapen the moment. I manage a smile and tell her that I really appreciate her taking time out to tell me that. I shuffle off to class but I'm proud and hurt at the the same time. I'm reminded of the friends I've lost in the Khobar Towers bombing, (and angry at people who have no damned idea that we were attacked and hundreds lost their lives so long before Sept. 11th) I'm reminded of the colleagues I lost in places I can't even talk about, and I'm reminded of the families that will never know the particulars of what happened to their loving spouse.

The point of all of this isn't to make you sad...it's a call to action. Take a second out of your day...tell a Vet that you appreciate what they have done for you. Whether they were a 9-5 'regular' who never saw action, or special forces flying to places unknown, they gave up their own freedom and put their lives on hold and on the line to make sure that your freedom was preserved. Whether you agree with the 'politics' of war or the government, these people live in squalid sub-par conditions in places you have never heard of to ensure your safety. All of this for you...

I leave you with a quote:
"It is the soldier, not the reporter who has given us the freedom of the press. It is the soldier, not the poet, who has given us the freedom of speech. It is the soldier, not the campus organizer, who gives us the freedom to demonstrate. It is the soldier who salutes the flag, who serves beneath the flag, and whose coffin is draped by the flag, who allows the protester to burn the flag."
Father Dennis Edward O'Brien, Sergeant, USMC

Saturday, November 09, 2002

WWIIOnline 1.7 is out.

I was going to do one of those "This is so cool I'm too busy playing to post anything tangible about it so go try it yourself." posts. However I just finished reading a rant at Megatokyo and I have this weird quirk of adopting the authors style in how I think, say and act. Plus I'm at work, so I'm not playing. I also have plenty of time, though I suppose I could be studying my medical text books.

Just the basics: New graphics code that allows Direct X. This is why the patch took so long. (5-6 months!) Direct X is NOT an easy thing to retro-implement.

The British got a lot of new toys, Vickers, Morris, and the imfamous Camile Bofors AAA gun.


Interestingly, you'll now get the same performance out of higher resolutions as you will lower ones.
Next thing for the Rats to work on? Well Thunder should be getting the new infantry out the door sooner rather than later, plus since Direct X Sound seems to have a habit of EATING soundblaster cards in the game, a new sound codex (Miles) will be implemented. Oh and something about full attrition, resupply, and reworked concussion damage was mentioned.
