The Lost Order of Akalabeth's Mission Statement:

The Lost Order was founded with one purpose, to create a dynamic, multi-faceted guild which would allow its members to not only enjoy individual games, but to be united with other people with the same belief system. To say the Lost Order is a guild is to sell it short. Over the years we have progressed from our original roots within the world of Ultima Online to encompass multiple games, across multiple worlds, one thing however has remained the same, our loyalty to one another. The Lost Order is a family and like most families, we at times, have miscommunications or arguments, but the true strength of the guild, our ability to analyze, adapt and overcome has kept us united during any and all periods of discontent. It is imperative that any who seeks to become a member of the Lost Order realize that above all you must be loyal and respectful to your guildmates. It is our unity, which not only makes us strong, but also makes us strive.

There are a certain set of regulations that are maintained throughout the various games, these regulations are not so much laws but a mixture of: common sense, professionalism, and ethical behavior. This includes but is not limited to: respect for any and all players and/or gaming staff, striving to increase the communities enjoyment of the game, an underlying sense of doing ‘good’ deeds, the commitment to not resorting to exploitation of system flaws or third party ‘cheats’ which would give you an unfair advantage, and above all a mature, guild orientated mindset.

The Lost Order is a guild based on diversity in any term applied. We respect that each individual has a specific style of gaming; a specific genre of gaming, which he/she may enjoy and we strive to ensure each member, reaches maximum enjoyment. However, certain anti-social behaviors shall not be tolerated these include: racism, sexism, harassment, or game specific anti-social behavior (such as mindless Pking in the realm of UO). By living up to the afore mentioned standards we ensure that the Lost Order remains the most professional, most enjoyable guild in the virtual universe we all encompass.

Important Notice:

Each chapter of LOA may have additional policies/rules based on each game’s specific mechanics. We strongly encourage you to read over this policy page (perhaps print it), then visit the chapter in which you seek to join. Review the specific chapter policies in depth before applying to the Lost Order of Akalabeth. If you have any questions about our policies whatsoever, please post to our public forums or contact our Councils, and you will receive a prompt answer from our members.

Non Dual-Guild Affiliation:

If you seek to join LOA, you cannot retain membership in another guild.  If you join LOA for our EverQuest or Shadowbane (Coming Soon) Chapter, you must then be involved with LOA for all other games that LOA is officially engaged in. Exception to the rule: If the LOA does NOT offer an official presence in a game, we allow members to join other clans/guilds that DO offer a presence in that game. If in the event that LOA does eventually enter a world that a member is involved in with another guild, we will ask that member to resign from that organization and sign up with the  Lost Order of Akalabeth.

The Meeting Place:

Though each game’s mechanics are different, one thing remains the same; the need for the Lost Order to remain unified. In order to achieve this, each game will be required, if possible, to designate a specific IG meeting place. This place shall for the most part be considered the base of operation. Here is were the guild members shall meet to discuss in game events, share adventures, and most importantly; maintain the close family structure that has aided us in developing. (E.g.: UO has the Village of the Lost, AC has Cragstone, EQ doesn’t easily support this, so it has no static IG meeting place, but instead a rotating meeting place.) As we expand further into the online universe our primary goal upon entering, a new world will be to determine a centralized starting point. This may change as time progresses and our knowledge and/or game mechanics force us to relocate, however; there will always be a specific ‘Chapter Agreed Upon’ base.

Participation:

The Lost Order has long been known for its affiliation and contributions to the online gaming world. We wish to keep this so. The only way to accomplish this task is by member participation. We expect that all members will be active participants not only in within the guild but within the community. This does not mean we expect to see you logged in to your specific game thirty-five hours a week. On the contrary, we realize that real life often throws each of us a curve from time to time. If you cannot be active on an in-game basis, we expect for you to at least remain active via the message boards, the mailing lists, or in some other form of guild approved involvement. It is requested that if you require some time away from the online world in general you inform someone of your hiatus. During this time, you will be placed into an in-active status until you once again return to your online family. Again, we shall never penalize anyone for issues that occur in real life. As a family, we expect you to take care of the issues that arise and while doing so know that we remain here always, ready to aid you in any way we can.

Trust Tax:

Regardless of which game or which combination of games you play, there will come a time when you have amassed more equipment then necessary. It is requested that any items, materials, etc that you may acquire in excess you donate to the guild. We do not enforce a weekly or monthly tax nor do we expect everyone to be able to give. It is suggested however that you help when possible. Your donations help new members and allow the guild to grow.

Becoming an Initiate:

Before you become an initiate into the Order, we require certain criteria to be met. The first of these is to fill out the appropriate chapter application. This application will be reviewed by those responsible for chapter recruiting. We urge you to take time when completing your application. This is your first impression upon the guild. Often times people blow through these forms thinking it a mere formality. Such is not the case, each application is sent to the chapter mailing list to be reviewed by all the members. Input is taken from personal meetings and the information you supply. Thusly, those that take the time to answer our questions completely find a faster and more enjoyable route to becoming a member. During the time of application review, we urge you to meet us in our IRC channel (explained below in the how to use IRC section) and get to know our members. During this period, we also expect to see you around our central area or grouped with members often so we can get a feel for your play style.

Acceptance into the Lost Order:

Once you are accepted into our family, many things will take place. You will be added to our mailing lists, and granted private access to our discussion forums.

Looting Policy:

Each chapter has specific rules regarding looting of creatures and ‘others’ killed in groups. The theory behind all the policies however is the same. Be fair. We realize everyone wants the ultimate weapon or the indestructible armor, or the billion gold pieces. We don’t wish to hinder your desire for wealth or power, we just ask that you are fair with those who participated, even if they are from outside the guild.

Using Internet Relay Chat (IRC):

It is suggested when possible that you log into IRC and chat with those members who happen to be on during the same hours as you. This will give you a chance to speak with other members, learn about changes, and increase your relationships with one another.

To connect to our server, type one of the following commands into your IRC client’s Status Window:

/server   fortress.bc.ca.mystical.net
/server  excalibur.ga.us.mystical.net

We hold daily gatherings on the Mystical.net IRC network. Our public chat room is #Akalabeth and is used to discuss all guild related issues, meet new friends, recruiting, declassified IN game communication, and more. We normally have about 5 to 25 members online, at any given time.

The chapter specific channels are:

#loa-uo -- Ultima Online Chapter
#loa-eq -- EverQuest Chapter
#loa-ac -- Asheron's Call Chapter

For our Wednesday night meetings, or emergency meetings, we have a private moderated room setup named, #Hall_of_Akalabeth. While in this room you must be granted permission to speak on a topic, this helps minimize channel traffic. ANY member can approach the podium and voice their thoughts, concerns, opinions, etc.

Using ICQ:

Most of our Guild members have ICQ. We ask that when you become a member, you add those within the guild whom you associate with to your contact list as well as the High and Grand Council members. This way, we can all communicate with each other and have a better chance of knowing who from the guild is online. ICQ can be found at http://www.mirabilis.com and is currently a free download and service. This tool is NOT required for LOA membership. It is simply recommended by our guild.

Meetings:

We have a meeting every Wednesday night at 10:00pm Eastern (GMT-5) which lasts up to one hour. The meetings are held in IRC in the private meeting room. Meeting logs are distributed via the CORE guild list, please review the log, particularly if you were unable to attend the meeting. We invite you to comment on anything brought up in the meeting log to a leader or to the CORE list.

If you have topics or ideas that you would like to see discussed in our meetings, send them to the Council via the guild "Council" mailing list.

Sometimes Freejack or Brimstone are not able to make it, when this happens, a senior member will be asked to host the meeting. Over the last year, Freejack has been the primary host of our guild meetings. Brimstone, due to changes in his "RL", has had to step away from hosting guild meetings and events.

 Leaving Our Guild:

If at any time you wish to be removed from our guild, simply email your chapter leader. We will remove your info from all areas of our site. We will not give you a hard time about leaving - things come up and we will respect your wishes. We do ask however that you try to keep your departure mature and professional. Those who depart rudely and/or callously will be banned from all communication mediums that are used by LOA. Additionally, people who leave in this nature will NOT be allowed back into LOA ever.

Negative Guild Actions:

  1. Personally insulting fellow members of the LOA.
  2. Disrespect of the Leaders, Founding Fathers or ANYONE ELSE in the LOA. We are ALL to be treated in a mature and respectful manner.
  3. Dispersal of information to other Guilds or players regarding the LOA, this refers to any internal communications, tactics or strategies.
  4. Cause harm to guild members or diplomats within the game unless it is in self-defense.
  5. Rude statements and/or foul language around people who do not appreciate it. Get to know people first, before you go running off at the mouth. :P
  6. Disregard for Chapter or Guild Policies.
  7. Improper use of in-game guild resources (i.e. tags, housing, guildstones, etc.) or out of game resources (i.e. mailing lists, forums, etc.) -- We feel that "improper" behavior is understood and/or common sense.

Inactivity:

If a member needs to step away from the guild and online gaming for awhile, we will completely understand. Members who request a "Retreat" from gaming will be removed from all lists (save the CORE list, unless absolutely necessary). We do ask that every so often, members who have gone on a "Retreat" should send a message to the guild simply saying, "Hello, I'm alive, I am doing well... see you later!". If we go a year or so without seeing/hearing from a member, we will most likely remove him/her from the guild roster and all forums of communication.  If the member seeks to rejoin the LOA, his/her reentry will not be as intense as someone who has never held LOA membership.

Interaction with Other Guilds:

Any interaction with another guild in regards to forming an alliance or a joint operation should first be referred to the Appropriate chapter’s diplomatic unit or chapter leader, or, if that is not feasible should be referred to all available members of the High Council. At no stage should a member of the guild make any promises to assist another guild or form an alliance without approval from one of those two sources.

Rule and Policy Violations:

Any member found to be in violation of guild policy and/or rules would, depending on the severity of the infraction, will be issued a warning. If the violation is severe enough, the individual will stand in judgment before the Grand Council. The strongest penalty is banishment.

Guild Mailing List:

All members are required to maintain an active status on the LOA-CORE mailing list. This list has been generated for all chapters to correspond with one another about guild and/or other community issues. In addition to the CORE list, you will be required to join the chapter list and the HC list if applicable. Information on the exact list addresses will be given to you upon your acceptance into the Order.

Reminder:

We would like to leave you with one final thought; the Lost Order’s motto is "Friendship Transcending Worlds". Take these words into your heart and you will thrive within the Order. We are a family first, a guild second. Treat others as you wish to be treated, and remember that we all do this for enjoyment. We look forward to hearing from you.
