Yes indeed, so you say the Ultima Dragons have a shard of their own? This is good. They have long been holder and guardian of the torch for the Ultima Worlds. Worlds long abandoned by their maker, the now departed Lord British and his former employer ORIGIN. Thus it shall be for UO Sosaria in time.

As some of the elder members of the Conservatory of th' Bardic Arts surely recall, I myself lived for some time on th' shard of Rising Sun , also known as Risunjuukara. It is a good land, and senseless killing was not tolerated, as they say on their web site, "PK is STRICTLY PROHIBITED in this realm, and carries severe penalty." This shard is still alive and well.

Yet every world must have at least one god to keep the world alive, and these have their failings as well as their virtues. One sad failing (back then anyway) was that a Bard had no enchantments in Rising Sun, though it was possible to stand on the corner and play annoying music for a few gold coins. I suppose. The population was low and I returned to th' Shard o' Pacific when Steel & Magic had need for a Guild Master.

After a while I shall continue my travels to the land known as Origin and also as UO2. Many, maybe all, here in the Conservatory of Bardic Arts have indicated their dismay at this new world. Even as our colleague Salkind has lamented, that UO Sosaria is not a world of Ultima. I agree in part. Yet what are we to do?

We may flee to other worlds, such as Ever Quest, or Rising Sun or The Ultimate Dragons. There are others. Yet there still one finds a world where some will benefit from the Bard's teachings. Further, how can we expect or want it differently? If we are only willing to live in the worlds of old, then we are doomed to libe in a game out of a box.

As I said to you, my friend Salkind, when we spoke through the ether the other night, these ARE worlds, and they still has the Virtues, despite all the lamenting of their loss. Where are the Virtues? Why the Virtuous guard and carry them. It is you Salkind, and other Virtuous such as the Bards of the CBA (mostly I say), the members of Steel & Magic, the members of the Guardians of Light (GoL) of Pacific, and others I have not yet met, who carry and guard and teach the Virtues.

What better calling for a Bard than to teach the Virtues far and wide? Teach the new of UO Sosaria and the young of th' RW that all is not kill or be killed. There is more, much more, than the sword and the spells of destruction. Not that I would remove one sword nor one spell nor one enchantment from any world. For without these there is no fun and if there is no fun why are we here? Yet a world does not have to be an Ultima World in order that Virtues may flourish.

Talesn of Wodclf, Bard
