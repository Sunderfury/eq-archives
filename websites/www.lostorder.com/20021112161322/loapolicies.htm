Membership:
Activity in Guild: When you join LOA, you are becoming part of an organization that is more than just "playing games". We are friends, we are associates and in some cases, we are like family. We only ask that you are involved as much as you can be, without sacrificing your RL commitments (i.e. spouse, kids, work, etc.). Some members are involved on a daily basis, while others are involved just a few times each month.
Requirements: The only requirement is that you remain on our CORE mailing list. This central list is the glue that keeps all our chapters/members communicating together. The list can be setup in two ways for you: 1.) Digest Mode, which is a collection of the days email messages that have been sent to the list, or 2.) Regular Mode, which gives you individual messages as they arrive to the list.
Cursing and Insults: At no time is it "ok" to curse at, or personally insult, a fellow member of the guild. This immature behavior often escalates a problem and leads to severely hurt feelings and overall guild moral. Cursing or insulting a fellow member will not be tolerated and will often lead to punishment and/or permanent banishment from our community. If you have a problem with a member of ours, try to work it out or seek one of our Guild Councilors/Founders/Council to help resolve the issue.
Difficult Decisions: Sometimes our guild evolves and adapts new rules or policies, often to deal with a situation that may not have existed before. The Council, Founders and some members of LOA are often consulted and work together to list the pro's and con's of a given decision. After weighing the options and possible results, a final decision is made and the policy is enacted. With a guild of 150+ members, people will both agree and disagree with the new rules or policies. We ask that all members do their best to accept and adhere to the new rules, knowing that we did our best for the GUILD overall, as opposed to a select group or chapter of members. We always strive to do things that are best for LOA overall and NEVER do anything that could hurt our guild intentionally.
w/Fellow Members:
Respect: Everyone in LOA has earned their way into our ranks. They are all good people, with talent and skill that lends itself to the reputation LOA is proud of. We ask that you remember this and do your best to work with fellow members on an equal level, respecting their play styles, time constraints and their opinions. You don't have to agree with everything you see or hear, but you should always strive to show respect for others, and for that matter, you have a right to expect the same. NOBODY, including our founders, council and veteran members are above this rule.

Networking: One of the greatest things about LOA, in my opinion, is the friendship and networking that takes place amongst our members. With over 150 members from around the world, you are most likely going to meet people who share MANY of your interests - be it gaming, music, movies, books, poetry or <insert things here>. We try to hold "Getting to Know You" mailing list events every 6 months or so. This is an effort to introduce our members to each other and hopefully spark some dialogue between each other.

True facts about LOA and Networking: Our members often hold large-scale (or small-scale) gatherings in real life. Some of our members have helped other members through difficult, emotional times (i.e. loss of job, divorce, financial problems, etc.). Some of our members have helped others get jobs in various industries (i.e. computer, gaming, etc.). Some of our members are friends with famous people, or people with connections. This can lead to unexpected things like free MLB (baseball) games, concert tickets, opera, etc. Respecting fellow members can lead to interesting friendships that will last a lifetime.
Reporting Issues: Every so often, two (or more members) have a problem with each other and can't seem to resolve it on their own. In this situation, we ask that before you blow things out of proportion or elevate the problem to a full-scale nuclear meltdown -- you speak with one of our council members, founders or our guild "Councilors". We will try to understand the issues on both sides and work with both parties to resolve, as best as possible, the problem. Remember, we should all be having fun and enjoying the guild and the games... so let's try to keep that in perspective at all times.
w/Fellow Gamers (non-LOA):

Respect: Just as respecting members of LOA is very important, it is also very important to have respect for other gamers who are not in LOA. This is a GOLDEN rule of ours, and something we take extremely serious. There is an expression that we live by: "What goes around, comes around." Through our actions and deeds with others players, we have built our reputation into one of maturity, honor, creativity and respect. Players know that LOA members are team players and trustworthy -- we will not allow any members, ever, to tarnish this image and reputation that we have worked so hard to build.

While respecting other players is a golden rule, we do not expect you to ever be treated poorly by another gamer and just 'take it'. If someone is causing you grief or frustration, we do not expect you to go out of your way for him/her. Ignore that person. Whatever you choose to do, make sure you do not curse, insult or do anything to that person that is immature and/or dishonorable. We cannot stress this enough.

For Your Information: An interesting fact that I would like to share with you: The majority of LOA members applied to our guild based on the actions of other LOA members. Through actions/deeds, the person realized that LOA members were giving, caring and generous. When it was time for them to look for a guild, they turned to LOA and sought membership. Truly, what goes around comes around... and we would like to capitalize on that.
