Posted by Cannibella on August 03, 2000 at 15:15:39:

Here is a copy of what I sent in. Please understand that I am expressing my opinion here. All my comments are ooc, however, in the game I usually role play unless I am with a group who do not role play at all.

"As you consider changes to barding remember that there are a lot of different roles for bards in the world of Britannia. I was just reading on the Conservatory Forum and I realized that there is a decided bent towards only certain aspects (PvM) of barding. Additionally, you have the complications of the two facets to deal with. Also, I see a certain prejudice expressed on the boards concerning the relative effectiveness of all the skills needed to be a bard. For a time I also felt that peacemake and entice were a waste of time. However, after trying different approaches to barding (more on the evil side...) I found out how useful they can be. I saw people refer to those who entice NPC's out of town so they can kill them referred to as twinks and grief players. Well... I would say that those who do this are hardly either as taking on NPC's does not harm other players... and is an outlet for those who want to play evil to do so without harming PCs.

I just learned about the enticement bug that allows someone to turn a pet on its owner. That does need to be fixed. But I do not agree with shutting down the skill until it is fixed.

I have not explored all the differences between barding in Trammel and Felucca. But I do dislike not being able to provoke a creature on myself. Keeping a MOB agro on myself while I provoke some creature on it is one of the most effective ways for a bard to operate.

Mostly, I bard on Felucca. I am not someone who likes to fight other people and I never have attacked someone who has not harmed me first. But I would like to mention that barding should have a place in the PvP arena as well. Not only as an offense tactic but from a defense aspect as well. If I am being attacked by a pk, I should be able to peacemake him or provoke him on something else so I can escape. Remember that for a bard, music is his weapon... and his shield.

In my opinion the reason that bard skills are so hard to raise is that skill checks are only done when you actually use the skill. Melee for example does a skill check each time you swing. Most skills are also difficulty based. It seems funny that you can get as far provoking mongbats as you can provoking elementals. However, I tried to play a bard on SP and found that you could never provoke higher monsters like orc mages no matter how good my barding skills were. That is why I stopped playing on SP... so I would hope that if you go to difficulty based skill advancement, you do not duplicate that.

Too often we forget about the strange relationship in UO of NPC's to Monsters and to PCs. In a world where NPC's roam about and are immune from attack by Monsters, they have become nothing more than some kind of animal. If they were attacked by monsters, then there would be something to defend. Right now my evil bard can entice NPC's who are wearing those lovely colored sandals out of town. She is not a violent person... and would far prefer to talk them out of the sandals... or even steal them. Unfortunately, they are also very attached to them. And the rules of the game require me to kill them in order to claim my prize....

Thank you for listening. I have been playing UO since release. Being a bard is about the only fun thing left for me."
