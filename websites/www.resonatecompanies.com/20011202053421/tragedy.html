September 12, 2001

 

Dear Resonate Client,

 

During this time of terrible tragedy, we join with you in praying for our nation and for those innocent individuals and families directly impacted by this act of terrorism. The full effect is still incomprehensible for many of us.

While times like these return us to the realization of what is really important in life, we also know that you rely on us for financial guidance and we want to be responsive to the economic questions that may enter your mind. Therefore, we have already gathered some information, which we hope will be helpful to you.

Based on the response of the European financial markets to the attack, it is projected by Nancy Kimmelman, SEI Investments chief economist, that the major US equity markets will immediately decline between 7-9% when they reopen. However, using on a market comparison to other American tragedies (Pearl Harbor, the Kennedy Assassination and the Gulf War), markets have historically recovered fairly quickly. Based on history, this could mean from a few days to a few weeks for the recovery from the initial decline. When the equity markets decline, US Treasury bonds will likely surge. However, this performance historically reverses itself as the equity markets recover.

What happens next is impossible to predict. Basically, investors have three choices at this point in time. Stay the course (assuming you have a diversified portfolio which includes stocks as the major asset class), increase bond positions to reduce overall market volatility or liquidate the portfolio. We are here to calmly help you determine which course of action is most appropriate for you. These decisions are critical because, if we sell equities and demonstrate a loss of consumer confidence and confidence in America, then the economic impact on all of us will deepen significantly.

While we realize that events of the past certainly cannot be guaranteed to predict the future, we, at Resonate, are choosing to believe in the resilience and spirit of the American people to prevail in tough times. As always, we are happy to discuss your individual situation and concerns. Simply call and request an appointment - either in person or via telephone if you prefer.

 

Sincerely,
