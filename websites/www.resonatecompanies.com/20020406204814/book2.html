The motivation for writing the book was to give women the guidance necessary to financially empower themselves.

This 192 page paperback covers important topics such as:

 

Couplehood and Parenthood: Choices that can have enormous financial components.

 

Planning for Children with Special Needs: Parents who have special needs spend more time and energy on creating a life that accomplishes those needs.

 

Career and Retirement: The passage into your employment life begins a chain of events that will shape your eventual retirement.

 

Divorce: Involves financial changes that must be contemplated within an emotional climate.

 

Widowhood: A passage no one chooses and no one is prepared for.

 

Estate Planning: Addresses a passage that none of us can avoid.

 

Philanthropy: Creating a financial plan that includes an intentional philanthropic component should not be initiated without a firm grasp of the many issues involved.
