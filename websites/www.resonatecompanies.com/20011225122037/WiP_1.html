FACT: Women are becoming an increasingly important part of our nation’s economic growth. Therefore, they can be an essential part of the success of each of our businesses.

 

 

Barbara A. Culver, CFP™, ChFC, CLU has recently created a Women’s Program to accompany the release of her second book “For Women By Women: Financial Passages”.  The book explores the various financial passages women experience throughout their lifetimes.   The Women’s Wealth and Wellness program compliments the book by identifying a series of workshops that can be designed to expand its content.  These workshops can include values-based life planning, traditional retirement and college planning, income tax and insurance planning.  They are created and conducted by you or professional advisors of your choosing.

 

Each attendee receives a 2-inch, 3-ringed binder at her initial workshop session.  The binder is divided into eight tabbed sections covering the topics described above.  Each section contains important, generic information pertinent to the tabbed topic. The participant is given additional pages for her binder as she continues to attend the workshop series. The professional advisor conducting the workshop is free to design the content of each workshop according to his/her particular style and creates the presentation to be included in the binder. .  The repeated exposure to the client through the workshop series creates multiple opportunities for the professional advisor to build trust with the attendees.

 

The binder itself becomes a valuable resource to the attendee because it helps her organize her financial life while also providing a place where all-important legal, financial and personal information is conveniently stored for her reference as well as for the reference of others.

 

In addition to extending this program to existing clients or using it to attract new clients, the not-for-profit sector offers a natural marketing opportunity as well.  “Women and Philanthropy Programs” are becoming increasingly popular today because women control more than half of the wealth in this nation.  This Women’s Wealth and Wellness program is easily transformed into a “Women and Philanthropy” program.
