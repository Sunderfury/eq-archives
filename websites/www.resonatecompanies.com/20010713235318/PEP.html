While it may be true that the only certainties are death and taxes, there are some steps that you can take to leave behind the legacy that matters most to you.  What really matters to you?  Is it family?  Is it your church?  Is it a social cause?  It really is your choice as to who should benefit from your efforts. 

ESTATE PLANNING is often thought of as being the process of examining your assets and choosing the methods that best transfer your assets to the next generation with a minimal amount of expense and maximum flexibility.  In reality, it is so much more than just saving taxes.  It is so much more than just moving “stuff” from one generation to the next.  At Culver Freeman Group, we have experts in estate planning on staff to assist you in choosing the option or options that best meet your specific needs.
 
PHILANTHROPIC ESTATE PLANNING allows you to exercise control over the ultimate disposition of your property.  Should you be fortunate enough to have a taxable estate, our society requires that some of your wealth be shared with other citizens at you death.  Many incorrectly assume that this means paying a lot of taxes.  It doesn’t have to be this way.  You can greatly reduce the amount that goes into Uncle Sam’s general fund by designating the organizations or causes that matter most to you and your family.  Our role, as your advisor, is to work with you to assist you in selecting the methods that best accomplish your desires. 
 
You don’t have to be ultra wealthy to benefit from philanthropic estate planning.  Even people of modest means can leverage their gifts through proper planning.  We are here to help.
