Making Fine plate armor

Basically, here is the steps... I’ll describe them in passing here, and in detail later in the page:

  1. Get some Large bricks of medium quality ore.
  2. Combine them in a normal forge with water to produce a Medium quality block of ore.
  3. Combine the Medium quality block of ore with water and a smithy hammer in a normal forge to produce a Medium quality folded sheet of metal.
  4. Take the resulting Medium quality folded sheet(s) of metal and combine it with Leather backing, Water, Smithy hammer, and the appropriate Plate mold to make a piece of Plate armor.

First for the ingredient recipies:

Ingredient Assembly Instructions

Medium quality block of ore

  • Combine the following ingredients in a NORMAL forge (don’t use the cultural specific forges):
    • 3 Large Bricks of medium quality ore
    • 1 Flask of water (NOT summoned)
  • and that will make 1 Medium quality block of ore.

Medium quality folded sheet of metal

  • Combine the following ingredients in a NORMAL forge (don’t use the cultural specific forges):
    • 1 Block of medium quality ore
    • 1 Flask of water (NOT summoned)
    • 1 Smithy hammer
  • and that will make 1 Medium quality folded sheet metal

Leather backing

  • Combine the following in a Sewing kit:
    • 1 Low quality pelt or skin
    • 1 Silk thread
  • and that will make 1 Leather backing

Silk thread

  • Combine the following in a Sewing kit:
    • 2 Spiderling silks
  • and that will make 1 Silk thread.

Low quality pelt

  • You need to use a Skinning knife to lower the quality of a higher quality pelt.  Following this recipe for will make a High quality pelt into a Medium quality pelt, a Medium quality pelt into a Low quality pelt, and a Low quality Pelt into a ruined pelt (not that you would want to do that!)  Combine the following in a Sewing kit:
    • 1 Low, Medium or High Quality Pelt or Skin
    • 1 Skinning Knife
  • and that will make 1 Pelt (of lesser quality)
  • Important: ONLY use pelts that have a name that states the quality. For example, a High Quality Cat Pelt is ok, a High Quality Bear skin is ok, a Grizzly Bear skin is NOT, a Lion skin is NOT, a Puma skin is NOT.  If you use one that doesn’t state the quality it WILL eat the skin, AND your skinning knife (trust me, I found out the hard way).

Plate Armor Assembly Instructions

Whew... ok... now you’ve gotten all your ingredients in order, and you’re ready to start REALLY smithing with fire.

The basic recipe is:

  • Combine in a normal forge:
    • 1 (insert size here) Plate armor mold
    • 1 Smithy hammer
    • 1 Leather backing
    • 1 Flask of water (NOT summoned)
    • 1 - 3 Medium quality folded sheets of metal
  • and that will make 1 piece of Fine plate armor

Here are the number of Medium quality folded sheets to use for each piece:

  • 1 Sheet
    • Visor (face)
    • Collar (neck)
    • Bracers
  • 2 Sheets
    • Helm
    • Pauldrons (shoulders)
    • Girdle (belt)
    • Vambraces (arms)
    • Gauntlets (hands)
    • Boots
  • 3 Sheets
    • Greaves (legs)
    • Breastplate
    • Cloak

Important things to note:

  • Don’t get confused like I did about the difference between blocks and bricks.
  • Don’t get confused like I did about the difference between Medium quality sheets of metal and Medium quality FOLDED sheets of metal. (notice a trend here? Learn from my mistakes, grasshopper. ;)  At 70-80pp per try on the 3 sheet items, it can be a VERY expensive lesson.
  • Watch your weight! Make sure you’ll be able to move once you gate/run back to the forge after smithing your bricks into blocks into folded sheets.  Those folded sheets weigh in at 15lbs each, so they WILL weigh you down.  If you truly get in trouble, and you aren’t able to move, wait until no-one is around, put all the heavy stuff in a backpack, and drop it on the ground, run a bit, then pick it up off the ground and do it all over again.
  • For casters - The best way to smith that I’ve found is bind at a forge and run to the ore merchant (I use Ak’anon personally, but others use Highhold Keep). Buy all your ore, and gate back to the forge.  Do your smithing for your sheets... combining your bricks into blocks, your blocks into sheets. Once you have your sheets, drag your heavy butt to the bank, and put all those sheets in the bank. Lather, rinse, repeat. If you want a REALLY optimized way of doing it, take molds with you when you make your sheets. Then, you can take your sheets and try to make armor with it in one fell swoop.  Unfortunately, due to failures you’ll be alot lighter on the way to the bank. Fortunately, due to failures you’ll be alot lighter on the way to the bank.  I load up on molds before I go make a bunch of sheets, making the armor as I get sheets, then fill up the rest of my bank with sheets. and do it all over again.
  • For non-casters - Well, bad news for you.  Find a caster friend to help you, or make a caster mule to help you.  Basically, I can’t think of a way to do it that won’t take 10 times longer than with a caster friend/mule.
  • To help me out with making absolutely sure that I have all the pieces in the forge, I made a chart that counts the items in the forge. For example, for greaves, I have the number 7 next to it.  This allows me to double check the item count in the forge to make sure I didn’t forget something.  At up to 80 or 90 a try for some of the items, you DON’T want to screw it up.
  • Trivials:  If anyone has any information about the trivials for Fine plate, please e-mail me. At 175, every piece of Fine plate is non-trivial.
