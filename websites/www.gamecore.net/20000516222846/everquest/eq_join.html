The LOA-EQ Initiation Program

We have over 150 members at this time, and we are not looking to expand at a rapid pace. Therefore we are cautiously monitoring who is applying to our guild and serious about the commitment involved in joining a guild such as the LOA. We embrace the phrase "quality over quantity".

The road to membership is a long one, but potentially very rewarding. It begins simply enough; get to know our members, both on our Conclave (Forum) and in-game. Issuing the command /w all lost lists all LOA members on-line at any one time. You may want to take a look at the page describing our Guide program, as our Norrathian Guides will be your best source for information about membership.

Our "initiation" phase is a two-way street. It is designed not only to ensure that LOA continues to recruit the best possible members, but also to ensure that prospective members understand who we are and what we are about.

We have recently implemented a new, automated application system. The following represent a significant change from our past policy for the Everquest faction. If you have questions about our Initiation process, or suggestions on how to improve it, please share them with Timbo, the Everquest Lord of Initiation.

Petitioner Phase

We strongly recommend getting to know several of our members, and thoroughly reviewing our web site, before completing an application for our Petitioner Program. Note that your application requires the approval of at least three Full Members to be accepted, and also that your application will expire after seven days if you have not received the requisite number of vouches. If you fail to receive three vouches the first time you apply, this does NOT mean that we don't want you to join the Order; it simply means that you need to spend some time getting to know us before re-applying.

You will receive an email notifying you of your acceptance into our Initiate phase. This represents our formal acknowledgement of your desire to join our ranks, and that you have earned the respect of at least three of our Full Members.

Initiate Phase

Once you are accepted as an Initiate, you are strongly encouraged to meet and group with as many full members as possible. Elevation to the next step of membership will require three additional "vouches" from full members (for a total of six vouches), so it is in your best interest to be as visible as possible. Group with members in-game, role-play with us, post stories and game tidbits to our Conclave… the sky's the limit! See the Commoner/Initiate page at left for further details.

Commoner Phase

After spending a minimum of 30 days as an Initiate, you will be eligible for Commoner status. If you are voted in, you will receive your "tags" at that time from a guild officer. This is your opportunity to get to know even more members, since elevation to full membership requires three additional "vouches" (for a grand total of nine). You will spend a minimum of one month as a Commoner, after which time you will be eligible for full membership. We strongly encourage Commoners to join us in our weekly mIRC meetings, so that you may gain a better impression of how our guild governs itself - see the Mission/Policies page at left for details on our weekly meetings.

Voting for Full Members occurs around the 15th of each month, so some Commoners will spend more time at this stage than others.

Full membership

Once you are accepted into our family, many things will take place. You will be granted higher security clearances in our Chat Room (Private Shrine on IRC), added to our mailing lists, given access to our "Real Life" forum, added to our Roster Pages, have the option for creating a character biography, granted voting rights, and the private guild structures will be open to you.
