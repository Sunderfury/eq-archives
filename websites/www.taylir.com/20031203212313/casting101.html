Spell Casting Mastery 101



The purpose of this page is to assist those casters out there master the casting skills with the least amount of pain possible.

Now, why is this necessary you might ask? Many casters acquire utility spells, that while not often used, are handy when you do need them. Since they are hardly used, they probably use a casting skill that is rarely practiced. Divination for Invisibility comes to mind. Now since these particular casting skills are rarely used they never really get a chance to get close to being mastered. As such, you will encounter the dreaded fizzle. In the grand scheme of things a fizzle might not be that bad, but ask any Wizard who just got the Minor Familiar spell how much that mana bar disappears when you first start to practice Conjuration. So this is going to be a down and dirty guide to using the ingame Social Key system to assist in leveling up your casting skills.

I must preface this by saying that if you have the Specialization skill, you will want to ensure that your skill is already over 50 in you desired specialazition before attemting this. Otherwise you might get stuck with a Specialization that is not beneficial to your class.

First pick a safe zone, Nexus is perfect for this. Then you need to pick the spells you are going to practice with. The lowest mana ones are the best, so you can get several of them off one right after the other without having to med too often. Below is a listing of the spells I consider to be the best to use for this, based on mana cost and availability at lower levels.

Finally you need to find out how long you need to pause between castings. The reason for figuring this out is because after you finish casting a spell, there is actually a delay preventing you from generally casting another spell. Most of the time, this delay is 2.25 seconds, though on some spells this will be different. This is different form the recast delay, the recast delay only applies to the recast of that particualr spell. The recast delay is also how long it takes the spell to be ready when you first scribe it in the book.

You can also modify this system ,combined with the new Spell Set feature, to efficiently buff a party or chaincast spells on mobs. Now fizzling in this aspect will cause some minor difficulties, but if you pay attention you should no which spell or spells did not stick.

Now, scribe your selected spells into slots 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 of your spell gems. You can even save this as a spell set and call it practice to use over and over again. Then you need to use one of your blank Social keys. Now this might look a little bit backwards, but SOE wrote it this way so this is how you have to do it. In my example I will use my Wizards spell set, as you can see I chose some very low level, low mana spells and have listed the casting time of each. To setup you Social key you will need to enter the text show in this screen capture:

A few notes about the /pause command, the number following pause is how many milliseconds of a delay you want to pause. There are 10 milliseconds in a second, so for casting fade the pause number would be 10, but there is the recast delay of 2.25 seconds to consider as well so the actual pause number would be 32.5, rounded up to 33. Also the /pause command is actually started second even though it is listed first in the social setup. Don't ask me why, ask SOE. So with one hit of the "Spell" hotkey you actually get 5 perfectly timed castings on 5 different spells, instead of click 5 different gems, or hitting 5 different hotkeys.

Hope this helps you guys/gals enjoy the game a little more.
