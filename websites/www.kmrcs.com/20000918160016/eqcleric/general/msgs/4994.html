Some people take this topic too seriously...

I would like everyone to consider this idea that
the cleric, alone among all other classes, has
some kind of "duty" to everyone else in the game.
I don't know about anyone else, but I find this
to be the most laughable, contemptible idea that
anyone has ever put to me.

This game is based upon social interaction, and,
society being what it is, that means that just
about everything in this game is driven by two
simple questions:

1) What do you have that I want?
2) What do I have that you want?

The fact that there is an economy built into the
game bears this fact out, but it is also present
in simple player-player interactions without
considering trade of money or items. For example
a cleric and warrior group together. Why is this?
The cleric asks, what does the warrior have that I
want? The warrior has the ability to melee in a
MUCH better capacity than the cleric, which means
that fighting battles becomes easier for the cleric,
hence advancing in the game becomes easier. What
does the cleric have that the warrior wants? The
cleric can heal, buff, and cast other spells that
make the warrior's downtime shorter, and in general
makes it easier for the warrior to carry out his
role. Together, they share in the profits (XP and
loot), and are more efficient than if they worked
solo.

If you insist on imposing a "duty" on a cleric to
heal, buff or resurrect without expectation of
compensation, I submit then, that:

* Warriors should kill everything in sight of any
other player, then give that player all the money
and loot from those mobs. It is their "duty" to
slay monsters and expect nothing from it beyond the
joy of battle. After all, THEY would be getting
all the experience. How selfish of them.

* Druids and Shamans should always buff and SoW
everyone they see, especially if they ask for it,
but as long as they're not in immediate danger
they should forsake all other activity to do their
"duty" and render these services for others.

* Trade skill products should always be given
away at the maker's expense in time and materials.
It is your duty to give these things away, because
without someone to demand them from you, you would
have no reason to make them.

* Likewise, all binds, buffs, teleports, and other
beneficial spell effects should be given freely
and immediately upon demand, because it is your
"duty" to ensure that others benefit from your
hard-earned skills and abilities.

Does anyone see a pattern of idiocy here? Of
COURSE no one will conform to these rules. Why
on EARTH should anyone expect a cleric to behave
differently?

My hat goes off to those clerics that sacrifice
their time and efforts to help others without
asking for or expecting payment of some kind. I
myself have spent much time offering free healing,
buffs and other spells, but it was because I FELT
LIKE IT. The minute someone tries to tell me I
have a "duty" to do it is the instant I'm off to
do other things. No one would stand on the corner
and give out their money, much less have people
tell you "it's about time, you cheapskate" while
you do it. That's the same thing that clerics
are having to put up with.
