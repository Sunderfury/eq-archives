Guide to Cazic Thule

(map from the EQ'Lizer)
Mikaela Tristram, Rathe
(edited: 8/19/1999)

Chart of Mobs/Drops
(Unbeliever, Bristlebane)

I will try to lay out Cazic Thule dungeon for you as best as I can. I have spent the last 17 levels there (since level 20), and have found no place better for both exp or loot (Yesterday, in 14 hours of hunting, I made 130 plat and was with a full group the entire time). However, one word of caution: it is very dangerous primarily due to mobs and trains, so if you are one of these folks that goes AFK a lot, you won't live very long. That having been said, let me see if I can give you a decent description of what to expect from Cazic. If I forget anything or screw something up, my apologies, and feel free to jump in with your own experiences or corrections in any errors I have made.

1. Monsters.

A. Lizards. Almost all the monsters in here are lizards, with the exception of a few, and they carry everything from 1 copper up to 4 plat plus and rusty, bronze and fs (fine steel) weapons, as well as the most coveted items of all, the rubicite armor, which I will discuss separately. Obviously, the higher level lizzies carry the best loot, but not always. It varies, although you won't find the fs weapons on the lower level lizzies. However, with a full party of level 20s you can take on the lizzies right away that carry the fs weapons. The lizzies in order of difficulty are as follows, from the easiest to the most difficult: broodling, warrior and guard (bout the same), defender (this is the one that starts carrying the fs weapons), sentinel, proselytyte, disciple, page, herald, protector, fanatic, zealot, justicar, and judicator. There are three other "special" lizzies that carry the rubicite (red) armor, but I'll discuss them later. Prosletytes, disciples, heralds, pages, justicars and judicators are casters, and by that I mean they vary in having the same great spells the clerics do (after all this IS a temple) such as wrath (oh yeah, they'll hit ya for 400 pts. with one wrath if you are low level) and then the buff/heal spells, bravery and greater heal (heals 300 hps) which they use not ONLY on themselves prior to and at the beginning of battle, but during battle as well AND on any other lizzies that you are fighting as well as them. This is one of the things that makes it so difficult....you rarely ever can pull just one lizzie, and hopefully, if you pull more than two or more, one isn't a caster. If it is, don't panic, just take the caster lizzie out first and fast. This is where wizzes come in REAL handy cause they can pop them for big dmg with their spells. At higher levels (29+), clerics should switch to the "wiz" mode if a big caster lizzie comes, and take it down FAST after stunning it with Force to stop its casting, then using wrath on it. Believe me, it will cost you less mana to take it out fast than it will cost you in healing all your tanks after the lizzie heals itself 4 or 5 times and the tanks have no hps left. The higher level lizzies are still sometimes hitting me for 64 dmg, even tho I am now level 37 with good armor.

B. Alligators The gators in here are little gators, about 1/10 the size of the caimens and crocodiles in oasis, but you ain't never been bitten by a gator til one of these takes a bite out of your arse. They are little but bite like lockjaw in oasis when you were in your teens. Don't even try to take these on til you are in your high 20s, because you can never pull just one from the tanks and sometimes one of the high level lizzies comes along. And they will attack anything that is near it, regardless of whether your group pulled it.

C. Golems So far, three golems have been found: clay, stone and steel. Golems, along with three other special lizards and one other monster, are what carry the rubicite armor. They are pretty tough critters in and of themselves, but the problem is, they are guarded by several other high level lizzies, including casters, and they ALWAYS come with it when you pull it. And the developers have located these golems in areas that aren't close to the zone, so you either have a far distance to run or have to swim out of a tank then run uptairs to the courtyard and THEN head for the zone. That's when you will be glad you are a cleric with GATE, or have a shaman/druid in your group that keeps everyone SOWd.

D. Gorillas (or Rillas, as they are affectionately called, or Magillas) These hairy guys are big, and guard the throne downstairs on the first level of the Throne Room. They are big, mean and tough and hit hard if you are under level 30.

2. Rubicite Armor
This is some of the most coveted armor AND most difficult to acquire in the game. It is red and the equivalent of plate, but is magical (although it doesn't add anything to stats) and very light. Not to mention how incredibly COOL it looks (LOL). The following monsters and their locations are what carry the rubicite armor. The problem is, these monsters spawn very infrequently (some take 3 - 4 hours between spawns), do not carry the rubi everytime (maybe one out of 4 or 5 times it spawns, and more), and are ALWAYS camped with a long list of people waiting to get to that area to get into the group and get in line for the rubi.

A. Greaves (leggins) The Taew Eew Templar lizzie carries the rubi leggins. It spawns (infrequently) on the Throne in the downstairs, or first level, of what is known as the Throne Room. It also drops the lizardscale cloak (AC8, +5 Dex).

B. Gloves The steel golem carries the rubi gloves, and the spawn rate and frequency of having the gloves is same as above for the leggins. The steel golem lives downstairs through one of the tanks you must swim down and through and then up a ladder in an area off of what is known as the gator tunnel. This used to be a relatively safe area until they made the gators aggro, so now, even mid-30s to level 40 players have to be careful because you can get mobbed by gators even if you don't pull the golem, and if you can't evac with a wiz or have gate, you are in a world of hurt trying to zone out.

C. Helmet The rubi helmet is carried by the stone golem, who spawns on a throne upstairs in the Throne Room, known as the Second Floor Throne Room. Spawn rate for golem and frequency of having the helmet is same. This is a tough area, and only groups of mid-35s should attempt it. It's tough because of the spawn that comes from downstairs, in the room itself, from a room to the left, and upstairs on third floor where the golem spawns. This room and its surrounds, with the exception of the downstairs, has all the high level lizzies, such as the zealots, heralds, prots, justicars and judicators.

D. Boots The Cazic Cenobite lizzie carries these, with same spawn rate and frequency. It spawns inside a little dip below the huge green bubble downstairs that you swim down to to get to an area to base to hunt from. It's not too tough, but it is a caster. Again, this is a spot for levels 35+, and has same high level lizzies and gators thrown in just to make it interesting.

E. Breastplate The toughest monster found so far in Cazic carries this, with same spawn and frequency. It spawns in same spot inside the big green bubble downstairs where the Cenobite lizzie spawns. It is known as the Avatar of Fear, and believe me, it is the biggest, ugliest thing in all Cazic, and will take you out in a heartbeat. If it is alone (infrequently), a group of full 35+ can do it ...the problem comes when a judicator or two shows up or spawns right after, and then stands there and buffs and heals the damn avatar while the group is trying to kill it, and at the same time starts whaling on YOU, the cleric, when you're trying to heal your tanks who are getting the crap kicked out of em by the avatar. A very dangerous monster, my friends. Beware.

F. Bracers The Taew Eew Diviner carries these, with same frequency and spawn rate. It spawns on the downstairs throne where the Templar with the greaves spawns. About the same difficulty, except for one difference: this little lizzie will CHARM and charm REAL good. Our group has almost been killed by our tank who pulled and then got charmed and started beating the crap outta all of us.  His secondary drop is the Lizardscale Mantle, +3 Int and +3 wisdom.

G. Rubicite Vambraces (Arms) The Tae Ew Archon (archaron?) drops these.  His common drop is the Lizardscale Belt, AC2, HP +10, Mana +10, All/All.  He is located at the top of the pyramid like temple which is on the other side of the maze.  Directions through the maze (ed. send in corrections, if any, please) are:

  • Enter the maze through the door in the courtyard
  • Turn left at the first crossroads
  • Follow the passage and take the right turn where you can see a room with four pillars ahead of you (2nd right turn?).
  • Go straight across this room and take the 2nd left turn.  
  • This is a long tunnel to some steps which lead into the temple area.
  • This is a very dangerous area with a lot of high level lizards (fanatics, zealots, justicars, and judicators).
  • At the top ring of the temple is a room inside.  This is where the Tae Ew Archon drops the arms.

 

3. Hunting Areas and Levels
The Cazic temple is HUGE with lots of hunting areas upstairs, downstairs, and throughout. I will try to tell you in general where they are, with appropriate levels to hunt at, but the best thing is to just go yourself and get with someone who is familiar with the dungeon.

Levels 20 - 30: You should stay either in the zone area (corridor) or the courtyard (cy) outside the double doors of the zone area. Broodlings, warriors and guards usually travel or guard this zone corridor and will attack you if you are medding. The danger with hunting in the corridor zone area is the huge number of trains that come by from folks that are zoning. You are better off to base around the perimeter of the cy to avoid these constant trains, and so your casters can med in relative safety. You should pull defenders and sentinels from what is known as the maze (named this for a good reason). The maze is through a door to the right when you enter the cy. Be careful pulling if you don't know the maze...there are many many lizzies in there who will attack you, and you will get lost easily in the corridors and die very fast. Don't go in very deep until you learn the maze.

Levels 30 - 35: You can now advance to the downstairs throne room (has med - high level lizzies with the rillas) which is through the door on the left side of the cy, or head downstairs to the gator tunnel, or pull gators to the courtyard from one of the tanks that are located up the ladder on one side of the courtyard, or up the ladder on the other side then downstairs. There is also an area known as the Arena, which is through a door and down several corridors on the opposite side of the throne room. There are the higher level lizzies here, such as the heralds, prots, zealots, fanatics, justicars and judicators, with some medium ones such as sentinels thrown in. You will have to fight your way down the corridors through sents and defenders to get to the arena. Another good area less frequented is known as the Upstairs Temple. You get to this by going through the maze. It is a square area, with higher level lizzies that spawn on the four corners, and some that are "wanderers". There are stairs up here that also lead to like 4 more levels and at the top level, a tank. You will have to fight your way through the maze to upstairs, unless you get a wiz who can inviso everyone (I highly recommend this...I do not travel the maze unless I am inviso) Once upstairs, clear the corner to the left, then move around clearing the next corner, and base in center of next hallway and pull as the corners respawn, or go up the stairs and pull from there.

Levels 35 +: You're big, bad and tough now, and with a full group the same levels, can take on anything else in cazic. But don't get cocky, because as I said, these lizzies and gators, not to mention the avatar, seldom come without their buds, so be prepared for fighting up to 4 at a time if you are in the second floor throne room, with a gator or two or three thrown in for good measure if you go downstairs to the Avatar area or steel golem area.

4. Tips on Hunting Cazic

A. Grouping. Always hunt with full group if you can. This is dangerous place. And have a well-rounded group, which is safest. Two clerics I've found in a group are great, along with someone who can SOW, two good tanks and a wiz who can nuke the bejeebers out of em. A monk or ranger with good range skills (bow) are TERRIFIC, since they have the ability to pull just one at a time. I've lived a lot longer with a good puller...a bad puller will get you killed LOTS and you'll spend all your time zoning with trains on you, that tend to make you VERY unpopular with the others hunting there.

B. Trains. Oh yeah, they are big, bad and frequent. Primarily because of the difficulty in pulling just one or two monsters. Most folks are good about yelling train tho...it's a close knit community inside Cazic and woe to the person who constantly trains without warning others and gets other groups killed. It isn't long before that person can't find anyone to hunt with. If a party zones with a train on them, the lizzies will return to the cy or stay in the corridor zone area and jump on anyone else who is around. This hurts big time when your group is itself engaged in a fight with 2 or 3 lizzies, and 3 more jump in.

C. Outside the zone. In all their great wisdom, the developers have placed Bouncers (big bad, very very fast and hard hitting ogres) who patrol outside and around the temple ramp where you need to enter, and have now started coming into the "safe" zone area (corridor) where everyone zones out to from the temple. One of these ogres can take me out in about 4 seconds flat, but they usually come into the "safe" corridor area in twosies, and with both whaling on you, have taken out over half my hps in like 1 or 2 seconds before I managed to zone back into cazic. They kill many people who have zoned out with low hps, only to be met by one of these nasty ogres. One of my friends died 4 times in that corridor in about 3 minutes...he was bound right there and got killed, and everytime he reappeared the ogres killed him again. He was NOT happy.

D. Healing. By now you have learned that when you are fighting a mob, the first heal you cast is gonna draw the mobs onto you big time. Nowhere is that more true than in Cazic. Lizzies HATE healers more than any other monster I've found, and they won't let you med during a fight, and will beat on you with a vengeance soon as you heal. Make damn sure you are in group that some can taunt or pull off you, and make sure they know that if you get hit, you will not be able to cast to keep the party alive. It is imperative that the group protect you, because you will die very quickly in a mob after you cast that first heal. Keep Root or Enstill memmed so you can at least root them until one of the tanks can pull them off you. I've also found Wave of Fear to be useful to make the whole mob run long enough for the group to zone and me to gate. ALWAYS keep gate and your invulnerable spell memmed and always SOW on you...it can mean the difference quite frequently in whether you make it out or not.

4. Conclusion
I hope I've given you sort of a thumbnail of Cazic. I know I've forgotten lots of things, so if you have any questions, post them on the board and I'll try to answer them for everyone. I have hunted Cazic almost exclusively since I was level 20 (I am level 37 now) and have found no other place that can beat it for exp and loot or making really good friends. This is where all the high level players on The Rathe levelled. I have been very fortunate in that I have been able to acquire rubi boots, gloves, bracer, greaves and helmet. BTW, the location of Cazic Thule is through the Innothule swamps. You have to swim across a short river and then follow the edge of the temple on your left around to the ramp that takes you up to the corridor that leads into the zone. The bad thing is, that with so many people in Cazic now, the PL and lag can be deadly, not to mention the constant trains. But once you get high enough and can go into those out of the way areas, the only trains you have to worry about are YOURS. Good luck to you all, and may the light of Mithaniel Marr forever keep you safe and guide you in your journeys.

Mikaela Tristram of The Rathe .
