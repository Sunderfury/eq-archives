Cleric Petition

 

We, the clerics of Norrath, have watched quietly as changes have been made throughout the land.  Some have been popular, while others have not.  We recognize and acknowledge the time, effort and trials that must be undertaken to achieve the goals of balance, playability and endurance of the game.

 

We love the diversity of the classes of Norrath and respect the uniqueness of each.  Every class has certain defining characteristics that to need to remain proprietary in order to retain the value and integration of all classes.  We also respect the need to have characters struggle for advancement.  Players cannot expect to be out slaying dragons after one day of game play. 

 

In this document, we offer our suggestions and comments on the cleric class, both as a whole and at certain levels of advancement.  While we enjoy playing clerics, we believe that there are a few areas that should be adjusted to improve the playability and viability of the class without either overpowering us or lessening the defining characteristics and role of other classes.

 

The very gods themselves have defined clerics by the following 6 characteristics:

 

1.        Supreme Healers of Norrath.   No other class should be able to match our ability to heal.

2.        Restorers of Life.   None but one so closely in touch with their god as the cleric should be able to restore life to the extent that we are capable.

3.        Masters of Destroying the Undead.  No other class should surpass the ability of a cleric to expunge these underworld denizens with the force and speed of a cleric carrying their god’s disapproval of the undead.

4.        Superior Protection.  Clerics have been given the ability to cloak ourselves with the highest protection in the land, platemail armor.  Clerics draw the attention of the mobs when healing others, and should possess the ability to adorn the best protection available.

5.        Less Effective Fighters.  Clerics are incapable of doing the damage at melee as the pure fighters and other hybrid classes.  Clerics are healers, and should not be warriors at melee.

6.        Less Effective at Destroying the Living.  Clerics are not capable of dealing damage to the living through our spells to the extent of the pure and hybrid caster classes.  Again, clerics are healers, not primary damage spell casters.

 

Having taken all of these defining characteristics of the cleric into consideration, we would like to propose a set of changes we feel will add to the playability, enjoyment and viability of our class without upsetting game balance or adversely impacting the other classes.

 

Item #1:  Stun While Invulnerable

 

This has been the source of much consternation and discussion in the cleric community.  If, while invulnerable, we are allegedly immune to being hurt or damaged in any way, how is it possible that we can be stunned?  Our line of invulnerability spells are highly useful, but invariably when the situation calls for their use, the creature(s) in question are more than capable of stunning us repeatedly and having us remain under their feet when our divine protection wears off.  We believe if the gods have gifted us with the ability to render ourselves invulnerable, then no type of harm should be able to penetrate that barrier, which includes protection against stunning blows.

 

Item #2:  Healing Spells

 

Casting times for clerics’ higher level healing spells need to be reduced.  In a difficult battle, party members are taking damage at a much faster rate than the cleric can keep them healed.  We may have the mana to heal, however, it takes entirely too long to cast the Greater, Superior, and Complete heals for us to be effective.  Reducing the casting time would not increase our healing power, e.g., the same amount of hps restored would remain unchanged, but would enhance our ability to sustain life among our party members.  We are, after all, the clerics, defined by the very gods themselves as the masters of healing.  We truly need to be able to do this in a much more efficient manner at the higher levels.

 

Item #3:  Resurrection

 

With the reductions in the death penalty, we now have less value as a class in this respect, and believe the balance needs to be restored in some way.  Restructuring the amount of experience restoration values for this line of our spells we feel would accomplish this.  The current first in the line, Revive, does nothing more than “summon” the person to their corpse, restoring no experience.  The second line, Resuscitate, restores very little of the lost exp.  And Resurrect, which is attained only at the current end of a cleric’s career and is the ultimate in this line, does not restore the amount of experience it should.  We believe each line should be changed to restore some experience at the first level with Revive, and enhance the restoration value for the remaining two.  This would restore the original value of our ability to raise the dead, which is still not unique to our class with paladins sharing our Revive spell.

 

Item #4:  Cleric Quests

 

More so than perhaps other classes, paladins and clerics need religious/guild-centric quests to follow from their home temple.  Clerical quests should exist in all temples throughout the lands.  These quests should also be capable of accomplishment without alienating a significant faction of the city guards or another city/race that renders you killed on sight.  Additionally, these quests should be designed for different levels and the specific temple/god from which they are received.  As an example, a quest item of a Robe of Mithaniel Marr could exist with the stats increasing each quest level up.  We also feel the rewards of a quest should be more commensurate with the difficulties involved in completion. Too many existing quests take either a full party of higher levels to complete or they result in a prize/reward vastly out of proportion to the time, effort and level required.

 

Item #5:  Cleric Specific Items

 

There appears to be fewer cleric-only items in the lands than exist for other classes, with the recent exception of the Plane of Fear, which is achievable only by those clerics of high level.  And even on the Fear Plane, it appears that the spawn rate of the denizen carrying the cleric-only armor as well as the frequency, with which it actually carries one of these items, is less than those of other classes.  There should be cleric-only items achievable by all levels that exist in the same amount as those for other classes.  These would include religious-type items unique to our class such as the Robe of Mithaniel Marr mentioned previously, and could be specific also to a servant of that particular god.  We believe that items of this nature would help clerics establish an identity and greatly improve the enjoyment of our play. 

 

Item #6:  Combat Against the Undead

 

Clerics should be capable of damaging the undead through their spells at least as well as other casting classes of equal level.  Slaying the undead is our charge.  We cannot compete for damaging undead the living and we cannot control the undead as pets, but putting the undead back into their grave is and should clearly be the cleric’s job.  Our spells against the undead should cast as quickly, be as mana efficient, and deal as much damage as other casters’ spells of equal level, but the fact is that they are not. We strongly agree that an enhancement to our direct damage line of spells against the undead is needed with respect to these three items of casting time, mana required and damage done. 

 

Item #7:  Combat Against Undead – Area Effects

 

As part of our defining characteristics as masters at dispatching the undead, we believe when faced with a mob of undead that we should have the ability to cast an area effect spell specifically for these creatures.  In keeping with our normal and area effect direct damage spells being much less effective against the living, we should have an area effect spell against the undead that is much more powerful.

 

Item #8:  Meditation

 

This request is made to benefit all casting classes.  We feel that meditation should be programmed as any other skill, such as melee or fishing or begging, with the ability to “hot key” and go directly to our spell book with meditation active, rather than having to go through a two-step process.

 

Item #9: Direct Damage Spells Against the Living

 

Clerics recently had their highest-level direct damage spell, Retribution, reduced in effect by 10% without what we feel is any acceptable or justifiable explanation.  Retribution is not achieved until level 44, and from that level up, the damage done by Retribution to those creatures we must fight for experience is not particularly great, even when it hits for full damage.  Its effectiveness against such creatures is also greatly reduced by the excessive length of time it takes for this spell to cast.  Additionally, there are no other direct damage spells for us between Wrath and Retribution, with Wrath becoming basically useless for the last 5 or 6 levels before a cleric can acquire Retribution.  We do not wish to be “nukers,” but feel that upon attaining the last six highest levels of our current career, we should be capable of assisting our party in offense through our spells by doing some decent damage, since we are incapable of doing any meaningful damage at melee.  Even non-pure caster classes are capable of doing a significantly greater amount of damage with their spells than we are, and at much lower levels.  From any class perspective, being able to do one thing only becomes extremely boring, and decreases enjoyment and desire to continue playing that class.  If all, we as clerics, are capable of doing is healing and meditating, it gets old quickly, especially when reaching the higher levels.  We would propose either adding an intermediate direct damage spell between Wrath and Retribution, or adding a higher direct damage spell at level 49, and restore the original damage done by Retribution.

 

Item #10:  The Hammer Line of Summon Spells

 

The Hammer series of summon spells that the gods have given to clerics is generally deemed useless by our class after level 12.  Stats for these weapons should be increased at higher levels to make them more viable and useful.

 

Item #11:  Bandages

 

We raise this issue with full awareness of previous statements regarding the summoning of bandages by other classes in their requests to you.  We do feel, however, that our inability to summon bandages, even if only useable by us is not in keeping with our role as primary healers.  We would like the ability to summon bandages, even if they are useable only by the summoner, in order to further our role as primary healers.

 

 

We wish to thank you for taking the time to read through our requests.  We understand you must receive hundreds of e-mails per week with suggestions, comments and/or complaints.  We hope that ours has been received in the spirit in which it was intended.

 

We clerics derive a great deal of enjoyment from EverQuest.  We are a different lot than most, prone to focusing on helping others and getting along rather than shouting, complaining and making threats.  We do feel, however, that it time for us, as a class, to make a statement about our role and viability as the primary healers of Norrath, and to see some positive enhancements made to our class as others have seen.

 

We thank you for creating this wonderful world for all of us to enjoy, and thank you for your time and consideration of our suggestions and requests.
