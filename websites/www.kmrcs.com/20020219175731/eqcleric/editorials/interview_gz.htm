Interview with Geoffrey Zatkin

Questions by Ballder (Fennin Ro)

 1.      Ok first off why don’t you tell us a little bit about yourself and your job at Verant so everyone can get to know the man behind the magic.

I’m Geoffrey Z.  I work at Verant Interactive as one of the Game Designers for EverQuest.  I have worked on all parts of EverQuest – Economy, Items, Quests, Population, etc.  My domain (each Game Designer generally has a domain that they are responsible for) is the Magic system.  This means that I spend a disproportionate amount of my time working with the Magic system – though I still have many other duties as well.

 

2.       What are your favorite and least favorite things about your job?

I love the creative aspect of this job – it is my job to be creative every day.  This isn’t always as easy as it sounds, but it is a lot of fun.  I really enjoy what I do.

Least favorite – well, anything you do long enough becomes work.  And this is definitely work – we put in a lot of hours. 

 

 3.       What was your vision for the cleric class and what we would mean to a group?

The Cleric has always been the armored healer, able to travel the world doing the will of her deity.  While not as combat oriented as the Paladin, her spells are an order of magnitude both more powerful and versatile. Clerics are a very well rounded class – they wield a good variety of magic, can wear plate armor (best of any caster in the game) and have decently good HP.  They have excellent Healing spells, Cures, Undead & Summoned Warding, Resurrect, Stun, Root, Fear, Pacify and AC & HP & Resistance Buffs. 

 

4.      Why would one play a cleric when druids and shaman are so much more diverse in addition to healing nearly as well?

Druids and Shamans don’t heal nearly as well as the Cleric does – at least, not past mid levels.  At low levels Clerics, Druids and Shamans all heal similar amounts of HP – the Cleric’s bonus is that she heals about 15% more HP the other two Priest classes.  The Cleric’s advantage is also that she gets her Healing spells sooner.  Past mid-level, the Cleric gets Superior Healing and Complete Healing.  This leaves the Druid and Shaman far behind on the healing curve – there is no class in EQ better suited to Healing than the Cleric.

Druids and Shamans have different areas where they are strong – but against the Cleric’s specialty, nobody comes close.

 

5.       Will religion ever play a bigger role in our spellcasting abilities?

Probably not in this incarnation of EverQuest.

 

6.       I think that generally most Clerics are pretty happy until they get to higher levels where our life is one of med/heal/med/heal/med/buff/med/ress/med/heal etc… does Verant have any future plans to help make clerics more exciting to play?

This is one of the problems with being the best healer in the EQ – people want you to heal.  We tried a lot of stuff in Beta to try to get around this problem (and I had good Beta testers – thanks guys!), but it came down to the player perception that if you were THE best healer class, you should be healing.  Clerics have very respectable Direct Damage, Great Defensive Buffs, Great Anti-Undead spells, Resurrect, Pacify, Stun, Root, etc. 

But people want them to heal.

Now personally, I adhere to the theory that a well played high level Cleric can do things other then heal.  Stun and Root are both good in combat – as is a Mace (extra damage never hurts, and most monsters don’t go after the Cleric for swinging her mace every once in a while).  However, a lot of players feel that if a Cleric isn’t meditating through combat, she is not doing their job.  We have heard suggestions that we shouldn’t let Clerics meditate while there is a fight nearby, or make monsters run across the room to strike a meditating caster (“so that my party won’t make me meditate during combat”).   We don’t, however, want to mandate that Clerics adhere to a certain style of play.  Clerics have the option to or not to meditate during combat right.

What we can not do is up the rate at which mana comes back during mediation, nor can we reduce the cost of healing spells.  These things were balanced during Beta and will not be changing – they work very well.

So, in short, no – we don’t have immediate plans for this.

 

7.       You must have heard this one by now, but why are we able to be stunned while divine aura is up and will this ever be changed?

Divine Aura protects from physical damage.  It does not protect from stun.  This goes along with our philosophy of having no “get-out-of-jail-free” cards in EQ.  Divine Aura is a nice way to protect yourself if things get bad – and as an added bonus, monsters tend to leave you alone once they figure out that they can’t hit you.  There is, however, a built in weakness – you can be stunned.

 

8.       The resurrect line of spells was recently changed again that has had a major impact on clerics in addition to the lowering of lost experience before.  Now we must resurrect a person, and then spend even more mana healing them.  This basically means that a cleric has to sit and med an extremely long time when they resurrect anyone, why was this done and will we get any type of compensation for this? 

We change spells (and other things) when we find that they are broken, or being used in ways that we think unbalanced the game.  Occasionally we change things because the players are using them in ways that we do not want in our game.

Let me give an example from Beta.  During Beta, running moved you pretty fast.  If you jumped while you ran, it gave you a tiny forward boost – a running jump.  This took no additional endurance.  After a while, ever player was jumping all the time (a.k.a. the jump-run).  People jump-ran through the cities.  People jump-ran through the countryside.  People jump-ran through dungeons.

It looked pretty silly.

So we held a meeting, and decided that we were going to fix this by making jumping take some endurance.  That way, a person would only be able to jump a few times before having to stop and rest.  When we put this in the Beta testers hated it – they were used to the tiny boost in speed, and used to being able to outrun monsters by jump-running away from them.  An unintentional bit of code had become a feature.

Now, because this was Beta, we had no problem with adjusting the style of play that the Beta Testers had gotten used to.  If we hadn’t done this, today you would see bouncing players running through every city – and I’m pretty sure everybody would agree this would look very silly.

This is a good example of players doing something in EQ that we did not intend.  We fixed it, leaving a more visually pleasing game.

Now, the reason that we fixed Resurrect was that people were using it to Kamikaze certain types of mobs – usually Dragons.  A big party would attack a Dragon.  Anybody who died would get Resurrected, putting them immediately into the fight again at full health and full mana.  Magi types would mana dump and then get the Dragon to kill them so that they could come back with full mana.  Warrior types would streak the Dragon and come back with full HP.  There was no fear of death, and players could with win without tactics – as long as there were Clerics, players could throw bodies at big monsters indefinitely until the monster finally died from overgorging on player bodies.

That was pretty silly too.

So we made it that players come back in with less HP and less Mana.  This still lets people lemming their bodies at big monsters if they want to – but it does not reward people for this style of play.

 

9.       Clerics seem to have to meditate a lot more than any other type of caster.   I think it is safe to say that we use more mana than anyone, is there any plans to help us recover mana or give us more mana in the future so that we don’t have to spend as much time with our heads in the spellbook and can enjoy the game more?

All casting classes can use up their mana fairly quickly.  Clerics do not use any more then a Wizard or an Enchanter, or any other casting class.  Each class has situations where they can go from full to empty very quickly.  The time where the Cleric tends to dump the most mana is combat – or just after.  The basic problem was stated above – Clerics are specialists – they do healing better then any other class.  Because of this, people want them to heal.  If we took out the Cleric’s healing spells (just an example, don’t panic), I think that Clerics would be pleasantly surprised at their effectiveness in doing other combat related casting.

This being said, we are not going to make Cleric healing spell cost less mana or heal more HP.  The amounts that they heal have been well tested.

 

10.   With specialize finally being implemented I think most people thought that not only would these types of spells be cheaper for us, but that they would be faster as well.  Is there any plans to change spellcasting so that we can improve our casting times through a skill?   

Specialization makes your spells take less mana and have a reduced chance of fizzling.  Specialization will not lower casting times – it was not designed to do this.  We did a lot of Beta testing work on balancing casting times of spells vs. each other and melee attacks, and are not going to be changing this.

 

11.    I think most players thought that as we got to higher levels we would get better and better at spellcasting and be able to cast spells faster at lvl 50 than at lvl 5 (Due to increased channeling skills.)  However the opposite of this seems to be true right now.  Why is this and are there any plans to change this? 

Spells do go faster – but higher level spells also have longer casting times.  There are occasions when it is more practical to use lower level spells – at least, if you absolutely NEED to cast a spell quickly.  Try casting a lower level spell once you have hit a much higher level – see how fast it goes.

Channeling also makes it so that you have the possibility of recovering a spell after a distraction – say getting hit.  Channeling is a great skill – faster casting times AND the ability to recover from an interruption.

12.     Will a players skill at spellcasting ever get good enough that he/she never fizzles?  Or that he/she can cast while running/walking/moving?

There is always a 5% chance that a spell will fizzle.  There is also always a 5% chance that your spell will work, even if your skill in that area negligible.  Channeling will let you get moved and sometimes recover to cast the spell (say from getting hit).  It will not let you move and cast.

 

13.    Clerics were touted as the master undead slayers.  However other classes can still out nuke clerics vs. undead monsters both casting faster and doing more damage, why is this and will it ever change?  Will we ever get an undead nuke near the strength of Ice Comet?

Clerics were given very efficient anti-undead spells, as were Necromancers.  I think it is players who have hyped their abilities as “master undead slayers” – not that they far from it.

Banish Undead – the level 44 Cleric anti-undead spell does 585 damage for 225 mana – that is a ratio of 2.6 points of damage for every point of mana spent.

Ice Comet – the level 49 Wizard direct damage spell does 1120 damage for 400 mana – that is a ratio of 2.8 points of damage for every point of mana spent.

This means that, verses undead, Clerics are very close to the “mana to damage” efficiency of a Wizard’s Ice Comet – the class that does the best direct damage with the best direct damage spell in the game.

 

14.    Complete heal is so slow that it is not really usable in combat situations.  Super heal is also very slow but fast enough that u can usually use it on combat situations.  Is there any plans for another heal spell between super heal (250 mana a cast) and complete heal (400 mana a cast)?

Superior Healing was meant for combat, Complete Healing was not.  At high levels Complete Healing is SO mana efficient that it needed to have a significant drawback – in this case, the long casting time.  At very high levels some players (especially if they have been HP buffed) have enough HP that Complete Healing can be cast in combat.  This situation aside, Superior Healing is the best of the quicker healing spells – and is powerful enough that only the Cleric gets it.

 

15.    As masters of healing why were the Clerics not given any regeneration spells like shaman/druids have?

The Clerics are masters of healing – but there are several ways in which healing can be done.  It is probably better to define Clerics as the masters of “quick” healing in the same way that Wizards are masters of “quick” damage.  Clerics don’t get Regeneration, just as Wizards don’t get DoT spells.  There are seven types of pure spellcasters – we needed classes with overlapping but different abilities.  I therefore split the Quick Healing and Quick Damage specialty to certain classes, leaving the slower Healing and Damage to other classes. 

 

16.    What is the purpose of the reckless strength line of spells?  Why weren’t we given a long duration normal strength buff – it would be a lot more useful and could be done with the rest of our buffing prior to combat?

When I designed their spell list, Clerics weren’t slotted to get ability score buffing spells.  That was the specialty of the Shaman, though certain other classes (Druid, Enchanter) have some spells of this type.  Working my way though their spell list, I decided that I wanted to add a different type of Strength spell to the Clerics.  Giving Clerics the best Add HP spell, the Best AC spells AND good other ability score buffing spells would have been too powerful.

For those who don’t know, Reckless Strength adds 20 points of strength.  Every six seconds after that, you lose a point of strength, until you are 10 points below where you started out.  The spell then ends, and you return to your normal strength.

Now, I personally like the Reckless Strength series.  If you cast it on yourself before combat, you can always end the spell before the negative strength kicks in.  Timed well, it can be a nice pre-combat spell (assuming that you don’t have a Shaman with you) since most fights are done in less then two minutes.

 

17.    Atone seems to have been stuck in all by itself later in a clerics level life.  It does not seem to work on mobs near your level but only low level green mobs.  Will there ever be anymore of this line of spells added across the whole spell list or is it just a teaser?

Atone (similar to the Enchanter’s Memory Blur) is a spell that makes that monster forget who it is angry at.  It is a nice spell when you get it – especially verses guards and other such.  There is a saving throw verses the spell.  Up until about a level 35 mob, it works very well (monsters get a very poor saving throw vs. Atone).  After that, the monsters get better saving throws.  This was done for reasons of game balance – effecting the Enchanters and Clerics both. 

During Beta we found that causing high level monsters forget who they were mad at meant that players never died.  If the fight was going poorly, somebody just cast Atone.  While this IS what the spell was intended for, it worked too well.  We therefore put in the better monster saves.  It is still an extremely effective spell at high levels – but it is not as powerful as it is at low levels.

I guess this is just a long way of saying that no, we won’t be putting in more effective versions for the Cleric.  The Enchanter gets a better version (this is one of the Enchanters areas of specialty), but even that one in not ultra powerful at high levels.  The spell is just too unbalancing.

 

18.    Priest classes use wisdom in order to determine their amount of mana.  Is there ever going to be a way that a player can increase his/her  damage/healing/buffing or whatever spell effects by raising his/her main spell casting attribute?  For example a cleric at say level 29 uses wrath for a DD and  with 150 wisdom being able to do more damage per spell than a cleric with 100 wisdom?

Nope.  The Cleric with the 150 Wisdom already has more mana then the Cleric with 100 Wisdom.  This means that they can cast the spell more often, with less chance of fizzling.  Letting them do more damage or grant a better effect for having a higher Wisdom would make Wisdom too powerful of an ability score – it is already a very desirable attribute.

 

19.  Currently, clerics have DD verses all mobs at level’s 14, 29 and 44.  Why so long inbetween these and are there any plans in the future to say redo these so that we can have one at 29, 39 and 49?  This would seem to help Clerics at all level ranges the most.  Clerics offensive DD spells seem to be really underpowered at high levels considering that this is really our only offense compared to other classes who have multiple forms of good offense at high levels.

It is very intentional that Clerics get very little Direct Damage – damage dealing is not one of the primary abilities of a Cleric.  This being said, they use the same Mana to Damage ratio as Magicians, Necromancers, Shamans, and Enchanters.  The advantage that some of these classes have over the Cleric is that they get DD spells more often.  Mana to Damage ratios are applied to the Rank of the spell – a Rank 6 spell has a better mana to damage ratio then a Rank 4 spell.

A lot of people think that we should give all classes DD spell at every Rank – the problem with this is that it blurs the distinction between the classes.  One of the reasons that a Wizard is so powerful is that she gets a new DD spell at every rank.  This, in conjunction with her better mana to damage ratio, makes her a very solid DD caster.  Giving every class DD at every level makes all classes more like Wizards – something we are trying to avoid.  Each of our seven primary spell casting classes has a distinct flavor and style of play.  You don’t play a Cleric the same way that you would play an Enchanter or a Wizard.

 

20.    A lot of times a player will be casting a spell and the casting time window appears and the player is hit or interrupted yet it does not say so and the spell continues on but doesn’t go off.  I believe when you are interrupted it should stop the casting right then so that you can start over again.  Is this a bug or is this how its supposed to be? 

This is part of the spell design – it is a disadvantage of being knocked out of casting.  Most fantasy games have some version of this – In AD&D you can’t cast a spell for the rest of the round.  In EQ, you have to wait a few seconds before you cast your next spell.

 

21.    The lull/calm/pacify line of spells seem to be very high risk and seldom used spells.  Are there any plans to change these in the future?

Actually, I see a lot of people using these spells.  They are a great pre-combat spell, and they add a lot to the ability of a low level to solo.

The Pacify series makes it so that the monster (for the duration of the spell) will not respond to calls for help or initiate combat.  If the monster is already in combat, this spell will have no effect.  So if you see a camp of three Gnolls, you can pacify two of them and then attack the third.  Parties love this spell – they can all group up on one monster and kill it quickly without taking the damage from the other two.

At higher levels, like the Atone spell, monsters get a better save verses this effect.  This was done because of how powerful the spell turned out to be during Beta.  At mid to high levels, you probably shouldn’t be casting it if you can’t take everything in the group – but the 50 to 70% of the time that it works, you can take one to two monsters out of a three monster combat.  Talk about mana well spent.

 

22.    Clerics usually have to buff the whole party with many of their spells, any group casts of our current spells coming in the future? 

Each class tends to get 1 or 2 party effect spells, with except for the Bard who tends to get nothing but.  The Cleric gets two group healing spells – they therefore probably won’t get additional party buffs.  The specialty of the Cleric is healing – hence the party healing spells.

I get a lot of good suggestions about new ideas for spells.  The reality is that we can only give each class so many spells before they become overpowered, or before they become a carbon copy of another class.  One of the things that makes the Bard unique is that a majority of her songs effect all members of her party.  Giving everybody lots of party effecting songs makes the Bard less unique – the same way that giving everybody Resurrect would make the Cleric less unique.

 

23.    Any chances of getting a locate corpse or summon corpse spell for clerics in the future?

No for right now – if we did a “summon corpse” spell, it would go to the Necromancer.  The “locate corpse” spell I have though about for a while, and we might add to the Cleric at some point – just not right now.

 

24.    If you could go back in time to production and change one thing about the cleric class what would it be?

I like the way that Clerics turned out – they are a well-balanced, fun and powerful class.  If we could think of a way to get Clerics into the action more and staring at their meditation screen less, I would have loved to implement it.  The problems with this were mentioned in the answer to the question dealing with Clerics and Healing – I won’t repeat it here.  I would probably also restrict Resurrection a lot more – certain ways that players are using it now are pretty silly.  Other then that, I think we did a pretty good job with Clerics.

 

25.    Verant seems to have done a lot of tweaks/fixes/upgrades to other classes since the game came out.  I think I along with every other cleric have patiently waited for some to come our way yet we have seen very few except for retribution and resurrect downgrades.  Why is this and do we have anything to look forward to in the future?

Class tweaking is not always a desirable thing.  Sometimes a class not receiving attention is better.

That being said, I tweak spells to help maintain the balance of the game.  This is a dynamic world and an evolving game – if a certain class seems underpowered or too powerful, they get looked at.  Only if we feel that it would positively effect the game do things get changed. 

A good example would be the change a month or two ago in DoT (Damage over Time) spells.  Across the board DoT spells had their duration lowered and their damage upped.  This addressed the issue with Druids, Shamans and Necromancers not casting their DoT spells in group combat situations.  With the change, DoT spells did much more damage in a shorter period of time, meaning that it was advantageous to cast a DoT spell at the beginning of combat.  Before this, when a poison spell would take two minutes to deal its full damage (as opposed to the 40 or so seconds now), the creature would often die before the full damage was dealt.  Now, there is a good chance that the creature will take the full damage from the spell.

This makes it more useful.

The DoT changes have had a very beneficial impacts on the game (especially now that we have fixed the DoT corpse disappearing bug).  It made the classes that cast them into better party classes – more of their spells could be used in a party situation.  This was particularly beneficial for the Necromancer, who at release was not overly situated towards a group situation.

Back to Clerics - Clerics have always been fairly well balanced.  The two changes you noted both were specific cases. 

In the Resurrect case, the Cleric was not really hurt because no other class can cast Resurrect.  They were never in competition with another class for this ability.  If we had lowered the Clerics ability to heal (without lowering the abilities of other classes to heal) then the Cleric would have been hurt – something that they were good at had been lowered in relation to how well other classes did it.

In the case of Retribution, there was a data error – a 7 had been entered instead of a 1 in one slot of my spell database.  Because of that, Clerics had a better mana to damage ratio with Retribution then any other class had with Direct Damage spells.  Now, Clerics are good at many things, but they have never been known for their ability to sling Direct Damage.  This error was found it was corrected – the same way it would have been for any other class.  Leaving Clerics with better DD at high levels would have made them too powerful in relation to other classes – something we try to avoid whenever possible.  All classes are balanced against each other – we don’t want any one class to be the “uber” class.

I am looking at a few things to add to the Cleric.  I am not sure if we will add them any time soon – most classes are considered “done” as far as their spell selection is involved.  But we will continue to tweak if we find errors or inconsistencies.

 

26     Will other classes every receive clerics resurrect line or heals above greater healing?

There are no plans right now to give other classes Superior Healing or Resurrect spells (and don’t think I’m not asked). 

 

27     What can clerics look forward to in the new expansion?

Can’t really say much right now.  Look for lots of beautiful new zones, wicked new monsters, more items, more spells and more quests.  This is an expansion of what we already have – and from the little bits of it that I have seen so far (only the artists are working on the expansion pack right now – everybody else is still working on EverQuest), it is going to be pretty cool.  I’m pretty excited about it myself.

 

28     New Spell: Divine Intervention, this spells is like a divine aura that we can cast on any other single player, this could be useful for those times when a person is getting hammered hard and the cleric has no chance of getting a heal off in time to save them but we could get a aura off on them, what do you think?

It is possible.  Can’t say more then that right now – but IF we ever put something like this in, it would be very similar to Divine Aura – and probably level 49.
