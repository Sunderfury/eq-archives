I play all 3 priest classes and a cleric is the last person
I would want as a low level warrior (at high levels a cleric would
be my number one want)

1) SOW makes death VERY unlikely. If I can break away at 2 bubbles and
the enemy can't catch my I can zone out and not lose exp.

2) In a group if you kill something quickly then healing is purely an after
battle affair. So a shaman with his DOT's and debuffs (he gets some of the same
speed affecting spells as a cleric) or a Druid with his DD's and Damage shields is
going to make my levelling much easier than a cleric.

3) Up until level 30 your can avoid fighting casters for the most part. Without casters providing a large
dump of damage then during combat healing is not as vital as it is when facing high level caster MOB's.

There is a reason why they sayShaman and Druids are much better fighters. Its not because they
are better at melee (all priest classes are the same, excepting racial differences) but that the DOT's,
speed affecting spells, and dmg shields make a huge difference. If you have a druid buddy have him
cast a damage shield on you before take on a MOB you would normally not be able to melee, cast a DOT on the mob and then
SOW you so that you could run far enough away so that you would never have to worry about your DD being interrupted.

Of the 4 abilities that aid in shortening a combat or running away:
(DOT, Debuffs, SOW, and Dmg Shields) a cleric has none and a Shaman or Druid has 3 of the 4...

you do the math...

My primary character is my cleric because I prefer playing as a Dark Elf.
but if a DE could be a druid i'd be there in a sec...
