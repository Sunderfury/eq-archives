I have a lot of sympathy, and I agree that 2 hours
is not enough time. I still think a limit of some
kind is an effective method of curbing some of the
problems that have been attributed to resses,
such as piles of corpses.

: Exp is not a side effect of these spells. The resurection line of spells is for clerics to bring people back to life. As there is no perminant death in this game exp is resored instead.

This is an important thing to note. And it also
makes it a little more reasonable that they would
put a time limit on it. Hey, you don't get the
resurrect in time? No problem. You just don't
get the XP back... maybe they should tie the XP
regain to an exponential function based on time...
with a fairly steep decay. You might still get
SOME XP back in a day, but the most XP would be
regained anywhere from the moment of death (almost
100% regain if a cleric is on the spot and casts
immediately after death) up to a couple hours
later, when you might still get 50%, and after
that it would become considerably less. If you
haven't done it within 24 hours of real time, then
no experience, although your corpse is still there
for you to loot.

: It was nurfed becouse people were looting and dragging there corpses back to the zone cousing lag, either to get exp reses or use as a teleport.

: This hack does not cure people using it as a teleport. It does certainly help reduce lag on corpses (For those that arn't after a corpse taxi service).

I'm curious... I've seen people post that they've
cast the spell after the 2 hours was up, and they
got a message that the spell didn't work... does
that mean there was no XP regain, or does it also
stop the teleport effect as well?

: The cost of this is that is yet again nurfes the ability of people grouped up to use the spell as it was intended.

Yes, I agree. If you die in an easily accessible
area, you should be able to get to your corpse for
a res from a group member with relative ease. If
you have to "hire" a cleric for the res, the
requisite travel time for the cleric factors in,
of course. If, however, you are DEEP in a dangerous
area, it is academic that retrieving and resurrecting
your corpse will be difficult. That's the risk
you take being in a place like that. Rather than
complain about the time limit, exercise some
caution, and take into consideration the possible
time to retrieve a corpse BEFORE you pick a spot
to hunt.

: Surley a simpler more realistic and less damaging solution would have been dont exp res the corpse after it has been looted.

Even better, but what happens if you start looting
your corpse, and then a high-level cleric friend
gets online and can now resurrect you? Too late.
Too many people would scream about that. I would
posit that if you are able to loot your corpse,
you only want the XP regain anyway, which is, to
me, a side-effect of resurrect, not the main
purpose (which is to return you quickly to your
corpse). It's a boon for the highest level clerics
to ALSO be able to restore some of the lost XP.
