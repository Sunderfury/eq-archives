October 14

Message board software, one more time

You may have noticed that a test board using the Ultimate BBS package was installed.  The Ultimate BBS package is very nice, it has a lot of features in the licensed version, creating forums is brainless, all in all a nice package.  It just has this one little quirk: the layout of the messages is really odd.  I personally like threaded messages.  My favorite board (appearance wise) is that used by the Crossroads site (Britain, Norrath).  My second favorite is the EQ Vault, excepting the fact that it barely works.

Here's my question: does anybody know of a package that supports both threaded messages and the kind that the Ultimate BBS package uses?  I'd love to use something that gives a user-selectable appearance.

Second question:  how many forums do we want?  Should we split up the board into spells, quests, rants, and general?  Or just one grab bag?

Your input is welcome as always.

"An update from your sponsor" 

This week has been a mixed bag for this site.  

On one hand, Verant has added a slew of new quests to the game, including a bunch for our class.  Whether you like the quests and armor or not, I think Verant deserves our thanks for making a positive change in the game.  While I am a little sad that I'll never have the chance to camp for Rubicite (our group certainly had it's opportunities), I feel that the quests are a positive step towards reducing the camping. Anything that Verant can implement in their software to reduce camping (and thereby not have to enforce with GMs) is something to be applauded.

On one hand, our board has been "discovered" by a less than desirable clientele.  By less desirable I do not mean players of classes other than clerics who come to share their viewpoint on the cleric class.  Player viewpoints are always welcome as they often give us another perspective.  Instead, I am referring to those players who insist on making inflammatory posts on the cleric class, those who make insults against the class or other posters, and those who bring up topics (sexual preferences, hate groups such as Nazis, etc) that are totally inappropriate for a hobby message board.

The message board is a place for players to share ideas about the cleric class specifically and the Everquest game in general.  Off topic posts are welcome, as long as they are of a nature that they are suitable for young children to read.  This is not a place to discuss adult themes (i.e. R rated topics), sexual practices, politics, hate groups, or your horrible childhood.  If the word cannot be said on television (I mean network TV, not cable) leave it out.  Do not make direct insults against other posters.  If you disagree with someone, restrict your comments to the ideas, not the person.   This board is a non-confrontational place to discuss Everquest.  Period.

End of Board Etiquette 101.

October 1

Petition - Final Word

I recently concluded swapping e-mails with GZ on the cleric's petition from August, and here is the final word on the items point-by-point.  For sake of brevity and confidentiality, I'm going to put the answers and not his complete text. 

Item #1: Stun while invulnerable:

  • Removal of Stuns: Sorry, it is there for purposes of game balance.  We are not going to remove this.
  • Beneficial Spells: Again, intentional.  While there has been some internal debate, and we *might*, again *might* change it, but don't get your hopes up.

Item #2: Healing Spells (faster casting times): No

Item #3: Resurrection (slight XP restoration at 29, slight increases on the other two): No

Item #4: Cleric Quests and #5 Cleric Specific Items: Yes and Yes

Item #6: Combat Against the Undead: No (more of a hey, you guys already rock)

Item #7: Undead AoE spells: No

Item #8: Meditation Key: Yes (sort of)

Item #9: Direct Damage spells against the living: No

Item #10: The Hammer line of spells: If they get bored ;P

Item #11: Summon Bandages: No

My thoughts? Many of these were dead on arrival and never had a chance (#2, #6, #9, #11).  I think #7 was still ok, albeit not real important (just a toy).  I personally wanted #1 and #8 (got 1/2) and was glad to see #4 and #5 (of course, those are no-brainers that every class wants and is one of Verant's prime focuses for the game).  

 Message Board

Now that I'm not pushing petitions or anything contentious we get very few flames on the board, but I saw one concerning the message board.  While I can't say I was enthralled with the person's  presentation, I can sympathize with their feelings about the board.  For those of you who do not access it with a T3 I suspect it's slow.  Also, the script is not the latest in technology.  Here are my thoughts on the subject:

  • I hate the EZBoard format, so unless I get my hands on a script that supports both don't expect to see it.  My complaint about it is I cannot tell at a glance who is responding to who. 
  • For some reason, I had a lot of trouble installing RPGBoard.  Our guild's website uses it, and I love the way the board works there, but I had trouble getting it to work well here.  Since I am now admin of that site also, I plan on snagging the script they have and then hand-modifying it to see if I can get it working here.  If I can make it work here that's what I will implement.
  • I realize I'm due to archive the current board; expect it to be done this weekend.  But remember, as usual, I retain the right to procrastinate; playing the game comes first =).
  • A trick I use when reading messages is I open a new window for every thread I read.  I use this technique on practically every board I visit.  It works for me; it might help some of you out.
  • Finally, the quality of a board is not determined by the script it runs on, but rather by the value of the information received/imparted.  There are a number of excellent posters here who make this an great place to gain information on the cleric class.
