A battle is not a foregone conclusion. If your taking on anything but
easy kills (in which case why are you grouping to begin with) then sometimes
things go wrong. Your warrior misses for 5 rounds in a row. you get 3 resists in a row
on your DD spell, whatever. In this case a normally predictable fight becomes a nailbiter.
This is when sow, (or gate, but your cleric can't gate you can he) really helps out.

You also mentioned that DOT's didn't have time to really affect a battle. Obviously you never team
with a necro or shaman (whose DOT's are about on par with each other, druid ranks 2nd). The druid more
than compensates for his lesser DOT's with a dmg shield and his DD which is better than either of them.
The last 2 changes have decreased the total time a DOT runs and added a tick of dmg.
DOT's work best when a tank is tanking for you now. I do agree that a shaman is a better partner than a druid
for 2 reasons
1) Better Tank due to racial abilities.
2) Buffs (although the effect is limited for some till after level20)

but either one is better than a cleric. Especially at the levels where the healing is tied.

again this all changes at high levels when the damage done IN battle requires healing.
