I play a level 22 ranger, 12 cleric, and 13 wizard. I'm not sure my comments are meaningful above level 30, but here they are:

Some tanks do feel better when their casters melee. It is a "boy's club" kind of mentality that I'm surprised you found at level 33. "Hey man, why didn't you melee?" I admit that when I play my ranger I do like it when my cleric will melee or burn a little bit. They need to stay out of trouble, though! A cleric is most effective when they are healing.

I like a cleric who will melee when they have a full tank of mana. That is what I do when I play my cleric - wait for the tank to build up some hate, then do a /assist on the tank. If they don't have a full tank of mana, then go ahead and med.

I see my cleric's melee starting to drop off even at level 12 though - I don't think a cleric is a warrior at all. My dwarven cleric is a tough little bugger, but sorry, he is not a paladin or warrior.

I like a cleric who burns in a limited and thoughtful way. Finish a runner, take down a caster. Just don't get the mob pounding on you - it is the tank's job get their head pounded.

I think the group you were in was _Wrong_. Their fundamental problems were:
1) Thinking that a caster (any caster, including a cleric) can cast while getting pounded on.
2) Thinking that a cleric's burn spells are more mana efficient than their healing spells.

I wish you better luck in the future, and think you have a better idea of how to play your role than your group did... Oh, and while Erudite clerics are rather tall and ugly, I think they can make fine healers.

Beril, dwarven cleric of The Rathe
