With the addition of the new armor, shield, and weapon, I'm a tank again! For sacrificing 15 wisdom I've gained a huge amount of AC, dex, agility, and strength.

Last night we went down into Lower Guk and I was testing out my new stuff. The monsters couldn't hit me! They were missing 90% of the time and when they hit, the only did about 20 or so damage. With an AC of 625, it's actually higher than monks I know of the same level. I was tanking everything, even the giant ice boned skeletons who can double hit for 70.

The tanks were having trouble hitting some monsters like Vampire Bats but a lot of times I would get a hit off. So they would whiff 2 or 3 time out 4 attacks and WAM I would hit too. That means I was doing about 25 to their 30 to 50 damage. Not so bad considering that I don't have double attack, dual wield, or weapon skill bonus. The monk actually asked me to come over and finish off a bat that just wouldn't die by meleeing it!

I was using Expel left and right to test out the armor and just having a ton of fun. I never even noticed the wisdom loss! The other cleric was primary healer so I could test and he was doing fine. I didn't realize until later that he only had 114 wisdom! He almost always had enough but he did spend most of his time medding. I did have to stop blasting at about 2 bubbles to make sure we were safe and I had enough for heals.

I learned something that night. I believe it was in Ghost in the Shell that said "To much specialization is a slow death. We become too predictable." If you concentrate on wisdom, of course you're gonna suck when fighting! You turn yourself into a portable bandaid, not your class. It's not important how much mana you have, it's important to have ENOUGH mana. Big difference.

Now, I'm not going to turn into a full blasting, fighting, tanking wizard, but you better believe I'm the Combat Medic!

Stop letting the numbers and other people dictate who you are and go be who you want to be.

Let's face it; the cleric is a jack of all trades type of class. The player's mind set and the composition of the party often dictate the tactics and equipment used. The type of mobs being encountered also may affect the clerics choice of equipment/spells.

My most successful parties have had me playing the roll of a primary healer to a balanced group of tanks and casters More mana is always desired, but the cleric can expect a mob to make an end run around the tanks every once in a while. Don't leave your armor at home.

I have also taken the roll of secondary healer when another cleric was available. My tasks in this situation include buffing, rooting, healing after the battle, or during a pitched battle when the primary healer runs out of mana and has to meditate. High mana is always good, but extra AC is nice since fighting early on is often prudent.

A third roll I have seen is that of a Nuker/Healer. This is only done when the party is Tank heavy. Give me a very high Wisdom over AC in this situation. Buffs, roots, and heals often leave a cleric reading his book.

The obvious forth roll is that of the combat healer. The cleric tanks, and even pulls, for a caster heavy party. This is my least favorite role due to the poor melee potential of the cleric. Gimme the AC when in this roll. The casters better be big guns because it is hard to cast heal when the combat healer is being beat upon.

The last roll of a cleric is that of the solo cleric. The solo cleric has to blend and balance all that I've mentioned above, including AC and Wisdom.

I guess my point is this: Every equipment/spell selection made is situational. Think about what you are doing, and then carry it out.

I've been trying to pound the idea of not overbalancing one stat to all manner of druids. Some get it, some don't and just go on and on about how much more wisdom they have. There is something wrong with their argument when two equal level druids duel each other and the one who has added to his STR, AGI, DEX, and kept an eye on AC as well as WIS beats the druid with 20 more wisdom to their name. Not only that, but the druid with the lower wisdom has time to sit, memorize cancel magic and dispel the DOT that the other druid put on them. Still has more than a full bubble of life too.

The point is not how much of one attribute you have. A warrior who blows off everything else for STR may be able to deal massive damage, but if he has no AC he's going to get pounded into the turf in a few hits. Monks who specialize in just AGI aren't getting hit much, but the monsters seem to take forever to die. The idea is to try to raise as many stats as you can. Carefully weigh one thing against another before tossing the old away. There can be a balance, and the AC you save today may spare your life long enough to get off one more heal tomorrow.

By now, many of you are wondering why I mentioned druids when this is a cleric board. Well, druids go out and max their WIS too. They feel they can't tank too. They think they can't deal damage with a weapon, so carry some book instead. Basically, they do the same thing as you.

If you want to truly consider whether you can do something, you have to look at it more accurately. People say they can't deal damage that competes with a warrior. Well, you are forgetting that warriors can't toss in a spell while they are fighting something. That has to be included. A weapon in the hands of a character whose skill has been kept up to snuff is an effective DOT. I may only hit 10 times instead of 30-40 in a fight. But if each hit is 20points, that's 200damage total. Along with those few big spells you got off, you can do 1000 total damage in a little under what a warrior can. And 200 damage with a weapon often means 150 less mana you spend.

This games has some checks and balances. but if you don't balance now and then, you just get checked. And I can tell you, I do not want to be bashed into the side of a hockey rink by anything in Kithicor. Have fun, carry two sets of gear if you like switching roles, and keep yer sticks on the ice.

Oh, I do know what I'm talking about. I play a cleric as well as a druid. Toodles.

I agree with Akane. Staring yourself blind at "I must have 200 wisdom" is simply not the whole truth. AC, stats and resistances are all important factors.

Since meditation will bring back a certain amount of mana every tick, more mana will require more meditation to get filled up.

This implies that every time you are not FM you are essentially throwing away all the extra wisdom.

It also implies that you will only be able to heal a certain amount of damage every time unit, regardless of your wisdom.

Higher AC and resistances -will- make you take less damage though, thus decreasing your own healing downtime.

I'm not saying that throwing away the +wis is something that we should strive towards, but at my level (mid 35) I am not prioritizing wisdom in a way that cuts down vastly on other things. I have 143 +- a couple of points depending of if I use 1hb or 2hb. I use a bloodstained mantle instead of a lizardscale mantle because of the nice +10 mr for example.
I have around 645 AC with guard and good resistances (very important on a PvP server).

Besides I am having fun when I whack a monster for 35 points of damage with my tainted teir'dal staff, and I feel alive when I take a few hits in combat :-). And I've never ever heard complaints that I run out of mana too quickly. If you run out of mana it's because of your play style, not your low wisdom.
