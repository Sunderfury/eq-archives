Petition Editorial

 

Let me provide some insight into why (and how) the petition was created and reached its present state.  I am a subscriber to the EQ-Cleric mailing list (provided by ONELIST.COM).  As with the other EQ specific mailing lists, we shared ideas, strategies, complaints, etc on the various issues regarding the cleric class.  Most of the techniques that I use I learned from various posts on that mailing list.

 

From day one of the list the problem with being stunned while Divine Aura was active was complained about.  There were other complaints, but never anything of the same magnitude.  For the most part the list concerned itself with different clerical strategies, hunting zones, and other topics of that nature.  All this changed with the 10% reduction to the damage with the Retribution spell.  This, along with the spell effects added to the Resurrection line of spells, upset the majority of the high-level clerics on the list.  (Please note that while the Res changes make sense, it does “gimp” the spell series somewhat and is a negative change to the class).  At this point a lot of people on the list started demanding changes and many ideas were circulated.  In the meantime I announced the availability of the web site and was getting a pretty steady dose of change requests through that forum also.  That was when I started putting polls up on the web site and then followed with the initial list of items. 

 

In the meantime another player, Ofbac Grimbeard, posted his petition to the mailing list.  There followed a confusing day as it looked as if there might be two competing petitions, both having basically the same ideas (I took many ideas from a prior post of his) and then Ofbac and I decided to work together and have a joint petition.  He wrote the original draft (and did a excellent job), followed by more draft work from Lemac Justerian and Mikaela R’achai.  My contribution to the process was the distillation of items to be included and final editing – all spelling and grammar errors at this point are my responsibility.

 

Before I go into the individual items I would like to say that in my opinion if Verant addresses even one of these issues the petition was a success.  The cleric class is a great class to play.  Obviously the class is playable if none of these changes are made.  After all, we have plenty of level 45+ clerics around.  With that said, the class could still use some enhancements that would make the class a better one and add to the enjoyment of playing a cleric.  Other classes are receiving attention from Verant and a great many clerics would like to see enhancements made for their class also.

 

Now let me present the reasoning behind the individual items.

 

Note: The next six items are NOT arguments asking for items; they were the basic underlying principals behind the cleric class.  Nowhere in this petition does it state that clerics should be better at melee.  It specifically states that they should not, yet I have read posts and received e-mail stating if we wanted to fight better we should have picked the paladin class.  Sigh.  I have to wonder if people really read sometimes.

 

1.        Supreme Healers of Norrath.   No other class should be able to match our ability to heal.

Yep, we have that now.

 

2.        Restorers of Life.   None but one so closely in touch with their god as the cleric should be able to restore life to the extent that we are capable.

Got this one too, so far so good.

 

3.        Masters of Destroying the Undead.  No other class should surpass the ability of a cleric to expunge these underworld denizens with the force and speed of a cleric carrying their god’s disapproval of the undead.

This one is admittedly debatable as it is one of those pre-conceived notions that many clerics brought to the game, primarily from A.D.&D.  However, a lot of clerics have this notion and Verant is in the business of keeping its customers happy.  If not, why are warriors so powerful now (and yep, give a warrior a BIBS and he becomes a killing machine – frankly, I think that is great)?  Anyway, I’ll address the specifics of this one below.

 

4.        Superior Protection.  Clerics have been given the ability to cloak ourselves with the highest protection in the land, platemail armor.  Clerics draw the attention of the mobs when healing others, and should possess the ability to adorn the best protection available.

That is admittedly a lot of verbiage to state that we get to wear plate armor.

 

5.        Less Effective Fighters.  Clerics are incapable of doing the damage at melee as the pure fighters and other hybrid classes.  Clerics are healers, and should not be warriors at melee.

Hmm, this one says we shouldn’t fight well, yet I bet this is the basis for the comments about our fighting ability.  I wonder what would have been said if I had included Dual Wield and Double Attack…. For the record, we are saying that we should continue to retain our poor fighting skills (yes, we suck at melee, yes, it’s ok).

 

6.        Less Effective at Destroying the Living.  Clerics are not capable of dealing damage to the living through our spells to the extent of the pure and hybrid caster classes.  Again, clerics are healers, not primary damage spell casters.

This is probably another one that is confused by many.  We are stating that we should not be as good as many of the other classes at dealing damage to living mobs and that this too is how it should remain.

 

For the specific requests I’m cutting out the verbiage; it can be seen on the petition.

 

 

Item #1:  Stun While Invulnerable

 

The poll was pretty clear on this one as 91% of the 751 responses were positive (78% felt strongly).  I’ll admit that the polling engine isn’t perfect by a long shot, but I do feel that it was a fair representation of the community.  I’d just like to hear why 12 people were against it strongly.  Regardless, this change would benefit clerics greatly while not damaging the other classes.  As a matter of fact, Verant should re-address this issue for all invuln effects (such as the Berserker effect from a PGT) as they all suffer from this defect.

 

Item #2:  Healing Spells

 

Higher (than me) level clerics say that the casting times are too long for Superior and Complete Healing.  They also note that specialization in Alteration does not have a very great effect.  I hope Verant either increases the impact of specialization or decreases the casting times on the spells.  Again, this would seem like a good thing for everybody.

 

Item #3:  Resurrection

 

Clerics who can res were miffed that Verant, with admittedly good reason, gimped the Res spells by adding the spell effects.  Nobody argued that Verant made a bad move, but in conjunction with Retribution it dampened the effectiveness of one of our signature spells and made it appear clerics were getting targeted for negative attention.  I doubt that Verant has it in for clerics, but doesn’t it suck when the only changes you get are negative (and yes, I know about the AE Heal changes – nobody was particularly impressed).  Anyway, it would be nice to get a smidgen of XP restored from the 29th level spell wouldn’t it? 

 

Item #4:  Cleric Quests

 

This one is pretty non-controversial and something I’m sure Verant would love to do as time and resources permit.  It can’t hurt to raise their awareness on this item.

 

Item #5:  Cleric Specific Items

 

This one is the same as #4.

 

Item #6:  Combat Against the Undead

 

Ouch, we come to a hard one.  Now that it’s over I’ll admit that we possibly went too far, but the authors are just as subsceptible to pre-conceived notions (that old A.D.&D. thing again) as others.  However, now that we are on it I have a few suggestions:

1)       Cut down the casting times a bit.  These spells are pretty darned slow.

2)       Why not give us a variety of undead spells that have the same dmg/mana ratio, but let us choose how much damage and mana we wish to burn?  For example, at level 24 I could do 140 points unresisted using Dismiss.  I was pretty impressed at the time.  At level 28 I can do 148.  I’m no longer impressed.  At level 33 I’ll do 156 and that is hardly worth mentioning.  Sure, I won’t be using a lot of mana, but maybe I WANT to kill that mob quickly.  My level 29 wizard friend can do over 200 (not sure how much) using his level 29 DD spells.  If the ratio at level 24 is 140/90, why not give us a Greater Dismiss that does 195 for 130 mana?  It’s the same ratio, but can let us actually decide how much damage we wish to do.  The balance doesn’t change, but our power does.  Why not just cast Dismiss twice you say?  Sometimes you can’t.

 

Item #7:  Combat Against Undead – Area Effects

 

Some people want it and it hardly hurts anyone if we have them.  AE spells can be dangerous, but it’s always good to have another tool available.

 

Item #8:  Meditation

 

An obvious no-brainer and one in which I take great pride since it’s the only original idea I had on the list.  I don’t care if it’s an actual button or if meditation is automatically activated when bringing up the spell book.  I just want to med without an extra mouse-click.  By the way, almost every class would benefit from this.

 

Item #9: Direct Damage Spells Against the Living

 

Clerics are not nukers, agreed.  That said, our DD ability against the living is really really bad.  At level 14 I could do 74 points with Smite.  That was pretty good at level 14.  At level 28 I can do 83 points with Smite.  83 points is basically useless.  The same is going to happen in the upper 30s with Wrath.  The level-44+ clerics say by level 50 Retribution was similarly unimpressive, then Verant reduced it by 10%.  They (the high level clerics) want the 10% restored.  I hope it happens, but I’m not real hopeful.

 

 

Item #10:  The Hammer Line of Summon Spells

 

The level 9 hammer is great because it lets low-level clerics get a magical weapon for practically nothing (even though it seemed expensive at the time).  The level 24 hammer is worthless.  I’m not sure what Verant could do here to help us; adding a Strike or Furor proc would still make it pretty worthless, but maybe they could think of something.  Maybe a little Ward Undead proc wouldn’t be too out of line.  This was a tough one to include, but a lot of people were not happy about these spells and I agree; it hurts to have our spells taken up by something of no value.

 

Item #11:  Bandages

 

Many clerics asked for this.  After reading GZ’s comments about Magicians and this spell I’m not real hopeful, but it does make a certain sense.  A cleric’s primary responsibility is to heal and if providing bandages to group members bandages for use in those OOM situations, wonderful.  It made the list at the last minute at the last minute due to popular demand on the message board.

 

Before I wrap up I want to make one more statement about the petition.  If I had removed items 7, 9, 10, and 11 the thing would have flown through public opinion with flying colors.  Why didn’t I do that?  If I had there would definitely be another petition floating around that had those items.  A LOT OF PEOPLE WANTED THEM.  This is not my petition; it is a petition for a large segment of the cleric class.  I realize that there exists a much greater number of clerics than those that post on the EQ-Cleric mailing list or my web site, but that is the audience I hear.  I suspect the vast majority of players don’t live on the boards or mailing lists at all.  They just play the game.  My responsibility was to support the audience I hear as much as possible and that is what I did.  With that in mind, regardless of how I feel about some of the items I stand behind all of them and honestly believe that other clerics should be able to do so also.  One other thing is that when negotiating it is often a good tactic to ask for more than you expect to receive.  I don’t realistically expect all of the items to be changed by Verant, but I do hope that they make a good number of changes.  Keep in mind they may not have the resources to do them all even if they wanted to do so.   But by presenting them a list of ideas we let them know what kinds of changes we are looking for them to provide.

 

I hope this helps you understand the reasoning behind each of the items in the petition.  I do not believe that granting all of these items would unbalance clerics in comparison to other classes.  It would hardly make us an uber-class.  It would provide greater gameplay enhancement to what is already an enjoyable and fulfilling class in Everquest.

 

Thank you for reading,

 

Rayne Woundbane,
