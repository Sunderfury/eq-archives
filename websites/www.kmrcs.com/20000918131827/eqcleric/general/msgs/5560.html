i disagree on NO DROP...

Posted by OrkinMan on October 15, 1999 at 16:41:54:

In Reply to: Hmmm...let me take the other side on this issue posted by Segal on October 15, 1999 at 15:14:49:

: I will agree that some camps are not fun, that trying to hunt some roaming mabs is not fun. I personally think that adding the new quests was a step backwards.

: Camping can be alot of fun, putting yourself in a spot where you have an opportunity for a good fight and maybe some nice loot. The problem is not camping, the problem is that things are camped, does this make sense?

: Example: Six players move into Unrest and want to get some boots from the Ghost. There is no one else in the zone. They camp the ghost. The kill it a number of times and have good luck to get a pair of boots. All the fights are tough and the party just barely makes it through some of them, very one is having a blast.

: Okay, perfect picture. That is what Verant was looking to happen, the design of the game. So what's the problem? The problem is Group B who enters the zone and wants to camp it. Now you have one group having a good time and the other group waiting to have a good time. My Point is that Camping and Waiting are not the same issue. It is a blast to camp a spot, it is not a blast to wait for the opportunity to camp.

: Lets investigate what causes the waiting problem.
: 1) Overcrowded Servers - Thats an easy one, the EQ world is simply too small for 1500 players
: 2) Maturing Servers - As the average level increases, the world gets smaller. Just ask the next level 40 player where he can hunt.
: 3) Too few places to Camp - Lots of places could be added in existing zones that players could camp
: 4) No-Drop - No-Drop items reduce the number of that specfic item in the market (with a few exceptions) forcing a line of people to go camp an item.

it actually reduces the number of campers, because now you only have people camping for themselves and not to gather for others. when you have people passing their rubicite breastplates to their level 5 characters and the level 5 characters of all their friends, you effectively have added those level 5 characters to the list of people camping the item. i totally understood the reasoning for making PoF stuff NO DROP, since they don't want level 5 characters starting the game with the best stuff there is (that is why they made the PoF unenterable by low levels, because people were dragging their newbies in and looting stuff that a high level group killed to twink them anyway). as for whether or not the new class-specific items should be NO DROP, i think that first they will want to make changes about how they are aquired so that evil clerics can get the items themselves. then they should decide which is more important, the prestige of the items or the marketplace.

: 5) Harvesters - High level players camping low level spots for items to sell. Verants first attempt (and really one of the few design mistakes they have made) was to adopt the no-drop policy which was in and of itself another problem.
: 6) Static Item Placement - I think this one is here to stay, I have yet to see an answer that worked. Random Mobs are worse than Static ones.

: Do I have all the answers to solve the waiting problem? Nope. I do have a few.

: 1) Get rid of no-drop, just an all around bad idea. Hurts the player economy, promotes camping, makes some camp spots less desirable moving more players to the good spots.
: 2) Add many many more places to camp. Lessor Faydark could easily hold a whole hive of bees with 2 or 3 nice loot items.
: 3) Split the minor and major loots up onto 2 seperate MOBs. Why does the Savant in Lower Guk have the Black Mace and the Black Breastplate. Put the Breastplate on the Ghoul Hero and have him spawn somewhere else, thus you just doubled your camping spots. Reduce the chance of the MOB spawning by 50% but now he will always have the item he is supposed to have.
: 4) Give all items certain level requirements, make them fairly low to reward those that accomplish feats over their heads. This prevents twinking.

this is a good idea and one that i've heard for a while now...in other words, they'll never do it.

: 5) Make good items hard to camp solo. Harvesters are usually solo players, make it difficult for them to camp whereas a group could accomplish the camp
: 6) Add 5 new servers, add another any time the average prime time capacity is over 1400 (The new server last night had the highest player count of any server, Yes people will move to them).
: 7) Bring up those new zones as fast as possible, fix Mistmoor and Hedge Keep.


for me the biggest change that they could make would be to enable people of larger differences in levels to group more effectively. as it is, the gain in to-hit and AC as well as the experience rationing is enough to make it difficult for people who don't play the exact same amount of time to stay in a group for any period of time. but like all good ideas, Verant will never implement them.

-OrkinMan
