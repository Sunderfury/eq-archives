Snare is a great spell, although it is resisted an awful lot. I shudder whenever it gets brought up in a petition or post that Verant might see.

The "stand still" behavior is easily explained. Snare only slows the mob down, and by itself will not ever wholly stop a mob. With some mobs, as you are aware, when you beat them down significantly, they turn and run away. As you hit them, they run slower and slower. If snare is active, they still turn and try to run (that's what they are doing by turning around). However, because of the damage done, they move off slowly or not at all (depends on how much damage and the timing of the snare). If you leave them alone, they eventually become unsnared and run off. They don't attack you because they are still in "flee" mode, and mobs in flee mode don't attack.

The problem is with Ensnare, a spell that druids get at 29. It is supposed to be the Snare upgrade. I have tested it, and unlike Snare, Ensnare lasts on average only 5 to 10 seconds (the time is greatly variable). Snare, when it takes, will last over 2 minutes consistently. Ensnare simply is useless with such a short duration.

With regard to DoTs, I have tested the new DoTs several times, and I have noticed no increase in the duration or damage based upon the target's melee status. It seems that just as when Verant nerfed necro and shaman DoTs several months ago, the supposedly "positive" side of a nerf change somehow slipped through the cracks.
