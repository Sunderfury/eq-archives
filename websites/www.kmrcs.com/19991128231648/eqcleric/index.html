November 17

Let's talk about customer relations

By now, most of you should be aware of a recent change to the Feign Death skill/spell, shared by Monks, Necromancers, and Shadowknights.   Yesterday afternoon I saw many posts complaining that Verant nerfed FD.  I un-subscribed from EQ Monks many months ago (just not my favorite class) so it was rather surprising to see these posts.  According to the many reports, FD was nerfed and there are many irate Monks out there (I have yet to check how Necro's and SK's feel about this).  Finally, when I logged on last night, I read Verant's rather detailed explanation of the changes and the effects they have on the FD skill (for the record many monks disagree with many of the points).

My complaint is this: why wasn't this announced Tuesday with the rest of the patch information?  There seems to be a trend of late when it comes to patches:

  1. Verify that Verant made the changes they say they did.
  2. Discover new changes (typically nerfs of some sort) that were not announced.

This is a disturbing trend and one they must cease immediately.  Any time they make a change announce it.  Players hate to log in and discover negative changes that were slipstreamed into the game.  I for one do not like to discover that certain skills or abilities were nerfed for any class.  It makes me feel that the company is sneaky, and frankly I had enough of that with a previous popular online game.  

Players are going to complain regardless, but when they have the added fuel of discovering a hidden change, they typically go ballistic with moral indignation.  Kind of hard to blame them.

On the other hand, I really enjoy the game

And have been very happy with many of the recent changes (e.g. Kithicor, Kedge Keep).  The re-tuning of Paw, one of my favorite dungeons, has me ecstatic.  I just hope they get it done before I out level it (well, no real danger there).  We complained about a lack of class-specific items; they added a slew of them.  The fact that some of the class/race restrictions lists are buggy is a somewhat minor point (actually rather humorous).  The only thing that I would like to see them change at this moment would be to restrict the higher level mobs to one side of the main path through Kithicor (preferably the side opposite Rivervale).  I don't think travel through the zone should be totally safe for low level characters, but right now it is extremely dangerous, perhaps overly dangerous.

November 3

A comment on the latest patch

If you haven't heard by now, with the most recent patch Paladins and Shadowknights had their quest armor's AC increased.  That's great; as plate wearing classes it was odd, to say the least, that their armor provided less protection than the quest armor available to chain classes.  Um, what about clerics?  Aren't we a plate class also?  Why do I still have the feeling that the designer's at Verant are not communicating on the quests.  I feel that our armor should be on par with the other plate classes, leaving aside the entire issue of the AC of the chain classes.

I have a suggestion for Verant.  Provide a forum where the rationales behind such decisions are made available.  Currently, if you want direct feedback you can either e-mail someone you happen to know at Verant, e-mail an address blindly and pray for a response, or post something on Alt.Games.Everquest and again hope that it catches Brad McQuaid's attention.  

There may be a very good reason why the cleric quest armor did not receive an upgrade.  Unfortunately, I can't imagine what it could be.

November 1

Feedback from GZ

Last weekend I received a reply from GZ on a few questions I asked him.  To paraphrase:

  • Manastones have been removed from the drop table and will return as a quest (I did not ask him when).
  • The conversion rate on Manastones is 60 Hit Points for 20 Mana (not the widely reported 60-30).
  • There is no such thing as Armor Absorption, it's purely AC.
  • Spells do not get better with use beyond that which is dictated by the character's level and skill with the particular spell discipline (e.g. Evocation or Alteration).  Therefore, casting Holy Might 1000s of times does not increase ones success with the spell once Evocation has been maxed for that level.
