Tricks of the Trade

 

Home Calm

The Lull, Soothe, Calm (etc) line of spells was designed to allow you to reduce the aggresion level on a mob.  This is mainly applicable to those times when you are looking at a camp of multiple mobs, any one (or more) of whom your group could take, but not all simultaneously.  This is where Calm comes in.  Start calming creatures, one at a time, until the only un-calmed ones left are the ones you wish to fight.  Pull those mobs away from the camp (and out of the creatures aggro range), kill them, go get more.

Note: I recommend starting with the easiest creatures and working your way up because the lower the creature's level the lower the chance of resist.   If a mob resists your spell, it (and all it's linked buddies) will come for you.

 

Fear and Spook the Dead (or "Begone with you!")

The idea with these spells are to chase off unwanted mobs.  When they are successful, the targeted mob leaves for approximately 30 - 45 seconds.  I have seen it used in the following situations:

  1. You have one or more extra mobs on you or your group.  The idea is to spook/fear the extra mobs thereby giving your party time to kill one and be better prepared for the next one when it returns.
  2. An unwanted green/low blue comes to the party and it's just not worth the mana, hit points, and time it takes to kill it.  Spooking it can give your party time to zone or be better prepared for the next fight.  And you may get lucky and some other group who really desires the mob might grab it for you.
  3. Your group is getting overwhelmed and it's time to go.  Getting rid of a few mobs might be the answer to gaining enough time for your party to safely vacate the premises.

 

Kill Stealers

Remember Fear and Spook the Dead?  Well, if another person/group is being obnoxious and grabbing your mobs ("I'll do what I want and you can't stop me....") try spooking their mob.  While this won't always work (spellcasters can Root of course), you might get lucky and send their tanks on a merry little run.  It has been reported by several clerics that doing this a few times is often enough to correct anti-social behavior :P.

 

Root them critters

Once you can get this spell, NEVER leave home without it.  It is by far one of the best spells we get.  It has numerous applications, but the idea is to hold the mob in its place.  Some examples of use are:

  1. Stopping mobs from fleeing.  There are two occasions when this is helpful or even necessary.  One is saving your tanks the trouble of having to chase it down.   Another is when you are in an area where the mobs will run for their (frequently bigger) brothers and sisters and bring them back to the party (Paw, Crushbone, and Upper Guk come to mind).  If the mob is successful in reaching help, a fight your party was winning can quickly turn into a route.  Don't let running mobs get away.
  2. You are fighting multiple mobs and one of your party needs a heal.  You heal and quickly gain the unwanted attention of  a mob (healing their enemy often makes them hate you more).  Suddenly you need to heal, but are getting pounded and can't get off that long heal.  Root the mob, step out of range, and cast your spells.   This can also be applied to the next example.
  3. Root and nuke.  Surely you've seen wizards soloing and doing this, and if those brainy wizards are doing it, there must be a good reason, right?  It's quite simple, just root your target, and nuke it to slag.  What's that?  It's so easy it feels like cheating?  If you aren't bleeding some it bothers you?  Get over it and enjoy it :).  There are many times when you will not be able to do it (that is actually the norm).  However, it works great at many of the popular spawn spots such as  the Chessboard against those undead pawns, the mummy camp in Lesser Faydark.
  4. When it's time to bug-out, rooting can hold that mob in place allowing you and your party time to escape.

A couple of notes about root.

  1. It is frequently resisted, especially against mobs of your level or higher.  Don't despair, just try again.  If you are fighting in a place where the monsters need to be held, just spend the mana and get it done.
  2. It does have a variable duration.  Sometimes it will hold for 30 or more seconds, sometimes, just a few.
  3. It will often make the monster go aggro on you if you do it too early.
  4. Depending upon the difficulty of your monster, you may want to wait a bit before rooting.   This, of course, depends upon your group composition, what you are fighting, the number of mobs, etc., but in a group with tanks fighting blue or greater mobs it's usually not necessary to root the mob until it's down 1/2 or so.  On the other hand, if you have a wizard who is nuking you may want to root it immediately.   I have seen wizards neglect this much to their regret....
