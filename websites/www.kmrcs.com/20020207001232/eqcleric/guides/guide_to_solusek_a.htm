Guide to Solusek A (Drops)

by Ferrik Bloodforge, Cazic Thule

*Beginning Note: This guide is written from a Cleric’s Point of view. I did not fight in Solusek A to level, so I will not address how to pull and fight to gain experience. I will focus on some locations and rare spawns.  I would like to thank Nicole and Joshua for information they posted to the Cleric’s Mailing List (Onelist) that helped with this guide.*

Ed. Note: Just after receiving this guide an excellent map to Solusek A was released by the Everlore web site, it can be reached here.

Getting there: Solusek’s Eye, or Solusek A can be found off of the Lavastorm Mountains. After zoning into Lavastorm, head north until you reach a large volcano with a Goblin Camp perched on the upper rim. Below this camp, down the slope and toward the lava, you will find 2 zone entrances. The one on the left leads to Nagafen’s Lair (Solusek B), the one on the right is our destination, SolA.

*One interesting thing to note at this point is that these Goblins are not aggro to Dwarves unless you have done something to lose faction with them. It is very possible to walk through this entire dungeon as a Dwarf and not be attacked by anything except a few of the rare spawns. Once you kill the Goblins for a short time, you will become KOS.*

Entrance: Immediately after zoning into SolA and taking a few steps you will be presented with the aspect of this zone that scares people the most…lava. And indeed, this may be the worst lava spot in the entire zone. You will find a short flight of curved stairs that traverses the room. On either side of this walkway, you will find a long drop into some nasty lava. In most lava areas in this zone, you can easily jump back out onto cool dungeon stone. However, in this spot the only way I know to survive is to have levitate or swim through an under-lava zone point into a very rough area of Solusek B. Basically, do not fall here. There will usually be a few 20ish Goblins near or on this stair – don’t let them spook you. Kill them on the flat ground near the zone or on the far side of the stair.

Across this stair/walkway you will see a wall with a large crack or hole in it - climb through. You will encounter low to mid 20s Goblins in this entire area so stay alert. Take an immediate right and you will come to a ledge (still more Gobs). Straight in front of you and down a small drop-off you will see a large metal door. Directly to your right is a tunnel that will drop off into a circular room with another tunnel leading out of it. This is hall is a zone point to a relatively safe spot in SolB (though there is a semi-frequent Greater Kobold that will spawn on the other side). Back to the large metal door…

Goblin Stronghold: Inside of that door is one of the main areas of what I refer to as the Goblin Stronghold. Within a short distance of this door you will find the Barroom (Bartender Spawn) and a Goblin Vendor whom you can buy and sell with if you have the faction (Dwarves and people with sneak or invis I believe). This section is quite large and the passages wind and interconnect like mad; getting turned around and running in circles is quite common. I won’t try and give exact directions in this area and hopefully you will find the map that accompanies this helpful. Inside of the Stronghold the Goblins begin to reach higher levels and in some spots will be 30ish (at the end of this document you will find a list of Goblin types from lowest to highest level). In this area you will find MOBs like the Bartender, Goblin Drunkard, Goblin High Shaman and Goblin King. There all also passages that will take you to other spawn spots such as the Kobold Predator and the Goblin Foreman (these 2 will be referenced again). Please see another appendix at the end of this guide that will list what these MOBs drop as loot.

Let us move back to the ledge again, facing the doorway to the Goblin Stronghold. If you turn left on this ledge you will see some Goblins (2 or 3) standing in front of some hallways that branch off from the path you are on. You will need to fight, sneak, or – if you’re a lucky dwarf – walk by them and take the first right that you are able to (this excludes the secret wall on the right that leads into the Goblin Stronghold). You should now begin to see Gnomes. The hall you are in will dead-end into a small alcove with a doorway that leads to an L-shaped bridge spanning some lava- cross this. Continue on, past the swinging pendulum (it will not hurt you, it is just for "looks") and you will come to a larger room with a lava lake. Point yourself to the left side of the room and you will see the Castle of the Solusek Mining Company (all hands on deck! Gnomes ahead!)

The Solusek Mining Company: To get over to the castle, you must jump into (almost over) and swim a very small section of lava. Do yourself a favor and pick the smallest width and take a leap over to the rock ground in front of the castle. You should not even take the typical 40 points of lava damage if you do this correctly. There is no real danger in this.

**NOTE: If you are of an evil race or have bad faction with Gnomes in general, you will most likely have been attacked before you get this far. If you are KOS here but have somehow managed to sneak into this area, I do not suggest continuing on. There are Gnomes and Clocks every few steps in the castle and many of them are in the high 20s and low 30s. Killing Kobolds in Solusek B will raise your faction with this group of Gnomes (Solusek Mining Co faction).**

There are two drawbridges here. The one on the left leads to various Gnomes and Gnome Clockworks, and a secret wall that eventually will lead to the Goblin Foreman and Kobold Predator (there is an easier way to get to them), but nothing particularly noteworthy. Nothing that I have found anyway. The bridge on the right is where we are headed…click on it and cross.

Once inside, follow the hall until you come to a 7-turn. Directly after this, on the left, you will see a set of steps going down. Go down the steps and push the button on the wall to open the door on your right. Go through the next 2 doors and turn to the left. Standing here you will see a door in front of you and a door to the right. The door on the right leads to a small room with a Gnomish Vendor. He sells the Bag of the Tinkerers. It sells for around 6,000pp. Buy it if you like. He is very helpful to those fighting in SolB who need to unload items without running to the gypsies in Lavastorm. He does not sell food or drink.

**NOTE: when fighting the Gnomes and Clockworks mentioned below – if your faction is not above indifferent, other MOBs in the area will attack you as well. If you are amiable or above, they will not bother you though they may heal the MOB you are killing. Healers are not common here. Also, when killing these MOBs you will lose faction with the Mining Co and some groups in Ak’anon. Unless you are "farming" items you should not go KOS anywhere – that is, if you were not close to KOS to begin with (but then you would be getting gang-banged here when attacking). Killing Kobolds in SolB will raise your Solusek Mining Company faction, but not the Ak’anon faction.**

The door in front of you houses the spawn point of Gabbie Mardoddle. Gabbie’s rare drop is one of the most sought-after items in the game, the Molten Cloak (4AC +4 WIS and INT). Gabbie spawns behind the desk in the small room. Gabbie is in the low or mid 30s and a caster. She can do a lot of damage in a small amount of time and if you do not kill her quickly, she will kill you. I have seen level 45+ tanks have trouble with her if she gets a few spells off. A level 50 Cleric (or a 44+) can enstill, stun and nuke her to death in about 20 seconds – rarely do I lose more than a bubble of health. I strongly suggest having a magic resistance buff on you.

Her spot pops about every 20 minutes, but it is very well known for skipping a spawn or two and not popping for 40 minutes to an hour (or more, ugh). Like most rare spawns, other MOBs will spawn in her spot (clockworks) and you will need to clear them. These clockworks are no pushovers and can hit hard. I am guessing they are in the low 30s.

Gabbie’s common drop is the Mithril Quill, a 4 damage 19 delay piercing weapon – pretty much worthless. You will see a lot of quills before the cloak unless you get extremely lucky. It took me about 15 spawns to get the cloak, which amounted to about 30 hours of non-consecutive playing time. As far as I am concerned, this is one of the longest and most frustrating camps in the game. Luckily, if you want to be an item hound, there are several other spawns very close by.

Near the door that leads to Gabbie, you will find a portcullis. Open it and go through. You are now in a small courtyard area that actually appears to be the font of the castle (there is a way to go up top and see the ballistas and Clockworks keeping watch over the battlements). To your right there is a hall leading out of this courtyard. Take it. In the hall you will see some Gnomish Conjurers and Miners – basically more of the same that you saw in the castle. Keep going on the path until you cross two bridges and come to a square room that should have two Clockworks in it. Stop. From here back to the portcullis is the walking path of the Clockwork Model EXG.

The EXG’s common drop is the Charred Guardian Shield (10AC +9 WIS) and the rare drop is the Charred Guardian Breastplate (16AC +3 STR +6 STA - LORE ITEM). The EXG is a female Clockwork who is larger than the rest of the clocks. If she is carrying the shield, you will see it on her. If not, she has the chest. She will pace back and forth between the square room and the castle portcullis. She is around level 30, hits hard and TOTALLY resistant to magic. This means that you can not root and nuke at all. You will need a tank. A level 50 Cleric can buff and Symbol and then heal in battle and solo the EXG down with melee, but it is very rough. As she paces between the castle and back room, the Gnome Conjurers will buff her. I suggest you Nullify Magic on her a few times before engaging. The best spot to fight her is on the castle-side of the larger bridge. There is a decent amount of room here, no other MOBs nearby to help if you do not have good faction, and no one in healing range.

Like Gabbie, the EXG spot spawns every 20 minutes or so and has "placeholders" that will spawn. These are other Clockworks and a Clockwork Spider Model SX. These are not quite at tough as the EXG and they can be cast upon.

Other Rare Spawns: There are several rare spawns within a short jaunt of the Gnome Castle. They may drop items that you need, or they may simply help relieve the boredom of camping Gabbie or the EXG.

Reckless Efreeti: The Reckless Efreeti can be found behind the Gnomish Castle. Go out the back door of the Gnome area where the two guards stand in the square room. (the point at which the EXG turns around and walks back to castle). You will be at a T intersection. This entire area has Goblins in it so be prepared. Turn to your right and you will see a spike trap that opens and closes in the hall. There are two ways you can get to the Efreeti from here: go past the trap (like all others, it will not hurt you) and hug the left wall – you will fall into a pit with two Blazing Elementals (mid 20s?) and possibly the Efreeti. The other way to reach this spot and possibly be more certain of what is in the pit is to go into the doorway (no door) on the left before you reach the spike trap. Take the next door to the right and open the other door after it. In this room you will see a table and probably a Gob or two. Facing in from the door, the corner to your immediate right is a secret wall. Go into the wall and you will be in the Efreeti pit.

The Efreeti spot spawns every 40 minutes or so and is located near the back of this small pit room. He drops the Platinum Armbands (No AC +3 WIS +3 STR) or the rare Obsidian Flamberge (20 dmg 50 dly effect: Obsidian Shatter WAR SK 2H Sword - No Drop/Lore). The bands are a very good item for Druids, and possibly others who have chosen to sacrifice AC for 3 wisdom. The Efreeti is most likely in the high 20s and is quite easy for a Cleric or Caster to kill. He only has around 1100 hit points. The two Elementals guard the Efreeti and spawn on a 20 minute cycle (so his spot spawns once every two of theirs). As far as I have seen, the two Ele’s are aggro, the Efreeti is not. A third Elemental will spawn in the Efreeti spot when he does not pop. Clear them all to get him to spawn and check back every 20 or so. There is a way to clear the Ele’s without having to do any real fighting – this is pretty obvious and I will let you find it out for yourself!

Goblin Captain: The Captain can be found a few doors down from the Efreeti. Get your bearings in the room where the secret wall to the pit is. There are only two doors: the one you came in and the one you are about to go through to reach the Captain. Open the door and step into the Banner Room. I suppose it is worth mentioning that this area if full of mid 20s Goblins – be careful and don’t go alone if they are aggro to you. This room has for doors and, the captain is through the one directly in front of you. His spawn spot is in the middle of the back wall in this small room. Like everything else, his spot pops about every 20 minutes and he does not spawn every time. There are two other Goblins that spawn in this room as well. The Captain’s common drop is Charred Boots (AC6 +3 AGI +5 vs Fire and Poison) and rare is Charred Gauntlets (AC7 +3 DEX +5 INT). Both of these items are plate based armor.

Singe and Kindle: Both of these MOBs wander the Goblin areas of SolA. Singe is a Fire Drake, Kindle a Fire Imp. Both are in the Mid to High 20s I believe. There are certain spots that players will camp to find these MOBs, but the place that I have come across them most often is at the T-intersection directly behind the Gnome’s backdoor (around the Spike Trap). Their "holders" wander by here. If you are looking for Singe and Kindle, kill the wandering Gobs and eventually they will pop. Singe carries the Obsidian Bead Hoop (AC2 +3 AGI +3 INT) and the Drake Scale Belt (AC5 +3 DEX +10 vs Fire). Kindle carries the Obsidian Ring (AC3 +3 DEX +10 HP) and the highly prized Imp Skin Gloves (AC2 +5 INT +10 Mana).

Goblin Foreman and Kobold Predator: You will find these two spawns between the Gnome Castle and the entrance from Lavastorm. Let us backtrack a little to L-bridge that brought us to the pendulum and lava room at the first. From this bridge, heading toward the pendulum…when you reach the end of the bridge – stop. There is a hallway to your left, take it. A few steps down this hall you will see a doorway. A few feet after this doorway, on the same wall, you will see a charred/darkened section of the wall. This is a secret door, go through it. You are now in another hallway. Go forward and take your first left, then right, then left again and you should be looking at a another secret doorway (you can usually tell by the small holes in the surface). Behind this wall is a small room with a well structure in it and three Goblins. The Foreman spawns in the middle spot here. Others will spawn in his spot. The Foreman Drops the Foreman Tunic (10AC 0 weight +3 STR +3 WIS) or the Obsidian Scimitar (6 dmg 27 dly +6 AGI).

There is one door in the Foreman’s room. This leads to another room with a set of steps. On top of the steps, is where you will find the Kobold Predator. This is doggie is in the mid 20s and drops the Kobold-Hide Boots (AC5 +3 AGI +10 HP) and the rare Obsidian Shard (6 dmg 25 dly Effect: Obsidian Shatter).

That is about if for the spawns, but I will leave you with this: There are rumors that there are more rare-spawn Gnomes in the Castle areas. There are also rumors of a full set of Charred Plate Armor coming from SolA. There is also one spawn that I have left unmentioned, due to the fact that it is so unknown I will wait to add it until it has become closer to common knowledge. It does not drop a cleric item.

Zones to Solusek B: There are several from SolA to SolB. The closest one to the Lavastorm entrance one is mentioned at the first of this guide. It is the zone near the ledge in front of the Stronghold. The Second is fairly tough to get to and found behind a secret wall in the middle of the Stronghold. It should be reflected on the map. The third and fourth zones are located near each other and can be found behind the Gnome Castle. Standing at the T-intersection, instead of turning right toward the spike-trap, turn left. The hall will immediately open into a small room area with a hall and door to the right. Go through this door and you are in yet another room (Gobs all around of course) there is a door to the right of you and a door in front. The door in front leads to a hallway that is a zone. The door on the right leads to a room that has a hallway to the left that is a zone. Those are all of the zones from A to B that I know of.

I believe I am done. Good hunting in SolA, one of the most interesting zones in the game.
