How to fix Stump Watch 99

or what Verant should do about our armor quests

This editorial is not a rant directed at Verant; instead, it is a list of what Verant did right and what they didn't do right in implementing the new quests, specifically the cleric quests.  It also contains a list of suggestions that would, in my opinion, improve both the cleric quests and the overall game.  Furthermore, this is not a petition.  There is a petition on the net, available at http://www.mindspring.com/~remage/ .  I agree with many of the points, but not all.  I also don't agree with the tone of the petition.

This weekend I had the distinct "pleasure" to participate in what I call Stump Watch 99.   What is Stump Watch you ask?  It is a name derived from some our quests, one of which has an item that spawns on top of a stump in Kithicor forest.  What can be easier than picking up an item off the ground?  Nothing, except that the item only spawns approximately every 7 hours.  While participating in my own little segment of Stump Watch I had ample opportunity to not only read a lot of class-specific message boards, but also think about the quests and what could be done to improve them.

What is good about the quests

Nothing.  Well, ok, seriously, there are some very good aspects to the quests.  One is that players are extremely spread out at the moment.  For example, there have been 30+ players actively hunting in Dagnor's Cauldron the last few nights.  How often has the cauldron been used as anything other than a place to pass through on the way to Unrest?  Anything Verant can do to increase utilization of all the zones is a good thing.

Secondly, some of these quests are a lot of fun.  I have helped two ranger friends complete four Ivy Bracers.  This is a good quest.  The mobs spawn frequently (Redwind seems to spawn every 10 - 15 minutes) and many of the surrounding mobs gave me experience.  I'm looking forward to the Ivy Tunic quest.  I much prefer working on these quests than I would camping Rubicite.  Sure, there will still be camping queues, but so far the ones I've participated in have been orderly and cordial.  There was a certain amount of camaraderie displayed; I didn't feel the competition I normally feel at heavily contested spawn spots.  This quest is a prime example of how they all should be.

What is not good about the quests

First, many clerics are upset that the armor doesn't have better stats, specifically that it doesn't have more increases to wisdom.  To an extent I agree; however, with one exception this armor is generally better than Rubicite.  The one exception is the Rubicite Breastplate with the Healing Aura which I'll address later.  The new bracers increase Wisdom by 5 each (for a total increase of 10).  None of the other classes are getting better increases to their prime attributes.  The disappointing part for us is that it comes on bracers.  I personally would have much preferred that it be 5 each on the tunic and greaves.  The increase of AC on those two items would have been much more valuable than on bracers.  I think we need to cut Verant a bit of slack on the armor.  Keep in mind they never promised us a pre-Plane of Fear suit.  They promised class specific replacement armor for Rubicite.  While it could have been better, it isn't awful either; it just isn't particularly desirable for a cleric interested in maximizing wisdom (and neither was Rubicite).

Second, let me address the issue of Stump Watch.  Or Cabin Watch or Water Watch.  All three of these were a bad idea.  A real bad idea.  The idea of quests, and the game, is to have fun.  Dear Verant, nobody, and I mean nobody, can tell me that watching a patch of static scenery for hours at a time is fun.  Many of us players want to do the quests, and some of us will tolerate whatever you do in order to complete them, but please, re-think this one.  If the seven hour spawn cycle is part of some master plan to keep down the quest armor count that's one thing, but give us a mob to kill.  Better yet, why can't our quests be more like other class's quests.  For example, to get the warrior greaves we went to Najena to kill Trazdon.  Two hours later we left with the Dark Circlet necessary to complete the quest.  By the way, the first hour was spent waiting for our turn.  He spawned every 20 - 30 minutes.  Most of the crafted items don't even require a named mob to be killed, just a specific type of mob who has a chance of dropping the item.  Why are clerical quests different? 

Third, alright, if Stump Watch isn't bad enough, who decided it would be a good idea to put the best wisdom enhancing item in the game on one of our quest mobs?  Specifically Bilge, a mid 30s aqua goblin in Dagnor's Cauldron, drops either a +18 Wisdom totem or a +5 Wisdom belt along with our icon (or ingot? who cares).  Come on Verant, don't make us compete with higher level players.  After all, what do you think the life expectancy of that mob is going to be?  If we aren't around when Bilge is dropped that's it, another six hours down the drain.  What if the player who takes Bilge out doesn't even know about the quest?  This is silly.  I also hear Bilge runs real fast.  It sounds to me as if this mob is designed for druids and shamans.

Fourth, um, gee, thanks for giving us replacement armor for Rubicite, but, well, I want a chance at a breastplate too.  The consequences of this decision are: Rubicite Breastplates are practically priceless now (I'm sure those who have one must be pleased) and Verant rewarded those who concentrated on leveling more quickly.  Conversely, they punished the slower leveling players by removing their opportunity to acquire one of the premier items in the game.  Not a good idea Verant.

What do I recommend

1) Eliminate Stump Watch.  Specifically, make our quests much more similar to the warrior's crafted quests.  Furthermore, don't make us track rare spawns (rare being one every 6 hours).  Put our items on regular mobs and work out a percentage of that item being dropped.  Our items are hardly special enough to justify the time involved.

2) Take our items off named NPCs such as Bilge.  Instead, implement #1.

3) Put Rubicite back into the game.  Just make it an extremely rare drop off high level (35+) mobs similar to the Box of Abu Kar and Black Pearls.  It is unfair that those who do not level as fast (for whatever reason) lose out on the opportunity to loot the Rubicite Breastplate.

4) Consider reworking the stat buffs on the armor a bit.  Specifically for the cleric class, put the wisdom enhancements on the tunic and greaves.  The existing wisdom-enhancing bracers are easily acquired, so putting the wisdom enhancement on the quest armor bracers is very disappointing.  Let us clerics interested in enhancing wisdom wear plate on our chest and legs.  That would be an outstanding reward for doing the quests.

Thank you for your time.

Rayne Woundbane
Cleric, Karana Server
