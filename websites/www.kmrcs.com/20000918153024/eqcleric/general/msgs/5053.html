If the tank is unable to get the mob off the cleric, who's to say that the 2nd tank would have any better luck? Having a 2nd cleric in the party mean that if a cleric pulled a mob onto themselves for DD (undead or not), then the other cleric can keep them healed up until the tank is able to pull the mob off of them. Or the other cleric can nuke the mon pulling the mob off the first cleric.

: How I play my cleric:

: Primarily the buff, heal, med routine.

: If we are fighting casters I'll throw in the occasional stuns as well as rooting or polishing off the runners, or atoning pets once the masters are dead.

: : Ok, I would argue that if you want two of anything in a party, it would be tanks. My regular group consists of a ranger, a warrior, an enchanter, a wizard, myself (a cleric, of course), and one other. On several occasions we have grouped with another cleric. In this situation our group was unstoppable. Everyone loved it.

: Part of the reason why 2 clerics aren't good is because sometimes that spot is best served with a different class. Just as an example, if you were in lets say a part of 3. 1 tank and 2 clerics or would you have 2 tanks and 1 cleric ?

: 2 high level clerics in an area like the undead side lower guk is a different story. True we have good DD against undead, but the cleric has to play it right. More often then naught I've seen a cleric open up with undead spells only to have the mob beat on him and the tanks unable to get it off him.

:
: : I also happen to know that one of the most powerful level 50 parties on our server has two clerics. Why is this? With two clerics in your party, you can pretty much auto-pull. If you're in an undead area like lower guk, any cleric level 44+ has terrific DD spells. And by sharing the buffs, both clerics have more mana for healing.

: : I really wonder how you play your cleric. Yes, if you don't like healing and buffing, you're out of place playing a cleric. There is more finesse to playing a cleric, though, then you give them credit for.
