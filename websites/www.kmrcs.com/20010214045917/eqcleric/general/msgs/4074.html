In Reply to: Resurection Spells posted by Hinur on October 01, 1999 at 15:41:13:

: The idea that rewarding hunting by zone boarders or safe hunting spots, rather than getting a good well balanced group and exploring/hunting deeper into dungeons strikes me as the wrong approch to take.

: This is effectivly what you seemed to be suggesting in your response, and to a certain extent what the devlopers have introduced with this approch.

But in what zones do you find satisfactory hunting
for all levels near zone borders? You will always
have some people that are willing and capable of
going a little further in. Those people are also
better equipped to get their corpse back, to a
certain degree. A level 12 wandering into Unrest
a little too far is NOT what I consider a
reasonable act. A level 20 at the same point has
considerably less difficulty returning to the
same point. THAT is the metric I am trying to
use. If you are stupid and go where you OBVIOUSLY
don't belong (or maybe you just didn't know any
better... Brell knows I've gone into a few places
I wish I hadn't, but didn't know I wasn't supposed
to be there until I was running back there naked)
then you are not the typical Resurrect candidate.
You are someone that made a poor choice and needs
bailed out. Resurrect is a high-level, group-
oriented spell that helps a party recover lost
members for reduced down-time. Why? Because at
high levels, they've EARNED it. A high-level
group in a high-level dungeon can't afford to
be down one or more party members for long. As
a reward for many levels of service, the cleric
gains the ability to summon his companions back
to their corpse. As an added bonus, they also
begin to restore lost XP. People that run around
/telling high-level clerics, asking for a Res are
simply buying a service, like buying a weapon, or
paying for a bind. They need to get back to their
group as well, but of what concern is that to the
cleric? It's only of concern to the cleric because
the economy of the game forces them to rely on it
for income, or their network of friends or guild
members imposes a sense of obligation. There is
nothing that says that a cleric MUST run off to
resurrect anyone and everyone that has enough
sense to type /w all cleric and look at the levels
displayed.

: While playing in safe areas death is rare, corpse recovery is easy, as is getting a res in two hours wether you have a cleric in your party or not (at least compared to the alternative).

: If you/party has the gut's to go out and explore or hunt deeper into dungeons the risk of death increases 10 fold (harder to get out, more mobs to get through, generaly harder mobs, etc ....). This by it's very nature means you will die more, and will spend much more time (and often more challanging than the inital hunt) in corpse recovary.

You disprove your own point. If you are venturing
into more dangerous areas, and take risks, you
are also assuming the responsibility and realizing
the possibility that you MIGHT NOT survive, and
further, you PROBABLY won't be able to retrieve
your corpse without outside assistance.

: For this more adventurous & fun risk we are know also saything that after dying and spending two+ hours in corpse recovary you will not even get exp back.

No, you are saying that you voluntarily got in
over your head, and you are accepting the
potential outcome, which may be that you will
permanently lose XP.

: Am I the only one who thinks this is backwards ?

It's not at all backwards. When you opt for glory,
adventure and high risk, your payoff is also MUCH
higher than the payoff I get playing it safe. You
just would like to have your cake and eat it too.
You want to have a chance at the big payoff (more
XP from kills, better money, nicer items in loot),
but won't accept the downside, which is that you
also stand to LOSE. Who WOULDN'T be rushing in
to fight yellows and reds if it was so easy to
get a resurrect and gain back everything you just
lost?

: The situation i was in last night is not a big deal but it does ephasis both how badly thought out the nurf was thought out. Both in the fact that a time limit rather than some other solution; and the reduculasly low time limit they actualy implimented.

: If they are going to nurf something they are going to get complaints. It's human nature especialy when people have grown used to how things work and adapted to them. The least they can do I would have thought is think things through a bit better first and nurf in a way that at least adds somthing back to the game.

: They at least managed this to a certain extent with the dot spells, making them harder to kite but increasing there effectivness in group situations (which was presumably what they were origonaly intended for). Why can't they put the same level of thought & effort into our cleric resurection spells ?

But they did. What they did was nerf this spell
for the people that run around piling up corpses
and asking clerics to resurrect them. What they
DIDN'T particularly nerf is the cleric that works
as part of a solid team, and can help out his
guildmates or groupmates. At least, I feel this
was their intent, but it's been perceived in a
totally different light, and now will be seen as
nothing but another in a long line of poorly
designed nerfs. With any luck, they'll tune the
time limit up a little, to 4 or 8 hours, and
that will make most people happy, or so it would
seem. A lot of complaints from clerics up until
this point seemed to be focused on the never-ending
succession of /tells from complete strangers asking
for a resurrect from high-level clerics from the
moment they log on until the moment they log out.
Unfortunately, human nature, as you cited earlier,
dictates that the people you would most like to help
are the considerate ones, who refuse to pester you,
while the ones who pester you will continue to
pester away, thus driving you slowly and inexorably
mad.

Gorgrim
