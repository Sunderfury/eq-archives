First, check out this thread and Rafeco's table. Rafeco thread.

Now think about this:


  • The chain classes, shamans, rangers, rogues?, for some odd reason, have better AC than the plate classes. Don't get upset, just absorb the info.

  • For the most part, the distribution of the stat bonuses are disappointing. With the exception of basically the rangers, it doesn't seem that anyone's chest/leg pieces are all that class specific.

  • Now, correct me if I'm wrong, but for the chain wearers this stuff is as good as it gets, right? Except for the PoF that is.

  • We plate wearers still have many better pieces available don't we? With the exception of Rubi chest, we can still acquire tons of great AC pieces. The only sacrifice we clerics would have to make is wisdom.

  • Verant only promised to make class-specific substitutes for Rubi. They never claimed to make pre-PoF armor.

  • Verant delivered on their promise.

  • I grant all those disappointed (I am too) that I wish they would have given us (and all the other classes) really good items for chests and legs. They just didn't. I have to wonder why.

  • I do feel, that looking at Rafeco's chart, that there was little, if any, overall coordination between the designers on armor creation. Furthermore, it appears that the stat buffs were not particularly well thought out for each class, once again Rangers being a possible exception.

  • I also think Verant has lost focus of why we play the game. We are not exactly interested in playing a Norrathian simulation; we play for fun. Stump Watch and it's cousins are not fun. They still, if they haven't, need to re-tool our quests, and possibly some of the others (I hear Paladin's have it worse).

Verant DID deliver on it's promise of class specific quests; they just missed an opportunity to score a huge win.

I've been going over this stuff for a week now and wondering why some of these choices were made. I still have no answers, and I am loathe to sink to the level of some who will claim there is favoritism to rangers (not you guys, just something else someone likes to say).

A guildmate, a paladin by trade, sent McQuaid a letter by email talking baout some issues with the paladins after the quests started. He recieved a short 4 line reply that pretty much missed the point. If one looks at historical references, literature and games, and even logic then one can easily get an idea of where things should stand and how it should all boil down.

As far as classes go, warriors are the most versatile and most skilled with their weapons and armor. This makes sense. They don't spend time skulking in forests or reading holy texts for enlightenment. They also have no real restrictions on armament or armor as they just want to kick tail.

Paladins are heavy fighters. They wear cumbersome plate armor. This makes them slow and offsets that benefit of armor a little bit. For the most part, at least in EQ, they wield heavier weapons because their armor prevents the freedom of movement for dual wielding or kicking. They get bash as a trade off for the kick though. You get the idea.

Rangers are lightly armored. I kind of got the idea of considering them like infantry. Damn good fighters, and very versatile, but they can't wear that heavy armor cause it would bog them down. They get the dual wield as they are free to move, and they get kick instead of bash. The freedom of movement offsets the benefits they would have gotten for heavier armor.

I'd have to say, and I'm likely to mess this up, that shamen are sort of the lightly armored and more versatile (as far as weapons and spells at least) version of a cleric. The above arguments for paladins and rangers can be twisted to suit this comparison.

Clerics of course are healers, and heavy plate wearers. Great heals means less versatility in other spells (still awe struck at some of our spells though) and or course heavy armor so we can stand our ground with resolution and defiance.

Just breaking the classes down so simply we can see how the AC values on the class armor don't quite fit. Everyone got some odd stats that don't fit of course, but I'm sticking to the AC for now.

If this stuff replaces rubicite, then shouldn't it have all started with the ac for rubi? It might have I suppose. But then someone seems to have gotten the idea that to add stats, you take away ac. Hardly seems right when the bards had stats and rubi ac was left alone. Warrior armor is great for the warriors, and they should have the highest ac of course. The other four seem to stick out like a sore thumb though. It looks like they were designed by four different people who didn't talk and lived in different states. *smiles slightly*

Now I am sure they were trying to balance it out by the fact that we have spells which raise AC by huge amounts and hitpoints as well. Oddly enough, rangers, shaman, and paladins all have spells that add to ac though. Maybe not as much (good lord though those shaman can raise practically everything else too. hehe), but they still get a bonus to their ac. So maybe they added more ac to classes that didn't have the best buffs. But then that would decrease the reasons to group, wouldn't it?

Well, there's all manner of ways to look at this and reason, and go backward and sideways. Unfortunately we can't know for sure what the people who made it were thinking until someone sends an email asking exactly that. I guess we just need to ask the reasoning behind this.

Of course the stats are another story. Maybe they think we try to min/max our primary stat too much. Personally I like some agility and I could use more strength to carry that stuff (which reminds me that if the ranger stuff has such a high ac, I think our stuff shouldn't weigh so much more than it). If one looks at the stats for various armor and compares them though, clerics just some pretty odd stat raises. Don't get me wrong, I like Int as much as anyone who raises their skills, but I just don't get it. Sure would have been nice to get +3 wis on the chest plate or greaves. Or maybe a strength boost on the chest plate instead. I'm far too baffled by this area of it to even offer suggestions why they do it this way.

There's definately something odd going on here. And I find it interesting that cleric armor was in on Test before the others. But for some reason it just doesn't jive with pre conceived notions of being a cleric or a plate wearer. I sure hope someone asks verant for a rational in this soon. At least then we'd have something that we could not at and either grudgingly agree or disagree with rather than just calling it bad.

I can only wonder what a quest armor for druids would have looked like. Probably pink and raise charisma. *smiles and winks* Paladins definately got the shaft though. I would still like to at least hear what color the paladin armor is.
