August 13

The "P" Word

Yesterday was interesting to say the least.  On one hand, there are 427 submissions as of the time I wrote this.  On the other, what happened to my peaceful message board?  No, don't answer, I think I can figure that one out.

Some people really like the petition.  Others think that it is a worthless whine that has no point whatsoever.  I'd even bet that some of those who voted closed their eyes and held their nose when they clicked submit.  Just for kicks I checked out a few message boards and saw the same dichotomy; one player, a rogue, felt that it was worthless because I included the med key request.  Another responded to him and felt that issue was worthwhile (along with a number of other points).  On this board a wizard thought everything was fine except for Undead burns (he didn't pick on Retribution).  A magician didn't like our encroachment of bandages, but had no problems with the rest.  A druid felt that the whole thing was pointless.  You pick any topic on the list and I'll find you a minimum of two opinions.  I have no idea if I properly picked the majority opinion on each item in the list; I thought I did at the time the list was finalized.  At this point I can only point to the submission count in defense of the whole.

August 3

More on the petition

I'm very happy to see all the discussion concerning the petition.  Between the message board, private e-mails directly to me (which are always welcome) and the EQ Cleric e-mail list the ideas are flying hot and heavy.  In the meantime, between work, reading and responding to the different posts and trying to get my poor character a new item I haven't had time to work on anything new.  Once the petition is done I'll put some new changes and additions into place.

While I'm on the topic of the petition I would like to state that it is extremely unlikely that everyone will be happy with everything on the petition.  I expect it would be a true statement that it's doubtful that anyone will be happy with everything on it.  The question you should ask yourself is: "Can I support everything on this even though some of the items are unimportant or that I disagree (mildly) with?"  

The biggest disagreement is over high-level DD damage.  Look, we're talking about 10%.  In my opinion neither side should have a problem with this issue.  If it wasn't on the list um, it's 10%.  If we don't get a new spell at 49, well, how bad is that really? Lots of clerics are hitting 50 in spite of that lack so things can't be that bad.  For those of you who are against it, hey, can't you support this increase?  The vast majority of high-level clerics feel that a lot more than a mere restoration is in order, so is it such a hard thing for you to support their getting a piddling 10% back?  For the record I'd prefer to leave it on because the majority of high-level clerics are in favor of it.  Until I've fought their mobs I'd prefer to reserve judgement so I'll support theirs.

The name of this game is compromise.  You want Summoned Bandages at level 5?  Then support your level 44 brothers on their damage spells.  Nobody is going to be happy with everything on the petition, including myself.  However, I will promise that every item will be carefully thought out and presented in a reasonable manner as befitting our class. You may not agree with it, but you will not be embarrassed to support it.

I never intended to make this an editorial, but it came pouring out once I started writing it.  Please let me know what you think, but better yet, post it on the board so that all may comment.

Rayne

August 2

Concerning the petition

I expect to put up a draft for the petition sometime tomorrow, but definitely no later than Wednesday.  Here's a partial list of things I expect to put in it:

  • Remove the stun effect from Divine Aura
  • God-specific low, medium, and high-level quests that yield god specific spells and items.  This would add meaning to the religion aspect in a relatively easy way for Verant to implement as well as give our class some quests of interest.
  • Give us low, medium, and high-level Area Effect Undead spells.
  • Review the undead spells we currently have in order to ensure that we are the equal of any class when casting on undead mobs.  (Note: If I'm off target on this one please let me know.  We should be at least as good as any other class at the same level against undead opponents in my opinion.  Frankly I think we should be a little better, but that's another issue).
  • Review our high level DD spells.  If upping the damage on Retribution isn't a possibility can we have a new spell at level 49?
  • Review the higher level hammer spells (the level 9 spell is fine) for potential upgrades.  They are currently less than useful.
  • Add a meditation key or make meditation automatically kick in when opening the spell book.

I will be reviewing old e-mails and this message board for other excellent ideas that I may have missed.  Please post any thoughts you have to the board; I do review it on a regular basis.

Rayne
