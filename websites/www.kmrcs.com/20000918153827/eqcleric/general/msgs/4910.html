I think you misunderstood my argument. I'm not saying druids suck, but am saying that clerics fill a role that neither a druid nor shaman can replace.

: The truth is, comparing Druids and Clerics is the old apples and oranges cliche. Sure, Verant considers Druids a, "Healing Class," but the healing is only a portion of what they are about. A very small portion.

I agree wholeheartedly. A druid is a great addition to a group at any level. I loved playing a druid and think they're one of the best classes in the game. But the point of the post, as I said above, is to show that druid's can only be a support class for the healing duties after 34th. They are still valuable group members, they just can't be the primary healer.

: But if we were to buy into your comparison we could look at a few things you say.

Scrutinize all you'd like

: You say that DoT spells are useless in group situations with other casters. I really beg to differ on this one. Obviously, at some point in the game you must have been in a group that was swarmed over by multiple MOBs. Sure the wizards can nuke, but a healthy dose of DoTs on the mobs that aren't under direct scrutiny of the Nukers certainly helps to soften up the rest of the attacker. And the average DoT is so much more mana efficient than any standard DD spell that they can be cast and still leave mana for other stuff.

Reread the post. At no point did I say DoTs were useless. I said DoTs were great for kiting, not so hot for group work. There are certainly occasions when a dot is mana efficient in groups and can even be a lifesaver. But I find that they aren't that great for grouping. I say this as someone who's played a druid and as someone who plays a cleric.

: OK, druids lose out on healing in later levels. I admit that. I haven't seen any complaints on my Druid site about it, like you claim, but maybe I am just blind.

This would have to be the case if you're a regular reader of The Druid's Grove. I can't count how many posts I've read saying that Druids needed a form of superior healing, otherwise they were useless in groups after 35th. They also complained about the inefficacy of Combust and Starfire because FR is so high on 35+ mobs. They started a petition and everything. The petition got Verant to change Ice from an area effect spell to a DD spell that lowers fire resistance and this placated them somewhat. But the lack of a better healing spell is a sore point for them. Just last week a druid posted in reply to me saying that Druids needed a better healing spell. If you think this isn't a common complaint for high level druids, I'd have to say either you don't read many message boards, or you don't pay attention to detail.

Anyway. What we do have is regeneration spells. Pack regeneration. Chloroplast. Ok, so maybe they don't heal the emergency situation, but they do help prevent the emergency situation from even happening.

Great spells for reducing downtime. Clerics love druids because it means we don't have to heal everyone to full health after a fight, med some more and then fight. Not sure about the preventing emergency situations, though. Healing 10 points a tick won't be the difference in many fights when the mobs are double hitting/dual-wielding for 119. But there are those occasional "skin of your teeth fights" where it could be the difference between winning and losing.

: And when it comes to emergency situations isn't it nice to know that your Druid can always cast a Succor and evac the party to safety instead of having to resurrect the dead guys in those situations when your group is in over its head?

Of course it's great.

: And who doesn't beg for SoW every chance they get? And then there's the bit about group teleports, but I digress...

I don't beg for sow. I have jboots now so no need for sow. Even before jboots I never asked for sow unless I had to pull for the party. My theory is (having played a druid), if being constantly sow'd when it's not essential to the party is so important to you, then you should have made a shaman or druid. But that's just me.

: What I am getting at, is any class is viable if played to its advantages properly. Druids have a very unique place in that they can take over many roles for many different situations. Are they better than clerics? Hmmmm. Won't answer that question. Don't think there is even an argument.

I think you just need to read my post more carefully. The two main ideas are that "Druids and Shaman are no substitute for a cleric after 34th" and "Clerics are just as valuable as a Druid or Shaman before 34th" in response to some moronic posts to the contrary down below. You seem to have misread it as saying Druids are worthless or that Druids have no place in a group. That's not what I was saying. Be sure to have the point of a post straight before you go about disproving it ;)
