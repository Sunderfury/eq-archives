: : First of all INT is the primary stat for ANY skill increase Check....Wisdom being the secondary stat once you fail the INT check...so a high INT equals faster skill maxing. Stamina is not just for straight HP..it effects numerous other game check areas. AC is important but to give up a stat increase for AC is to ignore the myriad of areas where stats effect in a trickle down. (bless yourself with your spells for AC/HP boost)

My suggestions would not be so generic...How you distribute your starting points depends heavily on the race you initially chose and an understanding of our role at the higher levels. My lvl 50 cleric doesnt even carry a weapon and rarely if Ever engages in melee ( Paw and Testement in each hand respectively) A an easy example of point allocation: Dwarf 25 points to Wis, 5 Points to Agility. Reason: Max WIS for max mana/ better secondary skill check %. +5 agility for more AC. Str isnt a dwarf concern( more than enough for any armour) Dex/Sta agian not a dwarf concern. Int is enough to max all skills by the 2nd bub of exp in a new level ( assuming they are worked evenly) and Charisma although important early on to make the most coin and to establish faction in some quest/city area , it can be overcome but smart playing (killing the right mobs to balance the faction issue)
