OK... poor choice of words... What I mean is that
a level 29 is more prepared (more mana, more HP,
higher skills, additional spells) to venture into
more dangerous territory. While the level 24 is
relegated to skulking at the border, the 29 can
effectively venture inside, while maintaining a
relatively same "danger potential" as the 24 at
the border. The lower level creatures at the zone
are as much of a threat to the lower level player
as the higher level creatures inside are to the
higher level player.

: This game definitely rewards 'zone border campers'. Since, IMO, the key to levelling is not getting killed (as opposed to getting *great* xp), I've almost completely stopped going deep into dungeons unless I have a great party. I much prefer to stay in outdoor areas now - the xp and certainly loot may not be as good, but the risk of death is greatly reduced and in the long run, I come out ahead.

Well, there are two ways of looking at this...
You can't really camp a lot just sitting at the
zone. You can park your team at the zone and PULL
there, which isn't really camping the zone, it's
exploiting the geography. I like South Ro for this:
you can't get too close to the Oasis border, or
the mobs will simply turn and run away. While
this is the same as zoning to escape danger, it's
really just an exaggerated example of this point.
By sitting at that zone area, all you will get is
the occasional mummy, tarantula or puma. You won't
ever get any of the more interesting wandering
mobs that are further into the zone, unless you
or someone else kites it back to the zone.

I have to admit, a naked high-level person is not
a lot better than an equipped low or mid-level
person, except for any spells they might have.
That didn't figure much into my calculations, but
it IS a big factor I suppose. But then I did say
"to a certain degree."

To me there is a big difference between pulling
nasty mobs to the border, and fighting deep in a
dungeon. My experience is rather limited, being
only level 14, but Befallen, for example... I just
can't see how you COULD camp the border of a place
like that. I don't think either of us are talking
about waiting for trains. :) But in Befallen,
you really have to go carve out a place to sit and
fight, because fighting your way down is either
too rough for people of reasonable level (10-15),
or simply results in lots of trains. At the same
time, if you can make your way to one of the rooms
on the second level, you can more or less sit in
that room and catch all the spawns without worrying
about anything, until someone pulls a train past
you.

I still posit that a person that a person of just
about any class that can venture deep into a zone
or dungeon and lose a CLOSE fight (the key is that
they're fighting roughly same-level mobs) will
still have a much easier time getting back to their
corpse than a whole GROUP of players 5-10 levels
lower, even though that same group might have
roughly the same (or likely better) combat potency
as a solo high-level character, even naked. If
not, the higher-level player might also have access
to friends or guildmembers of a higher level to
assist in a CR... a lot more low-level characters
are just more lambs for the slaughter.

I can see both sides of this... but I think that
largely we differ on the definition of "zone
camping." But with that distinction out of the
way, I don't think we're too far apart. The game
is largely slanted toward the border campers who
gain more slowly, but with fewer casualties. I
personally get more enjoyment out of dying deep in
the bowels of a subterranean crypt, but then I
guess I'm a glutton for punishment.
