: Lydia -- I'm sure you mean well... but a few facts to consider:

: 1. Nonclerics can't camp for the armor and sell it -- it's no drop. We're not talking ranger or bard armor here, we're talking cleric. Cleric is NO DROP. Period. No selling will be happening.

: 2. Other classes got armor that helps their primary purposes... Bards get charisma (look at the bracers, wow!), Rangers got a helm that is awesome, Warriors also got some pretty nifty stat increases... Clerics? Poison save? Disease save? Intelligence? Why no wisdom?

: 3. The other classes got spawns that generally are fairly quick -- I joined a ranger group to help them out, we got 4 rangers a completed quest in less than an hour. Let me repeat that: FOUR Rangers in ONE group EACH completed the same quest in less than an hour. They are working together and having a blast! Clerics? We won't even talk to each other because someone else might spot the bag and then we've blown 5 and a half hours of sitting!

: 4. Most of us picture quests being something we can do in a group, all gain experience, and get something decent at the end. For example: To complete our rangers' quest, we had to hunt a brownie that was a challenge to the group -- and we got experience... Then we had to kill Meldrath and his guards -- and we all got tons of experience (since he spawns about every 5 minutes). This was a good quest -- and we all had FUN (my god, something unheard of in this game for clerics -- please don't tell Verant). Now contrast this with the quest to spend 6 or 7 hours staring at a stump in Kithicor... No group will enjoy this, there is NO experience to be had, no treasures, no grand adventure... just sitting and staring. Granted, we're now staring at a stump instead of our spellbook, but the change in scenery isn't enough.
