Yield Rule Revisited - A Big Thumbs Down

I ran into my first ugly camping situation last night.  Unlike every prior experience I had, my party and I were the higher levels.  The spawn was a rather rare spawn (1 every hour or 2), and drops a decent item (rare drop).  To make a long story short, the lower level player (2 lower than me - I was low man in our group) was there first, and insisted he was staying there and taking all the spawns until he got the item he desired.  He invoked the Yield Rule (we laughed and explained it wasn't in effect), he called us kill stealers (hadn't actually killed anything yet), elaborated on how long he had been there, etc (had a hard time feeling sorry for someone sporting a Paw of Opolla).  We felt he should rotate the spawn.  The situation was eventually resolved and we left with what we came to acquire (we traded for it), but if that's the future of the Yield Rule I want no part of it.

September 12

High-level camping revisited

For those of you who have no idea what I'm referring to please scroll down to the item posted on the 10th.

I've read the posts, both for and against the proposed (?) new policy from Verant and have been attempting to devise a solution.

The problem as I see it is both a lack of itemization and odd item placement.  Let me give an example from Permafrost.  The Goblin Preacher, a level 24/25 mob, drops the Runed Circlet, +4 Wis/Int, AC4.  This mob is easily taken by a balanced group in their low 20s.  High Priest Zaharn, a level 30 mob (give or take one) drops Zaharn's Coronet, +3 Str, +25 Mana, AC 5, and to acquire the item requires a group around 30 because the King floor spawn is much tougher to break.  In my opinion the Circlet, an item easier to attain, is a better long term item (for a cleric) due to the fact it enhances a prime attribute and thus yields more mana in the long term.  Furthermore, I'm not sure I will replace it.  There are a few items which grant +5 Wisdom (or the equivalent), but all have less AC and may not be worth the trouble to acquire.  Where is the +8 Wisdom, AC 10 Item that 45th+ level Cleric would desire?

Exacerbating the problem more are the one of a kind items such as the Journeyman Boots, obtained from Drelzna, a high 20s mob in Najena.  I have read (unfortunately I do not have a pair) that the speed is somewhat less than SOW.  Why isn't there a pair of Traveler's Boots (which grants a speed greater enough than the Journeyman's Boots to make them desirable) that comes from a mid 40s level mob?  Before high-level players are denied access to items (the yield rule effectively does that) they should be given the opportunity to acquire drops from mobs appropriate to their level.

Here is a potential solution:

  1. For every nice mid-level item there should be a desirable high-level replacement.  This can be something between the mid-level item and items from the Plane of Fear.
  2. These items should be placed on mobs of the appropriate levels.
  3. Re-tune the Plane of Fear to make it a little more accessible to the high-level players.  This also helps spread the players among the zones.
  4. Once the first three are done, then implement and enforce the yield rule.  It would be fair at that point, and hopefully there would be much less need for it.

A further suggestion is they might consider adding the new items on random mobs, similar to the way the Box of Abu Kar is obtained.  This would have the added bonus of making every high level kill exciting.  While I can appreciate the reasons behind fixed encounters, the game would benefit from an added sense of surprise.

As always what do you think?  Please post your comments on the message board.

September 10

High-level camping

The EQ Vault has posted a message by Brad McQuaid confirming that high-level camping of items is no longer allowed if:

  • The mob does not yield experience; and
  • A lower leveled group who would get experience wishes to hunt the mob.

An example of this is if a group in their low 20s is hunting the Goblin Preacher in Permafrost a level 50 Druid (to use a real example) may not come in and kill the Preacher.  If he does, he may be warned, suspended, and/or banned.  Another example is if that same level 50 Druid is there (or any level that would no long gain experience from killing the Preacher) and a group that would get experience arrives, the first player must leave.  

I must admit that I have mixed feelings about this.  My first thought was "great, at least the high levels can't take our kills anymore."  My second was, "Hmm, I'm getting up there myself."  My third was "What if someone petitions about me and it isn't true?"

In general, I believe this is a good thing for the game.  We've all read posts about high level wizards sitting invisible waiting for Drelzna to spawn.  However, enforcing it properly will be difficult at best, and quite honestly may not be possible.

What do you think?  Please post your comments on the message board.

September 8

The boredom issue and a call for help

Many of the negative posts mentioned below are about the boredom of the heal/med/buff/med routine.  The basic complaint is we spend a large amount of time not seeing the game.  Keep in mind, all casting classes have this problem; we are not unique in this regard.

So I've been trying to solve it.  I was very proud yesterday and thought I had the answer.  Since the complaint is we can't see while meditating, why not change that?  Just break off the meditation function from the spell book.  I even wrote it up, but then thought what if Verant wants us to be in danger?  Isn't that the purpose of the Sentinel spell?  Help me here.  All casters spend a lot of time (more than not) looking at the same spells.  I've often thought dancing girls would be nice, but that would get old also (not to mention some of the other issues :P).  How can Verant keep us casters more engaged in the game while not affecting the balance?  Please post your comments on the message board. 

A word about Clerics

I saw a post on the board today that really made me feel bad.  A current cleric recommended this site to a friend who was interested in playing a cleric to learn more about the class.  After reading the board the friend decided the cleric class was just awful.  Now while I understand the need to vent (I too have this need as one of the most negative posts was by me), it shouldn't become the defining hallmark of our class.  If nothing is granted in our petition, we still don't have it all that bad; that was recognized in the petition.  I've since seen some positive posts on the board and I ask for more.  Our class is by far the most in demand class of them all, and we need to remember this.  I thank those who have posted their own positive thoughts.
