Interesting.

I have never before heard of a cleric having a
hard time getting a group. Every place I've ever
been clerics get snapped up with the speed of
light (assuming the group isn't full). This has
become more true as I go up in levels.

I've NEVER been in a single group who would rather
have a druid or shaman than a cleric. The only
time I've seen one of those classes chosen over a
cleric is when there is already a cleric in the
group.

I think others here have had the right idea that
too often druids and shaman do not feel a niche
well. Even more than us hybrids (I'm a paladin)
they try to play the jack of all trades. Now this
is not to say that there aren't good druids/shaman
who know when to burn, dot, damage shield, buff,
heal, etc. A good shaman knows how to stagger
buffs so that he's not OOM when healing is needed,
etc. On the average though, higher level clerics
seem to know their jobs better than druids and
shaman.

I truly am shocked that you've had a problem
getting groups. Places I've been where people
shout for a group, clerics shout the shortest
amount of time by a large margin.

I know the gem cost for the symbol line sucks (and
I'm just casting Transal at the moment). At 30th
level, I carry 120+ cat's eye agate and cast
transal on my party members unless there is a
real need for Ryltan, etc.

I'm sorry you're not satisfied with your class
choice. I know that a GOOD cleric is a pleasure
to work with and is always much in demand. I think
being a cleric is more complicated than most
people ever think and it is easier to fail as a
cleric (there is quite a list of clerics I will
NEVER group with again) than just about any class.

I do not think clerics should get teleport - I
think the set of cleric spells is quite good and
the cleric is extemely useful and versatile.

I'm really not sure what class would make you
happy. We tanks often have a hard time getting a
group, as do wizards. Maybe try a druid or shaman?
