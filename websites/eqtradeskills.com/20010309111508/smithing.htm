Okay these are just the basic recipes found in the basic blacksmithing guide.  I am still working on trivial levels for these so any input would be GREATLY appreciated.  The following is just a basic guide and what I did on my long journey to becoming a master smith.  I did this in Kelethin too, a long hike to get your supplies so if you want to minimize your running time, I would suggest doing your smithing in Freeport as everything you need is within 2 zones.  

When starting off as with any other trade skill you may want to start, you should practice at your guildmaster for the first 21 points in the skill, this will save you A LOT of money in the long run.  Also as with anything else the higher WIS you have will give you more successes and the INT you have will make your skill go up faster.

There are 3 books available to smiths and if the rumor of holding the book in your inventory or having it opened while you smith, it may help.  They are as follows:

Armor and Weapons guide

Dirge of the FireForge

Okay with all that out of the way, this is where we begin our adventure.  We start with metal bits.  Get used to making these as almost all the recipes for smithing require metal bits.  You may want to make large batches with this being the case.  Have at least one backpack free and fill it with 80 pieces of ore (stackable) and 40 FLASKS of water bought from an inn or other vendor.  DO NOT USE SUMMONED WATER as this will not work.  With that completed your backpack should look like this:

Now click and drag 2 pieces of ore from your backpack, and 1 flask of water into the forge like this:

 

Remember you can select one item at a time on a stack of items by holding down the CTRL key.  This will save your wrists trust me!  Also remember that you CANNOT stack items in the forge, they have to be in separate slots.

  + + =

Now when you click combine on the forge, you should get the phrase "The item is trivial for you to make."  This is of course you pumped the first 21 skill points in from your guildmaster.  If you did not do this, then I believe they become trivial at 21.  Some guides have you start with sharpening rusty weapons BAH!! This is a waste of time and they become trivial before 30.  Plus all the time it takes you to gather those rusty weapons really isn't worth it.  Starting off with bits will not make your skill go up but do not worry because you will be making TONS of these little buggers during your smithing career.  Once you make the bits place them into your backpack.

Repeat doing this until your backpack is empty of ore and made into metal bits.  This is the most boring and tedious part of smithing so get used to it early.  Your wrists and back will hurt after doing a few backpacks of these things but hey, it pays off in the long run.

*** UPDATE!!! ***

Toolboxes appear to have been fixed!  This means that you can make them very easily as they require no molds and they will go trivial at 51!!  I believe you could sell them for close to 7GP but of course the trade skill gods nerfed it so it is no longer profitable to make and sell them.

 

Okay here is where we start making the real stuff.  This is where all those metal bits you just made come into play.  Depending on how many bits you made, go back and buy that many flasks of water.  After that you need to find a merchant that sells SCALAR molds.  Buy as many as you can carry as you will need to make a few trips to use all these bits.  Molds DO NOT stack they will take up A LOT of room in your inventory.

Now put the following into the forge: 1 scalar mold, 1 metal bit, 1 flask of water.

+ + = and

This is the funny thing I have never figured out and never bothered asking about, I thought this item was just a bug and the large container mold you get out of it sells for over 1 GP  hehe go figure.  If you are successful you get a large container mold and a file out of it.  If you fail you only get a file.  At least you don't lose everything  *grin*  Keep doing this until your inventory is full or you run out of scalar molds.  Go sell all your goodies and buy more molds!  When you run out of metal bits, go make more and keep making scalars until they become trivial at 41.  YEAH!! Step two is done and you are ready to move on to the next item, lanterns.

If you have made it this far, you are doing well!  Most smiths quit by this time and I am sure you can see why (how are those wrists doing?)  By now you know the drill, go make a backpack of metal bits and head to the local tavern to pick up some bottles. (make sure you aren't buying red wine like I did OOPS!)   Also grab some water flasks to match the number of metal bits you have in your backpack.  You will now need to find lantern molds.  For locations of the molds, check out the smithing ingredient locations.  Now put 1 metal bit, 1 lantern mold, 1 bottle, and 1 flask of water into the forge.

+ +  + =

Lanterns are nice to make because it leaves more room in your inventory open so you won't be running around so much and they sell for more than 2 gp and this is the quickest stint of your smithing journey.  Going from 41 to 68 goes fairly quick and you make a decent amount of money as well.  Lanterns become trivial at 68 so we go to our next piece, skewers!

By this time you are over halfway to becoming a master at smithing and during this phase, (the longest ugh!) will be the phase that you achieve your master status yeah!!  By this time you can make studs (trivial at 41) and bonings very soon after (trivial at around 85).  You probably won't see too many tailors asking for these but the occasional one will.  You could even attempt at making some banded armor but I would advise against it as you will fail quite a bit and waste a lot of money at this point.  By now you are making metal bits with almost no failures and of course you are going to need a ton more for this phase so get out there and get your supplies loaded up!  Like all the others you have done so far you need 1 metal bit, 1 skewer mold, and 1 flask of water.

+ + =

Skewers will take you from 68 to 115 so you know what that means  MASTER SMITH BABY!!!  By this time you will have forearms of steel and be a well deserved master smith!  But believe it or not there is still more!  Once these babys become trivial at 115 it's time to move on up to pots.  You can make the basic banded items now as they are trivial but I still pressed on to decrease my failures and save a little dough (hehe I am a cheap bastard rogue).  This stage is really easy to go through and it will increase your success rate on a lot of the banded so here we go with pots.

Pots are very simple, the same basics as with everything else you have made.  You need 1 metal bit, 1 pot mold, 1 standing legs mold, and 1 flask of water.

+ + + =

Pots become trivial at 122 so you can see why this stage goes so fast  =)  The next item is the last standard item you can make to raise your skill.   After that you will have to make banded pieces to raise it past 135 and that will be expensive.  Anyway, from 122 to 135 we will be making sewing kits!  They of course require 1 metal bits, 1 needle mold, 1 thimble mold, and 1 flask of water.

+ + + =

With all that done, you will need to make banded cloaks, mantles, and gloves to increase your skill to the maximum you can have thus far in the game.  168 is the magic number which makes it look hopeful that we may eventually be able to make some kind of plate armor in the future WOO HOO!!  My guess would be in the new expansion pack coming out this summer but you never know!  You deserve a HUGE pat on the back as you have invested MANY hours at this skill and sprained your wrists about 5 times by now  *grin*  Continue on with the banded guide below.
