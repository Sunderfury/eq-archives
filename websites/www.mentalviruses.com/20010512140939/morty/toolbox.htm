Tricks of the Toolbox
Making mystical machines for fun and... well, fun

There was once a time, and it was not so long ago, when the civilized races of Norrath laughed at us gnomes for our tinkering. Yes, they laughed! But NOW, my friend, things are different! Now... well, they still laugh, but there are a couple of tinkered items that are somewhat useful so the laughing is more of a snickering.

Tinkering is a fine gnomish pursuit with a long and troubled history. Before getting too far into the nuts and bolts of things, one would do well to keep a few things in mind.

Rumors abound regarding what can and cannot be made, whether certain items even exist, and how difficult it is to advance in skill. Remember that before you start throwing hundreds of platinum on components you've never heard of.

There's another important point that, to the best of my knowledge, no one has commented on. Tinkering is properly the realm of the gnomish Rogue. Warriors come in a close second. This is worth noting point by point:

  • Any gnome can tinker, but spellcasting gnomes can easily duplicate through magic many of the effects of tinkered items. No magician is going to spend 18 pp on a Stalking Probe, no wizard is going to spend 25 pp for a Spyglass, and no spellcaster is going to spend 250 pp for an Aqualung. All of these things, however, could be handy to a Rogue or Warrior.
  • Rogues are the only class that can use every single tinkered item, specifically including the Standard Bow Cam and the Mechanized Lockpicks. (The exception to this is the Gem Cutter, which no one seems able to use.) Warriors can use everything but the Lockpicks.
  • More than anyone else, mid to high level Rogues can afford to buy the components.

That being said, the process of tinkering isn't particularly difficult, and can be broken down into six easy steps.

Visit Steamfont and Ak'Anon.

Nearly everything you need to start out your tinkering career can be acquired from Bom Knotwood in Steamfont. When you get to extremely advanced tinkering, Rylin Coil in Ak'Anon can give you some further direction.

Buy the books.

The books are cheap and entertaining, and they contain all of the recipes you'll need. Every gnome should be proud to have the complete Journals of Gimblestan in their library.

Buy a Toolbox.

The toolbox is the centerpiece of tinkering. You can't tinker without one. It can also be a handy container, as it's fairly light and you can fit nearly anything into it, as long as you're careful not to combine its contents by accident in a fit of tinkering frenzy.

Spend all of your money on components.

Unless you're limiting yourself to Animated Bait, you're going to be spending quite a lot of platinum just to get the fiddlybits that you'll need to even attempt a tinkered item. Most components can be found with Bom, and the rest are common items found in many places.

Combine the components in the Toolbox.

Follow the recipe for what you're trying to make. Place each item in the toolbox, making sure that nothing is stacked. When all of the items are in the toolbox, invoke the Spirit of Gimblestan and Combine. It's just that easy.

Cackle or curse.

Either you'll be left with your newfangled invention, or you'll be kicking and screaming and generally unpleasant. Such is the fate of the gnome.

"Watch out! I've got a toolbox, and I'm not afraid to use it!"
