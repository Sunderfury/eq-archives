The First Circle of the Dark Arts
for Necromancers of 1 Level

Cavorting Bones
being a Conjuration of 15 mana requiring 1 bone chip

Summons an undead skeleton to do the Necromancer's bidding.

The first in a long series of spells raising the dead to do your bidding. This is the defining art of the Necromancer, and wise use of the skeletal pet cannot be overemphasized.

Coldlight
being a Conjuration of 15 mana

Summons a Coldstone to provide light.

Yes, you've got good night vision. Yes, you'll have a much better light source soon. You could live without this spell... but sooner or later, you'll be running through a dark tunnel trying to retrieve your well-lit body, and you'll be happier if you can plunk one of these things out of the air. Trust me.

Disease Cloud
being a Conjuration of 10 mana

Afflicts the target with disease, doing a small amount of damage over a period of time.

The initial damage is inconsequential; the lingering damage will negate the regenerative abilities of most creatures for several levels. Disease is your friend. Praise Bertoxxulous!

Invisibility versus Undead
being a Divination of 40 mana

Renders the target invisible to most undead.

Note the word "most." Some powerful undead aren't fooled. Your pet will always be fooled, however, and will promptly turn to dust. Be aware that this spell tends to blink out, without warning, after an unpredictable period of time - typically when you need it most.

Lifetap
being an Alteration of 9 mana

Drains a small amount of health from the target and grants it to the Necromancer.

This is the first in the series of "drain you and heal me" spells. A nice addition to combat, but too expensive to be the only form of attack. When you're poisoned or diseased, though, this spell and its kin can save your hide. The world is your snack bar.

Locate Corpse
being a Divination of 5 mana

Points the Necromancer towards the nearest corpse. If a target is selected, this will point the Necromancer towards the nearest corpse of the target.

If a Necromancer needs help finding their own corpse, it's a professional disgrace. Get this one early. This is also one of the few spells that other people occasionally need, so here's your chance to make a useful contribution to society (if you're into that sort of thing) or mock the dead (which is much more entertaining: "Hey, this is still fresh. Are you gonna eat this?").

Minor Shielding
being an Abjuration of 10 mana

Provides the Necromancer with increased maximum health and an armor class bonus.

Here's the first of another line of spells. Unless you're feeling completely safe (and I know that I never do) you'll want to keep your best shielding spell up at all times.

Reclaim Energy
being a Conjuration of 5 mana

Destroys the Necromancer's summoned pet and returns a portion of the mana used to summon it.

The pet needs to be your target and within the range of the spell. It's easier and faster just to tell your pet to get lost, but if your pet isn't responding to commands for some reason, this can be handy. At lower levels you probably won't notice the amount of mana you get back; at level 16, I got half a bubble, which could be worthwhile.

Sense the Dead
being a Divination of 5 mana

Points the Necromancer towards the nearest undead being, not including the Necromancer's pet.

Even though it ignores your own pet, it will find the pets of other Necromancers, so if there are a lot of your dark brethren around don't expect this to be very helpful. Aside from that, it's a handy thing that any Necromancer should be able to do. Useful when you're on the hunt for bone chips.

Siphon Strength
being an Alteration of 5 mana

Drains a small amount of strength from the target and grants it to the Necromancer.

Anything that makes the enemy weaker and you stronger is a good thing. You may or may not notice any difference in the target's combat ability, but the personal strength boost is nice. Rumor has it that repeated castings will continue to drain the target's strength, but I haven't verified that.
