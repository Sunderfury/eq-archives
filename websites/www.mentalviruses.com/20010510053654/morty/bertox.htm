Hail Bertoxxulous
A few words about following the Plaguebringer

The gnome Necromancer is a servant of Bertoxxulous - there can be no other. This is well and good, but the wise Necromancer understands that there is a different between being the Plaguebringer's servant and being his victim.

Little has been written concerning Bertoxxulous and those who follow him. The oft-quoted description goes something like this:

"Followers of Bertoxxulous believe the only truth on Norrath is that everything dies. They view the decay of flesh as a thing of ultimate beauty. The subtle purples of a fresh bruise, the almost irridescent yellow green of an infested pustule, are but a few of the things that His followers relish. It is not suprising that many of His followers pursue the dark art of Necromancy, for to them nothing is more desirable than to be surrounded by beings who, even in unlife, continue to rot and decay. Do not take this to mean that His followers are suicidal or seek a quick death. To the contrary, they wish to live long, painful lives, spreading their dark, diseased stain across all of Norrath."

Well, this is true as far as it goes. The deeper truth is that the decay and disease that are the hallmarks of the Plaguebringer are, in truth, merely stripping away that which is weak - namely, the flesh. The power of necromancy is the power of the spirit that is beyond death, to which power the frail blossom of life is but a passing moment.

Decay and disease are our tools, and we should certainly use those tools to bring about death and the transcendence which follows. We would be wise not to lose sight of the true goal by focusing on the implements which we use to attain it.

Similarly, the Necromancer should not hesitate to embrace death should the situation call for it. While an untimely demise can be a setback, we know that it is but a short-lived transition, and by no means permanent for ones such as ourselves. Know death well, the better to command it in others.

"All things die. This is the Truth of Bertoxxulous."
