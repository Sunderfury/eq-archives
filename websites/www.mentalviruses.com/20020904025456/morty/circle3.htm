The Third Circle of the Dark Arts
for Necromancers of 8 Levels

Bone Walk
being a Conjuration of 80 mana requiring 2 bone chips

Summons an undead skeleton to do the Necromancer's bidding.

This one bashes, and I swear I saw one parry once.

Dark Empathy
being an Alteration of 20 mana

Drains a small amount of health from the Necromancer and grants it to the target.

Lifetap in reverse. This allows you to heal a comrade in an emergency situation. Used in conjunction with Mend Bones, you can keep your pet in good fighting condition during melee.

Dark Pact
being an Alteration of 5 mana

Drains the Necromancer's health over time and increases mana regeneration.

I tend to use this when I'm travelling. It's also useful when you're in a group with enough warrior types so you won't be involved in melee. As you progress in power, you tend to use it more and more as mana becomes more valuable than health.

Deadeye
being a Divination of 35 mana

Grants the Necromancer infravision and the ability to see things that are otherwise invisible.

The infravision portion of the spell doesn't do much for a gnome.

Gather Shadows
being a Divination of 35 mana

Grants the Necromancer invisibility for a variable period of time.

Like the common Invisibility spell, this spell tends to expire when you need it most, although you'll usually get a warning just before you appear. I once thought that this spell also granted some invisibility to undead, but I must have been mistaken. Remember there are things that can see the invisible. I fondly remember this as the "Escape from Steamfont" spell.

Impart Strength
being an Alteration of 15 mana

Drains a small amount of strength from the Necromancer and grants it to the target.

Your comrades will appreciate the extra strength, and you can replenish what you've lost with Siphon Strength. But if someone else can boost strength, let them.

Lesser Shielding
being an Abjuration of 25 mana

Provides the Necromancer with increased maximum health, an armor class bonus, and increased Magic Resistance.

A distinct improvement over Minor Shielding. The catch here is that your local Necromancer guild might not carry all of the Shielding spells, and you'll need to find the appropriate spell merchant at the local Magician's guild if they'll sell to you. But it's worth the trouble.

Mend Bones
being an Alteration of 25 mana

Heals the pet of the Necromancer.

I use this to keep my pet going in combat if I'm not in there mixing it up myself. This lets me avoid dealing with things that are poisonous. The amount of healing seems to progress with your casting power, as it's still quite effective on higher level pets.

Shadow Step
being an Alteration of 10 mana

Teleports the Necromancer a short distance in a random direction.

When I first picked up this spell, I tried it outside of the gates of Qeynos. I teleported maybe 5 feet, repeatedly. I was not impressed, and forgot about the spell for a while. Later (around level 16) I started toying with it again, this time in the Karanas, and it usually whisked me a fair distance off. This became one of my standard "get me out of here" spells, but I eventually came to prefer Feign Death. Still, it has uses.

Vampiric Embrace
being an Alteration of 30 mana

Grants the Necromancer the chance of stealing health from an enemy in melee attacks.

I used to have a love/hate relationship with this spell. Since I like stepping into the combat and getting my hands dirty, the extra life-stealing burst is great. Problem is, when you first get this spell, it doesn't seem to happen very often and the effect doesn't last very long. At higher levels, though, it lasts for a while, triggers several times in a combat, and does more and more damage. I used Vampiric Embrace consistently in my high teens and twenties.

Ward Undead
being an Evocation of 30 mana

Hits the target undead for a fair amount of damage, usually doing some damage even if resisted.

The good news is that this strikes undead about four times harder than Life Spike - decaying skeletons go in one hit, standard skeletons in two or three - so this is your hardest-hitting spell at this level. The bad news is that this is your first Evocation spell (you won't get the next one until level 20) so your skill level is pathetic. Use this (or it's brethren) whenever you can to increase your skill. I use it when I'm on the never-ending quest for bone chips.
