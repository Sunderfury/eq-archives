The Calling of Power
Skills in the dark arts

Magical skills cover the prime abililties of the Necromancer, from raising the dead to meditation. They will be used very frequently, and as a result most of them should not necessitate spending any training points beyond the initial "purchase" of the skill. With the exception of Research, these skills are limited by level to a maximum of 5x(level+1).

Three magical skills are determinations of general magical ability. Mastery is essential.

Channeling
Level 1

Channeling is the ability to manipulate mana, and it is the core of spellcasting. Among other things, it determines the chances of successfully recovering from an interrupted spellcasting. This skill will rise quickly.

Meditation
Level 4

The Necromancer in meditation regains mana faster than by merely sitting. However, as meditation involves poring over one's spellbook, meditating Necromancers don't see much, although they can still hear what goes on around them. It should also be noted that meditating Necromancers make excellent targets, and creatures which would normally walk right by may decide to attack instead. Once acquired from the guildmaster, this skill will go up quickly through use.

Research
Level 16

When the Necromancer reaches the 16th level of power, good spells become increasingly difficult to find, and it becomes necessary to acquire spells through research. This skill makes that possible. Research is limited by level to a maximum of 5x(level-15).

The spells that form the foundation of our Incredible Necromantic Power are found in all five classes of magic, and the skill of the Necromancer will rise rapidly in these areas through use - with one exception, noted below.

Abjuration
Level 1

Spells of abjuration are protective in nature, typically through enhancing armor or boosting resistance. While the Necromancer does not have a very wide selection of these spells, the ones available are quite beneficial and will be used very frequently.

Alteration
Level 1

Most of the spells that the Necromancer uses are alterations of one form or another. This includes the majority of the combat spells, along with the spells we use to boost the abilities of our pets.

Conjuration
Level 1

The necromantic spells that summon the dead are all conjurations, as are the combat spells involving disease and poison. Even though conjurations won't tend to be used as often as the alteration spells, they are still vital to the Necromancer's power and should be used frequently enough to keep the skill level high.

Divination
Level 1

There are only a few spells of necromantic divination, and they won't be used very often. However, when the time comes to pull one out, nothing else will do - these spells include Locate Corpse and Gather Shadows. Divination goes up quickly through casting, but you'll probably have to set aside some time for repeated castings of a simple spell - like Sense Magic - to keep your skill level at its peak.

Evocation
Level 1

The Necromancer has very few spells of evocation. All of them strike an enemy directly for damage, and most of them are only effective on the undead. They cast slowly, and there is a long wait between castings. In addition, the first evocation spell isn't available until level 8, so your skill level will be zero until then. Since these spells are useful in particular situations, it is worthwhile to make an effort towards raising the skill level, either through repeated castings (which means spending a lot of time blasting skeletons and such after level 8) or by spending training points with the guildmaster.

Specialization in a class of magic allows for the possibility of casting spells in that class for less mana, having a decreased chance of spell interruption, and producing fewer fizzles. As such , it is a good thing indeed. The catch is that only one specialization may increase beyond a skill level of 50 - the first of these skills to go over 50 is the Necromancer's prime specialization. Choosing the proper specialization, therefore, is worthy of some consideration. Specializations go up quickly, and should not require training points beyond the first spent to acquire the skill. Like other skills of magic, the primary specialization is limited by level to a maximum of 5x(level+1), while the other specializations will not exceed 50.

Specialize: Abjuration
Level 20

Since the Necromancer will almost always be using spells of abjuration previous to combat, and since these spells are not very mana-intensive to begin with, there is little to recommend this specialization as a Necromancer's primary choice.

Specialize: Alteration
Level 20

Alteration spells form the majority of the Necromancer's combat arsenal. While these spells do not tend to use an extreme amount of mana, anything that makes them cheaper and less likely to fail is a great boon. This is the obvious choice for the primary specialization.

Specialize: Conjuration
Level 20

Conjurations form the second largest class of spells for the Necromancer. Approximately half of the conjurations are the spells used to summon our undead pets, which are very expensive in terms of mana. Most of the rest are combat spells, where spell failure can be fatal. With that in mind, this would also be a reasonable choice as the primary specialization.

Specialize: Divination
Level 20

With less than a dozen divination spells for the Necromancer, all of them cheap and not likely to be cast during combat, there is no reason at all to make this a primarily specialization.

Specialize: Evocation
Level 20

All of the Necromancer's spells of evocation are combat spells, and all of them use more mana that you'd like, so it is somewhat conceivable that this could be a Necromancer's primary specialization. However, with less than ten spells of this nature, most of which only effect undead, evocation would be a very narrow specialization indeed.
