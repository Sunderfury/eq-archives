The Sixth Circle of the Dark Arts
for Necromancers of 20 Levels

Allure of Death
being an Alteration of 5 mana

Drains the Necromancer's health over time and increases mana regeneration.

About twice as effective as Dark Pact, and quite the time saver. Also known as "Spook the Cleric" - be aware that it can cause some panic in your group members if they don't know what's going on.

Animate Dead
being a Conjuration of 200 mana

Summons an undead skeleton to do the Necromancer's bidding.

I had to have an ogre get this for me. How embarrassing.

Dominate Undead
being an Alteration of 120 mana

Charms the target undead to do the Necromancer's bidding.

When it works, it's really nice - having a ghoul for a pet is a pleasant change. When it fails, though, it's expensive and hazardous. Terribly handy in the right group situation, but more than a little risky when you're alone in the dark. More charisma would probably be helpful, but who cares?

Expulse Undead
being an Evocation of 60 mana

Hits the target undead for a good amount of damage, usually doing some damage even if resisted.

A little more than twice as good as Ward Undead, for twice the mana. You have been keeping your Evocation skill up, haven't you?

Harmshield
being an Abjuration of 85 mana

Makes the Necromancer invulnerable for a brief amount of time.

Amazing, simply amazing. It seems to give about 30 seconds of absolutely no damage from anything, but necromantic testing continues. The long recast time means you'll only get it off once in any dire situation, but a suitably motivated gnome can cover a lot of ground in that 30 seconds. You can still be stunned, so don't get cocky. Depending on where I am, I always have either this or Feign Death memorized.

Identify
being a Divination of 50 mana

Reveals an item's lore.

The only items this has been at all useful for have been key parts of specific quests, and even then the information revealed is only a small hint. I imagine it might come in handy for some very obscure things one might not know what to do with. It will also identify what language a book or note is written in.

Shadow Compact
being an Alteration of 10 mana

Drains a good amount of health from the Necromancer over a short time and grants it to the target.

It's about the equivalent of hitting someone with Siphon Life, only slowly and in reverse. It has all sorts of uses - pets, poisoned comrades, whoever is in front taking hits - and uses almost no mana. Quite the good thing.

Shadow Vortex
being an Alteration of 40 mana

Drains strength from the target's armor, and grants it to the Necromancer's armor.

This is a good lead spell if you're in a group with a number of melee types, as it should make all of their attacks much more effective. And a little more protection is always a good thing.

Siphon Life
being an Alteration of 72 mana

Drains a good amount of health from the target and grants it to the Necromancer.

About one-and-a-half times as effective as Lifedraw, and for only a little more mana. A welcome upgrade indeed.

Word of Shadow
being an Evocation of 85 mana

Hits enemies in the vicinity of the Necromancer for a fair amount of damage.

You probably don't want to use this spell unless you've got at least three enemies packed tightly together, since it doesn't do all that much damage for the amount of mana that it requires - and if you've got that many enemies right on top of you, you might have other problems. It does count as Evocation practice, though, and it's a nice effect.

"Death always wins."
