Playing by the Numbers
Statistics and the Gnome Necromancer

All gnomes, by their very nature, would make skilled Necromancers if only they would embrace the path of Bertoxxulous. As it currently stands, though, only a select few follow the Plaguebringer. The rest are therefore doomed to be our eternal slaves in undeath - which works out pretty well for us Necromancers, so I'm not complaining.

The seven personal characteristics play a large part in determining anyone's success, progress, and longevity, and gnomes are no exception. Gnome Necromancers are particularly gifted in their two primary characteristics, Intelligence and Dexterity, giving them a distinct advantage over the not-so-quick dark elves and erudites, and the not-so-bright humans.

Strength

Strength determines carrying ability and the power of melee hits. Gnomes, being small and weak, are relatively limited in these areas. Thankfully, the Necromancer does not rely on heavy armor, so this hardly matters. Even low level Necromancers can siphon the strength of their enemies to make themselves stronger.

Stamina

Stamina influences how long strenuous tasks (like swimming, jumping, and melee) can be performed before tiring, as well as maximum health at any given level. While more health is always nice, most Necromancers spend quite a lot of time draining the health out of others, easily making up for any gnomish deficiency in this area.

Agility

Agility indicates the ability to avoid being hit in melee, and is defensive in nature. Gnomes are naturally agile, which benefits the Necromancer since they will be spending their fair share of time in melee, at least to begin with. At higher levels agility is much less important, since being a melee target for a powerful beastie is asking for certain doom regardless of how nimble you are.

Dexterity

Dexterity affects the chances of triggering special weapon abilities and Vampiric Embrace, and might have something to do with archery. Given that the Necromancer will be knee-deep in melee at lower levels and still occasionally trying to get a shot in at higher levels (while not becoming a target!), this may be important. Inherent gnomish dexterity is good, and basic gnome Necromancer dexterity is better.

Wisdom

Wisdom reflects the chances of recovering from a failure (such as when using trade skills), and resistance to some spells affecting the mind. These things are not completely unimportant to the Necromancer's well being, but gnomes are notoriously weak in this area. Boosting wisdom a bit isn't unreasonable, but is certainly not necessary.

Intelligence

Intelligence determines the ability to increase skills through use. For the Necromancer, it is also the cornerstone of power, for it sets the amount of mana available, influences the chance of a spell fizzling, and determines how effective the Specialization skills will be. Most if not all gnome Necromancers have extremely enhanced intelligence.

Charisma

Charisma dictates the prices offered by merchants, helps offset the negative effects of annoying certain groups, and also influences the degree of success for a few spells of the dominating variety. Gnomes learn early that merchants will overcharge them any chance that they get, and just get used to it. Gnome Necromancers in particular are not out to win popularity contests; in fact, powerful Necromancers often end up reducing their charisma as the commoners view them with greater and greater dread. Charisma is for high elf wimps who couldn't strike terror into the heart of a halfling child even if they ripped out their own brains and set them on fire.

"Death is just a statistic."
