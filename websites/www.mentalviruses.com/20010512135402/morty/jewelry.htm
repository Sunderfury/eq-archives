Jewelry
Shiny bits of magic

As the Necromancer progresses in power and strength, it becomes less and less wise to enter melee with opponents of similar stature. Enemies will be taken down with Incredible Necromantic Power alone, and kept at a distance. When this time comes, the benefits of additional armor are less than the benefits of personal enhancement. Jewelry becomes much more desireable.

Any jewelry items that increase personal abilities are obviously useful. Many such trinkets are available from jewelers, particular those jewelers who are also enchanters, and can be obtained for a reasonable fee without much effort. A few special items, though, are more difficult to come by, and will particularly attract the attention of the Necromancer.

Necklaces can offer many benefits, but may not be the first choice for jewelry since quite a few pieces of neck armor are also magical.

Beaded Slime Necklace
AC 0

This amusing little trinket grants the wearer a boon of +5 intelligence. And it's slime. What Necromancer doesn't like slime? At least in theory?

Intestine Necklace
AC 0, Magic

It makes a statement. It also grants the wearer a boon of +5 health.

Petrified Erudite Heart Amulet
AC 2, Magic

Ahhh, there's nothing quite like a good relic, especially if it means ripping the heart from an Erudite. Pretentious imposters, most of them, although I've meet a few who were worthy of respect. Wear this proudly and be granted a boon of +5 intelligence and +15 mana.

Rings are convenient bits of magical jewelry. They don't deprive you of otherwise available armor, there are many good rings to be found, and you can wear two of them. Of the many rings available, a few of them may offer the Necromancer more than a little amusement.

Band of Flesh
AC 0, Magic

This twisted bit of someone else's skin offers the wearer a boon of +2 strength.

Ring of Slime
AC 0, Magic

It goes well with the Beaded Slime Necklace, and grants the wearer a boon of +10 mana.

Ring of Shadows
AC 0, Magic

Not only will this necromantically-enhanced ring grant the wearer a boon of +2 dexterity, it also holds charges of Gather Shadows. This really isn't what a good Necromancer needs, but it makes a good gift to others to show your favor. Why not get a set of them - maybe nine - and give them to some mortal men doomed to die?

Platinum Skull Ring
AC 0, Magic

Flaunt your affiliation with death by wearing this elegant platinum ring, which grants the wearer a boon of +30 health and +20 mana. It scares the commoners, so there's a penalty of -5 charisma, but what do they know?

There's nothing like a bit of shiny metal to highlight the grandeur of the gnomish ear. Earrings are more than just decorations or an extra source of magic - they are part of a tradition that hearkens back to the very founders of Ak'Anon. Wear them proudly, even if you can't get one of these truly dark ones.

Polished Bone Hoop
AC 0

This cheerful ring of bone complements all of that bone armor, and grants the wearer a boon of +10 mana.

Batskull Earrings
AC 0, Magic

This quaint bit of jewelry reminds me of the tiny bats that used to flutter around my bedchamber back home. Why, I remember when I was just a wee little lad, and... well, I don't want to bore you with the details. One of these earrings will grant the wearer a boon of +5 intelligence, and you can't go wrong with that.

"Lord of the Earrings? Hmm. Needs work."
