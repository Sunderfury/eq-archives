Armor
Protecting the physical

While it's true that the robe is the defining armor of the Necromancer, protecting the rest of the body is certainly important. As the higher levels of necromantic power are approached, though, physical protection will tend to give way to magical enhancement, making jewelry and other such items more appealing.

The various types of standard armor cover most parts of the body in the form of chokers, tunics, gloves, and so on. Larger items such as tunics and leggings provide more armor than smaller pieces like bracers and gloves. Better materials can provide more armor, less weight, or both.

Cloth Armor
AC 1 to 4

Standard cloth armor offers the least amount of protection, but it's very light. Even cloth armor is better than nothing.

Cloth armor can be found on quite a few of the uglies that run around Norrath. Your best bet is your local orc clan, gnoll family, or goblin tribe.

Netted Armor
AC 1 to 4, Magic

Although it offers the same amount of protection as cloth armor, netted armor is magical in nature. Although this has no known effects on a Necromancer's combat abilities, I'm sure that you'll sleep better at night knowing that, if you took the time to Sense Magic, your armor would show up positive.

Netted armor is a bit harder to find, but I've seen it on lizardmen and frogloks.

Raw Silk Armor
AC 2 to 6

It's quite a bit more comfortable than that rough netted stuff, and it offers more protection. Promote your local tailors and be sure to offer a fair price to encourage them to greater efforts - the more time they spend sewing, the less they're in your way when you're trying to do some serious death-dealing.

Any tailor who finds patchwork armor trivial should be able to throw together some silk armor for you. Getting the silk may be more time-consuming, however.

Woven Armor
AC 2 to 6, Magic

This lightweight armor offers better protection than cloth or netted and is magical in nature. It's comfortable armor, if you can get that froggy smell out of it.

The frogloks seem to know more about magical cloth than anyone else. Natural weavers, perhaps?

Damask Armor
AC 2 to 6, Magic

Damask offers similar protection to woven armor, but was designed with the spellcaster in mind. Damask robes appear a dull off-white.

To get some nice damask pieces of your own, you'll probably have to pull it off the body of a dead spellcaster. Look for it wherever a group of spellcasters have made a permanent home.

Gossimer Armor
AC 3 to 8, Magic

Gossimer offers superior protection for approximately the weight of cloth. The robes show up as very white.

You'll find gossimer armor in many of the same places as you'd find damask, but it tends to be reserved for the elite in any group.

Shadowbound Armor
AC 5 to 9, Magic, Lore

Talk to the right people, and you can set yourself up with two or three pieces of shadowbound armor. It will involve quite a bit of travel, but the reward is well worth the time involved.

Sometime after the Necromancer has mastered, say, the eighth circle of spells, this armor can be successfully sought. One must prove one's worth, however, to a certain Temple inhabitant. You certainly won't find it just lying around.

Cryosilk Armor
AC 3 to 11, Magic, No Drop

The different pieces of cryosilk armor are imbued with many enchantments, providing a wide variety of boons for the wearer. Only the most powerful of Necromancers are capable of obtaining it.

This otherworldly armor is greatly desired by all of the spellcasting classes, and requires a trip to the Plane of Fear to obtain. Good luck.

Blighted Armor
AC 4 to 11, Magic, No Drop

Purely necromantic, and the best of its kind.

This armor is available in the Plane of Hate, but since only Necromancers can wear it, you won't be arm wrestling the Magician for it.

Bone armor is shunned by many spellcasters due to its weight. This is certainly understandable, as the stuff ranges from reasonably light to somewhat heavy... but what could be more appropriate for a Necromancer than raw bone? You might not be able to move, but at least you'll look menacing.

Sheer Bone Mask
AC 3, Magic

This mask has more necromantic style than anything else you'd care to attach to your face, and also grants the wearer a boon of +3 intelligence.

Chipped Bone Collar
AC 4, Magic

The magical nature of this neckpiece grants the wearer a boon of +10 health and +10 mana. It no doubt used to be glowing.

Glowing Bone Collar
AC 5, Magic

This neckpiece will grant the wearer a boon of +25 health and +25 mana. Try not to chip it.

Bone Armplates
AC 5, Magic

The power imbedded in these bulky sleeves grant the wearer a boon of +5 strength, but also impose a penalty of -4 dexterity. The added strength is just enough to balance out the weight.

Hollowed Bone Bracers
AC 6, Magic

Lighter than the armplates but still full of bony badness, these magical sleeves grant the wearer a boon of +7 magic resistance and +15 mana.

Chipped Bone Bracelet
AC 4, Magic

Dangling from a wrist, this bracelet will grant the wearer a boon of +5 wisdom.

Polished Bone Bracelet
AC 2, Magic

These bracelets are lightweight, stylish, and grant the wearer a boon of +3 wisdom and +3 intelligence, making them highly sought after by spellcasters of all persuasions. Only Necromancers really deserve them, though.

Bone-Clasped Girdle
AC 4, Magic

This evil artifact, when worn, will grant the wearer a boon of +7 strength, +7 dexterity, +7 stamina, +75 health, and +75 mana. Extremely difficult to obtain, but it gives the Necromancer a challenging goal if the desire is to be as bone-clad as possible.

Bone Legplates
AC 6, Magical

These heavy, grim leggings grant the wearer a boon of +5 strength, but also carry a penalty of -4 agility.

Sure, bone armor is nice, but it takes a long time and a lot of Incredible Necromantic Power to gather it. In the meantime, how about some choice rat items? Guaranteed to gag most elves, there's nothing like rat armor to get across the message, "I like to play with dead things."

Rat Fur Cap
AC 1

It's fuzzy and it fits. What more do you want?

Rat Pelt Cape
AC 1

Furry and warm, this sleek ratty garment grants the wearer a boon of +2 cold resistance.

Rat's Foot Necklace
AC 0

More of a good luck charm than any kind of armor, this little trinket grants the wearer a boon of +2 health. It disturbs most people, though, so there is also a penalty of -1 charisma. Laugh at their foibles. Wear it anyway.

After you've killed a few kinds of crocodiles, you start to get ideas. Someone with a bit of magical power got a couple of very specific ideas. If only we could get some luggage...

Gatorscale Sleeves
AC 6, Magic

In addition to great arm protection, these sleeves grant the wearer a boon of +4 intelligence and +15 health.

Gatorscale Leggings
AC 7, Magic

Another fine 'gator product, these tough leggings grant the wearer a boon of +4 wisdom and +15 health.

There are many, many pieces of armor available to the Necromancer. Here are a few that are particularly suited to a practitioner of the dark arts.

Batfang Headband
AC 2, Magic

We don't talk about it much, but Ak'Anon has a healthy bat population - not the huge bats like you see in the Faydark, but those little things that get through small cracks and fly around you when you sleep. A lot of gnomes think that's a problem, but I've always liked them. Why, I remember when I was just a wee little lad, and I'd chase the bats through... well, ask me about it if you want the full story. The design of this headpiece reminds of those little batlings. Aside from the nostalgic charm, this grants the wearer a boon of +3 wisdom and +3 intelligence, making it exceptionally nice.

Dark Circlet
AC 4, Magic, Lore, No Trade

Designed exclusively for the discriminating Necromancer, this evil piece of work sits nicely on a gnome's head (although other Necromancers can also make it fit). The circlet grants the wearer a boon of +4 intelligence and +10 health, but imposes a penalty of -2 charisma.

Nightshade Wreath
AC 1, Magic

Take a poisonous plant and wrap it around your head - it sounds silly, but it also has a certain amount of style. The resulting headgear grants the wearer a boon of +5 wisdom and +5 intelligence. This is what happens when powerful druids get drunk and start making things.

Collar of Undead Protection
AC 5, Magic

Sometimes they turn on you. Sometime they just don't listen. Sometimes the dark powers play a little joke on you. Whatever the reason, you're going to end up with an angry undead horror in your face sooner or later, and any extra protection is nice. In addition to being decent armor in and of itself, this neckpiece grants the wearer a boon of +7 poison resistance and +7 magic resistance.

Dusty Bloodstained Gloves
AC 2, Magic

While every Necromancer no doubt has a pair of dusty bloodstained gloves, these gloves in particular are magical in nature and grant the wearer a boon of +25 mana... which makes me wonder what kind of blood is responsible for the stains.

Cloak of Shadows
AC 12, Magic, Lore

You can barely tell that it's there, but it's as protective as the cover of night. This mystical item offers the possessor impressive protection as well as a boon of +13 dexterity and +10 disease resistance.

Festering Cloak
AC 5, Magic

Only a Necromancer would actually wear this, which works out well since only an actual Necromancer is capable of wearing it. The wearer is granted a boon of +10 health and +10 mana, but suffers a penalty of -10 disease resistance and -10 poison resistance. Embrace the gifts of Bertoxxulous.
