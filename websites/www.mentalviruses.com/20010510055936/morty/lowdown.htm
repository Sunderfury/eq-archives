Gnome Lowdown
The truth about the tiny terrors

Raw statistics aside, there are quite a few important things to consider in the life of a gnome Necromancer. Some items are common knowledge, but other bits of wisdom may have eluded the less-than-arduous seeker.

Gnomes are short.

Really short. This is usually an advantage, since a gnome can crawl through some spaces where a human would have troubles and a troll could never go. In a few instances, though, this can be a problem, typically when a step or a ledge is too high to reach by jumping. Unfortunately, one of these instances involves one of the boats that cross the Ocean of Tears - when it arrives at the dock, a gnome can't get on board without first jumping to one of the posts on the pier. As an interesting footnote, gnomes can't be shrunk, and when other races shrink they just become gnome-sized.

Gnomes are small.

Aside from just being short, this means that only items which can be worn by the small races are available. While this means that a gnome's armor tends to weigh less, it also limits the selection of items that can be worn or used. There are quite a few pieces of equipment that a Necromancer would find useful, but a gnome can't equip. On the other hand, there are a handful of interesting things that only a small race can wear.

Gnomes have infravision.

Some people have said that gnomes have ultravision, but trust me, it's infravision. Our ability to see in the dark is quite well developed and is handier than a squid with gloves.

Gnomes are tinkers.

In theory, this means that any gnome of sufficient development (call it level 16) can pick up a toolbox, throw in some odds and ends, and create a useful little widget that the world will love. In practice, the pieces are expensive, everything you need is only available in Steamfont and Ak'Anon, and we can only make a handful of doodads that are either relatively useless or too pricy for what they're worth. On the other hand, it can lead to a great deal of amusement.

Gnomes are neutral.

In Norrath, there are evil races (the ogres, trolls, and dark elves) and good races (pretty much everyone else). These races are at each others' throats in various states of war, and are typically killed on sight in opposing cities. Gnomes are recognized as neither good nor evil, and no one is trying to exterminate us. This gives us the potential, at least, to get along very well anywhere, although this is strongly counteracted by the Necromancer's religious beliefs.

Necromancers are not very popular.

Quite a lot of people, mostly of the "good" races, seem to have a problem with necromancy. While the sensible reaction would be to run away screaming in terror, the more typical reaction is to attack on sight.

Bertoxxulous and his worshippers are not very popular at all.

Some people who have no particular problem with necromancy will fly into a murderous rage when they spot a minion of the Plaguebringer. Sometimes this is predictable, as in the case of nearly every druid in the world. Sometimes this comes as a surprise, as in the case of the human merchant baker in the Western Plains of Karana. In either case, it can cause problems.

Necromancers can work independently.

While any class of adventurer can go solo to a greater or lesser degree, the Necromancer can rely on it for a long time. With a collection of spells that can call up a powerful pet, do a great deal of damage over time, and heal the caster, the Necromancer can be extremely self-sufficient.
