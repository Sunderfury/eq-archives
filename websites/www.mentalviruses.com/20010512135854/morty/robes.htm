Robes
The traditional garb of the magically inclined

More than anything else, the robe is what makes it clear to the world that you are versed in the arcane arts and are not to be trifled with. It also marks you as a prime target for your enemies, but that's the way it goes. Robes are the best body armor you've got, and also are the most important factor determining how you look.

Dark Gold Felt Robe
AC 2, Lore

This is the robe that you get when you turn in your note to the guildmaster. It will be your armor for far too long, unless you skip the robe look and go with some variety of shirt.

Dark Gold Jr. Apprentice Robe
AC 3, Magic, Lore

The improved version of your first robe, this provides better armor and gives you the minor perk of +1 mana. Since no city outside of Ak'Anon sells robes that will fit a gnome, and since the gold gnome robes can't be worn by a Necromancer, you don't have many options.

Dark Gold Sr. Apprentice Robe
AC 4, Magic, Lore

The best of the apprentice robes, with the minor perk of +2 mana. This robe is distinctly darker than the initial felt robe, much closer to brown than gold. It's a good, earthy look.

Thaumaturgist's Robe
AC 5, Magic

This rather heavy robe provides a fair amount of protection, and also grants the wearer a boost of +3 agility. Considering the nature of its original owner, it's really quite fitting.

Silk Robe
AC 6

A tailor with a little skill can craft this comfortable robe. It provides no particular bonuses, and is not magical, but it's a lot easier to come by.

Damask Robe
AC 6, Magic

A quite functional robe with an off-white appearance. It's heavier than silk, but it's magical - and I'm sure that counts for something to someone. They tend to be found more on dark elves than gnomes.

Gossimer Robe
AC 8, Magic

Gossimer armor in general is a good choice for the Necromancer. The robe in particular is quite nice, and is as white as bone.

Flowing Black Robe
AC 8, Magic, Lore

This robe provides very good protection (as far as robes go), and also grants the wearer a boon of +5 stamina and +3 intelligence. Dark, sinister, and an excellent addition to your necromantic wardrobe.

Green Silken Drape
AC 8, Magic

This excellent robe will grant the wearer a boon of +9 dexterity, +9 stamina, +3 intelligence, and +15 poison resistance, making it an excellent choice for the Necromancer who isn't afraid to wear green.

Robe of the Oracle
AC 9, Magic, Lore

In addition to a high armor rating, this robe grants the wearer a boon of +5 wisdom, +5 intelligence, and +25 mana, making it highly sought after by spellcasters everywhere. Difficult to obtain, but well worth the effort if you can abide the sheer gaudiness.

Robe of Enshroudment
AC 9, Magic, Lore

It's just so black, it's perfect. Beyond the blackness, it grants the wearer a boon of +4 intelligence, +4 agility, +4 fire resistance, and +4 cold resistance. The important thing, though, is that it's black.

Shining Metallic Robe
AC 10, Magic

Designed for defense, the wearer of this fine garment is granted a boon of +9 intelligence, +8 fire resistance, and +8 cold resistance.

Cryosilk Robe
AC 11, Magic, No Drop

It's comfortable, it has a stylish cut, and it grants the wearer boons of +9 dexterity, +13 stamina, +13 intelligence, and +35 mana. Only the most powerful Necromancers can even hope of obtaining one of these.

Blighted Robe
AC 11, Magic, No Drop

The ultimate in garb exclusively for the Necromancer, this dark robe resembles the Flowing Black Robe but sticks to the colors of night. Extremely difficult to get, but once obtained it will grant the wearer a boon of +10 dexterity, +10 stamina, +13 intelligence, and +40 mana. Ahhh.

Crimson Robe of Alendine
AC 12, Magic

Possibly the best robe in the world, this wonder of magical cloth grants the wearer a boon of +15 dexterity, +15 intelligence, +50 health, and +50 mana. Of course, most Necromancers will never even see this, much less possess it.
