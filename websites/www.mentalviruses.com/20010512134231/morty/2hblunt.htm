Two-Handed Blunt
Delivering the big crush

The great staff and its magical variations are the only two-handed blunt weapons we can use, with the one important exception noted below. No great surprise in that, and there's a certain satisfaction in whacking something upside the knee (hey, we're gnomes, what do you expect?) with a big stick.

The benefit of the two-handed staff is damage - with few exceptions, a great staff will do noticeably more damage in a single hit than a similar one-handed staff, and about twice as much damage as a comparable dagger. The price you pay for this extra damage is speed, with the two-handed staff being slower than the one-handed, and almost twice as slow as a dagger. As a result, you're going to be doing about the same amount of raw damage with any type of weapon you choose of the same quality.

An important (if obvious) point is that with a two-handed weapon, you don't have a free hand to hold anything else, such as something that increases your abilities. At low levels, you probably won't have one of these items, so it's not a big deal; at higher levels, it's something to consider.

The various forms of the great staff are the main weapons in this category.

Worn Great Staff
6 damage, 40 delay

The starter version, found on monsters all over Norrath.

Warped Great Staff
7 damage, 40 delay

Harder to find than the standard worn great staff, and it doesn't doo all that much more damage... but anything called "warped" deserves some room in your closet.

Great Staff
9 damage, 38 delay

The standard variety, it hits hard. What more do you want?

Fine Steel Great Staff
9 damage, 36 delay

The improved version, also found on monsters all over Norrath.

Combine Great Staff
9 damage, 36 delay, Magic

Back in the old days [hack, spit] they knew how to make a real staff, and this is it. Simple in design, effective in use, and capable of hitting creatures that another great staff won't touch. Expensive as all get-out as far as staves go.

If you know a Magician who will do you a favor, you can get a decent magical weapon that sheds a little light for the cost of a single spellcasting. Just remember that all of these conjured weapons will disappear from your hands when you camp.

Staff of Tracing
7 damage, 40 delay, Magic

This is the first staff that can be conjured up by a Magician, so most of them pick up the 8th level spell that does the trick. In addition to being a decent staff, it holds charges of Flare and provides a nice amount of flickering light.

Staff of Warding
8 damage, 38 delay, Magic

Conjured up by a Magician with the 16th level summoning spell, this staff provides light and holds charges of Gaze.

Staff of Runes
9 damage, 36 delay, Magic

A Magician can summon this with the 24th level spell. It provides light and holds a quick charge of Cancel Magic. Darned handy at times.

Staff of Symbols
10 damage, 34 delay, Magic

At 34th level, a Magician can pick up this spell and summon away. This glowing magical stick also holds charges of See Invisible.

Staff of Mystery
11 damage, 32 delay, Magic

All right, I'm making this one up. But admit it, there's a pattern going on here. A 44th level Magician really ought to have a spell to summon a staff like this, maybe with some charges of Bind Sight. If I were a Magician [shudder] I'd be poking around looking for those high-level summonings.

The more exotic examples of the great staff are noticeably harder to acquire, but any gnome with Incredible Necromantic Power shouldn't have too much trouble if you don't get in over your head.

Runed Totem Staff
9 damage, 37 delay, Magic, Lore

The mystical powers of the Runed Totem Staff grant a boon of +5 mana and +5 health, and no Necromancer in their right mind would argue with that.

Treant Staff
10 damage, 35 delay, Magic

If you're like me - and I know I am - you don't have a very good relationship with treants. So what could be more satisfying than taking a hunk of dead treant and cracking a few kneecaps? With the staff throwing Root on your target now and then, you can't go wrong.

Staff of Discord
10 damage, 40 delay, Magic, No Drop

Topped with a grinning skull, it just reeks of necromancy. You know you want one. Good point: It weighs less than most daggers. Bad point: It can't be dropped or traded. Guess who you have to kill to get one.

Staff of Undead Legions
20 damage, 47 delay, Magic

Designed exclusively for the necromancer, this evil artifact allows the wielder to Beguile Undead. Only the most impressive of Necromancers should even think about picking one up.

It's in a class all by itself. It looks good. And it answers that all-important question: Where did you get all of those bones?

Shovel
4 damage, 27 delay

You'll have to pry it out of the cold, clammy hands of a dead farmer, but any Necromancer will undoubtedly put it to a better use than digging turnips. Besides, every gnome should have one. Gnomes invented digging. Note that this is NOT the shovel dropped by magicians, which isn't a weapon.

"Walk softly and carry a big stick. Don't forget to hit people with it."
