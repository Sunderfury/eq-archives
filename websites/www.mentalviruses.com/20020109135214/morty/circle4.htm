The Fourth Circle of the Dark Arts
for Necromancers of 12 Levels

Bind Affinity
being an Alteration of 100 mana

Sets the target's return point of gating or death to their current position.

You can bind yourself anywhere - I would recommend not binding yourself too close to the guards, if you know what I mean. You can also bind other members of your group, but only near cities. This spell is a must.

Convoke Shadow
being a Conjuration of 120 mana requiring 2 bone chips

Summons an undead skeleton to do the Necromancer's bidding.

This is the first pet that hits for magical damage, so you can now go after wisps and ghouls.

Endure Disease
being an Abjuration of 20 mana

Increases the target's Disease Resistance.

Another one of the few buffing spells that you can share with your comrades.

Engulfing Darkness
being a Conjuration of 60 mana

Slows the target's movement and drains their health over time.

The next in the Darkness line - a little slower, and a little more damage.

Heat Blood
being an Alteration of 72 mana

Damages the target and drains their health over time.

The first of the Blood line of spells, which go up against the target's Fire Resistance to determine success - so don't expect it to be useful in Lavastorm, for example. Aside from that, the Darkness, Poison, and Blood spells combine nicely, even though your poison spell is no longer very effective at this point. Heat Blood will be useful for quite a while.

Leach
being an Alteration of 72 mana

Drains a good amount of health from the target over time and grants it to the Necromancer.

This drains approximately twice as much health from the target as Lifedraw, which is quite a deal if you're willing to wait for the full effect.

Lifedraw
being an Alteration of 63 mana

Drains a fair amount of health from the target and grants it to the Necromancer.

About four times as effective as Lifespike.

Scent of Dusk
being an Alteration of 50 mana

Reduces the target's Disease Resistance, Poison Resistance, and Fire Resistance by a small amount.

This is the first spell in the Scent series, which lowers the main resistances that a Necromancer cares about. I still haven't made up my mind if the high mana cost is worth it, but I'm sure there are situations where it's useful. Necromantic testing continues.

Sight Graft
being a Divination of 10 mana

Allows the Necromancer to see through the eyes of their pet.

About the only use I can think of for this is if you want to watch for the appearance of something, but don't actually want to be around when it happens. It's also interesting to get the skeleton-eye view of combat, just to see what it's like.

Spook the Dead
being an Alteration of 10 mana

Causes the target undead to flee.

Cheap for a 12th level spell, no sense not getting it. Uses a quarter of the mana that Fear does, and functionally equivalent. No Necromancer should be without the special "dealing with the dead" spells.

Wave of Enfeeblement
being an Alteration of 40 mana

Drains a fair amount of strength from enemies in the vicinity of the Necromancer.

This is a great way to get the attention of a large crowd, if that's what you're looking for. Also known as "Wave of My Pet Kills Me" if you're PvP.
