The Seventh Circle of the Dark Arts
for Necromancers of 24 Levels

Breath of the Dead
being an Alteration of 45 mana requiring 1 fish scale

Allows the Necromancer to breath underwater.

Ironically, now that I occasionally need fish scales, my fishing is so good that I usually get fish and only rarely get the scales.

Haunting Corpse
being a Conjuration of 240 mana requiring 2 bone chips

Summons an undead skeleton to do the Necromancer's bidding.

This is the first pet whose bones have a reddish appearance, still soaked in blood. This is also the first pet that absolutely must be researched.

Intensify Dead
being an Alteration of 50 mana

Increases the combat abilities of the Necromancer's pet.

As if your undead minion weren't terrifying enough, this spell makes it stronger, faster, and harder to hit. It takes a while to cast, so try to get it off just before the flailing begins. It seems to last for several minutes - long enough for one combat, but probably not two.

Leatherskin
being an Abjuration of 83 mana requiring 1 bloodstone

Absorbs damage before it can affect the Necromancer until its limit is reached.

The next in the Skin line of spells, and about twice as effective as Shieldskin. Like Shieldskin, I only use it on certain occasions since it's a bit expensive to keep constantly up.

Major Shielding
being an Abjuration of 80 mana

Provides the Necromancer with increased maximum health, an armor class bonus, and increased Magic Resistance.

Much better than Shielding. Get it.

Rapacious Subversion
being an Alteration of 200 mana

Grants the target a fair amount of mana.

The target seems to get a bit less than a third of the mana that you spend. While I can come up with all kinds of hypothetical situations where this might be useful, none of them seem very likely. Necromantic testing continues.

Resist Cold
being an Abjuration of 50 mana

Increases the target's Cold Resistance.

Quite a bit better than Endure Cold, and by now you're probably running into all sorts of things that can blast you with snow and frost.

Rest the Dead
being an Abjuration of 75 mana

Makes the target undead less likely to attack.

This is the upgrade to Numb the Dead.

Scent of Shadow
being an Alteration of 100 mana

Reduces the target's Disease Resistance, Poison Resistance, and Fire Resistance by a fair amount.

The second spell in the series, eventually about twice as effective as Scent of Dusk for twice the mana. Still expensive to cast, and I still haven't made up my mind about whether it's worth it.

Screaming Terror
being an Alteration of 60 mana

Paralyzes the target as they scream in abject terror, possibly inducing forgetfulness about any attackers.

Whacking them upside the head will stop the screaming, so leave them alone for the full effect.

Shadow Sight
being a Divination of 50 mana

Grants the Necromancer ultravision.

Ultravision is definitely better than infravision, so I almost always cast this around dusk. It lasts all night.

Shock of Poison
being a Conjuration of 100 mana

Hits the target for a good amount of poison damage.
