Good Question
Why do I spend my time with those do-gooders?

Despite the seeming contradiction, I am a proud member of the Stormseekers, a small and friendly guild filled with honorable sorts of folks. When I eventually establish my Demesne of Death across Norrath and fill the cities with the walking dead, I'll probably keep these people alive for company.

The Stormseekers was formed around the belief of "Aku Zoku Zan" - which roughly translates into "See Evil, Kill Evil." Makes you nervous, doesn't it? The fact that they haven't attempted to kill me yet gives me hope, but still leads to the question: "What are you doing with these people? Why would any Necromancer want to join up with any group of unbelievers?"

The reasons are many, and my contributions (which typically involve something dying) only make the benefits that much greater.

Power

There's something about a huge collection of people that appeals to the power-hungry madman in me, particularly when I might be able to persuade them towards some course of action. Being on familiar terms with a small standing army has its advantages.

Protection

If ever the point comes when I'm in need of something, I know that at any given time there are quite a few people who would be willing to offer assistance, all in the name of honor - which essentially means that should any of my evil schemes go awry, there are a number of "respectable" folks around to bail me out and clear my name. Call it a backup plan.

Logistics

The sheer variety in the ranks of the Stormseekers allows me access to some things that are otherwise hard to acquire - supplies from Kelethin, food from Halas, spells from the local wizards guild, and so on. I have also, on occasion, been whisked off to distant places with the helpful assistance of a wizard or <shudder> druid.

Trade

Without going into a rant on the benefits of free trade - a favorite topic of gnomish discussion - I'll just say that my fellow guild members provide a good market for obscure items. Since there are relatively few Necromancers in the guild, I have a decent chance of getting a fair deal for those rare words of research. Guild tradesfolk charge reasonable prices. In return, I have a place to get rid of all of those worthless runes and armor pieces that I run across.

Education

Some people see the guild as a cooperative mix of races and religions, working together for widespread benefit. That's true as far as it goes, but I also see it as a venue in which to spread the dark word of Bertoxxulous. Conversion is a slow process, but since there is already acceptance of the practice of necromancy as a worthwhile career, there is a much greater likelihood that an enlightened few will take the next step and walk into the shadows of the Plaguebringer.

Death

When it comes right down to it, the members of the Stormseekers are highly skilled killers. They may not think of themselves as such, but I've seen enough of them in action to know. Put a few of us together and lots of things die, quickly, in rapid succession. And isn't that really what a Necromancer wants?

"Do I know you?"
