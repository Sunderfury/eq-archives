Tinker Toys
Mechanical wonders for the masses

A tip o' the Silk Lined Steel Helm goes to Gnomad, who collected much of the information here and kept interest going through some painful stages of tinkering. Those who remember the table of failed attempts trying to make the Collapsible Fishing Pole will know what I mean. He will be [sniff] remembered fondly.

All of the original tinkering recipes are easily available in the vicinity of Ak'Anon. The same is true for most of the odd components necessary.

Tinker wisely, for attempting something far beyond your current ability will result in losing great gobs of platinum.

Costs are based on a gnome with typical gnomish charisma and an indifferent relationship to the vendors, and are rounded up to the nearest coin. Your prices may vary.

Animated Bait
Trivial at 50, cost 4 cp

Cork, Spriket.

It's bait. It looks like regular bait, works like regular bait, and costs just as much as regular bait. Weirdly enough, it's also food...

Compass
Trivial at 50, cost 3 pp

Pie Tin, Skewers, Water Flask, Static Orb, Cork.

You might have seen one of these for sale, as a few merchants carry them. Using the compass tells you what direction you're facing.

Firewater
Trivial at 50, cost 8 sp

Water Flask, Gnomish Spirits.

Firewater is useful for creating other tinkered items, but doesn't seem to do much by itself. Tests continue. If you're having trouble finding Gnomish Spirits, head for a nearby still and toss in some Rice, Spider Legs, Rat Ears, and a Bottle.

Gnomish Fireworks
Trivial at 50, cost 2 gp

Metal Shaft, Bat Wing, Firewater.

Mixing up a batch of these will give you four separate pieces of gnomish fireworks, each one with a slightly different effect. Early gnomish fireworks were [ahem] less impressive than one might have wanted, but current efforts seem to be well rewarded.

Collapsible Fishing Pole
Trivial at 50, cost 7 pp

Three Metal Rods, Gnomish Bolts.

The long-elusive Collapsible Fishing Pole is less than half the weight of a standard fishing pole, and fits into smaller containers. Aside from that, it works just like a fishing pole ought to. The downside to this is that it breaks just as often as a regular fishing pole, despite the fact that it costs 30 times as much. You make the call.

Flameless Lantern
Trivial at 68, cost 2 pp

Large Lantern, Twine, Firewater.

Hold it like a lantern, and it provides at least as much light as a greater lightstone. Unlike a regular lantern, this one will work underwater. Since not too many gnomes carry lanterns, it can also make a good conversation piece - I was once asked, "What kind of stein is that?"

Mechanized Lockpicks
Trivial at 68, cost 18 pp

Gear, Sprocket, Lockpicks, Rat Ears.

Rogues and Bards can use a set of these much as they would a standard set of lockpicks. We can hope that the mechanized lockpicks do a better job, but with the relatively low number of locked doors in the world, it's a bit hard to tell.

Thermal Cloak
Trivial at >68, cost at least 1 pp

Metal Rod, Firewater, Tattered Gnomish Cloak.

With the discovery of the Tattered Gnomish Cloak in Solusek's Eye, it is now possible to create the Thermal Cloak. Any gnome can wear this fine bit of protection, which has an armor class of 6 and provides a +20 bonus to cold resistance.

Standard Bow Cam
Trivial at 75, cost 15 pp

Gear, Grease, Gnomish Bolts.

These cams can be used by a skilled fletcher to create powerful bows (which would technically make them a bowyer, but we won't get into that). Unlike many other tinkered items, this is actually cheaper to make than it is to buy, so the standard bow cam enjoys some small degree of popularity.

Spyglass
Trivial at 95, cost 26 pp

Bottle, Metal Twine, Metal Rod, Reflective Shard, Collapsible Fishing Pole

If you haven't broken your collapsible fishing pole yet, this is the next logical thing to do with it after fishing. The effects of the spyglass are similar to a Wizard's Glimpse spell, giving the wielder telescopic vision. This can be handy for all sorts of things, which makes the spyglass one of the most useful bits of tinkering we've got.

Stalking Probes
Trivial at 102, cost 18 pp

Two Gears, Bottle, Metal Rod, Firewater.

This weird device allows the user to send out a giant floating eyeball to look around, similar to the Eye of Zomm spell. The probes hold five charges.

Gem Cutter
Trivial at 104?, cost at least 19 pp

Metal Rod, Cam, Metal Fastening, Firewater, Grease, Diamond Dust.

There are two oddities with the Gem Cutter. First off, you won't be able to buy Diamond Dust from a vendor - it only seems to be found on certain ice giants (possibly as a cosmetic). Second, no one has figured out how to make the darned thing work. You would think that, just maybe, it somehow cuts gems. Research continues.

Powered Gloves
Trivial at 122, cost 45 pp

Steel Lined Gloves, Sprocket, Gear, Metal Twine, Firewater.

If you often find yourself a bit overburdened, you can put together a pair of these fine gloves that any gnome can use. While they don't qualify as armor, they do add 5 strength while weighing next to nothing. Always handy to have around.

Catapult
Trivial at 135, cost at least 55 pp

Shaped Ashwood Bow with hemp string, Sproket, Gear, Metal Twine, Firewater.

Here's another example of how gnomes drive themselves crazy. You'll need a skilled fletcher to create the bow, and prices will vary. As if that weren't frustrating enough, no one has yet succeeding in getting any action out of thing, putting it into the same category as the Gem Cutter. You might expect it to throw things. Hmmph.

Aqualung
Trivial at 148, cost 262 pp

Fine Coral Mesh, Silk Lined Steel Helm, Metal Twine, Gnomish Bolts, Metal Rod, Fresh Fish.

A legendary item that was finally rediscovered, the aqualung (not surprisingly) allows the wearer to breath underwater. There are 50 charges.

Rebreather
Trivial at 175, cost at least 916 pp

Lime Coated Meshing, Shark Skin Tubing, Platemail Helm (medium), Metal Fastening, Firewater, Aqualung.

The improved version of the aqualung, this pricy gadget uses no charges and never leaves you gasping for air deep beneath the waves. I'm sure there will be a good market for them once they can be made with a relatively high degree of success.

The gnome pirates of Velious have extended the realm of tinkering, providing us with a few more odds and ends. Their most significant contribution is revealing the secrets of Watchman armor.

These recipes may require the use of the Deluxe Tool Kit, which is also available from the pirates.

Telescopic Eyepatch
Trivial at <104, cost ?? pp

Low Quality Dire Wolf Fur, Gnomish Bolts, Reflective Shard, Velium Lens.

This compact little device is the improved version of the Spyglass. Put it right on your face for an additional 3 AC and use it whenever you like.

Footwarming Boots
Trivial at ??, cost >142 pp

Small Fine Plate Boots (unstained), Sharkskin Tubing, Two Metal Rods, Metal Twine, Gnomish Bolts.

A fine set of plate boots to - yes - keep your feet warm. Designed for the those who could normally wear plate and yet have small feet, these boots offer AC 10 and a boon of +5 cold resistance.

Gnomish Vanishing Device
Trivial at ??, cost ?? pp

Diamond, Small Lantern, Steel Wire, Gnomish Bolts, Reflective Shard, Haze Panther Eye, Class Five Mana Battery.

Expensive to make, at the very least. The charges (however many there are, presumably of Invisibility of some kind) can be restored by replacing the mana battery.
