Piercing
A quick stab in the dark

In terms of weapon selection, the Necromancer has more sharp pointy things to choose from than either variety of blunt sticks. The piercing weapons are faster than the staves, but do less damage per hit. Faster is good, since it gives you a greater chance to interrupt your target if they're trying to cast a spell; faster can also be bad, since you can interrupt your own spellcasting with your attacks. More attacks per combat also means a greater chance of melee-related spells (like Vampiric Embrace) going off in any given combat.

Since piercing weapons leave the secondary hand free to hold other items, some Necromancers prefer those weapons over the more damaging two-handed staff.

The standard piercing weapon is the dagger.

Dagger (starter)
3 damage, 24 delay, No Drop

The dagger which you start out with cannot be dropped or traded. It is otherwise the equivalent of a Rusty Dagger, which makes you wonder exactly how much the Necromancer's guild values its new recruits.

Rusty Dagger
3 damage, 24 delay

The ubiquitous weapon. They're everywhere. Humans carry them. Monsters carry them. Undead carry them. You'll fish them out of lakes and rivers. There's just no escape from the Rusty Dagger.

Tarnished Dagger
3 damage, 22 delay

Take a Rusty Dagger and a sharpening stone, do a little blacksmithing, and you get a Tarnished Dagger. It's the equivalent of bronze in combat, but it still fails to impress anyone.

Bronze Dagger
3 damage, 22 delay

Functional.

Dagger
3 damage, 20 delay

The standard model dagger is 10 percent faster than the bronze model.

Fine Steel Dagger
3 damage, 19 delay

It's faster than your basic dagger, but it's hard to shake the feeling that someday you'll drop it in the woods and it will get rusty.

Combine Dagger
3 damage, 19 delay, Magic

If you need something that's quick, lightweight, and hits for magical damage, this is a reasonable weapon. The Combine knew how to make 'em.

A friendly Magician can call up a magical weapon for you or your pet. Like all conjured weapons, these will disappear from your hands when you camp.

Dagger
3 damage, 21 delay, Magic

This dagger can be conjured up by any Magician with the 1st level summoning spell. If they can't get you one of these, mock them.

Giant Snake Fang
5 damage, 26 delay, Magic

Very similar to the fangs found in the wild, this fang can be conjured up by a Magician of at least 12 levels with the proper spell. This one is a touch faster, though, and hits for magical damage, making it definitely superior.

Dagger of Symbols
5 damage, 20 delay, Magic

Conjured up by a Magician with the 39th level summoning spell, this mystical dagger grants the wielder a boon of +5 resistance to magic, poison, and disease. It can also be kept as a range weapon.

Exotic daggers are much, much nicer than any dagger the Combine ever made.

Dagger of Marnek
3 damage, 30 delay, Lore

The dark power invested in this dagger grants the wielder a boon of +3 intelligence and +15 mana. Created specifically for the Necromancer, this dagger is intended for ritual, not combat - it's slow, hits for little damage, and does not hit for magical damage. However, it can be usefully kept on hand by putting it where you'd place a throwing dagger. Just be careful not to throw it, of course.

Sacrificial Dagger
5 damage, 21 delay, Magic

Ahhh, sacrifice. The [ahem] lifeblood of the Necromancer. The stored energy of this grim blade's numerous victims allow it to grant it's wielder a boon of +5 intelligence. This is the dagger of choice for the discriminating Necromancer.

Dragoon Dirk
6 damage, 23 delay, Magic, Lore

The dirk's magic offers it's wielder a boon of +2 dexterity and +3 resistance to magic. Very nice, but perhaps better suited to a Rogue rather than a Necromancer.

Stiletto of the Bloodclaw
6 damage, 23 delay, Magic, Lore

In addition to quickly dealing out a respectable amount of magical damage, the stiletto occasionally hits its unfortunate victim with Blood Claw, which looks ever so necromantic when it happens. An excellent choice for the battle-oriented Necromancer.

Animal parts can make good weapons, too...

Swordfish Bill
4 damage, 30 delay

Interesting, yes, but imposing? Hardly. "Ooo, look out, it's that scary Necromancer with the fish nose!" I'd be embarassed to be seen with it.

Crookstinger
4 damage, 21 delay, Magic

This little sticker is the business end of a giant wasp, and it's got a weak poison on it just for extra kick. Nasty, but not particularly world-shattering.

Giant Snake Fang
5 damage, 27 delay

It's a little slow for a piercing weapon, but the damage is nice. Don't confuse it with the fang that Magicians can summon, though - this one isn't magical.

In the quest for stabbing with style, a few odd things have been picked up and poked into people. Some have been more effective than others.

Butcher Knife
3 damage, 24 delay, Lore

Honestly, I wouldn't call my last butcher knife injury a "piercing" by any stretch of the imagination.

Crysknife
4 damage, 21 delay, Magic

Magical and fast, and it's good enough for everyday use.

Mithril Quill
4 damage, 19 delay, Magic

Scary things happen when scholars defend themselves. Through powers best left to the imagination, this quill grants the wielder a boon of +20 health.

Obsidian Shard
6 damage, 25 delay, Magic

In addition to decent magical damage, the shard will occasionally fire off Obsidian Shatter on its melee target. There's a certain raw power about this that suits a Necromancer well. Find it, use it.
