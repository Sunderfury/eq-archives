The Spilling of Blood
Skills to share with your enemies

Combat skills will serve the Necromancer well for quite some time, although over time the defensive skills become far more important. With the exception of Dodge, all of these skills are available from the beginning of the Necromancer's career, and will rise at a reasonable pace during melee.

These skills are limited by level, and will not exceed a value of 3x(level+1).

Offense
Level 1

This is general attacking ability with any weapon. It cannot be trained, and only advances through attacks.

Defense
Level 1

Defense covers the general ability to avoid being hit in combat. Like Offense, it cannot be trained, and only advances through being attacked.

Dodge
Level 22

Once this skill is acquired, the Necromancer will occasionally dodge an attack that would have been successful. Dodge rises rapidly in combat, so spending more than the single practice point to acquire the skill should not be necessary.

Weapon skills improve attack abilities when a weapon of the particular type is used. These skills can be trained, and advance at an average pace with attacks using a weapon of the appropriate type. It is worth changing weapons occasionally at low levels so that some skill may be acquired in all of the weapon types - when a particularly nice magical weapon comes along later, it's nice to be able to use it effectively. Weapon skills are combat skills, limited by level to a maximum of 3x(level+1).

Hand-to-Hand
Level 1

This skill covers barehanded fighting. While this may be useful at very low levels when on a mission of corpse recovery, it will never offer much damage to an enemy. Engaging in hand-to-hand combat until it has advanced a few times will increase the damage done by a bit, but it's really not worth any practice points.

One-Handed Blunt
Level 1

The Necromancer is pretty much limited to the staff regarding weapons of this type, and may not have one to practice with at lower levels - few monsters carry a cracked staff, and the temptation to sell it is high since it commands a good price for a low level weapon. Since there are one-handed blunt weapons that will definitely attract the Necromancer's attention later on, though, it is worth finding a good staff keeping this skill up to par.

Two-Handed Blunt
Level 1

The great staff is much more common than the cracked staff, so finding a weapon to practice with at lower levels shouldn't be a problem. If there's a friendly magician in the neighborhood, have them conjure a staff up for you, as it will be much better than anything you'll find for a long time. Many two-handed blunt weapons are very good for the Necromancer, and this is the primary weapon for many.

Piercing
Level 1

Since the Necromancer starts out with a crude but effective dagger, this weapon skill tends to be the first to go up. Piercing weapons are necromantic favorites. Keeping this skill current is wise.

Throwing
Level 1

This skill covers the use of shurikens, throwing knives, and other such range weapons. These weapons hit for relatively little damage, and are primarily used for getting the attention of a wandering monster so it will close to melee distance. Since the Necromancer has a wide selection of spells that are much more effective and have a greater range than thrown weapons, this skill is not particularly vital. In fact, these weapons can be ignored or not entirely at the Necromancer's whim. If desired, skill in throwing weapons could be cultivated between spell castings while your pet engages the enemy.
