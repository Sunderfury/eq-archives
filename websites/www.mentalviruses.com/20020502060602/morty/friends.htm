Friends and Foes
Morty glares at you dubiously

Understanding the interplay between the various factions in Norrath is arguably more important for the Necromancer than for any other class. Many groups will attack Necromancers on sight in a foolish attempt to save themselves from their eventual fate as our loyal servants, and members of those groups are not always obvious. On the other hand, there are groups that look favorably on the necromantic cause, and maintaining good relations with those groups is highly recommended.

A quick comparison between these two lists will provide some insight as to why the path of the gnome necromancer is considered highly difficult.

Only two factions are completely supportive of the gnome Necromancer. They are, not surprisingly, the secretive followers of Bertoxxulous.

The Dark Reflection
Ak'Anon

All of the Plaguebringer's minions among the gnomes gather together to form the Dark Reflection. This includes all gnome Necromancers, those clerics who follow the sinister path, and a handful of other believers. The Dark Reflection is our home and the primary source for our training. The guild hall in the Mines of Malfunction is also the only place in Faydwer to buy necromantic spells and robes that fit. Getting on the bad side of the Dark Reflection would be extremely unwise.

Bloodsabers
Qeynos

The Shrine of Bertoxxulous is maintained by the Bloodsabers, and it is a wonder to behold. Every gnome Necromancer should make the pilgrimage to this concentration of grim power. The Bloodsabers themselves are doing an admirable job of infiltrating the ranks of the powerful in Qeynos. An adventurous gnome can do much to advance their cause.

Despite the secrecy which the faithful of Bertoxxulous tend to embrace, quite a few groups in Norrath are not only aware of our existence, but dedicated to our extermination. The young Necromancer is advised to avoid contact with these groups; the advanced Necromancer is advised to eliminate them.

Emerald Warriors
Kelethin, Greater Faydark

The members of the wood elf warriors guild are not your friends. Unfortunately, this includes the guards who patrol Greater Faydark, occasionally wandering right by the entrances to both Lesser Faydark and the Butcherblock Mountains. This makes travel through Greater Faydark tense even at the best of times. They're also wandering around the tree city of Kelethin, but what would you be going there for?

Faydark's Champions
Kelethin, Greater Faydark, Lesser Faydark

The Champions are the wood elf rangers, and they're not your friends either. For the most part, this won't have much effect on your life, unless you have to attend a social function in Felwithe.

Stormguard
Kaladim, Butcherblock Mountains

The dwarven warriors and guards don't like you either. This is a minor inconvenience, since travel through Butcherblock inevitably takes you past one or more guard posts. You shouldn't have any problems, though, if you give the guard posts a wide berth and stay out of Kaladim (a pitiful attempt to copy the glory of Ak'Anon).

Guardians of the Vale
Rivervale, Misty Thicket

Halflings warriors. Pfah. They seem to know instinctively that someday you'll be serving them in a light sauce with a side of greens, so they'll attack you given a chance. If you happen to be in Misty Thicket, watch out for them sneaking around - until you're ready to wipe them out. Just avoid Rivervale unless you're looking for something to spit on.

Highpass Guards
Highhold Keep, Highpass Hold

Most of the groups that despise Necromancers I don't really care about; they're weak and dull, and will eventually be our pets. The Highpass Guards, though, are different. Not only are they respectable warriors, but they've shown a superb grasp of strategy by establishing complete control over a rather critical piece of real estate. If you're traveling between Freeport and Qeynos, you only have three paths to choose from: the long southern route through Innothule and the Rathe Moutains; the annoying northern route through Rivervale and RunnyEye; or the direct route through Highpass. Unless you've made friends with the Highpass Guards, the direct route will give you nightmares for weeks.

JaggedPine Treefolk
Surefall Glade, Qeynos Hills, Qeynos

Druids are food, and the Treefolk are no exception. They seem to resist this notion, but there it is. Kill them before they try to defend themselves - which they will, so be careful until you're ready. If you stay out of Surefall Glade (until you're ready to make it your own personal cooking pit), the only problems you should have are Holly and Cros in the Qeynos Hills and the occasional wandering druid in Qeynos.

The Wolves of the North
Halas, Everfrost

Would you believe that the barbarian warriors want you dead too? Yup. By the time I found that out it wasn't that difficult to avoid them, since the big-boned louts are about as far from Ak'Anon as possible - which is good, considering the smell. I'm sure there might be a reason to get on their good side, but I can't think of what it would be offhand.

Da Bashers
Grobb, Innothule Swamp

The troll warriors are surprisingly unfriendly. I can't imagine why. Luckily, there's nothing of value in Grobb, and the only place you'll find an occasional troll guard is in Innothule Swamp.

Ogre Guard
Oggok, Feerrott

Another surprise, the ogre guards don't look kindly to gnome Necromancers. Again, I'm not sure what's behind this, since other ogres don't seem to have an issue with us. Unfriendly ogre bouncers are a bit more of a hazard than their trollish counterparts since they wander around Feerrott, which you'll likely need to pass through if you find yourself in the southern parts of Antonica. By the time you find yourself doing that, though, you should have a number of ways to keep yourself safe.
