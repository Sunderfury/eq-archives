The Fifth Circle of the Dark Arts
for Necromancers of 16 Levels

Banshee Aura
being an Abjuration of 60 mana requiring 1 pearl

Surrounds the Necromancer with a shield that damages attackers when struck by a melee hit.

Damage shields are wonderful things. You only need to have one pearl in your inventory, it is not used up when you cast the spell. The effect doesn't last very long, so you'll want to cast it right before you step into combat. The damage done increases at higher levels. You'll also want to cast it any time that you just want to look impressive - it's a nice effect.

Cancel Magic
being an Abjuration of 30 mana

Removes a magical effect from the target.

This is useful for taking down an opponent's damage shield, or getting that lingering damage spell to go away. It's also nice to have on hand when your Shadow Knight friend gets charmed and is smacking the living daylight out of you.

Cure Disease
being an Alteration of 20 mana

Removes a lingering disease from the target.

Make them beg for it. Have them shout praises to Bertoxxulous. Get some coins. Whatever. Just don't let them think you go around curing diseases for the fun of it. We have a sinister reputation to uphold here.

Feign Death
being an Abjuration of 60 mana

Brings the Necromancer to a catatonic state which is indistinguishable from death, causing attackers to lose interest.

Or at least that's the theory. While you might always fall down, you might not always be taken for dead - in which case, you may as well be. But the first time that your pet brings a giant to you, and you fall over and it goes along its merry way without squashing you like a bug, you really start to appreciate what a wonderful thing Feign Death really is.

Heart Flutter
being an Alteration of 80 mana

Lowers the target's strength, reduces their armor class, and drains their health over time.

Another line of damage over time spells, and it stacks with both the Darkness and the Blood lines (and probably Poison as well). Stacking all three of these on a target is nasty, cruel and mean. I love it. This gives you some serious damaging ability, and the debilitating effect should make your pet's attacks more effective.

Hungry Earth
being an Alteration of 30 mana

Holds the target undead immobile and strikes them for a fair amount of damage.

Or as the saying goes, "May the road rise up to eat you."

Infectious Cloud
being a Conjuration of 78 mana

Enemies near the target are afflicted with disease, preventing them from healing, and doing a fair amount of damage over time in the process.

The damage is comparable to Heart Flutter, so don't hesitate to use it against a single target - with multiple targets, this is one of the best damage-for-mana spells you've got.

Restless Bones
being a Conjuration of 160 mana requiring 2 bone chips

Summons an undead skeleton to do the Necromancer's bidding.

This doesn't seem to be available in Necromancer guilds, but a trip to your local Shadow Knight guild should do the trick.

Shielding
being an Abjuration of 50 mana

Provides the caster with increased maximum health, an armor class bonus, and increased Magic Resistance.

The next in the series, and like always, well worth your time and money.

Shieldskin
being an Abjuration of 41 mana requiring 1 cat's eye agate

Absorbs damage before it can affect the Necromancer until its limit is reached or magical damage is taken.

It seems like any spell hit will take this down, making it less than entirely worthwhile in a lot of situations. You can also go through a lot of agates. I tend to only use this for special occasions, but on those occasions it's really a lifesaver. This is the first spell of a series.

Spirit Armor
being an Abjuration of 75 mana

Provides the target with an armor class bonus.

This is another buff that you can share with your comrades and your pet. We get this spell before the clerics do.

Voice Graft
being a Divination of 10 mana

Allows the Necromancer to speak through the mouth of the pet.

Fun. Silly, useless, and flawed, but fun.
