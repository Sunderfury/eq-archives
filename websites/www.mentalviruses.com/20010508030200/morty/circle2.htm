The Second Circle of the Dark Arts
for Necromancers of 4 Levels

Clinging Darkness
being an Alteration of 20 mana

Slows the target's movement and drains their health over time.

The first of the Darkness line of spells, which go up against the target's Magic Resistance to determine success. I consider this a standard part of combat, even though it does seem to be resisted more than I might want.

Endure Cold
being an Abjuration of 20 mana

Increases the target's Cold Resistance.

Useful when you get into a disagreement with a Wizard or Shaman, and one of the few buffing spells you can share with your friends.

Fear
being an Alteration of 40 mana

Causes the target to flee.

They'll be back. They might bring friends with them. And the duration of the fear seems to be unpredictable. Still, this can make the difference if you suddenly find yourself with two enemies instead of one. When it becomes more affordable, Fear will be a standard part of your arsenal - I almost always have it memorized.

Gate
being an Alteration of 70 mana

Teleports the Necromancer to the place of binding.

If you haven't done a Bind Affinity, this teleports you to your home point. This makes your life easier in all kinds of ways. Takes a long time to cast, but can make for a good escape spell if your pet can keep the enemies away from you. This is also the spell I cast when I suddenly don't have a pet, and I don't remember seeing it wander off - when it happens to you, you'll know. This is another spell you'll have to track down, since some Necromancer guilds don't seem to carry it.

Grim Aura
being an Alteration of 25 mana

Increases the Necromancer's attack skill.

If you're in melee, have it on. If you're not, don't. Simple.

Leering Corpse
being a Conjuration of 40 mana requiring 2 bone chips

Summons an undead skeleton to do the Necromancer's bidding.

These dead-raising spells summon a skeleton of approximately the same level as the Necromancer needs to be to cast the spell. Since a level 4 Necromancer can cast this spell, it tends to summon a level 4 skeleton. This trend continues through the line of summong spells, more or less - well, less, but that's later. You'll always want your best summoning spell available.

Lifespike
being an Alteration of 18 mana

Drains a small amount of health from the target and grants it to the Necromancer.

The next spell in the Lifetap series, and about twice as effective as the original.

Numb the Dead
being an Abjuration of 20 mana requiring 1 bone chip

Makes the target undead less likely to attack.

Cast this on one member of a group, and it won't notice when you begin to destroy it's companion right next to it. It doesn't last very long, so act quickly to pull away.

Poison Bolt
being a Conjuration of 30 mana

Damages the target with poison and drains their health over time.

The first of the Poison line of spells, which go up against the target's Poison Resistance to determine success. Another standard combat spell. Since poison resistance seems less common than magic resistance, you might have better luck with this than with the Darkness line.

True North
being a Divination of 5 mana

Points the Necromancer directly north.

Believe it or not, I've had people walk up to me and ask which way is north. I'm not kidding. In any case, if you're bored, you can cast it over and over to raise your Divination skill.
