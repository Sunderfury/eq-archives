Tailoring Guide

Tailoring is a great trade skill for newbies. It will allow you to make some basic Armor that will be better than cloth. But is it profitable for the advanced user? Not really. Other than Patchwork you can make Studded and Reinforced armor, Backpacks, Quivers and now Silk Armor.

The problem with Studded and Reinforced is the fact that it is so hard and expensive to make. It is a better idea to just buy leather or banded armor instead of making tailored items. But the one thing Studded and Reinforced are good for is raising your skill in Tailoring.

Silk Armor is something new that has been added to Tailoring. It is made from Silk you would find on large spiders.

The only real money maker in Tailoring is the backpack. The reason it is profitable is because it is lighter than a normal backpack and also has 10 slots instead of 8. But the problem is it's hard to find the main component (which we'll talk about in a bit)

To start raising your skill in Tailoring you'll first want to start out by making Patchwork. What you'll need for Patchwork are the following:

Small sewing kit (costs 7 gold)
Ruined animal pelt
Pattern (around 7 silver)

The type of animal pelt makes a difference. Bear pelt makes Large, Cat pelt makes medium, and Wolf pelt makes small.

To make the armor just put all the components in the kit, hit combine, and hope you succeed! You can raise your skill all the way to 26 this way.

Next you will want to make Studded. And trust me it's not fun! Not only will you have to find Medium quality pelts, you'll also have to find a smith who is willing to make you tiny little studs which can be expensive to make. But doing this should raise your skill even higher. Keep doing this until it's trivial. Here is what you'll need:

Large sewing kit
Medium quality pelt
Pattern
Stud(s)

The number of studs you need depends on the peice of armor you want to make (make sure each stud is in a different slot.. don't stack them!)

Mask -1 stud
Gorget, Belt, and Bracers - 2 studs
Caps, Shoulderpads, and Sleeves - 3 studs
Cloak, Gloves, Pants, and Boots - 4 studs
Tunic - 5 studs

Now you will want to get to the final step in Armor making. And I hate to say it but it's not anymore more fun than making studded! To make Reinforced Armor you need the following:

Large sewing kit
High quality pelt
Pattern
Boning(s)

Once again the number of boning you need depends on the peice of armor you want to make..

Mask, Gorget, Belt, and Bracers - 1 boning
Caps, Shoulderpads, Sleeves, Cloak, and Gloves - 2 bonings
Pants, and Boots - 3 bonings
Tunic - 4 bonings

Keep making as much Reinforced armor as you can. It's hard to get the High Quality pelts but the higher your skill is the better chance you'll have at making backpacks. When Reinforced becomes trivial or you get sick of trying to find the pelts you should move on to the backpacks.

You will need the following things to make Backpacks...

Large sewing kit
High Quality Bear Skin
Backpack Pattern

I've heard people say you can use High Quality Bear pelts also but I'm not totally sure about that. I'm sure that skins work though. They are hard to find but when you make a backpack you can sell it for 30pp or more! If your skill in Tailoring is high enough and you can keep finding the skins you could make some good money.

To make Raw Silk Armor just combine 2 silks in a large sewing kit. This will make silk swatch. One swatch for gorget, cap, gloves, sandals, and sleeves. Two for leggings and tunic. It can be worn by Monk, Necromancer, Wizard, Magician, and Enchanter.

Well that's our little guide to Tailoring. It's not an easy trade to learn but it's not a flooded market like Smithing is. Go out and make some Patchwork!
