Guide to playing a cleric

Introduction

So, you're interested in playing a cleric? Why Play a cleric, you say??

Because, what other spell-casting class can wear platemail? None! Being able to wear that gives you access to some of the best armor in the game. Plus, they are At LEAST average fighers. Highly under-rated in that area. But, clerics arn't really great spell-casters you say? You are wrong. Clerics posses the BEST healing skills in the game. No other classes may area-heal or heal to full hit points. Clerics can do both at higher levels. And, they posses the best spells-against-undead in the game. You'd be quite suprised by how good the clerics direct damage spells do. and amazed by the undead ones. Clerics also have, the best hit points buff in the game. Not to mention an average amount of hit points int he first place, not quite a warrior or ranger, but far higher then a wizard or enchanter....At higher levels, they have spells that can give characters hundreds of extra hit points. And finally...... there is no better feeling then saving ones life. And you are the best at doing it in the game.

Chosing your race

The races a cleirc may be are:

Dark Elf, Dwarf, Erudite, Gnome, Halfling, High Elf, Human.

Each is different and better in their own ways. Humans advance quicker then all other races. a big plus.

Dwarves probably have the best attributes for clerics. High Elves posses good attributes for a cleric, whilst still having high intelligence. A very important attribute in the world of Norrath. Erudites posses very high intelligence and wisdom. Halflings are probably the second best attributes for clerics, and gnomes the 3rd.

You may also wish to pick your race based on personal prefrence, rather then attributes and the like... I chose dwarf.

Chosing your attributes

Clerics main attribute is wisdom. I suggest you put most of your points there. I cannot stress that enough, with my great ability to wear platemail.

I thought I'd dump my points into agility, because, they can't hurt you if they can't hit you right? wrong. Npc's spells still do, and theres more important things then missing hits...though Clerics are experts in the feild of dodging and armor, they are best at spells. And clerics derrive their mana from wisdom. For the first 20 levels that extra agility was great, but as you get higher, you become more of a caster and less of a warrior.

After that wisdom, I reccomend putting the last into strength.

Starting out

Well, the first few levels are by far the easiest. and you don't even really need any damage spells, just give yourself courage, and melee everything.

You may want to buy Strike for lvl 3 or 4 tho.

For the first couple of gold, buy yourself your first melee weapon (other then the starting one).

And, now, we have come to what is probably the hardest dicesion for a cleric. What kind of weapon? 1H or 2H blunt? People can fight for hours about which is better, but neither will ever win. By the time I figured out what was best to do, I was far to high a level to do it. but you can, Keep your weapon skill level maxed per level at both 1 and 2h blunt. Always keep both a 1handed and 2handed blunt weapon on you, and everytime you level, quickly max the skill out, and then fight with your preferered kind.

Many think, hey, 2H blunt does more damage so it must be better. And it does do more damage, but is not better. Nor is 1H better then 2H. Damage is not all that counts. 1Hblunt lets you use a sheild. And there are many VERY good sheilds out there, like ones that bring up wisdom. That is VERY important, because as I said, you become more of a caster as you get higher. And I don't mean just some wisdom. I mean, lots. With both a 1H blunt weapon and sheild with wisdom boosters, you can get 15+ more wisdom. That helps ALOT.

And the extra AC on the sheild is good too. So, its best you just stay maxed at both. Use a 2H blunt weapon against those weak opponents you can melee against, and the spells against those strong ones you need mana to root and cast against. By the way, for clerics, the max skill per lvl in 1H and 2H blunt, is:

Your level, x4 +4. Example: For lvl 8 it'd be 32 (4x8) +4. (36).

Advancing

As time progresses you will gain your 5th lvl. By now you should be fimiliar with the spell system and should be able to buy new spells with money you've saved up. Speaking of that. For clerics, money isn't easy. You need to buy weapons, spells and armor. All of which becomes very expensive.

But if you listen to my reccomendations and fight the things with good loot, money shoudn't be a problem. (having afew good friends who can lend you money from time to time isn't bad either) The best places to hunt as a cleric are places with undead. And its worth moving around alot for the extra money/easy kills you get by finding them.

The best place for undead is kaladim, the dwarf home or Freeport, the human home. If you do not pick a human or dwarf. Then I reccomend you go to one of those two cities once you reach 5th lvl and can probably make the trips safely. I reccomend kaladim, but if you chose freeport, The dungeon befallen (located in west commonlands) is good for lvls 5 and up. But it gets boring quick in there. The latest you'll ever be able to stay there without going mad is lvl 20. I reccomend taking the the other route, which is: Kaladim

From 5th lvl up, once obtaining the damage-against-undead spell, ward undead, Hunt Dwarf Skeletons at the skeleton tower.

Ask for driections in kaladim to it if you don't know where it is. Its near the cross-roads. I reccomend that until 8th or 9th level. Now, if you've stayed till lvl 9 like I reccomend, you can buy some new spells. Center, which is very useful, ROOT, which some say, is the best spell in the game, and afew useful others.

Then head to Steamfont Mountains (ask for directions) and kill dwarf skeletons past the Minotaur Caves (ask for directions). They are around your level. Then, at level 10 or 11 go kill undead pawns at the chessboard. They should all be blue or green to you by now. It is located in ButcherBlock Mountains near the Greater faydark zone (ask for driections). Every couple of spawns you'll find undead knights and bishops. usualy one or two levels higher then the others. Sometimes the Undead King spawns too. That isn't always good tho, I think he's level 13-15. Anyways, after that, I reccomend you go to a place called Highpass Hold. Far away. Past freeport, in between it and qeynos. Its located beside Kithicor Forest (a hunting ground near Rivervale). If you go in the actualy city, High Keep, there is a prison.

That is a GREAT place for levels 13, 14 ad 15. The prisoners are easy. And if you get in too much danger, just go into the cell with the princess and they can't hit ya. rest, and med, and go fight again. Once you reach 15 or 16 its time to do your first long stay in on place.

This place is one of my favourtie places on Norrath, Near freeport also, in between South and North desert of Ro. The Oasis of Marr.

So, go from highpass, to oasis, and kill orc warirors. (don't bother with the preists, they are mostly the same level and much harder) You can ussualy find a nice spot near the oasis (lake with the spectre-island)

By then you will have smite, 80 damage plus, and healing, heals 80 damage plus, and root, and center.

When you are lvl 19, most of the orcs will be green. So it'll be hard experience, and even at 18 several orcs will be green. But it'd be more then worth the wait at lvl 20 when you get a lastname and much more to do.

I reccomend staying there till level 20. Then, its time to start adventuring. You can still find high level undead in Unrest near Kaladim, but you can now travel to ALOT more of the world. And you'll find it alot more fun playing EverQuest.

Armor, weapons, spells

As for buying armor and weapons and spells. Spells are very expensive, but if you borrow some money from good friends afew times, you should do alright. The monsters I reccomended you fight all give reasonably good loot. And as for armor, stick with your patchwork or leather till about 6th, 7th lvl, when you've bought your 5th spells and can afford abit of ringmail.

After that, save up for your nineth level spells, and after that, you can start buying bronze. Bronze armor is a type of platemail that some clerics keep all their lives.

Keep saving up for spells tho, they become more and more expensive all the time. Only buy the ones you use of course. Now it comes to one of the last parts of my guide. Armor. There are two different routes a cleric may take: The "Buffing armor" and the "AC armor". AC armor is ussualy the one clerics take till around level 20. Then, you start wanting more wisdom for spells, and they start ditching that heavy bronze armor for light attribute rising armor. Always very expensive, but ussualy worth it. There isn't really much more I can say about that. You don't have to switch. But I reccomend you do, afew examples of such items are: Lizardscale Cloak. BloodStained Mantle. BloodStained Tunic. etc etc. They all bring up attributes and help alot in the long run. Start looking for them around level 20+, thats when they start becoming quite useful and affordable.
