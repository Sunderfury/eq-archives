10:00 AM REVENGE OF THE HELL SMURFS

I have been getting more mail than you can believe from the "Major Kyra and the Smurf Army" series of stories. Almost as much as the GM Darwin escapade. This is scary.

Many of you pointed out that the C/SRC/SLC program has been undergoing major reforms, and old-schoolers like me who have fond memories of kewld3ewd smurfs scouting ahead for their buds during guildwars is definitely ancient history. Apparently (and this is a novel concept, I must admit) those counselors who still are around actually answer player questions on a regular basis.

Many others pointed out that Cs also have the power to teleport people around.

One particularly knowledgable correspondent who asked to remain anonymous explained exactly how he was using his position to reform what he could, and remained as seperate from the GMs and politics as possible, and in general made me feel like a weasel.

Kyra wrote my wife asking her to get me to shut up, though, which made me feel better.

This has been a longstanding problem, as evidenced by a letter on the subject I published months ago. Basically, the smurfs have one hell of an image problem. (They know this, which I think is why they email-bombed me en masse last night.) Many are doing their best to combat this. Others couldn't give a crap, and spend all their time in IRC.

However, it appears to me that they are trying. And given the number of folks who are out there in positions of something approaching responsibility (1 SLC per shard, multiple SRCs per shard), there really have not been that many accounts of abuse of authority that have reached my ears. Some, but not many.

Another thing to consider is that many of the most virulent rumors are started by counselors who have fallen short of the glory of smurfdom and were derobed. They have consequent axes to grind. I like to think I usually do a pretty good job of taking this into account.

So it looks like I got one wrong. Not all SRCs/SLCs are useless ass-kissing politicians. Just some. And the sooner they are purged from the system, the better.

1:30 PM PH33R THE HELL SMURFS

*Very* interesting letter just came in. I can't say I agree with this correspondent's enthusiasm about OSI's "solution". The possibility for abuse staggers the imagination.

Your comments, and any confirmation on what Riff Raff witnessed, are encouraged.

Ok, I discovered something last night while playing on Siege Perilous that in my mind answers all questions about the future of SP, wipe or no-wipe, and the new anti-macro measures that are going in this weekend. Now some of this is speculation but if you look at all the facts, it makes a lot of sense.

So this week has been kind of a roaller-coaster ride of emotion, lots of rumours, and quite a lot of changes to the awesome new Shard we've been playing. For many it's felt like a new breath of fresh air for UO, it's "how UO was supposed to be." For others it's been a nightmare, for such things as being banned for going to the bathroom, being afk, and many other stores we heard about GM Abuse. And lately there has been a lot of discussion of wipe or no wipe for the shard.

The post from the OGD member basically summed up why a wipe should not occur, and it was dead-on. The players and OSI agreed that this was a test shard, and that it would be wiped ONLY in the event of a major unbalancing bug, on the magnitude of a gold dupe. Players like the ones in OGD who busted ass all week to succeded in the new shard with the added level of difficulty were going off the assumption that the deal would be kept by OSI, and that this wasn't just a "bust all the macroers and find their exploits" experiment. I am included into the group of people who sacrificed sleep and all spare time to satisfy my addiction to this new shard and to try and get the best possible start I could. All this talk of a possible wipe at the end of this 10 day marathon has made me upset as well, as that wasn't the deal, and all it did was punish the ones who played by the rules (no unattended macroing!), tried their hardest, and pulled no punches in building their characters and the economy of this brand new, and IMHO, most exciting shard to ever start up.

Now you have people like Myschyf whining to you and all over the message boards about how a wipe is needed. Now I call them whiners because they are trying to get OSI to change the deal. Well sorry, it wasn't "At the end of the week we'll hold a poll to help determine weither or not to wipe the shard". We could look at the people who are pro-wipe and try and figure out where they are coming from, read their complains, and still find no valid reason. Invalid reasons that come to mind: They were banned 48 hrs for macroing unattended like fools on the first day and "want a chance to catch up". They made a mistake on the skills they picked with their starting character, and were planning on starting over anyways, so why shouldn't everyone else <- lots of people in this category. And then there are those who were afraid of a possible wipe, did not want to invest time and effort into building characters or weath, and decide that if they can whine loud enuf like they have on other shards, OSI will wipe the shard to appease the masses. Then on Wednesday we get this funky mana/stamina depletion when using skills, and every single person in the game complains. And more pro-wipers are formed as all the sudden "anyone who got skills before that rule has a major advantage".. ok so they took it out 24 hrs later, and nobody's hurt one way or another.

So now everyone is unsure of whats going on. Do we spend more time and effort and build on what we accomplished up til now? Well for me, it seemed that a wipe was highly dependant on the "new solution to macroing" targeted for this weekend. It seemed obvious, that if it was anything like what we saw on wednesday, there would be nobody happy who didn't have a decent character already, as now it would be harder than before to build skills.. and therefore create an imbalance. So that left the people like myself and OGD who worked their tail off playing as hard as they could the last week wondering if it was all in vain and what possible solution could satisfy everyone's needs. 1:30am PST last night it all came together.

I was practicing healing in the Vesper Jewlry shop when I got an ICQ about a SRC showing up at the Vesper Bank. The message basically described how the purple-robed SRC Wysp showed up, dragging a macrower behind. Everyone crowded around and started talking to him, and all of the sudden a lightning bolt came down from the sky, blasting the macroer and kicking him from the server. (Friend who ICQ'd me started asking "when did SRCs get this power?" A few seconds later, to my surprise, SRC Wysp appeared next to me and my healing partner. he (she?) said "Are you guys there?" .. we responded that we were.. and then the counselor said "ok, thanks, have fun!" and dissappeared. Then the gears started turning... 9am delayed maintenance for Siege Perilous, a SRC showing GM-like powers that were talked about in a "top secret report on Dr. Twister's site" that had been long forgotten, and a "macro solution that was planned for implimentation this weekend".

Have you figured it out yet? SRC's (and maybe lesser counselors) are going to be the macro police. There are certainly more of them than GM's and, with their increased powers, can hand out justice to those who break the rules, and keep everyone honest. Wow this was one of those ideas that actually made a ton of sense! This certainly doesn't change the game balance, skills you could macro are not any more difficult after this "solution". And NO WIPE has to occur of all the people who put in tons of time and effort hoping that OSI would keep their word and not punish us for actually trying, and playing by the rules! The delayed 9am maintenance may have had something to do with this as well, to teach counselors how to use their new powers, or it could have nothing at all to do with it, doesn't really matter. But this, IMHO, is an incredibly smart answer to the macro "problem".

Now I can already hear it. "I dont trust countslors, they'll just help out their friends on the shard". Well sometimes you are going to have to trust the cops... I think the likelihood of them using these powers to ban/kick people to help their friends is remote. And it certainl beats any rediculous changes that affect gameplay or skill advancement such as the ones implemented on Wednesday (and quickly removed at the next maintenance). I for one am willing to trust these guys to do their "job" as macro-police. They certainly couldn't do any worse than some of the GM's have earlier this week.

This may be a new age for Ultima Players as we know it. Siege Perilous is by far the best shard to ever come out. When you hear about how much fun it is, and how it's "bringing players out of retirement", it's true. And it's not surprising that there has been so much emotion invovled in the discussions. People who worked hard, played by the rules, adapted, cooperated, did whatever it took to excel, don't want a wipe, as they would be ripped off of their time and that wasn't part of the deal. People who cheated and got banned, didn't make as much of an effort, or just are upset because some people are "ahead" or accomplished stuff like OGD, think they can "whine" their way to a wipe and excuse it as "it's only 2 weeks, we've played other chars on shards for 2 years.. whats the big deal?". Well, it is a big deal to us. And this solution is the best step towards getting past this wipe/no wipe issue and getting on with playing the game. When groups of 20 noto a grey for looting his fallen friend, the good guys have to step up. We're ready. Thanks Runesabre, for hopefully not letting the whiners ruin this shard like, many beleive, the other shards have been. Thanks for keeping your part of the deal. As you can already see in the first week of play, it's worth it.

1:00 AM WATCH THAT FUNKY SHARD WIPE, BOY EPISODE V: THE PLAYERS STRIKE BACK

As the debate on whether or not to wipe Siege Perilous rages, within and without Origin, the OGD guild has been one of the loudest voices in insisting that a wipe not take place. This makes sense to me; they absolutely tore up the shard, and under very difficult circumstances did what no guild, including my own, could do, and that was get a house and guildstone placed. Here's some words from another OGD player, which I must admit are fairly persuasive. I was convinced a shard wipe had to happen for the sake of balance; now I'm not as convinced.

I think the horse is still twitching, so let's beat on it some more.. :).

Time is a valuable personal commodity, and it's very upsetting when it's stolen. Let's look at things chronologically...

  • SP Test opens. Rune is explicit that a) they do not want to wipe, and would do so only upon the appearance of an imbalancing bug, and b) players would be notified as soon as possible in the event of a wipe.

  • Armed with this information, this implicit agreement, we make a "rational expectations" decision and decide to play SP *hard*. Forgoing character development we train our sights on the biggest gold faucet in the game, Deceit. (BTW, there are supposed to be gold faucets in this game. So long as they're not cheese like fancy shirt sewing, I don't see how you can complain). First step, a boat. After many hours of monster bashing and NPC escorting (2 full days), we find we have a boat. You know that giddy feeling you get when you've worked hard for something and can finally buy it? I think we all felt that very strongly as we saw our boat appear on the shore of Skara Brae. It was a happy bunch that rode off to Deceit that first day.

  • Rune updates us with the information that all is proceeding according to plan. No major game imbalances or bugs have been seen. A minor tinkering exploit is closed. Unattended macroers are worked hard by the GMs; many are banned. No one in OGD is banned.

  • We train healing. We kill liches.

  • Out of the blue the dev team implements a silly mechanism to cripple macroing. Now a wipe, which was supposed to occur only in the result of a bug in the current code under test, is almost necessitated. In the absence of a real game imbalance, they code one in. Naturally we are outraged, and I don't really understand how you can fault us.

  • We proudly place our house and stone.

  • The anti-macroing code is removed. Players call for a wipe. We protest. They reduce our legitimate reasoning to silly strawmen, characterizing us as crybabies and whiners. We hear news of further anti-macroing changes.

I really don't understand how we can be called crybabies or selfish, when we played by the rules and worked hard, together, to accomplish something. We're not saying that OSI ignore a major dupe or stat hack. We're saying, umm, hello, you changed the rules on us in mid-stream, and it's not fair. You've (potentially) stolen many hours of our lives. We're not macro'd up super-characters. In fact our rule in Deceit was engage no one, as we'd probably get our asses kicked.

For us, the argument is not about losing our precious macroing. Who cares? As long as we're on the same level, it's not an issue. But shouldn't we be compensated for the huge effort we extended in good faith and within the rules?

As I see it, the no wipe side consists of:

  • honest players who played hard under the initial guidelines set by the dev team.
  • macroers who want an edge. The type who enjoy 1-hit weapons and insta-death spell bugs and dupes.

Do NOT lump us in with the 2nd group.

The WIPE side is varied:

  • players who want to see the game balanced and level for all.
  • those who screwed up.
  • those who got in late.
  • those who had planned to wait all along.

Three of the four groups on the wipe side have very SELFISH motivations for wanting a wipe.

Think about THAT :P.

Meursault - OGD
