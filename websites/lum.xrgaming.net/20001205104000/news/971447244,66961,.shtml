A KINDER, GENTLER SIEGE PERILOUS
Friday, October 13, 2000 - 7:27 AM [Filed by lum]
Origin put forth its thoughts concerning Siege Perilous' future yesterday (for those unfamiliar with UO, SP was the "hard shard" with an advancement system that was far slower than other servers and completely unrestricted PvP) and the community took note.

Briefly, the proposed changes remove the Rate-Over-Time (ROT) skill system that caused character development to slow in favor of the burst-hour rapid skill development system on other shards, the tremendously buggy hero/evil rules fragments will finally be yanked, and regs and vendor upkeep will no longer cost 3x as much. In addition, NPC guards will be pulled from non-faction towns, which makes sense, since 99% of SP's population seems to be red.

Things that will remain constant include vendors not buying items from players, no stat loss, no recall, no mules and no Trammel.

Runesabre, the long-quiet UO programmer who's working on a seekrit project everyone already seems to know about (a new UO client) and the original designer of Siege Perilous, popped into the lion's den of Battle Vortex's Siege Perilous forums to clarify a few things. Guards will remain in faction cities since they are also starting cities and without guard protection hunting newbies for sport would probably cut down on said newbies joining SP. He also pointed out that with guard protection removed from factionless cities, players face a real choice in either patronizing a city owned by a faction or in risking going into a free-fire zone (where the established red guilds of Siege would probably be more dangerous than any faction).

I'm definitely interested at what's going on here. Siege's community has been steadily dwindling, and that is in the main because trying to develop a new character using RoT while under fire from PKs is an exercise in futility. The proposed changes bring Siege more in line with the concept it originally launched with -- a veteran PvP-oriented shard which is closer to the original vision of UO. No Trammel. No stat loss. No RoT. Just grab yer sword and go.

Most PvPers who remained on the other shards denigrated Siege as "Siege Tedious" precisely because of the RoT barrier and never joined up. And many Siege players liked it that way - the barrier to entry ensured that those who were on Siege were dedicated to "the cause" - willing to put up with the time investment to participate in a very uncrowded, "mature" shard.

I'd have to agree with Origin here though - if Siege is to survive, it needs new players. A steady influx of new players to replace the natural attrition of those who tire of Siege in specific, or UO in general. Obviously, it's not getting that right now. If the server is to survive - and to justify Origin's investment in maintaining the server farm it's hosted on - it requires people to actually use it.

Some of them will not be what Siege players will like. Some will be dewds. Some will be res gankers. Some will be clueless newbs who complain about being attacked. Some will be transvestite Elves with a lisp. You can't really choose your fellow players, unfortunately (at least not right now) and the community will be changed.

But in this case change is not only good, it's necessary. And Siege players shouldn't be mourning, but celebrating the commitment Origin's made to keep their little bastion of combat and old-school UO alive and well.

Yes, Glendor, I'm coming back to Siege. You can thank whoever drew up this latest proposal.
