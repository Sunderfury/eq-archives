EXPANDING THE RIGHTS OF PLAYERS
Thursday, October 26, 2000 - 6:43 PM [Filed by myschyf]
Today, at work, I offered to let a coworker play one of my UO accounts so he could see if he likes the game or not. I'll spend my time, my money (in the form of a paid account), and give him the benefit of my experience, in order to show him around the game. If he likes the game, he'll buy it, and I'll have given OSI another customer. However after I offered to let him play my account, I sat him down and went through a list of all the things that can get one banned from the game. This is a rather sad beginning for a game, don't you think? It's also rather disturbing that I feel I have to do this in order to protect my accounts. I know that, if he does something wrong, no matter how intended the wrong-doing might be, I'll be the one to pay the price. I also know that no one will care that I've been a customer for three years, never been a grief player, and that I was showing the game to a new person when the mishap occurred. This, coupled with the story Stormwaltz wrote and dedicated to Mystere and the recent VI bannings (or suspensions or whatever they were) has me rethinking bannings as a punishment. Where do the rights of the game company to control their intellectual property and their servers end and where do those of the player begin?

Thursday, October 26, 2000 - 4:03 PM [Filed by lum]
Gordon "Abashi" Wrinn posted the following clarification to Verant's EQ board regarding the bannings we referred to earlier:

I'm not sure where this came from. First, no one has been banned (yet). There are three player accounts that are currently suspended, but they all play from the same location, and correlate to information on the site and ICQ profiles related to the posters.

The action is being pursued because account warnings from our warning database were copied/pasted on the message boards there. Guides and GMs have access to that warning info, and the location where those three accounts post from is also the same location as the Guide.

I'm not sure who got the idea that the operators of that site provided us with IP addresses, but they haven't.
