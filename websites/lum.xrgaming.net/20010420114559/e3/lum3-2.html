E3-2K: Lum Gives Up And Shoots People At Random
Lum does E3, part two

I'm sorry, I tried. But E3 just beat the living crap out of me and sent me home to Momma. The lights, the screaming, the beeping, the $2.75 Diet Cokes and $7.95 salads, the pedestrians who honestly don't give a crap if people run them over, the bad Nirvana cover bands that play next door to meeting rooms, it's just all too goddam much.

Well, actually there wasn't too much scheduled, and what was scheduled, um, didn't happen. A planned meeting with Serafina Pechan this morning fell through, although I ran into the thundering Atriarch herd later -- literally, the entire Atriarch team moved about en masse in Atriarch t-shirts and descended on random website ranters demanding that I ask them questions. I didn't know. My brain hurt. The only question I had was would I ever feel my feet again. I asked them if they liked PK switches. Hah, they sneered condescendingly. We don't need PK switches because no one ever dies! (Well, they do, but it's hard.) Obviously I was confused and lost, so I fled. The various alien thingies on the back of the Atriarch dev team laughed and laughed.

I was also supposed to meet the illustrious Doctor TwistEr and, shamed into humility by the humanizing action of meeting him face to face, realize that we are all brothers and that, dammit, together we would get Betsy Sue's farmhouse built before the harvest. Unfortunatly Dr. T. stood me up. Damn them all to hell! I did get to meet J., but he's like the light side of the TwistEr force, so it doesn't really count. Oh well, I will continually miscapitalize his name in my own TwistEd revenge. Hah, I say! I did get to meet Jason Booth, lead artiste over at Turbine, and after a secret meeting I am authorized to announce this: Jason Booth is cool. There, now don't tell me I'm not producing hard news.

Later the entire MMORPG community - literally - descended on some hilariously overpriced Italian restaraunt where half were promptly turned away for not being Swedish enough or something. I don't understand L.A., and I don't pretend to. I spent most of the evening at a table with Scott "Samwise" Kurtz, Den Dragon of Stratics, Raph "Raph" Koster and Jeff "Knight" Anderson, whom I let know that guilds were REALLY GOOD IDEAS and would probably go into future detail later.

Anyway today was a light day, so we should have a heavy day tomorrow, being the law of swingsets or something. I don't know. My brain's fried. Here, have some pictures.
