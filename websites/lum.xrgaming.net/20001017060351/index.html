TGFKAUO2 NOT TO ACTUALLY TECHNICALLY HAVE A NAME
Friday, October 13, 2000 - 3:29 PM [Filed by lum]
Quite a bit of a furor raised by Jinx's snooping out of Origin's purchasing of ultima-nexus.com. You would think they might have some motivation behind it (like, say, making a website for a game of the same name) but David Swofford told gamers.com that no, they are REALLY GODDAM HAPPY to have the name of Worlds of Ultima Origin Worlds of Ultimate Online Dancing Happy Meer Bounce Bounce Britannia! Those kids and their websites today. However, we've scooped everyone with the following announcement from Windfeather over on Somerandomname.Stratics.com message board:

I'm sorry, but as much as we would like to, we cannot promise our game will have a name at ship. We only want to make promises we know we can fulfill to avoid disappointing our fans. We do realize the importance of having a name, however, and have done some preliminary design work in that area. We therefore can promise you that if our game were to have a name at ship, it would be fully integrated with the other parts of our design. This is because we have a crack team of developers who have in the past named other MMORPGs, and who would avoid any common naming mistakes made by other companies.

Reading this, I decided to get to the bottom of this mystery immediately, and after going through the dictionary applying Bree-Caitlin Phenome Analysis to every possible word in the English, French and German languages as they would apply to MMORPG gaming, I drew a complete blank. So I went to plan B. I flew down to Austin and met with my contact, known only as Deep GM. He told me the true, deep dark secret of TheGameFormerlyKnownAsUO2 - its name would actually be a symbol. We're looking for that symbol as we speak folks, and rest assured, when we find out the symbol that denotes the game that until really quite recently was known as something involving Origin, worlds, and online, we will let you know here first. Unless Jinx beats us to the punch.

TOMMY STRAND ON THE UO EX-VOLUNTEER SUIT
Wednesday, October 11, 2000 - 8:23 AM [Filed by lum]
Tommy Strand, producer of the anticipated Anarchy Online, had a few things to say about UO's current legal troubles in his Developer's Journal on AO Vault. It's a good read and a snapshot into what MMOG developers are thinking right now.

So what are the alternatives? If the plaintiff succeeds and the online games are brought to their knees, then what? The answer might be easy: import. The same thing that happens when local production costs become too high and companies import labor and components from other countries, the volunteer programs might suffer the same fate. People who live in countries where suing is a real risk, are denied the right to help other players no matter their devotion and wishes. For example Canadians might be picked over Americans. Canadian suing traditions are more like the European or so I have been told. You have all US time zones (and as a bonus you get two languages). It is also a known fact that a lot of volunteers in EQ and UO are Europeans without that being a big issue with anyone.
