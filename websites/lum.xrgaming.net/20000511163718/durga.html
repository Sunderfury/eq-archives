Interview with Jessica “Durga” Mulligan
Director of External Relations for Online Service, Origin Systems, Inc.
(12/21/99) In addition to being responsible for the operation of Ultima Online’s Volunteer system, Ms. Mulligan also pontificates on a weekly basis for Happy Puppy on the state of online gaming.

<Lum> Origin announced a few months back as part of a new "Six Month Plan" that over 3,000 volunteers would be added to the various UO shards. How's that coming along?

<Durga> Slowly.  We're about half-way there...
<Durga> with slightly over 1,250 active another 400 in training and expected to graduate into the various programs by February...
<Durga> And we'll be doing another round of recruiting in January...
<Durga> So I'm not sure we'll hit the 3,000 mark.  we ought to be pretty close, though.

<Lum> What specifically will you be recruiting for? Many folks who've applied haven't heard anything back.

<Durga> We'll be recruiting for all three programs (Companions, Counselors and Interest)...
<Durga> and anyone who hasn't heard back one way or the other from us about an application should email in...
<Durga> and ask...

<Lum> Who should they email?

<Durga> They can always email me at durga@uo.com for any program.  For the Interest program, goatboy@uo.com...

<Lum> Goatboy?
<Lum> Doesn't he have an 80's music show on MTV?

<Durga> LOL. ON the side, I'm sure.
<Durga> for Counselors, kal@uo.com or nocturne@uo.com...
<Durga> for Companions, bodi@uo.com...

<Lum> Speaking of Companion goats.. er, Companions...

<Durga> <g>

<Lum> I've been on the regular American shards (not Siege) quite a bit of late, and I don't think I've ever seen a (young) player.
<Lum> What do Companions do?

<Durga> One reason you haven't seen Young players, is the flag wasn't working correctly...
<Durga> so new accounts weren't always being correctly set to the Young state...
<Durga> however, as of last Friday, that was fixed and the Companions have been going great guns...

<Lum> About how many Companion calls are being answered daily on the US shards?

<Durga> I got one report yesterday of one Companion alone who worked with 20 Youngs over the weekend...
<Durga> Don't have those figure at fingertips, sorry. And not sure I'm allowed to divulge that kind of specific info; I'll ask my boss though...
<Durga> and if he has no objection, I'll get them to you.

<Lum> Well, I think the concern is more there *were* no young players. if the flag wasn't working, though that would probably explain it to a degree.

<Durga> Yep.  Believe me, there are plenty of Young players.
<Durga> As the Companions found out this weekend, :D

<Lum> Did anyone say anything before Friday? Like, "Hey, um, we're not getting any new players"?

<Durga> Yes, you bet (rubs ear)...
<Durga> we actually tried a couple different fixes...
<Durga> but it took us a while to get it nailed.
<Durga> No excuses, we just had a hard time with it.

<Lum> Is there any way of being able to tell who a Companion is when they're with a new player?

<Durga> Not at present.  We do have a title mechanism i place for it, that adds the Companion title to a character, but...
<Durga> it needs work.
<Durga> So we pulled it out.

<Lum> I'd think it would be helpful. Something like "Please don't kill me, I'm on duty"

<Durga> Agreed, <g>.

<Lum> Of course, that might have exactly the opposite effect.

<Lum> Maybe just give them spiffy hats.
<Lum> But then people would just kill them for the spiffy hats.
<Lum> You're right, it needs work.

<Durga> LOL.  Yep, you see the problems.

<Durga> Part of that problem will be solved with the 6 month plan patches...
<Durga> we're creating a place that will only allow in Youngs and Voluteers, for training and mentoring purposes.

<Lum> This is part of the new lands you're not supposed to tell me anything about?

<Durga> Probably, :D.
<Durga> I don't mind revealing that much.

<Lum> Aw come on. Give us a hint!

<Durga> Sorry, that's the extent of today's revelations, :D. A "new" only area makes sense, however.
<Durga> Give them time to get acclimated before they hear the words Corp Por

<Lum> Is there going to be a "newbie dungeon" like "mud school" on other MUDs, then?

<Durga> Similar in concept, but not in execution.  And that's all I can say right now, sorry.

<Lum> Let's move on to the Interest program for a moment.
<Lum> Gordon "Tyrant" Walton made a prediction about the number of events they'd be running a week...
<Lum> (sorry, looking up that exact figure :P thought I had it written down)

<Durga> 1,000 a week...

<Lum> That's cheating, I wasn't supposed to ask you.

<Durga> it wasn't a prediction, but a mandate to get to 1,000 a week by 12/15.
<Durga> <grin>

<Lum> Did we make it?

<Durga> Nope.  More like 200 a week.
<Durga> (Looks for a place to fall on her sword)

<Lum> Even that sounds like a lot.
<Lum> The only events I've heard of have been on the Asian shards...

<Durga> The main reason is that training the new Interest folk is taking a lot longer than expected.
<Durga> Actually, they are on all shards...
<Durga> We've already gotten with LadyMOI to start publicizing them better...
<Durga> so folks actually know about them.

<Lum> What's a typical event like? Just in case I run into one.

<Durga> Depends.  The other day, someone ran a poetry contest in the middle of Trinsic...
<Durga> and the other end of the island, an Elder got together about 10 new players and did a chicken hunt (I'm not making this up...)...
<Durga> that ended up with about 40 players and several Elders participating at various times.

<Lum> What's an elder? I don't even want to ask what a chicken hunt is.

<Durga> An Elder spends his/her time, for the most part, designing and running events and quests for the beginner to intermediate level player...
<Durga> and they are usually short and sharp, completed in 30 minutes to a few hours.

<Lum> So, like a Seer, but slightly lower and to the right.

<Durga> LOL.  More like just to the right and around the corner.  On the other end of the coin, Seers tend to specialize in long term fiction and plots...
<Durga> based around role-playing characters they have created.

<Lum> Do you have a big chart on your wall that draws out all these ranks?

<Durga> Indeed, I do keep an org chart.

<Lum> Let me fire off a few questions about that if you don't mind. I realize that some of it may not exactly fall under your purview.

<Durga> OK.

<Lum> The plot, not the org chart. Org charts scare me.

<Durga> Me, too <shivers>

<Lum> Is there a plan to make a plot for UO? One that makes sense?
<Lum> I mean a connected sort of "where the world is going" plot, not random disconnected events.

<Durga> What, you mean one large, over-riding piece of fiction that ties everything together?

<Lum> Uh huh.

<Durga> Yes, indeed.  We're working on it now.  I will reveal nothing about it.  except...
<Durga> Some players started seeing pieces of it today.

<Lum> Will it be the same for every shard, or will each shard develop on its own?

<Durga> Both.  Not to sound flaky, but...
<Durga> The fiction will apply to all shards, but the results of the player interaction with that fiction don't necessarily have to be the same...
<Durga> on all shards.

<Lum> Well, one problem I see with "every shard has the same plot" is that the players are denied the chance to shape it.
 <Lum> I mean, if there's a Big Evil Demon that's terrorizing Britain, I want to kill it.
<Lum> Someone else on another shard may want to make friends with it and find out why it's being antisocial

<Durga> True. Of course, it's harder n' hell to support that...
<Durga> even with hundreds of volunteers working at it day and night.

<Lum> Well yeah, that's why I'm here giving interviews and you're there.

<Durga> LOL
<Durga> We're trying to add that element in, though...
<Durga> I agree with you (and the players on your forum board who have been discussing it)..
<Durga> we've got to find some way to allow the players to determine at least some of the outcomes...
<Durga> and make permanent or at least semi-permanent changes to shards, on an individual basis.
<Durga> Once the fiction we're introducing now runs through it's major plot line, we can and will do that.

<Lum> I think one problem players have with the game is a feeling of disconnectedness from it... no matter what they or their guild do, Lord British still doesn't show up in his castle and the guards still thwack people for jaywalking.

<Durga> That will be changing...
<Durga> Let me tangent for a sec...

<Lum> OK

<Durga> I've spent a LOT of time reading your board, Stratics, CoB, several others...

<Lum> My apologies.

<Durga> (LOL)
<Durga> most tend to make the same points when it comes to Interest...

<Lum> Besides "where the hell is it???"

<Durga> and that is, they want to make a difference in the history (yeah, that, too).
<Durga> So to fix those two problems, we're recruiting and training volunteers like all get-out, to be able to support it....
<Durga> and we're actually putting on our thinking caps to determine how we can give "ownership" of some of these plot lines back to the payers...
<Durga> er, players, which is the same thing, in this case.

<Lum> Well, how *do* you support "making a difference"?
<Lum> I mean, really, I think there's a lot of infrastructure in the world that needs to be in place. Right now all we have in place is guild vs guild, so all we see in game is, um, guild vs guild.

<Durga> By support making the difference, I mean just having creative people trained and available to run the hundreds of separate smaller events required...
<Durga> to support a major plot line in which the actions of the players determine what happens on each shard.

<Lum> A somewhat related topic... there's a lot of players who accuse the Interest folks of favoritism.
<Lum> Hanging around established player communities, ignoring others, etc.

<Durga> I don't know how much of that was true before I got here...
<Durga> but I have heard all those rumors/complaints/what have you..

<Lum> I guess a question would be, how do you decide which groups of players to give "ownership" of the plot to?
<Lum> A PvP guild might want to do something very different from a "peaceful roleplaying" guild.

<Durga> Ah, OK, I see where you are going.

<Lum> And you thought I was just going to troll for abuse rumors!

<Durga> Let me give you an example...
<Durga> let's say we set up an attack on a city over a period of time, two weeks or a month...
<Durga> Do the players defend the city?  Do ENOUGH players defend the city?  What happens if enough defend on Atlatic, but not on Sonoma?
<Durga> We could end up with a situation where a major city was owned by the bad guys on several shards.
<Durga> Until enough players banded together to drive them out.

<Lum> Many would consider this a *good* thing.

<Durga> I agree.
<Durga> That's what I mean about giving players some ownership in this plots.
<Durga> That was just an example, of course, and I don't know if it was a good one...
<Durga> but you get the idea.

<Lum> Well, it's a good example in that it shows how the "one true plot" can diverge.

<Durga> That was my thinking.

<Lum> I take it then, there's a commitment for an active plot on each and every shard.
<Lum> Including that Australian one.

<Durga> Yep.  even the Australian one.
<Durga> Speaking of which, we am going to start recruiting Companions and Counselors for that one soon...
<Durga> just thought I'd mention it, to show that we ARE committed to an Aussie shard.
<Durga> It is a shard I begged for...

<Lum> They announced it on the aussie mailing list this week. It's called "Oceania", EA Australia is having the big rollout 1/10

<Durga> Cool-o, hadn't realized it had gone out to the mailing list.

<Lum> What the heck is a "Volunteer Shard"?

<Durga> to let the Interest people try out new events safely...

<Lum> Is it a regular shard? Will it get wiped like Test does?

<Durga> Nothing on the VS is permanent, that's the most important point player should know...
<Durga> it's there because I refuse to give anyone enhanced powers on a production shard until they are trained...
<Durga> and we can't train them withut giving them access to the powers.

<Lum> Are there any advancement gates planned for it?
<Lum> I went on it once to help a friend with his power testing and let me tell you, testing lich spawns with a newbie is not my personal definition of fun.

<Durga> I hadn't planned advancement gates, but I'm willing to be convinced...

<Lum> PLEEEEEEEEASSSSSSEEEE!

<Durga> You should see what happens to Durga there, :D
