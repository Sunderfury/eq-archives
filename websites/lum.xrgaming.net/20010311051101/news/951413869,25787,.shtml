TESTING SKILLS
Thursday, February 24, 2000 - 10:37 AM [Filed by myschyf]

I did some testing of the new skill system myself both last night and this morning. One small note on the test center: People don't go there to test do they? I never knew this before. I saw more pkers and scammers (they were bragging about pking and scamming at the bank therefore I assume they were pks and scammers) in the 20 minutes I tested last night and the 20 minutes I tested this morning than I have in the last 6 months on SP, GL, LS, or AT. I was testing meditation, magery, poisoning, and alchemy. At least 10 people came up to me to tell me that I didn't have to do this -- didn't I know I could set my skills? Then they called me a lamer when I said I was actually testing the new skill system. Gotta love it.

In any case I don't know how that guy was able to get to GM med in 20 mins. Maybe he was trying. I wasn't trying very hard and I went from 0 to 59.x last night and from 59.x to 68.x this morning. So there is definitely a slow down in skill at higher levels. All I did was cast a spell that took most of my mana and then hit my med macro once. It rocketed up from just that. I wasn't wearing any armor and I didn't have to hit the macro over and over like I do now. One other note -- I NEVER failed to med. I also gained skill without actively hitting the macro but I gained it faster when I did actively practice the skill.

In that same period of time I am only at 30.x from 0 in evaluate intelligence. Once again I wasn't trying. I never actively trained it. It went up .1 every time I casted.

Magery gains were somewhat dissapointing. I started with 50 magery but only 11 mana. I would cast bless or cunning to get my mana up and then the highest spell I could with the mana I had then which was Greater Heal until I gained .2 mana and then Incognito. I gained .5 magery in the 40 minutes that I spent testing.

I started Alchemy at 0 and reached about 20 before I quit. I only trained that last night. It goes up fast enough -- I'll still have to try it at higher levels. The thing is that its still 'not fun.' You've still got to go through an inordinate amount of clicks to get your skill up. Even with the faster skill gains if I was training alchemy I'd want to macro it. My initial impression is that folks would come on, macro anatomy for an hour, then do whatever it is they wanted to do for fun. Anyone can sit there and watch a script run for an hour I suppose.

Poisoning gains were nonexistent but I think that's becuase I had no greater poison to work with. I started with 49 poisoning and 1 sword to get a practice sword to poison. Using the poison I started with and what I was able to create at 20 I gained no skill.

Overall I think this is a move in the right direction. Making characters has come to mean many months of work. I work for a living.. I play games for fun. I'll be testing the skill system over the course of the week and would like to encourage more of you to do so as well. One note to the development team: It would be nice to see you do something similar with the stat system -- perhaps allowing us to tweak our stats in the direction we'd like once we've maxxed them out or something.
