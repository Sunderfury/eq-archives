WHAT DO XENA DRAGON, LUM THE MAD, AND GARY COLEMAN HAVE IN COMMON?
Friday, February 18, 2000 - 8:20 PM [Filed by lum]

Turfwar in Shadowbane land, boys and girls, and as always, it ends up being about the cash.

Web sites are big money these days. You'd be surprised how much people pay for those tiny ad banners. (TOTAL DISCLOSURE: As of this month, those people being paid includes myself.) This includes our own incestuously crowded little world of ORPGs -- although I don't think we are going to see Dr. TwisTer issuing stock options any time soon, at the same time actual cash is involved. Which, when disputes break out, tend to make people pissy.

Take the strange and terrible saga of the Shadowbane "Shared Dev Board". It was originally set up to be an independent message board - as in independent of the gaming combines, the Vaults, Stratics and XRGamings of the world. Originally set up on a free message board, it crashed and burned under the weight of its not insignificant traffic, where it was moved to Cyboards. Cyboards, for those of you keeping score, is a venture by the folks who bring you xrgaming.net. (Among those ventures includes yours truly, although I've never recieved any guidance from the XRGaming Powers other then "No Bugs and No Porn". I think I've probably broken both of those edicts several times since, including within this very sentence.)

The board continued to generate a massive amount of traffic, and people started to grumble that Cyboards wasn't up to handling the stress. (Longtime Lumophiles may be forgiven for blaming UGO's advertising server instead. Cynical Lumophiles may be forgiven for thinking UGO's advertising server is actually the most important server on the network.)

Then, a Brand! Spanking! New! "Shared Devboard" appeared... on Stratics' network.

This didn't sit well with Marduk, an XRGaming representative, who pointed out that the "Shared Devboard" was losing its independence by moving from an XRGaming.net server to a Stratics server. This cognitive dissonance was pointed out, none too subtly, by others.

What's it all mean? Not much. All of these companies - Stratics, XRGaming, Twister, VaultNetworks and the rest - are really throwing down the sturm and drang, fighting over the privilege of doing the yeoman work for Wolfpack Studios, benefactor of literally gigabytes worth of advance publicity without having to pay for a nybble of it. So far they've done a masterful job of guerilla marketing, managing to harness "the community" for free, that amorphous being that can turn on you faster than you can say Jack Precasting. Right now Wolfpack is in the cockpit, and they get to jump around between the XRGaming F-16 and the Stratics MiG-29, all for the benefit of those funky decals on the side of the plane that you never notice anyway. Wolfpack doesn't care, they'll post pretty much damn near anywhere.

Because, you know, it's all about the Benjamins, and that's the most ironic thing about all this. No matter who wins this little slapfest, the same company hosts both XRGaming and Stratics. The same people who think pimping out Gary Coleman is high concept.

The same company who, no matter how this all ends up, gets the money.

Hey, maybe YOU'LL host the Shared Dev Board next!
