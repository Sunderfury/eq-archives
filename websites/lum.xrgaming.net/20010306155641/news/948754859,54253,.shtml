WELL, I GUESS THIS IS ONE WAY TO RESOLVE OVERCROWDING
Monday, January 24, 2000 - 4:00 PM [Filed by lum]

Druids and wizards are reporting being banned from Everquest for the unpardonable exploit of casting a teleport spell. As this one reports:

After petitioning & spending an hour ingame on my brother's account I was contacted by GM Delanus of Bristlebain and was told that I was banned by another person at Verant "Because I was caught using a hack program". I was directed to email the other guy who will be off for the next 2 days and see if I can get reinstated that way. When asked what reimbursements I could expect to receive for being unable to play during time I was paying for (Especially, since I can see this stretching into WEEKS at this rate) Delanus was unable to give me an answer. Needless to say, I have never used a hack program in Everquest. For gods' sake, if anyone took the time to examine my substandard equipment they would know that I wasn't cheating. (Or that if I was I had the program in reverse or something) ; )

And again, with somewhat more detail:

I play a Dark-Elf Wizard character on the Bristlebane server and just recently made level 20. After meeting a friend at the arena in West Freeport and binding there, I had decided to head to Lake Rathe to kill some Aviaks. Being a wizard we have the luxary to teleporting, and at level 20, North Gate is the quickest way there. After memorizing the spell i proceded to use it, but where it took me was not where i had intended. After the zone had loaded, it read you have entered Dagnors Cauldrun (or something simular to that) and did one of those "Player is under world, returning to safe point". I then attempted to turn around to see exactly where i was and it froze my computer. (the computer freezing probably wasnt directly linked to the bug in the game, but the computer still froze) when i rebooted and relogged in, I was back at my bind point in freeport. I then petitioned to have the problem resolved, because after all, I wasnt supposed to be dumped in Dagnors Cauldrun in the first place. After talking to an aprentice guide, it had been escalated to a guide, and was consitered an underworld death. The guide that helped me was very friendly and helpfull. He brought me to my corpse and resurected me for the lost experience i had suffered (and losing my lvl 20). He instructed me to fill out a bug report and say what had happened, and that he would do the same. After filling out the report explaining what had happened, i tested the spell again a few times to see if it was only a one time bug, and it wasnt. It consistantly took me to the same place, but this time i did not die (luckily). After a few more minutes, and being tired of the whole deal, I logged off for a little while to get some food. When I tried to reconnect about an hour after, i was suprised to see that i had been Banned for "Inapropriate Behavior". I then called Tech support, but they were closed for the holiday (monday). The next day i waited until 11:30est to call tech support again, and they told me that they could not get any info on it, and to call back later, around 2:00. I waited a few hours, called back again, and was told i was banned for "Hacking" and had to email the GM in order for anything to be resolved.

And again:

Right after the patch I tried to teleport from Dagnors Cauldron to Lavastorm Mountains, I was teleported under the world then to a safe spot within Dagnors Cauldron. I tried repeatedly to teleport to Lavastorm, but experienced the same thing. Finally i teleported to West commons and ran into Lavastorm. No furthur problems were experienced that gaming session, I even teleported from Solusek B to Lavastorm mountains later that day.

Upon logging on to Everquest the following day, I received a "You have been banned for Hacking" notice

Abashi's response, when finally pinned down:

Sorry guys, we don't reply to these because it's between the owner of the account and our customer service department. Now I suppose if the owners of those accounts want their personally issues drug out in public, we can do that if they like. Of the two bannings I saw mentioned in the other thread, I looked at the evidence on one of them, and it’s very persuasive.

If anyone else in that thread wants to send me their station name (login name), I'll be happy to look into it directly. Bannings for "Hacking" are so clear-cut that they leave no room for misinterpretation. We don’t give warnings for it, we don’t even care if you are successful. If you even unsuccessfully attempt to hack the game then we don’t want you as a customer.

We are absolutely committed to keeping EverQuest cheat-free.

Well, either there's a bug in Everquest that somehow involves druids and wizards, or some new autobugtracker has gone wiggy.

Of course, we've never seen this before, have we, boys and girls?

I'd never use ShowEQ, no, no, not me!
