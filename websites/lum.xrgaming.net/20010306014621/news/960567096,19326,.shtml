STOP THAT! YOU'RE NOT PLAYING THE WAY WE THINK YOU SHOULD!
Friday, June 9, 2000 - 9:11 AM [Filed by lum]

Gordon "Mutant Dwarf Goat" Wrinn had this to say to a concerned user:

Why the hell cant I solo if I feel like it? This is beginning to feel more and more like Verant is trying to parent their customers.

If I want to sit at one spawn site and camp a NPC anywhere in the world, I should be allowed to do so. Not everyone is 16 with 12 hours to kill. I often have a lot of other things that must be done while I squeeze in some playing, and sometimes its nice to take a "break" in game and not have to worry about wandering PC's and trains where you're forced to stare at your screen for hours on end. I swear Verant is really starting to make this game just pathetic.

No Abashi, a lot of times I do not feel like sitting and waiting for Group member A to go to Freeport real quick, while Group member B has to teleport a guild mate to Lavastorm and Group member C just got telled by a friend who needs a Res in Lower Guk. Forcing everyone to group is just BS, I am an adult, I can decide however I want to play as long as I don't use cheats or abuse other players, period.

You talk like you consider players and their inputs, please. If you are not lying to us, your breaking the game.

But when it's the same people who are choosing to do that that go around to the boards and various gamesites declaring how EQ is boring, we have to ask why they choose to do that.

In general, we've found that a non-trivial population will take the quickest path possible to levelling, regardless of the tedium that that particular path might present. The way to avoid tedium is to see that risk and reward are in balance. If we give these solitary and stationary fast-spawns spawn-times equivalent to the level of the NPC, then doing the boring camp for hours on end isn't as quick as getting into an under utilized dungeon with a good group.

You mean, in a level-based game, some people will try to level quickly? Jesus Christ, don't make me whip out the Mighty Cudgel of Obvious Truth again.

Why did this fairly typical Abashi-ism catch my eye? Probably because of the last sentence, which cuts to the heart of Verant's design philosophy.

PROBLEM: Some dungeons are underutilized. Others are overcrowded.

INCREDIBLY OBVIOUS SOLUTION: Look at why players go to those overcrowded dungeons (phat lewt, quicker spawning monsters, less chance of dying from braindead pathing bugs), replicate those conditions in the dungeons that aren't being used.

VERANT'S SOLUTION: Hit the players with the Nerf Stick until they behave.

So instead of analyzing why groups are so hard to assemble (HINT: only two classes have travel/utility spells, no items in game grant this ability) Verant basically punishes the people who try to solo instead by making them wait EVEN LONGER for the mole they are waiting to whack to pop up. Someone somewhere obviously thinks Everquest doesn't have enough downtime. Let's add more!

I don't know, if I were a designer at Verant, I'd be coding in the ability for Shamen, Enchanters, and Gnomes to make teleportation/gate items RIGHT NOW THIS SECOND. And make them affordable. And stackable. Because, you know, in the REAL WORLD people don't want to run across 12 zones for hours just to meet up with their group just because they weren't smart enough to make a Druid.

Then again, considering that Verant can't even do what they announce they are doing in their patch updates, that may be way too much to ask for. Easier to just, you know, punish the players some more. Hell, if they don't like it, they can leave, right?

In a recent post, Windfeather of the OWO:O team, talking about PvP and pika-pika-PKers, coined a cute little phrase.

If you want to make the game suck for others, you must be prepared to live in a world of suck yourself.

EQ players know all about living in that world of suck. And most of them don't even get to kill people.

Weee all live in a yellow world-of-suck... a yellow world-of-suck... a yellow world-of-suck...
