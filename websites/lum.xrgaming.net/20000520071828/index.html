MO' MONEY, MO' PROBLEMS
Friday, May 19, 2000 - 1:48 PM [Filed by lum]

Trouble on the horizon for Origin, Verant, Turbine, and, um, any other ORPG in development. Here's the storm clouds.

Some ex-AOL "Community Leaders" have filed suit demanding compensation for back wages, saying that AOL violated labor laws by using "volunteer" laborers that were actually entitled to hourly and overtime wages.

This is one to watch - so far every ORPG has relied on volunteer labor to provide the sort of technical support that gaming companies can't afford to provide. Origin has only about 10 paid "game masters" on site, and about 10-15 more paid "Shard Lead Counselors" working from their home for a nominal, less-than-minimum-wage sum. The rest of the volunteer force, which numbers in the hundreds, is all unpaid. Everquest and Turbine have similar staff figures (Verant doesn't pay their senior volunteers, for example, but has more on-site GMs).

If companies are ruled liable for paying wages to their volunteer staff, expect them to go away. And the impact on the industry to be dramatic - if not catastrophic. There is no way that game companies can afford hundreds of on-site technical support staff and remain profitable.

Much easier to give them funny blue hats.

WELL, WE ALREADY FIXED SAMURAI KILLSTEALING AND RONIN CAMPING...
Thursday, May 18, 2000 - 6:57 AM [Filed by lum]

...so next up is ninja looting. Sensei at the Dojo, who obviously has better contacts at Verant than we do, is reporting that ninja looting is the next "behavior modification" to be patched in. To quote:

If confirmed by a Verant GM, ninja-looting will result in disciplinary action up to and including player suspension or banning. Ninja-Looting is when a player, disregarding the players or player who killed a mob, loots an item that is generally regarded as significant or valuable from the mob they did not kill. When a CS Rep receives a petition reporting ninja-looting, they are to contact the petitioner, gather preliminary information, and escalate to a GM for resolution. Determination of whether or not an item is 'generally regarded as significant or valuable' is left to the GM's discretion as directed by Verant Management.

OK, so what is "ninja looting" (or "yoinking", as I prefer to call it) in English? Here's the deal.

Most of the REALLY COOL LEWT in Everquest is what is called NO DROP. That means that you can't trade it, you can't give it to your level 4 friend, you can only use it if you loot it. No worries there, right? Well, the kicker is that as you increase in levels, your relative power in Everquest vs. monsters decreases consistently. At level 1, you can pretty much kick everything's butt that's your level or maybe even one or two higher. At level 25, you are going to have to group to get anything done. And at level 50 (taking everyone's word for it mind you, since level 50 characters to me are like minor godlings that move in more rarified planes of existence that I can only wonder at) the more important/interesting creatures require multiple groups of characters to kill. That's the kicker - because you can only loot something off of a monster that your group kills. There's no support for multiple-group kills in EQ.

So the upshot. You're a Shadowknight, and your guys just killed Erollsi Marr, who drops the Hairpin of Ungodly Might. It's a shadowknight only item. It's no drop. The only hope of your getting the Hairpin of Ungodly Might is to kill Erollsi Marr. Which you just did. Only, you were in the melee group, whose job it was to get hammered on while the group behind you, the finger-waggling caster brigade, actually got credit for the kill. No one in the caster group is a shadowknight. They don't want the hairpin. They can't use it. If they loot it, the only thing they can do with it is to throw it away.

So what do you do? Well, the only thing that groups do in this situation - sit and wait for the timer on the corpse to go off. You see, the limits on who can loot a corpse expire after 5 minutes or so. Then anyone can loot Erollsi Marr's steaming husk. So you sit around and wait 5 minutes so that you can get your rightful reward for helping to slay the Goddess of Love. So everyone's kicking back, cracking obscene jokes in /guildchat, waiting for the timer to expire, when some random stranger comes walking by and calmly loots the corpse. That's ninja looting. Needless to say, it pisses people off. A lot.

Now, there's a lot of things that can be done about ninja looting. You could code in support for multiple group credits for kills. You could set a timer for looting no drop items that allowed you to trade them, say, the first 3 minutes after you looted them. You could even allow the looting group to re-designate who has looting rights for the corpse. All these remedies would make sense, and would actually, you know, preserve the original intent of the loot timer - limiting who can loot a kill to who actually had something to do with it.

But, you know, coding remedies is HARD. Much easier to just write up an official sounding rule actually codifying what players are doing to work around a failure in the game system, saying "You can't ninja-loot" instead of fixing the game so that ninja looting isn't an option and make the guides enforce it. You know, since the guides in Everquest don't have enough to do already with flitting around the servers ensuring that everyone is playing nice under penalty of linkdeath.

You have dishonored my family! Prepare to have your kills ninja looted!
