E3-2K: The Bob Awards
Great Bob does E3, part three
So my last update (complete with babe shots) was cut in half.   I'm home now, and my brain is no longer throbbing from the sun, the beer and the blaring sounds of video games.  The whole event seems like a blur to me now, with only fragments of memories remaining.

My friend Ryan and I went to see Battlefield Earth, as I mentioned.  I'm not going to review it again.  Just thinking of that movie makes me shudder uncontrollably.  I will never be able to walk into a movie theater again with the same level of confidence I once did.  If you must know about it, you can read one review I posted here.

Enough of that.  When you take away the bimbos, the distractions and the hype, you're left with the real reason for going....the games.  With that in mind, I present the first ever presentation of The Bob Awards.  No statue or ribbon is given, but some day in the future these awards will be the most cherished by the gaming community.  What are the standards?  Well, it has to be a game that doesn't make me want to vomit in a violent fashion.  Here we go.

Best Game of Show:  Anarchy Online

Words can't begin to describe how good this game looks.  If you want to know what the next generation of MMORPGS is going to look like, this is it.  What makes it so good?  First you have a development team with a real love of their game.  You could tell how much they loved showing this off, and for good reason.  The animation is absolutely beautiful.  The game looks deep, with a meaningful fiction and a wide selection of possible skills.  Here's an example.  You can be a bureaucrat in this game, designing missions for other players to accomplish.  All you scheming manipulators, listen up.  This game might be for you.  They also planned out for housing, with every character being assigned an apartment at the start of the game, with fancier accommodations available for a price.  The story line is going to run for 4 years, with the servers shut down after that.

Programmer quote: "We want the players to feel like they are actually part of the world....and look at how the characters can dance!"

Best RTS Game: Warcraft 3

Yeah, like you couldn't have guessed this one.  The only other one that came close to matching this one was Mechcommander 2.  If you even had a mild interest in the previous Warcraft games, you MUST pick this one up.  I spent more time playing this one than any other game.  The orcs are just as much fun to play as ever.  At first this was conceived as more of a role-playing game, but since they re-tooled it, it's closer to it's roots than ever before.

 Programmer Quote: "Allright, now the army is going to kick the human's ass"

Best Online Strategy Game: Sovereign

From Verant St. Louis comes this promising little game.  Now, I'm a bit biased, since I used to love Empire years and years ago.  This game is pretty similar.  You start off as an engineer, produce resources and build a city.  You make troops, set up defense perimeters and crush your enemies.  Each world will hold 500 players at a time, with a maximum of over 4,000 cities total.  It's real time, and it looks fun.  Don't expect to be blown away by the graphics, but expect solid game play for all you armchair generals out there.  Verant is shooting for December of 2000, but the programer I talked to didn't think that was really going to happen.  It looks like it's worth the wait.

Programmer Quote: "If you want to write your own AI script for the game, that's fine.  We just don't want someone to hack in and make the rules of the game change.  If it became a problem though, we would put an end to it."

Best Space Sim You Will Never See: Noble Armada

Ripcord is publishing this little number.  The shame of it is that we'll most likely never get a chance to play it.  Here's why I liked it.  We've seen a number of really great games over the years that put you in the cockpit of a space fighter.  Independence War tried to put you in command of a larger ship, but it still played like a fighter sim.  This game is different.  The command interface is simple to use, let it still gives you a good level of strategic and tactical control.  The game itself will be similar to Privateer, with an open ended story line and a good economic system of trade.  The big problem?  It most likely will never get released.  Ripcord got fed up with the first developer, and now is trying to farm out bits and pieces to different shops....including one in Indonesia.  Damn shame.  Graphics looked good and the game was a great deal of fun to play.

Publisher Quote: "Uhh...let's call say it's very much pre-alpha...we had some problems."

Best RPG: Vampire

Although I doubt it will actually ship in 3 weeks, the game looks great.  The animation is smooth, the controls intuitive and you can't go wrong with vampires.  Well, that's not true.  Anybody recall the bad TV show on FOX they based on this franchise?  Well, this game doesn't screw things up.  The gameplay, in single player mode, looks like a great deal of fun, and the possibility of making games in Story Teller mode seem very promising.  This game may have a long life to it.  One word of warning though, if you had problem making Quake mods, you might not feel comfortable with the Storyteller tools.  Storytellers who get to know the tools will find a very powerful world creation system in place.

Programmer Quote: "Let's just drop a chaingun down here...."

Game I Was Skeptical About But Now Convinced: Ultima Worlds Online Origin

So I was watching a commercial on television where the tag line was "ever get the feeling some people just stopped trying?"  I was feeling this way about UO2.  No housing, no ridable mounts, NO GUILDS!!  I spent a half hour with the team looking the game over, and now I'm sold.  They're not going to revolutionize gaming with this product, but unlike UO that's not their goal.  It's going to be stable, good looking, and relatively bug free by the time it ships.  Will that be enough?  Well, the game looks great and you can tell they put a great deal of thought into it.  They have more people working on this game than any other MMORPG, and an infrastructure that can support it.  I'm not sure, yet, if I'll be spending a great deal of time in this world, but I'm beginning to have some faith that the team knows what they're doing.

Developer Quote: "We had to make some tough decisions based on our schedule."

There you have it folks, the first ever Bob awards.  Tomorrow, I'll wrap up my E3 coverage with a look at the aftermath, pictures of apes eating a sandwich and some final thoughts.  Not much more left.  Now I'm off to sleep in my own bed, and actually spend some time with the woman in my home who claims to be my wife.
