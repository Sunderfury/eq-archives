YOU MEAN DELETING CHARACTERS PEOPLE WORKED ON FOR OVER A YEAR IS BAD SOMEHOW?
Wednesday, June 21, 2000 - 6:16 AM [Filed by lum]

Brad "Aradune" McQuaid worked the fallout from the Test Center player wipe last night. First, he appeared on the Test Center to talk to the players affected personally.

Aradune shouts 'the main thing though, is that even though people should be here for testing, we feel bad about people losing a lot of time invested into play characters'

Aradune shouts, 'so I'm here to get feedback, and to talk about some possible options'

Aradune shouts, 'the difficulty, of course, is there are a lot of you'

Aradune shouts, 'but the other methods of feedback (email and public posts) are usually only maade by upset people and people who like to post and such'

Aradune shouts, 'so iwanted to come online and talk to you guys directly, even though like i said, its hard, because there are so many of you'

---

Aradune shouts, 'ok, I think that most people agree something needs to be done, but that this is too extreme'

Pallie says out of character, 'Agreed'

Aradune shouts, 'ok'

Aradune shouts, 'I'd like to talk about some options'

Aradune shouts, 'there are two that we are considering: 1. an item wipe only'

Aradune shouts, 'and 2. moving people's chars to live servers who want to play more than test (and I dont mean that to be insulting)'

Radius says out of character, 'So the GM's would buff their hand picked friends again? Tyhats what got you into this mess! :O('

Aradune shouts, 'I agree that was part of what started the problem, which is our bad, and I apologize. in the future we will do a better job at unequipping and debuffing people we use to test high lvl zones'

---

Elbacilppa shouts, 'how about restore levels, delete all items, give each player a leg-up kit for that level and a pile of money appropriate to buy weapons/spells?'

Aradune shouts, 'good ideaElbaclippa'

Clainn says out of character, 'Aradune, also a big concern for alot of the people here is the items no olonger obtainable.. manastone/guise/ few odd and end weapons.. i don't think its right to not allow us Some way of getting those back'

Aradune shouts, 'Clainn, thats the point -- we need to be tesitng things with realistically equipped characters'

---

Aradune shouts, 'and I really want to thank all of you for your feedback and for being so civil and constructive -- you really renewed my faith =)'

Aradune shouts, 'many would have thought this meeting undoable '

Aradune shouts, 'you proved them wrong'

Huppoizhere says out of character, 'GROUP HUG!'

Then he moved from the fuzzleness of the loyalists still on the test server to the fires of Whineplay...

Despite the Pwipe, more than 95% of the average population on the test server were playing tonight anyway.
They were there, starting anew, despite the huge time loss due to the PWipe.

We also received a lot of heartfelt emails (and very few flames).

So, after discussing this issue with 180 or so players on Test tonight, we've decided to seriously consider the following 2 options:

1. Do an item wipe on Test (i.e. restore the characters but without items).
2. Move on a voluntary basis characters from Test to Live servers. The characters would NOT go across with items.

It is also possible we will do both.

I got a lot of good feedback and passionate opinions and responses tonight, and I really appreciate it. The spirit of the vast majority of the players on Test is truly amazing and I admire you all.

While a total player wipe with select individuals being buffed to test mid and higher level zones might be clinically best for the Test Server, I think it's more fair to the players and testers there to do one or both of the options above.

In closing, if we choose option 2 (or both 1 and 2) I would really encourage those players on Test who are there more for playing and less for testing to seriously consider moving.

Another wipe, player or item, is not necessarily unlikely some time in the future.

Unfortunately, the hard truth about any "test server" is that you rarely get folks who are there to, you know, test. Given that players cannot self-buff themselves but must slog their way through the levels like any other player (or in the case of a few, make nicey-nice with a GM and have them bump them up a level or 40). Getting to the higher levels in EQ (45+) honestly, without someone else powerlevelling you, takes a good solid six months of dedicated effort.

On the other hand, despite Verant's clumsy efforts to rewrite disclaimers after the fact, players really should have known (and probably did, in the back of their head) that there always was a certain impermanence to a Test server. (You know, thus the name.)

Anyway, moving characters off of Test is probably the best solution. Of course, that doesn't solve the core problem of why people are playing on Test anyway - overcrowding - but hey, one problem at a time here.

On the bright side, no one seems to be duping in EQ
