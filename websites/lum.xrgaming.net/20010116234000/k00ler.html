11:45 PM K00LER THAN JESUS

There's been a somewhat active thread (well, active for the horribly crippled and painfully ugly message base here) about the Test Center and the new beta test client, and the subculture of "el33t" Test Center folks who inhabit them.

Here's a response from someone involved in the controversy. They remain anonymous, for obvious reasons.

I read the thread on your Discussion Board about TC -- the one where J calls TC players "stuckup reactionaries" -- and there is one thing you seem to be ignoring here. Senith and Darwin both commented about the UO Beta Test on the EQ side of Gate Traveler. (One "l," Lum. ;)) Why is that? Well, I would say it's because those stuck-up reactionaries J was referring to are the former TC Regs, now EQ's very own elite Test Guild and associated hangers-on -- the only guild allowed on EQ's TS, with their own special authorization-only board and a direct line to Verant's QA. And if you think TC is a country club, you should check out TS. Although the Test Guild claims not to be twinked (Well, except for that little boost to level 10 they got when it was offered to everybody who happened to be on TS at the time. Funny how all of them were on, isn't it?) you can apparently go from level 1 to rubicite-armored level 30 (or 40 or 50 for the really lucky twinks) in less time than it takes to goggle at the equipment, if you know the right people. It seems to be an unfortunate characteristic of test servers that some employees with too much power and too little sense regard them as their private playgrounds. By the way, there are no wipes on Test Server, so anything Verant hands out is yours to keep. I would suggest that anybody who wasn't happy with the way Test Center was handled check out Test Server. You have basically the same group of people pulling the same elitist bullshit.

FYI, what's left on UO's TC is for mostly just us players who were never part of the TC Regs, and new people join us all the time. Many of us have been there all along, you just never knew it because the Regs, well... Let's put it this way. Somebody once said that Charlie Chaplain thought that everyone should sit at his feet, and when they didn't he would go out and stand where they were sitting. That pretty much sums up the TC Regs. Anyway, things have changed drastically on Test Center. OSI rarely puts up advancement gates, and then only specific gates for specific skills. It's mostly for the drop-in testers from other shards since any residents testing the changes that the gates target are already at or near gate levels by the time the gates go up. Not only did OSI put a stop to QAs handing things out, they made them go back and retrieve most of the stuff they did give out last wipe. (In fact, the town criers' news late last wipe was "Please do not ask QA for things.") It pretty much made the QAs' lives hell, so I don't think they'll be handing things out regularly again. We still get faster advancement and lots of space and spawn, but then we get our characters wiped so it balances out. Oh, and "shardie" isn't necessarily an insult. It's just somebody who doesn't play TC regularly. Hang out in Britain, and you'll see lots of them. "Shardie attitude," now that's an insult. :)

Lastly, about the Beta registration process. TC's regular players got advance notice on GT. If that makes us "l33t" so be it. But, there aren't all that many of us and 2000 spots, so I don't think that we're going to preclude a whole lot of people from signing up. One thing you might want note though. OSI apparently had every intention of just giving us a few hours head start before announcing the registration on owo.com. They haven't because the registration process turned out to be a lot worse than a few tacky pages. The process requested your UO account and password. Unfortunately, it wasn't secure. Uh oh.

I didn't post this on your discussion board for the same reason I'm not going to give you my name and the hotmail e-mail addy is, obviously, a blind. I still play both servers and getting on the bad side of Test Guild can be a nightmare.
