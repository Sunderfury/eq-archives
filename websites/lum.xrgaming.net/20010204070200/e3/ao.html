Preview/Interview: Anarchy Online
Developer: FunCom
Publisher: TBA

Anarchy Online hasn't gotten much attention or press up to this point - but I guarantee you that will change. Soon to go into open beta, AO is not only the only MMORPG scheduled to be released in 2000, it is, quite simply, a technological marvel. Along with the Crossroads staff, I was given a chance to view a detailed demonstration of an alpha build of AO; even with some features still missing and the occasional crash, what we saw will set the standard for online games. It's that good.

Set in a far-future world with an evil corporation beset by rebels (hm, where have we seen this before), AO captures the "cyberpunk" feel fairly well. The game itself bears some similarities to Everquest and Asheron's Call: players gain in levels as they gain experience, and can use Increase Points (IP) they earn each level to increase certain skills. There's some key differences as well; players can purchase nanobots to augment their powers (a class, the nanomage, is AO's version of the magic user class).

AO's story is vital to the game; the developers claim to have developed a four-year story arc which the players can influence to a limited degree. Players who choose Omni-Tek (the "bad guys") get better equipment to start but are limited in what actions they can take (following orders and whatnot) while the rebels, being less organized, have a harder start but have much more freedom in their actions. Players have to choose a side, and can't have a high-level character on each side. AO has a geographical PK switch similar to Ultima Online - cities are safe, and the further you go out from a city, the more dangerous things get - first monsters, then the opposing side, and finally you can be fair game to anyone if far enough away. The developers emphasized that players could exist comfortably inside cities (which host an apartment for every player), but the rewards outside would encourage players to take the risk.

AO's quest system also looked impressive, even in its primitive state. The developers recognize that even with a paid staff of plot-advancers they can't provide unique tailored quests for everyone - so they've decided to give the players the ability to design their own. Players can use their own personal treasure and experience point pool to reward other players for designated actions. The list we were shown was fairly limited, but included such things as attacking opposing clans, killing a specific enemy, finding a specific item, etc. Even with this limited system, an ambitious group of players could use the system to reward others for working towards their own goals.

What truly impressed us about AO was its graphics. Quite simply, they are outstanding. Players have a dizzying array of "emote" animations that they can perform, for everything from berating other players to, using a text-based scripting system, performing entire plays. The animations themselves were unbelivable in the range of movement and detail of the models (the meshes actually change depending on the clothing used - a trenchcoat on a character really looks like a trenchcoat, not a form-fitted skin) and animations can even play simultaneously - being shot while running, for example. Landscapes can also be quite dramatic, with two suns casting different colors of light upon reflective surfaces. Really, no screenshot can do this game justice, especially the ones we were forced to take with digital cameras accompanying this article.

Look for the beta to begin in July, and the game itself to be released by the end of the year. This is definitely one to watch for.

Interview with Martin Amor (Lead Programmer) and Tommy Strand (Lead Designer).

(NOTE: This interview is largely unedited and includes some fractured syntax on the part of our interviewees. Please note that Mr. Amor is Norwegian and his English is far better than my Norwegian. At any rate they do seem to be quite fluent in C and Assembler...)


Martin Amor, AO Lead Programmer

Martin Amor: We are going to base Anarchy on its story – we have a lot of focus on creating an evolving, long-lasting story; we have actually written out a four-year long story line right now. 

Lum: A four year evolving story line?

MA: Yeah. And that story line will be supported by a support staff which will go in and bi-weekly, monthly, create events in the game.

L: Are these paid staff or volunteers?

MA: That’s paid staff. We will have volunteer staff as well, like guides, which will go around solving problems and answering tech questions and all that.


Paperdolls, required for every MMORPG.

The main thing in Anarchy is the conflict between Omni-Tek, which is a very large corporation, and the rebel clans. It’s on a distant planet called Rubi-Ka. Omni-Tek took this planet and is using it for mining. The rebels are people who lived there before, they know that Omni-Tek is going to use the planet and not care about how it looks and pollution and all that, so they are trying to fight them. Omni-Tek doesn’t really care about the rebels – they only care about profit, so if the clans attack Omni-Tek, they will strike back, because they can’t afford to lose any profit at all.

L: How much can the players affect the story line by their actions?

MA: The story line is already pre-written, but the players will have the possibility to change a little bit – of course, if everybody’s going to be an Omni-Tek soldier, that’s going to have a great impact on the game. We will then go and then try to help the rebels so that the balance will even out a little bit.

[Programmer begins to log in to AO.]


Tommy Strand, AO
Lead Designer

MA: We’re trying to make the login procedure as quick as possible. Creating a character, we have five different races, we have male and female, we have thieves and sneaky kind of types, we have the nanomages, designed to interface with nanobots. It’s the equivalent of magic, but done with technology. You buy certain nano-programs which you can use to control and evolve the nanobots that go around, weapons and defenses like shield, and last we have very strong brutes, warrior types.

Going in the game you will start in a newbie zone where you decide how the character will look like.

L: So you do that in the game itself?

MA: Yeah. We’ve been trying to get as much as possible inside the game itself. In the newbie zone, you’ll decide what kind of clothes you want to start with, what kind of face you have, your appearance, what kind of profession you have, do you want to be a doctor, or a soldier

Tommy Strand: We have soldiers, bureaucrats, social types of characters, we have adventuer, agent. It’s all in the FAQ on the web site.

MA: This is one of the cities, that is going to be a part of the larger Omni-Tek cities. The zone I’m going to show you is the entertainment district.

TS: Right now we are trying to cut loading time for zones down to 5 seconds. Each zone is about the size of an Everquest zone.

Lum's Other Half: Can you go into first person mode?

MA: Outside you can be either third person or first person. Inside there are some places you can only be first person, because there isn’t enough room to see.

We have a lot of focus on social interaction, and we have a lot of social moves. I can wave to other people, or bow. This is a list of all the social moves…

L: OK, I gotta see what flamenco looks like!


One of the emotes, the "chicken". Bawk bawk bawkaaa

MA: OK the view actually changes now, the weather actually  changes right now. We have variable weather, we have smoothly into in each other. Right now it’s a little stormy… we have rain, storms, sand storms, fallout storms, like radiation

Cassandra: Can you put some more clothes on her?
L: Yeah, let’s see the customization routines.

[programmer goes to buy-cube, begins to buy clothing/armor]

L: Do new players have enough money to buy these?

MA: If you start as Omni-Tek, you start with armor, that is one of the advantages of being Omni-Tek. If you start with the rebels, it will be a little bit harder in the beginning, but you have more freedom to do whatever you want to do.

You can buy implants. They are permanent. If you put an implant in your head, it will stay there. There will be some advantages and some disadvantages to them.

C: Can you exchange implants?

MA: No, they are permanent.


We're not worthy...

Here’s a trenchcoat, like Matrix style. If you put it on, you see it actually changes the mesh [of the character], so that you wear a trenchcoat. Here you can change faces. We have a lot of different faces.

L: How would you do that in game, though?

MA: Go to a special shop to change your appearance…

L: Like a beauty shop?

MA: Yes, like a beauty shop.

C: Can you change your appearance after you create your character?

MA: Yes. It costs a lot of money, so you can’t constantly do that, only if you are wealthy.

L: What’s to prevent someone from really irritating other people and then going and changing their appearance to hide from them?

MA: Nothing.

C: You still have the same name, though.

MA: Yeah. This is the reference review. I can take notes on items, and drag and drop them to others – “I really want you to go to this booth” for example. This is also used with our quest system. We are aiming to make a lot of content in the game, enough content for everybody. We don’t want any of the camping situations like what’s in Everquest. We have two ways of doing this, one is the pre-generated quests, and another is this system.

L: You can add quests to this, as a player?

MA: Yes. You can say whether this for a person or your clan and give them to others.

L: Like, a guild in game could make a quest open to anyone to kill an opposing guild.

MA: Yeah. When they create a quest, I’ll say we put a time limit in it. I can’t show you all the possible options here, but you can reward players with items, or with experience…

L: And these have to be things in your own inventory? What happens if you give experience, is that taken from your experience pool?

MA: Yes. Anarchy Online is level-based, like Everquest. You can’t have a high level character give a lot of experience to a low level character, that creates balance issues. So you have a high level character give a certain percentage of experience, and the low level character gets that percentage.

L: Are the quests you design using this system as well?

MA: Some are, and some are used with our design tools, and some with our autoquest system. You can go to an autoquest, and it will look at your level, and create a dungeon for you. It will be unique every time you get it, and you can decide if you want to share it with other characters or take it alone. And so we have unlimited numbers of areas in the game. We don’t have “dungeons” like in fantasy games, we have caves and mines, Omni-Tek buildings, etc.

LoH: Is most of the action supposed to be against the game or against other players?

MA: That’s up to  the players playing,. Because we have put a lot of focus on the company vs. the rebels. It’s like the Empire vs the Rebels in Star Wars. And people can stay inside cities, where it’s safe, and get autogenerated quests and play against monsters.  Or they can go outside the cities where it’s more dangerous. You’re not able to fight inside the cities, you are secure, you can eat and dance…

L: Like Ultima Online.

MA: Exactly. So we use some monsters and they will attack you and you can attack them. And if you go further away from the cities, you can attack the opposite side, and if you go even further away you will be able to attack anything, it’s like mayhem. People can decide what game they want. The further away they get from cities, the more dangerous, but the more rewards they will get.

L: Do you have unique items in the game, or are they randomly generated?

MA: We have both, We have unique items, and we have weapons designed by the skill system.

LoH: Do you have trade skills, or any other noncombat skills?

MA: I’ll show you. We have different professions… you can be a doctor, you can be a bureaucrat, and they don’t have to be combat oriented. You can use the skills inside the cities without combat. Like you are a doctor and you can create first aid packages. This is a place where people can sit and have a few beers, and dance. I’m going into third person now… I’m going to show you something not in the final version, but what other people would see…

L: So other people walking into the bar would see this.

MA: Yes, people will go inside and dance and have a good time… we are giving them other things than slaying monsters. We have a few dances. I can do a ballet…

L: This is like a new trend in MMORPGs. There has to be dance animations.

MA: Or I can do the pulp fiction dance…

L: What resolution is this running at right now?

MA: Right now it’s running at 800x600. You can run it in any resolution your graphics card will support… we have people running it in 2048x1024x64,000 colors. I can go into other inside areas. Let me show you some of the Omni-Tek housing. Every character will get an apartment when they start the game. They start with just a small room in a skyscraper complex. So they can have a bed, like a bank where they can put stuff.

L: Every character will have a room like this.

MA: Not exactly like this but every character will have a room.

L: Can other people visit them?

MA: They can decide if they want to have visitors. They can’t come in otherwise.

C: Can you store things in your room?

MA: You can store things in your closet it’s like a bank. You can’t randomly put out stuff, on the floor, that would cost the engine too much.

C: Can’t someone break into your room?

MA: No, it’s completely secure.

L: Is there any way of decorating your room?

MA: We have been thinking about it. I’m not going to say we’re not going to do it in the final but we’re thinking about it. It would be really nice if you could go buy furniture for your room, it would be great. I can show you a little bit of the outside now…

[shows off various animations]

MA: If you go inside first person mode you can see the camera actually follows your movements as you do these. You will be able to make chat scripts in a normal text editor “Two steps forward, bowing and say this, and your character will do this in game. That is so we can support people making performances. They can actually do a Shakespeare performance then, they can script the whole thing and make a performance for people.

C: Can you log your chats?

MA: Yes.

L: How many players can the server support at the same time?

MA: We don’t really know…. We’ve tested it with thousands of test clients running chat scripts, and it’s scaleable… if it’s too much in one place then it will exchange some load to another machine. But we will see how it goes in the beta test. We have a lot of people to sign up already, and we are aiming for 60,000 on one world at one time. We are trying to have only one world as opposed to other servers. It is much easier to maintain a story line if you only have one world.

LOH: How many slots for characters does each player have?

MA: You have 8 characters. But there will be restrictions on those characters; if you reach a high level in Omni-Tek, we will not allow you to have a high level in the clans, because the character will perceive what’s called what? Confidential information. So if you have a level 50 character on one side you will not be able to have a level 50 character on the other side.

Here is the skill system. When you get a new level, some of your new experience goes into increase points. You can go into IP mode and use your IP points to lift up your skills. You can’t just go on and use IP points, at one point you will have to get a high level character to tutor you to gain skill levels. And you can tutor yourself to other characters.

Here is the friends system, kind of like ICQ, you can just drop other players on it and talk to them if they are online,

L: How many can you have?

MA: The limit is about 20 now, but we’re going to look at that, because we don’t know how much data it will generate with a lot of people. So with these numbers will vary somewhat just because we have to tweak them.

LOH: How big is the world?

MA: It will take about 3 hours to run from one side of the map to the other?

L: How many zones are you going to have at launch?

MA: We’ll have 55 playfields. We call them playfields instead of zones. Loading is really quickly, right now it’s 8 seconds, we are aiming to get it to 5. This is the water, you can see the light reflecting off the water…


One thing about the animations, you can run and wave, and the animations will blend seamlessly into each other. And if you get hit by a bullet, while running and waving, all the animations will blend into each other. There is no running then stopping to do another animation. You can do it all at one time. Here’s a waterfall. We’ve been trying to create very beautiful landscapes.

C: Can you swim?

MA: No, you can go in the water but you can’t dive.

L: Is there any kind of teleportation in the game?

MA: You will have some teleporter pads to take you around the world, but they are very expensive. There is a subway system that moves between the major towns, go inside, take a train. You can actually go in the cars and go in wagon cars and baggage cars. It will take some time because we want people to seem it is a big world, but it will be much faster then running. It will not be totally secure, in some wagons people will be able to attack you.

L: Will players be able to craft their own weapons and armor?

MA: We have them as trade skills, yes. We have weapon smithing and designing armors?

L: Can you design custom made weapons to do various things?

MA: No, it’s not that flexible. But you have a lot of items and you combine those items. Kind of flexible but not infinitely so.

Marduk: Can you craft the same weapons as NPCs?

MA: Yes, they use the same crafting code.

L: When does the beta start?

MA: We are set to start beta in two months from now. We should release the game by the end of the year, second half of the year. I think we are the only MMRPG coming out this year.

C: Are you going to sign with a publisher?

MA: We haven’t decided that yet. That’s not really my area but I think we are going to try to publish this ourselves.
