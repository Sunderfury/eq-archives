UO2: Take all you like about UO, and stick it into Jennifer Lopez's dress
Monday, February 28, 2000 - 7:28 AM [Filed by arcadian del sol]

I admit it. I haven't visited the Official UO2 website that much. Frankly, there hasn't been that much to see lately. Plus, I wanted to re-read all my old Micronauts comic books - y'know; to get me into the proper frame of mind. Starr Long, the guy who, according to legends, was the one who walked up to Richard Garriott on "Donut and Coffee Friday" and said "You know what would be totally awesome? Dude, if like we made an Ultima game, but instead of like it being something you pay for at the store, we charge them a fee every month to keep playing it. Dude, that would be so radical. Are you gonna finish that donut?" Okay, so maybe it wasn't exactly like that - but you get the idea. So Starr Long has the job of returning to the well for more water, this time calling it "Ultima Online 2". There are no clever secondary titles yet, but I keep thinking it will be something like, "Blackthorne is back, and he's here to kick a little bot."

So I found a letter from Starr Long on the UO2 website, and he went into detail regarding a few of the more "totally radical" features we might see in UO2. I'll interpret each paragraph for you, in the event that you don't want to read and interpret yourself. Makes me feel like a journalist that way. Ladies and Gentleman, I give you Starr Long:

"Unique Characters: Of course, this includes carrying on the fine tradition that UO set of having your character look truly unique. Combinations of armor, clothing, weapons, skin color, hairstyle, etc. will give you that ability to shape your look. The addition of two new player races will offer even more in this department. However, uniqueness doesn't just include look, it also includes your character's abilities and activities. Expect to be able to select from a variety of skills ranging from sword fighting to blacksmithing to alchemy that will allow you to create a unique set of abilities for your character."
Translation: Eye Candy.

"New Setting: When we sat down to do this, we wanted to offer you different experiences in fiction, visuals, and gameplay. One way for us to do that was to introduce elements beyond medieval fantasy. Think of this as an expansion of the Ultima world, not a replacement of it. A huge section of the world is the Ultima you know and love. The cities of Britain, Yew, and Vesper are still around and are very 'medieval fantasy' in tone and setting. Our writer, Aaron De Orive, has been hard at work crafting a captivating story, which will blend the genre of the traditional Ultima with the creative expansions we are doing in Ultima Online 2. We have just released the first installment of a series of stories that will explain how the setting came to be. We felt this would be a better way to pull you into the story, rather than just put up a plot outline. In addition to our intensive work on the story, we have partnered with Todd McFarlane to work with our own art director, Andy Hoyos, to visualize the expanded world of Ultima. A natural extension of our partnership with McFarlane was to have him create action figures of the characters he was creating with us."
Translation: Some gameplay, oh and Some More Eye Candy.

"Adventure: Adventuring is one of the most exciting aspects of these kinds of games and we have spent a lot of our time making sure we have the most compelling combat around. Hundreds of motion-captured moves, including unarmed, swordplay, magic casting, deathblows, etc. make the combat really come alive. No longer will it just be watching the same repeated sword slash over and over and over. In addition, we are working with some of the finest minds in artificial intelligence right now to make sure our creatures are a real challenge and not just obstacles. In fact, one of them is presenting a paper at this year's Game Developer's Conference about his work in this field on games like Dungeon Keeper 2."
Translation: The AI you should have gotten in UO, oh and some More Eye Candy.

Correct me if I am wrong about this, but isn't the #1 complaint about this industry from it's consumers that developers have abandoned format, content, and gameplay in exchange for glitz, glitz, and super-enhanced surround-sound Dolby enhanced THX certified, .001 dot pitch resolutioned -GLITZ-?

I'm not a complete moron. I should have my Associate Moron Degree by the end of the spring semester. I'm certain there will be a measure of content, format, and gameplay in Ultima Online 2. The concern I have is that at a time when you want to create consumer "chatter" about a product, do you promote the secondary features, or do you try to push the best that your product has to offer? The answer is simple - you promote the best that your product has to offer. I'm concerned because Starr Long seems to think that the best Ultima Online 2 has to offer is More Eye Candy, More Eye Candy, and More Eye Candy.

To that, I can express my concerns in only three words:

Ultima Nine: Ascension


"Don't let the Starrs get in your eyes."
