E3-2K: When Midgets Go Bad
Great Bob does E3, part two

(Special early adopter note: Pictures along with this story will be posted when Bob and I can get back together -- E3 is a zoo and we're pretty much seperate for the great majority of it -- until then, experience the majesty and purity of plain text. - Lum, Charles In Charge)

Don't let anybody tell you Los Angeles sucks. That's not true. In fact, there's nothing wrong with the place that ten pounds of Sarin nerve gas can't fix. Really. After I exterminate the population, it'll be turned into a game preserve and a resort for my legions of undead shock troops. Everyone will love it.

Dragging along my friend Ryan, and leaving the women folks at home, I landed in L.A. I felt giddy as a school girl as I walked into the main hall. I used to take acid. I know what sensory overload is like. Brother, let me tell you it ain't nothing compared to E3. The convention hall was a cacophony of noise, lights and the smell of unwashed, eager geek. Desginers, ripped from the comfort of their cubicles, are forced to stand in front of the wave of humanity, desperatly trying to show what they've been working on. They look like tired prostitues, lifting their skirts in the hope that their pimps in the marketing department won't beat them too bad.

Since I cover MMORPGs, I decided that I would start there. Pushing past the booth babes, I headed to the Electronic Arts booth. A giant video screen pumped images of snowboarders and pixelated hockey players. To the left, stood the booth for the Sims. A waifish girl stood demonstrating a new game called "Sim Town". Surrounded by sweaty game fans, she demonstrated the features. "It looks a great deal like Sim City" I said. She nodded. "It's more personal" she told me. "In Sim City, you can tear down a factory and people move away, but this games shows who those people are. You can follow a family, and see what effect your decisions have". I nodded. I asked her if Will Wright was working on the game. She began to tremble. With wet, terrified eyesshe looked up at me. "He over-sees the game" she wispered. "If he doesn't like what he sees, he threatens to pay us in sim-checks, and force-feed us sim food." She broke into tears. "The man's a tyrant! He thinks of us like the characters in the Sims. He thinks he can tickle us to get us to like him! Help me!"

There was nothing I could do for her. With the madness around me, I couldn't even be sure if she had really said that last part, or if I was just hallucinating. The flashing lights were like an epeleptic strobe light burrowing right into my skull.

I had hopes to see the UO booth. After all, EQ had one complete with half naked elf chick. There was no booth for UO to be found. Instead was some pansy ass Brit wandering around in a UO shirt, looking over his shoulder for a death robe clad stalker. He told me that there was no booth, and that the only meeting room was upstairs...and, after looking to see if I had a press pass, he assured me I certainly wouldn't be allowed within a hundred feet of the UO team.

I might have had a few drinks in me, but I was able to resist the urge to punch him in the face. Fuck you though. And the limey horse you rode in on.

What's at E3

I would have wasted my time if I didn't bring you some news of what's actualy at this conference. Let's run down the list, shall we?

Anarchy Online: I swear to christ, I've been walking around with a hard on the size of Nebraska since seeing this game. Those Europeans might not be able to survive a walk through an American ghetto, but they understand gaming. Expect a great deal more coverage from Lumcorp in the future. You know what made the difference? Every single god damn developer seems almost reluctant to show their goods. Anarchy Online delights in it.

Warcraft III: Here's something fun to do. Ask a developer showing off the game if what we're seeing on the floor is the actual game itself, or the game after they decided to re-tool it. Watch them mumble and sweat.

Diablo II: They got a winner here, and they know it. I don't know it, since I couldn't get within twenty frickin' feet of the monitor. Given the puddle of drool at the feet of those who could actually get close to the game itself, it looks good.

Sovreign: I can't believe it. Verant may actually wind up releasing a game that doesn't make me want to gouge out my own eyeballs. No Skinner box here. They took Empire, slapped on some graphics on it, and there you go. Look for an interview with the devloper coming soon, here at Lumcorp.

Everquest: Ruins of Karnak: Here's a way to have some more fun at E3. Go up the EQ booth and pretend to be interested. Ask the devloper showing the game off questions like "So what is there to do in the game? Is there housing? Can you ride horses?". Look really disapointed when they can't give you a positive answer. Next year, you'll probably be able to do the same thing to UO2.

Vampire: The Masquerade: Three weeks. That's what they promised for the release date. Of course, they were pretty vauge about if they would have all the storyteller tools ready by then, but that's what patches are for, right? Right? Actually, the game looks good. Real good. I wouldn't even let people who like the game Vampire in my house, but this game I might just buy....after the patch, of course.

Shadowbane: The UO2 movie kicks ass. You've all seen it. I'm telling you now that Anarchy Online is the best damn game you haven't played yet...but what of Shadowbane? Well, perhaps it was the sun and the beer but I'm not ready to start praying at the altar of SB yet. As far as I can tell, they don't walk on water, they don't bear the marks of stigmata and not one of them has been nailed to a cross yet. I'm still waiting to be proved wrong, but if the UO2 movie and Anarchy Online are to be the main competition for this game, they may be in trouble. On the other hand, the flaming arows look pretty damn cool and they had midgets, beer and playboy bunnies at their booths, so what the hell.
