KNOWLEDGE IS POWER, ESPECIALLY WHEN YOU HAVE TO CLICK "OK" TO GET PAST IT
Sunday, February 27, 2000 - 3:37 AM [Filed by lietgardis]

Do you remember way back when the UO Auto-Patch message would tell you what was in development and the contents of the most recent patches? It’s a very dim recollection for me, and I don’t remember exactly when it ended and what the explanation for its discontinuation was. Would “our staff are too busy to continually update the message” be more important than keeping the players updated? And what’s the connection between this and the people that put “OSI fukin’ SuX” in their bank macros? Let’s see.

I watched someone perform an elaborate scam with a “tower scroll” at the Britain bank this evening. He swore the “new patch” enabled people to carry around a scroll representing placed buildings, and told people that they just weren’t up-to-date enough if they hadn’t heard about it. Ignoring my “don’t do it! you’ll be sorry! don’t say I didn’t tell you so!” warnings, the victim put in his 100k or whatever in the trade window with the "tower scroll," and found himself with nothing after clicking his diamond. But how was he to know that that this “tower scroll” wasn’t the way “secure house trading” works, as of last week’s update? The victim didn’t feel so betrayed by the scammer, but instead by Origin -- “fucking osi and their stupid patches.” (We could also argue about how stupid somebody is for believing anybody would sell a tower for so little when he or she could get $800 for it on Ebay, but that’s not the point.)

What with the recent New Idea a Day phase the dev team is going through and the ensuing outrages, it’s more important than ever that the entire playerbase keep updated on changes. Any amount of time spent at a high-traffic area in-game will prove to you that the majority of players are ignorant about what’s going on (the house scamming above is indicative of the phenomenon). Visiting a web site for the news is too much work; most people now would rather wait for a patch and then ICQ their friends to ask about it. Were players more educated back when the Auto-Patch message told them about it? I’d assume they were.

Most people probably just click through the Auto-Patch message as quickly as possible, and probably wouldn’t even notice if it was changed. But if a patch went in, and they knew the message would tell them what was in it, I’d imagine they’d read it the next time they loaded up the client. It would be even better if there was some way to access the information from within the game without loading a web browser, some kind of extention of the “Help” window that itemized everything included in the last update, but that’s probably not something we can hope for. Updating the Auto-Patch message with a summary of the changes is realistic, though -- it’s easy and accessible for everyone to see, and it doesn’t require visiting an external resource.

When the playerbase is forcefully kept up-to-date on changes in the game, they’ll not only be protected in whatever small way from scams taking advantage of their ignorance, but they’ll feel closer to Origin. This is the point -- the players will feel like they’re active, rather than sitting back and letting things happen to them by an unknown force; they’ll feel like Origin is going out of its way to reach out for them, freely giving them information, rather than tweaking things behind their backs. It might help stop some of the blind “I hate OSI” sentiments so many people adhere to. Alienation between the company and the playerbase is one of the worst things for the developer of a continually-changing “persistant world” to have, and alienation is what most players get when information isn’t forced down their throats.

I know you’re all up-to-date, but this site’s daily readers only make up .05% of all “UO subscribers.”
