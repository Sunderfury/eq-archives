10:00 AM MORE POST-PATCH-PARTUM FALLOUT

Got these two notes:

Mourning the loss of a friend.

Tis truly a sad time for me. This week a dear friend of mine has passed away. I met him in November of 1997. Throughout the years we have had good times and bad times. Often we would fight and not talk to each other for weeks or months. But still I have always come back apologizing for my ignorance in leaving him.

Ok ok so UO is not a real person and it's not dead per-se. But I still feel I have lost a dear friend. This game has been an outlet for frustration, a cure for depression, a veritable fountain of friendship and brotherhood. I have met people in game that have come to be great friends out of game. Most importantly it brought me and my brother together from literally a thousand miles apart.

I have quit the game numerous times and always I have come back to it. I look at what the game was as of a week ago compared to its inception and I can see a huge difference. The game had gotten so much better.

What happened this time? Well quite honestly I can't pinpoint exactly what makes me so upset about this latest patch. I don't buy the fact that OSI overlooked something as simple as telling the people what they are getting. It's just not believeable in the least.

I'm strongly leaning toward quitting the game again. Only this time I won't be coming back. Over the last 4 or 5 months I have had the best gaming experience in UO of all the time I've been playing. A week ago I would have said the game was just fine like it was and not to change a thing. Now I look at the hysteria that OSI has caused in their customers. The apparent lack of any thought at all at what we as the players (read customers) actually want and I'm disgusted.

I just can't see this continuation of forgiveness for everything OSI is and does. I won't do anything drastic like put my account up for sale yet. I'll give it a couple weeks more to let everything settle and re-assess the situation.

Good luck and good night,
Redhawk of LS aka (Matt) *if I remember correctly* :-)

---

I have warned on many occasion in the past that big patches are big problems waiting to happen. The latest patch was 4 months worth of work right? and yet I have seen mini patches with more substance. Dont get me wrong here - Im not gonna slam the dev team but they have got to get their acts together now. I have voiced my opinion to them and I hope others will begin to voice theirs. I believe that a patch every week or every other week would be ideal. Each patch should address one or two issues - and no more thought put into adding new stuff to the game. To much stiuff doesnt work right. Too many people are getting frustrated because a feature that if it worked right would make them very happy instead frustrates them by failing to do what it is intended to do. The dev team should promise less and quietly do more. Lady Moi asked me what I thought about the patch - I said Honestly I dont care about it. I mean Im not a newbie (dont need new haircut), I run UOA along with 90% of the rest of the players and I spend 2 seconds on the opening screen so the stone look doesnt effect me at all. I simply see 4 months worth of work for mostly stuff we all already had anyway. That being said Im very content with siege so there really isnt much the dev team could do to impress me further - except fix the old old bugs and issues that Tal raised yesterday and lots of others have been asking for for a year now. I intend to speak to dev team members soon (hopefully on the BV show) and specifically ask them - what are your priorities for UO? Sorry to see Arcadian playing EQ - but thats got to tell you something - I picked up the game and played to - something I never thought Id even have time to look at. All in all the patch simply was an annoyance that I couldnt play SP one day - but then no one else could either.

Please - Ultima Online Dev team - Fix the game, make it stable - do the dirty work of code cleanup - your players will love you for it.

3:00 PM PATCH DAY PATCH DAY ALL FALL DOWN

Woo boy. Where do I start.

Well, we could start with the good things from the patch. Being able to target health bars is very cool. In rapidly descending order, it's nice that newbies actually have playable archetypes to start with, it's nice that newbies get a PK switch to call their own, the Starcraft-esque health bars are sort of nifty, the quest engine may be actually somewhat useful once managed by human beings and the Last Shard shortcut switch is a small bit of a time saver.

Sorry folks, that's about it.

OK. What professional genius deemed it necessary to block reds from instalogging. No, wait, I know, let's force them to suffer stat loss by allowing people to kill them while they're not logged on! That makes sense! And if they try to get out of stat loss by macroing as a ghost, we can bust them for macroing. Harrr!

Of course for a red to instalog, they would first have to actually log on. This has not been easy in the past 24 hours, as servers went down more often then Presidential interns. This is understandable, though, since after all this is an entirely new patch being applied to servers for the first time... what? You mean it was in testing for over a month, and being bug tested by thousands of dedicated volunteers? Whoops.

Let's see, we have:

  • a patch which apparently went through QA while they were all out of the office watching "Dangerous Curves: The Autobiography of Traci Lords" again
  • significant gameplay changes which it was deemed unnecessary to actually inform the people who might be affected by them (hope you guys on the regular shards didn't try to open any spawned chests yesterday!)
  • evidence that, according to the dev team, THE most pressing issue in UO development is crafting a poorly implemented clone of UOA.

Meanwhile, guildstones are STILL useless (I wonder how many reds turned red simply because they got sick of trying to fight with the interface while declaring guildwars), there are STILL dozens of ways to crash the client, the client STILL occasionally takes your entire PC down with you when you lose connection, there is STILL no secure method of housing transfer, and the words "necromancy" and "alchemy" are STILL no more than bad jokes. And I'm just waiting for peope to bitch about folks who got their boat keys stolen and the theif then recalling off the key to loot them blind.

Siege Perilous was the best thing to happen development wise easily in over a year, and according to interviews it was an after-hours project. The graphic enhancements to the client was also an after-hours project. What the hell is being done during working hours, then? Hell, from the looks of it, whoever is budgeting the programmers' time needs to STOP and let them loose!

In fact, and this is REALLY just wacked ol'Lum talking out of his ass again, this whole patch fiasco strikes me as the result of a dictate from the Mountaintop. "You guys are taking too long in getting this patch out the door. Get it done, and get it released... next Wednesday." Because from where I sit, it's either the result of clueless new bosses who have no idea what is involved in patching and debugging the living organism that is UO.... or of a development team that STILL has failed to learn the lessons of the past two years.

But what do I know, I just play the thing.
