AC LEVELLING SERVICE A SCAM
Thursday, September 7, 2000 - 6:28 PM [Filed by myschyf]
Delusion did an article on this levelling service for AC last month. In it he warned of the perils of giving out your account name and password information to a stranger. Much to his dismay, a reader writes in that he has found Del's warning to be very true.

Lesaonar wrote me to tell me this sad tale of woe, told by Newt on the AC PC Punks Board:

This message is going out to all as an apology. Please do not flame me, I already feel disappointed as it is. I should have listened to all the warning, but, me being the questionable one, went ahead and did it anyways.
I contacted Grem concerning leveling to see what he can actually do. We made an agreement concerning leveling my level 32 mage character to level 40, since I will be preparing for school. The agreement was that I would pay him in full after he levels me to 35, where he will continue to level me to 40. I sent him my account's information. At this point, I made the mistake of not checking my acct everyday to see that it was in order, unfortunately, I could not do that...since I was very busy. I came back one week later, the account has not even been touched. I gained some experience from my vassals, that was it. So, I contacted Grem telling him, all he has to do was the boring wall draining in DM, minimal risk.

I made some precautions (bad precautions I might add...thinking that he or whoever he used to play my acct would not check the other characters after their slogon of "we are the ONLY one that can be trusted"). I "hide" my good items, Matty Robe, a few shards, Motes and a 113 Yumi on different characters.

I came back to check my character after another week has elapsed. I found all my mules with many items in the first bag. This means that the person was using it..and just unequiped it. My motes where combined to a pyreal bar (8 motes) where two motes where missing. I did not want to combine the motes for trading purposes. My one and only key that I have ever found was missing. I did see that I received a new yoroi pauldron with a 154al base. I am thinking here that whoever used my character also used the key to see what he/she can get from one of the urber chest. It gets worse =( My matty robe and yumi was missing. I contacted Grem concerning this and he says he has no idea what so ever concerning that. He stated that he never touched any characters other than the one I told him to use. Upon further arguing, he then stats that I am giving him too much of a hassal being a customer and therefore, he voids our contract agreement.

My character was never touched, my items moved, items that I cherished where it would be hard or impossible for me to ever get again are missing. I have to also say on Grem's behalf, that maybe our email was intercepted and a hacker took the password, I do not know. I am just depressed that this happened to me. Maybe know I should learn to listen to good advices.

I have canceled my account, it doesn't matter to me anymore, I am too busy with school now. What has resulted just proves to me that its not worth it anylonger. I apologies, I will miss those that knew me.

Sincerely,
Newt

After doing some further investigation consisting mostly of reading this scam artist's message boards I found yet another unsatisfied customer.

Hi,

Raydon is my char and was one of the first to request the service. I bought 6 hours at $10/hr
at the time the service was still $9/hr. I was paying extra as an incentive for Grem to do a good job.
I asked what times he had available, and he said he was free right now and could work on Raydon all afternoon (Thursday). great I send PayPal. Thursday evening rolls around and Raydon was barely touched.
I sent an email saying I will not play until Friday evening which more than enough time to finish Raydon.
Friday evening rolls around and raydon has hardly gained anything.

Ok Raydon is a swordman w/item magic. he was half way through lvl 11, when I bought the service. Grem said he had a mage on every server to help him lvl the char.
(I think Cryptic is helping him on Solclaim).
When I log in on Friday Raydon has grown to a whopping lvl 13. all his magic rings/clothes were out of mana. I was missing a magic res. ring, and I had a helm that was heavier and less AL then the one I had. I was also missing a peerless healing kit, I used to ID to help bring up my healing skill. I did have higher al leggs.
I gave up an evening of playtime and $60 to go from lvl 11 to lvl 13. umm...can you say rip off.
after some complaining (and explain the new rates didn't apply to me becuase we had an agreement BEFORE he changed rates) Grem said he had me covered and was going to get Raydon to lvl 18 or 19. that didn't happen. I changed my password and ask for my money back. In the beginning he had a satisfaction guarenteed clause. I got half my money back.
don't let someone else play your Char. they can mess with you inventory and if he can't easily lvl a lvl 11 he sure cvan't lvl a 20 or higher. Just find a mage to buff you and them him/her for thier time.
Grem is very unorganized and consitantly changing rates/promises.
you may msg me in game as Raydon or as Zyper both on Solclaim, if you have questions...I have emails to back up all my claims, and can provide them for anybody who wants them.

-Raydon of Solclaim

The very next post is by Grem. You would think he might deny stealing the items or some such but no, his posts offers to pay for the items he stole and wishes Raydon hadn't posted on his boards -- though I guess that would actually go without saying.

Theres one review that will put me off to a rickity start. ;-)

I havent had a buffer on Solclaim yet, big problem for me.
=( =(

Solclaim is a lot more difficult of a server to play because the economy is still unbalanced, and the server lacks players. As for the rates, they will remain unchanged.

I will give Raydon 100k today to make up for his lost items, I just wish he had told me his concerns before posting on the site. I also asked him if he wished for me to continue levelling him, but he never responded.

The warning bears repeating: do not give out your account information to anyone you don't know well and trust absolutely.
