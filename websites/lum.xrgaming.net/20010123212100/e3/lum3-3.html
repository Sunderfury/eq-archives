E3-2K: GIRLS! GIRLS! GIRLS!
Lum does E3, part three

E3 2000 can be summed up in this one picture.

Now all you people bitching at me because I never take pictures of lesbian sex when I come across it - there it is, big as life, smack on the Jumbotron. And not as many as you think are paying attention. They're tired, they're drunk, the synapses have been fused into a mass of glass, people have been trying to tell them all week what really important video games are being worked on, they want to go home.

And yes, that really is lesbian sex on the Jumbotron. And yes, LoH saw it. I'm not sure what made her more angry; looking up and seeing 75 foot high Catholic school girls fiddling with each other's nether bits, or seeing the line of kids peeking underneath the tarp looking up at the 75 foot high Catholic school girls.

Then again, there are the other highlights to E3. Here are probably the two coolest people even vaguely associated with online entertainment, and I got them both in one picture - Seanbaby and Balseraph.

Not telling you which one is which, either. Although Seanbaby told me he actually does read this site, which made me giggle like a little schoolgirl. I'm pretty sure Balseraph reads it too. So I'm just confused. Anyway, there's both of them.

Started the day doing the UO2 demo/conveyor belt ("Please keep your hands, feet and questions inside the blue lines at all times") and moseyed on over to the Verant booth. Unfortunately my camera puked all over itself around the time I was madly snapping pictures of LESBIAN PR0N and in some sort of karmic retribution erased both the photos of Starr "Blackthorne of Borg" Long doing the crrrrrrraw in front of the McFarlane Toys display and of the Everquest booth all displaying "YOU HAVE BEEN DISCONNECTED" messages on huge display screens. Disconnection was basically the order of the day; although I had scheduled an appointment with John "Vito" Smedley and Brad "Visionquest" McQuaid, Smedley was MIA and McQuaid was stuck signing autographs since Keith Parkinson, the Everquest/noted fantasy artist was stranded in an airport somewhere. No word on if anyone asked "who the hell is B. McQuaid and what did he do with Keith Parkinson?". Hell, everyone there was probably Evercrack junkies anyway. I did get the chance to (a) look at Sovereign, which looked like lots of numbers and very primitive yet oddly cool-looking graphics and (b) bop Brad McQuaid on the head with a rolled-up Keith Parkinson poster and scream "YOU MADE MY WIFE LOOK LIKE THAT!". He then ran away which pretty much made any further interviewing impossible. Please note that I am making absolutely none of this up (especially the LESBIAN PR0N).

Finished out E3 resting my dead feet, and hanging out at the GODGames booth since they had scantily clad midgets, Shadowbane developers, free drinks, and, of course, LESBIAN PR0N. You really could not ask for more from life. Well, except the chance to ply UO2 developers with drinks and then get them to talk on the record about all sorts of things, which is how we spent the evening. It made for an interesting discussion, especially when Ubiq discussed what the 3D0 people managed to do with one million spare little army men when bored late at night (hint: think marching columns).

Highlight of the show? I bet you think I'm gonna say Lesbian Pr0n. No, you can get that anywhere (well, most anywhere). Really the highlight was in just meeting a dizzying amount of people -- developers, webmasters, writers, even some Russian guy who I spent an hour arguing the finer points of immigration policy with for no apparent reason. My brain feels full.

In terms of games: Anarchy Online is going to hit 2000 like a fairly large nuclear warhead. The year after that we'll see Shadowbane and Ultima Online 2, both of which are appealing to similar yet somewhat different markets (and are very different games). After that? Haven't a clue, but I suspect in 2002 there will be a trade show just for online games. Which will be good, because I can safely assume that it will not be in Los Angeles - the lowlight of the show. GOD I hate Los Angeles. There is nothing more relaxing then driving down Santa Monica Boulevard, and having your passengers point out all the gay male botique shops.
