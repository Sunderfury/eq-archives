AWWWW, IT'S OKIE, WE STILL WUB YOU THIIIIIIIIIS MUCH, CHRIS
Monday, January 24, 2000 - 7:53 AM [Filed by lum]

Chris "FUCK YOU, Mushashi!" L'Etoile has a plan. You know all that already, mainly because since Azile just went kneedeep into the Darktide political hoopla, it's the only thing in the Asheron's Call universe worth reading that uses the word "fuck" (damn, there's that word again) until Musashi updates his damn website. Earlier last week he mentioned my name, which ordinarily would have been enough to set me off into a frenzy of self-referential spouting, except I was kind of busy trying to play Asheron's Call again.

Anyway, to summarize for you puds who can't read, he took issue with my gleefully shouting "KILL ALL THE DUPING BASTARDS!" at the latest UOE-related banning in UO, wondered how that jived with the (non-existent) bitching I did about the KoC duper banning in UO (hint - I approved of that one too) and then mused about how that squared with my bitching about his saying that threatening to ban your userbase is bad, mmmmkay? (Actually he makes a valid point about moral equivalence which would have, um, made more sense if he had made it in his original posting. But that's OK. Really.) The conclusion he then reached was that because I tried AC for a week and didn't like it, I was automatically an OSI fanboy.

Or, instead of distorting his words, let's just repeat them;

This made everything crystal clear to me. Lum's a cynical guy, with a dim view of humanity in general, and MMORPG developers in particular. Therefore, anything I could possibly say in a given situation will be automatically wrong to him.

However, since Lum enjoys UO, he's willing to give the benefit of the doubt to its developers. He may rant about how they handle issue X until he's blue in the face, but at the end of the day, they're still his game company. It's like when I see rabid Red Sox fans cursing the parentage of the manager and every member of the team as they sit in the stands at Fenway in a torrential downpour, waving their little foam "number one" hand.

This doesn't bother me anymore. He's not riding my ass because he thinks I, specifically, am wrong (although he might); he's riding it because that's what Lum does, and because he doesn't like AC.

OK. First off, if I ever am seen with one of those candy ass foam rubber "numbah one" things, you can goddamn well SHOOT ME ON SIGHT. It's for the best. ANYway.

Origin is NOT my game company. Really. It's some idiot that owns ERTS stock, not me. I like some of the folks that work there now, mainly because they're the fourth generation or so who went through the revolving doors and are actually former gamers themselves with clues. That being said, if you or anyone else think I'm giving them a free pass, wait until UO2 hits. I've got about 20 loads of buckshot just waiting to go off into someone based on what little I've already heard about that little atrocity.

But I think what really has Chris all pissy is that, well, I've been pretty vocal about not doing so well with Asheron's Call. That's understandable. It's like, his job and stuff. As he wrote to Mu, while indulging in that sport of ORPG developers everywhere called "We're Not OSI":

Bottom line, the Staff does not exist because she did "something cool on a site, or host(ed) an event, or suck(ed) miles of cock, or deliver(ed) chinese take-out to our offices." You of all people should know us better than to think that, Mu. I'm very disappointed to learn that one of the most clueful people in the community, and one of perhaps a dozen who's met us personally, can still think that we'd play favorites like OSI does. WE'RE NOT OSI, all right? We regard their behavior as absolute anathema, as a case in point of what not to do. Damn, don't I slam them enough in my .plan to make that clear?

Everyone had the same shot at getting the Staff - it certainly wasn't Sarneho's decision. If Caeryn's abusing it (I don't think she is as a matter of course, otherwise this would have come up before now), we'll take a hard, long look at what, if anything, can be done to reign it in. You know why? Because, unlike OSI, whenever we find that a mistake has been made, we admit it and we fucking fix it. The WoBF was in the game beginning in Preview2, but when it was discovered and exploited nine months later, we fixed it promptly.

Jesus Christ, does every AC player still have 'Nam flashbacks to the abuses of UO? Calm down and tell us what the problems are before you jump down are throats with all these accusations and threats. Don't think I missed that part about visiting the office with a baseball bat. I know you're mad, Mu, and you have good reason to be, but that's crossing a line. Go ahead and say we're cocksmoking moron losers who don't know how to design or run a game. Don't threaten us with physical harm.

So, without threatening ANY PHYSICAL HARM AT ALL to anyone at Turbine, let's come clean here. I have a secret I need to share with you all. When it comes to these games, I suck.

No, really. It's true. In Everquest, I'm a permanewb who can't seem to get the hang of getting past 11th level, and the only reason I keep going with it is because my wife's like a level 93 Hell Wizard and she looks at me forlornly, wordlessly thinking "Come on! It's only level 20! It's not that hard! You can do it!". Unfortunately, Everquest requires you to be nice to idiots, but that's a whole another topic. And in Ultima Online I was pretty much comic relief. UO is great in that you can suck and still pretty much be up to the standard of everyone else in the game, so I could run around in a death robe and make fun of others, and the fact that (remember) I suck at these games doesn't come out and hit you. I like to think that this means that UO has a thriving community, because anything that lets me be the village idiot in the same world as some idiot is all good in da hood.

But in the world of Dereth, I just suck. I wander around the newbie villages and some shreth or something will kill me and steal my one piece of armor and I'm sitting at a lifestone going "What the fuck?". At least on Darktide I could use the excuse that it was a PK.

What's funny, of course, is that this makes me the ORPG's perfect target market. I'm not the guy that sits in your game for 19 hours a day camping spawn points or waiting for houses to decay, I'm the guy who tries to play a couple hours a week or so because I have, like, stuff to do.

Asheron's Call does a lot of things wrong and a lot of things right. Someday I'll revisit that topic when I'm feeling more pundity. And I have no doubt that, like UO, which was a burning hellpit of bugs and damnation on it's release, it will improve into more right then wrong. By that time I may be 5th level.

However, OSI has learned one lesson that Chris and everyone else may need to learn: it's not about cool shit, it's not about dynamic landscape alteration, it's about having a community that's so whack you can run around with a newbie named Bob and still have a good time.

At any rate, it's late and I need to jet, but I leave you with these words: it's OK to suck, if you can make light of it on your web page later.

It's OK, Lum, we suck, too.
