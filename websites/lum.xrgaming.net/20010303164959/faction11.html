THE FACTION SYSTEM v1.1B

REVISIONS TO THE ORIGINAL FACTION SYSTEM DOCUMENT

 

Revision history

1.1A – First version

1.1B – Changes to taxation

 

 

Removing guilds/players

The faction leadership (Faction Leader, Ministers, and High Councilors) may remove guilds and/or players from the faction. This is done at the faction stone. Players need not be present to be removed.

 

Players removed from a guild may not rejoin another faction for a period of one week. During that week they may still vote in their faction’s elections, but are not otherwise considered faction members. (This is to allow a reaction to a purge within a faction.) During that week, any Faction Leader or High Councilor of the original faction may reinstate that player/guild with no penalty.

 

Ranks/Points System

Each faction generates a pool of “free points” which may be distributed by any faction officer as they see fit. These points are generated for the faction as individual members earn points, on a 1:10 scale (each point earned by a faction member also earns .1 point into the faction free point pool)

 

Faction officers may give these free points to any member of the faction, for any reason. Examples include payment to guild tradesmen, awards for particularly effective or valorous behavior in battle, or as bribes to influential members of the faction.

 

Faction officers may not give more than one half of their own points as “free points” to any one person. A faction officer also loses points when giving free points, on the same scale of 1:10 (1 point lost for every 10 points granted).

 

The exception to this rule is the Faction Leader. The Faction Leader may freely dispense points from the free point pool, with no limits and without any loss of points himself. There is of course nothing preventing an unscrupulous Faction Leader from simply granting all free points to himself.

 

Leaders and Voting

The faction leadership structure is as follows. Note that the process for calling elections for faction leadership is superseded by this version. Elections should be in theory rare, not a once-weekly affair. Of course in factions with high political strife they may happen even more frequently.

 

Faction Leader: Overall political leader of the faction. Can freely dispense points in the free point pool. May hire and fire Finance Ministers, War Ministers, and High Councilors at will. Must be elected, and may call an election at any time. May also execute the functions of the Lord Protector and Seneschal. Theoretically a Faction Leader could never name ANY Ministers and act as a dictator.

 

Lord Protector: Military leader of the faction. May hire War Ministers or High Councilors. May purchase, place and control faction guards of ANY city under faction control his/her using the faction treasury.  Named by Faction Leader. May dispense free points within limits. May call an election at any time, and if fired by the Faction Leader, a prompt will appear asking if the minister would like to call an election in response to this action. (This prompt will appear the next time the fired Minister logs in if offline, unless 7 days pass in the interim.) A Minister may also at any time call for a Quorum of the Council as described below, thus setting the No-Confidence vote in motion.

 

Seneschal: Economic leader of the faction. May hire Finance Ministers or High Councilors. Has control over the faction’s economic structure of faction-aligned player vendors, vendors within all faction owned cities, including special faction vendors, and controlled town vendor sales rates. Named by Faction Leader. May dispense free points within limits. A Minister may also at any time call for a Quorum of the Council as described below, thus setting the No-Confidence vote in motion.

 

War Minister for the town of X: May purchase, place and control faction guards of his/her using a percentage of the faction treasury. (Said percentage is derived from the number of cities controlled by the faction.) Named by Faction Leader or Lord Protector. May dispense free points within limits. May call an election at any time, and if fired by the Faction Leader or Lord Protector, a prompt will appear asking if the minister would like to call an election in response to this action. (This prompt will appear the next time the fired Minister logs in if offline, unless 7 days pass in the interim.) A Minister may also at any time call for a Quorum of the Council as described below, thus setting the No-Confidence vote in motion.

 

Finance Minister for the town of X: Has control over the faction’s economic structure of his/her city, including special faction vendors, and controlled town vendor sales rates. Named by Faction Leader. May dispense free points within limits. A Minister may also at any time call for a Quorum of the Council as described below, thus setting the No-Confidence vote in motion.

 

High Councilor: A set number of leaders, named by a Faction Leader. May dispense free points within limits. Serves at the sufferance of the Leader. At any time, a High Councilor may call for a Quorum of the Council. If a majority of the High Council, consisting of the two ministers and all High Councilors are currently online and agree via vote, they may call for a No-Confidence vote to be held. If a majority of ALL High Councilors and Ministers so vote over the next 24 hours, the Council falls and elections must be held. No Minister or Councilor may be fired during this 24-hour period.

 

Contempt: A guild master of the faction may declare his faction in Contempt of the current leadership. If two-thirds of the guilds within a faction declare themselves in Contempt, the Faction Leader falls and new elections are held. A list of guilds in Contempt of the faction is publicly accessible, and of course nothing prevents a Faction Leader from simply ejecting guilds in Contempt. Of course, if this is a routine course of action for that Leader, the faction will be fairly small indeed. Guilds that have declared themselves in Contempt pay no taxes but otherwise are members of the faction in every way.

 

As seen, in theory it is possible for unscrupulous Leaders to completely subvert the democratic process. This is intentional. The ultimate check on a faction’s political power will be, of course, the willingness of players to remain a member of that faction, or, failing that, declaring themselves in open revolt (the Contempt feature).

 

After an election, the Ministers and Councilors remain in place until removed and replaced. An election or No-Confidence vote may not be called within a week of one another (this allows new Faction Leaders a chance to name a new leadership without penalty).

 

The Treasury

 

Taxation

On a weekly basis, money is collected from every member of the faction’s bank box in line with taxation rates declared by the Seneschal. This money can be distributed by the Seneschal or Faction Leader as he or she deems fit. If a player has no money in his or her bank box, he or she pays no tax. If during a taxation cycle, more than half of a Guild does not pay taxes, they are considered in Contempt. If an individual fails to pay taxes twice in a row they are removed from the faction.

 

Taxation rates would be set at no less than zero GP and no higher than 200 GP. They would be collected once every seven to ten days at a day and time set by the Seneschal. It is recommended that this day and time be set randomly so that players will find it harder to purposely empty their bank to dodge taxation.

 

Taxation is optional for every faction, and it is perfectly viable for a faction to survive solely on sales taxes from vendor sales.

 

Player Vendors and Factions

Players may set their own vendors to be associated with a faction. Taxes are collected from those vendors in accordance with the rules involving taxable commerce within faction-owned cities. Additionally, player vendors act according to faction policy (no sales to enemy or neutral factions or higher prices charged, depending on how economic policy is set by the Seneschal). Thus, for example, the British faction can declare an economic policy of no sales to Murderers or Minax, high tariffs to Shadowlords, and discounted rates to Sages and fellow British. All player-owned vendors aligned to that faction would follow these rules.

 

Players belonging to a given faction have their vendors assume faction association automatically. Non-faction members may also associate their vendors with a given faction. It is assumed that the faction will give them some consideration in exchange for their revenue (protection, raw materials, etc.), however this is not handled by the game mechanics.
