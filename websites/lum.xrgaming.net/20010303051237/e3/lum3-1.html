The Horror, The Madness, The ...ooh, Warcraft 3!
e3-2k - day one

My Palm Pilot choked a hairball and lost my entire schedule. Our party got seperated and one was left without a pass. Did I mention traffic? It was a busy day, all around.

When I asked Raph Koster if he was going to be at E3, he told me that it was basically the world's largest video arcade and not much actual discussion went on. This was, of course, before Koster became Verant's newest acquisition and he, along with the entire Verant-Austin team, was out and around denying that they were actually working on anything. He was right partially, though - E3 is like a big video arcade where none of the big kids actually let you play the games. You have to shouldersurf and hope someone will walk off for a second so you can hijack the controls.


See that blur in the center? That's Warcraft 3, baby.
Feel the coolness.

I didn't spend much time wandering the booths, due to the aformentioned hell-morning, but did get to check in on Blizzard's booth, which was showing Warcraft 3. If you could actually elbow the hundreds of people who also wanted to see Warcraft 3 aside, you saw a game with the usual Blizzard insane intricate detail. Warcraft 3 looked a lot like Warcraft 2, only cooler. Cooler is, you know, cool. Thus ends my Warcraft 3 review based on my detailed source material (five minutes of watching some guy set waypoints)


And they said no UO2 developers
would be at E3.

After the Anarchy Online and Shadowbane sessions, Great Bob and I got some time with Gordon Walton, Tyrantus Supremus of what we think is still called Origin. He was kind enough to produce the marketeers who came up with "Enchantress Emily" and The Game Formerly Known As UO2's new title. He was then even more kind enough to block the door as we... questioned them. You will be pleased to know that we showed the author of Enchantress Emily the thread regarding her mailout on our gimped forum. We also explained to her that Enchanters were actually in their competitor's game. Gordon seemed remarkably amused by all of this.

As for TGFKAUO2, we were shown a mockup of the box. Hey, gotta get the important things done first. Actually it wasn't even pretending to be the final version of TGFKAUO2's box, but concept art to show that the name "Origin" was in REALLY REALLY REALLY BIG letters and the words "Ultima World Online" was in really really small type. Basically "Ultima World Online" is a brand name, sort of like Oldsmobile or Wu-Tang, and the name of the game is really "Origin". You know, like the company that dare not speak its name. Also, the woman on the Origin box was actually dressed. We're told that the artist who did the UO:R box is somewhere else now.


Raph Koster and Chris Meyer, just damned happy to
be here. That's Marduk behind them, going "Jesus,
Designer Dragon's wearing an Everquest shirt!"

Then came dinner with Gordon, the entire Verant-Austin team, Jessica Mulligan, who was promising everyone they would be the first or second to hear what she was actually working on now when she could say, and John Taylor, whose recent game "Legends of Kesmai" recieved a small amount of press in these parts. (His comment - "It had a run of 14 years where most games have a lifespan of about six months. I think we did pretty well.") Most of the conversation was immensely interesting and totally off the record, but Rich Vogel let us tell the world about his dream concept while at Origin --

ADULTIMA!

That's right, the Ultima Online you knew you always wanted. A full skill system, player craftable items, geographic seperation of player "preferences", real dungeons, and a whole new meaning to Buccaneer's Den. Basically someone at EA told him they wanted a game that could get a million subscribers, and well, you know...

As I write this, it's 3 am. You know, I am going to be one tired son of a bitch Monday.
