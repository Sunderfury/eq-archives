WE DON'T WANT YOU
Friday, June 16, 2000 - 8:50 AM [Filed by great bob]

Time for old Bob here to drag out the soapbox and yell at the loons.

Massively Multiplayer Online Role Playing Games are full of loons. From one stupid extreme to another we watch as people try to rip the community apart with bull headed extremism. The loons marshal their forces on the battle ground, and scream from the top of their lungs about how the other side "just doesn't get it". Let's break down this debate, shall we?

Role Players: Has anyone ever asked you "has the server gone down yet?" and have you answered "Server? I doth know not what thee are talking about!"? If so, then I'm talking to you. This is for the person who tells the story of their elf mother and human father dying in the streets of Trinsic and expects the rest of us not only to smile and nod, but to play along with them. This is for the person who gets very upset about the celebration of Christmas inside a game. Massively Multiplayer Role Playing Games are not for you. They're bad for you. They're going to raise your blood pressure and send you to an early grave. You see, nobody except for you cares about your meta-fiction. We really don't. We don't give a damn about the backstory unless it somehow makes a difference in the game itself. You people have focused on the role playing, and forgotten about the game. A game!!! You know, where people are trying to have some fun? You run your inbreed seer sponsored events, and you honestly wonder why people aren't excited about sitting around a tower talking about the plight of Lady so and so. You seem genuinely confused when people at your events become bored and start looking for something fun to do. I have no problem if you want to wander around pretending to be a drow vampire borg ninja, or whatever. That's your business. You can do that at your local 7-11 for all I care, but when you try to force your vision of roleplaying on me, don't be surprised if you get a punch in the nose for your efforts. Massively Multiplayer Role Playing Game. Understand this. The "massively" part means that there are many different kinds of players out there, and very few feel like you do. The solution? Small shards were everyone agrees that you really are an elf.

Player Killers: You people should be playing on smaller servers as well. Here's a quick newsflash you might want to be sitting down for. People don't like being killed by other people. Really. The market has shown this over and over again. PK servers are the minority. Given a choice, most players choose not to engage in PvP. Yet you scream and yell and compare yourself to oppressed Jews in Germany. You accuse companies of being on a witch hunt. You proudly proclaim how you make the game "more interesting". Time for a reality check. Even more than rabid role players, your playstyle, by it's very nature, interferes with the playstyles of others. Role players can at least be ignored. You can't. Now someday in the future there may be a game where the sheep love playing with the wolves. Today though, there isn't. There will always be limits placed upon you. Accept this simple fact, grasshopper. The masses have spoken, and they want you nerfed. This may not nessicarily be a good thing (as an avid PvP player, I'm not happy with this myself) but it's a fact of life. A company has a fiduciary responsibility to it's stock holders. It needs to do what it feels best for the majority of players....and you folks aren't the majority by a long shot.

Grief Players: Role players and PK's may be excused for their exuberance. They love their playstyles, and they have just cause to believe that what they do is "the way things ought to be". You people, on the other hand, just don't have a clue. You name yourself "Hitler", act like a boob and wonder why you were banned. "It's my right!" you shout to an exasperated audience. You wouldn't do this in the real world, of course, because of the ever-present "I'm going to be hit by someone" factor. In the virtual world, however, it's another story. As a paying customer I don't have to put up with you. If I'm eating at a nice restaurant, I have the right to tell the manager to remove the person sitting next to me who is buck naked and masturbating. The manager, if he's smart, will remove that person. You grief players are the naked person sitting next to me. Stop looking so surprised when the waiter kicks you out the door, k?

I'm sure many of the people in the above groups are fine people normally. Most of you don't beat up on nuns or do terrible things to goats. Why, then, are you being picked on? It's simple. You do not reflect the views of the players. There's nothing wrong with that. I don't reflect the views of players either. What is wrong, is that you people are zealots. Like Ralph Nadar or Pat Buchanan, you try to cram your bizzare world view down our throats, and wonder why we spit it back at you. Should developers take your wants into account? Of course they should...but that also has to be balanced against the wants of the rest of the game community.

You people can't accept that. That's why you need to be beaten with a stick. Brutally. We'll most likely have to kick you a few times for good measure.
