WHAT'S IN A NAME?
Wednesday, May 10, 2000 - 12:10 PM [Filed by great bob]

Don't call it UO2 anymore.

A new name has been announced. From the UO2 web page:

Last week, it was announced that Ultima Online 2 was a working title, and that we felt our game would be better represented with a different name. After much discussion and furrowing of brows, we have come to a decision and are happy to share with you the news that Ultima Online 2 will now be known as Ultima Worlds Online: Origin.

What's that you say? Isn't Origin the name of the company? Yes it is. Won't that be confusing? We don't think so. By the time this game ships, we won't be putting the Origin (the company) logo on the box. This will be our first game going out under the new EA Games logo. We expect this to be a game with a lifecycle spanning for many, many years, so the Origin name can live on.

In related news, Mcdonalds will now be calling it's new sandwich the "Mcdonald" and the Walt Disney company will be making a new cartoon character to join Mickey called "Disney".

It makes things so much simpler, really.

Ummm...why not?

MORE GAMES TO FALL UNDER THE EA.COM GUILLOTINE
Wednesday, May 10, 2000 - 10:11 AM [Filed by myschyf]

The players of I/LOK shouldn't feel alone. Poking around on the Gamestorm site I've found more games about to go under the executioner's axe on May 29. Of these, the one that I'm most disturbed about, is Air Warrior Classic. Air Warrior was first released by Kesmai Studios on GEnie in 1987* and has been described by some in the industry as one of the best roleplaying games ever -- not to mention a realistic flight simulation game. There are guilds that were formed in the first Air Warrior that have gone on to play UO, AC, EQ and still exist today -- with many of the original members.

The players of AW Classic are being shuffled into Air Warrior III. As I've never played either I can't speak to the differences but I know the differences between the old BattleTech and the new 'shell' currently available are phenomenal. It is not the same game. (A new BattleTech game is under development now but it is not live yet so I can't speak to how it will compare to the original).

Other games being cancelled are Aliens Online, Godzilla (ok that wasn't ever really much of a game in the first place), and Stellar Emperor (the descendent of Megawars, one of the first Kesmai games on CIS). There could be more but there isn't exactly a list. I had to go to the game page of each game to see if there was a death notice. How depressing.

Somehow it just doesn't seem like Kesmai anymore without I/LOK, Air Warrior (as it was), and BattleTech (as it was). Of course its not Kesmai anymore -- it's EA.COM. Our boys with the big bucks and they intend to keep it that way. Oh well... R.I.P.

*Taken from Raph Koster's timeline.
