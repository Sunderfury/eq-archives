ALL THINGS TO ALL PEOPLE
Saturday, June 24, 2000 - 9:14 PM [Filed by myschyf]

Yes its alright now
I've learned my lesson well
You see you can't please everyone
so you've got to please yourself
--Rick Nelson

Temporarily cancel
all available light
Place the index on nerve and
stroke until it goes soft
Don't lose hope
Don't lose hope
--Martha and the Muffins

I wanna be sedated
--The Ramones


I'm calmer now. Not sedated but no need to be, unlike last night where sedation was probably the better part of valor. I no longer feel the need to out-shrew Kate and screech at the top of my lungs about how unfair it all is. Its these small things in life that make me happy.

I've been ruminating on the horror show of last night. Reflecting on how I'll leave this game and die of very old age before I'll ever get that tower. Oh well. Life (and UO) is unfair to some. On the flip side my account is now worth about a grand on ebay. So rather than hitting that cancel button I'll be depositing a money order into the bank. Somehow I think I'm getting the better part of this deal. Before you ask -- no. I have no moral qualms whatsoever about selling my account with characters on five shards including Seige and a house on every shard I play on to the highest bidder. I can see the ad now "includes the famed Myschyf character!" (Do I have delusions of granduer or what?) Prostitution -- UO style.

Hindsight is a wonderful thing and reveals much. It occurs to me in thinking about this that last night's fiasco is in many ways the ultimate culmination of OSI trying to please everyone. Somewhere, spread out all over the city of Austin, TX, there are members of the many development teams walking around alternately screaming and muttering "PLEASE JUST STOP BITCHING AND WHINING!! WE'LL DO WHATEVER YOU WANT! JUST SHUT UP -- PLEASE! WE CAN'T TAKE IT ANYMORE." People throw dollar bills and quarters at these guys and think they are drunks or wonder why they didn't take their Prozac that morning. I envision a picture of Raph Koster or Runesabre or Sunsword sitting on a street corner holding a sign: "Will work for silence."

The various teams really have bent over backwards to try to please what they saw as a vocal majority while keeping everyone happy. They've failed miserably of course -- that's one of the stupidest thing I've ever heard any company try to do. I blame lack of cohesive vision. Not Vision(tm) but a vision. A strong, running theme where somebody somewhere said "we want this game to be a, b, and c." Instead they have tried to cater to everyone from A to Z and explored other, non-Roman alphabets besides. The result? The mess we saw last night.

You see it wasn't just the log-in servers, although that certainly was a large part of it. It wasn't just the shard instability, although that was a large part of it as well. It wasn't just that everyone and their mother and all members of their extended families have enough gold to buy 15 castle deeds each, although that, too, was a large part of it. Nor is it that many people (like myself) have multiple accounts and placed, in some cases, as many as 10-20 houses last night. I shit you not -- it happened. It's all of these things and more. Its the ease of the game now. Its the ease of building a character. Its that the game is designed to reward hoarding and houses make it much easier to do that. Its that houses are now status symbols. Its even that PKs have stat loss -- because while the team is busy trying to figure out ways to curb PKing that don't suck (hello Adrick and Evocare) they aren't trying to figure out how to get a house for everyone -- not that that is a stated goal of the game because, as I've been trying to say in a very long-winded way, the game doesn't have any stated goals.

Its just sort of been blown any old which way the wind blows. Take care of pks here. Put in more housing there. Add a new land way over yonder. Quests and in-game fiction are good, lets try to add that to the game. Making it easier to start a character sounds great, I'm sure we can do that. Any time enough people have bitched loudly the game got changed. I suppose that's good in one sense. I mean companies should try to please their customers. Its just that I'm not sure a company has ever been faced with so many customers that demanded it change its product every time they got a stick up their butt about something. Do the customers even know what they want?

I mean think about it -- I know exactly what to expect from EQ and, to a lesser extent, AC. The makers of Shadowbane have a stated purpose -- if you go to them and tell them you don't like PVP and you want a house in a land where you won't be killed they'll laugh at you and tell you to go play UO. If you go to EQ and tell those folks you want to play in a game rife with player vs. player combat, Abashi will tell you that that isn't part of the Vision(tm) and get a GM to ban you for exploiting. I know pretty much what to expect from AO. But when you go to OSI and say there's too many PKs, they institute stat loss. If you tell OSI there's not enough housing, they build Trammel. If you say there's not enough fiction, they get Minax to attack Trinsic. They've really tried very hard to accommodate us -- all of us, actually. Perhaps excluding the 'no stat loss, bring back precasting biotch' crowd.

So what do we expect from UO? Housing? Phat lewt? Easy-to-kill monsters? In-game quests and fiction? Roleplay? PVP? What kind of game is this anyway? And anytime any one of the various things UO has to offer doesn't please me, then I (and thousands of others) get on the various boards and rant sites and demand that it be changed. Here's the kicker -- most times it is, with varying degrees of success. Good grief no wonder this game is a mess!

If the goal of this game is to provide housing for everyone or even a large majority, then that can be done and done well. Other things will suffer but you will get that tower. If the goal of this game is to provide an exciting pvp arena, that can be done and done well. Housing might suffer but stat loss will be a thing of the past and factions would be done right. If the goal of the game is to do in-game fiction, provide interactive NPCs and exciting quests, then that, too, can be done and done well. But you won't have exciting PVP. If the goal of the game is to kill things, get more powerful, get more stuff, so you can kill bigger things (sort of like EQ) then that can be done well. But forget PVP. However in a game that tries to provide it all, to be all things to all people, nothing gets done well. Not PKing, not pvp, not quests, not in-game fiction, not AI, and certainly not housing. Michael Jordan trying his hand at baseball comes immediately to mind here when looking for an analogy.

I know it sounds that I am, in an off-hand way, trying to excuse OSI for what happened with housing and that isn't the case at all. It was just that in looking at it I found in it an interesting statement on the three years I've played this particular games and perhaps a lesson for development teams of MMORPGs.

To close up with yet another musical quote, what a long strange trip its been, eh?
