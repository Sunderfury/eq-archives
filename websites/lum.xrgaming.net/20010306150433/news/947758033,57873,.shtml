Somewhere in Austin, an e-mail administrator weeps.....
Thursday, January 13, 2000 - 3:07 AM [Filed by great bob]

You people must be slacking. Obviously sunsword doesn't get mailbombed enough with free pictures of gay animal sex or something. I say that because he's asked for input about a proposed party system. I can only hope that he has a decent filter system on his e-mail client, because god knows what actually useful e-mail he might miss the deluge of posts screaming "who cares about the party system bitch! Get rid of stat loss!". No matter, here at Lumcorp we pride ourselves on our inability to keep our big yaps shut, so here's his message with obligatory commentary and editorial nonsense at the end.
There has been a feature missing from UO since before it was launched. I remember that as a beta tester one of the first things I wanted to see added to the game was a party system. I wanted something that let me easily identify with other players, communicate with them and coordinate with them. I wanted something that didn’t require the kind of commitment associated with a guild. I wanted a Party System.

"And now that the reign of Koster lies in ruins I shall gain all I desired! The fools at the University called me mad when I wanted a party system! But I'll show them! I'll show them all!"

I wanted to be able to talk to any of my friends when they were in the game, at any time, without task switching. I wanted to be able to see where they were, anywhere in the world, so I could FIND them and meet up. I wanted my friends to be able to get my equipment off of my corpse without taking a reputation hit. I wanted to be able to cast spells on them easily, without mis-targetting. I wanted to be able to enter a dungeon with six of my buddies and work together as a team to clear the monsters out.

Monsters? Oh yes, those obstacles that get in the way of the slaughter fests we call dungeons. Allready I have to wonder if he means the same thing by "party system" that the rest of us mean when we say that. You see, we already have a communication system. It's called ICQ. We already have a way to find the location of fellow players. It's called UOAutomap. Does anyone but me remember the last time they tried to replace ICQ? They spent months devloping that UO Chat program that gets less use than a "Frankie Goes to Hollywood" album. Or perhaps we can go for a more recent example of those bizzare "greeting cards" that everyone had to disable due to numerous crashing incidents.

What's needed in a party system is far more simple then he makes it out to be. We need to be able to retrieve our dead friends goods without "JoE NoTo" jumping out of the woodwork. We need to be able to share experience points. A way to share loot might also be nice. It needs to be quick, easy to use, simple to get in and out of and to not crash our system. The last might seem obvious to you and me, but sometimes I wonder about the water supply in the office building. I'm just saying is all.....

I understand the urge to build new features into a project. I've been guilty of it myself in the past. Let us not, however, lose our focus about what the party system should be. There is no need to reinvent the wheel. If the dev team has that much free time, let them work on combat balancing, or perhaps even Necromancy. Trust me, there is plenty of things for you people to be doing besides yet another failed attempt at cross shard communication.

One thing that cannot be doubted is that a party system is a priority. With the development team dedicated into splitting the lands between "Act like an idiot without fear of being smacked" and "The days of the Dread Lord" one can close a number of future loopholes with a simple party system.

Let’s get this party going!

If somebody brings the hookers, I'll bring the beer.
