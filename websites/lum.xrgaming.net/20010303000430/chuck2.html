When I was Chuck's age, my parents warned me of the evils of "Kiss" and the horrible consequences of playing Dungeons and Dragons. They showed me a Tom Hanks movie about a kid who almost kills himself because the "Demon Game" got him messed up in the head. I saw pamphlets and books written by Pat Robertson and Jerry Falwell warning me, "Play Dungeons and Dragons? Go to Hell. Do not pass go, do not collect a harp or a halo."

Eventually, and it took almost 9 years, the media decided that the horse was dead. Instead, they began to froth over something called "Backwards Masking" where John Lennon sang, "Turn me on dead man." This too lasted about 9 years, until a backwards Mr. Ed the talking horse was heard to say, "I sing my song for Satan." Strangely enough, the "backwards masking" craze faded away.

Then it was Magic: The Gathering. Then it was...well, the list could just go on all day.

So mark your calendars. In about ten weeks, we'll hear all about the evil addictive nature of computer games. Games like Ultima Online are destroying our marriages, our families, our careers, our childhood! Destruction everywhere, because of the foul and evil Ultima Online. Now just for fun, replace "Ultima Online" in the above statements with "Dungeons and Dragons". oooooooh. Boy, that sure sounds familiar. Then replace it again with the phrase "Rock and Roll Music". Hrmmmmm. Again, that rings so familiar in the back of my mind.

When will people stop blaming other things for the mistakes they have made in life? When will we stop the molly-coddling irresponsibility? When will we begin to hold people accountable for their own actions and decisions? Based on what I've seen in my life, the blame placed on Rock Music, Dungeons and Dragons, and now Ultima Online; the answer to all these questions is, sadly; never never ever.

What everyone knows, but is afraid to admit out loud for fear of rejection and loss of potential TV airtime, is that the problem is not Rock Music, Dungeons and Dragons, or Ultima Online. The problem is with people who don't have the ability to control themselves. Do you have homework to do? Then do it! Do you have a project due at the office? Then do it! Do you have a family to take care of and support? THEN DO IT!

But stop blaming other things. Stop blaming an inanimate disk of plastic and vinyl. Stop blaming plastic dice and sheets of graph paper. Stop blaming a computer program that sits on 600 megs of your C: drive. If the first step to "recovery" is realization of a problem, then the second step has to be the realization that YOU are the problem. If anyone feels they are spending too much time playing Ultima Online, by all means do something else with your life! I personally recommend Rock Music and playing Dungeons and Dragons.

Definitely stay far far away from that Magic game - Magic bad. Bad bad Magic.

UO is not a drug. It isn't like heroin or cocaine or nicotine or even caffiene. There are no physical consequences if you get up and walk away. It is not a sickness. You won't die from it, it doesn't make you commit crimes (well that's arguable in the realm of hacking but I doubt anyone would commit murder because of UO), it is nothing a drug will be developed for. Playing too much may be a SYMPTOM of something larger, something going on with you and you alone (although many people may also have a similar problem) but it is not the cause. It didn't 'do it to you.' You did it to yourself.

Escapism isn't new to the world. People have been trying to escape from the doldrums of their everyday lives since a lot of the world became rich enough to afford leisure time -- and even, really, before then. Books, movies, TV, music, sports, religion, drugs, politics -- for every person there's a different type of escapism -- you could even call it a different type of addiction. The workaholic is addicted to his or her job -- but no one blames it on the job. We all know its the person, not the vehicle. Same with alcohol. Same with sports. Same with everything. More often than not when you get the person to give up one thing that person slides into the same behavior with another thing. The workaholic decides not to work so much and to spend time with his son. Next thing you know he's coaching little league and putting 24-7 type of energy into that. The list goes on. No escapism isn't new -- what is new is blaming the vehicle instead of the person.

I know that you have probably received about 100 different messages about the recent issue of Ultima Online Addiction, so I thought I should add in my own thoughts to take that to 101, just because I like to be awkward. I've been playing UO on and off since November 1997, and EverQuest for a month or two somewhere in that time. Before that, I was an avid Meridian 59 player, so I guess that I have been playing the ORPGs since about January '97. As I'm only 18 (would have started at around 15 years old) I had no experience in pen and paper RPG'ing - only in the computer variety.

One of the main differences about this story is that you all pay around $10 a month to subscribe to this addiction and keep getting your fix, whether it is in the shape of UO, EQ, AC or M59 (or ME in the future). Over in Europe, or in the UK where I am, we have the extra cost of the telephone bills to add on top of our outgoings. Connecting to the internet is charged by the minute due to it using the telephone lines. $10 a month is absolutely nothing, when many Europeans have phonebills around or in excess of $200 per month - the vast majority just for playing on UO. This makes this ORPG addiction even more costly and much more of a desperate situation. You would be hard pushed to find a European who macroed regularly, unless they were amazingly wealthy or incredibly stupid.

I have been addicted to UO, and then kicked the habit, and then picked it up again. I've been close to completely cancelling my internet access before just to stop me from playing these games and costing more money as the temptation to install UO and just reactivate the account has been too much in the past. It may sound pathetic and ignorant to many people, but I'm sure everyone in Europe will understand exactly what I'm getting at with this. When you play, you dont think about the money that it is costing on the phone bill - that is pushed to the back of your head and you only think about it when you check the phonebill and see the huge amounts that have mounted up.

So, if it costs so much money - then why bother playing? Without these games, what is left of the internet that is entertaining for such lengths of time? You can read newsgroups, post on a few message boards - but where does it get you in the end? You never get the enjoyment, satisfaction and sense of fulfillment that you can get from playing games and actually doing something worthwhile that actively involves other people. After a while, all webpages start to look the same and the whole novelty wears off (even hamsterdance). But with the online games, there is always something that you have to keep going back for. So why bother using the internet at all? Well, every one of your readers is guilty of that.

The situation will only get more serious for some people, and I predict that the online game will cause huge problems for many people - and families. With the new consoles coming out, internet gaming will be even more available for the most PC-illiterate of people. The popularity of the internet is always growing with thousands more people getting online every week. The steady release of ORPGs and standard games with multiplayer support will encourage people to play, and subsequently get addicted. Once you have started something with a game, it is very hard just to drop it and say 'no more'. Adults and children alike will start to play more, and run up huge bills due to this addiction, potentially causing financial problems within families. But once you have started, where do you get off?

The future will bring more cases of this addiction, and until the rest of the world catches up with the free internet access, alot of people are going to have alot of problems. I've done my fair share of things in Ultima, running a successful RP'ing group of Elves on Catskills, helping set up various player run towns, running a website dedicated to the OSI Volunteer Programme, being GM of 2 player run shards... and only now is it all starting to finally dawn on just how pointless it all is in the end. People who have the problem really should look into the future - in 2 or 3 years time, what are you going to have gained out of this all? It is very likely that you wont have anything to show for it, apart from a few entries on your credit card bill from OSI, sore eyes, a larger stomach and superb understanding of everything to do with UO ever. Maybe then you will look back and see how much time you wasted on something that gave you nothing but a few hours of 'getting-away-from-it-all' an evening.

And a final piece of advice for those who think they are having trouble getting away from it all - your life is your own responsibility. No one else is going to force you to stop and pull the plug out. If you want to do it - just pluck up the courage, give your non-existant items away, delete your characters and cancel your account. Without your characters there, the urge to reactivate the account is much less. If you are happy as you are and dont care about getting involved in an online world, then you can ignore everything you just read :)

-- Razi
