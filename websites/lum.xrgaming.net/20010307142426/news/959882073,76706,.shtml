VERANT ACQUIRED BY SONY
Thursday, June 1, 2000 - 10:54 AM [Filed by lum]

You saw Myschyf's squib from the Standard earlier, now here's some relevant parts from the actual press release.

Verant, a San Diego-based development studio that creates and markets cutting-edge online games, will be merged into Sony Online Entertainment (SOE) as part of SOE's effort to concentrate on becoming the premiere attraction for interactive gaming enthusiasts from its new San Diego headquarters.

Verant President John Smedley will remain with SOE as executive vice president and chief operating officer.

Kelly Flock [President and CEO of SOE] will supervise the development of engaging new interactive games by SOE and Verant, and will oversee SOE's award-winning Website, the Station@sony.com, which features EverQuest, the top selling online PC game title, and such popular attractions for non-paying casual users as Wheel of Fortune, JEOPARDY!. and Trivial Pursuit Online.

The entire press release is available online here.

Previously Verant was published by Sony; the acquisition is similar to Eidos coming in and taking over Ion Storm. The rumors floating around up to today make the Verant/Ion Storm analogies more numerous; although no one is accusing Brad McQuaid yet of dating one of his level designers, industry buzz has the company a victim of its own success, with teams overwhelmed with work and under pressure to produce multiple games on tight schedules.

If we hear anything else we'll keep you posted.
