Given everything we've already detailed, here is a quick and dirty analysis of the state of the art in MMRPGs. This, for better and for worse, is the hand we are currently dealt.

GAMES CURRENTLY IN PRODUCTION

Everquest

GOOD:

  • Rich, detailed, immersive environment.
    • 3D engine allows for some truly spectacular visual effects..
    • A complete backstory has been written for the game, that is reflected in many of the NPCs and their interaction with the world.
  • PK switch in place.
    • Players can play the game without fear of attack by other players.
  • Accessibility to occasional players
    • The simpilicity of the level-up experience system allows players to see progress in their character even if only playing 1-2 hours a week.
  • Addictive in its simplicity
    • Everquest reduces the RPG experience to its simplest core of advance-acquire-explore. Players always have a goal that they are striving for, be it better equipment or new spells/skills that come with greater experience.

BAD:

  • No real persistence in the game environment.
    • Players have no way of leaving a unique mark on their world.
    • The game feels canned; akin to a Disney amusement park ride where the animatrons move back and forth regardless of the world around them.
    • With no real persistence the player's experience is centered around the player him/herself; with no way to affect the world, the player feels no investment in the world, but only in his/her equipment and skill.
  • Game developers have little to no communication with the Everquest player community.
    • Events such as the aborted Racewar server launch and the pottery speculation profiteering show that no one at Verant is monitoring even the most basic barometers of player concerns/interests.
    • Classes are often "nerfed"; spells are changed and skills are added/subtracted with no notice to the player community as Verant strives to balance the game post-release.
  • In game economy is nonexistent
    • "Trade skills" were implemented, but they have one by one been made completely useless and unprofitable.
      • The long-promised "complete Trade Skills revamp" which was promised after much complaining about the tedium of developing trade skills by users after release was eventually revealed to consist of creating 5 arrows at a time instead of one.
      • Verant's habit of announcing the gutting of a trade skills' profitability as "enhancing the skill" sign of either black humor or total cluelessness.
      • Trade skills are completely undocumented beyond a few vague in-game pages of text that must be purchased.
        • As a result, thanks to Verant's shoddy programming, it is impossible to determine whether or not a Trade "recipe" is broken from bad programming or merely completely undocumented.
          • For example, all but two of the Tinkering "recipes" have been determined to be non-functional. This has been confirmed by a GM as "playbalancing". No announcement of this has ever been made, in-game or otherwise.
    • The only trade skills that are worth developing are those that create goods useable for slaying monsters and improving ones character, namely Blacksmithy and Jewelcrafting.
      • As a result, there is a tremendous glut of Blacksmiths and Jewelcrafters on many servers and thus profit potential has been lost even with those two skills.
    • Since items never decay or are destroyed, and NPCs purchase items far below market value, items never leave the game world. Thus item value deflation inevitably follows.
      • On older game servers, the combination of the destruction of the Blacksmithy market and the glut of bronze armor has made it impossible for new players to find player-made banded armor. Bronze armor, supposedly the next step after banded, is now as cheap as banded once was.
  • Game programming is of poor quality
    • Everquest interface easily one of the most horrid and amateurish ever seen in a professional software release, on a par with one-man shareware offerings
    • Everquest 3D engine a resource hog, especially compared to other 3D engines in the industry.
    • Game-stopping bugs were present in release and many, such as zone "holes" which allow players to fall outside of the world, continue to be present.
    • Little thought was given to balancing the available classes; classes continue to be radically redesigned months after game release
      • Many classes simply are not viable for long term play.
        • The Rogue class has inferior melee skills, no magic skills, and the few specialized skills that they have are useless in actual gameplay. The Make/Apply Poison set of skills, the main high level Rogue skill, were not even implemented until months after release, and when implemented proved to be little more use than a simple magic spell.
        • The Warrior class until recently was not a viable class; melee skills were identical to those of hybrid classes (Shadowknight/Ranger/Paladin) with none of the hybrid's benefits. The Warrior class was recently patched with more hit points and a new Critical Hit skill; I am currently testing a Warrior to see if this has improved.
      • Many classes are vastly overpowered.
        • The Cleric class has the best healing spells in the game, the best direct damage vs. undead in the game, good buffs, good direct damage, and can wear any armor. The only weakness is that they cannot use slashing weapons.
        • The Druid class has good healing, direct damage, damage shield and buffs, and can cast the coveted Spirit of Wolf mobility spell. The only weakness is that they cannot use many weapons and can only use leather armor.
    • The game world itself is unfinished
      • The continent of Odus, home of the Erudites, was shipped without two zones, The Hole (a dungeon) and Paineel (a necromancer-only city). These zones were obviously meant to be included as entrances to them exist; and were probably the victim of pressure to ship the game.
        • Without the city of Paineel, and the sewer entrance to Qeynos not functional due to a bug, it is impossible for an Erudite Necromancer to advance beyond the Erudin newbie zones.
      • The alternate planes of existence, which were advertised as a main draw of the game and reason for Wizards to achieve high levels, were not implemented.
        • The first plane, the Plane of Fear, was recently implemented. The continuing design changes indicate clearly that no development work was done prior to release
  • Player vs. Player combat (PvP) in Everquest is broken
    • More development work seems to have been done to shield players from PvP participants then the mechanics of PvP itself.
    • Due to the overwhelming disparity in equipment between melee classes and magic classes, melee classes are at a serious disadvantage in PvP since their equipment can be looted, whereas a mage's spells cannot be.
    • Due to the mechanics of the game, "zone-killing", where a player/group of players wait at the entrance to a zone and kill players entering the zone while they are loading the zone and before the game allows them to take any action, is a popular exploit.
    • Little thought of class balancing and skill usage has been made by the design team as to how they would be applied in a PvP environment.
  • GM support erratic
    • Only one GM per server, and perhaps 2-3 guides online at peak hours
    • Offpeak there is no GM support at all
    • Although enforcement of game rules was strong and firm the first month after release, enforcement of interpersonal support issues (harrassment, kill-stealing, etc) has noticeably declined dramatically.
      • Some zones (such as Greater Faydark) are extremely unpleasant to be in now that problem players know that they are basically immune from punishment for harassment

Ultima Online

GOOD:

  • UO the first game to market and the game with the greatest market penetration
    • Well developed player community
    • Game client runs on older machines well and has been the subject of two years of debugging
  • Game world allows complete freedom of action
  • Counselor support program ensures that new users will recieve personal tutoring in game mechanics when necessary
  • PvP rules mechanics have been subject to intense refinement and study
  • Game economy robust and for the large part player driven, with a flow of items into and out of the economy
  • Comparatively light resource usage of UO client and tolerance of multitasking allows for "power users" to simultaneously run other utilities such as powerful mapping clients and IRC programs to facilitate ingame communication.
  • Developers maintain a constant and thorough line of communication with the player community.

BAD:

  • Game system allows player victimization ("random PKing")
    • A game-killing problem in the first year of UO's release, this has been significantly reduced by changes in game mechanics
  • Game has a history of poor programming
    • Previous exploits have allowed some players to achieve unbelievable stockpiles of wealth, thus affecting game balance
  • GM support erratic
    • Usually only one GM per server, supported by an unknown number of senior counselors and counselors
    • Corruption within the support program well documented and poorly dealt with
    • Many GMs poorly trained, both in game mechanics and in dealing with players
  • Game mechanics tedious
    • Skill gain based on a curve generated by shard-wide player usage
      • This curve has been irretrievably corrupted by macroing of tedious tasks
    • Exponentional level of skill gain difficulty results in unbelievable difficulty in achieving "Grandmaster" status in many skills
    • Nature of skill gain results in the rewarding of "longterm" players who spend 8+ hours a day in game as opposed to more casual players
    • Mechanics of skill usage themselves often tedious

GAMES CURRENTLY IN DEVELOPMENT

Asheron's Call

GOOD:

  • Game is published by Microsoft
    • Microsoft is a very large corporation
      • Microsoft is your friend
        • Author hopes to work for Microsoft some day
          • Author is a worthless sellout
  • Very detailed skill/level system that merges the best features of EQ and UO
  • Game client/3D engine is of stellar quality; must be seen to be believed. Screenshots do NOT do it justice.
    • Game engine runs smoother at 1024x768 resolution than EQ's engine running at 640x480.

BAD:

  • No player economy
  • No player housing or banking
  • Game world is so large that players spend a lot of time wandering aimlessly with nothing to do

Neverwinter Nights

  • Developed by BioWare, based on an improved 3D version of the Baldur's Gate engine, designed to allow up to 64 people to participate simultaneously
  • Development tools will be released with the game so players can create own worlds

Sovereign

  • Developed by Verant, apparently a realtime persistent strategy game. Little is currently known.

Jane's World War

  • Developed by Origin, a persistent environment for military simulations similiar to Air Warrior.

Ultima Online 2

  • Developed by Origin, successor to Ultima Online
  • Will use 3D engine, although not the same engine as U9:A
  • Supposedly will allow users to import their UO1 characters
  • Little else is known

Privateer Online

  • Developed by Origin
  • Nothing about this title is known

Mythos Online

  • Domain name registered by Origin
  • Possible new non-Ultima related MMRPG that several ex-UO developers have been moved to
  • Nothing about this title is known
