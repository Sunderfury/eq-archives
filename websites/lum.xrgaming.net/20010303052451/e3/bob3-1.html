E3-2K: Whan that may with his shoures soote
Great Bob does E3, part one

Chaucer never would have written about this kind of crap. It's one thing to write about pilgrims on a holy march, but what about geeks going to a gaming convention? The epic poem could take place in an airport terminal. Instead of a tale of the knight, we can have the tale of the Unix administrator. Instead of the nun, we can have a tale told by a game booth girl. That's why I'm really going to E3, don't you know. I'm a journalist, and this is where the story is. By finding out why people go to E3, I hope to write the pivotal work of English literature of the 21st century. This is a noble calling.

Who the hell am I kidding? I'm going because I have crossed the threshold from geek to uber-geek. I can claim that I'm a journalist all I want, but when I look at my face in the mirror, I have to live with the fundamental fact that I'm traveling 1500 miles for a geek convention about computer games. I was planing on going long before I got this gig writing for Lum's. Somebody once said "when ignorance is bliss, 'tis folly to be wise". Quite so. In that spirit, I wish to present a list of excuses for going to E3:

It's a good place to network with the game industry:

Yeah, that sounds good. I want to work for a game company. I want game companies to know that they can hire me for cheap. I also want them to know that if they hire me, they can also have my journalistic integrity. Sick of being viciously made fun of on Lum's? Give me a nice office somewhere and some vague job description and I'll sell your product like a Mexican pimp. You hear me, Nintendo? I think Pokeman:Online is the best fucking game ever. How does that sound to you? You've been told that you can't buy good press. We both know that's not true. Oh sure, some would consider me a sell out, but there's much more pride involved in calling myself a corporate whore than admitting the truth that I'm actually excited about going to a gaming convention.

It's payback time:

The vicious bastards in the game industry have sucked twenty years out of my life. I think of all the countless hours I've spent in front of my computer, and I weep like a little girl. From Ultima on the Apple II, to Pirates on the Atari 800xl, to countless games of Civ and Wing Commander and finally Ultima Online, I've wasted my time on earth. I couldn't help it. Because of people like Will Wright, Sid, Chris Roberts and Raph Koster making such good frickin' games, I never stood a chance. Well, now it's payback time. They're all going to be there. Savage beatings for everyone...except that California has some crazy law where you're not allowed to get medieval on people's asses, so I guess that excuse doesn't work either.

It's my job to cover the story:

It's not my job. If it was my job, I would be getting bigger paychecks. Let's face it, I write about a very small segment of the gaming industry. Sure, I'll be able to see the booths by OSI, Verant, Turbine and G.O.D. but that's a small part of the convention. It's not like I couldn't cover this event from my own home instead. There will be so many damn game sites there, one more doesn't matter. That's not a good enough excuse either. You know the real reason? Booze and games. That's it. I want demo disks and posters, I want to play new games and drink heavily. I expect to be entertained by the suits in the booths, and they better do a damn good job of it. I want to be distracted. I want to be thrilled shocked and amazed and kept to busy to understand the reality of the situation. I want to walk out of the convention with a plastic bag full of freebies.

My wife shakes her head sadly when I excitedly tell her of my plans. She knows what's really going on.

I'm a game geek and I'm going to E3.

I don't want your pity, only your understanding.

Chaucer just wrote Canterbury Tales to cover up the fact that he was excited about going to a convention. I'm sure of it.
