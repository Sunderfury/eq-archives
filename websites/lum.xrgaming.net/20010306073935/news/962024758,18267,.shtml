YahooNews: Full Coverage
If UO was a shampoo, the instructions would be: "Release. Crash. Revert. Repeat."
Monday, June 26, 2000 - 6:05 AM [Filed by arcadian del sol]

Atlantic reverted for a second time, having once more attempted to reach that unreachable star called Housing Phase II. Following this as well as problems on a few other shards, Trammel housing retired to the coffin in the basement, still unable to exist in the light of the sun. OSI, via Tyrant, confessed that they were a bit surprised at the overwhelming response by the players to this new era in virtual real estate. Certainly this means interest rates are bound to rise in Sosaria this year. The phrase "slightly out of touch" comes to mind.

"Come break us," Tyrant invites. However, this time he wants us to break the test center. We did a real good job of breaking all the production shards this weekend, so now it's good ole TC's turn. This raises some thoughts in my brain. Get everyone into the storm cellar.

Test Center isn't working.
I don't mean that the shard is unstable or that the support is sub-standard. What I mean is that the purpose for having this server be a test shard is to bust the seams on any new code that is introduced. In that respect, it is not doing the job. In a perfect world, bugs and issues would be discovered and corrected before any update hit a production shard. Production shards thus wouldn't crash. Unfortunately, this can never be a full proof plan, but then again, the system should be working FAR BETTER than it is right now. So why isn't it working? Continue on, intrepid readers.

"What's in it for me?"
Test Center does not get nearly enough testing. That is a given. Even the users who do play test center seem to be using it like it was Abyss. That is all well and good when you are testing things like combat balance and GM combat "power ups". But even in this scenario, war hammers still entered production like they were the Hammer of Thor and had to be mortalized. I think, and this is only my impression, that test center needs more actual testing - more serious Q and A stuff. The problem is that you are asking paying customers to do your product Q and A instead of using the service they are paying to use (Historical note: OSI used to have an internal QA staff. They were released after Ultima 9 escaped).(note: I've been informed that there is indeed an internal QA staff on the UO Live team. -Arc) So how do you change this? I have two ideas; one very complicated and management heavy, and the other really stupid. I leave it to you to determine which is which.

IDEA: VOLUNTEER QA PROGRAM
(modeled after the counselor program)
Works like this: You apply, you get hired, and you get x number of hours on specific shifts to test specific features on the test shard. Your bug findings are reported immediately and a GM shows up to see what happened and to take down the information. No emails. No Web Forms. Only QA team members access the test center in order to ensure the testing can be monitored and controlled. You fill out a weekly report like counselors do, and you submit copies of your saved UO logs. In exchange, you don't pay for your UO account.

IDEA: TCHOTCKIES FROM HEAVEN
In the UO Beta, stress tests were successful because events and items were created that were desirable to the testing populace. Lord British would make a public address to the people (Yes, this was a huge draw in the UO beta), and near the end of the beta an event was created where magic shards of a giant comet rained down on the land and players were out searching for them. Basically, an Easter Egg Hunt. Yes these events crashed the test servers and yes these events nuked the login server. That was the whole point of the events. So now that we are invited to take up offer of debugging the new code (we are, after all, paying for this privilege), how do you make this happen on a shard that doesn't get nearly enough participation as it is? Maybe you need to hold more Easter Egg Hunts. Maybe offering a newbified sash that read "I am a Test Center Biatch" for any of your production characters would interest players to give test center a login or two. Maybe having your spellbook dyed bright blue would be attractive to the player base. Certainly they won't have any imbalancing effect on the shards. And I bet if you saw someone at the bank with a blue spellbook and they said, "Oh I won this on the test center" - I just bet you might see people logging into the test center themselves.

Now I'm not saying both ideas are perfect, but I have to think they kick ass over the "release, crash, revert, repeat" program currently in use.
