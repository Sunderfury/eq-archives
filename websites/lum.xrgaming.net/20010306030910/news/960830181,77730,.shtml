THE MISEDUCATION OF LAURYN TWISTER
Monday, June 12, 2000 - 10:16 AM [Filed by lum]

Well, Dr. Twister has emerged, phoenix-like from the ashes, ditching spurious upper-case letters in his name and, well, getting all Vault-like.

Of particular interest? The site philosophy. It's, um, changed. A lot. Some of you may remember his old site philosophy, which I've helpfully archived. (I'm SO damned helpful some days.) Here's some pertinent quotes from each.

OLD PHILOSOPHY: OSI encourages us to role play. The more we role play, the better the world will be for everyone. GREAT! From now on, I will be role playing a cheater and exploiter. After all, I don't cheat and exploit others in real life.

NEW PHILOSOPHY: In the past, the Dr.Twister Network has used methods of bridging the gap between developer and player that were at best unrefined, at worst counterproductive. Developers and players alike had to adjust to what was revealed in that time. So too has this network. What we have learned should become much more apparent as time progresses.

OLD PHILOSOPHY: You must take a look at what we are all trying to do. Screw OSI, because they have screwed us. We are all in this together, and we are all looking for that next big bug. Simply, to make the game more enjoyable. If you have a bug, keep it to yourself. Exploit it, have fun with it, use it as a party trick. Next, share it with a friend, someone who can help you define it and perfect it. Share it with me.

NEW PHILOSOPHY: The industry can't survive on listening alone. The desire and need a developer has to communicate with the public is crucial to the success of their game. This network is a public forum to allow a company to express their plans and goals in hopes to generate a better understanding about their game, supply high levels of customer service and to involve the players in the constant growth of the games they play.

OLD PHILOSOPHY: So, will bugs, exploits, hacks and cheats ever stop in games? NO! Is this sad, does it ruin the game for everyone? I guess, but they will still always be there.

NEW PHILOSOPHY: (There is nothing about bugs, exploits, hacks or cheats in the new manifesto.)

OLD PHILOSOPHY: I guarantee that my philosophies are going to be different from some of yours. If this is the case, don't come back. I really could care less. This is a hobby, I don't make money off of it and most important, I enjoy doing it. So relax, and prepare for the newest concept in bug boards.

NEW PHILOSOPHY: Our goals are simple... Bridge the gap between developers and game players in order to understand the differences between what is being created for the player's entertainment, and what entertainment value the players get out of what is created.

You know, I may not be the most Christian of folks, but I am a firm believer in redemption. (The philisophical concept, not the bad Diablo clone.) You know, maybe Twister has changed and really wants to go legit. Ditch all that nagging baggage surrounding his history -- the game-shattering bugs, the lawsuits, the forums that scared even Jessica Mulligan -- and start new, for in the end, we're all new creatures in Jesus. Or something.

I don't know. The Twister network, last I checked, had one kick ass site, a few moribund sites, and some pretty nasty history to deal with. Yes, some developers looking for instant street cred are going to love to deal with Twister. But will the mainstream? I mean, Origin makes a habit of suing him. It's going to be hard to cover UO2. Turbine pretty much called him an outright liar during their last bughunt. I guess that leaves Verant, and I'm sure Raph Koster has a few things to say there.

So can Twister transcend all that? Again, I don't know. By his works you shall know him, I guess. As always, the MMORPG scene is more interesting than the actual MMORPGs.

And, yes, the site hosts of drtwister.com did buy lumthemad.com before I could get it, and yes, that is why you're looking at lumthemad.NET right now. And then they turned it over to crackednuts.net, which is www.xrgaming.net now. So for a while, if you went to lumthemad.com, you get xrgaming.net. And no one knew why. (Now it's just broken.) Sometimes web site politics gets even more arcane than game politics.

You know it's bad when Lum breaks out the Good Book
