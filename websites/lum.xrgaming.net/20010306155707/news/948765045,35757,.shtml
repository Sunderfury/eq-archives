MIXED NUTS, PART III: It is good to be missed
Monday, January 24, 2000 - 6:50 PM [Filed by myschyf]

Awww gee. I didn't know you folks cared. I'm touched. Really. I can feel the love.

Before I get started on what else is bad and wrong about OSI's relationship with its player-base (or the lack thereof), let me clear up a semi-popular misconception: I was not dissing the Counselors and GMs in my last update. When I dis you, you'll know it -- trust me on this one. This series -- the entire thing -- the whole enchilada -- is about OSI's policies as concern their player-base. So when I write about things I think Counselors and GMs can do or not I'm speaking of policies concerning them at a level that would possibly be Durga or maybe Tyrant - NOT policies that I think GMs or Counselors can institute. Ok enough of that -- if you're still pissed I don't care.

So what else is wrong with OSI's relationship with its player-base? Oh gee, I dunno. Maybe having one PR guy for 200K+ players? I speak of none other than our beloved Calandryll, who valiantly peruses the more moderated of boards (that would be COB and Stratics for those of you not in the know) in search of posts that he can reply "I'll have to speak with the development team and get back to you on that" to. Poor guy. I can't believe there's only one of him for the entire UO player-base. Maybe OSI pays him a bundle because of the masochistic nature of his job and the sadistic tendencies of the player-base who have no one else to take their frustrations out on and can't afford someone to help him with the workload. Then again that almost seems like a match made in heaven... or hell.. I'm not sure which. The next time Cal doesn't answer your email take a moment to remember that there is only 1 of him and 200K+ of us before you blast him into perdition for not getting back to you.

But this would be a poor update indeed if all I had to write about was OSI's failure to hire more than one PR guy for 200K+ customers (might still be a poor update -- oh well, bite me). I think the singular thing that most shocked me about OSI when I first started and continues to this day is that their relationship with their player-base is entirely customer driven. Think not? How many message boards does OSI host? How many luncheons were OSI's idea? Compare the number of player-run events on any shard to the number of those hosted by OSI. Oh yeah sure its nice to see Callandryl, Sunsword, Runesabre, Tyrant, Lady MOI, Durga and the rest of the crew post on Lum's Gimped message board, COB, Stratics, the BV boards, etc. But what came first -- the chicken or the egg? In this case it was us chickens. Hell Comments from the Team didn't even hit the OSI website until a few months ago. And to my knowledge they've never hosted any message boards for their player-base. Shame on them. That Stratics is completely player-run and player-compiled blows my mind. Surely the statistics found on Stratics were available from OSI? So how hard would it have been to have it on OSI's website where most folks, having just purchased the game and perhaps not having heard of Stratics just yet, would go?

Speaking of OSI's website, Leilo has done a damn good job on it. Just in time too. I took a trip over there so I could finish this series of rants with a couple of really long paragraphs about how much it sucked. Alas, I am too late. They fixed it. Damn them for taking some topnotch material away from me. For the first 2.5 years of UO's existence their website went far beyond normal suckage. It was hard to read, slow to load (well its still slow to load), hard to navigate, contained no useful information, had some conflicting information, had information about one topic stored in many different places.. I could go on for days. Since it might be days until I update again maybe I should go on for days so that you guys can get good and sick of me and you won't care if Lum butchered his updaters and hid our mutilated bodies under the sink. But I digress.

What more can I say? I think OSI has treated its player-base as an afterthought since the game began. They've come a long way in the last 2.5 years -- I'll give them that. But they have so much further to go. We SHOULD see the developers, Durga, Tyrant, Calandryll, Lady MOI and ESPECIALLY Lord British and Starr Long (aka Lord Blackthorn) in the game occassionally. If they get mobbed its only for a short while -- they can put up with it. Would once a month be too much to ask? Obviously it would.

OSI should be reaching out to their player-base -- not the other way around. They SHOULD be hosting message boards and moderating them as well. Sure its a tough job but it comes with the territory. It should not be up to the players to find ways of communicating with game staff. OSI should host luncheons and not leave it to the players to beg for a few staff members to join them here or there. In other words -- the relationship should NOT be completely customer-driven.

I expect that an online gaming company would reach out to its player-base. I expect that the game staff would take the initiative. I expect to see game staff in the game, walking around, seeing what people are doing and meeting and greeting the players. I expect Counselors and GMs to be more than troubleshooters. I expect to receive more than a form letter when I write OSI. And so should you. So should all of you. That OSI has not done this in the past is not a good reason not to expect it and not to demand it and not to continue to expect and demand it. Any online gaming company should be expected to provide this level of customer service and to be that involved in the game staff/company/player-base relationship. Maybe its that OSI is used to providing merely a product -- a game in a box that you played until you 'won' and then you put it on the shelf or repeated the process until you were bored. Well this is not merely a product but both a product and a service. Maybe OSI just doesn't care. Personally I'll give them the benefit of the doubt and say that they do care and just haven't reached the point where they can begin to meet my expectations. But make no mistake about it -- without us, the player-base, UO is nothing. Without us the game is an empty shell. Without us there is no economy. Without us there is no PVP vs PK vs PVM vs RP discussion. Without us there are no RP events. Without us there is no game, no OSI, no paychecks for their employees. We, the player-base, should be game staff's best fucking friends. And if we are not then OSI needs to ask themselves why and take whatever steps are necessary to rectify that situation. Customer service and the relationship between game staff and the player-base should be OSI's number one priority.

By the way, I don't think OSI is alone in this. EQ seems much worse -- I'm really glad I don't play that game. And AC well... all I can say is that Chris L'Etoile better hope I don't buy it and put him and AC's relationship with THEIR player-base under the same microscope I just put OSI's under. On the other hand maybe he would enjoy having two people from this site to bitch about.

Wanna bitch about my shitty update? I knew you did.
