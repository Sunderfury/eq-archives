LOST HORIZONS
Wednesday, June 28, 2000 - 2:53 PM [Filed by lum]

Horizons is an MMORPG in development by David Allen of Artifact Entertainment (formerly VB Designs). He has been very vocal about his distaste for publishers, probably because his previous product, Demise, was supposed to be published by Interplay and was bounced around like the Happy Fun Ball for years before finally seeing the light of day as a self-published game sold primarily over the Internet (that, for some odd reason, looks years out of date).

Horizons was a wildly ambitious game that Mr. Allen originally planned to also publish himself. Those two conditions don't go together awfully well, and Horizons was finally in talks with an unnamed publisher. That's one reason why you never saw too much coverage of Horizons here -- frankly, I wanted to see if it was going to get the backing necessary to meet the goals it set for itself.

Unfortunately, today we got the answer. From the official Horizons site comes this:

It is with both hope and regret that I inform the gaming community of the serious problem that has arisen in relation to the HORIZONS project. As you know, HORIZONS was supposed to be a privately funded project; the problem is that the funding has fallen through, and the project is now facing extinction.

We have been in talks with a publisher for close to two months now and have just been informed that it's going to take them even longer to make their 'decision', and the problem is that we cannot last during this amount of time (we have already been privately and personally funding HORIZONS for the past 9 months). At this time, we have a team of roughly 20 people; many of them industry professionals that are migrating from other companies to work on HORIZONS. This team will fall apart and be disbanded over the next few weeks unless something is done.

He then goes on to mention Horizon's ambitious goals (some of which haven't even been mentioned) and flat out pleads for help:

I am personally in debt for hundreds of thousands of dollars with Artifact Entertainment and trying to make this project fly. I have put everything that I have into this, including my house, lifestyle, and even more (I sold my NSX a few weeks ago just to cover base business costs). I have tried to maintain a positive mentality with this project, but I can only do so much, and relying on others who apparently don't take the project as serious as we do has been nothing but degrading to the entire project.

As far as I can tell, the only way that HORIZONS will be bailed out is if somebody acts quickly and works with us to make this amazing project a reality with the proper financial support (millions of dollars). We have the people, the talent, the design & concept, and even more importantly, the drive and ability to make this project a reality. We are only interested in working with those who will not waste our time and are interested in making things happen instead of talking about it.

If you or anyone you know is capable of helping AE out with the HORIZONS project, please contact me immediately at davida@artifact-entertainment.com. We are open to both private funding and publishing deals.

So if you have "millions of dollars" handy, shoot David an email. No golf club salesmen, please.

Sic Transit Gloria Mundi
