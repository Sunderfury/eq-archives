Wanderers of the Stoic is a family-style guild, and every member is treated as a brother or sister.  To be a member is to learn, to teach, to give and to gain, to win and to lose.  The focus of Wanderers of the Stoic is betterment as a whole throughout the guild.  This involves levels, equipment, or exploration of new zones.  All events, raids, and often times general groups are aimed at that purpose.  Grouping is done primarily with the guildmates, as this promotes familiarity with their personalities and play styles, although making new friends and grouping with others is still highly encouraged.  We do not believe in a reservation system, however we do try not to step on others' toes.  We are not part of any guild alliances, nor will we ever be.  That would detract from the interior focus of the guild.  As a whole, we try to be inviting and welcome others to come see what we are all about.  To learn more about us, visit our Roster, or our Message Forum.  Thank you for your interest in Wanderers of the Stoic!

General Creed and Policy

While wearing the Wanderers of the Stoic Guild tag, you represent the guild as a whole with all of your actions.  As such, you are expected to uphold high standards, and wear the Guild tag proudly.  Training, kill stealing, and other disreputable acts are frowned upon.

Use of /anon is not permitted, although /roleplay and plain mode are fine.  Use of /anon is hiding your Guild tag, which should be displayed proudly.

All played alts should be a member of the guild.  We do not support the idea of multi-guilding.

Guild chat is not regulated.   If you are asked to stop an offensive line of conversation, please do so.

The Guild's message board must be checked daily by all members, so that they may keep abreast of planned events and general news within the guild.

Do not give out the password to the message board in public forums.  If you need it, send a tell to any of the officers.

Officers have the ability to remove disruptive members of the guild, or those that fail to comply to standards.  Once removed by an officer, only that officer or the Guild Leader may overturn that decision.

Use of the Wanderers of the Stoic name to threaten, intimidate, or gain favor with others is not permitted.  Your actions and guild tag must speak for themselves.

Ninja Looting is a one way trip to guild removal.  Regardless the reason, stealing from another player is wrong and will be handled severely.

While on a raid, do not criticize the raid leader, tactics, or the participants in open channel.  This harms overall moral, and reflects badly upon yourself and the guild.  Use tells instead.

Officers and the Guild Leader take precedence when on a raid.  Failure to listen to their instructions are cause for removal from the raid.

Lying to the Guild Leader or a Guild Officer is cause for Guild removal.

As a Wanderer of the Stoic, you will be asked to spend a lot of time with your guildmates.  This frequently means delaying what you are doing, spending a long night, or leaving a camp.  While we do try to avoid these instances, Wanderers of the Stoic isn’t a guild for soloists.  We are a guild of teammates, and this familiarity allows us to excel.

In large raid situations, don't get upset if you do not get exp.  Most of us won’t...

Treat others with the respect you would like to receive.  This includes non-guild mates.

Playing of alts during a raid in which you are needed is severely frowned upon.  The intent of the guild is not any one person, but rather the betterment of the guild as a whole.  Failure to assist us when you are needed, is a step in the wrong direction.

Looting Policy

The Set Rule

If any member of a given raid needs only one piece to complete a set of armor, the item will be defaulted to them without a roll.  This includes Breastplates, Leggings, Epic Weapon components, etc.  To qualify for this condition you must have every single piece of the set…  not just the good pieces.

A Wanderers of the Stoic raid, with invites.

Non-guild members specifically invited to attend any function will be entitled to a roll on the item in question.  However, the rolls are weighted slightly, so that those who have been at said function more often, have a better chance at winning the item.  The numbers rolled will be set, and determined before the raid starts,  by the raid leader.  In most instances the number sets will be 300 – 1000 for those who have been to said area with us before, and 1 – 1000 for those who are newly going to the zone with us.  In the case of special zones, in which one or more groups is risking more then the others (i.e. Fear-breaking teams) their roll will be adjusted accordingly, not to exceed 400 – 1000.  The set rule is in effect under this looting condition.

A Wanderers of the Stoic raid, with no invites.

In a raid organized and led by the Wanderers of the Stoic, in which only guildmates attend, loot will be distributed on a strictly need vs. greed basis.  Those who would get the most use of the item in question as determined by the Guild Leader, Guild Officer, or Raid Leader (in that order), will be awarded the item.  In the case of which this cannot be determined, all applicable users who would use said item will be entitled to a roll.  This roll will not be weighted.  In the instance that no one person would use the item on a main, all present will be entitled to a roll, and the set rule is again in effect.  Items which are awarded in this manor may not be given away or traded without the agreement of the party that awarded the item to you, and it is preferred that the item(s) stay in the guild.

Special Policy concerning any item that greatly benefits the guild.

In the case of a rare or difficult to obtain item that greatly benefits the guild (Cleric Epic notes for example), the player that would benefit the guild most by obtaining the item (as deemed by the Guild Leader, Guild Officer or Raid Leader (In that order) will be awarded it, in most cases.  This is based heavily upon average playtime, flexibility of schedule, and in some cases: seniority.
