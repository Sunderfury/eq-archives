Everquest Strategy FAQ

Is this a message board?

    Yes and no.  It is anticipated that questions and back and forth discussions will continue to be posted in the General Everquest Forum rather than here.  This forum is for more substantial posts containing information you feel will help other players with the game.  As such, messages will not scroll off in the manner of a standard message board.  That does not mean that you can't comment on another post to correct misinformation or add additional information.  The rating system should help eliminate the less useful posts.

How do the forum filters work?

    You can adjust the forum settings to your personal preferences.  Simply make the changes and hit the change button.  If you also check save, the settings you choose will be your default settings each time you visit the forums.  The filters at the top of the forum do the following from left to right:

    a)  Flat vrs Threaded.  Choosing flat will bring up the full text of each primary message on the forum.  Choosing threaded will only bring up the subject lines.  The default is threaded.  Be warned that choosing flat will significantly increase the loading time for each page.

    b)  Auto Expand.  You may choose to automatically expand messages higher than a certain rating so that the full text of those messages appear while the rest of the messages only show the subject line.  The default is to never auto expand.  Be warned that the more messages you auto expand, the longer the loading time will be for each page.

    c)  Order.  You can change the order of the messages to show you the newest messages first, oldest messages first or highest rated messages first.  The default is to show the newest messages first.

    d)  Filter @.  This filter lets you eliminate from view any messages rated below the threshold you set.  Thus setting it at no 2 (below average) will cause those messages rated 1 (awful) to not appear on any forum you visit and setting it at 5 (excellent) will only show you those messages rated excellent.  The default is 2.

Should I rate a comment that is already rated at the proper level?

    Absolutely.  The ratings system relies upon many people adding their rating.  Even if the post is rated about where you think it belongs, be sure to still add your rating.  Why?  Because if someone else comes along and rates it at a significantly different level, it will change the number.  The more ratings a post gets, the more likely it is that it will rise or fall to, or stay put at, its proper level.

How do I determine where to rate a comment?

    It is up to you to decide how useful and informative a post is.  In general, these are the ratings:

    Rating 1.  Awful.  Flames and generally worthless comments.  A useless post.
    Rating 2.  Below Average.  Contains some general insight.  A few people may find it useful.
    Rating 3.  Average.  Contains some interesting information.  Some people would find it useful.
    Rating 4.  Good.  Well written and informative.  Many people would find this information useful
    Rating 5.  Excellent.  A post you think almost everyone should read.

Why are some comments not rated?

    To make sure it gets listed correctly, it takes at least 3 separate ratings on a coment before it gets assigned a rating.

Why should I register?

    Registered posters get their posts rated at a 3 right from the start.   In contrast, anonymous posts are initially rated at a level 2.   After 15 posts, registered users are assigned a karma rating based upon the average ratings of their posts.  (see below for how that works).   From then on, each post you make is automatically rated at your karma level. Plus, your name gets highlighted if you achieve and maintain a high enough karma. Because most users will want to read the higher rated posts first, building up a good karma rating will ensure that your posts are read more often.  Registration also applies to the rated forums on every rated forum on allakhazam.com, carrying over your karma to new games as they are added.

I'm already registered for the forum and the auction.  Do I need to register again?

    Unfortunately yes.  This system is separate from the ubb forums and the auction.

What is my Karma Rating?

    Your Karma rating is the average score your posts have been rated at by site visitors.  The rating comes from posts made under your registered name on any of the rated forums for any game covered by allakhazam.com.  You must have at least 15 posts before you receive a karma rating.  Then any future post you make will start out at your karma level.  Titles are assigned to your posts according to the following scale:

    Karma below 3 = user.  Posts are in dark blue.
    Karma between 3.01 and 3.65  = scholar.  Posts are in yellow.
    Karma between 3.66 and 4.34 = sage.  Posts are in green.
    Karma between 4.35 and 5 = guru.  Posts are in red

What type of information should I post?

    Post anything you feel may be useful to the other players.  There is no limit on the topic and length of your post, except that it should fit the category in which it is posted.  In general, the more inciteful and detailed your post, the more likely it is to get a high rating.  Also feel free to post follow-up comments on other posts.

How do I add things like bold and italics to my post?

    You need to use the standard ubb codes for this.  These are similar to html codes.  Below are the most common:

        [b]Bold[/b]
        [i]Italic[/i]
        [u]Underline[/i]
        [quote]will use the blockquote tag[/quote]
        [link]link.link.com[/link]
        [link=http://urlgoeshere]Label goes here[/link]
