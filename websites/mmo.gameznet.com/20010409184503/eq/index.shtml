Can Online Games Make Money?
 1:33 EST - jcsmith

A new article at Red Herring ponders the question of whether or not online games can make money. Not much of a question there but there is some good good information inside. You can read the full article by clicking here or get a sneak peek by glancing down...

Sony subsidiary Verant Interactive created Everquest, the most popular, massively multiplayer online game with about 330,000 users paying $9.89 a month in subscriptions. That generates about twice what Sony spends to administer the game, a nice little profit center that has encouraged it to develop several other online titles, including titles for consoles that can connect to the Internet. Sony expects to add one massively multiplayer game a year; upcoming are the Planetside sci-fi game and Star Wars Galaxies.

Don't forget about Sovereign or Infantry Online and the elusive(but surely destined to be some day) Everquest sequel.

New Feature at Allakhazam
 13:13 EST - jcsmith

I just got word of a new feature at Allakhazam's Magical Realm. Here's the real deal...

Now you can use the equipment database, with its over 5,500 Everquest items, and list the items you have for sale for each of your characters (for platinum pieces of course). This is a great way to sell off items without going through the hassle of /auctioning, and a great way for craftsmen to advertise their wares to the rest of their server. It is also an easy way to find that item you have always wanted but didn't want to spend the time waiting around hoping it would get auctioned. All of the items for sale can be searched either by server or right from the item description page. No auctions and no hassles. All you need to do is arrange through e-mails for the time and place to make the trade.

EQ Catering to Power Gamer?
 15:02 EST - jcsmith

Absor made a post today on the Everquest forums to respond to a player concerns that Everquest caters to high level or powergamers rather than new players. Here's what he had to say...

Actually, our most recent addition was designed specifically for the level of character that you are talking about. The Warrens is exactly as you describe, a whole zone for lower level characters. Whatever impression you are taking away from reading web sites is going to be based on what the owners of that site find interesting. For the most part there will always be more 'hype' about the high level game and items and all that. That's what seems to interest people. Those that have characters that are of the level that they can think about getting that stuff are interested in hearing about it. But so are those that are looking ahead to what they might be doing later in the game.

That doesn't mean that we are not adding content for the early parts of the game, as you can see with The Warrens.
