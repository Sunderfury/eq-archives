LithWorlds Interview
 12:55 EST - jcsmith

In a general piece of MMO news, Rick Lambright had a chat with Home Land Fed recently where he spilled the beans on LithWorlds. LithWorlds, as you may remember, is a licensable massive multiplayer engine under development from LithTech. Here's a piece of his mind...

HomeLAN - The engines of massively multiplayer games lag behind in some ways from the engines like the Unreal or Quake engines in terms of graphics. Do you believe that will always be the case or will LithWorlds have a look that is comparable to those engines?

Rick Lambright - There really is no reason why the graphics quality of massively multi-player games needs to play second fiddle to their traditional multiplayer - and especially FPS - counterparts. This is starting to become evident, especially when you look at sneak previews of upcoming MMP games like Star Wars Galaxies. One thing large-scale more network-oriented games have had working against them is a longer development cycle. Longer development cycles, and in the case of MMP games longer betas, have traditionally made it difficult - and expensive - for a game to still have cutting edge graphics by the time it is officially released. At LithTech the engine is constantly being updated for higher performance and enhancements are continuously added that allow it to take advantage of the latest graphics hardware. We believe strongly that with regard to graphics quality the expectations for MMP games will be - and should be - close to their non-MMP counterparts and it is our commitment to be able to provide that same high level of quality to all of our licensees. Additionally, licensing MMP technology from LithTech should help shorten the development cycle which gets those subscription dollars coming in sooner.

Sullon Zek, Continued
 5:17 EST - jcsmith

Absor answered a couple more questions on the Sullon Zek point system changes at the EQ Developer's Corner earlier today. Here's one...

Q: It says on the point system rules that the VICTIM must be over level 20 to get points, but it says nothing about whether the killer needs to be over level 20. If I am level 19 can I kill folks over 20 and gain points with no risk of giving them points if I lose?

A: Yes, it's all about the level of the person killed. A level 19 that kills a level 20 will indeed gain points and be able to collect an insignia.

Keep in mind, we may change all this again… We're still keeping the records and just adjusting the point values for actions. You all know that we're not going to award toasting folks much lower level than you are, so if you're worried about points, try to avoid it.
