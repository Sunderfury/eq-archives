The Knight is Darkest

Just Before Dawn One night after a long days hunt in the Oasis, I sit to recover from my wounds on one of the many stony pillars protruding from the ocean. It had been a very successful day for me, as I had learned much about my newly acquired skill of dual weilding, and had made quite a bit a profit in coins and collected loot. The hunt was far from over for me, however, as I was determined to gain enough experience so that I may finally group with some of my older friends who have always been one step ahead of me. This night, as are many such in the oasis, was crowded with nearly one hundred other hunters who wished to gain experience as well. Many of which were hunting the docile crocodiles and camiens which inhabited the area, I despise such doings, but alas, was powerless to stop them, for now. There were also others of higher rank who hunted the various sand giants that wander around the dunes. I prayed for the souls of these foolish ones who would risk their lives against a being that could crush me with only one blow.

I myself was hunting something that not many people hunt there, which were dune taratulas, fearsome spiders that wander the sands. They were great experience, and I could use their silks to tailor some very nice armors for the many monks and magicians that inhabited the area, which I could then use to fund my fletching. I had gotten bored searching out these spiders, as I had slain too many and there numbers had become scarce, forcing me to track the sands for miles before finding them. I went back to the beach are to look for a partner to help me hunt the various undead that roam the sands. There are powerful undead here indeed. In my search for the spiders I had caught the scent of many mummies, a few rotting ghouls, and some very fearsome desert skeletons known as dry bones. I dared not hunt these creatures alone, for it would be certain death.

After combing the beach for a few moments, I saw a well armored knight sitting on the pier, recovering from battle. He wielded an almost gigantic sword, and on his back laid a quiver of arrows on top of a magnificently crafted darkwood bow. I had never seen a bow like his before, even my trueshot paled in comparison to this bow's beautiful craftsmanship. It must have taken truly a master in fletching to make such a bow, for it was created with two cams! I though truly, with our combined strengths we could take down any beast that roams these sands. So, I approached the knight and greeted him.

His name was Silias and he proceeded to tell me that he was looking for a partner as well. We made the agreement that I would find monsters for us to slay, since my tracking easily allowed me to find any foe that I wish. I sniffed the air and began to look around for a trail, then I caught an odor most foul indeed. "Orcs", I said to myself. I harbored a deep hatred for these creatures, for when I was young, I had a brother not more than a year older than me who I adored greatly. One day, while in the trees of kelethin, we were playing with our toys bows, dreaming about how we would both be great ranger when we got older. Then, either by fate or accident, my brother fell from the trees and broke his leg, our mother was at the market buying supplies, and there was noone around to help him. Suddenly an orc centurion set upon him, and I watched in fear, crying my eyes out as the orc tore my brother'' body to pieces and consumed him wholly before my very eyes.

Wiping a stray tear from my eye, I snapped back to the present. The orc was in my visual range, as I started toward him, I could feel a mighty anger and rage overcome me. But my trainig had taught me better than to run into a fight in such ways. Instead, I nocked my best arrow, and sent the bladed steel rod singing through the air. The arrow hit the beast in his lower abdomen, yet he yanked it out as if it were a mere thorn. This seemed very odd to me, but I shrugged it off and began to run back to where Silias lay in waiting. When I arrived, Silias was ready, sword in hand, for battle. Before I could even turn around, the orc was suddenly upon me, I felt his axe sink into my shoulder, nearly rending it from my body. I was stunned, unable to lift my sword, as the orc continued to beat me from behind. Silias was striking the orc in the back repeatedly, but the orc seemed hell bent on ending my life and continued to pummel me into the sand. Barely able to move, I turned around to face my attacker, determined to at least strike him once before I died. Amidst the beatings, I saw Silias in the background, chanting a spell. I thanked the gods, for I assumed my companion must be a paladin and was going to use his god-given powers to heal me, and turn the orc upon himself. However, instead of the glowing blue light I had seen coming from the hands of my high elven paladin brothers back home, Silias's hands glowed a sickly green, and all of a sudden his undead pet sprung forth from the sands.

Then I thought I would surely die, for this human had decieved me and was helping the orc to destroy me. Much to my surprise however, Silias and his evil pet began to attack the orc. Silias's pet was almost immediatley destroyed and the orc was upon me again. As I lay bleeding to death, I looked up to the sky, ready to greet tunare. I looked at Silias one last time, to see that his anger had grown and that his eye's began to glow red. He jumped on the orc's back and placed his hand's upon its shoudlers. The orc screamed in pain as the very life was drained from it by Silias' dark god.

The orc's body fell limp to the ground, as the last bit of consiousness left me. I awakend the next day, barley alive, drenched in my own blood and the green filth of the orc's. I found strength in my legs as I staggered to get up. As I looked about me, all I saw were tracks in the sand and a bloody note left by Silias. Alas, it was written in dark speech, which I cannot decipher. Perhaps one day I will be able to read it. I still do not understand what happened that eve, I had always been told that the followers of Innoruk are pure killers, yet this one saved my life. I gathered up my belongings and started down the tracked sand, determined to find out why...

Rhone
Tunare
