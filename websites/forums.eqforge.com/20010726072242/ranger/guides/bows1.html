Bow Making

Beginning Bow Making:

The base range of the 5 different bow staves is as follows:

Hickory = 50
Elm = 75
Ashwood = 100
Oak = 125
Darkwood = 150

The final range that a bow and arrow has is the total of the bow’s range and the arrow’s range.

The base Damage of the 5 different bow staves is as follows:

Hickory=10
Elm=13
Ashwood=16
Oak=21
Darkwood=25

The final damage that a bow and arrow has is the total of the bow’s damage and the arrow’s damage.

The base Delay of the 5 different bow staves is as follows:

Hickory=50
Elm=51
Ashwood=58
Oak=61
Darkwood=68

Arrows, as they stand now, have no delay so the only delay is that from the bow.

The cheapest bow of any wood class is made with a stave and a Hemp string. These bows will have the Highest Damage and Greatest Delay for that wood class. The simplest bows are made from only a stave and a string, but there are two other types of strings, Linen and Silk. Using a linen string will decreases the base damage of a bow by 1 and decreases the delay by 4, making it a less powerful weapon but a faster weapon. Using the silk string decreases the base damage by 2 and decreases the delay by 8. So, this leads to the following example. A Hickory stave with a hemp string produces a bow with a range of 50, a damage of 10 and a delay of 50. Using this same stave with a silk string produces a bow with the same range of 50 but a smaller damage of 8 and a smaller delay of 42.

NEVER use more than 1 stave and 1 string. As with arrows, if you put more pieces than are needed you will lose those parts without making a bow.

Depending on how an archer uses his bow he may opt for high damage over a faster bow and visa versa. As I see it, if the customer is only interested in pulling a creature to his group, he wants a high damage bow. If, however, the customer is going to be Bow Kiting then he wants the faster weapon. And this is usually how the sales of bows will go. Some will want the high damage and the others will want the fastest. I doubt you will sell any bows with weird configurations of a little less damage and a little faster.
