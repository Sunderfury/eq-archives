The BlackBurrow “Beacon”

Ok Ranger, you want to go gnoll hunting for a bit eh? Well, you better be at least level 6 if you want to come back out. The first thing about BlackBurrow is the trains. For those who have not heard the term, trains are when more than one gnoll, or any unfriendly, gives chase after a PC. In BlackBurrow, trains are a common site due to the given aspects of this zone.

1.The gnolls range from levels 3 up to 16. Some poor sods accidently picks on the wrong gnoll Other gnolls join in and the victim runs for…

2. ..the nearest zone. There are ONLY 2 zones borders and they are very close to each other.

3. Last, the zone is very vertically designed and complex. It took me about a dozen visits till I got an idea of where everything is. With gnolls spawning in various spots, and a victim in hysterics because he/she is lost, it doesn’t take much to form a train. So, if you are venturing into this zone at level 6, I’d first recommend taking on the gnolls that lead to BlackBurrow first. One the Qeynos Hills side, take the gnoll that spawns to the right of the cave entrance. If from the Everfrost zone, take on a guard. If you can beat them with relative ease, move on to BlackBurrow.

6 to 10 level strategy: I think the best experience move is to become part of the “Derailment Squad”. Stay near the zone border, mainly the Qeynos Hills border as most trains end here. As the train nears, make sure you don’t see a burly, elite, gaurdsman or commander. If you do, back off! They will make gnoll food of you. Pick one of the gnolls near the end of the train. Feel free to grab one up front if you’re of a higher level and think you can take the heat. If things get bad, run for the border. There are usually others of the squad nearby, so don’t worry too much. If things are quiet, go ahead and pick on the scrawny gnolls that around the top area there.

11 to 14 level strategy: Ok, it should be somewhat safe to roam around a bit. Remember the way out! Learn the caverns bit by bit, and where the nasties spawn. Avoid the elites and commanders as they will use your femurs and tibias to play “Fetch the Stick”.

15 to 18 level strategy: Alright! You’re ready to rock. There are certain area you will want to camp, but be nice and share with others. In the main area, deep in BlackBorrow, there is a ledge that over looks some pools. Single elites spawn there. In a room with two doors, below the ledge, is a stool. Anything from an elite, commander, and shaman appear there. Now in the upper part of the carvens, there are two good spots.

The Elite Room has 4-5 elites, and 1 commander that appear. Sometimes a shaman or even a high shaman. This is near the spike pit, and closer to what looks like a small bleacher stand. The entry is very narrow and curves right. Be careful when entering as you may have a full house of elite gnolls. Can you say, “Oops, time for a new train.”? In a little further to the caverns from the spike pit, on the left hand side, is a set of 3 alcoves. Two commanders tend to spawn here with an elite and gaurdsman. Good fighting here too.

Even at level 18, when a train of elites and commanders is on me, I’m deadmeat for the most part. All you can do is run for the top and zone. But, here’s what I do to help distance myself from the train when I’m near the Elite Room…

Sy’s Escape: When suddenly realizing in the Elite Room or the Commanders’ Alcoves, “Oops”, I’ve found an escape that seems to work 100% of the time. From the Elite Room, run along the left wall till you reach the Commanders’ Alcove. Don’t run into the alcove, you’re in enough trouble already! Instead of a left into the alcove, AND instead of going through the door ahead, make a right. You’ll wind up shortly at another door with a gaurdsman near it. Open it and jump off the ledge! Weeee, about a 100ft drop into the water. Swim to the only water level exit, and, congrats, the gnolls do not jump in after you. Still, don’t stop moving as they are up top looking for a way down other than jumping. But, you’re already ½ way home. From the water level exit, cross the short bridge to the door, do NOT go through the door. Make a right and follow the shore line. You’ll soon recognize the way up to the top from there.

Now if I remember right, by the time you make it the zone from the Elite Room, you may have 4 elites, 1 commander, 5 gaurdsmen, 8 regular gnolls, 6 scrawny, and a gnoll pup on your case. This depends on your level – the higher your level, the lesser gnolls leave you alone. Otherwise, 25 gnolls after one prey! This will surprise the Derailement Squad!!!

That’s about it. Some good experience and some decent loot, but no unique loot that I’m aware of.

Good luck and happy hunting,

Sy, The Vicious. 18th seasoned ranger.
