Stories from Norrath


The screams and howls echoed off every surface as he fled down the corridor, turning this way and that in a mad rush. The stone walls, dark and damp, reflected the sounds in such a way that trying to locate their source was almost fruitless. And to Aziraphale, they all looked very much alike as he ran for his life.

Blood covered his leather tunic and dripped heavily off of the blade of his two-handed sword. A lot of the blood was Gnoll, but his deep wounds were adding much of his own to the mix. His breath came in heaving gasps as he tried to increase the distance from his pursuers. He was beginning to feel dizzy from the blood loss, and the smell in this dungeon was rank enough to enhance the effect - his sensitive olfactory nerves, most useful for a ranger, were almost reeling from the strong stench.

He slid on a patch of moss and bounced off a sharp corner, grunting in pain. He slowed for a moment, and felt hot breath down the back of his neck. Moments later a powerful claw swiped across his cloak, adding a new tear to the already almost shredded garment. It bit deeply into the leather tunic and intense pain slammed through him, slowing him further. Turning into a larger cavern, he jumped down a small embankment and ducked a small outcropping. The satisfying sound of a thud came from behind and he was sure it was the thick skull of the Gnoll Guardsmen chasing him. Or at least one of them anyway - he dare not look back to see how many were now in pursuit.

He and Lyonnoch had become separated in the earlier melee and he carried the additional worry of his friend. The bard was not quite as strong as he was, though the songs he could carry into battle often made one feel braver and more powerful than usual. He hoped his friend had made it up to the surface - to the relative safety of the entrance to this hell. If only Aziraphale could get up top he thought he might have half a chance…

He finally spied some familiar ground and the sight of it gave his heart a boost. He quickened his pace to an almost dangerous gate - one false step and he could fall; and that would spell a quick death. Throughout his flight he passed many faces, some of which he’d seen on the way down. Almost all were engaged in battles of their own; help would not come from them this day.

He bounded across a wooden bridge and turned left, or tried to. At the full run he was doing he couldn’t quite negotiate the turn and banged hard off the far wall. The corridor leading up to the outside was narrow and steep - his energy was bleeding off fast. Something much smoother than a claw sliced into his right leg and felled him. Turning he saw that an Elite Gnoll had joined the pursuit - it’s sword now coated with Aziraphale’s blood. Hope faded quickly, but his faith in Tunare glowed brightly as ever. He would not go down easily.

Summoning his remaining strength, he brought his mighty two-handed sword down into the crowd of dog-like beasts before him. He connected well enough to knock the leaders off balance, and the steep pitch of the corridor did the rest. The pack fell back several feet and Aziraphale leapt on the chance. He turned back up the corridor and charged as hard as ever for the top. Three or four turns later and he could see the light peeking through the cracks in the door that marked the entrance to the den. But the door opened inward and he knew the damn creatures were only seconds behind…he softly cursed his rotten luck. Suddenly the light blossomed as an unwary adventurer threw open the portal. Though he did not think he had the air for it, Aziraphale took his last deep breath and shouted as loud as he could manage:

“WARE! GNOLLS COMING UP!”

He barely caught a glimpse of the stunned look on the face of the newcomer as he flew by out into the open, sweet air. As he turned the final left and sprinted with all his might for the opening that would lead him from this place, he was struck harder than ever from behind. Collapsing, he knew he would not make those last few yards, and the bitterness it left in his mouth was almost worse than the coppery taste of blood. As he fell to the ground, he tried to turn to face his foes. He would not die with his back to these bastards!

Without warning, a blue glow engulfed him and he felt several of his wounds close…another chance! He could take no time to look upon his savior; a kind-hearted healer he assumed. With his renewed strength he tried to bolt once again for the exit, but his stamina had been severely sapped; and the gap too far to bridge before the mangy curs would cut him down for the last time.

Yet once again the gods were to smile upon young Aziraphale. More merciful help than any Half-elf deserved in one day - from out of nowhere the sweet sounds of a mandolin. Not just any sounds, and not just any mandolin. He instantly recognized the music as being Lyonnoch’s, and the song was Selo’s Accelerado. The notes swept across the air and resonated almost magically through Aziraphale’s body. His feet now felt swifter than ever and he wasted not one moment in fleeing towards the opening ahead. The music meant one thing, that Lyonnoch lived; and this knowledge bolstered Aziraphale enough to provide him that final ounce of courage and willpower. The exhausted and near mortally wounded ranger half flew, half fell across the hidden border over which he knew the Gnolls would not pass. While dangerous, they lacked any great intellect and seemed to stay within a confining perimeter for the most part.

As Aziraphale lay there on the cool stone surface, he knew that he would live to return to Black Burrow…and with Tunare’s good grace, he would repay the Gnolls for their savagery with unbridled hatred and cold steel.
