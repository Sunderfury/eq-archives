MISTMOORE GUIDE

By Faenor Ravenwing of the Sacred Protectors

Once you have attained ‘adulthood’ in EverQuest which means you are now level 20 it is time to start looking for new hunting grounds. The places you visited in your teens like Crushbone and Blackburrow have become too easy for you. If you are based in Faydwer, Mistmoore is an interesting place to visit.

One thing has to be said up front: Mistmoore is evil. And I’m not just talking about the vampires. Trains are a part of life in Mistmoore and some of the most heinous trains I have ever seen in EverQuest were in Mistmoore. It is not unusual for a massive train to rage through the zone emptying it out and killing many hapless players. The Mistmoore zone is usually littered with bodies. If you arrive at the Mistmoore zone from Lesser Faydark and notice a large group of players milling about (some of them undoubtedly naked) chances are there is a welcoming committee at the other end of the zone you’d rather not encounter. If you can, always camo before entering Mistmoore, you never know what is waiting for you.

However Mistmoore is an outdoors zone so it’s perfect for Rangers. Camouflage is a life safer there and I seriously recommend a druid or shaman in your party because sow can mean the difference between life and death when escaping from a train. Once you get to know the place and are comfortable there, you can make good experience and carry home armfuls of loot. I hunted in Mistmoore off and on between my 22nd and 28th level and made several hundred platinum from Mistmoore trips alone.

Mistmoore is also pretty small, at least the area for levels 20-30 is. The initial lake area for levels 20-27 can support about 3 full groups. Any more and it gets crowded and dangerous. The graveyard area can support 1 maybe 2 groups, the secret room and tower another group and the ancille valley and cave another 2 groups. This guide will not cover the castle since I have never set foot in it. It houses monsters of terrifying capabilities and due to the nature of the zone it is extremely dangerous to even get near it. Raiding parties of level 40+ players have been wiped out there due to pathing problems, things attacking through walls and the tendency of the zone to generate trains of truly epic proportions. If you enter the zone and see there is a group of level 35+ people in there chances are it is going to be a dangerous time.

To get to Mistmoore, enter the forest of Lesser Faydark. This place used to be quiet and serene but nowadays it is overrun by shadowed men and dangerous brownies. So camo up and head to the south-west corner of the zone. The Mistmoore entrance is just past the Nybright camp by some ruins. Make sure you camo yourself and enter the zone. If there is a group of dark elves standing around zone back immediately. Especially if they have pets or if there are gargoyles. Things like ‘thought corruptors’, ‘will sappers’ and ‘ancille cooks’ will make mincemeat out of unsuspecting level 22 rangers wandering into Mistmoore unprepared. Most monsters in the zone cannot see through camouflage however so you should be ok. What I used to do is observe by the zone, just stand there during a train and watch the hordes pour forth. Do this at your own risk though, some monsters see through camo and you might not even make the ten steps it takes to get back to the zone!

There are also frequent ‘ghosts’ of monsters that you will see walk away from the zone and disappear into a wall. Or there could be pets from caster monsters standing in a corner. Always be careful and under NO circumstances attack these. The actual monster or pet’s owner could be deep in the zone and attacking it could cause a massive death-spreading train. You’ll be happily hacking away at the pet when shouts of terror start filling the zone and a tidal wave of half-dead players followed by an army of dark elves washes you away into oblivion. Trust me I have seen it happen more than once.

Sometimes people decide to make a stand at the zone and big chaotic battles ensue. If you are brave and foolish you can participate, but usually zoning out is the best thing to do. Being stunned repeatedly by a gang of familiars and dying two steps from the zone is frustrating.

The lake area (levels 22-26)

After you zone in head down the ramp into the lake area. There are two exits to this area, the ramp back to the zone and the gorge leading to the graveyard, ancille valley and castle. If you are below level 26 you really want to stay away from the graveyard and anything beyond it. At the bottom of the ramp you will find the lake with a circular area around it. To your immediate right there will be a tower on a large rocky outcropping. At the base of the tower two gargoyles appear. These are highly sought after for the eyes they sometimes drop. These eyes sell for up to 9 platinum at vendors. Going home after an evening in Mistmoore with 4 Gargoyle eyes in your pack is a good thing.

To your left the area circles around the lake and meets back up at the tower base and ramp up to the zone. Past the tower to the right is the entrance to the gorge leading to the graveyard. There are three prime spots for groups to camp and pull to:

  • At the other side of the tower base facing the gorge entrance. This is a very good spot for pulling from the gorge itself plus there are about 5 spawn spots close by. However this is the most dangerous location since trains usually come from the gorge and go right by this spot. If whoever is pulling the train takes the shortcut up the tower base (there is a lone familiar spawn spot there), the train will thunder right through your camp spot, most probably annihilating your group.

  • At the top of the lake shore, in the corner directly left of the bottom of the ramp. Probably the safest spot in the lake area but not ideal since pulling from the gorge means pulling past the people camped at the tree a bit further down towards the gorge entrance. Confusion will happen here and your pull might join their fight or attack their meditating people leading to kill-stealing arguments and death.

  • At the tree left of the gorge entrance. Good spot also, however when you have a runner they head down towards the open area in front of the gorge which is prime train territory.

Little note: as you might have gathered by now, life in Mistmoore is ruled by trains. Everything you do has to be done with possible trains in mind. More about trains and how to live with them later.

So what do you fight in the lake area you might wonder? Familiars. In vampire folklore, familiars are people who aspire to be vampires. They have to serve vampires and prove their worth before they are bitten so they can become vampires themselves. Once a vampire has claimed a familiar for itself, this familiar is then marked with a glyph and becomes a glyphed familiar. Killing a glyphed familiar means a very annoyed vampire owner. In Mistmoore however, glyphed familiars are simply tougher than the regular ones.

Familiars come in different levels. Some of them are casters, male or female ones (‘bikini-babes’ in Mistmoore slang) can be dangerous. When you see your puller’s health drop rapidly he or she probably pulled a caster. (or started a train in which case you’ll be legging it to the zone)

Lake area: pulling and living with trains

As a ranger it is likely you will be the puller and group leader. Two things are essential for survival in Mistmoore: a good puller and train awareness. Things will spawn around your camp spot. Be prepared for that. Have someone in your group create a hotkey that will warn you, something like "Faenor! Come back now we have enemies beating on us!". Nothing is worse to come back with a pull and seeing your group already in battle.

Always camo when pulling. Especially when pulling from the gorge area which you will be especially when it is crowded since quite a few familiars appear in there. Two reasons camo is important. First of all - I have survived quite a few trains this way - I would be walking down the gorge, minding my own business when all of a sudden a large group of hideously red monsters would come running past me. I would yell at my group to run to the zone NOW and pray camo would hold. Second, there is the famous runner. A patrolling monster (usually an initiate) will stroll down the gorge. When it sees a player or gets turned aggressive in any other way it will turn around and run back. All the way back to the dark and evil depths of Mistmoore. When it finds ancilles in the valley these plate-clad killing machines will come running out destroying everything in their path. And if the ancille valley is empty…it will run into the castle. I don’t have to explain what comes next then.

When camouflaged however this runner will not notice you. It will also allow you to detect the runner and warn the zone that evil is about to come in large numbers.

Sometimes, especially at off-peak hours or just after a big train has cleared out the zone, the gorge will be fully spawned. This means pulling becomes tricky. Beware of the initiate who spawns on top of the ledge at the left side of the gorge. When this one gets pulled it will run back down the ledge and cause most of the others to go aggressive. This will mean another train. Oh joy. However this is a great time to show off your harmony spell and pull singles from a fully spawned gorge. Never fails to impress your group. Just be careful is all I can say. Better to over-harmonise than to risk pulling more than you can handle. Harmony takes time to cast make sure you explain that to your group so they don’t get impatient and do anything stupid. I have seen the group cleric wander into the gorge while I was in mid-harmony to see what was going on only to cause death and destruction.

Train survival

When camped in the lake area, establish rules for train survival. When someone calls out train (especially someone over about level 27 who is likely to be in the graveyard or ancille valley or if they’re brave enough, the castle) do not hesitate. If you are in a safe enough spot the least you can do is camo the group. Make sure people who can camo or cast invisibility have designated people (warriors and others who cannot camo themselves) they cast on before they cast on themselves. I have seen a poor cleric stand alone and terrified while the rest of the group was invisible. Make sure that doesn’t happen :)

When you are fighting and someone calls a train, it is very tempting to try to finish off your kill. This is a sure-fire way of getting killed however. Being in a fight is the best way of attracting things from a passing train. Just head for the zone, that one familiar with the bronze sword is not worth the deaths of three party members.

Oh and if you see a high level monster with only a little bit of health left walk back to its lair, do not be tempted to try to kill it. Under no circumstance attack things from a train that are on their way back. Most likely the entire train will return and crush you and the rest of the people that are unfortunate enough to be around your foolish self.

Lake area: conclusion

You will spend a lot of time in the lake area when you are new to Mistmoore. You will fight many familiars here, die many deaths but also gain a lot of experience and loot. Soon you will be able to run to the zone from any point in this area with your eyes closed. You will get to know the monsters from the graveyard, ancille valley and castle before you ever set foot in those areas. And after they kill you, you become a bit more hardened, a bit more knowledgeable about this eerie zone.

The gorge

This is a transitional area. Only very foolish or very desperate (when the zone is completely overcrowded) people actually camp in here. It is a narrow area connecting the lake area and the rest of the zone. Familiars spawn in this gorge and it is a prime pulling area. It is also the major train route and can be a total death trap. Always camo before entering the gorge. I am level 36 right now and I still do it when on my way to the graveyard since I never know what will welcome me when I venture forth…

It curves gently to the right and ends in a small open area where you find the entrance to the tunnel leading to the ancille valley to your left and the gate to the graveyard and secret room to your right.

The Graveyard: levels 26-32

This is a small area with a tomb at the back. There are three sets of graves and glyphed familiars, glyphed guards and glyphed warders will appear by each of these graves. A full group of people in their high 20s can control the graveyard once the initial spawn has been broken. Again, a skilled ranger with harmony is invaluable. Once inside the graveyard you are actually safe from trains from deeper in the zone. Unless someone coming from the castle decides to take the shortcut through secret room and tower in which case you are in deep trouble. But that doesn’t happen that often. People usually die before they get anywhere if they cause a castle train…

You can enter the tomb through the door and have a look inside. It is empty, and at both sides of the tomb are trap doors that will cause you to fall into the tomb’s cellar. This is also empty however the stairway leading up goes through the secret door. Depending on what is inside the secret room at that time you may or may not make it out safely.

Behind the tomb is a good safe spot to sit and heal/meditate. You will be out of range from the monsters in the graveyard and well away from the train route. This is also the spot where a shadowed man named Ssynthi appears, this is the quest giver for the Shadowknight Darkforge armour quests. He does not attack me even though he cons scowling.

The spawn rate in the graveyard isn’t great. It usually gets quite boring especially if there is more than one group camping the place.

At the graves on left side of the graveyard the Imp Familiar sometimes spawns. This creature sometimes drops the sacrificial dagger, a much wanted item by caster types. If you get it, you have a nice sale/trade item. Another item dropped by the Imp Familiar is the bloodstone eyepatch.

Take care with the warders, they are cleric types and will heal themselves, no matter how fast and hard you beat on them. Fights with these can last a long time and if you get more than one you may be in trouble.

Secret room: levels 26-32

Behind the tomb in the far corner of the graveyard area is a secret wall that you can walk through. You end up in a small empty room, that has another secret wall. Through there you will enter what is known as the secret room. It looks like a prison of some sorts with three cells holding monsters. There are four doors in this room, the door directly opposite the entrance will open the other three as well. Now this place is a bit peculiar. The monsters will not leave their rooms when attacked. However if they are casters they will cast of course even through the door. This can be confusing. Also the doors will close automatically after a short while so it is possible to get trapped in a cell.

Taking the secret room can be easy or hard depending on what kind of monsters are in the cells at the time. If they are melee types like ushers or sappers, you will be fine since you can fight one at a time. If there are casters it gets harder since they will start casting away as soon as the fighting starts. Sometimes they even cast on you when you are simply there. I have wandered into the secret room under camo only to be blasted for many hit points, causing me to turn around and run back out. From that moment on the monster will stay aggressive to you so when you get back in it’s range it will cast on you again.

Since you have to fight inside the little cells, you need at least one person to operate the door. Your cleric or wizard preferably since they have no business being in the little cells at melee range anyway.

There are three exits to the secret room. One back to the graveyard, one down some stairs to the basement of the tomb and one up some stairs into the tower. If you take this exit you will end up and a T junction. Head left and you will end up inside the castle. Head right and you will go into the tower overlooking the lake area. People use this as a shortcut sometimes to get out of the graveyard or secret room in times of trouble. Up the stairs, and jump off the little bridge leading to the tower down to the base of the ramp leading up to the zone. Only do this when you have enough HP to survive the fall. I have never done this so do not know how many HP the fall takes. There are also hard-hitting monsters up in the tower that will happily send you to the afterlife so be careful. Rumour has it that Lord Mayong Mistmoore himself spawns in the tower sometimes. During all the time I have spent in Mistmoore I have never seen him so I don’t know if this is true or not.

On the stairs leading up to the tower the Avenging Caitiff sometimes spawns. This is the monster that is involved in the Electrum Bladed Wakizashi quest. It also drops the Crested Spaulders and rarely the Crested Helm. Needless to say it is short lived and the subject of some heated KS arguments.

Deathly ushers sometimes appear in the secret room. They can also be found in the castle. These creatures can drop the rune of ivy for the ranger ivy helmet quest or the black silk gloves for a bard armor quest.

The Glyphed Ghoul appears sometimes in the lake or graveyard area. He sometimes drops the Crested Mistmoore Shield, a very nice item for non-dual wielding classes. His common drop is the vial of vampire blood, something that gives you a one-time magic resistance buff.

A very rare cleric quest item called the Icon of the Sacrament sometimes appears in one of the cells. It looks like a voodoo doll or totem. It is no-drop and any cleric in the zone or in your group will be extremely grateful if you let them have it.

Ancille valley: levels 30 and up

The last area before the actual castle is known as the ‘ancille valley’. It is a large open area with a ramp leading up to the castle entrance on the left hand side. It extends quite far back up to a platform and a cave. The entire area is filled with ancilles. Ancilles are vampire protectors and servants. They do quite a good job since a fully spawned ancille valley in combination with a careless player or a runner will cause a lethal train. The melee-type ancilles are dressed in plate mail and hit pretty hard. Since they are melee however they shouldn’t pose that much of a problem. The casters however can be dangerous. Wraithish and Vampirish ancilles can do some serious damage so be careful.

This area is actually quite safe if you have a group strong enough to handle the ancilles (a full and well balanced group of lvl30ish people can do it) however taking a fully spawned valley can be tricky. A skilled ranger with harmony can do the pulling and a good enchanter or bard with crowd control skills in the group is useful when a pull gets more than the group can handle. Clear out the area in front of the graveyard, fight the initial pulls there. The ranger goes down the tunnel and inches forward until the first ancilles are visible. Cast harmony on the closest one and enter a bit further. Keep casting harmony on the ones you can see. Then cross your fingers and pull the closest one. Hopefully the careful harmonising worked and you get one or two ancilles. Return to the group, proceed to slaughter the pulls and repeat. Once the initial area is clear the group can move in and start clearing the area all the way to the back. There are some gargoyles in the back as well plus a dark elf called Enynti that sometimes drops the rune of fortune. Once cleared the ancilles should appear in little groups staggered over time allowing the group to fight them a few at a time. Holding the ancille valley for some time means great loot and exp. plus a much more safe Mistmoore for the rest of the people in the zone. You will be able to intercept runners from the gorge and graveyard that normally pull the ancilles or disappear into the castle. I remember being in the ancille valley for hours holding it safely and people shouting about what happened to the trains :). However if you miss a runner and see it sprint up the ramp into the castle, do not hesitate. Run for the zone immediately and warn people that death will follow soon. Also if a higher level group comes by and decides to try the castle, it might be wise to leave. Having a castle train descend upon you while you are in battle with ancilles will most probably kill you.

The Castle

I have only been a few times in the castle so I can’t say much about it. I have seen various monsters from the castle run around the zone however. There are the gypsies, the sages (absolute killer spell casters) the Mistmoore guards, the thought spoilers and corrupters, the deathly ushers and harbingers and some named monsters like Maid Issis and Butler Syncal (their teeth are for the Electrum Bladed Wakizashi quest, give them to the Avenging Caitiff), Xicotl (a lvl40+ troll shadow knight that drops the hilt for Soulfire), the negotiators (high level DE enchanters, cast charm, fear, darkness and other nastiness), a DE noble, the gypsy ambassador and lots of high level gargoyles. These monsters range from level 30 to 45+ and some of them are lethal. Xicotl has multiple harm touches that wipe out players in one spell. I have seen him go on a rampage and it wasn’t pretty…

Some nice items can be found in the castle including the diamondine earrings and the Mistmoore battle drum, bards kill for this one.

Going into the castle requires a well co-ordinated group of lvl35+ players. A bard or enchanter and a good healer are absolutely necessary since the initial pulls will be messy and crowded. Once you have a foothold and the puller knows what he or she is doing you will be allright. I have been in the kitchen controlling the spawns in the nearby corridor and servant’s quarters where the maid and butler spawn with a full group doing pretty well.

Conclusion

Mistmoore is a great zone, the risks are high but the rewards can be good indeed. When the zone is overcrowded and people start taking risks to get kills or when there is a group having trouble in the ancille valley or castle it can be very dangerous. Trains are frequent and deadly and can shut down the zone for 10-20 minutes at a time. On a good day you can spend a lot of time in there and make some great experience and loot. Just be careful, keep an eye out for trains and hone your pulling skills until people will beg you to stay and pull when you announce you have to leave…

Enjoy,
