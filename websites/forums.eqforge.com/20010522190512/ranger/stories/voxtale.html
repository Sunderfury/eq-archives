Stories from Norrath

The Quest for the Wyrm Queen

Where the air is cold & dry
deep in the lands of the north
Where giants roam, an orcs do hunt
half a three-score ventured forth

They came by 2's and 3's
to this place of unending ice
Divided by guild and feud
and wondering if their strength would suffice

At the gates of the Keep
they paused, gathered strength
As all could see,
come from the land's breadth and length

Raganork IceStorm was there
proud citizen of the north
With the spirits at his side
ready to burst forth

With a word and a prayer
Nino McHammer would cast
Upon his fellows
resolute wardings to last

Innoruk's Children were there as well
Tdira D'Rathia, wise beyond her years
Determined to face the depths
And bring to Vox new fears

Under a swirl of color and magic effect
Zamtil Litmaz wove his dwenomers true
To protect and enhance
enough to see the adventure through?

With the sound of the breeze
Askarii Windfury did arrive
Ranger Lord in title,
With dread anticipation in his eyes

Klague D'nartan was present too
Druidic standard bearer
Saddened by Tree's absence
yet here to stay Vox's terror

A great stench then arose,
a voice thundered, the earth quaked
For Ulik had come
bidding all to follow in his wake

With a flash and an ear-splitting roar
Ranstan Marnok did arrive
Determined to see, by the end of the night
only he or Vox would survive

Out of the shadows stepped a thief
Koraf Youngblood by name
Scarred by Ghoul Lord, Avatar claw, and mighty sword
Yet undaunted, and eager to face Vox's flame

As they waited
for their general to appear
They sported with the giants
in the castle, and showed no fear

Finally,
with the Captain no where in sight
A cry rang out from Nino
"To your groups men!, let us begin this fight!"

Each now joined his comrades,
and made ready for the fight
Each was with friends old, and friends new
lending confidence, this night

*Time was spent, waiting for the General to appear
but the time had come, should they wait? Nay
A cry again came from Nino,
"Tdira, have your group lead us into the fray!"

As they entered the halls of Permafrost Keep
the goblins all quaked in fear
For in their short lives they had never seen
such power as was gathered here

Into the shadows
the goblins faded away
Upon their knees they fell and prayed
So that they maight see yet another day

Yet, the heroes had no interest in those
who would move aside
However, those that didn't...
stood, fought, and died.

To the Goblin King's Court they now strode
their purpose firm, their confidence at it's crown
And in the face of the King's defiance
they charged forth, and struck him down.

Here they paused once more,
and waited for Nino's assent to go again.
For behind the King's court were two giant doors
here the challenges would begin.

Their pasue was short, and then warriors strode forth,
with great strength the doors were riven
Behind them stood a the Giant Diplomat,
And battle was given

The Giant could not stand long,
against such overwhelming Might
Such was the invaders' power
he was deprived at even the chance of flight

Now, surging forth,
they poured down the halls
Till two more Giants appeared
and the dungeon echoed with their battle calls

This melee was more difficult than the last
as both giants worked together to stomp their tiny prey
Yet, the swarm of skilled fighters and mages prevailed
and the heroes won the day.

Here the milling throng stopped again
for most were deeper than ever before
Fear now crept in
and some grew doubtful of the outcome of the war

Yet, as they paused, confronting their doubt
cheers were heard in the north
The Captain had arrived!
The battle could go forth!

Kalaran Windblade strode through the crowd
and into the dungeon the force he lead
With terrors and traps on all sides
in his footsteps they were careful to tread

Many dangers and fell creatures the heroes did overcome
they were burned, and frozen.... chilled, and thrown
Death lurked in every corner
The icy walls echoed with war cry, and wounded's moan.

Yet, through spell and sword they came on,
overcoming all that dared stand in their way.
Nothing could stand before them, and soon
they won their way to the cave of their prey.

Here they rested, and healed
For no one knew what the next battle would bring
No one had before tread where they were about to go
And would it be of victory, death, or cowardly retreat that the bards would sing.

Now, the Captain gathered them close
and issued his orders for the coming fight
All had to pull together, as one,
or death would be the only reward this night

Then, with a hue and cry, the heroes charged forth
the guardians of the wyrmqueen not slow to respond
Many Giants now opposed the heroes advance
clerics, magi, and wizards... enough magic to make even the stoutest heart despond

The battle which ensued rent the fabric of reality itself
the air was alive with force, fire, ice... pure magic power
As warriors tried to ply their doughy trade.
in the face of such an unearthly display, not a one could be seen to cower

A Priest of Veeshan was the first to fall
the ground trembled with his landing
His last act, to curse his killers
and to urge on his fellows still standing

But the tide had turned, the heroes felt it in the air
With renewed vigor they swung and cast
Their spells and swords struck true
And soon they were facing the last

The last giant, a priest like the first, saw his own death at hand,
and decided to do to the heroes the worst
With a prayer to Veeshan for the defense of his land
A fell magic he cast, and the hero's Captain fell cursed.

With the Giant's last breath, that fell magic was cast,
and as such, it had a power none could match
The Captain was not dead, that much was true
However, his spirit had been forced out, that was the catch

The heroes were left, alone in the room,
leaderless, and with Vox awake too
Giantish reinforcement could be heard on the way,
a cry went up, "What shall we do!?"

Attempts were made to move Kalaran's form,
the curse, however, held him bound
A choice then had to be made
to continue on, or to wait till a cure could be found.

Time was short, the pressure was on
and the decision had to be made
For out of all the choices
only through action could the heroes be saved

Koraf then stood, and with a hue and cry
ran straight for the Wyrm Queen's form
And cursing his folly and yelling their rage
the heroes came on in a storm

Lady Vox was ready and with a dragonish hiss
She summoned a pillar of flame
Into the charging mass she cast it
And with one swipe cut Stridar in twain

Now the battle was joined, and magics were cast
and fire raged all around
Yet such was the strength of Vox's armor
that she was unhurt even as she was flame crowned

The battle raged on, and damage was done
but more heroes were starting to fall
Gerahben and Krimson were struck down
and it appeared the heroes would die, all.

Yet the damage to Vox was mounting
As the heroes learned her evil ways
Kadail scaled her back and landed his Dag'
Even as he was thrown and landed, dazed

Vox was then smitten by the ArchWizard's magic
And turning she rushed him, past all in her way
Mighty in magic beyond all, Ranstan cast till the last
But he too tasted death that day.

With many now dead, and the Dragon still strong
most would have run away.
But not this group of heroes, with one voice they cried,
"Death to Vox! We shall have our revenge this day!"

The Dragon who had killed so many,
still faced many yet
And who's not to say that in her brain
she counted the odds and began to sweat

For the heroes continued fighting
As I've said, not a one
Had second thoughts about staying
or leaving before the job was done

Lady Vox, still dangerous, was more desperate now,
her spells came slower too
They redoubled their efforts, and sword and spell struck flesh
a cry rang out from Myrlynn, "Keep on men, she's through!"

With victory in the air, and justice in their hearts
they steeled themselves for the last
Broken and bloody, spent and torn
they readied themselves for Vox's final blast.

A sudden strike from behind,
and a bash with Ulikian grace
Vox was stunned and slightly off
And unable to dodge the strike of a magic mace

Armored scales cracked, and flesh was exposed
Willard dove head-first, ignoring his pain
And into her chest, his sword did plunge
in that instant, Vox was slain.

A CHEER rose up! The gathered heroes rejoiced
for history had been made that day
A momument to their power and wisdom
which would stand dispite what any would have to say.

The Dragon now dead, the curse was lifted
and Kalaran joined once again
With one voice the heroes now hailed their Captain
for his leadership, 'Dragonsbane'!

With joy in the air, the clerics begain working
on beseeching their gods to restore their companions' lives
And soon the dead heroes rejoined their friends
and in rejoicing each did strive.

Aradune himself came, clad in green armor and flaming sword
and with his godly might
Seven items of power were created
as a reward to those who fought that night.

Of such items, and the heroes
I can say but naught
For here my story ends,
for the battle has been fought

But think of this as days go by
and under setting sun,
Lizardmen, gnolls, and orcs have you down
know that with perserverence, in such a song you can be one.

Creediki Starsong Bard of Veeshan
