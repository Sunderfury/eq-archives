An Exerpt From The Mistmoore Campaign, Part II

The storm drove on deep into the night. Thunder rumbled and jagged streaks of lightening raced across a leaden Rivervale sky. Wet and so cold... moving gingerly to the town's small lake, nursing my aching legs, I dropped to my knees and peered over the embankment.

"Tunare..." a soft plea, a sigh maybe, but for certain an acknowledgment of the gruesome visage reflected in the dark, wind-swept lake escaped my lips. Was this really me staring back? I choked in dismay at the stringy, blood-matted hair... the swollen, bruised face. Angry welts lanced the weary, trembling arms straining to support the battered body peering up at me. A tattered, sopping-wet under-blouse barely clung about my shoulders.

"Ow," I groaned as I slowly stood back up. Yes, that was indeed me, I conceded. While Tunare had found me worthy, for she had restored me to the world of the living after my fearsome beating at the hands of Chief Goonda, she must also be unhappy with me about something. If she wasn't upset with something I'd done, then why had she sent me back in such a bedraggled state? What had I done to fall from grace?

"A lesson perhaps?"

"Oh no," I answered quietly, more to myself then to the stealthy, unannounced visitor standing behind me. I knew that voice, and though embarrassed by my current sorry state, I was happy for a friend and slowly turned around.

"Elly!" I said rather sheepishly, then curiously added. "Now what brings you out to the water's edge on such a frightful night?"

"I was up late. I suppose it was just chance that saw me staring out at the storm and you limping to water's edge."

I didn't believe that for a second, but then, Elly was prone to make light of things she wanted to hold close to herself. In fact, few could really tell when she was serious or just pulling your leg. Eleanor was her real name of course, Eleanor Cuckleburr, but those close to her affectionately called her Elly. I had known her for many years and a close friendship had developed between the diminutive halfling druid and myself.

"You are a sad looking mess," Elly bluntly declared. "Let's get you cleaned up, and a bit of food will work wonders also." Pointing to the docks, Elly threw me a bar of soap.

"You've got to be kidding?!" I exclaimed.

Elly smiled, "You just get yourself bathed. I'll take care of Fiddy so you'll not have to worry about his prying eyes. Once I get him yapping, he'll not stop to worry about some human washing up under his boardwalk.

"But this storm... I could wash up just as well in a room at The Fool's Gold." I countered.

"And how will you pay for that room? You haven't even the pockets to carry gold, much less actually have any, and the bank is certainly closed at this hour. Besides, looking like you do, I doubt they'd let you in. You'd probably scare away what few patrons they have."

"Fine! All right," I conceded. "You just keep Fiddy busy!"

Elly was right of course and after cleaning up I felt much better. Together, we sought shelter from the driving rain inside the dock house. Elly prepared a small meal from her pack supplies and before long I was fast asleep. Refreshingly clean, with a full stomach and a tight roof over my head, I slept soundlessly long past sunrise.

It was the drilling of Fiddy's incessant questioning "... send help?" that finally forced me from my dreams. "Oh please!" I mumbled, "don't you ever shut up?"

Thankfully Fiddy couldn't hear me over his self-absorbed monologue but Elly shouted out good-naturedly from the docks, "Lazy humans! It's about time you got up... and leave poor Fiddy alone dear, he's just been cooped up on dry dock a bit too long."

"I'll say!" I shouted back as I walked out to join Elly on the docks. It was already mid-morning and the day was shaping up to be fair and warm. My spirits were much improved over last night.

"I have some fresh fish cooked for breakfast... well, it's lunch-time now." Elly said. Let's get you a hot meal, a few supplies and I'll travel with you to West Karana. I'm sure you're anxious to recover your missing gear.

"Elly, thanks for your help last night, and thanks for the offer, but I'll be fine. I'm not without some resources and with a few simple precautions, the journey isn't hazardous. I'll be able to stay well clear of the ogres. In fact, I was a good distance from their camp when Goonda overtook me."

"Ah, so that's what happened!" Elly stared hard at me. "You are quite the foolish one trying to roust that camp. I thought you had better sense. What possessed you to attempt that?"

"I didn't," I hastened to explain. "I was scouting only and didn't realize the range of vision Goonda has. He caught me by surprise." Elly patiently listened as I sat down and told my tale. Finishing my story and the last bits of fish from my plate, I stood back up and turned towards the lake, "Last night, when you snuck up on me, you said 'Perhaps it was a lesson'. What did you mean Elly?"

"Snuck?" she chuckled. "You were so concerned about your looks a hill giant could have walked up, tapped you on the shoulder, and you would have scarcely noticed I think." Packing up her own gear, Elly got to her feet, "Chrys, I've told you before, you don't like to hear it, but I'll tell you again, your vanity is going to get you killed. I hope it isn't the death of us all."

Shaking my russet locks in the slight morning breeze, I turned to face Elly. "I've conceded to wear that heavy banded armor, even though it makes me feel like one of those dancing floozies in a cheap Qeynos tavern. I'll not, however, drape slabs of garish red metal about my body or stuff my head inside a tin sack!"

Shaking her head sadly, "Well, perhaps your Tunare was telling you that looks are not as important as you think."

"Elly, I stood no chance against Goonda and his men. No armor or hat would have saved me. No druid spell or roguish stealth could have prevented what was ordained." I calmed a bit, but Elly only continued shaking her head in her best matronly halfling manner.

We locked eyes for an eternity it seems, till Elly broke our silence. "You're going back not just to reclaim lost weapons. You're going to try and kill Goonda aren't you," she stated, more then asked, matter-of-factly.

Though I said nothing, the look on my face clearly marked my intent.

"Then you are twice the fool I feared. You cannot hope to vanquish him, much less that entire camp."

Though I tried to ignore Elly's last comment, it echoed with bitter resonance. "Goodbye Elly, I must be going. My thanks again for your aid," was all I answered. With fell determination I ran towards the tunneled exit from Rivervale... back towards West Karana.

* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *

Elly watched for a few moments as the figure of Chrysoli disappearing in the tunnel gloom. "May your Tunare protect you, my foolish young friend. Though, I suppose, were it Rivervale and Ogres had taken residence..."

Her mind made up, Elly quickly started an incantation, one that allowed her to travel vast distances in the blink of an eye. "Time to gather the wolves," she sighed. "And the other two will come also... to far off West Karana our fates are tied." With a brilliant flash, Eleanor disappeared from fair Rivervale.

Chrysoli Whitethistle
Human Ranger (30)
