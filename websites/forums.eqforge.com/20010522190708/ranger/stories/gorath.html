Stories from Norrath


Gorath stopped and looked up at the moon and smiled to himself, thinking this was a good night to hunt. He continued on the way, following the tracks of a caiman, whom he had spotted in the distance, the tracks still fresh. Looking up peridically for the dreaded sand giants and hiding when necessary, he began to make his way towards the docks, his prey finally coming into sight...from the depth of the tracks, and the size of the beast....Gorath was sure it would offer them a chance to increase their skills.

Motioning for his group to join him, the lovely magician Twilite and their new friend, the rogue Felzar, he came to a stop atop the hill looking down. Noticing something disturbing ahead, he murmured the words to a minor cantrip, and his vision doubled. What he saw caused him to stop in his tracks......a skeleton, a dry bone unless he is mistaken, and one well experienced. So well experienced that Gorath felt his skin turn cold and the hairs on the back of his neck start to stand up at the pure evilness of it. Pointing towards it and feeling a rush of courage, Gorath quickly asked his party....what do ye think...do we take the chance.

Quickly taking in his partys strengths....himself of the 15th circle, his companion Twilite a mage of the 15th circle and Felzar, a rogue of the 14th circle, who had proven himself steadfast in combat. Turning to the side he saw Twilites pet, a beast of fire...and nodded. Whispering to them, he said quickly.....these are dangerous beasts....but I believe we may take him out..what say you. Felzar studied the beast and nodded... keeping silent... while Twilite shivered a little where she stood and said....I do not know, but we must do something quickly, for it leaves!

Gorath turned back around, and sure enough, the beast was starting to leave....walking and swaying with its own delusions of life and power....and that was it. Gorath had to rid the world of this foul beast. Steeling himself for the upcoming battle, he whispers words of power, and the licks of flame left his hand and covered the beast, but to his dismay they seemed to have no effect. The beast charged them, and battle began!! Naturally the dry bones targeted Gorath, the one who had cast upon him, and began to tear into him. The battle continued for several moments, almost frozen in time, seemingly going exactly even....the beast having parts fall off, while his own party bleeding from several wounds, all in the battle extremely hurt. Begining to grow a little desperate, Gorath called upon the powers of tunare, and tried to root the foul beast.... 3 times over, but each time the powerful beast resisted the spells. He then tried to wash the beast in bursts of flame, but to no avail.

Begining to wobble on his feet now, his swings coming slower and slower, but still biting into the beast, he thought of backing out to take a breather and try a differnt tactic, but one look around told him that his party was so badly hurt if he even tried to take the time to heal once, they would surely perish, so he kept in the battle, yelling insults and swinging with all his might. Finally, the great dry bones almost dead itself landed a fercious blow... pain fell over Gorath and to the ground he fell...slain by the beast. As he was closing his eyes....he saw the dry bones fall to the ground next to him.....and his last thought was to look at his companions, and seeing them all about to fall down unconscious....

Awakening some time later at the docks of Freeport....he was immediatly assaulted by many voices congratulating him on his stand against the beast. His party members assured him it was dead, and that his acts had kept them alive at the end of the battle. He was told he should feel proud of the battle, for he held his own in a battle against a much more powerful creature. At the same time, Felzar pointed out that he felt sorry, for all they had gotten for their hard work was some bone chips, at the cost of Goraths life, but he shrugged it off...for that happens.

At that time, a spellcasting desert madman happened upon his party, and the mage gated to safety, while the rogue ran for the safety of the next land. After making sure they were ok, Gorath set out to retrieve his items...his mind still thinking about the battle with the dry bones, in which he held his own....but fell just short of personal victory...
