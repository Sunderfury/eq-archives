Stories from Norrath

The Slaying of the Griffawn

In my 16th level of existence, I got a call from a friend to help her dispatch some particularly vulgar gnolls named Reavers who were defiling the Eastern Plains of Karana. So I put down my halfling pie, and starting a quick jog out to the plains, necessarily calling upon my woodcraft skills to blend flawlessly into the surrounding through the perilous Gorge of King Xorbb.

In a few moments, I had joined her, along with her brother and another friend, and we began to decimate the reaver population including some of a very disturbing undead variety who filled us with a sense of horror just looking at them. Her brother was called away shortly and the three of us kept the pace. But then I saw a powerful creature i had previously known only from horror stories fly by on giant wings, a Griffawn. I considered it and, since it appeared to be an even fight, asked my companions if we shouldnt try to kill it. And received a resounding chorus of NO's!

By the next week, I had become experienced enough to make the journey back to Surefall Glade and learn the art of Duel Wielding from Master Hagar Sureshot. Then I returned to HighHold Pass, but after seeing that there were many more adventures than gnolls to kill, I returned to Eastern Karana. After a bit of practicing with using both my barbed whip and my Dwarven Axe simultaneously, I happened to see the griffawn fly past once more. The beast considered blue so I decided it was time to try my luck despite the warnings from my much wiser friends.

I drew its attention with a burst of fire, but suddenly found myself under a barrage of powerful hits. After a few moments of trying desperately to fend them off a conceded defeat and ensnared the creature while i attempted to make it pass the boundries of the Plain. But in my vastly weakened condition it quickly overtook me and buried its beak into my spine with one last horrible thrust.

When I awakened in Rivervale, even the joyous singing of the halflings could not drive thoughts of revenge from my mind. So I called upon a friend from hunts past, a noble halfling warrior of the 19th level, and together we trekked back to East Karana. After recovering my belongings, I quickly caught the whiff of the creature on the air.

Feeling confident in our power, we charged the beast and began to pummel it with whip, axe, and halberd. Perhaps weakened by overconfidence, we began to take brutal wounds fromt he powerful animal. We were both nearing the end of our strength when I saw the end end was pulling nigh, and not a very pleasant one at that. With a last desperate effort I threw all the fire I could summon at the beast. Enraged and wounded, it turned on me and knocked me to the ground with one mighty blow. After a time of laying on the ground, bleeding profusely, I regained consciousness through Karanas blessing.

As I rose to my knees, with the head in my hands and my weapons scattered before me, I saw the corpse of the griff laying next to a small halfling, alive, but so weakened that he could not even move over to loot the monster. As I sat to regain thought through meditation, he told me that after seeing me fall, and assuming me dead, he went into a berserker frenzy and managed to drive home the winning blow with only 5 pieces of his strength remaining to him.

Although our efforts were rewarded with but 5 gold pieces and it took us well into the night to recover from vast injuries, we walked away with a great sense of pride and honor that could never be gained from slaying snakes and rats. My last rational thoguht before falling into an exhausted slumber that night was to bemoan the lack of a good ale or wine to be found in Rivervale.

Rogosh
The Half-Elven Ranger of Surefall Glade
