Ranger Quests

Hello fellow Rangers,

I thought I would send out across the land a recounting of my adventures in making the Trueshot bows that I now possess and use proudly in the defense of the lands of Norrath.

First off, I should let it be known that I had tried the quest for the Trueshot Bow many seasons ago to no avail. I later learned from a wise old sage that my failure was due to the fact that I was not of sufficient skill level in my chosen trade of fletching. Now that I am a master fletcher, of skill level 163, I decided to again attempt to make the much sought after Trueshot bow.

To do this quest one must have the 4 main ingredients, Treant Wood, Dwarven Wire, Micro Servos and Spiderling Silk. These ingredients, when combined in the correct proportions, will yield you a single shaft of treant wood suitable for the making of a single bow. But, just aquiring the four ingredients is not sufficient. The gods of norrath must smile on you. And once you atain the shaft of Treant Wood the gods must smile on you a second time, and it is usually this second time that is the most difficult time.

Upon aquiring the four ingredients in large quantities, for I wished to help my fellow rangers by making them bows and also lining my pockets with platinum, I proceded to Kelethin. One must be in Kelethin because it is there where the four ingredients are combined and turned over to the Ranger’s Guild leader. Since the gods forbid a single fletcher from possessing more than one shaft of the wood of the Treant at any one time, one needs to be near the Rangers Guild of Kelethin for repeated attempts.

Once one successfully combines the four ingredients and turns them over to the head of the ranger's guild you are rewarded with experience, gold and the afore mentioned shaft of treant wood suitable for bow making. You then need to inquire and learn the steps and ingredients that are needed to transform the shaft of treant wood into the Trueshot Bow. To divulge those instructions outside of the realm of the Rangers Guild would be sacreligious act so I only warn you to seek and heed those instructions. And remember that you are only permited by the gods to possess a single shaft of treant wood at any one time. And the gods know where you bank.

Now after spending almost 13 platinum for one of the additional items needed to convert the shaft of treant wood to a trueshot bow, I placed the 4 items into my fletchers kit, all the while hoping that the gods of norath would smile on me. I closed the case only to be dissappointed by the message that "You are of unsufficient skill to create the famed Trueshot bow". Knowing that more tries might fulfil my desires to possess the Trueshot bow, I repeated the process of aquiring another shaft of treant wood, and placed it, along with the additional 3 items, into my fletching kit. Failure again, and again and again. But on the 5th attempt I was rewarded with a beautiful Trueshot Bow. A bow that is both sleak of line and bold in its power to smite the wicked creatures of the land of Norrath. As a weapon, it is ranked high, with its damage of 20 and delay of only 45 and range of 100. As a piece of hand crafted artwork it is unsurpassed in beauty.

I then proceded to roam the area of my birth cleansing the land of the dreaded orcs, goblins and other creatures that have harrarassed my people these many long years. The Trueshot Bow is a mighty weapon and it served me so well that I decided to try and make a second one so that a fellow ranger could also use it to rid this beautiful land of those ugly and hideous orcs and goblins.

I handed my Trueshot bow over to my squire and proceded to make another one. After laying down my 13 platinum and making 4 additional attempts I was rewarded with another Trueshot bow.

Now as I sit and ponder my future I await the presence of another ranger who will become the owner of this second Trueshot Bow and become an additional warrior in the battle to save our land from the scourge of the orcs, goblins and other creatures.

As an end to my tale I remind all fletchers who would attempt the making of a trueshot bow, you may only possess a single shaft of treant wood at any one time and you may only possess one of the trueshot bows at any one time. But this mighty bow is a powerful tool well worth the price you may pay a skilled fletcher for it or well worth the sweat and effort to create one of your very own.

Fare well and good luck in your travels and hunting,

Folletto L’Arciere, Woodland Elf Ranger of his 20th season.
