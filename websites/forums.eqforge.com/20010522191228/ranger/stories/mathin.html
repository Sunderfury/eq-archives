The meeting of my wife-

Back in the days of my youth I had made the trip to the city know as Freeport from my hometown of Surefall. This was a somewhat perilous trip, as I was but a young child, un-experienced in the ways of the world. Plus with only the companionship of my frail enchanter friend, we would not last long if we were confronted by any danger.

So we made our way across the planes of Karana, to the mountains where High Keep earns it's name. To the dark forests of Kithicor, and onto the Commonlands, which I must say, really are not that common. Which leads us to the gates of Freeport, the vile dark city it is. Infested by Dark elves, Trolls and Ogres. Refuse in the streets, and beggars on the side. A dark comparison to the beauty of the city of Qeynos near my home.

Well on exploration of this new town, I came across a group of ladies from the far lands of Faydwer. Morlinde, a Wood Elf of the same following as me. Kylea, a follower of the ways of a Warrior. And Shirenkali, a Paladin determined to banish evil. It seems these ladies were headed to the fabled Gypsy camps of North Karana to purchase some of the legendary combine weapons that they produce. Having traveled the area, and knowing the location of the camp, I offered to help them on their journey. They graciously accepted, and we went on our way.The trip back to the Karanas went rather un-eventful, we chatted a bit on the way, learned a bit about each other and some of our journeys.

Arriving at the camp, we kept a lookout for the various types of Griffon spawn that roamed the area, after a few close calls the ladies each had purchased their weapons. We moved to a safe location to discuss what their plans were from here on out. They all decided that they would return to their homeland for the time being, and needed a guide back to Freeport. Being the generous Ranger that I am, I donated my services again to them. We made our way back to High Hold, where they decided they could make it the rest of the way on their own. Saddened at having to leave these ladies defenseless and without a guide, I bid them farewell.

On my way back to my homeland I thought over the whole trip, and how the Warrior Kylea caught my eye. She was a lively one, and someone that I could really grow to like. Little did I know that she would become my wife that I love to this day. And so we were joined as I had seen, and we have a wonderful life together, many battles have been won and lost together, and I wouldn't have any other as my wife.

-Mathin SilverArrow
