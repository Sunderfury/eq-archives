Stories from Norrath


The sun had just risen in the Jagged Pine forest, this meant I was late. For a Ranger must be ever on the watch for those who would tarnish the sanctity of our home in search of the ever elusive Mammoth, mother of all bears who walk the plains of Norrath. As I fold up my tent and douse the fire that warmed me through the night, I see the druid Tian. He has a gift for his future bride Mirian and will pay me handsomely if I travel to Qeynos to retrieve it for him. Checking my pouch and short on coins, I of course accept the offer.

I traverse the dark cave and enter the Southern Hills of Qeynos, where as usual all is in chaos. Looks like rain, come to think of it, it always looks like rain in Norrath. All praise due to Karana the Rainkeeper and Father of the storm. About to head onto the road and a familiar smell hits me. Its the fragrance of the candles used by the Monks to light the Dojos that they train in. I turn around and follow the aroma, its Mafuyi. A kindred spirit and close friend who has probably gotten me into more trouble than he has gotten me out of. But such is life when you travel as the wind... He has healed nicely from the wounds inflicted upon him by my trusty sword when I was charmed by the evil necromancer Varsoon. We laugh and muse of how one day soon Varsoon and his blasted Skelton Pets will have their day in court. And on that day I shall be the Judge, Jury and well you know the rest...

It seems a traveler was ambushed in Black Burrow by my most hated of enemies the Gnolls; and needs assistance in the retrieval of a few of her personals. A Rangers work is never done, we join up and head to the stronghold to face our fates. We travel deep into the wood and Egrim informs me that we must enter the cave that leads to the heart of the Gnoll encampment and that many have lost their lives and to not let my hatred lead me to folly or death. I calmly nod in acknowledgment but the stench of Gnolls is all around me and I feel my rage begin to boil. I shall not sleep tonight if I do not quench my thirst for the spillage of the blood of the mangy dogs.

The trek down into the abyss goes quite uneventful. Kill a Gnoll here, kill a Gnoll there. hmmf a few silver pieces, hardly worth the fight he put up. heh heh heh. With his belonging firmly in hand we attempt to begin our descent, only to find that we are lost. My natural tracking is almost useless in the underground caverns. Oh how I long for the out of doors and the smell of the coming rain. A crash behind us, the gnoll commander has entered into the fray. Without thinking I react and pounce. Giving him all I can muster, but alas it is not enough. Slash, slash, kick, slash, concentrate. oof a blow to my skull, the blood rushes forth. I stumble and feel my strenght fade, all is about to go black. suddenly my strength is restored, no it has been fortified. I feel stronger than I have ever felt before, what magiks have been bestowed upon me? With renewed strength and the aid of my companions I press on and slay the mighty beast. You will not have my soul today Bertoxxulous. Ahh what is this we find? A nice spiked collar, a leather bag, plenty of room for those long journeys across the wastes and a bit of gold and I think I will take his scalp for memories.

A moment to rest and then I think I will ask someone for assistance in getting out of here. Imagine that me, a Ranger, Tracker extraordinaire asking for help because I am lost, how profound. Oh, I almost forgot, I must hurry, have to bring the statuette back to Tian before dinner. Life is great and for another day I am still alive.

As I head to Qeynos I look back one last time at Black Burrow and think, one day soon I will tear that place down. I will rain upon it with the full might of the Jagged Pine Clan, with the force of a raging Tempest. For nothing can stand in the way of Nature gone mad...

MegaTryphe
Protector of the Jagged Pine
Half-Elf Ranger
