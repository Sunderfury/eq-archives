Guide To Ranger Spells Up To Level Thirty

By Elmarnieh Sysvansansarian
Wood Elf Ranger- Mith Marr

First rank of spells, obtainable at level nine:

Endure Fire: Not the first spell I would pick up at this level if you are short on funds. Resists are nice, but hardly necessary at your level. Perhaps with this up you might fend off one or two more fire-based spells than you would have. At higher levels when you have already higher resists due to equipment this added bonus can be the deciding factor whether or not your skin ignites. I only have this up when I know I will be the target of fire based spells.

Flame Lick: The fourth spell I would by at this level, it reduces your targets ac and does slow damage to them over the course of the spell. When you first get it, it makes an excellent alternative to pulling with your bow and with the added bonus of increasing your chances to hit them due to their lower ac. At higher levels it makes an effective taunt (there are reasons why you want mobs to hit you) and since it uses very little mana it is still a good pulling tool.

Glimpse: One can definitely hold off a while before getting this spell. It pretty much acts as if your character has binoculars on. Yeah, that's it. While its useful in some cases, its not a spell I would have taking up a spell slot. (It might not be a problem now, but once you have a vast array of spells to choose form, you will have to pick the ones you want very carefully based on many factors.) The best use I can think is to get your divination up so that when you get camouflage you actually have a chance to cast it.

Lull Animal: This spell decreases the aggro radius of animals. This spell is one I never bothered to get and I would recommend you do the same. Since most animal types are roaming mobs this spell is very limited in its usefulness.

Minor Healing: Get this spell. I assume you've been tired enough of sitting down waiting to heal up so you can kill the next thing. With this spell you can heal 10 hit points at a time to anyone, reducing your and your group's downtime, making you more effective. GET THIS SPELL.

Skin Like Wood: This is the second spell to get, aside from the comical aspect of buffing someone and informing them that "You now have wood." It's a very useful spell adding a bit to both armor and hit points, this spell will increase your survivability. Don't forget however that you can cast it on others in your party increasing everyone's toughness.

Snare: The third spell I would recommend you get at this level, but one you should get soon. Unlike all the other spells you receive this level, this spell will most likely be with you through your entire journey to 60. It has the effect of slowing a mob's movement speed, allowing you not only to get away from it if you need to run, but also to almost always stay out of melee range. This tactic of attacking a mob following you is called kiting and usually done by spell caster classes, we can do it as well. An added benefit is that when a mob is almost dead it will try to run away from you. A snared mob however will stop in its tracks when it tries to run. This may seem a small benefit now, but later in life it means the difference of killing a mob, and having it run off and bring 20 of its friends to kill your group off.

Second rank of spells, obtainable at level fifteen:

Burst of Fire: While a 15 point direct damage spell is nice, I don't really consider it all that valuable at this level. If you want to root and soften up a mob with it or snare kite one a bit with this spell, you'll have the option. But with a good bow and arrows you should be able to deal more damage out than with this spell, and still have mana for healing up in the downtime.

Camouflage: Get this spell. Invisibility is priceless in a lot of instances. Sneaking past aggro mobs to join a group or get your corpse, or invising the entire party to get further into a zone. Hopefully you have practiced your divination up to a good level by using glimpse at lower levels. Warning though this spell gives a very short warning when it is about to drop and the duration fluctuates wildly at lower levels.

Cure Poison: In my honest opinion this spell is not worth much. It can cure a weak poison easily but by the time you get this spell, it will not really be worth stopping combat to cure the poison. Even near death it would be better to step back and cast a few healing spells yourself. Against any poison stronger than weak the multiple times of casting it will use more mana than healing yourself through the duration.

Feet Like Cat: A very good spell, one I always have up myself. There are many benefits from this spell aside from the obvious increase in ac it will give you. Agility also affects how often your defensive skills will activate. Right now you only have dodge, but soon you will get parry and eventually riposte. A higher agility will mean these go off more often and you will end up taking less damage. Another great thing about this is that druids do not get it, this is a shaman spell. This makes us still able to cast this on party members even when a druid of equal level is present. Other "tanks" are always glad when you increase their ac with this spell.

Grasping Roots: A great spell and I highly recommend that you purchase this one as son as you can. This spell allows you to totally immobilize a target and do some damage as well. Root a mob, back up and fill it full of arrows or dump some burn onto it with spells. It is also useful for "root pulling" and "root parking" which is a technique used to pull excess mobs of your group, root them farther away, and return to your group so that you face less mobs at once.

Invoke Lightning: A 25 point AoE spell. A very useful for crowded situations like orcs in HighHold. Fifty or seventy-five damage in the time it casts is great damage, just watch how many mobs you aggro onto yourself at once. Warning you can harm yourself with this spell so be careful.

Thistle Coat: A good spell at this level giving you increased and damaging the enemy each time they hit you. One point may not seem like much but by the end of the battle it usually adds up to two or three free swings of equivalent damage.

Dance of Fireflies: You will need to get this spell eventually. Since two-thirds of rangers already have infravision and the others most likely have a GLS or equivalent by now, most rangers would most likely not pick this up. Don't make this mistake, this is your ONLY way to practice conjuration before you get the 30th level spell of Stinging Swarm. If you haven't practiced Conjuration up at least to the 100 mark, you will fizzle a great deal and waste large amounts of mana and time trying to get Stinging Swarm to work.

Third rank of spells, obtainable at level twenty-two:

Bind Sight: Useful for watching a puller get mobs and to see where they go so you can learn a zone. Also a great tool for harmony pulling around corners. Play with it, if you get good, you will love this spell.

Enduring Breath: Most useful when you know you will be swimming a lot, and a life saver if you go ld. Once you get to mid thirties you might try hunting in Kedge Keep where you will need this spell and a healthy level in swimming.

Harmony: Another spell that will be making appearances in your spell slots for the rest of your rangerly life. It reduces a mobs aggro radius, but it's an aoe, so it reduces the aggro radius of mobs near your target as well. Harmony a mob standing next to 4 others, flame lick it and he runs right at you, leaving his friends just standing on you. You will love this spell, your groups will love this spell. Ignite: Not a bad spell, a few extra blasts of 37 before a mob reaches you is helpful. That's about all I am going to say about it.

Light Healing: I assume by now there is no real point in telling you to get this spell, unless you like healing yourself ten times for a bar of health. In case I am wrong, GET IT!!

Skin Like Rock: Improved Skin Like Wood, get it, keep it up all the time, buff others with it if possible. Ward Summoned: If you plan on fighting a lot of summoned creatures get it and replace ignite with it, if not then don't bother at all with this spell.

Fourth rank of spells, obtainable at level thirty:

Barb Coat: An upgrade of thistle coat providing more protection and more damage, obviously worth it.

Cancel Magic: Two words, damage shields. Hate taking damage every time you hit the mobs, especially with your really fast dual wielding weapons? Pull with a cancel magic. It has limited use, but when you need it, it is awesome.

Eyes of the Cat: If you're a human get it for Corpse runs, if not, save your plat.

Invigor: Restores lost stamina to your target. Good, especially useful when you are underwater using a Wurmslayer.

Shield of Thistles: When combined with Barb Coat it gives you an eight point damage shield. Pretty potent when you solo and this also allows you to damage shield group-mates before a big fight. Definitely a keeper.

Stinging Swarm: About 120 points of damage over time. This is great for opening a fight or for rooting a mob while hitting it with this and peppering it with arrows. Just watch you don't put it on a mob that the chanter wants to mez.

Strength of Earth: If at this point you don't want an extra 15 strength with you all the time, quit playing a melee. You will also be the love of the group once you can hand a warrior 15 agi 15 str and a damage shield.
