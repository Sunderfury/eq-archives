Stories from Norrath

‘Twas raining this night when I broke my camp. I checked my supplies and was sure I would have enough for some time. The water in my pack and the rabbits I had snared would suffice for this hunting night. Having just reached my fifth circle as a ranger the morning before, I was becoming confident in my skills, and my bow as well. I had just strung a fine one that morning, made from the limb of the majestic elm, with a fine silken string, I was quite sure it would serve me for some time to come. I was not of the skill(OOC-47fletching) t' craft this bow without help from Tunare herself, so I was dually reassured 'twas a blessed weapon.

With no real plan for this night of hunting, I merely wandered the greater faydark, communing with Tunare, experiencing the lavish woods of my homeland. In these meandering I happened upon a camp, with a banner flying that I did not recognize. It had no foul stench, other than some poorly cooked wolf, so my first thought was that it belonged to something other than the orcs. It was uninhabited at the moment so far as I could tell, so I decided to check the sign around me for indication of passing. There were scarce tracks indeed. I found what appeared t' be a hide boot print near a tree by the camp, and decided t' follow.

I was soon rewarded with the sounds of battle. Not far from the camp, I happened upon a fellow elf, in a fray with a bandit of some sort it would appear. He was in dire trouble t' be sure. While he had weakened the villain considerable(OOC <20%health), he was taking more hits than delivering, and looked t' be meeting Tunare soon. Not while my bow was strung and my back not broken! I broke t' within arrow range and let loose a volley. True t' my instincts, the fine bow was blessed. The first shot struck the bandit square, and left her reeling. I closed as fast as my legs would carry me and swung my broadsword hard. It met with a pleasant crunch as it met bone in the deep wound. Upon my last return t' my guildmaster upon reaching the fifth circle, he suggested I may be ready for a new combat tactic. He quickly taught me t' kick my opponents effectively in battle t' gain the advantage. True t' his teachings, I planted a rawhide boot right t' the midsection of the bandit, and I was rewarded with an 'oomph' as the wind was knocked out of her. Just before she turned her attention upon me, she planted one last blow with her dagger t' the elf I was trying t' aid, and alas, he went down hard. With a quick glimpse, I saw he was still breathing, in short, ragged breaths. He was unconscious t' be sure, but such is far better than dead under any circumstances.

I knew I had t' work fast now, lest the bandit deal the killing blow t' him. I thought of trying t' lead her away, but feared she would just turn back upon him. I silently prayed t' Tunare for the soul of the fallen elf, 'Give a few moments t' him, that I may dispatch this wench and aid him'.

The bandit saw I was enraged having seen one of my kin fall, and turned t' make an attempted retreat, but her gods were not watching this night. With her back t' me, I made quick work of her. Although it seemed as if she refused t' die, she just kept crawling away. I swung and missed several times, and repeatedly kicked nothing but air, but in the end, she would fall t' my blade.

I left the loot on her, as it was deservedly the prize of the other elf. With that thought I felt a chill, turned and sprinted back t' where I watched him fall. He was still there, slowing regaining his wits. I sat by him and tended t' my many wounds, lest I be unprepared for another ambush, with him in such a weakened state, I knew he could be of no help. As he finally came around, I was showered with thanks for my aid. I quickly dismissed such pleasantries, as the danger was not completely gone. I advised him t' hide as best he could, and told him once he was concealed from view. I stood watch until he was able t' move, and escorted him back t' our village in the trees.

Once back within the safety of the trees, we made our formal introductions. He was Nighfyer, of the faydark champions. A fellow ranger t' be sure. He offered me a gift for my efforts, and despite repeated refusals, he would eventually convince me t' accept, citing honor of a near fallen comrade, with which I could not dispute. 'Twas a small wooden shield, of which I had seen him wield in the battle. He had not just given me a gift, he had given me part of his battle gear. I thanked him graciously, we shared blessings of Tunare, and parted ways.

I returned t' my campsite. I chose this place carefully, so t' have a majestic view of the huts of Kelethin through the mist each morning. Once prepared, I sat down t' rest for the day. Morning would break soon, and another night of hunting had come t' a close. I took out the shield, and etched the name of the giver upon the outer edge with a dagger. Such a tale this gift would tell. I shall not part ways with it methinks...

Regere D'Aeterno
Wood Elven Ranger of the 10th Circle
Cazic Thule Realm
