The stones were thrown down by the ancient dwarf, as tears of blood streaked down his face. The pain swelled up in his head with a heat as intense as any forge fire in Kaladim. With eyelids sewn shut, this mysterious figure could see images that no other Cleric of Brell could possibly see. He lowers his head and moves his fingers over the stones, shaking hands grasping at images seen only in his mind. Suddenly, he throws up his head and outstretches his arms to the heavens and screams aloud, "Why Great Brell! Fer all of Kaladim, why?"

He came on a Dark Night and foretold a grim prophecy..one that King Kazon Stormhammer would not listen to, one that would foretell his own demise by an assassin's blade. The King, in a false belief of security behind Kaladim's stone walls, was too ignorant to listen. Now upon a funeral pyre he lays, cries of sorrow echoing throughout Kaladim's halls. The joy and wonder of Kaladim now lost, all because Kazon would not listen. Aazimar Coldstone, King of Clan Battlehammer, would listen.

The Old Paladin leaned forward and grabbed the blind seer, catching him before he falls. "What do ye see old one? What makes yur blood run cold?" The shaking seer pulls Aazimar forward and grabs his face. The Old Paladin begins to shake convulsively, as the seer whispers one word. "Learn..."

Upon a battlefield Aazimar stands, with sword drawn dripping in blood. Thousands of bodies are strewn about, some dead while others shake as death grips hold of them. The sounds of Battle can be heard all around, as Dark Warriors drive through Kaladim's forces. Waves of these creatures charge towards the dwarven defenders, in a battle frenzy that looks almost animalistic. All around him, his comrades of many years fall to the onslaught rushing upon Kaladim. A figure in Dark Platemail hurls himself at Aazimar, screaming in raspy tones, "For Innoruuk!" Blades meet and the years of training holds true for Aazimar as a counter thrust drives his sword deep within the creature's chest. A bright light bursts from Aazimar's weapon as the creature hisses and explodes. "In Brell's Name, dey are Undead!"

What seems to be hours Aazimar fights; for Home, for Family and for his God. Although the battle has been long, still wave after wave of undead creatures flow across the Butcherblock country side. The Dwarves fight Valiantly but soon they stand at the Gates of Kaladim itself. "Der is no end to dese Creatures!" Aazimar shouts, "Seal da Gates of Kaladim!"

Above the sound of Battle a giant mechanism can be heard. Gears grinding and giant stones moving from the places set many years ago. Kaladim's Last defense; a hundred tons of solid stone that when sealed cannot be opened. The "Gate" as it is known, is the Dwarven City's last defense, to be closed when all else fails and up until this point has never been used.

Slowly the mountain itself slides to keep out the onslaught. "Argh!" the old paladin screams, as an arrow shaft can be seen sticking out of his Chestplate. Aazimar reaches down and tries to pull it out, but his strength is lost. Darkness begins to overcome him as he feels himself falling. His last sight, the last thing he sees before he finally falls, is his assailant.

Upon a Black Horse a Creature rides, noticeably above the throngs of fowl creatures. In his hands he carries a dark bow that glows with an evil green light. This figure is covered from head to toe in black, but his face can be seen beneath his cowl. Upon the figure's lips, Aazimar sees him utter two words..."For Innoruuk." The old seer's face comes into view, as Aazimar whipes a cold sweat from his brow. He is shaken by this image...an Image of what may be or an Image of what WILL come.

Only Brell knows for sure.

Aazimar does know one thing as he begins to gather his thoughts. The face of the Dark Figure on horseback, the one that cut his life from him in battle. Aazimar met this person not too long ago and remembers his name..."Terrak".
