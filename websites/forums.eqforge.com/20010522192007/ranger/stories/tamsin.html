Stories from Norrath

'Twas a wonderful day in Kelethin... I felt quite proud in having just finished a set of Patchwork armor for myself. Having only been a part of the Ranger Guild for Three Seasons (though nearing my Fourth), I was still quite inexperienced in the ways of the wood, but this accomplishment gave me renewed confidence in my skills.

I set out from Kelethin late that evening to track more Black wolves. Several young adventurers had approached me about crafting them armor, and I needed the pelts to begin. I set out from the main lift and quickly passed the stone ruins. Crossing through the gully I set out on the track of a wily wolf. As I tracked I took time to forage for food, finding a variety of berries and an occasional rabbit.

Within a short time, I had gathered several pelts, but so lost in my task was I that I had lost track of time and direction (my ability to sense direction under the thick canopy of trees is still quite poor). Darkness had fallen and I was unsure in which direction my beloved city in the trees lay. To make matters worse, I had crossed paths with a growing number of those foul Orcs...

I quickly found a few trees and knelt down to gather my bearings... I picked up the trail of several Orcs, some of them with deeper prints than others. I had a growing feeling of dread that I had wandered close to Crushbone. Keeping close to the cover of trees, I began following a large ridgeline in what I guessed was a southerly direction.

It was then that I crossed paths with a young maiden as she finished off a vile Orc. She, a young Druid whose name escapes me, had become separated from her group and was waiting for them to return for her. As we sat and spoke as she rested from her battle, she confirmed that I was at Crushbone. Luckily, she was able to point me in the general direction of Kelethin. I offered to help her avoid Orcs and make it back to Kelethin, but she wanted to wait for her companions.

With that, we wished good fortune to one another and I set out in the general direction of Kelethin. I quickly came upon what looked to be tents or some other temporary structures. Fearing the worst, I set off at a run rather than to investigate further, startling quite a number of brave souls as I rushed by.

As I grew tired, I slowed back to a quick walk, and headed in what seemed to be a southerly direction. I took care to dodge most of the Orcs I could locate in the area, though a few Orc Pawns did bloody the blade of my father's tarnished scimitar. Soon I crossed a well worn path that I recognized as leading back to Kelethin, and not long after the welcome sound of the city lifts met my ears.

Dawn was just peeking through the canopy of trees, as I rode back up to the safety of the city. Having had a long night in the wood, I made my way to a variety of merchants to sell what items I had gathered and to buy the materials necessary to attempt fletching my first arrows. I figured that would give me a much needed reprieve and allow me time to contemplate my mistakes during the night...

Tamsin of Kelethin
Wood Elf Ranger of Four Seasons
