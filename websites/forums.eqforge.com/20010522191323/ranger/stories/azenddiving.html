Stories from Norrath

Having died at the deepest part of the pool and leaving all his worldly goods on his corpse Azend [5th season at this time] awakes near the campfire of Surefall Glade.

How can he ever hope to recover his lost wealth and equipment? His corpse lies at the bottom of the pool and the pool at the bottom of a great fall. What is worse the pool is at the lowest point of Blackburrow and if he should have the huge fortune to find it, the greater fortune to recover his belongings and the unbelievable luck to evade the razor fish that inhabit the pool then he will have to climb his way up through through the bowels of his enemies' camp. Leaving all this aside he resolves to make an attempt and departs post haste for Blackburrow. Upon arriving there he meets an old friend by the name of Vivian [lev 10 Magician] and her companion Tokusan 10th season ranger] so he asks for their assistance.

"I would help thee but I am afeared of dying myself" says Vivian "Indeed my body already lies in the pool yet I know of noone who has ever made it out alive". Not to be dissuaded so easily, and indeed now relishing the challenge, Azend stands at the head of the falls that plummet into the pool hundreds of feet below and ponders his destiny briefly before plunging into the chasm. [hitting f9 and watching himself from a distance for the full effect]

The blow as he hits the water removes all air from his lungs and he breaks the surface briefly to recover before kicking to the bottom as fast as he can. He realises now what Vivian was afraid of as he spies bodies too numerous to count littering the floor of the pool. Not daring to look around for the dreaded razorfish lest it give the monster an opportunity to savage him he scans the bottom of the pool, at last finding his body. Quickly he begins removing all the equipment from it yet the items are too numerous, the pool too deep and the air in his lungs is all but gone. He hastily crams the items into his pack realising he has no time to equip his shield and sword, let alone don his paltry armour. As he fits the last item in he turns and kicks for the surface with all his might and just as he thinks his lungs will surely explode he is breating air again. [We're talking last pixel on the air gauge]

At this point he feels something tug at his leg then realises that it is the razorfish which has just taken a large chunk out of his thigh. Trying hard not to panic about the lack of any armour or equipment he spins looking for a way to climb out of the pool, strikes for shore and finally hauls himself from the mire bleeding profusely and heaving for breath. He rests briefly to recover his wits and then readies himself for the climb through this labyrinth of evil. The climb is long and arduous and he encounters many vicous Gnolls along the way, many of which he is far too weak to contemplate engaging in battle although his rangers' instinct makes his blood boil at their mere existence.

At last he emerges from the foul pit and is able to tell his friend Vivian of his terrifying ordeal. She is amazed at his foolhardiness but congratulates him all the same and he departs for the sanctity of The Glade with a self-satisfied smile on his face.
