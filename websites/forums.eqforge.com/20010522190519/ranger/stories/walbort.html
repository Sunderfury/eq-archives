Stories from Norrath

Walbort's face was grim as he conjured a night's supply of bread and water, packing them into his belt pouch as they appeared. How had he gotten himself into this? After his first- and last, he'd sworn at the time- experience in Befallen, complete with so many deaths that Brell Sirilis himself had scolded him before restoring his life the sixth and final time before his hasty departure, he had fully expected never again to set foot inside its unholy walls. And yet when Royal, the friend of his youth, had come to him with the request for assistance in his quest for a mighty blade found only there in the possession of a demonic warrior, he had allowed himself to believe for a moment that it wouldn't be so bad this time. After all, Royal, who he had once stood watch over as he struggled to kill gnome skeletons, now far outmatched him both in skill and power. His full suit of bronze plate armor gleamed in the light cast by his shiny brass shield- surely he'd be safe with someone like that. Besides, Rolin Anduril would be coming along too- he of the magical tomahawk, lightning-fast whip, and swift kick to a monster's derriere. With bodyguards like them, he could count on being safe no matter how deep they descended into Befallen's bowels, so he agreed to go. But now that the time had arrived, his bravado had evaporated, replaced only by the desperate need to retain control of his bladder in the face of suffocating panic. A shout came from the nearby gates of Freeport: "Casting Spirit of the Wolf for donations!" Just the thing! He ran to the gates and received the prized enchantment, although the druid didn't seem to appreciate his reference to it afterwards as "Spirit of the Chicken". Lupine spirit swelling within, the ground began to fly past his feet at he climbed the nearby foothills for the run to Befallen.

It was dusk, so as he approached, an unnatural green light shone from the entrance. Swallowing hard, he thumbed through his spellbook and reviewed the spells he was planning to use- an assortment of spells designed to allow him to make a token stand while offering as many options as possible for rapid retreat. Healing, to mend the multiplicity of wounds he expected to receive. Symbol of Transal, for that little extra endurance that might keep Rolin and Royal alive long alive to allow him to run away in unreasoning hysteria. Expulse Undead, in case retreat somehow became impossible. Spook the Dead struck him as somewhat hypocritical, since they couldn't POSSIBLY be as afraid of him as he was of them. Invisibility to Undead, AKA "Out of Sight, Out Of Mind". Root, AKA "Simon Says Stop!". Gate, AKA "I meant to Root, I swear! Did you guys die?". And of course, Divine Aura- "What, they're hitting you? Funny, I'm not getting hurt..." So deep was his reverie that he ran right past Royal, who sat waiting for him inside the entrance. He might have betrayed his unease when Royal tapped him on the shoulder as he ran by, causing him to drop his hammer in fright and let out a high-pitched scream. Rolin and Royal looked on bemusedly as he gathered up his weapon again. They went inside a little deeper, then began to cast their incantations of protection on each other. Walbort was a little short in the cash department, so he made the somewhat miserly suggestion that they delay casting Symbol until further inside to avoid depleting his supply of Cat's Eye Agates, the reagent needed for the spell.

Desperate to delay further progress, he commenced a long charade of elaborate meditation, reporting intermittently that his progress had been slowed by the oppressive evil of the building. When they would no longer accept his protestations of unpreparedness, he rose to his feet and followed them to the first locked door, the hollow clang from his knocking, banded-armored knees echoing within the cavernous main hall. They picked way through the collapsed rubble to the second floor, then unlocked and opened the door to the third and final level. Rolin sprang through the door, bow at the ready, but the room was strangely empty. Walbort's face recovered a bit of the color it had lost as they had descended this far. A full group of hardy adventurers stood nearby, keeping watch for the appearance of further unholy creatures- they must be the ones who had secured this area. The three of them descended the stairs and turned right. Royal began to run his arm along the wall, feeling for the proper spot. Suddenly, his arm appeared to be halfway swallowed up by the wall, and Walbort let out an involuntary yelp as he prepared to flee. Royal only grinned fiercely at him, then stepped further into the wall and disappeared. Suddenly Walbort felt his surroundings blur around him- not another one of those migraines again...

He shook his head and the cobwebs miraculously cleared. His eyes grew wide as he realized he was alone in the hallway! Now where had Royal stepped through? Here? Ouch...guess not. Maybe it was... Nope, not there either. Sighing, he leaned against the wall to ponder his hopeless situation- then tumbled backwards through the illusory section and landed in a heap inside the shadow knight's lair. Royal and Rolin just shook their heads and marched on. Another group was keeping this area free of the numerous undead which rose from Innoruuk's lair to haunt it, and one of their number inquired as to Royal's intentions. Upon hearing that he intended to slay the shadow knight in order to gain possession of his fearsome blade, the mysterious benefactor warned that should the shadow knight detect a presence within the room containing his portal of entry from the nether realms, he may choose not to show himself. Hearing this, they all retreated to the mouth of the room and began to watch and wait, Rolin periodically putting his ear to the ground to listen for signs of distant monster activity.

Before long, the walls were starting to close in on Walbort. He sat crosslegged on the ground with his arms wrapped tightly around himself, rocking back and forth and repeating "Brell preserve me...Brell preserve me..." under his breath until Rolin flicked a grub at him in annoyance. As he picked the slimy creature out of his beard - curse that ranger aim! - he failed to notice both that his enchantment of invisibility had worn off and the huge skeleton bearing down on him from behind as a result. Faster than any eye could follow, Rolin's bow was in his hands, sending an arrow singing past the surpsied cleric's ear, knifing through the unliving servant of death. Royal's sword soon swung into action as well, and seeing that the skeleton was now doomed, Walbort felt enough strength return to his rubbery arms to swing his hammer a few times before the inevitable victory. Rolin spat, "Keep your encantation up next time- and clean up this mess!" Royal said nothing, as usual, but his eyes remained kind, as befit his virtuous nature. Cowed, Walbort pulled the meager items of value from the corpse and stuffed them into a backpack, kicking the shattered bones off against a nearby wall. Time passed, and despite a few other conflicts of a startlingly similar origin, the shadow knight had still not made his appearance. Rolin grew restless, continually glancing back the way they'd come and hinting that the wide expanse of the Karanas might suit him better, and soon. Even Royal's wise face grew troubled- could it be that they'd not complete the quest this night?

Suddenly Rolin's features snapped into a mask of intense concentration. "Inside!", he cried, and barrelled into the shadow knight's lair, followed closely by Royal. Peeking around the corner after them, Walbort beheld a creature right out of his most unspeakable nightmares. A spectre of pure evil, the troll's already sinister features were darkened further by the light of his unholy magicks, and this putrid package appeared to be wrapped in a shiny armored shell! His skeleton pet fought by his side, cackling madly, and a tough-looked ghoul joined in as well. Rolin's initial entrance meant that the three of them were exclusively focused on seperating him from his extremities, and despite his catlike agility, their blows were too-often finding their mark. "Heel!", he yelled abruptly. Walbort looked down in alarm- was something about to lay siege to his feet? Rolin lost all patience, desperation fueling his voice, "HEAL MY WOUNDS YOU @^%$#@@%$#@ MIDGET!!!!!"

At the sight of his comrades now in mortal danger, Walbort's fear suddenly evaporated, replaced only by the determination to preserve their lives at any cost. Raising his hands, he cast a binding incantation on the shadow knight, fixing him in place, then spooked his pet into a rapid retreat. Turning to Rolin, he allowed the mystical energies within him to take the form of healing power, which he turned full-force on the wiry ranger. His wounds quickly began to mend, cuts closing, bones knitting back together again, strength pouring back into strained muscles. "Yes!", Rolin cried as the next wave of healing arrived- it was as if no blow had ever been struck! The shadow knight's green eyes widened a bit as he sensed the tide of battle begin to turn. He growled a command to the ghoul, and it turned to rain blows on the diminutive cleric. "Stand and fight, friends, I am in no danger!", Walbort cried. It was something of a falsehood, since a visual inspection of the ghoul revealed him to be more than a match for the dwarf physically, but the strong banded armor Walbort wore was doing a good job of diminishing the impact of the many blows the ghoul landed on him- he would live long enough to save his friends, of that he was now sure. His attempt to spook it only added to its rage, so he settled into an exchange of blows with the evil beast. The battle raged on, but the healing power he directed at Rolin, still the apparent sole target of both shadow knight and pet, kept him safe and vigorous.

Suddenly, the shadow knight broke free of the binding spell and began to flee as quickly as his wounds allowed. Rolin and Royal uttered shouts of triumph, beating mercilessly at the troll's weaker back armor, until finally the evil warrior screamed a final furious cry and fell, dead. Seeing that the shadow knight had been defeated, Rolin ran back to Walbort's side, and with a few vicious attacks, eliminated the remaining danger. From behind them, Royal bellowed, "It is here! The sword is mine!" They found him clutching the shadow knight's grim sidearm- strangely, he had never drawn the awesome weapon, choosing instead to fight them with a rusty longsword he carried as well. His apparent disain for their abilities had cost him dearly. The nearby adventurers crowded around them, staring at the mighty weapon and voicing their congratulations, which the excited warriors responded to with thanks for their kind assistance.

Walbort stood a short distance away from the mad celebration and quietly enchanted himself back to full health. He caught Royal's eye for a moment, gave him a small wave and a smile, which Royal returned broadly, brandishing the weapon in exultation. Even Rolin cracked a sarcastic grin in his direction- "Keep an eye on that heel, priest." Walbort laughed at his folly, the thrill of shared triumph erasing all traces of embarrassment. "Till we meet again, my friends...", he said, as the tendrils of Gate began to wind around him. It had not been such a bad trip after all...
