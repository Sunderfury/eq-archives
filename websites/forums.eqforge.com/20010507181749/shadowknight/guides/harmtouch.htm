Harm Touch Tactics

Beld Demonblade of Fennin Ro
For starters, Harm Touch is NOT meant to help you kill things you could not normally take out. It is there to help you survive in a situation where things have gotten out of hand, but not extraordinarily so. This said, trying to use HT as a panic button is not the best way of utilizing it; my goal here is to explain some of the strategies I've found that maximize HT's effectiveness.

For the uninitiated, HT is an attack, useable by a shadow knight once per game day, that deals 10 * player level + 1 in damage to the target. i.e. a level 20 shadow knight can HT for 201 damage at maximum. Like all such attacks that deal damage, it is susceptible to being resisted by the target. However, after a great deal of experimentation with HT I have decided that it acts more like a melee attack than a DD spell; your position in relation to the target COUNTS. Also important is timing, which i will get into first:

The most important part of HT is teaching yourself the proper time to use it. It is a little difficult to explain, you just have to practice on your own and sort of develop an instinct for it. For starters, DON'T PANIC! EverQuest somehow seems to know when you are freaking out and hitting the HT button out of desparation, and you often end up with a resisted spell and an angry critter. You have to stay calm, at least until after you have used it :) If you are hunting in a new area, try using HT on a typical monster while you are in a safe fight (preferably grouped) just to test how much of the target's life is blasted off by your hit. Now you know how weak that particular monster has to be before a harm touch will finish it off. Now, if you get into a fight with too many monsters, you can blast one of them to death as soon as you have melee'd it down enough, and move on to the next. Just remember to only use it as an attack when the situation grows dire; if you can't kill it WITHOUT using harm touch, you shouldn't be fighting it! You are welcome to battle creatures this way, but you are only going to get yourself killed eventually, and probably in a messy way :) In addition to using HT as an attack, it is also one of your most effective taunts. If a group member is being mauled and in dire need of help, you can use your HT to anger the monster and pull it off on you. Just make sure you have enough HP remaining to survive the monster's ire! Using HT as a direct taunt works consistently until around lvl 30 or so, when monsters start getting HP much faster than your HT goes up. At this point, in order to taunt off of your group members, you will need to use other annoyances, such as taunt skill, darkness spells, and disease cloud to get yourself high enough on the monster's hate list for HT to put you over the top.

When using HT from the target's rear, and especially while it is fleeing or Fear'ed, I can consistently deal full damage on targets that are as many as 10 levels higher than me. This is in contrast to a wizard's direct damage spells, which don't care what direction you face the enemy from, and are virtually impossible to have hit an npc of 6 levels higher for full damage. This does sound like a simple way to use HT, but in combat it can be tricky to get around behind the enemy sometimes. If you are grouped, my suggestion is to lay off the enemy for a moment so that it builds up hate with another group member, allowing you to circle behind it without angering it. Remember to stop taunting and casting spells at this time! :) If you are fighting solo, it can be trickier. Don't count on your pet to hold the target's attention (you AREN'T trying to use it as a tank, are you?); drop a darkness spell on it, cast Fear, and pop it in the back once it starts running. If you are below lvl 15 and do not yet have Clinging Darkness and Fear, don't worry; until this point you SHOULD be able to solo without them anyway, if you are doing well :)

At very high level, the effectiveness of harm touch displays diminishing returns. When your typical monsters have thousands of HP, a few hundred points are not going to count for too much. Don't fret; as a shadow knight, your spells and sword should allow you to drop plenty of damage on your target anyways. It's just that harm touch will become less and less useful, except as a taunt and as a way of eliminating annoying low level casters that may accompany the higher level monsters you want to fight. There really isn't much to reccomend it at very high level, except that having an extra 400 to 500 points of damage dealt, instantly, can occasionally save your life. HT can still be a very powerful tool in PvP, however ;)

A brief note on using HT in PvP situations: many players, especially casters, are not used to having VERY large chunks of their life suddenly blasted off of them all of a sudden. I have seen a wizard suddenly gate out of a duel after bearing the brunt of a HT, even though he knew it was coming, and in my opinion was in a good position to win. In these cases, not only is HT a valuable damage tool in a duel, it is most effective for its psych-out factor. If you can panic your opponent, you can probably win, regardless of your relative levels. Just be careful when fighting a paladin, who can cancel your HT with a timely application of Lay on Hands. You will have to win a game of chicken, wait until they have already used LoH (and hope you have enough HP left) or find some way to keep them from using LoH altogether. I leave that part up to you ;)

Good luck, now go out there and kill something in the name of Innoruuk!
