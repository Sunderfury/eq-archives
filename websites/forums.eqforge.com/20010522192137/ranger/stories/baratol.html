Stories from Norrath

I’d always known that BlackBurrow was a dangerous place. I’d always ignored that fact. One day, not too long ago, I learned just how dangerous BlackBurrow really was. That was also the day I discovered what a true friend really is. It all started one bright morning when I was a ranger of the 7th circle…

I awaken to the bright rays of the dawn sun. The light shines down into Surefall Glade and creates flickering patterns through the tree leaves. I stand up and stretch out a bit, then pack up my bedroll. I pass by Hager Sureshot and nod as I head for Jarse Kedison. After purchasing some supplies, I sit on the barrels in front of the cabin and pull out my fletching kit. For the next ten minutes, I busy myself making some arrows for use in the coming day. I then place the completed arrows in my quiver and head into the cabin. Sitting at the table is my friend and fellow ranger Pirelis Windbearer. He waves me over and I see that he is studying some kind of old map.

"You see this map, Baratol?" he asks.

"Hmm. What about it?"

"It’s a map of BlackBurrow. I was digging through the druid archives and I found this." He points to a mark on the map. "Here is an old treasure vault. Want to do some treasure hunting?"

"Um…well. I’ve been in that accursed place before, Pirelis, it’s full of gnolls."

"Oh come on, Baratol, don’t be such a wimp. A few mangy dogs aren’t going to be able to best the both of us!"

Reluctantly, I agree with Pirelis and we immediately set off. As we pass through the cave leading out of our home, we go over some battle strategies. We agree that since I am a tad stronger and more experienced than Pirelis, I will be in front as we explore the caverns. We emerge from the caves and pause a moment to get our bearings. I point to the southeast and we silently trudge off. After a few minutes of travel, we arrive at the entrance to the gnolls’ lair. A giant gnoll head carved out of the stone marks the entrance. I look over and notice that Pirelis doesn’t seem so brave anymore. Chuckling to myself, I enter the cave and pass into the gnolls’ home.

We emerge into a small valley within the surrounding mountains. We are greeted by a gnoll who thumps to the floor in front of us bleeding from several deep wounds. I look up and see a weary paladin kneel down and loot the corpse. Carefully stepping around, we make our way to the cavern entrance as indicated by the map. I pull open the door and see a scrawny gnoll sitting on a log chewing on a chunk of wolf meat. I draw back my bow and let an arrow fly. The shaft digs itself deep into the gnoll’s thigh. He howls and draws a short sword, then charges straight at me. I quickly swing my bow around my shoulder and ready my trusty minotaur axe and shield. The gnoll wildly chops at my head and I easily deflect the blow with my shield. An arrow soars by and catches the gnoll in the shoulder. Out of the corner of my eye, I see Pirelis nock another arrow. The gnoll staggers backwards and turns to run. I rear back and deliver a mighty blow between the gnoll’s shoulder blades. It chokes up some blood and drops to the floor. I pick up the sword and put it in my pack, then split up the few coppers it had with Pirelis. "See? No problem!" he says. I mutely nod my head and we continue in deeper.

We follow the map and dispatch the few scrawny gnolls who attempt to stop us. We turn the final bend and come up face to face with a patrolling gnoll. Both of us loose arrows, which catch the gnoll in the stomach. Howling in pain, the filthy creature turns and runs away. Pirelis grins and pulls open the door to the room. Inside, we see several small chests lying on the floor. Pirelis pulls one open and gasps in astonishment. I peer over his shoulder and blink at the pile of gold coins lying within. Pirelis stands up and starts cheering, then cuts short. I spin around and choke at the sight of two very large gnolls standing in the doorway. The closer one bellows, "You half-elves aren’t welcome here!" and charges straight at me. I brace for the impact and it slams into me at full force. My shield straps snap and the protective wooden circle spins away. The gnoll raises up a wicked-looking bastard sword and brings it straight down. I deftly roll to the side and the sword crashes down on top of a chest. I look up and see that Pirelis seems to be having a hard time. The other gnoll has him backed into a corner and is chopping away, while my friend desperately tries to fend off the blows. I lash out with my foot and I hear a cracking sound as my foot connects with the gnoll’s knee. He growls in pain and shifts his weight slightly. I kick out and knock the gnoll back and he trips over a chest. I jump up and look over at Pirelis. The gnoll confronting him knocks Pirelis’s sword aside and bashes Pirelis across the face. Dazed, my friend lets his guard down for a moment. Seizing the opportunity, the gnoll drives his sword deep within Pirelis’s chest. "NOO!" I scream, and blindly rush at the gnoll, swinging wildly. The gnoll drops down on top of Pirelis, and I am roughly shoved forward from behind. There is a searing pain in the back of my leg, and I stumble into the wall. The large gnoll I knocked over before stands before me with my blood dripping from its sword. Drool drips from its mouth, and he licks his lips as he advances towards me. The pain from my leg shoots up my body and my vision fades. My last thought is "I wonder what the afterlife is like?"

I awaken after what seems to be an eternity, and to my disappointment, discover that I am not in the afterlife. In fact, I’m still in the same room. The only thing different is that the gnoll is now lying on the floor in front of me choking for breath. I crawl over to the corner and pull the dead gnoll off of Pirelis. His face is pale, and his breathing is shallow and ragged. Blood pours out of his chest wound. He looks over at me and gasps, "Guess it wasn’t so easy after all." I smile briefly and say, "Hold on, Pirelis, I’ll get help." He quietly shakes his head and leans back. His breathing slows, then stops. I stare blankly at the corpse, then start sobbing. A feeling of sorrow and hopelessness fills my entire being. Suddenly, a voice cuts through my haze. "I’m sorry, I couldn’t get here fast enough." I look up and see a beautiful female wood elf wearing enchanter’s robes standing in the doorway. Wondering why angels wear enchanter’s robes, I slide to the floor and black out.

My head throbs as a gentle light shines through my closed eyes. My leather cloak has been draped over me. In the center of the room, a lit lantern shines throughout the room. I slowly sit up, and glance around the rest of the room. The wood elf is in the opposite corner, meditating. Pirelis’s corpse has been covered with his cloak. The gnoll corpses are neatly lined up against the wall to my left. My equipment lies in the center of the room, as well as the things the gnolls had been carrying. I reach down to my leg and find that my wound has been bandaged. I shift and lean up against the wall. The wood elf opens her eyes and looks at me. I catch a nearly imperceptible flicker in her eyes, then they resolve into an emotionless state. "I am Verylia Celestias of Kelethin," she states. I start, and pause a moment before replying, "Uh…I am Baratol Alaryn of Surefall Glade. Um, pleased to make your acquaintance." Verylia smiles gently, and says, "Same. I am sorry about your friend. When I heard the sounds of combat, I rushed over, but not quickly enough." I glance over at the shrouded corpse of my friend and pause to collect my thoughts. "I just wish we had not been so foolish as to delve so deeply into this place." Verylia nods silently and says nothing. "Still, I…I should b…bury h…his…corpse." I struggle to hold back my tears and cup my face in my hands. After a few minutes, I manage to regain control of myself and slowly stand up. While stiff, my leg will support my weight. I gather my equipment and sling my pack over my back. Verylia stands and picks up her staff. She chants some words of magic, and my muscles tighten briefly, and I feel a new strength surging through me. Smiling my thanks, I bend over and lift the corpse of my friend over my shoulder.

We travel back towards the surface, and stop when we hear the sounds of combat nearby. I gently lower the corpse to the ground, and ready my bow. I step around the corner into total chaos. Several fellow adventurers have taken it upon themselves to stir up a hornet’s nest of gnolls. I count at least fifteen of those horrid creatures. Two of the gnolls are lying on the floor, gasping for breath. The adventurers fare worse, as three of their six are down. Whispering a prayer to Karana, I nock an arrow and release. The shaft flies true, burying itself within the neck of a gnoll. A strong-looking gnoll turns from the adventurer it had been hacking at and turns towards me. Immediately, it chokes and collapses to the floor, a dagger stuck in its back. The rogue nods and returns to the rest of the melee. Verylia casts a spell and a gnoll chokes and sputters. The creature turns and rushes, but I intercept its path with a well-placed blow to the shoulder. It turns towards me angrily and a brief clash ensues. I easily best the gnoll and look over to the rest of the battle. A cleric has been knocked to the floor and a paladin is desperately fending off three more gnolls. Verylia casts a spell and a glaze settles over a gnoll’s eyes. It howls in desperation and the paladin cleaves his mighty two-handed sword through the gnoll. I rush up from behind and slice a gnoll from shoulder to kidney. The few remaining gnolls rush away, screaming in defiance. I manage to pick off a couple with my bow, but some still escape my wrath.

When I finally return to the light of day, my clothing and equipment has been bloodied and dirtied from the multiple melees. Verylia has a few more dings in her hardwood staff, but is otherwise unharmed. I dig a shallow grave for my friend and place his body within. I place his equipment on the overturned dirt. Kneeling, I close my eyes and murmur a short prayer. We then pace to a small copse of trees to rest and recover.

I have closed my eyes for but a moment when my sensitive ranger senses catch a whiff of the gnolls’ stench. My eyes burst open and I shout out, "Alert!" With a growl, several gnoll scouts rush towards us. Verylia fumbles for her staff, but I see she will not reach it in time. In desperation, I hurl my axe with all my strength. The well-forged blade cuts through the air and buries itself in the gnoll’s chest. I quickly draw my bow and fire an arrow into a gnoll’s leg. The thing stumbles to the floor and yowls pitiably. Verylia grabs her staff in time to ward off a blow from a gnoll. She counters with a swift blow to the thing’s snout, knocking it back. Too late, I catch sight of the gnoll archer a way off. Its arrow pierces through the air, whistling towards Verylia. The sharp point digs into her shoulder and drives her to the ground. I return fire, and manage to drive it off. Three more gnolls carefully advance at me, while one more stands back. I grab hold of my trusty axe and turn to face them. They come at me with carefully coordinated blows, and I am forced up against a tree. I shout to Verylia "Run! I’ll hold them off as long as I can!" She fades into some of the surrounding trees. I hope I can fend the gnolls long enough for her to reach safety. One gnoll slips through my defense, and its sword nicks my ribs. I wince and my axe wavers. Sensing weakness, the gnoll swings again. At that moment, a green aura swirls about it and the gnoll abruptly staggers back, then falls dead. The next gnoll’s eyes suddenly cloud with fear, and it sprints away. The gnoll in back scowls, and chants a spell. Blue sparkles fill the air about me, and I feel my magical strength leave. I wilt slightly, then kick the gnoll in front of me squarely in the chest. It falls backwards on top of a sword of one of its fallen fellows. Verylia steps out besides me, and I’m sure that if she had not stayed, I would have been killed. She and the gnoll shaman begin casting spells simultaneously. Its spell resolves first, causing that same blue sparkle about her. I wince inwardly as I know this will remove the magical shielding used by spell-casters for protection. Her spell resolves next, and several large tendrils poke up and grasp the gnoll’s feet. I release an arrow. The shaft flies straight at the panic-stricken gnoll. It drops to the floor, arrow protruding from its eye. Weak from blood loss, I slump to the floor. I look up at Verylia. I blink and stare in shock as I realize something is very wrong. She is a dark elf. She must have noticed something in my eyes, as she looked down at herself. Verylia closes her eyes for a moment, then looks straight at me, pain evident in her eyes. "I’m sorry, Baratol. It was a necessary deception." She casts a spell, and a magical gate opens up before her. "Wait! It matters not!" I call out to the last flickering remnants of magic dissipating into the air. I have never seen her since.

To this very day, I harbor a special hatred towards the gnolls. In the same painful day, they cost me two true friends through their evil actions. I will not rest until the day that miserable race is wiped from the face of Norrath.

    Tale by Baratol Alaryn of Surefall Glade, Ranger of the 12th Circle (Erollisi Marr)
