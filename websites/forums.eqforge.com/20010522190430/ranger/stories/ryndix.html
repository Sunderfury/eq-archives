Stories from Norrath

Ryndix wiped Vengeance, his axe, and Retribution, his blade, clean. Glancing at the surrounding caves in Runnyeye, he looked for tracks of beasts that might disturb Alluro's meditation, because Tunare knew he'd need the stout druids healing before he bled to death.

Alluro stood and looked over his companions wounds. Clucking his tongue, he remarked how Ryndix shouldn't be so reckless in the heat of battle. Ryndix shrugged off the comment and closed his eyes for the healing to begin. Alluro raised his hands and prayed for Tunare to guide her healing graces through him. Blue lights surrounded Alluro's hands as he laid them on Ryn's wounds. Immediately the light began to bind his wounds, and the sounds of bones popping into place echoed throughout the caverns.

"My legs no longer feel as boisterous as they once were friend," remarked Ryndix. Alluro sighed, he didn't know how often a day he had to do this for his party members, or for others who would promise money for the enchantment, or for others who just begged for it until he cast it upon them just to shut them up. "We'll have to go outside for this, I need the fresh air and the sky for the Spirit of the Wolf to enter you," Alluro said.

They made their way up through the canverns, passing by Goblins who were too afraid to touch them for their reputation as great Goblin Slayers. One brave goblin with a rusty weapon in hand stood in front of Ryndix and growled. Ryn scoffed at the goblins foolishness, and drew Vengeance and Retribution. He closed his eyes and mumbled the spidery words of magic as flames leapt from his hands to encase the goblin. Alluro mumbled something about meeting outside when Ryn was finished, and proceeded out of the caverns. The goblin shrieked and ran toward Ryndix, weapon flailing wildly. Ryn easily parried and dodged the weak rain of steel that was assualting him. He swung and reversed, swung again and reversed, each swing bringing more pain for the goblin and bringing more terror to the eyes of his evil comrades watching him. With one mighty kick, Ryndix launched the goblin across the cavern where it hit the wall and collapsed in a lifeless heap. Ryn sauntered over and picked through the things money pouch for spare change, then took its weapon and destroyed it, so that other goblins wouldn't be able to use against him or his friends again.

Ryn's eyes widened and he placed his ear to the ground. He could feel a vibration as if many feet were running in his direction. Just then someone from deeper in the cavern shouted, 'TRAIN." Ryn cursed and healed his scratches for the onslaught that was approaching. From around the corner he saw 3 harried, young adventurers running for their lives from 2 dazed goblin guards. Ryndix pulled his bow and shot as fast as he could, trying to make the guards come his way. The arrows sailed through the cavern and took one guard in the neck and it went down. It had obviously been hurt very badly already to die so quickly. Ryn then mumbled the words of magic again and set a snare around the other guard. The adventurers thanked him as they ran past him. He made a show of setting himself in front of the guard and waiting for it to arrive, so that the young adventurers would think him brave. As soon as the young ones rounded the corner to the surface, he turned and began to run, knowing he could never take a dazed guard alone...

Suddenly his feet became rooted to the ground. He didn't see the shaman coming up slowly behind the guard, and he cursed his poor observation of the situation. He turned ready to face his oppenents, but he knew he would never survive... alone. Mentally he called out for Alluro to come and help him escape death yet again. Alluro responded that he'd be there as fast as possible. Then the fight began. The guard came at him with a slow, but powerful vengeance, constantly making large wounds. The shaman sat back and casted at his will. Spells of ice and poison and plague assualted Ryndix. He parried and swung as best as he could, but it was obvious that the battle would be lost soon if Alluro failed to show. He felt the wounds taking their toll, and he began to slump slightly. His blows were sluggish and un-accurate. The guard raised his fist for a final mashing of his skull as time seemed to slow down. The guard laughed maniacally as his fist crept toward Ryn's head, the shaman still uttering words of pain and suffering.

Then a light as the guards fist exploded from his hand and hit the shaman in the head, disturbing his casting. Ryn looked behind him to see a wolf casting another spell to bring the goblins pain just before he collapsed in a heap. Alluro made flame and swarms surround the guard over and over, and when the guard turned tail to run, so did the shaman. But Alluro would have none of that, vines and roots came up from the cavern ground to grasp the guard. Alluro ran up and began to bite the guard with his gnashing teeth. The guard fell, but the shaman got away. Alluro prayed to Tunare again and laid his hands on Ryndix who awoke with a start, and together they limped to the surface.
