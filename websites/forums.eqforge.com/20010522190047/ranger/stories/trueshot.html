Stories from Norrath

The Trueshot Longbow quest is very much in vogue right now, and for good reason as the bow is a very good one. My introduction to the quest was when Tanthilas approached me with the materials for the bow, asking if I'd make the attempt for him. At the time, I was at 148. I figured that if I'm good enough for an Ashwood, I'm good enough for a quest bow. Combine. Failed. Rats. Combine. Failed. RATS. Combine. Failed. RATS! I burned through poor Tanthilas's materials in no time, leaving him with nothing to show for his efforts.

This piqued my curiosity and made me intent to successfully fletch one of these bows. So I started in on the quest. To my surprise, this quest is a little entailed and definitely time-consuming. Make sure you have lots of room in your inventory for it. Especially if you're not sure if one attempt will be sufficient.

After a variety of flubs and miscues which are a bit of a story in themselves, I ended up with enough ingredients for 3 attempts of my own. Burning two microservos per attempt is painful, considering the difficulty of obtaining microservos.

An aside here. The first time I went microservo hunting, I found a valley FULL of beasts that are rumored to carry them. I methodically worked my way through them and landed quite a number of microservos. The next time I visited that valley, however, the pickings were quite a bit slimmer and I was obliged to actually pursue purchasing them from others.

So I had materials for three attempts, and attempt I did. Combine. Failed. Rats. Combine. Failed. RATS! Combine. Failed. Sigh. Obviously, 148 with a 75 WIS, 85 INT and 110AGI isn't going to cut it. I fletched a little more and gained one small insight into my chosen skill - after realizing that I was using the 3PP vanes instead of the 1PP vanes! So much money wasted!

The next day, while I was wandering through Kelethin, a dwarf put out a call for a master fletcher. "That's me" I thought with pride. "How can I help you, sir dwarf?" I asked. "I need a fletcher to assemble a Trushot Longbow for me". Rats. I informed the dwarf of my miserable record in attempting the bow, yet he was game for me to try another. Combine. Failed. Deep sigh. All I could do was offer my apologies to the noble dwarf who had entrusted to me the results of his quest.

Ah, but that's not ALL I could do. It was time to take steps. After consulting with more competent fletchers, I learned that increasing my intelligence should have a positive effect on my fletching. Well, I had my hard-won cash from my adventuring. What better use to put it to than my favorite project - fletching. A friend of mine is a jeweler, who offered me magical rings to make me more intelligent, and I purchased a necklace of charisma from a merchant passing through Kelethin. Ready now for Toastmasters and Mensa, I decided to dive into my fletching again wholeheartedly. I assembled arrow after arrow and found my skill increasing over time. But only to about 156. An admirable gain, but my adventuring obviously hadn't left me as rich as I might have hoped. That Bone Bladed Claymore had cut into my reserves quite a bit.

Well, I was at 156, was rather low on cash and had no materials to make another attempt on the Trueshot Longbow. Feeling the end of the day creeping up on me, I decided to visit Crushbone to see if the Warlord was about. Just before entering that den of evil, I heard another call from the direction of Kelethin - "Services of a Master Fletcher needed". "That's me" I thought, more confidently than before. I encountered Varrus, a tall warrior. "How can I help you?" I asked with a certain sense of deja vu. "Can you assemble a Trueshot Longbow for me?" he replied. This was either fate smiling on me or laughing at me. I was soon to discover which it was.

I again began my cautionary tale of my attempts at constructing this legendary bow, yet Varrus was intent upon the successful construction of one, and so concocted a scheme. He would finance me for a bit more training in fletching, and I would try to reach 160 in skill. He had heard good things about that skill level. Certainly this was a deal for me, and Varrus was tenacious in the extreme. I agreed to the plan.

We assembled 45 platinum pieces between us, and we also used every wiley technique we could imagine to make the most of the available cash. We pooled our magical items that contributed to intelligence and charisma, even borrowing items from kind Tanthilas who seemed to magically appear at the most propitious moment. And fletching began.

The arrow fletching pattern was a common one, and typically harrowing. The first fletching attempt immediately produced new insights into the miracles of fletching. But the next 20 fletchings produced nothing new. Sell what I had constructed, buy new raw materials and repeat. Almost all the way through the next batch with no new insights and then back to back insights! Each new insight gave both Varrus and myself increased confidence that I could fletch a Trueshot Longbow. But I had suspicions that I should delve as deeply as possible into the mysteries of fletching. So I fletched until we could only purchase components for a few bundles of arrows. I wasn't content that I knew enough, but we were at the end of our resources. And Tunare blessed us with two new insights in the last few arrows I fletched and I found that what I had found so challenging before was now trivial! This was certainly a sign.

In short, I had reached 162. <<...>>

Next we were off to visit the learned Maesyn Trueshot to obtain the needed materials for the bow. Varrus assembled fletching kits for my use and I performed the fletchings with my newly-found insights.

Combine. Failed. Oh no.

It all seemed so simple just moments before. What had I failed to comprehend? I stand before my new friend and my training master unable to construct this bow of Rangers. Vallus's tenacity was unaffected by my failure and he quietly presented me with a fletching kit, ready for another try. Inspired by his confidence, I set about my task.

Combine. SUCCESS! Praise Tunare!

Without a word, I handed this first masterpiece to Varrus. A silly grin spread across his face as he saw what I was handing him, and I found myself grinning with equal joy. But Varrus wasn't finished. He handed me another fletching kit, filled with the components for this magnificent bow. "Now one for you" he stated matter-of-factly. I held my breath and began my task, hoping and praying that Tunare would bless my current attempt as greatly as my prior.

Combine. Success! Woohoo!

I held the bow before me, smiling like a fool. Varrus turned and saw that I too possessed this fine bow. We smiled and laughed and started discussing the value of the bows, both as a trade good and as a weapon of a ranger. But having reached our goal of assembling the two bows, we found ourselves fading, unable to even keep our eyes open. The adrenalin of the chase had sustained us. But now, having achieved our goal and safe in the arms of the guild, we both quickly dozed off, Trueshot Longbows still gripped firmly in hand.

Tormanth Stormheart
Ranger of the 24th Season
