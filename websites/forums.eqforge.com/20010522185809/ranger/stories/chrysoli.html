Stories from Norrath

Mistmoore... as vile a place as any in Norrath! While I'd long harbored hopes that this place might one day be swept clean of it's evil taint, and had fought many a battle to that end, my resolve to continue on with the struggle faltered as I turned to face Mcgee. We stood together by a small pond. A clear pool that miraculously remained untainted by the foul winds that swirled amid the battlements of Lord Mistmoore's tower.

"I cannot stay any longer," I offered weakly. "I feel like I'm going mad… I have to get away from here."

It was a weak apology I knew. I was leaving my dear friend to continue the fight alone, yet I didn't know how else to explain. Another night of evil too intense... too far from home. I feared if I lingered in that cursed place another second I'd be driven to madness.

"I have to get away from here… I have to find renewed purpose to continue this fight," I said quietly. "I just… have to leave."

"Then let's go," Mcgee answered without question.

Within hours, we were far from Mistmoore and running swiftly through the hazy Lesser Faydark night.

"Wait!" I suddenly shouted and stopped abruptly. "Mcgee...," I faltered. Picking up my courage, "Mcgee, I fear I'm only going to lead you into far greater dangers then those we faced in Mistmoore. I have no destination in mind... in fact, I haven't any idea where this journey will take me."

I was making excuses... fumbling with words when in truth, I just needed to run. To be alone with my thoughts... to visit again the open plains of my homeland... to free myself of Mistmoore's taint. In retrospect, I realize the arrogance inherent in thinking I could better weigh the risks of a friendship's commitment. None-the-less, I forged ahead.

"Mcgee, rather then traipsing across the countryside with me, you would be better served to stay in Mistmoore and acquire additional experience. I simply cannot stay there any longer and may not gather additional experience for some time. You could do well in Mistmoore teaming with other adventurers and honing your skills further."

Though there was much truth to what I said, it still felt hollow… a cheap panacea. Mcgee and I had been together since we first adventured forth from our homes so long ago.

"I would be more of a hindrance... I do need more experience..." Mcgee started.

I cringed. That was not the crux, nor the truth of the matter. Yes, the gulf was widening between us, but it was only an artificial barrier, one that determines one's ability to further their fighting skills. I just needed a little time...

I was lost for words. Hugging my dearest friend, I stepped back and waved goodbye.

"We'll meet again soon! I'll send messages... I promise!" I said

"Goodbye for now then," Mcgee answered and then simply asked, "Chrys, don't get yourself killed?"

I smiled. "Not me! I run too fast!"

As Mcgee turned to head back to Mistmoore, I ached all over inside, a loneliness I hadn't known in years. Turning toward Greater Faydark, I ran. For days on end I ran, with scant rest, trying to purge the ugliness of Mistmoore from my heart and soul, until finally, exhausted, I collapsed on the dock in Butcherblock Mountains. Freeport, then home, was but a brief ocean voyage away.

The voyage, thankfully, was uneventful and the time onboard was spent sampling various liquors and brews collected while traveling the many different lands in Norrath. Docking in Freeport was similarly uneventful and feeling refreshed, I immediately started my overland journey toward the home of some friends in Rivervale.

Rivervale! What a unique little town. While a Halfling town for sure, it's seemed to have grown to become a major way-stop between east and west. A gathering place of different races and cultures. I stayed long enough for a night's rest, a bit of banking, re-supplying and a cheery local resident bound me to that fair burg. After the night's rest, I knew my destination and was anxious to see the plains of home again. But more then anything, I wondered if ogres still soiled my homeland with their presence in West Karana. I decided to scout out the area and see if they'd been driven off... or not.

On this, my second scouting trip to those lonely desolate reaches where ogres had dared set camp in my beloved West Karana, I approached once again from the north, cautious as before, yet determined to sneak a little closer then my previous journey. To my disgust, from a good distance away, I could see clearly this was no deserted camp! I decided then and there to better determine the make up of this camp. I threw up camo and started skirting the camp to the east.

From this new vantage point, I found I still couldn't get a good look at what type of pet Chief Goonda had with him, so I headed around to the south of the camp, hoping for a clearer view. Though I was a considerable distance from the camp, Chief Goonda apparently has excellent long range vision and saw through my camo.

My first warning of an attack was a frost spell that slammed into me and drained near a third of my life! Fortunately, Eluno, a close Druid friend who I'd met earlier in East Karana, had cast Spirit of the Wolf on me and without hesitation, I took off sprinting to the south. I knew I could never hope to stand against this entire ogre camp and swiftly I ran, an untimely abort to this scouting mission.

Unfortunately, Chief Goonda had other ideas and I'd barely made five strides into my escape before I withered in agony as another spell tore through me. I stumbled and faltered. Badly injured from Goonda's last spell, my steps were tentative and my strength failing.

Those were rapid spell casts! Though gravely wounded, I forced my tired legs onward and somehow found the will to resist a third spell (surely that would have been my doom). I made a good twenty paces... then thirty... a faint hope flared! I just might yet outrun deaths' cruel reach.

Ah, a fleeting thought is was though as Goonda quickly closed the distance between him and I. His first blow to my back crushed me... leaving me barely able to move. Staggering back into motion, I made a few more steps then stopped. The cold realization that there'd be no escaping my fate this night took hold.

Clearing my mind of all other thoughts, I quickly surveyed the surrounding location. Calm now, bloodied and battered, I slowly turned around to face my adversary. I was determined to land at least one blow in my defense... and that I did! Forsaking all thoughts of escape, I lashed out at him with every ounce of my remaining strength and mana.

Every reserve I had I threw at him, mindlessly... recklessly... a berserker frenzy upon me. Blow upon blow I rained down upon him and with each successful attack, Goonda's rage only seemed to intensify while a fearful toll was being exacted on my own slight frame. One final blow I struck, then Goonda screamed his outrage. My waning life trickled away... my arms, in the end, too heavy to lift in my own defense... I watched helplessly, yet defiant, as Chief Goonda struck out with a mighty punch that crumpled me to my knees... and sent my spirit fleeing back to Rivervale... Mcgee's parting request echoing faintly in the Karana breeze.

Chrysoli Whitethistle
Human Ranger (29)
