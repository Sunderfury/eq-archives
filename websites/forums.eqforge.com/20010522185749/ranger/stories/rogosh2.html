Stories from Norrath

The Wandering

For the first 12 levels of my life, I was petrified of the idea of being far enough away from my homelands that I would have to change my bindpoint from my beloved Surefall Glade, and the friends of the forest therein. But actions forced my hand one day.

While walking through nearby Qeynos on a quest to save our Ambassador Gash Flockwalker from the vile Unkempt Druids, I noticed that about half the Guards of fair Qeynos considered by amiably while the other half glowered dubiously. I figured out that it was the faction and the dubious Guards were the gnoll-loving corrupt guards. Being a member of the Noble Surefall Rangers I felt it my duty to introduce these corrupt pigs to my axe. Little did I know that this would change my life.

After a while of ridding fair Qeynos from its oppressors, the Corrupt Guards changed from dubious to all out hatred and began attacking me on sight. With multiple guards jumping me whenever I entered the city I found it impossible to make it to the bank or any where else which the rural Glade could not offer. Faced with this dilemma I took a last glace at my beloved homeland and, with a kind Halfling Druid friend, packed to leave for his town of Rivervale.

After a long and dangerous trek through East Karana and the Gorge of King Xorbb, much of which was done while camoed and following a tree, we entered Rivervale to the joyous music of the Halflings. Looking for something to hunt, we undertook a journey to Beholder Maze and punished some Gorge Minotaurs and Muddites (Muddites who, by the way, drop valuable ore for us smiths out there) who dared to oppose the Guardians of the Vale. The kind Halflings kindly took to me and, after many labors to prove my worth, awarded me the Squad Ring as an honorable Deputy and Guardian of the Vale.

The entire situation however had taught me to overcome the fear of travel and a disdain for cities which I had never felt before. I then vowed to wander the riches of all lands under the blessing of Karana and, with my good Druidic friend took off for Faydwer and the lands of the Noble Full Elven people.

We decided to take a quick trip to Chrushbone, which, hehe after 5 hours of not so quick killing, we both left carrying fabled Shiny Bronze Shields. ( On a side note, not to be a traitor to my race but Wood Elven women are the most beautiful women in Norrath! )After that, the wandurlust hit again and the two of us took off for the Minotaur caves of Steamfont.

And is where my story ends as I, formerly Rogosh of Surefall, am now on a quick stopover in Steamfont, fighting Minotaurs and the various interesting flying creatures around in service to King Ak'Anon. But who knows where the next hunting grounds may be found....

Rogosh
