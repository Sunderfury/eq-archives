Arrow Making

Advanced Fletching:

This section is, probably, only for a master fletcher and above, although, it is not restricted to master fletchers only. A master fletcher is one that has a level of 100 or better. You should become a level 100 fletcher while doing arrow #7 in the list above. At this point you may have figured out that the descriptors given to each arrow component tells you what Range and/or Damage boost you should get when using that part to make an arrow. For example, using a Parabolic fletch and a small nock will give you a 150 range arrow that deals 1 damage. This comes from the fact that the normal arrow gives you a 50 range arrow that deals 1 damage and the addition of the parabolic fletch adds 50 to the range and the addition of the small nock adds another 50 to the range for a total range of 150.

Once you have gotten the message about an arrow being trivial you should be able to use the part that is not one of: Field Point, Round Fletch, Wood Shaft or Large Nock, in any configuration along with other parts that have become trivial. In this way the fletcher can make some very useful and very saleable arrows that can help you earn plat to continue your fletching training.

A peculiar point about arrows number 12 and 13 is that once I had reached fletching skill level 200 via arrow number 11, these higher numbered arrows were trivial but the number 11 arrow had still not become trivial. Also, number 11 arrows can be used to make plat. If your CHR is 105 and you deal with a merchant that gives you the best price, you will be able to sell the #11 arrows for a profit once your productivity is around and average of 85%. At fletching skill level 185 you should have reached 85% productivity. Having a higher WIS will make your more productive earlier.
