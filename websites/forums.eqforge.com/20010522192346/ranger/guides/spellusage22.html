I recently entered my 22nd season as a wood-elf Ranger and earned the right to learn new spells from my guildmaster. During my life, I have come to appreciate and rely on certain spells, while others I wish I not wasted the gold on. So, in order to pass on a bit of my experiences, I give to you my list of best and worst Ranger spells.

Best:

1) Snare - This spell has so many uses, it's hard to list them all. It's saved me countless hours of tracking down and killing my prey when he runs, which also stops him from bringing back a whole squad of his higher level brethren. Finally, should the fight turn against me and my prey is snared, I have a good chance of running. I snare at the beginning of each battle and toward the end. I also use it to lure magic beasts since I can get out of their casting range faster. An absolutely invaluable spell that works on beasts that are higher level than me.

2) Healing line of spells - reduces your down time in between battles and is a welcome addition to your party members.

3) Skin line of spells - raises AC and HP. I ALWAYS have them on.

4) Thistlecoat - raises AC. Always have it on.

5) Camouflage - A great spell for getting through those nasty areas and/or retrieving your corpse. Downsides are that it can go at any time, and certain NPCs can see through it as well as the undead. I also cast it after I've died and am in my birthday suit and quite defenseless.

6) Fire line of spells (Flame Lick, Burst of Fire, Ignite) - Good for luring because of their excellent range. I'm beginning to appreciate Ignite for its excellent damage.

7) Feet like Cat - Raises agility and thus AC. Always have it on. Even my party members request it.

Worst:

1) Lull animal - Never used it. Most animals look upon me amiably now anyway.

2) Harmony - No one can quite figure this spell out.

3) Glimpse - Hardly useful.

4) Root - It would seem that this spell is better than snare, but I've found that's not the case. Beasts still have a lot of mobility after they've been rooted. Snare reduces their mobility by much more.

5) Bind sight - More of a play thing than a useful spell so far. It has a limited range and appears to give you false images sometimes. For instance, I had bound my sight to someone who appeared to fall into the lava! This turned out not to be the case, but it gave me quite a scare.

All other spells I memorize on some occasions, so they are worth your gold.
