Stories from Norrath

The sun setting over the hills in Qeynos has always set my mind at ease. Even when I stood face to face with undead, and my life slipped at a slow pace before my eyes; Tis a site to behold indeed.

I had ran across a wizard who I came to know as Dranis. His spells a life-send, his mind far from sane. But that's the price for knowing a spellcaster...they are a crazy lot.

We had made our way one night as the rains fell, (my skills leading us in the dark,) to the Entrance to Black Burrow and then on to the snow laced pathways of Everfrost. We dropped a few gnoll pups that crossed our path, and then proceeded to an open area new to us both. It was a large valley stretching as far as the darkened sky would permit us to see. We headed east i think it was, the snow giving way to green, that is until we reached the frozen river....

It was solid we soon discovered, so we crossed it, the wizard slipping and falling on his face the entire time. Now I tell you my Ranger brothers and sisters that wizards are not made for cross-land travel... for I havent used a road to guide me since i began this life.

We made our way up and over two more hills when the land suddnly changed. I noticed it first, the wizard being more occupied talking loudly and shouting at others who where a few paces from us .And then i beheld it...a frost giant standing before me; the clouds uncovering to reveal a creature so large I froze in my tracks. My mentor, Blackfalcon, another ranger, had told me stories of these beings who could crush a group of men in a heartbeat. And now I stood before one, or rather it towered before me......

Dabaron Von Harrkonnan
