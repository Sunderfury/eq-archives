    Re: EQ3 - IMA BACK BEOTCHES

    I guess you have to decide if you want a world or a game. Remember when EQ was a world to explore instead of a game to win? When Kithicor was scary and you had to shout random words at npcs to discover quests? Back before you couldn't get a group because you weren't the right class, and before everyone was made functionally the same in order to be fair and equal?

    WoW took EQ as a game and expanded on it, isn't it about time someone expanded on EQ as a world? I'm not saying give up on balance completely, but I'm so bored of everyone being the same because someone found the path of least resistance and exploited the hell out of it.

    I realize the idea was flawed, but I still like the spirit of what I was suggesting. The idea that classes can be different again while still retaining value. The wizard could use cantrips on trash mobs and then have a few powerful abilities they could use once per encounter. Wouldn't that be more interesting than memorizing some stupid rotation and very strictly managing food buffs and drink and all that nonsense? The harsh penalty would mean having access to something more powerful than you normally would. Some kind of aoe 60 second mez would be worth protecting a casting class as they meditate.

    I would also lessen the importance of pure dps, give more support options, as well as make it easier to level and swap between multiple characters. I'm imagining each player having a stable of characters for each archetype, all of which get leveled up simultaneously, but with challenge locks to surpass in order to get past important milestones and prove you are capable with the character. As it is, some of us are already used to sighing in resignation and respeccing to a heal spec in order to fill out a group. Why not have a group that instead of requiring tank, healer, dps dps dps, to have more interesting roles to replace the dps, and allow players to freely switch between character archetypes while still progressing with their main character?
