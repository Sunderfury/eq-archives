    Today, we're going to be looking [solely] at the representation of Anita Sarkeesian on the internet, as part of an examination [read: proof by induction] of the wider issues affecting those women [and only women?] who appear online with opinions [any opinion, no matter how controversial.
    You're kidding, right? Fact: Everyone who posts controversial opinions online is subject to abuse. Get back to me if you have falsifying evidence other than Anita Sarkeesian's self-serving word that the abuse against women is somehow special.

    I really don't care whether you believe me or not. I'm sure you won't, and will keep trying to post mediocre feminist propaganda videos out of spite. But I am convinced she deliberately courted this controversy and played it for personal gain. This thread would not exist and none of us would have watched any of her lame videos if the media hadn't made her the poster child of Women's Oppression on the internet.

    We expect this kind of analysis in a film review - it isnt enough for Kael to merely state that she did or didn't enjoy the film. She also looks at the film's relevance to, and its possible influence on, the society which produced it. Where did it come from? What does it mean?
    This skips an important critical step:

    Describe the game and offer an opinion.
    Identify the point of the game and evaluate the various features and details with respect to what it's trying to accomplish.
    Cultural commentary where you analyze the role of the game in the context of society.

    There is a clear difference between replicating something and critiquing it. Its not enough to simply present misery as miserable and exploitation as exploitative. Reproduction is not, in and of itself, a critical commentary. A critique must actually center on characters exploring, challenging, changing or struggling with oppressive social systems.
    So apparently Sarkeesian's argument is that Red Dead Redemption and God of War are not a social critiques. What a great insight.
