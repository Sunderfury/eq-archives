    Re: Guild Wars 2 - Thoughts and Reviews

    A couple thoughts of the higher level zones. Honestly? I don't find them terribly fun right now. They feel very overtuned to me. Enemies are extremely difficult and have tons of annoying abilities (Knockbacks, Pulls, Stuns, Fears and so on). This means that every trash mob you run across you need to be on your A game.

    Being forced to be on your A game wouldn't be so bad if not for my next issue. Mob density is WAY too high. I understand that the higher level zones (like Straits of Devestation and Malchor's Leap) represent areas that have been under control of Zhaitan (the main villain of the game) for years and the forces of good are just starting to establish a foothold in these areas which turns them into the frontlines of a massive war. However, functionality trumps lore in my opinion and, right now, you can't take 3 steps without aggroing something (getting attacked by a Risen Chicken is amusing at first but quickly gets old when a Risen Farmer joins in followed by another Risen Chicken and a Risen Bull). While I don't believe I should just be able to walk from one end of the zone to the other with no fear of danger, right now it can take well over an hour to go from one end to the other since I have to fight half of Zhaitan's forces to get there.

    Finally are the bugs. I put these last because they will get fixed eventually. But it's really annoying to be 99% completed with a map and not be able to complete it because the last skill point is bugged out.
    There are other worlds than these.

    Some things I would like to see:

    -Elite skills suck less. It wasn't until my third one that I found an elite skill for my guardian that was worth casing. I had the book and the human shapeshift, both of which reduced my damage output, defensive capability, and utility to a group

    -broken zones un-f&!@ed. Right now these are more than just small annoyances in the volcano zone there's a 1-5 second delay on every action through the entire zone, on top of all the broken events.

    -events that scale to just one person. Due to the "dynamic" nature of some of the late game zones, Many of them are all but abandoned. Yesterday I died and ended up half a map away because 3 towns in a row had contested waypoints because there was an event going on inside that there were not enough people to win. Actually to be fair this was only the case in two...the third one had an event where the waves stopped spawning and just wouldn't progress.

    -more involved story quest. I'm interested on mine and especially now it's better than grinding in the broken zones in swtor it carried you through every area leveling up and the overlapping quests were just filler, which I liked. This game however it's the filler and just provides some lore background to what you'd be doing anyway.

    -a way to know the status of some of the big events without being there in person. When the dragons are spawning etc the should mark that on people's maps, or make a crier go to the cities, something, anything.

    -mounts. Not really necessary for travel, but more a cosmetic and immersion staple.

    That said I immensely enjoyed this game levelling up to about 50. After that it feels a little grindey to me because I'm often frustrated at the broken things and the gap between story missions is too big.
