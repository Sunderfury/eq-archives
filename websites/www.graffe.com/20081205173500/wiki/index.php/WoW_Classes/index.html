[edit] Overview

Classes can be categorized in various ways. Due the huge difference that talenting provides, many classes are able to contribute in another role quite comparable also and therefore can end up in various categories.

Melee classes are classes that can essentially only work in close combat. Tank classes are melee classes that stand in the first line to withstand most of the incoming damage.

Ranged classes are classes that can work a mob down from a distance. The pet classes as the prime example can usually keep a mob at bay for an indefinite duration of a fight.

DPS classes (DPS literally means damage per second, but nowadays means just damage) are classes that primarily dish out damage. Another common description is DD (damage dealing) classes. DPS classes can be subcategorized into melee dps classes and ranged dps classes. They can be subcategorized into single-target dps classes and multi-target dps classes. Multi-target damage abilities are usually centered to an area around the target position, which is why they are called AE for area effect. DPS classes can further be subcategorized into physical dps classes and magical dps classes. Finally they can be subcategorized into direct-damage (DD) classes and damage-over-time (DOT) classes. Single-target direct magical damage classes are also called Nukers.

CC classes are classes with the ability to control crowds. The prime example is the mage with the ability to turn an opponent into an animal. Other well-known forms are sleep, sap, seduce, banish and root. Much less CC in the controlled sense are the fear variants which let a mob run away, due to the dangerous nature of this CC it is advisable to be at a good distance from other mobs to avoid that the feared mob 'brings some friends'. A very special form is mind control, which renders the original character motionless as the player then controls the mob including movement and (severely limited) spell casting. With the exception of banish and mind control, all CC variants can prematurely end if a careless player damages the CC'ed mob.

Most CC variants are single-target. Some fear variants are multi-target with a fixed maximum number of targets. The AE ice root 'Frost Nova' is multi-target with an unlimited number of targets.

Healer classes are classes that can... heal. Heal spells that heal over time are abbreviated as HOT. For heal spells that do their effect instantly there is no common abbreviation.

Rez(z)ers or rez(z)ing classes (rez(z) being short for resurrection, the spelling of the abbreviation is not definite, but all of the various variants start with 're' and end with some kind of s sound...) are classes that bring dead characters back to life. All healers are able to rezz. Each of the four rezzes (from Priest, Paladin, Shaman and Druid) is a bit different. Shaman are the only one who can rezz themselves (once per hour). All targetted rezzes except the Druid's have no cooldown, the Druid can only rezz one person every half an hour (which can be a wee bit annoying if the Druid is the groups' only healer deep in a dungeon), but on the bright side Druids are the only class which can rezz another character in an ongoing battle.

Hybrid classes are classes that were created as a amalgamation of two or more primary classes, able to perform or mimic some, but not all of the primary classes' abilities. Multirole classes are similar to hybrid classes, but cannot perform the abilities of different primary classes at the same time.

[edit] Druid

Druids are versatile hybrids with various shapeshift forms in which they can tank like a warrior (Bear Form), stealth and fight in melee like a rogue (Cat Form), heal like a priest (Tree of Life form), and cast spells like a mage (Moonkin Form). Druids are the only class who are able to resurrect other characters while in combat. Unlike other hybrids, druids do not perform several roles at once but instead shift between roles.

[edit] Hunter

Hunters are the only class that can effectively use ranged weapons (bows or guns) as a primary source of DPS. Very adept at kiting, they have plenty of tools to allow them to avoid melee combat, where they are fairly weak. Hunters may tame many varieties of beasts to serve as combat pets, which along with an array of scouting, tracking, and survival abilities make them excellent solo characters.

[edit] Mage

Mages are ranged magic damage dealers with very powerful direct and area effect damage spells, but weak armor and health. They also feature a variety of utility spells, including crowd control, teleportation and group portals to capital cities, and conjuration of food and water. Mages are reliant on mana for all their abilities.

[edit] Paladin

A hybrid class focused on defensive melee skills and healing abilities, paladins have very strong group support and buff abilities. They may wear heavy plate armor and carry shields like a warrior, but are also efficient healers, which combine to produce their renowned durability in combat. With talent specialization and equipment selection, paladins can fill the role of tank, offtank, healer, or even melee damage dealer, and excel when called upon to spot-fill all these roles in a group setting. They can even sacrifice themselves to provide wipe recovery when needed.

[edit] Priest

Priests are the quintessential healer, and naturally the most effective and versatile class in that role, though they may also specialize to become very powerful offensive casters. Priests have a wide variety of utility, buff, and crowd control spells, such as Dispel Magic, Power Word: Fortitude, and Mind Control.

[edit] Rogue

Rogues excel at dealing melee damage and incapacitating their foes, particularly low health and low armor targets such as casters. Stealth gives them a tactical advantage and allows them to start a fight on their own terms. Other important abilities include lockpicking and the creation and handling of poisons. Rogues do not use mana for their combat abilities. Instead they use energy and generate combo points which are used to execute finishing moves.

[edit] Shaman

Shamans are one of the most versatile classes in the game as they can switch seamlessly from offensive caster to healer to melee DPS as needed. Shamans can make good support characters, with the ability to step in and fill any role that may be lacking at the moment.Their high burst damage and healing also make them quite good at PVP, despite their small mana pool. They are able to drop stationary totems on the ground which provide a variety of effects and are unique in that they can provide complete wipe recovery by self-resurrecting and then resurrecting the rest of the party.

[edit] Warlock

Warlocks enslave demons and call upon dark magic and curses to dominate and destroy their foes. Like hunters, warlocks employ combat pets, but these are summoned demons rather than tamed beasts. They are primary ranged magic damage dealers with a focus on damage over time in addition to nukes and AoE, but lack the extreme burst damage capabilities of a mage. Demon pets and fear spells give them a variety of tactical options, and they have a variety of utility spells and abilities, including summoning party members and creating healthstones and soulstones.

[edit] Warrior

Warriors are close-combat fighters and usually fill the role of primary tank, as they are able to withstand and inflict fierce physical punishment and have the most versatile means of attracting and disabling mobs, keeping aggro off other characters. They can also contribute strong melee DPS when needed. Warrior abilities use rage, which is generated by dealing and taking damage, but decays over time. Warriors are highly gear dependent, as their ability to deal and take damage is controlled directly by the quality of their weapons and armor.
