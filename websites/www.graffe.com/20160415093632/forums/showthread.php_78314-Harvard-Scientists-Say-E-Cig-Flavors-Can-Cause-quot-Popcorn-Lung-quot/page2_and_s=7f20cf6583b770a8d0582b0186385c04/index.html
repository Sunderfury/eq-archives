    Re: Harvard Scientists Say E-Cig Flavors Can Cause "Popcorn Lung"

    Originally Posted by Nadiar
    Anything with a Lithium Ion battery can do that. If it happens while you're using it, you're an idiot. "wow, my device is getting extremely hot, let me put it closer to my face"
    Some observations from myself on these devices with respect to that are

    1) I have not seen any device that focuses on maximizing thermal dissipation.

    2) The devices that use an internal battery rather than allowing the batteries to be swapped out easily are incompetently designed when considering the use of the device which needs > 99% uptime. It is an idiotic design. Around half the devices I have seen use this though. It is a deal breaker however and prevents their usage in some cases entirely.

    3) Even when running the devices at 120 Watts+ simply taking off the battery cover and allowing the heat to dissipate and be cooled by ambient temperature is sufficient to prevent overheating.

    4) I have never encountered a device that is able to be turned on and off quickly, they should all use the same switch mechanisms as those in every fucking gun ever produced for the safety switch instead of require a byzantine path to do; the consequence is most people don't bother and as a result will put them in a container where they may be activated or turn back on, in comparison I have never had a gun's safety be unintentionally turned back on unlike these devices can be if the person does not take into account that they may come back on being bumped around in a bag. Every device I have used or tried out regardless of how expensive it was has had an extremely weird interface. All of them have had critical hardware design issues as far as integrating the firmware and hardware correctly. The most annoying one was in my SMOK X Cube II which has an LED screen for displaying relevant information, however even in the latest firmware it does not correctly prevent the screen from turning on when the firing button is depressed which it is supposed to do when on stealth mode. It flickers on for < .2 ms then turns back off. This is caused by the hardware not being designed correctly. It should never happen and it is an easy thing to make impossible but they fucked it up bad.

    5) Every device I have used has had a really buggy ohm meter as it is something I designed in Circuits I and worked a lot fucking better than what these devices use, so it is something I am embarassed that they fucked up so badly when any FOB EE student can design it and implement it easily. I am not sure how this is so routinely fucked up for devices that just need to have +/- .01 accuracy at the most extreme case. If they allowed manually setting the ohms that are correct and just tested for whether it is an open circuit or short circuit for safety purposes and also notifying the user that their coil has a bad connection that would be resolved. That is all some of the lower end devices use. Some of the higher end ones allow manipulating the ohm meters but not over sufficient range that their extreme inaccuracy can be fixed.

    6) Higher end devices are focusing on flash over substance. The DNA 200 chip is a clusterfuck of retarded design common in consumer devices. As it actually cost them more money to implement a shit ass design compared to one that implements only functional features it is inexcusable. Many higher end devices are moving to being the flashy bullshit reflected in consumer crap targeting gamers, which are essentially the ricers of HCI.

    I expect open source designs will become more prevalent as they focus on 100% substance. Their PCBs/circuits are also much less of a clusterfuck. The devices I have taken apart made by commercial manufacturers are shockingly fucked up all over the place to the point where I am not sure that whomever made it has any idea what the fuck they are doing. I would have gotten fired for such shit ass design straight away. Really easy to prevent or catch in testing is shipped for no justifiable reason. Simply using them for less than a day reveals design flaws, so it is inexcusable.

    7) Ease of use and quality of life improvements are rapidly improving at reasonable price points now even for rebuildable atomizers. Availability of information is becoming more prevalent as the consumer base expands. It is still overly time consuming to get a good experience reliably though. The ones that use pre-built coils that are used on lower end devices are really bad and really unnecessarily expensive. I am not super sure why none of them have a good vapor production with respect to flavor or density or volume compared to even a mediocre rebuildable atomizer with okay coils/wicking used.

    8) As mentioned elsewhere, there is a lot of entirely inaccurate marketing used that are essentiall snake oil for wicking materials and coil materials. Things like gplat, cotton bacon etc. are examples.

    One of the best wicking materials is really underutilized and not available anywhere as a sheet or pad that I can find on the English Internet which is hemp. Organic Japanese cotton always comes in sheets/pads and is much easier to use. The hemp wicking material you have to shape like you do for rolling your own tobacco or pot joints and is a skill unto itself that when done poorly results in something that will give burnt hits quickly or not soak up the eliquid in a reasonable window.

    Hemp is really great for dripping atomizers (RDA) as it can hold a fuck load of eliquid but does not reabsorb eliquid quickly merely through capillary forces compared to the Japanese organic cotton. However, not only can hemp hold a lot more eliquid at once compared to cotton, it also uses much less material compared to cotton for the same exact coil setup, but in the event of a dry hit, it can continue to be used afterwards once more eliquid is dropped onto the wick with only a marginal reduction in vape volume/density/flavor. With a cotton wick, it has to be replaced as it is fucking done and won't absorb eliquid well and its flavor production drops to near zero. Another benefit to hemp is that it lasts upwards of 7 days even with heavy usage, cotton lasts around 1 day for me between replacement when chain vaping all day. Hemp though is a stud.

    The absolutely only downsides to hemp wicks vs. cotton wicks are that it has a very precise ritual for breaking in the wick, benefits tremendously from tediously cleaning the hemp of remaining stems as not only will those stems not absorb eliquid at all (it is essentially a wood chip), those stems will incinerate much sooner than the flash point of hemp, damaging the surrounding material.

    I would not mind that but there does not exist the equivalent of the cotton gin for removing stems. I am very surprised that if that is true rather than me simply not being able to find such that the very prevalent and creative pot enthusiast community has never came up with a way to efficiently remove stems mechanically.

    Cotton though does not have as much non-cotton material in the pads/sheets and has a break in period that is pretty short and can simply be vaped through without any issues and it is much more widely available. There are only a handful of vendors that sell the hemp material needed for the wicks usable for vaping. The kind of hemp wicks that are used for lighting pot/tobacco instead of butane lighters that are soaked with beewax are not the kind needed for vaping wicks.

    For vaping, you can only use the degummed hemp fiber for making wicks. Some examples are the ones available from Hemptopia [1] or Elfwick [2]. And only Hemptopia is a large enough company to bulk order from. Elfwick produces a superior product and I absolutely adore its charming owner but is a mom and pop shop so it is not much of an option in the context of selling to the legions of vaping stores that exist now.

    [1] http://www.hemptopia.com/grade-a-degummed-hemp-fiber/

    [2] http://www.elfwick.com/ Elf is fucking hillarious. He puts in personalized notes with every order, every hemp package has cute sayings on the packaging kind of like a fortune cookie, and left a hillarious voice message when he was not able to get a shipping label printed due to FedEx's portal being down. I am not sure what is different about his hemp compared to hemptopia as they look exactly the same but Eflwicks works a lot better for me.

    I am not sure what his secret sauce is and he won't tell anyone, which is probably smart. Elf doesn't saturate the material with anything that stays on it after the very first firing but something on it makes it work a lot better somehow for vaping. I wish I knew what it was as I am extremely curious what he is doing different. All other characteristics is the same with Hemptopia's product though. They absorb the same amounts of eliquid, heat up just as quickly, reabsorb the same, but Elf's vapor production and flavor is noticeably better for some reason that is not at all apparent.
