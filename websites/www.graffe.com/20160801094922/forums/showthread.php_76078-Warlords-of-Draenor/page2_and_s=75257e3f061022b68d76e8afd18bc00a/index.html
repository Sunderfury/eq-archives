    Re: Warlords of Draenor

    More links with info:

    UI improvements: http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/2...-improvements/

    Personal notes: The UI is one of the strongest features of WoW, and it's amazing that they continue to refine it. The toybox is going to free up so much space in my various inventories! Good to see they've finally fixed the heirloom system so it can be used cross-server.

    Dungeons and Raids: http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/2...ons-and-raids/

    Personal notes: good to see they really learned from Cataclysm about not making difficult heroic dung... oh, wait, they're going to try that again. It's good to hear that additional five mans are planned during the expansion, though. The "all Normals and Heroics are Flex" thing is pretty nifty, although I'm not sure that current heroic guilds will be terribly happy with the Mythic thing. I'm also not sure the game really needs another tier of "super bestest" gear. It's too bad that they've effectively done away with the Flex tier, since it had some features - winged, individual loot - that really made it suitable for PUGs in a way the new "Flex Normal" is not. How do heroic raiders feel about all the different tiers being on separate lockouts?

    PvP: http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/2...a-look-at-pvp/

    Personal notes: I couldn't give less of a shit about PvP if I tried.

    Warcraft sound: http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/...nd-of-Warcraft
    Warcraft movie: http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/...e-Presentation

    Misc. Q&A: http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/...f-Warcraft-Q-A

    Personal notes: The Garrison will play a role in pet battles? "Tertiary stats won't have a role in progression because they'll be rare" - ahahahahahaha it's starting. I can't wait to see how they tinker with racials again, although I do like that they use Goblin/Worgen as the "this is balanced" template. Stats on armor changing for spec seems nice, or at least better than the alternative, "Changing how different specs work so they value stat X". Goodbye, Yak reforge vendor, I shall miss you - I assume all reforging is out the window now? "We'll scale up the difficulty of the launch dungeons as the expansion goes on" - um, don't do that. Scale up the REWARDS. Don't they learn?

    Raid, Gameplay Q&A (some duplicate info from above links): http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/...sting-and-More

    Personal notes: ah, so they do plan to drop the difficulty of the raids. So all the current progression guilds will have to turn themselves into 20 man crews. Ouch. I like that they plan fewer massive quest chains, so people can pick and choose more (or run ahead on opening night to avoid crowds). Oh, joy, the new events won't be soloable, more fun for people who don't play at prime time. I like the "treasures from exploration" thing - the ground spawns were a great feature in MoP. This quote scares me a great deal:

    World of Warcraft is a game you play with friends. WoD will remove barriers that stop you from playing with friends and encourage you to make new friends.
    I do like the "Stacking the Deck" idea, though. "Spirit will be good for healers" - shall I read that as "SPI on every item you own, how terribly exciting", like it is now? Seriously, when they changed everyone to have the same mana pool, I wish they'd instead changed it so everyone had the same mana regen, since mana regen is not a fun stat at all. The new D3-style stats should be interesting, so long as they're not totally random things. Item squish - yuck. I can't wait to see how they react to all the "this isn't as easy to kill" comments during Beta.

    Developer Q&A: http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/...-Developer-Q-A

    Personal notes: "compartmentalize systems added in an expansion". Heh. So I assume that means all the work players do on their garrison will be flushed down the toilet come next expansion? Sounds like the Raid Browser will be turned into oQueue. "Restrictions like Horde vs. Alliance on toy/heirloom panel" - well, depending on implementation that may very well kill the only positive thing I'd get from the heirloom panel. Item squish live with 6.0 - will 6.0 go live earlier than expansion evening, so players can get used to how things work (I assume "yes"). Good to hear that the prof. recipe UI will get some work. Hopefully they incorporate some of the "shows stuff you don't have and where you can get it" features from the pet panel.

    Re: Warlords of Draenor

    Lore expanded on the 20 man size:
    Originally Posted by Lore
    Seeing a lot of debate about the number 20. Let me take a stab at clearing up a few things:

    We chose to put Mythic at 20 largely for the function of raid design. One of the biggest issues we're currently facing with 10-player Heroic raiding is that of raid composition. It's impossible for every group to have every class, and often that means they're lacking in certain tools, which in turn means that we can't design encounters around those tools (or if we do, it becomes extremely frustrating for the 10-player Heroic guild that suddenly needs a Paladin for Hand of Protection).

    We want to be able to use those sorts of mechanics again. Those of you who have been with us for a while might remember things like Mage tanks on High King Maulgar, or Priests using Mind Control on Instructor Razuvious. We want it to be okay when, say, the Paladin can use Hand of Protection to clear a dangerous debuff, because we can reasonably assume that most guilds will have at least one Paladin in their raid. We like it when someone gets to feel awesome and have a special task on a fight because of class abilities that otherwise wouldn't get much use.

    We can't do that when we're designing with a 10-player raid size in mind. We don't think we'd be able to get away with it at 15 either. At 20, it becomes a lot more acceptable for us to say "you should probably bring a Mage to Spellsteal this." And honestly, that's just one example of the sort of encounter mechanics we can start to utilize in a larger group size.

    I'd also call into question the statement of "It's easier to drop people than it is to recruit them." It's technically true, yes -- finding new raiders is harder than just not inviting the ones you have -- but totally ignores the fact that cutting people from your roster often means losing people you like. Which feels better: making new friends, or telling your current ones that they don't get to play with you any more? We're already asking a lot of many 25-player Heroic groups to cut 5 people.

    As I mentioned before, this was not a decision we came to lightly. It's definitely going to be a very scary transition for a lot of people. We knew that when we made the decision. We just also feel quite strongly that, when the dust settles, we'll be able to provide a better raiding experience for everyone.
    "Silver bullet solutions are rare, silver bullet sales commonplace"

    Because the expansion sounded a little too fun: no flying until patch 6.1, and it'll take an epic questline to unlock.

    http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/2...-about-flying/
    http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/2...in-wod-launch/

    "Unlock flying". Who was the guy who created the heroic instances that required a certain amount of rep to unlock, again? Oh, right, that was Furor's bright idea, along with all the other key quests in TBC. Welcome back, jackass, the game really missed your clever ideas. What really gets me is the "you will skip our oh-so-awesome trash if we don't do this". A few responses to the whole thing:

    (1) Not if we play on opening day/week, when pretty much anything that moves will be dead on the ground. Your trash obstacles are only obstacles to players who level up after the big initial wave: the more casual players, and the alts. And if there's two groups that do NOT need more time-wasting trash, it's those two.

    (2) There's many ways to force players to deal with your trash. Here's some of them: put mobs in buildings/caves. Put quest targets in areas where it would be suicide to just fly to the target and land. Have the quest target only spawn once the pre-quest of killing enough trash and reaching a target location is completed. Have items necessary to deal with/summon the quest target drop from the trash. So this isn't a flying problem. This is a lazy developer that doesn't want to have to consider flying when designing things problem. Of course, there are other solutions: don't have boring quest chains of the form, "Step 1: kill X trash mobs, Step 2: kill a named boss", or have quests where fighting the boss is enough of a challenge that trash isn't necessary. But that's radical talk right there!

    (3) Bashiok talks about flying feeling like a reward. Listen, you can't take something away from people and call it a "reward" or "extra content" when you give it back to them in a neutered form. This feels like a shit sandwich, and no amount of spin will change that feeling.

    (4) You talk about wanting players out in the world more. Do you think forcing them to move at snail speed, potentially through dangerous trash, when they want to go from flight point A to interesting content area B will encourage or discourage this? You must have recognized this when you made Timeless Isle, because you placed various items on the island to hasten ground travel speed - and the island is the size of a postage stamp compared with what one expects of a full-fledged expansion zone.

    (5) If you actually implement this, could you also please remove summoning stones from outside of instances/raids and disable warlock summoning until 6.1 as well? I want to make sure that all the raiders in every developer's mythic guild tell them, over and over, exactly how stupid a decision "no flying" is as they all FP/ground mount their way to the target raid instance. Be sure to put the raid portal in a cave behind lots of trash, so they really get that "epic" feeling as they clear to the content they really want to do. It was good enough for original Molten Core, it's good enough for today!
