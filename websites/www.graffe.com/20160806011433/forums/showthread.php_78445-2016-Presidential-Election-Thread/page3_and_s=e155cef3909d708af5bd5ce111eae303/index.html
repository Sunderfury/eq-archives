    You having access to gigabit has nothing to do with gigabit to your home. The Backbone is the part that handles communication between say Comcast and AT&T. The part that has fucktons of old or failing equipment with massive congestion or packetloss. Backbone costs are almost nothing compared to the number of users paying for the bandwidth (you're looking at a 5000:1 ratio at least). The problem is that they do nothing to ensure what you're paying for is actually provided, and you can't do anything about it because they effectively have a monopoly on the routes they're broadcasting.

    edit example: On the West Coast, there has been severe congestion between Qwest and CogentCo for 6 months. This is equating to 5-10% packetloss during prime time.

    Re: 2016 Presidential Election Thread

    Originally Posted by Grindel
    Why not?

    Would you support quasi-nationalism, like certain public utilities, for other oligopolies of needed goods? Do you support public utilities?

    I'm not fishing for a right or wrong answer, I'm just interested in general sentiment around public vs. private distribution of resources.
    No, I was talking of things like nationalizing oil companies and other energy companies and other manufacturing. I like the government/private business relationship where competition (true competition, not the cartel/monopoly system we have in many industries right now) has a significant factor in day to day business operations but the government puts in pragmatic regulations to make sure the private businesses aren't screwing the public or raping the lands. I don't believe we really have that right now.

    Basically, I'm generally for private ownership of most things, with regulations. However, when it comes to things that really are for the common good of the people and the country, the profit motive is counterproductive. Things like healthcare, education, and basic infrastructure are too important for our country to function properly or the general well being of the population. Time and time again when it is left to private firms the ones who end up getting screwed are the public.
