    Originally Posted by Khieran
    Why was the blood elf having so much luck catching nothing but fish when the humans can't? Is there a racial advantage there? Or does location matter when fishing, even at beginner level?
    It's partly luck, but Stormwind actually seems to still have (all these years later) a higher fishing skill requirement than the surrounding human zones. It's just a tad harder to get anything good out of the canals or Hero Valley.

    If you head to the hoop lake in Elwynn, you'll probably have better luck.

    Then again, it doesn't matter where you do it too much, as long as you are successful actually catching things, as that is what affects skill gain.
    Once you hit skill 100, you can use Sharpened Fishing Hooks or even (by this point Worm Supreme) bought super-cheap on the AH as bait to help a little.

    Re: Returning player questions

    Originally Posted by Khieran
    So what is the expert flying skill for? Faster flight? It was also level 60 and was 225 gp.
    Apprentice Riding = level 20 = 60% ground mount
    Journeyman Riding = level 40 = 100% ground mount
    Expert Riding = level 60 = 150% flying, 100% ground mount
    Artisan Riding = level 70 = 280% flying, 100% ground mount
    Master Riding = level 80 = 310% flying, 100% ground mount

    Of course that's not getting into the continent-specific flying
    Flight Master's License = this is what I was probably forgetting about and mixing up with Expert = flying in Deepholm, EK and Kalimdor
    Cold Weather Flying = Northrend, level 68
    Wisdom of the Four Winds = Pandaria, level 90
