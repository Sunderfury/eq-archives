    Re: New Star Wars trailer out

    I enjoyed Star Wars: The Force Awakens, except that it was basically a superhero movie fashioned into an homage to the original Star Wars trilogy. Homages to older films are what JJ Abrams does best (see: Super 8, Star Trek) and none of my complaints have anything to do with lack of originality. Rey was fun and likable. Finn was good, although I was disappointed he was portrayed as such an incompetent.

    Biggest concern is that the themes of training and discipline seem to be absent for the first time in any Star Wars movie. Rey is skilled at everything and her only remotely negative character trait is a very mild lack of self-esteem. On the one hand, Rey's talents make her likable. On the other hand, she's already wtfpwned Kylo Ren twice, shown up Han Solo, where does she go from here?

    Other gripes:

    Kylo Ren was a weak villain. He looked cool and was impressive and scary in the prologue and a bit of the first act, but after that was just ridiculously incompetent.

    The political back story is a convoluted, nonsensical mess. Irrelevant to my enjoyment of that film in particular but it does not bode well for the continuation of the series.

    In a similar vein, the planet-sized death star did not harm my enjoyment of SW: TFA, but it sets a dumb precedent.

    The blatant Nazi imagery was heavy-handed and annoying. The original Star Wars' imperials were inspired by Nazis, but were suitably adapted to the fantasy universe that you didn't really think about it. Abrams shatters suspension of disbelief with overt parallel imagery and language.
