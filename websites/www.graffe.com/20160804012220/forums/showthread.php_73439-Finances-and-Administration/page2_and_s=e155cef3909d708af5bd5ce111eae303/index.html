    Here's an example of how Voca's "perception" proposal is horse shit:

    Irving Kristol wrote a book in 1979 entitled "Confessions of a True, Self-Confessed 'Neoconservative.'" Neoconservatism is an actual political movement, with powerful adherents (such as Dick Cheney and Donald Rumsfeld) and Neoconservatives formed a group called Project for a New American Century (PNAC). The PNAC has a web site, where they have many documents promoting Neoconservative causes, such as military expansion, especially in the Middle East, as well as other foreign policy recommendations and some domestic policy recommendations.

    It was well-known that the Bush Administration employed a large number of signatories to PNAC documents. But then Jonah Goldberg wrote an article that accused people of using the term Irving Kristol coined as anti-semitic, that "neoconservative was just a code-word for 'Jew.'"

    Obviously that was just more of the Neoconservative Movement's massive campaign of obfuscation, since the record up to that point indicated no such racist connotation. Goldberg was just trying to reduce the amount of criticism his favored political movement was rightfully receiving for its terrible influence on our foreign and domestic policies.

    But TORCER, well, he took Jonah Goldberg's article and used it as a sword and shield against anyone who argued against the Neoconservative Movement and its influence. Imagine trying to argue against the group that was actually in charge of the Executive Branch at the time but without being able to describe them in any meaningful sense as a group or a movement. That was strongarming the people who posted here in the worst way, by making them question their own motives.

    But Voca's proposal would make it even worse: In her world of "perception," Torcer's accusation would have been unquestioningly accepted, and prior restraint of the worst kind would have prevented many of the conversations we have subsequently had. Hey, we complained about bankers a lot, THAT might be construed as anti-Semitism for the exact same "reasons" as Jonah Goldberg's accusation that "Neoconservative is a code-word for Jew." Because some people associate banking with Jews, a "perception" rule might make complaining about bankers "racist."

    Re: Finances and Administration

    Originally Posted by Dale
    What does it cost to run the site per month? Is the $100 per month ($1200 annually) still a good number?
    Yes. Our problem is we basically will use ~1 2.5Ghz CPU (runs about 25% average over the day, but spikes to 60%) and ~3GB RAM.

    I think I could squeeze us into 2gb RAM if I beat it around a bit (mainly the problem is that we've had "unlimited ram" so I have no idea how much we absolutely require for an OS+LAMP+vB).

    If I squeezed us into 2GB, we could stay on rackspace and drop down to about $88/month. If we switched to most other providers we would be somewhere between $60-75 for that same service. Kicking it up to 4GB would push it to around $120 vs $90. I'm hesitant to switch to a dedicated service though, because if I weren't around, the person that takes over has to be able to admin an actual server. With our current setup, the person that takes over only has to admin the forums, and can honestly know almost nothing about servers.


    Honestly, if I could have fiber delivered to my home, I'd just buy 2 mac mini's, a dedicated UPS, a RAID1 NAS, and set them both up as virtualized servers.

    The first year would cost us about the same as what a year costs us, but after that, it would just be $20/mo or whatever I felt like charging for some % of my bandwidth+power.


    When we were on Baelish's servers (which were doing a lot of shifty shit in the background) we were not fault tolerant at all. A hard drive failure would have meant Graffes was completely lost. A lot of the dedicated server systems we can pay for run less than what we pay right now, but there are no backups, you have to generate them yourself, which is also additional cost to store the backups.

    Right now if we need to restore a backup, I just contact Rackspace, ask for the backup to be restored, and 10 minutes later it's back.

    Also, we're grandfathered in on Rackspace and new accounts cost $150 (because thats what you're actually getting... We're just only using about 25% of what we're getting). We could resell the remainder if anyone wanted to buy space... Would anyone feel better about paying say $15/month if you could get site along with it?
