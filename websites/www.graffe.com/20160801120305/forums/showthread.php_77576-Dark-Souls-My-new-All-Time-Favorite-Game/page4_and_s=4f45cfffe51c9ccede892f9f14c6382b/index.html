    Re: Dark Souls: My (new) All-Time Favorite Game.

    He is totally worth killing for his sweet loot, though.

    There are a couple of ways to fight him. The one thing that applies to basically any non-cheese method is being able to time roll-dodges to evade his attacks effectively. If you dodge too soon, he'll correct his swing and flatten you-- probably in the middle of a roll which is likely to kill you instantly. If you dodge too late... well, you dodged too late. But if you time it right, you'll have an opportunity to strike while he's lifting that Dragon Tooth off the ground.

    The standard tactic is to backstab him repeatedly. To get a good backstab, run toward him when locked on and then hit the attack button right as you swing around. Obviously, you'll need to time roll dodges pretty well to avoid his slams. Alternately, with a good high-stability shield you can have some success just baiting out attacks that you are comfortable dodging and blocking everything else. This takes a lot of patience or a pretty powerful weapon.

    He's very strong in all physical defense categories-- the strongest is slashing (katanas, scimitars, etc.) and the least strongest is striking (mace, club, etc.). He is strong against magic, fire, and lightning and is extremely difficult to bleed. Which basically leaves poison. He can be poisoned. A non-cheese method of poisoning would use rotten pine resin, poison daggers, and/or dung pies and then fighting him normally, with the DoT making the fight a little shorter than it would be otherwise.

    The cheese method is... once you get him poisoned, just run out of the tower and he'll de-aggro. Make sure you aren't near any hollows (if you're approaching from the burg) or crystal golems (if you're coming from darkroot) then go catch up on the hot chicks thread with pics while the poison finishes him off.

    Cheesing is harder than it sounds because, as you mentioned, there's no open space to hit him with the poison attacks. You have to engage with him at least long enough to get him poisoned (and the throwing daggers/dung pies/etc. are a bit difficult to use mid-melee)

    LOL oops. Andre is easily the most useful NPC in the game. Do NOT kill any more blacksmiths! Did you at least buy the weapon smithbox before killing him?

    he seemed to just snatch it for his own
    This actually gave him the ability to upgrade your weapons to divine, in the same way that Rickert upgrades your weapons to magic. That said, you can still make it through the game without him and get plenty of powerful weapons, you'll just be limited to magic and fire upgrade paths, or "unique" items that use twinkling titanite, like the Black Knight Sword.

    One question: I've ascended my weapons to +5 magic. Is divine above magic? I still can't seem to hit ghosts.
    Magic and Divine are basically equivalent, except that Divine weapons scale with faith and Magic weapons scale with Intelligence. Also, divine weapons get bonus damage against dark creatures. To upgrade magic weapons past Magic +5, you'll need the "Large Magic Ember" or the "Enchanted Ember." If you find one of those embers, Rickert will take it (don't kill him lol), and then when you 'Modify Equiment' you'll see that you'll be able to upgrade it to either Magic+6 or Enchanted (+0). Then you'll be able to upgrade to Magic +10 or Enchanted +5, which are both terminal upgrades.

    Ghosts can be damaged with cursed weapons, though you won't have any of those for awhile (the most common cursed weapon actually drops from the ghosts themselves... the other ones you won't be able to get since you killed andre :P) and even then, ghost's attacks will still ignore all your armor.

    The main way to fight ghosts is to be cursed yourself. When you are cursed it doesn't matter what weapon you use, and your armor will actually work. There are two ways to gain a cursed status, I'll leave you to discover those on your own.

    Re: Dark Souls: My (new) All-Time Favorite Game.

    There was no indication his menu had changed.
    Yeah this can be a little confusing.

    The key is the "Modify Equipment" line. Rickert has that one, too. At the start, when you try to modify equipment it'll tell you either "Need ember to ascend weapons" or "no weapons for ascension":

    You need to give an ember to the blacksmith to unlock ascension capability, and to actually have a weapon show up there to be eligible for ascension it needs to be maxed out in its current category. For example when you upgraded your weapon to +5 normal, it became eligible for ascension to magic by Rickert (who doesn't need an ember to do the magic upgrade). Before you upgraded it to +5 it would have told you "no weapons for ascension."

    After giving Andre the Divine Ember, if you'd looked in the Modify Equipment submenu, you'd have noticed a new selection at the top that looked like a white orb, and any +5 weapons would be there eligible for ascension to divine.

    Re: Dark Souls: My (new) All-Time Favorite Game.

    So you can convert any weapon to divine if the smith has an ember?
    Well, there's only one divine ember in the game, and Andre is the only blacksmith who can make divine weapons.

    I've invested too much time to hit restart because of one mistake.
    One thing to point out: A certain amount of time investment carries over to a second playthrough. While drops have to be gotten again, you don't have to spend as much time learning how to do stuff, where everything is, and what the enemy attack patterns are. You can, for example, take the Master Key and run and grab Astora's Straight Sword before you even go to undead burg.

    Speaking of Astora's, it's great in the first half of the game and great against undead skeletons in particular. But later in the game though, it will likely be outclassed by other weapons due to the low base damage and 3-way split on scaling (you need to level strength, dex, AND faith to get the most out of it). Astora's moveset is actually identical to the regular Longsword moveset, so you'd probably like the regular longsword, too. The broadsword is comparatively shorter and doesn't have that nice thrusting R2. I definitely recommend raising your strength to 20 so you can try out the Black Knight Sword, though. It's slower than Astora's, but can ultimately be far more damaging and is great for many bosses.

    One general tip: Consider raising your Endurance. It's the like king of stats. Endurance raises your stamina (the green bar in combat) which is used for everything except spells and items. Endurance also raises your equip load, which raises your rolling threshold. For example, if you had a 40 endurance, you'd be mid-rolling with your current equipment (currently you are fat-rolling. Notice that screen quake every time you roll?).

    For specific options you can take, given that you won't have access to divine weapons without Andre:

    • Keep leveling strength and use black knight weapons. There are 4 different variants and all of them are cool.
      Spoiler for other strength weapons:
      There's also the Giant's Halberd, Gravelord Sword, and the Dragon Tooth.
    • Stop leveling strength and dex, focus on endurance and vitality, and use mostly fire or chaos weapons (and switch to Astora's or whatever when you hit a fire-resistant enemy). Fire and Chaos weapons have no scaling and ignore stats completely, assuming you have the stats to wield the base weapon. I'd recommend trying out a Fire Battle Axe or Fire Longsword first. The weapons dropped by ghosts also have almost no scaling.
      Spoiler for Fire/Chaos weapons:
      There is a secret blacksmith in the Catacombs that can upgrade your normal +5 weapons to fire weapons. There are two embers you can find and give him to enable either +10 Fire or +5 Chaos weapons from there
    • Level dex and use:
      Spoiler for Unique Dex Weapons:
      Silver Knight Straight Sword and Silver Knight Spear. Both are great weapons. The Silver Knight Straight Sword is notably buffable
    • It's not too late to go the intelligence route; though if you do, I would stop leveling faith at 20 or so, unless you find some miracle you really want to use that requires something higher. Then, when your int gets high enough, you can use Velka's Talisman (buy from Oswald), which lets you cast miracles using your INT stat. Maybe play a corrupted paladin who turns to magic after losing his faith. You could level your int up to 40 and have access to lots of sorcery spells and any magic/enchanted weapon you want
    • There are three more faith-oriented weapons that you can get without Andre. Warning... definitely spoilers in this next section:
      Spoiler for MAJOR SPOILERS:
      The Crescent Axe, which basically works just like Astora's Straight sword except it's a weirdly long axe instead of a sword. Not a very popular weapon.

      Grant, a huge club requiring 50 strength and 30 faith to wield. Dropped by Paladin Leeroy (yep, WoW reference) when you defeat his phantom in the Tomb of the Giants. Note you must be human for him to invade, and Tomb of the Giants is an end-game area.

      The Dragonslayer Spear, Ornstein's weapon. While this requires 24 strength and 24 dex to wield, it's damage output will be mediocre until Dex, Strength, and Faith are all raised. It also requires defeating Ornstein last. It does have much more stamina/poise drain than typical spears, though, and attacks fast, so it's a pretty good weapon overall. Oh yeah, it shoots lightning. To get this without using Andre requires obtaining the Lightning Spear from Sen's fortress, downgrading it to +10 then using it with Ornstein's soul at the Giant Blacksmith.

    Without Andre, you are basically limited to the items on the bottom half of the page.
