    Re: 5.3 on PTR (Soon)

    Originally Posted by Schezar
    Err, no. The whole account gets points for the meta. I have it.
    There's two types of account-level achievements: ones that rely on individually tracked items, like the Loremaster ones, and ones that do not, like the "gold looted (combined)" achievement or the mount achievement. The ones that do not rely on individually tracked items are not only added to your account achievement score, but also to the individual scores of the characters on the account. You can verify this by using the guild tool, Roster, sort by Achievement Points (it shows the points the character has, versus the account score). For achievements that rely on individually tracked items, your account score will go up when you get it, but the character scores will not until they individually qualify for the meta. You can verify this by getting such a meta, then qualifying for it on a character that otherwise would not have had it. The next time the character zones, they'll get the toast for the meta and get the points.

    Re: 5.3 on PTR (Soon)

    So they've finally announced what the big thing super secret feature will be in 5.4... flexible raiding.

    http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/2...-raid-preview/

    Summary: you can raid with any number of people between 10 and 25, and the game will scale the challenge to match. Cross-realm invites apparently will work for current content (they need to verify this). Separate lockout from Normal/Heroic. Can get achievements in it. The loot is between LFR and Normal, and is awarded with the same system LFR uses.

    Initial thoughts: I like it, and I could see myself participating in it. I don't need to worry if I can't show up for whatever reason, because the raid won't be screwed without me. I don't know if the game is smart enough to scale the challenge versus role, though, so it sounds like "not many tanks" or "not many healers" will still screw a raid over, and I think that's the primary reason why guilds cancel raids.

    What do you folks think?
