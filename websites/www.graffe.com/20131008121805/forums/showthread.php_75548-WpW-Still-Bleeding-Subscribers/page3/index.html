    I think a couple of you are being a bit rough on Delores. Whether some of his assertions are wrong or not, he is replying to Dmitry. Dmitry. Are you usually calm when arguing with Dmitry about something and Dmitry is egging you on? Dmitry.



    I do feel like these questions are loaded though and that there is no right answer. Jumping puzzles optional? They are clearly unimportant then. Jumping puzzles have a reason to do them? Well then that sucks because I hate jumping puzzles. Spend money in the cash shop? No, well then the model is doomed and the game is going to fail. Yes? Why when it's all pay to win garbage?

    Originally Posted by Delores Mulva
    So far, five people have responded to the "how much have you spent" question. For two, the answer was "nothing". For two, the answer was, "More than $15/month, but I'm only counting months when I played." And for one, the answer was, "Less than $15/month." Anyone else?
    I don't remember how much I spent because I don't remember how much I bought with in game currencies and how much I spent with dollars. I got some bank slots, character slots, and a couple seasonal cosmetic things, I think.

    There are two things you need to understand about this kind of system, and why it is appealing to people like me.

    1) This is true of League of Legends as well, but when something is free to play, the barrier of entry is low, and when someone like me plays something for free for three months, they decide the developers earned something extra, so I buy a skin or something.

    2) At the same time, someone like me doesn't like to buy much because I have limited funds and that could go to another game. But there are different people using the system, and some of them make up the difference by spending far more than I do. Maybe they make more money, maybe they don't play other games. Even LoL is profitable in systems like this, and they don't even have a retail box.

    Anyway the subscription model is a dinosaur and is largely responsible for the worst things about mmo design. The idea that you have to create a never ending grind or else the players will get bored and cancel. So dumb.

    P.S. And for the record, I think it's fine that you don't think the game will be for you, but I do think you are too dismissive all the same. The reactionary combat is arguably the only real reason for you not to try the game. It even does alts better, if I remember correctly.

    Re: WpW Still Bleeding Subscribers

    Originally Posted by Axxin
    Could you elaborate on that? I'm a terminal altoholic, so this would be important to me.
    I haven't played in about 7 months (wow!) so I can't remember what exactly gave me that impression. I think the thing I most liked was the fact that your bank stash is shared across your account, so it's effortless to keep all your alt needs in check without resorting to ridiculous 30 minute sessions of mailing stuff to yourself. You will still end up with mule characters, but it's easier to shift things around with a shared stash. Guild and achievement tracking is also account based instead of character based, so you make progress even if you play an alt. I still haven't seen an mmo do this to the way I would do it, but it's a step.

    Thanks to the leveling system, content is viable for far longer than in other mmos, so you can play about three character's worth of pve content without getting bored and doing the same thing twice*. When you do find yourself wanting to play an alt, have exhausted your pve options, and don't want to pvp, you can use excess tradeskill resources and level up a bit that way. While it is possible to get to 80 this way, I used it a bit differently. One character would mine resources that they didn't use, they leveled up by mining the resources. I gathered it all in the bank instead of selling it to other players, and when I started a new character I would get about 10 levels with the resources. They would then also harvest the resources and replenish the stock for my next alt.


    *not including any content added after I left. I know this because I did it. I have a tendency to create an efficient plan to cover as much content as possible, and three characters is what I came up with across the various shared faction zones. In WoW there were always a couple zones you always had to do in some level ranges, and in other level ranges you would have more zones than you need, so you would out-level some of them. This is not a problem in GW2.
