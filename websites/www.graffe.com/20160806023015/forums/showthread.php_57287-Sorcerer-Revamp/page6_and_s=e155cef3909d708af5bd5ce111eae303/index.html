    My guess?

    We wont need the forms.


    Instead, we might get a complete base spell damage increase that places us inbetween where the brun dps form and efficient form would have placed us.

    So, if sorcerer's end up dealing 3k dps after the revamp on average, rogues, might be 2.8k, and down from there.



    I still think a "flat" dps hierarchy isnt a good idea, and that it should be more situationally prone.



    Also, energy WILL be an issue, this is something that has been reassured in multiple instances by developers and class leads, so "dps potential" and "efficient dps" will be two very different aspects.


    Lumping a classes damage into just "dps" doesnt mean crap in a game where energy conservation is a key gameplay element, and if they manage to add such a element, it could drastically change how the DPS balance is handled.




    In my game im designing called Paths, im looking into various ways to have multiple dps "roles," and so far there is a "primary" and a "secondary" dps role. Primary is more "wizardish," in that it deals strong direct damage, but really doesnt debuff or enhance damage, while secondary does more consistant damage while lowering resistances or buffing allies ect, more of a damage enabler.

    With six "classes", each containing 5 "paths", which as seperate roles, that leaves 6 primary dps classes and 6 secondary dps classes.

    Examples:

    Melee classes:

    In the Knight class, the Berserker is the primary dps, and the Cavalier (mounted pike-wielding combatant) is the secondary/ranged dps.

    In the Ranger class, the Bladedancer is the primary dps, and the Archer is the secondary, ranged based dps.

    Caster classes:

    The Priest class (spiritual casters), the Shade is a primary dps that uses shadow magic to deal devistating damage, while the Inquisitor is a secondary dps that uses divine retribution to demoralize foes and enhance the damage of allies.

    The Mage class, the Spellweaver uses raw magic energy to slash at their foe with their channeling blade (primary dps), while the Enchanter augments his allies while crushing his foes with telekentic and mental magic.





    Primary dps roles will not be shallow, though they are focused on damage almost exclusively.

    A berserker, for instance, will have to keep his health low while not dieing, in order to deal great dps, but he will have tools that allow him to do this with the normal risk a high damage class has (aka not as suicidal as the original VG berserker)

    A spellweaver will have to mix their spell attacks together on the fly to produce various different attacks, similar too but different than the Bladedancer, who composes Dances made of various Steps to perform combat arts in a sequence while fighting.


    So, you wont simply press 1 2 3 4 and deal loads of dps, but instead it requires the player to play smartly, look for openings, and watch hate gain.

    The secondary dps classes, while normally doing less damage than the Primary, will have situations where their reliable, lower hate dps is superior, and they will be able to enhance the damage potiental of Primary classes by a large amount.


    Also, with how my leveling is set up, you could be a Mage who is both a "good" Spellweaver and a "good" Enchanter if you wanted, though you would be very shallow in the other 3 Paths.

    Or, you could focus on simply one and "master" it, making you excell at that role, but you would be mediocre at a second role, and poor at the remaining 3.

    Plenty of options of how to design your character in my game, but yea, I've really tried to nail down the dps classes in my game so that they are all applicable and have various situations where they excell, and having all of them will give you the highest net total rather than stacking multiples.

Making CV a buff that increases the damage of other spells. That CV buff wouldn't scale anymore or do any type of damage. In a raid you would see us doing around maybe 15-20k damage every 4 seconds.
