    There are three broad categories of spells to fix with the sorcerer. Opinion on most forums, and indeed the game’s descriptions, seems to be overwhelming that these classifications are valid and describe the main spell lines and role for the sorcerer class in Vanguard. These fall into the damage output, the anti-caster and spell defense, and the utility/buffs/summoned items/etc. All three of these areas must be investigated to conduct a truly successful or viable revamp. So far it looks like the dps part is likely to be satisfactory. Right now there is not enough information to decide whether the spell defense and other things will be handled as effectively.

    In other words these points are the goal of the revamp:

    1. Elevate class damage to the proper level and ordained relative position among the classes.

    2. Enable the anti-magic/spell defense theme to be reflective of the class vision. This means make this category of spells more like the non-dps abilities of the druid and necromancer.

    3. Fix and upgrade the utility/buffs/etc.

    4. By far most importantly is to increase class flavor and fun value. This is the greatest of all the objectives.



    Here is a rough list of the major spells and their purposes. By classifying them it will be easier to diagnose problems and prescribe solutions.

    The forms should take care of a sizeable portion of the damage issue. The Flamespear enhancements, though perhaps a simplification, will aid this goal. Reducing Seradon’s FS refresh would also help however it needs to be kept in proportion with the other dds and should remain a heavy hitter that is used less often throughout a fight.

    As for Shocking Grasp if the other suggestions throughout last year and even in this year are not viable then it might be good to simply make it a heavy hitter with an 100 endurance cost and limited range. By doing this it wouldn’t be just another unused, non-unique spell. These direct damage spells need to be different and innovative. If arcane is the main element then each of these arcane spells should have their own separate roles. Not all of them should be so basic.


    With the anti-magic/spell defense category there are three key points: Make this specialty more useful, more flavorful, and more flexible. The Sanctuary spells are finally on the right track although the duration can be an annoyance.

    The Anti-magic aura/Sanctuary/CV proposal by Jades would be a massive step forward in the revamp. This concept is much better than piling up more and more wards or self-only buffs.


    When the revamp was discussed in 2007 it seemed that the developers were favoring dramatic changes to the wards. The Nullifying Ward and the Reflective one were slated to be altered to compensate for the dual challenges of ward bypass and potential overpower in raids. Chromatic Halo was slated to become a single target, across the board, % based spell mitigation effect. This version of CH actually sounds to fit better with the class theme than the self-rune but until this appears on the test server it will be hard to say for sure.

    It is well within the realm of feasibility to fix these issues within this category.



    As for utility the main thing here is to fix the energy shards and cut their refresh times. With buffs, as a general principle, it is better to have fewer, yet more effective ones than a multitude of junk as was often the case in Everquest and other games. It only makes sense to have a few self-only buffs since they are essentially an innate ability that has to be recast and having too many can lead to arguments against other changes such as better damage spells. It makes sense for the barriers and amplify lines to be self-only however other types of buffs should not be this way. The sorc should not have melee buffs and should not be a buffing class but it was kind of rediculous to have a minor + skill buff be self-only. At least with the change to + accuracy that sort of thing won't be an issue anymore.


    Adding more snares/roots or CC is something that would just be a fluff distraction from what really needs to be done and what really is within the sorc's true domain.
