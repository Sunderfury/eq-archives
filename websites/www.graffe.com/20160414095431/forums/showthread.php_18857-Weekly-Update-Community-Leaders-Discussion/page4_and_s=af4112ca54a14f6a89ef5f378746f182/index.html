    I remember around 2000 - 2001, Verant or Sony asked upon their boards ideas on how to balance the classes and improve the game. I remember printing hundreds of pages of ideas just on bards (since it was what I was playing) from the players to read later. As time marched onward, the players started questioning if there were any developments, any plans, any schedules from their work. While some of the ideas were not feasible, there were many that could have worked, at least in my opinion. Eventually Verant or Sony faded with everybody's ideas and went about business as usual.

    Over the years, I have come to the realization that Sony decided "nerf" was too drastic a term and replaced it with "balance".

    In my opinion, the whole gathering of ~famous~ people playing the game was just a smoke screen. I feel that if World of Warcraft was not in the works, then Omens of War would have already been released. Yes, the same expansion Sony is asking ideas about today. While something ~minor~ like a non-player character porting people to Hate appears to be no big thing, I remember when patches broke bards for days to weeks at a time. It was no big thing then either. Complaints fell upon deaf ears. EverQuest was the biggest game around and Sony knew people were not going to just up and leave for a shallower game elsewhere.

    So if we should act mature and responsible, then I have no complaints. If we need to collect our comments and thoughts to present them as possible ideas on expanding the game overall, then let Sony see what we can think up. But if a few months down the road, if the off-chance Warcraft "bombs", then can Sony at least lube us up before bending us over?

    Because if EverQuest remains the biggest game around, unless the players outright quit, we're going to get the shaft, again.

    I've posted this idea on a few boards; I'd like to get y'all's take on it.

    Here's what I would do to help flagging. Introduce the concept of "guild access."

    For each flagged zone, calculate the ratio of level 60+ players who are flagged for that zone over the total number of level 60+ players in the guild. If that ratio is above X%, any member of that guild who is level 60+ is granted access to that zone. Set the X% to a high number like 75%, or even 85% like current raids to limit abuse.

    Note that this is NOT a flag. It's a way of allowing guild members to bypass a flag. If an unflagged member joins the guild, he reduces the guild access ratio, but he gains access to the zones to which that guild has access. If an unflagged member leaves the guild, he loses access to those zones.

    For the purposes of forming a raid with unflagged guests, people who can enter the zone via guild access count as flagged for determining how many unflagged raid members can zone in. (In other words, you don't take up a "piggy" slot if you have guild access, regardless of whether it's a raid with your guild or a pickup raid.)

    Add a new tab to the guild manager to track guild access. For each restricted zone in the game, show whether the guild has access to that zone. If they don't, also show how many current guild members need to earn the flag to allow the guild access.

    Thus, guilds never have to backflag unless they fall below the X% threshold. If they do fall below it, then they have a significant number of people who need the flag, and it becomes worth their time to do the flagging event or arrange to get their members flagged by another guild still working on that content.

    What do you guys think?
