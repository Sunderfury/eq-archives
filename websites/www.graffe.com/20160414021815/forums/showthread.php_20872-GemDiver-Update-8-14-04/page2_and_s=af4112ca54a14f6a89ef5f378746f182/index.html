    As for fixing backflagging, I still think what I suggested in a previous thread would be ideal...

    Three ways to get a flag:
    1) Kill the flag mob/do the quest. (The "old way")
    2) Be a member of a flagged guild. A guild would be "flagged" after having completed the flag event X times and/or having Y% of it's membership flagged. This would be tied to the guild, not players - players flagged only by association with the guild would loose the flag should they disband, and obviously would not count toward the % of members flagged.
    3) Gain the flag via "naturalization" - be a member of a flagged guild for a given period of time and possibly do something (perhapse even just spend time) in the zone to which the flag applies. This is equivilant to the original flags, and stays with you even if you leave the guild.

    To those who have their flags I say congrats and you should be proud that you have them..whether they be the PoP or GoD ones. And I can understand the sentiment from those that have them that others behind should go through the same process.

    Having said this though you should consider the fact that getting flags now is not the same as it was a year or two ago. I think many servers are more mature now and the available pool of players trying to get them is smaller.

    I am in the process of finally doing something about getting flags for myself. I have several Planar flags now, and just need a couple of sewers or a Rhondo raid to get the KT flag.
    What I am experiencing on Veeshan anyway is that it is very difficult to get in on a flag raid. Many guilds that have them are not really backflagging much, and there are occasional public raids, but its hit or miss with those. There used to be a very active public raid thing called EQ Raiders on Veeshan but they sort of dissolved when much internal bickering started and people went their separate ways. I have been on a few public raids and trust me..they can get pretty ugly.

    My opinion is that there should be some alternative ways to get these flags..quests or groups or smaller guilds being able to get flags for people. As time goes by this situation is going to get worse for those that are trying to get flags, as there may be a smaller pool of people trying to get them, but more importantly not as many guilds capable enough to go after them. The guilds that are done with them now are busy in GoD and will be moving on to OoW when it is released. No one should have to sit around PoK or PoT hoping for some pick up raid to start..which is not what this game should be about.

    Well, there's more to "running out of things to do" than being max level/AA's...

    You don't see people quitting due running out of progression and burning out BECAUSE everquest is full of timesinks. Without them, I can guarantee you'd see a lot of it. Even with timesinks in place, top guilds have traditionally lost members, at least temporarily, at the end of most expansions due to simply not having anything interesting left to do until new content is released. Not having timesinks would basically magnify that many times over.

    Look at Diablo II - it has relatively few timesinks compared to EQ. How many people do you know who can play D2 with any regularity over a prolongued period of time? The longest it can hold my attention is a week or two at a couple hours a day.

    There are two things that comprise the backbone of a game like EQ: Timesinks and Depth. EQ is wildly successful because it has great depth and successful timesinks.
