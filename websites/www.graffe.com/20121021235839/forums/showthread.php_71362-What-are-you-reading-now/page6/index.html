    Re: What are you reading now?

    Personally I'm a big fan of Sanderson and I think he did a great job. You can definitely tell there's a major change in the writing style between the Gathering Storm and Towers of Midnight which leads me to believe that Robert Jordan had a sizable chunk of the Gathering Storm already complete and Sanderson didn't need to write as much new material as he had to do in Towers of Midnight. I believe that this is to be expected and I don't mind it at all. Even when Jordan was the only person writing you could tell he underwent several major writing style changes (particularly I've found that between Lord of Chaos and Crown of Swords the "feel" of the series took a turn).

    Overall though I think Sanderson did a very competent job of taking over the Wheel of Time and seeing the series through to it's conclusion. I honestly don't think that anyone else could have done a better job at finishing this series than him. He's an accomplished writer in his own right but he's very respectful towards Jordan so he's not trying to put his own spin on the series but is staying faithful to the source material. I can only imagine the rape scenes that Terry Goodkind would have thrown in there or Steve Erickson ratcheting up the power level of the Asha'man to the point where the Dark One becomes a minor power if they had been given the series instead of Sanderson.

    Finally it's nice to see a large number of plot lines finally getting resolved but I'm still curious how the series is going to wrap up a number of loose ends. For example, the whole Seanchan angle still hasn't been dealt with in a definitive manner yet and it seems like the final book's primary focus will be on the Last Battle between Rand's forces of Light and the Shadow. So I'm really interested to see how that is dealt with.

    I've been reading this series since my freshman year of college (right as Lord of Chaos came out so you can do the math) and I'm looking forward to it's conclusion.
    There are other worlds than these.
