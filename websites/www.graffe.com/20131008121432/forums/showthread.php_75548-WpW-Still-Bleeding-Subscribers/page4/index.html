    Originally Posted by Silke
    Right now Blizzard is trying to be too safe and they're failing as a result of it. They're losing subscribers at a rapid rate and will continue to do so for the foreseeable future and they deserve to. It's an old game based on an old business and game model that hasn't adapted to today's new paradigm....
    I'm not sure one can shout at Blizzard for "playing it safe" and in one's very next post complain about a fairly radical redesign of one class spec. It's certainly not true in game, where the patches starting with the final Cataclysm patch have seen the most massive changes in the history of the game. If anything, they're taking heat from players that feel that the change is too much, like the many haters of LFR. (I think they need to manage the change better and prioritize things like policing player interaction more, not roll back the change. But I digress.)

    I also don't have nearly the confidence in FTP that you do. Blizzard has seen numbers from the WoW store and from D3. They have a good idea what sorts of revenue can be expected from a FTP game. NCSoft has City of Heroes, Lineage, Aion, and Guild Wars data. Lineage had to shut down the NA servers. Aion was made FTP roughly a year ago, in response to radically declining subscription numbers. I'd tell you how that change worked out, but NCSoft doesn't release Aion player numbers and I can't find anyone outside South Korea who plays the game to ask. City of Heroes changed its model to FTP when numbers dipped low, and subsequently had to shut the servers. You saw the NCSoft revenue numbers I linked: once the box sales started drying out, the revenue crashed. The company PR hack explicitly promised an expansion box to shareholders, which is news to the GW2 community which has heard nothing but "free content" from the devs for months.

    Maybe you have blind faith that the economics of FTP will somehow work, and that one hand of a company contradicting the other hand when it comes to stuff is just a misunderstanding and not a bad sign. As I've said before, I'd be very happy if GW2 stays profitable. It has pushed the WoW devs to produce more and be more creative (with mixed success at both), it's quite clearly a quality game for those who like the features the devs have chosen (and those people have been ill-served by previous MMORPG releases), and if FTP worked and WoW lowered or dropped their sub price as a result I'd be pleased as punch. But given how many games have switched to FTP only to crater anyways (not just the NCSoft titles I listed), I have to question if the model has long-term viability.
