    Re: The Last of Us Launch Thread

    Originally Posted by Naean
    For what it's worth I am especially bad at playing this type of game and generally avoid them because I get motion sick from pretty much all first person and many over the shoulder shooters (not sure what factors make the difference).
    I sometimes have the same problem.

    It is most likely the Field of Vision (FOV) that is bugging you. Nearly every game made these days have a FOV slider (but rare on console games), just because people often get motion sickness when the FOV is set to 60-70 which is for some very strange reason often is set to as default. If you for instance watch TotalBiscuits WTF videos where he looks at games, he nearly always bitches at the developer if they do not add a FOV slider to the game, because he gets sick constantly if there isn´t one.

    Changing it to 80-90 will usually remove all sense of motion sickness. If you have Skyrim on the PC, that is a good game to test it out on as you can edit the FOV as well as it having first and third person perspectives to find the sweet spot so to speak for your eyes.

    Re: The Last of Us Launch Thread

    I think in that particular case it was a level design decision. They didn't want you clearing the area first, they wanted to make it tense and creepy.

    The thing is, this stuff isn't new, but usually games create an artificial explanation as to why enemies are spawning in order to preserve immersion. Overdone it still becomes silly, but at least it's not as jarring. In Dead Space enemies will crawl out of ventilation shafts for example. In Uncharted or Tomb Raider they might come from off the map, making you think the area you are in is larger than the space you actually play in. In The Last of Us they teleport in from a klingon battlecruiser in orbit. I suppose if it weren't for listen mode it wouldn't be quite as obvious. That's what you get for being addicted to wall hacks.

    Re: The Last of Us Launch Thread

    Finally beat it, was good. Had me on the edge of my seat right until the end, even though I sort of guessed most of the plot points anyway. I thought the writing and acting were pretty good!

    Tried some of the multiplayer. It's very slow paced and very, very tense. Uncharted stressed me out by being too hyperactive, this is stressing me out by being too slow! And with teams made up of only 4 people, I end up feeling kind of like I'm dragging the team down. I had one guy on mic tell me not to go through a window a second before I hit x to enter the window. I blew up. I felt bad.

    So multiplayer has this weird metagame pretty much designed to keep you playing for longer than you might normally play. You choose a clan and play a sort of campaign, you can't pick a new clan until after you either win or lose the campaign. Each match is considered a day, and your clan has to survive for about 12 weeks. You bring back supplies to the clan after each match, and the amount you bring back is based on how well you did in the match. You get more survivors over time, and so need more and more supplies over time. Eventually you also get to pick missions, I only had a few seconds before the next match started so I only looked at a few of them. The one I picked was to "down" three enemies a day for three days. If you succeed you get more survivors, I think if you fail you lose some.

    It's all very interesting, but I don't think the campaign is enough of a carrot to keep me playing. You do unlock stuff as you go, but since the game is more "realistic" than Uncharted, the bonuses are pretty simple and useful things instead of crazy nonsense like cluster grenades or whatever. It takes 84 matches to finish a campaign, and that's assuming you get good enough to not completely botch it halfway through by sucking so bad. I don't think I can commit that much time to this.
