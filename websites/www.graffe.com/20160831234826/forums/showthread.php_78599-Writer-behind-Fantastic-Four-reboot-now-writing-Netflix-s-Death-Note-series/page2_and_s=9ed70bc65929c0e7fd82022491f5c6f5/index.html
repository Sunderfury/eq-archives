    Re: Writer behind Fantastic Four reboot now writing Netflix's Death Note series...

    You goober, did you just quote yourself?

    On the subject of dubs vs subs, translation vs localization, I go both ways. Twenty years ago, subs were better because the quality of voice acting in the west was terrible. It has come a long, long way, and is sometimes enough to be superior even in English. The FLCL dub is so amazing, for example, that I could probably go back and forth between episodes and barely even notice.

    I find that a lot of the time the original Japanese sounds better because the language is so foreign that a western listener wouldn't be capable of discerning when it is bad in the first place. Furthermore, sometimes when something is localized rather than translated directly, it's because of a cultural difference that most people wouldn't understand.

    Contemporary Japanese stories, however, are best told from the original Japanese point of view. It makes no sense to force western values on a story about a teenager in modern Japan. At the end of the day, I will choose between subs or dubs on a case by case basis. Extremes from both arguments are responsible for some pretty amusing memes. Being too faithful to the original, vs westernizing so inappropriately that it becomes a huge joke. Amusingly enough, one of these examples is from Death Note.
