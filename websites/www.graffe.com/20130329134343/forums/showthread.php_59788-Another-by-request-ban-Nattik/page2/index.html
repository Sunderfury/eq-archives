    Re: Another by request ban: Nattik

    Calling an assumption a reasonable guess is to me about the same as the difference between the words alcoholic and drunk. The only difference is that one set is worded to not offend.
    An alcoholic is a description of a person who habitually imbibes alcohol at unhealthy levels, even if they are not currently drunk or haven't had a drink in years. "A drunk" is a similar and less precise description that means "someone who is always drunk." Drunk is primarily a description of a state of being impaired by alcohol. They are quite different. Furthermore, Daph already explained what the analogy was meant to show so if you're still hung up on the comparison that's your own error.

    The difference between a reasonable guess and an assumption is that an assumption is expected to be taken for granted as truth to support a logical conclusion.

    You and Dmitry assumed he had no good reason for this and consequently judged him that way. Daph put forth a sample hypothesis suggesting he should not be judged. Her assumption is that you share the value that someone trying to solve a problem like alcohol addiction shouldn't be interfered with.
