    Class Spell:

    Arcane Barrage:
    Cast time: 0.75 seconds
    Recast time: 2.25 seconds
    650 mana

    600 damage, magic resist mod -50.

    Special: Arcane Barrage Recourse: An arcane barrage is a fairly wasteful attack, spilling most of the mana into pools surrounding the caster. However, due to the forces that govern magical powers, a second barrage will align the pooling mana and gather it up, adding to its own power. (manifests as a 1 tick short duration buff)

    While you have this recourse, any Arcane Barrage cast within 100 feet of you will draw 50 mana from you, and strike the targeted creature for 700 damage (magic resist mod -50)


    Implications: Say 12 wizards gather together, all with 0 focus and 0 crit rate. They all cast this spell as fast as they can (once every 3 seconds). This keeps the recourse up on all of them at all times, granting every wizard the bonus damage from his/her comrades. Every 3 seconds, each wizard would be doing 600 + (700 * 11) = 8,300 damage for nearly 2800 dps (for roughly 1200 mana per cast), add in crits and spell foci and mana recursion (..... assuming all wizards had this, it would trigger at least 12 times per cast.... ouch) and whatever else you can throw on top, and this spell would quickly become quite overpowered (aka, needs massive tuning).

    Basically, the purpose of the spell is to significantly boost burst dps (so a real version of the spell might be much more mana intensive).

    Numbing Storm

    Casting time: 1.5 seconds
    Recast time: 2.25 seconds

    mana: 1500

    damage: 4500 cold, mod -50

    recourse on mob: Numb Skin

    duration: 6 seconds

    While this effect is active, the next ten fire damages taken by this monster is focused 50%/60%/100%. Also while this effect is active, the next cold damage taken by this monster is focused -150%/-125%/-100% and the effect fades.

    Heat Stroke:

    Casting time: 1.5 seconds
    Recast time: 2.25 seconds

    mana: 1500

    damage 4500 fire, mod -50

    Recourse on mob: Fiery Delirium:

    duration: 6 seconds

    While this effect is active, the next ten cold damages taken by this monster is focused 50%/60%/100%. Also while this effect is active, the next fire damage taken by this monster is focused -150%/-125%/-100% and the effect fades.


    These spells would try to force co-operation and syncronization among wizards and other direct damage dealers similar to the planning healers have gone through. Obviously, they need tuning, and at first seem completely illogical:

    while you're numb from cold, you do not notice cold, but you do notice fire much more (the temperature difference is what you feel, after all). The same principle applies to both spells. Also, even though the focuses granted by these spells would still be random like we have now, it would be multiplicitive with worn and flappy foci, and since it would happen when the spell actually landed, it would work on the critical parts of damage too.

    The recourses should ignore weapon procs (otherwise it will quickly render these spells useless).
