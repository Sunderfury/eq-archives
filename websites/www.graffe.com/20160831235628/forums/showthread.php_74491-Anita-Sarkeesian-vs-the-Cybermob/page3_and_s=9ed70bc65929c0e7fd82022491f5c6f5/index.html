    Re: Anita Sarkeesian vs the Cybermob

    I think the large amount of pushback she has gotten from the gaming community might be proof as to why something like this is needed.
    The gaming community is not that monolithic. I don't think dragging out select classic games and classic series to harp on "tropes vs women" in their plots is really all that useful.

    Criticism of the later Zelda and Mario games may be legit (I haven't played them), but then those games are very much tied to their ancestors when it comes to gameplay expectations. There's almost certainly some amount of playful, humorous self-parody going on when Sheik transforms into Zelda only to be captured almost immediately.

    Looking at the most popular RPGs of the Super Nintendo era reveals some Damsel In Distress plots but also plenty of real female characters.

    FFIV had Rosa, Rydia, and Porom as primary characters. None of them are super realistic and while cynics might consider Rosa's portrayal sexist (DiD plot included), her expressions of devotion and trust of Cecil are very sweet and the game ends with a wedding. Rydia is magical girl but has a distinct, independent personality. Porom is a stereotypical goody-two-shoes. They're all useful and capable party members (I've soloed half the game with Rydia).

    FFVI Terra and Celes are great female characters. They are the most protagonistic protagonists in the story. Relm is a bit annoying but still a notable character.

    FFVII maybe backslides, but then 3 weirdo chicks vying for the romantic attentions of a depressed and mopey leader with a crazy hairdo is not exactly unreasonable subject matter.

    In Chrono Trigger, half of the standard characters are female and all of them have agency. They relate to other characters as full-fledged humans in every respect. In fact Ayla's willpower and dominance is over-the-top. Marle's primary storyline involves her difficult relationship with her father. Speaking of Marle, she gets Damsel-Distressed, too.

    I just don't understand why you can't just spend time engaging with her arguments rather than, say, talking about how she's just out for page views.

    I do think that there is a substantial audience for pseudo-feminist raging against the man and that constitutes a large chunk of Sarkesiaan's viewership, I think that both in the past and in this video she specifically frames her arguments to piss men off and as such doesn't get a whole lot of sympathy from me when the "cybermob" attacks (although I don't in the least bit condone their behavior either). However, I don't mean to make her motives and audience the point of the discussion (although they were relevant to TED talk that started the thread.)

    Overall my primary issue with the video I linked is the lack of perspective, which I have brought up and I think the linked video addresses well, and her framing, using vague and judgmental words like problematic and pernicious instead of just critiquing the narratives or plot devices.

    For one specific example: her first gripe is about Starfox Adventures. Her data point is that the game was original conceived to feature Krystal (another character in the Star Fox universe) but was later switched to feature Fox McCloud. She spends a lot of time playing up how unfair this was for poor Krystal and how, in the new version, she's reduced to Damsel in Distress. The juxtaposition is an excellent way to highlight the difference between a DiD and a real character, but in the context of her overall point (that this is "problematic and pernicious") it's not appropriate to simply ignore the role of other practical considerations that may have led to the role changes and simply construct the narrative as if it was a selfish patriarch who wanted his golden boy creation Fox McCloud to have the starring role in their Zelda clone. Maybe that's exactly how it happened, but Anita does not persuade.

    I have to re-watch the rest of the video to address her other points. One minor one-- as I understand, the plot of Dragon's Lair was farcical. Even in the few clips shown in the videos, the game seems extremely self-conscious that they are using a silly trope and deliberately playing up the absurdity of it for laughs. Even in the narrow context of "DiD tropes," missing that is another failure of perspective.

    Your current approach reinforces the current perception of you with respect to issues that touch on women or feminism, and really doesn't do much to bolster your arguments.
