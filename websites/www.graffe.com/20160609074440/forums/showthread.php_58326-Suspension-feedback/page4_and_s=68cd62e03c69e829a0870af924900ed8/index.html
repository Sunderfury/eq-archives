    Re: Suspension feedback

    I am not sure we need to have a mod fill out a form every time they ban a spammer or a jihad'er. I suppose it's not a bad idea for people banned/suspended for other reasons though - the mod will probably have to justify themselves publically at least once anyway - is probably easier if they can just post a link to the form from then on.

    On the alt banning: In this case Dmitry was posting on his alt account what he believed to be the private PM Voca acted on, so I think that qualifies as trying to circumvent the reason for the suspension, which was to let everything cool down to bring back rational discussion instead of hectoring. He was clearly hoping to sway key parties into continuing the discussion on his behalf. I suppose a footnote could be tagged onto the original suspension post to let people know an alt has been suspended/banned. I don't think there should be an automatic extra 1 week suspension for doing so, but then I think mods should be allowed to use their judgement for all of the rules. Laying rules down too firmly allows them to be exploited imo.

    Honestly, I'm not sure I buy the 'emergency need to speak to board members' thing. Anything serious enough to be a true RL emergency, people should not be relying on advice from random strangers on a message board anyway; they should be talking to RL doctors or lawyers imo. In the rare-rare-rare exception, they can always make an alt account and post their question, and the mods will be able to use their judgement to make an exception, if it really is that rare-rare-rare exception.

    On the original suspension: The original problem was that Voca locked a thread based on her belief things could escalate in RL if she didn't, based on the fact that things did escalate in RL previously, and not too many details could be posted at the time lest it bring about the very RL escalation they were trying to prevent.

    Once the hours and the days passed, and it was clear no RL escalation was going to happen, more details couild be posted, and it began to look like an overreaction. Of course we cannot actually know how things would have turned out had the thread not been locked, cooling down the parties involved.

    Dmitry kept making the same accusation over and over and over again, and the mistake the mods made (imo) was to keep replying to it over and over and over again. They should simply have referred Dmitry to their previous reply. But in this time of transition, they kept wanting to be helpful and explain their judgement call, but Dmitry wouldn't let it lie.

    I see it as how Lysandor posted in another thread:

    Originally Posted by Lysandor
    Just mod him. Feedback is great but at a certain point a moderation decision must be given and it must be final. That's why we should elect mods every 3 or 6 months as Ringo suggested. If you don't like the decisions of a particular mod, elect someone else.

    The real problem with graffes is most of us think we are entitled to keep bitching about stuff for as long as we want. Thats fine for threads about which superhero is better then another superhero. Thats ok in TV show threads, gaming threads and political threads (for the most part), but it doesn't work in moderation issues. Render the damn decision and then stick by it. YES I WILL or NO I WONT. After that, send anyone who keeps poking at the issue with a sharp stick to the cornfield for 3 days. If you cant train them with a kiss, train them with a stick.

    I don't know anyone who would want to mod this place if there is no end to arguments over decisions made. Who the hell has that kind of time? And if they do have that kind of time, do we really want them to be a mod?
    I hear and I forget. I see and I remember. I do and I understand
