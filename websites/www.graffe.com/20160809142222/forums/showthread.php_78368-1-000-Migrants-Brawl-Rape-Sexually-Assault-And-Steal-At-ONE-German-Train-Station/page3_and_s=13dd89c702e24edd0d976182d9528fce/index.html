    In my opinion, the best thing it could lead to is de-polarization of the pro-refugee, anti-refugee factions within the EU. Recognize that both sides are problematic: the bleeding heart "welcome refugees!!!" radicals on the one side and the xenophobic reactionary agitators on the other. Admit the reality: settling a huge wave of over a million Muslim migrants in Europe in the span of a couple of years is not going to be all sunshine and roses. Be open about what's happening, no pointing fingers at each other, leave blame for later, focus on maintaining order and safety for everyone, and at least begin discussing alternative coping strategies.

    Sometimes, the forces in motion are too powerful for there to be any simple and clear solution. By the time hurricane Katrina was observed to be on a path for New Orleans, it was way too late to fix the levees. Nor is there going to be any way to stop the hurricane and turn it back to sea. But at least you should recognize what's going to happen and begin mass evacuations as soon as possible.
