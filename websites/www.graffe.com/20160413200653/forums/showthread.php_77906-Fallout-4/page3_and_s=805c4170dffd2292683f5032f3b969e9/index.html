    Re: Fallout 4

    Originally Posted by Uggliey
    Apparently it's getting beat to shit on metacritic.com. Almost 2:1 giving it negative.
    It isn't 2:1. But also, their scoring system is why having 10 point systems is fucking stupid. When asking for a crowd sourced review, what you really want was "liked it" "didn't like it" "it was terrible or unplayable"

    Breaking the User Reviews down into categories, you can see that a lot of players are complaining about the Keyboard/Mouse controls. They're fucking horrible. Unplayable horrible. I'm playing with a controller, so that didn't bother me too much. I played Skyrim with keyboard/mouse and needed mods to be able to handle it.

    The inventory UI is pretty fucking disgusting (hey, you can sort by name! now let's have some modifiers that you can add and subtract from all items that change the fucking names so they sort gets fucked!)

    And third, you have people who didn't like the story, but somehow they were up to that point yesterday morning (meaning they had to skip almost the entirety of the side quests or they played it straight through without sleep).

    Those are the three semi valid reasons to knock down the rating. But if you look at a lot of these reviews, it doesn't seem like they actually played the game. In fact, I've seen a few people say they watched someone stream this on Twitch and are reviewing it from that.

    1) People complaining about poor graphics. There is a MIP Mapping bug that causes textures to get fucked up if you load them, get too far away or out of sight, and then get close again.
    1a) People are complaining because of features like being locked to 60fps, or not supporting SLI. Yeah, because fucking no one uses SLI, and most people have shitty monitors, so locking it down makes sense. This is something you can adjust, but
    1b) People complaining that most of the graphics options have to be modified by editing an INI file. Apparently that was fine for the previous games and Elder Scrolls, but not for Fallout 4.
    1c) Complaints that framerate messes with aim speed. Yeah thats shitty. It's also a bug on a retarded amount of games, but its only noticeable on FPS games, and then its only a huge issue for a small segment of the population (Which I'm in, but I just kind of sigh about it and hope someone releases a mod/hack to fix it)

    2) People constantly saying its the Skyrim Engine, with 0 graphical upgrades, and the exact same bugs. Yeah, it's the same basic engine. A game engine is the most expensive, complicated part of any games development. Look at Destiny: Their engine is such shit, it takes >8 hours to open a map in their map editor. But the graphics are way better, and anyone that doesn't think so needs to go load the basic, unmodded Skyrim game again. People are just upset because it doesn't look as good as the massive crowd sourced Skyrim version they were playing with a fuckton of mods that required quad SLI to run. With the changes to the engine for FO4, the mods will be able to do the same thing. I had a rock solid 60fps at Ultra Settings (lowered AA, increased to 1440p) on a single nvidia 970 GTX. As I scroll through the review, I see someone saying they were using 2x R9 290X (game doesn't support SLICrossfire, so basically 1x) on a 4K monitor and were only getting 40fps.

    Note: I'm not saying this is all acceptable and Bethesda shouldn't take flak for them. I'm saying if you say Skyrim was a 10/10, and Fallout 4 is 0/10 because of the same fucking problems that Skyrim had, then you're a fucking shithead and helped cause the problem in the first place.

    Also, I hate review sites that let people post a single review and have as much weight as people who actually do several reviews. I had to look at 10 0-3 score reviews to find someone who had reviewed a second game. That other game was Titanfall, and they gave it an even lower score. LOL. Oh, just found one where someone reviewed True Detectives and gave it a 0.

    I'd give it a 7/10 for PC until they fix some of this shit. 9/10 on Console where I can ignore the keyboard/mouse control issue. It isn't as good as New Vegas, but its better than Fallout 3. A game with such stupid story decisions, they originally had dialog to explain why their shitty plot wasn't shitty (it's your destiny to die here!). I hope that we can get an Obsidian sequel (New Vegas 2), but I don't think we will.

    Spoiler for Late Story Main Questline DO NOT CLICK IF YOU CARE ABOUT SPOILERS:
    So I was talking to "Father" and listened to his explanations, but the fact that Kellogg didn't look as old as he should be to match their story made me consider it a lie. So I went along with everything, then fired my Combat Shotgun directly into his face. Then went upstairs and snooped around the Terminal, where it indicated that Kellogg really was given life extension treatments, so the timeline was more buyable.

    Oops.

    You will find that the number of games that get a positive user score on Metacritic to be low. User reviews tend to be unreliable because people in general are over-emotional and lack perspective. Metacritic in particular has always been a bit of a shit show though, full of more agenda and bias than the most paranoid "games journalism" conspiracy theory. It's enough to make me dismiss user reviews entirely, but the ones on Steam aren't so bad. Once you remove the obsession with score and can just focus on opinions, it's easy to compare a positive review with a negative one and decide if it sounds okay to you.
