    Originally Posted by camrael
    I also meant to add that the main web site of graffe.com needs some love too. Anyone have any ideas on that?
    I think its silly to not try and find a way to bring some life to it. There's so many people on Graffe's with knowledge that love sharing that knowledge every day on the forums. We can have a random news blog with artices on video games, politics, computer hardware, whatever our resident experts on a subject want to talk about. Tie some cool benefits to writing articles (like the return of flappy avatars and effects or bigger PM boxes, etc.) I think very quickly we'd have some good content that people would be interested in reading. Imagine all the fresh faces who discovered Ringo's WoW testing thread. I think setting something like this would achieve 3 basic goals:

    1) Bring in some fresh faces of people intelligent and interested in the same topics as us. It's fair to assume if someone hits one of our articles off of google, they wont try and join the forum unless they found the article interesting. So it would be like a "Did you enjoy this article about Obama versus Clinton? Well there's a 500 post thread going on right now on the forum you can see."

    2) Brings back some respect and love to the face of the site which has been neglected for so long. When you introduce someone to Graffe's, it'd be nice if there was something they could see before the forums. I think its not so much the forums are the heart of the site as the community is the heart of the site and right now the forum is the easiest and most logical place for the community to gather. It's like we're all in a room...and theres a couch and a TV....so we all watch TV. Then one day, someone puts in a ping pong table, and some people go to watch ping pong because not everyone wants to watch M*A*S*H. Then they add a computer....then a bookshelf....then a stripper's pole....you guys get the idea.

    3) Support the site to keep the forums ad-free. We can place ads on these articles outside of the forum, with the pages tracked by Google News and other spiders, and we'll get good hits of people just looking to read a specific article...but some of them might go to another article, and a few might decide "hey this is a pretty cool place, maybe I can stick around." Take that ad revenue we're generating on the non-forum pages, and dump it all into maintaining our servers and keeping the forums ad-free. If its successful enough we might reach the point where we dont even need donations anymore and the ads on the articles pay for all the server/usage that we need. If we go over how much we need and after a good reserve is setup for future hosting needs, maybe we could spend anything leftover on something like Graffe tshirts for those with 5,000+ posts or send out actual Graffie trophies. We're non-profit so I think we're required to either spend or give it all away to avoid paying taxes on it. We'd need one of the legal guru's to help with that one I think.

    It could be pretty easily achieved quickly and securely with a blogging software called ExpressionEngine. Out of the box, it looks like this:

    http://www.gemdiver.com/

    With about 20 minutes of love you can get it looking like this:

    http://www.guruexcel.com/

    and for a little bit more time, you can get this:

    http://www.castersrealm.com/

    Its pretty much fully customizable and fast and easy to setup. If we're interested in going that route, I don't mind taking my license off guruexcel and transferring it here since I never really have had the time to start working on it like I'd hoped.
