    Is this what it's like in OT all the time? Sniping back and forth at each other under the guise of semantics?

    I've lurked a few threads since this sub-forum popped up, and IMO, if its goal is to promote the acceptance of the judgement of the newer moderators, it's going to be highly counterproductive. It's ugly enough when mud gets slung in the first place; it makes a bigger mess when you wallow around in it afterwards.

    Good forum moderator-ship should be like good guild leadership -- you find some solid people, give 'em a big stick, and turn them loose to do their job. No one enjoys having an asshole in guild who's on their Third Official Warning; good guilds weed those folks out immediately and move on, no loss.

    I don't know all the new mods and their track record, but if there's an issue with someone's credentials or approach, that needs to be addressed at a higher level -- whether they should have the job in the first place. If the answer is yes, then as far as I'm concerned, they shouldn't have to justify their decisions to the kangaroo court.
