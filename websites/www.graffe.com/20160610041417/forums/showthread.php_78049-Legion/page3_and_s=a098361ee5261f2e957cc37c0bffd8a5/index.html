    Re: Legion

    Yogscast had a decent interview with Tom Chilton. Certainly a lot better than the "Q&A" crap on sunday.
    Wowhead summed up the points in a neat fashion, so I'll just copy/paste for the firewall impaired:
    Spoiler for Wowhead summary:

    Demon Hunters
    Wishes DH's could have shipped with original game, and also considered it for BC.
    After that, didn't make huge lore sense. Super happy the opportunity is finally here.
    Focus on their mobility and distinguish it further from the monk and the rogue. Rogue is more about CC, DH is more about "in your face mobility" but getting out when in trouble but also less hybridy than monk. Focus is on their aggressiveness.
    Fury vs. Rage - similar in a lot of ways. The way in which they build it up/spend is different. Rage has changed over time in that way but the core distinction is there.
    Skill shots: Eye beam is a skill shot. Narrow cone attack. Going to be mindful that it doesn't feel too much like a monk. Abilities will come and go thought Alpha and Beta.
    We might add more races to DH's in future expansions.
    They will start between 95-100. When they complete their starting experience, they will be ready to start the Legion expansion.
    Double Jump can be used in dungeons and raids and bgs. We will be careful in current content to make sure Double Jump doesn't break the game.

    Class Order Halls, etc.
    More different than they are the same (from garrisons) - no choices here, they are a public space, both factions. Only crossover similarity is that it's the place you send your champions off on missions.
    No city overlap i.e. no AH, no Bank. Not a place to spend all your time.
    Dalaran will be slightly different, modified for the story line. Texture updates. Level 70 Dalaran remains the same. Bribe guards to go away in the Dalaran Sewers while it's a free for all PvP space!

    Classes
    It's dicey to contemplate eliminating talent trees ( reference to DH's having only 2 specs vs. other classes) - don't really see it. We are going class by class to try and make them more distinctive.
    Upcoming Hunter changes are a good example for differentiation.
    Disc priest - going more offensive for Disc, more Atonement based. Holy will stay heal oriented.
    Rogues - sub is more ninja, combat is more pirate, assassination will be poisons/dots.

    PvP Changes
    PvP changes are that honor is no longer a currency/spend type of system, more of an exp system. Also talents.
    Dedicated PvPers get to max power and haven't had much of a reason to continue playing after that. No extrinsic rewards is not great. Prestige-type of system provides incentive to play at max power.
    PvP gear itself will have much less of an impact - no pvp specific sets or stats anymore. Higher item level stuff will have drastically diminishing returns in PvP. Dramatically compressed.

    Transmog/Artifacts
    Looking at changes to some transmog rules. Possibly Legendary cloak (and TC mentioned the ring?).
    Warglaives are remaining them. There is a new set of glaives that will be their Artifact weapon.
    The Ashbringer you get as an artifact is completely unique and Ret Pally only.

    Tanaan, etc.
    We view Tanaan as a success from the standpoint of it's a big awesome zone with a ton of really cool spawning and lots of engagement but we do want to continue to evolve our model of how we do end game world content. We aren't totally happy of just dailies and bonus objectives. We want more with story development (like 5.1) - we want mechanics that make the content more replayable.
    Gul'dan isn't the big bad in Legion, he's an instigator and isn't the last boss of the expansion.


    It's nice to hear that they want to build something similar to 5.1, but at this point in time I need to see it to believe it. Too many corners were cut recently.
    Ring-transmogging is kinda... eh ? Besides the legendary ring effect, I guess there's this one (also a quested one if you're cheap) and this one.

    I actually like parts of the class distinction idea - I think that a melee survival hunter with a spear would be awesome. White Lion in WAR worked this way, and that was a neat class (holy moly that was a long time ago...)
    I do fear an extreme amount of variations over the name Nidalee, though

    [minirant]
    What I don't understand: You have a priest spec, disc, that already has a very very defined "frame". If I face a disc priest in an arena, or have a disc priest in my raid, I instantly know that he's disc. I don't have to wonder about it, his playstyle makes it crystal clear from the start. So why mess with it ? It's a constant headache for the devs, I get that, but fiddle with the knobs instead of ripping them out to install a completely new dashboard. It's very tiresome.

    I can't possibly see how a "more damage-focused playstyle" can be compatible with being a solid healer - Mistweavers have that option, and while it has its uses (farm content and Horridon-style bosses with damage buffs), they also have a healing form to switch to when things get rough.

    It doesn't seem like that's the plan for disc. All I heard was "damage, dammage, dooooommmaaage!".
    [/minirant]

    Anyhow, this was more of a delayed rant in response to the expansion announcement (echoing what Delores have said). The Yogscast video is still good
