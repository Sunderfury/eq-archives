    Re: The Division Beta

    I played it for 8 hours so far, and I experienced enough to make up my mind about it...

    The city is really really cool, and it looks awesome when you run around New York in a blizzard, even on a middle-of-the-range computer like mine.
    The movement sort of grew on me, and I wasn't as bothered by it as when I first started out. The gun-play is still a bit bland, with mainly just ducking in and out of cover.

    The real problem for this game though is the lack of game...I cannot see how this game is supposed to keep players around for long. The PvE part of the beta is REALLY short, and pretty bland. The PvP part (Dark Zone) is more interesting, but with their PvP flagging system (going Rogue) being a mess, and the plentiful setup for a gankers/griefers paradise, it quickly became a big turn off for me. My gaming time is just too short to spend it on being ganked repeatedly.
    All in all I'd say that the game really lacks an actual game, because the grindfest part of getting new loot that's 1% better than the previous is not going to hold many peoples attention for more than a few hours at most, and the rest of the game just doesn't seem to be there - Apart from the beautiful surroundings, that is.

    Re: The Division Beta

    I finally finished downloading the thing and played it for a couple of hours. I'm not sure how exactly I feel about it. I wasn't blown away, but it was a lot better than I thought it was going to be. There are things I don't like, but it seems polished and designed well. Even the options menu is fully featured to a degree you don't always see in multiplatform games. I thought it felt like a console game at first, but I don't think the enemies would be much fun to fight with less accurate headshots, and I eventually got used to the controls.

    It sort of feels like a shooter mmo to me. I was expecting something sort of like Destiny, but it feels a little bit more like someone turned The Last of Us into an mmo. Gunplay isn't fast and satisfying as much as it is deliberate and tense. Everything outside of the dark zone was trivially easy though, and you guys are right that the outside areas feel empty with not many npcs or players running around. I'm actually not sure why you would need a group for anything else.

    There are a lot of little touches that are easy to overlook. Shooting gas tanks (whether on someone's back or on the ground) is a shooter staple, but you can also shoot fuse boxes to stun them, or even just spray at them while they are in cover to create a suppression effect. The environmental effects in general are also very nice. I just wish AI behavior changed accordingly. The AI definitely feels mmo level simple.

    I'm dubious about the longevity of the game. I don't think I like giving it a "make the numbers go up" loot progression system, instead of simply having each weapon or piece of gear have a set value. The weapons aren't exotic enough to make grinding for new ones that interesting, so a solid fundamental base to work from seems like it would feel more natural. People do love their loot games, I guess.

    Originally Posted by Wool
    [...]Did you join a group and get ganked by them at the end? [...]
    I have gotten killed at extraction sites 3 times. All times it's been 2 people against my group of 3-4 people. With the gear available in the beta, it seems you can die real fast from concentrated fire. At least I got killed super fast (few seconds) with all blue level 8 gear.

    Yesterday I played some more hours, because I wanted to try it out with a dedicated group. I got together with 3 other people I knew from raiding back in the WoW days, and we did both the 2 PvE missions on hard, and we went into the Dark Zone together. We ended up replaying the PvE missions to make sure everyone was level 8, so we could enter the same instance of the Dark Zone. Replaying the PvE missions is really not much fun. They are short, with few or no interesting mechanics, so it sort of feels like a dull dungeon-run if it wasn't for the really nice scenery.
    Doing the Dark Zone with a dedicated group is more quite a bit more fun, than with a random group. Still I got the same sort of empty feeling after we ran around, collected blue loot that was neither interesting nor an upgrade, and extracted it. I would say that the extraction part was probably the most interesting thing, because it does create a tense situation when there are 10 guys circling the extraction zone, and everyone knows that only 4 people get to extract their loot.
    I did experience getting ganked at one extraction zone, and I also met several people "abusing" the proximity voice chat to be as offensive as they could. The ganking is probably only going to get a lot worse in retail, when people get more bored + creative.
