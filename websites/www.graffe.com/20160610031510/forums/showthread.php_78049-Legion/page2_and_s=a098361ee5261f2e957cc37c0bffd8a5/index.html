    Re: Legion

    I'll take that gentleman's bet. I think they're really throwing effort behind this and feel it's a key selling point. From what they've said so far, the quests are a big part of the expansion, the power progression is important (there will be NO WEAPONS dropping in Legion - everyone will be doing this instead), it forces players to do things they otherwise wouldn't do like completing all the outdoor quests (each complete quest gives progress on the weapon), it gives purpose to a second announced feature, the class hall. You can't scrap this without changing a ton of other things, and given the tight schedule, I'm not sure they have time to make those changes and still deliver the level of content they need to make people happy.

    Many dev interviews. MMO-Champ summary post: http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/...ent-Legion-Art

    Things that stick out to me:

    More details on Demon Hunters. No shock that they'll start close to 100. Surprise that they'll be leather tanks relying on avoidance mechanics. Haven't we been down this road before, and didn't it turn out poorly? The weapon thing seems like it'll be a problem, too, although not until the subsequent expansion.

    HUGE changes for hunters - Survival becoming a melee spec? Marks losing their pets for good? I know they want to make the specs distinct, but doing so by killing the big reasons to play a hunter for two thirds of the specs is not a great way to do it. I feel bad for every hunter that went around collecting different pets, only to find out now that if they want to continue to play ranged odds are they'll have to never use them again.

    The demonology changes sound weak, but it could work out. Past dev antipathy towards demonology has, in part, been based around how much passive damage the spec does, so moving more damage to the demon seems like a bad plan. But they could also introduce a lot of interactive demon stuff to make it... oh, who am I kidding. At best it'll be Beast Mastery with demons, and BM sucks to play.

    The changes to Disc will certainly "fix the absorb heavy nature of Disc priests", as nobody will be playing them. Who the hell has time to throw out a temporary buff that does nothing to heal a person, and then cast a nuke to actually heal? The "temporary buff" could be longer-lasting or something, but then what's the real point of having it? Healing, to be effective, has to be responsive, not planned well in advance, because damage is rarely (sometimes, like with AE, but in general rarely) something that can be anticipated well in advance. As described by the devs, this is a clunky system, much like the old Uplift system for Mistwalkers. But hey, I bet Disc will get better at doing quest content. That's something!

    The team isn't sure how to get players to keep going back to dungeons? Really? If they want to avoid being called the "B" team, they need to do better than this. There were SEVERAL YEARS where players were incentivized to keep going back to dungeons. Get over the whole "we don't want players steamrolling content for good rewards" thing and put the valor system back in place. Christ, talk about "the Vision" making bad game decisions.

    The team likes challenge modes. Good to hear someone does.

    No catch-up mechanic for artifacts on alts. Remember, the artifact will be the only weapon a player can have in the expansion, and the artifact will be progressed by things like doing every quest in the expansion. If you wanted proof that the current dev team just doesn't like or want alts, here it is. This is one of those things where I read it and say to myself, "Glad I won't be playing this expansion."

    Expanding the item bonus system sounds nice. I did enjoy randomly getting better stuff than normal, and never felt like I was being held back from being optimal by not getting an enhanced item. They need to look at sockets, though. The lack of sockets in gear really killed JC this expansion. Perhaps make it so if an item is enhanced, it gets (randomly determined special thing) + 1 socket, always?

    The order halls, currently, sound a little lame. As presented, they're quest hubs, located in the middle of nowhere for the most part (under Light's Hope Chapel? Really?), and... that's it. I'm sure as more details come out they'll become more compelling, but right now they just seem like a nuisance.

    The PvP changes are so big, and I'm so out of the PvP loop, I can't even begin to comment on them. One PvP thing I can comment on, though: Dalaran not a Sanctuary? Hey, make the class halls not be sanctuaries either! People on PvP servers should always feel like faction balance impacts their play, amirite?

    The team is happy with how flying worked out in Draenor. Good to hear someone liked that.

    The transmog changes sound great. On the few occasions I've used it, I've wondered why it didn't work exactly like how they're saying it will work.

    The team's strong belief that WoW must be played with others is a large part of why I'm leaving the game. I don't think they understand that increasing social bonds doesn't make the game sticky, binding players to the game. It makes the game clumpy - players are bound to one another, and leave in large groups. It also creates huge disincentives for disorganized players to continue playing. This is a thing that started under Ghostcrawler, and I really wish someone would sit down with the devs and explain to them that pushing organized gaming to the degree that they have (by removing or punishing disorganized content) really hurts the game as a whole.

    I like that they've heard the feedback on WoD professions. I don't like that there's no details that they can give about Legion professions. It almost feels like the dungeon thing - "We know there's a problem, but we have no idea how to fix it and maintain our current organizational biases!" Here's hoping that they don't dump every feature of WoD profs like they did with daily quests after MoP. There's a lot of good stuff in there that could, and should, be carried forward.
