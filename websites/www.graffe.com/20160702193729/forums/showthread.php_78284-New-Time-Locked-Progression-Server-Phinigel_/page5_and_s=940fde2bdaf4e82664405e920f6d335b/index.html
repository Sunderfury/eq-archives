    Re: New Time Locked Progression Server: Phinigel!

    Originally Posted by Wool
    It's a little unfair to hold EQ to a more modern standard of game design....
    Not when others say that the game is still top-notch. A certain sense of nostalgia, I get. I even get the "slow pace allows for chatting" social angle, although I didn't often have issues chatting in more button-mashy games like WoW. (I don't get the "forced grouping is great for social players" part, given that the people on Graffe's could easily form their own guild and go wherever they want in groups in any multiplayer game they liked.) But it's not a great game.

    Originally Posted by Wool
    You are talking about the difference in structured and cultured content versus emergent gameplay.
    EQ isn't a great sandbox (I'm not a fan of sandbox, obviously, but for sake of discussion will consider it as a positive). PvP gameplay in EQ was always considered weak by PvP players. From a PvE perspective, the environment is very difficult to change. You can knock out the big raid targets for a week, and keep other mobs dead for all of what, 30 minutes? There's been other games discussed here, including that EQ Next thing, that do a much better job at either PvP (which is very much about emergent gameplay) or sandbox PvE.

    Originally Posted by Wool
    As for the spoiler sites thing, WoW suffers from the same exact thing. There are people who want to experience things themselves, and people who want to be told what to do and finish it as quickly as possible.
    EQ punishes unguided exploration in two ways. One, there's not a lot of content out there, in the way I use the word (non-trash mobs, quest/lore stuff). Think of how many zones were giant "run through to get to the place that has content" spaces. I understand why those spaces couldn't be more populated - it would have made travel brutally long given the small difference between character and mob power - but it still makes exploration unrewarding. The second way is the spawning method for named mobs. You could go through an entire dungeon, never see a named mob, and have no idea where those mobs would spawn if they did spawn without consulting a spoiler site (some zones have those subtle spelling/name differences in the names of the PHs, some do not).

    Let me compare that with something WoW did in that last clusterfuck of an expansion they released. Every outdoor zone had multiple areas where, upon entering the area, a quest would pop up granting XP/gold if enough creatures in that area were killed. This is boring, and mildly rewarding at best. But, at least during beta, almost every one of these areas also had other things of interest - quests, ground spawns, silver elite mobs, followers that could be obtained for your garrison. So once you entered one of these areas, you were encouraged to poke around and look for the other good stuff you knew was there somewhere, and while you ran around the mobs you'd kill would progress you on the area "kill lots" quest. Now, by the end of beta they'd pulled goodies from a couple of areas and added a few more areas that were bereft of content, thus destroying the whole "rewards exploration" point of the areas and turning them into EQ-lite grindfests. But the original idea - find a place, get a quest, and know that there's something more there if you just look around - was a great way to reward exploration. From what I read here, GW2 is also a game that really knows how to reward exploration. There's plenty of games that do that well.

    Originally Posted by Wool
    A very large portion of WoW players don't seem to even read the quests text.
    Honestly, they don't even need to do it for most modern quests. They updated the map to show shaded areas where objectives are located for any quest you're on, with numbers linked to a sidebar telling you which quest has objectives there, what they are, and how many you have completed. I get why they did it - people were using quest add-ons that did all this anyways, and making quests easier to complete actually reduced the load on GMs - but it really contributes to the whole "theme park vs. world" thing.

    Re: New Time Locked Progression Server: Phinigel!

    I think EQ did a lot of damage by having loot drop the way it did and never changing. It's only recently that games have started to just have raids drop loot for everyone.

    I think if MMO Dev's branched out to other game systems more, they'd realize there are some great methods of loot distribution. I'd love to see a system that worked like this:

    You kill Gloxicor.

    Everyone that participated gets a loot option: Random, Receive Token, Spend Tokens, Lottery Chance.
    Random is pretty obvious: You get something from the loot table. This requires that some of the things on the loot table are nonsense, but once you get the item (from Random or Tokens), it should be removed from your possible chances on that boss. So if they drop 10 things, killing him 10 times means you get 100% of his loot. This is where the nonsense items would come in. Example, a 10x2% chance to drop 1000 Gold. Once you receive all items, or if you're on lock-out for that boss already "Random" becomes greyed out and is replaced with "[Gold/XP/Whatever The Useless Drop Is] Reward."
    Receive Token gives you a Token, but not the normal kind of Token you can exchange for class gear or whatever. See the next option.
    Spend Token allows you to use Tokens earned from killing Gloxicor to buy something he (and only he) can drop. The items should cost something related to the value and drop chance, +1 (e.g. 50% drop chance = 3 Tokens). You can buy items you've already received from Random. The "booby" prizes would just be 1 Token.
    Lottery Chance gives you a rolling bonus for the Raid. This would let you get nothing from a boss, but on a later boss in the raid when you click "Random" you can either loot the item, or click "Random" again. Greyed out once you have all loot in the zone.

    The idea would be if you only want 1 specific item, or absolutely need a specific item, you should use Tokens. If there are multiple items you want, you should use Random. If you have everything you want, you either take the pity prize, or Lottery Chance.


    I also think more raids need to have optional bosses that are much harder and have much better (or interesting) loot than the rest of the raid. If you can get better loot chances by clearing the rest of the raid, it makes it easier to gear up peoples alts or newbies. I remember AoW being the only reason people killed some of the weaker bosses, and I never understood why games have completely dropped that aspect.
