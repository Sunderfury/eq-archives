    Re: NFL Playoffs

    I could make picks for the NFC but frankly they are all a shot in the dark. I went 2/4 in the wildcard round only because the Vikings and Colts were wishful thinking on my part (If I were doing it for real stakes I'd have picked the Packers and Ravens). But this time I have no idea. I'd certainly pick Denver and New England in the AFC. I'd give Houston a better shot an upset than Baltimore but I think they'll both lose. So that's the AFC.

    The NFC though... The strongest, most physically intimidating teams are the 49ers and Seahawks. They both hammer defenses with their run game, and both have agile, capable quarterbacks. Both have physical defenses with good secondaries. But both QBs are essentially rookies. Wilson is 24 and Kaepernick is 25 and not "technically" a rookie, but neither of them have more than 1 season of experience.

    The Packers have had defensive and line issues. But I do think Rodgers is the best QB in the league; in his prime at the top of his game with his full array of receivers, and they've looked good on defense and running the ball in the last couple of games. So I think they've got a good shot at beating the Niners, especially if Justin Smith is injured.

    The Falcons have fantastic wide receivers and Matt Ryan is the best pure QB in the playoffs not named Rodgers, Manning, or Brady. Falcons also have the next best defense after the 49ers and Seahawks. They are rested and playing at home. I think this gives them the advantage they need to stop Seattle's rampage.

The 10.00001th time your high school QB actually makes the play.

By playoff backup standards he sucked. But there are dozens of backup quarterbacks in the league. A bunch of them take snaps every year and most of the ones who fail badly are never heard from again. Nick Foles shit the bed at first, and while he's not destined for the hall of fame he certainly has shown some potential.
