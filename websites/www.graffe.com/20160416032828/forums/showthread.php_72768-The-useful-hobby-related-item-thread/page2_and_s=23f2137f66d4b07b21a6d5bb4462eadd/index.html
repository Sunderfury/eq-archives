    Re: The useful [hobby related item] thread.

    Originally Posted by Mihoko Ishii
    I've found my leatherman wave very clutch while fishing for changing baits, cutting lines, etc. It also can open bottles which is sometimes useful. You can handle pretty much most tasks with just the pliers due to how that tool is designed. And you can use it for wire stripping etc. It can replace a significant amount of screwdriver bits too. You are still going to need another knife for skinning/gutting/filleting purposes though.

    Okay. Consider me swayed and converted.

    While turning an apartment last month, I came across a Snap-On version of the Leatherman. It was left behind, so I put it in a box of other similar things, and it sat in the shop for a couple weeks.

    Before going on vacation this week, I decided to clean out the shop, and this was one of the things I took home with me.

    As we were doing camping this weekend, and I thought about how often we need different tools to complete some tasks that would otherwise be more difficult done only with tooth and nail, i decided to grab it from the kitchen cabinet, and stick it in my side cargo pocket.

    The very first night, we were experiencing awful winds, and I was having to cook over charcoal. It just would not heat the chicken we had going on the barbie, despite there being an entire bag. So I took out the "13-in-1," opened it up, and started cutting some aluminum trays we had and fashioning them as wind breakers. They worked wonderfully.

    Later on, we had a similar situation in the morning, and instead, I took out the saw knife and cut out wire bracing from the same aluminum trays and fashioned them around the same trays to reinforce them aroudn the camp stove so that I coudl cook breakfast.

    All in all, I used it here and there every once in a while to cut or stab pieces of plastic and what not, and it was really quite handy.

    I'll never put one in my tool bag at work (i'd just damage and dull it over time, and never get around to sharpening it), but i'll definitely keep it in the kitchen-tool cubby, so that it is always at the ready for when we need a quick tool.

    Re: The useful [hobby related item] thread.

    I noticed home depot is drastically reducing many of the Li-Ion battery packs that they keep in stock for their Ridgid and Ryobi house brands.

    About a year ago, I'd picked up a Dual 4000mAh 18v battery pack for my Ryobis for $100. They normally sell by themselves for $100. i was going to split the cost with somebody, but I couldn't find any takers and returned it.

    For a year, they didn't sell. They stayed on the aisle caps, and didn't sell. Then about a week ago, I saw them marked down to $84. I decided, what the hell, grabbed it, and when i purchased it, they'd dropped down to $75. THIS IS LESS EXPENSIVE THAN REBUILDING THEM WITH YOUR OWN LI-ION. If you were to buy hi-drain 18650 batteries, they'd cost about $10 each ( a good quality samsung or sony ) 5 of them are necessary for a 2amp battery pack. These are the high capacity 3400mAh batteries which typically cost 15 to 20 dollars per cell.

    The value in these suckers is ridiculous.

    In addition to the ryobi battery packs, the 12v RIDGID are down fro $70 a piece, to $35 for a 2 pack.

    I believe these use the 18350 half-size cells. I'm not absolutely certain. I might take one of them apart to see what is in there (or see if I can find it on youtube).

    All in all, it is something to be excited over, especially if you collect this stuff yourself.

    My only upset, and it is a slightly minor one, is that I noticed that the lifetime warranty tag that used to be on all the RIDGID tools is no longer listed. It looks like they've gone to 3 year warranties again, which is okay with me. I've been highly considering getting a set of Milwaukees, lately.

    We'll see what happens.
