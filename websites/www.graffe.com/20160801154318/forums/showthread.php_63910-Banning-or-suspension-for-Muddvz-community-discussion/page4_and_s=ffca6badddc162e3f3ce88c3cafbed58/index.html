    Re: Banning or suspension for Muddvz -- community discussion

    No, it’s not a Legal Board, but legal principles of requiting Intent for culpability developed precisely because it’s generally not just to seriously punish people for being dumb asses. We may just be an argue board, but the desire for “fairness” is universal. If we’re treating muddvz actions so gravely then we should treat how we review his actions with equal gravity. Either we’re an argueboard and what he did was no big deal, or we really don’t think we’re just an argueboard and actually do care.

    We have two potential punishments – Banning and Suspension. How should we decide between which of the two are warranted? IMO, the principle of basing culpability upon a person’s mental state when committing an act is the most just and has the advantage of being well developed, easily understood and is the most widely used standard.

    I’m not certain what risk he put us in, but assuming that he did put us at risk, then we should not use a standard that ignores if he meant to put us at risk. What happens if someone else puts us at risk without realizing it, do we apply the same Strict Liability to them that we applied to muddvz, or do we act arbitrarily in the future? What about people who went to Graffe in the past and put us at risk for him pulling the plug – should we retroactively ban them?

    The punishment should fit the crime and the severity of the crime depends upon mental state. In every fair and equitable system of Justice, the severest punishments require Intent. We’re an argueboard, but the community still wants fairness. On a Message Board, the severest punishment is Banning. Since Banning is the most severe form of punishment then we should require the Intent.

    Suspension is still a punishment. A Suspension would reprimand him for going to mosso, and deter other people from doing so in the future. If the case is made that going to mosso did in fact endanger the community, then we’d all be on notice and it would be much more likely in the future that someone going to mosso did so in order to damage the community.
