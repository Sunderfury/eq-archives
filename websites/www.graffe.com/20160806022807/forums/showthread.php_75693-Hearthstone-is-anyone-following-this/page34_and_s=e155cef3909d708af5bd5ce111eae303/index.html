    The "pick one" thing seems a little slanted towards murloc and spell. I've heard that this brawl is a dry run for "aura" effects, for the next expansion. I'm surprised they haven't done those earlier than this.

    Recently, I started disenchanting gold copies of spells that I get, if those cards don't make a minion somehow. I enjoy looking at the animated cards when they're in play, but otherwise don't care about gold versus non-gold, so dusting those when I get non-gold duplicates is a way to fill in some of the many holes in my collection. As of right now I have 65 gold commons, 47 gold rares, 9 gold epics, 3 gold legendaries (Cho, Nesingwary, Geddon), and the gold Gelbin from the beta. Something to consider when you see streamers with all gold decks - it takes a ton of dust to be able to do that.

    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    Inc. major changes to Hearthstone:

    http://www.hearthpwn.com/news/1180-a...deck-slots-new

    Summary: splitting play into two sections: Wild, where you can use any card ever released, and Standard, which only allows the use of current sets. There will be separate Ranked play ladders for both, and the end-of-season reward will use your best of the two scores, although anything tournament-related will only use your Standard score. ALL SETS/ADVENTURES NO LONGER IN STANDARD WILL NO LONGER BE AVAILABLE FOR PURCHASE (Naxxramas and Goblins vs. Gnomes to start), although you will be able to craft cards from them (including currently-uncraftable adventure cards). They will be reviewing the cards in Standard just before it starts and making nerfs/buffs. If you have all 9 heroes unlocked, you'll get 9 more deck slots.

    I'm not a fan of the "can't buy this" thing, especially being unable to buy adventures. I get that they don't want new players to buy cards for the barely-supported "Wild" format, but I don't think that walling players out of the format completely (and having to craft every Naxxramas card does that) is the way to go. I'm happy with the new deck slots, and I'm cautiously optimistic that there will be a good number of buffs and nerfs with the Standard review. As an older player I don't like the "new stuff only" format, even though that's what Magic did and it was inevitable that they'd do something like that to make it easier on new players. I just checked all nine of my decks, and not a single one would qualify for Standard - ugh.

    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    Announcement made for the expansion: http://www.hearthpwn.com/news/1215-w...on-reveal-live

    Summary: 134 cards, theme is the Old Gods. Logging in during the promotion period will give you three packs and one of the legendaries, C'Thun, and if you pre-order 50 packs (heh) you can get a special card back (whoop-de-doo). Launch date late April, early May. There's four Old Gods in the set, and the big mechanic seems to be cards that boost the Old Gods. Example: C'Thun is a 6/6 for 10 that does damage to enemies, randomly split, equal to its power as a battlecry. There's also many cards that boost the stats of C'Thun, no matter where it is (play, hand, deck), like a 3/4 for 3 that gives C'Thun +1/+1 at the end of every turn, or a 2/3 for 2 that gives C'Thun +2/+2 as a battlecry. The other cards that have been revealed seem to follow the existing "huge creatures for the mana cost which have a small drawback" power creep theme we've seen previously.

    The basic set nerfs will happen one week prior to the expansion, looks to be about a dozen cards, Big Game Hunter is one of the cards that was singled out as being on the list. Given the disclosed mechanics for C'Thun, this isn't a surprise. "Face an eldritch horror from the core of the world, older than time itself!" "I've got the beast in my sights... *BLAM*"
