Quests and things are bugged, and the only way I've been getting credit on some things is the very loose credit system on kills.

    I keep finding lore inconsistencies and slaps in the face. People acting and saying things they shouldn't.

    The main story is neat. It was nice running back into Rana and her sarge back in Kragenmoor. Some things are working for the characters and storylines, but many things aren't because everyone is set up to be like an MMO would do it.

    The constant texture popping is an OFFENSE to me and my computer. I don't even get that with my huge textures in Skyrim. That needs to be fixed.

    I still can't think of a reason to interact with anyone. I get credit all the same for leapfrogging and helping people blast stuff at random. Group content seems to be said to be the hallway dungeons of WoW. I couldn't care about anyone in this game, and I don't see any need to talk with people because I'm always trying to be busy and productive. The multiplayer part of this MMO is just as pointless as it is in Most other MMOs these days. Where's my housing? Collections? Downtime to talk with Sexy Dark elf booty?

    The lore slaps me in the face, the UI doesn't tell me wtf is going on, the controls are lagged to bugged half the time, the world isn't even finished, crafting is stupid wow crafting, the quests are bugged, the load times and texture popping are disgusting...

    but man, dat phasing!

    The rest is standard MMO garbage. The game is held up by the fact it's Elder Scrolls, but a MMO perversion of TES.

    Re: Who Here is Gonna Play Elder Scrolls Online?

    Originally Posted by DmitrytheWizzy
    I don't think it is done. I think this is going to be one of those games that we read about the mess internally afterward. We will end up reading about money problems and the personality conflicts under the pressure. There will be lies and misrepresentations. The finger pointing will be glorious. Think about Vanguard and Warhammer and Age of Conan and SWTOR. If this game is not finished, think about all the times release has happened with incomplete games and what came next. Fun stuff.
    While what you just described can be "fun" in a "Look kids! It's a train wreck! Take pictures!" sort of way, the fact is that it's inexcusable today. Developers, publishers and investors have a solid history of games to look back on which were all failures simply due to them being released way too early (in fact, you named a good chunk of them). If I was a developer and being pushed by my publisher to release too soon, I'd turn around and just start reciting off all the names of the games that failed for releasing too early. There comes a time where you need to poop or get off the pot, but some games weren't at that point yet. Star Wars was a really good example. The game needed a solid 3 months of development time prior to being released at a minimum to fix stuff. And keep in mind, the time it takes to fix and correct stuff goes way up post-launch because there are a lot more issues that are competing for priority that don't exist before launch (such as server stability and similar issues).

    I don't think I'd ever have been persuaded to buy ESO because the universe doesn't appeal to me as a MMO format. However, I still wanted to see the game succeed and choke off some of WOW's success. From all the negativity I'm hearing about the game though, that doesn't seem likely so I guess the best case is to enjoy the show that Dimitry is looking forward to.
    There are other worlds than these.
