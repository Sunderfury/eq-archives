    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    Originally Posted by Dawlin
    He's been on board for a long long time, though, so I consider him a game designer now. I don't agree with a lot of his choices, but he does have his credentials in place.
    I think it brings you to the game in different ways, though. If you're a game designer making a new game, you make it like a game - something you know people will want to play. The biases you bring with you are "how can we make this more game-y and more enjoyable", and you don't limit yourself to specific subsets of your player base. If you come in with no game design experience, but tons of experience as a player, you make the game you always wanted to see as a player.

    Think about Furor's first big area where he handled all the quests - TBC Nagrand. It was a total grindfest, but as an EQ player, that's what he knew and what he made - lots of killing, lots of XP/upgrades, an efficient way to level up. It's what he would have wanted as a player, but for people who wanted different things (storyline, variety), it was sorely lacking.

    Now think about the "no flying" decision from the position of a mythic raider. What matters most to you, flying around the game world or getting content faster? You don't need to fly around Draenor, for the most part. There's nothing out there you need, what with mats being farmed in the garrison, and the first few people at the raid target can summon the rest. But content? ALWAYS need more content. So which way will they bias when it comes time to make a decision?
