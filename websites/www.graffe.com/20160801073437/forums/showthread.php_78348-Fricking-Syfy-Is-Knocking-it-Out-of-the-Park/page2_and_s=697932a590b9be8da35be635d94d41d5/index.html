    Re: Fricking Syfy Is Knocking it Out of the Park

    For people who don't know about The Expanse: it's based off a series of novels. The books, in order:

    Leviathan Wakes (2011)
    Caliban's War (2012)
    Abaddon's Gate (2013)
    Cibola Burn (2014)
    Nemesis Games (2015)
    Babylon's Ashes (2016, likely in June - every other novel has come out in June)
    Three more novels are planned after these, for 2017, 2018, and 2019.

    Author is James S.A. Corey, which is a pen name for two guys, one of whom has worked as a writing assistant to George R.R. Martin. The show, so far, has been staying VERY close to the first book. Presumably, this will continue, so if you look up any info on the books consider it a spoiler for the TV show. I looked up a rough outline of the plots from the existing books, and based on that and the special effects from the last show, I plan to keep the show as a permanent part of what I watch. Now my Awesome TV Tuesday has become amazing, since we see The Expanse on Tuesdays.

    Re: Fricking Syfy Is Knocking it Out of the Park

    Originally Posted by Daxil Solshok
    Also, I cannot stand Magician's. I made it through about one commercial break before I realized what it was .. an attempt to recapture an aging Harry Potter audience which is now in their late teens/early 20s. Teen angst, no!
    It isn't a good show, although the first couple episodes are the worst. The "Harry Potter" likeness is kind of the problem a lot of people have with the book. I feel like it needed some sort of prologue so you knew from the start what kind of book you were getting into. You don't know what you're reading, so when the book starts you get "Harry Potter" and try to frame the rest of the book as Adult Harry Potter and then when it doesn't conform, you're guaranteed to have problems with the story. By the end of the first book, the book becomes "Adult Narnia." Then after that, it's just a mishmash of things going on, because comparing it to other book series is meaningless. I think if they'd started off with a Prologue about the Beast and Ember/Umber before having Quentin discuss how awesome and amazing Fillory is, people would have a better idea what was happening in the books.

    The real problem is that they're trying to capture the books too closely, but their writers aren't good enough to portray something so difficult. Here is a brief description of the book cycle:

    The book trilogy is basically this:
    Book 1) Depressed, self-centered narcissist gets his 'friends' maimed or killed
    Book 2) Above character realizes he's kind of a piece of shit, and is analyzing his life. He's still kind of adrift, but he starts paying attention to those around him and respecting their abilities
    Book 3) Above character decides to try to help other people, not for himself, but for others, tries to fix some of his fuck ups.

    Basically the book series is a fantasy version of older Sci-Fi. The Fantasy setting and familiar tropes are just scenery for a book discussing the personal growth of the main character. The thing is, taking that as such, there is little payoff until the third book. The First book ends with a bit of a pyrrhic victory, and you find out there has been significant manipulation going on.

    The fantasy elements The show can't possibly show how self-centered he is, so they've just tried to replace it with angst. The show moves too fast, and in order to cover up their inability to write the character well they insert a lot of unnecessary scenes.
    The A.V. Club said this about the first book: "a sad dream of what it means to want something badly and never fully reach it." The Show is trying so hard to keep the emotional duress aspect that they've completely forgotten that. They're trying so hard that they completely wrote out the comedic relief character and replaced him with an angsty love interest for Penny.
