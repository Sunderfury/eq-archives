    That's going to be awesome. One thing I wish they had: the ability to keep a long queue (like LFR) inside of a scenario. I can't see it being much of a problem if someone leaves a scenario to go to LFR - you'll get a replacement in seconds.

    The other thing I wish the game had, but it never will: the ability to specify if you want a fresh instance or not. I know the devs are concerned that they won't be able to get replacement players into partially completed LFRs, but my experiences say otherwise:

    (1) When people drop after one boss and replacements come in, very frequently those replacements ask, "What boss?" If they don't get the answer they want, they drop. I've done it myself - join ToT4 to get tier pieces, see that the group is heading to Lei Shen, drop and go to another alt. I've been very fussy now that I'm collecting Secrets - if it's not a full run, it's useless to me, and I'm not getting back in queue and wasting an hour or so of my time just to get one boss and 45 valor.

    (2) I have numerous alts that don't care about the early bosses at all. As I said above, I do LFR for two types of items: tier set pieces and weapons. The set pieces in ToT1-3 all drop off the final boss, so for those raids I'd very cheerfully select "don't need fresh instance" and be delighted if I got a raid in progress on my alts. I'm sure I'm not the only player that thinks this way.

    I think the devs would find that there wouldn't be a lot of raids waiting on players for long if they allowed this kind of selection. But I guess we'll never know.

    I did it with the help of the Salyin Battle Banner and this advice from WoWhead:
    Originally Posted by Wowhead comment
    For anyone with a way to drop combat (Vanish, Feign Death, etc.) who wants to solo this, you have to be quick on dropping combat after using the spear. I recommend making a macro. I'm a Night Elf, so my macro looks like this:

    /use Lightning Lance
    /use Shadowmeld

    With that prepared, here's what you do:

    1. Get in range of Nalak of some ability that will aggro him.
    2. Use that ability and immediately start strafing away (but don't face away)
    3. Use the macro you made. You'll drop combat with Nalak, and then the add will spawn. (If you don't use a macro, you just have to be very quick between using the spear and dropping combat, or the add will despawn as well).
    4. Kite the add around Nalak's courtyard until it dies on its own. Don't dps it yourself.
    I just put /use Salyin Battle Banner in the macro instead of /use Shadowmeld. It worked like a charm, didn't need any help.

    Interesting addition for the organized (flex, normal, heroic) versions of the Garrosh raid: he drops heirlooms that have the same ilvl as his loot table, which currently scale to level 100.

    http://media.mmo-champion.com/images...irloomBoAs.jpg

    Remember when Ghostcrawler, on multiple occasions, told people who wanted catch-up mechanics that keyed on a player's main that the point of playing an alt is to play the alt, not the main as a proxy? I guess he forgot about that a little bit. I like the basic idea, to give players a reason to grind Garrosh all the way to the next expansion, but giving organized raiders a significant leg-up on leveling (versus a one-time boost with better gear) in the next expansion seems a bit too much. These are the same people who are so concerned about the economy of sha crystals that they've made it so stuff that used to be valor gear, which will be justice gear post-5.4, will only disenchant into sha crystal shards, which can be combined to form a sha crystal. Priorities, you know.

    They've also released the new "special tradeskill stuff". It's great for most people, but a bit of a bummer for me. The mats requirements (all of these recipes have a version that can be done more than once per day, which requires the new "disenchanted from raid gear" item like a Blood Spirit; these are the once per day ones):

    Balanced Trillium Bar: one trillium bar.
    Celestial Cloth: 10 Bolts of Windwool Cloth.
    Hardened Magnificent Hide: 2 Magnificent Hides.

    You learn a recipe for either the new ilvl 476 PvP gear or the new PvE gear when you do the daily combine, same as with the patch 5.2 recipes. The PvE gear is belt and legs; belt takes 21 items to make, legs takes 28. The PvE gear is ilvl 553, which is the gear level for Normal raids. But the PvE gear is also BoP. My rogue tailor is sad. On the plus side, glad I kept all the magnificent hides I created getting three leatherworkers all the discovery recipes! (For AH players: invest in windwool cloth. Seriously. A normal tailor making bags and special cloth is going to go through 18 to 23 bolts per day, which is 90 to 115 cloth (23 is for days when they have a Spirit to use).)
