    Quiet ensued for ~48 hours. Then Dmitry returned. Over the span of about three hours, he began posting thread after thread. (Indeed, I'm not aware that anything had been moderated at all before he began posting the names of reporters.) Those revelationary posts have been purged, but Voca's pictures, above, show more-or-less what happened. Dmitry was able, by dint of his moderator position, to see the private reports coming into the moderator forum and chose to make their existence public to all users.

    Apologies to all if I've missed anything - I'll happily edit as I go to make sure the record is stated clearly and accurately, but I'm piecing this back together from recollection and in a rush. Feel free to submit updates or corrections to me via PM and I'll add them here.

    Spoiler for Opinion Thus Far:
    Regardless of whether Dmitry's titles were originally intended to push an issue of policy disagreement into the light or not (and regardless of whether they represent objectionable content to our homosexual posters) the revealing of reports and the names of reporters in public is clearly a violation of moderator responsibility. Discussion about making reports public has occurred in the past, but has always been rejected to date, and the expectation is that reports are made and handled privately to enure the absence of retaliation and recriminations about "snitching" over rule violations. Further, this marks at least the second occasion Dmitry has intentionally used access to the moderator forums inappropriately, to my knowledge.

    While I had initially been inclined to agree that Dmitry was being objectionable for the purpose of advancing the discussion of the original moderation action (cf. Rana's thread and titles of his new threads), his actions subsequent (revealing reports) are unacceptable. That his response to all of this is another screed leaves me unconvinced of the benign nature of his motives, and makes a temporary solution suspect.
