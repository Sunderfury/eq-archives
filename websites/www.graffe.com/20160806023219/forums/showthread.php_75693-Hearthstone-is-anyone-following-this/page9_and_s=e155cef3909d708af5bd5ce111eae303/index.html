    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    Originally Posted by Silke
    I also played another Arena last night and 2 of my opponents were dropping Legendaries on me like crazy.
    This is very rare, unless you were at a fairly high number of wins for the run. Legendaries are not common cards at all in Arena - I've been offered the chance to pick one less than a handful of times, and I'm somewhere north of 240 Arena wins right now. Now you can see them more frequently as you get higher in wins for the run, since you're being matched against other people with similar win records and having a legendary usually improves your odds of winning. But be aware that seeing multiple opponents dropping multiple legendaries is very, very, very rare.

    Originally Posted by Silke
    Last night I lost about 10 games straight (and if I never see a Hunter, Buzzard, Buzzard, Wolf, Wolf, Wolf, Boar, Boar, Unleash the Hounds combo again it will be too soon) simply because my deck options are extremely limited.
    That specific deck will likely get punched in the teeth by the next round of card adjustments. There's been too many complaints about it for it to stay the way it is. Note that I do agree with your basic premise about the game being easier if you have the cards, and that obtaining cards (even common cards) is currently too slow. However, you can be competitive with a limited card set.

    This is my hunter deck. I play it when I want to rack up a number of quick wins. It has two rares, and the rest of the cards are common or basic. It doesn't rely on goofy combos, although there are combos available to it. One should play it for a bit, get used to how it should be played and what the limitations are, and one can do very well with it.

    Arcane Shot x 2
    Acidic Swamp Ooze x 2
    Bloodfen Raptor
    Dire Wolf Alpha x 2
    River Crocolisk x 2
    Scavenging Hyena x 2
    Starving Buzzard x 2
    Animal Companion x 2
    Deadly Shot x 2
    Kill Command x 2
    Ironfur Grizzly x 2
    Houndmaster x 2
    Stranglethorn Tiger
    Tundra Rhino x 2
    Savannah Highmane x 2 (the rares)
    Core Hound x 2

    Seven commons, two rares, 21 basic set cards. And it does well. Again, I agree with you on "wealthy players will do better". But I disagree with "spendthrift players will get chain-stomped", and offer that deck as my proof.

Like I said, priest matchups are tough. One thing to be aware of with priests: they usually can't deal with weapons, other than by healing. If you want a more aggressive deck, pull the Shield Blocks for something else.

    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    The more you play, the more you learn the cards, just like MtG. Give it time. If you want to spruce up your mage deck a little bit, save up the dust to make a Gadgetzan Auctioneer (five casting cost neutral card). If that hits the table and the opponent doesn't immediately remove it, you'll get a lot of advantage from it, and removing a 4/4 isn't trivial when your deck isn't letting him build threats.

    Originally Posted by Silke
    And I'm in love with Mirror Entity. First time I used the card was against a Druid who played an 8 / 8 Taunt Ironbark Protector. I really wish I could have seen the look on his face when Mirror Entity went off and copied it.
    There's a mage card, Kirin Tor Mage, a 4/3 for 3 that allows you to cast one Secret for free the turn the KTM comes into play. It's an excellent critter if you're already playing with secrets (4 attack = priests have trouble removing it, six mana worth of cards played for 3 cost), and a star in the mage aggro deck along with Mana Wyrm and Ethereal Arcanist.
