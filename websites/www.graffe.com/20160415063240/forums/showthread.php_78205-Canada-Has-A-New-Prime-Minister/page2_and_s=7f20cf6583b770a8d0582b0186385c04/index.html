    Re: Canada Has A New Prime Minister.

    Canada has a new cabinet. American progressives that had to put up with Donald Trump's "whoops, another intern" racist Tweets today, read this and weep.

    -30 people, 15 of them women. When asked why 15 women, Trudeau said simply, "It's 2015."
    -Two disabled persons - a quadriplegic in charge of Veterans' Affairs, and a former Paralympian in charge of Sports and Persons With Disabilities.
    -Seven minorities, including a native as the country's Justice Minister/Attorney General, and this badass as our Defense Minister (photo is from the linked article on Mr. Sajjan):

    harjit-sajjan.jpg

    http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/britis...ster-1.3304931

    -We now have ministries for both pure science (Science) and applied science (Innovation, Science, and Economic Development).
    -There's been some very pointed name changes to some of the ministries, such as Environment becoming Environment and Climate Change; Aboriginal Affairs and Northern Development becoming Indigenous and Northern Affairs; Citizenship and Immigration becoming Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship.

    http://www.cbc.ca/news/politics/full...inet-1.3300699

    And in a move that surprised me somewhat, they're getting to work immediately. Parliament's next session starts in early December, rather than waiting until after Christmas. There's plenty of rookies in the new cabinet, so I'm sure there will be gaffes aplenty; the many promises of the Liberals will be difficult to implement (such as taking in 25,000 Syrian refugees before the end of the year); and there will be issues where I'll be disappointed at the Liberal response (I suspect ratification of the TPP will be the first). But I have to say, I haven't been this excited about Canadian politics in ages.
