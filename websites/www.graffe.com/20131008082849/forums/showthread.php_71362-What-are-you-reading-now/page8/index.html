    Re: What are you reading now?

    Originally Posted by DmitrytheWizzy
    Post when you finish. I liked Under the Dome a ton and loved Duma Key as well. King seems to be exploring some big ideas lately and handling them pretty well while also maintaining good stories and great characters. Not sure I buy the premise of 11/22/63 just reading about it at Amazon so I need to hold off a bit.
    I loved Under the Dome. It reminded me a lot of King's older stuff (like It and the Stand). I haven't read Duma Key or Cell yet but I'm liking the current Stephen King writing style since it's a lot similar to the old Stephen King's writing style (just a bit more mature).

    I'm not as far into 11/22/63 as I'd like to be due to all the holiday stuff but so far it's VERY good. Basic premise is about a guy who goes back in time to stop the Kennedy assassination. So it's a time travel book but King does a very good job at handling the complexities. He set up a lot of his own Time Travel Rules and since the book is in the first person he can resort to answering some complex "what if?" style questions with his character having absolutely no idea what consequences and paradoxes might result. Reading "It" beforehand is a good idea since a chunk of the book takes place in Derry a year after the Loser's club defeated It back in the 50's.

    It's very suspenseful and I want to read more (I just haven't had the chance). I'm about a third of the way in and we'll see if the rest of the book holds up but so far it's just as good as Under the Dome.
    There are other worlds than these.
