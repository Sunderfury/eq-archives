    Spoilers for Merkus

    Spoiler for Egwene:
    Simply put, she kicks alot of ass, while her Warder (Gawaine) gets himself killed by Damodred. After kicking much ass, she finds a way to counter the Balefire weave and uses said weave to crystalize Taim + a bunch of other enemy channelers, at the sacrifice of herself.



    Spoiler for Rand:
    Rand battles Moridin in the Dark Ones' Cave and goes thru a series of "out of this world" events with the Dark One. Ends up in a body swap with Moridin, with Rands' old body dying. Rand ends burnt out, however, in the epilogue it shows him "willing" something to happen.


    Spoiler for Deaths:
    Notable deaths: Egwene, Suane Sanche, The Bashere's, Rhuarc, Many Many Aes Sedai, Many Many Ashamen

    Re: Final Wheel of Time Book out today

    Originally Posted by Neofelis
    Ok, now that it's finished I guess I'll go back and re-read from the beginning.
    My last read through of the series ended over a year ago (my estimate for A Memory of Light's release was off by a year). I didn't have any trouble with this one at all. The book moves VERY fast compared to some of the others and the main focus is the Last Battle. As such a lot of the plot lines aren't as relevant in AMOL so even though there is a large cast of characters, it's not too hard keeping track of them.

    As for the ending, I've had a few days to stew on it and I still like it. It's a good ending. No it doesn't answer everything but it covers the most important things. The stuff that isn't answered was meant for additional books.

    Spoiler for Sequels:
    The following books were going to follow Mat and Tuon's children as they struggle to rebuild the Seanchan empire and deal with the rest of the world. Brandon Sanderson posted that Robert Jordan's notes regarding this follow up series was a 2 sentence summary and has indicated that the series will not be produced.


    With that said, I'm still satisfied not knowing how everything turned out in the future. The series ended where it needed to and anything else would not have been as climactic. The series was, ultimately, about Rand fighting the Dark One in the Last Battle and Jordan created an entire world with a lot of rich detail and history. I'd love to have seen additional books come out to follow up on his world but I can still sleep knowing how the Last Battle ended.

    One last thought on the ending:

    Spoiler for Deaths:
    The one death that bothered me more than any other was Bela's. I'll admit that she died nobly and with honor but she was a horse for crying out loud and I really thought that if anyone earned the right to survive the Last Battle it was her.
