    Anyway im getting off topic.


    They topic we should be focused on is this:


    ALL damage-based classes are comprised of two generalized methods-

    1.) Damage Generater.

    This is the actual damage production, your personal dps.


    and

    2.) Damage Enabler.

    This is the damage augmentation via buffs and debuffs that strongly influences the generated damage.





    When classes are truely balanced, with the case of 4 classes, you should find the following to be true:

    You have one class priamrily devoted to Damage Generation with highly limited damage enabling.

    2 classes that are some where in the middle, generally being heavy on Damage Generation, with one focusing on "enhancing" and the other on "enfeebling."

    Finally you have one class that is strongly focused on Damage Enabling and mediocre at Damage Generating.




    By combining all four methods, you achieve the HIGHEST possible net gain in terms of dps.




    That means that if the Rogue is ment to be the "top personal dps," then they should be limited in the extent of how they can improve other classes.

    Rangers should be close to a rogues "fast" dps, and should be able to spike higher than monks, and aid in damage Enabling by buffing their allies, which they do very well actually.

    Monks should be close to a rogue's sustain dps, and should be able to keep a high, constant dps that superceeds the ranger. They should focus on Damage Enabling by weakening their foes, allowing every to strike them harder (initially the weakness system was to be their method)

    Bards should be noticably below the other 3 fighters in spike and sustained dps, which i believe everyone can agree they are, and they should both enhance allies and weaken foes well enough to strongly influence the damage of their allies.



    I personally think bards make a bit too much of a difference, to the point that "not" having a bard is just plain stupidity. They should make a noticable difference, but their absence should not be the downfall of a raid; we are not to have any "required" class in any point in the game.
