Jewelcrafting is a profession introduced with The Burning Crusade, the first expansion for World of Warcraft. Skilled jewelers can make rings, necklaces, and trinkets, as well as cut gems to augment certain gear obtained in the Outlands.

[edit] Socketing

Certain gear in the Outlands have gem sockets on them, typically one, two, or three sockets. Those sockets can be filled with cut gems that add to the stats on the gear. Many pieces also receive an additional bonus when all the sockets are filled. If that piece has such a bonus, it will be listed on the piece.

Sockets come in four varieties: red, yellow, blue, and meta. These sockets determine which gems can fit in them. Gems come in at least seven varieties: blue, green, orange, purple, red, yellow, and meta. Gems can be found by mining, in drops or chests, or by prospecting Outland ore. Certain gems are created using the Alchemy tradeskill.

There is no skill required to socket a gem - once you have a cut gem you just place it in the socket, and it attaches itself. Once you attach a gem, you cannot remove it. You can replace it with a different gem, but when you do so the first gem is destroyed.

Any uncommon or rare gem can fit in any yellow/red/blue socket. The color requirements listed are ONLY to receive the additional socket bonus.

Gem colors match the sockets according to the color tables you learned in elementary school: orange = red + yellow, so an orange gem can go in a red or yellow socket. purple = red + blue, so a purple gem can go in a red or blue socket.

Each color has an uncommon and a rare gem. When cut in the same way, the rare gem does slightly more for a socket than the uncommon gem does. The rare gems also require a higher skilled jewelcrafter to cut the gem properly.

[edit] Uncommon and Rare Gems

Here's a list of uncommon and rare gems by color, uncommon listed first:

  • Blue - Azure Moonstone, Star of Elune
  • Green - Deep Peridot, Talasite
  • Orange - Flame Spessarite, Noble Topaz
  • Purple - Shadow Draenite, Nightseye
  • Red - Blood Garnet, Living Ruby
  • Yellow - Golden Draenite, Dawnstone

[edit] Meta Gems

Metagems are all rare, and need even higher skill than rare colored gems. They also require dropped patterns to learn how to cut them.
