    Re: E3 Thread 2013

    Originally Posted by Nadiar
    Microsoft should do a commercial of a caveman shoving stone tablets into another stone tablet, talk about gaming in the Stone Age, and then show some future looking setting where one guy talking to another says "did you play Halo 5 yet??" "yeah, of course..." Hits a floating Mute button, says "Xbox, Buy Halo 5... Xbox, Play Halo 5" Removes Mute "See? I'm online now!"
    The PS4 interface video I posted does that (the functionality, not the cavemen). It then shows a girl order and download a game to her console from her phone while she is not home. No voice integration required. No DRM required. They even suggested you could choose whether or not to download the sp or the mp first. Point being, this is something both companies will need to address going into the future, and neither has an obvious advantage over the other as a result of their DRM and hardware strategy.

    As for the controller issue, I think I mentioned before, but I think Microsoft learned their lesson. You can see this in their games that were once kinect games and no longer are. With the kinect in every box, there is no longer a reason to force it's use. Developers will find ways to use it when they feel like it adds something to the experience.

    And yeah, the price is completely bone headed, but chances are the PS4 will be impossible to find this year, and if Microsoft takes a beating, they might lower the price next year.

    I'm mostly surprised that none of the previous consoles got price drops. No PS3 or 360 for $200, no Vita price drop, no Wii U price drop.

    Originally Posted by Ialnyien
    Actually it boils down to 3 reasons now:

    $500
    Used Games
    Online authentication.

    If you support 1 of those, no big deal imho. Having all 3 on the same system? Just makes them seem out of touch with the entire gaming community.
    As somone used to PC gaming, used games and online authentication are irrelevant to me personally.

    I still think it was a bone headed move, but I actually wonder if holding on to the used games thing hurts consumers more in the end. PC games are cheaper. Is it because PC gamers are less likely to buy something because they know they can't trade it in? Would killing used games force console gamers to be more responsible with their purchases? Because while I admit the arguments about using that money to buy a new game makes perfect sense, I still think people are incredibly irresponsible with buying into launch day hype for every damn game and then reselling it for $17 a week later.

    These issues about which console is better have a way of working themselves out in the first year.     We are very early in the process and I do want to see the titles side by side on a list as we get closer, but as of right now, I'd buy a PS4 - and this is coming from a guy that just traded in his PS3 for chump change since it was talking up shelf space and never being used.

    Re: E3 Thread 2013

    E3 isn't over, but the big surprises typically are by this point. From now on we mostly get more in depth looks at what was already shown. Having said that, this is the happiest I've been with an E3 in a long time. Even ignoring the Microsoft vs Sony stuff, there seem to be more things to look forward to than there have been for several years. Publishers like Ubisoft who I had taken for granted and assumed would just milk a couple franchises to death are taking risks with new games that are not only new IPs, but new takes on gameplay.

    So anyway, two more videos to share. The first is a video showing FF15's gameplay. I think anyone who cares about this game has already seen the story trailer, but might not know this one exists. It looks very good, although I've seen a lot of bewildering complaints that it isn't a "real" Final Fantasy because it isn't turn based. I'm not really sure why it was okay when it was named Versus but is somehow offensive now, especially considering the series having two dang mmos as numbered titles, and the ridiculously long development times these games go through. Would you rather wait another 10 years for Agni's to be FF15, as opposed to waiting 10 years and having it be FF16? I just don't get it.

    Anyway, the second video is the rest of that facial animation tech demo made by Quantic Dream. I'm not really sure what ultimate purpose this tech demo serves, considering MGS5, FF15, and Quantum Break all already display fantastic facial animations, but it's a pretty funny video and is worth watching if you have an even passing interest in this sort of thing.
