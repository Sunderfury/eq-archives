    What he's talking about are things like the extra special gems JCs get, the extra gem slots for blacksmiths, the special self-only wrist embosses for leatherworkers, etc. Honestly, I would be fine with those going away. It's a little silly that people min-max professions based on what gives the most stats, as opposed to picking what works for larger goals (how do you see this character's personality, do you want to make your own gear/gear for alts, what can you use to make gold for your character, etc.) I think the only thing holding them back from pulling the plug on perks is Engineering, where the perks are those nifty self-enchants that add a fair amount of flavour to engineers. It'd be a shame to lose those, but you'd have to get rid of them if you got rid of the other perks.

    Re: Warlords of Draenor

    The question was floated that your professions would be account-wide. Previously when Blizzard has floated questions, it means they're pretty certain thats exactly what they're going to implement, and I think it would be pretty awesome. You wouldn't have to pass by a rare node and wish you were on a class that could harvest it, you could harvest it yourself. Then you just level characters up to unlock additional professions. It gets rid of the requirement that all professions be equally balanced, because people could just level another character if they decide "I need to have Engineering!"

    If they do that, I don't think they'll remove Profession specific things like the Belt gem slot (although it might get removed due to Gem and gear changes), because you could level Jewelcrafting on one character, and all characters would have access to the slot. But the passive options, like +Stamina would likely need to be removed.
