    Spoiler for fight club:

    Don't level again once you reach 23. You will then cap at 3 million XP (eventually). Once your XP caps, quest counters no longer increment and you keep getting the same XP every time you did based on where it last was.

    So, save the high XP epic quests that are quick to do until you hit the 3,000,000XP... that will definitely make getting your Twists a lot less of a grind.

    Re: DDO Back in the Game Getting ready for the expansion....

    At level 7 see if you can get into a group doing elite necropolis. Tomb of XXX heart. Doing all 5 on elite will get you to 8 or very close to it. If you've been gone a while I would strongly suggest starting a fresh toon at 1 in Korthos just to get back into the swing of things. That entire area is a n00b guide. One thing I would advise is if you're a caster class that can scribe scrolls don't bother with AH. There's vendors everywhere waiting to drop your rank 1-7 spells on you. Wasted a lot of money last night before I figured that one out. Hit P to open up the adventure and wilderness lists. That will have places for you to go at any level. Fastest xp is still usually just grab whoever is running elites (you can start groups that way since you're vip) and just run the chains or the challenges like Gwylan's stand. Remember to get a rogue or artificer for all runs for the +15% trap disarm xp bonuses.

    Re: DDO Back in the Game Getting ready for the expansion....

    Originally Posted by Ialnyien
    What else should I look at grabbing (have the VIP status) from the store, if anything?
    If you have VIP, then there's little you really need now. Should you ever go F2P, I'd suggest picking up Shared Bank access.

    Originally Posted by Ialnyien
    Where's a good place to go @ level 7 to get my feet wet again?
    Delera's is excellent. Four quests (well, five technically, but one is literally favour for walk in, talk to someone, walk out), they're not horribly long, has a decent storyline (narrated by Gary Gygax!), and excellent rewards/xp. Just make sure to have a form of deathblock by the end (the last boss insta-kills), and if you use weapons, bring Ghost Touch and Good aligned weapons.

    I'll also suggest the same thing I suggest to everyone who skips levels - go back and run the lower content. Since you're VIP, you can unlock it on elite first run. You'll plow through it. You'll want the favour unlocks; extra bag slots, better thieves' tools, things like that. You'll also pick up gear that you missed and things like collectables that you can sell to afford the stuff you still need.
