    Originally Posted by Wool
    While the tweet was unprofessional, ignore the noise pollution in the gaming community for a moment and think about this from another point of view. Brevik, while polite in doing so, was critical of Diablo 3, and implied the team was not as good as his own. Chris Haga was a huge Diablo 2 fan growing up, got his dream job to help make a new Diablo game, and in turn got depressed because of Brevik's comments. Jay Wilson chimes in, and either in anger, or in an attempt to make his team feel better, says to screw that guy.

    It would be a perfectly normal chain of events in a real conversation, but people seem to constantly forget that hundreds of thousands of people will read everything you say and freak the hell out if you aren't careful with your words.

    People have such short memories. People don't like a real money auction house, so Diablo is the devil and anything someone who used to work at Blizzard North is making is the savior of all gaming kind. Let us worship anything "a guy who made Diablo 2" is making. But you know what else these guys made? They made Hellgate London, and I am one of the only people in existence that wasn't pissed to hell off by that game. So can we please end this ridiculous idoltry? If only Jay Wilson was smart enough to point this out himself.
    No, he said "Fuck that Loser" on a public forum as the lead of a video game in response to his predecessor. It was completely uncalled for. D3 is by all measures a worse game. D3's most recent successes were due to them copying D2 features the community clamored for. At launch D3 was basically a beta of a game as most of the advertised features were not in the game, and still a lot of them are not.

    Anyways, that's beside the point. The reason it is cause for me hating Wilson and thinking he is mildly sentient afterbirth is that not only did he say something very offensive that was unwarranted, and not only did he do it as the boss of the franchise, he did it in response to someone who a large reason that Jay Wilson was even employed working on Diablo 3 as there would be no Diablo 3 if Diablo 2 had not been a paradigm changing success.

    And when Jay Wilson does make that remark, he does so from a position of utter failure as a developer and should be taking notes instead of acting like a jerk off in public. If he had not oversaw the implosion of the Diablo fanbase and brought the franchise to ruin he could have had such hubris somewhat justified. Instead it is like an apprentice calling his master an epitaph when the master is trying to help him.

    If one of his employees was devastatingly butthurt that should have been done not in the public eye for show. Then the apology was utter nonsense just adding to the complete failure Blizzard's CS and PR team is.
