    Re: Legion

    The different resources thing is something they've wanted for ages. It helps make specs feel like different classes, in a way, and not just "priest whose nukes hit harder". Glad to see you caught the "daily dungeon run is for crafting" thing. Frozen/Chaos Orbs all over again, likely including the "Soulbound until final patch" part. That, combined with the three worn pieces limit, is a great way to kill crafting. Multistrike's days were numbered after that interview where the one dev said it didn't feel different enough, which is one of the complaints they had about Armor Piercing before they killed that stat. Isn't it great to know that things like flying at release won't happen because development time is being taken up by redesigning stats (and all the specs that had multistrike as top stat) that didn't feel "different enough"? And a rofl at trying to sell the sewers as anything other than a giant "oops". Paladins can hike all the way out to Light's Hope Chapel, but rogues can't go to Ravenholdt. Bizarre.

    Other stuff: I'm surprised that you think Survival being melee is a good idea. The hunters I talked to before quitting (one an achievement player, one a mythic guild raider) couldn't stand the idea. They don't want to be melees, because melees are dumped on in just about any encounter you can name. The mythic guy doubted any raid guild would even allow a hunter to go Survival unless the DPS gain was colossal.

    Glaives being DH only seems silly. You give hunters a melee spec so more than one class uses AGI polearms, and then create a class that has a unique weapon type? It won't matter so much in Legion, since everyone will be using an artifact weapon and there won't be space taken up on drop tables by glaives. Hey, I guess they can always scrap this idea by the next expansion!

    I wonder how the item upgrade thing will work in practice. Say I've bought a ring off the AH, and I want to upgrade it. How do I destroy it to create this Obliterium if I'm not a JC, and does that mean I don't have a ring until I buy the upgraded ring off someone else? Do the stats stay the same - do I have "Obliterium - ring - haste/SPI"? Sounds like a stupid change compared to the current system, which has issues like method of obtaining the upgrade limiting material but which is fairly straight forward to understand and is very AH-friendly.

    It's too bad about daily CD's going away. I'd rather they changed it to a seven charge CD with one charge coming back per day. That makes it essentially a daily CD, but if you plan to be away from the game for a while you can use extra charges and not miss anything. But, as we've seen with daily dungeons, that's a little too complicated for them.

    "There won't be specific item art for each race and class" - heh. But they're not trying desperately to push this out in a limited time frame, no, not at all. Glad I'm out - it's going to be a hot mess.

    Non-Legion-related aside: is the Timewalking grind to get the toys/mounts as bad as it sounded when I talked to someone in game yesterday? I mean, I know they're desperate to fill the time between now and Legion with something to do, but this seems grindy and lame even for Furor.

    There are hundreds of mounts in WoW, and they are obtainable by a wide variety of different means. Some are simple to get, and cheaply bought off vendors; some require great feats of skill like Gladiator and current Mythic raid mounts; some require tremendous luck (e.g. the Elegon mount); and some require long-term effort (e.g. the Violet Proto-Drake from A Long Strange Trip, or Darkmoon Faire prizes).

    Ultimately they are all cosmetic rewards, and Timewalking is designed to be an evergreen system that we will be expanding over the years. Unlike, say, a Tanaan rep mount that clearly was designed to be earned within the scope of this patch, there will be people doing Timewalking (especially as more dungeons, and more expansions' content get added) throughout the future of WoW, and this represents a long-term goal within that framework.

    If you want to rush through dozens of Timewalking dungeons in a short timeframe to be one of the first to get the mount, and you'd find that experience satisfying, then by all means do so. But I certainly wouldn't say that's the intended approach here. The 500-Badge quest turn-in is a once-per-event thing (which we definitely could have made clearer), so if you want to take a more measured pace and just do the Timewalking quest each time the event comes around while you happen to otherwise actively be playing, you can get the mount with just a few total hours spent (though over a longer period of real time). It's up to you. In the meantime, there are other items (notably some great alt catch-up gear) available through the system, and those are much more attainably priced.

    Re: Legion

    My friend in game said it was 8500 badges to get the one mount and the two toys. I did the math for her: 30 weeks, so about seven months, doing two Timewalking dungeons per day each of the five days the event is available. That gets you 500 x 2 x 5 = 5000 badges from the turn-in, plus 2 x 5 x 2 x 5 x 35 (average number of badges per dungeon completion) = 3500 badges from runs. She was seeing 20 minute queues as a DPS, so unless she manages to arrange for a group to chain-run dungeons, she's looking at 10 x 2 x 5 x 20 = 2000 minutes, or 33-ish HOURS, of being in game and being unable to do anything other than outdoor activities, which for her right now means the two things she's put off because she hates them so much, pet battles and Archaeology. For a mount and two toys, none of which she's likely to actually use once she has them - they're just +1's for the toy and mount collection achievements.

    It's pretty awful when you step back to see the grind for what it is. That seven month figure I used is pretty interesting, given that the expansion is supposed to launch shortly after the movie in seven months, isn't it?

    Originally Posted by Dawlin
    I'm getting sick of them using "alt catch-up gear" as good excuse, while simultaneously destroying altitis with the other hand.
    It's especially horrible when any alt you've actually been playing wouldn't use the gear in question. I barely touched the Timewalking dungeons because everything that dropped there was below what my alts already had from crafting and Apexis gear. Because Timewalking is only available at certain times, many alts will never bother with it, being geared in stuff like LFR/Apexis well before the event comes around. The design goals of Timewalking were clear when they announced it: lower queue times for people leveling alts, give people who like dungeons something to do without actually having to devote resources to designing a new dungeon. Don't try and dress it up as much more than that, because it fails at those other things and that reflects negatively on the dev team's ability to deliver on design goals.
