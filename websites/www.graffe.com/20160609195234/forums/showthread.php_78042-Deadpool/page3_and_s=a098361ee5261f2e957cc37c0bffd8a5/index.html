    Re: Deadpool

    Originally Posted by Cee
    I loved it! There hasn't been a movie in a long long time that I have actually wanted to see a second time which is why I don't buy DVD's of movies 99% of the time. This one however, I will be first in line when its released!
    Exactly. Deadpool is the first movie I have ever saw in theater where I walked out thinking I cannot wait to buy the blu-ray for this film. It is such a goddamn fun and well executed movie. Do you think the analogy to Ghostbusters is appropriate with respect to how Ghostbusters was fun and able to tell a story sincerely without taking itself so seriously at the same time? Both films go off the rails and allow the observer to see how the interactions play out.

    Elsewhere, writers feel they have to adhere to some typical behavior in films to be successful and end up betraying their own ideas. So, I was enthusiastic about the film for not repeating that habit even if it had ended up being truly terrible. Luckily, it is a shooting star.

    Another aspect of the film that was great is that the writers did not become self-indulgent in overusing aspects of Deadpool. All of Deadpool's traits were utilized at some point but no dead horses were beaten, something which would have been very easy to do with Deadpool and easily motivated to simply provide fan service for dollars.

    With that being said, Ryan Reynolds missed a great opportunity to hang dong. It was such low hanging fruit.
