    Zedd, Dmitry's second visit to the boards came after the deadline for voting had passed, so the vote was finished basically. (Dumb, but that's how much we try to play it by the rules as mods).

    Personally, after much thought, I was one of the initial two mods who voted to ban Dmitry. It was not something I did lightly, and my vote was very late in the day because of it. It's only the second time I have thought we should ban someone (Smarty's racist stuff wa the other occasion). I like Dmitry, we joked together after I suspended him for the line chorus of dancing goats in dresses, and over the years I have enjoyed chatting to him, and I felt really shit for doing so. I've defended him many times against other mods & actions, and if he only would calm down the nutty campaigns he would remain one of the best posters on the board. I also was one of the people who voted for him to be a mod in the last election.

    But, I felt as a mod, if we didn't ban someone who descended to that level repeatedly, after being asked to not continue posting, when they were suppossed to be one of the people setting the standards for the board, then there's no point us being mods. Some actions are indefensible, and Dmitry knew exactly what he was gunning for when he started the numerous "Gays & paedophiles" posts, followed by all the faggot stuff.

    Yes the atmosphere was charged and a heated debate was going on about the initial mod actions, but at every step of the way Dmitry tried harder and harder to escalate things. We're not in a show trial, and you don't prove your point by being so outrageous and insulting even your supporters and friends can't back you any longer. At some point someone just has to say "Stop, there has to be a line somewhere". Graffes is, I hope, not a board where the faggot and gay paedophile thing is tolerated, even if it's done by someone who isn't at all homophobic and just doing it to prove a political point about mod actions.

    So, in the end, I voted to ban someone I liked and considered a friend on Graffes. Which sucks, because even though I think it the right decision it was by no means an easy decision, and I wish he hadn't gone down that route. And yes, I'd change my vote if after an appropriate length of time Dmitry could demonstrate he wasn't going to be a complete raging lunatic the instant he disagrees with something, and I wish him well. (note that's different to suspending him, which expires automatically).
