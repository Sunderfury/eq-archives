    Warlock basics (more)

    Warlocks have a lot of variety in what they can do, but currently in beta the level cap is 30, whereas in final it's thought to be up to 60.

    Still, even with them only reaching half their power, they get lots of neat tricks. The basics first, though. You get spells every 2 levels, except for first, when you start out with a couple. You get quests for your pets, starting at 5th level for your Imp pet (who can nuke fireballs but runs like a chicken when the baddies come after him, screaming), 10th for your Voidwalker (a tanking pet which can taunt), 20th for your Succubus (a rogue type pet which can de-taunt, making the monster attack others, and can do a backstab style attack from behind, and later can do a single-target mez), and presumably 30th for your Felhound (not in yet). Each quest gets progressively harder, and it's pretty cool how each race & culture has their own spin on the quests.

    The Voidwalker and Succubus (and presumably later pets which aren't in yet), use Soul Shards; these are also used by other spells such as a spell that converts one to a quick heal of a certain amount, or a Soulstone (see below). Soul Shards are gathered with the spell you get at level 10 called "Drain Soul," which is generally cast near the end of the fight. It does a bit of DOT damage, but more importantly, if it's still on the monster while it dies, you get a Soul Shard, draining its essence for fuel, basically. There are higher level versions which do more DOT damage, also. These items are Soulbound, so you can't trade them off, but the other creations you make with them (healing bloodstone, or soulstone for a sel-rez) can be traded to others. It takes a shard to Summon another person to your position too, as well as the help of others in the spell (see below). It takes 2 shards to make a Soulstone for a self-rez, so pack a lot of them when you can harvest them. Each pet can also be trained at later levels for other abilities, such as a +Stamina buff from the Imp for the whole party, or the "Seduction" power of the Succubus (single-target mez). You visit the Demon Pet Trainer for those, who is generally in the same area as your trainers are in the capital cities (except for Ironforge, for some reason, so gnomes have to train their pets in Stormwind, the human city).

    More basics: They use Fire Magic, Shadow Magic, Curses & Demon Mastery spells. What that means is that they can use a Fire DOT with a DD component and also use Wands that are based on fire (which I like to do when I run across creatures that are highly resistant to Shadow magic). Shadow Magic covers their DD Shadow Bolts (very cool looking), their Corruption line of DOTs, and their Fear spells (I think). Curses have a few varieties, including one that weakens the foe's physical attacks, one that actually raises their attacks but prevents them from fleeing (good in dungeons and crowded areas), one that does another DOT, and one that forces the caster foe to speak in Demon Tongue, thus reducing their casting time by 100%.

    You can only have one curse on at a time, but for the fire and shadow magics, a higher level of one will overwrite the lower one.

    Their other tricks include: they can summon a non-aggro "Eye" to scout ahead (level 28 I think), an AE Rain of Fire (level 20 I think, fun to drop one in town and watch newbies freak out), a summoned Nightmare mount so you don't have to buy a horse or other mount (not in game yet), Detect Invisibility, Demon Breath (waterbreathing), Summon people to your location from anywhere (needs the help of 2 other people, who right click the portal to help you concentrate on it), Siphon Mana (as well as Siphon Health, which is earlier), convert Health to Mana, Sense Demons (show up on the mini-map in the top right), Command Demons (effectively giving you a new Demon pet, by taking over control of the one that was just running at you), they can make Bloodstones to heal and can create Soulstones (which can be given out, and let a player resurrect on the spot rather than having to do a time-consuming corpse run as a ghost), they can "Banish" also (freezes a single target for a short time, but makes it immune to any damage until the spell wears off; good to stop a quick add), they can do a last-chance "Hellfire" spell, which does a PBAE fire damage around them, but also damages themselves. They can do even more, but some of those things aren't in the game yet, so I can't comment. I'll look to see what they are the next time I visit my trainer to see what I forgot.

    Warlocks wear cloth armor and start off knowing the Dagger skill, but they can elect to spend 2 Skill Points to learn Staves (you generally get 1 SP per level, except Humans who get a bonus skill point every 5 levels, which are used for some weapons or for tradeskills). I am doing both weapons with this most recent Warlock, good stuff.

    Stamina is important for a Warlock. Spirit is good to help you regain both mana and health, and Intellect is good to give you more mana, and Strength is good to help you do more melee damage, but Stamina is very important. After all, you get a spell line that converts health to mana, and also after you get your Succubus pet, you'll find that you will have her casting her spell to make the monster attack another target, which usually means you, bub. She does her best work while being behind the mob anyway, so she can do her backstab-style Lash of Pain. So, it's vital to be able to survive the hits the mobs dish out until your spells, your melee and her melee, and your DOTs kill it.

    In a group scenario, the Warlock is pretty versatile. Besides the Summons or the Soulstones to self-rez or the Bloodstones to heal they can hand out, they have several pets to choose from, depending on the situation. The Imp can stand back and nuke, generating little aggro, and gives the party a Stamina boost. The Voidwalker runs in and helps dish out tanking damage; you can elect to "turn off" his taunt ability, too, if you just want him to bash but not taunt off the main tank. The Succubus can dish out a lot of damage with her backstab style attack, and can mez a single add for a short time. The Warlock has to be careful with his DD Shadow Bolts not to do too much damage too quickly, as the monsters don't like that and will turn on foolish casters, but 2 of the 3 DOTs don't do damage at first when they land, so they can be thrown on the monster with relative impunity. They can weaken a mob, or cast the Curse which prevents them from running away and potentially aggro'ing other mobs, siphon off some of the mob's health or mana for their own, and when the need really arises, they have their AE rain of fire or Hellfire. Don't overlook their melee abilities, either. They generally do decent damage, but don't often do more than the tank, so they can aid by poking or bashing the monster, also.

    EDIT: Forgot to mention the Pet healing line of spells, which let you channel some of your hitpoints to your pet. Also, forgot to mention that if you change your mind later, you can get your Skill Points back by unlearning the Staves skill, or any tradeskill you might have been working on, so you're not stuck with a staff skill you never use or a tradeskill you ended up hating.

    Originally Posted by Flintnilf
    How has the cs been in alpha so far? As far as answers to petitions, bug fixes, exploits taken care of etc. How quick are they?
    There was only a /bug and a /suggest command. If you were seriously hindered in your testing abilities by being stuck on the geometry or something, you could use the /stuck command which would take you to your bind point after about a 5 minute time period. The 5 minutes is there to stop people from exploiting it, which happened during the first human push.

    Many times, there were devs or GM's online and if they were, you simply had to ask in the general chat if they could help you. There really was no full Customer Support during the Alpha stages. You mainly relied on your own smarts, the forums, and other players. This isn't a fault in the game, it's an alpha.

    As far as getting rid of bugs and exploits, they are very much so on top of it. I can only remember one instance (during the horde push) when the level cap was raised from 25 to 30 and warriors found a way to level very quickly. The person that found the exploit reported it as soon as he / she found it, however they continued to play with that character and hit level 30 very, very quickly. The only reason this exploit wasn't taken care of faster was simply because it was right after a patch that was implemented late on a Friday night, leaving a good portion of the weekend for people to abuse it.

    Now that beta has officially started, they have implemented a true CS. It is in it's infancy so I'm sure they may have some bumps in the road. However, in my opinion, Blizzard has done everything right so far.. so I doubt those bumps will be that big.

    Gnomes are how big?
    They are very small, I kid you not. Dwarves tower over them! See picture for height comparison.



    Talk to me about guilds!! How hard are they to form/get tabards for!?
    Like Savanna said, not sure how hard it will be to form a guild in retail. We were able to create a guild instantly because there were so few of us. It did cost ten gold to *set* a tabard design (only guild leader can do this), but if you wanted to wear a tabard, you needed to cough up a gold and buy a regular tabard from a vendor. The tabard will be blank (pure white) if you are not in a guild or your guild leader didn't set a design. In every major city (human, dwarf, etc), the tabard NPCs are located in the visitor center.



    Is there any sense of "camping" By that I mean just sort of repeatedly doing the same thing ad naseum?
    No, not at all. The only thing that comes close are when players need tradeskill drops. Animals naturally drop leather and certain NPCs drop linen/wool for tailoring. I would go to certain areas and kill mobs to farm the drops, but it was never really "camping." People are good about staying out of your way if you're there to complete a quest and they will leave certain mobs alone. But even so, they improved tradeskill drops to reduce the need to farm, IMO. They just added in "skinning" so you can skin your kill and get more leather that way. People now just wait for you to finish killing an animal before skinning your corpse. (I don't mean they are right there while you're killing it -- they will run up to it after you've looted the corpse and left because you might be doing your own skinning for all they know.)
