    Re: 'Supergirl' found: CBS casts 'Glee' actress

    Originally Posted by PPatty
    Wasn't bad. Light hearted, entertaining enough for me to keep watching. The wham-bam-thank-you-ma'am-keep-it-moving storytelling works in this case.
    Yeah, as pilots go, this one REALLY kept up the momentum.

    Normally I see trailers for things and I think I've seen most of the best of what I'm going to see, but this was consistently good throughout.

    It had interpersonal drama, it had good reveals, it had action that was only a step or maybe two below things like "Age of Ultron" (More on par with Iron Man I action, actually) but the action wasn't as egregious as "Man of Steel" (which I liked, a LOT, but it had a very different pacing than most superhero movies).

    Originally Posted by Delores Mulva
    It felt a lot like the Flash pilot, which seems right given they have the same showrunner. Hopefully it develops into just as good of a show. I really liked how the special effects showed how much strength the two of them had.
    I actually liked it a LOT more than the flash pilot for one simple reason:

    The people in charge didn't seem to care whether you knew the characters or not. (Which I liked)

    They avoided belaboring the backstory points early on, went straight into hooking you, and you learned the characters and setting as things went along.

    The Flash pilot spent a lot more time covering bases, which made sense because it's central to the character's motivations in the story, but while I grew up as a huge green arrow and billy batson fan, I never spent much time with flash, so it worked for them (the people in charge of the flash tv series) to educate me, because without it, I would have gotten entirely un-interested shortly into the first season.

    Kara Zor-El gets to start off with basically a clean slate the same way Oliver Queen did with the Arrow TV show -- A mostly unimprinted image on the expectations of the average viewer who likes comic books.

    Spoiler for unrelated to supergirl:
    (edit -- Although, with Arrow -- HARDCORE nod to aspects of Mike Grell's The Longbow Hunters) which has a permanent place on the bookshelf outside of my house's restroom, lest any guest who is a comic book fan ask for reading material. Then again, he was the guy who brought green arrow into an actual ongoing series in the first place...)


    Even if you count the justice league animated stuff that some of the target audience watched as kids, those two got FAR less screen time than Flash's comic relief.

    Originally Posted by Khieran
    I saw Dean Cain
    I *KNEW* that face and voice were familiar!

    The damned stubble and haircut threw me.

    I didn't recognize helen slater at all because I didn't even KNOW there'd been a supergirl movie back in the 80's.

    That was a cool nod on both parts.

    All in all, this was really enjoyable and I'm so into it that even if they shove in a bunch of low budget filler episodes because they blew too much on the pilot, I'll watch it because it's clear they have a plot and I REALLY enjoy the actors and characterization on this, despite having only seen a tiny handful of them in anything before.

    Re: 'Supergirl' found: CBS casts 'Glee' actress

    Originally Posted by Nerkahia
    With all the super villains escaping with her and a crew helping her hunt them down? Sounds like the EXACT SAME plot as The Flash.
    The devil is in the details. Barry had a handful of geeks, and was dealing with (in general) a motley assortment of isolated villains. Kara has a government department that is perfectly capable of taking on alien threats on her side, and is fighting a villain group that is (mostly) working towards a common goal under her aunt's control. When we saw Barry training, everyone was learning about what he could do. Kara is a known quantity - the government group even brought Kryptonite to take her down the first time they talked to her - and she's not being tested, she's being trained. The two shows feel very different, although in terms of tone Supergirl is closer to The Flash than it is the other DC properties (Arrow, Gotham).
