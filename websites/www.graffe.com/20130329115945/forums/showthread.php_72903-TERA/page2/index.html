    Re: TERA

    I was actually planning to do a write up today before seeing Dmitry’s post.

    Despite what I said in earlier posts, I went ahead and bought the game and have been playing it. I figured that $50 wasn’t too bad of a price even with a monthly subscription. I went in knowing that this isn’t going to be a long term commitment like Everquest or World of Warcraft. Basically this is just to hold me over until Guild Wars 2 launches.

    Here’s a quick summary followed by my more in depth thoughts.

    First, TERA is very fun, refreshing and beautiful.

    Second, TERA is very complex and there’s not a lot of hand-holding.

    Third, TERA is not and will never be a WOW-Killer. I realize that people here don’t go in for that silly title and convention but it needs to be said.

    Now with that out of the way, here’s my in depth thoughts on TERA.

    I’m playing a High Elven Sorcerer and am currently level 22 on Celestial Hills (the Role-Playing server). When creating your character there is a difficulty rating for each class and a Sorcerer is a 1 out of 5 in terms of difficulty where the more melee oriented classes tend to be much more difficult. I’ve heard that melee classes are more “fun” to play and that they shine in TERA but I enjoy my Sorcerer immensely.

    From the moment you launch, the game is beautiful. I’m running on max settings and the graphics, characters, landscapes, everything is simply gorgeous. Even at max settings I don’t get a whole lot of graphical lag even with a lot of action going on. I’ve got a pretty solid rig though so older computers will probably need to scale things back a bit. If your system has trouble with WOW, you probably won’t be able to run TERA all that well. Finally it’s worth noting that the graphics have a very Asian / Korean feel to them. It’s not anime exactly but everything seems heavily influenced by Korean artists developing the game.

    The combat in this game is phenomenal and it truly sets TERA apart from other MMOs out there. Combat is very fluid and responsive. In WOW you press 2 or 3 buttons and the average, non-elite, mob dies and you go on to the next. In TERA you end up using a lot more abilities as you actually fight the creature. One of the main components is the ability to dodge attacks. This isn’t a percentage based rating. If a creature swings a hammer and you’re underneath it, you get hit. If you side step or roll backwards out of the way, you avoid the attack. Similarly a lot of attacks do not act like heat-seeking missiles. If you shoot an arrow and the creature moves to the side, you miss. There are attacks that lock-on and will “follow” a player or enemy. Overall, the game takes WOW’s concept of “don’t stand in the fire” and runs with it all out.

    As a Sorcerer, I have a mana pool. While soloing quest and leveling content, mana is never an issue and might as well not exist. In these situations my main limits are ability cooldowns. In groups, mana is a concern against bosses and it’s very easy to run out before the creature is dead. But there’s enough abilities that let me regen mana that I’m not helpless. It’s not like in Everquest or WOW where once I’m out of mana, I’m merely a spectator until the end of the fight. I just need to take some time to recover my mana and then get going again (time which I just spend dodging attacks). It’s not as bad as it sounds.

    While melee is supposed to be much more difficult to play well (considering how brutal the combat can be), it’s also supposed to be very satisfying. I only played a Warrior briefly and felt like a buzzsaw tearing through things. At least until I hit the tutorial boss and I realized how difficult melee could be to play well. So Ranged is easy to play, does solid damage but isn’t as “special” as melee. Melee is more difficult but highly satisfying and personally rewarding when executed well.

    The quest, storylines and lore are nothing to write home about. Quests are very much of the “go kill 10 orcs and collect 5 bear asses” variety. There is a central story but it’s nothing all that special. What makes the questing fun is the combat aspect of it. Since combat is so well done it’s not all that important to get great quests since it’s simply a joy to go out and slaughter things to complete that quest. One problem is that this game is going to be VERY unfriendly towards alts. There is only a single leveling path so once you’ve seen the content once, you don’t have the option to go explore a different set of zones on an alt to avoid the monotony. On the other hand, the zones are very lush and vibrant. They feel alive and are not static like in Star Wars. There’s also a lot of terrain variety but the early game is focused mainly on plains and forests.

    Big Ass Monsters (BAMs) are great fun and another thing that makes TERA appealing. These are creatures that are massive and can easily stomp you flat in a couple of hits. The prologue tutorial introduces you to your first, you get a taste of another around level 11 if you stick to the storyline on newbie island and then they start appearing more regularly from level 20 onwards. While a lot of classes can solo some of these, it’s not really worthwhile doing so. It takes forever and a single mistake can get you killed.

    Now speaking about the Prologue, it sucks. This is not the newbie island content which is actually pretty well done. The first time you create a character, you do the prologue which is supposed to serve as a tutorial and show you what your character is going to be like at later levels. So let’s say you pick a Warrior, the prologue upranks you to level 20 with all the skills and gear appropriate for a level 20 Warrior. The problem is that it’s too much too fast. A brand new player gets lost with all of the abilites and trying to figure out when to use certain abilities. And then all of that is stripped away when you finish the prologue and start newbie island. And the final boss is a beast. As a melee I died about 6 times before killing it while my Sorcerer had no problems. At least once you’ve done the prologue once, you can skip it later. Or you can use it if you want to try out a new class and see what types of abilities they get later on.

    Now I’m going to go into some negatives. The game is very complex and doesn’t do a good job of explaining how it’s various systems works. Enchanting, Glyhping, Crafting and so on are all pretty complicated aspects of the game and I had to look at outside sources to figure out how they work. These are areas of the game where the Korean influence is showcased by their grindiness and complexity. I haven’t really explored Crafting much yet either because people complain that at low levels crafting is pointless as quested gear is much better. They also complain that it’s VERY expensive. Enchanting is the same way. It’s expensive and it doesn’t make sense until you read some guides. Again, most people say that enchanting is best saved until later levels and then learned.

    The first Dungeon, Bastion of Lok (or BOL) is great fun but it’s also the first time that people are likely to come together as a group so expect the typical issues inherent with that. In particular the looting system is complex and the default settings blow. I ran the dungeon twice. The first time loot was FFA (whoever clicked first got it) and it was a mad scramble every time something died to click fast. The second time I did the dungeon we spent half the dungeon experimenting with settings before finally finding how to set things to be rolled on. Seemed to work better but I’m still not happy with the way looting works. It’s also interesting to note that you can’t trade items to people across realms but boss drops are tradeable and sellable (so as a Sorcerer, I won a blue quality sword form the first boss and I can drop it in Auction or give to an alt). I’m hoping that loot issues will go away as people figure out the most fair settings and start using them as standards. Also, a party leader gets full control over the group. Even in Instance Matching, there are no vote/kick options. The Party Leader has full kick, invite, queue, loot, creature marking powers.

    I won’t say that this is a negative but the game is VERY fan-service oriented. Females are, universally, well endowed and wear very little clothing. The guys tend towards the young bare chested stereo-type so popular in anime. Now as much as an uproar made over the Elin ( an ancient, wise, race of beings that are over 200 years old but look like 12 year old school girls), they did a very good job of covering them up and not sexualizing them. People still have issues based on principle but, honestly, I don’t even notice them. I’d prefer the race not be designed as they are but for the North American release they gave them more clothing which helps a lot. However, in the newbie zones someone is, inevitably, going to start trolling the zone and create a massive flame-fest centered on the Elin. As I said though, I haven’t really noticed them much and I’m on the Role-Play server where you might actually expect to see them in large numbers.

    I also like how EnMasse was realistic with their servers. There are about 10 of them total and the game seems to have a healthy population. They didn’t make the mistake of Star Wars with 50 servers at launch (of which 30 are now ghost towns).

    I won’t speak about the high level game or PVP as I’m not there yet. From what I understand the political system at end game is pretty well thought out but I haven’t seen it yet so I really can’t comment.

    Overall, this is a great game and will definitely hold me over for a bit until Guild Wars 2 comes out. I know Graffes is better than the Blizzard forums but it is worth noting that this is not a WOW-Killer. It has the potential to be a very good game and while there were some day 1 issues, overall the game is very polished and I haven’t really seen any bugs (major or otherwise).

    So my recommendation is that if you have the money, are bored and don’t mind the over-sexualized character models (particularly females), TERA is a very fun game to spend a bit of time in while waiting on the next big thing. And holy crap was this post long.
    There are other worlds than these.

    Re: TERA

    It's worth noting, this game does some things exceptionally well at launch.

    Channels: In each of the zones/areas, there at eight copies of that zone/area that you can switch to if you're finding your current channel overpopulated. It works similar to the same way Everquest 2 does it, however, instead of creating another copy when the 1st one is deemed "over populated" there are always 8 copies available at all times. This works REALLY well when you are trying to kill a quest mob that someone has just killed. Instead of waiting a minute to respawn or fight someone else waiting to kill it, you just select another channel and kill that mob in the new channel instead.

    Server Transfers: Available for free.

    Cross-Server Dungeon Finder: As a DPS, it take about 15-25 minutes to queue up and find a group. Coming from SWTOR, it's really nice to have this tool available at the beginning of a game's existence regardless of what the naysayers have to say. It's effective and efficient, and these dungeons are worth experiencing.

    Buffs From Harvesting: When you successfully collect from a harvesting node, you can receive any number of combat buffs that last for 10m and can stack. It's a neat little feature that makes harvesting somewhat rewarding.

    Looking-For-Group Tool: This is different from the Dungeon Finder. Basically, you open up the tool, trigger the /lfg flag, describe what you want to do and it shows up into the tool to whomever is looking for something to do. Now, if you're are in fact looking for a group and you open the tool, you'll see a list of these group advertisement. If you click on one of these groups, it'll list the name of the players currently in the group along with their class/level. It also give you the option to join their group instantly. If people decide to use it, it could be really successful.

    These are just a few neat things off the top of my head, but they did some really cool things.

    Re: TERA

    TERA's looting is actually a step back in some regards. Remember in Everquest how you killed a creature and that creatures loot was locked to you for 2 minutes and then would open up so that any random stranger could loot the corpse? TERA is similar. When you kill something in TERA, a pile of loot explodes from the body. As the person who killed the creature, you get have about 3 minutes or so to go around collecting the stuff and then it opens up so that anyone can take it.

    Again, it sounds worse than it is because quest "drops" aren't really drops at all. You kill a creature with the quest item and you get notification that you obtained the quest item in question and it increases the counter by 1. And, basically, the timer is long enough that if you don't loot the stuff by the time the timer is up, odds are it wasn't something you wanted in the first place. But I did find it ironic that it seems like a step backwards.

    Party Loot, on the other hand, is just annoying. The problem is that the traditional WOW style group looting is present but it's not defaulted (default is a Round Robin system) so each time you enter a dungeon or group with others, you need to go through and make looting rules selections.
