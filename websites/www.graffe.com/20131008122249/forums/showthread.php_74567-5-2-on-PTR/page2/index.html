    Re: 5.2 on PTR

    I realize they are upping the drop rate of 5.0 lfr content when 5.2 drops. But I really feel like they are making a huge mistake with keeping 5.2 drop rates the same as they are. They have it timed so that, by the next patch, you'll get most of what you need, but you'll never fully gear out your character. Meaning you'll never get a break between patches. I get their reasoning, they don't want people to have weeks of nothing to do, afraid they'll just quit altogether.

    However I personally like having a few weeks off between raid content. Gives you a chance to recharge and refocus. I really feel like they are just going to burn out a large chunk of their casual playerbase this way. As it is amazingly slow to gear up through LFR. So much so that I would never dream of going on my alts, despite the fact they could use at least some gear to get through dailies easier.

    It's the same thing with the large amount of time it takes to valor cap and their stupid first heroic of the day vs first 7 of the week policy. Everything is designed around artificially making sure you always have something to do. But after previous expansions the only thing it succeeds in doing is always making you feel like you are behind. And the minute that carrot and stick feel unachievable, people quit. People want to feel like they've done everything they can to advance their character for the week. They might be behind the heroic raiders, but at least they did the max for the week. This current system just leaves you feeling incomplete, week after week after week. And I don't understand how blizzard can't see how badly it's effecting the playerbase.

    I am however glad they are getting rid of the gear upgrade vendor, and I hope it never comes back. It might have been a good idea on paper, but in practice it is simply demoralizing. There should never be a time when you get an item from a raid and say "well, this item is slightly better than what I have, but only if I grind out 1400 valor and upgrade it twice. So maybe I can use it in 2 weeks."
