    Re: EQ3 - IMA BACK BEOTCHES

    I didn't watch the stream and haven't seen many previews yet. So far I am neither excited nor disappointed with what I have heard.

    I will say concerning art styles, however, that I vastly prefer stylistic art for this kind of game over attempts at photo-realism. Games with "realistic" characters tend to be incredibly dull to watch once you get over how impressive it may or may not be. I mean, I'm one of the few people that was okay with the Luclin models, but just look at how much people hated those.

    Anyway it doesn't look like WoW to me, and I like it to a point, but still think it could be better. While I like "new fantasy" just fine, I think I would have liked the next EQ to instead follow a more "classical fantasy" style. I'm not an artist so don't know how to explain it, but fantasy art nowadays is bold and exaggerated, while it used to be elegant and flowing.

    The female mage looks pretty good (reminds me more of Bioshock than WoW), but the beast man in the plate armor looks bad. It's mostly the plate armor that I dislike, that does remind me of WoW. I think the mmo world has seen enough giant shoulderpads. I don't like animal races in general, but it's much better to have cartoon ones than the kind you see in Skyrim. /shudder


    P.S. There will be gnomes, but they won't be playable. New characters will have a quest to punt them into the sea. You have to do the quest to get out of the tutorial.

    Edit: I would be shocked if this doesn't also come out on PS4.

Since this is already a thing, I am 99% sure there will be region locked servers.

    Reasons for it are usually tied to latency, which is less of an issue these days, but increase with the amount of active combat. The big reason for it in this game though, might be that in EQ, depending on the servers, various timezones sometimes had issues with raidbosses being down due to competing guilds with their peak timezone hours before others. In EQ:N, with a supposedly ever changing world, people on some timezones might outright miss content. A role call event that changes the world for 3 months, where the majority of the activity happens 12 hours past someones peak time. A persistent yet changing destructable world might actually be the thing that gives a good reason to join a server with a peak population time closer to your own.

    I still think direclty forcing it upon players is a bad idea. Idiotic considering EQ allowed for it. How many of "these are my EQ memories" would not have changed if the had blocked servers?

    Re: EQ3 - IMA BACK BEOTCHES

    Didn't watch the stream, basing impressions on the site.

    I like that they're bringing in the history, to appeal to older players. EQ had a great deal of lore which was poorly-communicated. Using that (and presumably fixing the communication) is wise.

    I'm not a fan of the ever-changing world, at all. But for sandbox fans, this seems to be the ultimate in sandbox games. Question: they talk about everything can be destroyed, and they also talk about the best player-made structures lasting forever. Should I take away from this that everything can be destroyed, but will regenerate over time, and that player-built stuff that is "good enough" will qualify for regeneration over time? I can't imagine having to sit on top of one's house or whatever to protect it every day, but like I said I'm not a sandbox person. The "this is your world now" is a nice twist on EQ's original "welcome to our world".

    The mix-and-match aspect of classes sounds interesting, but hopefully there's something in place to prevent the "this is the only spec tank-mage". Did they talk about this at all during the stream?

    That Player Studio thing is very interesting. Best way to generate store content? Let the players spend the time generating content they want, and take a cut of everything they make. That's an impressive business model. I like that they've also integrated a phone app as part of the game, too.

    Best part of the site: it looks like the dwarf women will have beards, at least as an option. Huzzah!
