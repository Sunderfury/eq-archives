    Re: Final Wheel of Time Book out today

    I got this a little late, and obsessed over it for about a week. I barely got any sleep! Then as I approached the end, I started (for one reason or another) putting off finishing it.

    Well I finally did, and while there were some things I would have wanted done differently, on the whole I really enjoyed it. In fact, the majority of the book I thought was very good, with only the last hundred pages really seeming off to me. The pacing got weird, events rushed by, and some seemed like they could have had better outcomes.

    As a matter of fact, and don't hate me for this, I think the last 100 pages should have been a book of their own, expanded to maybe three or four hundred pages. This book should have ended when
    Spoiler for spoiler:
    Lan fought Demandred.


    Anyway, I found it to be suitably bleak for a book about the end of the world. It seemed to allow for spin off novels, Demandred for example seemed to have an interesting backstory hinted at.

    Spoiler for spoiler:

    And so, the reason why Robert Jordan created two million freaking characters was finally revealed. This way he could kill them all and have them be more than just faceless nobodies that the reader doesn't really care about during the end.



    Edit:

    Originally Posted by Silke

    Spoiler for Deaths:
    The one death that bothered me more than any other was Bela's. I'll admit that she died nobly and with honor but she was a horse for crying out loud and I really thought that if anyone earned the right to survive the Last Battle it was her.
    Haha, I felt the same way at first.

    Spoiler for spoiler:

    They got kept piling on though though, and were almost all depressing and sudden. Only Egwene really got the hero treatment. More than anything I hated the ones turned against their will.
