    Re: Anita Sarkeesian vs the Cybermob

    Originally Posted by Goladus
    People who have a strong association with Link as the playable hero of the Zelda franchise and will be disappointed if he is replaced by Zelda.
    Over 25 years they have had about 15 games starring Link. How much could they actually be losing? It's not like Zelda would replace Link for all time.

    So while technically there may always be people who benefit more than others, in some cases the disparity in one situation just isn't as severe as it might be in another.

    Originally Posted by Naean
    It just seems to me a waste of a lot of money to make the videos. Is she really educating anyone? It seems to me her audience is a good portion people that already agree with her but are in no position to do anything about it (ie not game developers) and people that aren't going to agree with her no matter what she says. I guess it remains to be seen if anyone that matters takes her up on correcting the issues she's pointing out.
    I don't know what this will achieve, but I can tell you what it could possibly achieve. There are basically two desired paths of change here. As a general point, change is always harder if it is covered up or ignored. There is no incentive to do something if not enough people care about it to even talk about it. And regardless of who the audience of her videos are, they have sparked quite a bit of debate across internet gaming communities.

    1) Market forces. By shining light on the issue investors and game makers are made aware of a demographic that is not properly being addressed. I mean, just look at how women were ignored as a demographic in fantasy fiction, but then Twilight and Hunger Games come out and make a ton of money. There was a market there that no one was exploiting because of prior stereotypes and assumptions.

    For the record, Twilight is just an example of an ignored market. I think it's arguable whether or not it's a good example for the empowerment of women. I say no, others might disagree with me.

    2) Community self growth. By addressing these issues, people are more likely to think about what it means to themselves, and are more likely to accept points of view that are different from their own. Limited exposure to different ideas is one of the primary motivators of bigotry in my opinion. You get this closed group of people who all have pre-defined expectations, and without outside forces are given no reason to change for the better.

    Bottom line to #2 is that by exposing people to these ideas now, they become more likely to accept them when an investor takes a shot and does #1.
