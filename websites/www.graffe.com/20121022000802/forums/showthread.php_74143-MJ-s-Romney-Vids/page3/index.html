    I work with someone who has cashed unemployment checks yet believes the government shouldn't offer unemployment insurance. His family farms, and buys crop insurance, but he believes the farm bill is "welfare" and the government shouldn't offer the subsidies that incentivize private companies to actually offer crop insurance. He earnestly and steadfastly believes that he would be better off in life if Social Security and Medicare didn't exist, because if he didn't have to pay taxes he could afford to care for his family - including parents and living grandparents all well above the retirement age - and be ahead of where he's at. If government didn't do those things, "there would be more money in the private sector."

    No matter how hard I try, I just can't wrap my head around the rationale of that argument. I can see why people bought into trickle-down economics: everyone wants to be a millionaire some day, and they believe that when they are they'll be the generous soul who eschews another few dollars of profit to provide a good workplace. Some of them might even actually do it. But every scrap of fact, practice, and empirical evidence says those situations are idealistic exceptions. And that makes me a socialist.

    In the solace of my mind and the anonymity of the internet, I reply that that's better than being an anarchist.
