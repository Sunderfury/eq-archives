    It's so easy to play around with context in a movie trailer, which makes theorizing based on one so much more precarious. For instance...

    Spoiler for Theory:
    The idea of Rey being Han and Leia's daughter makes sense, but the trailer is actually setting her up as something more akin to the new subject of the classic hero's journey. An orphaned scavenger, living hand to mouth on Tatooine... tell me that shot of her watching the ship lift off isn't screaming "dreaming of a better life in the stars." Not exactly how anyone would picture the daughter of a couple war heroes. But again, context. She could have run away from home/struck out to find her own path and the trailer is just playing with our perception.

    The same could be said about Finn. While the fact that he's wielding a lightsaber in the official poster makes it unlikely - or a very good J.J. Abrams long con - that he's not Force sensitive, all we have is one quick shot of him with a lightsaber from the movie itself, and voiceover dialogue of an unknown someone talking to another unknown someone about feeling the Force.

    Re: New Star Wars trailer out

    I hold no residual ire for the guy, but his being unable to correctly process criticism is why his movies are bad. Yeah sure, it's tiring to have to deal with rude people with no self-control, but not being able to learn from any criticism is just asking for failure. I'm sure we've all seen the awkward behind the scenes footage of the prequels, but Lucas is notoriously hard to work with and refuses to ever be wrong.

    I think I've mentioned before how Lucas wanted to name the protagonist of The Force Unleashed either Darth Icky or Darth Insanius, but he has a reputation in general for railroading game development with bizarre input. These articles are not about Georce Lucas, but are interesting in their own right.

    GameInformer - The Canceled Darth Maul Game

    Originally Posted by excerpt
    The next day, Red Fly finally met with George Lucas, but not before being told how to talk to him. Our source says they were told to never say “No” to him, or to say, “Yeah, that will be easy.” They were also told not to mention Force Unleashed’s protagonist, Starkiller. If he’s referred to by George, it will be “that guy.” The most important rule, much like not feeding a Mogwai after midnight, was “Don’t tell George how the Force works.”

    A friendly George Lucas entered the room and was eager to hear the pitch from Red Fly’s creatives. “Before they could finish their spiel, Lucas cut them off, stood up, walked over to the statues, rotated them to be facing the same direction, pushed them together, and said ‘They’re friends!’” adds the source. “He wanted these characters to be friends, and to play off of each other. He talked about the show Burn Notice as a reference point. He likened Darth Maul to Sonny from The Godfather, and he likened Darth Talon to Lauren Bacall. He actually did an impersonation of her. It was supposedly the weirdest impersonation of a ’40s actress going, ‘Don’t you know how to whistle? Put your lips together and blow.’”

    The problem with the idea of Maul and Talon teaming up for a buddy cop-like experience was that they were separated by over 170 years of Star Wars fiction – as ridiculous of an idea as Frédéric Chopin forming a band with Dave Grohl. When this vast time divide was brought up to Lucas’ attention, he brushed off the notion of it not working, and said that it could instead be a descendant of Darth Maul or a clone of him.
    Bear in mind, Red Fly's original concept for the game was supposed to be an origin story about Darth Maul becoming a Sith. They felt Anakin's turn was flat and wanted to do better. Their inspiration was deemed less important than his whim.

    GameInformer - The Fall of Lucasarts

    Originally Posted by excerpt
    The project moved on slowly until George Lucas became heavily involved with its direction, much to the initial delight and eventual ire of the team. They embraced his idea to make the protagonist a new bounty hunter, but often had their work derailed by Lucas suddenly changing his mind or demanding significant alterations to the script and the game’s tone.
    I would be more prone to giving him the benefit of the doubt as a misunderstood and weird guy if it weren't for the constant stories about how you either agree with what he says, or don't work with him.

    As an aside, this is the first most I've ever heard about what happened to the original Battlefront 3. I'm kind of surprised, but Free Radical sort of come off as assholes unable to admit their own failure in this article.
