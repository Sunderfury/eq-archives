    Re: First Look at AMC's The Walking Dead (Zombie Apocalypse Show)

    Originally Posted by Lunal
    Meh. It was way too decompressed for my tastes. Meaning, the story didn't move along at a fast enough pace and as a result it really didn't provide much of a hook for continuing to watch. And I'm a big fan of the comics! They should have at least gotten to the point where Rick reconciles with Lori. Ten minutes of excitement amid an hour and a half of programming. It just had exorbitant amounts of navel gazing.

    Probably not going to bother continuing to watch it.
    I don't think so. The comics didn't have any build up to explain who Rick was and how he changes, and that really needed to be in there.

    I think people think it was going too slow because either they wanted an action movie, or they wanted it to get to the "good" parts.

    1) Rick started as easy going, but once things started going downhill he took the responsibility to save his family onto himself.
    2) They demonstrated that this isn't an action movie. It was fucking with the survivors to have to kill people.
    3) They introduced how far people are willing to go to prevent becoming a zombie (the farmhouse)
    4) It introduced that Lori is with Shane right now.
    5) It introduced that Shane is the asshole/bad cop.
    6) It introduced Carl as a smart kid (it was kind of subtle).
    7) It showed the camp of the survivors, which is completely vulnerable.
    7a) This is why its going to be important to have a leader that can actually look objectively at what they have and do something about it.
    8) It introduced that the zombies are smart enough to do uncomplicated things they could do when they were alive, just not very well.
    8a) Zombies can run. Not well, but they can run
    8b) Zombies can open doors.

    Re: First Look at AMC's The Walking Dead (Zombie Apocalypse Show)

    It also seemed that zombies seem to have a vague memory of who they once were, or at can at least recognize things that seem familiar to them - IE: the wife coming up to her house and gazing at the door, then trying to open it; the little girl picking the stuffed animal up off the ground.

    I like these additions because it establishes the conflict that the zombies may retain a part of their humanity, but are at the same time flesh crazed monsters that can't be reasoned with. It makes the zombie that much harder to simply dispose of.

    As far as Rick going into the city -- keep in mind that was the only tip on possibly finding his family that he had to go on. Even if he knew ahead of time that Atlanta had been overrun, he'd probably still have gone.
