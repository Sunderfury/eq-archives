    Re: EQ3 - IMA BACK BEOTCHES

    I finally got a chance to watch some of the streams yesterday. I am impressed with a lot of what I see, but also concerned.

    The destructible terrain is nice, but I can see a lot of opportunity for griefing other players. Even if the stuff grows back over time, I can see someone taking out a path to an important quest NPC and keeping people from completing the quest for a while. Still, the fact that you can dig a hole and maybe unearth some hidden temple, or a cave that lets some creature out is amazing. I hope it works in the way they intend it to work.

    I didn't hear any mention of instancing... is any of this instanced?

    I love the fact that players can play around with the Landmark game and maybe build something that gets put into the actual game. That's pretty cool, but I love how they qualified that as having to be approved by customer service... otherwise I can foresee a giant forest of penis statues springing up

    As for whether or not they will "capture the glory of EQ", there's no way. EQ came along at a time when there was no other MMO out there (except Ultima) and the internet was still new to a lot of people. I remember raiding Fear and Hate on my old dial-up connection... and they aren't fond memories LOL. Back then, there weren't that many spoiler sites and the ones that were out there took a while to get going. The first few months of EQ people had to rely on each other in-game for information or on message boards like this.

    Nowadays, new content is spoiled almost before it's released. I played GuildWars2 for several months over the winter and every time there was some new event, or new holiday thing that came out, it was up on several sites almost as soon as the patch was downloaded (sometimes even before). That is a huge change from EQ's infancy and will have a huge impact on the community building chances of the game.

    Still, I signed up for the beta and it looks promising enough to follow for a while. At least they are trying something new. It's not just another WoW clone. I have to give them huge props for that.

    Have there been any crafting videos released yet?

    Re: EQ3 - IMA BACK BEOTCHES

    Originally Posted by Filan
    And the amazing thing is that looking stuff up for EQ did not feel like work, Yet with WoW it does.
    EQ was a very basic game made complex by secrecy. There wasn't a lot of complexity to the strategies and the "what should I wear" aspect of the game, and looking up items/spawns/maps was a matter of looking for basic facts. Once someone told you the basics of the game or of an encounter, pretty much anybody could fill in the blanks.

    WoW is very complex. I'd argue it's too complex, in many ways. Many specs have to run simulations to determine what the best priority system and stat allocations are for themselves, and most people can't just create a simulation on the fly. You look for information in WoW, and you get an answer specific to a very detailed set of assumptions. You don't know why that's the answer, you can't really figure out for yourself what the answer is if the assumptions change, you just know to do X because someone told you to do X. Where EQ had understanding, WoW has temporary knowledge, which will likely change come the next patch.

    Originally Posted by Khieran
    I hate going through a zone and then a year later I go back there and the same bandit camp is sitting in the same spot, the same NPC is collecting wolf hides, etc.
    I like this, to be honest. Games with a persistent world reward learning. I remember back in EQ that I knew exactly what mobs I could solo pull from certain camps, and how that changed as I gained levels. So when Alt #X went to the camp, I would be rewarded for learning the camp on another alt by being able to pull it and break the spawn. It also creates nostalgia. Would you have fond memories of camp X or zone Y if the place had changed every couple of weeks? Being able to say "I beat that", where everyone knows exactly what is involved in beating that and remembers when they beat that, requires permanence.

    Finally, I like the idea of being done with something. If the content in a place is always changing, are you ever done with that area? Think about WoW and the transmog system. If new critters are constantly moving into an area, presumably they also have new items with new models and aren't just "random camp C" plopped down in the middle of a clearing. Someone well beyond the level range for the region may want the items for transmog. So they're constantly schlepping back there to check on what's there now, or checking online to see if they should be laying waste to a low-level zone for that thing they want. I don't want my time to be taken up with trivial content. I want to be able to say, "Yup, done, never coming back." I think that's one of the big problems devs didn't anticipate with daily quests in MoP. One of the best parts of doing dailies is that moment when you hit Exalted and say to yourself, "Done, never coming back." In MoP, the devs made a system where you never get that moment of satisfaction, you're always coming back for more valor or lessers.
