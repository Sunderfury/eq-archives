    Re: The Big Bang Theory

    Originally Posted by Moozh
    Seems like valid stuff to me. I'm 28 and grew up watching the original trilogy on VHS. I really don't care much about the prequels. I have same-age friends into AD&D, though I was more into games like Magic: The Gathering. I didn't get into Star Trek but I love old sci-fi and horror movies (classic Universal monsters, Godzilla, etc). Not sure if it makes a difference, but I don't self-identify as a geek even though I like lots of typically geeky things.
    I'm 35.

    Star Wars: The original trilogy was a part of my life for almost as long as I can remember (one of my earliest memories is watching the garbage flu scene). When we played as kids, I was Luke my brother was Han. For all of my childhood including high school, there were no prequels. The first prequel was a big event for me, I saw it opening night. I enjoyed the experience. It wasn't before watching it again that I realized it didn't really compare to the originals (or, perhaps more relevantly, did not compare well to The Matrix, which came out around the same time). I am kind of done caring about it, though. The prequels are what they are and while I will happily criticize them and argue about it if there's someone to argue with, Lucas didn't ruin my childhood. I read the Zhan trilogy in college and they're part of my personal star trek canon.

    PnP RPG: AD&D 2nd edition. Dragonlance. I'd already mostly tuned out when 3e came along and only care about later editions in an academic sense. And honestly I never really played D&D much outside of video games. I'd always been more fascinated with the game concepts more than the actual playing.

    Star Trek: Movies first (I, II, III, IV, and VI), then TNG then TOS. I watched DS9 but it didn't make quite the same impression as TNG. I can still remember all the characters and everything, but don't get a sense of nostalgia re-watching it like I do when watching TNG (as horrible as it might be). But regardless, even though there was a time when I liked TNG more than anything, for most of my life when you say "Star Trek" I think of the Capt. Kirk in the red uniform commanding the Enterprise. One of the most memorable movie experiences of my entire childhood was watching Star Trek VI with my father. Never watched Voyager or any of the other spin-offs.

    LOTR was big for me though I didn't watch Fellowship when it was in theaters and only caught up when TTT came out.

    Was never into comic books. Watched the Richard Donner Superman movies (the Superman Returns intro gave me chills). Watched the George Reeves TV show on Nick At Nite. Watched Lois and Clark starring Teri Hatcher's breasts. Watched X-Men and Gargoyles on Fox (though barely remember Gargoyles despite it being awesome, mostly because I could not watch consistently and keep up with the story and characters). Enjoyed the Bryan Singer X-Men movies. Enjoyed the Sam Raimi Spider Man movies though thought 3 was not that great. Sidenote: saw Evil Dead after Spiderman 1. Another sidenode: Watching Freaks and Geeks on Netflix right now and it is awesome. Not perfect but I'm kind of disappointed I missed its original run. Not surprised at all to see that so many of the people involved in that show went on to future greatness (or at least undeniably successful careers).

    I read some Fantasy books, the type that hardcore SF/F types scoff at (Dragonlance, David Eddings) but certainly no non-nerds read that kind of thing.
