    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    If I put up glyphs below a certain price, they'll all be bought out before I can even post the next batch of glyphs. Sniping is real.

    New dev watercooler on itemization in 6.2: http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/3...ization-in-62/

    Summary of changes:

    -Personal loot is changing. Chances are no longer independent: the boss will award X items, distributed randomly, with X being larger than the normal amount a Group Loot boss would award to cover for the randomness. When personal loot is awarded, everyone will get a flashy message that YOU GOT LOOT.

    This change is all about making personal loot a better system for organized raiding. In theory, someone in LFR shouldn't see a change in total loot obtained. It will be curious to see if this somehow affects loot rolls in some buggy way.

    -Items will have wider ranges between secondary stats, to make items more spec-specific and make it so ilvl isn't the only factor determining value.

    This is the sort of change an organized raider makes without realizing what it does to others. Right now, it's already close to impossible to tell, in-game, which items improve your character the most. You can guess some of the time when a stat is stupidly important (bonus armor, your ears should be burning), but in general, a player can't look at two similar items and say, "This one is better." The only shorthand in-game is ilvl, which is a fair shorthand since access to content is also gated by ilvl. So now players will be encouraged to lower their ilvl, which could deny access to content, to improve performance, performance which can only be assessed by out-of-game tools like Mr. Robot? I have to laugh when I go to places like Curse and Mr. Robot and see tons of ads for WoW's competition, like Rift and GW2. "You sent me to a place where the ads tell me not to play your game, Mr. WoW Dev. Good job." I do see one potential benefit: will personal loot give out the optimum pieces and NOT the pieces that aren't optimum for your spec? If it did, that would be a huge plus: the game would be telling you what to wear, to take the external sites out of the equation.

    -ilvl of items will increase on bosses deeper in the raid.

    Good change for organized raiding. I wonder if LFR will also have this feature, given that LFR has wings that allow players to completely bypass earlier bosses, or will this simply create an even greater divide between LFR gear and organized raid gear?

    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    I must be getting more and more pessimistic as I get older. I see the loot change and figure that it can't help but make a negative change in LFR, it'll feel like there is less loot (even if mathematically its nigh equal). And for those who didn't need any gear off a particular boss it'll feel like you took some loot from someone who needed it. Next logical step is people will ask to trade gear to other players in the raid who might be able to use it.

    The wider ranges in secondary stats is a really dumb idea. And yes, its clearly an idea from a raider, from someone who is happy to immerse themselves in stats and how it affects specs etc. I used to be that way, I used to know caps and weightings for about 7 specs across 3 different classes. And I'll freely admit, over half the 'fun' in knowing all that being able to use it to shake my head at those who were 'doing it wrong'. But now, now I just cannot be bothered. If its got a higher ilevel then my characters equip it. If its equal, then sell or DE. And that's as far as I give a crap basically. I've got better things to do than worry about the 'gimp my character by using something that's not an upgrade' minigame. It reminds me so much of seeing people apply to my old guild for a raiding position and watching people laugh and savage them for their choice of stat weightings. It's almost the definition of an elitest system and really has no place below Mythic (at most - Heroic) raiding.

    If the system does end up working as Delores suggests and only sends items that fit your spec at you then I agree that would be good. But at that point you may as well just dump the second stat ranges thing altogether because it won't be significant. I'd much rather see warforged grant anywhere between say 1-5 extra ilevels, not just a flat boost. And make warforged happen more often. That would work far better at the LFR/normal raid level and be far easier for people to understand.

    News up on MMO Champ. No flying in this expansion. Probably no flying in any future expansions either.

    Originally Posted by MMO Champ summary from interview
    Flying

    The team will not be adding flying to Draenor.
    Moving forward, new expansions are also unlikely to have flying outside of specific zones or expansion ideas.
    The team will continue to improve flight paths.
    Being able to fly added convenience, but removed the ability to add things like targeted exploration of the world and made the world feel smaller and less dangerous
    Originally Posted by Blizzard forum blue post
    Thanks for creating a thread on this. As you noted, lead designer Ion Hazzikostas mentioned in a recent interview with Polygon that weve decided not to allow the use of flying mounts on Draenor. Theres been a lot of debate about the pros and cons of flying over the course of this expansionnot just in the community, but in the hallways, offices, and break rooms at Blizzardand we know its something everyone feels passionately about.

    During Patch 6.2s development, we continued to discuss how flight would fundamentally change the way players experience all of Draenors ground-based content, and carefully weighed that against the benefits flying would bring to the game. Ultimately, after much deliberation, we came to the conclusion that the world of Draenor would simply no longer provide the experience we envisioned it if we were to reintroduce flight in this expansion.

    We appreciate everyone who has taken the time to weigh in on this topic. Youve provided some great insight and feedback, and we ask that you please be respectful of one another as you continue to discuss your thoughts and experiences.

    Long talk about this decision - they touch on the "flying is not immersive" and "the world should be dangerous" arguments and debunk them:


    One thing that they don't touch on at all, and which I believe to be the prime driving factor behind this decision: 3D artist time. It's very very hard to find and then train GOOD 3D artists.
