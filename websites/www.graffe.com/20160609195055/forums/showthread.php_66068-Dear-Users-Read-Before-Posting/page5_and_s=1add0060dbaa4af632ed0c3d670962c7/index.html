    Re: Dear Users. Read Before Posting.

    Maledict,

    Tone, or "style" is important because it reflects the underlying attitude and approach to moderation that affects everyone. If this is really just an anomaly, that's great. But if it really was just an anomaly, I don't think everyone would have taken me so seriously either. The point wasn't to get the post changed, since the post itself wasn't a big deal. The point also wasn't to attack Mdar. It doesn't really matter who posted it. The point is to raise awareness, and that's it.

    I thought Mdar's post was funny but arrogant so I threw it back in his face. I got modded, whatever. So I spent 30 seconds and made a one-sentence response to replace the deleted content. I avoided complaining about the deletion and focused on my original concern. I did not attack Mdar I attacked his post. Mdar has not responded, and I have respected that decision. I have not addressed him since the initial post. Had others not come to his defense, I would not have pressed the case and the thread would have ended.

    I can respond out of proportion, if you'd like to see the difference.

    I can do open field day, if you want to see the difference.

    I can hound people, if you want to see the difference.

    My point doesn't have to stand, but if I believe in it I'm not going to back down just because people are upset. I will be respectful, honest, and straightforward, I hope it is comforting that I have not resorted to name-calling and insults and non-sequitor images of ermine sex or something, but I will only consider backing down under the following conditions: it is too upsetting for people to handle, someone has a better point, or I get bored.

    Mdar's clearly not intending to actually insult folks (unlike say, telling him to go **** himself),
    That is not clear to me. In fact, intent is a secondary concern in this case. If Mdar wanted to address my reaction, he could have done so at any point.

    but folks would rather argue the point and cry mod foul than actually get the thing changed?
    If you slap someone in the face, you can't un-slap them. Changing the post is really not the issue. It's more important that it be understood why it was a mistake than for it to change.

    You just get the impression that folsk aren't really bothered by the post, it's just another tiny point to attack and complain about. Because who on *earth* thinks this style of posting get's the change you want?
    The change I want is for as many people to understand the problem as possible. I think my approach is the most effective one I could have taken.
