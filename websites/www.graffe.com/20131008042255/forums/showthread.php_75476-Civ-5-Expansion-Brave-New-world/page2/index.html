    Re: Civ 5 Expansion: Brave New world

    Yes - its been that way in civ for the last couple of games IIRC. It's detailed over at civfanatics.

    the games *are* tweaked - both int erms of map size and turn length, but nothing gets away from the fact that slowing things down and adding more turns gives the player an advantage. That's because the more turns you have, and the lower things are, the less the AI bonuses come into play (which are raw boosts to power) and the more human bonuses come into play - the ability to execute a long term plan, the ability to micro stuff, the ability to respond appropriately to threats, the ability to play the AI off against each other etc.

    Worth noting that with Venice it's not actually one city - you will need puppets up fast I find to pump food and hammers back to Venice to make it the capitol of the world. Heck, you can even conquer the world as Venice if you want - buying all those units is tasty!

    # In Game Editor (alter the game at will in almost anyway, absolutely fantastic, often use this to fix idiot placements, like Rock of Gibraltar in middle of nowhere, or way the game may build a small inland sea that you know means the AIs will build ships on pointlessly etc so I excavate out a channel to sea, etc)
    # Reseed! (though don't actually use it much because it doesn't work with many of the new map types, my favourite being "Pangea Plus", which reseed! can't work with)
    # +1 Range Gatling/Machine Gun Unit Update (So gatling guns/machine guns get same range as a bo, like DUH!
    # Several different "wonders" like Arecibo Antenna and few others
    # Buildings - Upgrade System (it actually lets you make a new building in reality, if you have a specific one say, Granary - Upgrade adds +1 food, and similar ofr many other buildings, but at increased upkeep and building costs)
    # InfoAddict, that's great lets you see actual rankings, military total, relationships etc
    # Improvement Upgrades, lets you add a few new terrain upgrades like "advanced mines" etc late in game
    # Classical Espionage (so it starts earlier, I use it for info not tech stealing, so I'm glad they added the "diplomat" choice for spies in Brave New World. Balances out well since they all steal tech or can "shennanigan" city states or coups, it means I have to keep on my toes :P)
    # national Naval Academy (really love that one, adds an upgrade to ships)
