    Re: Apple laptop refresh

    I keep hearing people talk about build quality of Apple as being so superior, but I have had zero issues with a whole truck load of Dells. I have literally 22 D600s that I used to teach computer class to a private school of K-5 students. They where 2 years old when I got them and they all survived 5 years of abuse. I never replaced a single part on any of them and other than a single USB port that was broken by a kid jamming a pencil in it, everything works on all of them. I paid $239 each for them 5 years ago and I expect to get about $75-100 each back from them. I also have a couple of XPS laptops that are fantastic. Mine is coming up on 5 years old and still works great. Never had any issues with it and I recently added an SSD and Win7 without any issues. Now it hums along like a brand new computer. My wifes is about a year old and it's got all the fancy stuff MacBooks have, but I paid less than $700 for it.

    I can understand iPhones and iPads (well, not the Mini, that's just stupid), but it makes no sense to me to spend the money for a Mac. The internal hardware is exactly the same and there are plenty of PC manufacturers that have pretty aluminum cases and backlit keyboards. If you take care of your equipment even a little bit, you'll get years of use out of it for less than half the price in most cases. Or, better yet, you can spend about the same amount and get a supercharged hardware beast. I don't hate Apple, I just don't see the point in spending so much extra money.
