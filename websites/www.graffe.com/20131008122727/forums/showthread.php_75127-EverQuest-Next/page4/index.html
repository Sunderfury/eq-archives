    Re: EverQuest Next

    Originally Posted by FilanFyretracker
    to be fair that spell was not too bad, The issue was of course the devs had no idea the game would make it far enough for the spell to become the linchpin of every single raid to the point of having a whole team devoted to spamming it. Of course in 1999 I cannot blame them for being somewhat short sighted, Who knew at that point that EQ would lay the framework for MMOs going mainstream in western markets.

    I think one of the reasons that Kunark was held off was that they did not know if the game would even survive a year.
    Yeah, SOE and Verant were making up a lot of things as they went along. Nobody really knew how things were going to, eventually, fall into place so that's why there were things that were ridiculously out of balance. And I think that's why Verant had such a bad reputation because they tried to be overly conservative about things (and hid behind their "Vision" to do so).

    Complete Heal was an abomination but it was never intended to be chained together. The original intent of Complete Heal was to be used during downtime as an efficient way to heal the group up between pulls. They figured that a 10 second cast time would make it impractical to be used during combat. And during Vanilla and Kunark they were, mostly, right. Because of the limitations of the code at the time, bosses didn't have more than 32K hitpoints (if I remember correctly) so they compensated for this by jacking up the bosses damage. Vanilla and Kunark boss fights were VERY messy. It wasn't until Velious where they were able to break the 32K hitpoint limit and get rid of the stupid raid wide AOE damage going out. Enter the era of Tank n Spank where it was easy to focus all incoming damage onto one high hitpoint tank and keep Complete Heals staggered enough to keep tanks alive.

    I remember the Avatar of War fight in particular. The challenge was making a clean pull so you didn't lose half the raid in the first 30 seconds due to sloppy aggro. Once you got that part down the rest was staggering CHs to land every second or so on the tank while /disc defensive was active. Finally it simply came down to mana management to make sure that the Clerics could sustain the CH chain long enough to pull off the kill. I remember that we positioned the clerics in a line and the magicians would drop mod rods in front of each cleric. I also remember some of our necromancers getting VERY angry when they were told to feed mana to us (the clerics). They thought it was very degrading.

    Raids were more about logistics than strategy. Strategy, for quite awhile, simply boiled down to the spacing between Complete Heals (2 seconds or 3 seconds as an example).
    There are other worlds than these.

    Re: EverQuest Next

    One general change but not related to healing that would help fight designers in MMOs is to convince the rule makers that casters do not have to stand still. This is the number one complaint in WoW I know about high movement fights, Casters become useless. Though some of this is that WoW does not have channeling, A great stat from EQ that let you have a chance to cast anyway after movement. In a future game one could enhance on that and if you had maxed out channeling you would say only have to stop for the brief moment to launch the cast but could dodge during the cast.

    Of course the other solution is not allow movement but let casters be true glass cannons with output that outstrips anybody period.

    Of course nowdays MMOs are min/max so everybody would play casters. And of course you could not have PVP in a game where the wizard does 32,000 damage a shot at 50m.

    I think it is safe to say there is no "safe" way to build an MMO. You either have the WoW style of min/max and debuff whack-a-mole while trying not to stand in fire and manage to cast a nuke or two, Or you have the EQ style of CH the hell out of the tank while the DPS uses line of sight to avoid the biggest AEs and resists stacking for the smaller ones. Because really all things get broken down into their simple parts when one progression raids.

    I will be honest though, my only stat min/max complaint in WoW is that Enh Shaman need to have both the expertise and hit rating. Blizzard never found a way to make them need one or the other, Though like CH in EQ I am sure its merely a limitation of the code.

    Bragging time for Yttrium
    The MUD I played on hadn't defined the "hard edge" before a guild I helped found. We had a "complete heal" type spell, but no one bothered to "train" it up to a reliable level of non-fizzling. I aggressively recruited clerics for the guild and required they all max it out. We then went and fought things that could not previously be beaten, based entirely on
    - best-AC tank in the game
    - clerics could heal him from 1 hp to (max - 1d4) every single round with 99.999% successful casting rate
    - sufficient support bandwidth from other classes to keep everyone else going / energized / etc.

    Essentially, it's the same idea as the CH rotation, though I never made the mental leap to EQ's Complete Heal spell. However, when I first heard of the rotations, I had an "oh, yeah, I know this" moment.

    I doubt I was the first person to do this in a mechanical sense. I'm sure other MUDs had similar mechanics that were exploited. Given the original designers' backgrounds in MUDs, I'm surprised they didn't think this would be an issue. It became the final edge of what was do-able.

    On a personal note (hah, like I"m not already there), my experience on that MUD was the only time I was on the hardcore bleeding edge of a community-based game. I've been in a variety of powerful raiding guilds, at various levels of accomplishment, in both EQ and WoW, but I wouldn't want to do it again, much less be able to. However, I, too, would like to see some sort of meaningful EQ recreation.

    Re: EverQuest Next

    Personally, I'd like to see a different split: PvE vs. PvP servers. On PvE servers, the killing of Garrosh would bring the Horde and Alliance together, ending the war. There'd be no more world PvP, the two sides could communicate and group together, and battlegrounds would be "war games" training the next generation of soldiers to face the challenges of the Old Gods / Burning Legion / naga / Enemy of the Year. On PvP servers, same old same old, with the two sides still against one another. But I doubt that would ever happen in any MMORPG, much less WoW: that different servers would have different storylines, requiring different content. Closest to that I can think of would be releasing Kerafyrm in EQ, and that was handled very poorly.

    On faction jumping in general: yes, everyone loves to be "different" by building up rep with those who should be the enemy. I'd rather see a rep system where the player:

    (a) doesn't start out hated by anybody. Sure, some races don't get along, but that doesn't mean some nobody is going to be despised by an entire city.
    (b) knows up-front what the results of any particular actions will be with respect to faction. No "oops, killing that guy destroyed your rep with a completely unrelated group". I also don't think low-level quests and mobs should give any rep hits - it should all be positive until the player has enough experience with the game world to make informed choices about who he likes and hates.
    (c) isn't put in the situation of having to raise a rep to get cool_thing01. Reps should be about shaping how your character sees the world, not about who gives the phattest lewt. Note that I have no problem with requiring SOMEONE to like the character for the character to participate in story lines. Something like "to get the quest to be sent to the new continent, you must talk to a faction leader with whom you are Honored or higher" works for me. "You must be Warmly to High Elves to get the quest for the unique illusion mask" does not.

    Sadly, I think (c) gives most people who care about reps problems. Those folks want reps to have real differences, consequences to picking one over another, and having the consequence be that you're searching for artifacts for the Explorer's League instead of for the Asura (but you're still looking in the same place, for the same thing, for the same reward) isn't enough.
