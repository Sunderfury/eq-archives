    Re: Solo Things

    A lot of the old raids have been soloable since Cataclysm. I was soloing MC even before Firelands was out. There was a massive stat inflation from level 80 to 85 which made things much easier.

    Back during Cataclysm, the only tough boss (for me) was Golemagg. Even at 85 he hit hard and his two dogs made things a lot worse. I got the hang of the fight though and it became a "Guitar Hero" style fight for me. And by that I mean I had to juggle my cooldowns and abilities perfectly and I'd win. Sethekk Halls at level 80 felt the same way when I was farming for the Raven mount. My mage is still only level 85 but is in full Firelands Heroic and a smattering of Dragon Soul LFR and Normal gear and I was able to faceroll Molten Core. Nothing was even slightly challenging.

    One thing I do know about soloing Cataclysm raids was that certain classes and specs were farming Alysrazor solo for a chance at the mount. I actually won the mount when my raid group was clearing Firelands when it was current content and I know how rare the thing is. I actually felt kind of bad quitting the game after winning the mount because we never saw another one drop again and I only knew of 1 other person on my server with it. I doubt it's faceroll easy, but at level 90 I'm sure a lot more classes and specs can farm him for a chance at the mount today.
    There are other worlds than these.

    Re: Solo Things

    Solo cleared BWL over the weekend as ret.

    Razorgore: Is cake, with one tip and one caveat:
    Tip - break possession early (around 30-45 seconds remaining) to help drag the adds to yourself, and if necessary, re-possess him, run him to the other side of the room, and re-break it again to kill adds.
    Caveat - as my paladin, I healed him with instant WoG and shielded him with Sacred Shield. It helped a lot.

    Vaelastrasz: Cake, but yet another boss with a retarded instant-death mechanic.

    Broodlord: Holy CRAP is the room before him super-shitty as a non-rogue. Ended up dragging almost every mob in the room to his door, planting my back against it, and going to town.

    Firemaw: I tried to be slick and take all of the mobs between him and the top of the ramps, until I found out after dying a quick and painful death that he does a Flame Buffet and makes all fire damage bigger. So those 30+ goblin grenadiers were hitting me for 2-3k fire. Very fast. Ouch. But he's really cake. As long as you don't do that too.

    Ebonroc/Flamegor: Cake separately. (probably together too, but they pulled separately) One of them heals itself, but it's easily DPS'ed through.

    Chromaggus: As a paladin, Cleansing the poison and disease afflictions was a good idea, and I had almost 100 Hourglass Sand to go through (only used 2).

    Nefarian: There was a boss? No, seriously, he was cake. Took all his adds by a pillar, and when they respawned they all died with one HoR.

    Walked out with Chrominius battle pet, Chromatically Tempered Sword, and Ashkandi, Judgement bp and legs

    Re: Solo Things

    Black Temple (ret pal)

    Najentus: Tank and spank really, but with the added bonus of having to re-dps him when he bubble-heals.

    Supremus: Tank and spank

    Shade of Akama: Kill the little adds, then kill him.

    Teron: Tank and spank

    Gurtogg: This one was a DPS race until I decided I didn't want to screw with his armor debuff, and when it hit 50 stacks, I bubbled and splattered him.

    Reliquary:
    First phase - cake
    Second phase - OMG annoying. There's this aura the Essence of Desire does that reduces your mana periodically. Actually, it reduces your MAXIMUM mana, so by the time I got it to 300khp, I had zero mana, and what I would regen with Seal of Insight wasn't enough to cast a damn thing.
    So I ended up spending over 15 minutes to damage through the last ~240khp - took three Guardians.
    If I hadn't had Insight up when my mana vanished I wouldn't have one-shotted it.
    Third phase - cake

    Mother Shahraz: Tank and spank cake, though her debuffs were a little annoying.

    Council: They ganked me. Got them to 400khp (I don't know why that is my magical number) before the healed to full and splattered me.
