    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    On the long post: I have problems with some of the things he says.

    Tradeskills: there's a world of difference between "use" and "best in slot". Current tradeskills give you, as a casual, three best-in-slot items, in any slot you desire (including the essential weapon slots). With the upgrade system, these will be a disorganized player's BiS all expansion. In MoP, the expansion he cites, you could craft a trinket that was obsoleted by LFR gear early on, a weapon that was obsoleted by SoO LFR, and belt/legs that were good all expansion - after the patch that introduced them. Cataclysm was belt/chest, with weapons added later and goggles for engineers - and the belt/chest didn't upgrade. Wrath had limited slots, they didn't upgrade. And so on. I get what the author is trying to say: it feels wrong to make something and not be able to wear it. I think there would have been value in making the base 640 piece not be worn(3), although that could have given rise to confusion and wasted mats when players go to upgrade piece #4. But the point remains: this system is actually the best system they've ever had for disorganized players in terms of the end results.

    Pruning of enchants and gem slots: that helps raiders, but I feel like it helps disorganized players more. Progression raiders have more sources of gold for guild improvement, like BoE epics, and inevitably have access to all professions through guild members. Disorganized players are more likely to lack funds and/or access to crafters. They also get dumped on in LFR if anything goes wrong and it's discovered that they lack gems/enchants - I've seen that happen a LOT in MoP. If I have a problem with gems/enchants, it's that they were put on the daily cooldown system, which has inflated their value out of all proportion to their value.

    His strongest point is the simplest: take a look at the endgame for a disorganized player, and look at how it's impacted by the changes that have been made this expansion. In most cases, it's been impacted negatively. World travel is crap, level 100 questing is garbage, non-LFR item upgrades require massive time investment for the same basic power increase one could have gotten under the valor system (time investment is pretty large for the crafted stuff, but you can spend your way around that), the reps are all massive grinds that award little more than the rep. There's not much to do, and what is there isn't worth the time it takes to do it, and that's entirely due to changes made that made all these things take longer.

    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    The chore post is really, really unfortunate. The poster absolutely nails stamina and "forced to do chores", in a way that makes it obvious to everyone without pointing fingers at anyone. He also has some good suggestions on structure, although at this point we'll see structure in Tanaan, not implemented in current WoD content. But then he goes off the rails with the chores suggestions, and that is the last impression his post leaves.

    Changing the mine and garden: he's completely ignoring how essential the mine and garden are to current professions, with the emphasis on supplying materials on a daily basis (cooldown and work orders). Nobody has excess mats, because excess mats can be converted to Primal Spirits and from there to Savage Bloods for use/sale. Yes, this sucks for gathering profs. They get their revenge in the next patch, when you either have gathering, you fish, or you pay through the nose to upgrade your tradeskill items. I've given my "follower does the gathering" idea often enough here, no need to repeat it. The "inactive follower in buildings" is a fair idea, but it doesn't work with the way work orders can now give XP unless you're OK with being able to level an inactive follower (and it feels wrong, to me at least).

    Work order frequency: he gets the right idea - checking less often - and comes to the wrong conclusion - reduced order frequency. The correct conclusion would be "you need to check less often", which means being able to set up more work orders than currently. Dawlin's said it before: make it so a full queue can churn stuff out for a week before needing to be restocked. It's close now, but only with a bank. The idea of trying to reward players for not checking is bad, period. The whole idea is to make it so players don't need to schedule their lives around the game. Creating an optimal time for removing work orders does the exact opposite of that: it creates a time when the player feels obligated to log on and reset work orders.

    Long follower missions: Macfudd said it before I could. I'd rather see follower missions work like daily quests: you give them a mission, it takes until the daily reset that day for the mission to end. You could set it up at any time during the day, and it doesn't end until that reset. That way the player doesn't have to check missions constantly: you do it once per day, at a time convenient for you, and you're done. You'd have to fiddle around with XP rewards and/or XP to level to account for the reduced frequency of missions, especially the low level missions, but it would make for a far better experience for players.
