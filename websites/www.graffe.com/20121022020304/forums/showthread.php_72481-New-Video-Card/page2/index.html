    Re: New Video Card

    The biggest concern I see is that he tried to put his old video card back in and got the same problem. It makes me think something happened to the other components in the computer during the video card install. Do you see anything that suggests the computer is trying to boot with no display, such as hard disk drive activity?

    Keep the old video card installed and reseat your memory, remove any other cards that are plugged into the motherboard and reseat all of the power supply plugs on the motherboard. If that doesn't work then reseat your CPU. If none of this works then the first unit I would replace is the power supply, and then see if there's any difference in behavior. At this point if it doesn't work, then I would start to think the motherboard was damaged.

    Determine how your board reports problems - most commonly it will be a series of beeps and in this case make sure that there is a PC speaker hooked up to the motherboard speaker output (if you are hearing no beeps then there isn't a speaker hooked up). More advanced motherboards will report errors on a digit display or in an arrangement of LEDs, with the different combinations of lights signifying the error code to lookup in your manual.
