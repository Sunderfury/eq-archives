    Re: Destiny Impressions - Things you may want to know

    My warlock is 24 or so at the moment. Have had zero luck with any of the level 20+ strikes. I'll probably level up a hunter before I get sick of the game and find something else to play.

    PvP is so hit and miss with me. Sometimes I have really good matches, but most of the time I am either putting up with excessive amounts of cheese, or people who clearly have better weapons than I do. I know damage is normalized, but some of the things people do are ridiculous.

    And that's ignoring some of the more annoying deviations of player behavior that have popped up. That moon map always seems to devolve into 4 people on each team doing nothing but sniping. They aren't hard to dislodge, but it drags the flow of the game to a screeching halt and is incredibly boring.


    Originally Posted by Silke
    I just finished the story and hit level 20. What should I be doing next?
    Short Answer: hard mode strikes and pvp. Supplement both with bounties.

    Long Answer: At 20 you will start to get level 18 blue gear with the "light" stat. As you accumulate light, your level grows above 20. Once you hit 22, you will start to get level 20 blue gear with the light stat. I think this takes you to 24 or so. While all this is happening, the activities you should be doing should be giving you either crucible or vanguard marks, while the bounties give you crucible and vanguard reputation. Once you have accumulated enough you can buy purple gear from their vendors (and you may also get some as random drops along the way). The max light level without doing the raid is 29.

    The cap on marks is 100 a week, but you need somewhere near 400 marks for a set, so that will take four weeks unless you mix pvp and pve, or get lucky on drops. You also start getting motes of light and strange coins, which can also be used to upgrade or buy exotic gear (strange coins can only be used on friday and saturday from a special merchant). There are also exotic bounties that you will get randomly which are very hard but give you a unique weapon when you are done. One requires you beat an optional boss in a level 26 heroic version of a strike.

    The end goal of all this is to allow you to play harder and harder versions of the strikes. But half of this stuff locks you out if you don't have a pre-made group, and the other half seems to be plagued with leavers and afkers (I haven't finished a single one). Sort of seems like you need an established group of friends to get the most out of high end Destiny.

    Now, you may recoil at the idea of pvp, but crucible marks seem to be pretty easy to come by. You get 2 for losing a match and 3 for winning, so it doesn't matter how good you are. Supposedly farming vanguard marks is easy if you just do the earth public event rotation, but I haven't tried that yet.

    Also, if you want to be a cheeseball, there is a cave on earth that people are abusing to farm gear with no effort. Basically the outdoor mobs spawn based on line of sight, but there is one spot where you can stand and they will spawn anyway. People just stand there and shoot into the cave for hours to collect engrams and hope one ends up being a legendary.

    One last thing, Bungie is attempting to support the game by having timed events that give you a reason to log in when you otherwise might not. The strange coin vendor is part of that, they have also done special pvp modes only available on the weekend. This week they are doing special bounties that tie into the Queen of the Awoken.

    The Ogres and Harpies are all Hive, which are the pre-Collapse ex-Guardians the Traveler used for their Army. AKA "The Darkness."

    It's important to note though, that both "evil" and "good" or "The Darkness" and "The Traveler" have to stop the Vex. If you manage to seriously thwart the Vex, they will just send some Vex back in time to a point where they can unwrite you from reality. Kabr was a Titan who managed to get into the Vault of Glass and kill a Gorgon. The Gorgons are their quantum super computers that can analyze any threat and dedicate the Vex to eliminate it. Kabr took the shell of the leftover Gorgon and his own Light (the only thing keeping him alive) and created the Relic. The Relic is basically another AI that is focused on making the Vault of Glass possible for a future attack.
