    Re: Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.

    Okay, I'm really getting sick of out-of-linear-temporal-sequence vignettes in film/tv.

    At least this episode didn't pull the subtitled "we must display the year and location" bullsnot that's been constant for a few years, but I really don't think any semi-intelligent audience needs a "thrilling" piece of ornamentation hanging off of the already-existing plot and characterization in order to keep them interested as towards what "happens next".

    I can understand that character-driven stories work from perspectives, but this style of scene timing is gone from beyond annoying to near-complete acceptance across multiple formats and I feel it really just serves as a distraction technique and detracts from the story as a whole.

    I enjoy a good flashback, but they have a place and a time.

    The presentation is not a 1/4" box nail and the flashback needs to be used less as a nailgun into the drywall of the presentation and more like the 8oz tack hammer that makes everything flush without dimpling the gypsum.

    Is this just something that became possible as digital filming got so cheap, or do most people who are not me just really enjoy disjointed scenes?
