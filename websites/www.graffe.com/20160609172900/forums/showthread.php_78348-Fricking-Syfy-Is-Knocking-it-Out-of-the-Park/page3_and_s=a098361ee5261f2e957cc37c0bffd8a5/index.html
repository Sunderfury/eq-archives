    Re: Fricking Syfy Is Knocking it Out of the Park

    Originally Posted by Delores Mulva
    They're going to Fillory from here! It's interesting reading comments on other review boards from folks who have read the books. Apparently there's a huge number of changes in both characters and pacing - they're burning through plot quickly.
    They've stuck to the overarching themes of the books, but the TV show runners are largely setting the plot on fire and creating their own. I enjoyed the books a lot, but the changes work very well for TV, and I commend them for it. Even as they rip through much of the original storyline quickly, it's easy to see how their deviations can stretch the TV show out to run at least three seasons and probably more; some stuff (the characters of Eliot and Penny) might grow tiresome, but character evolution (which should happen in any series over time anyway) can take of that.

    And yeah, the TV series feels way darker than the books, which have a slightly detached feel compared to truly dark fantasies such as Covenant books or ASoIF. Probably the right move for this medium.
