    Re: Legion

    Ok, one thing just got announced that is pure joy and light. Two words: gnome hunters!

    The rogue blog is done: I like what they've done to Assassination, overall. Very low-key changes, adding Garrote as a combo-builder outside of stealth breaks the tedium of "Mutilate Mutilate Mutilate", they have a clear theme. I don't like that Dispatch seems to be gone, and they haven't talked about how poisons are applied to weapons (do you still have choices or is it "your blades are poisoned" - guessing the latter given that every spec has been given a snare ability), but the core gameplay is still there and I do like that Garrote + new Venomous Wounds will speed up ability use fairly significantly.

    Outlaw sounds silly. Proc'ed point blank pistol shots? ONE opener from stealth? And going back to having to use combo points to keep Slice and Dice up (which presumably means that the other specs have lost it for good)? Very lame, especially combined with poisons being gone. I can see why they held the rogue blog until the end - a lot of those changes are pretty controversial, all in the name of making the specs feel "different".

    Now that all the blogs are out, MMO-Champ is starting to do round-up Twitter stuff for the classes:

    http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/...adiator-Stance

    Things that stick out to me:

    -Marks can get a pet that can tank by getting a specific talent that can proc an UNDEAD minion? I get the whole Sylvanas thing, but is this the sort of pet, oh, say, a dwarf hunter should have? Is this a solution to being petless for questing? Ugh.
    -A LOT of questions about Mistweavers. You can see some of the holes in the spec from the answers. You can feel the "this is still up in the air" aspect of it. The Mastery being a flat amount of healing instead of a percentage - that will require constant re-tuning, UGH.
    -Heh, they consider Light of the Martyr to be a core part of the Holy Paladin's rotation, to allow them to maintain proximity to the target(s) (for their Mastery). What an awful ability, causing damage to the paladin right as they have to move, presumably because something that does AE damage is making them move.
    -It's too bad Disc will lose Holy Nova, since it was nice for questing and clearing grey mobs. R.I.P. Circle of Healing - being able to do some AE healing on the move was very useful. It's nice that Weakened Soul is finally gone, but too bad that it was done as PW:S got a 6 s. CD.
    -One of the rogue questions makes me wonder why the Assassination combo point building bleed wasn't Hemo instead of Garrote.
    -The Enhancement "reliable AE" spell sounds good. A little too much like how Death and Decay triggers AE Scourge Strikes, what with Crash Lightning triggering AE Storm Strikes / Lava Lashes, but still, pressing one button and having things take AE damage will be a nice change for Enhancement. And they did leave in a tiny amount of utility with Healing Surge and Lightning Bolt, although no idea if LB will be castable on demand or not to pull (I assume casting it in combat would be a huge DPS loss).
    -"Mastering when to cast Demonic Empowerment will be a key part of mastering Demonology gameplay." Exciting like watching paint dry. So fucked up.
    -The clarification on Ignore Pain's mechanics is huge. Now I understand why they say warriors will always be keeping it up, because they will. I'm not sure I like warriors alternating between a 90% damage reduction state and a full damage state, as a healer. If this is what they're doing to the spec to allow them to remove more avoidance (and I imagine it is), colour me unimpressed.

    Re: Legion

    Originally Posted by Dru
    Playing GW2 makes you realize a lot of MMOs just have way too many abilities to be fun.
    Did you play during WoD? The devs already made a serious pruning pass, to the point that my hunter could fit all her "standard combat" abilities on one 12-slot action bar, including talent abilities. They are now removing even more abilities. Some abilities are getting replacements, like Steady Shot being replaced by Arcane Shot. But some, like Chimera, are not, and of course everything to do with a pet is also gone. The current Marks "rotation" sounds lame:

    Your Arcane Shot has a chance to put up Hunter's Mark for six seconds. When Hunter's Mark is up, you're allowed to Marked Shot, which puts up a debuff that increases Aimed damage (stacks three times) and which fades in ten seconds. So being Marks is a button-mashing extravaganza where you're carefully monitoring not one, but two debuffs on the target, one of which is a random proc and is required to keep up the second one, the loss of which would be a fair DPS loss and which only lasts for ten seconds.

    The old rotation: if target is above 90% health, Aimed Shot, using Steady to get back focus. If the target is below 90% health but above the point at which Kill Shot lights up, do Chimera/Barrage if able, if not do Aimed (pooling enough focus to do Chimera/Barrage ASAP when they come off CD), using Steady to regain focus. If Kill Shot lights up, you hit it before any other button (it activates below 45% health iirc, has a CD). Completely a priority-based system, no debuffs to monitor at all.

    From some of the developer comments, it sounds like some of the current Survival gameplay is being migrated over to Marks in Legion. I don't know if Survival has this sort of debuff micromanagement aspect to its gameplay - I never touched the spec. But with Survival becoming the melee spec, and BM being fairly similar to how it is now, that means that the Marks gameplay currently in game is disappearing entirely. The same thing is happening with specs like Demonology warlock - everything about the current gameplay is being eliminated for a new style. Maybe those new styles will be fun, but it doesn't sound like they will be. And if your core rotation isn't fun, and you've been stripped of even more abilities than were removed in the last expansion, what's left to enjoy?

    Gnome hunters will be fun, though, if only for the joy of feigning over and over to get the hilarious gnomish death animation/sound.
