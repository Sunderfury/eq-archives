    Re: Destiny Impressions - Things you may want to know

    Okay, so here are my impressions on The Dark Below. To recap, three new story missions, three new multiplayer maps, two new strikes, and one new raid. There is a new npc that offers unique bounties and missions for the expansion, and there are new events as a part of these bounties.

    I don't really like the new single player missions. The first two are very short, and don't really offer anything that new. The third one was longer than both of these combined, but I thought it was pretty dull. The second one is probably the only one I would care to replay, but I found the level 30 version to be too difficult for me when I tried it. (I'll get to why that matters later) There are hardly any new environments to the levels. What is new is probably small enough to fit inside the Tower hub. Honestly the single player missions feel like they exist to teach you the mechanics of the raid, as that was a criticism of the Vault of Glass. On the bright side, I am not sick of these like I am all the other story missions I've played infinity times by now. Three aren't going to go very far in fixing that though.

    I thought the new strike was really fun. I ended up having to play it twice for the bounties and enjoyed it both times (gets really hard at the end though). The playstation exclusive strike, on the other hand, was boring. I sort of hated it and am glad it won't be in any rotations. The boss was okay I guess. It was basically just a more manageable version of the Nexus boss with easier to predict adds. It also has zero connection to the Dark Below storyline, and feels like it probably should have been included in the base game.

    Most of the new bounties are just a part of the new faction grind, or a way to drag out the extremely short lived story missions (you have to return to the npc to unlock each one after you beat the previous one), but there are a few longer ones that behave similarly to the exotic weapons bounties. I sort of find these to be enjoyable, even though the reward is seldom worth the effort involved. The story quest chain will reward you with a pretty decent fusion rifle, while the one you unlock afterwards gives you a set of gloves equivalent to what you will find off of merchants now. The latter is pretty disappointing, as if you are anything like me, you will end up buying new gloves long before finishing this quest.

    I have not tried the multiplayer maps. I got pretty fed up with multiplayer before I stopped playing, but was considering trying them out just to see what they were like until I got distracted by the raid.

    I'm expecting mixed feelings on the raid. It looks pretty fun to me, but it also seems much simpler and shorter than the Vault of Glass. I did not care for the mechanics and gimmicks of VoG, but it required a level of teamwork that made it challenging (and rewarding?) for those that did like it. The mechanics of this raid are much simpler. In fact, someone more hardcore than of us will ever be (the same guy that did VoG with only one other person) has managed to solo it. It's still more interesting than anything you will see anywhere else in the game though.

    For my part, I sort of regret ignoring this DLC the first week, as apparently not only does Bungie let you enter the raid alone, you can very easily get to the first loot chest on your own. I, sadly, only got ascendant materials on all three characters, and am expecting a nerf that will ensure that's all this chest ever drops. I also managed to get past the first challenge by abusing the hell out of invisibility on my hunter. I got a raid hunter chest piece for my troubles, which is slightly disappointing as it isn't my "main", and I haven't been able to duplicate the feat without invisibility, but still fairly amusing to have gotten it. I might could at 30, but by then I bet this will be changed too.


    Which brings me to my next point, which I already sort of complained about with exotics, but every other new feature in this expansion DLC has made me feel the same way. It probably takes an hour, tops, to beat the new story levels. The new bounties and faction are a new (but expected) grind, but there are less of them so it will take longer. In addition to the exotic change, you now need "commendations" to buy stuff. All the vendor gear has been changed, and you need to earn it over again to increase your light level. You previously needed to be level 28 to do the dailies and weeklies and get a decent reward, you now need to be 30. But after playing this for a week I'm still level 28. They had already made it harder to solo strikes (locking me out of nightfall), but harder strikes + higher levels means it is harder to get the faction, marks, and strange coins you need for upgrades now.

    What I'm getting at with all this: now more than ever, the game feels like a relentless grind attempting to cover up a lack of content. I have been unable to explain how this feels to anyone who doesn't play Destiny. It isn't just a new carrot on the treadmill, I feel like my legs have been chopped off before being told to do the same exact thing I was doing before. I'm not getting new gear that lets me explore new content. I'm doing the same content, at a handicap, just in an attempt to break even, let alone progress. My hunter is my only character that has done this, and that was through cheesing. It's not like I would expect a group of 28s to be able to do the raid normally (the mobs there are level 31, and there are a lot of them).


    In conclusion, I like the strike but think the vast majority of development time went into creating the raid, which looks pretty good. I don't think the DLC as a whole is a good value, but if you want to keep playing you will probably need to get it regardless. I do not know if the multiplayer maps add any value, but I sort of feel like pvp is not the reason why you play Destiny, but something you do because you are already playing it.

    The raid looks very nice, and it seems to have a ton of work put into it, but I have a hard time thinking this deserves to be called an "expansion". Not sure how much work it will take to get to light level 30, or if I will bother.
