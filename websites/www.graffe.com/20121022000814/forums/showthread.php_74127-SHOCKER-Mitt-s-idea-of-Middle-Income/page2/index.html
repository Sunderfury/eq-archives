    Are we a diamond or a pyramid? Does the middle class still encompass the bulk of people or are most of us poor now?     My wife and I bring in under $100k per year combined (including my additional Schedule C income). We aren't top 10% but we are in the top 15-25%. $100k is in the top 25%. That is scary. We are definitely middle class, but not even what I would consider upper-middle class. That doesn't make sense to me though. If we are top 25% doesn't that make me rich? My wife and I are the poorest household at her (small) office. In my department of 8 I am in the lower half (6th or 7th) but for our office I am probably in the top half (but not by much). I worry about how to pay for stuff when stuff breaks (car, dental, house, whatever) and stress about money in general. The only reason we have the savings that we do is because I am damned good at money management. How do I reconcile my worry with the knowledge that there are more households with much less than me than there are above me?

    It is disrespectful and shows a complete lack of understanding to think $200-250k is anything close to average. Rich and well off doesn't mean you can not work and sit on your ass all day. When the thought of a car breaking down or the A/C in the house going up doesn't give you pause though it is probably a sign that you are above middle class (upper middle class at a minimum).

    Re: SHOCKER, Mitt's idea of Middle Income

    Sadly Lower used to be what will I do if I loose my job...now that is the middle. A lot of what used to define the Middle used to deal with total net worth in relation to debt and income. Supply side economics created a debt based consumer society where even those making $250K can face disaster if they become unemployed. The safety net pays less than poverty to many, and to higher income earners it would be a disaster. Unemployment at the highest rate in my state is ~38% of what I get paid when I'm working. I can't imagine what would happen to someone living on a $250K lifestyle. There seem to be plateaus of safety that people reach in incomes, the key then is to figure out how to jump from plateau to plateau, because the in-betweens are filled with insecurity.
