    Solo Things

    Gratz on all of those
    The bear run has a couple of tricks to it:
    Nalorakk, the bear boss, will charge the person farthest from him for a decent amount of damage, and put a debuff on him that increases damage taken by the next charge by 100%.
    Since you'll be the only one there, you WILL get the debuff, and you will get charged for a LOT of damage the subsequent times.

    When you're aware of this, you can CD out of it - but if you mistime your defensive cool down, you will be splattered.
    Another part is the layout of the dungeon - you have to know the route pretty well, and know what trash can do annoying stuff (summon adds etc.) - and what trash you can skip.

    But it's a lot of fun

    Re: Solo Things

    Has anybody here done the Winterspring Cub quest yet? You get the only unarmored Nightsaber in the game to ride for that one, and it can be soloed by doing 20 simple dailies. You go to the Nighsaber Rock in the middle north end of Winterspring, and a guy standing a little ways off from the cave offers the quest. You bring him a sack of kittens, and he gives you one to raise for the next 20 days. After you've satisfied your cub's needs for the next 20 days, you bring the whiskers he's shed, plus a few items that are really common in the AH and cost less than a hundred gold total (20 Runecloths and 20 Heavy Leathers), and he gives you the reins of a Winterspring Nightsaber, a big cat mount that looks like the Darnassian ones except no armor and he's white and fluffy.

    Oh, and I think Horde can do this one, and get a similarly-unarmored Ravasaur to ride. Not sure about where that quest NPC stands though.
    Alikat is more right than anyone who has ever been right in the history of correctness.-Istari
