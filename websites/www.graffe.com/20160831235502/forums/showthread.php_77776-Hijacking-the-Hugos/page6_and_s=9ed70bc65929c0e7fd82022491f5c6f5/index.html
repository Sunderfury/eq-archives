    Re: Hijacking the Hugos?

    Ok I lied:

    to the Puppies for trying to control the process. The whole Sad Puppies thing is the very definition of "politically motivated". If you claim it's no longer a left-right thing, sure, ok. But trying to change the bylaws of an organization? That's politics, and that's been the goal of the Sad Puppies since day one.
    More accurately: trying to influence a process that is already political (something GRRM admits in his history of the hugos post). The fact that they managed to dominate a few categories this year was apparently an accident. The most obvious evidence supporting this is that they did not even have 5 entries in many (most?) categories. Next evidence is that the puppy voters did not vote strictly in a bloc fashion and indeed were encouraged to vote however they wanted to vote.

    Incidentally, controlling the process was what Vox Day originally wanted to do before the Sad Puppies told him to fuck off: flood every category with nominations most likely to cause PCfucks to flip their shit and vote No Award. (Which is what essentially happened anyway in 5 categories).

    Also, I never claimed that changes to the bylaws was the plan from day one. As you can easily read for yourself, the plan from day one was only to get a conservative writer (who hadn't sucked up to the existing cliques) on the ballot for a writing award, to see what would happen. He apparently accomplished this by making a few posts on his blog and encouraging his readers to participate in Worldcon. So far as I understand he violated no rules while doing this, and according to Correia the only difference between what he was doing and what everyone else had been doing for ages is that he was just a little more open about it. Basically he saw a problem and wanted to expose it. Changes to the bylaws, changes in attitude or culture, any of that would have been putting the cart before the horse. Indeed, if you read through Larry's responses you GRRM, you'll see that in his view the puppies have already accomplished a number of things, including getting people like him to admit that the Hugos are a Worldcon award not an "all of science fiction" award.

    What is clear, though, is that the Sad Puppies started by Larry in 2013 that became Sad Puppies 3 of 2015 is something that has grown gradually and organically over the course of the last 3 years.

    Re: Hijacking the Hugos?

    Eric Flint's latest related blog post: THE DIVERGENCE BETWEEN POPULARITY AND AWARDS IN FANTASY AND SCIENCE FICTION. My favorite bit:

    Compare Pratchett’s immense popularity, a career that spanned four decades, a knighthood and honorary doctorates from ten—count ‘em, ten—universities to the awards he got from the F&SF community. Those came to the following:

    Hugo: two nominations, one declined, no wins.
    Nebula: two nominations, no wins.
    WFA: one nomination, no wins.

    Yes, yes, there was also the Word Fantasy Award for Lifetime Achievement, but I am no more impressed by that than he was. They might as well have just called it the Oops, We Really Goofed Award.
    Five nominations, total, from the three major F&SF awards, with no award actually given to him—and ten honorary doctorates and a knighthood. That, in a nutshell, captures the problem with the awards in the modern era. Even academicians and the queen of England have a better grasp of what really matters than it seems the people who vote for awards do.
    Also, I found a write-up of the weird split point proposal, on this year's Worldcon site no less: E Pluribus Hugo FAQ. There's even a PowerPoint/PDF presentation.
