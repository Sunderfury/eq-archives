    Did we get any reports on IOC corruption after the Mossack Fonseca thing?

    It hasn't made news here in the states in ages, but it's long-past time to stop scheduling olympic events in third world countries or countries that can't support the glut.

    The IOC needs to be taken to task and only use cities with massive EXISTING and USABLE facilities and the infrastructure to support the influx of spectators.

    I haven't been glued to the TV watching any event I could get my hands on since 1996 for summer and I barely watched Nagano for winter.

    Mind you, I've tried, but the air times are almost impossible now and subject to whichever american network gets the contract. They decide what airs and what doesn't and you're screwed if you want to see something else.

    It's a racket and they need to move to a model that involves FREE streaming online of all events LIVE (with perhaps paid watching later) and vary ads or price based on the most popular viewership.

    It'll never happen, and I'll happily pay for content I want to see, but if I get railroaded into watching shitshows of stuff I don't care about in a bad venue?

    There's a reason fewer people in the US care about the Olympics.
