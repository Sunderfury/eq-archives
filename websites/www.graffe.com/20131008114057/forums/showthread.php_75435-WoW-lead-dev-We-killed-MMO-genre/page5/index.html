    Re: WoW lead dev: We killed MMO genre

    My guild didn't like boxing Clerics. We preferred live bodies behind our complete heal chains. And our cleric crew was pretty tight.

    I know that lots of guilds used different methods for their CH chains with lots of complex (at the time) macros saying who should go next and using pauses in them. Our guild kept it really simple. Each Cleric was assigned a number and we were told to space them out 3 seconds apart. So if I was #3 I would wait until the second Cleric started casting and then I'd do the "one one thousand, two one thousand, three one thousand" thing and hit my macro which would spit out something like:

    #3 #3 #3 Casting Complete Heal #3 #3 #3

    We kept our messages uniform as well so as to avoid confusion. This worked well for us because pauses in the macro could mess things up and adding "So and so is next" could get confusing if they weren't available to be next (due to mana issues for example when they needed to sit out a cycle to regen mana).

    I didn't mind the CH chain. I felt useful to raids. What I hated was the absurdly long setup times. It seemed that we would stare at a boss for an hour waiting for the stars to align before it was finally pulled.
    There are other worlds than these.

    Re: WoW lead dev: We killed MMO genre

    What defines hard in a game will always be subjective. Everquest felt harder because in some aspects the technology of the time made it more work to plan and execute raids, And lots of EQ raiding was endurance I remember more than a few PoFear late nighters. WoW is more like advanced Whack-a-Mole... You have to hit your priority system but also worry about the raid event effects. EQ was more an execution in zerg herding and WoW is more about player reaction time. I mean lets face it as a wizard you could play EQ raiding and watch TV while keeping an eye on the text box for orders from the leader or emotes from the boss. Another factor that made EQ feel more challenging was that voice chat was non-existent when EQ was big this meant everybody had to communicate raid important stuff by typing not just hitting a mouse button to key up Ventrilo.


    Originally Posted by Maledict
    Indeed - when Conquest threw a fit of pique and published their Molten Core strats because Blizzard refused to kow-tow to the top end guilds who cheated to get kills they changed the nature of raiding forever. They certainly didn't intend it at the time - they thought they were punishing Blizzard by publishing the strats.

    Of course in reality what they did was allow for thousands of people who wouldn't have been able to raid to get into the raid game. That fundamental change from EQ broadened the scope of raiding far beyond what it was in EQ in a single stroke and cement WoWs position as the market leader.

    If conquest hadn't done what they did, and the same culture of secrecy surrounded every bit of raid strategy, WoW wouldn't have had as many subscribers as it did. Don't get me wrong - it would still have had millions upon millions, but opening up raiding like that allowed a lot of people to stick around and keep playing when previously they would have been locked out of the endgame.
    I think in the long term though strats would be leaked like they are today in WoW simply because WoW has no competition to worry about in raiding. Due to instancing nobody can steal your raid, This was a main reason to keep highly effective strats locked up tight so that any guilds trying to follow your coat tails would have to burn time learning it on their own.
