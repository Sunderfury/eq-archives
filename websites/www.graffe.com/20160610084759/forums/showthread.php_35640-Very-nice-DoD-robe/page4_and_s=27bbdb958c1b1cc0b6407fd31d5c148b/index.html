    Well, in the interest of fairness I do believe that the difficulty scaling on missions could use some serious study and adjustment. I think that certain factors should be tested before an instance is spawned: Right now it seems to only check the level of the group members, but it should check how many AA's (average) across the whole group, and what is the greatest deviation in number of AA's. What class is the group member with the most hitpoints, Warrior, Paladin, Shadowknight, or Ranger? How many healing classes? Is a slowing class present?

    If the Missions scaled based on the group's actual makeup via a formula, rather than just checking for the level of the character requesting the mission, then the Normal and Hard difficulty ratings would actually be Normal or Hard no matter what group went in, as long as they had tanks and healers and could output DPS. That would place the entire Mission system into The Skill Zone, where player skill was what made the difference between success and failure.

    However, they would then have to scale the rewards too. A group that would consistently fail today's missions hasn't done as much character development as a group that consistently wins today's missions, and the extra prep work should garner a better reward, plus the rewards have to scale or else there's little incentive for the higher-tiered players to do them at all if we're talking about stuff with 100 HP/Mana or something like that. So instead of just popping a new neck, mask, shoulders, belt, spell scroll, or whatever onto your cursor upon completion, a point system is the only way to go. DoN was more sensible than DoDh in that respect, except the points should not be tradeable.

    Frankly, you should be able to take a similarly-equipped level 70 group into a DoDh spell mission and come out with points you can apply towards purchasing your spells if the group's player skills are reasonably decent. And certain items could be unlocked for purchase by completing certain missions, to prevent someone just farming one mission. An Anguish-geared group with loads of AA's should get the max points per win, and the average "bazaar-geared" group with fewer AA's should get fewer crystals per win, perhaps half the maximum.
    Alikat is more right than anyone who has ever been right in the history of correctness.-Istari
