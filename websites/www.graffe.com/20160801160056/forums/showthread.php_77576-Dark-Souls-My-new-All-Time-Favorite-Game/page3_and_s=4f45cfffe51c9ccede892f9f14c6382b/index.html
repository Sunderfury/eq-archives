It's possible it means clicking the left stick, but it might also mean that once you start running you will continue running and can take off pressure long enough to tap a direction.
    Jumping is a little tricky but not too bad. Tapping the run button (not the stick) while running makes you jump. So you can't jump without a running start (though you can roll of course). Otherwise the latter is 100% correct. Taking pressure off the run button doesn't cause you to stop instantly.

    Note that in general, jumping is primarily a horizontal thing. You can't jump UP on things. (You may have seen a treasure in the Asylum that seems like it's just out of reach. There's no way to get it by jumping, you just have to come back later and come from the other direction with a key)

    Re: Dark Souls: My (new) All-Time Favorite Game.

    If you're talking about the fire-breathing dragon, that's not a boss. Don't bother trying to kill it. It can be done, but I've never done it. You only have to get past him and into room with the bonfire across the bridge and he will fly away. There's also a stairway that goes back to the burg bonfire and a way underneath the dragon on the bridge (but if you go that way you'll miss the next bonfire)

    The Black Knight and The Rock (at the bottom of the tower) are optional for now. I wouldn't use the Gold Pine Resin on either of them, although it is probably effective.

    The vendor with the repairbox is underneath the platform with the two Spear-wielding hollows. (Note: I think that if you two-hand your shortsword, one light attack will drain enough stamina to stagger them and break their guard. Kicking also works). The stairway is surrounded by a bunch of breakable crates. He sells a lot of good newbie stuff actually, I'd recommend buying a bunch of firebombs, throwing daggers, and a show bow w/ wooden arrows.

    One more tip: longsword vs shortsword. They are nearly identical except that the longsword has better reach and more damage, but weighs 1.0 more.

    Re: Dark Souls: My (new) All-Time Favorite Game.

    The sword seems super nice but would require me to level like 10 or 11 times while the spell would require 3 so I'm leaning towards faith.
    FYI: The Black Knight Sword is a random drop from that first black knight in Undead Burg, and it's WAY overpowered in the early part of the game. Meanwhile, it won't be long before you'll find vendors that sell Homeward Bones for 500 souls a pop. In terms of making the game easier, there's no question about it. The Black Knight Sword will make a way bigger difference. Also, in the grand scheme of things, 20/18 is a fairly low requirement. If you have 20 strength and 18 dex, you're going to have a LOT of different weapons and shields to choose from. 18 faith isn't really going to do much except let you use Homeward and two other miracles.

    That said: If you want a paladin-like character with high (30+) faith, you'll probably have to focus on either strength or dex but not both. Forgoing the Black Knight sword is not unreasonable from that perspective. Homeward is undeniably useful and besides, the Black Knight Sword may spoil some of the challenge of your first playthrough, so there's no wrong choice here.

    Only other advice I have for leveling is that it basically never hurts to put points into endurance (up to 40, by the time you get there you should know whether you'll want to invest any more). Vitality is also very useful, although once I started getting better at the time, I put points into Strength and Dexterity because I wanted to wield All The Weapons.

    Normal 'scaling' weapons generally fall into 3 categories: Strength weapons, Dex weapons, and "Quality" weapons. Strength weapons do more damage when your strength is high and include weapons like clubs and hammers. Most Axes are strength weapons though dex often factors in a little. Dexterity weapons do more damage when your dex is high. Scimitars, rapiers, katanas are usually dexterity weapons. "Quality" weapons scale with both both strength and dex. Straight swords (longsword, short sword, etc.), Great Swords, and Ultra Greatswords are considered "Quality" weapons (although the ultra greats usually favor strength). Additionally, there are weapons that scale with faith and intelligence but that's a bit more complicated... you'll get to that later.
