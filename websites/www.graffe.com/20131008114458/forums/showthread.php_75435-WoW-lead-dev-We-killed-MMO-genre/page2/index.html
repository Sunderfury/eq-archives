    Re: WoW lead dev: We killed MMO genre

    Originally Posted by Alikat
    WoW heroic raids are solved by three things: NOT standing in the fire/whirlwinds/othershit, having the right numbers on your equipment, and pressing keys in a "rotation."
    I'm not a fan of raiding, but two points (one related to this and one related to another of your posts):

    1. Nobody has a "rotation" any more. Nobody. Every class has random procs which create a priority system. Managing a priority system, while reacting to the encounter and timing major cooldowns, is not the walk in the park you seem to think it is.

    2. There's no "script" for these raids, if only because in 10 man every guild has a unique composition. Hey, it's great that DREAM Paragon beat encounter X by stacking whatever classes were necessary to advance. I guess if all players of WoW were paid to play, they too could have fully geared alts ready to insert like that. But for a guild like mine, where three of their ten raiders are priests (and they aren't our healing crew) because that's who is in the guild, things are a little different. Now, something like LFR has a script. But LFR is a much different beast than the thing you talked about, heroic raiding, and is intended to be easy.

    Originally Posted by Dmitry
    EQ: 72 people in multiple organzed groups of six with content not designed to be done. Often took incredibly innovative strats months after release.
    Heh, were you a progression raider in EQ? I was, server-leading guild. EQ content was trivial, with the primary delay in solution being the time it took to gear and recruit a raid force. You should read up on the progression servers they made, where the game started with just original content available and opened up expansions as the player base voted to open it up. The raid encounters died within a week or two of the expansions opening. That ain't tough content.

    BTW, I wouldn't call "set up the CH chain here, have these people catch adds here, and locate the boss here" innovative. Now some of the exploits guilds used to kill bosses, those were pretty clever. Stacking unstackable health buffs with bard songs, setting up the boss rampage list then having the person at the top of the list stand OOR so rampage never landed, the list of "innovative" strats was endless. Not that anyone admitted to any of that.

    It's not like hard raiding started in MoP. World of Warcraft has *always* had significantly harder raids than EQ, even back befor heroic modes.
