    Re: E3 Thread 2013

    Microsoft killed it. Games games games, and no kinect. This is going to be hard for Sony to top. My stream kept dying though, so some of this I need to rewatch later.

    I'm going to start with what they ended with. The price. $499. They must be out of their damn minds. The PS3 launched at $499 for the low end model and they got skewered.

    Anyway, the games. They opened with Metal Gear Solid 5, which looked amazing. Mostly showed off how open the world was, but also showed how the passage of time was 1:1 (I believe). Keifer Sutherland is doing the voice acting now (which is actually harder to get used to than I expected). The plot looks darker, with characters losing limbs, child soldiers, etc. That open world stuff though, it looks exactly like what people expect from next gen (not all the games displayed do).


    360
    • Redesigned to look like One
    • World of Tanks 360
    • Max: Curse of Brotherhood - sort of a kid game, platformer
    • Xbox Live was only touched on briefly, now like with PS+, you will get two free (old) games a month. The first offering is Assassin's Creed 2 and Halo 3.


    One
    • They went through way too many things, I don't have time to do my full recap, here's a quick rundown:
    • Killer Instinct - very brief gameplay
    • Crimson Dragon - Panzer Dragoon game, looks amazing
    • Dead Rising 3 - open world city, looks more serious than the previous games, guns as weapons instead of golf carts and giant sombreros
    • Titanfall - ended the conference with this, looks good, but my stream died during this portion
    • Battlefield 4 - stage demo failed, was embarrassing, once it finally started it looked great
    • Ryse - Not a kinect game anymore, looks incredibly scripted though, button prompts out the wazoo
    • Forza 5 - talked about a cool new AI, although the buzzwords were really annoying
    • Halo - CG trailer, coming out in 2014, I'm surprised, I thought they would just port an old game
    • Sunset Overdrive - Insomniac open world shooter exclusive, shaking my head at this one, not sure what Insomniac is thinking, they left Sony to do multiplatform, failed, then went to Microsoft
    • Quantum Break - Missed part of this, they talked briefly about merging a television show and a game, the graphics were actually a little offputting, they looked like game engine graphics and actors overlayed on top of each other (think LA Noire, but not as realistic).
    • Project Spark - LittleBigPlanet style game, where users create their own content, not as cutesy obviously
    • Witcher 3 - trailer and gameplay
    • Black Tusks's game - 15 second trailer, nothing really to go by, some kind of spy game maybe?
    • Below - some kind of bird's eye view dungeon crawler
    • D4 - episodic murder mystery, not really talked about in detail


    EA and Microsoft seem so buddy buddy now. So many EA games being shown off instead of Call of Duty. I wasn't expecting to see CoD again, but I'm still really surprised that they were so readily put to the side by Microsoft. A lot of the rumored EA stuff never materialized though (Mirror's Edge 2), will that be at EA's show?

    They also showed off smartglass a little. They also followed Sony's lead and implemented a way to record and share videos online. Funny, it's usually Sony copying someone else. Anyway they also partnered with Twitch for streaming.

    Looks like the kinect titles were reserved for GT's after and pre-show coverage.

    Re: E3 Thread 2013

    Sony did two things they have a bad habit of doing. They started twenty minutes late, and they tried to show or mention every game they possibly could, whether it was exciting or not. I'm going to have to format this summary to be legible.

    I can't decide which conference I liked better as far as games go. I'm a little disappointed in Sony because I only got half of what I wanted, although I'm extremely happy about that half. They showed very little new stuff, and started to parrot Microsoft with the constant "exclusive content" garbage.

    System and Services

    As you guys mentioned, $400 (400 euros and 350 pounds). They made a point to say used games and no online checking and all that. Really rubbed salt in the wound and just went nuts. Rumor was they were going to launch at $350 and Microsoft's bone headed price allowed them to raise it to $400 and still be heroes. Wtg Microsoft, I could have had a $350 PS4!

    The system itself looks like a PS2 and a Wii had a baby. Not sure how else to describe it.

    They announced that the backwards compatibility cloud service would be live in 2014, but would only be in the US at first.

    They repeated the social media and share button stuff. They announced Drive Club would be free on launch if you have Playstation Plus. I'm disappointed in PS+ for the same reason why I'm disappointed in Live. They didn't really announce ways to expand the service (beyond the BC stuff I guess). And one thing I think a lot of people might be missing during all the applause for no DRM, you now need to pay to play multiplayer on PSN. It was on their splash screen of PS+ features, right at the end. $5 a month to play mp on PS4 (PS3 isn't changing presumably).

    Not what I was hoping for when I wanted a reason to get PS+. I think when the dust settles people are going to be upset. Although I have to say, it's a pretty amusing situation, because the only people with a right to be upset are people who play on PSN now (me). Xbox fanboys trying to use it as ammo are just going to end up looking stupid. The two services basically took steps to becoming more alike. Xbox gets free games now, PSN now pays for multiplayer. This is a bit of a blow to the free to play games people were getting excited about earlier.

    They blah blah blahed about media a bit. It's funny because I was accusing Microsoft of copying Sony with the video sharing in order to downplay a feature of their competitor, and Sony just did the same thing by announcing exclusive media content like what Microsoft is doing. They also blah blah blahed about Netflix and a bunch of other boring stuff. Luckily it didn't last long, zzzzzzz.

    They also showed off a demo from Quantic Dream showing off facial animation capabilities. I thought this was a good way to demonstrate what next gen can offer, although I think people might mistake it for a game. (it's not)


    Games

    PS4:

    Best
    • Updates on the four titles shown previously, all but Infamous will be launch titles. Infamous is Quarter 1. Infamous looks good.
    • New IP, Order 1886, looks steampunky
    • Amazingly excellent Versus trailer, which then teases you and renames it FF15 (is it multiplatform now?)
    • Kingdom Hearts 3 teaser, extremely early, looked like a mock up
    • Destiny demo, looks good, the Guild Wars 2 of fps, it had loot, talent trees, and public boss events

    Other
    • Assassin's Creed - demo kept freezing on stage, worrying, embarrassing
    • The Elder Scrolls Online - feed died, didn't see how it looked
    • Watch Dogs - pretty extensive gameplay demo, looked good
    • Diablo 3 - only really mentioned briefly
    • 2K Sports - showed facial animation technology, was kind of... off
    • A Mad Max game?


    Nothing new from Naughty Dog. No additional bombs to be dropped. If judging by sheer quantity, they had less surprises for me than Microsoft did and this disappoints me. I already knew about The Order, and I was expecting Versus. Where they made up the slack was with indie titles. They brought several out on stage and announced they were all PS4 exclusive. They honestly went a little nuts.

    Indie

    • Transistor - from the guys that made Bastion, I posted about it awhile back, I'm so hyped for this game
    • Don't Starve - played it on PC, it's great
    • Mercenary Kings - Looks like Metal Slug with the art from the guy that did the Scott Pilgrim game (Paul Robertson, he's awesome), yes please
    • Octodad's Deadliest Catch? - crazy ass weird thing
    • Secret Ponchos - a bird's eye view action shooter
    • Ray's Dead - You play a zombie and lead an army of zombies
    • Outlast - survival horror first person (like Amnesia)
    • Oddworld remake
    • GalaxZ - top down space shooter



    PS3
    • GTA 5 is getting a PS3 bundle and headset
    • Beyond Two Souls - it has gameplay now! Not a Heavy Rain game, was actually a little weirded out by how much of an action game it seems to be
    • The Last of Us
    • Puppeteer
    • rain
    • Gran Turismo 6
    • Batman Arkham Origins

    Vita
    • Killzone Mercenary probably the biggest title shown
    • Tearaway - made by the LBP guys
    • Destiny of Spirits - mentioned earlier today
    • Doki Doki Universe - quirky title that had already been revealed
    • Some kind of spy game
    • Versions of Batman Origins, God of War HD, FFX HD, Flower, and Dead Nation
    • Walking Dead and it's 400 Days DLC announced


      So, TLDR, I'm extremely happy with finally seeing Versus, and I liked a few other things, but I was expecting more. I came into this thinking Sony would "win" E3 (and they might still have done so), but now I actually think Nintendo has a chance to top what we have seen so far.

      All in all, it's still far better than last year. Heck, probably the last few years.
