    Hello fellow sorcerers,
    I am a long time reader, first time poster on these forums. First I'd like to introduce myself. I am Asmadi Vaevictus of Seradon server. I have been playing my sorcerer since 3 days after release. She is level 50 and pretty decently geared. She has fireweave armor, nice foci, etc. but she is not yet APW geared. She has max SDR, and is geared heavily in SDR and SCR. (About 22% vs lvl 50 in both). INT when raid buffed maxxes at around 750-800. Her highest legendary hit to date is 74,910. First of all, after reading up on recent events here, I finally feel compelled to input my opinion on the matters at hand I am not here with the intent to suggest coding that we already know isnt possible to implement yet, nor am I here to complain. Im simply here to offer my perspective and hopefully be a part of what is to come for our class. Secondly, I have not yet copied my character to test server but I intend to do so in the next few days as time permits.

    1) Let's look at CV for a moment. First, as it is now:
    Pro's:
    Increased damage with every cast up to 14 casts.
    High chance of large criticals (30k+).
    Low recast time.
    Low energy cost.

    Con's:
    Deals significant damage to the caster at higher chain levels. This can be a burden on a single healer group who needs to focus on the tank. To me this represents an even further degradation of our survivability.
    Generates very high hate rating.

    --Now lets look at the proposed change as has been vaguely described...

    Pro's:
    Does not damage the caster when the spell is cast therefore removing the need for healers to burn mana on CV'ing Sorcs. That alone aleviates one of our burdens.
    Continued casting will not only cause CV to do more damage with each successive cast but it will also cause ALL our spells to do incremental damage as well as applying the same benefit to all group members be they melee or squishies. So, essentially, I see that as an aggro tool. Instead of just the Sorcerers damage and hate going up quickly, that hate and damage will be distributed to the group as well, possibly enough to offset our spike crit aggro. This concept, if handled correctly, could potentially add a dps boosting utility to sorcerers, over time, equivalent or even surpassing that of a bard.

    2) On to fire and ice:
    This subject strongly tempts me to compare Sorcerers to Druids. Both classes make use of fire based spells. That I know. I am not entirely familiar with druid spells however, as far as what type of magic each specific spell is based upon or its skill attribute. What I do know is that the Druid is lended to all aspects of nature and in VG, at least to me, the Druid seems to be the true "Elementalist". They boast fire, ice, arcane, physical (insects, poison), and earth abilities, can heal themselves and others, and have numerous beneficial buffs which can be cast on others. They can root mobs for extended periods as well. After GU4, they can often now out DPS a sorcerer, but I have noticed an increase in druid deaths in my groups.

    So, whats the answer? Let's remember here what Jadis has told us and what we have to work with....
    We are getting a fire dot that will be highly effective against spell casting mobs. We know that much...(I may be wrong in assuming this will have a fire dot component but it would make sense).
    Increasing the duration and damage/tic on spells like Char and Flamespear would be a significant boost. It makes total sense to me that if a Sorcerer should any DoT it should be fire/ice maybe even arcane but I wont get to dreaming hehe. As far as the range on Flamespear, My Idea is to leave the range the same or maybe increase it a bit, give it more damage and a longer dot component and also give it a minimum range. As in, you gotta be 15m away from target at least to cast, similar to a bard using a bow.
    This could be balanced by the revamp of Shocking Grasp. Make SG a close range based dot. IE: for x amount of time your defensive target is wracked with electrocution so long as you remain within a certain distance. This could also be an endurance based ability and would only be sustainable for a short period. In addition, I personally feel that the durations of the Freeze/Icequake line of spells could stand to be altered not in duration but in a debuff manner such as an ice based spell causing weakness to fire and vise versa.
    Fireball should have a blind effect, gives us a lil bit better chance to live when utilizing the spell.

    3) Falling star/Meteor Storm:
    Faster refresh on Seradons? Im all for it lol. No arguments here.
    MS does FAR too little damage in relation to its cost and cast time. Personally, I'd like to see this spell beefed up and moved to a finisher. Our only AE finisher is cold based. Move incinerate to a regular spell slot like MS, put Inidrias inferno as the initial finisher and a single target MS as the final fire finisher. Or, just make the fire finisher AE as well, and give us better deaggro abilities

    Woops 1 more thing...
    Mimic. Fix it. Make it do what it says its supposed to do.... "Copies and magnifies the LAST SPELL CAST". That means if I cast Tag's and I crit for 16k and then mimic it, it shouldnt hit for 6k... It should hit for AT LEAST 16k...(the last spell I cast hit for 16k, so, if its being copied and then amplified and ends up 1/5 of the mimic'd damage? Its like retiring and being told your 401k never existed. The best way to bring Sorcs to where we will all be happy with them is to go back to some root concepts and start with giving us back some of the handicaps you designed into us in the beginning.
    Allow Seradon's Falling Star and Meteor Storm to be mimic'd if they crit...
    Make our amplify spells usable on ALL our spells. Yes, this includes any crit level. If Im AE'ing a group of mobs and I have frigid blast crit lit and ready to fire, I should be able to use an amplify spell and have it affect my finisher. And if that sets off a crit mimic would be up again...


    Thats all I got for tonight.

    -Asmadi Vaevictus
