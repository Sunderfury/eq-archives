    I see mainly 3 pillars that need work for Wizards: Mana Regen, Hate reduction and Damage output.

    Mana Regen:

    - Fix the orignal Harvest versions or create a new version e.g. Harvest of Ro (3/6/9) 500/750/1000 mana, recast 5 minutes, cast time 10 sec, no stun component
    - Upgrade our Familiars with Mana regen - Devoted Familiar should be upped to 15 Mana per tick if not even 18 - the Foci Familiars should be upped to 8 mana per tick

    Hate reduction:

    2 possibilities...
    a.) A new Concussion (name idea: "Oblivion") spell which should decrease hate by 1000 else same cast time and mana cost as AGC (2 secs / 25 mana)
    or...
    b.) Lower the reuse of Mind Crash significantly down to 9 minutes - something like "Advanced Hastened Mind Crash" (6/9/12), lowers the reuse time of the Hastened Mind Crash by further 25% per rank.
    This would have more impact on long duration fights where you build up significant aggro overtime

    - Fix the Silent Casting at last level from random to a certain guaranteed Hate reduction in the range of at least 70%+

    I personally would prefer a new Concussion spell overall since it would have generally more impact and utility

    Damage Output:

    - "Advanced Quick Damage" (3/6/9), Lowers the casting time of all your damage spells by 5%,10% and 15%
    - "Prismatic Focus" (5/10), lowers the resist modifier on all your damage spells by 50 and 100

    Spells:
    - Up the damage of Mana Weave and Gelidin Comet to same base damage as Corona Flare. This would give us more of a freedom which to use
    - "Freeze" (3/6/9), same as Pyromancy just for Cold based spells
    - As for new damage spell in the next expansion "Frost Strike" (or whatever name) with around same base damage as Ether Flame


    General Utility

    - "Project Teleport" (10), after activation your next Translocate spell will send all players in range to the destination of your Translocate, same mods as regular Teleport
    - Spell:Second Recall Teleport, sends all players in range to your Secondary Bind spot, same mods as regular Teleport


    So as summary for the upcomming expansion:
    New Spells: Oblivion, Frost Strike, Second Recall Teleport
    New AAs: Harvest of Ro, Advanced Quick Damage, Prismatic Focus, Freeze, Project Teleport


    Just my 2cp on the matter.

    Spellcasting Overhaste
    A one minute duration group buff which acts to supplement the current line of cleric spellhaste spells.
    It would work to do so by reducing the recast timer by a percentage (25%) for spells where recast timer is less than 180.

    A buff like this is great for two reasons. It would be highly coveted by wizards wanting to do better burst dps. And it would also be highly coveted by all casters, healers, knights, caster dps as well.

    So this gives us utility and yet remains focused on improving our damage capability.

    An idea I had, reposted from a discussion in another topic thread:

    How about a Wizard gets a nuke that does 500 DD. When cast, it places a buff in the target's buff box that indicates "Level One" of that spell. Next time the Wizard casts the same DD spell, it does 1K DD, and the buff is overwritten with a buff indicating "Level Two" of that spell. If the target has the Level Two icon and is hit by the same spell, it does 2K DD and the buff is overwritten with a "Level Three" icon. Put in five levels, and the spell would do up to 16K per nuke, but no crit capacity or focus will work (like the old dragon/giantbane nukes). Or four levels for up to 8K per nuke, but with the normal crit/focus capacity. At max level, the buff would become ablative, meaning it would fade after being nuked with that same spell.

    That would allow Wizards to nuke for high damage, low mana on raids, but without infinite stackability, since they would not each get their own buff icon, but would be overwriting for each other on a single buff icon. It would take a single Wizard multiple casts to ramp the buff up, and the final buff icon would not be over-writable, but would fade after being hit with again at max level, requiring another ramp-up. Raiding Wizards would like doing loads of DPS for low mana costs, and grouping Wizards would like doing large nukes for moderate mana costs (the nuke itself, which would do 500 DD at Level Zero, would be around 250 mana per cast, which would become as efficient as CoF at Level One, subsequent levels would bring the mana cost down well below that of CoV). In group settings, the more hitpoints/longer fight times per mob, better this nuke would be. In raid settings, the Wizzies could coordinate to use the tactic of timing their nukes so that they ramped the level up to maximum as quickly as possible.

    That would give Wizzies a finitely-stackable instant DD line that could be high-damage/high-efficiency, but would not be immediately overpowering, and the finite stackability would favor raiding parties with five Wizards per raid, though fewer or more Wizards would still be able to use it, just not at peak efficiency. That would make Wizards desirable but not in large numbers like the current DD model.
    Alikat is more right than anyone who has ever been right in the history of correctness.-Istari
