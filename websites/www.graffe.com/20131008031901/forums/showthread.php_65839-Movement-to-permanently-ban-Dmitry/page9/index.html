    Re: Movement to permanently ban Dmitry

    Originally Posted by Delores Mulva
    If you accept that War was Dmitry, you must also accept that he was posting in this forum in an acceptable manner after being told to do so.
    Regardless of the manner of posting, circumvention of a suspension via the creation of alt accounts is itself a violation, and is never acceptable nor permitted. What he posts, or how he posts it are entirely irrelevant.

    He's circumvented suspension on three occasions now - at what point do we chalk it up and acknowledge that the refusal to play nice in the sandbox with the other kids is grounds not to be invited back? I have argued that circumvention should result in stronger consequences, but that didn't make it into the last rules draft. (I'd personally prefer an immediate ban for circumvention - no review, poof, you're toast.) We don't tolerate people reverse-editing a modded post, and we should not tolerate people using sockpuppets to get around a decision with which they don't agree after having abused the ability to post while suspended.

    Seriously - it's not rocket science. If a decision is made that you don't agree with, challenge the decision in a level-headed fashion, or get the rules changed in the SFA forum. It's been explained, and the majority of the people posting here seem to have no problem with the process that we've collectively adopted - there are discussions going on about rule changes RIGHT NOW.

    I put it to you all - if I took on the mods and the community, today, in the same fashion as Dmitry, how long would it be before I was suspended? If I returned to do it again AFTER circumventing my suspension twice, how long would it be before I was banned?

    I'd put long odds that I'd last half as long as he did, because I have neither the "off-camera" friendships, nor the posting history to support the nostalgic view of longtime posters. I'd be hooked, booked and cooked inside a day - no agonizing, no discussion, wham, bam, gone. That's subjective application. It's also called reality. I'm fine with it. I know the rules, and I don't go tilting at windmills to see how large a disruption I can make. Neither do most of you. I figure most of you would take a formal PM warning from a moderator as a signal that a different and more civil approach is needed, rather than as a challenge to find a new way to piss people off.

    Hey, if we want "Graffes the Anarchy", just let me know - I'll find another place to discuss the games I play, and I won't have to watch as tit-for-tat counting coup flushes the board down the drain by degrees.

    That also proves one of Dmitry's points, about how unenforceable or subjective many of the rules are.
    Why, one might almost think that this is how things work in the real world!

    Let's look at an example:
    Small crimes (like jaywalking) are rarely enforced, because even though there's a clear and 100% objective definition of them, they're not generally worth pursuing. However, if you spend your days calling people at 2am to let them know you're going to be jaywalking repeatedly between 4pm and 5pm right in front of police HQ, you should probably expect to reap the consequences of pissing in their Wheaties.

    Don't flaunt your minor violations, and they tend to fade into the woodwork because people won't be inclined to report you for "minor" problems. Be a dick and piss everyone off, and you'll get reported left, right and center.

    Whodathunk it - the rules are as subjectively enforced as the ones about jaywalking are!?

    Since the moderators operate on reports, the consequences of pissing everyone off should be immediately apparent - more reports is more actions taken. This really isn't about controversial topics, or memes, or homosexual slander. It's about not pissing all over the community just because the rules don't explicitly say "don't piss all over the community". No other forum on the web tolerates disruptive posters for long - at least, no forum worth mentioning. Why should we?

    Nostalgia isn't a good enough reason anymore. Neither is an overstrict interpretation of the rules relying on semantic arguments like "I made up a word and I'll use it to mock and belittle but I didn't mean to mock or belittle THAT WAY". Neither is "this sockpuppet that just happened to crawl out of the woodwork and has three posts in the forum, all defending Dmitry might not be Dmitry" - who cares?

    It's time we closed the book on this one and got on with being a community.
