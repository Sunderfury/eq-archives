WoW: Class: Warrior: Berserker Stance

From GraffeWiki

Jump to: navigation, search
Gained: Level 30, quest referral from Warrior Trainer
Bonuses: +3% critical hit chance, -20% threat generation
Penalties: +10% damage taken



Barely a stance at all in the traditional sense and more a righteous thirst for destruction, let's just call it a sign that the Warrior's a little pissed off. By it's definition, it renders the berserking player furiously reckless, increasing both damage dealt and recieved. The stance works best with either two very fast weapons, or one very big one.

Abilities unique to the Berserker Stance: Intercept, Berserker Rage, Whirlwind, Pummel, and Recklessness; Berserker Stance shares some abilities with Battle Stance, but none with the Defensive Stance. rendering its focus on the rapid accrual and usage of Rage.

This stance is mostly a soloing one, although it has some use in Main Assist positions, provided the Main Tank is able to regain aggro from a lucky run of berserk critical hits, given that it is the opposite of Defensive Stance.

Over the long run, the Berseker Stance is marginally more successful and time efficient for solo xp work than Battle Stance, with quicker fights, but longer rest required between fights, so in the end, it's a matter of personal preference. For farming low-level (Grey or Green) enemies; to complete old quests or harvest loot, Berserker Stance helps speed the process up enormously.

Useful in PvP, especially for the Fear protection that Berserker Rage provides. Most non-melee enemies will try to avoid a direct brawl with the Warrior, and use any type of fear available, so clever timing with this ability can turn the tables completely. Pummel is also an invaluable PvP tool. A good tactic is to start in Battle Stance, use Charge, then swap to Berserker Stance for the rest of the fight.

To get the most out of the Critical Bonus, consider Cruelty and Axe Specialization Talents as part of an accompanying Talent Build. The bonuses work best either with lots of very fast hits, or very large ones, so use Dual Wielded fast swords or daggers, or a very large Two Handed weapon, and use Slam to counter the slow timer.

To acquire the berserker stance, a quest must be completed on an island south of Ratchet any level 30 or after. For Horde characters, the quest can be obtained from a warrior trainer in any major city.
