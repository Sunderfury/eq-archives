    Re: EQ3 - IMA BACK BEOTCHES

    Not seen this posted here.

    Everquest Next will also include Storybricks.

    http://massively.joystiq.com/2013/04...ing-in-a-tree/

    I find the videos of it confusing, but I get the jist of it. It seems to be a quest/faction/npc interaction system that gives a much larger scope of NPCs interactions and how the world reacts to you.

    A post on the rerolled (new foh) forums said it like this


    http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OMbOkJRHhfU

    -key points after 31 min and especially after 43 min.

    Rundown.
    -NPCs will have emotional traits and connections (in love with, bethrothed to, owes a debt too, loves flowers, whatever)
    -PCs will also have emotional traits and moods (dopey, sneezy, creepy, lovey, noble, etc) that actually matter.

    Certain relationship connections will trigger other emotional traits based on PC action.
    (Ben loves Jerry, if you kill Jerry Ben will hate you. Hating you means he will want to do any number of possible actions to you: kill you or hire someone to kill you (perhaps based on your level, size and strength)).

    EQN will contain a bunch of these relationships. I'll give the example from the video: 3NPCs King Arthur, Guinevere and Lancelot.

    Before EQN/SB
    A is married to and loves G, G is married to A but loves L, L is duty-bound to A but loves G.
    You as a PC get to do quests for A,G and L related to this love triangle. All other players can do the same quests.

    With EQN/SB
    Lancelot goes away. You the player character can take the role of Lancelot if you develop a love relationship with G and take an oath to A. Once appropriate conditions are met, the story will start. There is no quest to take, the only question is whether you want to roleplay the love relationship with G and oath relationship with A. Once that happens the rest of the story will commence with you taking the part of Lancelot (with normal story progression and story arcs/breaks and forks based on the pre-created AGL storybrick).

    I'm explaining it poorly so I'll do it this way:

    Before EQN/SB
    Drunk means having funny things happen on your computer.

    With EQN/SB
    Drunk means getting rolled by pickpockets. Pickpocket AI will be alert for anyone who gets drunk. You get drunk, you join the 'rolling the drunk' story.

    Anyway, if SB is implemented in EQN as is suggested in the video I get the best-of: there are no quests, there are no quest hubs. Only how and where you want to roleplay your character. Each player will only take on the stories (quests) that he or she wants to take on based on how they roleplay their character.

    All you want to do is fight: set your mood to adventureous and talk with people who look like they're in trouble. You want to romance a queen, set your mood to romantic and start looking for a queen.
    So, while we do not know much about the game yet, they do have tech in it that "could" set this game quite far apart from its competition (might be the reason to the awards by mmorpg and tth). Smed also recently twittered a response to a question on his twitter

    Re: EQ3 - IMA BACK BEOTCHES

    Originally Posted by Destructis
    LOL no but that was funny. Don't get me wrong, I am not being critical of others play style. I am just past all of that shit.

    No point in taking anything to seriously.
    That sounds nice, however like most platitudes they are hollow and nonsensical. Being competent does not entail foregoing fun, indeed it is exactly the opposite that is true: being incompetent in a situation that requires competence entails foregoing fun for anyone involved with those who are competent i.e. by choosing to "have fun" and not prepare the incompetent member will force everyone to endure the consequences of those actions. In the context of a MMO usually this means wiping or losing matches and wasting the time of anyone who is a competent player.

    As far as the MMOs previously mentioned, they have action elements, but they do not embrace action combat entirely or rather their action combat systems could be a lot better. A player still is limited by factors that the player cannot overcome no matter their level of preparation or execution. Interestingly, these combat systems are also why class composition is important, a fact that is obnoxious as it involves carrying people most of the time and ends up restricting what encounter designers can do or forces organizations to have a deep bench so that they can have the necessary line up for an encounter. Dice rolls have no reason to exist in modern MMOs.

    They punish players for playing well by causing them to lose regardless of their actions. They are necessary in board games and pen and paper games because they have no capacity to simulate the game play directly. In MMOs with modern systems that is not true and refusing to take advantage of that means immersion is hamstrung. In MMOs especially RNG should be squashed whenever possible as there is no way to redo a trial with the exact same game state like there is in games with saves.
