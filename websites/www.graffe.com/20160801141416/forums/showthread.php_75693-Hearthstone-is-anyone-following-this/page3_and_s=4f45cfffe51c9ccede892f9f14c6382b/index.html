    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    I beat someone tonight who was playing a Warrior deck. The player's BattleTag was Planedefiler. I was smiling more at the thought that I just stomped Furor than I did when I beat the guy who played three Legendaries in a row on me (Deathwing - Polymorph, Hogger - Flamestrike, Illidan - Fireball).

    Ok, since WoW is down for 5.4 and Hearthstone will be down later for an update, I can write a bit about what I've seen so far. To give you the TL;DR version: no, I currently would not purchase the game for myself. But the reason why may very well change.

    When you first start Hearthstone, you begin in a six game tutorial. You play Jaina, the mage, against opponents of increasing difficulty (the first one is Hogger). In addition to increasing difficulty, each new opponent brings new abilities to the game. So the first game is about the very basics: mana, attacking opponents and creatures, dealing damage. Then additional complexities are brought in, like Taunt and Spell Power, until you have a fairly good grounding on the mechanics of the game. From there, you're thrown into the Practice Mode area.

    Practice Mode is where you play against the computer AI, in either normal or expert difficulty. At first, you can only play as Jaina, using the basic deck provided for Practice Mode. Your opponent can be any of the nine original classes from World of Warcraft: Mage, Hunter, Warrior, Rogue, Warlock, Shaman, Paladin, Priest, Druid. The AI's play relatively intelligently (although druids and warlocks have known AI bugs making them poor opponents), and only the AI has some of the "kill most/all creatures on the board" cards. This really teaches you to play smart, not overextend, work for card advantage, and not skate by on lucky board sweep draws when you're behind. If you beat one of these AI's, you unlock that class for play.

    You also gain XP as you play a given class, starting from level 1. Every even level, you gain some basic cards of that class for your personal collection, until level 10. Once you hit level 10, you can no longer gain XP from playing against the computer, only from playing in The Arena (pack draft format) or Play mode (constructed deck format). I strongly recommend anyone who gets in the Beta play every class against every other class, in Expert mode, until all your classes are level 10. There's no sense wasting XP later, since it starts slowing down considerably at higher levels. It's also worth unlocking all the classes because doing so gives you your first achievement, and 100 gold from the achievement. There's currently no list in the game of what achievements you've obtained, what achievements you could obtain, or what the rewards will be. Future patch, I hope.

    So, you've now leveled all classes to level 10 (roughly 100 games - you get XP when you lose too). It's time for either Play or Arena. Play Mode is constructed decks. When you go into the "My Collection" page, you can see all the cards you own. Making a deck is as simple as clicking "Construct New Deck", picking a class, and drag-dropping cards from your collection into the deck. A deck is 30 cards total, and there are both class-specific cards and general cards that can go in any deck. You want to build your deck such that, on any given turn, odds are you have a card in hand that you can play. This means paying attention to the mana costs of what goes in your deck, and also having cards that accelerate card drawing. Both these are crucial concepts in constructed play.

    Once you have built your deck, you click play and an opponent that is supposed to be at your level is found for you. I don't know what criteria are used to match people, but unless I'm playing at a very odd time of the night you generally get matched against someone with similar skill/cards to what you have. There will be the odd stomping (many games end in under eight rounds), but some of that is due to class mismatches and bad draws. You can play in either Ranked or Unranked mode. At this point I have no idea what the difference is other than Ranked games are advancing my "Ranked Play Medal" (I'm currently at one star diamond, with 73 total wins), and Unranked games don't, so I always play Ranked. There's no reward for winning other than advancing the medal, and you get 5 gold for every five wins you get. If there's an XP bonus for winning, I have yet to see it - XP gains seem to correlate with number of rounds played and nothing else. When you hit level 20 in a given class, you start getting gold versions of the basic cards you got from 2-10. There's nothing special about the cards other than they have gold borders - it's the equivalent of the foil cards in CCGs.

    As you play games, you may complete quests. There's some quests given to you when you first start the game, and there's also a new daily quest every day (1 am reset). Quests can be completed in Play or Arena mode, and are things like "Win Two Games as a Paladin". The daily quest reward is 40 gold. With all the starter quests, achievements, and what not, you start with 500 or so gold. What can you do with that gold? You can either buy packs, or play Arena.

    The Arena costs 150 gold or $2 to enter, with your first time being free. The game will randomly select three of the nine classes, and you must play as one of those three. It will then present you with a selection of three cards, all of the same rarity. You pick one of the three for your deck, and the next set of three appears. You do this until all 30 cards are filled in, and then you go play with the deck you've constructed, being matched against people with a similar win-loss record. You play until you hit three losses or nine wins, whichever comes first. At that point, you claim your reward: a selection of five items. One of these items is always a pack of cards. Since packs cost 100 gold, this isn't a bad deal. The other four items will be gold, dust (see below), or potentially another pack of cards. You get more stuff the better you did. I believe in my worst outing (an abysmal 1-3 - every other time I played I was 5+ wins) I got something like 40 gold, 20 dust, and the pack of cards. So Arena is pretty much always worth doing, if you have the gold (and that's a huge "if"). The game also advances your Arena medal as you play. I'm currently at 49 wins, which is Master (next level: Grandmaster).

    Other than winning packs in the Arena, you can also buy them. 100 g per pack, or actual money (from $3 for 2 to $50 for 40). Each pack contains at least one Rare or better card, and there's something like a 1.25% chance of any given card in a pack being gold. I've opened a pack and gotten two golds in it, so that can happen. Opening packs isn't the only way to get cards, though. You can also Disenchant rare or better cards, and use the Dust that you get from that to create cards that you desire. The exchange rate is pretty steep. For example, if you disenchant a Rare, you get 20 dust. Crafting a Rare takes 100 Dust. I've opened somewhere in the teens number of packs, and with the big bonus you get for your first disenchant (I think it was something like 100 free dust) I'm at 365 Dust, with none of it used for cards yet.

    So by now those of you who are good at keeping track of numbers know why I'm not interested in purchasing the game. The amount of gold you get per day is pathetic (40 g from daily plus 5/5 wins in Play mode), and doesn't come close to allowing you to play even one Arena round per day. Without Arena rounds, the only way to advance in constructed play is to purchase card packs. Personally, I prefer Arena to Play mode. I like pack drafting, and have since my Magic: the Gathering days. But with the system as-is, I'd have to pay $2 every time I wanted to play one round of Arena. Screw that. Now, the devs are aware of how bad the gold thing is, since the forums are filled with whining about it. So I assume they'll bump up the gold per win, gold per daily, or number of dailies per day. They could also award gold through achievements, like "collecting every Murloc-themed card". But right now there's nothing in game that shows achievements earned or that could be earned, so that would need to be added.

    So there's a basic rundown of the game. If anyone not in Beta wants to ask questions, fire away. The game play is fast-paced and fun, and it feels like a Warcraft-themed game. It's too bad that, right now, the financial elements of the game are so onerous.
