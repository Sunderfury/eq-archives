    Re: Dark Tower Movie in Preliminary Stages

    I honestly can think of no other way for that book to end.

    SPOILER ALERT! (Seriously? I was just outsmarted by the goddamn spoiler tags, how sad am I?) If a mod wants to warp the following in spoiler tags, that'd be spiffy. Though if you haven't read the book, I'd avoid it.









    Roland was challenging god. He was walking up to the gates of the heaven, beating down the door, and challenging god. You don't get to walk away from that. Maybe, eventually, he'll learn his place and his life will stop looping, but until he does, this is his own personal hell.

    I can think of no other way to end that book that would make sense. What happens, Roland defeats the Crimson King, walks up to the door, knocks, announces himself, and the god thanks him for the hand? No.

    When I first read it, I felt robbed. As if I should have been given a happy ending, one where Roland can finally set down his burden, after having overcome the world to finish his quest.

    But it doesn't work that way, and a few months later, I was thinking about it, and there is really no other way for that series to end.

    There is the hint of redemption, he seems to make it through each loop with "something" from the previous loop, maybe, just maybe his "slow plodding methodical mind" will figure it out. But until then, he is serving his sentence in hell. And his hell, is his quest. Repeated over, and over, and over again. Watching his friends die, over and over again.
    "We live in a society absolutely dependent on science and technology and yet have cleverly arranged things so that almost no one understands science and technology. That's a clear prescription for disaster."
    -Carl Sagan

He has a very nasally thing he does with his Ps and Ls when you put them together and it takes some getting used to.

    Not unlike the Hitchhiker's Guide series which was also originally read unabridged by Douglas Adams but is now read by at least one other reader. But no one read that story like Douglas Adams himself.

    Full disclosure, I am a huge audio book junkie and have been for 20 years.

    And my final recommendation, which is really ultra rare, is A Night in the Lonesome October read by the author Roger Zelazny. I listen to it the first week of October every year to get in the mood for Halloween.
