    How did I not realize I hadn't seen this episode yet? Somehow Hulu Plus didn't flag it as "New Episode" on my account.

    I would have watched this yesterday if it had flagged.

    I'm only a few minutes in and I'd LOVE to know how much (if at all), as a director, Smith had to adjust the performance of the opening extremely dramatic scenes.

    Fast-paced drama is one of the hardest things to choreograph and the potential for failure was high on this, so I'm glad they chose a director who's good at it.

    I'm not really a huge Kevin Smith fan, but I walk away from all of his movies with that feeling that the scene from Ferris Bueller's Day off exudes, where Cameron, Sloane, and Ferris are in the Art Institute.

    You come away from that scene thinking the characters felt like "This was a moment where I just stepped out of myself and experienced something that was profound to me. For just a little while, I forgot all about myself"

    There are a few authors who can do that to me (Melissa F. Olson, Carol Bosselman, Rachel A. Marks, Anthony Ryan) but with regards to video, pretty much only Kevin Smith's stuff does that to me. Well, that and anything where Ron Livingston has screen time. Something about the timbre of his voice just pulls me out of reality.

    Okay, now to rewind and lose myself in the episode.
