    Re: Wizard Spell Data (all dps,dpm,time til oom, etc etc)

    Originally Posted by Turielaen
    Hmm, small question about how you compute the average spell damage, it seems a bit high compared to my experience.
    I saw that your formula is (non crit percentage * average dmg) + (crit percentage * crit average dmg) + some other modifiers.
    What sounds weird to me (but maybe I'm wrong) is that in your crit average damage calculation, crit_base and increased_crit_damage count for full, I'm wondering if they should not be divided by 2, like the familiar and focus dmg...
    As far as I know the Increased Crit modifiers aren't divided by two, If you just go to a random spot take your focus off and nuke away, you will notice it's always a set amount unless a partial resist occurs, but at least 9/10 will hit for the same amount which is the max,

    Now to answer why these values seem high, which I agree they are high and they are DEFINATELY higher then what you will parse in game.

    There are Reasons that will drop all the numbers (DPS,DPM etc) though that I will explain:

    1) Resists/Partials not calculated, So you'll notice sometimes a 4000base nuke will hit for 2000 or crit for 400 even, this is because partial resists. This is not factored into the calculator because every mob has different ratios for resists and it's impossible to predict on a big scale.

    2) Fizzles, Fizzles will occur, not accounted for at this time, I will add this in if i can find out the equations. I'm sure these are predictable enough to get some sort of perchantage of chance of occuring.

    3) This program assumes absolutely no lag, and absolutely no reaction time. like the instant a spell can be cast this assumes it was.

    4) Some mobs even have spell shield (Like our gear I think) or other types of spell dmg procs or reflects or whatever, which will make your nukes land for less or not at all, this isn't calculated either
