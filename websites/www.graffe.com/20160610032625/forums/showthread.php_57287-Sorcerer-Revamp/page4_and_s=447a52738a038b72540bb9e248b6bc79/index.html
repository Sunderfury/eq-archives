    The forms and Asaya's Scorn will be great. Is the Anti-magic aura still being considered? That is such a fabulous concept that should be implemented. The ring changes sound allright.


    Flamespear: Changes to make it better are good as is the crit bonus but the developers really should consider making some of the fire dot effects last 8 seconds and not 4. The sizzling would be a bit less gimmicky this way.

    Shocking Grasp: There are a few better options. One is to make it a special, risky type of Arcane direct damage. This could take the form of a very rapid endurance unloading dot or a very deadly attack which place an endurance regen debuff penalty on the sorcerer for next 30 or so seconds.

    This way SG could be used twice at close range as a sort of emergency attack. Not the same as the dk's word abilities but something unique and fitting. Either of these two possibilities would keep the spell unique, reasonably effective and flavorful.




    Chromatic Halo: Is a self-rune really the best way to go? We will have to wait and see what it is like to truely judge it. I liked the idea of making a maintained enchantment in which you would choose an element to heal x % from for some amount of damage/hits/time. This would have the double advantage of making it so that if the Sanctuary spells are merged into a single spell some of the complexity can be restored. Might be hard to code or balance though.

    What the plans are for Nullifying Ward and the reflect one will very be important factors and will directly affect the outcome of CH.



    Proposal: Add a + conju/evocation/alteration group buff like the Blood mage has. This way sorcs would have both Seradon's Vision and a + skill buff. Both are trivial compared to other classes' buffs so it cannot logically be used as an argument to lower sorc dps. Two resist buffs, + spell id, + skills does not equal a a buff class. What it does equal are a few minor sorc flavored buffs.

    Other proposal: Make new spells conj spells. More sorc spells should be conj to create greater balance amongst the three types.
