    Right. Just saw the link to your magelo, Gala - is it up to date?

    If it is, Fureya's right about one thing - you've got to do a few things before you can reasonably expect to complete these missions.

    1. Level - your magelo says your'e 66. Get to 70 - you'll be able to get into much more solid groups at 70. Level 70 toons are *much* more likely to have a decent number of AAs (which makes a huge difference) than sub-70 toons are - most people focus on leveling first, and skip AAs until 70. A 10k level 69 twink tank probably has about 20 AAs, and he'll get smacked around a hell of a lot more than a 10k level 70 tank with 300 AAs ( HP/AC isn't everything). Actually, to be honest, I wouldn't take a level 66 *anything* into one of those missions (well, maybe a couple of the easier ones).

    2. AAs - finish off that last level of SCM - as a grouping wizard, it's huge, and some of these missions are up to 3 hours long - you'll need to sustain your DPS. At least one of the AA Flappies is nice to have too (particularly when you die - and you *will* die in these missions).

    3. Find some good, competent players whose abilities you can trust, and who you can group with on a regular / semi-regular basis. You'll need both competence, persistence, and the ability to work well together in order to succeed here.

    4. Spells - I strongly advise getting the lvl 69 DoN Claw of Vox if you can, or at least lvl 61 Claw of Frost. It's not big DPS, but it's decent, and it's sustainable for a loooooong time.

    5. Gear - contrary to Fureya, I don't really advise focusing on +mana (yet) - manapool is more important for raids, not so much for grouping (which focuses more on mana regen). Get your focuses covered first. +200 mana is 1 cast of Claw of Vox. MP5 is 10% savings on *every* spell you cast (i.e for a 6k mana pool, it'll save you about 600 mana) - and as your mana pool gets larger, the total amt. saved gets larger. Augs from DoN you absolutely *need*: MP5, ID5 - get them ASAP (~280ish crystals each, IIRC).


    Now keep in mind, this is just friendly advice - and it's all dependent on your magelo being up to date. If it's not, and you're 70 already, and have all the things listed above... well, that's a different story. But I wouldn't reasonably expect a level 66 wizard, and more importantly, *the type of group that a level 66 wizard tends to get invited to* to be able to complete these missions.

    Level up, AA a little bit, get your major Foci covered, and try again. They're hard missions, but they do get a fair bit easier when you have an appropriate force to tackle them.

    And never listen to anyone who tells you to delete your character. It just means they woke up on the wall side of the bed that day.

    Dsavereide -- Your points are valid. This list is crazy hard. You'd have to play 5-8 hours a day, 5-7 days a week, and constantly seek to progress this list in order to have any chance to see these items. This is what raiders do. 5-8 hours a day, most of that on raids. 80% of this list would be a great achievement, and you'd still be geared better than your average Qvic level raider. Its true, you can't start in bazaar gear and jump instantly to this stage. You can however start in any gear and build yourself to this stage using the intervening steps available, and it wouldn't even take that long, compared to the length of time most raiding guilds have been building critical mass. Many raiding cores have been raiding together for 5 years or so.


    Building up to this pinnacle gearing stage has a lot of synergy, also. Note that every slot MPG trials offers is NOT a slot that the top tradeskill gear offers. Thats obviously planned. Crystals sell for loads of cash. The obvious connection is replace off-GM DoN slots with MPG loot and sell back the DoN loots for 70% crystals, then sell the crystals in the bazaar to be able to afford the Grandmaster's items. More than that though, doing enough DoNs to get a full + aug set of gear (even using the 180 cost augs instead of the 450) will offer you more than enough Metallic Drake Scales & Glossy Drake Hides to cover the costs of the Grandmasters gear.


    It means a lot of DoN missions, to be sure. But DoN missions, particularly The Creator, has a nice little benefit to wizards -- we can solo it, which means we can gaurantee a group for it 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Its amazing how fast a group can form when you are the driving force of the group. If you have several computers, get your friend's passwords and log 2 or 3 in to soak crystals while you take 2 or 3 other dps and do the mission, and triple your crystal return. If the 70 version hits to hard to make it safe, do the level 65 version by ensuring that the group's makeup is < 68 average. Mobs hit for 1/2 as much but you only lose 3 crystals.

    Make it work, and work at it, and the returns come quickly. In just 2 months of doing Creators with a random 4 people after raids, I got my bard box 21 slots + 20 augs, myself 13 augs (the 55 and 50 hp/mana ones), sold 400,000pp worth of crystals & TS items to barters, and gave nearly 2000 crystals to my guildmates. The only reason I gave the bard any loot at all was because the amount of crystals that were coming into the bazaar every night was depressing the buy price more than I liked. But still, even with just DoN gear my bard is geared equivalently or better than most Time geared bards, with 11khp raid buffed. More than that though, I developed a social network which allows me to ensure that I can always get a DoD progression group -- people think of me because I was there for them when they needed an EXP group over the months before DoD came out.


    As for MPG trials, the social network you build by having a solid exp option for people (kited camps) is gonna get you into trials when you're ready to survive them. And you're gonna have to do a lot of them, to be sure. I've done ~25 fear trials (and lots of others) helping get guild mates the items they want from it, and I have a trial-aholic friend who's done over 150 trials -- but he has everything he wants from trials, too. The trick is getting with a group of people who are capable, and perhaps building an "all trials, all the time" channel with them, and keep after them so you can always have a trial group set up. Success breeds success, just make sure you're dealing with people who keep trying, so you wont miss an item you need. With luck, by the halfway point in your trial press, you can build yourself into groups where if item X drops, its default to you.


    On LDoN: There is an entire culture of high level raiders who want to finish off thier LDoN stone, and finding these people will help you get groups built to do the LDoNs, as well as get yourself known to quality players, which will help in MPG and DoD. Doing LDoNs is a great way to break into the racket. If you're grouped with Qvic+ players, offer to shroud to 60 and make the LDoN easier (just 2 people need to shroud down to get a level 65 mission). Sure you're not gonna offer much DPS, but you're also in a unique position to have high level raiders beholden to you for your help.


    I avoided DoD gear while doing my list because it is getting upgraded and we wont know what it looks like for a while yet, but let me just say it also has this MPG/Grandmaster synergy, offering lots of options to put 130-150+ gear in slots you might not have yet. Its a great option right now if you're looking to avoid a few hundred DoN missions, and thats not even counting the upgrade that is coming. The missions are hard, to be sure -- but all you have to do is win 1 mission that offers and upgrade every 2 weeks to keep up with the "raid quota" and if you were working DoD every day each week, that would certainly be the case. Many of the missions (hive particularly) are wizard soloable / group kitable. I did 69x2 on hard with a mage pet tank, and that mission can drop the 1100 DD clicky which is a great boon to non-raiders. I expect the list will change drastically when the DoD upgrade comes through, and make it even easier for non-raiders to reach qvic+ levels of gearing.


    All in all, it comes down to the fact that if your putting in the time and effort, the options are available for major progression, to a point where you'd be known on the server for your level of gearing, and wouldn't be a detriment to any group.
