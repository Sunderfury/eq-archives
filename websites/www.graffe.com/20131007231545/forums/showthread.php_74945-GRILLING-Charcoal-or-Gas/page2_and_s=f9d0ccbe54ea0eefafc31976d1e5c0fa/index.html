    Re: GRILLING: Charcoal or Gas?

    Charcoal > Gas > No Grill

    That said, I almost always Gas Grill for convenience reasons.

    And those people saying they can taste propane are mainly tasting a psycho-somatic effect. Propane is so insanely volatile that it evaporates almost instantly, so while some small amount of unburnt propane can absorb into the food during cooking, the majority of that is almost-instantly released once the lid is opened on the grill. To really remove any possibility, just let the meat rest after grilling. Which should be done for juiciness's sake anyways.

    Re: GRILLING: Charcoal or Gas?

    Well, I upgraded the BBQ my wife and I got as a wedding gift. the miniscule 2 burner Thermos Brand LP BBQ is still on the back patio until I decide what to do with it, but it now has a slightly better 4 Burner + 1 Backyard Grill (Walmart) BBQ sitting next to it.

    Nothing especially fancy. Walmart started their BBQ sales today. I kept looking for a combo Charcoal/Gas or one of the Charbroil ones that has the smoker box on the side. Just couldn't find anything, so because this one wasn't very expensive ($150), I picked it up without very much hesitation.

    Actually, i was just going to get a 3 burner +1 for 128, but my wife pointed out that the one we picked up had cast-iron grill plates. THen I saw a bag of lava rocks sitting next to it, and my brain really started clicking.

    I don't know if I'll mod it, but I do remember my parents BBQ having lava rocks (I believe it was a craftsman) and it was just fantastic results. We picked up a couple bags of lava rocks, and I might take out those silly tent-like drip trays, and put a rock grate over top, and see what the lava rocks are like. There seems to be a pretty enthusiastic minority out there who does this on the net. PLus, when using smoke chips over a gas grill, especially with indirect heat, the results are much better when done over lava rocks, so I'm really hoping this will provide some fantastic results.

    **edit** Nearly forgot to point out the results of the first grill! **edit**

    Went to my local Carniceria and picked up some Asada and Bistec Pollo. The insulation on that grill is insane. The amount of heat it retains and blasts out as soon as you open the top is just ridiculous. I think it gave me a sunburn.

    THe old grill we had, would cook what I picked up in about 20 minutes. What I put on the grill tonight, it took 10. ANd to be quite honest, that was severely over doing it. I could have gotten away with 2, maybe 3 minutes, as thin as that asada and bistec is.
