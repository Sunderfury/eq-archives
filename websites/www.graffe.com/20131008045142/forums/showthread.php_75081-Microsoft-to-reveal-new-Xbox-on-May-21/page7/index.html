    Originally Posted by Yaxa

    Sony is very aggressively advertising it as a gaming console very specifically to counter the idea that the Xbox is a media console. You see a lot of it in every interview they have done lately; they see the weakness in Microsoft's PR and are exploiting the heck out of it. Their entire strategy is to capture the early adopter core gaming crowd and let word of mouth spread from there, while Microsoft is trying to have their cake and eat it too by going straight for the mass market. They have faith the people they have hooked on Live will migrate to their new platform. Even in the reveal, Sony only talked a little bit about sharing with social networks, and spent most of the time talking about how they reached out to developers to make the best gaming experience possible.

    And while Sony has managed to keep all their big guns secret, it has been revealed that several free to play PC games are coming to the PS4 already. Planetside 2 was the big one, but they also have Warframe and that DC mmo. I think this is an indication of their strategy.

    If you do want to still be skeptical, you can always keep in mind that it is a marketing strategy. They believe they need to market the device as a gaming platform, so that is what they are doing.

    Re: Microsoft to reveal new Xbox on May 21

    Its interesting - both companies want the same thing from their console but are going about it completely differently. Both want their machine to be the centre of your front room and the hub for what you do.

    Microsoft have seen that 50% of XBox usage is now the multi-media stuff rather than games, and so is assuming that will migrate over and they now have a large market of people who want multi-media and not games.

    Sony believe that games sell machines and the multi-media stuff follows - if you have Netflix on your console you'll use it, but you won't buy a console for Netflix.

    Two opposite approaches. Personally I think (and hope) Sony are right here simply because it puts the emphasis on games rather than shitty TV hookups that don't actually work outside of the USA>

    Re: Microsoft to reveal new Xbox on May 21

    Apparently those constant quick time events you saw in the Ryse demonstration are completely optional. It doesn't matter if you hit the wrong button, it doesn't matter if you put the controller down and don't hit any button at all. The animation still executes, you still get the kill. The only thing that changes is you don't get an exp bonus, and maybe the canned animation is different. When asked, the rep said they "didn't want players to feel frustrated". I'm sure the transition from kinect to gamepad style game is partly to blame, but this sounds like a pretty bad idea to me.

    Killer Instinct is also getting a lot of hate. Turns out it is being made by Double Helix, who are known for versions of other franchises that fans hated (Silent Hill Homecoming, Front Mission Evolved, etc). If you subscribe to Live, you get the game and one character free, and have to pay to unlock additional characters. This is actually something that several fighting games are attempting (Dead or Alive, and I think Tekken?), but as a much anticipated comeback it's causing fans to react harshly in this particular game.
