    Re: Who Here is Gonna Play Elder Scrolls Online?

    Why are you guys talking about story in an Elder Scrolls game? It amuses me to imagine an mmo having a better story than it's single player equivalent.

    Originally Posted by Balthis
    So many people in game complaining that Box +15 is way too much to pay for a game. How many years have we been paying that for WoW? It was ok then, but not now? I think it will be worth it at least once to get a toon to level cap to experience the story line and gameplay. How they do the endgame will be the big decider.
    WoW's box never cost $60. Heck, did it even cost $50 after that first year? $20 might not seem like much, but when both your time and your money is on a premium, it makes a difference. It's only when you stick to a single genre (in this case, mmos), when the distinction loses meaning, because you don't have as many games competing for your time.

    Granted, an mmo can be worth buying the box for if you get good value out of your first month, but for every person that spends $60 on that shiny new 10 hour single player game, there are a dozen other people waiting for a better price. It's the same for mmos. I know The Secret World got a ton of new players when it went on sale. The problem then becomes that while a single player game's level of fun doesn't change based on when you get it, an mmo is usually more fun when it's at its most expensive. The desire to play at a lower cost leads to griping.


    I do feel your pain though. The chat during a beta is almost always intolerable. Even when they are saying good things, they are saying good things in order to criticize something else.
