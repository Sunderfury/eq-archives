    Nope, gender/race lock is here to stay. Also an Asian MMO staple.

    I know there are more classes to be implemented. Looks like there's a lot more available on the Korean servers. Odd that they weren't ready to switch everything right away. Is there more than just language issues?
    They're patching the game in the same order that the original versions were patched. The classes will get there, but, yes, it's gated.

    I have no idea how the node system is supposed to work


    or how those conversations work.


    Sometimes, I will get smiley faces for every person I put in there and gain 0 amity. Also, it seems that if I try and go more than two times in a row I lose whatever amity I built up, regardless of how well I did on the third try. Pretty confusing system, although I like the idea of getting to know NPC's better and then they give you more options.
    Most important fact that helped me in the conversation minigame: Meeting the goal at the top of the screen allows you to continue the conversation without spending energy, as he points out in the guide.
    Sometimes those goals are counter-intuitive: Fail to spark interest 3 times, for example, will give you next to no amity - but it WILL allow you to continue on to the next round, where the requirements are probably different.

    Another fact that's also easy to miss: The NPCs you use in the game have both an interest level AND an average favor level. Some NPCs will give you 100% interest chance, but only 2-4 favor. They're extremely useful for the "Spark interest 5 consecutive times" goals, but contributes very little to overall amity gain. Using a 70% interest 30-40 NPC, for example, is usually a better idea in the overall favor scheme.

    One of the harder goals (IMO) is one where you have to gain "maximum favor", which (as I understand it) is the individual favor roll of a NPC. When this goal is active, you sometimes have to choose a somewhat low-interest, high-favor NPC if that's the only NPC with a chance of making the favor roll. I still don't understand this fully.

    Also note that the threshold for interest changes every time you start a conversation. The higher your amity level, the higher the threshold, and the more difficult the conversation will be. Unless they're at a really low threshold, and you're told to fail 5 consecutive times.
    This actually happened to me

    Last conversation-related thing: Conversations always stop after round 3, no matter how well you did.

    - - - - - -

    Go through all of the 2-minute guides here. Very much worth it. Straight and to the point.
    Black Desert is not the most transparent of games - a fact that already lead me to drop the game once - but once you get the hang of it... man, it's addicting.

    The other stuff that he's complaining about mechanically - I haven't gotten those issues. I'm running on a relatively old system that I bought for playing GW2 - so the base machine is from 2012 (i7, 8GB ram).
    I did buy a GeForce 950 GTX specifically because I loved the way that Black Desert looked, but that's the only upgrade I've made.
    Graphics do "pop in", and sometimes you'll see NPCs as strange black clay-like men before the skin loads.

    Loading times were OK on a standard 7200 RPM drive, but since I change characters ever so often (and he's right about the 10-second wait period being annoying), I did clean out my SSD to make space for the game.

    One thing that I don't think he mentions - I did skip part of the video - is the fact that you have to wait 10 minutes after a character change to use a trade manager. That is pretty damn annoying, since I'll sometimes send stuff via wagon from one city to another where I have an alt for trading - and they can't just log in, get the stuff from the bank and run to the trade manager.

    That's another thing that annoys me: Trading, the skill, is character bound. If the game really wants you to see your characters as a family, having the trading skill bound to the account would not be too much of a stretch.
    If I really want to make a profit (and make sure that I actually get a decent level of trading), I have to ride to the specific city with my main. I should probably just say "Fuckit!" and use my main alt for trading in Calpheon.
