    I know the answer is Captain America, but as a 32 year old male, black leather suit hacker girl would be preferable.....

    But that is the point though, the show is in a world with villains that are so powerful that they pretty much require a superhero to beat them. This show is about a team in an organization that involves said superheroes, an organization above any normal law enforcement agency, meaning their jobs also would be above any normal law enforcement agency, which again then means that the people involved in it should have abilities to set them apart from any law enforcement agency. As it stands now, you have the equivalent of three CIA agents (Cole, Grant and May) and three NSA tech people (two lab rats and Skye) being the strike team towards "unknown" missions that might lead them face to face with what SHIELD does, fighting super villains that is a threat to the world.

    That isn't saying that Whedon won't be able to make a good show, with good characters, but that will be the only thing that saves it then if the action in the show will always be limited to the limitations of the current characters. Because at best their team can be trained to be 6x May, which seems so far to be the indicated strongest fighter, but that is unrealistic considering the long time it takes to hone such skills, so May + Grant will always be their two main fighters. Unsure of the full abilities of May still, but Grant would get destroyed by any super villain that I have seen in the Marvel universe.

    I'd say that Whedon is very fond of melee combat. All vampires from Buffy and Angel (even if just made into one) apparently had the innate ability to use martial arts and even in Firefly the guns used were limited, while the real action sequences came from River. However, in this series, they are fighting people who nearly all carry guns, at least their guards. It will turn fairly generic after a while if we keep seeing the agents encounter a groups of guards that for some reason engage in melee combat instead of stepping back and opening fire, or open fire and miss in close range even though they are supposedly highly trained guards.

    If the SHIELD agents never get better by evolving their abilities, by mutation or something else, then this show seems to be very limited in its scope compared to the name. Very wasted potential.
    So if it is true then that they can't use all Marvel related features, then.... Stupid copyright crap ruining things once again. Imagine the creation of just ... things, movies, music, technology, etc, that would be possible, and would happen, if copyright laws were abolished and you rather created something and you get to sell that, but others could also build on what you did. /sigh

    Corporations owning patents and copyright laws. Stifling creativity since....
    *shakes fist at gnome lawyer*

    As to the series, the one hope I have then, if any sort of mutating super power is out of the question for any the characters, is that they will use their tech experts and the availability of alien technology that exists in the Marvel world. They already showed now that they have the ability for X-ray vision. So could have episodic progression where they gain new abilities by finding new tech, which then would give them the ability to take on enemies fitting a Marvel world.

    Question though, are the Avengers copyrighted someone else too then? So they can never have a special episode or season finale where perhaps Ironman or The Hulk made an appearance? Could have a continuing story arch in the last 4 episodes. Bruce Banner comes on board to help with tech, May and him fall in love, May turns out to be a double agent for the inevitable evil organization they will face. Last episode ends on the plane after a fight, lights are out, plane on autopilot since May and them are in the lab, gas is filling the room. Cole shoots and kills May in front of Bruce, Bruce screams, and you see him fall to his knees, a growl of angry, then a massive figure starts to rise from the gassy smoke. Episode ends with a yell of HULK SMASH with a short light flicker illuminating the Hulk, the door of the plane is ripped open and you see Ironman, camera pans out to see the plane heading towards what appears to be the portal for summoning from other worlds like Valhalla, then episode ends.

    I'd kickstarter a season 2 to see the continuation of that.

    (this was a very nerdy post)
