    Re: EQ3 - IMA BACK BEOTCHES

    Yes, LoY, the expansion that proved two things:

    (1) Don't place zones at the far side of several difficult-to-travel zones if you expect them to be used.
    (2) Especially don't do this if the vast majority of the zones aren't for max level characters.
    (3) And really don't do this if the expansion doesn't have some easy-to-farm BiS items to sell to twinks.

    I explored it a fair bit with D, since I liked going to places that weren't popular with either the masses or the elites (I did a few levels solo in Kaesora on D). It was a good expansion, with some neat features. But like hell anyone was going to either make a premade group and schlep all the way out there, or go there hoping to find a PUG.

    I think the devs needed time to catch up after PoP. They were rushing to fix bugs ahead of the raiders all the way to Time. I think they also wanted to experiment with instancing, since other MMOs were playing with it. You loved LDoN? I thought you were one of the people who hated it because of Morden Rasp? I loved the dungeons. The leaderboard was fun for players in competitive guilds like me, and people like my buddy (casual player) loved the ability to log on, get a group very quickly, and play, and have a fair chance at loot items in addition to the points stuff. LDoN's only failing was the raids. They were tuned well above most players, to the point that guilds that could do raid X had such good gear that they needed nothing that dropped in raid X. This was one of the first raids that my guild, which had long since cleared Time, finally managed to down after more than a few attempts:

    http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/q...tml?quest=4168

    130 HP/MANA items for a Time guild? Really? I pity the guild going in there that actually would have gotten some use from that.

    GoD forgot what PoP knew: players at all levels of organization need to have content in an expansion. GoD was not only horribly tuned for raiders (due to the five level thing), it had almost no content for non-progression raiders. There was nifty stuff in the expansion: the tradeskill items were amazing, and Inktu'ta was my favourite raid zone in the entire game. But all the good stuff was locked away behind several layers of shit (single group trials, Uq'ua, the Ikkinz "did you bring one of every class" raid, etc.)
