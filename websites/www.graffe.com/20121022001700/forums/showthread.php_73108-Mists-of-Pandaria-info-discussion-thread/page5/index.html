    Hrm. I really don't like the idea of yet another spec grabbing healer gear. Let's pretend, for sake of argument, that some other DPS stat was used instead. Have Demo locks been so completely redesigned that they'd never be expected to cast a spell with a casting time when tanking, like Hand of Gul'Dan? Casters lose all evasion when casting.

    Originally Posted by Dawlin
    I don't see that: If you can keep enough demonic fury up via spells, it can be a permanent state.
    So they're turning a ranged spec into a melee spec? Yuck. At least people won't have to deal with half-and-half. I'd be disappointed, though. I've always loved Demonology, but I'd hate to play it as a melee spec.

    Originally Posted by Dawlin
    Just because the developers have a vision about something, doesn't mean that the playerbase changes overnight into courteous, non-fire-standing decent DPS'ers.
    It's not quite that. Let me see if I can get it across with some examples:

    (1) Mana pool change. Eliminates possibly the only easy identifier for a character with better gear, in much the way that higher total health generally identifies better-geared tanks. It also eliminates much of the advantage of better gear in content where mana pool size would matter, since healers don't have the toolbox necessary to change what heals they cast when as their spellpower increases.

    (2) Lightwell. The problem with Lightwell was never ease of use, or throughput, or minutiae with how it worked. The problem was that it replaced the healer's judgement on when to heal someone with the non-healer's. It took part of the healer's job away from the healer.

    (3) The healing changes. The healing changes didn't create exciting new gameplay for healers. The healing changes reduced the prior gameplay into a resource management model with two massively important stats, INT and SPI, and one tertiary one, haste. It was done to make playbalancing encounters easier, not to make healing fun.

    (4) The nerf to the duration of Power Word: Shield. The primary motivation for this was eliminating the ability to pre-Shield players in five man content. No thought was given to the role of PW:S in healer spec leveling.

    Can you see what I'm getting at here? Healing issues are being looked at from the point of view of someone who wants to be healed (my "second box" comment), or someone who wants to design encounters. They're not being looked at by someone who wants to have fun as a healer. You run into the same thing when you try to level as a healing spec, where making sure the healers can't deal DPS spec-level DPS was more of a concern than creating a system where healers can level at close to the same pace as any other spec.
