    Re: Mists of Pandaria info/discussion thread.

    Originally Posted by Delores Mulva
    Do you know if accounts with multiples of a given pet will get access to all of them when the pets are first merged into account-level? Like, if Bob has a Dark Whelpling and Mary has a Dark Whelpling, when the account with Bob and Mary on it gets the global pet update will it have access to two Dark Whelplings? I assume not, since again lazy solution, but as someone with rare duplicate pets on multiple alts it'd sure be nice, and since they are allowing you to own multiples of a given pet and to trade them off it's something they could do within the framework of what they're already doing. It wouldn't work for the mounts, since they're not letting you own multiples or sell ones that you have, so I wouldn't even hope for that (even though I'd get several thousand gold just from things like multiples of the Wyrmrest dragon mount).
    Short answer, yes. You can have up to 3 duplicates of each pet in the pet interface.

    So here's what currently happens in beta. You log onto toon A and get all his pets. Log into toon B and all his pets get merged, you end up with duplicates of any pet you had on both toons. Same for toon C, only now you have triplicates. Now, when toon D logs in his pets are merged. But if he has a pet you already have triplicates of it's automatically caged and put into your bags. I'm unsure what will happen if you have lots of 4+ pets and not enough inventory space though.

    Originally Posted by Delores Mulva
    One thing I'm wondering about with pets and mounts: what happens when characters are moved between accounts? I have ten alts on account A. I move one alt to an empty account. Does that alt take a copy of all the pets and mounts with him? What if the account had characters on it - do they get a new set of pets and mounts equal to the sum of the two? And what about achievement pets/mounts that were achieved at the account level, but not by the character being moved? Do they keep them (I assume not)? They haven't updated the character move info on the official website, which currently would give answers of "yes except for store stuff, yes except for store stuff from the moved character, yes". I wonder if they know what they want to do with this?
    One thing they have not clarified is the definition of 'account'. Do they mean wow account or battle.net account? If achievements are at the battle.net account level then all your wow accounts get merged under one giant total. If it stops at the wow account level, and they each have their own set of achievements, then I would have to guess that moving a character between accounts would not allow him to take any pets / mounts / achievements with him on the move. Since they are bound to the account, not a character. But that is entirely a guess on my part. While I technically have 2 accounts under one battle.net, one is inactive, so I can't test this.

    Originally Posted by Naean
    What would you do with multiples of the same pet available to all your characters?
    Because you can cage most pets, meaning make them a inventory item and tradable again. This allows you to sell them off. You can even sell off pets that have been leveled in the new pet battle system and they retain their levels.

The only exception is at the early part of an expansion when you're required to kill Lord Bob and 15 people are sitting on his spawn AOEing. Then it's group up and disband after 30 seconds. And honestly? The only reason you have to group for these quests is because of the other people, remove those other people (like on a low population server) and it's a non-issue.

    If I have time I'll give you some zone stats tomorrow from my server, Bronzebeard, during the day. I think you'll be surprised at how many wee people are out and about (and I don't mean sitting in a capital waiting on some sort of queue).

    RealID allows people to be grouped cross-server for raids now, in addition to five mans. Like I said last post, yes, it still relies on RealID, but you can have "raiders" with you who aren't on your server.
    Cross-server raiding is disabled for current tier raiding. I don't see ANY guilds with the following recruiting message "Guild recruiting for previous raiding tiers. We are NOT accepting members for current raids as we have no desire to do them at all. We are only doing Current Tier Minus One and older raids." So again, no, cross-server relationships found through merged zones does absolutely zero to help guilds on a low population server achieve critical mass and have a pool of players to recruit from to replenish depleted ranks.

    They don't want to close servers because that could cause a stampede for the door (omg closing servers, game is dying). It also is a hassle if, in fact, numbers start to pick back up again, like I imagine they will for MoP release. So they're going with solutions that don't panic the playerbase and which automatically adjust no matter which way the player population goes. It makes sense that they'd go that way. The issue is if they're going to have any success with it, and that'll depend on the details.
    It's time to yank the band-aid off. They've been ignoring it for years and hoping that it will fall off on it's own (i.e. just waiting and letting people spend their cash to transfer off).

    And while MOP will help numbers, it's not going to be a permanent solution to subscription losses. The only reason the numbers have been as good as they are is because they're pulling out all the stops for short term gains to make their investor numbers look good. The real telling mark from last investors meeting was that while subscriptions didn't change, their quarterly revenue decreased by a massive amount. That tells me that Western subscription numbers are going down while they are still gaining in the Asian market. But because of the Asian Market dynamics, an Asian subscriber is worth less than a Western subscriber. Now all indications are that MOP is simply more of the same. So you'll see a boost of people buying the expansion, playing through the content and quitting again after the first quarter. WOW will never hit 12 million again and even with MOP around the corner my guess is that it will continue to bleed subs until it hits the 6 to 7 million mark (it won't happen overnight and it might take a couple years but, again, WOW's glory days are behind it). A huge success by any measure but enough people are bored with WOWs stale game model and are looking for something new.

    Face it, cross-server realms do nothing to fix the real issues of a low population server. If anything it makes things worse for a low population server. One of the only advantages that a low population server has is that questing is very easy because there is no competition for quest objectives. Now even that advantage has been taken away. While I never would have transferred to a low population server for it, things would have been MUCH easier for me when I decided to camp my Time-Lost Proto Drake and Aeonaxx. Having little to no competition would have made those a lot easier.
