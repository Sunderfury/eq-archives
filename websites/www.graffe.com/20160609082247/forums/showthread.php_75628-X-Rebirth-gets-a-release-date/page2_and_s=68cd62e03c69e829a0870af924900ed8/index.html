    Re: X Rebirth gets a release date

    So, I went ahead and got this and have been playing for a little bit now. I haven't ever played the other games so this is coming from someone who never had any other experiences to compare my experience to. My initial impressions:

    Graphics: Very beautiful. As good as the trailers. Yes, not having a 3rd person perspective is kind of a throwback, but if immersion is what they want I understand why they did it. The problem I have with this is...
    Trading: ...trading. As Silver said, this is done in about the most confusing way possible. Hell, it took me 10 minutes just to figure out that when somebody said they needed supplies delivered somewhere that I needed to actually go make/mine/whatever the goods as well. Not to mention they provide absolutely zero information about how to do this or where to get the materials. Granted, maybe I was doing this too early in the game because I spent all my money hiring a defense officer. So I overspent there, fine, but the tutorial probably should have gone over some simple ways to make money before throwing situations like this in the game. Still, that is a relatively minor issue and can be filed under "Don't be a stupid dumbass with your money".
    UI: Mediocre. It works, it's somewhat functional although the way they do the menus is a pain in the ass. Oh, if you are going to have a bunch of hotkeys bound to something then there should be a way to check to see what those keys are outside of the one sentence of explanation that comes in the tutorial. I managed to fire off 2 wasted missiles trying to figure how to switch missile types since I missed the part where the copilot explained it. Silver is also correct, the map is completely useless. Even after finally figuring out where it was located it did absolutely no good. Speaking of the copilot....
    Voice Acting: Not since that abortion of a game that was Star Ocean IV have I heard voice acting this bad. Although, this was probably worse since it sounds like to save money one of the game developers just brought in their 14 year old daughter to read lines of the script. The voice acting for the main character is just as bad. Honestly, if they just killed all the voice acting altogether and went back to text chat it would be a huge improvement. At least then I could use my imagination as to what they sound like and not have to deal with bring your kids to work day voice acting.

    I'm not sure how they plan on fixing these things but clearly the making of this game was epically mismanaged. Silver said this game was in development for 7 years? Obviously there was a poor manager in charge.

    Edit: One other thing, given how often the game just randomly crashes an autosave feature would be nice. It's not 1996 anymore.

    Re: X Rebirth gets a release date

    My copy arrived last night so I fired it up for about 3 hours.

    Initial thoughts... now I can see what people meant by this game having some of the ugliest female characters. The co-pilot is ok, but does every female in the future have to have a shaved head, and why does no one smile.

    Stations are way too dark and samey. Though at least there isn't too much walking around needed inside them. Looting all the boxes and lockers felt very much like borderlands, but with zero point. I'm picking stuff up (well I assume I am) but I don't know what it is or where its going. The tutorial doesn't explain beyond encouraging me to take anything that isn't nailed down.

    A couple of hours in I was instructed to go and find a particular type of professional, but it was up to me to track someone down. So then I discovered the joy of a) not being able to target major installations but only being able to find them visually, and then b) Not having a clue how to locate entrances (and a station can have a lot of different ones). Finally I figure out that all the little (i) marks that are visible a long way off are modules that can be scanned, though not used in any other way. But it's not until I'm a lot closer that I can see things like docking areas. And different docking areas seem to have different icons so I can't even easily tell whether something is failing to respond because its not meant to, or if I'm just not clicking it right, and of course I have to land to know what people I will find inside. Also, I have to be within 500 meters to land, which on the scale of these stations means I've basically parked up myself anyway.

    So I hired my specialist, and picked up an engineer too as the game had suggested it was a good idea. Then I unload both into my freighter, but then decide my engineer is better placed on my own ship... and I could not for the life of me figure out how to get someone to come back aboard. She's standing there right in front of me but she's only willing to tell me her skills (which are now finally visible).

    Space is pretty, love flying around. I can even handle the flightpath minigame (but I can see looking up the arse of other spaceships getting old seeing as thats usually the most featureless bit).

    Menu system is a horrid abomination of a thing. At one point I had to go through about 8 options to ask how to find a drone dealer. It was insane. Oh, and when I finally did find the option, the person I was asking told me where to shove it, as did the next two people I asked.

    Final thought, I have to look away from the screen when I walk back to my chair to depart a station. It's like being on a ship in a storm.
