    Re: Legion

    The game systems summary: http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/...n-Game-Systems

    The very short Q&A summary: http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/...f-Warcraft-Q-A

    Things that stand out to me, first from the Q&A:

    -multiple guilds for one character. Combining that with the "better cross-server communication" will hopefully happen, since it would be amazing, especially given declining numbers. Stay in your original friend guild, raid with guild #2, and cross-server PvP with guild #3 because your server doesn't do PvP much? Awesomesauce.
    -the tapping thing is long-overdue. Something like "first person gets non-quest loot, full XP, and all quest stuff, everyone else gets quest credit/loot and XP based on %damage dealt" would be great.
    -Heh, "no flying for j00"

    -Artifact power comes from BGs and Arenas, huh? It's like they don't remember all the bitching from the PvP parts of the violet proto-drake. I doubt it won't be required in some manner, given that a lot of the other sources of artifact power look to be one-time things (quest bosses, rare drops, rare mobs). Unless, of course, they have some system like in Tanaan where you kill rare mobs endlessly, but only get credit once per day. That should be just as much fun as the Tanaan system! (Not at all.)
    -The "smaller power increases after that" means the weapon is an endless grind, right? I'm reading that correctly - do whatever increases power every day/week to get a little bit more? They found a way to put AA points in the game, way to go!

    -I see they came up with a handwaving reason why Moonglade isn't the druid home base. It's too bad they didn't think to, oh, I don't know, change the giant sections of the zone that currently have zero associated content to match their vision for a druid home.
    -"Hunters gain a special flight path network!" They really don't get the whole flight thing, do they?
    -A lot of the other class hall features sound neat. It's too bad the whole thing will go in the trash next expansion, as expansion-specific features like the Scouting Map make obvious.

    -I'm with Dawlin on the "WTF, no tank option info" thing. I mean, seriously - they "maybe" have parry? Some things you should know, and that's one of them.
    -The other class stuff sounds, from the perspective of someone who loved playing different alts, terrible. A lot of things that were class- or spec-defining - hunters being ranged, Demonology and Metamorphosis, Disc and shielding - are gone. In some cases, they've been handed to specs that never asked for them, like the near-Metamorphosis that shadow priests will get. People want tweaks, not full f'ing retcons, and they've been telling you that in every expansion.
    -That said, there's a couple of nice tweaks. Streamlined runes for DKs - good. Arcane not having to play a bizarre mana management game - good, although the Arcane mastery increasing mana pool is a very bad sign. So long, Honor Among Thieves, never wanted you. Guardian Druids becoming full-blown mitigation tanks - great stuff.
    -That Subtlety change to Shadow Dance - is that going to make the spec as frantic and random to play as it seems? Every GCD, you'd need to see if you were in stealth, when presumably better options for damage would exist than when not in stealth. I never played Sub, so I can't really judge what that does to the playstyle.
    -"100s of new talents!" Yay, more massive changes for the sake of change! It will be interesting to see what they come up with, though, and the potential is there for it to be really cool.

    -The recipes thing sounds like Vanilla, where instead of going to the trainer in town you had to find the trainer somewhere out in the world. A little unfortunate, since that directly impacts what zones you want to start leveling in, instead of following whatever sounded coolest.
    -Shared nodes, which presumably means personal nodes (each person has their own "node phase" in a zone)? Sounds awesome.
    -Learned and Unlearned tab? VERY nice.
    -Obliterum worries me. Will crafters end up making crap just to obliterate it to make better stuff? How will that impact things like the AH and Enchanting? And will it be remotely fun taking mats, making something, destroying that something, and then taking more mats to make something better, as opposed to just using a bunch of mats and skipping the destroying step?
    -Inscription as week-long consumables, with no limit on how much stuff you can boost? I used to make a lot of gold off Inscription, and I feel dread just reading that description. Seriously - Inscription was already a high-gold profession, it didn't need this in the least, and adding a bunch of micro-management to everyone in the game doesn't sound remotely fun. Hopefully this dies in beta.
    -The Arch changes sound very vague, which means they have no idea what to do. It will still suck, because of the massive hate-on they have for flying.
    -It sounds like they understand HOW Fishing needs to change, but there's no indication of WHAT will be done to change it. Which, again, means they don't know.

    -The random world loot stuff sounds awful. I mean, at some point someone should explain to the devs that they can, without loss of generality, replace the word "random" with the word "terrible" in all their descriptions and it'll still be correct. "It could be the best loot in the game!" - really? I want to spend weeks and weeks in the magic spot, grinding away on mobs in the hope that the spec-defining item drops, while other people show up and get it on their first kill? How will those "I only want to raid" friends of yours feel about this, dev team? Fire every person on the team that ever played Everquest, it's the only way to get garbage like this out of the game for good.
    -I'm on the fence about the duplicate personal loot thing. It's going to be great when you're grouped with friends. It's going to suck hearing "plllllllzzzzzzz you have better" whenever something drops in a random group, especially if the system doesn't take into account "same ilvl, better results" items.
    -The Transmog system sounds worlds and worlds better. Should free up a pile of space for folks, and give a great system for customizing look (no more shoulders). I think the only thing I didn't see listed that could make it better is a "preview" pane when you're picking a transmog, that changes your look as you mouse over the different options. If you have to pick from, potentially, hundreds of different items when looking for a new look, it would speed things up tremendously if you could see the new look as you mouse over the options, rather than having to pick an option to see how it looks. Who knows, it may work like that already, and I just don't know. But this is an amazing set of changes overall, and is a great example of "when Blizzard gets something right, they get it RIGHT".

    Re: Legion

    Yeah, some of those legendaries looked terrible. I suppose they could have been "throw something in that's hugely OP to drive excitement among non-theorycrafting players, then claw it back during Beta" things, but still, why do this?

    There's more specifics on Wowhead: http://www.wowhead.com/news=249804/d...-class-changes

    -mana being gone for non-Resto Shamans - not as huge as it seems. My Enhancement shaman would only notice mana if she started to chain heals on someone. I hope this doesn't mean that all ability to do stuff like that is gone, but given the direction they've been taking with the healer DPS specs it wouldn't surprise me.
    -all warlocks are back on a soul shard system - sounds terribly lame. The idea behind demonology summoning waves of demons is OK, but I feel like there's too many negatives in practice (the usual problems with pets in combat, lost attack time while the demons close distance, being able to keep track of when demons are up/down) and they'll work "as DoTs, but much crappier". I look forward to hearing all about how Demonology is bad for raids because they cause lag, too.
    -the "This may be a more complex healing spec now" for Disc is a big understatement. Healing is about healing who I want, when I want, in an amount I can control to some degree. Tell me how I can, for example, heal only the melee DPS group as an off-healer with a damage to healing system, or how I can switch from that role to add heals to the tank if I see them going low. What, it doesn't work well at that? Worthless!
    -"With Marksmanship hunters, we saw most take Lone Wolf talent so we said hey let's go all the way there and remove the pet." That is so misguided. They made a talent that absolutely punished the damage of MM hunters who didn't take it, and then used that to decide that MM didn't want pets? Echo chamber, indeed, or at least a poor understanding of cause and effect.
    -healers doing more damage: it's good that they understand that people like to level as healers. It's good that they're going to try and make that less of a chore. I like that they specifically mentioned Resto Druids getting Thorns back, since pincushioning is a good model for healer DPS increases. Given past experiences with the devs saying "healer DPS is better", and then seeing changes that didn't help much at all ("Judgement damage is HUGE, guys! Trust us!"), I'll believe it when I see it.
    -longer CD on PW:S - this goes to the "leveling will be easier as a healer, promise!" thing, but I hope they understand how important PW:S is to a leveling priest right now.
    -everyone having access to every spec is cool. In practice, I didn't spec swap on most of my characters. But there were alts where I did, and there would have been times where being able to switch freely would have helped (like with PvP). I just hope the specs have some compatibility to them. It doesn't help me if Spec A has Stat A as best stat, and Spec B has Stat A as worst stat.
    -I like that Chakras are finally gone for Holy Priests. That's a good change. I don't like that Disc is losing PoH. Making one spec good at AE healing and one spec bad at it is NOT the way to differentiate the specs. All specs need to be roughly as good at the core demands of healers, or people will be forced to spec-change to meet group/raid needs and you have "first priest has to be Disc" all over again.

    Re: Legion

    Looked at the class blogs so far. From the perspective of the specs I'd normally play, PvE:

    Unholy DK: just having one disease is OK. But having to re-apply that disease every 21 seconds, as seems to be the case, is no good. I think I've said it before, but it bears repeating: the Unholy gameplay has always been frantically mashing buttons the moment they light up. Having to hold runes for things like Soul Reaper or re-applying diseases just isn't fun. I wish the devs would take that to heart. The long-term AE also looks a little soft - you have to use the disease applier to spread diseases (six second CD), no Boil Blood, and the AE spurt of damage when diseases fall off after 21 seconds is unlikely to matter. The burst AE damage looks really solid with Death and Decay's new "Scourge Strike hits everything in D&D" addition. The one new talent listed looks like a lot of fun.

    Guardian druid: I'm not sure how, exactly, the devs plan to balance a tank that takes less physical damage due to armour, and is healed more (40%!) by healing, with the other tank specs. I'm pretty sure I wouldn't want to be a Guardian tank, or the healer trying to keep them alive, during that tuning process. I like Thrash applying Lacerate, instead of its own bleed effect - it makes single-target stuff simpler. They didn't link anything consuming Lacerate, like Guardian has now, so I have to wonder what will fill GCDs - Thrash just for the hell of it? Ironfur/Mark of Ursol/Frenzied Regen are like the Shield Blocks of the Guardian world, and look to be just as annoying to micro-manage while tanking. Hooray, active tanking BS.

    Marksman Hunter: ugh, just ugh, to the pet being removed. Extra UGH for Aimed Shot being a "no movement" shot. Make a spec that relies on kiting to do anything, then staple them to the ground. But hey, you can take a talent to make them maybe sometimes be instan..o screw that. Marked Shot makes it clear that you WILL be using Aimed, in some sort of rotation very unlike the old Marks "what health is the target at" rotation. It's nice that they got an instant cast, always available shot back in Arcane, but this spec is nothing like it was, nothing like it ever was, and doesn't look remotely fun to play. But hey, at least the Mastery doesn't stink - they moved the crappy part of it into the core spec mechanics.

    Arcane Mage: the change to Arcane's Mastery is awesome, as is the UI change to arcane charges. This is a great example of taking a spec's core mechanics, tweaking them just a little to add a lot of quality of life, and making the spec markedly better. I'd still want to play one to see if Evocation on a 90 s. CD, plus the extra mana regen from Mastery, is good enough to keep you from running out of gas. But I would go into that playtesting highly optimistic, compared with the shitshow that is Marks Hunter.

    Mistweaver Monk: fistweaving gone, Chi gone. Ouch. So right away, one has to assess Mistweavers as a whole new spec, and not remotely related to what came before. That said, I like what I see. The Mastery is fixed from what it was, floating balls of useless. The Soothing Mist mechanic looks nifty, and maintains the visual style of the spec while removing some of the annoying channeling stuff from it. There's a good selection of heals for all occasions, and the big heal kept its HoT aspect from before. I would want to see how Mistweavers quest - how do they deal damage, how do they self-heal while doing it - and see that the spec can hold its own without a lot of the free healing it used to get. But I could see this setup working really well.

    Holy paladin: you're the healer that has to stand next to the person you're healing! "That's exactly what I wanted," said no Holy paladin ever! Seriously, what the fuck. Tank healing? Yay, I get to stand in front of the mob with the tankERSPLAT. They're reaching to create differences between the healers, and hopefully this experiment gets abandoned. Light of the Martyr doesn't excite me - the spec does have Holy Shock already - but I don't PvP.

    Holy priest/Disc priest: on D, I would heal as Holy, quest as Disc. The new Chakra-less Holy looks awesome. I like what they've done with the Holy Words, and I long thought that Holy should lose access to shadow stuff, even if that removes Holy's instant-cast ranged "tag" spell. I think the only thing I'd want to see in writing before declaring new Holy a success is an indication that the "direct heal refreshes Renew" mechanic is still in place, since that really was something that set Holy apart from other specs. Questing as Disc looks good. I like that the cooldown and duration on PW:S mean you can keep it up 24/7 unless it gets knocked off, and you have a good variety of damage spells. I wouldn't want to try and heal a group with that godawful mess, but for questing? Good stuff! (Note: both priest specs lose a little if the Holy Nova glyph is pulled from the game - I'm assuming every non-Shadow priest will get it as part of the glyph overhaul.)

    Enhancement shaman: "Danger, Will Robinson, danger!" The core mechanics listed are a little too "react to the random proc" for me, but they look fairly solid. But do you notice how there's nothing listed that works at range (well, over 10 yard range!), like the shocks/nukes you used to use would? No mention of the healing utility that the spec used to have? That's a big warning sign that the spec is being nerfed to hell and back, turned into just another melee range class. Blech to that. Also notice that you have no method of generating the super-important resource you need to do, well, anything, if you're further away than ten yards from the target. If I still played I'd be very unhappy at the direction the spec is being taken.

    Demonology warlock: speaking of being unhappy at the direction a spec is being taken, hello, Demonology! Hey, it doesn't matter that this one ability has been the CORE PART of your spec since TBC - it's needed to prop up a new class! But don't worry, we made sure to take the obnoxious part of Demonology - watching over two separate resource pools - and roll that into this new version! And here's a slap to the face: your Mastery? No, it doesn't improve YOUR damage, or even the damage of your pets directly, like it used to do. That's too easy! It improves the damage modifier of a BUFF you get to cast on your pets. And that shit drops off them in 12 seconds, and stacks, so you will be spending a LOT of time casting that crap. Because hitting buff buttons as part of core gameplay is fun! Just ask rogues about Slice and Dice, or shamans about Unleash Weapons, or the old warrior Shield Block, or... wait, what? All those things were removed from the game because they sucked so much? Hah, no luck for you Demonology, your spec will be considered a success if no raider ever has to play you! And they won't, since you rely so strongly on pet damage and a company that doesn't have the time to put flying in an expansion at release sure as hell won't be fixing bad pathing any time soon! And I didn't even tell you that re-summoning pets during combat will be a core mechanic of your class, hah!

    I suppose I should be happy that, hey, at least Hellfire is no longer the AE for the spec. Even though Demonwrath sounds worse.

    Protection warrior: some nice quality-of-life changes here. Revenge being full frontal arc is very nice. I also like how Spell Reflection is a proper "raid spell damage" CD for the warrior. I'm not a fan of Ignore Pain, but I'm also not a fan in general of active tanking. For those who like active tanking, I bet Ignore Pain will be all kinds of awesome. I like that the spec has regained "gain rage from damage taken", which it missed. This is a very solid retune, and while I imagine the numbers stuff will be a pain to balance (rage generation vs. Ignore Pain/Shield Block uptime), once tuned it should be a good tank spec. If you like active tanking.
