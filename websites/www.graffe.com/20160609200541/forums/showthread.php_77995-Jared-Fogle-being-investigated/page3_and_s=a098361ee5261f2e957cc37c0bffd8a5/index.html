    This ties back to the pornography situation in that it is a specific case which falls into the general concept of how censorship and more specifically attempting to prevent honest discourse affects a society's ability to make choices that are in its best interests. What I mean specifically is this: if people, particularly decision makers or people who do have influence over an outcome (voters do not in reality, at least in the US), never are exposed to real world data, they are subject to making all decisions which have such a constraint (no reliable data being available reflective of how a system actually behaves) then those decision makers will always be in the position where they are reaching a conclusion based solely on blind emotion.

    And when honest discourse or quality research is prevented on the basis of causing discomfort amongst a relevant power, then whatever outcome does follow will be exceedingly unlikely to have been one that is desirable. And pedophilia in particular is a really difficult problem to try and minimize, so it is important that whatever decision is made, it is done in an emotionally detached way so that when we observe what actually happens afterwards we are able to iterate upon that (any initial decision is probably not going to be a very good one regardless of efforts made beforehand due to so many unknowns in play). But, that can never happen if we have not identified why we are making a decision and what the expected outcomes are in a detached manner. [1]

    Basically, it is the responsibility of competent leadership to not become emotional about a situation so that there is an outside chance of actually improving a given context's outcomes. A great example that is problematic in the US is every facet of the prison system and the preceeding judicial events. Our elected officials won't even touch changes and alternatives get dismissed out of hand for a variety of reasons, meanwhile it is trivial to manipulate people in this context to keeping the status quo as is despite costing heavily in net taxes necessary to avoid the government collapsing in the first place.

    [1] Life is frequently counterintuitive with hidden variables in play and emotions remain unreliable for achieving a satisfactory outcome in practice, for better or worse, so letting ourselves become emotionally volatile and make a decision while in that state is exceedingly unlikely to result in a socially beneficial outcome.
