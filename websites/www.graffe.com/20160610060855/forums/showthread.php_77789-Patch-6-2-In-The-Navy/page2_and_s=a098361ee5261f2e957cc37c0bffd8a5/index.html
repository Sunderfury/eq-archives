    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    Legendary ring procs changed, to stem the tide of tears: now, the DPS rings have an "on use" effect: for 15 s., everyone wearing a DPS ring gains 25% damage for 15 seconds. At the end of the 15 seconds, every player with a DPS ring unleashes a 20 yard range AE, dealing 25% of all damage they dealt during that 15 seconds. The rings have a 2 minute shared CD, so if one person uses their ring, everyone's ring goes on CD. The healing rings have the same thing, only the AE at the end gives absorb shields for 25% of total healing done, divided equally amongst all players hit by the AE.

    In "prepare for the future" news: new vendors at the menagerie, selling pets and stuff for pet charms. Start banking charms, if you weren't already to level the pets that will come in the patch.

    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    Interesting tidbit from the official forums:

    Are the time walker dungeons being designed just for level 100 characters, or is the intent for the level 100 people to group with people leveling up through the content now? So e.g. Level 100s would join groups with characters level 70 for BC dungeons, etc?
    The intent is to group together anyone queuing for Timewalking when available. So, level-70 characters will be matched with level-100 characters for TBC Timewalking dungeons, for instance.
    That's the first downlevel system in WoW, ever. No idea why they haven't made this available in groups already - should have been there years ago. In fact, this is my biggest beef with WoW: They've never had a decent mentor/sidekick system. Yes, you can buy level 90 charcters or use RAF, but the beauty and fun of WoW is also running the lowlevel dungeons.

    I'm currently playing some with my son and his friend - leveling stuff. I've had to make another hunter, since I have all classes at 92+. Quite silly, since they have the tech to downlevel characters (and have had for years). I have no idea why I can't join with my level 100 hunter, rightclick on my son's level 50something character, and choose "Match character level" or somesuch.

    Granted, the heirloom tab really makes gear a breeze - bracer/belt/hands/rings (for us plebs without the heirloom ring ) are the only positions that need improving. But I still have to maintain a decent level on that character if I happen to miss a few playsessions.

    It's quite silly that this is the case in 2015 - if it's the power of a downlevelled level 100 character that they're afraid of, an heirloom-geared character ALREADY mows through dungeons like a knife through hot butter.
