    Re: F2P November 15th

    Wow. Maybe I was just spoiled by GW2's FTP style, but this is almost hilariously bad by comparison.

    Just glancing through their "Cartel Market" (and the in-game link to buy currency is broken on day one, smooth), you have to subscribe or purchase "luxuries" such as:

    • Access to Guild Banks: Okay... not a big deal, I guess, as all I really want to do is see the single-player content.
    • The ability to equip companion customizations: The customizations I had already equipped stuck, at least. But the real kicker is there are separate purchases required for not just the customizations, but the ability to disable displaying your companion's head slot item and the ability to "unify colors" on their gear. Considering how shitty the armor textures look, I'm not sure if that last is a good or bad thing.
    • Displaying earned titles: Seriously? I have to pay real life cash to show that my character is a Jedi Master?
    • Displaying your Legacy name: Ok, now you're just being petty.
    • The ability to add quickbars: You have got to be joking.


    Yes, you have to pay cash to BioWare to unlock a portion of the user interface.

    Holy hell. I don't mind paying for knickknacks and cosmetics, or even paying to unlock stuff that you can otherwise access with a bit of effort (such as paying for early access to Legacy race/class combos). But you combine all this with the experience penalties imposed on non-subcribers, and all I can say is that the FTP implementation of The Old Republic is a disincentive to patronize the game. At this point, I'm not even sure it was worth the time it took to patch my client.
