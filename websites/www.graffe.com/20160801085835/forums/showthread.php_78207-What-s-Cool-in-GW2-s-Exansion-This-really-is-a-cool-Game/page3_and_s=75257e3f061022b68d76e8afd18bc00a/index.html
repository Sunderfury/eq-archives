    Re: What's Cool in GW2's Exansion? This really is a cool Game

    Originally Posted by DmitrytheWizzy
    Oh mah god it's like you cannot be broken of your Everquestiness. The healing is an illusion. There is no triumvirate. Once raids are in there will be some healing but you really have to just stop thinking of healing as a thing. Control specs are starting to be talked about if you want something non-power dps. Also I run a condition dps now and it's great (but was a tricky thing to spec out). I think raids will bring some tankier survival specs too.

    This game at the high end of play is all about fast deployed combos and fields. It's not your momma's tank heal three dps game.
    I love action combat MMOs. I have no burning desire to return to the holy trinity - although I prefer the EQ holy trinity, really. DPS is just that, DPS - I'd love for PvE CC to play an active role.
    So my GW2 questions stem from the fact that I've seen very little coordination in PUG play. Yes, that's the way of the world when you solo queue in just about any game, but the world bosses I've seen in GW2 amount to zerg zerg zerg, when you boil it down.

    The PUG dungeon runs I've done in GW2 have all been of the "zerg this, have berserker gear, hope we do enough DPS before the boss kills us all". No Berserker gear ? OMG WTF n00b! GTFO!

    I like support/tank roles. They're the means to end the chaos, if you will - a well-played tank/healer in WoW can make a bad group go well. An awesome CC'er could(/can, I guess...) make an EQ group shine.
    Making people not-dead by buffing/healing/debuff removing/standing in the way is stuff that I like. That can be done without having to ress people all the time - the "ress people faster" trait that one of the GW2 classes (I forget which) get is the most defeatist piece of crap that you can create in my book.

    If the buffing/healing/debuff removing/standing in the way-effect is so tiny that it doesn't influence the fight in a meaningful way - then it's moot.
    And, yes, I've played a might-stacking warrior/elementalist. Making stuff dead faster is also in the buffing spectrum, I get that. It's just too tightly coupled with the whole "zerg zerg Berserker"-thing.

    Are bleed-dots still capped at 25 stacks ? I did love a build that I made that was focused on that, but it was completely useless in group play.
    "Silver bullet solutions are rare, silver bullet sales commonplace"
