    Re: Guild Wars 2 - Thoughts and Reviews

    I just hit 73 and don't really feel like I've been rushing. The thing is that the game gives you experience for EVERYTHING. For example, earlier I decided to get past the wool hump in Tailoring. I raised my skill about 70 points (almost done with cotton now) and that gave me 2 levels. 2 levels and I never left Lion's Arch.

    With that being said, I still have a TON of things to do. I'm only about 30% map completion and, last night, I did my first dungeon. I still have a ton of maps to complete (heck I still have most of the Charr city and all of the Asura city to go through), lots of dungeons, tradeskills and lots of world bosses. I've been farming the dragon in Sparkfly Fen as often as I can. It's a fun fight and gives a ton of experience, karma and a bunch of items (blues with a smattering of greens but it all vendors).

    Right now my biggest problem is that Dimitri is right (and I cringed while typing that). The high level content hasn't been tested very well. In the 50+ zones, there are a LOT of events that are bugged out and simply don't work. One in particular is keeping me from 100% in Timberline Falls because I can't get past a Gate. The gate goes away after the completion of a certain event. The problem is that the NPCs involved with the event are bugged and just stand there, doing nothing, so it never has the chance to trigger.
    There are other worlds than these.

    Re: Guild Wars 2 - Thoughts and Reviews

    I have spied out the land, and made trial upon the vittles of GW2. I have a 20 engineer and a 14 elementalist now, and both are fun in different ways. The elementalist is beginning to seem like the Bard of GW2, which could be both good and bad.

    Having tasted the fruit, I can now see why people were so excited. The game is *quite* innovative. I've been asking for a game without dedicated healers and a game without dedicated tanks for years now, and somebody listened. Hearts are a huge improvement over quests. Shared credit, shared XP, loot-just-for-you, and (my favorite) shared crafting nodes are genius. I have concerns about how that last will affect the economy, but oh well.

    The game rewards exploring. I haven't been an explorer since Vanilla WoW, and I haven't been a *Real* explorer since EQ. I didn't expect to explore a lot, but I just noticed I'm at 9% map completion at level 20, with 2 zones fully explored. (Edit 3. I forgot about Divinity's Reach.)

    They have a *hide your shoulderpads* button. How many years did we ask Blizzard for that, only to be told that their ugly ass shoulderpad graphics were important elements of PvP gameplay?

    Will it still be fun in a month? Who knows! But it's great now.

    Re: Guild Wars 2 - Thoughts and Reviews

    Make no mistake, while I do think some people got to 80 by skipping content, I am not criticizing you for being close. If I didn't waste time with alts I would probably be 60 myself. It doesn't take much to level in this game. I have no doubt I will have an effective level of 100-120 by the time I do all the solo content.

    No, I'm mostly just perplexed by people more concerned with the destination than the journey. The people who think 80 is the end, or the people who rush through content to get 100% checked off on maps that they were not particularly engaged with in the first place. I mean, for every person that sees 30% completion and marvels at all the content they have left to do, there is someone who sees a checklist to mark off.

    It's natural to advance when you play a lot, but some people just seem to be in a hurry, and the combination of being in a hurry and preoccupation with WoW made me come up with the original hypothesis that they wanted to be done with the game by the time MoP came out.

    This is slightly off topic, but you mentioned buggy zones. I was in Brisbane Wildlands (or whatever), and there was a bugged event with some skritt. When it finally corrected itself and the skritt got back to the home base, one of them (I forget his name) got stuck in a dialogue loop, and shouted "Wooooo! Shinies!" in zone chat for about 10 minutes. It was funny but VERY annoying. I asked map chat if I could report him for spamming, but no one seemed amused.
