    This game validates video games as a medium for story telling IMO. If Roger Ebert was still alive, we could have pointed to this game as a counter example to his assertion that video games are not at least on par with movies as a story telling medium. I cannot think of a game where I have felt immersed in the world, felt actual fear, and cared about the characters at all. In any situation my choices actually mattered. Resources felt tangibly scarce. The crafting system was plausible and fun instead of being tedious, although there was still the situation of holding an absurd amount of weaponry at once. The AI was not incredibly idiotic and had visible strategy in play. One scene I had successfully taken down a floor of people through sneaking, then later on messed up and instead of the AI letting me mow them down from the stairs they split up and came from behind as well. Scavenging felt dangerous as I was exposing myself to more potential harm, however I had to balance that with acquiring materials, but there was no certainty that exploring would lead to a net return if I was not careful. Last of Us is definitely one of the best games I have ever played and one of the best story experiences in any medium. I just wish every game I played was as thoughtfully executed. I think my favorite part of this game was the crafting system. It really complemented everything extremely well. In every other game I have played using the crafting system was something that may be required or virtually mandatory due to having such nice utility but never has it felt so seamless. I loved how upgrading weaponry was restricted to finding a workbench. The gating mechanism with toolboxes was kind of strange though. The game had nice pacing too. It is virtually flawless.
