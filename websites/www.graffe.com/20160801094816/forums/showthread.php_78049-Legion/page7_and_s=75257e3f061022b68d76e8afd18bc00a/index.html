    Re: Legion

    The wardrobe tab looks pretty slick, including the progress bar on unlocked items. That could be something that gets people running old content a lot, especially if they make achievements (with unique look rewards) linked to how many items you've unlocked.

    I see they're randomly killing off big names in the lore just to make this feel big. I'm not sure which "big lore" moment made me angrier - when my king was turned into a giant gemstone at random, with only a holiday quest to explain what happened, or when I finally had a chance to kill a major antagonist, only to be pushed aside so Orc Jesus could get the kill. Here's hoping these new lore moments are handled better than that.

    I looked at the MMO-Champ beta talent calculator: http://beta.wowdb.com/talent-calculator#b

    The talents are a really mixed bag. They're not supposed to overlap with PvP talents, but some of them are pretty much only PvP talents. Take the Unholy DK level 60 March of the Damned: can't have speed reduced below 100%, loss of control effects have 20% lower duration on you. This is something that will matter in PvE? Will any important loss of control effect, like a raid boss ability, ever be reduced by 20%? They need to cut the "useless utility".

    There's also still some chaff that won't get picked save in really oddball circumstances. Take Guardian druids, level 15 tier: a talent that can give the druid a permanent 15% damage reduction after some ramp-up time, a talent that generates extra rage (leading to extra damage reduction), and a talent that deals extra damage and has absolutely no damage reduction. You're a tank. Are you going to pick the talent with no damage reduction when there's two that offer damage reduction? Some of these things could be fixed with a little swapping. Using Guardian druids as the example again, if Brambles and Galactic Guardian swapped spots, the choices at 15 and 75 would be a lot more even. For the ones that can't be fixed by swapping, hopefully beta knocks them out.

    Some of them are just a mess. I've been vocal about how bad Marksmanship hunters are being handled. Look at the talents. A talent that requires you to keep Flare up on a target for boosted damage? A talent that boosts damage by 50% after a Disengage? Two CC talents on a tier with a damage boosting talent that may become an annoying minor DPS boost on a two minute CD? None of these things sound remotely fun to do, but people will be sucked into taking them if they provide the best DPS boost. Yeah, it's early, all subject to change, blah blah blah, but it's not a good sign if obnoxious rotation-changing talents make it through the most basic viability screening.

    Re: Legion

    There's a lot of "iconic" ability pruning, with the abilities showing up again as talents. A cynic would claim this was being done because the devs have run out of ideas for talents, so they're using existing abilities as talents since hey, you already know they're balanced. D, for example, would have lost Binding Heal and Circle of Healing as Holy, and both are available as talents now.

    Interesting aside: the Holy priest talents also include something I don't think I've ever seen before: a talent whose sole use is in raids. Level 60 tier, Hymn of Hope, 12 sec. channeled spell, while the spell is going all other healers in your raid/party and within 40 yards can cast heals for free. In a five man there's not going to be another healer, so this is pretty much a raid-only talent. Ignoring the balance issues with the actual talent, I don't think I'd be thrilled with a raid talent. The expectation from guilds would always be that Holy priests would take it if it's any good. You could fix that by having a raid-only tier of talents, but having a full tier of stuff that doesn't work anywhere except raids sounds even worse. I'm a lot happier with the reverse, which they did with Divine Hymn: it has a throughput penalty in raids versus parties, so it's not such a massive AE heal.

    Re: Legion

    So, Demon Hunter tank abilities were datamined, and they look pretty good:

    Offensive:
    Hateful Strike: An instant strike that causes 200% Physical damage and has a chance to spawn a Soul Fragment nearby. Generates 30-40 Fury.
    Felblade (50 Fury): Carve off a piece of your target's soul, causing 350% Physical damage and spawning a Soul Fragment nearby.
    Immolation Aura (10 sec CD) Engulf yourself in flames, instantly causing 140% Fire damage to nearby enemies and radiating 40% every sec for 5 sec.
    Infernal Strike (15 sec CD, 2 Charges): Leap through the air toward a targeted location, slamming down with destructive force to deal 70% Fire damage to all enemies within 6 yards.

    Defensive:
    Soul Cleave (45 Fury): A vicious strike that deals 275% Physical damage to all enemies in front of you and consumes up to 5 nearby Soul Fragments, healing you for 20% of your maximum health per Soul.
    Blade Barrier (20 Fury, 15 sec CD, 2 Charges): Raise your blades, increasing your parry chance by 30% for 8 sec.
    Blade Turning (Passive): Reduces physical damage taken by 120% of your Parry chance.
    Demonic Wards (Passive): Your tattoos reduce magical damage taken by 30%, your chance to be critically hit by 6%, and reduces the chance for your attacks to be parried by 3%.
    Mastery: Glaive Master (Passive): Increases Parry by 10.00%.

    Utility:
    Sigil of Inquisition (1 min CD): Place a Sigil of Inquisition at the target location that activates after 3 sec. All enemies affected by the sigil are pulled to its center and are snared, reducing movement speed by 70% for 6 sec.
    Sigil of Persecution (1 min CD): Place a Sigil of Persecution at the target location that activates after 3 sec. Taunts all enemies affected by the sigil, forcing them to attack you for 8 sec.
    Sigil of Silence (1 min CD): Place a Sigil of Silence at the target location that activates after 3 sec. Silences all enemies affected by the sigil for 4 sec.
    Torment (8 sec CD): Taunts the target to attack you, and increases threat that you generate against the target for 3 sec.

    Cooldowns:
    Blur (Could be Havoc only, unsure at this time) (1 min CD): Increases your dodge by 50% and allows you to dodge spells and ranged attacks for 3 sec. Dealing direct damage to any target refreshes the duration, up to a maximum total duration of 10 sec.
    Fiery Brand (45 sec CD): Brand your target with a burning demonic symbol, instantly dealing 200% Fire damage and reducing the damage they do to you by 40% for 10 sec.
    Metamorphosis (3 min CD): Transform to demon form for 10 sec, increasing current and maximum health by 30% and reducing damage taken by 30%. While in demon form, Hateful Strike always generates a Soul Fragment.

    ----
    My thoughts: No AP on mastery indicates that they're not done at all. If they also happen to put AP on the mastery, then the stat weighs will be Mastery >>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>> Bonus Armor > all else.
    Mastery is double-dipping and reinforcing itself. Crazy, even without AP.

    The playstyle combines warrior leaps with monk mobility.
    Since the two tanks that I played for the last year that I was subscribed to WoW were Warrior and Monk, I'm a pretty happy guy, to say the least.

    There's always the overhealing issue to deal with, especially since this is a tank that can literally plan ahead to get a full heal. Will require quite a bit of tank/healer communication, but I like that. Will be good in organized groups, will cause MASSIVE overhealing in LFR and the like.

    Still need info on a couple of key issues:
    * How long do the fragments last.

    * Can you, like the brewmaster, collect Soul Fragments by interacting with them individually (click/waking over) ?
    I don't think so, I think you can only interact with them via Soul Cleave. I was looking for a single target/non-attack ability to collect Soul Fragments.

    Grains of salt, etc. etc. etc.
