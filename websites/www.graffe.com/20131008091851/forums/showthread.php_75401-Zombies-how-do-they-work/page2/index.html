    Re: Zombies: how do they work?

    Originally Posted by Tinthalas
    The majority of those who fal victim are unaware of zombie movie protocol, and the zombies just look like live, sick or disillusioned individuals, and fall victim to their own compassion with humnanity; even a crack head attacking them is likely just going for their wallet, so a bite seems very superficial in the big scheme of things. But once they are bit, they run and infect others, and the original zombie is still there.
    This. Assuming the problem starts with one zombie, it's not hard to see how people can get infected without anyone knowing what is going on. It's easy to escape being eaten by one zombie, or even a few zombies, but it's not easy to escape being injured in any way, shape, or form, especially if you just think the zombies are people who need help and don't know what it takes to kill them. You don't get people being completely devoured until the zombies massively outnumber people and can prevent the people from escaping.

    Originally Posted by Wool
    You always seem to see abandoned tanks and IFVs next to all the abandoned cars, but why? A zombie isn't getting in there, and it doesn't even have to waste ammo, just run over them.
    Let's say you're in the tank. You see the typical wave of zombies coming at you. You starting shooting at them. They keep coming. The ones you shot stand right back up and keep coming. Do you stay in the slow-moving tank, the one that apparently can't do shit to these things, or run in panic? Do you retreat once you see your infantry friends around you start getting their faces eaten off, hear their screams, and then see them stand up and join in the attack? You'll need food and water eventually, so someone will need to leave the tank, and if they get bitten or scratched, well, you don't know that they'll turn, so when they do in that tight, enclosed space.... The whole "military failed to stop them" thing is based on the assumption that initially nobody knows a thing about the zombies. Nobody knows what created them, what continues to create them, or how to kill them. Once people get knowledge, they get power, and they start setting up safe areas and fighting back. It's a matter of surviving the initial onslaught.

    Re: Zombies: how do they work?

    Originally Posted by Wool
    I have not, but I get the impression that part of what makes people like that stuff is because of it's unconventional take on zombies. I would assume that it doesn't offer explanations for other works of fiction. One out of twenty still results in 19 zombie stories that don't make sense.
    It covers all cases where the setting includes the population not being aware of zombie lore. In particular the books emphasize how asymmetric information prevents anyone from responding rationally early on, which is what leads to the military being ineffective. The asymmetric information combined with national prejudices leads to some interesting international relations during the events. In the survival guide it goes into different item choices, weapons, mobility options and their benefits and minuses.

    Also in the setting zombies respond to noise which is important in the survival guide. They are both really good. The WWZ book is readable for anyone, even those not interested in zombies because the zombies are only a plot device. The real story are the survivors.
