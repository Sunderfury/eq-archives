    Re: Why you should play GW2

    Its not just the level down system, its that paired with the dynamic events that makes lower level content worthwhile. In other games, you do a zone, and you run out of quests and move on at around the right level you are supposed to. If you come back all you have are 2-3 quests you missed the first time and a bunch of mobs and nothing else. The content is essentially dead now, except for a little nostalgia or if I want to level up an alt. If I go back to the newbie zones in this game, even if I did it, I can still do long and cool event chains all the time like the frozen maw, or the shadow behemoth and have a little challenge to boot.

    Re: Why you should play GW2

    I'm having a ton of fun in Guild Wars, especially now that some friends are playing along with me. However, as much fun as exploring is I have a semi-major issue with the game right now; every single ability is unlocked already. Oh, sure, I don't have ALL of the slot skills, but I certainly have all of the ones that are worth a damn, and weapons aren't going to change any time soon. I hop on the elementalist to solo, and realize that I'm going to be doing the same thing, using the same strategies, from here (45) until 80. It gets a bit discouraging, and I'm finding I'm only playing with friends now, or maybe a little bit of zone exploration. But, since there's no subscription, that's okay! It's both relaxing and also a tiny bit disappointing.

    With regards to Blizzard and WoW, I am waiting with baited fucking breath for someone else to implement player-customizable UI. One of the things Blizzard did right, and everyone else seems to be avoiding it like the damn plague.
