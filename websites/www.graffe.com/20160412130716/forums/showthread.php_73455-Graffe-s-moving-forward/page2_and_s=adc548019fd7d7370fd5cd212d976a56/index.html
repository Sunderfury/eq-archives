    Re: Graffe's moving forward

    The thing is, while we might be past the point of getting many genuine new permanent members, that doesn't mean the site won't ever come up in google searches or random users won't surf by and investigate. God knows how many random vbulletin forums I've wound up at while investigating other topics, many of them much less active than Graffe's. In those situations, I quickly find myself trying to answer the questions "what is this site?", "what else can it do for me?", "how do I find it?". A good front page helps with all that.

    Regular forum members don't visit the front page, that's the main reason I'm hesitant to suggest trying to put much content there, especially transient article-style content-- which is precisely what the forums themselves are for. But that doesn't mean there aren't ways it could be made useful.

    Re: Graffe's moving forward

    Originally Posted by Nadiar
    A site like that would probably be in a better status right now, because it would have been filled with the people that like crunching numbers, and while that would have had a pretty small membership, it would have been in a good position to keep growing after all games followed the WoW model and had their own forums for each class.

    Vdou and I push Reddit because Reddit is primarily focused on sharing existing stories from somewhere else. Outside of Wools posts in the game forums, we have maybe a couple "new content" threads in a given month that people would be interested to read.
    Around WoW/EQ2 the marketing arms of the game companies realized how many eyeballs they were losing to community sites. They really sucked then. They've gotten a LOT better. Now every game has its own forums which are full of the kinds of people we used to have around here. Combine that with the main discussions centering around a dying game (all time most users on this site is STILL Ringo's WoW Beta post) and it devolves into people that used to play these games, maybe play some new ones, whom had common interests and less exciting things to do with their time.

    Reddit is great for whatever happens to this site when it runs out of funding. Whether that time is now is not for us to really pontificate on, it'll be pretty obvious -- the lights will go out.

    There has been more than one initiative in the past few years to *DO SOMETHING* with the front page/attract eyeballs. We had great plans! I spent a lot of time starting to work on building a site and it petered out (for a number of reasons).

    I'm more than willing to take another shot at reinventing the site, however if we're going to pivot into something other than a discussion board; it's time to trim the fat and start fraiche. I think there is a significant amount of potential within the community to make that happen and do a good job.
