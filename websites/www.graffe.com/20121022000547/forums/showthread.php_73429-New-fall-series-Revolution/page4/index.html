    Re: New fall series: Revolution

    I finally remembered to watch this. I thought it was okay. The premise was fine, but the execution was off. Didn't make a compelling enough case with the scenario they started with. It isn't unusual for a pilot to be rough around the edges though, hopefully it gets better.

    The two stated complaints I found myself sharing were 1) it feels rushed, leaving plot points feeling contrived. 2) the apparent lack of empathy in people's deaths is extremely jarring and breaks immersion.

    I like the militia "villain". The uncle seems good too. The nerd is okay.

    I wanted to like the female lead, but she is sort of pissing the hell out of me. I didn't like her step-mother much either, until she killed a couple would-be rapists. That was kickass, but the stupid girl decided that was reason to distrust the step-mom more, and instead put trust in the pretty boy that just happened along to "save" her. Such bullshit. Show some freaking gratitude. I can't stand "strong" female leads whose main claim to fame is infatuation with the male lead.

    Hopefully it's just character arc. I remember characters in books I've read in the past being written as extremely annoying in order to make their eventual growth all the more powerful later on, but if her character arc continues to revolve around pretty boy, I'm going to be annoyed. It's clear it's going to be a theme going forward, but it's incredibly annoying that the woman she hates is more self-sufficient than she is. Argh, Wool smash.

    The "twist" with someone breaking the rules established by the universe and using power is fine. Somehow the device itself is what allows for power to be used in a limited environment, obviously. I also like the ties the Uncle has had with the main antagonist.
