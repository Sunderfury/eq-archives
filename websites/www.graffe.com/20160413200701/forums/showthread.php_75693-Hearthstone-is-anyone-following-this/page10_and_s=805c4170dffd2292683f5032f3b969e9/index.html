    Play is the unranked/ranked mode. I play unranked because the only difference it supposedly makes is whatever special medal icon you earn and I see no reason to jump into a ladder when I don't even have real decks yet (I used "suggest" drafting mode in the custom deck builder out of laziness).

    A few things are generally always nice to have. Taunt minions, silence effects, and removal. It's important not to overextend and play all your cards just because you can. One good removal spell will leave you with nothing. A lot of impatient players try to focus the player down and ignore minions, but this usually only works if their opponent gets an unlucky draw. If they get a taunt or two out, suddenly you can't attack them anymore and they have a bunch of minions you can no longer deal with.

    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    Hey, sometimes I heal the other player to make my Lightbringer level up, or heal an Enrage minion to remove the Enrage effect. Or because I have nothing to do at 2 mana, and he's at full life, and it's the friendly thing to do!

    Eventually, there will be rewards for the medal. Right now, they're just testing the ladder and matching. I wouldn't worry about which you play - anecdotal evidence suggests that the "unranked" mode will still match you against "ranked" mode players (one player in unranked got matched against a streamer, then checked the guy's video to see what he said about him and saw the guy was playing ranked). The daily quests not poofing at the end of the day is a great feature, isn't it? There are 60 g dailies you can get, most of them being "win 5 as X or Y".

    Since I'm just off another 9 in Arena (thank God that 0 last run was an outlier), let me add what I look for in Arena cards.

    (a) Removal. AE removal - spells that do more than 1 damage to multiple targets - is key. Weapons, if your hero can use them, are pretty amazing - almost nothing can destroy them, they last for multiple uses.
    (b) No minions with 1 health, unless they have an ability that will go off even if they die. There's too many opponents that can kill a minion like that using their hero power, which leaves you down a card.
    (c) Minions that change the board. Think of something like Dark Iron Dwarf, which is a 4/4 for 4 that gives +2/+0 permanently to another minion when it comes into play. If your opponent left any of your minions alive, he did so assuming that you couldn't kill off certain of his minions with yours (or that it didn't matter if you did). That Dark Iron Dwarf changes the battlefield, and suddenly your weak minion can take out his strong minion. There's a few minions that give permanent health or damage boosts to your other minions, and they're all good. As a rule: if it boosts other minions, and it's an X/X for X, it's amazing. Charge, Divine Shield, and Taunt can also be like this.
    (d) Permanents that have triggered "draw a card" effects. Scavenging Buzzard, Northshire Cleric, Gadgetzan Auctioneer, the 4/2 guy for 4 that gives you a card every time one of your minions dies - these cards are gold. I just hit 9 wins basically because I had three of the 4/2 guys as a paladin. I'd fill the board with crap, then next turn when my opponent played a bunch of his crap to block/kill, I'd play the 4/2 guy and get a card out of every creature trade I made. One game ended with me with seven cards in hand and four creatures on the board, to his one creature, no cards. In particular, if you play a hunter, draft beasts and pick every Scavenging Buzzard you see.
    (e) "Whores." As you start playing you'll find some cards are colossal pains that you want to kill as soon as they come down. The card draw cards are like that, or cards that grow (Scavenging Ghoul, the warrior 2/4 that gets +1/+0 every time a creature takes damage, the 2/2 that gets +1/+1 every time you cast a spell, etc.). It's great to have those cards, if only because you can usually suck removal cards out of an opponent's hand by playing them. And if they don't remove them, well, remember that game I mentioned with the 4 creatures and seven cards in hand? One of the creatures was a 14/4 Divine Shielded Scavenging Ghoul. Om nom nom!
    (f) Don't try and draft a theme unless it's very general, like "fast creatures". Inevitably you won't get the cards, either when drafting or when playing. Just pick strong individual cards, and the deck will work.

    Originally Posted by Droigan
    How do the cards work when picking for arena? Is it completely random from the whole pool of cards?
    From what I can tell: you will get three cards, all of the same rarity, in a given pick. The rarity chosen is random, with basic and common weighted higher than rare/epic/legendary. The whole pool of cards is available for a given rarity, which is why sometimes you'll see lots of what you want and other times nothing. The game gives you the noise and flash if you get a rare/epic/legendary pick.
