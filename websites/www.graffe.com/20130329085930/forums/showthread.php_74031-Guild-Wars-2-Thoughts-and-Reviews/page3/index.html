    Re: Guild Wars 2 - Thoughts and Reviews

    Woof, servers came down and I should be asleep, but here I am posting away. Got an elementalist (who I will call a mage from now on, because it is easier to type) to 26, my guardian's... 15? I think, and I brought the thief up to level 11 tonight. I'm somehow certain all five characters are going to hit 11 at least before I focus down on one.

    Combat is way tricky for someone used to WoW tanking, but I'm slowly getting the hang of it. My mage is full of squishiness, and I need to be careful when I switch to her from the guardian, who is decidedly tougher to kill. The thief has a penchant for leaping bodily into crowds of mobs and blenderizing them right before exploding. Which is fun, but I really need to work on that part where I keel over and die.

    The areas are absolutely gorgeous, most notably the main cities; one of the first things I do on a new character is explore their main city, since it's usually very pretty and I net a level and a half or so before I go out and start helping the locals. The Grove is alright, and I absolutely adore Rata Sum, but Divinity's Reach and Lion's Arch are... simply spectacular. There's a reason I have two human characters, oh yes.

    Combat. Man, you know what, I'm gonna go make a new post on combat, because it's worth digging more into.

    Let's see though, some cons and frustrations from the past few days of playing:

    - Not NEARLY enough bank space by default. Not even close. I blew $20 to get enough gems for two expansions, and things are much better now.
    - Some very annoying things lacking in the UI. No way to self-target (I wanted to see how my guild name looked over my head), no clock, no latency display. I understand the reason for no trading but it's still kind of frustrating from time to time. I can't seem to find a decent expanded stats readout, which is frustrating because I like comparing numbers. No way to inspect other players.
    - Tradeskills, man. I've poured all my mats from three characters into my mage and I can't seem to even smelt iron yet, despite the 15-25 level zones having nothing but iron, silver, and better trees. I've never really enjoyed farming crafting supplies, and it's looking like all my characters are going to be leveled out of the zones with copper and green wood before I'm done skilling up past such things.

    Re: Guild Wars 2 - Thoughts and Reviews

    The combat system has so much depth it's scary. It looks really simple on the surface, but when you start to tinker and really push stuff the level of skill difference is astounding.

    Ettins for example - normally I can take one on at a time. Last night I accidentally ended up with3 at once,but instead of panicking I used everything in my toolbox, dodged the hellandkilled all 3 without dipping below half health. You can prevent so much damage by appropriate use of abilities at the right time, and mobility can increase your damage by a huge factor. Sending multiple clones out to distract enemies, then exploding them at the right time to daze and protect me makes a huge difference. Really opened my eyes to the fact it's not just world of Warcraft with a dodge system thrown on top.

    Spite: 20 points - VI & IX
