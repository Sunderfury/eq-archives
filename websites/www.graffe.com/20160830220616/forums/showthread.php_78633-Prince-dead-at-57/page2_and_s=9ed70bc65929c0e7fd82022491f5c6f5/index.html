    Re: Prince dead at 57

    Prince was my first concert. Only a few ever topped it. When I saw him, he was 21 and could move like a great athlete. He could already crush an audience with the rises and falls of a show. This was before Purple Rain and so the music had more funk. It was a sublime performance. Just so fun. Later, I really liked his more spiritual hymnal stuff. In the 2000's catalogue their are some truly beautiful songs that have never received radio play. His performances are strangely vulnerable and confident at the same time. He had so much sex appeal/charisma that was oddly antithetical to his obvious spirituality/shyness. This is really sad to me because he was still making interesting music and being an interesting person. They say he had a vault with thousands of unreleased songs. I hope that gets curated and some at least are released.

    Re: Prince dead at 57

    His sister Tyka Nelson and the six or so half siblings have filed formal claim in MN that Prince had no known will and so begun the probate process.

    This sadly sounds very much like him; I learned in an interview that his secret to looking so young was he stopped counting time. He probably saw no need for that sort of planning with his unique life view.

    I have been learning so much from the friends' recollections and interviews dug up. I sort of outgrew Prince for grunge, but he was a surprisingly bigger part of my high school and college years than I gave him credit for (from searching as well this weekend for what I could find on Youtube of his music). I regret not keeping up on him. The last musician I followed intensely was Stevie Ray Vaughn, and I guess I've just wandered since then.

    Another thing that saddens me is to learn that he was suffering from a hip injury. I figured that cane to be another Prince affectation. To see how athletic he was as a performer in the 80's; but then you have to remember he was 57 and we all get old.

    Just watched "Partyman" from the Bat Man Sound Track from what appears to be a bootleg VHS copy off MTV.

    His way with words. "If I want trombone, anyone will do. But if I want sax, I want Candy," and he looks up from the floor (with that Prince sly look) between the legs of a female saxophone player apparently named Candy.

    Oh, I haven't seen Beyonce's "Lemonade" but read some articles/reviews today. He would have liked Queen B's latest I think, considering their friendship and his support of female artists.
