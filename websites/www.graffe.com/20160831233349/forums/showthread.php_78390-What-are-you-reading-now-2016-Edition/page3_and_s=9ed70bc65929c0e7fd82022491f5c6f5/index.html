    Re: What are you reading now, 2016 Edition

    preffer more "Pulp", heavy reading is a drag now :P

    I recommend THE EMPIRE'S CORP by Christopher Nuttall
    scifi series, 4000 years in future, lot of tech has stagnated and recognizable, because the entire "system" is about keeping everything from changing, so the corporations etc keep control
    but things are falling apart...about the only group with any sense of the looming catastrophe and any morals is the Imperial marines, almost exactly US marines but used more like the British Marines
    large part of the storyline is how things fall apart, and the marines rescue a history professor who had the naïve realization to suddenly grasps this disaster ahead when a student asks him what seems ot be a simple question, and then published his conclusions...so LOTS of groups want his head

    Shades of Heinlein and 2000AD, don't agree with all the author's points, but many are valid (the History professor's extract's begin each chapter, explaining how Earth goes haywire)
    as usual with his writing, pretty subversive in an odd way.

    Every "even" number book in the series deals with different characters/aspects, rather than just the marine unit that escapes just before Earth melts down which is the main thrust of the series
    The Marines are sick fed up putting down resistance movements the scumbag corporation's crap keep triggering, and the insurrections then become utter messes 99% of the time no matter what happens, or the planet's population gets flattened by mass drivers as hey the corporate media don't care if a few million are wiped out beyond the main part of the settled galaxy
    The Marine's realize they are in deep crap and have to make a settled, sane place of the system they are sent off to in exile, ostensibly to get the unit away form political assholes whom they pissed off, but really because the head of the marine Corp realizes Earth is about to go to hell and wants to preserve some order somewhere
    But...it's never that easy

    the trope setc are obvious but it's good fun

    Well worth a read IMHO
    only on Kindle.

    https://www.amazon.com/Empires-Corps...8RX4K2G#navbar

    Re: What are you reading now, 2016 Edition

    And hey, Larry Correia's "Heavy magic" is a fun series :P (regarding "Sad Puppies")
    1st "Monster Hunter" was good, rest not so good, though the character of "Franks" is a good one

    Empire's Corp is really a social commentary, like most of the author's books, hence keeping the technology not too advanced keeps it accessible and the social disintegration which includes rape and murder as society devolves, is logical part of the story as Earth implodes, ultimate "bread and circuses" taken to the end conclusion. What, you think it would be "nice"?
    Don't agree with all of his points but he has some, we can already see strangulation and fighting between corporations against anyone trying to innovate (really Mercantilism or Fascism no longer Capitalism), and failure to fix never mind create more infrastructure.
    As the stories go on the different political/economic ideas are shown, grow, ally or clash
    the interstellar free traders and the "Rock Hoppers" (asteroid miners) are interesting groups
    at one point folk begin suspecting the purely space-based/living factions for using giant cobalt bombs to wipe out entire planets as no sane person would sterilize something that's of value unless you lived only in space and so don't value planets very much...which is itself an interesting point.
