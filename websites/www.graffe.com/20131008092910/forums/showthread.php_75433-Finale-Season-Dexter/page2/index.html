    Re: Finale Season Dexter

    Originally Posted by Gnizmo
    The fact that they made the character so sympathetic is an amazing feat of writing really. We have seen him kill countless people, and often in a brutal fashion. Especially early on before the knife became his go-to method of killing. Hell, the show even points out the inconsistency of Dexter being the hero and a serial killer in the Season 1 finale. Still they have gotten millions to want him to win and kill. If you want everything to be perfectly realistic go watch documentaries. I am watching to see a story unfold and gladly suspend disbelief for a bit in order to enjoy it.
    I would not use viewership as a metric for quality. The Walking Dead and Honey Boo Boo both pull down millions of viewers and are terrible shows. I would agree that presenting Dexter in such a way that he is sympathetic is a great feat, and I will agree without provisions that Michael Hall has done an excellent job acting Dexter to the extent that his character is one of my all time favorites.

    However, and I think this has more to do with Showtime pushing for extra seasons than a fault of the writer's overall vision of how the show would go initially, in a nontrivial number of situations the outcome requires an implausibly high amount of incompetence by competent detectives as well as none of the character's who have cause for seeking revenge on Dexter actually doing so that an outcome where none of these end up doing in Dexter is impossible to believe.

    I was very interested in seeing how episode 1 of season 8 would play out given that such a significant actor in the police department is killed and there already existed so much suspicion of Dexter.

    The uneventful manner in which the police department transitioned from that is beyond belief.

    Consider that never do we see Dexter destroy or remove the battery of his cell phone and neither does his sister and the person they end up blaming La Guerta's death on was not anywhere near the scene of the crime and both Dexter's and his sister's cars were on site, as well as La Guerta's and La Guerta was knowingly investigating Dexter at the time that only the most incompetent department would not have at least looked into that, or at least seen how there was no way that the person it was blamed on actually could have done it..

    That is but one obvious manner in which the state of events should have been much more intense for Dexter or Debra when we see them in episode 1.

    How quickly the police department abandoned the investigation is in itself implausible.

    Both of these being written around so poorly are but one example of the extremely awful writing that has taken place in the past 4 seasons.

    Compare that to Breaking Bad or The Wire where the writers actually write plausible and believable reasons for the outcomes.

    Dexter's writers don't bother doing that whatsoever, which is sad as the show could have been excellent.

    Another major issue I have with the writing is that we have seen Dexter not only abandon the code in the cases where he is on one of his darker spells, but also actively thwart Miami metro investigations even when they were on pace to arrest the person.

    The plausibly sympathetic motivation for Dexter killing only bad guys that otherwise would have escaped is thrown out due to this.

    The idea of Dexter not actually lacking empathy like Rudy is interesting, however it is haphazardly executed and forgotten about as much as it is actually followed.

    The show is still enjoyable, especially since the writers started focusing on winding the series down (or so it seems) with season 7 and alleviating the issue of Debra, the Miami Metro super detective, being utterly incompetent in every situation involving Dexter's activities which seriously undermines her character, but due to the frequent lazy writing it is impossible to hold it to high standards; it's just a low quality flick like an action movie where it is necessary for the viewer to leave their brain at the door to enjoy it at all instead of something comparable in quality to shows that actually have a coherent storyline.

    I am concerned though that with the introduction of the psychiatrist woman and how the writers reduced the level of pressure on Dexter with the segue that they are going to not wrap things up finally and instead drag the story further and continue to dilute its quality.

    We will have to wait and see though.

    They still have enough episodes not to rush things I think.
