    Re: First Look at AMC's The Walking Dead (Zombie Apocalypse Show)

    A woman who follows XDTalk? *swoon*

    I'll go out on a limb as the resident reloading expert. Been doing it for nearly 30 years and even own and run a popular hunting / reloading / ballistics website. Truth is most ammo will outlast an average human, expecially in times of the Zombie Apocalypse. If I had to stock up on anti-zombie ammo, it would be .22 cal hollow point copper coated, probably Federal brand on a budget, Stingers if you want to go all out. You can carry *thousands* of rounds with you at all times vs the weight of a couple full 30round AR15 clips. Pick a good reliable semi-auto, the Rugar 10-22 is extremely popular with lots of add-on mods like folding stock, laser sight etc. Then get *GOOD* quality high cap mags. The cheap crap will jam and you are zombie fodder so don't skimp here.

    If its truely Apocalypse time and ammo has run out. The formula for black powder is pretty simple 7:5:5 (although some people prefer 10:7.1:7.1). Lead alloy for bullet making is fairly easy to come by, lots of abandoned cars around with wheel weights. That is a harder alloy though than pure lead so it wont expand as much at termination. Fishing sinkers would be the best bet if available as they are still primarily lead.

    Casting bullets for pistols is fairly straight forward.... if its Zombie apocalypse time you arent striving for competition shooting accuracy so you only need to hit a head sized object at maybe 10-20 yards. This type of accuracy is easily attainable even with crude bullets slightly oversized (although they will lead up barrel at a insane rate). Undersized & paper patched bullets will work as well for short range use.

    You will *NOT* want to be relying on a Glock expecially if you need to reload ammo. I'm sure I may make some people make some people irrate but Glocks are trash imo. Too many exploded glocks out there and due to the unsupported case rim, it bulges the cases so reloading becomes extremely difficult. I love my XD40 subcompact, the power of a .45auto but more rounds in the stack.

    Cast bullets for a centerfire rifle wont work unless you really drop down the velocity. They have to have that copper jacket to seal the bullet against the bore or the lead will litterally liquify or fragement as the gas blows past. A Muzzleloading rifle might be a great long(er) range choice when the shit hits the fan. Easy to cast bullets for, easy to make powder for. You only need the ignition source.

    In my house even with just old-stock powders, bullets, primers... I could probably go the remainder of my life without buying any additional rifle/pistol ammo. I have 5-6 cans of black powder, maybe another dozen of various rifle powders (H4831, H4350, MR223, VARGET, RL19, RL22, RL25 etc etc).

    This reminds me, I need to load up a couple hundred more .243 hunting rounds this weekend. Its actually really fun reloading.

    100 parts of saltpeter, 71 p coal, 71 p sulfur.


    You still will need the equipment to cast the bullets plus make the casings. I guess you could find them at the local gun store.
    For the bullet: Drill bit, 2 blocks of metal. Hold together in a clamp. Drill down where the two halves meet. Done.

    As for the cases, they are reloadable by themselves. You should use a resizing die as the case mouth expands to the chamber wall on firing... but techically its not required. This is of course, pure survival / zombie apocalypse thinking. If you really feel you need a "crap has hit the fan" solution, Lee has a cheap ($22) simple (ie sloooowwwww) reloader:
