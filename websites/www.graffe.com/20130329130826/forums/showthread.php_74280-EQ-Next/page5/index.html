    Originally Posted by Delores Mulva
    Even though the degree of customization of the UI is a good feature of WoW, I think the ability of macros and scripts to gather information is too high. In particular, were I in charge of the UI group, I'd kill all ability for Auctioneer and other add-ons to pull any information from the AH, nor should that information be available to scripts outside the game. Auto-posting of new auctions? No problem. Want a price profile of everything in game, including pricing history? Get out a pad of paper or open up a spreadsheet in a second window, and prepare to be very busy for quite some time.
    It was the spreadsheets with auto-updated data (pulled from tuj or blizzard) that were the worst. The economy was effectively a solved problem after that. There were some private SS that took into account all items in the game and doing some intelligent analysis on it that essentially decide what actions the scripts had to take.

    All of the time consuming stuff (processing materials, posting stuff) was done through scripts built using techniques that multi-boxers used. It got to the point where I could play another game entirely or do work and by virtue of moving any mouse or keyboard input would do some economy related activity. It made having social networks and outsourcing stuff pointless.

    Even strategy regarding stockpiling etc. became rote as far as how to best profit off of any patch given a millions of gold to do so with, which was common at that point. Everyone who was efficient at the economy reduced themselves to a thoughtless robot when making gold. At which point, it was like why even do this having essentially beaten WoW's economy? I wish Blizzard had murdered the ability to interact so precisely with the game without resorting to sophisticated bots, but they didn't and still haven't which when combined with the rampant duping issue (they did not admit to it until months and months after the fact, but anyone who paid attention to prices and their usual valuation knew the score, as an example crimson death chargers would be posted at 42k frequently despite being valued at 90k+ due to an exploit with how the chest worked, there was a similar thing with landro's lock box (sp?) and exploits for epic gems and FL essences that resold for 10x the duped price) at the time ruined the game for me. I'm glad they did not allow addons in D3 or provide an auction API. There's still a degree of mystery to the game due to not having historical data easily available.
