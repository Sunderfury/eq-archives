    Re: Dark Souls: My (new) All-Time Favorite Game.

    Originally Posted by Daxil Solshok
    There's a lot of depth to it too. Just figured out that lighter armor decreases your aggro radius. I imagine that's one of the benefits of playing a thief. Can sneak right up and backstab.
    Yep, in fact its even a little deeper than that. Enemies have sight and hearing ranges. If they're not facing you, they won't see you, but they might hear you. Lighter armor makes less sound.

    Practically speaking, I've found it's pretty hard to sneak up on an enemy to get backstab, unless you have the ring that suppresses all sound entirely. Wearing lighter armor can help for slipping by certain enemies though, or getting close enough to get a good pre-emptive strike.

    Re: Dark Souls: My (new) All-Time Favorite Game.

    Originally Posted by Daxil Solshok
    It's funny, but if you watch the melee scenes in Game of Thrones you actually learn some combat techniques for this game. Bron matches my current character's setup. He'll back up and tire them out if they're heavily armored and slowly nip at them. Also, when there's adds I'll try and take one out quick with throwing daggers/fire bombs. Learned that from Bron.
    It's true that enemies can burn through stamina. Might help to know that almost no two-handed attacks can be "deflected" by the shield (eg when you hit a shield and bounce backward). When you hit a shield and don't get deflected, you drain a good chunk of stamina. So if you think an enemy is low on stamina and it's got its shield up, sometimes you can just two-hand your weapon and start spamming r1 to stagger them. Also: Kick is a huge stamina drain, and can be very effective against turtling enemies.

    But yeah I would say "Bron's strategy" was my primary/go-to tactic for any new encounter. Though it's more about learning the attacks and vulnerability windows of the opponent than tiring them out. Sometimes it's also about waiting for a particular attack that you know you can punish.
