    Re: What are you reading now?

    Just finished "Jonathan Strange and Mr. Norrell" by Susanna Clarke. Great book about the return of magic to England in the early 19th Century. Nice intelligent take on a practical magic system while also being a little bit creepy at times. It reads a bit like a history novel, with abundant references in footnotes to fictional published works and historical footnotes about how famous events were influenced by magic. Its a little heavy on story and lite on characterization, but the story does manage to carry to book. My only other complaint is that the book will occasionally drone on too much about irrelevant sidetracks.

    Currently reading "All Fisherman are Liars" by Linda Greenlaw. Fun book that relates a whole bunch of unrelated stories and anecdotes from Linda's life and that of many of her acquaintances and friends in the fishing industry.
