    Re: Why Romney might be the only one able to stop the US from driving off a cliff

    My tuppence:

    If Clinton hadn't opened up trade with China I don't think America would be in a terribly different situation today. Manufacturing would have just been outsourced to India or the like instead. India, being a democracy, makes it harder to make a human rights argument to oppose it. Indeed, the human rights argument implies opening up trade, not closing it down: Hundreds of millions of people around the world have been raised out of poverty and into a lower-middle-class existence through trade. Trade is effective at doing that in a way aid never could be.

    Clinton, Obama and all the rest really are pretty right-wing though in the sense that were they the leader of almost any other country in the world they'd belong to the right-wing party there. The fact they were to the left of the American political alternatives at the time doesn't change that fact. Lithiun is simply viewing politics in global absolute terms not relative terms - which is reasonable in that a right wing policy has the same effect anywhere regardless of whether or not an even more extreme policy was on the table.

    I remain unconvinced it wouldn't have been better for America for McCain to have won the last election. (a) It was always a poisoned chalice for the person who won due to economic problems always likely to drag on for the duration of the Presidency and (b) it have been much more difficult politically for Republicans to have been so obstructionist. Your system is set up like an enormous game of chicken - except that if the cars crash it's the American people that suffer not the politicians, and that means the more sociopathic political party will always win the day (the dems always blink first). I much prefer the parliamentary system of government for that reason: Yes, a bad government can pass more bad policies but the next government can always reverse them. At least a good government can pass good policies unobstructed! Maybe you'd have a decent cheap, effective UHC system by now!
