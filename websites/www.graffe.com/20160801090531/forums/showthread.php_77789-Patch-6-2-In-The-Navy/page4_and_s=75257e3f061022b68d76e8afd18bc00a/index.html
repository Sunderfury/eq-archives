    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    More info on the bonus events and the adventure guide:

    http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/3...venture-guide/

    There's some nice Blizzard touches in there. The idea of being able to launch everything from within the adventure guide is pretty inspired. Don't just tell them something exists, get them started on it! Some of the "stretch goal" rewards available also make it much more worthwhile to do the bonus event. The stretch awards:

    Timewalking: completing three of them awards a bonus Seal above and beyond the weekly cap.
    Draenor Dungeons: completing two Mythics awards a Heroic Hellfire Citadel raid cache (so one random item from H-HC).
    Battlegrounds: winning "a few" random BGs gives 500 Conquest above and beyond the cap.
    Arena Skirmishes: winning "several" random skirmishes gives 500 Conquest above and beyond the cap.
    Apexis: completing three area objectives awards a "sizeable" amount of Oil, the resource the shipyard and ship missions use.
    Pet Battles: winning five PvP battles gives an Ultimate Battle-Training Stone, one of those "instantly level 25" things.

    Given what I like to do, I could see doing Apexis, trying PvP Pet Battles (by 6.2 all my pets will be 25, including any new ones in the patch, but I can always bank the stones), and trying mythic dungeons. Two of those things are things I would not have tried without the additional reward system. I don't know if I'll stick with them, though, and time constraints from Chorecraft could sink the whole thing.

    Originally Posted by Dawlin
    Less rewards for the same time spent playing - I can't restructure my free time just because someone decides to change a mechanic in a game.
    This is the essence of why seeing players as organized versus disorganized matters so much. My experience has been very clear: players don't change what type of player they are. If they try, whatever was keeping them as one type of player pushes back until inevitably they snap: the guy shorting his job takes heat at work, the guy shorting his family risks divorce, the anti-social guy trying to be social loses it. When that happens, the player always blames the game for what happened and leaves, almost never to return. Pushing people out of their comfort zone is bad for long-term subscriber retention.

    The winning strategy for the company would be to make rewarding, fun activities for all types of players, organized and disorganized. When Bashiok sees players saying that they hit a brick wall in progression when they don't raid and his response is, "Just make a few friends", he shows that either he just doesn't get what the real problem is or the real solution, or that he sees it and for philosophical reasons ("no welfare epics") doesn't want that solution to happen. He, and the other developers, should be focused on creating solutions that cater to the customers they have, not on trying to mold those customers into the sort of player they want. Everquest is dead, stop trying to push "the Vision" back into the MMORPG world.

    Originally Posted by Dawlin
    Yes, this is me speaking for faceroll heroics. If you want a challenge and titles/mounts, go do challenge/mythic modes. Let the rest of us have fun bashing skulls in after work.

Challenge not matched to reward? ISN'T THAT THE ENTIRE POINT OF THESE BONUS WEEKENDS AND THEIR SPECIAL REWARDS? The devs can see what players want, but the devs refuse to give it to players except on terms dictated by the devs. But I'm sure those 2.9 million subs lost were all due to price point competition, and not because the reward systems for non-organized raiders are soul-sucking grinds of the worst kind.

    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    Originally Posted by Sehra
    I don't think the problem is that people don't know what's there.
    I used to think that, but after spending a lot of time in my server's Trade chat (because I never leave the garrison), I think the devs are right about this.

    Example: there was a guy trying to sell tradeskill item upgrades, your mats + 5 K. People told him he'd be more likely to sell if he got the bloods himself and made the upgrades to sell directly on the AH. Ok, he says, how do you get bloods? People mention the AH, I mention the primal spirit vendor. Ok, how do I get lots of primal spirits? Well, there's the random tradeskill vendor. I don't have one of those in my garrison, how do I get one? I then tell him about all the steps for the random tradeskill vendor:

    Groups and Raids panel (shortcut "i"), Premade Groups, Custom Groups, search for keyword "Trader", if the trader is one where you have extra stuff to turn in click on one of the groups and they'll auto-invite you, right click on your portrait and select "View Leader's Garrison" if you're in your garrison, the trader is in the foyer of the town hall.

    And then came the avalanche of comments from different people. "Oh, THAT is how people know what trader it is today!" "My god, look at all the custom groups for stuff like rep grinding and barn trapping and...." "People will auto-invite you to use their enchanter shack/trader/pet guy? For free?" There were a ton of people who had no idea they could do things like this with the base Blizzard UI. I answer questions all the time from people who don't know about the purchasable apexis mission things, or the outdoor mount droppers in WoD, or where/what the BMAH is, or.... I take all this stuff for granted, but for the players who hadn't played since Cataclysm or even further back who came back for WoD (and there were a lot of those), this is all new.

    It's not all going to work like magic. A lot of players only want to do things that improve their gear, and there's not a lot of activities that do that. But teaching players about all the new stuff in the game, and giving them one panel from which they can launch into any of those activities, feels like time well spent to me.
