    Just a note on DNA evidence: a lack of DNA on an object carries little weight, while the presence of DNA on an object carries a lot of weight. One cannot draw an inference or conclusion from the lack of DNA (blood, skin cells, semen, etc) on an object. For example, it is my understanding that DNA was not located on the flashlight yet we know it was carried to the scene by Zimmerman. DNA from the officer who collected the pistol was not located, yet he testified that he collected it with his bare hands. It is a common occurrence to touch something and not leave DNA evidence, or if DNA evidence was left, for it not to be collected and/or detected during testing.
