    Re: Star Trek Online

    Originally Posted by Shatter
    Yeah but look at the track history of most MMO's that come out, they all fail with rare exceptions. Its become an unfortunate repetitive problem that these companies in fact CANT produce a game that keeps people interested for anything longer then a month half the time. AOC, WAR, Tabula Rasa, Hellgate, CO, STO, etc etc. Its become a sad reality in the MMO industry
    Ironically, Jack "Statesman" Emmert calls this problem out in his July 2007 PC Gamer Magazine interview, where he basically blames it all on World of Warcraft -- or, to be more accurate, he blames it on publisher reaction to WoW; they all basically want to be "the next WoW", rather than wanting to innovate.

    The problem is as follows: very few development houses have access to the type of capital and timeline necessary to produce an 'everyman's MMO' capable of competing with World of Warcraft (considering Vanilla development cost over $100 Million to produce, and was done over the course of more than five years). However, publishers are really only interested in getting "WoW-like numbers"; they are generally not interested in niche-MMO's. Combine the two realities together and you get what has been the standard ever since WoW launched -- dozens of companies half-assing a 12-18 month development cycle off of a dozen million dollars to try and create a mediocre everyman's MMO to compete with WoW. Is it any surprise that they fail?

    Emmert argues that this cycle has to break by people creating niche MMOs -- games that do not pretend to be everything to everyone, but that do a very specific, unique experience remarkably well. Star Trek Online may or may not be that experience; we probably won't know for sure. It definitely lacks the polish necessary to be an everyman's MMO, so it has to thrive through some form of innovation to make it stand out as a niche game. It has accomplishes a few things really well:

    1. Unprecedented customization of characters, uniforms, and ships.
    2. Automatic grouping into "open" instances and "events".
    3. A cutting edge 3D physics accelerator (next-gen City of Heroes AGEIA dynamic)


    Bioware probably has the best shot of creating the new non-Blizzard everyman's MMO -- it has been spending many lengthy development cycles working on Star Wars: The Old Republic -- and so it will have the polish and quality to compete on a global scale, head-to-head, with other everyman's MMOs. They also have the capital necessary to fund a real challenge to World of Warcraft -- as they are targetting the same core demographic (role playing achievers and explorers), while appealing to the secondary industry demographic (killers and exploiters through a competitive side-game).

    Cryptic's only shot at producing profitable and enduring products is to shoot for a niche experience that other mainstream firms cannot afford to emulate. Hence, I think that's what they are trying to do here with STO.
