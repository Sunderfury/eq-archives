    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    I did the same, Silke. One purchase, to get the Legendary just in case I kept playing after Beta, and the rest of my collection will be from Arena packs.

    Originally Posted by Droigan
    But is the game at all viable without buying decks? Will you be able to earn enough gold to get enough packs to be competitive?
    There are deck archetypes that rely mostly on basic or common cards, like the Hunter decks. If you're determined to rack up wins to get gold, you can do it without spending money. Remember, in the early going you'll be matched against people who are just as cardless as yourself. I still recommend playing each class up to 10 against the expert AI first, playing each class to a win against each other class. It really does help you learn the basic stuff people will play against you.

    Originally Posted by Wool
    This game needs a replay system like in their rts games.
    You can look back a few moves with the thing on the side, but yeah, a log would be nice. Here's something that happened to me in an Arena match today that blew the thing on the side to pieces:

    I have out a 3/2 Knife Juggler, who throws a 1 damage knife at a random enemy target every time a friendly creature comes into play. He has out a 1/3 Acolyte of Pain, which lets him draw a card every time it takes damage, and he's given it +0/+2 with a Power Word: Shield (so it's 1/5). I play a 2/2 Starving Buzzard, which lets me draw a card every time I put a Beast into play, and the knife juggler hits the acolyte once (so 1/4 with 1 damage, he draws a card). I then put up a Secret, a Snake Trap, which summons 3 1/1 snakes when one of my creatures is attacked. They count as Beasts.

    You can guess what happened next.

    He attacks my Buzzard with his Acolyte, to deal a little damage to the Acolyte and let him draw a card. I imagine he planned to heal it with his Priest hero power after the attack, so he could continue to draw cards off it. However, that attack triggers the Snake Trap, which first fires off the Knife Juggler's ability three times in a row. All three knives hit the Acolyte, which gives my opponent three cards. Then the Buzzard triggers, so I get to draw three cards. Finally, the attack actually lands, the Acolyte dies from Buzzard damage, and he draws another card. We both had full hands (he drew 4 cards, I drew 3), I had a 2/1 Buzzard, a 3/2 Knife Juggler, and three 1/1 snakes in play. It took the game roughly a minute to resolve all the effects triggered by the one attack. Yes, that hunter deck made it to nine wins (I had three buzzards and two snake traps in the deck, and pulled that move on a couple more people, including my final match).
