    Re: Infraction for Frozen-Solid: Inappropriate Language

    Exactly! There are two very big sides of the debate. I'll respond to both

    1) I am a web admin. I do this for a living. I've been doing it since high school. I've been doing generic computer tech support as long as I can remember. This is my life.

    You are right, stealing bandwidth and image leeching IS a problem only to web admins. It is our job to make sure our sites are visible to our visitors no matter what happens. An image embedded into a Slashdot article's comment page can immediately bring down websites or use up bandwidth.

    Yes, bandwidth is extremely cheap now, and to be 100% honest "bandwidth" for me is unlimited. It's just easier to describe the issue as bandwidth than as anything else. Until recently my site was hosted in a friend's basement. Any bandwidth used by my site was not mine, it was his. It affected his business. I policed my site very very well to make sure I was not harming his business. If something was fishy, he'd e-mail me and I'd take care of it. What IS an issue for me right now on "unlimited bandwidth" is that I am on shared hosting. If my website's CPU usage gets too high, I will have my website removed, and every website hosted within my shared hosting space along with it. This affects many of my close friends. People image leeching is a possible threat to my hosting, and thus I have a zero tolerance.

    2) This would make a great thread elsewhere, but I'll summarize. Copyright in the US is broken as fuck. It's broken as fuck in most of the world. We'll use the example you did. You took an image of my dog, copy it, put it on tinypic, and post it on the forums with biting commentary about my character. Despite making a copy and taking it out of context, you are not actually breaking my copyright to that image. You can use that image as fair use, and in this case that means using the image in a fair way for comedy purposes to make commentary on me. If you claimed the image as your own, that would be copyright infringement. If I wanted to make a claim about the copyright, I could then go to tinypic and request they delete my copyright content. They would comply, and you could argue why you think it was fair use.

    Sadly, a huge majority of the world abuses copyright as a form of censorship to keep people from speaking badly about their copyrighted works.
