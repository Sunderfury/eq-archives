    Re: Suspension feedback

    Originally, this started as a response to this comment:

    In the future it looks like we'll also be tacking on an extra week if someone who is suspended comes back and registers another login in order to bypass it. At this time though, we've just banned the second account that Dmitry used. If you want me to struggle through filling out the form (difficult in this case), I can.

    I began by questioning a number of assumptions, most of which have not actually been satisfactorily answered. Voca at least stated the account was "Dmitrytest" which answered one of my questions. We still don't know how the account was discovered and what it had been used for.

    "comes back and registers" -- Do we have evidence of that? The Dmitrytest account has existed for quite some time.

    in order to bypass it. -- Do we have evidence it was used to try to bypass the suspension?

    we've just banned the second account that Dmitry used -- How do we know it was Dmitry?

    These should not be difficult questions to answer.

    In the future it looks like we'll also be tacking on an extra week -- With all due respect to Nadiar I don't think he is really in a position to dictate forum policy. This makes my questioning mildly more urgent than otherwise.

    If you want me to struggle through filling out the form (difficult in this case), I can. -- This just set off red flags for me. My whole purpose behind a form like that is to establish a baseline of discipline that a moderator must have before acting on an impulse. They are questions that should not ever be difficult to answer-- indeed in one of the rules threads I used an example where I answered every question as if I was banning a spammer, just to show how fundamental those questions were. It's exactly the situations where filling out the form is difficult that it is most important that attention be paid to it. Because it means either:

    (1) The form is deficient
    (2) The rule is deficient
    (3) The action may have been a mistake and deserves closer examination
    (4) The form is too much of a hassle

    If no attempt is made, then we can't figure out any of those things. In this case, we discovered a conflict between the notion of an "account" vs. "member"
    If Dmitry was suspended, then came back during that suspension under the guise of another account, he was breaking his suspension...and his action was dealt with.
    If he did, that is a fairly sensible position to have given your definition of suspension. The problem is that it seems like no one other than Nadiar and possibly other moderators have any evidence that the Dmitrytest account was in fact Dmitry. Somehow they had to discover that. Either he was engaging in behavior that drew attention to himself, or someone was being extra vigilant* and discovered/reported it.

    He's still unsuspended at 5pm today, nothing was changed, except his vehicle to try and skirt around his suspension was removed.
    A large part of participation in an online community like this is the reputation associated with your handle. The idea that creating an alternate account is actually circumventing a suspension is very much up to debate. Unless the association is made clear, the experience is completely different. People treat you differently, and your words have no impact on the reputation of your primary avatar. In many cases, the act of suspending the primary account is sufficient to achieve the moderation desired.

    Obviously, it is not difficult to make an association clear. The account could be named something like "Dmitrytest" and the poster could immediately return to the discussion that caused the suspension and attempt to keep it going. That, in my view, would be sufficient cause for an extra suspensions, etc.

    *one might say this is being a busybody.
