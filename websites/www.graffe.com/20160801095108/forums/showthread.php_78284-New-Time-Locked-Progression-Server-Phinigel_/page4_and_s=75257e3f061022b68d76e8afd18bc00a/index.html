    Re: New Time Locked Progression Server: Phinigel!

    Originally Posted by Dmitry
    You are married to the content as defined by the MMO on rails like WoW.
    The "rails" part isn't necessary. I think current WoW goes too far in making quests be planned XP boosts along a leveling curve and not rewards for exploration. Vanilla WoW, where quests were all over the place and didn't have clear indications of where to go, was fine by me. Same with the EQ quest system, IF the quests had actually been in game and working. Imagine pre-Kunark EQ with 30 or so quests in each zone. That would work for me. No "I'm killing aviaks because aviaks have a favourable spawn pattern and relatively low health for the level, allowing me to grind XP X% faster on them than on other comparable-level creatures". It would be "I'm killing aviaks for a chance at the nameds in the area, and for feathers and talons to turn in for repeatable quests for rep/coin/XP, and for the rare quest drops to complete some item quests - and I get XP while doing this". Everything placed in the game needs a reason to be there.

    If you think about it, this would be incredible for a game like EQ. Just knowing that, for example, there's something more to the spectres in Oasis than just solo XP would get people exploring the area, asking NPCs "What spectres" and "What Oasis" to try and trigger things, and folks would know that all this effort wasn't just some wild goose chase. That's what really hurt EQ - so many things were just fruitless wastes of time. You can't have that in a game as open-ended as EQ if you want people to engage in exploration.

    Think about the four gamer archetypes, from Bartle, and how they relate to random rusty weapon pinatas in the game world:

    Achiever: killing random mobs doesn't push a player towards completion of any of the completionist goals in EQ (maxing reps, doing all quests, etc.) It would if those mobs had more to them than just being XP on legs, but that's what I've been saying here, isn't it?

    Explorer: finding random mobs in EQ does very little for the explorer. They get a rush out of hidden areas and Easter eggs, which these areas in EQ typically do not have. You could add things like this, like say having two named aviaks have a conversation in Aviak if they manage to path together that can only be understood if a player has managed to master the language somehow. Again, that's what I've been saying here.

    Socializer: there's nothing to random mobs that turns the crank of the socializer. The aviak tree draws players to it, which the socializer likes. But the aviak tree with nameds, quests, etc. would also draw players, and I'd argue that it would draw more players.

    Re: New Time Locked Progression Server: Phinigel!

    It's a little unfair to hold EQ to a more modern standard of game design, particularly when that standard itself (WoW questing) has also become outdated. Besides, when you get right down to it, there isn't a right answer to this. You are talking about the difference in structured and cultured content versus emergent gameplay. There will always be people who prefer one over the other.

    The less directed a game is, the more autonomy the playerbase has in determining the course it takes. Some people find this gratifying. Others want the directed experience. They don't want to make their own fun and want to see something someone designed. There is no right answer, save the one we decide for ourselves. Though I will say that while I find your position harsh, I don't necessarily disagree with everything you are saying.


    As for the spoiler sites thing, WoW suffers from the same exact thing. There are people who want to experience things themselves, and people who want to be told what to do and finish it as quickly as possible. A very large portion of WoW players don't seem to even read the quests text. For these people, the WoW quest grind isn't that different from that old Kunark exp farming route that was so popular for most players. They just want to see the numbers go up.
