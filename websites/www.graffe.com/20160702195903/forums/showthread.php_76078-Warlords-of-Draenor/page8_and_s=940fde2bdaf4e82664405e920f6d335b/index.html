    Re: Warlords of Draenor

    Large Q&A with the PvP guy and the lead encounter designer guy.
    Some more info on heroic 5-mans (since that's my thing):
    Originally Posted by Ion Hazzikostas
    To some extent we’re looking to make heroic dungeons in Warlords of Draenor more difficult than they were in Mists. They aren’t going to be as difficult as they were in Burning Crusade, but we want to have a more robust tier of normal dungeons at max level for people who just want to casually queue up get some more or less guaranteed loot without too much of a challenge. We like heroic dungeons to require a bit more coordination, but we do recognise that there is this audience for really hardcore Burning Crusade-style or Challenge Mode-style dungeons. However, in Challenge Mode right now, the focus is so much on speed you don’t get that feeling of having to go through and mark your CC, polymorph that, sap that, for example. But we’re actually exploring the possibility of having Challenge Modes, as well as the speed component, also offer a chance at some gear rewards, LFR quality perhaps, so someone who doesn’t want to do LFR can do some hard content with friends and get epics that way. Further down the line, we have this new Mythic raid difficulty – we’re not planning on Mythic dungeons at launch, but that’s something that we could do. If we wanted to have an additional tier of difficulty, that option is open to us. It’s not a really good fit for random matchmaking, which is something we learned quickly at the start of Cataclysm, since there would be a lot of frustration. We do recognise that there is a lot of really cool play there, a lot of interesting MMO co-ordination in a smaller group, so we want to find a way to do it.
    Still no word on NOT being able to LFD for the WoD Heroic 5-mans. Sounds like they're aiming for Cataclysm-style difficulty, since they were a step down from BC 5-mans.
    Interesting part about the challenge modes possibly rewarding LFR-quality loot. The only problem: You can sleep through an LFR, and people tend to pick the path of least resistance.

    I'd put it somewhere between LFR and Flex, like the upgraded Timeless gear. If I have to actually work for it, it'd better outclass LFR.

    I play Feral, but I don't think melee need a damage boost.

    I'd like to see all classes get a regenerating shield (regenerates charges). That shield absorbs a percent of non-targeted (aoe, or ground effects) incoming damage, and regenerates after a period of not taking (any) damage, even if that damage is blocked, dodged, or absorbed. A direct, targeted attack removes all charges. That prevents it from being very useful in PvP.

    Then you can fine-tune the regeneration speed without adversely affecting actual balance. Lets say there is a base regen of 10 seconds per charge, and each character gets 2 charges.

    (The classes information here is for example only)

    Resto Druids have a lot of rapid-casting heals, so they have the full 10 seconds.
    Hunters are also the full 10 seconds, because they're never tied down.
    Mages, Warlocks, and Resto/Elemental Shaman have 9 seconds.
    Holy Paladins have a longer cast time heals, so they gain charges every 8 seconds, to reflect the likelihood that they stand in something for a tiny bit too long.
    Guardian Druids, Enhancement Shaman, Warriors, and Ret Paladins have few positional requirements, so they regenerate charges in 8 seconds.
    Rogues and Feral druids have positional requirements, so they regenerate a charge in 7 seconds.

    If you're an idiot, and stand in the burning fire on the floor, you'll live through 2 ticks, and then die. If your positioning or role causes you to pause for a half second before making a decision, you lost a charge, but as long as you don't run immediately into another one, you'll be okay.

Most of the time I get hit with something, is because I have some massive creatures asshole taking up half my screen. I dodge whatever fireball was about to hit me in the face, only to find out I dodged into the fireball that was about to hit someone else.
