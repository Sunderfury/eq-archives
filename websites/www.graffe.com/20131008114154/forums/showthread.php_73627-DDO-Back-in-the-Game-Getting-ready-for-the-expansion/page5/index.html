    Re: DDO Back in the Game Getting ready for the expansion....

    Originally Posted by Pilsain Silverback
    If you go from 8 to 16, can you go back to 8? Do you have to purchase the content for lvl 16? I'm f2p but with a lot of content unlocked around 8.
    No. That character is permanently leveled, and the only way to 'reset' him is to finish leveling to 20 and then True Reincarnate back to level 1.

    It is possible to level to 20 using only Free To Play content, but it's slow and boring. Most people suggest that the first adventure pack you buy with points is a 16+ level pack, just so that you have something to do at higher levels than run old, out-leveled content for scraps of XP.

    I seriously recommend people think hard about using the Stone of Experience on a favourite toon. I did it with my Favoured Soul, who was level 9. The gear he received was all about level 12 in power, and while it has some nice pieces, over all it is pretty shit. The best content I can run with him is the same content that my now level 11 cleric/rogue/ranger is now capable of running.

    However, that's fine for me, because I just wanted someone to farm higher level gear for said cleric as she was leveling. And he can do that. But if you're expecting to power level yourself past eight levels and suddenly do okay in higher level dungeons, you're going to be in for a rude awakening. You're still going to need to spend some time slumming in lower level dungeons, likely by yourself, farming for the gear you need to survive.

    Just as an example, for the vets - the gear that is given to you for using a Stone of Exchange includes only one Fortification item, and it is Light Fort. (However, the bracers are nice; wish I had a pair for my cleric!)

    Re: DDO Back in the Game Getting ready for the expansion....

    Originally Posted by DmitrytheWizzy
    If you do Lords of Dust like nine times on normal you can pretty much gear up a character at 16. You can do it on normal in about 8 minutes. It has four maybe five chests.
    Lords of Dust is one of the four highest level quests available to Free To Play accounts. There are two other sixteens and one eighteen. To cap a FtP character to level 20 requires micromanaging which quests you play at what times. If you're running that quest fresh out of the gate at level 16 on normal or casual mode just to get gear so you can run other things, you're screwing yourself out of potential experience points. A FtP will have a far longer and harder time leveling up following your method.

    My point isn't that it's possible. My point is that many people who use the Stone on their first character find the game far less enjoyable afterwards than they expected since they still need to run the lower level instances in order to be effective.
