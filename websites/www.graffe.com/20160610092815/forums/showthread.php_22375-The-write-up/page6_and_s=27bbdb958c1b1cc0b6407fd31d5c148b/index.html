    I still don't agree with the point though... it's equally accessible and doable by people who "just dinged max level" and the top end guild people, it just takes more people. Any family guild that can do the cursed has the basics down for ESR, and could probably kill him to boot.
    Listen, yes... anyone can GET to these mobs. That is not the point.

    It is easy for people to forget how much gear matters when they are in a upper end raiding guild and begin to take it all for granted. Take this from someone who was in a guild raiding time, then went back to a pre-ele guild to play with friends.

    Let's talk Elite Sun Reverant here (which, by the way... is 10x harder than Cursed cycle, lol.) Quite obviously, pre-ele geared clerics cannot stand in the AE and do a CHO, as there's no way in hell they'll have enough resists to consistnantly resist the AE (even with bard song) and one hit of that mana drain will completely kill their mana pool.

    So, that means that they'd have to spam heal on the tank... now... try spam healing on a pre-ele geared tank on a mob that flurries for ~ 1500's and procs for... what? 3000, I think.. Yea, it doesn't last very long. And the guy is no push over on HP's either.

    edit: Also, after reading my post and thinking... I guess maybe clerics could do a CHO if they all had extended range items. That works with CH, aye? (been a while since I played a cleric.) Even so, that a is pretty rough mob for pre ele geared tank, not even counting the two AE's.

    What happends when the tank dies? The mob ping pongs around, hits everyone with the AE, and everyone is good as dead... no matter how many players you have.

    Point? No matter how many players you have, you need the essentials like a tank that can take the hits from the mob, and healers that can keep up with heals. Family guilds and any other guilds that are pre-ele just do not have that, so whether they can or cannot access the zone means jack squat.
