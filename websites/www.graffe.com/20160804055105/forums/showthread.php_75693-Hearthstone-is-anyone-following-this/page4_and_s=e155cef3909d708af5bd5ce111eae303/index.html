    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    The Ringleader is fine if The Coin gets nerfed the way it should (neither counts as a spell cast (so it won't trigger combos) or a card in hand). Backstab isn't so bad, either. What I hate when I face a rogue (other than crazy coin tricks) is something that's not even a rogue set card - an early Twilight Drake. Those things are usually massive, and the next turn the rogue can empty their hand because of how their card set is built for speed. I might also complain about Van Cleef if more people were playing with it.

    If there's one class-specific card that I think needs retuning, it's the Starving Buzzard. It's one thing when a weenie rush deck can unload and try to push for the win. It's another when the same deck can unload and, even if you manage to do a board wipe, it has still got more than enough cards to rush again. If I were doing a first pass at tuning Hunters, I'd add one mana to the cost of the Buzzard, add one mana to the cost of Unleash The Hounds, and make the effects of Unleash The Hounds only last until end of turn. That should delay the Wave of Beast Death long enough to give the opponent a reasonable chance to set up some D.

    Other adjustments on class cards I'd try: -1 health on Flametongue Totem; -1 Attack, +1 health on Dust Devil (because right now it's terrible); -1 health on Northshire Cleric.

    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    I played a rogue deck this evening that got out an Auctioneer with maybe five cards in hand and three mana left. By the end of that turn he had an 8/4 stealthed Auctioneer, and by end of next turn he had a 12/4 stealthed Auctioneer (it had attacked that turn), a 1/2, and a 9/9 Van Cleef in play and ten cards in hand. It was so crazy he didn't even Headcrack me, because he would have lost the Headcrack because of too many cards in hand. I recognize he had a perfect hand - I saw two Preps, two Cold Bloods, two Conceals, etc. - but it was still impressive to watch. The problem there isn't the rogue cards - it's the Auctioneer. "Draw a card after the first spell you play in a turn" would make him a lot more reasonable.

    Originally Posted by Dru
    The big problem with rogue is that they can play their own threats AND handle yours, and one way they do this (in arena) is by drafting a lot of backstabs.
    They have to see the backstabs first. Trust me, as someone who has thought many times "oh, I'll draft all of card X" only to see none of them appear, it happens. You can also counter-draft if you think rogues will be a problem by keying in on 3+ health creatures. I find rogues will usually use the dagger plus Backstab to take my creatures out when I do that, which means they (a) aren't getting ahead on cards, (b) are taking damage along with me, and (c) are using mana every turn, keeping them from developing a really awful board position. It's not fun - you draft creatures to play with them, not to watch them die the moment they hit the board - but it lets you keep pace with the rogue until you can start dropping big things that can't be taken out by direct damage.

    Originally Posted by Dru
    The Defias Ring Leader could be fixed if it was just 2/2.
    A 2/2 for 2 which only has a special ability if you cast something else that turn, meaning it's not really a fast 2 mana creature? Hrm. I'd rather nerf The Coin first, and see if that does it, or if rogues are still able to pop it out early on a regular basis with Backstab or something.

    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    I was beating up on the rogues this morning with the class I picked, druid. So long as you survive the initial onslaught, there's so much card advantage built into the workhorse cards of the class that you can't help but steamroll (assuming they show up for you in the draft). I was more worried that one of them would draft multiple Assassinates. The class I saw at the end of the run was paladins, and the one class that almost beat me (seven life before I gained permanent board control) was a shaman.

    Originally Posted by Dru
    You can't draft a deck to counter all 9 classes, shouldn't have to draft a deck to counter rogues
    When you draft, you pick your poison, assuming you see the cards. For example, I refuse to draft anything with one health because of the classes that can remove a one health creature with card advantage (mage, rogue, warlock, paladin). I also tend to draft higher mana cost than I would in a constructed deck, under the assumption that the classic weenie decks (rogue, shaman, hunter, potentially warlock) won't have access to the necessary combo cards in sufficient numbers to pull off a win before I establish board control.

    Originally Posted by Dru
    A card can't be good on its own and then even better when comboed.
    A 2/3 for 2 isn't good on its own. You can get that with that 2/3 generic card beast, and it's a beast (which has a lot of synergy for hunters). This was the opening I had against one of the rogues this morning, he had the Coin (of course):

    Me: nothing.
    Him: Coin Defias
    Me: hero power, kill the 2/1 (take 1 damage)
    Him: weapon, attack attack
    Me: 3/1 charge, take out Defias.

    It's now his turn three. He's done four damage at the cost of the Coin, we've traded a card each, and he has a 1/1 weapon equipped. I really don't care what he's going to do next, because I'll have four mana and can start bringing out things he must Assassinate or weapon plus card (taking damage in the process) to clear. If you asked me, "Would you take The Coin out of the rogue's starting hand at a cost to you of four life", I'd do it every time. And he's just not going to have a Coin Defias Shadowstep Shadowstep opening hand in draft.

    As an aside, best move of the morning against the rogue: later game, I have an ogre mage out as my only creature, he brings out that 4/2 stealth tiger as his only creature. I'm just establishing board control, so I don't want to lose the ogre or eat 4+ damage (could Cold Blood). Target the rogue, Swipe (5 damage - yay ogre mage) with 2 damage to the tiger, killing it. You could tell he did NOT like that!
