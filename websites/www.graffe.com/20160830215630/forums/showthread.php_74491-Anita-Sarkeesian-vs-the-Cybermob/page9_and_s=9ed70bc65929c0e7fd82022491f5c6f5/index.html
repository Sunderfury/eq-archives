    Re: Anita Sarkeesian vs the Cybermob

    I'm sure I will watch it eventually, but I haven't yet. Heck I can't remember if I watched the previous one. These are just taking way too long to make. What was originally sort of a catalyzing agent for the topic in the online gaming community has largely been left behind by the continued evolution of the debate. I know this is a little optimistic, but at the rate these are going out I just can't help but wonder if the topic will even be relevant anymore when she finally finishes these.

    Just this past week, Ubisoft got a ton of negative feedback from their decision not to have a female character represented among the four playable co-op characters in Assassin's Creed Unity. A huge flaming controversy erupted over whether or not the "link" character in the new Zelda trailer was a woman or not. Transistor exists, Child of Light exists, Hyrule warriors's first three announced non-link playable characters are all women, etc etc.

    There are still people who get angry and push back, but public opinion seems to largely be in the pro-women favor, and publishers and developers will start to notice. It actually is becoming something that can be marketable as a bullet point in the advertising and not something you hide away in shame because the bros don't get it. There are still people who probably won't play as a female protagonist, but there are a lot more people who are hungry for diversity of any kind. Women are being represented more and more in playable stage demos of multiplayer games as well.
