    As for GMs, in my experience most of the "earlier" ones really did the job out of a love for the game, and often knew it inside out. The more SOE streamlined customer service (such as shifting most tasks to the offline interface rather than having GMs actually interact with people within the game), the less personal the "CSRs" became, to a point where most of them seemingly knew nothing about the game beyond what they could read off scripts.

    It also has to be said that players often weren't (probably still aren't) particularly polite in their dealings with Guides and GMs, let alone appreciative of the work they did/do. There was a lot of yelling and cursing and name calling, sometimes even threats. If it weren't for the occasional, genuinely grateful player, it would be a completely thankless job.
