    Re: Banning: Ialnyien

    We don't ban people to stop them suing Graffes. I'm well aware that it probably won't. We ban people because threats of RL harassment, especially of a legal nature, are simply unconscionable - they cause a huge amount of stress for the people involved, they are an attack on the board itself and the absolute weapon against conversation, and they show someone who has decided they don't want to be part of the community anymore. I'm not going to background check everyone who makes a threat of legal action to see if they are a lawyer or not - if you make the threat, you're gone. You don't get to threaten people in that way, full stop.

    Re. the goatse picture - Frozen got a warning for that, but given the circumstances around it he wasn't banned because he didn't link the picture. Mercus hotlinked from his site, and Frozen changed the picture in response. It wasn't a highlight of the boards best behaviour, but you can't reduce it to "He linked goatse" because that isn't what happened. I also think that, given events since then, it was clearly the right decision - I've enjoyed a lot of Frozen's posts, and glad he's contributing. He got a warning, the picture was removed incredibly fast, and we got a good discussion on the RL info part of the rules set out of it.

    Kaelon - this has always been the case. I think the issue is where the line lies. The moderators in my opinion generally feel that we apply the rules in 99% of situations, and that the times we do actions outside the rules should be extremely rare and only in those unforseen circumstances. Whereas I think other people would prefer the line was at the 90% mark rather than the 99% mark, for example.
    Other than some of the recent stuff involving Dmitry and Ianylien, I can't really think of a time when I don't think the rules covered what we did sufficiently. Even when I voted to ban Dmitry and the other mods didn't, I don't think that meant the rules were broken - banning is extremely serious, and I'm glad we have those checks built into the system no matter how I or other people voted.

    The updated rules set does contain a more explicit point about this "unforseen circumstances" stuff though, to make it clear to folks, along with what Voca's posted about the job of lead mod and admin. But what folks have to remember is that 99% of moderation on the board happens without anyone noticing it (hence my old signiture) - a private PM asking someone to tone it down, or remove a contentious line, or deleting a spambot / double post, moving a thread to the right forum etc. You get a lot more done through a calm, private PM to someone than you ever do in a threat shouting at them and telling them off in my experiance - what works in RL management works here!
