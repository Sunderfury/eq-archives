    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    So we now know what the special weekend events will be. There's seven in total:

    Pet Battle Bonus Event - triple pet XP.
    Draenor Dungeon Event - dungeon mobs will give appropriate rep.
    BG Bonus Event - random BGs will award triple honor.
    Timewalking Dungeon, WotLK - characters 81+ can do the Timewalking thing in Wrath dungeons.
    Arena Skirmish Bonus Event - Arena Skirmishes will award triple honor.
    Timewalking Dungeon, TBC - characters 71+ can do the Timewalking thing in TBC dungeons.
    Apexis Bonus Event - mobs that drop Apexis crystals will drop "far more" crystals (if I had to guess, 3x as many).

    What strikes me about these "events" is two of them are actually limited reversals of prior nerfs: the dungeon rep one and the BG honor one. Not a single one of these events will get me to change my current behaviour - none of them award enough for me to want to engage with them (although the Apexis Bonus one has potential if the bonus is really cranked up).

    There's also a PTR change that I will quote directly from the patch notes:

    Originally Posted by PTR notes
    Crafting the new upgrade items requires Felblight, a new reagent that can be obtained through Fishing, Herbalism, Mining, or Skinning in Tanaan Jungle.
    You can shutter the barn again, and everyone that dropped gathering profs can gnash their teeth in rage. I didn't drop my gathering stuff, but I'm really not sure about this, since the idea of having to gather tons of mats to get enough felblights, with no flying and with everyone else competing for the same resource nodes, sounds about as unfun as it gets. We'll see how it turns out.

    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    Good news: the navy stuff is down to a much more reasonable 150 garrison resources to get started.

    Unsurprising news: the game is down to 7.1 million subscribers. This is down 2.9 million from the end of last quarter, putting them below the numbers at the end of MoP, and it has no "mostly Asian subs" qualifier on it. This feels consistent with what I've seen in game: of the people that came back to play, many have stopped playing. The garrison grind seems to be the primary cause of people heading for the door, followed by "nothing to do" because, if you don't have a regular raid group, progression hits a brick wall.

    Remember back in WotLK, when you could slowly get Normal raid-quality gear by running five mans that were fun to do? Remember when you could do that back in Cataclysm, after everyone left and they were forced to nerf the five mans? Remember when you could do that in MoP, before the endless grind of Timeless Isle? I guess the replacements now are the random, often-useless item obtained from your BRF mission once per week, and the apexis gear that takes forever and a day for a single piece. I guess now we know why we're getting this "things to do" journal: to keep people from heading for the exit the moment meaningful character progression stalls.
