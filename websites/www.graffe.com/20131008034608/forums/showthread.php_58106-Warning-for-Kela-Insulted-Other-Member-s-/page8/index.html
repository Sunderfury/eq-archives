    I very much agree with the idea we should avoid deletion by mods as much as possible. There will be times it's called for but ...

    To me the biggest problem with the deletions is that too many times I happen across these deleted posts and discover I have no idea whatsoever what the offending behavior was. I prefer to have a clear idea of what's expected of me. Posts deleted or edited by mods as often happens leave me confused and frustrated.

    Kela alleges that Cinexa had deleted threads so she could appear to be the first to post on a subject. I want to believe there's a misunderstanding here and no mod at graffe stooped to such. If it did happen that person should be kicked to the curb unless there was a compelling reason to justify what happened. I'd be hard pressed to think what that latter could be.

    I had actually read the offending thread two days ago after at least one of Kela's posts got 'modded.' From there it's confusing as to just who said what. I was a bit surprised to see the moderation at EQ1 since I thought we were in FFA on the rules at the moment. Guess I misunderstood that.

    This certainly has become quite the thread and too much has been said I want to respond to. Firstly, it has been my experience and from what I've read at graffe the experience of a few others that people can and do kiss and make up. One of my most strident enemies became one of my closest friends till he left graffe. There's also one person I continue to detest. Generally, I try to stear clear of that person.

    I do agree that bad moderating is gonna drive people away. I'm sure we've driven new posters away too. I couldn't link the thread now, but I remember the drubbing one fellow got who showed up in OT ~1 year ago. He made the mistake of weighing in with strong opinions, got kicked around by the old guard AND a mod warning for dishing back what he was taking. It was a classic example of different spanks for different ranks.

    No doubt there's an element of knowing what is and is not gonna be tolerated by mods too that a newbie to the forum isn't gonna know. Still, my perspective was this guy got ridden out of town on a rail. Afaik he wasn't suspended, he just stopped posting.

    I don't doubt for one minute that Kela's posting style and conflict(s) drove some people away. Question is, what value do we want to put on that? If we're gonna go the way Goladus wants, we can be sure that thin skinned people will either lurk, or leave. I lean more toward what Qeul is arguing for here.

    That comment that you can't legislate morality? I first heard that 35 years ago. I have the utmost of respect for the person who said it, but back then I thought it was one of the dumbest assertions I ever heard. I still think it's a dumb assertion.

    We legislate morality all the time. Thing of it is, unless you get a high level of voluntary compliance, that legislation is unenforceable and meaningless. Rule of thumb I learned, and experience seems to back it up is you need 90% or better voluntary compliance.

    Interestingly, where dealing with crowds, the public etc. 90% seems to crop up a lot in terms of what you need to deal with. I do agree with most of what you said though Edimas.

    As for the idea we should avoid punishing and avoid trying to modify behavior? I don't see a need to molly coddle and engage in a bunch of touchy feely encounter group positive thinking only. When you want to extinguish an undesirable behavior you use punishment, plain and simple. Now mild punishment may be called for, or the banhammer.

    To me, as I've said before the important thing is consistency. If we're gonna go Goladus way, fine ... but then let me deal in my way w/those who try to yank my crank. I'm capable, if y'all wind me up of taking on all takers and standing alone. It'll be unpleasant for me, but it'll be more unpleasant for those yanking my chain. It'd take a whole lot to get me there but ... (more often than not I'll walk away for a few days.)

    I'd prefer to see us come up with simple and civilized rules and appoint mods who are able to fairly enforce, and be seen to fairly enforce the rules and keep things civil.

    It's worth noting that at least one person actually posted in the offending thread that he doesn't post much because of Kela. Unless that's a dupe account it speaks volumes.

    Yeah I retired mid-GoD expansion but I've continued to subscribe to EQ1 to keep an eye on things. I miss EQ dearly. Frankly, once things got stupid on the thread I think it'd have made more sense to move that one to rants. If people want to be civilized, fine start a new thread but drop the trash talk.
