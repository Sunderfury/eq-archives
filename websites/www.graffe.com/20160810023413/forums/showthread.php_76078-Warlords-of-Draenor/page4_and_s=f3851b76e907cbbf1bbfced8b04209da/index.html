    Right, but those paths should only trigger on the initial run, or for landing and taking off at a point. For all other points, you could just do a direct flyby.


    I was thinking a lot about this, as I was thinking flight was a mistake, because it shrank the game world too much, and removed World PvP.

    I was writing this response and I realized I'm wrong. Flight removes all of the tedious, stupid parts about doing older content in WoW. The levels are usually designed in such a way, that if you want to get around, you have to take long tedious paths. Need to go mining? Enjoy constantly being in combat. The problem was that flight is too fast, and land travel is too slow. Land travel has all of the possibilities of danger, but it doesn't get any benefits to trade off with that.

    Once you make land mounts equal to flying mounts, it's not that difficult to make mounts into Combat Vehicles to enable PvP, and/or add rewards for land travel. Maybe land travel can trigger phases for players based on the activities they seem to be performing. Are they flying around mining shit? Maybe have a random NPC encounter that results in the player finding a (small) instanced mine. They can clear the mine and mine several minerals with a better success rate than they might find out in the non-instanced area. It can't be completely random though. I'd make it pretty easy to determine what you were going to get (say, mine 5 nodes in 10 minutes, and you cause a mineral event to appear). Throw a marker up on the map, but the only way to get the NPC to trigger it is to explore while on the ground.

    When you try to make players do what you want by removing something (i.e. nerfing them) they're not going to be happy about it. Provide an incentive for doing what you want, and they'll pile on it.

    Re: Warlords of Draenor

    Details released on gear changes in WoD: http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/2...ds-of-draenor/

    So you don't need to dig in Celestalon's Twitter account (that was a silly way to do things, they should have just listed the new stats in their post):

    Pets will inherit all these new stats.
    The tertiary stats can currently be on all types of gear. They're still trying to figure out what that means in the oddball cases, like Life Steal on healer gear.
    X% Amplify means: +X% crit damage multiplier and Multistrike damage multiplier, and +X% more Haste/Mastery/Readiness/Spirit/Armor from gear.
    X% Multistrike means: each ability has two (X/2)% chances to hit each target an additional time for 30% damage. Since there's two chances, you can hit something three times (original hit, two Multistrike hits). It also applies to heals. Multistrikes are considered procs, and cannot proc additional effects. Multistrike and Cleave (below) are based off the original damage of the ability, and can crit. So for example: nuke does 100 damage. You crit on the target for 200. Your Multistrikes from that nuke will land for 30, but can crit for 60.
    X% Readiness lowers the cooldown on some class abilities (divide current timer by 1 + Readiness). The class abilities will be chosen for each spec.
    X% Cleave means: X% chance for single-target abilities to also hit a second nearby target, +X% damage/healing on multi-target abilities. Only works with damage or healing (no cleaving Polymorphs), the cleave can't proc additional effects, cleave won't hit cc'ed targets. For DoT/HoT effects, each tick is checked individually - it's not one check for the effect. That's part of the larger change, no more snapshotting for DoTs/HoTs.
    X% Avoidance means -X% damage from all AE effects.
    Life Steal will work on every hit.
    Movement Speed buffs will all work additively, so a 50% and another 50% will add up to 100%, not 125%.


    As someone with a priest main, I worry about how screwed up I'll be while they try to take a class that pretty much had to stack spirit in every slot (unlike other healers) and adapt them to the new spirit model. Same goes for my mistweaver, in the opposite direction (currently doesn't need spirit much). I can understand why they're doing the tertiary stat thing - so people keep doing runs of various sorts to try and get that "optimal" item - but I can't see this leading to anything other than burnout for a lot of people. It's RNG on top of RNG, and that never ends well.

    I love the "switch roles" thing and how it'll be implemented, although I wonder what will be done to make all the stats useful for all the specs. My warrior tank, for example, currently has almost no use for crit or haste. So how will those stats be made useful for her, or will I need to use other stats? And speaking of my warrior tank, how will all the dodge/parry/hit/expertise being removed from her gear be replaced (I assume it'll be replaced and not just lost)? I assume they're not going to add any of the new stats, since that didn't happen the last time stats disappeared (RIP Armor Penetration), but that's a lot of stats to be replaced and I can't see any generic "all hit is now crit" system working.

    Re: Warlords of Draenor

    Secondary stats on armor pieces will no longer include Hit, Expertise, Dodge, or Parry. These are being retired in Warlords, and will likely be removed even from existing gear.
    Secondary stats on armor pieces will also no longer include Spirit, nor will they include Bonus Armor, which are reserved exclusively for non-armor pieces (which are explained below).
    In addition, secondary stats on armor pieces may include several new stats we are exploring such as Readiness, Amplify, and Multi-strike. Technical Game Designer Chadd “Celestalon” Nervig has been sharing more details about these on his Twitter account.
    It sounds like they're thinking about just swapping them out for the new ones. Assuming they go one-for-one, Dodge and Parry would likely roll into Armor since they're all defensive. Hit to Amplify since all offensive classes would use both, and Expertise to Multi-strike since both are melee focused. Not sure where Readiness would fit in for that.
