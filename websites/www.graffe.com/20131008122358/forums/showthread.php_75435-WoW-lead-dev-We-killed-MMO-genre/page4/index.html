    Re: WoW lead dev: We killed MMO genre

    Indeed - when Conquest threw a fit of pique and published their Molten Core strats because Blizzard refused to kow-tow to the top end guilds who cheated to get kills they changed the nature of raiding forever. They certainly didn't intend it at the time - they thought they were punishing Blizzard by publishing the strats.

    Of course in reality what they did was allow for thousands of people who wouldn't have been able to raid to get into the raid game. That fundamental change from EQ broadened the scope of raiding far beyond what it was in EQ in a single stroke and cement WoWs position as the market leader.

    If conquest hadn't done what they did, and the same culture of secrecy surrounded every bit of raid strategy, WoW wouldn't have had as many subscribers as it did. Don't get me wrong - it would still have had millions upon millions, but opening up raiding like that allowed a lot of people to stick around and keep playing when previously they would have been locked out of the endgame.

    Re: WoW lead dev: We killed MMO genre

    I think you need to articulate what you class as hard more clearly Dmitry because right now I don't think your point is coming across clearly.

    Objectively, the stresses and requirements placed upon an individual raider in WoW are greater than they were in EQ by a *long* way. That's not an opinion, its just fact. Reaction times are much shorter, you have a lot more to actually do and the penalties for failing are much higher. Thats the nature of the advances in technology, gamesystems and internet speed. At the same time, the fights are balanced much tightly so that you can't win with 5 people down as we did in EQ all the time.

    As a raid leader, I think fights like the original Four Horsemen, BWL fight 1 (can't remember the name!), Vael, Netharion etc were much more complicated in terms of strategy than EQ fights from my experience - but that's a more subjective opinion I'm happy to argue on. I've used launch game fights to try and compare the era's better.

    EDIT: as a measure of objectivity, M'uru took the best guilds on the world on average 250 attempts to kill. There wasn't a single boss in EQ that has ever come close to that. Given that at that point we are still talking about some of those top guilds (including Fires of Heaven) that to me seems to be a good measure of difficulty. Of course any boss that took250 attempts to kill would have taken about 3 years in real time in EQ due to the way raid set-up and recovery worked.
