    Originally Posted by Yaxa

    Maledict, I'm curious, where do you import your games from? For me, once I add shipping, customs fees and taxes, the price advantage is gone. The last time I got something from the US, the fees were higher than the value of the product.

    Nowadays, when I do import stuff, it's usually from the UK, for a minor price difference. And most of the time, that's just to get an English version of it. The 360 version of the new Tomb Raider only had German and Spanish options, for instance. And the EU and UK are one region as far as DVDs, Blu-rays and all gaming systems are concerned.

    And for completeness' sake, not all 360 games are region locked, either.
    I use Videogamesplus.ca

    Canadian currency, similar to that of the US. They ship everything as "toy game, value $20", making it pass through most customs. My 60 gig PS3, two games and extra controller came in a giant box with a 20 dollar value sticker on it and I got no customs fees on it (which would have been customs fee + 24 percent tax.

    Save around 20 to 30 dollars per game compared to retail prices in Norway, after shipping. Shipping cost is 7.50 canadian per game.
