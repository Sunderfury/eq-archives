    If this was an isolated event, I wouldn't mind the complete lack of action on the part of the Graffe.com staff.

    However. There is a small issue of poster history here. I do not think that long running disputes are healthy for these boards. I truly doubt we can trust the parties involved in any long running dispute to sit down and work their differences out. It simply will not happen.

    I strongly believe in moderation focused on fixing problems and resolving disputes. I am strongly against moderation focused on correcting behavior.
    I have a problem with this. It limits moderators to simply treating the symptoms of the issue at hand and not addressing the actual problem. There is clear evidence of prior history at work here. That issue is being swept under the proverbial rug and simply not addressed. I don't like that. This is not a resolution of the dispute. There are still high tensions on both sides. This will occur again and the tensions will erupt in another thread, derailing another, possibly more important, discussion.

    As far as correcting behavior? Moderation at Graffe's has always been about maintaining the nice cultural wall between us and the rest of the internet. I think it's fair to say that the members of the Graffe forums stay here at least partially for the fact that we get all the benefits of internet anonymity with none of the drawbacks. We are a fairly civil forum at Graffe.com. It is our history of moderation that keeps that civility in place. Graffe's moderation in "the good old days" was swift, temporary, and it stung. I seem to remember a lot of suspensions being handed out when people got out of hand. A suspension isn't that bad. A little time to cool off before posting again, and it serves as a very clear "You did a bad thing". We've always moderated to correct behavior here. Personal attacks have never been acceptable. What this thread is showing me is very simple - the Graffe.com population is apparently now fine with two posters attacking each other. So long as they're not personally involved.

    And yes, I will use the word infraction. Derailing a thread with vitriol and flames constitutes an infraction against the community. The decision in this thread irks me because the moderators have decided to set a precedent of non-action. I may be wrong, but the exact reason all of this board remastering magic is happening was because of non-action was it not? I worry that if the moderators show a lack of willingness to use the punishments that are available to them, we will see an increase in snide potshots from posters that think they can get away with it. I think it's laughable that we're talking about moderation not equating to punishment in a forum named after a moderator famous for his Banhammer.
