    Re: Star Trek Online

    I'm 3 grades away from Captain. I mostly enjoy playing. I think whoever decided STO environments were acceptable should be repeatedly kicked in the crotch, but I mostly enjoy playing.

    Likes: Space combat. Character customization. Skill system. Bridge Officers. Episode missions.

    Meh: All other missions.

    Dislikes: Every environmental art decision in the game. "Crafting". Ship customization.


    This game was hamstrung from the get-go. Perpetual Entertainment basically shelved a million dollar IP for four years and then folded, leaving CBS Studios no choice but to go with whoever could get it built by their original deadline. Which is fucking stupid. I get that stockholders and board members want to see black on the bottom line, and I get that beancounters are too busy counting their beans to play MMOs, but only giving Cryptic 18 months to build a complete MMO is insane, and the game shows this.

    Every art decision in the game was based around what would be easy and fast to implement, which is why sector space looks like a Star Trek board game. Every system you enter looks like the asteroid field in Empire Strikes Back, and the 3 systems that aren't strewn with asteroids look like they're floating inside a glowing nebula. Why is it everywhere looks like the Eagle Nebula? Space didn't look like that in the television shows.
    And the ship interiors! They're HUGE! I figured I'd visit my bridge the other day; it's massive. It's 32 meters (104 feet) from the viewscreen to the back stations. The ship interiors were not this cavernous on the television shows. A captain will need a fucking blowhorn to issue orders on that bridge.
    I get that their art guys last job was making interiors for superheroes, where they can fly around and shit, but c'mon! This is a different game, right?

    I like most of the ship models, but the way they upgrade as you advance is stupid; it's designed to look more confusing than it actually is. The character models are OK. Their character customization is as fun as always. I love having bridge officers unless I'm leading them through an enemy base or ship. Then I hate my bridge officers. I lose more BOs to sharp corners than I do to enemy fire.
    And you gotta wonder how they designed a game where everyone has 4 pets following them around and then made maps where there are so many obstacles for them to get stuck on. Whoever designed the interiors of the Klingon ships and the so-called "abandoned base" needs to be punched in the face.

    But space combat is fun to me. The way the shields work make positioning and movement much more interesting than what occurs in Eve, though space combat is completely unbalanced in STO PvE. An even encounter should provide a little challenge, but anybody with half a brain can destroy pretty much anything they come up against.

    The game is fun so far. I've got about 120 hours played since open beta started and I'm not tired of it yet. If you guys are about, say hey to Unity.
    Last edited by Jarnin; February 9th, 2010 at 08:32 AM.

    Re: Star Trek Online

    I decided to pick this up.

    I'm not sure how long I'll play it. I love the space combat, but the ground game is a sloppy catastrophe. Horrible pathing for your NPC crew members, tons of rubberbanding (never seen in space), and a weird camera bug where it constantly zooms in and out are just a few of the problems. Not to mention how ridiculous it is to have to shoot a klingon 20 times with a phaser to kill him.

    I'm actually fine with the sector map, I just wish you could move faster on it. I find myself compelled to scan every anomaly I come across, and I have quite a stockpile of crap, but the things you can make out of it don't seem to be worth the effort of collecting it, and definitely not worth the annoyance of traveling all the way to Memory Alpha. What is the game balance reason for not being able to "craft" on my ship? The crew of the Enterprise were always developing cutting edge tech.

    It also bugs me that your profession influences which ship you should use. Apparently Scotty and LaForge should have been in charge of the Enterprise(s), not Kirk and Picard.

    Building and naming your bridge crew is lots of fun. It's disappointing that you can't stroll through your ship though. Also, I can't designate someone as my first officer. How do I know who my Number 1 is?
