    Originally Posted by Istari
    Why is it more important that Dmitry be suspended than Graffe's have a sane rule list that prohibits obnoxious behavior?
    The rules can't be changed until the next election cycle.

    And I think you'll find when we were formulating the rules, I repeatedly requested either we have a broad set of rules which COULD be applied to any situation, and had moderators that the members elected which they felt would do the best job of enforcing the rules when someone was going TOO FAR, or we had a strong list of strict rules which were enforced by moderators that the members elected who would be lax in enforcement.

    The differences are:
    Broad & Small Rule Set, Reactive & Empowered Moderators: Allows the moderators to enforce the spirit of the rules when people are being disruptive.
    + Prevents people from skirting the rules
    - Lots of arguments about interpretation
    - Allows for 'liberal' moderators to choose to do nothing all the time

    Narrow & Large Rule Set, Lax and Unempowered Moderators: Allows he moderators to enforce the rules easily when needed.
    + Few arguments about the interpretation
    - Arguments about moderator cliques
    - Allows for 'nazi' moderators to go on a power trip

    What we ended up with was kind of a mish-mash of both systems, and effectively was a Broad & Small Set Set, Lax and Unempowered Moderators system.

    Given the choice without knowing who the moderators would be, I'd aim for the "Narrow and Large" version, as I feel that gives the best feedback to new members on what is expected from them. I think that having elected moderators solves the problem people have with possible 'nazi' or 'liberal' moderators, because they can choose the moderators that best represent them.

    Re: Banning or suspension for Dmitry - community discussion

    There are some questions asked that I am on the fence on, and others that I have an absolute sense of an answer. So I will answer these in a rather different order.

    To start:

    Should Dmitry be stripped of moderator powers: Yes, now and forever. No matter the outcome of "banned or suspended", he should never be allowed to run for, or get elected moderator again.

    Dmitry has broken the faith of the moderator position, and in my opinion that is not something that should EVER be messed with. Sure "it's a message board" and all that, but responsibility is responsibility, and if you break the trust you should pay for it. If someone has shown themselves to be untrustworthy in that position, they might still be decent members of the community, but they should never be given a position of authority again. In other words, "Fool me once, shame on you. Fool me twice, shame on me".



    As for the suspension/ban question . . . this is both simple and complex, so I feel I must explain my reasoning. If you just want the answer, skip to the end.

    In general I feel that a poster, or member of any community honestly, should be given a chance to prove they've changed, which would lead me to a suspension usually.

    The problem here is that suspensions have many times been proven to not work in the case of Dmitry. He has been suspended many times over for disruptive behavior, he has been warned from The Man himself that he was on probation, and he still went there. He has shown repeatedly that he does not feel the rules apply to him, and as such is beyond the power of a "simple" suspension to affect him. Therefore, more drastic measures must be taken. If he is not permanently banned, then "this will all happen again", just as it has happened before.

    In Simple Terms: Banned Permanently or Banned a minimum of 1 year. Suspension isn't an option at this point due to his ability to read the forums while not logged in, and harass/continue via PM's.
