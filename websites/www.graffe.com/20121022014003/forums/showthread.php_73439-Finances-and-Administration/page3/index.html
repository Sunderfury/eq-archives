    Re: Finances and Administration

    Also: annual moderator elections is a good idea. The idea of a by-election would have to be reconciled with the current system of electing moderators, which is not built around having a fixed number of moderators but rather about maintaining a fixed level of popular support for moderators. One way out would be to have annual elections unless the number of active moderators falls below X, in which case we simply hold new elections for which all currently-serving moderators are automatically renominated. Our rate of moderator attrition is pretty low, so this would be a failsafe more than anything.

    We might also consider having a suitably unobtrusive way to recall moderators. Maybe a persistent confidence poll for each moderator, with moderators being expected to resign if their confidence polls show more people voting no than originally voted yes at the last general election. We might just keep their original election polls open indefinitely and just let people change their votes. That way the system would be almost entirely automated and wouldn't require much oversight beyond simply checking for fraudulent votes if a poll started looking close.
