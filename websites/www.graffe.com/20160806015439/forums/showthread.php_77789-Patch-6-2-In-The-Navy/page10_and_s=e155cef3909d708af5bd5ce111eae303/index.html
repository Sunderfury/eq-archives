    Re: Patch 6.2: In The Navy.

    The new dungeons comment caught me, too. I got that he meant future new dungeons, which is an indication that they want timewalking to scale up in future expansions and not just be a level 100 thing. But the obsession with matching risk to reward, and making sure rewards are right... stop. Just, stop. You can go too far with this.

    The quick answer on "how did this get through approval": in beta, we saw that there wasn't enough to do at max level. We told them this. So they threw in time sinks to keep us busy while they caught up. And that's a very bad sign, when things that are flat-out expected of an expansion are done last-minute because of lack of time. They're getting slower, not faster as promised. Take the "6.1 is really 6.0.5" comment: so, that means that the time between the first two major patches this expansion is close to nine months? I do accept that the apexis dailies were made the way they are for the reason he gave, though. Really, the only problems with them are (a) apexis crystals aren't worth the effort, and (b) doing anything other than killing mindlessly cripples the reward - less rep, takes longer to spawn invasions.

    The whole thing was a shill show. Many of the questions were easy lobs designed to communicate things the devs wanted to say, not that reflect the community's concerns. Have you ever heard anyone, at all, complain about the amount of gold from garrisons? If anything, it was one of the bright spots, as that combined with WoW tokens meant people could play for "free" with no effort beyond doing something they wanted to do anyways. And Lore's frequent "yeah, I totally agree" comments were pretty bad. Really, Lore, you can't stand the time it takes to reset missions on seven alts? I could pound that crap out in seven minutes tops, and so can he.

    The demonology thing was hugely disappointing (I've been Demonology since TBC). You control ALL THE NUMBERS. If you wanted a less complicated rotation, a few numbers changes could leave a spec with competitive DPS and an easier rotation. What I feel is the real reason behind this: a historical hatred by the team for Demonology because of how much of the spec's damage is passive. The devs have never shied away from the complex specs dealing higher damage in the past (like when Feral's priority system looked like a nuclear plant operating manual), so saying that now rings false.

    Yeah, of course they were forced to flip over on flying. Nobody believes the "we never considered what 'no flying, ever' meant" thing. So, you think every decision through and work to reach large group consensus, but nobody in the entire building said any of the things that eventually won you over, like "screw you for making me unable to ever use that hard-camped mount again"? I remember plenty of people saying it before - it motivated the "using flying mounts as full-speed ground mounts" change. I guess when you post it on the forums, or on Twitter, they don't really read it, but when you post it in the "Reason For Cancelling Your Account" box, THEN they read it.

    From MMO-Champ's notes on the Q&A:

    -The "patch 6.2 reps are better" thing is a half-truth. Some are better. One, Saberstalkers, is all grind with one weekly quest. Yay, Emperor Shaohao 2.0.

    -The "structure" in the 6.2 daily quests is pretty weak, from what's been seen so far. "Loot ten items from rare elites and/or treasures" isn't exactly a narrow-focus quest with a strong motivating force, nor is "kill any 20 orcs / 20 demons". It's ok to have dailies like MoP had if the reps that are boosted by them don't have character-boosting rewards (or if they do, open up alternate methods of advancing the reps like tabards for those who hate questing).

    -The "multiple alts" comment just grates on me. Yes, dev team, we get it, you don't want any mythic players to feel obligated to play alts for any reason, ever. But gold from missions? Really? Has anyone, anywhere, in WoW said to themselves, "Well, I could go out and do activities to get gold on a character I currently have at level cap, or I could level a new character from 1, and somehow come out with more gold from missions than I would have using that time to do something else"? THAT should be the point of comparison: if starting a brand new character and earning gold with missions after they level is more lucrative, over a sufficiently short time frame, than doing anything else with your main. And I flat-out refuse to believe that it currently is, especially with the effort required to get a geared/leveled follower group and the gold required to boost the garrison.

    -Garrison rewards being too high isn't the problem with world content. There being very little world content, and what content is there having weak rewards compared with easy activities like LFR is. Nerfing garrison rewards just takes away content. Give me a reason to do outdoor stuff, and I'll do it (and, to their credit, the apexis cost for 6.2 looks much more reasonable). Note: saying "we're reducing garrison rewards" at the same time as saying "naval missions will have more meaningful rewards" doesn't work. Either the naval mission rewards pale in comparison to outdoor stuff, and nobody will care about them, or they will be comparable to/better than/stack with outdoor stuff, and will be as mandatory as follower missions are currently.

    -I'm OK with Felblight, assuming a reasonable drop rate. But I never dumped my gathering profs like some people did. It should be interesting to see how much pushback they get on this from the community.

    -You felt the need to justify the disproportionate amount of time spent on developing Mythic content, hrm? That a disproportionate amount of time in a Q&A was spent talking about Mythic raiding says a lot about priorities. The length of Mythic Mar'gok and the lack of 735 weapons on Archimonde, pressing community issues? Really?

    -The whole "easy dungeons for better loot is bad" thing was awful. I *liked* doing speed-runs for gear. It gave me a chance at achievements that would have otherwise required organized gameplay, and made me feel the progression my characters had earned. I also liked that my characters were able to help newer players by carrying them through dungeons. If I had to rely on them to play at peak skill, I'd resent them for every mistake or under-equipped slot. When the place is easy, I'm a lot more relaxed and willing to help people with friendly advice and tips.

    -The focus of Timewalking dungeons - alt gearing - seems off. And, frankly, I don't buy the whole "not competing with new dungeons" thing when we got the fewest new dungeons of any expansion in WoD, without even smaller instanced content like scenarios.

    -"Shamans may not need Unleash" - if he wants to turn Unleash passive, he's a saint. Having a button to press that does nothing but apply a buff, and which needs to be mashed every dozen seconds, is stupid design. But keeping the benefits of Unleash is important. Most of the class stuff, other than "stop playing Demonology", was actually very on-point.

    -Why was important question time devoted to defending patch 6.1? You're not talking about the future, you're covering your ass for past mistakes. The Q&A really should have had a stronger focus on stuff that will change or not change in the future, not on one-off stuff from the past. (It's also pretty hard to say that the SELFIE camera was just a side project when not only was it made a focus of the patch notes, it also is the focus of a full-blown out-of-game promotion.)

    -The Adventure Journal sounds better and better the more they describe it. If it can actually capture the changes in patches and point players to current content over older level-cap content, it'd be amazing.

    -I don't think you can dump on portals making a world feel small when you've created the smallest expansion in the history of the game.

    -That armour colour question is a great example of a question I'd never want to see answered, but is likely important to a lot of players. At least there weren't many questions like it.
