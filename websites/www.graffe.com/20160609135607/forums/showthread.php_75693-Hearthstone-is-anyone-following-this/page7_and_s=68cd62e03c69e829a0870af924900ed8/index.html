    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    The suggestions I've made on gold and cards:

    1. The Arena rewards should never include 5 dust as an option. Instead of 5 dust, give the player a random common. They DE to the same amount of dust, and they might give a useful card to someone who really needs it without significantly diminishing the desire to buy packs for rares/epics/legendaries.

    2. There's many levels where your character doesn't get anything for dinging. In the 11-20 range, a ding should also give one class-specific common, so by the time you're 20 you have one of all of the commons for that class. At higher levels, when you ding if you don't get a golden basic card you should get a small amount of gold. That way you'd still get gold, win or lose, but it's not a huge amount and you wouldn't get odd behaviour like people conceding for gold (XP seems to scale with the number of turns played, with a bonus for winning).

    Originally Posted by Fyrie
    If there was a better UI for the online MTG game I think I might just play that instead because there's so many tournaments to go in there and I find that personally I find some of the tactics in MTG (like the effect of interrupts) to be more interesting.
    Hearthstone is very much like those MTG puzzles you'd see where you need to do X by the end of the turn. I like that about the game: you really feel like you're in control of your turn (unless the opponent is playing one of the two secret archetype decks). I also like how they've mostly taken the mana screw out of the game. It's not a complicated game, but it is fun and engaging. I wonder what they'll do when the time comes for an expansion. Will they bring in new concepts, like the graveyard, or stick with what they have and flesh it out more (like more secrets)?

    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    Originally Posted by Dru
    Sure if you make Mind Control cost 4 mana... otherwise make Polymorph, Hex, and even Assassinate countered by silence.
    Each of those cards is one for one (technically, Poly and Hex are one card for zero, since the opponent still has something on the board). Mind Control is one for two: the creature you lost, and whatever card you use to eliminate the creature you lost. That the creature you lost is likely the best creature you have (see below) makes it even worse.

    Originally Posted by Dru
    Most people wouldn't be frustrated at all by it if they just learned to hold onto their giant minions instead of dumping them as soon as turn 8 rolls around.
    How do you plan to kill the priest by holding onto cards? Remember, the priest has a disturbing amount of removal baseline: the Shadow Words, Smite, Holy Nova, the mind control minion whose name escapes me because I don't own one yet, Shadow Madness, Auchenai Priestess, and that you're a shadow priest now spell. Then tack on some of the removal from your deck, courtesy of the "copy cards from opponent's hand/deck" cards. A priest that isn't against the ropes on turn eight has a lot of say over what creatures stay on the board, which creatures get removed, and who has the biggest threat. There are creatures that are tough for a priest to remove, like four attack creatures with high toughness. But if your deck isn't built to kill priests, and the opposing priest has some idea what threats to expect from your deck, "hold your biggest threat" is easier said than done.

    Re: Hearthstone - is anyone following this?

    Everyone has a lot of removal baseline. Warrior has: Upgrade!, Cruel Taskmaster, Whirlwind and Inner Rage for 1 health minions; Arathi Weaponsmith's weapon, Cleave, and Slam for 2 health minions; Fiery War Axe for 3 health minions; Heroic Strike and Mortal Strike for 4 health minions, Arcanite Reaper for 5 health minions; Execute for anything health but damaged minions; and Shield Slam for any minion with health less than or equal to the warrior's armor. Every class has a similar list. The game is all about holding onto your cards until you can 2 for 1 (or 3 for 1 or better!) your opponent. If every card in the game was designed just to trade for 1 other card we'd have 20 minute slug fests that ended in fatigue where Warriors, Priests, and Hunter's win every game.

    Originally Posted by Dru
    Everyone has a lot of removal baseline.
    That doesn't change what I said about not having critters on the board that the priest doesn't want there.

    Originally Posted by Dru
    The game is all about holding onto your cards until you can 2 for 1 (or 3 for 1 or better!) your opponent.
    That's a control deck outlook. I'd say there's two basic ways to win: either send out threats faster than your opponent can remove/nullify them, and overwhelm them, or control which threats stay on the board until some of the opponent's cards can no longer trade one-for-one with yours (you can also outdraw the opponent and achieve the same thing). Mind Control effects are expensive for the same reason board clears are expensive: they're designed to cost the opponent multiple cards at once. The difference is that board clears generally have limits: they only do so much damage. The limits on Mind Control are very few: one common, one of the cards you can draw from Ysera, and any Stealth minion that hasn't attacked yet. Right now priests have too many ways to get to the late game in a position where they can pick and choose what they take from you, to ensure that it's 2+ cards of yours for one of theirs.

    A data point for my "give the players more common cards" idea: right now, I'm at 235 total character levels. I have 219 Rated Play mode wins, and 212 Arena wins.
