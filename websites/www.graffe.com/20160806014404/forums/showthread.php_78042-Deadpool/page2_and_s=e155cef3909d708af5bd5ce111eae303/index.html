    Re: Deadpool

    Lives up to the hype IMO. Everyone should go see it in IMAX. I don't think any of it was filmed in 70mm (not all IMAX are set up for 70mm anyways, most are not [1]), but I have endless praise for the costume department for this film. All of the costumes were awesome. Everyone should see Morena Baccarin and Ryan Reynolds in those outfits at buildings scale, it redirected my future schemes from empire building to building an IMAX theatre to project home made porn at that scale, it was awesome.

    Anyways, back to the movie. It is the most *fun* movie since Ghostbusters. It is a rare example of any creative endeavor executing on its premises without compromise at any point. Almost everything is complete shit because of the absence of this, people are okay with doing shit work, putting out shit products, and racing to the bottom rather than trying to make something that realizes its premises all the way through. Whatever comes out of that may be complete shit still, but it will at least be informative, unlike the other shit work which due to its endless half-measures tells us nothing about whether the concept is good or not and whether it should be pursued further.

    Deadpool is fucking amazing. It is the perfect Deadpool film. Ryan Reynolds is to Deadpool as RDJ is to Iron Man. I have no criticisms of the film.

    Everything in the film was done correctly. Even the body types of the women cast was correct in the context they were participating in. It was also nice to see the Hair and Makeup department not have someone's makeup not matching their skin tone at any point.

    Spoiler for film:

    Teenage Ninja Mutant Turtle Sonic or whatever her name is had curiously little dialogue. She was pretty bad ass. I wonder if a lot of her dialogue got left on the cutting room floor.

    I was filled with turmoil during the Francis v. Deadpool scene where Colossus is giving his speech on morality. I was screaming NOOO! Shoot him in the fucking face Deadpool. And he did. It was carthartic. Finally a movie where the writers do not bitch out on their premises half-way through and hamstring the "bad" guy so that the "good" guys can press their I Win button and leave the audience will feel good chubbies rather than bringing the characters actions towards their logical conclusion.

    The scene where both Morena and Deadpool are injured and Deadpool is seeing the animated characters around her was by far my favorite part. So freaking amazing. The audience was cracking up the most during that part.

    I was hoping Morena would stay with Deadpool as I felt that she would in real life: consider that yes, Deadpool is hideous, however Morena did not engage Deadpool because of his looks in the first place, Morena can get dick attached to a nice face and body any day of the week. She engaged Deadpool because of his personality that separated him from the other dicks. So, that Deadpool is now not particularly handsome in an ordinary way, is not as big of a deal as it would be for some partners. For most people that would be a deal breaker but for Morena and Deadpool it remains plausible.

    Also, Morena leaving Deadpool or Morena dying would be the expected thing so it is a lame story element.

    The absolutely only thing I did not enjoy 100% was the song choice at around mid-way through, I forget the exact context, but it was basically a rap about Deadpool. All the other song choices were great, DMX's and Salt-n-pepa's tracks in particular, but that one was pretty weak. Deadpool controlling the timing of X Gon' Give It To Ya was fun.

    On Morena's part, during the scene where she is trapped in the pressure chamber contraption, I think whoever was giving her directions did not describe well the differences between being affected by changes in pressure and breath. It seemed like she was acting like she was intermittently suffocating, but it is different. The way Ryan Reynolds' portrayed it is more visually compelling.

    Morena's a proficient actress, so I don't think it was a matter of capability. That was the only strange part of the film to me. It is extremely, extremely minor though. I don't think I would have noticed had Ryan's protrayal of it not been a significant couple of scenes early on that the audience is asked to focus on. It struck me as weird though when I saw it again with Morena and it was portrayed significantly differently. I think she must have been given different directions at different points in the film's timeline as how she is reacting to it is changing as well, at some points she is acting like she is being asphyxiated in others she is acting like she is experiencing pressure difference. It is strange.

    The after credits scenes were some of my favorite parts of the film as well as the animation during the beginning of the credits. Great stuff.

    P.S. Stay for the credits.

    A rubric for will I enjoy this? is, if you enjoyed Blazing Saddles (see it if you have not already, it is another Mel Brooks classic) and don't mind graphic violence, then you will enjoy Deadpool. '

    Deadpool does not have any substantial social commentary like Blazing Saddles does, so don't expect anything like that. Deadpool is just for fun and profit.

    [1] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_IMAX_venues DP can do 70mm or digital projection, 70mm obviously can do 70mm, follow the legend
