    Re: "Shannara" comes to TV

    It's more than that for me.

    These were books I re-read seven or eight times each (the public library didn't believe in stocking much sci-fi or fantasy. They had, literally, only two shelves of one bookshelf of fantasy in the entire 4,000 square feet.)

    25 years ago I could have told you every detail of every map and noted the differences between books (fucker never could figure out how rivers worked on a geological scale) and recounted the history of the druids from Galaphile to Cogline to Bremen all the way to Walker Boh. I probably could have drawn a map of what was described of the interior of Paranor.

    So...yeah. I don't even see the cheese.

    Then again, the books were pretty cheesy most of the time themselves, especially the early stuff, but I'd say, on a whole, this is FAR less cheesy than the Sword of Truth series was, and that was fairly decent for a fantasy offering.

    I guess the bar is pretty low for me.

    There hasn't exactly been a lot of "sword and sorcery" fantasy television that moves above mediocre unless you're in love with the setting.

    (edit -- I'm not counting Game of Thrones because it's so utterly stellar that it's in a class of its own, so putting it on the scale would tip it so rapidly as to fling everything else as far skyward is generally needed to enter low earth orbit.)

    Shannara has that exact thing going on.

    If you watch the trailer, you can see what looks like the Space Needle fallen over and decaying.

    There are multiple mentions in the books that eventually lead you to draw the conclusion that this is earth, it's just earth in the far future after humanity nuked itself out of existence and people had to start over.

    The elves came back out of the realm of faerie, the dwarves came up out of the ground, and the radiation mutated some portions of humanity into gnomes and trolls.

    3,000 years after that, we're in the era of Elfstones.

    It's much more subtle in the books, but it's still nodded at.

    The tv show basically makes it outright apparent, especially when Eretria and Amberle fall into a ballroom that hosted a high school prom.

    I love alt-history stuff. I look forward to reading yours.
