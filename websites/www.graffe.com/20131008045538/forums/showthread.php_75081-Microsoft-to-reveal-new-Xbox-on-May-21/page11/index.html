    Re: Microsoft to reveal new Xbox on May 21

    As it's a shorter response, I'll reply to the PS4 RAM stuff first, even though I typed it second :

    Wait and see. Even the people that leaked it are telling people to chill the heck out, that it affects developers more than it will the consumers. That sounds so ridiculous that it's borderline unbelievable (the fact Sony hasn't said anything is the only reason why I do believe it). I can understand why Microsoft would reserve so much RAM, but not Sony. And (again), I'm not a developer so for all I know this stuff is normal.

    Anyway I've seen a few possible explanations: either Sony is reserving room for growth later (which is what Microsoft says they are doing), or it's simply a limitation of the first round of dev kits themselves and that future kits will allow more RAM to be used by developers, but either way: wait and see. I actually want to hear what Gearbox has to say about it, as they claim to be the ones that pushed so hard for the extra RAM in the first place.

    Originally Posted by Ninetoes
    We are discussing consoles, games and development specifically the X Box, I think someone who works at MS has valuable perspective we should be more respectful of.
    Yeah, allow me to voice the concerns I think most people are having in a less aggressive manner:

    The reason why no one is talking about Microsoft's promises to render graphics on "the cloud" is because no one believes they can do it. Until people see it working, "the infinite power of the cloud" is just marketing fluff.

    I am not a developer or programmer myself, but I've seen people with hardware backgrounds saying that those particular claims are unrealistic. I think I've posted it before, but here is an article detailing why: digitalfoundry-in-theory-can-xbox-one-cloud-transform-gaming. I don't think this article covers it, but I've also seen people suggesting that the memory bandwidth (or something) within the hardware itself is also a limiting factor.

    I do think Microsoft is attempting to be forward thinking, and for all I know they are laying the foundation of something that will be possible in ten years, much like they laid the foundation for console internet gaming, but like I said, the true test is going to be when and if someone actually does it. As of right now, the two most prominent uses of Microsoft's cloud are AI storage and dedicated servers. These are good things to have, but they don't have the buzz and the magic of saying your console is 10x more powerful thanks to being connected to the internet.
