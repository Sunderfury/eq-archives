    Actually, these are almost exactly the numbers that Varressa projected. Your new damage is 87.2% of your old damage....a 12.8% nerf. As I recall (since I am far too lazy to click the button and go back) the initial projection was 12.5%.

    Your actual raw numbers will vary, but the percentage on resists, RNG, and harvest times are constants. They can be discarded for purposes of determining the percentage of the nerf since they were the same before and after. So while you may not actually be able to do 193614 damage on one bar of mana now, likewise you were not able to do 221996 beforehand.

    If the resist mod on the spells you chose was changed, which is very possible, then that would need to get factored in...what spells were you using on the tool? Chaining flashfires? Chaining Flashfires and EC / SF / CI ?

    If you want to figure out an ideal nuking strategy...

    You should have an ordered list of nukes, a mana pool, a mana regen, and a time limit.

    The wizard uses the first nuke by default.
    If the wizard would not go OOM chain-casting the first nuke, they cast the second nuke as often as possible until they do just barely go OOM in the time limit.

    If casting the second nuke as often as possible doesn't let the wizard go OOM, they start casting the 3rd nuke more and more often.

    Etc.

    On top of that, on a GOM proc, you should cast a specific GOM nuke.

    This will give you "How much damage can I do in X minutes".
