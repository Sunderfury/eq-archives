    Re: Game of Thrones: Season 3 Starts Soon!

    I'm going to have to watch the second half of the episode on a replay; I turned it off when Robb found Qyburn at Harrenhal. This show is excellent, well-acted, lovingly produced, and is the best fantasy to be found on television. However, I've come to accept that for some reason I just can't acclimate to the differences from the books. I've never had this problem with other adaptations, but I'm spending too much time grumbling at my television compared to enjoying what I'm watching.

    I knew things would be different going in. Faces wouldn't match my mental pictures. Every battle wasn't going to look like the Pelennor Fields. Characters and plots would be shifted around or excised entirely. But half a hundred little annoyances have built upon each other and robbed me of the enjoyment of a very good series. I almost wish I hadn't read the books, considering the TV series is far more likely to reach a conclusion.

    I buy the bluray versions once they come out because the picture quality is notably better than the original broadcast. Not all shows benefit from the added quality, but GoT definitely does IMO, plus the story is so good that it is worth rewatching.

    A significant motivator for piracy (according to VALVE's data anyways) is the asymmetry in availability, even among English speaking countries and changing that improved VALVE's sales and net profits, as well as their Steam sales (which may not be an appropriate model for lower priced content).

    People want to watch the show as soon as possible and not making those available concurrently with other regions means their only rational alternative is to pirate.

    These are people who would pay for the content but can't because it simply is unavailable in a legal route.

    Then there exists another group who would pay for the content but can't due to being unwilling to access HBO through having to have cable/satellite to do so.

    There exists another group which would pay for the content through legal distribution sources but are unwilling to pay more for worse quality (true of all iTunes/Amazon content, and in some cases they inject ads to worsen the blow and why I refuse to buy content on those stores).

    Another case is where even if it was available the people would be unwilling to pay due to wage differences so they aren't accessible customers regardless of availability and have to pirate to get the content (not really sure what to do here, motivator for region pricing on many products though) and including them in the sum is disingenuous as they do not represent lost money in any way.

    Allegedly HBO in Europe is unbundled from cable/satellite and you can purchase HBO Go directly which would be a huge improvement in the US.

    If Netflix can offer 1080p streams (and they do w/ their SuperHD content) there's no reason HBO can't also.

    There are probably user groups I am overlooking here that want to but rationally can't access the content legally.
