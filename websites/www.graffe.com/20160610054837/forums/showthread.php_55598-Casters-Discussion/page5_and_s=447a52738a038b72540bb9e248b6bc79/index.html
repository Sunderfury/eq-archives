    Lol actually i showed how we could change nothing else on our spells other than filling in the levels BETWEEN the ranks.

    Just a highly simplistic way, and this works on any heal or damage spell:


    Take the higher rank, subtract the lower rank, and that shows the "difference" in damage/healing/effect and energy that happens over that 6 ot 8 level span inbetween the spells.

    Simply divide these numbers by how ever many levels are between spells, and you then have exactly how much to "add" to each spell for each level "over" the lower rank.


    Example would be Taqmir's. The level 42 and 48 spell, since taqmir's has a level span of 6, i found the difference between them, and divided it by 6, and thus how i found out that at level 46, taqmir's should be 4 times the value increase per level, or if you wanted, two times the obtained value subtracted from the higher rank.


    42 43 44 45 46 47 48

    For each level above 42, we add X amount of damage and energy to the base of the spell.

    Thus, the spell grows naturally with your character at the base level inbetween each rank, instead of getting a large "jolt" at every 6th level after a "lag" for the past 5 levels.



    Also, i would aurgue that magic should be strongly amplified through our weapons, rather than the weapons just being "another piece of gear."


    It also allows for more customization, as a wand could have higher critic % boost and staffs higher crit damage, just as 1 hand weapons have the +5% crit bonus while two hand weapons have higher crit damage.

    Imagine, in a real overhaul of the system, if spell casting speed and damage was largely based on gear.



    right now we can have some rather different builds based on what stats you focus on, but imagine if this was taken a step further, that would could chose to have foci, staffs, and wands as different weapons, perhaps even "spell based shields" that offer direct spell mitigation.

    If a wand is for faster casting and a lowered global reuse on instant spells, and increased spell crit chance by 5%, and if we had a Spell Shield of some sort in our off hand, and a sorcerer was utalizing Chaos Volley as his main offense?

    Or if a sorcerer wanted the highest possible "bang" potiential, and went heavy on Int, SDF, and used a 2 hand staff, placing a large amount of +critic on his armor to best benifit from his improved damage on criticals, as well as taking advantage of forced critical spells and abilities in a group/raid setting?



    Would be rather neat to see how they could diversify spell-damage weaponry, making advantages for each type.

    Imagine if:

    Wands/Daggers = faster casting/cool downs with increased crit chance
    2 hand Staffs = hard hitting criticals
    Foci = Improved accuracy and lowered costs
    Runes = Improved spell avoidance and mitigation (spell "shields" if you will)


    These would be innate effects on the weapons, while you could still add any crafting effect you desire, allowing for my versatility as well as balancing another descrepency.

    Also, with melee auto attack being seperated from global cool down, casters need to get an equivilent "no cost" method based on weapon damage, this would at least give staffs/daggers/wands some "use" over foci, where as currently using anything other than foci in the majority of the game is no where near as effective.
