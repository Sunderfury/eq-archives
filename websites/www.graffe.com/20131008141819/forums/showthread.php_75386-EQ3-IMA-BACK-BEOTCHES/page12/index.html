    Re: EQ3 - IMA BACK BEOTCHES

    Originally Posted by Droigan
    Contacting SOE now to get my old account back for that very reason. I have forgotten my password and the answer to the everchanging question, what my favorite book movie is

    But, as to the fact that they have a different company doing the EU parts does not look good for being able to play on both servers. Do not think I have ever played a mmo where the billing is split between different companies and at the same time have similar servers. Since this is already a thing, I am 99% sure there will be region locked servers.

    Reasons for it are usually tied to latency, which is less of an issue these days, but increase with the amount of active combat. The big reason for it in this game though, might be that in EQ, depending on the servers, various timezones sometimes had issues with raidbosses being down due to competing guilds with their peak timezone hours before others. In EQ:N, with a supposedly ever changing world, people on some timezones might outright miss content. A role call event that changes the world for 3 months, where the majority of the activity happens 12 hours past someones peak time. A persistent yet changing destructable world might actually be the thing that gives a good reason to join a server with a peak population time closer to your own.

    I still think direclty forcing it upon players is a bad idea. Idiotic considering EQ allowed for it. How many of "these are my EQ memories" would not have changed if the had blocked servers?
    There might not be an actual EU/US split in the way LOTRO split players through Turbine/Codemasters accounts. Seeing as EQN is using the PS2 engine, this is a good place to start:

    I installed PS2 launcher through their website and was playing fine and dandy (I chose an EU server). Later, when I added the game through Steam (to make grouping & chat with my friends easier), for some reason the game did not recognise my login at all and I had to create a new login. I had a new character but otherwise carried on the same server with the same friends. Steam's version ignored the existing installation and wanted to redownload the entire game from scratch.

    Turns out that the 1st account was with Proseiben (or whatever they are called) from when I installed and joined PS2 through the websites game launcher. For whatever reason that I never bothered to look in to, the Steam version of the PS2 launcher only connects directly to SOE and cannot access the Proseiben (EU only) accounts. Technically Steam and Proseiben's clients are separate installations that can be patched independently.

    Now I have 2 separate PS2 accounts but I have to use the correct client to access the appropriate login account. The in-game characters both have access to the same game servers and in-game are not restricted from accessing anything I have noticed so far.

    So the only real difference with the way this has been handled in PS2 is about method to log in - once in game you have full access to everything anyone else has worldwide.

    Hopefully they'll not mess around with this for EQN.

    Dear Jesus, is that what I think it is re: raising levels?

    Originally Posted by Article
    So our progression system is not based on levels, it’s based on tiers. There’s a shallow tier pool, because one of our focuses is on horizontal gameplay. We definitely have vertical gameplay, where a player increases in power in a particular class, but remember, there’s 40 classes. There’s a lot of classes out there to gain, and each one of these is advanced individually.

    It’s based on player accomplishments. It’s not skill based, so you don’t have to use your mace over and over again to level your mace skill up. But your tier three warrior, in order to advance to tier four, has to collect a full suit of tier three armor. You have to have spent enough points in that class. You have to have accomplished a certain amount of things in the game in order to have advanced.
    That sounds very much like the Final Fantasy XI subjob system, where you needed certain quests to advance past certain levels in a given class. And it was the most colossal grind in all of gaming. Hopefully the "obtain all your tier 3 armor" is much easier than it sounds.

    "Attributes are useful to everybody" doesn't sound too interesting. The optimal builds will be mathed out, and people will min-max attributes accordingly. If attributes don't matter to the core design of a build, players will throw the points wherever, without paying much attention. It's the same thing that's happened with WoW's new talent system - the DPS/HPS/tanking talents are taken, and the rest are ignored by everyone except PvP players. Still, it's good that the devs are trying to make loot drama happen from day one! "INT gloves are hunter loot."

    "Discovering" classes sounds interesting. It'll be the sort of thing that's spoiled from day one, but at least it comes with content and not just with a generic grind or click on some skill points.

    Did your best healer quit the guild? No worries, it's no longer going to be detrimental. Time to move on. #EQNext

    No Trinity.

    No dedicated healers or focused tanks based on combat mechanics.

    Combat model – visceral, active movement orientated gameplay.

    Everyone is responsible for their own safety, and part of the action.

    ++++++++++++++++++++++++

    This will be, as Dmitry likes to state, an easymode cartoon.

    With no interdependency in combat, you can expect no interdependency in terms of death mechanics. With no interdependence in death mechanics, it will be a "solo" feature to die. Dying will have little risk/hassle at that point. Game will have to follow the common route MMOs have gone of having to increase loot tables to give you constant rewards to offset the lack of feeling you get when a reward comes at little to no risk.

    It might be a good game, but it will not be EQ. It will have far more in common with the current popular games than EQ, and mechanics EQ had that are no longer in use (death mechanics and interdependency) or downtrending (holy trinity) will not play a large feature, if at all, in EQ:N. It will not be a WoW clone, but more like a superclone made to capitalize on everything popular at the moment including Minecraft.

    EDIT: And with a focus on active combat, there will be little time to chat. Voice chat will probably end up being mandatory. Can expect automated grouping mechanics as the game will most likely be built to be console friendly. Basically the known format of MMOs of today, "here is the world, now YOU go explore it." Little to no need to chat, and if you do, integrated voice chat so you can quickly respond to the attacks. Little to no downtime, so constant progression. Talk in groups will be like they usually are in PUGs today. Silence followed by precise statements on what to do. Getting to know other people, nonsense. There is a world to explore (by yourself).

    /hypetrain derail.

    EDIT 2: Also, they stated that while it may be hard to top SOE live 2013 with the EQN reveal, they hoped that at SOE live 2014 there would be more to talk about as they would have a more fleshed out game. Do not expect EQN until Winter 2014.
