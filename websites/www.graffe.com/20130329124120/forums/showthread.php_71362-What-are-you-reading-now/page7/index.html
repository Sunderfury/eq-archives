    Re: What are you reading now?

    Originally Posted by Ruldar
    Just started Alloy of Law, a Mistborn sequel novella by Sanderson. It's fairly short for him, only a few hundred pages or so.
    I just started this myself, and yes it is very short, I'm probably going to finish it today.

    And I love it. It's kind of hokey at parts, but that seems appropriate for the genre, so I'm not bothered by it. The characters are hilarious, and the way the magic system works within the western society is great fun too.

    Edit: Finished it. He alludes to some crazy stuff for future mistborn novels without going into too much details. Be sure to read the couple pages at the back about how the metal magic systems work. It isn't just an index for people that can't remember, it's written from the point of view of an unknown character. Combined with some of the not very subtle foreshadowing in the actual novel, it's going to get weird! I find myself wondering if he can pull it off without jumping several sharks and a whale.

    OK so let me go into more detail without spoilers on why Cold Commands is bad. Cold Commands has the three GREAT characters again. They really ARE amazingly cool, complex characters that Morgan has sort of reimagined from some of my favorite fantasy types.

    Ringol Eskiath in particular is AMAZINGLY cool. Gay blade indeed. He is one of the best fantasy creations of the past thirty years. You even get a smidge more backstory on him - though definitely not enough. Add to that the aging barbarian Edgar Dragonbane and Arceth the half Black Elf last of the Cirieth and you have the makings for a GREAT book.

    It even starts out great. You feel the coming of an epic plot including exploration and mysterious places but then...

    HOLY SHIT THE BOOK ENDS.

    Honest to god, 9/10 of the book is getting the main characters back together to go on a big quest and then BOOM the book is over. The ending is so contrived and so disappointing that you want to reach out and KILL MORGAN.

    Morgan's writing itself on a sentence by sentence scene by scene basis is just as good as it always is.
