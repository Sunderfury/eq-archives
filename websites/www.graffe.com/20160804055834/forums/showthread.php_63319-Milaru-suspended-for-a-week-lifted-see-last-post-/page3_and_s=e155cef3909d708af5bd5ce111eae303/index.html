    Re: Milaru - suspended for a week

    I would like to clarify a couple of things.

    1) The moderation policy calls for a 7-day suspension for posters who violate the civility rule twice in a 24 hour period. (I notice now that the numbers didn't get moved around correctly. 5 used to be the civility rule. I won't fix that yet in case it looks like I am trying to cover something up.) After the second report my view was that he had violated the civility rule 3 times in a 24 hour period: the first posting, the undoing of my original edit, and then the subsequent post here. I sent Milaru a pm asking him to cease and desist after the first report and after the re-edit.

    The rule I applied was not "certain words get an automatic suspension". Nor was it "multiple violations get an automatic suspension". It is "multiple violations within a short window of time get an automatic suspension". We also might describe this as the "don't be a jackass when the mods are trying to smooth things over without punishing anyone" rule. I cut Milaru some slack on the edit because it was borderline and because as he told me, he did it at 5:30 am and who hasn't done stupid stuff before coffee.

    2) I personally don't have an issue with the word. But the impression I have gotten both from the history of reports of the word going back well before my tenure and the discussions we have had is that those people who find it offensive find it very very offensive. The site and admin forum is the most heavily moderated forum on the site -- and many of you think it is not heavily moderated enough. I don't want any poster to feel alienated from the forum they are supposed to use to discuss the governance of Graffes. I try to balance that against the general freedom of expression encouraged here. I would have responded the same way to reports of the use of the n-word in the site and admin forums.

    3) It is inevitable that with a 'report to enforce' system that there will be cases where two posters do the exact same thing and one is cautioned and the other is not, simply because no report is made. It's an injustice and I am not sure anything can be done about it. The system is that way to prevent another evil, that of Moderator's imposing their personal standard of politeness on the board.

    4) Milaru has been arguing to me in email that the second post wasn't a violation because he mentioned not used the word. He says that if it was a violation then so is my first post above.

    5) Milaru also thinks that I am biased -- that is, he thinks in actively not trying to show favouritism to him I am treating him harsher than others. It would be fair to say that when I saw the second report I was at the end of my patience and that no doubt influenced my decision. In addition to the public actions he sent me some PMs in which he continued to express his personal dislike of Alikat in rather explicit terms. So it's quite possible he is right that I was not entirely objective about what was actually posted in public. I am certainly human and make mistakes.

    Now to Mdar's threads. First thank you Mdar for that information. It is helpful and salient. I had focused on Milaru's claim that the reporting was vindictive in my searches, and so had been searching the report forum and the reporters own posts.

    The first, as has been pointed out, was in rants where the rule doesn't apply.

    I honestly never noticed the second, which was in site and forum admin, which goes back to me not having a problem with the word myself. I skim past it without registering. Plus in all the election threads I tended to skim past any post that was hostile in favour of those with reasoned discussion. (Yes, the secret is out, if you can't argue like a grown up and keep your temper Voca ignores you.) If someone had reported it I would have edited it out, but of course I can't prove that now.

    I'm also faced with the issue of whether I should go edit it now, since (a) the thread is dead and (b) there is still no report. Milaru certainly would think it was inconsistent of me not to, but I am somewhat wedded to the no report/no action, which was put in as a safeguard against Moderator abuse of the civility rules.

    The third also was not reported, and checking I see that none of the people who reported Milaru this time posted in that thread -- of course they may have read it, no way to establish that -- but there is no strong evidence that they were favouring Grindel over Milaru.
