    Re: Avengers Trailer Released

    Does anyone really care if Thor learns how to understand humans more or if Hulk gets in touch with his sensitive side?
    No but navel-gazing is not the only way to develop a character. Loki is the villain. Obviously, he and Thor have unfinished business so there's plenty of room for development there without going off on tangents. It's hinted that Iron Man may not have been invited due to his personality. How does Stark find out about that and how does he handle it? Both those aspects could be good or disappointing, the trailers don't give me any hints either way.

    Also I should clarify that I only mean "character development" in a very general sense. Scenarios with conflicts that reveal something interesting or novel about a character are better than scenarios and conflicts where you could swap out any other random overpowered character in a colorful costume and get the same result. That's all I'm saying.

    Hemsworth and Downey Jr. are both quality actors and Hiddleston was good in Thor. Chris Evans is not bad and I don't know anything about Mark Ruffalo but the very brief clips in the trailer look promising. In other words they have talent it will be a shame if they're overshadowed by effects magic.

    The last ensemble superhero movie I enjoyed was X2, which had at least 9 heroes (Xavier, Wolverine, Storm, Jean Grey, Cyclops, Rogue, Nightcrawler, Pyro, Iceman, with cameos from others like Colossus). Yes, that one suffered from dilution, but it was still a good story and had effective character development. It didn't help that Halle Berry was miscast as Storm.
