The Truth about Archery

First off, let me quantify my qualifications: I am an archer and fletcher in RL. At this point in my life I would also consider myself a master archer, fletcher, and hunter. I have been involved in archery for over 15 years (we are talking practice everyday for at least 2 hours), have won many tournaments, slain many a mighty beast, and only use arrows that I build myself. I am also have a Masters of Science in mechanical engineering and have studied deeply the actual physics behind archery. Now you are all thinking, wow, he is tooting his horn pretty loud, but I am only trying to show you that I have alot of experience with how bows and arrows work.

Let's look at archery in the real world and in EQ. Many will say 'but EQ is not the real world!' But EQ should closely follow the physics of how archery works (ok, I can hear you thinking it, what about the physics of magic? I won't touch that one ;) Anyhow, here goes: Cheap arrow and a cheap bow--this combination as one would imagine, will produce poor results. A sub-par arrow shaft will not be true (perfectly straight) and thus when launched from the bow will spend much of its energy into flexing and wobbling instead of propelling itself forward at a high velocity, thus limiting its range and force upon impact. A bow made of inferior components will also suffer the same way, energy will not be effectively imparted to the arrow because the defects in the bow will cause energy to be lost. If the quality of the arrow and the bow are increased, the main result is that energy is more effectively moved from the archer's muscles, to the bow limbs, to the bow string, and into the arrow. This causes the arrow to exit the bow at a higher velocity. Now anyone who has had any kind of high school physics knows that more velocity equals more energy equals more damage when the arrow strikes the target. (if you want to understand exactly why, say so and I will explain how kinetic energy and arrow velocity and energy work) Cams: these little wonders were invented so that when an archer pulls the bow back, a peak weight is reached (maximum draw weight) and then is reduced at full draw. This is possible because of how a cam works; by creating a lever arm (kind of like using a fulcrum) to help the archer hold the full weight. Therefore the bow still has all the energy of the peak draw weight stored in the limbs, yet the archer can more easily hold the string back. This creates a couple of advantages: the archer can hold the bow back for a longer period and thus take more time to carefully aim before releasing. Generally speaking, and archer using a cammed bow can also pull a bow having a higher peak weight than if he/she were pulling a recurve bow; once again adding more energy and range to the bow. The drawback to cams? Well, a cammed bow is slower to shoot. Therefore we have the trade off of (in EQ terms) damage versus delay. But, the damage more than makes up for the delay and a more efficient arrow is shot.

Now let's look at range a little since this seems to be debated quite a bit. The overall range that an arrow will reach is determine by both the quality of the arrow and the quality of the bow. Better arrow plus better bow equals more range. Thus in my opinion, and if we are trying to reflect true archery, the ranges should be additive, i.e. range of the arrow plus range of the bow equals total range that the arrow will fly. With my real life high quality bow and arrows, it is not unrealistic for me to hit a target the size of pop can at 50 yards consistently. Remember back to the previous paragraph, a cammed, high quality bow will produce more energy and fling an arrow farther. Thus, the highest quality bow with cams will have the longest range and highest damages, BUT a longer delay.

I would also like to say that I would sure rather have a swordsman take a whack at me than to have a skilled archer put an arrow into my body. It is guaranteed that one well placed arrow will do more that enough damgage to end your life (of course if the swordsman swings across your neck and decapitates you, that will most likely end your life also). But overall, and I would bet good money on this, a single arrow is going to do a hell of a lot more damage than a single swing of a hand to hand weapon. Not to mention that armor is much more susceptible to penetration by a very small, pointy object going at a very high velocity.

Everything stated above is real world FACT. I have left out a grundle of details that could fill a whole book but will spare you all the pain of reading it. If you would however like more detail about a specific aspect, I will more than gladly do my best to answer any questions.

Now, MY OPINION regarding EQ: a single shot from a very high quality bow with a high quality arrow should do more damage per round than any other weapon in the game and have a better range. OUTRAGE!! many will call. But remember, you will most likely only be flinging one, two, maybe 3 (if you are very lucky) arrows at a target while you will be swinging your sword at the same target many times. Thus in the overall picture, the bow should do more damage per round, but more damage overall should be done hand to hand. There should be a good reason to spend a fortune to fletch up a steel shafted, vaned, bladed arrow and shoot it through a very expensive, cammed darkwood bow. This arrow should be doing damage on par with a decent mage spell (which remember, cost only mana). Now I don't think that arrow should be doing 200+ damage, but something on the scale of 50-70 is not unreasonable for an archer with 120+ skill. Not to mention that this would be a great way to get money out of players hands and back into the NPCs hands and take it out of circulation. If you want a fast bow at the sacrifice of damage, you should be using a recurve. If you want more damage and range at the expense of speed (delay), than you should use a compound. This is NOT how it works in EQ right now as cams produce less damage and more speed WHICH IS DEAD WRONG if you are to model a real bow. I am not advocating that archery become some God-like skill, but rather it be made more reasonable to when compared to actual archery. I also love the idea of questing or hunting for special components (both bow and arrow) and being able to have enchanted arrows to be able to hit magic-only creatures (thus creating the need for enchanters and rangers to work together).
