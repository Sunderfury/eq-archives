How to get there

Go to the Ferrott, stick to your left, swim past the first river, go past cazic thule, swim past the second river and before you reach the south/west corner of the zone there is a hidden entrance to your left. This is the spectra hidden cave, you will need invisibility to undead to go through safely. In the cave stick to your right more or less, it takes about 30 seconds to get to the exit of the cave. When you exit the cave you will see a giant portal which leads to the plane of fear, better go through quickly as there is an NPC called Cindreela near the portal and she's not friendly.

The zone

The zone itself is not very big, aside from the portal it has a castle which I have yet to explore, a few pillars, a couple of houses (with undead inside) and the rest of the zone is forest like. The castle has a fire wall around it which is like lava, you will take about 100 damage swimming across it. The zone is pretty crowded in terms of the number of monsters compared to the size of the zone. And all the mobs in the zone are wanderers, including cazic thule himself. All the mobs in the zone are over level 40, most are over 45.

Bestiary

Cazic thule - He is a shadowknight, hits for 185, casts some nasty DOT spells. Like all shadowknights he can harm touch, and it hurts. His pet is very weak (since he is a shadow knight probably). When you attack him, all the mobs in the area will go aggro on you. He wanders around the zone usually the other side of the castle (if you're coming from the portal).

Dread - a unique mob. You will find him at his small temple at -550, -1230. He is guarded by two Amygdalan knights. I have no idea what are his offensive abilities as the Amygdalan knights always killed me before I got a chance to find out (they seem to be more aggressive than him.

Fright - Same as Dread. Only this one looks like a clay golem instead of a stone.

Scareling (they are the little red flying thing you see in the screenshot) - One of the nastiest mobs in the plane of fear. They hit for 117 lightning fast and backstab for 369. They also teleport you to different points of the zone upon hitting you sometimes. They are very fast and also have a huge range of sight, they can and will (they are very agressive) go aggro on you from very far away. And if that's not enough they are so small and they move so fast it's really hard to detect them before they attack you.

Nightmare - They look like black horses, hit for 139+  and cast a fear spell. They are not very aggressive and are not likely to attack you if you run by em.

Shiverback - Guerilla like mobs. They hit for 117 and kick for 37. They also have some sort of a cold attack which is triggered when they hit you but I'm not sure what it does. They drop Shiverback-hide armor which is Monk only.

Amygdalan - I've seen two types of Amygdalans, warriors and knights. They are shadowknights and They usually don't wander around and only hang around near the castle and dread/fright.

Glare lord - They look like Evil Eyes, only they are magicians. They hit for 139 and cast very nasty spells including a mana draining spell.

Phoboplasm - Weird looking mobs, they don't seem to cast spells but they can sure pack a punch. They hit for 139 and kick for 47. Even though they scowl at me, they never attacked me before I attacked them.

Phantasm - Spectra looking mob. They slice for 139 and cast very nasty spells including a stun spell that lasts a very long time (and they cast it instantly while moving towards you ofcourse).

Samhain - Another wierd looking mob, you will find them mostly near dread and fright. They hit for 139+ and don't seem to cast any spells.

Boogeyman - They look like a combination of a goblin and a froglok. Very agressive and hit for over 139.

Gorgon - They hit for 139 and don't seem to cast any spells. They are also not too agressive and you would probably be able to walk behind one without him attacking you.

Dracholiche - They hit for 170 and are pretty agressive and rare. They drop necromancer armor.

Spinechiller spider - They look like white spiders. They hit pretty hard and poison as well, they drop caster armor.

Turmoil Toad - They are pretty rare but incredibly nasty. They look like small frogloks and are one of the fastest mobs I've seen yet. They hit very hard and I believe they can charm and fear. Both times I encountered one I was charmed and killed before I could get a screenshot.

Worry wairth - They look like willowisps, but they are enchanters. They hit for 139 and cast all those nasty enchanter spells.

Fighting tactics

The biggest problem in the plane of fear is getting mobbed. The first problem you encounter is zoning in, you need everyone to zone in at the same time because you don't want to be alone there for more than a few seconds as a few mobs can jump you as soon as you zone. Walking around there and fighting the wandering mobs in the middle of the forest is suicide, sooner or later you will be jumped by more mobs than you can handle, not to mention your tanks will get teleported away by scarelings and bring trains or die. What I suggest is to follow the left wall as you zone in and head to 1350, -1400. This is a high place which is more or less a safe spot, very few mobs will wander to that area. When you get there keep all your party there and have one person pull to the party. That is a pretty big and comfortable area, you can spend alot of time there and be relatively safe and get great loot and exp. Scarelings are less of a problem there because first of all they don't seem to wander to that area much and secondly if you are near a zone line you're usually not teleported far. Just make sure you root everything and don't pull cazic thule by accident!

Killing Cazic Thule

Cazic Thule himself is not very mighty, Lord Nagfen for example is much tougher. However, in order to have a chance of killing him you must clear out the zone first. As things are now, nothing respawns in the zone unless it crashes. It seems, fighting boogeyman makes the zone crash. So if you leave the boogeyman alone and kill everything else, you should find it easy to pull cazic alone. Unless you have alot of necromancers, killing cazic will take quite a few people and quite a bit of time. You will need alot of tanks and alot of clerics. Wizards and the like are very ineffective against cazic thule as he is a flat resist. However if you have alot of necromancers (say 10 level 50 necromancers), killing cazic is a piece of cake (assuming you cleared out the zone first). Send out a few tanks and the necromancer pets and have the necromancers start life tapping (level 49 life tap does 350 damage and is unresistable). Cazic Thule will most likely die before the necromancers are oom and without many casualties.
