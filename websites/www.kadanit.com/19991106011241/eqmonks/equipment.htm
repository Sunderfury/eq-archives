General

As a Monk, no matter what your Strength is, if you carry 15 pounds or more -  you will start losing AC. Not only do you lose AC, you also hit, dodge, block, riposte, duel wield and double attack less often. At low levels, the AC loss isn't as significant as it is in higher levels. But either way, it's not good to weigh more then 14 pounds.

So if you are out hunting for EXP, you would want to leave town weighing 14 with only armor, food, water and maybe a lightsource. And not loot anything that will make you weigh 15 pounds. Basically that means you will loot only coins, and not alot of them. In case you didn't know, coins weigh too. 40 coins or so weigh 1 pound. So lose that copper! At my level I don't even loot gold usually, just plat. When you get to town, visit the bank and convert all your coins, so you will have as little as possible. Depositing some is also not a bad idea.

Some people don't know this, but When you stack items, such as food, water or bandages, they weigh the same as a single item would. So 20 food, 20 water and 20 bandages weigh the same as 1 food, 1 water and 1 bandage.

Armor

Monks can wear up to leather, including some magical armor. A full suit of leather weighs way too much for a Monk. Although it will give you more AC then a full suit of cloth, it's not worth it. Let's see what type of Armor is good for Monks.

Cloth - A set of cloth weighs maybe 5 pounds and give like 40AC, I recommend getting a full set of cloth as soon as you can afford it.

Leather - At low levels, you may want to mix some leather with your cloth for maximum effectiveness. Just don't weigh more then 15 pounds.

Netted - This Magical armor can be found on Low level (1-5) Froglocks in the Innothule Swamp. It is also found on Frogloks in the Qeynos swears. It gives the same AC as cloth, but weighs a bit less. With netted gloves you can hit monsters that can be hit by Magical attacks only.

Woven - This Magical armor is Found in the Innothule Swamp and Guk on mobs ranging from level 6 to 12. This armor weighs as little as netted and has the same AC as patchwork. (the tunic gives 6 AC compared to the 4 AC that cloth gives). With Woven gloves you can hit monsters that can be hit by Magical attacks only.

Mesh - This Magical armor is found in Guk on 20+ level mobs starting with Froglok Gaz Warriors (actually they are level 17-19). However Mesh is rarely found on Gaz Warriors, Its most commonly found on the Ton type Frogloks, Vis, Shin, Tzu etc. Mesh armor is as light as Netted and has the same AC as Leather. With Mesh gloves you can hit monsters that can be hit by Magical attacks only. Mesh armor is not only better then leather because it weighs less, but it also absorbs damage like chain!

Azure  - Found in lower Guk on Minotaur Patricarchs.  This armor is stronger then platemail and very light. I'm not sure how many pieces of this armor are available, I only know of Azure sleeves which are  AC  12 and weight only .4

SnakeSkin - This armor is found in the dungeon Paw on Nobles (Level 14). It is a bit stronger then leather and weighs the same as leather. It also gives magical bonuses. This armor is not magical in nature, you wont be able to hit magic only mobs with it. This armor is ok to mix with Mesh or Woven for maximum effectiveness. The Gloves give a Dex bonus (but you wont want to use them because they don't let you hit magical monsters, but when you reach level 30 your punches are magical naturally and you can use snakeskin gloves and hit magical monsters), the mask gives resist magic and the Jerkin resist poison.

Rubicite platemail - Monks used to be able to wear this armor, but they can't anymore.

Lupine Armor - This armor was actually too heavy for monks, but it's weight has been reduced. The gauntlets weigh 1. It can be found on lycanthropes, and can also be made by Karg Icebear, a rare spawn in EverFrost. Karg Icebear will make you lupine armor if you bring him the right components (werewolf pelts or claws) and some money. Lupine armor has the same AC as leather and gives magical bonuses.

Green Platemail - The strongest armor in the game. Most people thought it is a GM only item, but Brad McQuiad said, after helping those guys kill Lady Vox that all the stuff he had on were attainable by regular people and he was wearing Green Splinted platemail. It is wearable by Monks, however, I'm not sure how much it weighs (EQlizer says the gloves weigh 5, but I'm told from a more reliable source that they weigh only .5). If you have any information on this Armor please E-mail me.

Weapons

Do not use them. Why? because first of all, a Monk's fists are the best weapon in the game. (well maybe a fiery avenger is). But your fists are certainly better then any Blunt weapon you may find. But even if you do have an awesome blunt weapon that is actually better then your fists, there is another - more important reason not to use it. Blunt weapons weigh a TON! Blunt weapons weigh on AVG like 8 and if you're going to duel wield them, that's 16 pounds, more then you should carry with your armor, food etc...

There are, however, rumeors of Monk special weapons. I have no information on any of them. It was thought that Demon Claws are a Monk only weapon, when it was seen on a GM. However it turns out it's just a 1H   slashing weapon. If you have any information on any Monk special weapons, please E-mail me.
