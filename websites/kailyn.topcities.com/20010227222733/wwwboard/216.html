Anyway, it was that great research (which was, I think started by a very nice post on the stratics boards) which enabled me to say (with a little justification) that we (the EQ community in general and the dude who ran the experiments in particular)had 'solved' that portion of the melee equation.

If we consider that solved, then we currently have:

1. Average damage as a f(level, delay, damage), basically.
2. Dual weild percentage as f(skill, level) which really could be expressed as f(level).
3. Double attack percentage as f(skill, level) again, f(level).

I mean, with this, we ALMOST have the full combat system. Of course this last part, the 'to-hit' number is BY FAR the most difficult to determine. As you pointed out, it is really tough.

However, if I had a melee character I would test it out with NPCs which have constant levels and fast spawn rates (the hermit and the bard come to mind). I would also go to see them as I leveled.

I would bet (or perhaps I hope that the following is true, as it would make determing the function consideriable easier) that the 'to hit' function is

f(level difference)

instead of

f(your level, mobs level).

if it is the 2nd, it will be REALLY tough to determine the function. If it is the 1st (or is close enough for an aproximation) then we may be able to get somewhere.

Heck, I guess I could do some of the experiments. When I get my DSL reconnected (moved) and I get my two computer setup back up I'll start powerleveling my monk (damn going to have to get the skills up) so I can beat on the hermit about 500 times (as I go from level 18 to 24 or so).

I can not stress enough how important this is! I mean this is IT! If we get this formula then we know where it is at!

I currently have the mana regen formula, and it is ~ 0.07 (maybe 0.08, the data points I got from other people on message boards were really off by quite a bit) points of mana per second per level. So a level 31 charatcer gets ...


2.1 to 2.4 mana per second mediating (2.4 is the number that I got).

It seems that it is caped at 3.3 for 50+ characters.

Further clairty gives ~ 1.2 mana per second.

Anyway. All of this data to say that with the to-hit information, we will be pretty close to expressing a LARGE portion of this game with some very nice and easy formula! WOOT! If that does not turn your crank, well, uhm... well I don't know what to say, but it does turn mine! WOOT!
