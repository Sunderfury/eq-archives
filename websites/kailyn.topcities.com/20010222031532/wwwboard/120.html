60 STR = 2 levels?
[ Follow Ups ] [ Post Followup ] [ EQ Melee Board ]

Posted by Kaledan on April 25, 202000 at 16:48:37:

One thing that seems to be a bit unclear at the moement is the affect of STR on the distribution of damage.

Now Brad's statement on the EQ board said that STR affected 'maximum and average' damage.

Now obviously, raising the maximum will affect the average, but that is a bit of a strange way to put it, if there is only that one effect.

Now, presumably everyone agrees that there is an effect of level (in some way) on average damage, in that a level 50 is more likely to hit for average or better damage when fighting a decaying skeleton than than they are when fighting Vox.

This is different from the chance to hit, which from both logging and Brad's statements seems to be strictly level/skill based.

Now, in theory, if you give any credence to the ATK numbers, each level gets you about 30 ATK points from skills.
So, again, purely from theory, you would expect 60 STR to be the equivalent of 2 levels.

So if you take this theory seriously, you can predict several things:

- a level one troll warrior will have the same
damage distribution as a level 3 gnome warrior.

- a level 11 barbarian rogue will have a
higher average damage, when they hit, than a
level 12 dark elf rogue.

- a level 17 barbarian rogue will do about the
same average damage as a level 19 gnome rogue.

- a level 34 paladin with a +60 STR buff will have
the same damage distribution (ignoring the
fixed level bonus) as a level 36 paladin.

Now, the first three cases seem to match up, but I don't have a paladin high enough to test the last.
Those who have run such tests say +60 STR makes a barely detectable difference.

You might think this would blow my theory out of the water. But there is a way to save it.

Does 2 levels make a detectable difference in the 30's or higher?

In other words, if you look at the ratios of min/magic/max hits for the same character 2 levels apart, can you detect a statistically significant difference?
Obviously, you have to compensate for any changes in the fixed (level -25)/3 bonus.

If this is true, it would reverse the current recieved wisdom that STR is irrelevant until level 20, when the max damage bonus kicks in.
Instead, STR would be king up to about level 20, gradually becoming insignificant afterwards, for the same reasons that the grouping and con ranges expand to cover more levels.

Kaledan
