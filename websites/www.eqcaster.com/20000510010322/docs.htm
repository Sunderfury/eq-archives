Documentation

Spell Name - The official name of the spell

Description - Every spell can have up to 4 attribute changes. The description lists all four effects together.

Level - The level that the current spell caster receives this spell. A level of 61 means that it is not an obtainable spell

Mana - The amount of mana consumed when casting the spell.

Cast Time - Time it takes before the spell is cast

Recovery Time - Amount of time it takes to recover from this spell and get ready to cast a different one.

Re-Casting Time - Amount of time required for this particular spell to become re-castable. Some spells like Spirit of Cheetah can take up to a full game day to recover.

Indoor - Two types of spells are indicated here.

  • Indoor - Spells like 'Shrink' require that the user cast it in an indoor dungeon
  • Outdoor - Spells like 'Spirit of the Wolf' require the caster to be in an outside zone.

Change - Does your current location

  • Location - Changes your current x,y position. Spells like 'Shadow Step'
  • Floating - Changes your current z position.. Spells like 'Levitate'

Casting - Type of spell/song

  • Divination, Evocation, etc. - Arcane Spells
  • Wind, Bass, etc. - Bard Songs

Duration - Number of ticks the spell is in effect. One ticks is approximately 3 seconds.

Pos/Neg? - Very interesting field. Unknown effect

  • Positive - Buffing & Healing style spells
  • Neutral - Non-player effecting spell
  • Negative - Damage style spells
  • Bard A - Don't know.
  • Bard B - Don't know

Category? - Some style of category field. Unknown reason

Type? - Unknown field ranging from 0 to 11 with a 50 thrown in for confusement.

Radius? - Maybe some sort of area effect spell radius?

Attrib - What attribute is being adjusted?

Base - Base attribute setting

  • What is the lowest level the attribute can be adjusted is the most common use.

Max - Maximum attribute setting

  • What is the highest level the attribute can be adjusted is the most common use.

Calc - How does level effect the attribute adjustment. Still some formulas to understand.

Reageant - What reageants are required to cast this spell.

Qty - What quanties of each reageant are required for the spell.

  • Magicians require 2 to 3 Cats Eye Agate for their 'Phantom Chain' and 'Phantom Plate' spells

Creates - What is created when some summon spells are cast. Mainly for 'Summon Pet' to determine the pet type.

Resistance - What resistance check is tested when the spell is cast. 'None' means unresistable.

Res Adj - How much additional resistance is given the receiver of the spell.

  • A resistance adjustment of 25 would mean that the mob would receive 25 extra points of resistance for a spell. Use this field to see if the spell that you are casting will be resisted often, or rarely. Some Enchanter spells are unresistable.

Spell Gem Icon- The actual color and design of the small spell 'gems'. I should have this complete by version 1.2

Spell Range - How far away can the spell be cast upon a target.

AoE Range - The radius of the Area Effect spell.

Push Back Range - How many feet is the target pushed back. Most 'force' style spells have a push back range.

Push Up Range - Several druid spells actually 'throw' the target into the air. I'm assuming that the larger throw spells can cause damage if the target doesn't have a Safe Fall ability.

Q1? - Values 0 thru 8. Some people have e-mailed me saying that this field describes how much light the surrounding area received when the spell is cast. Still investigating.

Q2? - Unknown

Q3? - Unknown

Q4? - Unknown

Q5? - Unknown. Not used.

Q6? - Unknown. Leaning toward some sort of restriction indicator when the spells are cast.

  • 16 - Feign Death
