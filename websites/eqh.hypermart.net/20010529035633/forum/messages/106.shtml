Erudites vs. Gnomes as Magicians

Posted by Cattaputta (207.54.181.89) on January 17, 1999 at 14:39:27

I posted an analysis of playing a Gnome vs. an Erudite on our forum page, but it occurred to me that perhaps we could use a different perspective. After all, we may be a *bit* biased towards Gnomes at Gnomes of Norrath. Anyone here want to add to the discussion?

Let them know I invited some Erudites to balance the discussion.

We are a flame free board.

(PS - we have listed you as our ally now on our page. Next time you update your page, don't forget us!)

*** *** ***

Gnomes vs. Erudites as Magicians.

I thought this may be an interesting topic for discussion, even if our audience here may be (slightly) biased. I plan to play a Magician so I will focus on that, but perhaps other magic users can discuss their class in this context also. For now, let's not consider that Gnomes certainly would be more FUN to play, and just look at the mechanics. I'm not in Phase 3, so am basing my logic on what I have read.

For someone who wanted to be a Magician, two excellent race choices would be Erudite and Gnome. Both are highly intelligent, which is the main stat for Magicians. Let's assume the player puts 25 points into Intelligence, which I assume is the max allowed for a single attribute. The remaining 5 I would put into stamina (or perhaps agility) for either race.

Now, here are the stats that result:

RACE STR STA AGI DEX WIS INT CHA
Erudite 60 85 70 70 83 140 70
Gnome 60 85 85 85 67 133 60

Wow! Those Erudites sure are smart! They will have lots-o-mana and learn skill fast! But a Gnome is only 5% lower! We will have 95% of that great amount of mana. So we will certainly hold our own against our tall brothers there.

The next most important stats are agility and stamina. We tie on stamina, but look at Agility! Wow! We have 21.4% more agility. Also, from guide posts on EQVAULT, I know we get extra Armor Class (AC) in avoiding hits because we are small. That means we are very hard to hit, whereas our tall brother will be hit more often. If you figure that you have to restart your spell if you are interupted, that means we could actually have a casting ADVANTAGE despite our slightly lower intelligence.

Now an Erudite will get slightly better prices in the shops (CHA), and may resist magic better (WIS), but when we do hit something, we'll hit it more (does DEX help at all in casting magic?) But these are all less important stats.

Now, that analysis would apply to Wizards and Magicians. But Magicians have one addition factor -- Pets! Now, a pet can be summoned when first entering a zone, and the Magician can "recharge" his mana before going further. This effectively allows us to use past mana for future battles, reducing the effect of our reduced mana.

Finally, we have a few other non-tangible benefits such as Infravision and Tinkering skills that should add value to our characters.

So, while we must certainly be impressed by the Erudite's great intelligence, we know that they have several significant disadvantages when compared to a Gnome. Our tall brother's at Order of Erudin will certainly make worthy allies, but for me, I'll play a Gnome!
