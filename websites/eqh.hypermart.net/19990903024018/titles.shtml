The Order of Erudin's Individual Award/Title System

What happens if a person is so great, so magnificient, that there is no 'rank' for him? Or what happens when someone is more than that level 50 'Lord' title, or more than that 'Lord of the Arcane' title? They have to be recognized. And here is how we do it.

How It Works
You might consider this an award system, but without any physical awards. Sometimes a member who is already high in rank or somesuch does something that's great, they will be given a title. Or if they continue doing something, they will be given a title relevant to that. Or maybe a member who isn't high in rank repeatedly performs a certain action, we may promote him and give him a title, or just give him a title. This title represents that action always. It is to be respected, and titles are unique. No member is restricted from earning any title, no member can't earn a title.
   The title will be listed on the roster.

How To Earn a Title
As mentioned above, you may earn a title by performing one feat or several repeatedly. Take a look at some of these examples:

  • If Member A is always hunting alone in dangerous places and always returns with incredible treasure, rarely leaving the physical world, then he may earn a title meaning something to do with brave.
  • If Member B somehow becomes rich, or already is rich, but spends his entire fortune on something for the guild. Perhaps magical items or perhaps a house (if he is very rich) then he may earn a title meaning something to do with generous.
  • If Member C shows to fight extremely well, incredibly well, in battles of most sorts, then he may earn a title meaning something like seasoned.
    A member does not retrieve a title by asking for one. An individual title is for personal feats. It gives you no special advantages, no special disadvantages. They are for roleplaying purposes, and are there to show recognition for certain people who are exceptional players in some way. While no one will hand you 2,000 platinum by receiving a title, all titles are genuine... and members holding such a title are definately to be respected.
