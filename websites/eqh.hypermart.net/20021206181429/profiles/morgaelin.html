Morgaelin's History

Past:
I do not know much about my parents or their practices. My father’s name was Torvox Orcrist. His past is a mystery to me since no one will speak of him. Certain rumor says he studied the forbidden arts of necromancy.

   He disappeared when I was five years of age leaving my mother, Jaana Orcrist and I alone. My mother was an Enchantress (which led to my interest in the art of Enchanting). Her power amazed me... baffled me. It was my birthright to continue my mother's studies in the arcane art. She researched binding magical energies into substances, weapons, armor, and even places. I was very young, an only child, when we were chased from the city. I don’t know why and my mother wouldn't tell me. I suspect it had something to do with my accursed father. We fled to the city of Paineel, where it was rumored my father ran to, and made new home there. Many years past and my mother passed away from old age, leaving me alone in the evil city at the age of but fifteen. I knew what I had to do. Return to Erudin. My real name is Morien Orcrist. The very name banishes me from the great city of the Erudites... my heritage. I changed my name, in secrecy, to Morgaelin. This document may never be discovered, but my family past must not be lost!

Present:
I am now twenty-five years of age. Ten years have passed. I have lived in practical harmony amongst my heritage, using the identity of Morgaelin, Erudite Enchanter, who I have now become. I have just begun my studies in the art of Enchanting. I keep my mother’s research books hidden from society. My dearest fellow scholar, Seginus, and I are establishing an Order... the Order of Erudin. He only knows of my true identity and it shall stay that way. The order shall follow our goals of knowledge and research the arcane arts. We share our hate for necromancy, as do our Erud brothers. We have gathered the locals to found our new order. Under Quellious, the order shall achieve power, knowledge, and the Erud supremacy we deserve at birthright. I shall continue this journal of our history and research.

May Quellious protect you.

Morien Orcrist, better known as Morgaelin.
December 24th, 1998 (ooc)
