Character Description?!

This is one my other half just told me about. While I personally dislike all the folks who peek at my equipment by right clicking on me (I feel like there's a bunch of peeping toms ogling me), there is some way to add to the eyefull folks get when they do that to you. Take a good long look at yourself from an external view (i.e., press the f9 key a time or two, so you can see yourself on your screen). Right click on yourself, and it should bring up a picture of you, with a blank area below it. Click on that area and it will let you type in a character history (or whatever you prefer) that will show when anyone looks at your equipment.

Fun With Verbs I

Actions: (that make your character move)

Most folks know /cheer and /wave, but have you tried ...

/cry {no target} - you cry

/cry <target> - you cry over <target> -- try this on a corpse!

Emotes:

Again, most folks know /hug and /poke, but have you tried ...

/panic {no target} - you panic

/panic <target> - you panic at the sight of <target> - just the thing when you see a sand giant coming over the crest of the hill

Hunting Tips

"Hail a sand giant" - By now, if you've done much group hunting, you've probably hear this one, however, here's some RP justification/explanation for it.  Your group just got done beating on something nasty, and most of the spellcasters are busy meditating. Something nasty comes over the hill, and you need to get out a timely warning before it stomps on your party.  You can try, "Guys, look out, there's a ..." followed by the sounds of one or more party members getting blindsided. There's a faster way to warn, them, however, to make up for the time that it's slower to type a warning than it is to physically say it. Target the critter and hit the "h" key to hail it.  Think of this as the RP equivalent of pointing at it and blurting a warning. Once you've hailed it, then you can give more accurate information about where it is and who is in danger ... as you yourself either backpedal out of it's path, or move to defend your group's spellcasters. For those of you busy meditating with your noses in your spellbooks, if you hear someone hail a nasty critter, get your nose out of that book and be ready to move or fight. It might just save your life and the lives of those with you.

Corpse fetching - You died somewhere you should not have been ... again. And, well, you really would die if you tried to loot your body in that particular spot.  Get yourself, and a couple bodyguards into the general area where you died. Then carefully edge your way closer to your body, trying a /corpse every step or two. The moment the command works, BACK up many steps, and /corpse again. Repeat this process until you've got your body somewhere that is a little safer. Think of this as the RP equivalent of grabbing your corpse and dragging it to somewhere you can loot it in safety. You have to get close to it to "grab" it, and can only drag in tiny spurts because, well, it has all your gear on it, so is heavier than the reborn you.

A very good guide to group hunting can be found on the eq.stratics site. I highly recommend it to all who want to make the most of their group hunting experience, and the various skills of their party members.

Language lessons?

All the races, other than human, know at least one language other than Common Tongue when they start out.  You can learn other languages as you go along, both for the pure fun of it, and for things like spell research later on.

There are two main ways to learn other languages, and you can intermix them.

  1. As you get older, your trainer can teach you various languages, should you wish to spend the training points.

  2. You can find a native speaker, listen to them talk the language a while, and then practice the language yourself.

This second method is a great way for a group to pass 10 or 15 minutes while they are waiting for someone or something to happen. Make sure you are grouped, and in close physical proximity to each other. Each member of the party should go to their speech selection screen and change their language to something that at least 1 other in the group does not know, or knows to a much lesser degree than you. Then go to your socials menu and reprogram one of your buttons to /gsay two or three sentences. Put that socials button onto one of your hotkeys that is currently unused. Take a deep breath, and then everyone begin babbling at once, via the hotkeys, in the various languages. (This is called "spamming")  Have one person ready to switch back to Common at the drop of a hat and spam "HOLD!" until everyone stops if there is danger approaching. Since the spamming can be pretty intense, I recommend only sending across 2 or 3 sentences at a time, with at least a 1-second delay between your spams to the others, more if you want to see what is going on. You will see a lot of gibberish go across your screen from the languages you don't know, and as your knowledge of the language increases, you will recognize more of what is being said. At random times, you will see a message flash across your screen that says "Your language skills have improved."  However, you won't see those improvements on your skill list until you zone or log off and back on. It's amazing how quickly you can become very multilingual, with a few good friends from other races to assist you.

Once you have learned that language, you can also try to increase your skill by speaking it to another, but it's much slower learning to laboriously try to pronounce all those foreign words than it is to learn by listening.
