Basic Bowyering
 


There are a few parts to Bow crafting, not fully explained in the manual. I will attempt to clarify this wonderful craft to they Laymen.

Rough Wood Staffs
First you must decide on the strength of the bow you wish. Damage done buy the bow is dependant on the strength of wood and the string used. The more costly the wood the stronger it is, and subsequently the harder to craft. Starting from weakest the selections are: Hickory, Elm, Ash, Oak, Darkwood. You may also fashion a bow from Steel and Ceramic, these being stronger still than wood.

String
The strength of string also determines the damage done by the bow. The better quality of string the better the damage. There are 3 types of string, they are: Hemp, Linen, and Silk. Only the best bows are strung with silk.

Tools
Next we come to increasing the quality of the bow, wich usualy means decreasing the dealy or rate at wich the bow is fired. Unfortunately damage is also decreased by this process. There are 2 tools to date: Whittling Knife, and the Plane. Once again each is an increase in quality and price. Each again is more difficult to use.

Pulleys
To pack a whallop, adding pulleys will cause damage to increase dramaticaly. There is only one class of pulley but you may add one or two, they are unfortnately verry expensive!

There is only one kit to buy for crafting both Bows and Arrows. To use it for bows, it is fairly simple. At beginning levels you need only buy the staff and string, place both in the kit and press combine. As with any craft you will wind up making a bow, or not. Using the tools is both expensive and risky as they are expended when added to the kit. A tool can be used with Elm or better wood, it is simply placed in the kit with the staff and string. Pulleys, I have not used as of yet but I will have to assume you place them, either 1 or 2 into the kit with the staff and string. I will update as soon as I can aford to find out.
