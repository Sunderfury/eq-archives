Freeport Arena

The Freeport Arena is one of those places that remains unknown to most players even though the fact that there is no where, I cant stress NO WHERE enough, in Norrath where you would level up from 39 to 45 as fast as you will here. Remember how quickly you hit lvl 29 in Akanon and how amazingly uncamped the mechanic spawn was? Well, this is pretty much the same.

Ok, in the Freeport Arena you will be hunting warriors from the warriors guild, which is placed in the building next to the Arena (see map). Also, there are some warriors underneath guarding some prisioners. These warriors are lvl 34 to 36 ( I happen to recall) and therefore they shoudnt resist your spells oftenly. Actually, you can start camping them before lvl 39, but you would burn your whole mana bar on every fight due to the warriors s  high Magic Resistance.

There are four ( and only four ) warriors you can pull away : Monia and Trencil ( placed below the arena, by the jail), Vun ( who spawns in the building right next to the Arena) and Jak (who spawns actually inside the Arena coliseum). Each of them have been marked down on the map with their iniatials.

The easiest one to pull is Jak, since he is already inside the amphitheatre. Monia and Trencil will come along together, so either you have got a mate who can take one while you deal with the other, or better leave them till you are acustomed with this area so you can take two fellows at once. When I was hunting there I used to take 3 at once, so dont panic, it is not that difficult. To pull these 2 just circle around the outter perimeter of the Arena and you will see a back door leading to the jail (see map). And Vun himself spawns in another building. He will come to the doorway walking from any of the 2 side corridors, but theres a catch here. You can only pull him (either cast on him or letting him see you when finally become KoS) exactly where the blue cross lies down on the map. Why? Because if you pull him from anywhere else but there, a lvl 60 female warrior leader called Brenda will come along and she punches for double 300s, and man....she is a bitch ! So stick to the blue cross while pulling Vun.

Ok now, lets say you got your prey into the amphitheatre already. Well, just follow the yellow arrows (see map) till you get to the very end of the upper circle. Then, when he is getting close to you, lets say about 30 feets away, just jump off to the lower circle. The warrior will have to run all the way back in order to scale down to the lower level using the stairs. This is probably because there is a path bug. DONT let him catch up with you or he will jump off with you to the lower level. Now that you have got about 10 seconds untill the guy does all the walk around, you open up the offence : Root - Scourge - Boild Blood - Venom of the Snake (see spells list section if needed). Reroot and recast Venon of the Snake as needed. Root is the base of all this technique because :

             1.- Dots max their damage when the mob is feared or stopped still

             2.- You can sit down and med some mana up with Call of bones spell

             3.- Gives you some time to run across to the oposite side of the Arena pit.

Everytime you stick Root on the warrior, run to the oposite side of the Arena so you will have few secs when he brakes free of the root effect to recast it quickly. Believe me, this Root strategy is, in this very certain hunting area, better than the Darkness - Fear - Pet combo, since they will tend to run outside the Arena building while feared and thats not what we want. Actually I didnt use my pet at all when I was hunting there, because when you root the warrior, he will melee your pet and eventually will destroy it.

Tip 1 : Whenever root spell wouldnt stick on the warrior and he reaches to you, just climb up the stairs leading to the upper circle and again jump off at the ending. This will give you the chance to start all over again.

Tip 2 : You see the greenish cross and arrows on the map ? There is another path finding bug. If you stand in the cross and move forth and back as shown, the warrior for some only-known-by-God reason will in stead of walking straight towards you, will hug the wall all around like the blue arrows state (see map). This can give you time to cast the quick 2 seconds long casting spell Root.

Tip 3 : Whenever you think that the situation is getting too freaky, or you running out of mana, or Jak spawns up and still you havent taken down your current prey, or the microwave timer rings up letting you know the pizza is ready, just hoop into the Arena pit itself (where the battles between PCs are fought) and let them to kill you without fighting em back. Why? Because the game will record your death as a PvP battle and hence no exp loss will be dealed !!  I swear on God when I first realize about this little trick I wanted to pray to Innothule. This is the ever wish of any Everquest player : a hunting zone where, with a quick jump into the Arena, you can die peacefully knowing of the fact that it is exp loss free !!!  Maaan, too great to bare with !!

Tip 4 : Id keep donating few pp for SOW (speeding up druid, shamman and ranger buff). With SOW on you, it will be far safer cause you will outrun the warrior while circling around the Arena pit in case he reisits couple of Roots in a row.

What about the loot ? Well, they mostly drop few plats. And ocassionally some steel armour pieces worth up to 4 pp at vendors. Not to bad, but dont expect to became Norrath s Donald Trump after having hunted for few levels in the Freeport Arena : )

And do not worry about faction. You dont get faction hits against Freeport militia or citizens, just against the warriors guild and against Qeynos militia, but who cares about that? You have always been KoS in Qeynos havent you ?  : )  Oh yeah, I almost forgot. Since North Freeport guards are not members of the militia but of the warriors guild, you will not be welcome in North Freeport either. Only concern about this is that the bank is within that zone, so you probably wont be able to bank in Freeport  : (

Important : You might have problems with any GM if by chance he sees you hunting here. Why? Because he might think you are exploiting the several path finding bugs reported in this area. Thats why I do not recomend you to abuse of either the under-balcony-bug (the one shown with the green cross on the map) or the jumping-down-at-the-end-of-the-upper-circle bug (shown with yellow arrows on the map). Exploitation is highly punishable, and you could get banned out of the game and your account ended up being cancelled off if they catch you few times being a naughty kid. But hey, calm down. Im here to assist you remember? I once talked to a GM and he stated that as long as you stick to the Root-Dots-circling around the lower stage, you should be fine because :   

1.- The warrior has got the chance to catch up with you ( if you wouldnt root ) and hence to defend himself.

2.- You do not take any shortcuts, meaning that you follow the very same path than the warrior does. 

If a GM ever gives you a hard time because of this, expound these two arguments and he might leave you alone. But if he doesnt, do not risk it : apologize and walk away, and dont you ever hunt here again because he might have written a brief note on your account and if few days later another GM walks by and sees you hunting there and decides to check out your records you would be in deep shit.
