Old Sebilis

Hail and well met, brave adventurer, for you have entered the king of the dungeons : Sebilis, lair of the mighty Trakanon, destroyer and curse of the fallen Iksar civilization. 

Oh boy, here we are. Sebilis is the place where most of us have made our way thru to 60. You can say that Sebilis is to Kunark what Sol B was to the Old World (first EQ, with no expansions) : the toughest zone, with a wide lvl range and with uber bosses waiting for you at the very bottom of the zone.

Sebilis is just too large and too complicate to describe in this necro guide. I would probably need a whole new guide apart to fully describe Sebilis's areas, camps, mobs and loot. So basically what I will do is to name the most common camps, loot and a spot where you can partner up and duo in case you don't find group.

First of all, I need to say that Sebilis is kind of exquisite place (well, considering this is just a game, don't get me wrong) in the way that you will only find here very high lvl players (post 55 most of em) in full plannar and dragon armour. Besides, high end level comunity is not as large as pre 50 is, so everybody knows each other, either from guild events or from having grouped in the past. With this, I want to point out that you should try to work on your reputation with your fellow high level players of your server. Otherwise you will have a hard time to get invited in groups or raids. Be friendly, corteous, and most important of all, learn how to play your class in groups (take a look to the how to play a necro in group section).

Well, first of all you need to complete a quest in order to obtain the key to Sebilis. You will have to track (a tracker will be a must) and kill the froglok hunter and the froglok forager in Trakanon's Teeth zone, which is the zone right next to Sebilis. Then, once you loot the pendant from each of those froggies, turn them in to the named undead in the cave placed in the SW corner of the zone.

Sebilis, like chardok, has got the zoning in point far away from the zone out, meaning you won't be able to just walk back out like you did in, say, Karnor in case things would turn nasty. But dont worry, Sebilis entrance is pretty safe. Now sit down and start shout for a group  : )  I do not recommend you to set your hunting headquarters in Sebilis before you hit 55. Why? well, necromancers are not very group orientated class (please don't flame me. I know we can be very useful in groups, but we ain't any group's wet dream) so if you are low level -meaning lower than 55- either you have previously built a long list of ingame friends/guildmates, or expect long downtimes looking for group. Later on, or at least in my personal experience, once people get to know you and apreciate you as valuable group coleague, you will get into a group pretty soon.

I will now comment some of the most camped rooms in Sebilis, with its named and the loot :

  • Exit : Marked in the map as Ex. This is the hall that leads to the zoning out port. Three Golems and few frogs spawn here. Very safe camp (exit within few feets) and nice exp. No named spawn here though.

  • Necrosis-Guardian : Marked in the map as N-G. Very very easy room, no need of enchanter nor cleric (high level shaman/druid just as good here). Not the best place expwise in the other hand. Nameds are Guardian Golem (drops a 10 charges invisibility mask and the Lamentation) and Necrosis Scarab (drops cane of tranquil and Crystalline short sword).

  • Disco 1 and Disco 2 : Marked in the map as D1 and D2. Disco 1 is, as long as the puller is experienced and you time the spawning time in room, quite easy to handle with a good group. Disco 2 is a bit tougher, requiring an enchanter. Exp is great here, non stop pulling. Named is Frenzied Pox Beetle (drops beetle stinger, Deepwater bracer, inbued fighter staff and runed fighter staff) in D1, and Brogg (drops Ebon mace, Clay guardian shield and Tolan's bracer) in D2.

  • Necro jail : Marked in the map as NJ. Another easy room if the group is balanced, not even need of and enchanter or cleric if there is none available. One of the best exp rooms in the dungeon in my opinion. Named is Gangrenous Scarab (drops Elder Spirit Heml, Staff of waterwalker and Gangrenous Beetle Mask).

  • Cheff : Marked in the map as Ch. Deep inside the dungeon, will almost be impossible to hit the exit running if you ought to. One of the nastiest rooms, where everything might look peaceful but if you don't control the spawning time you might end up with five frogs on your butt (several spawning point in this room). Best room expwise in my opinion. Named here is the froglok chef (drops Fayguard Bladecatcher, Sebilite scale cape, Sebilite scale vambracers and Mrylokar's bracer), Froglok Repairer (drops singing steel vambracers) and Froglok Armsman (drops Truncheon of Doom, Mrylokar's helm and sebilite scale leggings).

  • Four Rooms : Marked in the map as 4R. Also very deep inside, with plenty of undeads around. You need a rogue to open the crypt room door. Lots of named spawns here with one of the greatest loot of the game (hierophant's cloak, Jaudiced bone bracer, silken snare whip, etc.). High level mobs but most of them are single pulls, so not very tough. Exp is ok only if you pull the emperor's room whereabouts too.

  • Bar - Armoury : Marked in the map as Ba and Ar. These rooms, as the cheff, are very tricky, with trains forming up all of a suden out of nowhere. Named are Froglok Armourer (drops singing steel bracer) and Froglok Bartender (Mrylokar's arms, Dark scale greaves, Sebilite scale cape and gloves).

  • Lower Exit : Marked in the map as LEx. There is another porting out point in the lower floor of sebilis. Same characteristics as upper exit, but much tougher.

  • Illies Jail : Marked in the map as IJ. Very tough area where the bulky frogloks illie spawn (lvl 52-53 ish) with loads of HP. Very nice exp too, but no named here.

  • King area :  Marked in the map as K. Deep inside Sebilis, almost before Trakanon's Lair itself, a weird race call the Myrconid, which look like giant purple mushrooms and are level 55ish. Of course, you better go there only with a good post 55 group or you're toast. Named is the King (drops Fungus tunic, Fungus great staff and robe of the living fungus). In my opinion, exp ain't very good here because the bloody mushrooms got around 15000 HP and some complete heal themselves !!

  • Trakanon's Lair : Marked in the map as Trak. This place is not camped for exp of course. Only most powerful guilds can make it to here. You will find juggernauts (drop 55 to 58 spells), Protector (drops Hardened Clay Bracer, Poison wind censer, sebilite croaking dirk and staff of battle), Tolapumj (drops Tolapumj robe, Peacebringer and Cap of the insubstancial) and Trakanon the undead dragon (drops all the best class specific chest armour).

Well, that was about it. You will eventually know Sebilis like tha palm of your hand since you will most likely spend 75%+  of your post 55 time here. Great dungeon, but very packed sometimes, which makes it sometimes hard to find a group, and even then hard to find any open room  : (  

As I said, there is a spot where you can duo with someone (high level shamans are the best necro parters in my opinion : slow, pet, regrowth, buffs, etc. Also another necro, a druid, mage or even an enchanter will make of the two of you a dangerous gang). I marked that spot on the map as Duo. Basically, pull the mob with Dooming Darkness and Paralizing Earth it once it's on the safety spot. Now the two of you load some DoTs (Pyrocuror, splurt and cessation of corr prefered, Envenomed bolt might brake the root). If the frog is of the Zol, Dar or Jin type (easy froggies), then send both pets in right after the first root has landed (forget about 53 or 56 pets, they die too fast. Go for 49 or 59 pet). If the mob is a bok type, wait till the DoTs have eaten half of his life. Don't try on anything higher (krups, beetles, illies, golems, etc. They got just too many HPs and they summon). 

To duo in Sebilis is very fun and challeging. It won't be as good expwise as a nice group, but sometimes you just can't get into any, or only have 1 hour to play, or you just fucking feel like. Here you go some tips to duo in Sebilis :

  • Only try on single pulls. If you see that two froggies might come along, don't pull and wait till a group jumps on them.

  • Keep the mob always snared, rooted and slowed if possible. If you let a frog to get loose you will be taking mayor risks.

  • Always med up with lich while Root-DoTing.

  • Spare some beating with your pets : root the mob, cast bond of death or Vexinia Mordana and walk into melee, take few rounds of damage and walk back. Otherwise pets will die oftenly.

  • Have always Harmshield, Feing dead and Screaming terror ready to save your butt.

  • Enjoy  : )

And that's it, we got to the end of this long way. Be proud of yourself, adverturer, for you have now been officially promoted to Warlock yourself, and I have nothing left to teach you. Well done, sir.
