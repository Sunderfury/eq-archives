Fleabums Tailoring Guide

This guide has been developed from my experiences with the trade skill of tailoring. If you find any mistakes or have anything to add then mail me fleabum@hotmail.com and I will get it updated.

Other Items

In this section I will cover various things, like type of pelt for race, other tailoring items that can be created and also other items with regards to tailoring like Heady Kiola's.


Backpack

Tailor made backpacks seem to weigh the same as regular backpacks (3.0) but have ten slots for you to put items into. They require a backpack pattern and a high quality bear skin. It is possible to begin making backpacks with a 25% or so success rate at skill level 40, but I don't recommend it. With High Quality Bear skins costing about 30pp failures can be quite expensive. The selling price of ten slot backpacks is about 80-120pp.


Bonings


These are made by combining a small brick of ore, file and flask of water in a forge. Studs use the blacksmithing skill.


Heady Kiola


Cured silk armor requires a new item, which is the Heady Kiola drink, this is created using the brewing skill. To create the Heady Kiola drink, you need to combine two kiola saps, one water flask, and one bottle in a brewers barrel. This item requires only a low skill at brewing, so any tailor should be able to make them as needed. The cost to make them, assuming no failures, is just under 3 gold each. Heady Kiola becomes trivial at brewing skill 46. If you want to cut down on failures, I suggest you max. out making bog juice as it only takes snake scales, water and a bottle.


Quiver and Fleeting Quiver


The items required to make these haven't been found to my knowledge, so that means there either broken or its another Verant mystery.


Sewing Kits

Sewing kits come in two sizes, small and large. Try and keep clear of the small sewing kit as it doesn't make all the items. Just purchase the large sewing kit and be happy that it can do all items. This is the most used item in tailoring, well it would be, you can't tailor without it ;).



Silk Swatch


These are made by combining two "large" type spider (i.e. Large Spider, Dune Spider, Heart Spider) silks in a sewing kit. Silk Swatches become trivial at skill level 17. Let it be noted though, they never fail, and as such this is the best way to get your skill upto 17.


Shadow Silk


These are used in various Necromancer quests. There are three types, and they seem to get progressively harder to make. I am not sure of the current trivial skill level of these items. All shadow silk takes at least one of the following items, Shadow Wolf pelt, Silk Swatch and Gather Shadows scroll.

Studs


These are made by combining three metal bits (unstacked), file and flask of water in a forge. Studs use the blacksmithing skill.


Tailor Made Whip


A tailor made whip is a one hand slash weapon with the stats of damage 7 and 33 delay. To make this weapon you need a Medium Quality Wolf pelt, four Heady Kiola's, and a whip pattern. The selling price is usually about 10pp. There is not much call for this item as there are other weapons about that are better for the same price.


Viscous Mana


This is an enchanter only made item. It uses the 12th level enchanter spell Thicken Mana, which is available in the Brownie village in Lesser Faydark. Casting the spell requires the material components of a poison vial and a pearl. This can be an annoying item to find, as most enchanters don't bother to get the spell, as currently it is pretty limited.

Wu's Fighting Gauntlets


These magical glowing silk gloves are useable by the Monk class only. With AC 4, with a weight of 0.1 they are very light and strong, but they also have a great boon for Humans, they give off light as a greater lightstone. The recipe is a glove pattern, a swatch of silk, a greater lightstone, a vial of viscous mana, and four Heady Kiola's. Assuming no failures, and considering the market value of the components, these gloves cost a minimum of 17 plat to make, so this makes the sale price about 40-60pp.
