This page is a listing of what the members of the clan think that "Divided Loyalties" means. They are in the order that I received them.


Whiteboy There's more to the world than Quake. I didn't wanna be strapped down to just playing Quake all the time as a clan, if we wanted to play Jedi Knight, or Starcraft, or Rainbow Six, or whatever. Divided Loyalties allows its members that flexibility — the ability to play as a clan whatever they want — not just Quake.

Khelan I used to think that Divided Loyalties was just a name. But now, it's an idea. The idea that we can play anyway or anything we want without having to worry about "does our clan approve?" It's a novel concept.

Krakker Divided Loyalties is not just our clan name it is the way we interact with each other. During normal game play we divide up and play our favorite online game, and are very adamant that it is the best game. Even when we do play together, we find it invigorating to play against each other using the rest of the team for fodder. Though When the time comes we can come together to create a "force to be reckoned with" [quote from Bandit_Z]. When we play together we have the most fun, assuming there is an opposing force that poses a challenge.

Jorus Divided Loyalties means that we have many personal objectives. Like the hydra that has many heads but one body, we have skill, independent thought, and goals that come together to form one purpose. Each head deadly in it's own right, but devastating as a whole.

Flip Well, to me DLC means we are, in respect, individuals. We can gather the power when we get together but when we are alone people fear us. DLC, to me, is the greatest power in the universe. DLC comes in many forms from Quake2-3 to Tribes. We can take any game and turn it into DLC power. We are the most extrovert players on the net. Even though we have good ping doesn't mean we are good but we showed Clan_Z and the rest of the people at the LAN party what we are made of. No one I mean NO one stands in the way of DLC. NO ONE!

JJ Divided Loyalties describes us, we are as diverse in our gaming preferences as we are in life. We are not tied to one game only, but rather are loyal to many different titles. It describes our entirely different playing styles. It describes our individuality, and freedom. And in one statement: Each of us is deadly as an individual, but as a group, the power of the whole is greater than the sum of the power of it's parts.

DLC, Fear Me, Fear It!!!!!!!!
