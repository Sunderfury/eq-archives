    Did you know?
    Posted by Optimus on Thursday, May 10 @ 23:52:32 CDT (read: 13 times)
    The Dvorak keyboard has a different key layout than the more common qwerty key arrangement. The qwerty arrangement was designed when typewriters were first invented. They key locations were intended to require you to move your hands when typing. The idea was to prevent you from typing faster than the mechanism could handle. The dvorak layout, named for its inventor, Peter Dvorak, is intended to do the opposite. The keys are arranged to reduce hand and finger motion, thus allowing faster typing speeds. (1996 PC Secrets 2nd Edition)
