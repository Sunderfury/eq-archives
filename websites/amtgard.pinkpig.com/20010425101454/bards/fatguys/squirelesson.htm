A Squire's First Lesson on Courtesy

(By Gorlan of the Red Lands)
(To the Tune of Good King Wenceslas)

    The care and feeding of wenches
    Is a noble art so fair -o
    Always see she has a seat
    By giving up your chair-o
    Never let her carry loads
    See that her gear's transported
    And if she's called before the crown
    Ensure that she's escorted.

    Listen to her every word
    Give her your full attention
    Always praise the garb she wears
    Her beauty you should mention
    Be sure to rub her shoulders fair
    Till tension has receded
    And if invited do her back
    Leave it nice and kneaded

    A wench should not be left alone
    She should be well attended
    Make sure her cup is ever full
    And she'll think you are splendid
    Have plenty of chocolate close hand
    Her smiles it will sweeten
    And if you follow all these steps
    The results can not be beaten.

The Story Behind this Song

There are many different views on what is the basis for the SCA "Dream". In my humble opinion it is courtesty. With the SCA in Europe there are many cultural differences. In some countries, the ladies feel a strong need to assert their independance. The only lords in those countries that escort their ladies are those that have had exposure to the SCA in the USA. I have, many a time, been chewed out by ladies for offering to help carry a load, offer a seat, or an escort when a lady is called before the crown. This song started as subtle reminder to both lords and laidies of the basic actions of courtesy in the SCA. My first title was going to be "Clues for the Clueless". It kind of deviated from the original goal, but I liked the end result anyway.
