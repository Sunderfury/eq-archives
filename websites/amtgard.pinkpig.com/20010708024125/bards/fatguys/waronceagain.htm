I'm In a War Once Again

(by Gorlan of the Red Lands, 1999)
(Tune: "On the Street Where You Live" by Frederick Lowe and Alan J. Lerner from "My Fair Lady")

    Oh to be at war
    Is where I want to be
    Tournaments no longer do too much for me
    I'm a happy guy
    When the arrows fly
    Knowing I'm in a war once again

    A large shield wall
    Does not bother me
    It means that there are lots of folks to kill you see
    To charge and knock it down
    Is better than a crown
    Knowing I'm in a war once again

    Fighting in a town
    Fighting on a bridge
    Fighting in the fields, or woods to take their land
    You must understand
    That I feel so grand
    Knowing I'm in a war once again

    There are Kings to kill
    Dukes and Earls as well
    Towns to take, or defend until you get your fill
    Fighting in a war
    Who could ask for more?
    Knowing I'm in a war once again

    At a tournament
    The action is too slow
    When I kill, I have to wait an hour before my next blow
    But in a war
    Too many to keep score
    Yes I'm happy fighting in a war again

The Story Behind this Song

Nothing new here. I prefer fighting in wars instead of Tourneys.
