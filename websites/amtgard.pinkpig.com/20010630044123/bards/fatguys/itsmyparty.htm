It's My Party, But Nobody's Coming

(by Gorlan of the Red Lands, based on a suggestion from Lord James Le Cray, 1998)
(Tune: "It's My Party" by Leslie Gore)

    I held a party one night at a War
    The squires had a meeting at the same time
    It seems that they didn't like something I said
    It must be some kind of crime

      Chorus:
      So I'm having a party
      But there's nobody coming
      Nobody's coming, nobody's coming
      You would whine too if it happened to you.


    I only want to have things my own way
    Regardless of tradition or vows
    I told everyone they must do what I say
    And that they had to do it right now

    Chorus:

    Don't they understand that since I was once king
    I'm much wiser than they
    And because my belt is all white
    My every whim must be obeyed

    Chorus:

    I don't understand why no one comes anymore
    Don't they see the pointy hat on my head
    Instead they're all going off somewhere else
    I might as well just go to bed

    So I had a party
    But nobody showed up
    Nobody showed up, nobody showed up
    You would whine too if it happened to you.

The Story Behind this Song

Started as a suggestion. After the principal subject of this song bitterly complained that I had him wrong, I put the song on hold for a year to see if I changed my opinion. After a year, my opinion hasn't changed. In my eyes, it describes him to a tee.
