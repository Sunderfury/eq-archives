Journal of a Traveler

This is the journal of Talies, a half-elven lad who joined the Bard's Guild on Bristlebane, and set out for numerous adventures. Although the most current page will be on the front, later pages are in order. To start at the beginning, click here.

April 8th, 3172

I spent a whole day doing nothing but exploring, poking into shops, and checking out what was available. Prices seem a bit higher here than in Kelethin, and people are a bit less friendly, but it's still a nice enough town. I went back to the lake to see if that old Gnome was there again, but I didn't see him. Of course, that doesn't mean much, considering his skills, but I wasn't going to go look foolish talking to every lamp and bush.

I decided instead to try something I had seen a few people around the lake doing: fishing. I had heard it was fun, and the benefit, catching your own food! The pole was rather expensive, but at least bait only ran 4 copper each. I spent the next several hours getting rather frustrated, catching nothing but an old sandal and some soggy fish scales. Finally, my patience was rewarded by a firm tug on the line, and a splash as the fish I had hooked tried to jump away. I kept a firm grip on it, and then, like I had seen other people do, I slowly walked the fish back in to the shore, pulling the line in only after I had it in the shallows. After what seemed like hours, but was in reality only a couple of minutes, I pulled out my very own fresh-caught fish! I was so excited, I nearly dropped it back into the lake, but I recovered it just in time. Slippery buggers!

After all that sun and hard work, I discovered that I was somewhat hot and sweaty. Seeing how much fun everyone else seemed to be having in the water, I decided to join them. Leaving my pole and gear on the shore, I slowly stepped into the shallows, letting the cool water soak my feet. Let me tell you - there's nothing like the feeling of cool water on sun-soaked skin. Seeing how everyone else was just diving in with their clothes on, I figured that a little water wouldn't hurt mine, either, so I stepped in further, until finally I was standing with my head just barely above water. I had to keep waving my hands back and forth to keep my balance, and a few waves from across the lake threatened to push me over, but it was refreshing and exciting at the same time, so I enjoyed myself.

I noticed the sun beginning to dip once more, so I waded out, wrung myself out as best I could, then decided that I would dry on my way back to Kelethin. It was really time for me to check in with Sylia.
