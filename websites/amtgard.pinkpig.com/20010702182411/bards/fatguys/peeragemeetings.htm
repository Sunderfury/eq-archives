Peerage Meetings

(by Gorlan of the Red Lands, 1999)
(Tune: "Little Ball of Yarn", Traditional)

    In Merry Drachenwald
    Where the king is going bald
    And Queen had guzzled too much Helmet's home brew
    The peerage did sit down,
    And meet with the crown
    And discuss who should become a new peer now

      Chorus:
      *** Peerage meetings, Peerage meetings
      *** Lots of talk, but nothing really gets done-------
      *** Peerage Meetings, Peerage Meetings
      *** I wouldn't wish this upon anyone.


    The Chivalry did meet
    And each knight took a seat
    And Time moved so slow it seemed to crawl
    And when they were finally done
    You could no longer see the sun
    You could see the king and queen were bored to tears

    Chorus:

    Then the Pelicans sat down
    You see the Royals frown
    And again the meeting went without end
    When they ended it at last
    You could hear the Royals gasp
    There's still one more to go through before we're done

    Chorus:

    The Laurels were the end
    While the crowns did pretend
    To be alert while they talked on and onnnnnnnnn.
    When at last they did adjourn
    The crowns we sad to learn
    That all the food in camp was eaten and gone.

    Chorus:

    So if you think you want a crown
    And to wear a regal gown
    It involves much more than sitting on a throne
    For when the Peerage meet
    They reserve for you a seat
    It is sure make you wish you were alone.

    Chorus:

The Story Behind this Song

While I am not a peer, after 25 years around the SCA you pick up a few things. Too many monarchs, the worst part of the job is sitting in on the Peerage meetings when they occur. There are some SCAdians who live for such meetings, while others avoid them like the plague. Whenever somebody mentions that they are going to fight for a crown, my normal reply is "Two Words - Peerage Meetings". This one is based upon a set of meetings at a Double wars a few years ago. We gave King and Queen a hard time when they finally dragged themselves back to camp and rubbed in all the fun that they missed.
