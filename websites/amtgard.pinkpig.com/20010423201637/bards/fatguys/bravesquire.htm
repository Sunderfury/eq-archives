The Brave Squire

(By Gorlan of the Red Lands - based on an old joke, 1998)
(Traditional tune)

    Three knights one eve were bragging
    About their squire's bravery,
    On how they each possessed the best
    Of the aspiring nobility.

    The first said "Mines the bravest
    "And to prove it he will go
    "And slay the worm who lives in the woods
    "Armed with his sword and bow."

    And bravely he went into the woods
    And was heard a savage fight.
    But then his flaming helm returned
    And landed at the feet of his knight.

    The second said "Mines the bravest
    "And to prove it he will go
    "And slay the ogre who lives in the cave
    "In the valley down below."

    And bravely he went into the cave,
    And again was heard a savage fight.
    Then out from the cave rolled a battered helm
    "What a pity" said the second knight.

    The third said "Mines the bravest,
    "Of that there can be no doubt!
    "Just watch this," the third knight said,
    And "Squire!" he did shout.

    "What do you want?" the Squire said
    To the three knights by the fire.
    His knight said "Fetch us some beer, my boy!"
    "Get it yourself," bravely said the Squire.

The Story Behind this Song

This is an old joke that has been drifting around campfires for at least 15 years.
