Weekend Notes

Hot damn! Thanksgiving week! You have to love this week! You only have 3 days of work or school, then you get to sit around with family and eat like a total pig and watch football! If you think about it Thanksgiving is one of the only Holidays not totally soiled by gimmicks and retail. You don't often see houses decorated with turkey paraphernalia or pumpkin pie decorations. This holiday is pretty much just about being with family. There is no Thanksgiving mascot that comes around and gives you free gifts or candy, instead you get Grandma slaving away in the kitchen all day so that the rest of the family get some really good turkey and stuffing. No cutesy commercials on TV or giant turkeys in the mall to have your picture taken with. Now stop and think about what we are actually celebrating when we celebrate Thanksgiving....

Pretty good weekend for me overall. Drank *WAY* too much beer on Saturday though. The beloved University of Michigan played the much hated Ohio State Buckeyes so of course me and the crew got together with a ton of beer and snacks and pretty much sat around for the entire day watching football. Michigan won, by the way, so yay for me! I paid the price for it on Sunday though when my hung over ass couldn't hardly get out of bed the entire day. I took advantage of it by watching a movie that I have been waiting to see, The Matrix. That movie kicked so much ass! I watched it with no idea what it was about, I just heard it was good and went from there. It is so rare that you can watch a movie now-a-days and actually be surprised by it. Between the newspaper reviews and overblown previews, movies are generally never a surprise. You almost always have a general idea of what the movie is about and where it is going to go. It was nice to just sit back and enjoy a good movie for a change.

For those of you who are into this sort of thing, you may be happy, or disappointed to hear that Quake III has gone gold. In layman's terms the game has been sent off for mass reproduction and will be sitting on a store shelf near you soon. Hmm...this just on the heals of Epic sending Unreal Tournament out for mass reproduction, coincidence, ah who the hell cares, as long ass I get two new kick ass games.

Tuesday, November 2nd

Expand this

For those of you living under a rock, Verant has announced that they will release the EQ expansion in March of 2000. I am sure a lot of people are overjoyed about this. For those that haven't heard about it, here is a list of the things that you can expect to see in the expansion:

-- New continent "Kunark," located south of Faydwer
-- More than 20 new adventure zones featuring both beginner and advanced zones including: lakes, jungles, crypts, caves, dungeons, ancient towers and underground castles
-- New playable race, the Iksar (Lizardman)
-- A new cast of visually stunning non-player characters; grey dragons, reptilian monsters, giant scorpions, man-eating plants and more
-- More object animations featured throughout Kunark that include earthquakes and other violent natural          phenomenon's   
-- A highly detailed world with three times the amount of polygons and larger textures than the EverQuest world
-- All-new ships that will sail from both Antonica and Faydwer to Kunark
-- Newly created art by famous fantasy artist Keith Parkinson
-- Increased level difficulty range allows players to advance further in the game

This doesn't really excite me too much. Like Amanda said on the message board, we already pay to play the game and now Verant is going to expect us to shell out some extra money to access a new land. Instead of concentrating on fixing the problems that people think exist already, they concentrate their efforts on coming up with a new land that will probably introduce a whole new batch of problems. But Verant isn't stupid, they are a business. They know that people will pay for this because no one is going to want to be left out. OSI did the same thing in UO when they released T2A (and the upcoming UO expansion). As long as we are suckers and continue to pay they will continuously find things for us to pay for.
