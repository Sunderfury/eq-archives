I'd recommend going toward a PS2. It's a good balance of the kiddie stuff that's due out for the Gamecube (although, Smash Melee is exclusively for the Gamecube) and the more hardcore stuff that's due out on the Xbox (as a side note, Dead or Alive 3 is FUN - best looking chicks I've seen in a game and some pretty fucked up moves). Additionally, Squaresoft has signed a deal with Sony saying that they will only release for Sony systems for the next few years. If you like RPGs, I'd stay with a PS2.

However, Capcom has signed a deal with Nintendo saying that they will produce exclusively for the Gamecube. So if you like the Street Fighter series, I'd go after a Gamecube.

Then, of course, there is Grand Theft Auto 3. That game, alone, is worth buying the PS2.

My console roundup:

PS2 - All around badass system. From what I've read/heard, it's very hard to develop for the PS2, and the first generation of games that are out for it don't really utilize its full potential (we've all seen screenshots that more than outdo any of the best stuff that's out now). As soon as a few engines are developed, be on the lookout for some very dynamic and FUN games. Sony also has the patend on the laser technology in the PS2, which allows DVD viewing and gaming all with the same laser. This will lead to some very stunning cut scenes, as well as scenematic gameplay.

Xbox - I've played Halo, which is their big title right now, and I don't see it as the Second Coming. It's fun and all, but it's just Goldeneye on crack. Then again, I HATE shooters without a mouse and the wasd system of moving. I played DOA3, and despite wishing there was a nude patch, it wasn't any better than the Street Fighter games (actually, it was far more cheap, even if the moves were fun to pull off. Think Tekken + M. Bison + Killer Instinct in its cheapness). In addition, it has the shittiest controller out of the three. The right hand buttons are very hard to reach, even with an experienced thumb. They are set up roughly like the n64 controllers, but the six buttons are at a nearly 80* angle. The buttons also get progressively smaller and hard to find in a pinch. This, alone, would make me hold off until someone makes a better layout. Also, as the DVD player REQUIRES a remote to play standard DVDs, you will need to tack $50 on to the price tag, not to mention the fact that they are damn near impossible to find. In addition to the remote problem, games will have to be shorter to match the eye candy of the PS2 due to the fact that the Xbox doesn't have the DVD/Rom/all in one handy dandy laser.

Gamecube: Nintendo has yet to release a bad system. Even the VirtualBoy was good, although a few years ahead of its time. Smash Melee is enough to wet your pants, and Pikmin is a fucked up version of warhammer and Dungeon Keeper. I haven't had a chance to play much else, but the system seems solid enough for me to put it on my wish list.

Summary (in order of recommendation): PS2, Gamecube, CD-R supporting CD player/Dreamcast, Xbox.


Hope this helped you a bit in your decision.
