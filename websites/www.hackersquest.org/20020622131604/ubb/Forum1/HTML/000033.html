Not sure what level of depth your looking for as far as assembly is concerned, but an excellent resource if your interested in begining to understand the lowest levels of pc hardware is "The 80x86 IBM PC & Compatible Computers, Assembly Language, Design, and Interfacing" by Muhammad Ali Mazidi. Not sure if its still in print or not, but its an excellent begining if your interested in assembly. It will take you not only through the concepts of programming at the register lvl but will explain how the hardware works and give you an understanding of what the language is doing physically. The architecture has changed, and there is ALOT more to it now, but learning how it evolved is, in my opinion at least, the best way to understand why things are the way they are. hehe there is even a schematic at the end of the original 80286 proccesor.
