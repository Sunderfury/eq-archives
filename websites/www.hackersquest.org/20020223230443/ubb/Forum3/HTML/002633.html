actually, i was planning on replying to this post on the seq boards at sf, but if i'm going to spend the time helping someone i like to feel like i have thier undivided attention, and a single point of contact for the issue for follow ups.

i understand the issue you are encountering well enough, but you dont explain yourself very well.

i assume by your wording that your connection is

dsl <--> win98 <--> linux

although i'm not exactly sure where the 56k modem is coming into play, most people use ethernet for networking.

if that the net is setup as above, showeq cannot work, as the EQ packets from the win98 box will go directly out over the dsl and never go to the linux box.

the 2 generalized setups to get showeq to work are as follows

Internet <---> Linux gateway <---> Internel network

In this case, you cna use a hub or a switch for your internal clients, as the packets will always traverse the linux box.

the other option is

Internet <---> some box or device (not EQ box) <---> Lan with linux box and EQ box on it.

In this case you must be using a hub to interface the linux and EQ boxes together, as the way a switch works inhibits the ability to sniff packets. no, i dont feel like explaining why.
