Decaying Skeleton 		posted 01-03-2002 02:37 AM            
Tux: Yes, most consoles are 500MHz and below, but the problem comes with the emulator. Unless the products have really changed, it takes a lot more clock cycles on your Pentium to decode what a system that is designed for that code could do in one. Case in point is an SNES emulator. The SNES is physically about 3.5MHz processor, but when emulating on a computer you need at least a 200MHz to get smooth framerates.

So I believe even though consoles seem like slow processors, since the emulators need a lot of power to decode the information, you will need much beefier hardware than the console has.

One exception might be X-Box, seeing as how it is actually made of computer components, but then again that custom GeForce that they threw in there might take quite a bit of juice to emulate properly.

And maybe the new-generation consoles are so much like computers it doesn't take too much extra power to decode them. I'm not sure...
