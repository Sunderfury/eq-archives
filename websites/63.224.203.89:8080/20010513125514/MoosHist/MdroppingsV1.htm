What's a Guild, Anyway?
By Moos Alini, Capitan, Clachan Brigh

Greetings friend,

The time has come that I have been asked to put into pen what thing it is that makes up a guild. I hope you enjoy this missive. Whether it causes you stop and ponder whether you are made of such stuff as to seek to join Clachan Brigh or perhaps not, I hope you find something of value in the ramblings of an oft-drunk servant of Tunare…

First, let us examine the conventional definition of the term Guild. A noted source (Merriam-Webster in fact) defines the word as such:

Main Entry: guild
Pronunciation: 'gild
Function: noun
Etymology: Middle English gilde, from Old Norse gildi payment, guild; akin to Old English gield tribute, guild -- more at GELD
Date: 14th century
: an association of people with similar interests or pursuits; especially : a medieval association of merchants or craftsmen
- guild·ship /'gil(d)-"ship/ noun

Is this really, then, all that a guild is? Is this all, then, that makes up Clachan Brigh? Well, yes and no…

True, we at Clachan Brigh are an association with similar interests. We hunt together killing the various critters that we may encounter, as is befitting to our skills. We pursue unique items together and desire collectively to increase our knowledge and reputation in the land of Norrath. We are all of noble races (meaning no offense to those Dark Elves who are either reading this missive or translating it for Ogres and Trolls) and many of us are of similar professional class. We enjoy as much as any others drinking copious amounts of brew, hopefully brewed by one of our own and carousing about.

But it is so much more than that, which binds us together. Quite frankly, a guild which is nothing more than a collection of folks who only want to hunt together for maximum knowledge gain (read experience) and accumulation of wealth does not interest me. There is an intangible involved which I don't believe that any dictionary or definition really has put a finger upon. It is not enough merely to BE in a guild, you must truly LOVE your guildmates, as they are your brothers and sisters. How, then, is this love for one another developed? Quite simply, friend - it cannot be faked, it cannot be assumed, it cannot be bought nor sold. However, it is given freely as we spend time with one another, help one another, rescue one another's corpses from some misadventure or another… in short, love is the fruit of shared experience over the passage of time.

And that, dear friend, is what separates a Guild from a club…

Many regards,

Moos Alini
