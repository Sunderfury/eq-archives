I. Articles of Constitution

A) The purpose of the Guild is to facilitate member growth and also contribute to the maturation process of the Bristlebane server as a whole. The name of the Guild “Clachan Brigh” literally means “strength through growth”.

B) It is the belief of the Guild that all “Players” are equal and should be treated with respect and kindness at all times. We define a “Player” to be the person controlling, via mouse and keyboard, an in-game character.

C) It is the belief of the Guild that all Characters of good alignment are to be helped and encouraged as much as reasonably possible. It is understood that many Characters, which may seem to be members of the noble races, may actually be Trolls in disguise. Any of these types will be duly noted by the Guild and will be treated as such.

D) It is the belief of the Guild that all Characters of evil alignment should be tolerated, in that they might somehow be found to be an agent for good. Should said evil character prove that this trust is misplaced, appropriate reciprocating action may be taken by any guild member.

E) It is the belief of the Guild that Everquest is a role-playing game. Role-play is encouraged and expected from members. However, the Guild does not want to put itself in the position of becoming role-play police. While non-roleplayers may not be allowed into the Guild, they will not in any other way be shunned by Clachan Brigh members.

F) Guild membership is held to be a privilege, not a right. Membership may be revoked through means described below.

G) The assistance of the Guild is freely given, yet is not subject to demand.


II. Guild Membership

A) Any Player who has a valid EQ account is welcome to apply for membership regardless of their race, creed, gender or any other personal preference. We will not discriminate against any person for any reason.

B) Additional characters are to be allowed in the guild, with a two character limit.  If a player wishes to guild another character removal of one of the current characters is required.  All additional characters are subject to Officer approval.

C) The Guild is comprised strictly of honorable characters. We will only accept for membership characters of similar type. [Note: the difference between Player (human being) and Character (virtual being)]. The following class and races need not apply: Trolls, Ogres, Dark Elves, Necromancers. However, Rogues may apply with the understanding that the Guild leaders will be very interested in how they choose to apply their unique talents.

D) Any member who is found to participate in any activity which constitutes a violation of the service agreement with Verant (this includes hacking, lewd behavior, etc.) will be removed from the Guild immediately. Further, the ex-member will be reported to Verant.


E) Should any member in any way attempt to disrupt or discredit Clachan Brigh, that player's membership may be brought up for review by any two members of the Guild. A member under review may be removed from the Guild by unanimous vote of the elected Officers. If the Officers cannot come to a unanimous agreement on the matter (either all in favor or against continuing membership privilages), the member will be placed on suspension until the next election period. At that time, Clachan Brigh members will vote for new officers (see below) and also on whether to retain or dismiss the suspended player. This vote is final. Review is not a trivial issue and, as such, this matter should be very carefully considered before any member suggests this to an Officer.

F) Guild membership is comprised into 3 classes: Officers, Charter Members and Members. There is no difference between a Charter Member and a Member other than Charter Members are Members who are on the original Charter.



III. Government

A) Officers shall be elected by the guild membership on a quarterly basis. The three positions are Guild Master, Guild Captain, and Guild Treasurer. Elections shall function as such: Candidates for office are nominated by any member (including themselves) prior to election. On the day of voting all members are given a 24 hour period of time to cast their votes electronically. All members have two votes which they use to endorse 2 of the members running. The three members who receive the most votes are named Officers of the Guild and decide between themselves who will act as Treasurer, who as Captain and who as Master.

B) The three officers of the guild are collectively given totalitarian power over all Clachan Brigh functions during their period in office. New member candidates need to earn the unanimous approval of the 3 officers before being accepted for membership. Members who have in some fashion disgraced the guild may be removed from the Guild by the unanimous vote of the officers. Officers may impose fees upon members to raise funds for whatever purpose they deem beneficial to the purposes of the Guild. In short, when members vote for Officers of Clachan Brigh, they are entrusting them absolutely with the day to day functions of the Guild.


C) In broad strokes: The Guild Master is responsible for dealing with Verant Interactive as GM, maintaining the Offical Clachan Brigh website ( ), electronic communication, Guild roster and resolving member disputes; The Captain is responsible for all Guild events, questing, and rescue operations; The Treasurer is responsible for guild holdings, collection, and dispersal.

D) A candidate for new membership must fulfill all the requirments stated in the Clachan Brigh Membership Requirements. The Officers may set forth any tasks for the Candidate so that s/he may prove themself worthy to become a Clachan Brigh Member.


IV) Guild Activities and Functions

A) The primary purpose of the Guild is to promote membership growth. EverQuest is a level-based game and thus growth and increasing a character's level will, in many ways, be one and the same. Growth may also mean encouraging member excellence in the various crafts especially blacksmithing, tailoring, and jewel craft.

B) The membership of the guild is expected to hunt together with great frequency. As such, the Guild Captain will form organized hunts on a frequent basis. Characters of all levels and abilities are highly encouraged to attend.


C) As the Guild matures, membership increases (not merely in numbers but in strength), and the wealth of the Guild grows, the Guild may sponsor hunts, quests and tournaments for the benefit of all the players of Bristlebane. These events may also be thematic events.

D) The Guild Treasurer is empowered to collect pre-set Guild dues from each Clachan Brigh Member. The amount of the due, frequency collected, and dispersal critiria are detailed in Clachan Brigh Newsletter

E) Anything that Members consider “fun” and embodies a “good” character, the Guild may do either as a group or individually. We do not tell our Members how to act. We respect our Members as adults and anticipate that any actions taken will be appropriate.
