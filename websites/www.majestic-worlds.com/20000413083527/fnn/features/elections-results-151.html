Political Elections Results by Phalanx

In an complete upset, the Discovery Coalition, a darkhorse in the frontier's political scene, has won the latest political election. The incumbants, the Freedom Party, has been in polticial power for the last 16 years.

In what seems to be even more shocking news, the Discovery Coalition has choose the Freedom Party to form a majority coalition. The new President of the Council of Worlds, Grand Consul Tre'eka stated "The Freedom Party has always been strong supporters of the Discovery Coalition and we will need their strength and support in the times ahead."

The final votes were Discovery Coalition (34.1%), the Freedom Party (28.7%), the Plantary Paradise Party (21.3%), the Historical Society (7.4%), the Commerce Crusade (5.3%) and the Frontier Future Foundation (3.2%)

Political Parties and their leaders:

Commerce Crusade led by Elsin Marrik (Hm)
Believing that the strength of the Federation is provided by the economic strength of the Federation's businesses, promoting the trade and commerce of the frontier is the highest of their priorities.

Discovery Coalition led by Jezra Tre'eka (Vf)
The path to true happiness and success is through the discovery of all things unknown from science to many forms of art.

Freedom Party led by Brendon Harinov (Hm)
The current leaders of the Federation believe that the current policies of a balanced and strong central goverment is what keeps the Federation from collapsing.

Frontier Future Foundation led by Trassa Krogen (Yf)
The Federation has become a bloated, stagnent organization that needs new blood to more forward in the next 150 years.

Historical Society led by Raena Freen (Hf)
By relying more on technology, the members of the Federation will be allowed to excell in areas that are hardly touched right now.

Planetary Paradise Party led by Z'eken Nour'grath (Vm)
With all of the undeveloped worlds within the Federation's borders, the development, terraforming, and habitation of these worlds are paramount to the expansion of the Federation.
