What is brewing?

Brewing is a trade skill like smiting, fletching, jewelcraft and others. In brewing, you make...well huh.. brews! Ales, wines, liquors and other alcoholic drinks. It's not a very profitable skill but it's fun and doesn't cost much to become master (less than 50pp).

Why is there alcohol in EQ?

Basically, I'm not sure. Some drinks taken in large quantities (or a few high alcohol drinks) boost your stamina and strength for some period of time (or so I heard). But to be able to take that much alcohol and still be able to think and walk, you need to rise your alcohol tolerance skill. How do you do that? Well... you drink! You tolerance skill will go up as you drink. Our guild leader, Rassal, now has 160 alcohol tolerance (thus his surname "The drunken Wizard").

There is however one drink (the Heady kiola) that is used by tailors and you can actually sell to other players. But I wont give out this tailor's secret here. Only the recipe for the Heady kiola

How do I brew?

Brewing is fairly easy. You need to find a Brew barrel or a still in a city. All the major cities have one and it's generally (but not always) located in or near a bar (see Where to buy to know where they are). I haven't seen any difference between the barrel and the still, they're the same device with different names.

 

 

 

Once you find it, you left click on it. A new window will open. There is 10 slots box with a Combine button that will open. You put the ingredients in the slots and press Combine. You'll get one of 4 things:

  • "You have fashioned a new item!", a drink (success!)
  • "You lack the skill to fashion the items together", which means you miss
  • "Those items did not combine in those quantities to produce anything", which means you have the wrong recipe
  • "You have become trivial at this item", which is explained later

OK, I get it. But how do I become Master!

To raise your brewing skill, the first thing you can do is put practice points at your guild in this skill. However, the guild master can only train you up to 21 skill. After that, you'll need to practice by making drinks over and over again. After a few, your skill will go up 1 point. A drink recipe will raise your skill until you have come too good a brewer for it. This is what we call a "trivial score". For example, your can practice your brewing by making Kalish until your reach 31 skill. Then, you wont learn more about brewing by making Kalish. You need to practice with harder drinks.

The harder the drink, the more chances you have to miss it. From what I've seen, skill goes up faster with successful tries than failures (which is kind of logical). So the trick is always to do drinks closest (but above) your current skill to make it rise faster. In the previous example, the logical step after Kalish would be Mead (it's trivial at 41 skill).

Once you've become trivial at a drink, it doesn't mean you'll always have success with it. Once trivial, I've heard that your reach maximum probability which is around 70% success. Look up the Recipes section to see all trivial scores.

What are recipes and where do I find the ingredients?

The recipes represent the items you have to put in the still to produce alcohol. You can find all the recipes I know in the Recipes section. As for ingredients, there are 4 groups. See the Economy section to find vendors:

Ingredients found in bars:

  • Casks
  • Grapes
  • Bottles
  • Kiola sap

Ingredients found in specialty shops (brew shops or vendors):

  • Barley
  • Hops
  • Malt
  • Yeast
  • Wine yeast
  • Rice
  • Spices (baking shop)
  • Kiola Nut (shop in OoT)

Ingredients only found with Forage skill (druids, rangers, bards and Wood elves):

  • Berries
  • Fruits
  • Veggies
  • Morning dew

Ingredients found in loots:

  • Rat's ears
  • Froglok meat
  • Fire beetle eye
  • Snake skin
  • Spider legs

You will also need water flasks in great quantity. But this is a really common item bought almost anywhere. One item require Fish, you can get a fish by either Fishing skill, killing a fish in water (sometimes loots a fish) or buying it.

The hard part for most people are the foraged items. This is why I said the Wood Elves are the best brewers. We are born with a basic 50 skill in forage. Since most high skill drinks require foraged items, we have a great advantage.
