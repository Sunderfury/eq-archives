John Randle is looked upon as one of the top players in the NFL due to his hard work and dedication. He has never missed a game in his illustrious 10-year career. Signed as a non-drafted free agent in 1990, John has more sacks than any player in the NFL since 1991. He has caused many sleepless nights for opposing offensive coordinators. Opponents are never sure where he’ll line up, but they know they have to double-team him. If they don’t, they will be the recipient off a trademark "John Randle Sack Dance."

Despite only starting eight games in 1991, he registered 9.5 sacks. In 1992, he finished first in the NFC with 11 sacks among interior lineman. One year later, John was selected to start in his first Pro Bowl while garnering All-NFC honors. In 1994, he led all NFL interior linemen and tied for first overall in the NFC in sacks with 13.5. In 1995 and 1996, John started in the Pro Bowl and led the team in sacks. After posting such impressive statistics, he didn’t rest on his accomplishments. In 1997, he led the entire NFL in sacks (15.5) for the first time in his career. In 1998, he cemented his place among the best ever-defensive players by starting in his fifth straight Pro Bowl. He also became only the fourth player (the others being Reggie White, Lawrence Taylor and Bruce Smith) in NFL history with six straight seasons with 10 or more sacks.

John played a huge role in the making of NFL Xtreme 2. He, along with 22 other Pro Bowl players like Jessie Tuggle and Warren Sapp, provided smack talk for use in the video game. There are 350 new animations courtesy of motion capture from John Randle, Mike Alstott and Hardy Nickerson. He is also featured in the NFL Xtreme 2 print advertisement, TV commercial as well as the package front.
