989 SPORTS’ NEW BASEBALL VIDEOGAME MLB 2000 STEPS UP TO THE PLATE WITH EXPANDED GAME PLAY MODES AND ALL-STAR LINEUP
-- Hall Of Fame Announcer Vin Scully And ESPN Analyst Dave Campbell Join Cover Star Mo Vaughn To Make MLB 2000 The Most Authentic Baseball Videogame --

FOSTER CITY, Calif., March 29, 1999 – 989 Sports™ announced today that MLB™ 2000, the next installment of 989 Sports’ incredibly popular MLB baseball videogame series, will be available tomorrow for the PlayStation® game console. With all 30 MLB teams and players, MLB 2000 utilizes new 3D player and stadium models, expanded game play modes and two-man commentary from legendary announcer Vin Scully and ESPN analyst Dave Campbell.

"MLB 2000 provides the most authentic baseball experience available in a videogame," said Jeffrey Fox, vice president, marketing, 989 Studios. "Fans of the MLB series will find that the detail in this year’s title is so in-depth, it will feel like they’re actually watching a live baseball game. Plus, with the involvement of baseball’s leading figures such as Vin Scully, Mo Vaughn and Dave Campbell, all the realism of Major League Baseball is in MLB 2000."

MLB 2000 will display every Major League player in unbelievable detail right down to the wrinkles and folds in the player’s uniform. In addition, the stances and deliveries for more than 200 MLB batters and pitchers have been personalized. Even the stadiums are sculpted to perfection with every realistic detail.

To further increase the realism of the game, motion capture animation was used to incorporate the movements of real baseball stars. Some of the players used for motion capture include Anaheim Angels All-Star first baseman Mo Vaughn, San Francisco Giants All-Star pitcher Shawn Estes, Chicago White Sox All-Star second baseman Ray Durham and Colorado Rockies All-Star pitcher Darryl Kile.

The great depth of MLB 2000 has been expanded even further. This year, with the help of former MLB scouts, 989 Sports will give the gamer the chance to work as the GM and build a team to be the next baseball dynasty. Gamers can create, draft, trade, call up, waive, release and sign players as free agents. Turn on the new Manager Mode option to call all the shots and utilize the in-game scouting reports to set up hitters and make lineup changes.

A sneak preview of MLB 2000 was given to MLB teams at their practice facilities during Spring Training. MLB players didn’t just want to be a part of the development process of MLB 2000, they wanted to play the game. All MLB teams will be given a copy of MLB 2000 to play throughout the 1999 season in the 989 Sports’ MLB 2000 Locker Room Challenge. This will be the second year 989 Sports has hosted this challenge to see who will be the MLB 2000 videogame MVP from each team.

MLB 2000 is now available and features All-Star Mo Vaughn of the Anaheim Angels on the package.

MLB 2000 – Key Features:

  • All MLB teams and players with the latest signings and trades
  • All the MLB stadiums with specific stadium ambiance. See Wrigley Field’s Waveland Avenue and Yankee Stadium’s outfield monuments like never before
  • mpeccable play-by-play and in-depth color commentary featuring Hall of Fame announcer Vin Scully and ESPN analyst Dave Campbell
  • 3D players scaled to their actual height and weight
  • More than 200 new personalized deliveries and stances of MLB pitchers and batters
  • Realistic player performance. McGwire is one of the most prolific power hitters ever, Kevin Brown has nasty stuff and Tony Gwynn is one of the greatest hitters of all time
  • New expanded play modes let the gamer take over the day-to-day operations of running a team
  • Create, draft, trade, call up, waive, release and sign players as free agents in the new GM mode
  • With the new Manager mode, gamers can utilize in-game scouting reports prepared by former Major League scouts for on-the-fly stats and lineup changes
  • Total Control Fielding™ is back and better than ever. Fielders dive, throw from their knees, crow-hop, twirl throw, throw on the run and make bare-handed grabs
  • Total Control Batting™ returns as the best batting interface in any baseball videogame
  • Players used for motion capture include Anaheim Angels Mo Vaughn, San Francisco Giants Shawn Estes, Chicago White Sox Ray Durham, Colorado Rockies Darryl Kile and Darryl Hamilton, Cincinnati Reds Brett Tomko and Detroit Tigers Karim Garcia
  • Create a player and give him all the features imaginable
  • Play Modes: Spring Training, Home Run Derby™, Exhibition, full season with multiple season lengths, All-Star™
  • End-of-season awards: AL and NL MVP, Batting and saves titles, Rookie of the Year and Cy Young
  • Three difficulty levels (rookie, veteran and all-star)
