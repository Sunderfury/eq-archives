989 STUDIOS’ ONLINE ROLE PLAYING GAME EVERQUEST™
EVOLVES INTO A WHOLE NEW WORLD

FOSTER CITY, Calif., March 16, 1999 – 989 Studios™ announced today that EverQuest™, its massive online role playing game capable of supporting more than 1,500 players per server, is now available.

"With free roaming environments, superior graphics and an evolving storyline, EverQuest will be the most comprehensive and versatile role playing game available on the Internet," said Jeffrey Fox, vice president, marketing, 989 Studios. "EverQuest’s evolving storyline puts the power of the character’s destiny and, ultimately, the Quest, where it belongs, in the player’s hands."
EverQuest features a multitude of adventures and quests with an interactive storyline that evolves according to a player's individual decisions. Players can choose to begin the game in any of the three continents, with more than 70 adventure zones and 12 cities. Each of these continents comes with its own diverse species, economic systems, alliances and politics. Throughout this adventure, players form groups, strategic alliances and gather magical artifacts in their perilous and exciting quest to conquer the world of Norrath.

On their journey, players have the opportunity to earn experience points, discover hidden treasures and acquire new skills. Over 60 unique skills and innate abilities per character allows the player to become the noblest of knights or the most vicious elf. For those who enjoy magic, there are more than 800 spells in five distinct magic classes to protect them as they explore the rich and vast world of dungeons, towers, crypts and evil abbeys throughout the game’s many dimensions.

Adventurers can explore the world of Norrath alone, but are encouraged to form alliances to acquire knowledge, solve puzzles and travel to strange, sometimes dangerous lands. Players can also interact with characters controlled by other gamers -- some good, some bad -- as well as computer controlled non-player characters.

Unlike other Internet role playing games, EverQuest allows players to view the 3D environment from a number of different camera angles, and is optimized to support the latest 3D graphic accelerator technology from 3Dfx Interactive‘ and Microsoft“ Direct 3D.
The Station@sony.com will be the official provider of EverQuest software updates and news about the game, message boards and other community features, as well as an easy-to-use billing system for EverQuest’s monthly subscription fee.

EverQuest Key Features

  • Supports more than 1,500 players simultaneously per server
  • Immerse yourself in beautiful picturesque 3D environments
  • Provides SVGA (640x480) 16-bit color scenery of dungeons, arctic tundra, vast deserts and even a completely underwater environment
  • Player killing (Pkilling) is available to those who wish to participate in this mode, while those who do not wish to participate in Pkilling are immune
  • Choose from 12 races and 14 classes, including dwarves, ogres, trolls, rangers, wizards and rogues
  • More than 60 unique skills and innate abilities per character
  • Collect thousands of magical items, including artifacts of great power
  • Learn hundreds of spells in five separate magic using classes
  • Optimized for the latest 3D graphic accelerator technology, EverQuest supports Voodoo Graphics‘ by 3Dfx Interactive‘ and Microsoft® Direct3D‘

 

EverQuest System Requirements

  • Operating System: Windows® 95
  • CPU: Intel Pentium® 166 required. Intel Pentium® 200+ recommended with a 3D graphics accelerator card
  • Memory: 32 MB RAM required
  • CD-ROM: 4X CD-ROM required, 8X recommended
  • Sound Card: 100 percent Sound Blaster compatible
  • DirectX: Supports Microsoft DirectX 6.0™ drivers
  • 3D Graphics Card: Supports Microsoft Direct3D™ and Voodoo Graphics‘ by 3Dfx Interactive‘
  • Modem: 28.8 Kpbs required, 33.6 Kpbs or higher recommended
  • Internet Play: Internet access required for internet play
