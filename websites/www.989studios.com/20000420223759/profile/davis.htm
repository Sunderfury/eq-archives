Terrell Davis

Team: Denver Broncos
Number: 30
Position: Running Back
Height: 5-11
Weight: 210
Age: 27
College: Georgia
Other: NFL GameDay™ Guru

In only four short years, Terrell Davis has elevated himself into the upper echelon of running backs in pro football history. Davis went to Georgia after transferring from Long Beach State. He was drafted by the Broncos 196th overall in the sixth round of the 1995 NFL draft. Terrell already ranks first on the Bronco’s all-time rushing yardage list (6,413), rushing touchdowns (56), total touchdowns (61) and career rushing average (4.8).

In 1995, Terrell Davis became the lowest drafted player to rush for 1,000 yards in his rookie season, doing so with his 1,117-yard performance in 1995. He was voted by his teammates as the club’s Offensive MVP and selected Rookie of the Year by Football Digest. In 1996, he led the AFC and was second overall in the NFL in both rushing yards and total yards from scrimmage. He also set franchise single-season records in rushing attempts, rushing yards and total yards. In 1997, he set a career and franchise highs with 1,750 yards rushing on 369 carries with 15 rushing touchdowns. Terrell was voted Super Bowl XXXII MVP while securing the Denver Broncos first World Championship. In 1998, Davis was voted the NFL’s Most Valuable Player by the Associated Press. He won his first NFL rushing title with 2,008 yards and 21 touchdowns and became the fourth player in league history to top the 2,000-yard rushing mark in a single season. With Terrell Davis leading the charge, the Denver Broncos defeated the Atlanta Falcons 34-19 in Super Bowl XXXIII and became only the fifth team in NFL history to repeat as Super Bowl Champions.

Terrell played a huge role in the development of NFL GameDay. He, along with 32 NFL players like Antonio Freeman and Chad Brown, designed 1,240 new plays. There are 200 new animations courtesy of motion capture from 13 players like Charles Woodson, Jimmy Smith and Jerome Bettis. He provided gameplay consultation and is featured on the package front of NFL GameDay for a second straight year.
