BUST A GROOVE‘ TURNS THE PLAYSTATION“ INTO A DANCE MACHINE
Japanese Videogame Hit Adds New Versatility to PlayStation Game Console’s Original Rhythm Music Genre

FOSTER CITY, Calif., November 25, 1998 – Living rooms around the country are being converted into dance clubs with 989 Studios’™ North America release of Bust A Groove‘ for the PlayStation“ game console. Already a videogame hit in Japan, Bust A Groove is now bringing pure dancing fun to the rest of the videogame world."With its overwhelming popularity, the rhythm and music game category has defined an incredible new growth area for the PlayStation, as well as a whole new group of PlayStation game enthusiasts," said Jeffrey Fox, vice president, marketing, 989 Studios. "Last year’s introduction of this genre set a new precedent in the industry and pushed the levels of creative and original product development to new highs. The Bust A Groove videogame is another step in this direction."

Developed by Enix Corporation, a game software developer based in Tokyo, Japan, Bust A Groove combines state-of-the-art technology with the best dancing action ever developed for a next-generation game system. In order to create the most realistic gameplay, dance moves were choreographed by professional dancers and incorporated into the game. Bust A Groove is the first dance videogame to use motion capture technology. Typically used for fighting or sports games, motion capture in music games creates a whole new level of gaming realism.

In Bust A Groove, players compete against one another in an all-out dance-off to determine who has the best dance moves. Players must rhythmically push buttons in sync with the beat of the game’s music, performing a variety of dance styles, such as, hip-hop, jazz, disco and more.

Also featured in the game is a One or Two-Player Mode; Practice Mode, which allows players to freely test their dancing and rhythm skills; and Dance Edit, where the player pre-sets dance moves. In addition, players can also choose from 10 very unique dancers. Bust A Groove’s special musical talent was contributed by Hatsumi Morinaga, a popular singer in Japan whose debut single just released and was named Japan’s biggest dance label.

Bust A Groove Key Features

  • One or two players
  • 10 different 3D polygon characters to choose from, each with their own unique dance styles, combos and attacks
  • Four hidden characters
  • One-Player Mode (player vs. computer)
  • Two-Player Mode (player vs. player)
  • Practice Mode (player freely inputs commands in sync with game music)
  • Dance Edit (player pre-sets the dance moves in advance)

989 Studios, a Sony Computer Entertainment Group Company, is a wholly-owned division of Sony Computer Entertainment America Inc. and is the third-largest PlayStation® software publisher in North America.
