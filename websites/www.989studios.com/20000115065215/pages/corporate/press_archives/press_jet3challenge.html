TEAM KAWASAKI JET SKI® CHAMPIONSHIP RACERS COMPETE
IN 989 STUDIOS’ JET MOTO® 3 VIDEOGAME CHALLENGE
– Chris MacClugage, Minoru Kanamori and Dustin Motzouris Battle for the
Championship Title at the Jet Moto 3 Videogame Challenge in Chicago, Illinois –

FOSTER CITY, Calif., July 29, 1999 – 989 Studios announced today that the Kawasaki championship personal watercraft racers will compete head-to-head on Jet Moto® 3, a hover bike racing videogame for the PlayStation® game console, available in stores in September. 989 Studios’ Jet Moto 3 Videogame Challenge will take place Saturday, July 31, in Chicago, a precursor to the final round of the 1999 International Jet Sports Boating Association (IJSBA) national tour, which begins the same day.
"We are very excited to have the opportunity to host the Jet Moto 3 Videogame Challenge with Kawasaki and its pro riders," said Jeffrey Fox, vice president, marketing, 989 Studios. "There’s no doubt that Kawasaki Pros have what it takes on a personal watercraft; but we’ll see if they have what it takes on a Jet Moto 3 jet hover bike. With Jet Moto 3’s advanced gameplay mechanics, these guys are definitely in for a daring new experience."

Jet Moto 3 will challenge the Kawasaki riders to complete a virtual race on blazing fast jet hover bikes. Rocketing through rigorous all-terrain racing tracks, the riders will compete to see who has the skill and stamina to take the Jet Moto 3 championship title.

"Kawasaki is thrilled to be a part of the Jet Moto 3 Videogame Challenge," said Steve Fischer, watercraft marketing manager, Kawasaki. "This is the perfect platform for our pro racers to showcase their talents and have fun."

In the game, gameplayers race across 19 obstacle-ridden environments and are tested by eleven challenging racing terrains: Lava, Grass, Rock, Sand, Dirt, Water, Metal, Wood, Ice, Snow and Crystal. Gameplayers can choose from 11 jet hover bikes with varied racing attributes -- acceleration, top speed and handling -- for optimized performance. MotoPhysics‘ technology allows gameplayers to feel every jolt as they race through treacherous caverns, subterranean graveyards, ancient cities and other action-packed game environments.

Kawasaki Motors Corp., U.S.A. (KMC) sells motorcycles, JET SKI® watercraft, all terrain vehicles (ATVs) and MULE™ utility vehicles through three separate product divisions. Kawasaki also markets small industrial engines, power products and hydraulic products. Kawasaki created the personal watercraft industry when it introduced the JET SKI to the marine market in 1973. Kawasaki’s headquarters in Irvine, Calif. include a 224,000-square-foot-facility, corporate offices, as well as a regional parts warehouse.

989 Studios, a Sony Computer Entertainment Group Company, is a wholly-owned division of Sony Computer Entertainment America Inc. and is the third-largest PlayStation® software publisher in North America. Headquartered in Foster City, California, with additional development studios in San Diego and Santa Monica, 989 Studios develops software for the PlayStation game console and online markets. 989 Studios is a recognized leader in the videogame industry, with top franchise titles such as NFL GameDay™, Twisted Metal®, Jet Moto® and Cool Boarders®.
