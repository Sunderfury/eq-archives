NHL FACEOFF™ ’99 LAUNCHES WITH ALL-NEW 3D GAME ENGINE
989 Sports Goes Into The Crease With New Hockey Videogame

FOSTER CITY, Calif., October 1, 1998 – 989 Sports announced today that NHL FaceOff™ ‘99, the all-new version of the popular PlayStation®hockey videogame series, is now available. NHL Face Off ‘99 delivers all the NHL teams and players, with hard-hitting, lightening-quick never-before-seen hockey action, which is sure to satisfy even the most discriminating hockey fans.

NHL FaceOff ‘99 boasts an all-new 3D game engine that uses an impressive polygonal graphic environment. With 25 percent more polygons per player and twice the amount of textures of last year’s edition, NHL FaceOff ‘99 features 3D players with real texture mapped faces that capture the true appearances of hundreds of NHL stars. NHL FaceOff ’99 is also the first hockey videogame to have motion capture animations on ice, featuring moves from St. Louis winger Scott Young and past San Jose goaltender Kelly Hrudey. Every subtle and personalized move has been incorporated into the game with amazing detail, as wingers blast vicious slap-shots, defensemen dish out brutal checks and goalies make flashy butterfly saves.

"No one re-creates NHL hockey like the NHL FaceOff ‘99 development team," said Jeffrey Fox, vice president, marketing, 989 Studios. "The realism that a 3D polygonal environment adds to gameplay is unparalleled. Fans of the NHL FaceOff series will find that the action of this year’s title is so quick and in-depth, they can’t blink or they’ll miss a great play."

Detailed arena models are created from actual blueprints with each arena’s unique charm brought to life. A new TV-style presentation incorporates dramatic camera "zoom-ins" and "cut-aways" as well as commentary from renowned New Jersey Devil play-by-play man Mike Emrick and ESPN announcer Darren Pang.

Players can choose from all 27 NHL teams, with updated rosters and the ability to take their squad right through an entire season culminating with the Stanley Cup Playoffs. The all-new Authentic Hockey Intelligence™ and enhanced Icon Passing‘ gives the game incredible realism and control, as players think and react just like they do in the NHL. Players in NHL FaceOff `99 turn their head to receive a pass, lose and regain their balance, perform bone-crushing checks, powerful slap shots, and, fans even throw their hats on the ice to salute hat tricks.

NHL FaceOff ‘99 is a 989 Sports production, developed by Killer Game.

Realistic goalie moves including glove, stick, blocker, kick, butterfly and stack-the-pads saves
