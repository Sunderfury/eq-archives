COOL BOARDERS 3 offers gamers the opportunity to compete in six different events on 34 diverse, 3D courses. Knowing what to expect from each event and the type of board to use is very important to your success in COOL BOARDERS 3. Below is a brief description of each event and the type of board recommended to use.

Board type to use: Alpine
Boarder X is a race against the clock so your only concern is getting down the mountain as fast as possible. Whenever you have the chance tuck yourself in the crouch position (D-Pad Down) for maximum speed. Establish the early lead out the gate by punching your opponent (L1,R1) as you race. Make certain that you make it through every gate otherwise you will be penalized one second for every missed gate. Those missed gates can mean the difference between first and last place.

Board type to use: Alpine
The Slalom run will definitely test your maneuverability skills in COOL BOARDERS 3. The objective in the slalom event is to weave left and right past each flag of your color making sure you pass on the pointy side of your flag. Failure to pass on the pointy side will result in a one second penalty for each flag missed. Be sure to take advantage of the hard carve (Square Button) when trying to shred through the slalom run.

Board type to use: Alpine
For all you gamers who love the element of speed here is your chance to barrel down the mountain at lightning fast speeds. Take advantage of the tuck position (D-Pad Down) for the highest maximum speed. The downhill is also where you can pull off some serious sick combinations due to the high element of speed. Time your jump with the power meter and bust into some killer trick "combos."

Board type to use: Freestyle
Your skills at pulling off and sticking "combos" will determine how successful you are in the halfpipe competition. Pulling off grabs and flips while rotating will pump up your point total in a hurry. Avoid getting caught doing doubles and triples of the same grab. Displaying a variety of grabs and flips is where the big points wait. Timing your jump in accordance with the power meter will make or break you in the halfpipe.

Board type to use: Freeride
The slopestyle courses are loaded with different jumps, rails, picnic tables, buses and cars that give you the opportunity to get crazy and have some fun. Experiment with the different courses and find out what you can jump without wiping out. If you are going down a rail use your L2 or R2 buttons to move around while on the rail. At the end of a railslide try and stick a fat trick for some extra bonus points.

Board type to use: Freeride
The Big Air event is your one shining moment to pull off as many tricks and "combos" in one jump.
