NCAA GAMEBREAKER 2000 TACKLES PLAYSTATION COLLEGE FOOTBALL COMPETITION
-- 989 Sports’ Popular College Football Videogame Franchise Has Exclusive Features Such As Keith Jackson Play-By-Play And The Ability ToDraft Players Into The NFL --

FOSTER CITY, Calif., May 13, 1999 – 989 Sports™ announced today that NCAA® GameBreaker™ 2000, designed and developed by the makers of NFL GameDay™, will return to the PlayStation® game console this September. NCAA GameBreaker 2000 boasts exclusive features such as play-by-play from college football broadcasting legend Keith Jackson and the ability to draft players into the NFL.

Those who will miss the insightful play-calling of recently-retired legendary college football announcer Keith Jackson need not worry. Jackson’s voice can still be found calling the play-by-play for NCAA GameBreaker 2000. And, for the first time in a 989 Sports videogame, gamers will be able to save senior players from an NCAA GameBreaker 2000 season and draft them by downloading into NFL GameDay™ 2000 -- 989 Sports’ best-selling pro football videogame.

"NCAA GameBreaker 2000 has exclusive features that brings the realism of college football directly into the hands of the videogamer," said Jeffrey Fox, vice president, marketing, 989 Studios. "No other college football videogame provides the realism, emotion and pageantry like NCAA GameBreaker 2000. Taking the outstanding commentary of Keith Jackson combined with the ability to draft college players into the NFL and have them continue playing into the next season, NCAA GameBreaker 2000 rises above the competition."

NCAA GameBreaker 2000 will also feature entirely new animations, including: ball carriers stumbling and extending for the first down, facemask-grabbing, players strapping on chin straps and players pounding teammates’ shoulder pads. NCAA GameBreaker 2000 also features all-new gameplay modes like "Career Mode," which enables the gamer to assume the duties of a head coach or offensive and defensive coordinator in an attempt to guide their team to the National Championship.

Gamers can choose from all 114 NCAA Division I-A teams and battle for the National Championship while competing against conference foes. A fast and fluid style of gameplay is the backbone of NCAA GameBreaker 2000, including an extensive playbook with plays designed by actual college coaches. The new "Play Editor" feature enables users to easily create and customize their own playbook by redesigning existing plays.

NCAA GameBreaker 2000 has all the features gamers have grown to love. "Blue Chip Recruiting" returns as a key feature, enabling users to heavily recruit at both the high school and junior college level. Total Control Passing‘ allows gamers to under-throw or overthrow receivers in any direction and the "Pummel Button" lets gamers deliver hard-hitting tackles. Exclusively in NCAA GameBreaker 2000, gamers can compete for the Heisman Trophy.

NCAA GameBreaker 2000 will be available in September and features former UCLA Bruins QB Cade Mcnown on the package.

NCAA GameBreaker 2000 Key Features:

  • All 114 NCAA Division I-A teams and stadiums, plus more than 50 classic teams from the past
  • New "Career Mode" enables the user to assume the duties of a head coach or offensive and defensive coordinator in leading a run at the National Championship
  • TV-style presentation with legendary college football announcer Keith Jackson
  • Updated "Play Editor" feature enables users to create and customize their own playbook
  • NFL GameDay 2000 compatible -- users will be able to save senior players from an NCAA GameBreaker 2000 season and download them into NFL GameDay 2000
  • All-new gameplay animations include players stumbling and extending for the first down, grabbing facemasks, cut blocks, strapping on chin straps and pounding one another’s shoulder pads
  • All 3D polygonal players scaled to actual height and weight
  • "Blue Chip Recruiting" feature allows gamers to sign top recruits and build powerhouse teams
  • Includes 21 different bowl games and lets players compete for the Heisman Trophy
  • Utilizes motion capture animation of nine former college standouts and current NFL Pros including Jerome Bettis, Tim Brown, Chad Brown, Mike Alstott, Jason Sehorn, Hardy Nickerson, Ryan Leaf, Christian Fauria and Lamont Warren
  • Authentic college play formations designed by college coaches
  • True-to-life "GameBreakers" can change the outcome of a game every time they touch the ball
  • Supports the Dual Shock™ Analog Controller for the ultra-realistic experience
  • Total Control Passing allows players to overthrow or under-throw receivers, depending upon coverage
  • The "Pummel Button" enables players to mete out particularly vicious hits
  • Wind, snow and rain all affect gameplay, while uniforms show mud and grass stains depending on field conditions
  • Tracking of game and full season statistics in every major category
