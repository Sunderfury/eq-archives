KEITH JACKSON CONTINUES TO ANNOUNCE COLLEGE FOOTBALL
-- Legendary Voice of College Football Lives on in 989 Sports’ NCAA® GameBreaker™ Football Videogame Series --

FOSTER CITY, Calif., January 6, 1999 – 989 Sports announced today that Keith Jackson, the legendary college football announcer, will be returning to the microphone to call the play-by-play for NCAA® GameBreaker™ 2000 -- the latest version of the popular college football videogame for the PlayStation® game console -- available in stores Fall 1999. Jackson made his debut as a 989 Sports announcer in the top selling NCAA® GameBreaker™ ’99, which is currently available in stores."Although we’ll all miss Keith Jackson on Saturdays, college football fans can still hear his legendary voice for years to come calling the plays in the NCAA GameBreaker series," said Jeffrey Fox, vice president, marketing, 989 Studios. "The combination of Jackson’s distinctive commentary and 989 Sports’ unbeatable graphics and gameplay, makes the NCAA GameBreaker series the only choice for real college football videogame fans."

With all 112 NCAA Division I-A schools and 140 stadiums, NCAA GameBreaker ’99 features 3D player and stadium models that generate incredible polygonal graphics and ultra realistic gameplay. Players are scaled to their actual height and weight, with motion capture animation utilizing nine former college standouts, including USC grad Jason Sehorn and Purdue star player Mike Alstott.

More than 2,200 NCAA plays were designed by college coaches and incorporated into NCAA GameBreaker ‘99. Life-like animations include receivers turning their heads to catch a pass, QB’s throwing off their back foot and tons of jarring player tackles. Uniforms actually display dirt, mud and grass stains depending on conditions.

989 Sports is the sports software brand of 989 Studios. Known for its top-selling sports franchise titles such as NFL GameDay™, NFL Xtreme™, NCAA GameBreaker™, MLB, NBA ShootOut and
NHL FaceOff™, 989 Sports is committed to developing authentic and innovative sports games to appeal to both the professional athlete and sports videogame fan.
