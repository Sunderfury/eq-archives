• Use Total Control Batting™ for more power and accuracy when hitting.
  • When you "lock-on" to a pitch, you can hit the ball by to right field by pressing the D-Pad to the right and hitting the ball by pressing "X".
  • When you "lock-on" to a pitch, you can hit the ball by to left field by pressing the D-Pad to the left and hitting the ball by pressing "X".
  • When you "lock-on" to a pitch, you can hit the ball by to center field by pressing the D-Pad down and hitting the ball by pressing "X".
  • When you "lock-on" to a pitch, you can hit the ball by in the air by pressing the D-Pad up and hitting the ball by pressing "X".
  • When you "lock-on," if you hold the D-pad down you will nearly always get a hit up the middle.
  • When you "lock-on" to a pitch, quickly press "square" in order to swing with power.
  • When you have selected the right pitch only the batting cursor gets larger.
  • Use the pitching history to get an idea of what the pitcher will throw next, remember he does not want to walk you.
  • If you are in desperate need of a base runner try crowding the plate by moving your batter as close to the plate as possible by holding R2 and the D-pad to take one for the team.
  • When bunting, always angle your bat down the lines by holding down "O" and the D-pad Left for the 3rd base line or D-pad Right for the 1st base line. Keeping the ball as far away from the pitcher and catcher as possible is key to reaching first safely.

    • You have the best chance of stealing a base with your leadoff hitter.
    • You have a better chance of stealing a base on an off-speed pitch.
    • You have a better chance of stealing a base if you lead off by pressing the L1 button.
    • Be sure to freeze your runners on lie drives by pressing the R2 button.
    • With less than 2-outs, be sure to send your players back immediately on fly balls in order to tag-up by pressing the "O" button.
    • With 2 outs make sure to send your runners on any ball hit to give them the best chance to score on a gapper or an error.
    • When running, continually press the R1 button for speed bursts.
    • Take advantage of deep fly balls by tagging up with your lead runner and advancing him by pressing "triangle" and the D-pad.
    • To Hit & Run, press "triangle" when the pitcher starts his motion and hit the ball to right field.
    • To perform the Squeeze Play, with a runner on third and less then 2 outs, press "triangle when the pitcher is in his motion and have your batter bunt.

    • Use Total Control Fielding™ in order to increase your range and make spectacular plays.
    • When chasing a ball, continually press the "O" button for speed bursts
    • In order to rob a home run, get to the wall before the ball then time your jump just right and press "Triangle" to leap up over the wall and make the catch.
    • When playing a ball deep in the outfield throw to your cut-off man by holding down R1 and pressing any throw button in order to have a better chance of throwing out an advancing runner.
    • With a runner on first, be sure to adjust your infield to Double-play depth by pressing R1
    • In a close game in the later innings with a runner on first, be sure to adjust your infield to bunt defense by pressing R1
    • In a close game with the bases loaded adjust your infield to bring your corners-in by pressing R1. This way the corners can cut the run off at the plate.
    • In a close game in the late innings with a runner on third adjust your infield-in to cut the run off at the plate.
    • Make sure to adjust the infield for double plays and bunt situations by pressing the R1 button.
    • Make sure to adjust the outfield deep for power hitters by pressing the R2 button.
    • Make sure to adjust the outfield in for hitters with little or no power by pressing the R2 button.

    • As in the show, you need to change your pitch type, location and speed constantly in order to be successful.
    • You do not need to throw every pitch in the strike zone in order to get hitters out.
    • In order to save the pitcher’s energy do not throw every pitch hard. Just tap the "X" button occasionally to throw a pitch slower.
    • With speedy runners on base make pick-off attempts by pressing "O" for first, "triangle" for second, or "square" for third in order to keep your opponent honest.
    • To throw a pitch harder, hold down the "X" button longer.
    • In order to throw a pitch slower, tap the "X" button.
    • A great combination of pitches is a hard fastball up and in and then a slow curve ball down and away.
    • A great combination of pitches is a medium fastball on the outside corner followed by a hard slider on the outside corner.

    • Select hitters before pitchers.
    • Select power hitters first and then average hitters.
    • Don’t forget to get a quality player at each position

    • It is harder to gain points as a catcher or pitcher than it is as a position player.
    • The easiest position to gain ability points with is shortstop because you get so many plays.
    • Put yourself in the middle of the line-up in order to have a better chance to drive in RBIs
    • Increase your batting average ability points as much as possible in order to increase your chance of hitting the ball.

    • Keep on eye on your pitcher’s stamina and replace him before he has nothing left.
      When replacing your pitcher try to bring in a Lefty to face the Left handed hitters and a Righty to face the Right handed hitters.
    • When your opponent changes pitchers consider pinch hitting a Lefty to face a Right handed pitcher and a Righty to face a Left handed pitcher.
