Perspective

Patches are a crap shoot. No matter how much you plan, no matter how carefully you test your build, when deploying a major update to an MMO across several rooms full of servers, there is a reasonable possibility that bad shit can happen.

Back when I was the community manager on EQ2, stuff went wrong more than once. When it did and servers stayed down longer than originally announced, anxious players turned to the forums to vent their frustration. When we were on the ball and kept subscribers informed ofÂ the latest news, most were understanding and refrained from making a big deal out of it. No matter how diligent we were, of course, some were determined to raise hell and let the universe know how incompetent the company was and how much they deserved to be compensated for their time. When you combine abject frustration with the anonymity of the Internet, things can get pretty crazy, and there were times I wanted to run screaming to the hills.

But what I went through is nothing compared to the WoW forums during unexpected downtime. Holy crap. I mean, really. It’s enough to make you go eat bees or something. Some of the posts are just mind-boggling.

What occurred to me tonight, as I sat waiting for Shu’halo to come back up, was that if dreams I’ve never dared dream come true and the MMO I’m currently working on ends up with millions of subscribers, we will inevitably have some unplanned downtime. And we will have forums. And there will be ruthless threads started by inconsolable masses, lashing out with all the venom they can muster for being deprived of the game they want to play.

And if I’m that fortunate, I’ll be thankful for every single one of them.
