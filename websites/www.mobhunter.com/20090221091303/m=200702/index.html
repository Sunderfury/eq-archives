I Love You, I Fear You

Way back when I was just a little Moorgard, one of the games that kept my Atari 800 busy was a gem called Archon. My friend Kirk and I spent countless hours battling each other (who says I don’t like PvP?). It was one of those simple, compelling, cleverÂ games that entertained us endlessly — the kind Electronic Arts used to make long before they became a sports-franchise juggernaut. “Power points are proof against magic. Spell is cancelled, choose another.”

Archon II came out later and wasn’t nearly as fun. I eventually moved on to other brands of computers and other types of games, but I’ve always had a soft spot in my heart for the original Archon.

Thus it is with both hope and dread that I learned Archon is coming back. I’m excited at the chance to play an old favorite again, but I usually end up hating modernized versions of classic games. In the attempt to improve upon the original, something often ends up being lost. I’ll keep my fingers crossed, though, since Jon Freeman is working on it. As long as they include the untampered original in some way, I’ll be happy. A PSP version that lets you go head to head wirelessly would be amazing.

Anyone else as old as I am want to talk about classic 8-bit computer games?

A Multi-Console Household

The entertainment center got more interesting this week as Shwayder’s Wii is now perched just a few feet from my PS3. Given the enormous size of my unit (double entendre intentional), it’s a wonder that my PS3 doesn’t devour the petite Wii. The only thing stopping it from happening is the presence of a tiny PS2 between them. The PS3 wouldn’t eat a PS2; that would be incestuous.

The Wii is a hell of a lot of fun, though. The sports title is the clear winner around the house, though the boxing game makes me long for a version of Fight Night that uses the Wiimote. Bowling is great, and doesn’t leave your fingers all gross… assuming you’ve wiped down the controller after Shwayder last used it, of course.

We’ve also got Zelda, but Shwayder boycotted it due to his frustration at being unable to figure out that you’re supposed to send the bird to attack the monkey so it gives up the basket. Yeah, I know how that sounds.

My PS3 is patiently waiting for a time when it can play original games that are as fun as what’s currently on the Wii (come on, MotorStorm!). Until then, it’s going to be watching the Wii… hungrily.
