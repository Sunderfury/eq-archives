Wet Wet Wet

This afternoon my plane left a wet and dreary Minneapolis only to arrive in a wet and dreary San Diego. Thus ends my vacation.

The cold is better, but not gone. I’m really sick of coughing, lemme tell ya.

I will say that it felt good to avoid my work email and (for the most part) the forums. Tomorrow I will have thousands of messages to go through as I catch up on all the wonder that has taken place in my absence.

Once more unto the breach, dear friends.

A Lovely Vacation

So a few days after arriving in Minnesota, I head back to my hometown to visit my mom. I don’t get to see her that often, so it’s nice to stay with her a while.

I woke up the morning after arriving at her place with a sensation of pressure in my lungs. You know, the feeling that a cough is on the way and there’s nothing you can do to stop it. Well, the cough ended up being only part of my problem. It exploded into a full-on raging cold with coughing, stuffy head, fever, and general achiness. In the Sun

I have escaped the eternal sun of San Diego for a week or so of much-needed rest. It’s the final phase of decompression after the long, crunchy stretch that comes before an expansion launches, which serves to prepare us to ramp up for the next one. It’s an eternal cycle that makes working on an MMO so unique and challenging.

So where have I gone to flee reality? Good old Minnesota, of course. As if on cue, cold weather has greeted me like a slap in the face for betraying my home state by moving to southern California. It’s not as bad as the testicle-challenging cold I reported at Christmas, but it’s bracing nonetheless. Youth Culture Killed My Dog

Tuesday is a ritual. We eat lunch at Panera, then head across the street to Best Buy to check out the new release table. Even when there isn’t much new, it’s still fun to see what has come in. My old friend Daryl, who brought me into the music business when I not much more than a boy, always liked to work on Tuesday. As long as there was something worthwhile put out, he said, the job was worth it. Inevitably, though, Tuesdays stopped being fun (hey, only so many Alison Krauss records can come out in a given year), so he moved on to teaching, or some shit.

Anyhow, today was a pretty good release day for DVDs. The big titles were Good Night, and Good Luck, which I bought because I saw it in the theaters and loved it. A History of Violence came out as well, and although I enjoyed it a lot and would gladly watch it again, I didn’t make the purchase. I have to wonder sometimes, though, why I bother buying DVDs at all. Continue Reading »
