Sun, 27 Dec 2015 21:33:40 +0000 hourly 1 http://wordpress.org/?v=4.1 Comment on The Blog Awakens by Wendy http://www.mobhunter.com/?p=906&cpage=1#comment-1085330 Sun, 27 Dec 2015 21:33:40 +0000 http://www.mobhunter.com/?p=906#comment-1085330 I think the problem is that we assume this movie is for us first and foremost, instead of being a welcoming for new fans; a place for them to jump in. And we forget that we’re not looking at this movie through our young, naive eyes anymore. When it was first released, there was nothing like it (visually — there was everything like it storywise). TFA now is a reflection of the society we live in where 140 characters tells a story. There are so many ridiculous plot holes in this movie. So many astounding leaps required of the audience’s suspension of disbelief. But in the moment, it works. In the moment, when I was sitting with my 7yo daughter on my lap and when she came out of the theatre and told me how much she loved Rey and Poe, I was very, very happy to be able to — not pass this torch — but share it with her.

More practically speaking, Star Wars was initially Lucas fanboying over the stories he loved, and then it became him selling toys. It’s no different now, though more hands are in the pot, grabbing at that cash. I’d like to believe that even though money is the top priority, those involved in the resurgence of this legacy have put their heart and soul into it because they love Star Wars too.

That said, I am cautiously optimistic about where things will go for the future movies now that everything has been established. I hope it does slow down a bit to tell a proper story that doesn’t rely on convenient coincidental plot devices and bigger explosions. Abrams sucked in the newcomers and established fans with all sorts of questions to ponder. I don’t want the next movies to fill in all the blanks as the prequel tried to, but I do hope for more depth and meaning in those blanks.
