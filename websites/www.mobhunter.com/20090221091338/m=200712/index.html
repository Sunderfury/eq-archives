“That Is Why You Fail.”

Stolen from Lum’s commentary without wanting to get tangled up in an epeen battle, the following quote fromÂ  Auran’s CEO on the future of FURY speaks volumes:

“The future will focus on a smaller, more agile core team of Fury developers. These are people who are incredibly passionate about the game and work until 4 in the morning to ensure they get things done.”

Burning out your dev team seldom leads to positive results. While it’s all fine and dandy to envision dedicated staffers pouring their hearts into your game 16 hours a day, the fact is that all you’ll end up with is unhappy people incapable of doing anything but mediocre work to meet your insane scheduling demands. It’s a sure recipe for failure.

Buzzkill

Over on Gamasutra, Richard Garriott’s Tabula Rasa’s Richard Garriott (see what I did there?) gave an interview that touches on what was done right and wrong in the marketing of the game:

“I actually think the biggest mistake was made not by the marketing department, but by the development team. We invited too many people into the beta when the game was still too broken.”

I’ve discussed the change in perception about what beta means before, and Garriott’s interview illustrates my points nicely. Clearly the marketing imperative to get the product name out in front of the public trumped the dev team’s imperative to make a fun game. A common tale, I fear.
