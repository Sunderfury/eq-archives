Sacred Cows

A bit of a nerd fight erupted in the blogosphere recently as a Richard Bartle interview on Massively caused eyebrows to crinkle, and when some folks raised questions about Bartle’s current relevance, such discussion was met with rebuttal or backlash.

What this boils down to is that you have the Old Guard protecting their sacred cows, and the New Guard (many of whom have World of Warcraft as their primary or sole point of reference) questioning their elders. This pattern should sound familiar, because it happens in every generational shift around pretty much every art form. Congratulations to the MMO industry for finally being old enough to have a generational gap!

The Old Guard must come to terms with the fact that this is the natural order of things, and they need to stop getting pissy about it. Youth questions its elders; that’s how progress happens. People should stop feeling like they need to pounce on anyone who dares question Bartle’s position in history, for as he himself noted in a response to this mess, he’s relevant for as long as people think he is and are willing to pay him for his input. That seems fair to me.

Was There Ever Any Doubt That It Would Come to This?

It took only hours for the Spore creature creator to be used to make very unsafe for work, very disturbing porn-like images known as “Sporn.”

I have to believe that Will Wright knew this would happen. After all, everyone knows that porn drives all things on the Internet. But I wonder if he realized how quickly we’d see giant penises shooting purple clouds or multi-legged creatures seemingly self-fertilizing themselves.

If someone figures out a way to get the Sporn–I mean Spore creatures–into Second Life, the universe just might collapse upon itself. At least, we can all hope so.

Talkin’ Loud And Sayin’ Nothing

We interrupt this vacation/holiday/general malaise update blackout to bring you a link to an interview that Jason Roberts and I gave to Linda “Brasse” Carlson at the ION Conference in Seattle a few weeks back. As you’ll see, I have honed my community management ability to say lots of words without really giving anything away.

We’ve gotten a favorable response to the interview, despite the fact that it gives few specifics about what we at 38 Studios are up to. Actually, if someone went to the trouble of stitching together a few things we’ve said across various interviews, that person would have enough information to make a pretty good guess about what Copernicus will be. Of course, we’ve also been vague enough that a number of hypotheses could be made, so I’m not about to connect the dots for anyone.

Unlike the delight I took in torturing detail-starved fans before EQ2 launched, the reason we’re being so tight-lipped is not to be smarmy or cagey. We just honestly want to show people what we’re doing rather than talk about what we plan to do. So as soon as we can do that in a meaningful and concrete way, we will. Until then, you’ll just have to shake your fist and call us dirty names.

You know, like you do already.
