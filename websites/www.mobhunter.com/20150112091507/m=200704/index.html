You’re All Swimming in My Wake

CNBC broadcast a feature segment on my life and career, touching on 38 Studios and the people around me. It was a very moving piece that really captured my human side, I thought. Yes, I too can cry.

Of course it may be hard to absorb all the nuances of my character in the .2 seconds that the camera is panning across me as baseball blowhard Curt Schilling hogs all the interviewer’s time and superhunk Brett Close strikes his patented pose. Even so, I think it’s pretty obvious that I dominate every scene I’m in.

I Am a Patriot

Today is Patriots Day. What the hell is that? Many of my transplanted coworkers (read: pretty much all of us) asked the very same thing when we first heard of it. It’s a mostly local holiday celebrating events from the Revolutionary War. There are parades, battle re-encactments, a home game from the Sox, and the running of the Boston Maraton.

Many local businesses are closed, including 38 Studios (although I’m writing this from the office… don’t ask). You can also thank Patriots Day for your taxes being due a day later this year, because the processing office is located in Massachusetts and thus all the employees get the day off. I thought they might call off the holiday since Captain America was murdered, but it seems to be taking place nonetheless.

I imagine most celebrations of Patriots Day are pleasant spring affairs, but this year we have downpours of rain that are causing flooding in some areas. The marathon was still run and the Sox are still playing, so sports fans should be happy.

Okay, time for me to salvage the rest of this holiday and get out of here. I should be out enjoying the fact that Curt Schilling has to pay me for sitting on my ass somewhere other than the office.

Min Spec Is the Only Spec

Historically this has been quite accurate, and along with this rising number of transistors came a corresponding increase in system performance. Each generation of personal computers got significantly more powerful than the last, and Intel CPUs became the flagship for Moore’s prophecy fulfilled.

The Law itself is still holding up pretty well. Companies like Intel and AMD keep finding ways to cram moreÂ transistors onto chips, thanks to piling up multiple cores and such. However, recently the corollary of Moore’s Law, which had been nearly as solid, has begun to break down. While the performance of the latest generations of CPUs has been improving, the practical performance increase hasn’t been as startling as in the past.
