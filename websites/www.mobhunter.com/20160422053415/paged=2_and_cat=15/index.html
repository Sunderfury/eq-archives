The Olympics Are Like Crack

It’s not like I was looking forward to the Olympics. As a kid who never had much interest in televised sports, the Olympics made me a little bitter because they meant that my usual TV shows weren’t on. While I don’t bear that grudge anymore, I have never really developed a fondness for the games.

But I’ll be damned if the Olympics aren’t impossible to turn off. Part of it must be the drama, part the incredible record-shattering physical feats. Whatever it is, you gotta give NBC credit for some really well-paced coverage.

People blame MMOs for causing addiction, but what about the Olympics? I’m not getting anything done because I’m too busy wondering if Phelps is going to sweep. There ought to be laws regulating this stuff!

Going Home Again Is Deadly

Often as I eat my lunch I will jump into a battleground in WoW or run a quest or two. Since the servers are usually down on Tuesdays during my lunch hour here on the east coast, I decided it was an excellent opportunity to enjoy a bit of nostalgia and log into EverQuest. After all, it is the ninth anniversary of the MMO I have loved the most, so I’ve been kind of itching to jump in and take a look around.

I dusted off Sensei Moorgard Ghostfist and proceeded to find my way from Crescent Reach (how the hell did I end up there?) to take my first tour of the redone Highpass Hold, a place I spent a hell of a lot of time back in the day. The sensei entered Highkeep, where he did his duty and cleared out some nasty goblins from the basement. Seeing as how he spent nearly all of levels 19 to 30 in that place, he feels a certain obligation to its inhabitents–especially since there was no one else in the zone to battle the nasties.

My only real difficulty, aside from the horrible map window, was relearning EQ’s archaic control scheme. I swear to you, it’s maddening. Usually the controls make me insane after a few minutes and I log out in a huff, wondering how a modern gamer puts up with them. But by this time I had an audience of coworkers looking on, including our Creator of Worlds, R.A. Salvatore. We chatted about old times, and I amassed a mighty train through Kithicor Forest as I went to have a look around the Commonlands. I stopped to pay my respects to Kizdean Gix, pulverizing him in short order (burn in hell, you bastard!). Then I spotted a hill giant near the toll gate and decided to administer a critical beatdown upon his oversized noggin.

I happened to notice that a couple aggro orcs were chasing me. No big deal; I would handle them after the giant met his maker.

Instead, I glanced up at my health bar to find it receding rapidly. What the hell, orcs in the Commonlands were kicking my butt? I am Sensei Moorgard Ghostfist, holder of the Celestial Fists, defiler of planes, and future Avatar of Tranquility! This is madness!

All of a sudden I was experiencing my first naked corpse run in untold years. And, like all retired monks, I was bound in Plane of Tranquility. Unf.

Okay, take the book to PoK, then use the Freeport stone, and run to my corpse from the other direction. I could handle that. When I realized that the map didn’t show my corpse, I was again aghast at how spoiled I’ve become. I ran to the general area of the toll booth and started looking.

Orcs. More of them. Welcome to corpse run number two.

The first corpse run was nostalgic–funny, even–but by this point I was just pissed. Why the hell were there blue orcs in the Commonlands who could kill my level 65 monk? So I did a search and found out. Leave it to me to get nostalgic in the midst of some kind of zone event.

This time I was successful, snagging my equipment (how can it take so long to re-equip my gear?) and the naked corpse. Suitably humbled, the noble sensei logged off atop the Commonlands wizard tower.

The moral of the story? You can go home again. But if you do, a gang of overpowered orcs is likely to hand you your ass.

Happy birthday, EverQuest!
