Reflections

I was working as a proofreader at an advertising agency. It started not long after I’d gotten in that day. I overheard someone in the next row of cubes asking someone else if they’d heard about a small plane crashing into the World Trade Center. I asked my buddy John what was going on. He was in the process of trying to look it up online, but the Internet had slowed to a crawl. Initial reports were confused, disjointed, but soon we learned it wasn’t just a small plane.

There was an exercise room downstairs with a TV, so I headed down to see what was going on. About a half dozen others were already there, and more filed in over time. It all seemed so surreal. Once in a while somebody wouldÂ turn towardÂ another person with a look that was part shock, part horror, but mostly disbelief. We saw the plane crash into the second tower, then later watched as the buildings collapsed. It was a long day, and it was hard to find any words that could fill it. So we watched and listened and cried.

Life was different after that. We were all different after that.

Now it’s five years later. A lot has changed, but not enough. The world is still full of hate that doesn’t seem likely to go away. But that doesn’t mean we stop trying to make things better. Nor should we forget those who were lost.
