Part 3 – The Arrival

I can finallyÂ write the last part of my “moving across the nation” post.

In what will be a surprise to few of you, I am now living in the Boston area where I work for Green Monster Games. I waited until today to talk about it because a press release was planned to announce the hiring of a bunch of us.

This is a very exciting move for me and one I did not make lightly. I really loved my work on EQ2 and had many great friends at SOE, but GMG is an incredible opportunity. It came up rather suddenly, but sometimes there are chances in life that you just know are right. This is one of those, and I’m fortunate it came my way. The level of talent here is unbelievable, with more coming on board in the months ahead.

I’m not going to write a lot about the company on this site, because part of my job is going to be helping folks get more familiar with us over the course of our development. There has been no shortage of rumors and assumptions about GMG, especially considering the three big-name guys involved in the project. What I will say is that yes, we are a real game studio, not just a bunch of rich guys working on a vanity project. If we didn’t have some extremely experienced team members with a strong direction and drive, this isn’t something I would have become involved with.

It’s going to be an amazing next few years. In the meantime, I will need to adapt to the New England lifestyle. Thankfully, Curt assured me that it doesn’t really snow here and the coldest day during winter is about 50 degrees. I know I’m in good hands.

Anatomy of a Move, Part 2

Though it’s nice to relax at home in Minnesota, there are always a lot of things I want to get done when I’m there and I never seem to get to them all (such as making Kendricke buy me drinks). Still, it was a very nice visit and it was good to have a break after many overtime hours in my last months at SOE.

Step 5: Minneapolis to Holt, MI (Wednesday). Longer drive than anticipated due partly to road construction in Wisconsin and getting stuck in Chicago traffic. Bad traffic. That’ll teach me never to take the “scenic” route downtown again, though getting a perspective on the size of the Sears Tower was very cool. I stopped in Holt to visit with my nephew and his wife. Road Rule #463: Never turn down a chance to mooch off relatives.

Biggest road pet peeve: Truck drivers who pass other semi trailers doing 1 mph more than the truck they’re passing. These guys seem oblivious to the fact that they are holding up motorists who want to drive (e.g. speed) at a reasonable rate. Nine times out of ten, they will make you brake and wait the two minutes it takes them to pass the other truck instead of braking themselves and letting you quickly pass them first. Truck drivers piss me off.

One more stretch of road ahead of me tomorrow. w00t!!!!
