Lake Mead
October 10, 1998 through October 18, 1998

We traveled to Burrow Bay (fondly renamed Burrow Boy) on the second night. Again the weather was clear and the swimming nice, though a bit chilly.

We sailed out of Burrow Boy easily the next morning, the winds were just right and we decided we didn't need the motor. We traveled east and north, back up to Cathederal Cove for the third night. There were a few mosquitoes but it was not bad at all.

On Thursday night we camped in another sheltered bay, the name escapes me now. We were rocked and rained on, the south tip of the storm which threw six inches of snow on Cedar City hit us. But not much. It left us the next morning with gusty winds up to 35mph. We tried to sail out of the bay, but the winds were flaky and we ended up motoring back into the heart of the lake. We considered reefing the main, as the winds were high and so were the waves. We decided instead to sail on only the 35% jib. It was a good choice, for we were reaching 6 knots on only the 35%. It was a great ride, the waves were reaching 5 feet high, but the sky was clear and sunny, and although we were chilled by the wind we had a wild ride. Unfortunately one of the crew caught some sea sickness and spent the day recovering. However, it was a fun ride and we'd do it all over again!
