San Francisco Bay
March 13, 1999 through March 20, 1999

Vessel:
Grey Ghost - Ranger 22

[Intro | 1 | 2 | 3 ]

On March 13, 1999, some friends and I hitched the trailered Grey Ghost from the Great Salt Lake South Shore Marina and drove towards San Francisco. The above map shows where we traveled.

We slept in Sacramento on Saturday night, and arrived at Ballena Bay Marina (#2) on Sunday morning. We were going to launch the boat that day, but we were advised against doing so because it was low tide and the depth at the launch ramp was only about 3 feet, much too shallow for our 4.5 foot draft. So we toured the town instead, and slept at a local hotel Sunday night.

On Monday morning we pulled the boat to the Grant Street Public Launch ramp (#1) and launched the Grey Ghost onto the San Francisco Bay Estuary. The weather was quite nice, and after the boat was packed, and the mast was up it was so warm we took off some clothes. We motored away from the launch ramp and put up the sails. It was pretty narrow in the estuary but we wanted to have some fun anyway. Shortly after a large tug pulled by us our depth meter shot from 35 feet to 2 feet, and we were nervous. However, we realized the wakes of some of the large vessels in the Bay screwed up our depth meter.

We sailed the Grey Ghost north and west through the estuary and south towards Ballena Bay Marina (#2). There were military maneuvers being conducted that day and the coast guard stopped us twice to tell us to sail west around the large aircraft carrier and destroyers instead of going between them and the east shore. The waves were about 4 feet high and the wind was strong, with %35 jib and reefed main we made 6 knots to the Ballena Bay Marina. We spent the night on the boat.

The next day, Tuesday, we toured San Francisco and went shopping around Alameda.

On Wednesday Dave Oswald joined our crew. He took a ferry from San Francisco (he was there on business) and we picked him up. We then sailed from the Ballena Bay Marina (#2) up to Pier 39 at Fisherman's Warf (#3). We did some more sight seeing of San Francisco and Fisherman's warf, ate in a restaurant in China Town (The Golden Dragon) and then wandered back to the boat to sleep.

The next day, Thursday, we sailed from Pier 39 to Angel Island, passing Alcatraz on the way. Over to our west we could see the Golden Gate Bridge, but we were not willing to tackle the 5 to 6 knots of current to sail the open ocean. Perhaps we will next time. Angel Island (#4) was beautiful and very greeen. After walking around the island, we motored south back to Ballena Bay (#2). Unfortunately, we picked the time of ebb tide. Since the tide was going out, we were fighting the currents the entire way back to Ballena Bay. Sailing would not have been possible, as the current was so strong. Our knot meter read 5.5 knots, while Dave's GPS read 1.5 to 2.5 knots on the trip. Sometimes (though he didn't admit it at the time) the GPS read zero. It was a long trip!

During our motoring south to Ballena bay, we spotted a humpback whale, and many sea lions were floating about in the water. Also, when Dave looked behind us at one time, the military stealth ship was behind us.

It was an exciting trip and we hope to do it again soon. As well as other trips. Below is a picture of the stealth military ship when it was docked.
