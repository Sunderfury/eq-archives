Written March 2000

The fire drake searched for her. She could smell its breath, hear the beat of its wings on the wind as it remained aloft, hovering a few feet above the earth. Her hair danced with the movement of the air. She didn't move. Her arms were covered with goose bumps, her heart was in her throat, and her knuckles were white as she held her staff with a death grip. It had come upon her suddenly, perhaps defending the puma she had been facing. Perhaps looking for a magician for its next meal. Regardless, it was upon her now, and all she could do was keep her earth elementalkin close at her side and pray to the goddess to banish it.

Karranza bit her lip and closed her eyes, extending other senses, hoping it would disappear without taking her as its meal. A shudder ran through her and she cursed herself. What was she doing in these mountains anyway? Was it to prove herself? Was it to keep Trannin back home from calling her a coward? Most likely. Now, however, it didn't matter. Weary from fighting the puma, her mana nearly exhausted, all she could do was hide behind the boulder and pray. She was too far from the caverns which were too small for it to enter. If she ran, it would grab her in claw and render her with its terrible beak. Her elementalkin would be of little use, as two swift strokes and it would, also, be dead.

She needed the experience, she needed the practice, and this place was the prime area, for many of the creatures here often attacked nearby villages and travelers. However, she didn't know enough spells yet. She should have taken someone with her. Someone like Trannin with his sword… but she stifled an angry laugh. He would only have mocked her.

Suddenly the drake squealed, the noise raising the hair on the back of her neck. Her elementalkin even noticed, and would have run from behind their rocky sanctuary had she not kept a tight magical leash on it. Her heart pounded faster. Karranza expected it to round the rockface any minute, for surely the squeal was the sign of finding its prey. But after a few moments it didn't come, and the squealing continued. Pain. It was in pain. She heard the grunt of a spellcaster and hope, which she had recently stopped feeling, reemerged in her heart.

Grasping her staff she crept from behind the rock. He was beautiful. A cleric, she could tell by his armor, by the spells he cast. She stood and watched for some time, in awe, as he toyed with the drake. The monster grew angry. It should have run, should have known in an instant it was bested. However, it remained, its unnatural foolish pride keeping it from fleeing to safety.

The cleric was clad in silver mail, straight and tall, his skin reflecting the light of the sun. His hair, only a few strands showing from beneath his helm, was brown and gently curled. His skills were beyond any she had before seen, and she stood in the open, her mind no longer thinking, for she was vulnerable not only to the drake, but to a number of other creatures which prowled these mountains. She was unaware. Her elementalkin took off, of its own accord, and proceeded to stab the drake. It was then the cleric noticed her, and, from the corner of his eye, smiled at her. Her heart leapt into her throat, and she could have fainted right then and there.

As the drake shrieked in pain and anger the cleric met her gaze, unafraid. "Would you like the kill?" he asked. The corners of his mouth nearly turning up into a smile.

She heard his voice easily over the screeching drake, but didn't think to wonder why. Instead she gathered herself up, remembered who and where she was. She lifted her chin in the highest elven fashion she could manage under the circumstances.

"I would," she replied and nodded, turning to the drake.

Her elementalkin was still stabbing at the beast as she gathered the remaining tatters of her mana and began to cast. She felt stronger suddenly, and knew the cleric, as clerics were wont to do, had cast his own spell upon her. Her fear subsided. The drake was tired now, about to turn and run, its fear and self preservation finally taking over its anger. Before it could, however, she felt the magic flow through her, course through her chest and arms, fill her mind with its wonder and beauty, and she cast at the drake.

One final screech and it fell. A loud crash and a cloud of dust rose as it hit the ground. Wings flailing about, the elementalkin still hacked at its corpse. One wingtip twitched, then it was still. Her elementalkin stopped its attack and regained its position by her side.

She took a deep breath, a sudden cold feeling creeping through her chest. She nearly fell, exhausted, knowing she would have died had he not rescued her. And how beautiful he was! The most handsome high elf she had ever seen. He stood tall and proud, his armor obviously his pride and joy. His skills honed to a precision she thought she may never achieve.

As he approached her he removed his helm. She had not seen many high elves with curls such as his, or eyes as deep as a forest. As he looked at her she nearly melted. She knew she was showing her youth, her fascination, her innocence, all the things Trannin kidded her about. But this elf, this cleric, there was nothing in his eyes but genuine joy.

"Fray," he said to her and offered a small bow. One almost deserving of a princess.

Her voice stuck in her throat a moment. She cleared it, and nodded. "Karranza. I thank you for your aid. Surely I would have lost my life, had you not come by. Is there any way I can repay you?"

"It was my pleasure. I do not come through here often," he knelt to the drake's corpse and removed a portion of wing with a small blade he carried, "but when I do I keep my eyes open for young elves. Seems this is a good training ground, and you're not the first I've found in some danger."

The grace with which he moved caused Karranza's legs to grow weak. He seemed to dance, each movement precise, each drawing of breath a magical dance.

"If you would allow, I would heal your wounds."

Again she had to swallow, and stop admiring him so very much. "Yes, I would appreciate your help. I don't feel so well," and as if the words somehow commanded it, she nearly fell to the ground.

He caught her with a swift movement and a strong arm. "Here, we'll be safe for a while. Sit, and relax." With those words he stood near her and gathered his skills. He was a dream to watch. He must be very highborn, she found herself thinking. The magic he called responded to him as though it was made for him. As if all the magic in the world were there for his beck and call, forming itself just for him, and she could not imagine anything which might threaten him. Ah, to have skills such as his, how would it be? She could only dream of it. Though the mage guild had told her she had the talent, and with perseverance she could achieve her potential. still, it was easier to remain in her home and read the books. Instead of going out into the world and practicing. She didn't like the ice which crept up her spine when she left her home. When she walked about town and suffered the jeers from the other mages. Her mother told her it was only in her mind, but she knew better. She was a laughing stock, and she'd prefer not to expose herself to such ridicule.

Fray cast a number of healing spells upon her. Her wounds closed, her blood stopped flowing, and energy reemerged in her. She closed her eyes and felt the strength as it coursed around her, drinking in the cleric's gift. It was wonderful, and she thought she had never felt so good. Can a cleric make one feel better than usual? Do the healing spells resemble brushweed, which gives the illusion of a strength and serenity greater than one naturally possessed?

It didn't matter. When he was done she sat for a moment before opening her eyes. Feeling the sun on her skin and hearing Fray's breathing next to her as he sat.

"Thank you," she offered at last, knowing it was all she could offer, and that offering was so very meager.

"My pleasure."

"Will you be in Felwithe this eve? I could offer you dinner at the house of my mother. She is a very good cook." Karranza offered him a smile.

He looked at her for a moment, his brown eyes seeming to examine her more closely than she had ever before been examined. She almost turned away, but the pleasure she saw there gave her confidence, and she remained where she was.

"I wasn't planning on Felwithe, but I would enjoy the company."

Karranza smiled then, and nodded. "If you wouldn't mind, a companion on the way home would be appreciated."

Fray understood the meaning behind her words, the fear she didn't want to express, and merely replied, "I would enjoy the company."
