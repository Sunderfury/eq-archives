San Francisco Bay
March 13, 1999 through March 20, 1999

Vessel:
Grey Ghost - Ranger 22

[Intro | 1 | 2 | 3 ]

The left picture above shows us picking up the Grey Ghost at the Great Salt Lake Marina. It was Saturday morning and we were looking forward to a great week long trip. We launched the Ghost at the Grant Street Public Launch Ramp. It was a nice ramp and we launched into the estuary where the water is calm. As the tide went out we noticed the depth slowly getting more shallow.

The above pictures show the square rigged battle we watched from Fisherman's Warf (left). They were shooting at each other, we saw the cannon smoke and heard the shots. The picture on the right is the Hornet, an air craft carrier. We found out it's nickname is The Grey Ghost and took a tour.

The above pictures compare the six foot tide. The left shows the low tide, and the right shows the high tide. A very big difference. With our 4.5 foot draft we were very concerned by the tide.

The above picture is more of sailing on the bay. Cynthia tried to snap pictures at every opportunity, even while others were shouting at her to sail and not take pictures!
