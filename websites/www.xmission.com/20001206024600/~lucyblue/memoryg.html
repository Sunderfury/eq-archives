Memory Grove

Memory Grove is located near the Capitol Building. It was hit hard by the 1999 tornado that swept through the downtown area, knocking down trees and tearing up buildings. There is an official off-lead area located north through the park and to the east of the river which flows down the canyon. Many joggers and bike riders can be seen using the canyon for exercise and health benefits, as well as walking their dogs. Before the tornado the park was canopied with large trees. Now, however, the grass is exposed to the sun much more often.

The park is still beautiful. It has many monuments and landmarks throughout, and is a nice place for a picnic in the summer, or a dog walk anytime. There are signs posted in the park with directions to, and the boundaries of, the off-lead area. There are also plastic bags provided to dog owners for their convenience in picking up after their dogs. The stream is a great place for the dogs to get wet and have some fun. There are many other dogs, mostly in the summer, to get to know and watch run and play. There is a fairly steep canyon wall to the east of the trail, and many dogs like to run off their energy chasing through the underbrush and getting familiar with the territory.

For people who like to park at the capitol building, there is a trail leading from the capitol down to memory grove. The step, switchback trail is well paved and easy to follow. However, there are still many an uprooted tree to observe from the recent tornado.
