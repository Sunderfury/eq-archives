San Francisco Bay
March 13, 1999 through March 20, 1999

Vessel:
Grey Ghost - Ranger 22

[ Intro | 1 | 2 | 3 ]

While sailing we were repeatedly pushed along by helicopters. Each time they flew by the Ghost would noticibly heel. Going under the Oakland Bay Bridge was fun.

The Oakland Bay Bridge is very high. San Francisco is a beautiful city. We didn't realize how large the bay was until we were out on it in our little boat. Our Ranger 22 must have been one of the smallest boats on the Bay. But she handled the rough seas well. The huge container ships were easy to avoid, unless you were in that crazy sailboat we saw where the guy was riding on their bow wake. Ouch!

We motored into Pier 39. It was fun to come into Fisherman's Warf from the Bay instead of driving in like we usually do.

Sailing by Alcatraz was fun. We looked for prisoners in the water trying to swim for safety... but we could not find any. Oh, but I guess it's been many years since it was an active prison. :)

The Sea Lions floated in the water with their noses in the air. The first time one swam right by the boat we thought it was a piece of garbage. Wow, life in the water! That is very different from the Great Salt Lake. The only life we usually see is brine shrimp. Angel Island (above right) is a California State Park and was very green. We walked around and considered staying the night. However, the next day's weather was uncertain, so we decided to head back to Ballena Bay Marina.
