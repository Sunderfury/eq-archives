Millcreek Canyon

Millcreek Canyon is located in the Wasatch Front, on the eastern most mountains bordering the Salt Lake Valley. Wasatch Blvd stretches along the mountain front and is the best road to use to reach the canyon. The entrance to the canyon is on Wasatch Blvd and 3800 South. There are brown signs marking the entrance and it is easily seen from the road. Turning east will bring the traveler through a quiet and comfortable subdivision nestled in the foothills. About a half a mile to a mile upward is the fee station. You will be asked to pay a fee when you are leaving the canyon, so unless you have questions you can continue upward. The fee for visiting the canyon is $2.25 per carload. Or you can buy a year pass for $22.00.

In the winter the canyon is closed five miles up. On even numbered days dogs and their people are welcome at the top of the road and past the road closed signs. Many dogs and their people love this winter trail. It is an easy, slightly upward slope which can be walked for many miles. A variety of dogs romp and play in the snow, both off-lead and on. On odd numbered days this portion of the canyon is limited to skiers and hikers and snowshoers in the winter, and mountain bikers in the summer. There are, however, other places dogs and their people can go on odd numbered days. Church fork is a good area, as are other of camping areas along the canyon.

In the summer, between May and November, the road block is removed and the canyon is opened. Driving to the top on a nice warm summer day is a great sight seeing experience. There is limited parking both in the summer and in the winter, and you might have to park a ways from the trail heads. There is a trail to dog lake, and streams abound through the woods. As of yet there are no summer pictures on my pages because I started these in winter. I will put up some summer pictures when the weather turns.

Many friendly and happing dogs are running around the trials. Of course there will be people and children, and other dogs, so it is in everyone's best interest to make sure your dog can be recalled by your voice. Sometimes large packs of dogs will gather in the middle of the trail where they all can get to know each other. It is a great way to socialize your dogs, and yourself!
