Astronomy
Spending nights gazing at the stars,wondering at their beauty and mystery, and thepossibilities offered by the heavens... it's acompelling hobby, one many Utahns have chosen tospend a great deal of time and energy pursuing.

There has been a very active astronomical population in Utah over the years. Many of those interested inastronomy travel to various areas of the state, and evenoutside the state. Instate observing sites rangefrom desert like locations such as Rush Valleyto the forested Uintah Mountains in placessuch as Elizabeth Pass. Many of those who loveastronomy have joined the various astronomicalsocieties which also call Utah home. Various astronomicalenthusiasts have traveled outside the state, too,in order to pursue their hobbies and increasetheir knowledge in this field. There are star partiessuch as the Texas Star Party and the RiversideTelescope Makers Coference (RTMC). Some astronomicalenthusiast even travel the world watching solareclipses, seeing the southern cross, and doing othervarious observing.

An email list has been created in order to bring Utah astronomy enthusiast together on theInternet. This list was created in October of 1999 to serve the astronomical community in Utah and surroundingareas. It is the hope of the list administratorfor this list to develop into a community resource forquestions, discussion, and general banter aboutastronomical issues in Utah and the surrounding areas.As of October this is a fledgling list, and not manypeople have joined. Hopefully it will growand become a resource for the experienced astronomeras well as the novice.

If you're not familiar with an Email list I'll explaina little about how they work. Each person who subscribes to the list will be put on a majordomolist server. Majordomo is an automated program whichhandles the list. When you post a message to the list the message is sent to every person who hassubscribed. Then, people can reply and discusswhatever topic is brought up. It's a great source ofinformation.

The amount of support and information this list endsup providing is up to those who join. Please tellfriends about this list and encourage them to join.The more members we have, the greater the knowledgebase will be. And who knows, maybe the next one tojoin will have the answer to that question which hasbeen bothering you for some time!
