Welcome to the Utah Dogs Webring

This webring was created in January 2000. Its purpose is to provide information and resources to people and dogs who live in Utah. It is my hope, as the ringmistress, that this ring will continue to grow and provide many resources to Utah residents regarding dogs in Utah. This ring is not intended to have an emphasis on any single type of dog activity such as rescue, breeding, animal rights, assistance dogs, competition, or personal pages... rather I hope this ring will include all these types of web sites and more. So please, if you have a dog, and you live in Utah, I hope you consider joining this ring!
Thank you.

1. Description of Webring
 The Utah Dogs Webring will consist of sites dedicated to dogs and their people in Utah. (For more information about what webrings are and how they work, click here).
I, being the ring mistress, will be the one responsible for approving sites for this ring. The ring will be limited to sites relating to both dogs and Utah. Anything outside of Utah will not be entered into the ring.

To add your site to the Utah Dogs Webring, you must complete two steps--you must complete the following form, and you must add a fragment of HTML code to your Utah Dogs-related web page.
First complete this form and click on the 'Submit Site' button. Then write down the information on the subsequently displayed page. Then use the 'Back' button on your browser to get back here and continue with the next step.

Note that you must also:
1) Replace --id-- with your site's assigned ID. Your site's ID will be e-mailed to you once the above form has been completed.
2) Replace --your email address-- with your email address, and --your name-- with your name.
3) Upload the udlogo.gif graphic to your web site location

HINTS:
1) You can use any graphic you want (or none at all), but it will be easier to find the Utah Dogs Webring link on your page if you use the same graphic as everybody else. To obtain the graphic, right-click on it and save it to your local fixed disk drive (hard drive).
2) You can view the HTML source of this page and then cut-and-paste the required HTML Code Fragment out of this page and paste it into your page--you will still need to change my ID to your ID, my email address to your email address, and my name to your name.
3) If you're an HTML expert and want to customize the way the text, graphics, or links are presented--go for it. The only limitation is that visitors to your site should be able to easily discern that it's part of the Utah Dogs Webring.
4) If you need more help, you can e-mail me at Webmistress@UtahDogs.com

PLEASE NOTE:
As responsible dog owners we must preserve the privileges we have with our dogs. To do so we must follow some basic rules:
Always pick up after your dog.
Always make sure your dog responds to voice commands.
Always make sure your dog is not aggressive towards other dogs or people.
