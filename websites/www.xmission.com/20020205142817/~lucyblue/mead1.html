Lake Mead
October 10, 1998 through October 18, 1998

1| 2 | 3 | 4 ]

The Grey Ghost was brought home to be packed on Friday night, October 9, 1998 for it's 400 mile trip to Lake Mead from the Great Salt Lake. The Grey Ghost is quite large on the trailer and we were all nervous about driving her so far. My little toyota pickup was up to the task, however, and both trailered Ghost and truck were reliable.

It was strange to look in the water and actually see fish! So they were carp, but they were not brine shrimp, so it was different for us. The first day was beautiful and we spinnakered the entire day, travelling south. The depth of the lake reached 200 feet easily and we were astonished, considering we are used to a lake which usually has a maximum depth of 26 feet under the keel of the Ghost. The first night we camped in Cathederal Cove, a beautiful bay with tall rock walls. The water was 22 feet deep where we anchored, and we also tied off to the shore because we were so close. Cynthia was very nervous about being so close to shore, but the anchors were solid and we had little wind and no waves in our bay. It seemed like we were sleeping on dry land.

Swimming was great in Lake Mead. We didn't even have to shower after to get two inches of salt off. The weather was wonderful, sunny and windy in the mornings, giving us an average of 4 knots. And sunny and fairly still in the afternoons. When it was still we would swim to cool off, because it was so very warm.
