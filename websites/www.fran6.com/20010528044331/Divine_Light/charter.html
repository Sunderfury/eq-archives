Order of the Divine Light.

Charter

 

Codex:

1. Role-playing is the guild's primary tenet. Maintaining a role-playing atmosphere is of primary concerns to guild members. /OOC should be kept to an absolute minimum, and /tell should be used as a replacement when at all possible.

2. Members will treat others as they would wish others to treat them.

3. Guild members will offer aid to other adventurers who may need it, and will provide aid if it is requested. Oftentimes guarding someone to prevent other NPC's from entering a fight, or taunting an NPC to draw it off, will be sufficient.

4. The helping of new players will be left at the discretion of the members involved. This will be encouraged but not required. Guild members are encouraged to give aid to younger guild members, but not at the expense of learning and normal progression on the part of the younger member.

5. Members will be obliged to uphold the basic goodness on this server. If a PC or NPC is picking on or disrupting a PC character, regardless of alignment, every effort should be made to protect the offended party. The reasoning is that every criminal deserves a trial before punishment.

Meetings:

Meetings will be held bimonthly online at a pre-posted time and place set by the guild. A quick visit to the guild homepage will have all of the information needed. Attendance at meetings is strongly encouraged, but not mandatory.

Dues:

Dues can be paid monthly in moneys or crafts. Dues will be based on character level. The formula will be level/5*1pp. Guild dues will be kept by the treasurer and used based on decisions of the guild council. Payment to the treasurer may be made at regular guild meetings, or wherever possible if meetings cannot be attended.

Current members:

Once a character is a member, they will always be a member, with the exception of crimes being committed, etc. Should a guild member commit acts contrary to the code of conduct of the order, that member will be judged by the guild officers, and may be removed at the officers' discretion. If a member quits the guild voluntarily, and wishes to return to the order, they must go through all of the trials of becoming a member again.

New Members:

New members will not necessarily be actively sought out. The guild will concentrate its efforts on its mission, building it's reputation as a good and noble organization that people will be drawn to in times of need, desperation, and guidance. The Ortis are the only exception to this rule. Our member base will come from those who see what we are accomplishing and have a desire to help. Initiates will be required to join under the following circumstances:

1. They must have a Mentor. This can either be a guild member they already know, or one will be assigned to them.

2. They must spend at least 6 hours of game time adventuring and communicating with at least the Mentor and one other guild member. These hours should be spread among at least 2, and preferably 3, individual gaming sessions. Interaction with other guild members during this time is encouraged, but not required. In the case of players whose online time is limited, the number of hours may be reduced at the discretion of the officers.

3. Upon completion of the required hours, the initiate must complete a trial as deemed worthy by the guild council. The Mentor, who will petition the Guild Council in the initiate's name, will oversee this task.
