Evil vs Paladins V 2.1

Evil

Joining evil

You can sign up for being evil by merely saying a key phrase at a shrine. There is a minimum character age requirement for being able to join evil. This makes you permanently red, and gives you the title of "the Evil." In addition, it puts "[evil]" over your head when you are looked at. Looking at yourself if you are evil gives you your current score and lifeforce values.

Evil people are not automatically killed in justice regions, but they are not allowed to resurrect at any shrines except the Chaos Shrine.

Evil killing people

The effects of evil people killing others depends on the nature of the person being killed. The determination of whether the victim died to an evil person is based on the amount of damage done to the victim-the kill is considered to be "by an evil" if the most damage done was done by an evil character.

Evil killing wisps

Wisps are a source of lifeforce. Hence evils should try to kill wisps as much as they can in order to gain lifeforce and points.

Evil killing innocents

When an innocent dies, they keel half over, and then sparklies appear around them. After a brief moment, the character's corpse is deleted. All items carried remain with the ghost. The evil character's stats and skills are kicked down by 50% while the evil character "loses lifeforce" for five minutes. During this time, they are also unable to recall or hide. Lastly, this also costs the evil character points and lifeforce. If the evil player has 0 points, then they can also be reported for murder.

Evil killing paladins

When a paladin is killed by an evil player, they are fully lootable. In addition, it confers points and lifeforce to the evil character, in a manner akin to the Abyss scoring system. You cannot gain lifeforce at all if you have any murder counts. At a certain point, the points you gain confer special powers.

Evil killing other evils

There is no effect from this.

Evil powers

Elder players start with higher amounts of initial points and lifeforce when they join evil. As you gain standing in the evil hierarchy, you gain both points and lifeforce. Special powers kick in when you reach certain amounts of points. Each use of the power costs lifeforce, and if you do not have sufficient lifeforce to use the power, you will have to go gain more by killing paladins in order to use the power again.

You can reach a menu of your powers by using a speech trigger, "I invoke my evil powers!" Selecting the menu option for a specific power then simply barks the words of power, to be caught by a speech trigger. Alternatively, you can access a power directly by using the words of power associated with the power.

The powers, in order of difficulty and in order of increasing cost, are:

Black armor

Saying the word, and then targeting an equipped piece of armor, turns it true black. When unequipped, the armor returns to its natural color. If you cease to be evil, the armor returns to its natural color as well.

Detect Good

This does a getPlayersInRange, and returns information on the proximity of paladins.

Summon Familiar

This summons a black wolf which is the evil's pet. You cannot summon one if you already have one. This pet has maxxed loyalty, but only lasts for a set period of time (say, a half hour).

Vile Blade

This makes your weapon have powerful poison for the next few blows. Then the effect expires.

Unholy Steed

This summons a black horse which is your pet, with maxxed out loyalty. You cannot summon one if you already have one.

Blight

This does area effect damage to paladins in range.

Monster Ignore

This attaches the monster ignore script to the evil person, for a fixed duration of time.

Unholy Word

This brings up a targeting cursor allowing you to target a paladin and instantly kill them. It does not confer any points or lifeforce for the kill.

 

Paladins

Becoming a paladin

Merely killing evils increases your lifeforce and your points. You cannot gain points while you are red, nor while you have any short murder counts nor if you are a member of the Thieves Guild. At a certain threshold, you are automatically declared a paladin. You gain [paladin] over your head as well. Looking at yourself if you are a paladin gives you your current score and lifeforce values.

Paladins killing people

Paladins killing innocents or paladins Being reported for murder immediately sets your lifeforce and your points to zero. In addition, you are prevented from gaining any lifeforce or points while you still have the short murder count. In other words, you are kicked out of paladinhood.

Paladins killing murderers

This behaves exactly like anyone killing a murderer now.

Paladins killing evil

When you kill an evil, your lifeforce and points increase using a scoring system akin to that on the Abyss. The evil individual is fully lootable.

Paladin powers

The functioning of powers is by the same interface style as for evil powers.

White armor

Exactly like the black armor power of evils. This will require a patch of HUES.MUL so that all players are running with the T2A hues, which includes the white hue required.

Detect Evil

Exactly like the Detect Good power, except it detects evils.

Summon Familiar

Exactly like the evil version, except it summons a dog (with equivalent stats to the black wolf's).

Holy Blade

Effectively gives "silver" ability to whatever weapon is wielded, for a brief while. This ability does extra damage to undead and evils.

Holy Steed

Just like the evil version, except the horse is white.

Bless

An area effect spell that gives a temporary stat boost to all innocents in the effect range, excepting guild enemies.

Holy Shield

Uses the shield effect developed for potions. Puts an enterrange trigger of 1 around the paladin, preventing monsters or evils from approaching. Innocents however may still approach.

Holy Word

Like Unholy Word for evils, except it only harms evils.

How innocents fit in

A principal goal of the system is to prevent it from impinging too much on the normal gameplay of innocent players.

Innocents dying to evil

Evil is strongly discouraged from attacking innocents, since evil loses 50% of all stats and skills temporarily, plus loses lifeforce and standing, plus cannot loot.

Innocents dying to paladins

This falls under the standard rep system. In addition, it costs the paladin all of their paladin status, and the paladin cannot loot that victim.

Innocents killing evil

Innocents who kill evil enough will automatically become paladins.

Innocents killing paladins

This is treated exactly like a standard murder.
