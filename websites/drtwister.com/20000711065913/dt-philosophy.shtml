The Dr.Twister Network - Site Philosophy

Overview

When I began thinking about how to approach this new philosophy in order to accommodate the many changes that we have experienced within the game industry, I realized that the only way to do so was to create a philosophy that would always evolve as the industry is doing itself.

In the past, the Dr.Twister Network has used methods of bridging the gap between developer and player that were at best unrefined, at worst counterproductive. Developers and players alike had to adjust to what was revealed in that time. So too has this network. What we have learned should become much more apparent as time progresses.

The concept of multiplayer games allows for constant growth. This same theory can be looked at with the companies developing multiplayer games, the people who play them and the communities that intertwine both. With this in mind, the ability to progress with whatever change that may occur is essential to the continued growth of these philosophies.

Considerations

When this network began back in 1998, the most significant change that the industry was experiencing was the transformation between the one time production of a single player game to the daily development of a multiplayer game. There was never a need to be in constant communication with the players of a game. A game company could release their product to the retail chains, consumers would buy it, and the idea of customer service was simply to supply refunds if the package wasn't opened. The world changed when in order to have the constant evolution in the game world, you had to pay close attention to what the player base was asking for.

The interactive nature of this genre of games demands that players and developers meet each other halfway, for the benefit and enjoyment of both. Instead, the groups have more often than not taken sides, and no amount of company propaganda can fix the blame when something goes wrong. Even when they go right, the message is skewed and people gain nothing from the exchange. More often than not, the game itself is left at risk and future players miss out on the improvements that should be facilitated by both parties.

Since our first generation of MMORPG (Massive Multiplayer Online Role Playing Games), this new industry of community driven companies have prepared themselves for a never-ending and often times never appreciated onslaught of gaming opinions. Countless hours can be wasted simply from sifting through information from message boards, chat forums, fan pages and even an occasional auction site. The Dr.Twister Network hopes to supply developers with enough tools and resources to minimize the time needed for researching player's opinions.

The industry can't survive on listening alone. The desire and need a developer has to communicate with the public is crucial to the success of their game. This network is a public forum to allow a company to express their plans and goals in hopes to generate a better understanding about their game, supply high levels of customer service and to involve the players in the constant growth of the games they play.

Goals

The Dr.Twister Network will offer endless opportunities for developers and game players to succeed in what both parties enjoy doing the most. Our open door policy will allow for unlimited potential in communicating exactly what is going on in the games that we play. Our desire is that the information supplied can be used in the continuous development and improvement for future generations of online games.

Our goals are simple...

Bridge the gap between developers and game players in order to understand the differences between what is being created for the player's entertainment, and what entertainment value the players get out of what is created. The pros and cons are strong on both sides of the issue, but from a purely business perspective, the game industry must ultimately understand that players pay for a service, and the service should not stop with the CD and the pretty box art. Customers in this day and age are much less likely to be "sold" on vagary and weak promises. They want and deserve better attention to their game desires.

A Look Ahead

We understand that the industry has only started to see what is in store for us and future game players. The ability to work together, communicate and grow on a daily basis is exactly what we are attempting to do.

No doubt you have begun noticing some of the changes mentioned above. Most significantly the way we will be communicating news on a daily basis. I invite you to have a look around the various sites that make of the network. I do want to hear your thoughts and suggestions. Please email me any time at drtwister@drtwister.com to express your opinions about a specific game, or a way to improve how the industry is currently operating.

If your passion is strong, I invite you to join our team to actively assist in the continued growth and production of the online gaming arena.

Thank you all for your time and understanding. I look forward to the years ahead.


Dr.Twister
