For a "pure" war mage? The template is not *all* bad, just take the points out of Coord and put them somewhere usefull...Focus and Self. In fact, that might work fine for all magic schools except for Life.

If you build a mage that uses Life magic, then Endurance is a must--even to the point of sacrificing some Self. My mage (Life/War/Item) started out:

Str 25

I posted the details of my response already in a previous message in this board, so I'm not going to say much in that regard.

General guidelines though:

1> Quickness is the newbie mages most important asset. It effects running speed and casting speed.

2> Strength is hard to tell you, you need at least 30 though to keep yourself in components.

3> Endurance should be at least 60 if you take life magic from the start [for Stamina to mana spell]. If you decide to take creature vs. item then you can grab points from other skills and buff yourself when needed and put them here, any "extra" pts are best spent here.

4> Focus and Self, focus at 75+, self at 90+ [you need the mana pool]. Focus is not so important, someone with 50 focus and 100 self is just as good a mage as someone with 75 focus and 75 self. You should raise these skills equally from the get-go, for every 2 pts you put in both of these you get 1 in all your spell skills.. Totally worth it.

As far as skills, bare minimum is:

life/war/conversion/lore

optional is item or creature magic [one or the other]

Whether you need one or the other is basically a matter of preference [you will have them all by level 20 no matter what, so it's not to important either way] in that if you like to walk a loot take creature, if you value lifestone recall and buffing your friends to make them fight extremely well take item -- they're both good. The creature person has the advantage of being able to buff themselves to higher levels of spellcraft, but of course -- they're walking; sometimes that lifestone recall is a lifesaver [lag/backpack of loot standing near a camp of tuskers heheh]
