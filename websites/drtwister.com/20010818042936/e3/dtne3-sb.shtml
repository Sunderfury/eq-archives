Dr.Twister's Game Previews
Shadowbane

In short, Shadowbane will kick your butt before you even put the CD into your machine. I think that everyone at WolfPack are a bunch of PKs and they simply wanted to impose their strength on every player who logs into SB. That's ok though, at least they're not hiding the type of game this is really supposed to be and if you end up playing Shadowbane, you better expect it rough from the start.

Shadowbane has been designed for the hardcore gamers. WolfPack has come out on several occasions and stated, this game is not for everyone, and if you don't like it, just don't play. They are going straight after a market, and some of the recent announcements they made regarding game play, demonstrates even more why Shadowbane is going to rock.

The demonstration I watched this year at E3 was tremendously more improved from last year. Granted, instead of a closed in trailer last year, we got to see SB in a nice tent this year - but other than that, both game play, and the dreaded graphics have been improved as well. Finally we had the opportunity to see what potential the graphics really could have.

But it's the game play that has received the most attention recently. An economy and environment catered towards the grief players and that refreshes our memories of early UO days. Add in the ability to destroy houses, capture what's inside, buy insurance deeds on houses, and hire peons to do your macroing for you - you've got all of the benefits in game now, that people desperately used third-party programs for to get a head in previous games. Basically, instead of fighting them, WolfPack is joining them. No need to macro mining, smelting, smithing and selling when the game will manage it for you.

WolfPack also takes the concept of Dread Lords vs. Great Lords one step further. It's something I've expressed on more occasions than once - recognition. Take away the titles from UO, replace them with a crest/shield, and now take over a plot of land. You own land, your shield is displayed on the map. Someone comes along and destroys your guild, their shield takes your shields place. Players now have instant gratification and recognition for their accomplishments. What? We're giving recognition for destruction and chaos of another players land? Cool...

There are concerns that were addressed with this style of game play. Supposedly there are limits in place to prevent one guild, of say 200 players, from taking over an entire server. What I don't think is in place though is a check of multiple accounts, where one account owns one section of land, the second account owns the land right next door and since it's the same person, they obviously become allies.

WolfPack is smart though, not everything has a negative impact on your player. Now, instead of losing everything (remembrance of UO days), if your house is destroyed, you do have the potential of receiving a good % of the resources it took you to build your house back. So before you decide to quit because you have nothing, you are easily back in the game with the chance to reconstruct your empire.

So as you can see, this game is going to be fast, furious and fun without the worries of not being able to keep up. It will be easy at lower levels, offering anyone the ability to build a kingdom - and expect it to get harder and harder to more powerful you get. That's obvious, because everyone else will be trying to destroy what you've built up. So, for those out there who claim their good, now you'll finally be able to prove it. I'll be anxiously waiting myself to destroy your world.
