Words of Wisdom
Posted on Thursday, April 26, 2001 2:00 AM EST by Mr.Orange

Georgia, a player on fennin ro, a staff member at Everlore and a complete ass to me said some things that we should all ponder on... A friend over at CLAW re-posted her words so i grabbed them from him ) Read on.......


Its interesting. Coming from a non uber, once a month( if lucky) plane running guild.. We have fun by ourselves, we dont need a 80 man guild where people know other people because they are there to help you get items. We are there for each other and have a blast playing EQ. What do you guys think about this?

I’ve played EverQuest for over two years now. I’ve beta tested. I’ve role-played. I’ve
adventured. I’ve leveled, and I’ve gone for the epics. Although the game still holds me
closely to it’s bosom after all of this time, I have found that perhaps players have lost a
sense of morality and honor. Is this the “phat lewt” fever? Perhaps. Perhaps not.
Players today are driven by one hope, and that is to “beat” EverQuest. Why beat it?
There is no end-goal other than perhaps reaching level 60 and owning the best
equipment available to one’s class and/or race. These players are so driven by their lust
for “lewt” that they have become monsters to their former friends and guilds. They rush
to battles one after the other… Gorenaire in the Dreadlands… Trakanon in Old Sebilis…
and of course, the famed “hot spot” Ragefire in Nagafen’s Lair… all for loot. Morality
stops here. Care for those who have spent hours attempting to kill a specific mob has
been laid to waste by larger guilds with loot hungry minds that rush in at the last second
to “steal” something that smaller guilds worked very hard to achieve. GMs? What are
those? Are they creatures I create for my children’s fairy tales? They know not of the
situations that occur behind their backs.

Oh, it is true that many larger guilds are quite noble, though there are some, unnamed by
me, that strive to crush those that get in their way. These guilds, once friends of many,
grew, changed, but allowed their morals and honor to deteriorate as they progressed in
the game. They no longer desire to be friendly and respectable of the trials and torture
that smaller, lower-level guilds have to go through to make it to the top of the server
food chain; they only desire their loot.


Take the epic quests for example. They are meant to be epic in every way, shape, and
form. Players unite to gather the items for these wonderful weapons, though the end
always seems to trip them. Guilds fight over something as trivial as Ragefire, for example.
A mob, which spawns every 0 to 90 hours (variable spawn time), is the last part needed
to complete the cleric epic quest. Every 90 hours, GMs have to deal with fights between
as many as four guilds over this bundle of 1’s and 0’s in a virtual world. Is this epic? Is
this what Verant really wants us to go through when attempting to complete our quests?
Petty arguments ensue between guilds over these items… over loot. Is there honor left in
EverQuest? Maybe… I hope and dream that it remains.


There are some, who allow themselves to be walked all over, so they don’t end up
stooping to the record low of many guilds. This isn’t meant to be an attack upon those
who run from uber spawn to uber spawn hoping for their lovely “phat lewtz;” rather, a
question to the general public regarding honor and morality left in the game today. How
do we see ourselves in the game today? How will we be tomorrow? Do we see ourselves
as being the best of the best and allowed to trample over those who are not as “uber?”
Or are we honorable beings? These things and more I am curious to hear about. I want
to know, WHAT drives players to drop all respect, morality, and integrity, to obtain one
piece of loot. Moreover, what of honor?


Words we should all concider...even though i thought she was in DOA, a fennin "uber" guild )
. Then again,whos to say how i or you should spend our EQ time. If i choose to be a Uber player in an uber guild railroading the weak ...whos to say i cant...after all i pay the same monthly fee.
