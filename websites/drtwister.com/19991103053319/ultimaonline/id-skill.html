Proposal for Skill Point Change by -

As I was talking this over with some of my Uo friends, here are some of the
things that we came up with. With all the new skills and needs for those
skills coming up in UO we decided plain and simple the 700-750 softcap for
BASE skills is simply too low. WAY WAY too low. Here is a proposed change

There are many many skills in UO that we all have to pick up from time to
time, even the biggest baddest warrior needs to hide occasionally. So he may
tend to have 50-60, maybe 65 hiding if he hides a LOT. Our sugegstion is to
make skills NOT count unless they break a certain limit (this could be based
on a PER skill basis). For example if there is a bad ole' warrior who had
98.8 hiding, the fact is he worked, and macroed that hiding up that high -
therefore it is obviously a skill he wanted and feels he needed. so our
suggestion would be this. Let's say hiding is a free skill up to 60 hiding.
if you get 100 hiding then you now consume 40 base skill points intsead of
100.

The fact is we ALL need a LITTLE of every skill. We need to be able to cook
occasionally to make ourselves ribs, we need to be able to pitch a tent and
sleep, and start a campfire.. I personally have 67 camping beacuse i log out
in the woods ALL the time. I have never WORKED on gaining camping skill. So
camping would be another one of those skills that would have a HIGH free
tolerance. maybe 70-80 FREE camping before it charges you any skill cap
points. so if you were a GM camper you'd only require 20 skill points above
the free cap on camping.

However this would NOT be the case with combat or magery type skills. These
are generall skills that REQUIRE work even at the smallest level. Let's say
all weapon skills were free up to about 30. (we all pick up a spear from
time to time to goof off, so at least we know how to use one, we're just not
proficient with it) - beyond 30 we actually have to TRAIN to knwo how to use
that weapon better, therefore it would require skill points. Magery could
be set the same, casting 1st and 2nd circle spells is pretty easy to do,
read the words off your book, and wa-lah, you have a fat "ham" for you to
eat. However for the occasional fireball or lightning bolt, you'd have to
study magery much more. So maybe magery is "free" up to 25-30 or so. (to
make it more realistic this could be a sliding scale based on intelligence -
obviously the smarter you are the more you coudl learn about magery without
taxing your brain.. perhaps maxing out at 35 or 40)

Here is the motive behind all this. With the new changes to "rogues" going
in. a steretypical "rogue" in the D&D sense would have the following skills
and be GM'd in all of them.

Fencing, tactics, archery, detect hidden, detect traps, hiding, snooping,
stealing, sneaking (stealth), pick locks.

The problem is that is 10 skills not 7, and makes it impossible to be a good
all around rogue. with the proposed change it would look more like this.

 

Skill Free Used Total
Fencing 30 70 100 (gm)
tactics 30 70 100 (gm)
archery 25 75 100 (gm)
detect hidden 50 50 100 (gm)
detect traps 70 30 100 (gm)
hiding 60 40 100 (gm)
snooping 40 60 100 (gm)
stealing 10 90 100 (gm)
sneaking 45 55 100 (gm)
pick locks 10 90 100 (gm)
TOTALS: 370 630 1000

 

If you'll notice things like detect hidden/traps, and hiding have a very
high "free skill" rate, because those are skills that ANY person as a UO
adventurer would need to use, regardless of their proficiency. One could
look at this as a "common knowledge" factor - However skills like picklocks,
and stealing have a very low "free" threshold because you HAVE to want to
learn those things and have to work at them from the beginning to get good
at them. Therefore they have a very LOW amount of "common knowledge" factor
programmed into them.

With the proposed change, it is possible to have a much more rounded
character, and the numbers above are just suggestions, and certainly have
not been well thought out. This would allow someone to play a role to it's
fullest without running against the hard skill cap as it stands currently.
