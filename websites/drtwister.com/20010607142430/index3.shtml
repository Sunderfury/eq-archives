Friday, June 1, 2001

UO Advisors
Posted on Friday, June 1, 2001 by Pheonix Cain

I was just browsing through IRC when a freind brought my attention to this:

Customer Support Advisors Jun 1 2001 3:29PM CST (GMT -06:00)
EA has arranged with a small group of international customer support representatives to aid in handling game-related customer support, as well as other tasks, for EA.com. As of Friday, June 1st, they will be spending some time addressing Ultima Online customer concerns on the Chesapeake and Atlantic shards from 5:00 pm to 1:00 am EDT as advisors, appearing in peach-colored robes. Please keep in mind that Advisors will only address game play issues. This is an experimental program, and we will post updates when and if the program is to be expanded.

So it looks like EA have decided to replace counselors with paid up staff. Sounds good except firstly none of these Advisors are ex-counselors - a quick survey in the ex-counselor IRC rooms revealed that to be the case and secondly after a bit more digging around I managed to discover that these new advisors may well consist in the main of asian staff. It doesn't take much to guess that these new advisors are probably working for EA for next to nothing.



Mimesis Online Beta
Posted on Friday, June 1, 2001 by Dr.Twister

Received a quick note from the team bringing us Mimesis Online:
Most people prefer to get the bad news out of the way first, so here it is - the start of the Mimesis Online Beta Tests has been moved back - the tester selection, which was due to be done at the end of May, will take place in July, and the tests will start after that. The decision to move the Tests back wasn't taken lightly, and two major factors played a role.

One of them is the studio's expansion - coming up to the Tests, new staff were taken on, and there are currently over thirty of us working in the same studio we started out in. So we've moved the bulk of the studio to a new address, a move which will give us all space and a better atmosphere to focus on the game in.

Which brings us to the good news, which is the second reason for our decision. We know that the longer wait will be something of a disappointment to many of you; this might make the wait more worth it. We've decided to add a few things to the Beta version that we had previously not planned to introduce until later. When the Beta version "knocks on your door", you can expect to see it with the full trade system installed, for example. Not to mention the full range of grouping possibilities. And perhaps most importantly of all, we'll be testing the Player vs. Player system in its entirety, with four new options added to the arena combat rules, including plaver vs. NPC and party vs. party.

So, hang in there. The studio will be putting out quite a few things in those weeks before the Betas, and we promise that the wait won't be a day longer than we need to make sure that the version you get will be the best possible. Thanks for your patience, and we'll be in touch with more news as soon as it comes in.

The Tannhauser Gate team
