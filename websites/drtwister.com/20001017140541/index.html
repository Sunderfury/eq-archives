Wizards & Warriors
Posted: Monday, October 9, 2000 by Dr.Twister

When we got our copy of D.W. Bradley's Wizards & Warriors, we were prepared for a nice, interesting RPG. But after four days of intense gameplay, we know that we have received a gem. Have a look at our Wizards & Warriors First Impressions why we think this game is outstanding, despite some flaws. Here's a snippit:

My summary so far? Go and buy the game if you like old school RPG’s. Go and buy it if you don’t know much about the older RPG’s like Ultima Underworld or Bards Tale. Chances are that you will fall in love with Wizards & Warriors nevertheless. It’s epic, it’s fun, and it’s a game many RPG fans will play over and over again. It has its flaws, but look at this game as you would look at your girlfriend: if you are in love, you tend to love those flaws, too. For me, Wizards & Warriors is a classic, a game that will be named with the 5 most important CRPGs ever. It won’t be number one, but it won’t be forgotten, either.

Here's the link: http://www.rpgdot.com/html/ww1l.html

Thermaltake Super Orb Scoopage
Posted: Monday, October 9, 2000 by Dr.Twister

With CPU's becoming faster and faster, more and more heat is emitted. So whats a good way to combat this heat? How about Thermaltake's new Super Orb! Yes that's right, following in the footsteps of it's smaller (yet very powerful) brother the Chrome Orb, the Super Orb is spec'ed to run a 1.5GHz Thunderbird CPU in Socket A format. All I can say is look at this beast!
