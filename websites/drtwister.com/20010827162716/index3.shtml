What's what at DTN
Posted on Thursday, August 23, 2001 10:43 PM EST by Dr.Twister

Over the past week or so, several rumors have been surfacing regarding the state of DTN, so it's time to put some of those rumors to rest and let everyone know what the true story behind the scenes at DTN really is.

First, IGN has approached DTN and has made me an offer to buy DTN. Although I am honored by the offer, I must decline. The reality is, as I stated a few weeks ago, money is running low and costs are increasing. I bring in roughly $800.00 per month off of DTN, and that was breaking even for our collocation services. Now with the increased bandwidth and popularity of DTN, the monthly fees are going to be raised to $1,200.00 - I simply cannot support that cost. I've asked for donations, and for now, that will be the only way we will be able to stay online.

IGN did offer to keep the site online, as well as give me full editorial rights to the content I present. However, they would own the network and the services I supplied. This doesn't go immediately against what I stand for, but after dedicating three years to this network, I wasn't ready to just let it go. Also, the deal would include the much rumored "bug database"... which brings me to the next topic.

Before I go any further, I, Dr.Twister, Leader, owner, president and founder of this network have never once publicly made comments or promises of releasing a bug database at any given time. Those rumors were started by staff members who report at DTN, and any promises made by them, you should address back to them.

Setting that aside though, the rumors are in fact true. Over the past several months, we have been developing an Online Bug Database, that I approached the Developers of various MMORPGs with back during E3. This database has never once been completely ready to go public, thus I never was ready to make the announcement. The much-guarded secret however escaped when x-reporters and the IGN deal came about.

However, this bug database, known as "Bug Alert" (The original name of my first web site), is the root of all that is happening at DTN right now. See, I basically realized that the bug database was something I felt extremely passionate about. It's something that I believe will bring great good to the gaming industry while at the same time, bring back bugs to DTN in a way that I was never truly able to accomplish when I had first started this network three years ago. It's back to my original founding that I had to go in order to realize what decisions I was being faced with.

The Dr.Twister Network started with once simple philosophy... free information and free bugs for all. That philosophy has grown into what you may now know as "The Dr.Twister Manifesto". Together with Bug Alert, I feel that I will be presenting the ideals of what this site is about and finally opening up a communication opportunity with the game developers. Whether they participate has yet to be proven, but the opportunity will be right here.

Because of my personal philosophies and the reasons behind why I established this network, I simply felt no amount of money would ever be able to replace the goals of this network and of my own. Unfortunately, it also means that DTN is about to close its doors if we cannot find the additional resources to continue our operation. At least I feel that this way, I'm still going down with a fight.

Since the current state of DTN is up in the air, although the Bug Database has still many more improvements and features being developed, plus there is a great amount of anticipation, as well as "wannabe" DTN bug sites starting to pop up, it's time for us to get back to the basics of what I started a short time ago.

I have additional comments within the system. The Bug Alert database will have regular internal messages posted by me helping those of you who use the system to understand what problems you are encountering in your favorite games. You will need to sign up for an account, but what I can tell you, "It's Free!" - no subscription and no need to supply a valid bug to gain entry. Just sign up and get an account.

Once you’re in, feel free to start reporting the problems you have. Currently online are databases for EverQuest, Asheron's Call, Anarchy Online, Dark Age of Camelot, World War II Online and yes, we bring back Ultima Online!

You will be able to find additional details within Bug Alert once you log in. If you have questions though, please feel free to contact me and let me know what or how I can help. You are able to communicate through an internal message forum, commenting on each bug individually, and it's you the players who will help identify the urgency or criticality of each bug presented. Finally, with the help of the game developers, after assigning a bug to be fixed, we'll be able to track just how fast they get the bugs resolved. A developer will also be able to share with us reasons behind why a bug is there, reasons for fixing or not fixing and the ability to identify features or areas that may appear to be bugs but really are not.

So with out much more talk, DTN is staying, DTN needs money and DTN is proud to announce for your "entertainment" - The Bug Alert, Bug Database. Enjoy and thank you for your support!
