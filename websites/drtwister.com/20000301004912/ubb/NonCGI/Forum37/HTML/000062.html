I've seen an awful lot of complaints regarding the latest patch--and quite frankly, I can't figure out what people think they have to complain about.

(Before I go on much further--quick comment. Dr. Twister is as wrong as he can be when he says "it is our game". No, its not. We have a license to play the game--but its not ours. If you dont like it, you have a choice to go play somebody else's game).

Anyway...for the most part I have to disagree with statements about how mages have been crippled by the patch. My main character is a 31st level mage (all Schools) and was a little bummed out when I the creature I was blasting was "out of range" from my Drain Life when before patch it was considerable in range. However, (gasp) I found that by getting closer (ie, taking some risk) I was able to still use the spell just fine with the same devastating effect.

More than the nerfing of Life spells and the programmers finally fixing the creature vulnerabilities, the author seems to have more of a problem with the fact that there are only 9 hotkeys for spells. While the 9 keys work fine for me (there are only 9 spells I use that I need *that* fast, I have about 20 more spells arranged in the sliding area next to the hot keys), I can understand where some people might need more. It would be nice if Turbine would make spells accessable via some other macro system.

I'm not saying what the author wrote is wrong, but I do think its a little biased and that his conclusions derive from that bias. The patch nerfed mages, true. However, that nerfing has not stopped me from wreaking havoc anyplace that I couldnt wreak havoc before. I think the patch has added something significant--the requirement that mages know tactics and strategy.

And to those that "had to create a new character because mages were nerfed so bad".... Boo hoo! I feel sooo sorry that you cant kill Lugians from a nice safe distance anymore.

The patch came along when I was level 26, so you might need to refine levels affected by patch clause.

Anyway, *every* creature resists higher level spells, not just banderlings. That has absolutely nothing to do witht he patch. The higher level (ie, more complex) a spell is the more likely it is to be resisted. I still use Drain Health I/Harm II when I choose the Life Magic route to killing a beast.

I believe you are very wrong in your opinion of what a mages starting stats should be. First, you are already complaining of how crippled you feel, but now you are suggesting you further cripple yourself. You've just significantly reduced the most important stat a mage has (Focus) (some would argue Self, but Focus is used in more formulae). Not only does this compound your problems as a starting mage, but it also cripples you as you reach higher levels. Are you suggesting that before the patch I didnt die even though I had a low Quickness? Stop kidding yourself. I died quite frequently. The difference is I had the skills/stats to remove my penalty quickly and keep advancing. I didnt run from problems--I killed them.

However, you dont have to take my opinion as theory. My roommate started a mage a week ago (post patch) and is having excellent results using my advice. He's not leveling quite as quickly as I did, but the patch has nothing to do with that. He has another character he plays more often (an unarmed fighter) and all the spots I used to hang out at to level are so crowded now he cant get the same ratio time/kills as I did. And trust me...if he can do it *anyone* can.

Components dont burn any faster than they ever have. Life Magic (especially Drains/Harm) has always burned components at an outrageous rate and moving to higher level spells increases that.

As far as I am concerned, the patch did me a huge favor (though it took me a week or two to realize it). I primarily used my Life Magic in dungeons, and War Magic outside. But now War Magic has come to the forefront again--and with a vengance. Now, rather draining the crap out of a monster (using most of my mana pool in the process), I cast an Vulnerability (exploiting whichever weakness the monster may have), then cast the War spell exploiting that Vuln spell. The damage is *incrediable*! I was so dependant on Life Magic before the patch that I hadnt realized what I was missing out on.
