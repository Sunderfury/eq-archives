Anarchy Online

I spent all of about 5 minutes at the FunCom booth looking over Anarchy Online, the first Sci-fi MMORPG. It was about the same amount of time I spent playing the beta. Don't get me wrong, it's by no means that the game is not impressive, it's just not my style. Interesting enough, the guy on the right probably caught more eyes than most booth babes.

Yes, Anarchy Online is different, but it's a first. With that honor, the development team should be prepared for either a lengthy success, or an immediate failure. It will only be through word of mouth that this game becomes successful. The most difficult question being, how does a non-US based company support a game geared towards the American gamer? Well maybe that's it! Maybe they are banking on the fact that no one has really gone after the European market of gamers.

You've probably noticed by looking at some of the screenshots for AO, there is no doubt in my mind that there is a certain European feel to the characters, monsters and even the overall game engine. The music even has a certain ring. I do realize it's because the programmers are in fact from overseas, but it's still interesting to see just how much recognition AO has received from the US. It either shows us that we are indeed looking for a Sci-fi genre, or we're just looking for a change, and Anarchy Online is the next in line behind Asheron's Call.

I did notice, although I couldn't get a straight answer, that the graphics being displayed at E3 seemed a lot crisper than what I remember from beta. It's possible they did an upgrade on the graphics end, in which case, I was impressed with the improvements.

There are several things I do like in AO, some of which are unique to other MMOGs. There are also several things I don't like about AO. Once the NDA is released, I'll hit everyone up again with another review. In the meantime, as I said earlier, I'll be very interested to see how the second generation of Online Games starts out even if it's a sci-fi.
