Author Topic:   Ever get bored with AC?
WarBringer
Member 		posted February 07, 2000 01:41 AM             
I have been playing AC for nearly a month now and right away I realized that I wasn't having nearly as much fun as I did when I first started playing Ultima Online.

Well, after playing for a while I realized it all came down to just a very few reasons why. One, was that it sometime takes me about 20 minutes to get from one town to another. I spend the majority of my time in AC running. Whether it be running from that Shadow Lt that spawned in a very popular area for lvls 8-15 or running from portal to portal to portal to portal to get from one town to the next I'm always running. I don't know about the rest of you people but I myself don't find it a fun experience running. Hell, with as much running as I do, I should get skill points in running for it.

Secondly, I think its much harder to interact in AC than it is in UO. I think the way that the communication system is set up is probably the best way that it can be done in a 3D game but remember how its done in UO? Your text pops up over your head so you know who's saying what. Thats just the greatest, unfortunately an easier way for AC can't be developed. Ever noticed when you're not hanging out with friends, if you ask a question in town about 90% of the time it goes ignored?

Lastly, we all know that everyone who has been playing for over a week has a mule and anyone who has been playing since the beginning has atleast 2 mules. We store stuff on our mules such as pyreals, armor, weapons and so forth. They may be for ourselves, vassals, or friends. Whoever they may be for, we still have them stored there. It can be a real pain in the ass to mule during peak hours sometimes. So, I think the AC Dev team should incorporate an easier way. They may have had intentions of making the game so that you only carry whats really important to you but they need cater to the customers needs.

Till you get portal magic, and even after, running is about all you can do in this game. There are 3 portal loops that connect many of the towns though, and I'd suggest you get them all memorized to help you get around.

Don't know about UO's style of communication, but not being 3d is a major drawback, even if the communication is better. Btw, they do put in "select next player" and "select next item" keys for a reason you know. One is for finding corpses/odd items, the other is for finding out where a certain person is that just said something.

Think about it, the only time you'll have a hard time finding someone is when you are in a crowded town. And if you are in a crowded town, it makes sense that it'd be hard to find the person you want to trade with. I guess the best advice I can give is not to be a town homie and explore around elsewhere. Contrary to popular belief, you will *not* find the best armor in an auction.

Mules are not supported by Turbine. It's one of those things that no one talks about, but everyone knows fully well what is going on.

About it being a pain in the rear to mule at peak hours.. Listen, if you don't have the smarts to run far enough out of town and then mule, you're like the guy I stole a 12K shirt off of the other day on thistledown.

I basically like to run up and grab the item and when they log in with their mule, tell them that I'll be handing this item to the nearest advocate, who is required to immediately drop the item.

Or I'll run into town and say "Everyone, I'm getting ready to drop an item. First one to pick it up gets it!"

Heh. I really don't do that, but I think I probably should. First off, muling is not endorsed by Turbine, so I don't know why they'd all of a sudden "cater to the customer's needs". Secondly, they like the fact that there is no bank because they claim it "promotes giving stuff to your vassals".

That's all fine and dandy, but I'll keep muling, outside of town of course.

Lastly, about houses, they are horribly disgusting. I wouldn't mind if they didn't put them in at all. In fact, I would appreciate it if they didn't. Unfortunately, this is something Turbine themselves wanted in the retail version of this game, and they'll eventually implement it.

So thanks a lot guys, because of your whines and moans, we are now going to have a really pathetic looking game where houses clutter the hillside.

I'm not blaming this on everyone, just the few folks that spend more time whining about what's wrong than figuring out ways to get around it and benefit.

Raize
