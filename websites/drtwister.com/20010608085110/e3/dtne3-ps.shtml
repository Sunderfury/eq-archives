PlanetSide

This game is going to rock - but at the same time, this game is about to match up with it's toughest competition. No not Asheron's Call or Ultima Online. Not even Shadowbane is in the same realm as this game. PlanetSide has to confront Unreal Tournament and Quake Arena. More accurately, the First Person Shooters who play them.

The number one thing that stands before PlanetSide is the monthly subscription fee. The gamers who play FPS style games have yet to experience this type of service. To them, it's as easy as logging in and fragging a few people. The ability now to endlessly frag, keep stats, evolve and develop your character all while managing a stat based ladder system will no doubt appeal to the hardcore fans of FPS, but will they be attracted enough to spend money each month?

PlanetSide has a formula that could by far be the next evolution in online games. At the same time, it creates a world where the egos have an opportunity to shine. All while maintaining a seemingly non-modulated gaming experience. This is the second concern that PlanetSide will have to face.

In today's world of online play with games such as UT and QA, the players have grown accustomed to mods, skins, hacks and codes that will do everything from enhancing the way the game looks and feels, to actually targeting and shooting any player that comes within your screen view. This poses a new threat not only to PlanetSide, but the rest of the Massive Multiplayers on the market. A similar comparison could be made to GEAR or SpeedHack that was originally developed for Counter Strike and was modified to work with Asheron's Call. See just like PlanetSide is a game unlike any other FPS, the gamers who play shooters are unlike any players Massive Multiplayers have seen.

PlanetSide has one chance of getting it right. If the development team does, it will succeed. These players however are not as forgiving as the Role Players multiplayers are used to dealing with so far. In addition, the majority of the cheats, hacks and mods come from these players. We all know that the server/client relationship is much different than Quake or Unreal, but we also know what has happened in the past when games boast of unbreakable code. It will happen, and Sony Online needs to be prepared to deal with it.

I'm really looking forward to PlanetSide. The graphics are wonderful, play is smooth, and game concept perfect for doing something new. The last thing I'll be interested in watching for, how quickly a server is dominated by those leet clans.
