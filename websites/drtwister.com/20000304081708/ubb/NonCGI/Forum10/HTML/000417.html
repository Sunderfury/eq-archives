Sorry Artemis, I can only assume you are asking for the exact formulae which probably nobody outside of Turbine knows. If you're *not* asking for the exact formulae, then you might try rephrasing your questions.

For example, I'm not sure what you mean by "skill=defense formulae". "Magic Resistance" skill is a modifier that gets applied whenever a creature casts a spell at you. You must keep in mind that the skill level of the creature/person casting the spell AND the level of the spell are also used in determining how well you resist.

I notice by your comment that you imply you resist lower level spells easier than than higher level spells, however, this is NOT the case. Higher level spells are more resistable. The reason it *seems* as though you resist lowever level spells is because the creatures casting the lower level spells have less skill. If you are able to resist a Drudge Lurker level 4 spell 50% of the time, you would probably only resist a level 1 spell (if they cast a level 1 spell, which they dont) 20% of the time.

From years and years of dealing with online games I came to the conclusion that the term 100% never really means 100% of anything, in fact, in most cases it comes closer to 75% because there are always outside factors that affect any skill that modify the actual percentage.

A good way to test for a formula would be to find out what the highest level a skill can get up to then figure out what percentage of the skill you have, the exp you give, and the exp your patron recieves (or vice versa).

In actuality however, this is probably impossible to tell since there are so many variables involved.

Try this test out though. Find a creature that gives you 100xp (or any round number) then see how much of that 100 xp goes to your patron based on your loayalty and their leadership. Then take the average air velocity of an unlaidened swallow and multiply it by the square of the distance a person would travel if they were walking in a straight line from Tou Tou to Shoushi. Add the distance of the Dereth coastline subtracted from the number of *******s running around complaining about people "kill stealing". Finally, multiply the following value you get: If 2 Granite Golems were to start running at full speed, one at 1:30 Am (est) and the other at 1:32 AM (est) from one hundred kilometers apart, directly towards eachother. How long would it take a deaf mute in Singapore to hear them?
.... and you should have your answer!
