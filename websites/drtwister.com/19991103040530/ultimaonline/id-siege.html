City under Siege by - Excalibur Bane

Well, I didn't really know where else to put this so I thought i would put it here. Imagine an event, an OSI event of massive proportions. A GM dressed in a black robe robe would lead a massive army of the undead to lay siege to the city of Trinsic. The GM would be stripped of most of his powers, perhaps the ability to kill a single player once every 24 hours just by pointing at him. (Or something equally interesting :) He'd have otherwise virtually the same statistics as your average Lich Lord. He would ride atop a nightmare and sit at the back of the vandguard of his army, watching as his minions cut down warrior after warrior.

The Town Cryers would not be alerted for this event. The main force of the undead army would consist of zombies, skeletons and ghouls with a medium sized contingent of skeleton knights and lichs to reinforce their weaker siblings. This would not be a small disorganized army like previous events where players in mass flocked to the event site and massacred the monsters. It would not be hundreds of undead, but thousands! As soon as the undead begain their siege of Trinsic the city guards would be turned off and a magical barrier would encase Trinsic, preventing recalling into the town as well as gates. Teleport would not function either. You could still recall out if you wished but you could not gain access to the town by magical means. Banks and Inns would no longer function. Those inside would be the only hope for defending Trinsic and it's citizens once the attack begins upon the city. Hopefully they can unite and make a stand against the onslaught that is sure to come..

The undead would pour through the West and South gates, slamming into the hastily organized wall of warriors encased in plate, chain and ringmail. Atop the arch of the West gate archers would be raining down arrows upon the hordes of undead while alchemists hurled explosive potions into their ranks. Meanwhile in the street below, the zombies would be ripping the flesh from the wall of warriors that has been assembled to block the horde from gaining open ground and bursting through into the city. The warriors themselves were not without their own victories, far from it. They too were cutting down their fair share of the undead, one after another until it looked like the corpses of the dead could go no higher and still more of the rotting corpses slumbered forward, a strange blackness visible inside their hollow eye sockets. Warriors fell on the side of light but the healers stood somber behind the great wall of iron and steel warriors, bandaging the wounded and providing what aid they could. The few great mages stood even further back from the frontlines whispering their words of power that sent great fireballs streaking at the undead, lightining bolts struck down from the heavens and some even summoning great elementals from the very cobblestone of the streets and from the streams that flowed through Trinsic.

If the defenders lose this battle, and there would be few of them because of the surprising nature of the event, the undead as well as the Shadowlord GM would occupy the town. As soon as they reached the center of the town, all remaining NPC's would be instantly slaughtered and would not regenerate. The brownish sand color stone of the town would twist into a tainted black (A small client patch would be needed before hand with no explanation of it's purpose of course.)

The Shadowlord would erect a small, powerful obelisk in the center of town to maintain the decayed state of the city as well as the magical barrier that prevented entrance to the city by magical means. The Shadowlord would be linked directly to this artifact, if he perishes, the artifact shatters and everything reverts to normal. He would sit on his throne near the artifact and direct the defense of the city. (It would always be a human GM, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. They could just cycle the GM's every 3 or 6 hour periods). All entrances into the city would be heavily defended by the undead as well as a sizeable force of Lich lords to guard the Shadowlord and the artifact. Patrols of undead would frequent the city streets to mop up any wannabe heroes.

In essence, the city of Trinsic could be considered lost. For the first time in UO, people would feel a real loss. The loss of an entire city. Murderer and Great Lord would set aside their differences and unite to fight a common foe and restore their beautiful city...or would they? Perhaps the Shadowlord has other plans and invites the criminals and murderers of the land to share his haven and help defend it from the armies of light. An entire shard would be split into two factions. That would be rather interesting I think. Anything is possible.

It would create a unique scenario and for once, present us with a tangible fore and an achievable goal. Something that was sorely lacking in other events. The beaten and battered defenders would retreat into the forests surrounding Trinsic to regroup and organize their counterstrike. (Warriors of Free Trinsic? :) Warriors, mages, smiths, alchemists, assassins and healers would come from all corners of Britannia bringing supplies and offers of their services to free the great city of Trinsic. You could extent the magical barrier for a good pace so that people would have to travel on foot to Trinsic to help out, or even block recall in the entire world until the artifact was shattered.

That's more or less a brief summary on the idea, I've been tossing it back and forth for a good year now adding to it and refining it a bit. It'll never happen but I figured some of you might be interested in hearing it :)
