My 2 cents.

This is the dumbest way to power-level ever thought of by anyone. First, does your archer have melee defense? Mine do, it don't raise much if nothing is beating on you. Beyond that this helps your healing skill 0, I like my melee defense and heal skill. :]

I don't know if you're really getting that much xp per kill due to the fact that there aren't that many beasties to shoot.

There are basically two forms of xp: First, from the kill, second from skill use. [Why a fletcher can level, for example.] While you are defintely drastically increasing the per-kill xp you are drastically decreasing the skill use xp [you crit less often, obviously]. Basically, what that boils down to is that it doesn't matter what "level" the things you are killing have, what matters is their missile defense rating. As long as their missile defense is higher than your skill by a little bit [not a lot! you have to have reasonably good chances to cause a critical hit for skill gain sake] skill gain is high, and no points of xp need be spent. That's exactly why at low level it doesn't make any difference whether you shoot banderling scouts or hunter shreth, both their missile defense ratings are very similar and are going to raise your skill about the same [though, the scout will carry you a little further even though the level is lower!]

I guess my perspective is unique, I could give a damn about your level -- I want to know the skill/level ratio. But, the moral of the story is that if you're camping that place you are half as good as you can be. In my own experience the fluctation of the missile defense values of the various monsters [ever amoungst their own type] forces you to be very migratory in that you must keep finding stuff with the right amount of defense to kill [same applies for melee guys] which basically means I'm never hunting the same places two days in a row [exception being that I frequently help friends obtain certain items, but hey that's what alliegences are for!]

As far as I can see, it doesn't seem to matter whether your opponents missile defense is 10 pts above you or 30, skill gains are the same.. 'cept you usually get killed more cuz of the higher creatures supporting stats [more hp, stamina, whatever]. In terms of things drastically higher [otholi], you're losing serious xp because your ability to crit them is seriously reduced. I've seen all kinds of crazy stuff -- level 9 chars attacking lugians, etc. Lugian killing does NOTHING for a level 9, yes you get XP, you get no melee defense [cuz if the damn thing hit you once you would be nothing but a stain on the floor of the outpost]. If I lost anyone here I'll apologize now, getting tired, hopefully I helped someone.

So what do you hunt? Look at your attack skill and your melee defense, and find the best match. It's so damn character specific that there ain't any point on me telling anyone anything without knowing digits. :]

If the resulting combo yields some lower level numbers on the beasties, don't worry about it too much -- repairs are needed in one of your primary skills. Hunt the lower creature to avoid artifically inflating your level while you're repairing a bad skill.

Now, none of this applies to anyone that does not use melee defense, obviously. Perching is the best thing to do if your a mage without melee defense, but then again who the hell really likes to perch? Make a wish, ye might reincarnate as a pigeon.

- Syn

Now, for melee defense. I plan on getting it right after I get item which is soon. He's level 6 now. (yes, I jumped the gun with my original post.) In approximately 12 hours of play time he WILL be lvl 14 easily. I've played him 2 1/2 - 4 hours. Mostly preperation and learning spells.

When I get melee defense, I will use a weapon other than a bow. Raising both skills at the same rate. What causes these skills to raise is the amount of damage that the skill avoids, and likewise for weapons, the amount of damage dealt by the weapon skill. The exact formulae I have no clue. But I've found this to be very true. If for instance I am fighting a drudge I'll evade till my heart's content and no skill increase that is noticeable. But one hit evasion from a granite golem will raise my skill dramatically. Now as long as he hits me there's no skill increase. It only increases when it's used, or when you evade due to the skill. I am very good at skill progression and advancement. There is a trade off. Your skills experience gives approximately 10% as much ap (assignable xp) as a kill does. So you must balance both. If, for instance, you found some neato macro that used a skill periodically, say 15 mins apart, by a day's end you may have gained your first 5 levels, but you will have very little ap to assign. Now, had you fought creatures that entire time, you would not only have about the same skill experience, but you would also have used other skills, at least quadrupled the ammount of xp total gained, have about 200% more ap to assign, etc, etc..

I am not going to take the time to sit down and figure the exact formulae since it's not necessary. But it does exist, and I have seen it in action. I've created two characters, macro'd one to lvl 5 exactly with one skill. Took about 4-5 hours of game time. Then made a second character and he was at lvl 5 in less than 3 hours. The difference between the two was astounding. This would theoretically prove true at any given level. Usage does make a difference, but using them during regular gameplay is the only efficient way to raise them.
