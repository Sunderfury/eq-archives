Great Ideas for UO by - Mazrim Taim

Dear Origin Systems Incorporated and staff,

A person going by the name of spirrow posted a message on Dr. TwisTer’s
message board. He stated that OSI had replied to him saying that " it would
take at least 250 people e-mailing and asking for something new in the
game." Here is a short essay I have written to show how much we care about
this game. Creating worlds and playing god are no easy feat. This is why we
petition you to make the game better for us, the customers. I applaud you,
you have done something that is a great work of art. We all love this game
and do not want to see it sink into a hole. Following are a few problems,
proposals, and solutions.

Housing problemSolution:
Make apartments. Put them on a separate map like Green Acres. Put a
tele-port in the bank. Use your key to your apartment on the tele-porter and
you’re in your apartment. This could be elaborated more. Like if you have an
apartment you may not own a house. This would appeal to the merchant class
because they would not have to leave the city to go to their homes and they
could easily access their storage space while spamming at the bank.
Make house boats. Boats already have a lot of space, but another alternative
would be to cover it. Sort of like a pontoon boat. While I am on the subject
of boats. What is the matter with having a forge and anvil on a boat. I’m
sure on any good pirate ship there would be one for making weapons andarmour.

HaircutsSolution:
Make barbering a skill. When a barber uses the barbering skill a menu pops
up to show what style you want. As an extra bonus make an area in the window
for gratitude sort of like the current trading window. This would be a
little more realistic compared to this next proposal. Barbers could make
hair coupons, which already exist and sell them to players for modest
prices. Such as 50gp. You, OSI, could make hair grow. It could be set up as
to grow in the order of Short->Curly->Pony tail-> Long hair. Something
similar could be made for facial hair.

Weapon problemsSolution:
Add a few new weapons. Throwing knives would be great. Roleplaying thieves
would definitely use them. Throwing knives of course would have to do more
damage. If they don’t know one would even consider using them. It would be
hard to differentiate between daggers and throwing knives. Just naming them
different, but using the same graphic would be fine. Javalons would be
nice. Instead of throwing spears you could throw javalons. Javalons could be
configured to hit the same way arrows do. Accept a javalon would puncture a
wooden shield. This would make it easier to figure out how you are going to
attack. If you throw it you no longer have it, but a javalon through the
stomach would most likely kill or at least make you stager. A new style of
bows would also be useful. Longbows are in much need as would horn bows be
nice. A longbow as it’s name suggests would be able to shoot from afar, but
would not be accurate, so a troop of archers could hale down arrows upon
their enemies. Horns could be then used for what modern day calls sniping.
They would be collapsible (I wish), good for short ranges, and accurate to a
fair degree.

There is also a realism problem with archery. When you shoot an arrow it
does not go around corners. The only way this could possibly happen is if it
was magic. That kind of bow better be so rare it only occurs in 1:6 servers.
When shooting arrows you should have to fire an arrow where you want it to
land. This means that if a person is running you would have to shoot in
front of them to get a hit. If a person is running away from you, back to
you, then hitting them in the back would be easier. Another funny, but gory
idea would be make arrows stick out of people when you hit them. If you keep
feathering someone you should be able to see the arrows or bolts. Else the
arrows should fall to the ground or shatter. I know OSI will never do this,
but I can dream.
At this moment in time, going by the T2A Guide for the New Age, there are
as follows: 6 fencing weapons (including the dagger), 18 swordship weapons
(3 being plain knives), 12 mace fighting weapons, and 3 ranger weapons.
There are serious flaws in this. In truth there only five half way decent
weapons fencers can use. The best weapon you can possibly have is heavy
crossbow. So long as you keep moving you can dog anyone down that doesn’t
use archery. Then if they do use it, it comes down to who has the bestarmour.
Some weapons have no link to the skill you have to master to use them. I
have no idea how halberds relate to sword fighting. Halberds, bardiches,
spears, short spears, and javalons should be classed as pole arms. Being
able to throw a spear would be nice. A pole arm means you can strike from a
distance. That is why they are so damn slow. The halberds in UO now move at
a slow rate and you can’t attack anything that isn’t right next to you.
Jousting should also be possible. I have no how this could be possible in
this game.

Noto system problemSolution:
Some one on Dr. TwisTer’s board said something like "Why not if you get
murder karma class then you turn red." OSI says that the karma system was
created for npc-pc interaction only. It could be easily converted. If you
drop below a certain level you turn red. Then you must do good deeds such as
quests and killing monsters.

MiscellaneousNew items:
Leashes for pets. Problems with this might be: Can you truly pull three
llamas behind you? Well the llamas would have to be very obedient and loyal.
If not they would just pull you in the other direction. This would solve the
problem of stupid llamas and other animals getting hung up on trees and what
not. Have you ever really seen a person say "llama follow me" and the thing
comply, not run away because it has no reins? Heck no! That llama would be
gone the second you looked away.
Hitching posts. These were mentioned before and I, and others, hope they
will be craftable items. You attach these to your house or in your house. I
believe I have seen hitching posts in Delucia. They were outside the stable
area. It looked like a post with a ring on the top of it.
Rugs. We need rugs to decorate our homes. All the home owners out there are
wanting rugs to cover the floor. There are not many choices for floor
coverings. Chess boards, furs, bed rolls. Otherwise you can’t have beautiful
rugs like they do in the Britain bank. Also potted plants would be nice toown.
Gohlems. These were stated as a possibility in the UOHoC irc board. A
combination tinker/smith/mage would be needed to build these great works of
art. This comes from the idea that they are made from metal and not stone.
This would be more feasible. Automatons were available in Ultima VII why not
Ultima Online?
