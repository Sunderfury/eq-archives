Dr.Twister's E3 Diary 2001
Not Sure They Understand

This is more of a preview / commentary on the development of Artifact's Horizons. You see, I spent a few moments at E3 speaking with David Allen, regarding his plans for the MMOG project and how my network could possibly open a formal communication path with his team.

I've been following Horizons for a while now. I am very impressed so far by their development efforts, and I do feel that it has the potential to be a very good game. So why did spending just under ten minutes with the main man change my opinions of the final product?

They obviously need the benefit of the doubt. Nothing has been released yet, the game is far from beta, and we shouldn't compare a new company to the likes of Origin or Verant in terms of customer support. However even being at an infantile state, it would appear that they already have the confidence to beat everyone in the dirt.

This may be in part to the amount of press they are receiving at such an early stage. It may also be the fact they have a proven publisher, NCSoft, where other games much farther a long in the cycle are still searching for publishing help. Maybe it's the fact that their new publisher now has direct ties to Richard Garriott. Whatever the case is, these guys are just not prepared for what is ahead of them.

In my opinion, not necessarily anyone else's - this team doesn't understand the onslaught they could potentially experience on a successful game like Horizons could be. While at the same time, although you can quickly fix a team too naive to plan ahead, there is also a touch of arrogance that in the long run, will not be fixed, and is probably the most damaging to the player base when the game is finally released.

I'm currently getting a lot of complaints from folks who play Asheron's Call. The comments are in regards to the developers and programmers not caring about the players and too arrogant to admit any faults in the programming. These attitudes usually don't start budding until well into the release of the game and every game so far has experienced it. Usually this attitude proceeds the realization that their game is so successful they become overwhelmed with support issues they were simply not prepared to handle. Once the initial flaws are ironed out, the proclamation of success goes straight to their heads.

I hate to say it, but Horizons may already be at that level without even a completed game. If this quick report does anything, I hope it does this - Artifact, prepare yourselves - understand - research - see how other companies like yours have failed and are still failing today. Take into consideration what the players are saying, what they want and how they want to be treated. Sure sure sure, it's one thing to say it now, it's another to do it now, it's even another doing it a year from release. Keep in mind, the customers play and pay for your game - be prepared to support the players.
