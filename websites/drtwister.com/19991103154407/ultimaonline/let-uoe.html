My Opinion on UOE - By Protofear

Ya it use to let you be able to see hidden people, but now it's nothing more than a handy utility. All the newbies can argue, bitch, moan, and gripe all they want, but I say don't knock it until you have tried it.
All UOE/UOA/UOP does is let you use your keyboard instead of your mouse? Is that "cheating" (OSI's label on 3rd party use)?? I don't think so.

It's just like in flight SIMMs You have access to all your options via your mouse, but your still have the ability to hit hotkeys on your keyboard to access those features quickly. It's handy being able to use a potion or a bandage with one button instead of using that clunky point and click interface. It's great being able to equip/unequip your weapon with one hotkey. Well, that's what UOE allows. That's what players want.

The point and click interface is great for single player games but come on; this isn't exactly a single player game is it?
I tried making a couple of fancy shirts since OSI's latest attempt to stop macroing and I swear I actually got a cramp in my finger. WTF? Hey, I can load up UOE and set it to automatically target the cloth in my pack and save my poor guitar fingers from more unnecessary strain, plus I can also use my time more wisely and do more productive things besides UO. I PERSONALLY wanna adventure in the game not make things like shirts or bows.

The only way OSI can ever truly eliminate 3rd Party Use is by getting rid of the things that make people want to use them and all UOE does is make your UO interface easier to use. It doesn't allow for massive "cheating" as OSI would like you to believe.

Ya see, I have a theory about OSI and 3rd party programs.

Basically I think that OSI wants it to be hard on you. They don't want you to be able to quickly do things as UOE allows. They want you to be a mindless zombie sitting in front of your monitor making shirts for hours upon hours and all the while giving them your hard earned money. If they made things easier for us, then that would result in the players getting satisfied sooner and in the long run would result in them getting less money. That's a no no. So their tactic is to draw things out to make you have to play for longer. This kinda reminds me of the "You must wait to perform another action" message.

That's kinda a low blow if you ask me.

I refuse to play by the rules as long as a company that practices such low tactics is making them. When I say rules I am referring to the User Agreement that all anti 3rd party program users constantly shove in my face.

What's the harm in being able to set your best friend as an object and being able to heal him with one button??

Have you ever seen your friends health drop to almost death?? Well, you quickly cast GHeal and are about to heal him when he realizes that he is almost dead and bolts. Right when your target cursor comes up he starts running for his life while the whole time you are thinking "HOLD STILL DAMNIT!" Your friend ends up dying because you couldn't get that heal in. This results in your friend having to play longer to get back to where he was before. OSI likes this. This is what OSI wants.

UOE doesn't allow people to magically kill you. It only let's you do mundane things like drinking potions, equipping/unequiping, using bandages, and targeting self without using your mouse.

A bonus to all this is that there are FAR more uses for UOE and they have nothing to do with PvP at all.

I can instantly use as many shovels or mortars as I please with the push of ONE button. I can mine a spot dry with ONE button. I can make over 100 GH potions in less than 3 minutes all with ONE button.

Playing UO with UOE is a far greater experience for the player who just wants to have fun and doesn't wanna make a life on UO. I have real life things to do and my UO time is VERY limited so anything that helps me maximize my online UO time is a welcome thing.
OSI: Give us the ability to use our keyboards to do some of the more tedious and repetitive tasks in UO and watch the number of 3rd party users decline.

To the anti UOE / UOA/ UOP users out there:

You could learn a life lesson from this right here: Don't go by what you've heard and what other people have told you! Try it yourself and make up your own mind. Don't let OSI make up your mind for you.
