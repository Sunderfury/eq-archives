Online Gaming Glossary

MMORPG
 (M)assive (M)ultiplayer (O)nline (R)ole (P)laying (G)ame. Ultima Online coined this phrase with the release of the first game of it's kind. Since it's release, dozens of game companies have or are developing role playing worlds giving hundreds of thousands of players the ability to interact with each other within a persistent online world.

Community
 Although open to interpretation, the basis of a community involves any set group of individuals who communicate about any one particular game. Large communities have begun to appear mostly in part due to the number of people playing online games and the popularity of the Internet. A community will share daily experiences, help one another and critique the game all outside of the actual world that they role play in.

Dev / DevTeam
 Short for Development / Development Team or Developers. These are the individuals who create the game. A complete team will handle all aspects within a game. Graphics, sound, interface, character, and game engine are just a few of the essential parts that a development team work on.

Short Terms

NPC
 (N)on (P)layer (C)haracters are the computer generated and operated characters that make of the inhabitants of the game world and assist players throughout their journeys. Guards, Shop Owners and Monsters are examples of NPCs.

PC
(P)layer (C)haracter - usually in reference to an individual who plays a character within a game. MMORPGs allow for thousands of Player Character's to participate and interact with each other at the same time.

PvP
 Player vs. Player is the combat system within a game that allows a player to battle against another player. Most agree that this type of game play is the most enjoyable due to the unpredictability of a human component. To date, the artificial intelligence of NPCs do not supply enough competition to make daily game play enjoyable.

PK / Anti-PK / NPK
 (P)layer (K)iller / Anti-Player Killer are individual players who enjoy killing other players within the game over the typical monsters, animals or other creatures that are there to be killed. However, most individuals that PK do it to unprepared or inexperienced players. Thus groups of Anti-PKs simply play in order to protect the innocent and to hunt the PK. NPK or Non-PKs are individual's who do not participate in the killing of other players.

PvM
 (P)layer vs. (M)onster - Players who enjoy killing monsters in game. This is typical game design. NPC monsters are placed around the game world in order for players to hunt and kill. Most Role Players (see RPer) are satisfied with this activity alone and will never participate in Player vs. Player (see PvP) combat.

RP/RPer
(R)ole (P)layer - To RP is to immerse yourself into the fictional world of the game as if you lived in that world for real. A role player takes on various characteristics such as speech and actions of the person they play in the game.

GM
(G)ame (M)aster - usually in reference to an employee of the game company who participates within the game world in order to help players, handle situations or in the traditional sense, assist with story development throughout the game's time frame.

In Game Glossary

Guild/Clan
 A group of players that join together and play a specific game usually in the same area, at the same time and with similar interests and goals to accomplish within the game.

Guildmaster
 An individual or small group of players who control and manage a guild. These folks usually enjoy the leadership aspect of a guild and several games take advantage of the a guild environment.

Spawn
 The time at which a creature appears within the game world. There are usually hundreds of various spots throughout the world where creatures will spawn. Calculations are then performed in order to place specific creatures in specific areas, and when the creature is no longer present, a new creature appears. Several players will find these spots and use them to gain experience or rare items. (See Camping)

Exp
 Short for experience, it is the term used to identify the level of play a character has reached. The more experience, the higher level your character is thus usually translating into how long an individual has played the game.

Stats
 Specific identifiers that help determine a characters ability. Higher stats usually means that the particular character can accomplish more or survive longer.

Skills
 The individual qualities of a character. Each character throughout the game world would have a different set of skills. The skills are usually determined by the type of character an individual chooses to play. Skill development can be accomplished through several methods all determined by the game's developers.

Level/Leveling
 In the traditional sense, to level means to gain more experience with your character in order to advance. Several players attempt to do this process at an accelerated rate which is also referred to as leveling or power leveling. Finding the quickest method of advancing your character's stats, skills and experience.

Game Help Glossary

Cheat
 A lot of different things fall under this category. The most common is Cheat Codes. These are secret codes typed in while playing or while at a menu in the game. This will give you invulnerability, all weapons, more money etc. In the beginning these codes were left in the game by the programmers for easier beta testing, but have lately more and more become a regular "feature" like any other option. (ie. in "Turok: Dinosaur Hunter" there is even a menu option for the Cheatcodes)

Easter Egg
 This is something extra that the programmers have hidden in the software that is usually activated by some sort of secret code or series of commands. Easter eggs usually do not provide advantages in the game, but are more of "Jokes" the author included for fun. These can range from hidden pictures of the author to changing the appearance of the character you're playing. OSI's development team, definatly has a llama fetish. A popular easter egg in Ultima Online is the purple llama vortex. In Asheron's Call, a print of the Dereth map can be found on some cows.

Exploit
A coding problem that is in a game, usually known as a bug, that a game player will use in order to take advantage of another game player or help them to advance at a faster pace.

Dupe
This particular exploit not only can at time have lengthy discussions, but also has history to go with it. To dupe in an online game is the method by which to take an item and make an exact copy of the same item. The most common desire is to dupe money within the game. This often leads to an unbalanced game world where one or more players have more resources than everyone else. To most, this particular bug is considered the most dangerous in a game. The first cases of item duping went without notice. Today, most games have tracking codes which monitor the game world and flags any situation in which two items appear with the same ID tag. Most cases of dupes have been found on accident, however the desire to find a working dupe by some game players usually results in endless hours of searching.

Macro
Usually an external method by which to record and repeat a set script of directions. Players will then run the automated scripts in order to assist them in developing various aspects of their character. Because of the automated nature of a macro, they can be run over night or while a player is away from the computer. There is controversy to whether or not this should be considered bad game play of if the technique makes an unbalanced game world.

Camping
The practice of staying at a specific spot within the game world in order to fight monsters that return to the same spot and which usually give players high levels of experience or valuable items.

FAQ
 Short for (F)requently (A)sked (Q)uestions - a FAQ contains answers to commonly asked questions pertaining to a specific program or game. This information could be hints on problem solving, general playing tips, or just basic background info on the program itself.

Solve/Walkthrough
 This is a text or DOC file that contains step-by-step instructions for completing a game. Mostly for adventure games.

Strategy Guide
 This is more of a guide to help the player with different tactics and approaches to win the game. This is used mostly for strategy games and simulations, but lately also for action/multiplayer games like Quake2 and Unreal.

HexCheat
 This is probably the most difficult cheat to pull off. The cheat will usually contain a "hex address" and a value to change it to. You must use a hex editor in order to use the cheat. Hex editing allows you to alter the game by changing values stored in the original .EXE or data files. Always make a backup before attempting hex editing. (For more detailed info on hex editing, please read the documentation packaged with the editor).

Trainer
 A 3rd party program that can either be a TSR (Terminal Stay Resident - usually used for DOS based games), or can simply remain running while your game is in progress (for Win98 based games). Trainers allow you to turn on and off certain qualities or enhancements that were not included in the original game. This could be Unlimited Lives, Unlimited Money, Invulnerability, etc. The trainer alters the original memory locations to allow you to "Cheat" where you wouldn't be able to do so without it.

Editor
 This is usually a small program that allows the user to modify files saved from the game. These can be saved games, or .ini files containing game-specific information. An editor is basically a front-end for a hex cheat. Someone has created a program to do the editing for you based on user-defined criteria.

SaveGame
 This is a copy of a "saved game file" from a particular game. This might be a game saved after completing a very difficult level, or simply with modified characters for easy gameplay. These files should be copied into the appropriate save-game directory of the game you are playing.
