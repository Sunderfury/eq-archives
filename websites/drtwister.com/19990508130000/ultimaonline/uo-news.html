Current PvP State
Friday -5:30 pm EST - Dr.TwisTer

I have started to receive alot of emails from good PvPers stating that GMs are giving them a hard time. The current state of PvP is a big question mark. As I reported a few days ago, it appears that OSI has taken the stance, "20 people's opinions are better than 1" - even if that 1 is correct. This basically boils down to guilds complaining about any one person they don't like, just so that OSI will ban that one player. No questions asked, because they would rather lose one player than keep 20 players unhappy.

I have two letters here that refer to current situations based on this topic. Both of these players obey the rules and handle the situations with maturity. I leave it to you to make your decisions on whether OSI is taking the correct stance.

Letter 1 - Attacking a Grey in town is considered a bug.

Letter 2 - Attacking a Grey anywhere is considered not nice.

So what do you think. Should you and your guild friends be allowed to ban who ever you want? Or should something be done to stop this madness? A few players have suggested a PvP switch, others don't like the idea. Here is one opinion against PvP switches, and I have a tendency, to agree. PvP Switch?

Hiding Inside House
Friday -5:30 pm EST - Dr.TwisTer
This is old, but some of you newer players may not realize it. There are several skills that you can perform in your house without hardly failing. Hiding and detect hidden are two of these skills. Detect hidden, you can perform, but will never gain skill inside your house. Hiding is just the opposite. You will constantly gain hiding skill, and sense you hardly fail, the rate at which your skill goes up is alot better.
Duping
Friday -5:30 pm EST - Dr.TwisTer

This was discussed some time ago, and I have yet to turn blue to get into town and test it. Basically it works as follows. If you gate from outside of to inside town, or specifically to a location that is either invalid, or extremely lagged, you could potentially create a time warp. Once open your gate to the laggy/invalid location, walk in. Most of you at one point in time or another have experienced blackness when going through a gate. This is essential. If you hit this mysterious blackness, log out. At this time, you are stuck, and can not return to the game. The server thinks you have vanished and then performs a backup of your character. If timed right, the backup will be prior to walking through the gate - a Time Warp. Several people have written me explaining this has happened successfully to them. With a time warp, you can dupe. Simply take an item and give it to a friend. Perform the gate to blackness trick, and when you log back in, your character will be backed up prior to giving your friend that item. However, your friend will also, still have the item. Now you have 2 - a successful dupe.

I have been told this does work, yet is extremely difficult to achieve. Like I said, my main character has been red for sometime now, and I haven't had the chance or opportunity to set up what would need to be, to successfully reproduce this bug. If anyone can fine tune the steps and get them to work, please let me know.
Multiple House Ownership
Friday -5:30 pm EST - Dr.TwisTer

There may be some use to this letter for a few of you out there, who can't get both character's logged in at the same time. This bug is mostly used to friend another character on your same account, to another house that you own. What this letter stated, was that they managed to get 2 houses under the same character. See if anyone can get it to work, but I would of course caution you, not to transfer ownership over to just anyone.

I stumbled upon this tonight. A friend was leaving UO and I was at his house. He transferred the house to me. The only thing is that I already had a house owned by that character. Doubleclicking on both house signs showed that the same character was the owner, and I could run the house menus. The only thing out of the ordinary is that the first house I owned was public and the second house is private.
