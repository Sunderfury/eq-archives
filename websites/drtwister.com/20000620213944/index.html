Sunday, June 18, 2000

No Cheating in UO2?
Posted: Sunday, June 18, 2000 by Dr.Twister

The VaultNetwork managed to squeeze out a few words from Damion Schubert, lead designer for Ultima Online 2 about the state that the game will be in order to prevent cheating.

Now what is interesting about his comments is that he states there are two major forms of exploiting. First, macro programs, allowing players to repeat their commands over and over, and data stream manipulation, allowing a player to alter what commands their characters actually perform.

The good news for Ultima: Origin is that we have a lot of online experience on this team, and as a result, know a lot of potential holes that could appear in the dyke. As a result, I feel fairly comfortable both on the design side as well as on the programming side that we will prove difficult to hack. The bad news is, of course, that we are a mere 25 or so people, competing against the brainpower of a couple hundred thousand. Those are odds I don't like. Eventually, someone will find something somewhere. But we should cover most of the obvious stuff pretty well. =)

It also appear that the team is taking a HARD stance against these types of hacks and exploits. From day one, there will be a zero tollerance policy.

Take a moment and read all of the comments on the UO2 Vault.

A Look at Dark Zion
Posted: Saturday, June 17, 2000 by Dr.Twister

GA-RPG had a chance to sit down with the guys from Wombat Games and discuss their upcoming online game Dark Zion.
Dark Zion is an online world in which tens of thousands of players form a new civilization on the hostile terrain of an abandoned planet. Players control and are responsible for the creation and actions of their characters, their societies, and the state of the world at large; there are no arbitrary rules, no laws, no governments beyond player influence. Dark Zion is a world that encourages social roleplay, and provides an alternative focus to 'kill creature, get item, gain experience' gameplay.

Take a moment and read the full interview on GA-RPG.com.

Vampire Reviewed on RPGDot
Posted: Friday, June 16, 2000 by Dr.Twister

The reviews of Vampire: The Masquerade - Redemption have been a mixed bag so far. The one by Vincent Austin at RPGDot.com gives the game an overall rating of 7.8. Here's a snippit:
"For me, one of the most lacking areas of the game was the monster and party AI. I could kill a monster and its body would fall at the feet of another monster who would not react because he could not see me on the other side of a wall or open door. The party AI is absolutely awful. It was very frustrating to watch them shoot valuable arrows and bullets into walls when aiming at monsters on the other side that they could not even see. At other times, some of the party members would just stand around while I got beat up on by a bunch of monsters. If the whole party had melee weapons, they would pile up behind each other trying to get to the monster instead of moving around and surrounding the monster. Trying to control the entire party in combat takes very fast fingers, since each time you switch characters, you have to swivel the camara around to find the action. Many times in non-combat situations, my party would get in my way and I would have to click several times to get them to move. Often party members would get caught on doors and tables and I wouldn't realize until I was in the next room in the middle of an ambush. I also found that I had to turn off the party's ability to cast most spells (except self-healing), since they would cast useless spells at every monster they saw, wasting valuable blood. This option got reset everytime I restarted Vampire."

We would be glad if you could inform your visitors about our review - the link is: http://www.rpgdot.com/html/vampire.shtml
