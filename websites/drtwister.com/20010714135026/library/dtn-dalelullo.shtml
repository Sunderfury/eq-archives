Fighting Legends Interview With Dale Lullo
June 15, 2001

Pic 1 - Pic 2 - Pic 3 - Pic 4

1) Dale what is your most favored clan type when you've played Fighting Legends?

I like the Wee Clan. They are the clan that takes themselves the least serious. There is just something enjoyable about playing a small blue character that kicks butt then giggles.

2) Where did your game designers take the inspiration for clan bases? Since they look somewhat familiar to the bases used in the game "Giants: Citizen Kabuto", in structured set-up that is.

Our base designs were inspired mostly from religious architecture in the Buddhist and Hindu traditions. The layout of the huts and core has a similar feel to many classic base-games in that it follows a simple keep/towers radial pattern.

3) Besides fighting other players, hero's, regular units, and doing quests. Will there be any NPC Mob's not associated to players to fight with? If so how will the game designers prevent the Issue of camping spawns?

There will be NPC mobs. As for prevention of spawn camping, we have a spawn-point randomizer and NPC's who wander to alleviate the problem. Camping can still be done in large regions, but not right on a spawn point. (Note: the randomizer is not yet implemented in beta!)

4) Your game / world designers have done an excellent job of generating an epic storyline filled with content. Will Maximum Charisma consider turning these assets into a form of merchandising, such a novels, art books, and of course but not least a dedicated Wee toy line? :o)

We have considered doing a cartoon, books, and action figures/plush dolls, however, right now, our focus is to get the game out in November. Once that is behind us we'll investigate merchandising further. But it's really up to the fans of Fighting Legends. Obviously, if they want merchandise we will not let them down. But if the demand isn't there, we will not waste resources on that avenue.

5) How big is your customer service support team at this moment during beta, and how much bigger will it become when Fighting Legends goes Gold?

At the moment, we only have one Customer Service member. He is currently looking to build the core team. At this time, (Beta) we offer FAQ, and superb email support. The Apprentice and G.M. program is 65%complete, and will initiate sometime mid beta. In addition, MCS will be outsourcing customer service "direct calls" to a non-disclosed company. This additional level of support will be the fourth level of a six level customer service support mechanism.


6) For example, what is the background you seek in a customer service Rep / GM inside or outside of the game? Since your Fighting Legends game site support page cites you are willing to recruit qualified gamers for these volunteer and paid roles.
http://www.mcszone.com/CS/intro.htm

We are looking for people who enjoy helping others have a better gaming experience. Obviously, beta testers are an excellent group to consider when looking for the volunteer positions since they can work from anywhere and the major requirements are a good understanding of the game and the patients to help the novice/new players. However, even the volunteers will be required to complete a training course. The in-house team is a little more difficult to fill as we are based in Colorado and only a handful of our testers are local.


7) Since I read on your general questions about Fighting Legends FAQ page, question # 4. That Maximum Charisma will be utilizing volunteers in the game world. Why is Maximum Charisma taking this approach when your counter-part developers are bailing out of these volunteer type player support programs?

http://www.mcszone.com/CS/faq1.htm


We are accepting volunteers as a value added service to the gamers. Players enjoy being GM's; it's another level of playing the game. It is also, beneficial to the players to have these knowledgeable people around and the volunteer status allows us to have a good number of them available. On the flip side, they are only one of many levels of support that we offer. Therefore, the burden on them is very light and enjoyable. I think in the past it felt more like a job without pay and for us it's a way to reward players for doing a good deed.

8) With your future map creator feature for Fighting Legends, is Maximum Charisma seeking to grab some of that market that "Never Winter Nights" is in part shooting for? With the games ability to generate its own maps and content with editing tools for an environment.

We definitely want to capture the "player-as-developer" audience. Right now our tools are made for use in-house only, so they are pretty rough around the edges. In the grand vision, though, players can even build their own, from-scratch, 3D terrain models for use in engine. That's a ways down the road...toward the expansion pack...but the way we have built the tools they will absolutely wind up in player hands, at some point. Our modular worlds use a simple base-terrain and mini-component system to assemble everything. Players will love it.

9) How many phases of closed and open beta will there be before going Gold with Fighting Legends?

June is our closed beta with 1000 players - we are testing technical aspects, hardware compatibility and game playability flaws. July has 4000 new players added to the existing 1000 and tests for bugs and polishing of game play. August is open beta, with a minimum of 25,000 testers, and will be focused on final polishing and fun factor. By fun factor I mean how to keep the game fresh and exciting with monthly updates/events and what direction the expansions will go in.

10) When Fighting Legends warrants it will the game expansion be based further on the present game world of Exisle? Or will Maximum Charisma take the expansion somewhere else in the game's solar system?

The world of Exisle is unique in that it is a karmic node in the multiverse...a barely real place created by destiny, not matter. Thus, our expansion worlds will probably build off of Exisle.

11) I would like to thank you Mr. Dale Lullo for your time in answering these questions. Are there any closing statements you would like to add to your gamer fans of Fighting Legends?

It's Ms. but I get that a lot.

I'd like to say that with Fighting Legends, we've tried to bridge some of the gaps left by the current selection of games. We believe FL will appeal to the casual gamer as well as the hard core, women as well as men, and the young and young at heart. That people who have never thought to play an online game will enjoy the FL experience. We want to make the game more enjoyable to all, and can only do that with feedback from players. That is why we are having an open beta phase with a minimum of 25,000 testers. We'd like to invite everyone to sign up for beta on our web site at www.mcszone.com.
