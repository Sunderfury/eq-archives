Dr.Twister's E3 Diary 2001
Denied at Turbine

Before I went to E3, I began making arrangements with contacts at the various game companies, in hopes to introduce myself, and chat about some of the upcoming changes that I will be introducing to the site soon. One of the more entertaining meetings was with Turbine, developers of Asheron's Call.

For those of you who may not know, I recently started focusing a lot of effort towards Asheron's Call, due to a few game exploits that Turbine doesn't seem to want to fix. Interesting enough, I only began emphasizing some of these problems when I returned to action on the network just a few weeks before E3 even started.

I coordinated a time to meet with a Turbine staff member, (I won't reveal names in this story, because honestly, I think some of them actually did want to talk).

Once I arrived, before introducing myself to anyone, there was a delay. Since they were behind a little in their schedule, I thought I would just hang out anonymously to see what was going on. I find it's usually a good idea to hide out before coming straight forward and telling everyone who I am.

I began recognizing a few names, individual's from XRGaming, Stratics, Vault - heck the usual 'news' networks, and also a few names from Turbine. I made contact with someone to let them know I was there and ready to chat. They stated there was a delay in the schedule, and they'd be with me in about a half an hour. So I waited. I stood there, watching and waiting.

Lots of people gathered, lots of people walked into the small little closed door cubicle and lots of people looked my way and left. They were showing off the early stages of Asheron's Call 2. No big deal, heck, what could I say about a game that is several years from release? Well the folks at Turbine must have thought I could find something negative about it...

The time came and I was approached with "We're not going to let you in."

I replied, "uh? WHO is not going to let me in? Let me in for WHAT?"

Turbine response, "We don't think it would be a good idea for you to come in."

I state, "Um, I'm just here to chat..."

Turbine, "Well, because of some of the things you have recently posted on your site, we took a vote inside and determined it just wouldn't be a good idea to talk with you."

(Maybe they meant it wouldn't be a good idea to be SEEN with me) Sort of like talking with the enemy uh?

Turbine continued with, "You should expect this type of treatment, it goes with the territory."

I explained, "You're right, it's not a problem with me - if you don't want to talk that's fine. I'm offering the opportunity for you to communicate with me and get things resolved quicker. I KNOW it goes with the territory, I'm obviously making an impact."

There were a few more words said, nothing exciting, but none the less, I left.

I received similar words of encouragement from the folks at Sony Online and the developers of EverQuest. Turbine was by no means alone. The difference with them however was, they were open to the opportunity to communicate and receive an understanding of where I'm coming from. The EverQuest team specifically said I should change my ways and become a legit game site. Different words but they have the same meaning as what Turbine was trying to say - Stop bugging us (No pun intended).

The fact is, I must be having an impact on them. I don't admit to being a news site catering to the developers needs for good publicity. The network is alternative, and just as I understand what I say online and how people perceive me goes with the territory, game developers must understand developing buggy games goes with the territory of what appears on my sites.
