Here's an interesting exploit I just heard of. Works best with high level casters, though really any caster team can do it.

Go to attack the PoF, PoH, Kedge, Nagafen, Vox, etc. Something really tough involving a big group. Bind yourself near the scene of the battle. Dual another caster.

Let's say you can cast 5 Ice Comets on a load of mana. Cast 4 of them at Vox, have your buddy cast all 5, now use your last spell to blast him to death.

He pops back up at his bind spot, not having lost any experience (because he died in a duel), memorizes Ice Comet, /d on you, you accept and he blasts you to death.

You come back at your bind spot, not having lost any experience, mem Ice Comet. By now he's blown his load on Vox, so you /d him, he accepts, you blast him to death and spend the rest of your mana on Vox. He pops back up with full mana, and, well, you get the idea. Since you get full mana back when you return to life, and since you do not lose any experience in a dual, and since you can bind yourself anywhere you want, you effectively have infinite mana to throw endless high level spells at a mob. As long as you have warriors and clerics up there keeping the mob distracted, you can do this exploit as long as you want. In the end you'll end up doing obscene damage, and the great thing is you get wiped off the hate-list everytime you die. Save just enough mana to blast your buddy to death, then have him blast you to death as soon as he gets back. With a number of casters doing this, you get a constant stream of free mana to throw at monsters, and you lose nothing.

Note this works for everyone really. Have the cleric dual a wizard. Heal, heal, heal, OOM, get blasted to death. Come back, full mana, no entries on the hate-list, start healing again. You'll be able to heal all day long and the mob will never get mad enough at you to get off the tanks (who are the only ones that have been alive this whole time).

Note, this also works great in PvP. You and your buddy go and attack some other group, expend all your mana and run away to a secluded bind point. Now kill each other, and you'll be back in action with full mana before your opponets have even meditated a bubble back.

So if you see a lot of dual messages scroll by, now you know what it means.

Incidentally, here's a cookie for those who read this far:

If you want to do the nastier parts of Kedge, the Planes, etc, and want another handy tip, here's one that's not such an exploit. If someone ends up pulling too much stuff and you KNOW your whole group of 24 is going to be wiped out, instead of evac'ing have all the clerics sit and camp. Everyone else just stay there and die. As long as one of the high level clerics safely camps, you're in business. He logs in with a secondary character, and they let him know when everyone is dead. All the clerics that camped successfully log back in, ressurect everyone and you're back in business. This is actually a great deal for some places (like deep in Kedge) where if you Evac out it's going to take too much time to work your way back to where you were. This way your whole group can get back to business in no time, without any real extensive XP loss.

And, of course, if you want to ressurect faster, make sure you get a wizard to camp early too, so he can duel you. He blasts you to bits, you come back with full mana, ressurect some more, etc.
