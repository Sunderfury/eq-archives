Carhan, I thought you had stopped posting here. Glad to have you back.
In regards to spell weakening:
Yes, spells are degraded the more people there are casting them. HOWEVER, spell damage is not one of the things that appears to be affected. Range is reduced, as is duration for those spells with duration. I've got a 7th level mage with 2nd level spells and while I have noticed the change via the message (the magic Surges vs rushes mightly). I'm still able to dish out up to the max damage for the spell category.
As for the Human nature argument, I would also like to point out there is an entire scientific community doing research on various topics that SHARE their findings with everyone else. Why shouldn't that apply to Ashernon's call?
What do you think would keep a newbie mage online longer:
<Newbie>: Wow, cool spell. How do you cast that?
<High level Mage>: Go research it on your own!
(High level Mage runs on to kill other things)

Or this:
<Newbie>: Wow, cool spell. How do you cast that?
<High level Mage>: This is how you do it...
(next 5-10 minutes spend conversing and giving tips on how to stay alive).

Condeming people for sharing information just because you think it runs against the spirit of the game isn't right. Even the Developers have said they expected people to share the spells in and out of game.
Besides considering Sybex went and published all the major starting secerts (like monster stats, Portal locations, etc.) what is the big deal? I personally think it would be more fun to find out about these places by word of mouth (creating a need for more interaction) then to have it published on page xx of the guide.
