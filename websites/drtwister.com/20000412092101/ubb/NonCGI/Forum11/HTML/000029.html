I've been playing with the storebought potions. I can't help but think they're buggy because of what they do and what they cost.

1) Bark potion. Effect: Stalwart Regeneration. Costs my troll about 12 platinum and grants him regeneration which does 2 hit points per tick for 12 ticks. That's right, at level 39 I have 1400 hit points and a 12 platinum potion heals me for only 24 points. It also roots you the whole time. That CAN'T be right. It's totally worthless to anyone who can afford it.
(Suggestion: make it cast a level 24 shaman Regeneration spell and root you. That's an effect that lasts about 4 minutes and adds 4 hit points per tick. That would justify a 12 plat price tag. You still wouldn't be able to use one all the time, but at least it would be useful for emergencies.)

2) Potion of light healing. Effect: light healing. Costs my troll over 20 platinum and heals 30 points. You sure this wasn't supposed to be Greater Healing? What am I supposed to do with 30 lousy hit points? That's a big chunk of change for such a weak effect. Even if it cast Superior Healing you couldn't afford to just chug em down. At least give them Greater Healing.

3) Blinding Step. Effect: teleport/blind. Costs my troll about 12 platinum. It teleports you a random distance but blinds you for OVER A MINUTE. That can't be right. It blinds you so long it negates the purpose of using it. By the time you can see again whatever was chasing you has caught up, and since the teleport is random, it may only teleport you 3 feet and then you're hosed because you can't see. (I suggest changing the blindness to no more than 2 ticks, which would be 12 seconds tops. Just removing the blindness altogether wouldn't be that bad. Paying 12 platinum for 1 charge of Yonder is still a lot.)

3) Reckless Health. Effect: Gives you an immediate +41 hit points and does a DOT that will take away about 50 hit points. That CAN'T be right. It costs about 12 platinum and in the end does more harm than good. And it's only 41 health anyway. What's the use of that supposed to be? (Suggestion: Make it grant 350 health and then do -5 per tick for 70 ticks (ie, -350 health) Still pretty crappy, but would have SOME use.)

---

I love those Pumice Stones (nullify magic) and Cloudy Potions (invis), but the others need looking into. I can't imagine they were given those stats on purpose. I remember hearing in beta about how warriors would buy those Bark potions because it was sometimes worth it to spend the money for shortened downtime. Well 24 hit points of healing isn't worth 12 platinum, so the potion effect must have gotten hosed up somewhere along the line is what I'm thinking.
