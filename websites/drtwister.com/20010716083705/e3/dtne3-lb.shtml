Dr.Twister's E3 Diary 2001
Lord British Meets Dr.Twister

So the day came. The day Dr.Twister would meet Lord British. There was a casual event setup for Richard Garriott and the ex-Origin Systems folks who came together to form Destination Games.


This presentation was mostly about the future of DG and the recent merger with NCSoft developers of Lineage the Bloodpledge. Garriott went on about how Lineage is the largest MMOG in the world, boasting over 2 million subscribers. 99.9 % of which are in the Asian market.

Garriott also touched on Project X, which philosophies are suppose to stem from the original Ultima concepts, but completely different from any Ultima series game. The X standing for the Roman numberal 10 which would have been the next in the sequence for Ultima.

The group also discussed the opportunities for game developers to survive solely on one game title. This of course would mostly be done by raising the monthly subscription rates from the $10.00 we know today, upwards to $20. or $30. per month.

However what we never seemed to get a clear answer on, was if their new focus was on developing one truly unique game, then why does the new company (joint of NCSoft and Destination Games) already have affiliation with 3 online games. The one that was never responded to or mentioned was Horizons, which just before E3 signed up NCSoft to be the publisher. I asked David Allen about this prior to the DG presentation, and he was unable to comment.

Afterwards, "ONE OF A KIND" posters for Destination Games were available, and each of the new crew including Star Long and Carly Staehlin were on hand for autographs. I stood in line, at the end of course, and simply asked Garriott to personalize a poster for me. When he asked who he should sign it to, I stated, "Dr.Twister" - an outburst from the panel could probably be heard across the hall.

Well, he did it, To Dr.Twister from Lord British. It's classic. I'll scan it later so you can all see.
