"U-Lands"
Visual tour of the ruins found around the University of Minnesota's Agricultural Experiment Station in Rosemount.
Leftovers from a World War II munitions plant...



 
 
 
Building "A" - This building is right up next to the five towers.  It has not been compromised.  You can, however, tour Building "C" which served as storage for a bunch of 50 gallon drums
Five Towers - Each Tower has a front and back entrance, two windows, and absolutely nothing inside.
Stone Henge - There are rows upon rows of these structures...  Not all of them are fenced.
Stone Henge - This up-close shot, unfortunately, gives no more clues as to what all this stuff is.
Silo - This structure sits about a hundred yards from the arenas.
Four Towers - Much the same as the five towers.  These, however, have a lot more surrounding structures to explore.
The Arena - Without knowing what they are, I would say these would make excellent rain collecting devices.
The Arena - A number of pipes lead into the arenas by way of a single dropoff at the far end.
Slabs of Concrete -  The most interesting thing about these is that there are railroad tracks, not even long enough to hold a single car, attatched on either side.
