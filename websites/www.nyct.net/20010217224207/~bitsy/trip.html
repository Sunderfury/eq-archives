Sunday and Monday, August 22nd and 23rd - - - - - - - - - - - Vegas, Vegas2la    

"Well, since the pool is surrounded by astro-turf, I'd say the chances of there being a hot tub are minimal." Lexi

Did I mention I don't like Vegas? Sorry, Ken. No offense.

We got up late and went to a breakfast buffet at Treasure Island. I'm not usually a fan of such staples of middle american eating-out fare, but after ten days or so on the road with nothing but burgers and chicken fried steak, the salad bar was a blessing. Caught up and talked about old times with my oldest friend Lexi and her friend Jennifer, who is a sarcastic, spunky, gruff son of a bitch. Loved her instantly.

We explored the Venetian, a new casino, which from the inside looks like all the others but on the outside looks like St. Marco's and the Rialto and tiny gondolas and chlorinated-water-sipping mini-swans. I don't remember any swans in Venice, but that was probably because they were eatten by the locals.

Sabrina and I, no gamblers we, veered off to explore a few other casinos like the Mirage, where we saw the white tigers.

We met back again with them at the Flamingo and Barbary Coast where I was learned Pai Gow real good and lost pretty damn fine. We returned to Maxim's for a short siesta and then I actually dressed up to go out. Sabrina, Lexi and I went to Caesar's for dinner and Luxor's to explore.

Interesting factlet (brought to you by Jennifer):

The Luxor cost $300,000,000 to build. This amount was made up in the first three months it was open.

I mean, think about that, right? One hundred million a month lost in one casino by gamblers. I didn't feel quite so bad about my twenty, but it's still spooky...

"Ghost dance sinners'/messiah in the form of/a one arm retribution/spark spark sparkle in the cinkcaching machine..."

The next day was another buffet, the mood kind of low. We drove through the desert, last natural beauty we would see on the trip. Then the hills of San Bernadino, and the expected LA haze.
