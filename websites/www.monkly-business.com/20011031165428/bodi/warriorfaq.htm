Section 1- Defining a warrior's abilities and characteristics

1. Why do people play warriors?

People play warriors for many reasons. The item-collection aspect is considered by many to be a lot of fun. People like being in the thick of things, like having lots of HP and a high AC, or enjoy the roleplaying aspects of it. Others enjoy the role a warrior plays in a group.

2. Isn't the complete and total lack of spells really limiting?

Yes, very. But people who play warriors either learn to work around that admittedly large restriction, or switch classes.

3. But it doesn't seem like it'd be any fun.

Whether a class is fun or not is totally a personal thing. Certain people enjoy certain classes for reason they may or may not be able to explain. You are encouraged to try every class/race combo that seems even minorly fun until you find the one (or more than one for some people) that you can enjoy the most.

1.1 What stats are important to a warrior? Strength, Stamina, Agility, and Dexterity. The order of importance is up to the individual to decide.

1.2 What do the stats actually do? For the melee stats we have received no formulae from Verant detailing their effects, only generalized statements. Independant testing by warriors and non-warriors alike has supported each of the statements, and at this time all are believed to be true. They are: ---

DEX: how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills; how hard you hit with bows (per Abashi, dex also increases the chance of critical hits)

STR: determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills

STA: affects how many hit points you have, how long you can hold your breath

INT: directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights

WIS: affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers/paladins; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT.

CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular)

AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit ---

Testing has also revealed that stat increases don't have a very large impact on real-world performance. It takes a large increase to see a small difference, and in most cases the trade-offs required to make the large increase aren't worth the minimal increases gained.

1.3 What other stats are important to warriors? Your armor class (AC), your ATK (attack), and your hit points (HP) are all very important.

1.4 What affects AC and what does AC do? Your AC is a combination of your Defense skill, your Agility, and any items you have equipped that give AC. Iksars also have an innate AC bonus, approximately 40 AC at 50th level. AC is a composite skill, comprising both your ability to avoid damage, and in the event of being hit, your ability to mitigate the damage. The higher the better, and warrior armor has the highest AC in the game.

1.5 What affects ATK and what does it do? Your ATK is affected by your strength, your Offense skill, and your skill with whichever weapon type you are currently using. ATK is a composite of your ability to hit, and your ability to do damage. Strength does not increase your ability to hit, only to damage. No stats affect your ability to hit.

1.6 How are my hit points determined? You gain a certain number of hit points per level (starting in the mid 20's and eventually rising as high as 40 per level for ogres with max stamina). At 20 you gain approximately 2x what you received at 19th level. At 30 you receive approximately 2x what you received at 20th level, and at 40 you receive approximately 3x what you received at 20th level. There is also a large HP boost at level 53, and possibly more HP boosts. [more info will be forthcoming on this as the facts become available] In the 40's, your naked HP can be accurately determined with this formula: HP = Lvl*(27+stm/11). Note that this formula is inaccurate at lower levels due to the rising number of HP gained per level. Warriors have the most hit points of any class.

1.7 What is berserk? Berserk is what happens when a warrior gets beat to 40% health or less. Strength increases a bit, thus increasing ATK a bit, but more importantly it enables you to land crippling blows, which can do large amounts of damage.

1.8 Why is my magic resist stat in green? Warriors have innately higher magic resistance than other classes. It goes up 1 point every even level. If your base magic resist is 25, at 50th level it will hit 50. Resistances are more fully explained in a later section.

1.9 What skills do warriors have? Offensive skills: kick, bash (slam), 1H slash, 2H slash, 1H blunt, 2H blunt, piercing, throwing, hand to hand, archery, offense Defensive skills: dodge, parry, riposte, defense Misc skills: dual wield, double attack, bind wounds, taunt, disarm

1.10 What do all the skills do?

Kick: Allows you to kick an opponent for low to moderate damage. This is a secondary attack and functions on its own timer, which is shared with bash (you can't do both at the same time). At level 55 kick gains a chance to stun. Warriors start with kick.

Bash: The slam skill posessed by ogres, trolls, and barbarians allows them to bash without a shield, but it uses the same skill. For other races, bash is a secondary attack that shares its timer with kick. It requires a shield (except for large races) and does low to moderate damage, plus has a chance to stun the monster, causing it to stop moving/attacking for a couple seconds as well as interrupting its spellcasting. Large races start with slam (at zero skill), bash is gained at level 6.

1H Slash, 2H Slash, 1H Blunt, 2H Blunt, and Piercing: These weapon skills are used for weapons of the appropriate type. Right-clicking and holding on a weapon will show you what type of weapon it is. With the expansion Ruins of Kunark there are good warrior-usable weapons in all 5 categories. All weapon skills are available for use at level 1.

Throwing: Of limited usefulness to warriors, throwing is your skill with thrown weapons, such as throwing daggers, shuriken, and throwing boulders. Thrown weapons typically have much shorter ranges than arrows, but shorter delays as well. Available at level 1.

Hand to Hand: This is your ability to use your fists as a weapon. Even with very high hand to hand skill, a warrior cannot duplicate a monk's fists. This skill is of very limited use to warriors. Available at level 1.

Archery: This is your skill with a bow and arrows. This is a highly useful skill to posess for pulling purposes, though technically your ability to hit doesn't matter, because a monster will attack you whether you damaged it or not. On PvP servers, it's much more valuable to have a high archery skill. Available at level 1.

Offense: This is your general ability to attack and do damage, which is applied in conjuction with your weapon skill and strength to determine your ATK rating.

Dodge: This is your ability to dodge an attack. It is not affected by your AC. Dodge is gained at level 6.

Parry: This is your ability to parry an attack with a weapon. It is not affected by your AC. Parry is gained at level 10.

Riposte: This is your ability to riposte an attack (parry their attack and get a free attack in on them). It is not affected by your AC. Riposte is gained at level 25.

Defense: This is your general ability to avoid attacks and mitigate damage. It is applied in conjuction with your agility and your armor to determine your AC.

Dual Wield: This is your ability to use two weapons at the same time. The formula is (LVL+Skill)/600, so a 20th level warrior with 105 dual wield skill has a 20.8% chance of swinging his second weapon each time its delay cycles. The delay of the primary weapon does not affect the secondary weapon. Dual wield is gained at level 13.

Double Attack: This is your ability to attack twice with a weapon per delay cycle. It is slightly easier to double attack than to dual wield. The formula is (LVL+Skill)/500, so a 20th level warrior with 105 double attack skill has a 25% chance of double attacking on each successful attack. Double attacks with your offhand are almost unmeasurably rare until sometime around 150 skill. Double attack is gained at level 15.

Bind Wounds: This is your ability to bind wounds. It requires one bandage per attempt, and the bandage is consumed. Bind wounds never fails unless you are interrupted during the process. You heal yourself (or your target) Skill/4 HP, rounded down, per bandage. Warriors begin with the ability to bind wounds.

Taunt: This is your ability to taunt a monster to cause it to switch targets to you. This is an extremely valuable skill. If you successfully taunt, the monster switches to you regardless of the damage or taunting of others. If you fail, nothing happens. Taunting a monster that is already attacking you is useless, whether it succeeds or not. Warriors begin with the ability to taunt.

Disarm: This is your ability to disarm your target's weapon. This is of virtually no use except when dualling or on PvP servers. Even with very high skill it is very difficult to disarm, and you can't disarm magic weapons (on monsters). Disarm is gained at level 35.

1.11 What are the skill caps? Before level 51, all skills for warriors rise at 5 per level, with an additional 5 to start. The formula to determine max skill is (LVL*5)+5. (LVL+1)*5 also works. After level 51 when the skill caps are increased, some skills gain more than 5 points per level and some gain less. [Post 50 skill increase information to follow at a later date]

The caps are in Pre-50/Post-50 format:

1H Blunt, 2H Blunt, 1H Slash, 2H Slash: 200/250

Archery: 200/240

Defense: 210/252

Offense: 210/252

Piercing: 200/240

Bind Wounds: 100/210

Kick: 149/210

Bash: 200/240

Dodge: 140/175

Parry: 200/230

Riposte: 200/225

Disarm: 200/200

Taunt: 200/200

Double Attack: 205/245

Dual Wield: 200/240

Hand to Hand: 100/200

1.12 Which skills do I need to train before I can use them? You can use any weapon skill by just equipping a weapon of the desired type and going into battle. To gain weapon skills you need to be fighting something from which you can still get exp.

Some skills require you to first invest at least one training point in order to be able to use and/or advance them. Go to any warrior trainer and right click on them, scroll down the list to find the skill, and train as desired (you get 5 training points to start with and 5 more each time you level). You cannot Dodge, Parry, Riposte, Double Attack, Dual Wield, or Bash unless you have trained them at least once. Note that large races can slam from the beginning, but with a zero skill. When they gain bash the slam ability starts using the bash skill. You can use the Taunt skill without training it first but you will be unable to advance the skill, so you should definitely place at least one point in Taunt at a trainer.
