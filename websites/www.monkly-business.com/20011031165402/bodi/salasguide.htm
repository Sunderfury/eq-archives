Salas's Guide to being a Ranger

Tips for being a Ranger:

As a ranger you are expected to know your way around because a ranger should always have a good sense of direction, so here is some help so you don't get lost (which can help even if your not a ranger.) First the very best way to not get lost, is every where you travel to remember things you pass such as a building or a statue or clump of trees or a clearing or anything that will help you know that you've already seen this before and as you go over a zone you'll soon know all the short cuts all the places to sell and fight which helps you and anyone who asks you (also making you look like an expert ranger).

Another thing you can do is if you are holding a weapon with a handle and a point end (even a mace will do) then throw it to the floor and look at the way its pointing which ever the way the point end points to is true north and this has helped me before so it does work and is useful unless you haven't got any weapons.

Another thing you can do is just follow paths but that won't teach you anything about traveling even if you don't follow paths and you get lost you will learn where your going or will know that , that way is a dead end or a waste of time etc. The final best way to know where things are is to use location which you can make a quick button, by making the command as /loc in the window if you right click on a button you don't use such as anon. When you press this button it will give you your longitude and latitude (example -3918.46 +409.83 or +1094.04 +5038.94 and so on). So now what you do is stand outside a shop you may wish to return to and press your button (/loc) and write down the numbers then if your somewhere else in the same zone you can just keep pressing the loc button and watch the numbers go up or down then just walk in the direction in which the numbers will meet the same as you've written down and there you go you will be back to the shop you wanted to return to. Then of course you've got your trusty radar your tracking which you can use to find people or monsters or even to track a big monster you wish to keep out the way of and its always useful to know that the big 35 level giant is coming right at you!

A Guide to spells:

You wont get your spells till level 9th so the wait is long but when you get your first spells it bursts new life into your ranger which seems like a fighter till now. First thing you must do at level 9 is return to your guild master and learn all five spell casting categories Abjure, Alter, Conjure, Divinate, and Evoke and all will be used very soon so it is worth using those five level points to get all five skills.

All your first spells will fit into your casting slots so put them all in as you will use them all at different times. Rather then go into detail over all of the Ranger spells available at each level, go to one of the links from my page, all of which have spell info on them.

A Guide to Weapons:

As a ranger you have one of the best array or weapon choice and you can get many unique weapons that many classes cannot use and you can use all types of weapon fighting styles such as two handed, one handed in both blunt and slashing. Archery skill is the best, as we can get criticals with our bows, so a bow is worth using a lot. At later levels we get duel wield and double attack, which not many classes get both of. So as a Ranger you get the best choices of weapon styles. I personally use two whips, but keep my two handed slash weapon to practice with, and now at level 18 i've got a one handed slash skill of 95 and a two handed slash skill of about 87.It is a good idea to use other weapons so to be flexable, as there are many good 2 Handed weapons out there, or possibly to be introduced. Obviously its your choice, so play as you wish, as my Barbarian Warrioir friend Luthior has always used a two handed slash weapon, even though warriors can duel wield, but as he is a stubern Barberian, I will leave him be with his choice in weaponry.

A Guide on Roleplaying:

On this subject you may do as you wish because no two people are the same, but heres a short guide to how I play as Salas. Rangers are of a kind nature but can be very deadly if needed. We should look to animals as friends like a druid as we are also woodsman (or woodswomen,) and should never use animals as a way to gain any experiance and never to hunt them or treat them in a cruel manner after all they live in our woods also. We use our stealth skills a lot to pass through places un-noticed or to hide to ambush a stupid orc or gnoll and we use our survival skills such as finding food which lowers the cost of long traveling or waiting. Finally we use our sense of direction and tracking to find who or what we need more quickly.
