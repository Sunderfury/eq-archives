I.  Mission

SoF is a helping guild.  We like to assist others either by teaching them how to play the game better, or sometimes, providing material or even financial assistance.  But beware, SoF doesn't cater to beggars, we only help those who are willing to help themselves.

II.  Guild Membership

A person seeking membership to the guild must seek a sponsor.  A sponsor must be a member of SoF in good standing.  Each member may sponsor up to 3 apprentices at any given time.

An applicant must fill out an application form in the guild's web page. This will inform the chairperson of the Member Advisory Board who oversees the recruiting process.  Once the application has been reviewed and approved by the Membership Advisory Board, it is forwarded to the Officers to notify them of the applicant's approval for Apprenticeship. The applicant will also receive an email informing them of their approval and will be given a list of the active Officers to contact in-game.

The SoF apprenticeship period lasts four (4) weeks.  During this time, the applicant is responsible for learning the guild’s rules and policies and also to spend time to get to know the members.  The chairman of the advisory board will monitor the apprentice's progress with the assistance of the sponsor.    Apprentices are required to attend a class to learn about the guild's history and its purpose.  At the end of the apprenticeship, and after satisfying all requirements, the apprentice is offered full membership at the rank of Page.

III.  Code of Conduct

Our code of conduct is based on medieval customs - but with a few concessions to reflect common courtesy in current times and the realities of on-line gaming. It is not SoF policy to belittle anyone - regardless of guild rank, age, gender, etc.  SoF will always stand for helping others, friendship, camaraderie among its members, and having fun. We respect the right of other guilds to exist - regardless of their alignment or basic code of conduct.

When an individual or group tries to deny us our right to exist peacefully, we will retaliate appropriately.

Also, SoF is an anti-theft and anti-player killing guild.  Members are required to be non-pvp status.  Members are not allowed to do pick-pocketing, scamming, burglary or any other form of dishonest behavior.   Note that player-killing is different from participating in duels.  The guild does not prohibit its members from dueling if there is proper reason to do so.  Dueling for profit is not allowed.

Violations to guild rules will be subject to disciplinary action as decided by the Queen and/or Prince of the respective chapter and may result in expulsion from the guild.

IV.  Guild Talk Policies

SoF strictly maintains an ethical and family environment in Guild Talk.  SoF member’s ages vary from pre-teen to senior age and we have members of different cultures all over the world.  In online RPGs you have no way of knowing how old, or what nationality or what religion (if any) the other person you are talking to.  Therefore, please exercise suitable discretion and keep in mind that anything you say or do must be ethical and is socially acceptable behavior.  Bashing and insulting other people, cussing on Guild Talk are considered violations of our chat policy and you will be asked to leave.

This policy is also enforced on the Guild Message Boards, Email, and any other public Guild communication area.

V.  Rank and Organization Structure

SoF role-plays a guild in a medieval-fantasy setting.  It has a rank structure that resembles the rank structure of the Middle-Ages.   A new member starts at the rank of Page.  As he or she spends more time playing the game and continue to be an active member, he or she may be rewarded with a promotion to the next higher rank.  See Promotion Criteria for details.

SoF Guild Positions & Duties

Ambassador - A member or members who seek out allies with other like-minded guilds and help further communication between SoF and its allies

Aide to the Ambassador - Assists the Ambassador(s) in their duties.

Royal QuestMaster - A member designated by the Queen, with advice from the High Council, to oversee the duties of the QuestMaster Order on all Chapters of SoF.

Scribe - Each Chapter may have one or more Scribes. Their responsibility is to take notes at weekly general membership meetings and to forward the notes to the Royal Scribe/Newsletter editor.

Guild Webmaster - A person who is responsible for creating and updating the website(s) for the Guild.

Guild Newsletter Editor - (commonly associated with the Royal Scribe position) This person is responsible for writing the weekly Guild newsletter and emailing it out to the membership.

VI.  Promotion Criteria

Promotions are based on the following considerations

 i. Length of membership period with good behavior
 ii. Development of your character skills
 iii. Knowledge of the game
 iv. Helping others, fellow members and non-members alike
 v. Recruiting suitable members for SoF
 vi. Learning, leading and teaching quests
 vii. Outstanding service to SoF

As a member go higher in rank, promotions will be mostly based on the last 4 criteria mentioned above.

Note: Upon attaining the rank of Royal Guard, it is now the member's responsibility to choose from one of the three Orders of SoF. Please review the Organization page for details on each of the Orders.

Members are allowed to have three characters in the guild. This means that any activity or service performed by the other characters would also count towards the promotion of the primary character.

Basically, the secondary characters hold the same rank and privileges as the primary, but it is recommended that the primary character should be the one to attend guild meetings and formal events to avoid confusion.  

In addition, any other character voluntarily revealed by a member as an alternate character shall also be governed by the guild's policies on ethical behavior.  Any violation shall be accountable by the main character even if the secondary character is not officially on the guild roster.

VII.  Membership Status

A member is considered Active if they play EverQuest regularly.  All active members are expected to attend the scheduled meetings.  If a member can never (or very rarely) attend meetings, he or she is supposed to inform the Royal Scribe or the Prince/Princess with the reason why they are unable to attend (e.g. work, not home, time difference, etc.).

If a member is unable to play EverQuest for an extended period of time, but wishes to remain a member, he or she may request to be placed on LEAVE OF ABSENCE (LOA) status, by notifying the Queen or the Prince/Princess.
Any member that has not attended a guild activity (in EverQuest or any other SoF chapter, e.g. The Realm) for a period of 60 consecutive days will be placed on INACTIVE status.  The Royal Scribe will then notify the member by e-mail informing of their new status and the member must respond to the e-mail within 30 days whether they wish to change their status to LOA or be dropped from the roster.   Failure to do so may result to termination of guild membership.

The High Council may grant long time members who leave the guild permanently and are in good standing at the time a RETIRED status.

VIII.  Reinstatement Policy

Any former member requesting re-instatement must submit a letter addressed to the Queen.  The Queen will decide whether to accept or deny the request with advice from the High Council if necessary. If the request is approved, the High Council will also vote on what rank the reinstatee will have on EverQuest.
