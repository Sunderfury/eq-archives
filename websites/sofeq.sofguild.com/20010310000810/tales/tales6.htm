Bucher's Story

 written by Pipp


**OOC: Hiyaz everyone Pipp here.  This is a story that I have been developing for a while.  Its about Bucher Blokk an Ogre Shadow Knight that I play on Mithanial Marr server. I hope yall enjoy it <Grin>**

Bucher stirred from his slumber; he stretched and several satisfying cracks sounded throughout his body.  Reaching for his axe, Bucher smiled evilly as he scanned the area for his first victim of the day. 

It’s hard being a runt; my smallest sister is at least two feet taller than me and out weights me by three hundred pounds.  All the other runts I’ve known, of which there have been few, didn’t last their first five years.  They were killed and eaten by their own families.  “Humph!”  I guess that goes to show that I’m smarter than the average ogre.  I learned early to hide and hide well when my littermates were around.  On those occasions when I wasn’t fast enough I ended up being the ball that got kicked around, or, more often, the “Dwarf” that got beet within an inch of his life.  Luckily I was good at hiding, or perhaps, it was the fact that I feared the Lizard Men that lived in the forest less than I feared my siblings.  At least with the lizards I knew that my death would be quick. They would chop off my head, put it on the end of a stick as a warning to others, and throw my body in a pot of boiling water to be had for tomorrow’s lunch.  My brothers and sisters on the other hand would leave my head intact so that my screams would be a warning to others.

That’s all in the past now.  Now I’m a Greenblood.  True, I’m only a peon now; but that will change soon.  I have every intention of earning the rank of Squire Apprentice within the fortnight.  That is why I spend most of my days here in the Feerott killing Lizard Men for Soonog.  Not only does this give me the weapons experience that I need to survive; but it also raises my standings within the guild.  “Humph!” Guild Leader Soonog isn’t nothing but a fat, lazy slob that has all the peons kill lizards and bring the meat to him so he can get even fatter.   We take all the risks and he reaps all the benefits.  Grevak isn't much better, but at least he only wants the lizards’ tails; and he gives me a spell scroll for my trouble.  “Grevak.”  He is a mean one though.  He doesn’t think that the Greenbloods’ need any more new recruits.  He thinks that the only Shadow Knight that will ever be needed is Grevak.  True, he is strong and powerful, but even he can be killed, of this I am sure.  Of all the knights in the guild Bonlarg, Soonog’s second in command, is the only one I trust.  He is the only one who has actually tried to help me with my training.  I think that this may be because he knows that our ranks are getting weak.  Perhaps he is aware of something that none of the others know about.  He has offered me the Shadow Tunic if I bring him three lizard fifes.  The tunic may not be much, but at least with it I will be recognized as more than just a peon, I think too that this quest is more a test to see if I am worthy of his training.

I hate the desert.  It is nothing like my home, the Feerott forest. I would rather be there right now.  The forest has huge trees as big around as five ogres are.  The scraggly things that grow here in the Desert of Ro could barely be called shrubs.  It rains a lot in the Feerott, which, combined with decades of fallen leaves, gives the forest the sweet smell of decaying vegetation.  There is no smell at all here, just the burning heat.  “Humph! Rain, I have not seen that in weeks.”  The brightness of Ro is almost too much for me to stand; it is blinding.  There is always a fog drifting through the Feerott.  It gives the forest a haunted feeling.  I suppose that is why there are so few travelers there.  Then again, it could be because of the multitude of ogres and trolls that inhabit the area that keeps them away.  Here it definitely has to be the heat, no one in his or her right mind would even think about living here.  Bucher laughs.  I guess that is why the only thing that I have seen living here are mad humans.  There is a river that splits the Feerott in half.  Oh what I would not do for a swim in its cool waters right now.  There are fish in the river, mostly piranha, I should know, they are what I have lived on for most of my life.  Having lost most of the fights at the dinner table to my siblings, I had to learn how to fish in order to survive.  The Desert of Ro, on the other hand, has no ready source of water or food, so a person could starve to death quickly here, if the heat doesn’t kill him first.  The heat of Ro is near unbearable unlike the cool soothing dampness of the Feerott.  There is nothing here.  Nothing here but sands as far as the eye can see.  The desert, in my opinion, is dead.  The Feerott, on the other hand, is teaming with life, a life that I hope to see again soon. 

I have earned a lot of respect over the last few months, Bucher thought.  Only a few months ago I was just a peon—now I am feared.  Per haps it is me the others fear, or perhaps it is The Reaper of the Dead that is the source of their fear.  Bucher caressed the giant Scythe affectionately.  The handle is twelve feet long and the three-foot curved serrated blade is razor sharp,.  The whole assembly glows with an evil bluish green aura of magic.  The Reaper is an ancient artifact that was once carried by the leader of a rival shadow knight guild.  The fight in which I killed him was where I earned most of the respect of my masters.  They thought that no one of my level of training would be able to kill him.  I guess my masters were wrong.  Soonog keeps a watchful eye on me now; perhaps he thinks I want to be guild leader.  My prize for killing the enemy leader, and subsequently allowing the Greenbloods to wipe out the entire opposing guild, was The Reaper.  I advanced through the ranks quickly after that.  Even Grevak, the guild bully, now gives me the respect hat I have earned.  Few ever master the skills required to wield the scythe as a weapon.  I had to search for weeks to find an instructor that would teach me.  Most consider the scythe to be too big and bulky, too slow for combat.  Not I, I have trained hard and long to master the skills necessary and now The Reaper and I are a force to be reckoned with.

Soonog has sent me on another “training mission.”  He told me that this task is to improve my ability to fight under harsh conditions. So, he sent me to the Desert of Ro.  I think Soonog just wants me as far away from him as he can get me.  My mission is to destroy a band of dervishes that are operating out of the desert.  Apparently they are making raids into the Feerott and further into Oggok, my home, killing ogres for sport.   “Humph!” I suppose I should stop reminiscing and get on with it. 

Bucher approached the top of the sand dune with caution.  The dervishes were on the other side; he could smell them.  Peering over the top of the dune, Bucher saw three of the rogues.  One was an outer guard and two were sitting by the campfire talking while roasting a lizard; a tent was sitting in the background.  Bucher lowered himself back down the dune.  While waiting for nightfall, Bucher began preparing the spell components that he would need for the coming battle and checking his armour one final time.  This will be easy, he thought. 

As night fell, Bucher climbed back to the top of the sand dune.  He began casting a Clinging Darkness spell at the outer guard.  As soon as he was hit the guard sounded the alarm and charged Bucher.  As the dervish approached, Bucher hit him with his next spell, Disease Cloud.  By the time Bucher finished casting the rogue had reached him.  Ducking a wild swing, Bucher cleaved him in two with one mighty swing as the other two dervishes approached.  With a swift thrusting motion, Bucher back the fastest of the remaining men back down the dune.  The third dervish started maneuvering to the right; he was trying to get behind Bucher.  Sweeping The Reaper in a wide arch, Bucher caught him with a glancing blow putting the third man out of the fight for a few seconds.  Turning back to the fast dervish, Bucher feinted left, spun the scythe in his grip and caught him with a back swing, taking off his arm and most of the shoulder.  The third rogue, having finally regained his footing, charged in under Butcher’s guard and slashed his leg.  Bucher roared in pain and anger.  Swinging his fist down at an angle, Bucher crushed the last dervish’s neck.  Pausing for a moment to catch his breath Bucher began to bandage his wounds.  Then disaster struck.  While Bucher had been busy with the other three rogues a fourth had come out of the tent, a shaman.  The shaman had cast some sort of weakening spell on him.  He charged the shaman, fighting off the weakness that was settling in.  Pain and anger and hatred were boiling up in Bucher to the point of eruption.  As he ran toward the shaman, Bucher was hit by several more spells; he dropped The Reaper and continued on toward the shaman with his bare hands.  As he reached his opponent Bucher grabbed him around the throat and forced all his hatred, anger, and pain onto the shaman.  This action caused the innate power of a shadow knight, the Touch of Death, to come to life in Bucher.  The shaman withered and screamed in pain and agony; Bucher’s raw hatred was killing the man.  Bucher felt the drain caused by his death touch.  When the shaman ceased to move, Bucher collapsed from the multiple wounds caused by the battle and exhaustion. 

Several hours later, after the magical poisoning had worn off, Bucher awoke.  Not so easy after all he thought.  Limping to the corpses of the dervishes, he began to rummage though their pockets and purses to find anything that might prove to his masters that he had accomplished his task.  Each of the dervishes had a Cutthroat Insignia ring on, that should do the trick.  Bucher also found a note.  From what little of the common language he could understand this note was not good news for Oggok.  I suppose I will have to take this to Soonog.  All of the Greenbloods will need to know about this.  And I suppose that the Krakneks, the warriors’ guild, will need to know too.  This note, if it is true, means that all ogres are going to have to unite in order to survive.  This note means that we will be going to war.
