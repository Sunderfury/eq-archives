A Guide to The Lost Temple of Cazic Thule

 written by Kellen Caldorien

This is my favorite dungeon in the game, which is one of the more popular hunting spots for mid-level players.  Most folks get intimidated here by the sheer number of mob trains occurring, usually within 3 minutes of each other hehehe.. don't let that deter you, all you need is to learn the zone and how to avoid them.  I hope this guide will prepare you a bit if you are going to hunt here for the first time.

First of all this is an outdoor zone hence, Selo's Accelerando, Spirit of Wolf and Journeyman's Boots work here, which is very nice when trying to run away from bad pulls or other player's trains.  The bad part is, if you are good-aligned, the closest binding spots are 1 - East Freeport  2 - The Arena (Lake Rathe).   Getting bound in East Freeport is what I recommend, even though it is farther than the Arena, but it is the safer route if you're running back naked to Cazic for corpse retrieval.

I suggest a level 22 minimum.  Yes you can start as early as level 18, but getting attacked by lizard broodlings and warriors even though they con green already is very annoying and going within aggro radius while fighting another lizard can be fatal, so I think it's not worth going here below level 22 as you'll probably spending half your time zoning back to Feerrott.  The entrance to the Temple can be found in the south wall of the Feerrott zone. Watch out for roaming Ogre Bouncer guards, camouflage or invisibility helps if you don't have running speed enhancements.

MONSTERS & LOOT

Monsters here ranges from level 12 broodlings to level 35 lizard champions, and the level 40 Avatar of Fear.  Most drop fine steel weapons which sell for 4pp to 6pp a piece to vendors.  They also drop coins up to 4pp sometimes, and the occasional gems, research components and large cloth armor (whooopiee!!). What I don't like about this place is that lizard warders and up, will forever be aggressive and attack you on sight.  Nothing more annoying that fighting "green" mobs and this is a place where you'll be forced to, a lot! The following are the estimated levels (as far as I remember hehehe) of the mobs you can find inside.

lizard broodlings, warriors, proselytes (cleric) - dungeon crap
lizard warder - level 18-20
lizard defender - level 20-24
lizard sentinel - level 22-26
lizard herald - level 24-28 (cleric)
hulking gorilla - level 25
lizard protector - level 26-30
lizard justicator - level 28-32  (cleric)
lizard fanatic - level 30-34
lizard judicator - level 30-34 (cleric)
alligator - level 30-33
lizard champion - level 35

The reason why this is a very popular (hence crowded) zone is because it's the only place to find rubicite armor, which is very nice armor for rangers, shaman, and rogues who normally can't wear platemail.  The AC of the rubicite pieces is generally higher than store-bought plate and it is colored red, which looks great as well.  The demand for these items are high and count yourself lucky if you ever get one.  Rubicite is dropped by the rare mobs and as expected, the spawn spots of these mobs are heavily camped.   They are:

Tae Ew Diviner - drops Rubicite Bracer or lizardscale mantle (lvl 25 enchanter)
Tae Ew Templar - drops Rubicite Greaves or lizardscale cloak (lvl 25 enchanter)
Tae Ew Archon - drops Rubicite Vambracers or lizardscale belt (lvl 35 wizard)
Clay Golem - drops Lizardskin Tribal Mask or potion of earth elemental illusion (lvl 25)
Stone Golem - drops Rubicite Helm or wand of pillage enchantment (lvl 30)
Steel Golem - drops Rubicite Gauntlets or wand of pillage enchantment (lvl 35)
Cazic Cenobite - drops Rubicite Boots or potion of Cazic-gate (lvl 35 cleric)
Avatar of Fear - drops Rubicite Breastplate or Bladed Thullian Claws (lvl 40 nasty brute)

HUNTING TIPS & STRATEGIES

Okay, so you decide that this dungeon is interesting and you decide to brave it's depths.  First get a group formed, like I said minimum of level 22 is recommended.  A 2-person group can hunt here (not all classes though, bard and druid (from personal experience) works out the best) but try to have at least 3 people.  The ideal composition is a group of 4 people consisting of 2 tanks, a nuker, and a healer.  

Most of your time will be spent in the Courtyard (commonly called the CY).  This is where all trains pass by as it is the first area you'd see upon entering the temple.  There are 2 spots where you can avoid trains, the NE corner (behind the tree), and the SW corner (also behind the tree).  Having a bard with group invis is a nice to have, so when someone yells "TRAIN TO ZONE!" go invis and wait for the lizard train to dissipate, or just stay in the corners and watch the lizards trot back to where they came.   DO NOT try to pull any lizards from a train, their aggro radius is wide so unless you feel you can handle 3 to 4 lizards at a time, I suggest you let the train pass until the CY is free.

These are my suggestions for hunting spots -

Level 22-30 - When you enter the courtyard, there is a door to the right (east).  This is a huge maze-like room where lizard warders, defenders and sentinel roam.  Setup in one corner of the CY and have a tank pull lizards to your group.  It is easy to get lost here (#1 cause of trains from the maze) so for the first-time puller, stay close to the maze entrance and familiarize yourself first with the area before going deeper.  A ranger or bard is a good choice to pull since they have camouflage/invis and can look around a lizard to see if it has any buddies hiding around corners that are in aggro range and would be unintentionally pulled along.   The clay golem spawns deep inside the maze, during peak times when the maze is empty, it's easy to pull him out to the courtyard since few or no lizards will be joining him when he runs after your puller going back.  If the maze is crowded, forget pulling the golem.

Level 28-35 - There are 4 places where you can go.  The "Throne Room", "Temple", "Gator Tunnel" and "Sewers"

First, the Throne Room - To the west of the courtyard, is a door leading to the throne room.  This is where the Diviner and Templar spawns.  It is a heavily camped room for obvious reasons.  A sole group of 6 here is a must to hold it, unless there are several groups (which is usually the case).  You'd find all types of lizards here, except for the broodlings, warriors, and warders, and lizard champion.  You'll also see 2 hulking gorillas spawn on the first floor.  The second floor is where the fanatics, judicators are and where the stone golem spawns.  That floor is very hard to clear and can only be held by a good group of level 35ish folks.

The Temple (or pyramid) lies behind the maze, you have to go through the maze and find the stairs leading to it.   Invisibility is good so you don't have to fight your way in.  Once in the temple, you'll be "safe" from lizards in the maze.   Groups usually setup in the North side of the pyramid.  The 4 corners of the pyramid are spawn spots, usually lizard heralds, sentinels and protectors.  A cleric, bard, or druid are good pullers here to "lull" one of the lizards so you don't have to take 2 at a time, unless your group can handle it.  BEWARE of roaming lizards - fanatics, justicars and judicators.  The justicars and judicators cast a 250-300dmg Wrath spell and are very deadly.  Running away from a bad pull is difficult since you are so far away from the zone, so a wizard or druid with evac/succor is a nice to have.  The pyramid is a spiral structure and you'd see lizard heralds, protectors, fanatics, justicators, and judicators along the way.  At the very top is where the Tae Ew Archon spawns and it has 4 or 5 lizard bodyguards with him.  For level 33-35 players, the top of the pyramid is a good place to setup and it is a fast spawning spot.  Inside the pyramid are pools of water full of, guess what?  'gators =)  These pools connect to the gator pool found in the Sewers.

Gator Tunnel is deep in the dungeon.  South of the courtyard, there are 2 ladders leading to a room with a well.  You have to dive in the well and swim through a short underwater pass that leads to a cave with a lake inside.  The lake is full of alligators, and you can pull gators from the lake and fight them in the tunnel area.  At the deep end of the lake is where the steel golem spawns, usually you'd find a lizard champion as a placeholder. You have to kill the placeholder so that the golem will have a chance to spawn.  Again, running from here is not easy and the gators are tougher than Lockjaw, do not be fooled by their size =)

The Sewers is also deep in the dungeon.  This time you have to dive in the well found up the ladder at the north of the courtyard.  A huge pool full of gators can be found here and a stairs leading up to the entrance to the sewers.  The play the sewers, you normally setup in the courtyard and your puller (who should be master in swimming) pulls gators from either the pool or from the sewers.  Lizard heralds, protectors, fanatics, justicators, judicators also spawn inside the sewers and can make pulling very challenging =)  There is another gator pool at one section of the sewer which leads to the temple, as mentioned earlier.  One section of the sewer leads to the Avatar room, where the famous Avatar of Fear spawns, which leads me to my next topic -

Level 35+ - At level 35, if you are not yet bored from killing lizards =), there are still spots where you can get good experience.  The sewers and temple can still give good xp and the Avatar room is a place where most high levels hang out for a chance to kill the Avatar and Cenobite.

There are several ways to get to the Avatar room, there is a door just outside the throne room area that leads to an underground pass, and also a door at the north wall of the courtyard that leads to an open area which is full of lizards of all levels. The way I usually take is through the sewers, at the branch, the left plank leads to the Avatar room, and the right plank leads to the gator pool.  The Avatar of Fear and the Cazic Cenobite spawns right underneath the big "green bubble".  Usually a lizard champion as placeholder will spawn and you must kill it to allow the rares to pop.

I think that's it. Obviously this is not what all you need to know about Cazic-Thule Temple, but I leave that for you to discover.  Cazic-Thule is an awesome place to hunt, a very dangerous and exciting place.  This is one dungeon where most friendships are formed and most reputations are destroyed, for players who do not cooperate or do not learn to share (i.e. kill-steals) or worse, causing a lot of deadly trains, will find themselves short of friends when they need to find a group to hunt in the next higher level dungeons like Nagafen's Lair and Lower Guk.  Being a team player and being a helpful adventurer goes a long way in the world of Norrath.  

So with this advice, I bid you good luck!
