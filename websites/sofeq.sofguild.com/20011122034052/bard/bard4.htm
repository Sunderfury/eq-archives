01-30-2001

Assault on MistMoore

written by Wolana

This sonnet was voted as the winner in the first ever sonnet contest held in 2000! Congratulations to Wolana!


The Glorious Assault on Mistmoore

 We gathered in the forest
Known as Lesser Fay.
We planned on going questing
For our Pali’s on that day.

But first we had to get there!
And some ran into grief.
Poor Thrym and poor Wolana
Needed Saithew’s res relief.

Soon many Soldiers gathered
And headed through the zone.
We formed up into fighting groups:
No Soldier fights alone!

The seniors of our party
Took charge and led the way.
They buffed and offered guidance
On good ways to win the fray.

One party held the courtyard
And kept escape paths clear.
Another hit the graveyard
And killed glyphed familiars drear.

 But what we came for needed
Brave souls to venture deep
Inside the dreaded structure,
Dire Mistmoore’s castle keep. 

With daring and with guile
The Soldiers battled on.
We killed the gargoyles bravely,
Each granite-bearing spawn. 

We killed the maids, the butlers,
We killed the gypsies too.
And when young Thrym kept dying
We called on dear Saithew. 

When day at last was dawning
We Soldiers headed home.
The Pali’s carried granite,
The ogres snacked on gnome, 

The druids grouped for portals,
The wizzies did the same.
Young Thrym checked out the ResXpress,
Five markings ‘gainst his name.

The rangers fletched new arrows,
The casters memmed new spells,
The /gu say convo’s dwindled as
Friends said goodbye in /tells. 

Bone weary but exalted,
We Soldiers knew full well
We’d won a greater victory
Than dinging level bell— 

We’d won the fight for friendship,
We’d won the battle true,
We’d fought to help our brethren,
We’d fought for me—and YOU!

That’s what we Soldiers value
When sunset ends the day:
The Fortune that we truly seek:
The Soldiers’ Noble way!
