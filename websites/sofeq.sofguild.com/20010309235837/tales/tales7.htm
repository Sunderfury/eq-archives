01-30-2001

Orcfall in Faydwer

written by Wolana


Letrea Peregrine slung her Trueshot bow over her shoulder, grabbed her jade reaver and headed for the lift to the floor of the Faydark.  She had no time to savor the soft whisper of breezes through the trees that made up the aerie of Kelethin, the city she called home.  Word had just arrived to the guildhall of Faydark’s Champions that younglings from Kelethin were under attack in the citadel of the Crushbone orcs, where they had gone in an attempt to free their enslaved brothers and cousins.

“Grab your bow and follow me!” she yelled to her Elder Brother Leithreas as she passed him strolling the platforms of their airy tree city with his Lady Maiia.

“Maiia, she added, turning to her sisterfriend, “we need your gifts from Tunare now!   Grant us wolf-speed and steely skin, so that we may help our brothers!”

The three sped to the lift, then jittered impatiently as it creaked its way downward.  The guards looked to be on extra alert, patrolling more frequently around the tree bases, stopping only to exchange a word or two with their officers.  Clearly, asking them to abandon their posts to head for Crushbone would be futile. 

Using the magiks granted to them as Soldiers of Fortune, Letrea, Leithreas and Maiia sent calls to their guildmates, urging them to converge on the entrance to the orc fortress so that they could turn back the evil creatures and rescue the young elves who had ventured in too deeply to make their way out safely.

Several brave Soldiers responded.  Baloador, the noble cleric, promised to churn his way as fast as his dwarven legs could carry him from Kaladim.  Tiffini, the sprightly elven enchanter, sped back from the Oasis of Marr where she’d been subduing the cousins of the Crushbone orcs, gaining her instant return from Jalea Regina, the wise and gifted leader of the Soldiers.  Traveling with her was Salley, ogre and warrior, serving goodness despite the misleading fact of his race.

Meeting near the unholy sizzle of the firepits at the mouth of the fortress, the six Soldiers joined their forces together in a unity, re-cementing their bonds of friendship in a group form that allowed them magiks of communication.  Then they entered the dank tunnels that would deposit them on the turf of Emperor Crush.

Emerging from the tunnel’s other end, Letrea and her colleagues saw a dire sight.  Elven bodies were strewn around the tunnel mouth.  Occasional gnome and dwarf forms lay there as well.  Some fortunate souls were standing over their corpses removing their possessions and re-donning them; other forms were still unlooted, presenting a sorry picture of disruption and disaster.

Other elves leaned against the walls of the tunnel or slumped in corners, nearly dead.  Balo began making his rounds, blessing and healing wherever he could.  “Go on if you need to,” he told the party.  “These young folk need my help now.  I’ll join you as soon as I can.”

Echoing from the far side of the fortress enclosure the party could hear screaming.  Then a small group of elves staggered to the tunnel mouth dragging another elf, one who seemed to be kicking and struggling.

“He-help us, please!” one of the elves cried out.  “This is my sister! She was captured by the orc centurions near the northernmost lift to Kelethin and they’ve drugged her or something!  She was their slave but now she doesn’t want to come with us back to freedom!”

“Yes,” another of the bloodied, panting, exhausted young elves said.  “They attacked us.  The orcs make their slaves turn on us when we come to rescue them.  Not just the elves, either!  There are dwarves in there who are so strong we couldn’t subdue them and drag them out.”

Tiffini looked at the struggling slave intently.  Then with a wave of her hand she hypnotized the girl, who thereupon stood still, gazing blankly about.  Maiia appealed to Tunare and then cast a regenerative aura around the party and their captive.  The goddess’ gift in this case not only helped the raiding party recover its strength and health, it seemed to restore sanity to the slave.

The girl threw her arms around her brother.  “Oh Keain!” she cried, “you came and saved me!  What horrors those orcs are, especially the slavemasters.  We were working in the mines all day and at night….  Her voice faded away.

Letrea blenched.  She refused to think of what awful things might have happened to the slaves.  Instead, she began organizing her party, using the skills her sister Wolana had taught her.

“We’re going to kill Crush and D’Vinn, his evil advisor.  Once they’re gone the slavers will lose heart.  Without a leader they’ll lack the direction they have always needed.  When they’re disorganized we can free the rest of the slaves, or help the younglings like this party do so.”

Turning to the youngsters and their former captive, she gave directions.  “You go back to town.  Drop your sister off with your family or with the healers.  Then gather as many strong-spirited elves as you can, casters and warriors both, and return here.  You will have many slavers to kill this evening.”

Letrea watched the youngsters head off down the tunnel, then turned to her party.

“Leith, you and Salley are going to take the brunt of the damage in the first assault.  I’m going to stand back, slow their speed by magiking their feet with a snare, shoot arrows, and watch for Crush and D’Vinn to come charging out.  They won’t stay hidden while they listen to their guards being killed; they are too stubborn to run away.

“Balo, as soon as you are done… “

“I’m ready now!” he interjected.

“Good, then.  You know your job; you do it superbly.  Tiff, you’ve got to keep those guards controlled as long as you can, so that we can take them out one at a time.  Maiia, keep us protected; give Leith and Salley thorny coats so that each blow from the enemy will hurt them as it hurts us.

“Let’s head for the throne room.  Camouflage up, please!”

The party moved rapidly and stealthily down the gore-streaked paving stones toward the palace.  Crossing the bridge, they eased open the door and moved past the blood fountain and into the throne room.

A mage huddled in the corner under a table.  Seeing the party enter, he eased out toward them.  “Please, good druid, a gift of wolf spirit so that I can get out of this place?” he begged.

Letrea turned on him.  “You would let your fellows die here, and hide in corners?  And you a Soldier?” she asked, a touch of scorn in her voice.

“No, no, Letrea!” the mage replied.  “I too uphold the values of the Soldiers!  I was here with a party from Felwithe, all of us hoping to reclaim our brothers and sisters enslaved by the foul Emperor Crush.  All the others are… are…” He hung his head in sorrow.  “Dead.  My pet was the only thing that saved me; it drove off the attackers while I darted in here, not realizing I was in the throne room.  I heard the pet expire, but the guards didn’t seem to know I was here. But I haven’t dared try to get out, because I know they’ll be back!  And our friend and leader Zackary the paladin is coming here; I’ve called him with magiks.  He’s bringing two friends, both barbarians by breeding but not by attitude.  They’ll be here soon!”

“My apologies, good Goosewyn,” Letrea said, bowing.  “You showed great bravery by coming here as you did and your magiks will be helpful, as will the barbarians’ strength.  You can…”

A squeaking sound interrupted her, and a rather squeaking voice followed, announcing itself as a tiny head popped out of the large urn in the other corner.

A gnome eased himself out of the urn, saying “I’ll join… those big barbarians will surely allow a small rogue to sneak behind them to get to Crush and stab him in the back!”

The party couldn’t resist smiling as Prank swaggered over to the mage, Goosewin, and lined up smartly next to him.  The mage waved his arms and summoned another pet, a firey-looking creature.

At that moment two very large women came storming in, identical in their freckles and kilts, but one carrying an enormous 2-handed axe and the other an equally enormous staff.  “Boomshaka and Shakalaka at yer service, mates!” they cried.

“My senses tell me Crush’s guards are approaching,” Leithreas warned.

Boomshaka reached into her pack and pulled out some rather impressive armor, which she donned rapidly.  At that moment another dwarf, this one in silvery shining armor, charged into the room.  “Zackary, am I glad you are here!” Goosewyn said.

Letrea looked at her assembled troops.  “We must be ready; if Leith says they’re coming, believe me they are coming.  Leith, Salley, you stand by the hall to take them on as they enter. Tiff, be ready!  Balo, in the corner where you’ll be out of the melee.  Goosewyn, send your pet with Zackary and Boomshaka to attack; save your magik fires until you know which guards the others are fighting, for Tiffini will be controlling the mob.  Maiia, Shakalaka, you know your roles! Prank, none of your roguish nonsense; you can backstab all you like but only the engaged guards.”

Thudding footsteps made the hangings on the walls flutter, and a group of guards charged into the room.  The teams went into action, blades flying, supportive and attacking magiks making the air sparkle with their powerss.  Letrea sent arrows into the guards when she could, but most of her senses were focused on the space behind the throne.  From that space, she knew, Crush and D’Vinn could be expected to attack.  Crush was simply a brute like so many of his orcish subjects.  Even his shaman and priests, despite their spells, were essentially stupid.

But Ambassador D’Vinn… now he was a different story.  He was a sly and deceptive Dark Elf and he was as likely to slip his dirk between her ribs before she even saw him as he was to stand and fight.

The two parties were reducing the guard troop to bloodied corpses in rapid pace.  Balo was keeping their injuries in control, with Shakalaka backing him up between magiking the enemies.  Maiia was keeping the fighters covered in magical armors and Prank was slipping between the orcs’ legs and backstabbing them.  Tiffini managed to keep the unengaged guards mesmerized, and Goosewyn placed his magikal fires expertly.

As the last two guards slumped to the floor, Letrea cried out a warning.  “The Emperor is alerted, and D’Vinn with him!”

As one, the two parties converged on the area around the throne.  Salley, being so large, could not seem to fit, but Shakalaka waved her staff and suddenly he was just as strong but half his former size.  Leith and Letrea stood on one side of the throne, blocking the path into the room, while Zackary and Salley stood on the other.  The magikworkers stood further back, ready to do whatever they could.

“There’s others with them!” Letrea suddenly warned.  “It’s… it’s… oh no!  It’s the Prophet!  He will keep them magiked, making our task much harder.  And the Royal Guard as well!  First, the Prophet, then the Guard.  Then when they are done for, take D’Vinn and Crush!”

Expecting at any moment to see the brutish emperor and his inky blue advisor, the party tensed and stood alert.  Yet, though the rangers Letrea and Leithreas knew through their extended senses that the enemy was close, and Maiia’s druidic senses confirmed it, no enemy appeared.

Finally Letrea said, “I’m going up into the Emperor’s private tower room and draw him out.  Be ready to support me, because I shall be returning hurt, I’m sure.”

Up the ramp Letrea darted, her bow ready, hoping to draw one of the four vile opponents down toward her.  Suddenly she was shocked by a bolt of ice particles penetrating even the ivy armor she wore.  Shaken, she nonetheless drew back the arrow and let it fly into the Prophet.

The high shaman roared in pain and anger and charged at her.  Letrea backpedaled down the corridor, then turned and ran for the throne room, hoping her speed, a gift from Maiia, would get her there before he could cast again.

As she rounded the edge of the throne, Salley leaped between her and the orc, swinging his giant axe.  Leithreas joined the fray, hitting the Prophet as Tiffini magiked the other three.  Letrea backed away to regather her senses and strength, but was stunned by a blow from perhaps the largest orc she had ever seen.

“Move, Let,” Tiff cried urgently.  “Crush is on you!”

The rest of the team members moved in, while Balo tended to Letrea.  The ranger, however, brushed the dwarven healer aside impatiently.

“There’s still one more to come,” she said urgently.  “Watch for D’Vinn!”

As though her words announced him, the wirey blueskinned Dark Elf slid into the space, his dirk in his hand and a haughty sneer on his face.  He appeared to be headed through the throne room to the hallway and the outdoors, then wheeled and sank the dirk into Tiffini’s side.

“No! Not Tiff!” Letrea screamed when she saw her adopted baby sister fall.  Ignoring the Prophet, Crush and her own injuries, Letrea grabbed up her reaver and began swinging, her pain and grief lending strength to her arms while blinding her to the strategies she had devised.  She engaged D’Vinn with a double swing of the powerful weapon.  D’Vinn riposted and stabbed at her repeatedly.  Already weakened, Letrea was about to fall when Zackary called on his god for power and touched her, laying on his hands to heal her.  Then he grabbed up his weapon once more and joined her.

Fortunately, Leithreas had managed to get the Prophet down to his last few measures of health, and Salley was dispatching the Royal Guard, while the barbarian twins were controlling the Emperor and slowly defeating him.  Goosewyn was husbanding his magikal resources and placing fiery bolts with care, while sending his pet first to help Leith then to help Salley.

Meanwhile Maiia used her gifts to restore health to the battlers as Balo tended to Tiffini, bringing her back from the brink of death.  She staggered to her feet in time to watch all four of the foes fall at once, a quadruple victory for the Soldiers.

The noble Soldiers came together in the center of the throne room, battered, bleeding, but triumphant.  Using their magiks, they sent messages to the younglings in Kelethin and Felwithe, and even in Kaladim, to rally together and come rescue the slaves.  Then they began healing each other as best they could, pausing to regain the magik strength they had expended in the battle.  Salley and Boomshaka stood guard, vigilant in case some of the guards, who were milling confusedly throughout Crushbone, wandered back inside.

Zackary bent down and, using the villain’s own dirk, sliced out his black heart.  Then the company gathered up the valuables from their foes’ lifeless forms and put them in a backpack to store in the guild vaults.  Then, weary but righteously satisfied, they headed home, pausing only to cast benisons on the younglings dispatching guards and rescuing slaves.

Later that afternoon, as Letrea sat curled up next her sweet baboo Lhes, just back from his battles in Kunark, she reflected on the day’s accomplishments.

“You know, Sweetie,” she said, “while we won the battle, and gained much loot for the guild coffers, they weren’t the biggest victory.”

“What do you think you really won?” Lhes asked her while making sure that the last of her wounds were healing.

“The most important conquest was of ourselves,” Letrea said.  “We learned that only when we work as a group, following a plan, helping each other, and being ready no matter what happens, can we really win the day.  

“Soldiers of Fortune, we make our own fortunes as we work together.  We’ve grown stronger because we have grown together.  Someday soon, we’ll come to Kunark with you, my Sweet, and help find and rescue Firiona.  Because we are united, family, team, Soldiers of Fortune.”
