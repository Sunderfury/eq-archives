01-26-2000

The Minotaur Caves

written by Beryllan

Preamble to the Minotaur Caves.

Regretably, I was not able to accumulate much data for a ballad upon the Mino Cave get-together. So, I decided to focus upon the slaying of the Hero and leave the rest a bit vague. I opted for Anglo-Saxon-style word painting, in this case. With that in mind, it is most effective, as is ALL poetry, if read aloud.

-Beryllan

Gather now soldiers, for the tale-telling.
Great was the gathering. Steamfont stones shook.
Soldiers of Fortune, treading the valleys, 
came to the lair of Meldrath's minions.

Glorious the plan, by Stingray provided:  
caves to be stormed and tokens wrested 
from the fierce foe, gore-horned bull-men. 
Bright gleamed the blades, but eye-gleam was brighter.

In swept the heroes, hewing, bespelling.  
Dweomercrafty and doughty the daughters, 
as well as the sons, Dame Fortune's soldiers. 
Minotaurs fell, fled, shame-filled cravens.

Then came Meldrath, foretold, yet dreaded:  
hero of slavers, savage and miscreant, 
fell-handed, thick-thewed, ill-omened monster, 
minotaur hero, slayer of heroes.

Many the fallen, as forth he charged.  
Out of the cave; there his doom waited: 
Ceeron the mighty, Druid and peerless, 
Jalea Regina, Queen of our order. 

Long was the striving, bloody the contest. 
Great were the blows smote by Ceeron and Meldrath. 
Jalea's staff, skull-crowned, Troll-feared, 
rained death. Mighty spells she wrought.

Great was the crying: Meldrath is conquered!  
Great the rejoicing, both near and far. 
Vengeance requited for friends fresh fallen: 
Jalea's blow quit the monster of life.

Praise to the heroes, Jalea, Ceeron;  
praise to the many who strove in the caves. 
Soldiers of Fortune, fighting together: 
work we ever, for the common weal.
