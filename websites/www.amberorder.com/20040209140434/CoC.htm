Amber Order Charter


“I tell you that as long as I can conceive something better than myself I cannot be easy unless I am striving to bring it into existence or clearing the way for it. That is the law of my life. That is the working within me of Life's incessant aspiration to higher organization, wider, deeper, intenser self-consciousness, and clearer self-understanding.”

“This is the true joy in life, the being used for a purpose recognized by yourself as a mighty one; the being thoroughly worn out before you are thrown on the scrap heap; the being a force of Nature instead of a feverish selfish little clod of ailments and grievances complaining that the world will not devote itself to making you happy.”

  From Man and Superman: A Comedy and a Play, by George Bernard Shaw, 1903

Taking a moment to give a friend a rare find item for which they have been looking. Fighting back to back with people you trust.  Leading a group of younger brothers and sisters to a new place and teaching them the ways of the place and how to fend for themselves.  Bravely descending into dark depths of a dungeon to help a fallen comrade.  Sharing a “virtual beer” with a buddy and discussing the mysteries of life while you prepare for the next battle.  A lone warrior watching her life ebb away as she holds monsters at bay so her fleeing brothers and sisters may escape -- dying to save those she loves.

These are all commonplace events among AO Brothers and Sisters.  To be a part of AO is to be a part of something special – something that is greater than the sum of its parts.  To be a part of AO is to share in comradeship and love that has spanned over half a decade and multiple on-line games.  Being a part of The Amber Order is an honor that brings immeasurable benefits, but also carries responsibilities.  This Code of Conduct is intended help AO member and prospective members understand both those benefits and the responsibilities that go with them.

One of the most wonderful things about the AO family is that we can help each other learn to become better people while we share and play together. So, even as we lay out a Code of Conduct, know that Love, Compassion, and Kindness comprise the true Soul of the AO, and the framework that these ideas are hung upon. If your actions stem from these ideals then you are a rich person and you will enrich those around you.

This is what is expected of a member of the AO. This way of life is not for everyone. Do you think that it suits you? Decide carefully.

The Code of the Amber Order
Rules and guidelines are very important in any large organization. However, life is very complicated and right and wrong are often a matter of perspective. Our Code is intended to give all our members a core idea of what kind of behavior we expect of them. They are intended to make our time together more fun and enriching than it would be in the absence of these guidelines.

We will strive to never lose sight of the individual in any situation. However, our goal is “The Good of the Many”, or “The Greater Good”. In our quest for this ideal, sometimes punishments are necessary to protect  “The Greater Good” and the soul of the AO. The Code is as follows:

·  Loyalty - Members of the AO are expected and required to be Loyal to their guildmates and guild ideals. One does not have to like every member of ones guild, every ideal, or every officer, but loyalty requires that you work as best you can within the group. If there are problems or differences of opinion, we will deal with them as a guild and accept the decisions of the guild.

· Responsibility – Our members are expected to be responsible for their actions. Our officers have responsibilities to the members and to all of Norrath. The responsibility of each member is no less. Take time to help your guildmates. We each have our individual strengths and weaknesses. It is each members responsibility to share what is best about them, and your fellows will help teach you in areas where you are weakest. This has nothing to do with power or level. This has everything to do with making our guild greater and more wonderful than the sum of its entire fantastic people.

· Appreciation and Expectation - One must not expect or rely on the guild to help them in most situations, or give them powerful items. Members must also not expect spell casters in the guild to always come help them with some useful spell. Becoming efficient and powerful is not what our guild is about. I can not say this strongly enough, as this seems to be an expectation of many new members. While your friends may help you when you most need it, or give a gift out of friendship and love, to expect such treatment is wrong. One cannot learn responsibility, or values, if all is given to them or if they regularly use others. This is not what we want our members to learn, or worse yet, to teach others. To succeed in a game such as this one has only to enjoy it, and it is even better if in your enjoyment, others have fun as well. This can be done at all levels of play, and what you do and who you do it with is far more important than what you are wearing or how powerful you are. This is easy to forget. Try not to.

· Passion – While passion is not truly a required behavior, it does underlie many a good deed.  If you see something wrong, fix it. If you think that there should be more newbie quests or guild events, organize one. If you would like to see changes or new policies, promote them. Use your passion; it is a wonderful part of who you are, and what you can share with your friends.

·  Profanity – This is an easy one. Don’t do it. AO Members will not use profanity or inappropriate language in public.  “Public” includes server, zone, and Guild Chat.  Within private tells or group tells, members are allowed to establish agreed upon norms.  However, even in those modes, if someone gets offended, or is obviously too young, we expect AO members to apologize and/or adjust their behavior appropriately.

· Kill Stealing – This one is just as easy (don’t do it either). AO Members will not intentionally steal kills.  We recognize that accidents happen, however, we will not tolerate members who repeatedly and openly exhibit this behavior.  If at all possible, we suggest coming to an agreement with others in the area about how to share the kills.  If someone insists on stealing your kills, call a GM and let AO know.  We will try to help resolve the situation. When all else fails, walk away and come back another time. The thing you are after will still be there.  Usually.

· Assistance - If an AO brother or sister dies and needs assistance with body recovery, anyone of an appropriate level and within reasonable proximity should come to help.  However, if you die, you must allow the guild time to respond.  People have gotten upset because they died multiple times trying to recover a body, even though help was on the way.  You must be patient.  If you do not have time to wait, leave the body and it will be there when more help can be organized or when next you come on. Remember, it is a member’s responsibility to help their fellows, but expecting such help too much or too quickly is unrealistic and leads to unjustified bad feelings.

· Cheating – Another easy one (don’t do it). AO members do not use Pathing Bugs, Duplication Bugs, or any other kind of cheat.

Amber Order Punishment
Sometimes people are admitted into the guild and we later find that they do not act in accordance with our rather ethical Code of Conduct. This may be for many varying reasons, but if such inappropriate behavior is allowed, our guild ideals, goals, and our role in Norrath can quickly fail. As such, even though a guild as truly “Good” as the AO must have punishments for when our guidelines are severely broken. These punishments include but are not necessarily limited to the following:

· Reprimand - If an offense is not too great, a public reprimand can be issued. This is a repeated public “guildsay” of the action to allow other members of the guild to understand what has transpired. Hopefully, such behavior will not often be repeated if is publicly condemned.

· Assigned Quest - This can also be issued for moderate offenses. An Assigned Quest is a task assigned by the leadership to the offending individual.  Such quests will be of a nature as to help emphasize the importance of the broken code of conduct. This is meant as a learning experience.

Generally, Reprimands and Quests will not be used lightly.  A member might receive several private, informal warnings or advice from other members or Guild Officers before more formal actions are taken.

·  Expulsion - If a member’s offense is great, for example, malicious behavior, or if they do not respond after Reprimands or Quests, they can be ejected from the guild.  We are loving, loyal, and compassionate, but not everyone is meant to be in the AO. We are very selective about who we invite, but we do make mistakes.  Our cohesive, family atmosphere is important to us and we will not long tolerate those who disrupt it. Remember, we strive for “The Greater Good”. This striving does it suit everyone’s playing style.

Conclusion
So I ask you again, is this for you? This is not a question you should answer lightly. We stand for Goodness, Love, Compassion and Kindness. Is that the kind of game you want to play? Do you want to be part of a large organization committed to others as much as to themselves? Think about it. If you want to join and are accepted, don’t forget what you have read here. Live it, breathe it, enjoy it, and share it. And remember, we’re all people who play these games for enjoyment – do your share to spread the fun, and you will have a much better time yourself.

Amber Order Charter
