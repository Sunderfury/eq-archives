  Demiplane Bug Fixed  [ Comment ]
Print News
Developer Curt believes he has fixed the charm/death bug in Demiplane, to the relief of many raiding guilds, I am sure:

CurtEQ writes: "I found a crash. THE crash? hard to say. But a crash for sure, reproducible (now that I know the missing factor) and fixed. It will go out on the next live patch. I can tell you without a doubt it was responsible for the core dumps that I could make heads/tails of, but as for the semi-corrupt ones? Good chance, not great.. but good.

No I don't know when the next live patch will be, and I'm not authorized to speculate, but I can assure you it will be soon.

No I'm not going to tell you the details, except what everyone already knows; its related to charm and death. I'm sorry, I truly am, for the honest guilds and raid leaders who would use the info correctly, but if I post "don't do this" I might as well code in a button that just crashes the zone when you press it."



-- Kwill @ 4:57PM Thu 16 Nov 2006


Maddoc Resurrects Boat Functionality  [ 1 Comment ]
Print News
Boats will return to Norrath, but some of their functionality involves a revamp of the Ocean of Tears.

Maddoc says:

"The boats can, indeed, be resurrected.

The boat in Qeynos works very well ... The boat in Freeport, however... Ran into a couple snags, primarily with the Ocean of Tears zone and how it's laid out. That will be fixed in the relatively near future, making the Freeport boat as functional as the Qeynos boat.

The speed that the boats were running during beta was a hyper-accellerated speed, such that I could test out the functionality of my new boat system, without having to wait an hour for the boat to show up. The boats will move faster than they used to, but they won't be moving that fast. I expect an 8-10 minute round trip for the boats.

Boats should be ready to go live around the same time as the revamped ocean of tears. The issue that I ran into was with the orientation of the Ocean of Tears, and I am currently working on the revamp of Ocean of Tears, which is necessary to facilitate proper functionality of the boats."


-- Kwill @ 4:54PM Thu 16 Nov 2006

  Triality Guild Frostcrypt Video  [ Comment ]
Print News
One of the end zones of The Serpent's Spine expansion is Frostcrypt, and if you would like to take a peek at what raiding it might be like, check out Triality guild's excellent video of their encounter in this instance on their website.

Wycca, who posted the video on the EQPlayers forums had this to say about the zone:

"I think overall (despite some bugs and tuning issues) this is one of the most interesting/fun raiding experiences in the game to date. The encounters are mostly fun and sometimes downright clever as they introduce some new mechanics and re-use some old ones. If anyone is curious, Frostcrypt #2 continues those traits mentioned, and with a little nudging, you may see another video of it soon. The entire Valdeholme/Frostcrypt side of progression and raiding has been a combination of frustration to bugs, delight at the scripts, excitement at winning, and some of the most intense rushes I've experienced in the game (Redfang, Performer, and Mayong came close, but not near our Odeen, Grenwald, and Harfange kills)."

They are recruiting an Enchanter, a Paladin, a Shadowknight and a Warrior, according to their website. Send Orruar or Dalnoth a tell in game if you are interested, and if you think you are uber enough, seriously!

  Google Ad Help  
Print News
The Vault Network is asking the community to help us preserve our "Don't Destroy the Game" philosophy and mantra. Because we told Google that these gold farmer type ads suck and don't belong on the Vault Network. They were more than happy to work with us to keep such ads off our sites. There is a gold lining to this situation (pun intended!). We have a system in place where any community member can submit an ad they might see via a simple and easy web form. Hotzone Update Pushed Back  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Hotzones, the areas of extra experience with special item drops, will remain the same until the next patch. They want to make sure they are great!

Nodyin posted on EQLive:

Hotzones....will not change with this patch [the patch this past week].

This was the patch I was aiming for to change the Hot Zones. Following the pattern we set with the last set of zones we are going to introduce some small amount of new itemization with the next set of zones. Unfortunately Merloc was too busy doing a tuneing pass on TSS weapons and items for this patch to finish making the items I'll need for the Hot Zones. I apologize to those that were eagerly waiting for a new set of zones but I hope that when we do the next patch and you see the zones and play in them you'll find that the wait was worth it. I have a brand new trick to suprise you with and the items Merloc is making are, I think, pretty fun. Also the selection of zones should please a number of you (and be "blah" to an equal number, I'm sure!)

I just wanted to let you guys know so you can plan accordingly and aren't suprised when we push out the Patch. Again I'm sorry we missed the target on this.

  Artcles Worth Reading  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Features
I hope that you have a chance to visit mobhunter.com to read some of what Loral Circlelight has to say about EverQuest. His articles are always well written and thought-provoking. Recently, he has written an article about the past and future of MMOs, "Are Massively Online Games Getting Worse?" I was especially struck by this turn of phrase:

"It is the sharp-edged rubbing of a MMO's population that creates the most dynamic and unique situations. When you remove those sharp edges and stick those players in smooth, clean instances where no one bumps elbows and no one gets hurt, that's when the life is removed from a MMO."

I think his metaphor is very striking, although I am not sure I agree that instances really take away all the life from an MMO. Conflict over contested spawns may have been part of the fun for some players, but for most it just soured the game experience. It's an ongoing debate, but Loral is certainly one of the more intelligent voices engaging in it.



-- Kwill @ 12:19PM Wed 25 Oct 2006
