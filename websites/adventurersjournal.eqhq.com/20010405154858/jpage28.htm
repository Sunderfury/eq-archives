      Slowly he opened his eyes, the soft light from the morning greeted him, gently stirring his senses. Sitting up, he wipes the sleep from his eyes and looks around his room. It is sparse, with barely any decorations to distinguish it from any other room, but that is the way he likes it, simple, plain, functional. Getting out of bed he starts thinking about all the things that have happened in this room, all the discussions that occurred here, all the Love that was shared here, his head starts pounding again as more thoughts come to him. He hadn’t slept well last night, as a matter of fact he hasn’t slept well for quite a while now, ever since he had returned from Kunark, so he is moving rather slowly this morning. As he goes about his morning routine, the thoughts of this place keep filling his head, not only thoughts of this room, but also thoughts of the entire Guildhall. Ever since his return he has had many thoughts, but no feelings, his heart has been seemingly turned to stone. Recently he has been thinking about what he must do, what action he must take in the face of all that happened. He has considered all the possibilities and has come to one single conclusion, one course of action that seems inevitable, inescapable. This morning he has decided that he would take that action, and so all these memories keep flooding in, all these experiences keep filling his head, and the pain in his head grows with the memories.
As he leaves his room he notices that the place seems empty, deserted. He is not concerned for it is late morning and everyone has probably already gone for the day, "All the better" he thinks to himself. When he reaches the main hall he sits down at the table to write a letter, a letter he has been putting off writing, but which now must be written. As he sits there thinking about the wording of the letter he is flooded with memories. Memories of this place, of the things that have happened to him over his life, of the people he has met, the places he has been. The pain in his head grows from a mild throbbing to a tremendous thumping almost as if someone is beating on his head. He lifts his hands up to his head to try and fend off the pain, his eyes scanning the room seeing the ghosts of his memories everywhere he looks. He is overwhelmed by the memories, the images filling his mind, the pain in his head an unbearable, steady assault on him. He looks up and a single tear breaks from his eye and rolls down his cheek, and with that tear he hears a loud cracking sound from inside his chest, the pain in his head disappears instantly and suddenly he is overcome with emotion. His entire body fills with it like a glass being filled with water, the anger, the frustration, the sorrow, the pity, all of it returns in that one instant, shocking his system for a moment. He is once again filled with Love, and then filled with pain, the pain and sorrow for what he is about to do. The tears come bursting out of him like water from a broken dam. He bends over and sobs like a child, unable to control himself. For weeks he has been without these emotions, he has been a stone, unfeeling, uncaring. Now in an instant these feelings have returned and he is laid to waste by the awesome power that they carry. As he slowly regains some composure over himself, he remembers why he is here and what he is about to do, and the pain in his heart grows larger, the anguish of what he is feeling right now as he realizes that this changes nothing, that he still must do what he has set out to do, and so he turns and begins writing even as the tears continue to flow.

 Dear Brothers, Sisters, and Friends,

It is with a very sad, and heavy heart that I must do the following, but I see no other course of action that will allow me to continue. Due to recent events and circumstances beyond my control, I must resign my position as the First Officer of the Holy Order of Tunare.
It brings much pain to my heart to have to do this, but the pain I already carry is much greater and staying would simply bring more of it, which I don’t think I could endure. I would like to say that I have been most honored to represent you all as one of your leaders. Each and every one of you has brought happiness, joy, and honor to my life, in your own unique way. The friendships that I have developed with some of you are more like extensions of my own family, indeed, I truly consider some of you to be my kin. Even those of you whom I do not know very well, I still consider to be my friends. I hope that these friendships will continue to be there, for losing them would make this already unbearable situation even worse.
I want everyone to know that I am terribly sorry for this, and I wish that there was some other way, but alas, I can see none. I wish all of you the very best that life has to offer, for each and every one of you deserve it and so much more.

 Standing and removing the Emblem of the First Officer of The Holy Order of Tunare from his tunic and placing it on the table, he takes one last look around the Guild Hall, tears streaming down his face. Bowing deeply, he stops for a moment near the tree in the entranceway, wiping his face and straightening his tunic, he turns and walks slowly out the door.
