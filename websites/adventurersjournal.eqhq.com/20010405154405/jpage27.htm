~ OOC ~ Just a little backround for these next sets of journal entries. Temyez has returned home from Kunark and is trying to fit back in to regular life, however things have changed for him and he is uneasy. As I have stated before, some of these entries have been written to reflect witings from other people involved in what was happening at the time, so some of it may seem as though there are things missing, but, it is enough for you to understand what happened and where Temyez is today. All of this happened about a month and a half ago, perhaps even longer. I apologize for not posting anything sooner, but I needed some time to adjust and accept. You may not entirely understand why these events have taken place, let me assure that there are reasons for what has happened and although I will not go into them or what brought all this on, at least you will know where it has all led to. ~ OOC ~

       Heaping a mound of potatoes onto his plate to go along with the healthy portion of meat that he had, Temyez began to eat. He didn't speak much during the dinner, He didn't seem to speak much at all lately. In the weeks following his return from Kunark and the loss of his heart and soul he found that he just didn't have much to say. He hadn't told anyone what had happened, he hadn't told anyone that he had lost his feelings, hadn't said a word about the seeming loss of his emotions. He had avoided telling the Lady Siniana about any of it, with all of the memories they had shared it seemed to him that it would be somewhat difficult. When she had approached him and asked him what was wrong, he had simply said that he didn't know, but he felt he needed some space to figure some things out. She had understood that and had given him the space he requested, for that was the kind of relationship they had, each of them free to do what they wished, each of them giving as much space as the other may need, no questions, no regrets.

Finishing his dinner he took his plate into the kitchen and noticed right away that Lady Siniana was not there, not that there was anything wrong with that, she was free to go where she wished. He just found it a little unusual that she had excused herself from the table into the kitchen and now was not here. Not thinking anything more about it he washed his plate and set it into the strainer, next he washed his tableware and placed them into the straining cup to be put away later. He left the kitchen through the back hall, avoiding the others who were laughing in the dining room, as was his custom for the last few weeks. He would retire to his chambers for the night to write some for his Journal, or perhaps work on some drawings, as a matter of fact, he thought, it was about time to be heading out for a few weeks in the wilderness. Back to the nature that he loved, where he could be alone, not having to think about how he might be acting, not having to think about what the others may think was wrong. Back into the wilderness where it would be just him, alone with his memories.
