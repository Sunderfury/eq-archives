July 18
The Goddess Tunare herself has allowed me to advance in the rangers guild, I have been promoted to a ranger of the 17th season, what a glorious day! I have been taught the secrets of wielding two weapons at the same time and have abandoned my shield for a second weapon, I will currently wield a minotaurs axe and a combine longsword.

My guild brother Valint has asked me to meet him in the High Pass Hold to battle rogue orcs who are on the war path. After meeting him in the pass, we battled for a short time. but there were many adventurers there, and not many orcs, so the pickings were slim. Later that day brother Valint had some other business to attend to and I decided to move on to another area.

Wielding two weapons is fantastic, although I have a long way to go until I am very effective at it, I can already see a large improvement in my battles. I have been thinking about the type of weapons that I should be using, perhaps a smaller, faster weapon in my right hand would be better than this axe. I will have to ask some others what kinds of smaller weapons are available.

Guild brother Klymeth has asked me to help a friend of his complete a small quest. The quest is in the region of Everfrost and I have been wanting to see the lengendary Permafrost Dungeon anyways, so off we go to help. The quest was successful and brother Klymeth's friend now has a new weapon, a Langseax of the wolves, I believe it was called. It can only be wielded by a warrior and is very powerful. I immediatly headed for Permafrost, to see whatever might be seen there. I can tell you this, Permafrost Dungeon is a frightful place, located in the northern most reaches of the land. A cold and unforgiving place, Its entrance is guarded by frost giants who oppose all who would enter. Once inside you are attacked by the hordes of goblins that live in the dungeon. I never made it farther than the entrance hall before another adventurer went running for his life past me, heading for the exit, with a very large trail of goblins following right behind him. About half of the goblins turned towards me and I hightailed it out of there as quick as I could. This dungeon was not for a lone hunter, so I decided I would return another day with some of my guild members to explore the frozen wastes of the Permafrost and maybe retrieve some of the treasure I have heard about.
