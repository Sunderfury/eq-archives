      I sat there totally stunned, unable to entirely believe what had happened, as the voices of everyone around me joined into what seemed to be some distant background noise. It had been so long now since I had someplace to call home, so long since I had anyone to turn to in time of need. As the discussions of everyone around me went on, my mind slowly drifts back to the events that started this wonderful day.
        I had been hunting on the Northern edge of the frozen plains, on the newfound continent of Velious, in an area known as the East Wastes. The place was filled with the enormous and extremely ugly walrus men called Ulthorks and a variety of other creatures including frost giants, rygorr orcs, and rhino’s. The hunting in this region is good, and relatively safe, so I had been here for at least the last few weeks, all the while hunting Ulthork Warriors. Everything was normal, just as it should be, just as it has been, for what seems like an eternity now. Me, alone, tracking one of the lumbering hulks across the snow-covered ground, preparing to launch an arrow or two just as soon as I saw it. When all of a sudden I hear a shout ring out through the crisp, cold air, “What ho! My old friend!” I turn slightly towards the sound and there but a short distance away, standing on a small hill, is the figure of a halfling. His blonde hair sticking haphazardly out from under the cap he is wearing, his green eyes shining in the sunlight, a huge smile on his face. The figure is very recognizable to me and instantly my mind starts racing, “What the …? How can this be?” Not paying any attention, I lose my footing and stumble forward, landing face first in a small snow bank. A moment later he is there next to me, pulling me out of the snow, brushing me off. Slowly, I open my eyes and see a ghost from my past, “Cecrops?” I say. The look of concern leaves his face and is replaced by a broad smile, “Temyez! My friend! How are you?” He shouts, as he embraces me in a hug. I am confused, I am bewildered, I loosely hold on to him for a moment and then he pulls back looking at me with a smile. “I,..…but you’re ……, someone said that you were …..” I stutter, unable to complete a sentence, staring at him with wide eyes. “Rumors of my death have been greatly exaggerated,” he says with a wink, smiling even wider, then laughing. I stare at him for a moment longer, this halfling that I haven’t seen in ages, this good friend whom I have missed very much, sitting in the snow next to me laughing out loud. Then I too, begin laughing, and for the next few minutes we both laugh hysterically, rolling around in the snow like a couple of madmen. Afterwards, when we have both regained our senses, and composure, we sit for a short time talking, sharing some of the things that have happened to us. Even with the sharing there remain many questions in my mind, but Cecrops is anxious to explore this new land, he has heard rumors of the great treasures and riches that await adventurers here and he is eager to get started, and I know that all of my questions will be answered with time. The remainder of the day we spent hunting and exploring new areas, enjoying each other’s friendship. Truthfully, I don’t fully remember the events of the day, as it was so good to just travel and explore with a good friend. At some point later in the day Cecrops asked me if I would like to join him at a Solstice meeting of his Guild, I accepted, and shortly after we teleported to the Steamfont Mountains.
        Now, sitting here surrounded by the members of Natural Selection, surrounded by my new family, I feel at home. My attention returns to the discussion at hand, something about a contest to collect the most hides from Warslikes Woods. Cecrops asks me if I will be coming along, “No” I reply, “I really must rest my friend.” He understands and takes my hand in his, congratulating me once again on becoming one of the family. Others approach and introduce themselves, welcoming me into the Guild, we exchange pleasantries, shaking hands, bowing to each other, hugging, until finally, the last one has left and I am alone again. The darkness is coming on quickly now, and my mind is still racing with all that has happened. I look up to the sky and feel the first of the tears as they break from my eyes and roll down my cheeks. Remembering all that I have lost, and all that I have gained, I say a quiet, “Thank you Tunare, thank you Great Mother” as I lower my head and cry.
