The Scarecrow

"That night
the scarecrow came into the house:
a tall, thin hideous thing
wearing the blackest of clothes.
He sat on the bench
and the cards fell from our hands.
One man
was telling us a folktale
and the words froze on his lips.
A woman was sitting on a stool,
singing songs, and he took the goodness
out of her voice.
The breath left our lungs
and he filled our breasts with dread"

Based on a Gaelic Poem

 

The corn barely swayed in the breeze, each stalk straight and rigid like a foot soldier standing at attention in some great army. The rustling of the coarse, dry leaves seeming to speak some ancient lost language that had long since been forgotten. The silent call of the corn, beckoning to travelers and adventurers alike, "Come and explore! See what may be hidden inside our shadowy rows." The children Especially, they were always fascinated by the cornfields, spending hours on end, running through the endless rows, playing hide and seek, enjoying the fact that you could literally disappear only a few steps in.

He liked it that way, it provided him with the opportunity to feed often, and of course it worked to his advantage being able to hide in the fields, not that it mattered much. During this time of year he was at his most powerful, almost unstoppable. He preferred however, to remain hidden and unknown, only a terrifying story to be told around a campfire late at night. He had haunted the corn for many, many years now and had been known by many different names throughout the centuries. Keargis the keeper, or Blearkin the Soul Eater, only the oldest of the local villagers knew him as Sorjonek, the faceless one, just an old superstition.

He smiled as he moved silently through the rows, his eyes glowing a furious red, deep-set in the hollow black pits of his empty sockets, his black teeth hidden from view in the deep recess of his mouth. He was in his most common form now, the living scarecrow, a being of the very cornfield he now walked through, made from the stalks and leaves of the corn itself. He made no noise whatsoever as he moved, almost as if his very will was preventing the dried leaves, sticks, and straw that made up his body from making even the tiniest sound. The stench that accompanied him was horrible, rotten dead flesh and blood staining the ragged clothes that hung from his straw and leaf stuffed body. Maggots and worms crawled through the blood soaked stuffing and dropped off as he walked, countless flies swarmed about him, creating a dark cloud that seemed to envelope him. Not that he cared, this was only a vehicle to him, a tool to be used for moving about, feeding, and killing, the smile growing ever wider on his bloody, mud-caked face.

He broke through the corn into a small clearing and walked to the ancient wooden post that stood in the center. Kneeling down he surveyed the area, this was his home, if any place on this world could even be called that. The corn nearby had been broken and trampled down to create the small clearing in the center of this vast cornfield. Old bones stuck up out of the ground like the broken corn stalks that surrounded them, with smaller piles of bones and skulls strewn about the entire area. The decaying remains of a few bodies also littered the clearing, some human some animal, unrecognizable, torn apart and half eaten, blackened from rot and decomposition. The crows sat almost ignoring him picking at the rotting corpses, they were used to his coming and going and gave him little notice. It was the ultimate irony that he could strike terror into the hearts of men and animals alike, yet couldn’t frighten the very crows that the body he now inhabited was designed to scare. One very large crow approached him and pecked at some of the loose flesh that hung from his clothes. He grabbed at it with his straw covered claw, squeezing it tightly the bird squawked loudly before he shoved its head into his open mouth and bit down, biting its head completely off, he squeezed even harder as he pulled it away from his mouth, blood and guts sprayed out and onto his face, dripping down onto the ground. He smiled when he felt the warmth of the bird’s blood on his face. Throwing he rest of the bird’s body across the clearing his smile vanished quickly as he realized he would have to sleep soon. The Solstice had passed and the corn was starting to die off, before long he would have to rest until next spring. He frowned and reached down to tear off a piece of the rotting corpse nearest him and began to eat. Could it almost be that time again so soon, he was still so very hungry, it almost seemed as if he hadn’t fed at all this season. As he chewed the putrid meat he thought back to earlier this evening when he had missed a chance to feed. The emptiness in his gut instantly replaced with the anger as it grew inside of him, the fiery glow of his eyes intensifying.

The young girl child had been so close, so very close to the corn. Just another few moments and she would have been his. He watched her as she walked slowly towards the rows, mesmerized by his little spell, calling her to him. Then, from out of nowhere, an adventuring ranger had called out to her, "Hi Ho, little lady!" She had ignored him and continued to shuffle towards the corn as if she had heard nothing. He too, had ignored the outburst, continuing to concentrate on the little girl, continuing to will her into the cornfield. The ranger had approached her then, and called to her once again, " I say young Lady, might you tell a weary traveler where your village is?" She had stopped then staring at the ranger with a somewhat bewildered look on her face. The faceless one had clenched his straw hands into fists, "No" he had thought, "Don’t stop." But, it was too late, the spell had been broken. The little girl had looked up at the ranger with a puzzled stare, "What’s that sir?" she had said, her eyes blinking as if waking from a dream. The Ranger had begun talking to her then, "My name is Temyez D’argos, an outrider of Tunare, and I am looking for a place to rest for ……."

The faceless one sat in the corn staring out at the two of them. He could no longer hear the words they spoke as the anger rose inside of him. His only thoughts were on the two of them. He could have taken them right there. Oh, the ranger would have put up a good fight he was certain, but in the end not much could stand against him right now. He had almost done it, but to take two people from out in the open like that was risky. He may have been seen, and it was just getting too late in the season to risk that. It might bring the wrath of the villagers down on him, they might burn the cornfield or some such nonsense, not that it would hurt him in the least, but then he would have to move on to another area and that was such a pain. This was his home after all.

He had watched as they walked away, the girl turning once to look back at the corn. He could almost taste her delicate flesh, almost feel the warmth of her blood spraying on him. He turned and crept silently back into the depths of the cornfield. Another day, another time perhaps, she had been marked by the call of the corn and would never forget. She would be back.

"Well", he thought, as he sat in his clearing pulling rotten meat off a corpse and eating. He could wait for another opportunity, the corn would give him that, it always did.
