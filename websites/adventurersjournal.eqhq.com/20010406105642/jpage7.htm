December 12
I wanted to update everyone on the story of my encounter with the ghoul messenger. After the recent guild meeting, a few of my brothers helped me to obtain the Short Sword of Morin. A group consisting of Klymeth, Conwen, Haemish, Valint, and myself, traveled to the Western Karanas to hunt down and kill the Ghoul messenger. After much turmoil, a failed first attack in which many of us were killed, being lost in the mountains, being chased out by a werewolf, and the general frustration of waiting, we finally managed to locate and kill the beast. The strategy we used amounted to Brother Haemish taunting the creature to chase him for some time while he assaulted it with spells, then the fighters engaged it in melee, while the healers primarily cast heal spells on any fighters needing it, even then the creature fought violently. In the end we were victorious with the help of Tunare. I would like to take a moment to thank my fellow brothers without whose help I would not have such a magnificent sword, Thank you friends, you were there for me, and Tunare willing, I will be there for you.  May the Glory and Blessings of The Great Mother be upon you always.

December 20
I am roused from my meditations by the shouts and commotion along the shore. Arriving at the docks, I am relieved, the long journey across the Ocean of Tears is behind me and though it is a dull and boring trip it gave me time to reflect upon recent events. Our guild has been formed, we have had our first ‘official’ meeting and the responsibilities of a leadership position loom before me. Walking down the docks I am jarred from these thoughts by a minor collision with a dwarf, "Excuse me" I say, as the dwarf continues moving past, "Harummff, scuse yerself haf-breed!" the dwarf cries as he turns slightly to face me. My hand instinctively moves down to the hilt of my sword, his words have caught me unprepared, they have hurt me more than anything else he might have said and my anger rises from deep within. The dwarf looks at me more closely now, mayhaps realizing his mistake, staring at a fully armed and armored ranger his eyes widen as he begins to back away. "Watch your tongue dwarf" I growl, stepping forward. "Ye have no right, Ye have no right" he yells as he begins to turn and run. I spring into action and swiftly plant the fore of my boot squarely on his arse, the dwarf flies through the air flailing wildly and cursing profusely as he goes over the side of the dock and lands with loud splash in the bay. The crowd has somewhat cleared away as two guards come running up and ask if I know what happened, "I think a drunk has fallen into the water", I reply as I make my way back down the dock. The anger is still with me, probably will stay with me for some time, but eventually it will die down. Years ago I may have killed the dwarf for making such a statement, but with age comes maturity and with maturity increased responsibility. Ahhh well, it will never cease to amaze me the many ways people will show their ignorance.
As I entered the open square, the docks behind me, I look into the distance at the Butcherblock Mountains and think about the trials and tribulations that are ahead, the guild is already growing and we must make a name for ourselves. Out of the corner of my eye I see the petite and shapely form of a wood elf woman, I turn and watch her walk down the path, a smile creeps on to my face. Behind me I hear a commotion, turning I can barely see a small gathering of people as they watch an even smaller figure emerge from the water yelling and screaming obscenities. My smile becomes a laugh as I duck into a merchant’s shop. I begin scanning the shops wares looking for a particular piece of armor. I ask the shopkeeper if he has a bloodstained tunic for sale, he responds by saying he has never seen such a thing, I am not surprised, I have heard they are fairly rare, oh well, my search continues. As I head out of the merchant’s shop and turn toward the mountain path my thoughts return to the recent guild meeting and our new initiates, two new initiates in particular, Snydarea and Siniana, both beautiful women, both worthy additions to the guild. The lovely Lady Snydarea, any man would desire such a creature, and the enchanting Lady Siniana, I had been entranced by her at the meeting unable to concentrate on anything for long without returning my gaze to her. A smile once again creeps on to my face, and as I walk down the path towards the mountains the smile stays with me as my mind races ahead.
