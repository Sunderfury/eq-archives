December 25
The Night Before Battle

Twas the night before battle, and all through the ranks not a person was stirring, not even the tanks.
The weapons were hung at their side with great care, in hopes that they would remain there.

The clerics had all of the spells in their head, in hopes they would not have to raise any dead.
My friend Klymeth with his mace, and I with my sword, had prepared ourselves to battle a great horde.

When out on the plains there arose such a clatter, I sprang from the ranks to see what was the matter.
I ran to the cliff edge and stopped fairly clean, and looked over the edge to see what could be seen.

The moons waning light falling full on the ground made mysterious shadows of everything around.
When what to my wondering eyes should appear, but a great ball of light, which made everything clear.

At the center of light, beautiful as a spring day, I knew in a moment, The Great Mother Tunare.
More rapid than eagles the light expanded and grew, and she shouted out greetings as fast as She flew.

"Now, Ogres! Now, Trolls! Now, Elves of all types! Now, Humans! Now, Dwarves and Barbarians alike.
Across all the plains to the top of the cliffs, the voice rose like thunder in high tones and low riffs.

As dry leaves that before the wild hurricane fly, when they meet with an obstacle, mount to the sky.
So up, high up went the light into the air, flew The Mother Tunare, while all could just stare.

And then, in a twinkling, I heard above all, as if spoken to me personally, I heard The Mothers call.
As I drew in my breath, and was turning around, down came Tunare and alighted on the ground.

She was dressed all in white, from Her head to Her toe, and Her clothes they gave off a wonderful glow.
Many wondrous lights flew and circled around, and She looked like an angel floating above ground.

Her eyes, how they twinkled! Her dimples how merry! Her cheeks were like roses, Her face like a fairy.
Her beautiful mouth was drawn up like a bow, and Her hair was as white as new fallen snow.

A staff of great light She held tight in Her hand, and around both Her wrists she wore a gold band.
She had a narrow face and was very slim, and Her outfit was laced with a glowing yellow trim.

She was beautiful and charming, a right jolly elf, and I smiled when I saw Her, in spite of myself.
A wink of Her eye and a twist of Her head, soon gave me to know I had nothing to dread.

She spoke not a word, but went straight to Her work, filling everyone with peace; then turned with a jerk.
Lifting Her staff it gave off a great light, and giving a nod, She rose into the night.

Then again came the light, and with it a loud roar, up and away She flew and with Her all thoughts of war.
And we heard Her exclaim, as She flew out of sight, "Peace and Joy to all, and to all a GoodNight!"
