March 12
Leaving Lake Rathe behind me, I move forward into the mountains. The sun is shining full from high in the sky and yet it is still very cool as I progress into the pass. I have come seeking a certain charm, the charm of Tarskuk the Cyclops. It is needed by one of the leaders of the Rangers Guild in Kithicor Woods to make the Ivy Etched Helmet. This helmet is quite a piece of armor, it provides protection and also adds magical enhancements to the wearer. I do wish to have the helmet, but somehow I do not think that Tarskuk will just hand over his charm, so I fear that I will have quite a battle ahead of me. My hope is that I will find a small band of adventurers to join up with along the way for I will be unable to defeat a Cyclops alone, but if I can’t find anyone to join with then I will at least scout the area.
As I make my way out of the pass I see great mountains rising and between them vast areas of flatlands. There are many creatures roaming the area. I can see giant skeletons in the distance, kodiak bears, lizardmen, and basilisks, there are also signs and tracks of hill giants. Examining the hill giant tracks my mind is flooded with images. Images of my broken and battered father lying beside a dead giant. Images of my screaming mother running to my father. I can feel the tears welling up, "No" I say, as I lower my head, "I will not let this happen now." Fighting back the tears I focus on why I am here, Tarskuk, the charm, yes, I have come for the charm and I must remain on track.
From somewhere close by I hear a twig snap, ducking and turning while I draw my sword and dagger I see a lizardman warrior staring at me from a small distance. He is sizing me up, deciding if he might be able to defeat me in battle. Standing, I point my dagger at him and flick the tip upward in somewhat of a rude gesture, he hisses and runs away. The lizardmen here are no match for me, they will keep their distance if they know what’s good for them.
Crossing over a ridge I am caught unaware by a giant skeleton, the creature immediately starts towards me. Speaking quickly I cast a snare spell on it and instantly the skeletons movements are slowed. Pulling my bow I send an arrow into its dead eyeball, the creature doesn’t even slow down. The undead are nasty, already dead they are even harder to kill the second time around. The creature kicks at me with its boney foot, I easily dodge the blow and hack off its leg at the knee. Somewhat off balance it swings at me with a clawed hand, I block the swing and move around behind, saying the ancient words of magic, the creature is rocked as the explosion of fire from my ignite spell goes off right in its chest. The skeleton falls as it tries to turn, lifting its arm it swings towards me, my sword finds the crease at its elbow and slices easily through the dried tendons separating its lower arm from the remainder of its body. After a few more minutes the giant skeleton has been reduced to a large pile of bones, its one eye stares up at me from its socket. Removing my arrow from the other eye I crush the skull under my boot, stomping the dried bones into a pile of dust.
Moving deeper into the mountains I find many tracks of Cyclops, the creatures roam freely here. It will be very hard to track down this Tarskuk. Climbing up on a ridge I look down and see a group of adventurers hunting skeletons and Cyclops. What luck, perhaps they can help me find what I seek. Running down the hill, I approach the group "Greetings Friends" I say, "Greetings" replies a tall, thin wizard. "I see you have been hunting Cyclops, I too am hunting a Cyclops, his name is Tarskuk." The wizard replies "I know nothing of this Tarskuk, what makes this Clops so special?" "He wears a charm, nothing more than a rock really, but very important to me" I said. A gnome from the group stepped forward "I saw some kind of charm around the neck of one of these clops we have killed, I left it on the body as it didn’t look to be worth anything, you may have it if you find it." Could it be? Could the charm I seek be so close? Checking the many corpses I finally find it, the rune of ivy hanging from the neck of the Great Tarskuk. Cutting the leather that holds the rune, I hold it up to the sky, the sun reflects off the smooth black surface and shoots beams of light onto the ground. As I walk away with the rune in my pack I can’t help but think that good things are in store for me, Tunare continues to look after me as I continue my journey towards my destiny.
