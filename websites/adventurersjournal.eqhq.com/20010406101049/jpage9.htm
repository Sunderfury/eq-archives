January 25
Our recent guild meeting at Surefall Glade was pleasant as usual. I met with some of the new additions to the guild and I caught up on the travels and adventures of some of the brothers that I have not seen in a while. We discussed many matters of importance to the guild, and voiced concerns about various issues that have come up throughout the land. The guild itself grows stronger by the day and I can see that Tunare herself has given us Her blessing, I truly believe that soon we will be a force against evil like no other.
The Meeting was not all business however for one of the high points of my day was once again seeing the Lady Siniana, I truly believe she has become more beautiful than the last time I saw her. She has also become much more comfortable and confident within the guild itself as was demonstrated by the way she moved amongst us, chatting here, flirting there, a giggle, a laugh, an angel on Norrath. I found myself once again completely absorbed by her manner and beauty, yet somehow I managed to remain focused on the business at hand. After all the Lady Siniana is very young, mayhaps too young for an old ranger such as myself. Yet she advances in level quite fast and perhaps someday we might travel together, exploring the wonders of Norrath. When the meeting had ended and we were all saying our good-byes, Siniana graced me with a kiss, what a pleasant surprise, ah yes, twas quite sweet indeed.
After the meeting I had planned on travelling to the Rathe Mountains to battle some of the creatures there. I had scouted the area earlier in the week and found it to be infested with giant skeletons and hill giants among other things, and I had thought that a few of my brothers and myself might make our purses bulge with the coin from these giant skeletons. However it was not to be, Klymeth was unable to make the meeting and most everyone else was going into Surefall to replenish supplies. Brothers’ Haemish and Greogor were heading to the frozen wastelands of the North to locate a particular item needed to make Ivy Etched Bracers and I decided to go along with them. The trip to the Everfrost Mountains was quite short as we were able to get the Spirit of Wolf spell cast on us from brother Conwen before we left. The knolls of Blackburrow gave us no trouble at all as we passed through the caves leading North, and soon we emerged into the snow and ice that is Everfrost. As we wandered through the snow and cold searching for signs of a small orc tribe I kept my thoughts on the warmer climates of the plains in an effort to keep from freezing. Soon we picked up the trail for what we hunted, a small group of icy orcs led by an orc named Redwind. It was rumored that the leader of these orcs, the one named Redwind, carried the runes needed to make the Etched bracers. Now before us encamped in long forgotten ruins was this Redwind and another icy orc. We watched them for a little while to make sure there were no others, these two would be no trouble for us, however two more could present a problem as the icy orcs are much larger and stronger than any of the other orc clans. When we were satisfied that there were no others, we made ready to attack. The plan was for brother Greogor to move in a little closer and draw them out of the ruins into battle, then Haemish and I would attack from behind. We would all focus our attack on the leader Redwind as best we could, then turn on the other when Redwind fell. Redwind was a superior foe, but with my brothers Haemish and Greogor with me, who are both somewhat more experienced, I felt confident that we would be all right. Greogor moved in and began taunting them, they charged out of the ruins immediately and engaged him, stronger they might be but obviously not any smarter than their cousin orcs from the east. Haemish pulled back on his Trueshot bow and let loose an arrow which sunk deep into the leg of Redwind, he roared with pain just as he engaged Greogor. In a moment we were both on him from behind, slashing and hacking away. The creature was immense and quite strong, he fought ferociously and it seemed like forever before he finally fell. We turned to face the other orc who was in a fit of rage over his leader being killed. Alas no amount of rage could save him from his fate. The three of us made short work of him, and he fell with a scream. We had all been slightly hurt but thank Tunare nothing very serious at all. We quickly searched the bodies, finding the runes we had come for and a fair amount of gold coin.
In the distance I heard the roar of one of the giant mammoths that roam the wastes of the north, the afternoon was waning away and I wanted nothing more than to be gone from this cold place. Later that evening as we made our way through the hills of Qeynos I pulled the rune from my pack and considered it, was it worth a life to obtain such a rune? I do not know, I know only that Tunare wishes me to continue my battle against evil and I will do that as long as She requires it. As the darkness approached brother Haemish made camp in the hills and Greogor wanted to travel east as fast as possible on business, so we parted ways. After wishing them both well, I decided that I would head into Qeynos and see what the city had to offer this evening. Tis not often that I am near a city, and perhaps I might find some more adventure within its walls, perhaps purchase some equipment, perhaps more likely than not, find a lovely young woman to help remove the chill from my bones.
