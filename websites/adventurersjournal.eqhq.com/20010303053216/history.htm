FAMILY HISTORY
    

          I was born on the 8th day of June in the year 3155, in the Surefall Forest just north of the city of Qeynos. My mother, Etherael, a beautiful and proud elf enchantress and my father, Robert, a human knight raised me with a good mixture of love and discipline. We lived in the forest, apart from the city and my mother taught me the ways of the wood very well, in addition she was careful to teach me the ways of different cultures, races, and civilizations. She also taught me some magic, very little magic compared to what she knew, but enough to be useful in more than a few situations. Because she was an enchantress we had many books and I read a lot, these books gave me an insight into a world that I had never seen. A world of huge gleaming cities, of small villages and towns, of dark castles and dungeons, of mountains and plains, and of all the people and the many different creatures that inhabit them, both friendly and dangerous. My mother taught me many things and I loved her very much.

          I only knew my father for a few years, what I remember most about him was his size, he was huge, towering over me at close to seven feet tall. His massive arms fully the size of most adults legs, yet he could be as gentle as a soft summer breeze when he wanted to be. Robert was his name, he was a warrior, a human knight, and he taught me how to be a man. I remember him spending hours teaching me the ways of battle, much to the dismay of my mother, who would say that it was no use teaching me these things, since she would never let me become a warrior. She would often remind him during these training sessions how they had come to meet. How she had found him gravely wounded after a great battle. At first she had thought him dead, until she heard a small groan and whisper from him. She had knelt down beside him to check his wounds, (often my father would interrupt at this point in the story), "Twas then my son, that I knew there were angels", his voice would boom, "When I opened my eyes and saw the most beautiful creature that I had ever seen." My mother would quickly continue the story (with a huge smile and quite a blush on her cheeks) by saying in addition to the great wounds to his body, during the battle he must have received quite a blow to his head to have rattled his brains so much. She had taken him home that day and cared for his wounds and their love grew quickly and immensely. They were never apart from each other again, until my father died.

          When I was nine years old we left the forest for a lunch outing and were moving through the foothills of the Qeynos Mountains when a wandering hill giant stumbled upon us, the giant immediately rushed us intending, I am quite sure, to kill us all. With blinding speed my father drew his weapon and was on him, it was over in minutes. The giant lay dead on the ground and my father lay beside him. During the battle the giant had scored a devastating blow to my father with his club. My mother worked over him for days, but in the end she could do nothing, except hold him while he slipped away. My father taught me how to fight, how to treat a woman, and how to be an honorable man, I loved him and I miss him to this day.

          My mother wasn’t quite the same after my father died, it was as though a piece of her had died also, she didn’t smile as much and oftentimes she seemed very distant. Life was good for us, but for my mother the days just seemed to drag on, she tried not to let it show, but I could see it. A year ago she come down with Red Elm fever and slowly began to waste away, I held her as she died, as she had held my father. I know she was sad to leave her home and her son, but I believe she was happy to be going to be with my father again, at the very end she whispered his name, then she was gone.

          I left my home on March 26th of the year 3172 to begin my adventure, to find fame and fortune, and hopefully, some of the happiness that my mother and father knew.

                                                                                                                            Temyez
