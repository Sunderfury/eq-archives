Why is Mozilla Better?

Why should you think about using Mozilla? Well first of all lots of people think Mozilla is Netscape. They are dead wrong. Mozilla can be thought of as a more updated, better, and open source version of Netscape. Things have changed. Lots of people try comparing Netscape 4.x with Internet Explorer 5.x. Well gee, Internet Explorer wins that match obviously. But when you start to compare Internet Explorer 5.x with Mozilla 0.9.2. The battle starts to get a bit harder for Internet Explorer. The first thing most Mozilla users notice is by default it looks just like old Netscape. But when you change to the latest "Modern" theme things start to look very nice.


[Click to Enlarge]

Don't like that look? Then simply download another theme. Which is another great feature. Also, it has a very nice feature to block advertisements (one of the many features taken out of the Netscape version). You simply right click on any image to block images from that server. This is a very nice feature for those of you with 56k connections or less.

Very easy to block advertisements

You can also selectively block cookies and remember to block cookies only from certain servers, such as doubleclick.com (an advertising company with a reputation for selling information it gathers by tracking users with cookies). You can also view cookies and selectively delete them, and even block the cookies you delete from ever coming back!


As you can see you can view and remove the cookies you want. You can also remove all of the cookies.


This is an optional option that you can disable if it gets annoying. Clicking "Remember this
decision" will make it always block cookies from 'www.doubleclick.com' without asking you.

Once more, after you remove the cookies they are really removed. Internet Explorer stores cookies and other tracking information in a hidden file called index.dat, even after you chose to delete all cookies (click here to read more). Mozilla also stores form information much like Internet Explorer. Only you can enable Mozilla to fill forms automatically, you don't even have to start typing your first name for it to pop-up for example. You can also just double click form boxes instead to fill them in. Mozilla also supports the optional ability to have different "Profiles", basically different identities when you start up, you can password protect your identity as well. This stops other from viewing your form information, history of the websites you have visited, cookies, etc...

Mozilla also has a nice feature of being able to notify you when a website has been updated:

For web developers, Mozilla supports the recommended w3c set HTML/CSS/CSS2 standards better then any other browser currently available. There are also nice CSS features. Such as the ability to have different colors for objects when the mouse is over them (like MSIE first used, but not just for links). You can also be more specific with the types of objects you want to have styles for. For example if you only want the password fields in the form to have a certain style you just use input[type="password"] there is no way to specify anything but input with Internet Explorer. You can also have different colors for items that are active or have the mouse hovering over them for everything. Take this example from the Linux.com style sheet:

input[type="submit"] { background-color: #ccc; color: #555; margin: 1px; border: 1px solid #777;}
input[type="submit"]:hover { background-color: #eee; color: #36c; }
input[type="submit"]:active { background-color: #eee; color: #9ac; }
div input[type="text"], input[type="password"], textarea { background-color: #eee; border: 1px solid #777; }

input[type="submit"]:active { background-color: #eee; color: #9ac; }

This means when you click on a text input field the background color turns from gray to white. Or when you hover your mouse over a button it makes the background of the button turn from dark gray to light gray and the text turns blue.
