Comments:
Liq's take on march of the wee
Got this song awhile ago and after testing it on greens solo I kinda ignored it because of the damage absorb bringing the song down. Last night I tried playing with it in a group and found out it's not bad at all....

Evidently playing this song brings up an icon which is roughly 18 agility 14 ac and a 12 point damage absorb. Using a selo's drum from nag, the stats are 42 agility, 32 ac and roughly a 28 point damage shield. Once the damage absorb is used, the whole effect goes down.

When I started using this song, the group I was in consisted of monk (puller), ranger, paly, shaman, cleric and me.. all level 52. All the mobs Pulled were between 43 and 50 in singles.

What I first tried was twisting this song while killing level 43+ mobs. I started using weapons and twisting mana/vov but found that the song was getting knocked down as soon as it went up. When I instead went to chain casting march alone (i.e. start and stop the same song so it refreshes every 3 seconds), the tanks started taking a lot less damage while the mobs died faster. When I switched to using nag's drum for extra points, the effect was even more pronounced.

After killing a mob that normally took at least one complete heal (level 49ish bookoo hp and hits for 130ish), we found that this song made the mob concentrate on the monk who only lost a bubble hp all fight. The cleric ending the fight at fm was proof enough alone that there is something to this song.

This was only an hours worth of testing but I so far this is my take.

1. The song needs to be refreshed constantly to be of any real use.
2. The drum improves the song a lot
3. A shaman to slow really improves this song as you have time to put up a new shield between hits.
4. Evidently the mob was sticking to the monk when this song was playing... not sure why exactly (I have a guess) but neither the ranger nor the paly were taunting the mob off him. Thinking that not getting smacked around helped the monk's damage count and hence taunt.

All in all a very worthwhile song for the tank + shamenmojo group I was in. Am thinking that slow especially is important so you have time to refresh the song.

Cheers!
-Liq

Strategies:

The damage absorption seems to help mitigate stuns as well.
[Clavicus] 1. It is most useful at the beginning of a battle (as you can't heal the unwounded).
2. During a battle it only seems better than Nivs / Hymn when you are fighting greens (since they hit for less and less often the AC difference and damage absorption can often work on multiple swings).
3. It is semi useful soloing when charm/kiting with the cycle, Charm/Selo's/Wee (or Hymn) to reduce the damage you take when something goes wrong (unseen mob, lag, etc)
4. Since it uses the drum it is easiest to employ when you are in chant mode.
5. The damage absorption has a small chance to decrease interrupts on your groups casters.
[Rokenn] On a multi-mob pull I was pulsing this while the enchanter mez'ed the extras. The enchanter did not lose too much health, but the surprising thing was that the mez'ed mobs came after me instead of the enchanter when mez broke. Since we generally have better AC/Defense then enchanters this looks like a good way to help the enchanters live longer.
[Fintle] A very effective twist to use with this song is: SoS, NMoTW, Cantata.  When there is a haster in the group (chanter, shaman), this is a great twist to play.  I have seen a noticeable difference in how often the mob hits the tanks.  This is a great way to help keep enchanters alive (in addition to them over eager mages and wiz's who have no idea how to manage agro) . I have used this twist quite often in Drolvarg Captain groups in KC and the poor cleric gets bored (and sometime nukes a little bit as they are just brimming full with mana).  Give it a shot, you might be pleasantly surprised.

Cautions:

Once the damage absorption is used all song effects are lost.
