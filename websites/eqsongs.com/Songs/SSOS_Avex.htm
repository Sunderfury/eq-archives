Feedback on song 27 - SOLON's SONG OF THE SIRENS

While the pet is charmed press F1 to toggle selection between you and the pet. When charm wears off or the pet dies the pet can no longer be selected.

A list of pet commands is as follows -
/pet guard here - tells the pet to guard its current location
/pet guard me - tells the pet to attack anyone that attacks it's master
/pet follow me - tells the pet to follow you, but ends when you cross a zone line.
/pet attack - tells the pet to attack the selected target.
/pet back off - tells the pet to stop its attack.
/pet as you were - returns the pet to neutral, belaying all prior orders.
/pet report health - pet gives you percentage of it's health left.
/pet who the leader - pet reports who commands it (notify their party
that creature is a pet).
/pet get lost - makes a summoned pet kill itself.
/pet taunt on/off
/pet sit
/pet stand
Pet report health and pet who the leader can be useful, (but as the pet says them as opposed to tells you or group say) if you are outside speaking range then you wont hear the result. As charm
doesn't give a success message the command pet who the leader can be very useful to let your party know that you have charmed (if within speaking range anyway).

If unsure how charm works ask an enchanter friend
If unsure how pets work ask a magician or a necromancer friend

If you are within teleport distance an ex pet may teleport to you when charm wears off. This usually happens when indoors where pathing can be an issue. This can be "very" dangerous, so just be aware of it. Aggro teleporting mobs teleport directly into the middle of your body where they can hit you but you can't hit them unless you move slightly.

If anyone casts invis, invis vs undead or camo on you while you have a pet charmed this will break charm instantly. This can be used to start combat against a pet immediately.

Selo's accelerando (unlike SoW) does not effect pets only player party members.

Resistance check for this song is based on the bards charisma, the mob's magic resistance (not fire, cold, poison or disease resistance) and the relative level difference between you and the mob being charmed. The higher the charisma the lower the chance for resistance AND the longer the chance for a full duration charm. Better to charm blues and whites as you will get a better chance of a full length charm. Straight from GZ

Mobs with high magic resistance will resist being charmed a lot even if blue or green. Learn these mobs through trial and error.

There is a 1 in 20 automatic spell failure chance (all other things being equal). Sometimes the odds just seem to go against you. It can be difficult to know sometimes whether you were unlucky or the mob has a high magic resistance. Don't judge the ability/inability to charm a particular mob on a single instance. Give it a couple of goes at least. Best to do your experimentation soling or with a regular partner and getting your charming routine and hot keys sorted out before "inflicting" your charming ability on a group.

Always tell your group that you are charming before you do it and preferably what you are charming as well. Setting up a "Charming now..." hot key is probably the simplest method.

When soloing make sure that the mob being charmed is at least the same level as the mob being attacked otherwise your pet will most likely die first. The idea is to charm, have the pet kill another mob and then kill the pet once charm wears off to get xp from both mobs. Exceptions to this rule are mobs like griffawns/ons/ins/enes that have combat ability above their actual level.

Yellow & especially red con mobs resist more often and charm for less duration. But they hit harder. If you have very high charisma you may wish to "try" your luck. Deep reds are almost impossible to charm or even if you do the charm lasts for such a short time that it is effectively useless.

Avex from Tunare
