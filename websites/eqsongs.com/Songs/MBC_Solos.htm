Berserker has its *advantage* over Celerity. (txt)
by Solos Chimamire

posted 12/21/99 3:23:36 PM
If you arm yourself with some quick weapon that your skills are capped (I would say, perhaps some ~20 delay weapons), then you'll notice that singing berserker not only give you speed (slightly faster than Celerity, but not noticeable), it also gives you a + chance of hitting with dual wield.

(To test it yourself, preferably wield 2 weapons of the same speed, e.g. 2 FS dagger, 2 EBW, etc - so when your secondary hand attack, you'll see 2 lines of attack instead of one.)

I tested it myself in many occasion and proved it with EQP - I'm in fact hitting a LOT more with Berserker than Celerity.

With Celerity, I'll hit with my secondary hand about 30% of the time (eg. 3 swings from secondary hand for every 10 swings), while with Berserker, I'm hitting a whopping 60 to 70% of the time (6 to 7 swings from secondary hand for every 10 swings)

This has been tested in many different combat with different mobs, at different times. Berserker always improves my chances of hitting with secondary weapon.

EQP shows that when I'm using Berserker, I'm doing about 20% more damage over using Celerity, assuming the speed bonus difference is minimal.

Since other class have double attack it's hard for them to see the difference but my SK friend did told me berserker makes his double attack lands more often.

I was also told by my other bardic friends that VoV have the same + chance of hit as the Berserker, but not with other lower level haste songs such as Celerity/Anthem/CoB, etc.

So Berserker doesn't only give str and speed bonus, it also improves our chance of hitting with secondary hand.

My 2 coppers.
