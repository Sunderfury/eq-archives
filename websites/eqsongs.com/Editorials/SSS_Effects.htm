Bottom line is the sword augments percussion and wind songs 100% (same as a store-bought instrument); and string, brass, and singing 80% (80% of a store-bought instrument) rounded down. Hence the disparity we saw with some people saying it augmented 100% and some people saying it augmented 75%.

Since Verant took the time to specifically augment the different musical skills differently, it makes it more likely that the sword augmenting singing is not a bug. In addition, self-only singing buffs and DDs were not augmented at all by the sword. Hastes and slows were not affected either (no surprise there).

The sword seems to proc a lot. Unfortunately, the procs are very streaky (c.f. all the stuff I've said about streaky RNGs being bad because it causes more extreme situations than the odds would normally allow). It's not unusual to go 3-4 fights without a proc, then get 3-6 procs in one fight. Double-procs (two procs on sequential weapon swings) are also very common.

Dance of the Blades stacks with all the enchanter hastes I've tried it with (augment line, swift like the wind, enchanter epic weapon buff). It cancels out Verses when it first procs, but you can sing Verses on top of it. It sorta doesn't stack with illusion: wolf and illusion: werewolf. Those illusions give a +atk bonus as does the proc. A higher level enchanter and lower level druid were not able to cast the illusion over the proc.

The proc did not take hold on the higher level enchanter when he was already in werewolf form. The proc did take hold on the lower level druid's wolf form. In most cases it stacked, but in a couple cases it canceled wolf form. I haven't yet figured out what's causing it to cancel wolf form. It caused a really weird situation where one of our group members had both wolf form and SoW up at the same time.
