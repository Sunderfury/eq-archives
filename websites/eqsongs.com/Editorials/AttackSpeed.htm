Comments
There's a 0.5 delay biasing for weapon delays. i.e. a 19 delay weapon is really 19.5 delay. This extra 0.05 seconds per attack round is mostly negligible, but skews some of the % speedup figures if you use the documented weapon delay instead of the actual.

The % speedup remains the same regardless of the delay of the weapon you're using. i.e. Verses gave roughly a 30% speedup whether I was using my 35 delay fists, or a 19 delay dagger. 

So far any haste item plus any haste song/spell speedup gives you a 75% speedup. This may be a bug. Best way to take advantage of this is via the Augmentation spell, since it replenishes stamina.

If you have two haste song/spell effects, the faster overrides.
Chain does not overwrite Shiftless Deeds. I tested this both while continuously Chaining, and after letting Chain up. The mob continued to attack at the speed of Shiftless Deeds. I did not test this with Tepid or Languid.

-Kaearia

Information on Kae's testing methodology:
Make sure you have 0% PL or very close to it. Make sure your ping time is relatively stable too. 
Equip an instrument and remove your lambent gloves (they're magical), or equip a FS weapon and a shield. Find a wisp.
Get it to attack you without hurting it (Chain works fine).
 
Stopwatch ready, attack it. Ignore the first attack message so you don't bias the results because it's in synch with you turning on auto-attack. Upon seeing the second attack message, start your stopwatch. This is attack #0. Count the attacks. At #50 (or #20 or #10), stop your stopwatch. Enter the time into a spreadsheet.

Be sure you're counting attack messages, not swing animations or sound effects. I log these tests so I can go back later and recount the attack messages to be sure I haven't mis-counted.
