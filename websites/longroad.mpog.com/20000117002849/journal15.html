Bad Luck

Day Eighty-Three  - South Qeynos

When I awoke I gathered with my temple brothers and sisters for breakfast. After we all finished eating, there was usually a lot of conversation about the table. Sister Leora recounted her recent trip to the hellish Lavastorm Mountains, a place she described as the most foul corner of Norrath. We all listened intently to her descriptions of wild beasts made of fire and fire breathing dragons no larger than a horse. While most seemed frightened, I have to admit I was somewhat excited by the tale. That place sounded like something Surfal would enjoy as well. I made a note to myself to tell him of it when next we met.

As we broke from the table, I went to the courtyard to enjoy the morning sun. Sir Wolten, who had been my mentor, approached me and we spoke at length about my recent travels. He had been reading my letters and he had many questions about the vile necromancer we encountered on Erud's Crossing. Suddenly, the conversation changed course and I realized he had been waiting to bring something up.

"Jhared. Do you remember the letter you brought me from Drosco?"

I shuddered slightly and gathered myself. "Of course, master. I remember it quite well. In fact, I seem to remember you had wanted to ask me something when I returned from Erud's Crossing."

Wolten smiled. "Yes, I admit I have been hesitant to bring it up. I know that Drosco's death disturbed you." Wolten looked up into the sky and continued, "Jhared, I need your help. But this time, you will not find the mission to be so pleasant."

"Master, I do not fear the course Karana choses for me. If there is something you require of me, just ask. I will not disappoint you." 

Wolten frowned, which caused a certain amount of anxiety for me. "It isn't that you would disappoint me, Jhared. The mission I am sending you on requires you to travel a great distance. Once you reach your destination, your life would be in great danger. I am concerned. These same forces you would be at risk from are the same ones who killed Drosco."

It was only a small gulp, but Wolten noticed it and grinned ever so slighty. "Master, I would be honored to continue Drosco's work. As you know, I always admired him when I was studying here. It would be my pleasure. I have many friends who would watch my back, as well. I will be fine."

"Your friends are exactly why I chose you for this, Jhared. You have a strong group of allies in them. Their assistance may save your hide if things go bad in Freeport."

Freeport? I never imagined he meant Freeport. All my life I had heard stories of that cursed town and its dirty streets. Compared to Qeynos, Freeport was pure chaos. Travelers often tell of the rampant corruption in the City Guard there and I have even heard that necromancers walk the streets in some parts of the city. However, if Wolten needs me to go there I suppose I shall have to go.

"What is it you need, exactly?"

"You must deliver this message to the Guildmaster in the Hall of Truth. She will be expecting you, but beware...she tells me that the Knights of Truth are at war with several other factions within the city. The Bloodsabres will not be your only foe in the streets of Freeport. Keep an open eye."

I took the note and I headed back to say farewell to my comrades. I also wanted to thank Renic for his support. While I was packing my backpacks for the trip, Renic swung the dorr to my room wide open and his face was ashen white. "Isn't your traveling companion named Surfal Dayspring?"

"Aye, Master Renic. Surfal is one of best friends. I was just about to go looking for him in the Qeynos Hills."

Renic led me to the courtyard where a bloody corpse lay across the grass. It was a Dark Elf, dressed in fine silks and jewelry. From the look of him, he had been dead for at least a day.

Most of the temple knights were assembled around the body, and Wolten nodded my way in a sign of support. Renic started to speak but I knew what he was going to say before it left his lips, "This is Jracol Brestiage, the man your father suspected as a spy for Carson McCabe. It appears that your friend Surfal got into a fight with him outside the city."

Wolten Put his hands on my shoulders, "Looks like made a good accounting for himself, too."

Renic frowned at Wolten. "Jhared, you realize that this puts your friend Surfal in great danger. Jracol was no mere errand boy. He was a highly valued agent for McCabe. He will send out trackers."

I knew that the minute he confirmed that Surfal had been the one that killed Jracol. 

"Jhared, there is something else." Renic put his hand out, revealing a bloody note. "He had this on him."

Carson,

Your suspicions were correct. The Irsei child is the same one our people spotted in Rivervale. But he is no servant to Bayle. He walks under Karana's wing in the tutelage of Renic Losaren. He will hard to kill until he leaves the city. But I think I found a way to make sure he comes to you. 

A message I left in the Qeynos taverns begs for the aid of noble adventurers. It asks for them to come to Highpass to save the Keep from the Mucktails and Shraloks. Surely many will answer the note, and I imagine Ashlan will be gald to have them in the pass, but most importantly we will bring the young Knight of Thunder out of his shell.

Make sure you make a warm reception for him. My sources say he has no idea his father ever served you. All he needs now is a little bow around his head to make this anymore perfect. Happy Birthday, Carson and enjoy your present.

Jracol

I tried to stand straight but I couldn't. Only yesterday I learned of my father's past and already it was on my doorstep, with an assassin's blade in hand. 

Renic leaned over to me. "Jhared, the letter never reached Carson. He doesn't know about Jracol's death just yet, though I cannot imagine it will take long for word to reach him. You must find your friend and make sure he stays clear of Highhold Keep for some time. I will try and think of a good way to resolve this while you and Surfal lay low."

I thanked everyone and started back for my room. I finished packing my backpacks and I headed out for Surefall Glade. Surfal was not the kind of man who liked to lay low. It would take take some convincing. As I headed out of the city gates, a familiar voice called my name.

I turned to see Fyodor and a High Elf walking my way. "Jhared, I tried to visit you this morning but that Temple of Thunder is locked up tighter than a drum. What's going on?"

I looked at the High Elf and back to Fyodor and said, "Nothing really. Just some important ceremonies, nothing of interest."

Fyodor broke out into laughter. "Jhared, this is Hybrow. This young looking Elf is one of my oldest friends, though he is twice my age for sure. You can speak freely in front of him. I trust him with my life, so I doubt your secrets are at risk."

Hybrow bowed and introduced himself. His words flowed like poetry as he spoke. High Elves were an unusual people and they made me a little nervous. They looked so perfect, as if their bodies and faces were made of porcelain. It was all I could do to keep from touching him to make sure he was real.

As Hybrow shook my hands, he turned his head to the right and sniffed my shirt cuffs. "You smell of Dark Elf, little one. Has one of my cousins troubled you lately?" Hybrow seemed insistent in his query and his face was very serious, despite his words.

"Yes, it is why I must speak to Fyodor. If you would both like to accompany me to the Hills, we could talk along the way."

Hybrow grimaced openly, causing another round of laughter from Fyodor. "My old friend just hates interacting with his lesser cousins. Therefore, I insist we join you." Hybrow gave Fyodor a wry smile and we set out across the Hills.

Day Eighty-Four - Qeynos Hills

"So Jhared, are you planning on joining us on our expedition?" Fyodor asked as we came within sight of Blackburrow's face in the mountainside.

"What trip is that, Fyodor?" I asked.

"Didn't you get Surfal's note? He sent word to Grannith, Brent and I that he was in need of our assistance. Something about a city under siege from Gnolls and Orcs. The place is up in the mountains near Rivervale. I think its called..."

"Highhold Keep."

"Yes! That's the place. Surfal headed out two days back, he wanted to scout ahead and asked that we follow him along as soon as we could. That's why Hybrow and I came by the Temple this morning."

At the pieces were still fitting together within my head. Surfal must have seen the note Jracol left in one of the taverns. Then Jracol must have encountered him before he left. With a two day lead, Surfal would be there by tomorrow. "Fyodor, there is no time to lose! We have to stop him."

Hybrow smiled a twisted grin. "Suddenly I am beginning to suspect this trip is going to be worth my time. Why don't you explain why we must stop Surfal from helping the people of Highhold, Jhared?"

As I told them the story of my father and Carson McCabe, I felt their moods take a turn for the worst. It was no secret that Fyodor considered Surfal his pupil. When I recounted the battle with Jracol, Fyodor smiled to hear that the ranger had fared so well against the Dark Elf. But when I showed them the note, they both understood.

Fyodor immediately went into action. "We'll need transportation. We'll never catch Surfal on foot."

Hybrow shook his head. "There isn't a wizard in this entire region who could teleport any further than West Karana. Our best bet is to hire a Bard."

I knew that bards were thought to be the ultimate traveling companions. I hadn't thought to use one now, but Hybrow was right. With a Bard's songs, we might reach the Pass by tomorrow night.

Fyodor took off in the direction of Qeynos. "I'll return with a Bard. You two try and find Grannith. We'll need that little blockhead for sure."
