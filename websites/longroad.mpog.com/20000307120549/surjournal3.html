The Hunt for Cros Treewind

Day Fifteen - Qeynos Hills

As dawn broke we roused from our make shift camp and proceeded to find Cros Treewind. Qeynos Hills covers a very large area and if a Ranger doesn't want to be found then odds are very likely that you won't find them. However, I had taken a special interest in my tracking training and felt very much up for the task. I can just imagine the look on Fyodor's face when I prove him wrong, prove them all wrong.

By late morning I caught my first glimpse of what might be Cros Treewind's trail. My concerns were that the trail was leading in a northwest direction would could take us dangerously close to Black Burrow. Tales of what Sabertooth clan members have been doing to near by settlements and lone adventures are well documented. Just as I was about to warn the others of the possible danger the world went crazy.

The first one out of the bushes caught me flush in the lower back with a cracked staff while two other Gnolls jumped on Jhared from both sides. I could hear Root begin an incantation when a forth gnoll attacked him wielding a rusty short sword. The other three were quickly on Fyodor. I have never heard such loud yelping in my life. I was able to push the gnoll pup back a couple steps and draw my blade. As I ended my assault on the first gnoll I spun to see that Jhared had killed his attackers as well and was casting an enchantment on Root. Fyodor had killed the three on him before any of them even had a chance to scratch him. As Root's gnoll finally fell Root screamed, "How in the name of Karana could a Ranger lead us into an ambush setup by Gnolls. Damn boy, look at the paw prints".

I was devastated. I was so intent on proving my tracking skills worthy that I completely ignored the obvious tracks in search of the more obscure ones. Perhaps I would have been better off had I allowed the gnoll to end my life right there. At least that would have been a quicker death than the lacerations that Root's words were putting on me. Jhared calmed everyone down and soon we were on our way. The only thing that can save what little pride I have left is watching them eat crow as Cros Treewind reveals himself to be the hero I know he is.

Day Sixteen - Qeynos Hills

Barely thirty minutes into the trail and a man covered in blood ran screaming past us. I have never seen such terror in all my days. He kept screaming Treewind's name over and over. Jhared and Root tried to stop the man to administer first aid through healing magic but he was running to fast. We gathered ourselves together and proceeded slowly down the trail in the direction from which the man was running. A hundred yards up the trail my heart stopped and my blood ran cold. A man was lying face down in the dirt beside a rabid grizzly and on the man's back was carved the words, "A Good Poacher". I lost my breakfast.

The scene was easy enough to read. Two men, apparently merchants, had been walking the trail when a grizzly, mad from rabies, attacked them. It appeared that during the struggle a third set of prints entered the area and attacked the two men. The tracks were the same ones that I had been following for the past two days. I suddenly remembered the other man and started to runoff in the direction that he was headed when he passed us when I heard a blood-chilling scream. By the time we reached him he was dead, the same words carved into him as well. No one had to say anything, their silence let me know what we they were thinking.

That night, after I thought everyone was asleep, I packed my things and started to leave. "Where are you going?", Jhared asked. I threw my ranger tunic at his feet and simply said, "away". "That would be easiest, wouldn't it", he said. What doers he know of easy? My life has been filled with disappointments and failures. This is the natural conclusion. Jhared cast some protective magic on me and told me that if I truly wanted to leave then he wished me well. However, he saw something in me, something bigger. Chance didn't bring us together, it was Karana. Also, he said, could I really let Root and Fyodor get the best of me by sneaking out? Or was I going to show them what a true ranger was like. For a second time, Jhared Irsei has saved my life. I slept well.
