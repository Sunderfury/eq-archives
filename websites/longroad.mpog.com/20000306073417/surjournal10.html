Running for Home

Day Forty-Four - Western Plains of Karana

Four foot long, no, probably closer to four and a half. The indent has to be close to twelve inches. The worst ranger on his worst day would have no trouble tracking this beast. There were Hill Giants near by, very near by. This footprint was all the proof anyone needed.

I had thought I had heard a low rumble in the night but with the ringing in my ears my senses could not be trusted. At the very worst I feared it might be approaching rains but never a Hill Giant. Thank Karana that the tracks were leading to the north and our journey was taking us to the west, towards the Qeynos Hills.

Part of me was curious to see how Fyodor would react to seeing a Hill Giant; a bigger part of me hoped I never found out. Jhared had asked if it was a male or female and told him male. It was purely a guess but I had my pride at stake. I fully believe that once my tracking improves that I will be able to answer such questions truthfully but for now, a slight deception wouldn’t hurt. Besides, after the imbroglio with the Wisp my ego couldn’t stand much more whipping.

It was decided that we distance as much land between it and us as possible. This would keep us closer to the water and add perhaps an extra day to the journey but better to not tempt fate and live then be foolish and bury your friends.
