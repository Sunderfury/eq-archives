The Mucktail War

Day Ninety-One - Highhold Pass

I listened in to Fyodor and Surfal's conversation with a certain amount of distraction. Fyodor was trying to find out all he could about Surfal's encounter with the Lady Isabo. We were all astounded with the incredible gifts she had bestowed upon him.

I remembered my own encounter with the beautiful Isabo. Fresh out of the Temple she had appeared from nowhere and given me a mace. When I asked her about it, she had said, "Though you do not know it yet, you and I will someday be strong allies. Use this and we will meet again."

I had recognized her name immediately when Surfal recounted his story of meeting her and I had decided to keep it to myself. Perhaps she was some Avatar of Karana, a blessed angel of mercy meant to watch over his followers. I wasn't sure but I knew that when I met her again I had even more to thank her for.

We stayed in the tavern waiting for Captain Ashlan. We had been told that he wanted to meet with us regarding our helping in the defense of the Keep. I was still a bit nervous about exposing myself to Carson's revenge but I knew that my friends were right. I needed to face this before I let it rule my life.

Ashlan arrived shortly after dusk. He bought us all a nice dinner and after a long discussion we agreed to take on the role of Keep defenders for a while. Ashlan had heard of Grannith and expressed his hope that we would be able to convince our Dwarven friend to come and join us. It seems that word of Grannith's exploits in the Battle of the Wall in Rivervale had preceded him.

McCabe must have a fourtune at his disposal, because we were all to be paid a healthy sum for every Gnoll and Orc scalp we delivered. We were even given a small stipend every week to maintain our living expenses while we stayed here.

I told myself that I would only stay on until I had something to connect Carson to the illegal actions he was involved in. I know that Guildmaster Lorsaren had not wanted me to come here, but now that I was I thought it best to do a little investigating.

We all went to bed after the meeting and agreed to meet up at the South Gate in the morning to watch for Mucktail Gnolls.

Day Ninety-Three - Highhold Pass

The past two days have passed like hours. Within minutes of arriving at the South Gate, we were immediately under siege. There were two other group of mercenaries at the gate and we all tried our best to watch each other's backs. The Gnolls attacked from the south with no consideration for their own safety. Gnoll Soothsayers and Shaman keep the Gnolls well healed and rooted the more dangerous of our forces, leaving them at the mercy of the Gnoll attackers. Each wave was led by Gnoll Champions, large brutish Gnolls who proved far superior in combat to most of our allies. Only our quick thinking brought us through alive.

The Gnolls retreated when a large group of reinforcements arrived from the Keep, but we spent the next 36 hours resting and fighting. Occasionally the Gnolls brought with them their tainted wizards, the dreaded Flamepaws and those fights were usually the worst. I watched many a Volunteer die a horrible burning death at their hands. Once I caught sight of a particularly well armored Gnoll in the pass, but he never ventured close enough to get a good sight of. Some of the others said they had seen him as well.

Ashlan called him Grenix and said they believed he was a leader of some kind amongst the Mucktails. Very little was known about him. Ashlan also said he believed the Mucktails had a base camp in the Eastern Plains of Karan but he had never successfully found it. Surfal's eye lit when he heard that and I knew it wouldn't be long before my curious friend went tracking their lair.

We only have about 6 more hours until we are needed back at our posts, so I will end this journal here. I can only hope that my letters find Grannith, Valarie, Brent and Root soon. We need all the assistance we can get and I fear the Mucktails will overcome us by the week's end.

Day Ninety-Eight - Highhold Pass

What a strange hell is battle. Twice I have fought in large scale skirmishes and both times I have found them to be frightening and wonderful. Though the carnage sickened me at first, I now find myself reveling in the frenzy of each day. I have been learning tactics and strategy from a Dwarven Cleric from Kaladim. He has taught me many things and I have found that I am a very effective leader in small group battles. It is a role I enjoy and as the days pass I find myself getting lost in it all. I have made many new friends and I am glad to be surrounded by my oldest friends as well. Grannith, Valarie and Brent arrived a few days ago and have proven to be wonderful additions to the squad. An elven enchanter named Arsindrick has also proven to be a wonderful ally and I find his company to be particularly comforting, perhaps it is his relaxed nature.

I know my goals were to look into Carson's business but how can I leave my friends and allies at the mercy of the Mucktails for such personal reasons. They need me and I know I must serve them first. I received a letter from Guildmaster Lorsaren and he is not pleased. He feels I am losing sight of my more important goals. He wants me to move on to Freeport to deliver the message from Sir Wolten. I informed him I would do as he asked as soon as the situation improved here in the pass and no sooner.

Karana would never forgive me for abandoning these people.

I have killed more Gnolls than I can count and though I take little pleasure in it, I rest well at night knowing that the next day may finally be the end of the onslaught. Ashlan fears that the Shralok Orcs to the North will wait until we are at our weakest and then attack the North Gate. I cannot imagine an Orc being so intelligent but he assures me that the Shraloks are not to be underestimated. I have gained Ashlan's trust and soon I hope I shall have his respect as well. He is much like my father, only more ravaged by battle.

Perhaps tomorrow will be the end.

Day One Hundred and Two - Highhold Keep

We waited at our posts for 36 hours to be sure, but it seems the siege is finally over. The Mucktails no longer swarm the South Gate and it appears we have won a lasting victory. Ashlan is organizing search and destroy parties to run down the remaining Mucktails in the Eastern Plains but I doubt I will sign up for that duty. The time has come for me to address my responsibilities to the Temple. I will try and leave in the morning. I hope my friends decide to join me.
