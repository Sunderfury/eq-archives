The Voice of an Angel

Day Eighty-Seven - Highhold Pass

Gnolls dropped with one slice of my blade or one swing of my mace.  As soon as one would attack me I would just as quickly defeat it.  When it came to hunting gnolls outside of Qeynos I had honed my skill and was prepared for anything.  This is not Qeynos.

As soon as I rounded the first bend in Highhold Pass a gnoll soothsayer assaulted me.  The creature stood about two feet taller than myself but I have fought taller opponents in the past and Fyodor had taught me well in the art of defending blows.  As it started to cast it’s spells I kicked him in his knee effectively breaking its concentration.  My mace struck true twice, once in his arm and the next to his ribs.  The cracking sound was glorious, but short-lived.  The spell caster twirled his hands in the air and chanted something in gnollish and my feet were rooted to the ground.  In my initial panic I let down my guard and the gnoll bashed my head with his great staff.  Had my feet not been rooted I surely would have fallen back.

His second blow glanced off my tunic and I responded with a thrust that went wide left.  The gnoll’s next three punches hit hard and consecutively in my neck and shoulder.  Thgough my throat was on fire I managed to get off a Flame Lick spell.  With the soothsayere slightly easier to hit I connected with a low slash to his legs and then it happened.  I looked up hoping to see his face twisted in pain just as he got off a spell that sound like an explosion.  My skin was covered in flames and my eyes burned.  The air seemed to be sucked out of my lungs.

Though my eyes were clouded I had full functionality of my hearing.  Through the barks of the gnol and the sounds of his magic cracking the air I could also hear a loud thumping noise.  Within seconds the air was still and the smoke cleared.  Standing before me was a vision of beauty.  Was this a messenger of Karana or Tunare come to take me to the next plane.

“So, are you getting to sit there with that stupid look on your face or are you going to follow me to safety”, the lady asked.

I staggered to my feet and cast minor healing on myself a few times so that I would at least be able to keep pace with her.  She asked me if I would verbally commit to following her.  Once I agreed she began to sing the most hypnotic song.  Although I consider myself to be a fast runner I realized just how slow I really was.  Keeping pace with the lady was easy but our speed had to be twice that of normal men. 

She led me to the Keep entrance and asked me too verbally state that I would disband with her.  I did as she requested and her music seemed to no longer affect me.

“Who are you?” I asked

“You may call me Isabo for now, what are you doing here?” she asked.  “Are you one with McCabe or do you seek the princess?”

“Lady Isabo, I know not what you speak.  I am merely here to assist the citizens in their fight against the orcs and gnolls.”

“Let me see your weapon” she demanded.  “This sword and mace or perhaps the shoddiest craftsmanship I have ever seen.  Follow me to my strong box and don’t be slow, I have other matters to attend to besides saving wood elves from there own ambitions.”

She led me into the keep and down a short flight of stairs.  We turned to the right and saw a banker wielding a scimitar.

“What sort of banker needs to be so well armed, Isabo?”

“You are new here, Surfal, but you will learn quickly.  All is not as it seems and very soon you will have to make a stand with one faction or the other.  There is no middle road.”

“How do you know my name?” I asked.

“I know much for I have been around longer than you and I usually listen instead of speak all the time.  I am going to give you something that should assist you in your travels.  It is a polished granite tomahawk.  I removed it from the severed hand of a gnoll.  Its brother has one just like it and will no doubt be coming to claim this one.  I may ask for this back someday so you best guard it with your life.”

I tried to speak but she cut me off.

“Why do you carry a shield?  Has your guild master not taught you the art of dual wield?  Never the less, I have a spare whip that you can practise the art of dual wield on.  Need I say that the exact rule applies, I will be asking for these back some day so guard my possessions with your life.”

I started to thank her for the arms but before I could she hit her drum and was gone.  Could Tunare herself be as beautiful as this lady could?  Her voice was that of an angel and she moved like water.  It was obvious that she had seen many battles and lived to tell the tale but yet she remained very feminine.  I can only hope that Karana or Tunare allow me to see her once more, the fair Isabo.
