A Mountain Trek

Day Eighty-Five - Northern Plains of Karana

The encounter with the treant was uplifting.  My new Trueshot bow would prove an invaluable weapon I was certain.  I could not wait to show it Binwin. He would be the one to most appreciate it for what it truly was.  I would even like to show it to Root so that he could see that I was truly a ranger worthy of title.  However, those things must wait, for now my attention must be square on trying to navigate the less familiar area of East Karana.

I had been in this area before on my trips to Rivervale with my Ka-Tet but this was my first time here alone.  I opted to stop for the night near the large wooden bridge and continue on in the morning.  There were many adventurers here and I have learned that there is some safety in numbers.  As I made my camp I was joined by a young druid named Encino.  He shared his summoned food and drink with me and we shared stories well into night.  At long last, when the fire was nearly burned out, he cast a few protective spells upon us and welcomed sleep took me instantly.

Day Eighty-Six - Eastern Plains of Karana

I awoke about thirty minutes before dawn and gathered my gear.  Encino did likewise and we parted company.  Before leaving, though, he cast his protective spells upon me and then enchanted my legs with Spirit of the Wolf.  He explained that this area was very hostile and the place I was going was up a long mountain pass.  Without this spell upon me I would most likely die before I reached the pass, or, if I did make it that far, whatever was behind me would catch me on the long incline.   I thanked him once more and was off.

Encino had spoken the truth.  I learned to recognize many new tracks on my way to Highhold.  The lions in West Karana were mere kittens compared to the patriarchs here.  The snakes were twice as long and the wolves were ten times more ferocious.  In my haste to cross the land I nearly ran straight into a one eyed man that stood nearly as tall as a hill giant.  Perhaps it would have been wiser to wait on Jhared or perhaps sent word to Valarie for assistance but those options were no longer available to me.  I used my tracking skills to the best of my ability and had one encounter that lasted no longer than six swings. 

The slope in the pass was exactly as it was described to me.  Though the sun was well past its zenith I decided to make the journey while my feet were still blessed.  The slope has little to offer in the ways of excitement but went on forever.  I was not allowed the luxury of letting my thoughts wander, though, for even the smallest missed step would send me over the ridge to a most horrible death.  After what seemed to be an age I at last reached my destination.  The pass was narrow which would hinder most fighting I was certain.  Rather than brave the area at night, I opted for camping at the pass entrance.  The sounds of barking and steel on steel would make sleep hard at best and most probably impossible, but Fyodor has taught me that a fighter with tired legs will most likely end up taking the rest that never ends. 

I ate the last of my muffins from Qeynos and sparingly drank from my last flask.  Standing, I cast Skin Like Wood and Endure Fire upon myself.  Feeling as safe as I was going to get, I closed my eyes, and blessed sleep came.
