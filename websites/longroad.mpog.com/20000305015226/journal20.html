Facing McCabe

Day One Hundred and Four - Highhold Keep

When I awoke this morning, I knew I had to make some hard decisions. I knew in my heart that Hybrow had done no wrong with the Tier Dal and that my reaction was based purely on my sheltered upbringing. I needed to find Hybrow and make sure he understood that. Perhaps even more important than that, I needed to determine what Carson's next move would be. This assassin would likely not be the last. For the safety of my friends and myself, I needed to do something quick. For reasons I couldn't begin to explain, I didn't feel a drive for revenge against McCabe.

In Surfal's case, he had been avenging the "murder" of one of his own men. In my case, he was trying to settle a score with my father. Somehow that made everything seem less personal. In the back of mind I know part of this is that I like it here so much. I enjoy my position with the volunteers and I love the respect and trust they all have in me. Perhaps I am letting all this go to my head, that must be it, because I find myself willing to forgive and forget with a man who just tried to have me killed.

There is only one course of action.

I will have to approach McCabe myself.

Day One Hundred and Four (evening) - Highhold Keep

I left my room this morning to try and find Carson McCabe. I had expected he would be a difficult man to find and approach. In my mind, I was modeling him after a similar man in a similar position in Qeynos, Antonius Bayle. Nothing could have been farther from the truth.

As I walked the hallways of the Keep, I noticed a man standing with the guards who had an air of confidence about him so overwhelming that it seemed to shine. He stood with presence and his words were precise and intelligent. Looking at the way everyone listened to him and watched him, it was easy to tell he was a person of high respect in the Keep. I quickly gathered he was McCabe.

As I approached him, I saw his eyes squint as he looked at me. I had always been told I was a good likeness of my father, except for the beard which he never wore. His eyes showed a small measure of confusion then gleamed as if he were greatly amused. He smiled.

"You must be the man I have heard so much about. The hero of the recent Gnoll attack, the mighty Jhared Irsei." His comments seemed genuine enough but I knew he was testing to see if knew who had sent the "messenger" last night.

"Lord McCabe, I am Jhared Irsei, Knight of Thunder and Loyal Servant of Karana. Just as my father was before me, I am at your service."

Carson's face went ashen. He did not know about my father's journal. He had obviously not expected me to know about their feud. "Perhaps we should speak privately where we can better enjoy each other's company, Jhared." As he began to walk away from me, urging me to follow, I knew I was taking a great risk. He could easily have had men waiting behind any of these doors who would kill me without a thought. More than likely he could do the job quite nicely himself.

I swallowed my fear and followed.

We went up several flights of stairs and settled into a well furnished office on the third floor. He sat in a comfortable wooden chair and offered me another, which faced him. He put no table or desk between us, which I took as a good sign. He was letting me know that he meant me no harm just then. It also showed a certain informality which I took to suggest his desire to speak bluntly.

"If you know of your father's days here, I assume you know how he left my service."

I smiled. "McCabe, my father betrayed you. In order to save you from committing a terrible act of violence against an innocent child, he disobeyed you and for that you targeted him for execution. Just as you targeted me last night."

He showed no sign of surprise at my bold accusation.

"Your assassin was unable to complete her mission and I assume that one of her poisoned darts was meant for Surfal Dayspring, after she finished with me." Carson nodded in agreement. That admittedly threw me off, he was so confident that he simply admitted to the crime without any explanation.

"Jhared, you amaze me. You knew before you came here that I would likely make an attempt on your life but you came anyway. At first I believed you were unaware of your father's situation but now that I know you are I am confused as to why you have taken such a passionate role in the defense of my Keep. Are you plotting against me or do you simply have a death wish?"

"Neither. My friend was attacked by your spy in Qeynos. He defended himself and killed Jracol as I am sure you know. He then left bound for here, unaware of the danger. I could not allow him to come to harm, so I rushed here to tell him. I then decided that I was needed here. The people of this Keep do not deserve to die at teh hands of those wretched Gnolls, no matter what their Lord does under the table."

Carson laughed. "You are so much like your father, Jhared. God, how I enjoyed his company. Can I offer you a drink?" He stood and walked to a chianti of wine and filled two glasses. I knew the wine might be poisoned but I also knew he could kill me with his bare hands as easily and decided to play it out. I accepted a glass and drank. It was quite delicious.

"Elven wine. It has no equal. Your father loved Elven wine." I was forced to agree with my father here. It was wonderful. I had never known my father to drink, but I am not surprised he did.

"Carson, if you loved my father so, why did you decide to try and kill him?"

"Don't be foolish, Jhared? You strike as far too intelligent to ask that question. Your father betrayed me and regardless of my feelings, I had to punish him. It was business. Also, I wanted him dead for what he did to me. He was like a younger brother to me and he stabbed me in the back over a stupid peasant child. I should have known that day would come but I resented it anyway."

"So where does that leave us, Carson?" I tried my best to avoid showing the fear his words had caused in me.

"I do not know, Jhared. You have bettered my assassin. Your friend has uncovered and bettered my spy. You have proven to be a very expensive hobby and I am starting to see a benefit in keeping you alive. I will make you a deal, Jhared Irsei. I will forgive your father's transgressions and those of your friend Surfal in exchange for one thing."

I steeled myself for his request. I was sure it would be something I could not do, no matter what the gain.

"I want you to do something your father failed to accomplish in his tenure with me here. I want you bring me the head of Recnek Shralok and I want 300 platinum as well. I am a business man and I am willing to be reasonable. Your life, the life of your friend and my gratitude in exchange for those two things. What do you say?"

"I assume this Recnek Shralok is a leader of some kind to the Orcs which plague your North Gate. I assume the 300 plat is the ransom you asked for on the child my father saved. Your offer is generous and therefore I accept. You and I will be at peace until this deed is accomplished?"

"Unless I suspect you have given up your quest, you will be safe in my Keep. But I cannot save you from the Tier Dal, that is the one thing I cannot give you. They will seek revenge for their fallen sister. She was rather expensive and well respected amongst her people. You will surely hear from associates at some point."

"Let me worry about the Dark Elves, Carson. I will gladly accept the responsibility for my own actions and for those of my father. You will have the head of this Recnek Shralok and you will get your blood money. Then we are settled and I will move on and go about my business."

Carson nodded. "I will miss you, Jhared. Your mother must have been a spirited lass. Your father was never this bold." With that he laughed and walked to his desk, waving me out of the room.

I wasn't sure if I had just made a deal with the devil or not, but I found Carson remarkably likable for a man who wanted to kill me. Under different circumstances I even think I could have admired him. I thank Karana those circumstances never came to fruition.
