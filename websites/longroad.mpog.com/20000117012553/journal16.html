Samwize and the Giant

Day Eighty-Five  - Western Plains of Karana

Fyodor had arrived early this morning with a young Woof Elf named Samwize. Samwize explained that if we would pay him a platinum a piece, he would run with us all the way into Highhold Pass.

We all coughed up the coin and set out across the plains. Traveling with Samwize was everything I had ever heard. We traveled with the speed of birds, and if you looked away from the ground below you, your eyes would fool you into thinking you were flying. It was wonderful. Had I not been so worried about Surfal, I would have wanted it to continue for days.

When we reached the second Guard Tower, I begged that we stop while I rested. Samwize laughed. "Silly Human. You needed only tell me you were winded. Allow me to demonstrate..." As Samwize changed the song he was singing to a lively jig, I felt my weary legs rejuvenate with life. I was no longer tired and in fact I felt unusually vigorous. We set out again with no need for rest.

I was starting to like this Bard, despite his sarcastic tone.

As we rounded a large hill in the middle of the plains, Samwize stopped dead in his tracks. "Everyone stay low, Giant ahead."

I peeked over the crest of the hill to get a look below. Hill Giants were a frightening sight, but one I couldn't resist. As the Giant grew closer, Fyodor suddenly jumped to his feet. "My God! There is a child down there."

We all stood and saw the poor child. She was running across the plains and the Giant was only minutes from her. As I looked in the direction the Giant had come from, I could see smoke rising from a small cottage.

When I looked back, Samwize was gone. "Distract the Gianr, I will get the girl."

I was in amazement as Samwize sped right in the path of the mighty Giant. I turned to Hybrow. "Light him up, my Elven friend. Let's see those fireworks of yours."

Hybrow's eyes gleamed with delight. "Very well then, hold on your trousers gentleman." Suddenly a giant ball of fire flew from his hands and soared towards the Giant. It struck true and gained us the pleasure of the Giant's attention.

The Giant bolted in our direction.

"Stand your ground, boys. We will likely fall to this one, but we must give Sammy the time to get that child clear." Fyodor tightened the grip on his axe. I pulled my hammer from its sling and offered a prayer to Karana. Hybrow simply danced a little jig. I was starting to see why Fyodor liked this snooty Elf.

The Giant towered above us. Our initial strategy was simply not to get driven into the Earth. We managed to dodge the first of the Giant's powerful blows but the second caught me in shoulder. I flew a good ten feet through the air and landed hard. My shoulder was crushed for sure and I could feel the shattered ribs in my chest as they scraped at my lungs.

Fyodor's best hits were like mosquito bites to the mighty Giant and it made it way to finish me off. As it raised its hands for the kill, I suddenly felt my body lifted from the ground. As I did my best to keep conscious, I realized what had happened. Samwize had returned for us. Fyodor had slung me over his shoulder and we were running for our lives. The Giant gave chase but could not catch us. As we crossed the plains into the Northern fields, the Giant turned and gave up.

"Sorry for the delay, boys. I had to get that child to a safe spot before I could get back. These blasted plains offer little cover. I will be back momentarily."

Samwize sped off in the direction of the Giant, presumably to recover the child. I tried my best not to pass out. Hybrow and Fyodor bound my wounds, but I knew my chances were slim off making through the night.

"Jhared, do you have the strength to heal yourself?" Hybrow whispered. I knew I did not and the world turned black.

Day Eighty-Six - Northern Plains of Karana

As my vision took shape, I saw four bodies around the campfire. Fyodor, Samwize, Hybrow and the young girl. I tried to move but my chest burned with pain, so I lay back on the ground. Fyodor came to my side and smiled.  "I am quite glad you survived, Cleric. I would have missed your whining." I tried to smile but managed more of a smirk.

I was introduced to the child next. Her name was Chaeli and Samwize made sure I knew not to mention her family before we spoke. They had food for the Giant and her home was destroyed. Chaeli was bright for her age, which we guessed was about 13. She was quiet but I attributed that more to shock than her nature. She prepared a bowl of soup for me and fed me while I regained my strength.

Samwize played a song on his lute while we camped there which seemed to somehow aid my body's ability to heal itself. Unlike my healing magicks, this song worked slowly, aiding the body with a constant encouragement to mend. Once I was strong enough to work my magic, I healed myself. My chest and shoulder would be sore and weak for days but I felt much better.

As we set out for Highhold Pass, I asked Fyodor what we planned to do with Chaeli. He explained that she had no family and that we would have to take her along, at least until we could find a safe house for her to stay at while we journeyed to the Keep.

We found an Inn in the Eastern Plains, where Hybrow knew the Innkeeper. He promised to watch over her until we returned. Though I had only been in her company for a matter of hours, I felt responsible for Chaeli. I made a vow to Karana to find her a good home. At her age, she would be welcomed by the Temple at the very least.

We said our farewells and moved on across the plains towards the mountains, we were close but not there yet.
