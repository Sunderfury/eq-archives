A Date with the Darkpaws

Day Eighty-Three - Qeynos

The bark seemed to echo throughout the entire zone.  Though my understanding of the gnoll language was very limited I was able to make a few words and phrases.  It would appear that one of the leaders of clan Darkpaw had entered the area around Qeynos and was intent on slaughtering new, raw recruits.  If that were indeed his intention, perhaps he would be unprepared to meet someone such as myself, at least, that is what I kept telling myself.

The tracks were overly easy to follow.  To an unseasoned ranger it would seem as though one gnoll was making the tracks but there signs that led me to believe that there were in fact two walking in one another’s tracks.   Light was almost completely gone now but the signs couldn’t have been clearer if it were in the middle of the day.  Another loud bark, followed closing by what seemed to be dwarven screams, and I knew I was close.  I could not swear to it, but I believe the barks were boasting that his name was Fippy Darkpaw and he was here to extract his revenge.  I quickened my pace.

About a 100 paces ahead of me I saw the gnoll, fresh from his kill.  He was facing off to the right of me, which left me a clear shot with my bow.  I drew the string and let my initial arrow fly.  It struck true into the gnoll’s arm and with uncanny quickness and agility he was on me before I could draw another arrow. 

The smell on his fur was sickening.  In his right hand he swung a long sword, which looked very tarnished.  Though the speed at which he closed the distance between was impressive he skills with a sword were very lax.  I was easily able to dodge his blows while landing a majority of my own.  The moon was not full enough for my shiny brass shield to be used as a tool but it wasn’t needed.  As quickly as it began the fight ended.  The gnoll was dead at my feet and I hardly broken a sweat. 

They say that with experience comes knowledge.  They also told me in my training that you must learn from your mistakes or you are doomed to repeat them.  Karana, when will I learn from mine?  In my excitement over dropping this gnoll I completely forgot about the tracks and my discovery that there were in fact two Gnolls.  If I had remembered then a longsword wouldn’t have cut into my lower back.

As I fell to the ground I heard a low growl and the broken words, “I am Nixx Darkpaw, and I will feast on your flesh”.

His sword came slashing down again but this time I was able to roll away and avoid the razor sharp blade.  I sprung to my feet and quickly cast Skin Like Wood.  His next blow connected with my shield but the force of it was still great enough to knock me off balance.  I tried to dodge his next blow but it connected squarely with my upper thigh.  My instincts told me to attack him low so I obeyed.  My long sword  found his flesh and opened a wound from his knee to his paw.  I dodged his next blow and long sword found his groin and sliced into him about five inches. 

As blood gushed from his wounds he fell to his knees yelping.  I raised my blade for a final time and ended his life.  I checked his corpse and found nothing of value.  The corpses on the ground reminded me of the times I spent in Blackburrow.  I had hoped that the raids would have come to an end by now considering the damage that had been done to the their home but it seems I was foolish to believe such a thing.  When last I was there Fyodor had tried to convince me that we should attack the gnoll commander in his very chamber.  At that time I was worried of the guardians and elite Gnolls that he kept by his side but the time for such concerns was maybe past.  The need in Highhold was indeed urgent and I still have not journeyed to Greater Faydark to meet with the Champions Of Faydark but this is important as well.  Perhaps I will send word to Fyodor once I have completed these tasks and ask for his assistance.  It has been too long since I have shared ale with my mentor and I feel he will be more than eager to assist me.

The walk back to the Qeynos gate was short.  I know where my destiny lies but the path has many forks.  It is urgent that I find Jhared and speak of these developments.  Aye, we have much to discuss indeed.
