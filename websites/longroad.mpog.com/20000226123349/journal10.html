A Fallen Hero

Day Seventy-Two - Qeynos

There was little mistaking what Surfal had found. The medallion was indeed an Order of Thunder amulet and the bloodstained letter was sealed with the sigil of the Temple of Thunder. I tried to rationalize what Surfal had said when he gave them to me. He described a grisly undead warrior who attacked him and spoke in the tongue of a madman. He said that although he barely survived, the ghastly monster had thanked him when Surfal ended its miserable existence.

My mind struggled with the details.

If the monster were truly an undead man, warped by foul magicks, who had he been? In my lifetime there was only one man who had ever been given the Order of Thunder amulet and that was Sir Drosco. I knew most of the Temple Knights who had trained me carried them, having received them long before I came into this world, but I had seen them all only days before and they all were fine.

As my heart tried its best to keep the truth from my mind, I thought back to my younger days in the temple. Amongst the active Knights of Thunder, there were no others lke Drosco. His honor and faith were unmatched and Karana held him close. He once returned from an expedition to Southern Ro with the headdress of a mighty Sand Giant. It was a gift for Sir Wolten, whose family had been slain in the deserts by such a creature. Drosco earned his amulet for a mission he undertook in Innothule Swamp. He ventured into the very heart of the Troll Lands to rescue the daughter of a Lake Rathe fisherman. He was the envy of every Knight of Thunder in the temple and the inspiration for everyone whom he met.

I knew that I needed to take Surfal's findings to the Temple of Thunder, so I asked my friends to wait for me at the Fish's Ale. They agreed and I set out for the temple.

Sir Wolten beamed a smile my way as he saw me approaching from across the courtyard. When I handed him the letter and the amulet, I saw him shake a little. I had never seen my old master tremble and the sight of it caused my skin to goosepimple. I knew from his reaction, even before he said it, that the wretched zombie in the aqueducts must have been Drosco. My eyes watered and my chest seemed empty.

"Your friend found this where, Jhared?" Wolten asked through blank eyes.

"In the catacombs below the city, master."

Wolten crumpled the letter in his hand and I watched his grief boil into fury. He rubbed the amulet in between his fingers and his face grew red. "Bertoluxxus will pay for this! His foul Bloodsabres will know the wrath of the Rainkeeper and they will rue this day!" Wolten threw the amulet to the ground and stormed away towards the armory. I was not sure what to do and my mind raced with confusion.

The Bloodsabres? But they were only a children's story. I spent many a sleepless night in my bed dreading the dark worshippers of Bertoluxxus who storied said were animating corpses in the sewers under the city. But all that was fantasy. Any man over the age of eighteen had long since given up believing in such ridiculous stories. Commander Bayle would never allow an army of Necromancers to live free under the city. It was impossible.

I looked at the crumpled letter on the ground. I picked it and the discarded amulet up and went to my quarters to rest. As I read the letter, I felt as though I were a boy of eight again, long dead fears rising up from their hiding places to grip at my heart. Wolten had sent Drosco to infiltrate the Bloodsabres. They were real and they had discovered Drosco's true nature. The horrors the letter described were sure to haunt my dreams. The letter had been written after his reanimation and the haunting "thank you" Drosco had uttered to Surfal as he fell suddenly made sense. Trapped in the decaying body, his mind twisted and warped by magic and pain, he still sought to be released.

I thought of my poor master and the anguish he must be feeling. He had sent Drosco into the hands of these evil ones and they had tortured and killed him, then remade him into unlife. I prayed that Karana would watch over my master and that his grief would not destroy him. That was when it hit me. He had run for the armory! He meant to go down there and avenge Drosco. I jumped from my bed and ran to the door. I sprinted to the main hall and I stopped in my tracks. The guildmaster himself was standing over Sir Wolten, who was lying down on a pew. His holiness looked up and saw me and he smiled.

"Do not worry, Jhared. He will be alright. Until he can come to terms with Drosco's death, I have asked Karana to help him rest. May I have the letter and the amulet?"

I handed them over without hesitation. "Your holiness, what will we do? Bloodsabres truly exist!"

"Jhared, you were not meant to know of this until you were better prepared. We have always known the Bloodsabres were real. Recent events forced us to try and infiltrate their ranks and Drosco had volunteered. Now that they know we are on to them, things may move more quickly. There may be very hard times ahead for us, my son. Hard times indeed."

I looked over the two of them. "How may I help, your holiness?"

"You must try and forget all of this for now, Jhared. You are not ready to face the Bloodsabres and should word of this leak outside the Temple, panic would ensue. That would work in the favor of our adversaries, do you understand?"

I did. I promised to keep the news secret and I was instructed to go on about my business. He knew I had agreed to aid the Erudite woman whose apprentice was lost at sea. He ordered me to fulfill my obligations to her and take the boat to Erud's Crossing as soon as possible.

I went to pack for the trip.

When I walked into the Fish's Ale, I tried to erase the dread from my face.
