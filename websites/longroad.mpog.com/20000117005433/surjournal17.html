A Dark Elf Encounter

Day Eighty-One - Qeynos

"You will have a surprise waiting in Highhold fair skinned one" and then he died.

I had heard rumors and half-truths about such Dark Elves living in the forest and in swamps but I never had imagined that I would come face to face with one. I wish events had not unfolded the way they had but there is little I can do about that now it seems. If only he hadn’t pushed me so far.

My initial searches for Jhared proved fruitless. I could only assume that his business with the lady had taken him longer than he had thought or perhaps he was engaged in some guild business. Those matters were his own and, even though I could easily track him, I felt it best to allow him his privacy. Besides, my news of the recent events at Highhold could keep for a little while, I was certain. Besides, I had some other business to attend too myself and now would be the perfect time.

As I walked through the merchant square a group of people ran past me almost knocking me over. I could scarcely make out their words as they ran past but it seemed there was a fight taking place somewhere close between a young cleric and local ruffian named Trumpy Irontoe. I imagine Jhared would like to see that confrontation. However, I was more interested in the merchants near the front gate to Qeynos so I decided to pass on the local excitement.

Near the front gate I found the merchant that I had been seeking. It seems he had been having some trouble with rats and was in need of assistance. I was very eager to help him since I wanted to be known and well liked by these merchants but my search for these mangy rats proved fruitless. I was about to doubt my tracking skills when I caught a whiff of a most disturbing odor. It was very faint, but very distinct. I can’t truly say that it was a bad odor, just ominous if that is possible. The scent led me out the front gate of the city and to the right. Daylight was failing but my vision is as sharp at night as it is during the day. About three hundred yards ahead of me I saw an elf sized being shrouded in dark robes and dark painted skin. I began to sneak towards his direction.

"What do you want fairy" he yelled to me as I was approaching his flank. "Think you can get the drop on Jracol Brestiage that easily?"

"No sir" I replied, alarmed that he had so easily detected me. "I was merely investigating…."

"Shut up" he barked. "You light skinned worshippers of Tunare make me sick and need to be cleansed from Norrath. Innoruk has deemed the dark Elves to be the only race fit to inhabit these lands".

My heart stopped beating entirely I am sure. To even speak the name of the God of Hate was to invite his wrath. Closer inspection proved that my early observations were wrong, this man was not painted dark, his skin actually was dark.

"Are you going to just stand there and wet your pants? Or will you run to mommy and tell her the big bad dark elf scared me" he mused.

"I will not take your insults much longer, stranger, you had best leave these parts before I…."

"Before you what, whelp? Run to the guards to save your life much like these sheep I see out here. I had hoped that at least some of the dark elf backbone had crept into you, my fledgling cousin, but I see I was mistaken. Go cry to your weak god before I make you my servant!"

I drew my blade and advanced on his position. Perhaps he was a little surprised for I drew first blood using a move Fyodor had shown me which I had honed in Erud’s Crossing. I blinded him with the Shiny Brass Shield Fyodor had given me and slashed with my sword. He parried my second blow and pierced my left leg with his blade. Never had I fought an opponent that relied on a piercing weapon before but my initial surprise would not last. I missed with my next blow but kicked him in the knee a second later. I dodged his weak attempt to pierce my ring mail tunic and sliced his stomach open with my next assault.

He fell slowly to ground trying to keep his internal organs in place but was failing miserably. I was surprised to see that his body looked much the same as mine did when the zombie laid me open on that beach. As blood bubbled from his lips he stared at me with his black eyes and laughed.

"You have no idea who I am, do you? Well (cough, cough) I know of you and your Ka Tet and you will not see another season fair skinned freak." He whispered. "You will have a surprise waiting for you in Highhold fair skinned one."

What secrets did he take to his grave? Curse my short temper. I shouldn’t have let him push me like that. Maybe I should have just walked away. Maybe I should have reasoned with him. Maybe…
