A Date with My Father's Journal

Day Eighty-Two  - South Qeynos

My father had not always been a Hero. I suppose that should have been no surprise to me, but it was unexpectedly difficult to swallow. My father had begun his career as a militiaman  in the service of a young Carson McCabe, the Lord of Highhold Keep. He was trained to defend Highhold Pass and the Keep therein from the vicious assaults of the nearby Shraloks and Mucktails. That in itself seemed no surprise. Later on in the journal however, I learned that Carson McCabe was not a good man.

My father's first assignment as a Squad Leader was to help raise funds for the militia by kidnapping the children of wealthy merchants from the Karanas and from Rivervale. McCabe extorted high ransoms for their return. As a young man, my father was only too happy to serve his master and having been raised in that environment, never saw the evil in his deeds.

But when a merchant failed to pay the ransom Carson demanded, he had ordered the child slaughtered to set an example. Something in my father had changed that day, for he raised his sword in defense of the child and fought his way free of the Keep. In making his exit, he cut Carson deep across the arm, a wound that McCabe never forgot.

My father made it to the Northern Plains of Karana and returned the child to his family. Then he wandered the plains for several years, eluding the mercenaries McCabe had looking for him. When things seemed to be getting too dangerous, my father headed to Qeynos. There he took a post in the Qeynos Guard and his whole life changed. McCabe would never move against the Qeynos Guard and things began to settle down.

Years passed and my father tried to reconcile with McCabe through letters. McCabe never wrote back. Only once did Father suspect McCabe tried to move against him and that was when a vile Dark Elf assassin was spotted in the Qeynos area. His name was Jracol Brestiage and though they encountered each other directly, Father always suspected Brestiage was spying for McCabe. One of his last entries in the journal was that he planned to confront Brestiage soon, something I now planned on doing myself.

I recounted the journal's content to Master Renic and he listened intently. "Jhared, the Knights of Thunder have always suspected that McCabe was dirty, but we have never been able to prove anything. I can tell you this, though. You had best keep clear of McCabe and Highhold Keep. Were he to find out you were your father's son, he would surely seek to harm you. Men like McCabe never lose their drive for revenge."

I agreed and went back to my room to sleep . I had many things to consider.
