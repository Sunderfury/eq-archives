Secrets from the Past

Day Eighty - South Qeynos

I was never more pleased to see the docks of Qeynos. When the ship landed in the harbor, I almost bent down to kiss the rotten planks beneath my feet. Surfal, Brent and Valarie all went their own ways and I pledged to deliver the Blurred Map to the mysterious Erudite woman.

I found her near the Mermaid's Lute just a few yards from the ship. I consoled her as she wept for her lost apprentice and gave her the map. She thanked me and said she would mention my deeds to her people. Then she walked to the docks and awaited the next ship.

I felt terrible that there was no more I could do. I decided to visit the Temple of Thunder and check in with my brothers in faith.

When I arrived, I was encouraged first to get a good night's sleep and a decent meal. I would have an audience with Renic Losaren, the guildmaster, in the morning. That filled me with excitement. Never before had I been summoned by Master Renic! 

Day Eighty-One - South Qeynos

Guildmaster Losaren had prepared a breakfast feast for he and myself to dine on while we talked. I was honored to be treated so well and I tried my best to contain my excitement.

Master Renic asked for me to recount the details of my travels over the last months and he had many interesting comments about different parts of my adventures. As I finished the tale of our visit to Erud's Crossing, his eyes filled with pride. "You have done well to defeat this Necromancer, Jhared. He was known to me. He has been  eluding my best trackers for years. I had heard reports that perhaps you were the one who slew him on Erud's Crossing, which is why I called you here today."

I made sure to stress the importance of my friends' roles in his defeat and he acknowledged them saying, "Your friends are turning into quite a formidable force, Jhared. You should cultivate your relationship with them. Friends you can trust are more valuable than platinum."

We talked for some time, well past the lunch and dinner hours, and finally he took me by surprise. "I have some news about your father, Jhared."

"A journal was delivered to me, which was found in his barracks here in Qeynos. It had been hidden under the boards near his bunk and was not discovered until a carpenter was hired to replace the old flooring of the Barracks. I have it here for you, but I wanted to speak to you before you read it."

Lumps formed in my throat. My father kept a secret journal? I never knew my father to write a word in his life.

"Jhared, I am concerned that no matter what is contained in these pages, you will find yourself questioning your understanding of your father. I know how you admired and respected your father and I want you to be prepared to judge what you read with a forgiving heart. Few sons are given the opportunity to see inside their father's heart and mind, and sometimes that vision can be hard to cope with. Just remember that despite your father's heroics and great deeds, he was merely human."

I nodded and reached out for the dusty book he held in his hand. It was tied with string and stuffed with notes and  hand drawn maps. My heart was racing as I pulled at the string.

A hand covered mine, stopping my progress. "You should read this in private, Jhared. Don't you think?"

I smiled at this man whom I suddenly felt so close to. "Thank you, Master Renic. I will retire to my chambers."

As I walked to my room, I tried to imagine what secrets I would find within the book's dusty pages.
