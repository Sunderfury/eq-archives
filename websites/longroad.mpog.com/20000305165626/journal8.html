The Frozen Peaks of Everfrost

Day Fifty-Four - Qeynos Hills/Blackburrow

We were supposed to go to Qeynos. I had business with the Temple of Thunder, Fyodor was supposed to meet a man about buying some equipment and Root had an appointment with Captain Tillin to pass on some news from Sheriff Roglio of Rivervale.

But when we came up over the Qeynos Hills for the first time in almost a month we looked over the city and without a word between us, we turned and headed for Blackburrow. I think we are still unprepared to face the civility of the city after our days at war.

As we approached the familiar horrors of the carved Gnoll head in the side of the great hill, we heard a familiar sound. "Ye don't have tha mettle for me, ya snaggletoothed furballs!" we all heard as clear as day. We looked down into the entrance to Blackburrow and beamed a smile. Grannith was surrounded with Gnolls, at least six. With only one other in the fight, Grannith was still taunting his opponents.

We charged the Gnolls and with even odds, we made quick work of them. Grannith didn't even smile when he saw us. "It's about time ya dolts arrived! I been waitin two days for ya to show up." The quiet man who had been fighting with Gran sat down and lowered his head, as if meditating. He wore no armor of any kind and fought without weapons, so I quickly assumed he was one of the Silent Fist.

"A Silent Fist and loud mouthed Dwarf!" I challenged. "Now that is funny!"

The monk sat up straight and smiled. "Greetings! I take it from your appearance and familiarity with this dwarf that you must be Jhared, Fyodor, Root and Surfal. This little juggernaut speaks more of you than he does of himself, which is saying quite a lot." Gran grumbled at the mention of his kind words. "I am honored to meet you all. My name is Brent. I am indeed a member of the Silent Fist clan and I am at your service."

Root smirked, "Great! Another tree trunk to block my view."

We all laughed, even Grannith and shared a skin of water and some bread Fyodor had bought in West Karana. Grannith explained that he and the young monk were headed to the frozen wastes of Everfrost Peaks to search for an elusive brewing element the young monk required to fulfill a personal quest. Root offered to join them as he also had always wanted to see the White World as many called it. Surfal, Fyodor and I were always up for adventure and Qeynos seemed a dreadful alternative so we joined in as well.

After the meal, we headed back into the familiar darkness of Blackburrow, but somehow it didn't seem quite as horrible anymore.

Day Fifty-Six - Everfrost Peaks

As you cross through Blackburrow's twisting tunnels, there is a long, wide passageway which is worn by cold winds. We followed that tunnel until we stepped out into a vast cave. The nighttime air was freezing cold and the winds were fierce. We decided to camp inside the tunnel and embark across the tundra in daytime.

Day Fifty-Seven - Everfrost Peaks

The frozen canyons in the mountains are littered with Polar Bears, White Wolves and Ice Goblins. We did some hunting and moved North to through the canyons towards Halas, the city of the Barbarians. It was here that Brent said he could obtain an elusive and rare brewing component. The tundra was beautiful. The moon light up the white snow on the ground and played wild tricks with the eyes. Were it not for the biting winds, I would call it a paradise.

We reached Halas and did some trading with the Barbarians. Fyodor and I bought some heavy cloaks, Grannith inquired about an axe and Root and Brent picked up some herbs and plants. We decided to camp in the tundra that night as the city was so crowded.

Grannith and Root were obviously uncomfortable around the giant men and women of the White World. It was an amusing sight to see, two little men worming through the feet and legs of these powerful people.

We camped in the hills and decided that we would try and do some more hunting in the morning.

Day Fifty-Eight - Everfrost Peaks

Surfal picked up some strange tracks just east of our camp and we all decided to follow them. As we broke free of the mountain peaks we were awestruck when we looked out across the frozen plains. It was like looking at a flat sheet of white glass, stretching as far as the eye could see in every direction. It was marvelous to behold.

Just then we spotted what was surely the beast Surfal was tracking. It was huge and covered in a thick fur. It had giant teeth stretching from its mouth and an arm swiveling out from its face. It was at once frightening and majestic. I had never seen anything like it and I wandered what drunken fit had prompted Karana to create such a strange beast. As we looked it over, it opened its mouth and roared. The sound echoed across the plains.

"Now that is what I call a trophy!" Grannith said, slapping our shields with his axe.

Surfal looked it over, "You know...I bet we could take that beast if we worked as a team."

I gulped and looked to Fyodor. More than any of us, he would be the best judge of what we could or could not handle. I am not sure if I was excited or terrified as I waited for his decision.

"Move us up on its flank, Surfal." Fyodor adjusted his armor and tightened his shield straps. "Grannith and I will get its attention from the front. You four focus your efforts from the sides and behind."

We all smiled and set out to stalk the mightiest beast we had ever seen.

It took almost half an hour to sneak up close enough to it to attack without drawing attention to ourselves. Once everyone was in position, we waited for Fyodor's signal. Once the shield waved back and forth giving the sign , we all charged from our hiding spot and tore into the beast. Much as I expected, it swung its mighty teeth like a sword, slashing at Fyodor and Grannith. As we continued the fight, the beast had even more surprises. It used the strange arm-like appendage on its face to pick Grannith up off the ground and throw him twenty feet into the snow. He hit hard and for a moment I feared we would not make it.

But Gran stood back up and charged like a bull. Adrenaline surged through our veins and we fought on. In the end, it was Brent who delivered the final blow, a precise kick to the huge creature's throat. When it fell to the ground, it shook the earth. We camped and began to consider the amount of meat and hides we could retrieve from the beast.

"I want the big teeth," Fyodor asked. "Those are a phenomenal trophy and if no one has any objections I would like to take them back to Qeynos with me."

We all agreed and helped Fyodor cut the giant bones from the beast.

It was a remarkable hunt and one I shall not forget. When we reached Halas, to sell off the excess meat and hides we were told by a trader that the beast we slew was called a Mammoth. We all laughed and thanked him for the information. As we were walking away he caught sight of the teeth Fyodor has strapped to his back. He started laughing and blurted out, "Oh dear. I didn't realize it was only a calf! Well, you outlanders should be careful if you carry those tusks in the frozen plains. This little calf's mother is going to be looking for it and she won't be happy to see those tusks on your back."

"You mean that thing was a baby!" I shouted.

"Oh yes, a full grown Mammoth is significantly larger and ten times as deadly."

We all thought back to the fierce battle and the tremendous accomplishment we thought we had attained. Fyodor unstrapped the tusks and threw them into the snow.  We all turned and headed for Blackburrow.
