The Shade of a Kind Tree

Day Eighty-Four - Western Plains of Karana

I have never truly appreciated the comforts of companionship until this night.  Although the trip from Qeynos to Highhold was but a short journey considering my final destination lie across the Ocean of Tears I was never the less very apprehensive about taking it alone.  Many dangers await the unprepared traveler on this journey and I had already faced several beasts that wished me harmed.  However, my training, experience and tracking skills had served their purpose and kept me safe for the past two sunrises.  With Karana’s help I should be in Highhold by nightfall.

In the past I had opted to stay near the water’s bed and optimize my sneaking abilities but confidence and the pressure of time had forced my hand in taking another, more direct path.  I would keep to the mountainside and, avoiding the ogre hordes, slip past and into northern karana undetected.  What was it that Hager used to say about the best-laid plans of giant rats and men?

As I passed the area controlled by two huge ogre brothers I happened across a very interesting set of tracks.  To the less trained ranger it might appear as though a hill giant was dragging a huge tree behind him but the tracks had a pattern to them.  It seemed to indented into the ground about 3 to 4 inches and every four feet the indention would be deeper on one side, then the other.  Whatever made these tracks was very heavy and moving slowly.  If I were to run I could catch up to whatever was making the patterns in less than two hours.  The November skies looked to promise rain so I picked up my pace.

Topping the hill I was met with disappointment.  After forty-five minutes of pursuing the tracks they led only to a huge tree.  From a distance it seemed that someone must have drug the tree upright and left it in that very spot.  Having come such a long way and at such a fast pace I was in need of rest so I decided to walk to the tree and rest there.  My tracking ability had already informed me that there were no dangers around.  Once under the branches I opened my rations and sat down, hoping to enjoy my meal and ponder the mystery of how the tree had moved. And that, my oversized friend, is how I came to be in your company.

“Very interesting tale, young Surfal”, the treant mused, “However I find it difficult to imagine that in all your training you never once heard of me or my kind”.

“Tis true, though.  Until the very moment that you bent down and shook me from my sleep I had no knowledge of you” I answered.  “I have had many contacts with druids and shared many tales but to this day the knowledge of treants has escaped me”.

 “I choose to believe you.  Lesser men have run away from the mere sight of me yet you did not, why?”  the treant asked.

“Not because I did not wish to, I’m afraid, but rather because you startled me to the point that my feet would not respond.  Had you not spoken words of kindness upon waking me I surely would have passed away from fright.”  I responded.

“Many seek me for my knowledge on matters or perhaps to challenge me in a fight to the death, I cannot recall ever just speaking to an individual for no purpose.  You are unique, Surfal”.

“I could easily sit with you all day but I have matters to attend to elsewhere” I responded.

“True, you have matters to attend to in Highhold.  The Shralok orcs are a menace to both humanoid and animal life alike.  Take this gift, it will help you in your travels” he whispered.

As I extended my hand towards his branches he gave me a bow.  Closer inspection proved it to be a bow of superior quality.  It felt perfectly right in my hands, as though it was made for me and me alone.

“This bow was a gift from a young ranger many, many years ago named Maesyn Trueshot.  He now resides in the treetop city of Kelethin.  Tell him I sent you and that he is to train you.  Show him the bow as proof.  The bow is usable only by those who follow the ranger’s path so never stray.  I hope to see you again Surfal, as a champion of Faydark.”
