Carson Finally Makes His Move

Day One Hundred and Three - Highhold Keep

I was awakened in the night by the sound of someone entering my room. My mind raced as I grabbed my hammer. Perhaps now that the siege was over, Carson had sent someone to punish me for my father's betrayal. I prepared to strike as they entered the room and my heart ran like a rabbit.

As the door swung open I held my hammer high, waiting to strike.

Instead, the door only cracked and a long tube crept into my room at eye level. At first I thought it to be some kind of telescope but it seemed too small for that. I followed its aim and saw that it pointed to my bed. With my hurried exit, I had left the bed linens in such a way that they appeared to be occupied.

I decided to wait and see what this was about, but my mind already knew this was an assassin. Suddenly a small projectile flew from the end of the tube and into the bunched up linens that seemed to still cover me. Had I been lying there I am quite sure that I would be twisting in poisoned agony. When the tube began to retract, I swung the door open fast. Before I even made eye contact with the figure in the doorway my hammer had struck its mark sending the assassin into the stone wall across the way. I summoned my magics and soothed the figure to a state of almost sleeplike relaxation. Then I moved in close.

I was astonished to find that this assassin was in fact, a woman. I tried to cloud that fact in my mind and I hit her swiftly in the back of the skull with the handle of my hammer. Unconscious, I dragged her to Surfal's room.

Under the lamp light, Surfal and I looked her over while Surfal did his best to clear his head. He had probably only slept a few hours in the last two weeks and he looked it. "She is Teir Dal, Jhared." I pulled away the mask she wore and although I was not familiar with the phrase, I knew immediately what it meant when I saw her skin. Dark Elves! This only further proved that Carson was involved in something far worse than gambling. The Dark Elves were known for their wretched, murderous ways. No man who could call on the Dark Elves for his dirty work coudl possibly be anything less than a murdering thief himself. He presence alone would prove my father's accusations in a Qeynos Tribunal.

Surfal found three more "darts" in a pouch on her hip and he quickly verified that they were poisoned. I pointed out that one of them had probably been meant for him. He cursed Jracol under his breath and we went about tying up our captive.

"What will we do with her, Jhared? She is dangerous and I do not trust these ropes to hold her for long once she awakens, even without her tools." Surfal slipped the bag of fine metal instruments into his backpack for safe keeping.

"I do not know, Surfal. To flush out Carson, maybe we should turn her over to Ashlan and hope he is not involved in her presence here." Surfal frowned. "Well I am not used to assassins showing up in my bedroom, Surfal. What do you suggest?"

"Let's get the others."

Surfal stayed to watch her while I fetched our friends. As we entered Surfal's room, there was a collective gasp as everyone realized what she was. "A Tier Dal assassin attacked you and you lived, Jhared. You should thank your god for that!" Grannith grumbled as he looked her over.

Everyone began musing over our next step while Hybrow simply stared at her. I should have known something was wrong then, but I didn't act quickly enough. Fyodor must have sensed Hybrow's actions as I did because we both reached for his hands at the same time, which ended up to be too late to stop him.

He drove the small dagger deep into her heart with precision and as her body twitched and flailed in response he whispered something in Elvish into her ears. Surfal, Arsindrick and Samwize all nodded their heads in support. I pulled Hybrow off of her and pushed him against the stone wall. "What in the name of God are you doing, Hy?"

His eyes chilled and they seemed to stare into the back of my brain. "Tier Dal assassins killed every member of my family's House. In eight of your human years I watched as my cousins, siblings and even my parents turned up murdered. To be Tier Dal, is to be evil. There are no exceptions. This one will hurt no more and I will not waste one more moment of my life explaining myself to you for this piece of filth. Take your hands off of me now, Human!"

Fyodor pulled me back and Hybrow left Surfal's room. I looked in astonishment as Sam, Arsin and Surfal spit on the corpse of the lifeless assassin. I know she had meant me dead, but I had never meant to kill her. Not a woman, not even a Dark Elf woman. My sheltered Human upbringing was being challenged and I simply didn't know what to say next.

"It is better this way, I think," Fyodor said as he released my arms. "Prisoners are only enemies you will have to kill another day. You should thank Hybrow for his decision. It saved you the moral dilemma you are facing now. It was the right choice and I do not think you could have made it, Jhared."

I must have looked like a lost puppy to them. I stumbled to my room and fell on my bed. I looked at the dart, carefully avoiding its poison tip. They were right, but my mind just couldn't wrap around it all.

As I fell to sleep, I prayed I did not see her face in my dreams.
