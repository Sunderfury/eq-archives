The Search for the Dwarven Demolition Man

Day Twenty-One - Qeynos

The letter found me in Qeynos. After Surfal's eye opening experience with Cros Treewind, we decided to all split up for a few days of rest and relaxation. I had decided to go home to the Temple of Thunder and check in with Master Wolten. When the letter arrived I was studying up on the flora and fauna of the Western Plains of Karana, which became something of a fortuitous coincidence.

The letter was from a young Dwarf named Grannith. His father and mine had shared many battles together and I knew of him from stories my father had told to me as a child. Grannith was leaving Kaladim, according to the letter, and was headed to Rivervale. Seeing it as a wonderful halfway point to finally meeting one another, Grannith hoped I could head out as soon as possible.

I had heard wondrous tales of Rivervale from Root and I knew he had pressing business in his homeland, so I was excited to have a good excuse to finally go. I consulted with Master Wolten and he concurred that Rivervale was in dire need of a Knight of Thunder. I packed my bags.

Day Twenty-Two - Qeynos Hills

I was able to track down Fyodor and Surfal at the Fish's Ale. Though they had enjoyed the fine wares of the establishment, they were actually on a fact finding mission regarding rumors of shipments from Blackburrow making it into the shops of several Qeynos merchants.

The trail had led them to the Fish's Ale but had gotten very cold. They were still missing a crucial piece of information which they felt would break the mystery wide open. As they couldn't find it at the bottom of a flagon, they hadn't made much progress.

I told them about the situation in Rivervale and that I had business there and both sobered quickly.

"I'm all for new surroundings Jhared," Fyodor beamed. "I have had it up to my eyeballs with Sabretooth Gnolls. If I never see another I couldn't be luckier."

Surfal's demeanor perked up and then soured all at once. "The more distance we put between us and Cros Treewind the better," he sulked. "Let's go."

We gathered Root in the Hills near Surefall Glade and headed out for the Western Plains of Karana. The trip to Rivervale is a treacherous one and the best map we were able to obtain was far from reliable. Root however, had a strong knowledge of the course so we followed his every step as closely as possible.

Day Twenty-Three - Plains of Karana

The plains are unbelievable. I watched with awe as a mighty Treant crossed the horizon from north to south this morning. Though little is known about these mystical treefolk, the Druids have great respect and trust for them according to Root. Surfal added that the Gladesmen also revered the massive creatures who serve the will of Tunare as well.

Karana blessed us as we traveled the lands bearing his name. We reached the Northern Plains shortly before nightfall and decided to camp for the night. Traveling any further after nightfall was suicide according to Root and I believed him. The Karanas are filled with the sounds of many a wild beast and none of them sounded too friendly.

As an aspiring chef and tailor, I was very excited to see the great quantity of bears, lions and wolves that traveled the plains. I made a note to come back and do some hunting soon.

Day Twenty-Four - The Gorge of King Xorrb

Daylight struck and we headed out for the wooden bridge that separated the Northern and Eastern Plains of Karana. We crossed the bridge and paused before heading east towards the Gorge of King Xorbb. 

"These Eastern Plains are really rough but they are kiddie play compared to the Gorge," Root warned. "Watch your step, you bunch of oversized targets or you'll likely have them torn off by a  Griffon." 

Fyodor laughed confidently and we set out for the Gorge. Our laughter soon turned to fear as we were beset with Griffons and Lion Patriarchs. With the Spirit of the Wolf coursing through our veins we were able to outdistance our aggressors but it was close.

As we entered the Gorge, Root tensed up. "Okay. The Minotaurs and Evil Eyes don't take kindly to our trespassing and the Muddites hate everybody including each other so run along the tops of the Gorge Wall and don't stop for anything!"

We started running, this time heeding Root's warnings with greater respect. 

We saw the Gorge Minotaurs and Muddites that he promised we would and their reaction was instantaneous. By the time I could see the entrance to Runnyeye, I was bleeding from several open wounds and limping severely from several broken bones in my side and my right leg. I dove for the Runnyeye entrance and collapsed in a heap as I hit the cave floor.

Surprisingly, our pursuers stopped outside the cave. "They'll not come in here friends," Root explained. 

I smiled through the pain as Root began to bind my wounds. I was far too weak to use my magic. "I'm glad of it, Root. But why?" I asked.

Root's face tightened up. "Oh the Minotaurs and Muddites avoid Runnyeye for the same reason as everyone else -- they are too scared to come in here."

Surfal shot up from his resting place. "They are too SCARED to come in here?" he shouted. "Then what are we doing in this godforsaken cave!"

Root smiled and said, "well...I figure we have a better chance in here than we do out there with those beasties."

We all sighed and tried to rest.

Day Twenty-Five - Runnyeye

I was awakened by Surfal's hushed voice. "Don't move, they are close," he whispered. We awoke Fyodor and Root in the same manner while Surfal explained that a small patrol of Goblins were making their way down our tunnel.

Root shifted on his heels. "Guys...I don't know what to tell ya. To be honest, we are in a bad spot. If we go back out those Minotaurs and Muddites will have us for sure. These Goblins will do pretty much the same."

"I have a plan," Fyodor said gathering us close. "Lets charge the Gobs. My grandfather told me stories of these foul little cusses and they startle easy. By the time they regain their composure we will be in the Thicket." Fyodor looked to Root. "You do know the way through to the Thicket?"

Root shifted again. "Well...I know the general direction anyway." 

As the Gobs came around the corner Fyodor, Surfal and I screamed out a battle cry and charged full force. Root managed a lackluster "Arrrghh!" and ran in behind us. The effect was successful, the mob of Gobs turned and ran for the nearest hallway and we went the other direction, following Root's lead.

We came to a fork in the tunnels and Root stopped to ponder. We all looked amongst each other timidly as Root considered the options.

"I hate to push Root, but the Gobs have turned around," Surfal said with his ear to the floor. "In fact, they are getting very close."

Root muttered something about Mini-Miney-Mo and took off down the right-hand tunnel. The Gobs were closing and Fyodor turned and ran backwards to defend our rear. "How much further little one?" Fyodor shouted, his voice trembling ever so slightly.

Before Root could make a reply, Fyodor flew past us, his feet a good two feet from the ground. I turned to look back and saw that the Gobs had grown in number and one in particular looked like an incredibly formidable opponent.

As I started to look forward again, I tripped over Fyodor and landed on the gravel floor. As the Gobs raised their weapons, six Halfling Deputies charged in from the cave exit. "Get your long legs moving Junior!" one shouted as he brought his staff to bear across the nearest Gob's skull.

Fyodor, Surfal, Root and I ran for the exit and turned to help the Deputies as they slowly backed out of the cave. When they reached the bright light of the sun and saw that they now faced 10 of us instead of 4, the Gobs decided to retreat to the safety of Runnyeye.

The abrasive little Deputy slapped Fyodor on the hip (which was arm's length for him) and laughed, "Glad to have ya, Long Legs! I am Deputy Budo of the Wall and I welcome you to Rivervale and the Misty Thicket."

After Root exchanged many happy returns with the Deputies, we all traveled to the city where Budo promised us a fine meal.

I hope to find Grannith tomorrow.
