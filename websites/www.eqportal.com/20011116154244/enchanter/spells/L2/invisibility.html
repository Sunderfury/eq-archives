Invisibility
Spell Rank: Rank 2 - learnable by enchanters at player level 4

Spell Effect:

 Causes the target to become invisible to normal sight.
Area of Effect: a single grouped and targeted being.
School of Magic: Divination

Cost in Mana:

 30

Component/s/ Needed:

 -none-

Classes:

 SHM, WIZ, MAG, ENC

Locations Found:

 Ak'Anon - in the Mechanamagica, near Juline Erncaller. (1093, -976)
Felwithe
Freeport
Qeynos
Neriak
Tips: Coupled with gate, this makes an enchanter one of the best early explorers in the game.  Enchanters get invisibility earlier than the other classes and can get around quite nicely with it.  To test this out, I spent a fair deal of time exploring HighPass when I hit level 4 and first aquired invisibility, just to see how effective it was, and I had no problems.

Invisibility is also very useful when recovering corpses.  Although fighting and looting corpses removes invisibility, using the /corpse command does not.  If you died in an area that is somewhat dangerous, you can go in invisibly and drag your corpse to a safer location with /corpse before looting it.  You can even grant invisibility to others (provided you are grouped with them) so they can recover their own corpses in this manner.

As nice as it is there are only a few minor drawbacks to invisibility.  For one thing, undead aren't fooled by it so don't bother trying to sneak past undead while invisible.  Some high level mobs can see right through invisibility too.  A good way to determine if you can be seen is to /con the mob in question before approaching it.   While invisible, all mobs that can't see you /con as indifferent to you.  If you get any other message, amiable or ready to attack for example, then the mob can quite clearly see you.

A word of caution with Invisibility and pets.  Pets need to be able to see you or they think you've zoned and they destroy themselves.  Casting invisibility on yourself while you have a pet is in the area will cause the pet to destroy itself.  If the pet happens to be one you captured via a charm spell, they don't just go away, they break the charm spell and attack you just as you go invisible making you visible again and suddenly in a fight.  Finally, its duration is somewhat variable.  Although it typically lasts a good long while for me it can sometimes last a few seconds.  The duration seems to range from about 1 seconds to about 20 minutes.  In addition to the variable duration, it doesn't always give any warning before going away it simply wears off.  Usually its icon will blink before it goes away, but once in a while it will simply just go away.
