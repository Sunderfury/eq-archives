Sentinel
Spell Rank: Rank 3 - learnable by enchanters at player level 8

Spell Effect:

 Allows you to remotely observe an area.
Area of Effect: You must target yourself, however the effect actually targets a fairly wide area centered on the area you initially cast from.
School of Magic: Divination

Cost in Mana:

 25

Component/s/ Needed:

 -none-

Classes:

 ENC

Locations Found:

 Ak'anon - in Mechanamagica, near Juline Erncaller. (1101, -983)
Felwithe
Freeport
Qeynos
Neriak
Tips: Another utility spell, this has little combat value.   Its a little peculiar in that you can not cancel it like other spells.  A spell icon does not appear in the upper corner of your screen like other spells, instead you will be surrounded by an orb of sparkling lights that leave trails when you run sufficiently impressive to make a bard pale with envy.  Unfortunately, you will be the only one to be able to see this effect.  You will know the spell has ended when the sparkles go away.  Anyone entering within the area of your sentinel's affect will be informed that they feel watched.

Once cast the area on the spell is sufficiently wide enough and sufficiently long enough to provide a measure of safety while meditating.   It does not give you visual feedback, rather, it sends any movement to your text message box.  If anything, be it player or npc, enters the range or even moves from a stationary position within it you will receive a message giving you its name and that it has moved within your sentinel's perimeter.  Sentinel is also good for revealing concealed or invisible players.  While you won't see them, you will see an orb of sparkling light wherever something triggers your sentinel (if you are within visible limit of the occurance) accomanied by the text message of who, or what, just triggered your sentinel.

There is a limit on how far away you can move before you will no longer receive input from the sentinel, however it remains in affect and you can fall back into the range and resume receiving input.  You'll have to play with the spell to determine the range to a point of your understanding. 

This spell can also be used to monitor a nearby repop.  If you're waiting for a specific npc to return, either to hunt it or speak with it when it returns from traveling or being killed, you can cast sentinel near its repop point and go off to do something else.  You will be alerted when it returns.  Always a good way to keep track of when the boat arrives at the dock.  A word of caution though, on busy docks you're likely to annoy everyone there with the "you feel that you are being watched" message they will continue to receive the entire time the spell is in effect.

Sentinel is particularly good in dungeons or places with long halls.  You can cast it at one location and move further down the hall.  If anything comes running in your general direction you will receive that much more warning before it reaches you.  Not much beats a few seconds warning when sitting at the zone entrance of Crushbone and you see Amassador D'vinn, Emporer Crush and several of his attendants come barreling down the hall chasing someone.  If there's just you and they die or zone in time that leaves you the only target and that's not a place you want to be standing when that happens.

Its also useful to note that you can cast multiple sentinel spells.  You will have no way of knowing which is being triggered, other than actually looking, but it does allow you to set up an effective perimeter watch. 

Oddities:  If you cast minor illusion, keeping you stationary, then cast sentinel, then remove the minor illusion affect, the sparkling affect will remain centered on where you cast it and you will be able to move around without the flashing lights interfering with your field of vision.

Another oddity, if you cast sentinel, then cast any other illusion spell, such as illusion: human, the visual affect disapears.   Even after you remove the illusion the sparkling affect does not return.  The only way to tell it has disipated is that you will simply no longer receive information on the targeted area.
