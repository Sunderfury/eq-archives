Enchanter Research Components

Spell research is necessary to find some spells in the game and is available to each of the spell casters in different base forms.  Enchanters use Grimoires, Tomes and Writs to research some of their spells.  These components appear either as an unfurled blank parchment or as the left or right half or a parchment page ripped in half.

The various research components seem to be found on certain mobs in certain zones.  If a mob loads a research item, hunt that mob consistently and its peers should turn up the same and other related research items.

  • At level 16 an enchanter may place 1 (and only 1) practice into Research skill.   The rest of the points must be earned the hard way, practice.
  • Enchanter's must combine matching pages of various books/tomes to create new spells.   Where a left & right half of a page exist they are combined to make a new spell.  Adjoining whole pages are combined to create new spells.
  • At higher levels (around 30) we use faded pages from Velishoul's Tome.  Place a single faded page into the Tome of Endless Enchantments and hit combine to unfade the page.  Combine the, now unfaded, apropriate pages (see the list below) to create a new spell.
  • While various languages are needed to read the "recipes" found in various books, it appears to have little or nothing to do with the actual creation.
  • Quill and scroll are NOT needed to create the spells, including them will result in failure.
  • To actually create the researched spell you must purchase the Tome of Endless Enchanments (found on guild vendors), place the pages being used in the research process within, and combine them (much like a trade skill).
  • You can not research spells until you reach the minimum Level to Create them.
  • Some spells may be available to purchase them elsewhere in the game.

As a side note, other spell-casters use different research components:

Necromancers use "words" which appear as unfurled blank pages of parchment, much like some of the enchanter components.  Do not let this fool you as you can easily tell what it is by clicking on it and it will tell you that it is a "word of something".

Wizards use runes, which all look the same and are easily identifiable.

Magicians use a wide variety of odd components, including water of something, breath of something, etc.
