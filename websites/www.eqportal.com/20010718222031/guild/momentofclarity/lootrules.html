Loot Rules

Being late:  If you are late to a raid, you must wait 2 hours, or 1 random for your class, before you can enter randoms, whichever comes first.  (This is primarily for planes - other raids are at the officers discretion).  Posting that you will be late (or acts of God) do NOT exempt you from late penalties.  Late is defined as arriving after the first pull of the raid.  If you are in transit to the raid, the raid officers can take that into consideration.  You will be told if you are subject to the two hour limit, and if you are unsure, ask a raid leader.

If:
1) No one else can use the item, or
2) All others who can use the item have it, or
3) You were brought in late for the specific purpose that the raid could not continue without you,
    then you can random for the item.
 

Loot Priority:

Primary character - The main character you play.  You need to pick one as your Primary.
Secondary characters - Another character (even high level) that is not your Primary character.

    If a Secondary character is asked to go on the raid for the use of their skills, then for the raid, they are considered a Primary character. You have to be on the raid to be eligible for loot from the raid.   (There are rare circumstances like class specific loot being dropped and nobody of that class to loot the item).  In that case the item will go up for general random.

Single class specific loot:

    Goes to Class Primary characters of the class that need it first. If there is no Class Primary on the raid to loot the item (or no Class Primary on the raid wants the item), then the item will go up for general random.

Non Class Specific loot:

    Always goes to Primary characters that can use it most.  (The officers/raid leaders will decide if need be).  You will not be allowed to random for an item that you would not use daily. If no Primary will use it daily, then the item can go up for general random to all Primary characters that can use it. If no Primary wants it that can use it, it will go up for general random.

Spell Drops:

    Any spells that drop will go to the classes (present when the spell drops) who need the spell. If no one present needs the spell, then the spell will be offered (over guild channel) to see if anyone in the guild needs the spell. If multiple people in the guild need the spell, it will be randomed among them. If no one in the guild needs the spell, it will go up for general random.

Loot rules in a pick-up group:

    If you are in a pick-up group, or invited on a raid with a different guild, please abide by thier loot rules.

Need:
-An item that is an upgrade to an item you are currently using.
-An item that you would use daily.
-An item that will remain equipped until you find a better item to replace it.

General Random (a.k.a. gravy loot):
-The item you win in a general random is yours to do with as you please.
   examples:
   -sell it.
   -twink with it .
   -trade it.
   -give it to a fellow guildmate that may need it (highly recommended).
   -offer it to fellow guildmates at a reduced price.
