Bind Affinity
Spell Rank: Rank 4 - learnable by enchanters at player level 12

Spell Effect:

 The bind point is the location you return to whenever you are killed or, in the case of spell casters, when you cast the gate spell.  This spell sets the target's bind point to the place upon which they are standing at the time the spell is cast.
Area of Effect: A single targeted being.
School of Magic: Alteration

Cost in Mana:

 100

Component/s/ Needed:

 -none-

Classes:

 CLR, DRU, SHM, NEC, WIZ, MAG, ENC

Locations Found:

 Ak'anon
Felwithe
Freeport
Qeynos
Neriak
Tips: For the exploring mage, this spell provides you with a great deal of freedom.  You are now able to explore far away areas without the fear of death resulting in a three hour journey just to recover your corpse.  Simply bind yourself at a safe point, a nearby town or guard perhaps, near the area you will be exploring.

This spell also makes those money runs that much easier.  As most mages have a noticable deficit in the area of strength, any serious amount of time spent gathering goods to sell in town can rappidly overwhelm their meager capacity to haul loot the distances required.  By simply binding yourself in front of your favorite shop keeper you can now hunt and scavenge until you can barely crawl about then gate straight to the shop saving you valuable carrying time.

Two final points on functionality.  First, while mages can bind themselves just about anywhere (the ships being the only exception that comes to mind) they can only bind other people in town zones.  A few zones, such as Greater Faydark, Qeynos Hills, and such that have towns in the same zone as commonly hunted creatures are popular as you can bind others anywhere in them even though they are both town and non-town type zones.

Lastly, this is a consent spell.  Consent spells are those that require the consent of the target to work.  How does your target consent?  By being grouped with you.  Anyone grouped with you has essentially consented to any spells you wish to cast on them, such as Bind Affinity or Inivisibility.

This was done to prevent abuse from one player by another.  As an example, binding a troll between two guards could cause the troll to be slain every time he reloaded from being killed.  Eventually this will lead to the troll character being reduced back to level 5 or so where they stop losing experience from being killed.  Requiring consent fixes the problem, for the most part.  The situation can could still exist, but requires a measure of deception now rather than simply targeting a victim running willy-nilly past the guards.
