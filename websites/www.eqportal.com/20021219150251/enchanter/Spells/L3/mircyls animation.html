Mircyl's Animation
Spell Rank: Rank 3 - learnable by enchanters at player level 8

Spell Effect:

 Summons an animated sword as a pet to protect the caster.
Area of Effect: targets self, summons a "pet".
School of Magic: Conjuration

Cost in Mana:

 24

Component/s/ Needed:

 three tiny daggers

Classes:

 ENC

Locations Found:

 HighPass Hold only - in the library
Tips: The first pet we receive that has definite combat value.  It can frequently do more damage than warriors your level, frequently has a good armor, heals very fast after combat and does an effective job of taunting your attacker away from you during combat (provided you do not cast a mega-taunt spell like Chaotic Feedback) leaving you free to cast your spells in relative safety.

By judicious use of Fear and damage over time (DoT) spells, like Suffocating Sphere, you can find this pet is quite effective.  Pets can do one thing far better than players, which is run and hit things without lagging.  However, this pet may be sufficiently strong enough that you are not limited to this.  A good many enchanters also allow their pet to stand toe-to-toe with the target at this level while they judiciously use debuffs, DoT spells, and direct damage spells to finish it off.

The thing to be aware of with this pet is that its strengths are now variable enough that you may receive a variety from significantly weaker to equal to your own strength via /con.  Some enchanters use reclaim energy to destroy weaker pets and continue re-summoning new ones until they receive a sufficiently powerful one, however, given the cost of three tiny daggers and limited carrying space this may not be cost-efficient for you yet.  Do not lose heart though as your next level pet only costs a single tiny dagger to summon.

Words of caution, unlike normal pets these can not be commanded to attack or defend you.  They are not true pets in that sense, rather, they will defend you after you first take damage.  Unless you take damage in a fight it will not aid in combat, so if grouped with others and you want your pet to help you need to get hit at least once.

Perhaps a bug, perhaps intentional, but if you allow your pet to get the killing blow it takes  portion of the experience from the kill.  If you, or a group member, gets the killing blow your animated pet does not seem to take a share of the experience from the split.
