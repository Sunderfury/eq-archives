Enchanter Spell Review (LONG)
by Zamtil Litmaz - Veeshan
posted 5/20/99 10:59:29 PM


Here's my thoughts and suggestions on the Enchanter class after
reaching Level 50. Let's start with the recent controversy over
Whirl. It was too powerful prior to the patch; however, it's been
gimped beyond use now (Color Flux & Shift are much better, however
they are Area Effect). Clerics and Wizards both still have single
target stun spells with fixed durations, yet the class whose
responsible for damage control doesn't?

Recent posts suggest Whirl was gimped because of a Cleric & Enchanter
combo taking down mobs that would normally require a full group.
This doesn't make alot of sense, because a Cleric & Enchanter combo
still relies on Root & Nuke, not melee which is where Whirl is
useful. The main party I was with for levels 40 to 50 consisted of
myself, a cleric, 2 rogues, and normally 2 druids. Yes, we took down
mobs all the way up to level 50. Yes, Whirl allowed the rogues to
backstab like mad; however, it was never full proof, when Whirl was
resisted, the rogues took a beating, we just had plenty of healing
power.

Verant seems to expect multiple groups to work together, when
organizing a single group of 6 can take an hour already. I can just
imagine the groups competing to do the most damage so they get the
loot and XP. If the high level mobs are supposed to take 12 people
(or more) Verant needs to provide a way to make larger groups. As
is, on a nightly basis, myself or another friend is getting booted
due to the 6 person limit -- great social aspect.

I'll be focusing almost entirely on the Enchanter class with my
ideas, because that's what I've played. I know every class has
problems needing solutions.

Now, let's review the Enchanter spell book ... let me just mention
that at level 50, I finally got my level 24 Tepid Deeds spell. Spell
components are still too rare, I'll end up with 5 right sides of a
page and 0 lefts.

Choke Series, Damage over Time (DoT) ... my biggest complaint is that
our Strength debuffs and DoTs overlap, so for the most part the
Strength debuff is useless, since a little more mana does damage, as
well as, debuff.

Brain Melt Series, Direct Damage ... I can't complain about this. I'd
MUCH rather have full damage when a spell hits, then deal with
partial damage resists. I keep my DD memorized for emergencies, but
rarely cast it, mostly because I'm too busy trying to control the
battle. Naturally I use it when I solo .. Root & Nuke.

Tashan(i), great spells, too bad the higher level version was missing
for so long. It does make a difference, just let the melee guys do
some damage before you debuff the mob though. =)

Mesmerize Series ... probably our best set of spells, unfortunatly
the most useful one is Level 4 Mesmerize, because it casts so fast.
Our AE Mesmerization is OK, but as long as you have tanks and clerics
to take the blows, the single target spells are the best bet. As it
stands, AE is generally only useful if you want to commit suicide.

Charm Series ... well, they were cool at first, but their reliability
leaves alot to be desired. The bard charm song seems alot more
useful because the duration is basically fixed AND they can continue
to re-charm since it requires no mana. Without tanks, charm is
great; with tanks, just mesmorize and save the pain. Best use for
Charm - Charm Sand Giants on each other and let the crowd in Oasis
place their bets.

Shadow Series ... great ... unless there's a cleric handy. Even
still ours are more mana efficient, just a few less AC points.

Strengthen Series ... doesn't make alot of sense. Our first
(Strengthen) is a long-lasting buff and quite useful at the lower
levels. Our higher level Strength buffs are Combat Duration only so
a relatively poor use of mana in comparison to a Druids or Shamans.
Same thing goes for Augmentation (AGI Buff) ... good spell, duration
too short. Intelligence buff at higher levels rocks, Charisma buffs
probably help Charm but are best for getting better NPC prices.

Endure Magic buffs ... great, but again Cleric can cast everything
but Group Resist Magic (Level 49).

Animation Series ... pets. I know Verant intends these as Shields
and not a true pet, but something has to be done to keep them
leashed. They're fine to use killing crocodiles in Oasis, but almost
guaranteed to cause trains in dungeons. My suggestion is to give
them a fixed roam range around the caster. This way if the caster
doesn't chase the fleeing mob, neither does the pet.

Ally Series ... does anyone even know what these were intended to do?
My idea for this Series is to raise faction on the target ... so this
becomes a PC buff not an NPC debuff. This would become an alternate
method of sneaking through green trash in zones (if you've tried it
with invisible or invisible to undead...). Only problem is some zones
have multiple factions, so how do you choose the right one to raise?

Taper Enchantment Series ... these would be great if players could
dispel the NPCs like they dispel us. Otherwise, not worth keeping
memorized. Burn spam from NPC with damage shields cast on them is
very annoying.

Rune Series (Wizard Shieldskin Series) ... these spells are very
pricey for their limited duration. Clerics use the same components
and add hitpoints which can be healed. Good for solo'ing, but cleric
buffs and heals are better in a group.

Enchant Metal Series ... Hahahaha, jewelry deserves an entirely
seperate rant.

Fear Series ... not a whole lot of use, since the mob will bring back
a train in most places. Fun to use on town guards though.

Memory Wipe Series ... amazingly enough, I never really used these
alot. Normally just kept extra mobs mesmerized until it's their time
to die. Quickness Series ... melee types love these, extremely long
cast times and high mana costs for such short duration buffs though.

Illusion Series ... big problem here is mobs see through illusions
due to Diety choices. A troll worshipping Brell just doesn't seem to
cut it. At most, illusions seem to knock off one level of bad
faction - if frogs in Guktop look at me threateningly in gnome form,
they'll look at me dubiously as an Earth Elemental. If Druids can
have a Pack Illusion, then an Enchanter certainly deserves them as
well.
