To: The Illustrious Guild of Tinkers and Inventors, Ak'anon Chapter
From: Omnipotus
Subject: The Fall of Gear Norther

It is with a heavy heart that I must report the most recent accident regarding the Tinkers and the trials we underwent to restore one gnome's hope for the future.

It began with a frantic cry coming from the windmills of our beloved Steamfont, as one of our number ran about, seemingly lost, begging for help. His name was Fringle Doon and he was in the midst of completing his greatest creation: a device which would eliminate all hunger from the world. Somehow, though, a foul thief broke into his laboratory and stole the last of the components!

Desperately, Fringle begged us to track down the thief, a halfling by the name of Meepenny. Calling upon the impressive forces of those who had gathered at the windmills, we were magically transported to the Misty Thicket, where the hunt for the dastardly rogue began. While I myself am of this illustrious profession, I must say I found myself driven to track her down and retrieve her ill-gotten gains in the interest of furthering the ways of the tinker.

Finally, we found her, and she was dispatched with brutal efficiency. Once more, Eldritch winds transported us back to Steamfont, where we raced back to the windmills with the stolen parts.

When we arrived, we found Fringle along with his creation, called Gear Norther. Although it resembled the clockworks that roam Steamfont, this one was quite docile..at first. Hurriedly, Fringle completed the last bits of work, and a collective cheer rose from the group. Gear Norther began to move from person to person, distributing food to each of us. Truly a wondrous creation!!

During the theft, however, Meepenny must have done something to damage the delicate parts, because suddenly a horrid screeching arose from Gear Norther and it began to shake fiercely. Suddenly, it turned and attacked one of our companions!! It was trying to turn him into food!!

With a heavy heart, we drew our blades and lunged into the foray, beating the creation with every ounce of strength. Ultimately, the clockwork fell to our blows, and Fringle's sobs were heard throughout Steamfont. We sought to comfort him in this time of need, and he thanked us for our help, gifting each of us with some gold he had gathered together during his travels.

Each of us turned and headed back to our homes, and I could not help but wonder if my tinkering career might meet such an ignoble fate. Can the path of the rogue and that of the tinker co-exist? Must they clash against one another, leaving only death and destruction in their wake?

Surely there must be another way...

April 13, 2000 - King Ak'Anon and the T.B.I.
Greetings all! Some interesting news today...

To begin with, it would appear that King Ak'Anon is actually a clockwork! Grouchie Grumbles of Solusek Ro wrote in yesterday (as have others since, and it has also been discussed at the Ak'Anon Bar) that he was called in front of the King two days ago to discover that the King is no longer using his human illusion. There is now a clockwork claiming to be King Ak'Anon himself.

Now, I do not know what the rest of you think about this, but I am concerned. While I embrace my Tinker heritage, there have been times when I wander the corridors of Ak'Anon that I ponder who is really the master of our great society. We created the clockworks to help out with our common day-to-day tasks such as cleaning, policing and commerce. Their creation has allowed Gnomes to focus on more intellectual pursuits. However, have we come to rely on them to such a great extent that without them we would no longer be able to function as a society? Have we become a slave to our own inventions?

Now, with a clockwork in a position that we have all sworn loyalty to, I can only hope that this incarnation is only temporary and that our flesh and blood King will return. Perhaps the King currently fears for his life and has put a duplicate in his place to draw out the attacker? Only time will tell.

On to other possibly disturbing news. Babaaeyen of Povar wrote in yesterday (and others have discussed this at the Ak'Anon Bar) regarding a discussion with a fellow Gnome by the name of Kozyn Gigglephizz. Kozyn was apparently looking for any Tinmizer's Fabulous Compactors that may have been handed out by Sanfyrd at the scrapyard, and was offering a reward. Babaaeyen also mentioned that Kozyn was a part of the T.B.I., a group that I had not heard of. Given this information, and the fact that I own a Compactor, I headed off in search of Kozyn. Unfortunately, I was not able to find him (Babaaeyen mentioned that Kozyn is usually found in the little room next to the exit from Ak'Anon). I decided to go have a chat with Sanfyrd regarding the whole situation, and this is what transpired:

You say, 'What is tinmizer's fabulous compactor?'

Sanfyrd Featherhead is frightened by your inquiry. I cannot speak of it. I can tell you to seek a gnome in Ak'Anon named Kozyn. That is all. Now please go and leave me in peace.

Sanfyrd would offer up no further information regarding either the Compactor or Kozyn. Following my discussion with Sanfyrd, I decided to check to see if Kozyn had returned, and indeed he had. This was my discussion with him:

You say, 'hail Kozyn Gigglephizz'

Kozyn Gigglephizz bows politely. Greetings my friend. Have you come to turn in one of those malfunctioned containers called, [Tinmizer]'s [Fabulous Compactor]? I shall gladly exchange it for a few nice gems.

You say, 'Who is Tinmizer?'

Kozyn Gigglephizz chortles with glee. Ho, ho, ho!! Oh my. Tinmizer was a bit of a goof when it came to tinkering. I am from the [T.B.I.] and I was sent to clean up the mess he made. He was tinkering defective containers and selling them. We exiled him and destroyed all his containers. Now we hear the scrapman found some and was using them as a reward for deeds. We have warned to him.

You say, 'What is the TBI?'

Kozyn Gigglephizz lowers his voice and leans closer to your ear. Ssshhh. This is against regulations, but you look like you could keep a secret. T.B.I. Stands for the Tinkerers Bureau of Investigation. We are a clandestine organization working strictly with King Ak'Anon. We handle all tinkering glitches, if you know what I mean.

You say, 'What is the fabulous compactor?'

Kozyn Gigglephizz looks sternly at you. The compactor is a defective piece of junk. Crafted by the clumsy hands of [Tinmizer]. My work shall be done as soon as I exchange all the compactors for precious gems. We take a loss here, you know!!?

I then gave Kozyn my Compactor:

Kozyn Gigglephizz snatches the container from you before you could even let go of it. Yes, yes. And here is your exchange. Now please go and tell all who have the compactor to bring to me for an exchange. Pleasure to serve you.

Kozyn gave me a turquoise gem and went on his way.

Now you may be wondering why I felt this was all a little disturbing. On the surface, it would seem great that someone is willing to trade for an item that no one has been able to find a use for. However, the emergence of the T.B.I. does cause me some concern. It would appear that the T.B.I. has the power to exile any Gnome that has been accused of defective Tinkering, without any sort of appeal. Have any of you heard of Tinmizer before any of this? I certainly cannot find any record of him. He seems to have completely disappeared, as if he has never existed...

The next time you attempt to Tinker an item, take a look over your shoulder. There may be a Gnome watching you with great interest...

To: The Illustrious Guild of Tinkers and Inventors, Ak'anon Chapter
From: Omnipotus
Subject: The Fall of Gear Norther

It is with a heavy heart that I must report the most recent accident regarding the Tinkers and the trials we underwent to restore one gnome's hope for the future.

It began with a frantic cry coming from the windmills of our beloved Steamfont, as one of our number ran about, seemingly lost, begging for help. His name was Fringle Doon and he was in the midst of completing his greatest creation: a device which would eliminate all hunger from the world. Somehow, though, a foul thief broke into his laboratory and stole the last of the components!

Desperately, Fringle begged us to track down the thief, a halfling by the name of Meepenny. Calling upon the impressive forces of those who had gathered at the windmills, we were magically transported to the Misty Thicket, where the hunt for the dastardly rogue began. While I myself am of this illustrious profession, I must say I found myself driven to track her down and retrieve her ill-gotten gains in the interest of furthering the ways of the tinker.

Finally, we found her, and she was dispatched with brutal efficiency. Once more, Eldritch winds transported us back to Steamfont, where we raced back to the windmills with the stolen parts.

When we arrived, we found Fringle along with his creation, called Gear Norther. Although it resembled the clockworks that roam Steamfont, this one was quite docile..at first. Hurriedly, Fringle completed the last bits of work, and a collective cheer rose from the group. Gear Norther began to move from person to person, distributing food to each of us. Truly a wondrous creation!!

During the theft, however, Meepenny must have done something to damage the delicate parts, because suddenly a horrid screeching arose from Gear Norther and it began to shake fiercely. Suddenly, it turned and attacked one of our companions!! It was trying to turn him into food!!

With a heavy heart, we drew our blades and lunged into the foray, beating the creation with every ounce of strength. Ultimately, the clockwork fell to our blows, and Fringle's sobs were heard throughout Steamfont. We sought to comfort him in this time of need, and he thanked us for our help, gifting each of us with some gold he had gathered together during his travels.

Each of us turned and headed back to our homes, and I could not help but wonder if my tinkering career might meet such an ignoble fate. Can the path of the rogue and that of the tinker co-exist? Must they clash against one another, leaving only death and destruction in their wake?

Surely there must be another way...

Sanfyrd Featherhead is frightened by your inquiry. I cannot speak of it. I can tell you to seek a gnome in Ak'Anon named Kozyn. That is all. Now please go and leave me in peace.

You say, 'hail Kozyn Gigglephizz'

Kozyn Gigglephizz bows politely. Greetings my friend. Have you come to turn in one of those malfunctioned containers called, [Tinmizer]'s [Fabulous Compactor]? I shall gladly exchange it for a few nice gems.

You say, 'What is the TBI?'

Kozyn Gigglephizz lowers his voice and leans closer to your ear. Ssshhh. This is against regulations, but you look like you could keep a secret. T.B.I. You say, 'What is the fabulous compactor?'

Kozyn Gigglephizz snatches the container from you before you could even let go of it. Yes, yes. And here is your exchange. Now please go and tell all who have the compactor to bring to me for an exchange. Pleasure to serve you.

December 14, 1999 - Fireworks
