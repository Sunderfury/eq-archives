As with most of the other classes, race is not a huge factor.  It really determines personal style more than anything else.  The big three tend to be Agility, Dexterity, and Strength.  But don't discount the others.  Keep in mind that Wisdom is your defense against magic, Intelligence is your ability to learn that myriad of skills you have to master, Stamina is how long you can keep making double attacks with two weapons, and charisma... well... forget charisma, steal what you need.  :) Below is a breakdown.  

Human

Extra points: 30

STR: 75
STA: 75
AGI: 85
DEX: 85
WIS: 75
INT: 75
CHA: 75

 

 A good all around race with no super strengths, and no super weaknesses.  You also have the advantage of being accepted in all but the evil cities, but you do lack the ability to be able to see at night.  Personally, I say leave this race for the monks.  But, if you want, you can still make a descent rogue out of one.
Half-Elf
Extra points: 30

STR: 70
STA: 70
AGI: 100
DEX: 95
WIS: 60
INT: 75
CHA: 75

 

 

This is a good hybrid race.  With nearly the same strength and stamina as a human you also get the added agility and dexterity of being part elf.  Using those 30 pts wisely you can make an excellent rogue with a Half-Elf.  I have to say the best part about being Half-Elf vs. Wood Elf is that you don't have to start in Kelethin.  I've had 4 Wood Elf characters, and Kelethin has always drove me nuts.  The newbie garden is extremely overcrowded, you have to take lifts to get to the city, and the roaming guards take alot of kills from you.
Wood-Elf
Extra points: 30

STR: 65
STA: 65
AGI: 105
DEX: 90
WIS: 80
INT: 75
CHA: 75

 

Second only to the Halfling in dexterity, this is a wonderful class for the rogue.  The big downside is strength and stamina.  The strength is rumored to be one of the key factors in determining your backstab damage.  With backstab being your most prized skill, this can lead to be a very big distractor.  Be careful not to discount wisdom here, it helps resist spells.  That, coupled with the intelligence and charisma make for a good rogue as well.
Barbarian
Extra points: 30

STR: 103
STA: 95
AGI: 92
DEX: 80
WIS: 70
INT: 60
CHA: 55

 

I have to say that this race seems a bit crude to become a rogue.  Although the strenght must make for a stunning backstab damgage, the lack of everything else really brings it down.  With a wise distribution of the extra points, you could still make a descent rogue here as well.
Dark Elf
Extra points: 30

STR: 60
STA: 65
AGI: 100
DEX: 85
WIS: 83
INT: 99
CHA: 60

 

The Dark-Elf would have to be the best race for multiple skill mastery.  The high intelligence and wisdom help with magic resistance, and learning your skills quickly.  The lack of strength must be dealt with though.  I would suggest putting at least 10 points into strength or else you will lose alot in respect to backstab damage.  I wouldn't worry about the charisma too much though, you'll be swimming in money with your pick pocket skill.
Dwarf
Extra points: 30

STR: 90
STA: 90
AGI: 80
DEX: 100
WIS: 83
INT: 60
CHA: 45

 

A good choice, with the most glaring problem being the lack of intelligence and low agility.  Agility in particular is a key skill for a rogue.  The intelligence is troublesome due to the number of different skills you have to learn and master.  If choosing this race, I would suggest using all of your points on your agility, save 5 for intelligence.  
Halfling
Extra points: 30

STR: 70
STA: 75
AGI: 105
DEX: 100
WIS: 80
INT: 67
CHA: 50

 

I'm told that this is the best class for the rogue, but I guess I just don't like being short.  Strength, stamina, agility, dexterity, and wisdom are all ideal for this class.  The intelligence isn't too bad, and the charisma is a non-necessity.  This is a great class that is easily tune-able to your particular tastes.
Gnome
Extra points: 30

STR: 60
STA: 70
AGI: 95
DEX: 95
WIS: 67
INT: 98
CHA: 60

 

 

The big thing the Gnome has going for it is the intelligence.  The agility and dexterity aren't too bad, but should definitely be boosted some.  The rest of the points should go into strength and then you'll have a great Gnome rogue.  With the condition that you don't mind being short. :)
