Your wide variety of skills are your real edge in the dangerous world of Norrath.  Therefore you must make sure you practice them as much as possible so that you will be able to withstand the trials that await you.  Some general tips include...
1) Practice each one of your combat skills starting at level 1.  Start with Piercing (So that it will be maxed for Backstabbing), then move to Slashing, then to Blunt.  The latter may fall behind a bit at times, don't worry, just keep them as high as possible.  Consider fighting creatures that you have to take extra swings at to kill, maybe even kill some greens if you really need to catch up.
2) Practice your hide and sneak constantly.  These go up fairly easily with use, so when you see the button pop up, stop, press them both, then keep going.  You won't regret it in the long run.

 

Picking Pockets is the Rogue's most efficient way of "earning" money.  It's going to be very important for you to increase the skill as fast as possible.  Here are a couple of tips for doing just that.
1) You don't have to fight or be ready to fight your victim.  I made several    gold at level 7 by stealing off Smugglers that other people were fighting.     :^)
2) Pick Pocket while you're fighting.  You should have a hot key set to PP.  Start you fight off with a PP then when it pops back up turn off attack,  tap then PP, then turn attack back on.  Doing this I could normally get 6 PP's off one blue MOB. 
Using these methods I was able to move from level 4 to level 40 PP in just under two hours!!  And make descent money for a level 7 character.

 

Dealing with MOBs and PC's that have damage shields can be VERY dangerous for a rogue, considering the number of attacks per round we get.  Luckily, there is a way to prevent this sure death.  Collect some pumice stones, you can get them from herb merchants, and keep them in a bag at all times.  When you come up against said enemy target them, then click one of the pumice stones and it will dispell one of their buffs.  Use at least 4 when fighting a Druid, Shaman, or Cleric, and you will be reasonably sure of not only dispelling the accursed damage shield, but also their buffs that give them such an edge.  These things are INVALUABLE in PvP.  There are rumors of multi-charge pumice stones, but still under research for me.
