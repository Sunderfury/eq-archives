A long essay I'm revising to send to verant
by Calenth
posted 5/20/99 9:19:14 PM


Here's an old rant (originally aimed at kastur, heh) that i'm revising to outline what I see as problems and what I
see as solutions re ur class. Give me some feedback before I send it off, if you would. Thanks.

****************

Regardless of our ability to do damage by extremely indirect means, our lack of a generalized ability to
efficiently and reliably effect combat presents a severe problem and danger to the class at all levels of play.
The more efficient and reliable damage sources are denied us, to a greater extent than any other class. It has
been established that our direct damage and DoT spells are the weakest overall of the casting classes. GZ has
confirmed that and stated it as intentional. We're not supposed to have melee, we're an int based caster, not a
wis. I'll move on.

There are essentially two effective ways in which an enchanter may attempt to alter combat directly: the first
being by the mesmerize/stun assortment of spells, the recently nerfed Whirl being (at one time) the most famous
of these; the other is by use of debuff spells of various types. Primarily, these are ineffective due to the
all-too-often mentioned resist rate problems. The most generalized problems with these are caused by the
broken random number generator; while a resist rate of 5% or 10% or 15% is fine for a tashaned mob, getting
those five out of a hundred resists all one after the other is not. Furthermore, casting that initial tashan is not
always practical or even possible; you can’t tashan before a lull, you can’t tashan three things all running
towards you at once, you can’t tashan an enemy wizard and hope to get a second spell off before you’ve been
reduced to your last bubble of health. There are more specific problems with individual spells also; whirl has
been reduced to simply unacceptable levels of effectiveness, for example, and the high-level debuffs are
actually less effective than comparable first and second circle spells (at least against NPCs) due to the rising
discrepancy at high levels between NPC and PC statistics.


There are numerous ways, however, by which an enchanter can affect combat indirectly . .via the animations,
charmed pets, or buffs on other party members. Because these abilities are inefficient and unreliable, the
enchanter is unfortunately one of the weakest and slowest-leveling classes in the game. [Note: this is not
necessarily a bad thing. This is a game, not a race. But it is a fact, regardless.]

1. Animations. These are great, for what they are. At low levels especially, they are a wonderful addition to the
class; the ability to strike magically at 8th is the reason every enchanter I know did wisps between 8 and 12.
However: you couldn't pick a worse class to give uncommandable pets. As enchanters rise in levels, they
require more and more intelligence of play and overall use of tactics; and the eternal chance of a runaway,
uncontrollable, Surprise-pulling pet (especially in dungeons) is simply too dangerous to allow for the pet's
regular use. (This is aside from the fact of the pet's low general hp's -- our assorted stun spells render this only a
minor problem. [NOTE: this last sentence was written before the recent Whirl changes. It is a MAJOR problem
now.) While the pets are great in their way, they are simply unreliable; and that causes far too many problems.

2. Charm. "When an enchanter wants a pet, he charms one." This is a true statement. I use currently use charm
to the almost complete exclusion of the animations. I love the spell, for what it is; but what it is is not reliable. It
may be resisted all too easily; it may break at any given moment. It has a nasty tendency to get both the
once-charmed mob and whatever it attacked simultaneously melleeing the now hapless (get out that color flux
and run, boy!) enchanter. Whatever a given players stats or level, (200 chr level 50 or 75 chr level 12) charm is a
wonderful and immensely powerful spell . .but it is unreliable.

3. The buffing spells; the quickness line especially seems mentioned here. Again, in their way, these are
wonderful spells. We are not the game's best buffers by any means; but we do have a few very useful spells,
and the quickness line (especially when cast on monks) can bring about a marvelous increase in the party's
overall strength and ability to deal damage. However, they are just as unreliable (and In many ways even more
so) than the above two methods. Buffs on animations or charmed subjects are unreliable for the reasons the
pets themselves are unreliable: so I'll dispense with what should be an obvious argument. People may argue
that buffs on party members circumvent the problems above; in a way, they do, but they bring more problems of
their own. The primary problem of course is that party members tend to be fairly unreliable, if not moreso than a
charmed pet. One can at least be certain that a charmed pet will follow commands as long as the spell lasts. All
to frequently, a reliance on others (albiet buffed others) results in failure of the given endeavor. I'm sure we've all
dealt with monks who attacked whatever they felt like; warriors who thought that the word 'tactics' meant makig
sure they had at least half hp before they went to pull ; clerics who thought that healing spells somehow only
worked on themselves; wizards who thought that the answer to mob frenzied on themselves was to burn mana
even faster; and so on, and so forth, all down the line. . the point being, of course, that while good reliable party
members are out there, they are extremely difficult to find. .and they are a requirement for the successfull play of
a buffing enchanter.

In short, then -- while I love this class, and doubt I could even feasibly enjoy playing another -- the class suffers
from a hobbling and largely hidden weakness; overall unreliability. (The resist rates are a small part of this, but
are not really central to the problem.) We simply have no way to reliably and efficiently affect combat without
reliance on externals. All classes experience this to a degree. Some, however, experience it to a much higher
degree than others; enchanters, I believe, experience this weakness to the highest degree of any class. As
such, the enchanter experiences a much keener need for a reliable and efficient party in order to advance; and
this, I believe, is the primary cause of the extremely notable discrepancy between enchanter /played times to
level ratios and the /played ratios of other classes.


That being said, I don’t think the class is unsaveable. There are a few changes I’d like to see. Here they are, in
short order.

I’d like to see the random number generator fixed. I’d like to see Tashan, or at least it’s higher-level equivalent
Tashani, have a very fast casting time and very short delay time on recast. I’d like to see a ten or fifteen second
minimum duration on Charm spells. I’d like to see the memblur series made unresista
