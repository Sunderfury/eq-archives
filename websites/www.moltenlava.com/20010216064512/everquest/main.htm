EverQuest is a Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game that offers complete submersion to a gamer. The developer, 989 Studios, wanted to create a game that was not only of utmost quality, but a game that they want to play, which is why it is so successful. Brad McQuaid, lead designer, made sure that everyone on the team, from artists to programmers, was a fan of the old text-based MUD games, AD&D, and Tolkien's literature.  They used past online favorites and combined their top traits to create a game that any role-player would greatly enjoy. The amount of depth and realism, despite the fantasy genre, surpasses any game that has ever been created. This is helped by the fact that EverQuest's world is completely 3D. With graphics comparable to Quake II and gameplay comparable to AD&D, this game was sure to be a hit from the beginning.

However, 989 Studios did not stop there! They brought in a few things that really makes this game shine over other persistent online worlds. Everyone knows that one of the downfalls of Ultima Online was the over-rampant, random player-killing. Well, EverQuest has a little design called the PK switch which allows people to choose whether or not they want to participate in player-kill/stealing. If a player turns his PK switch on, he may kill others that have the PK switch on, and they can kill him. This also applies to stealing. However, if a person does not want to be burdened with that nuisance, they can leave their switch off and be immune to all anti-social behavior. In this way, players who want to enjoy the game for what it is may do so without worrying if it is safe to step out of town.

Another aspect of EverQuest that many players enjoy is the emphasis on group play. Though it is possible to advance a character solo, it will be much more difficult to do so than if that character was in a group. The groups that are well-balanced will succeed. Since all classes have a disadvantage, the group that counters it with a different class's advantage will do well. An ideal group usually contains two fighters, a spell-caster, and a healer (which does not mean two warriors, a wizard, and a cleric).  Conversely, a group that has all fighters will have a hard time shooting down run-aways, a group of spell-casters won't be able to take damage, and a group of healers won't be able to dish out damage. This is why balancing groups is so important!

989 Studios has spent a great amount of time creating a sense of community. From the moment a new character is born in his home town, he becomes a part of the world. By walking around and meeting other people, that character will belong to a community that never dies. You will begin to notice the same players in a zone, and you will advance at approximately the same rate as them. By adventuring with these people, you will have a feeling of camaraderie that never dies. In fact, people that adventure together will start looking for one-another and real friendships bond. There is nothing better than logging into a zone and receiving many pleas from people to join their group just because they have adventured together before.

All in all, EverQuest promises to be a game that people will be playing for years to come. There is just so much to the game that it would be impossible to do everything. With 12 different races, 14 different classes, and three huge continents to explore, a player will never find himself with nothing to do. In fact, if there is one downfall to EverQuest, it is the fact that it is so big, not even the greatest power-gamer can 'complete' the game.
