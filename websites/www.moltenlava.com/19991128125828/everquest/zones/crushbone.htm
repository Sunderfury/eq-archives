Name:  Dwarven Slave
Level:  6-8

Experience:  Poor
Faction Change:  Yes, Bad

Dwarven Slaves are the poor captured souls of the orcish hoards that inhabit Faydwer.  After being beaten down by the orcs in harsh conditions, they are not in any position to fight.  In fact, it will be unwise to attack them since the other Dwarves don't appreciate it.  They will not attack you on sight, so basically, leave them alone.

Name:  Orc Centurion
Level:  5-9
Loot:  Tattered Belt, 2-5 Silver
Experience:  Poor
Faction Change:  Yes
Melee Damage Max:  16
Magical Damage Max:  N/A

Orc Centurions are the second weakest type of Orc in Crushbone.  Generally, they are weak, spawn in numbers, and aren't much trouble for a well-balanced group of the same level.  In fact, they have the best loot for a creature of that level!  On the other hand, they also have one of the worst experience values.  It is a balance that you will have to decide which side you want to be on.  On a side note, since they do spawn in numbers, be wary that you do not get too many of them on you.  Also, they are very rarely the only type of monster around--keep an eye out for Legionnaires

Name:  Orc Emissary
Level:  13-16
Loot:  Gold and Weapons
Experience:  Fair
Faction Change:  Yes
Melee Damage Max:  24
Magical Damage Max:  N/A

Orc Emissaries are the equivalent to an Enchanter in Orcish terms.  They are very strong, though, which shows their difference.  They are able to cast spells, but only those which buff/debuff other characters and the occasional heal.  They are not too difficult to take down, as long as they are alone.  Also, their loot is well-worth the fight.

Orc Legionnaires are the leaders of the Centurions.  They are very often found together and will be the strength of the fight while the Centurions disrupt your casting and slowly take you down.  They can be found all across Crushbone, especially where Centurions are found.  They can be very nasty if a few team up on you, however, they are not that bad, for the most part.

Name:  Orc Pawn
Level: 3-5
Loot:  A Few Silver or Copper, Occasional Weapon
Experience:  Poor
Faction Change:  Yes
Melee Damage Max:  8
Magical Damage Max:  N/A

If you are strong enough to fight in Crushbone, these guys will be green to you.   They are very weak, basically newbie fighters.  They only spawn near the entrance, but they can be a real nuisance if provoked for a spellcaster since it will be difficult to get spells off between their hits.  However, they will not attack you if you do not attack them (assuming they are green to you).
