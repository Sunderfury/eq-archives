City Name:  Halas
Native Race:  Barbarian
Guard Allignment:  Good
Guild Masters: Rogue, Shaman, Warrior

Halas is the northernmost city found in Norrath.  Due to its distance from the equator, it is completely frozen over with a layer of permafrost and snow covering everything.  The Barbarians that live there, though, have grown used to the cold climate and can actually live in sub-zero temperatures with little to no clothing.  It is an adaptation that has allowed them to live in Halas all their lives.

The Barbarians in Halas have many more adaptations, also.  They have created their buildings so that they can endure the cold and snow.   Many of the buildings are made of thick, stone blocks so that the heat of the fire within won't escape.  In fact, some of these houses are much warmer than the ones found in the southern city of Grobb. 

Barbarians take their hunting seriously.  Throughout the town, you can find the mounted heads of just about every creature in Norrath.  Being primarily Warriors, this isn't much of a suprise.  In fact, the Barbarian Warrior is just about as brutal as it gets with little regard to pansy things like animal rights or mercy.   If they are physically stronger, they believe they have the right to kill the creature and hang its head on a wall as a trophy!

Another thing that Barbarians take seriously is their death matches.  They have a huge arena set up complete with spiked logs and bloody snow where they trap either two animals, two humans, or any combination of both.   Erudites or High Elves watching in on the monstrosity would consider it completely uncivilized, yet it is a popular part of Barbarian culture where even the women and children are invited to watch.  Just be sure not to eat the red snow!

An interesting thing to note about Halas is that there is only one entrance into the city--and it isn't by foot, either!  In order to get to Halas, you must travel through a small tunnel, which opens up into a large, near-freezing lake.  You will then see a dock on either side of the lake, with a raft connecting them together.   To get from one side to another, you must take the raft over.  No one knows if the city was originally planned out this way to avoid invasion, or if it was just an added bonus they just got lucky with.  Either way, the Barbarian guards have little to worry about throughout the course of a day.
