City Name:  Oggok
Native Race:  Ogres
Guard Alignment: Evil
Guild Masters: Shadow Knight, Shaman, Warrior

Oggok is the disgusting little town that the Ogres somehow built (and is remarkably still standing).  The first thing a new ogre will notice about the city is the number of guards wandering around the entire city just waiting for a human, elf, or other creature to find its way inside.  They wear their skull-faced platemail and wield their enormous swords with a full-faced helmet on.  Of all the races, the Ogre guards are the strongest and least sympathetic towards their enemies.   They hit with almost two-hundred points per shot.  With their strong hits and massive numbers, the Ogre women and children won't have to worry about anything invading their city any time soon.

"Flintstones, meet the Flintstones," is the second thing that will get into anyone's head when they enter Oggok.  All of their buildings are created from five giant slabs of stone holding each other up just like Fred and Wilma's humble abode.  They have carved windows on the sides, and placed a few torches inside.  The floor is nothing but the muddy ground that was originally there since Ogres have neither the talent nor want of a floor.   They prefer the disgusting houses they create, and actually think it is charming in their own distorted sense.

Past the few houses of the town is the heart of the city.  This consists of a huge maze of tunnels twisting and turning in a totally unpredictable way.  This is because the original diggers of the city never planned out what they were doing, so they just improvised as they went.  The tunnels also lead into a few open balconies that show on the mountain-face, but could be very unsafe if an Ogre were to fall.  Within the tunnels, an Ogre can find the two of the three guild masters--Warrior and Shadow Knight.  The Shaman guildmaster can be found right outside the town entrance.

The Ogre men and women of the town can usually be found wandering around the square outside the tunnels, practicing their primitive spells, hunting the local vermin, or just bashing things and eating them.  The majority of them, though, are so lazy they just sit around and do nothing!  Every now and then a little commotion would be raised when a young Ogre finds he got himself a little in over his head in the nearby forest or desert.  However, Oggok is a pretty uneventful town for the most part.
