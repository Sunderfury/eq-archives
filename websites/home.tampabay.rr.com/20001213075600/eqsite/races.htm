Races of Norrath

There has been limited information regarding the races and the particulars of their culture, but here is what we know: there are 12 player races, and there are also NPC races with which you can interact, such as Orcs and Gnolls. You can be either male or female, but not all races can be all classes! Traditionally, after all, Halflings have been less proficient in the magical arts, as have Trolls...

  • Humans. This is the normal, average of all the races, by which other races are compared. They will undoubtedly not get innate special abilities, but will have a wide variety of classes available.   Additionally, they do not have the experience penalties that many other races share.
  • Barbarians. This race is an offshoot of the normal humans, a much more hardy and physical variety, as is to be expected from their wintery home of Halas. They are less cultured than the average human, and more warlike. The rugged cold of the Northlands makes for a strong people, with hearts inspired to the Justice of the Tribunal.
  • Erudites (High Men). This variant of humanity values their intellect over their brawn, and have attained great status with magical ability. Founded by their leader, Erud, they originally settled the island of Odus, and pride themselves on their social and mental abilties.
  • Woodland Elves.   The Feir'Dal. These elves are forest dwellers, with their home of Kelethin high in the trees, and have more pointed features than humans, including pointed ears. Their intellect matches that of a High Man, and their speed and nimble reflexes are superior to those of men. Their charms and comeliness are greater as well, but their physical strength and prowess is not as great as an average man.
  • High Elves. This is the noble variant of the elves, the most lordly, although the Dark Elves would challenge their "royalty." As a more intellectual race of elves, they are thinner, paler, and more attractive than their woodland cousins. They can be found on their continent of Faydwer, in the palatial, marble city of Felwithe.
  • Dark Elves. The Teir'Dal. This race is the master of the undercaverns, where they can see perfectly well in normal darkness. Their race is devoted to evil gods, and they plot their vile schemes from their home city of Neriak. Their hair is stark white, while their skin is bluish black.
  • Half-Elves. This is the union of a Human and an Elf, having some of the characteristics of both parents. Their features are slightly pointed, but less than a true Elf's.  Their home city of origin is chosen at character creation by the choice of deity, which would seemingly indicate which of their parents they were raised with, at least on a cultural level (e.g., Freeport or Qeynos for the humans, Felwithe or Kelethin for the Elves).
  • Dwarves. An extremely hardy and also short race, the dwarf prides himself on his beard or moustache. They are also quite agile, and skilled.  Their stoic and proud city of Kaladim is a sight to behold, and their sharp axes and keen vision is the first greeting a visitor to the continent of Faydwer encounters.
  • Trolls. This race averages around 8 feet in height, making small entrances and most beds out of the question! Their size is equalled in their strength, but their green skin is often smelly and unwashed. They are not the most hygeinic race on Norrath, nor are they extremely bright.  Their stunted version of the common tongue is practically a sublanguage in itself, and the violent nature of this race is reflected by the fact that cannibalism is both accepted and at times encouraged.
  • Ogres. This is arguably the strongest race on Norrath, and equal to the Trolls in endurance. Their height averages 10 feet, and their weight averages 400 pounds! They are, unfortunately, extremely stupid, and it is a cause for celebration if they remember to cook their food... Rallos Zek is credited with the creation of this powerful race.
  • Halflings. They are a rather new race, like Humans, but are shorter than men. They are quick, agile, and stocky, being first encountered in their home of Rivervale. They love a good meal and good conversation, and their nimble natures have given them a natural advantage in their adventuring careers.
  • Gnomes. This race spends most of its time underground in their home city of Ak'Anon, where they have intricate homes. They are much like the Dwarves, but their skin is more of a brown hue, and their heads are sparsely covered with white hair.  The most unique nature of these crafty, intelligent peoples is their affinity for their clockwork items, which are both their servants and creations.
