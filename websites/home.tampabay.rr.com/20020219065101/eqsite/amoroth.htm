Overview

Welcome to my EverQuest site, devoted to the astoundingly enchanting world of Norrath, developed by Sony Interactive Studios America. Their game, EverQuest, promises to revolutionize the field of online gaming. Seeped in the fantasy traditions of tableside gaming such as Advanced Dungeons & Dragons, Rolemaster, and GURPS Fantasy, as well as the literary traditions of such novelists as J. R. R. Tolkien, Margaret Weiss & Tracy Hickman, and R. A. Salvatore, EverQuest offers a true outlet for anyone who has ever envisioned an Elf, proudly standing on a precipice with his lordly locks blowing in the wind, or of an Orc, gnashing his jagged, tooth-filled maw while he cleaves downward viciously with his axe.

Add to this richly imaginative fantasy setting the technological aspect: 3Dfx compliant using the latest Direct 3D technology, first person perspective ala Quake or Doom, polygonal characters who can be personalized, rich sounds, and 1000 or so people online per server, with the commitment to advanced code that promises to reduce lag. Anyone who has every played Ultima Online can definitely appreciate the last comment regarding lag...

 

Admirable Elements

  • Naming Convention. According to a recent post by Brad McQuaid, the producer of the game,the player must choose a name that is within the "high fantasy genre." That means none of those foolish "c00L d00D" idiots who ruin both Diablo and Ultima Online with their unimaginative childsplay. Forgive my tirade, but EverQuest is NOT to be a graphical chatroom. Keep the casing and lack of roleplaying to the teen webchats, please.
  • 3Dfx and Direct 3D. One cannot emphasize enough the wonder of this technology. My wife's gift this past holiday season, the 3Dfx card in my machine eagerly awaits EverQuest's stimuli. This devotion to brilliant graphics will emphasize the stunning landscapes as your Troll gazes down a cliffside at a group of marching Gnomes, or else bring to vivid life the water splashing around your Dark Elven warrior as he dives into underwater caverns. Brad McQuaid even recently verified that the new Voodoo 2 chipset will work with EQ! With all that, though, there is still the choice of a variety of viewpoints, from first person, to many topside views, for tactical maneuvers.
  • Variety in Gaming. With 12 races and 14 classes, there should be no end to the hours of gameplay possible. Hopefully, if one does not opt for the "tank mage" thinking of Ultima Online, each of the combinations of race and profession allowable will be explored by the players. As a balancing issue, not all races can be all classes, which is not such a bad thing; I would cringe at the idea of a brutally strong Troll being able to cast the most damaging Wizard spells! The range includes the popular Dark Elf, with the characteristic blue-black skin and white hair of Drizzt Du'orden, as well as the more lordly High Elf, such as Legolas; there proves to be variety in both culture and racial choices in Norrath.
  • To PK or not to PK. That is the question. The creators of EverQuest actually listened to the complaints of Ultima Online players, who, after months of building up a beloved character, gets ambushed by a gang of teenage thugs (PK's, or "player killers"), all chanting "F**k you d00d" or some other obscenity. Finally, a game comes along that permits those who want to wargame to wargame, and those who just want to roleplay to roleplay. During character creation, one can opt whether or not to have one's "PK switch" turned on, allowing inter-player combat, or turned off, not permitting one to be harmed by another player. Certain actions one takes can allow the switch to become activated, though, such as aiding a PK friend or the like, so don't think of it as an invulnerability switch! (Credit to Designer Dragon of UO: he did make an eloquent explanation of why he doesn't believe a "PK switch" was the correct road for UO, but I would at least prefer the option).
  • 3Dfx. Did I mention this already? :)
  • Ogres and Trolls as Player Races. One of my favorite characters in a Rolemaster campaign was an Ogre played by my brother, who, when we were backed in a corner and accused by crooked town guards of assaulting an insane snitch with whom we were only previously conversing, promptly picked up the small man and said "that's not assault! THIS is assault!" and brained the guy against the wall. Ah, the good ol' days....
  • Over 40 unique skills should allow for plenty of variety and individualization, including innate racial abilities. I wonder if Dark Elves will get "Faerie Fire?" :)
  • A unique magic system. Admittedly, details of this system have not yet been released, but it will provide a basis for the magical classes, which include the Cleric, who can heal and channel the power of his/her deity; the Paladin, a combination of Warrior and Cleric; the Necromancer, who can animate the dead and steal lifeforce from opponents; the Shadow Knight, a combination of Warrior and Necromancer; Magician, the summoners and conjurers of Norrath; Wizard, who tap the flow of magic directly, allowing for powerfully destructive spells; Enchanters, who can both magically augment and enchant items, as well as charm people; and Druids, who tap the natural world's inherent power. Will the system use mana, spellbooks, spell components/reagents? We don't know yet, but there will be "hundreds" of spells.
