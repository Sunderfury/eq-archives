Amoroth

Pale waxing light streaming through to illuminate the symbols,
Reminding me I’ve spent another night awake in my chambers
Secretly coveting the words of the Goddess Erollisi Marr
While balancing my duty to my creator, my patron Tunare.

Silently striding past marble pillars, over marbled floors
To replace the tome of Erollisi before the morning rites
While the other initiates break the slumber of night,
Back now, protected by Tunare’s double-handed swords.

The searing pride wells during the training,
Mock combats and meditative prayer, focusing,
But still the memory evokes, following me along,
Her spry, sylvan smile as she breathes life to her song.

Suppressing a smile as I concentrate
Yet still I am haunted by that lovely face
Those eyes that captured my duty-bound heart
Spinning the melodies of Kelethin’s art.

Days pass onwards, studious tutelage
Absorbing the wisdom of Tynkale’s words,
Balancing lunges with heavy worn swords,
Meditating for wisdom, serene rites of passage.

Running the missions, proving my worth,
Tempering my mettle with love-stricken words,
Dropping the gauntlet on our Orcish foes,
Whose torturous fangs add to our woes.

Trying to honor Lord Jael’s commands,
While pushing back the vision of Orcish cruelty,
The rended flesh of proud Elven fealty,
Displayed horridly near Orc-claimed lands.

The path of Tunare’s chosen winds onward,
Swearing my oaths through prayer and action
While sharing my growth, my satisfaction
With the lovely bard, my sensuous ward.
