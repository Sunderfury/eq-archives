Bob Becks' Professional Interests

I have recently worked at SliceWare, Inc. (web-based support for small business internet services), and Share This, Inc. (a software startup in the Portland area), working on internet based real time collaborative software.

I was previously at (another young software company in the Portland area), from 3/1997 thru 5/2000.

Prior to Webridge, I worked at Intel Corporation (2/1995 - 3/1997), in the Application Components Lab (ACL), part of the Intel Architecture Labs (IAL) in Hillsboro Oregon. I was involved with a variety of things related to the Internet, and was fortuneate to become expert with the Java language and environment. My last project was Intel Distributed Moo 2.0, an infrastructure for creating multi-user distributed applications. This infrasturcture leverages features of Java to support highly dynamic multi-user environments.

I worked at Sequent Computer Systems (1/1983 - 2/1995), where I was a co-founder of the company. At Sequent I was a key contributer to their Symmetric Multiprocessing version of UNIX (DYNIX), and in later years was involved with Object Technology (lots of Smalltalk, C++, RDBMS, and OODBMS work).

Before Sequent, I was with Intel, where I worked on RMX/86 and did Intel's first UNIX implemention (Xenix/86).

I hold a BS and MS in Mathematics from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (1975), and a MS in Computer Science from the University of Wisconsin, Madison (1977).

I have published a number of papers in Operating Systems and Object Technology areas, and have taught classes in Object Oriented Programming.
