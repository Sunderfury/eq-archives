Bob Becks' Professional Interests

I am a co-founder of(a software startup in the Portland area). There is now some publicly available information about what we're doing; see the Webridge web site for details.

I previously worked at Intel Corporation (2/95 - 3/97), in the Application Components Lab (ACL), part of the Intel Architecture Labs (IAL) in Hillsboro Oregon. I was involved with a variety of things related to the Internet, and was fortuneate to become expert with the Java language and environment. My last project was Intel Distributed Moo 2.0, an infrastructure for creating multi-user distributed applications. This infrasturcture leverages features of Java to support highly dynamic multi-user environments.

I worked at Sequent Computer Systems (1/83 - 2/95), where I was a co-founder of the company. At Sequent I was a key contributer to their Symmetric Multiprocessing version of UNIX (DYNIX), and in later years was involved with Object Technology (lots of Smalltalk, C++, RDBMS, and OODBMS work).

Before Sequent, I was with Intel, where I worked on RMX/86 and did Intel's first UNIX implemention (Xenix/86).

I hold a BS and MS in Mathematics from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (1975), and a MS in Computer Science from the University of Wisconsin, Madison (1977).

I have published a number of papers in Operating Systems and Object Technology areas, and have taught classes in Object Oriented Programming.
