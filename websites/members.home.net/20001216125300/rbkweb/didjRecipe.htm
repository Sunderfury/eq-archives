Bob Becks' Plastic 'Didj Recipe

If you've ever thought about trying out a 'didj but not spending much $$ for one, this page is for you! You can make a (very) inexpensive, yet usable, plastic 'didj for a parts cost of less than $5, and all parts can be found at almost any hardware store! The nice thing about a plastic 'didj is that it's cheap (a good price), you can take it lots of places and not worry about it getting damaged ('cause it's cheap), and you can make more if desired ('cause it's cheap ;-) If and when you decide you really like playing the 'didj, you can spend a few more bucks on a real one (good wooden ones can be found for $200 - $400, some for more, so you want to really know you'll keep playing before you shell out the big bucks).

Parts:

  • One 4 foot (approx) piece of 1.5" ABS pipe (the black, lightweight, PVC-like piping). An inch or so longer is better than an inch or so shorter, but anywhere around 4 feet is fine. I have one that's 4'6", and it works fine (the tone is a bit lower, maybe a little harder to play). I think anything between about 4 feet and 4.5 feet is probably fine. The folks at most hardware stores will cut the pipe for you. I like the black ABS pipe better than the white PVC pipe, since it's lighter in weight and I think it looks better unpainted.
  • One 1.5" to 1.25" female trap adapter, for use as a mouthpiece. A 1.5" to 1.25" male trap adapter can work also, if that's all you can find.
  • Optional: a curved ABS pipe joint (30 degrees or less), if you like, for the bell end of the 'didj - this is not necessary, and may add $1.50 or so to the price. I like it for looks.

Assembly:

  • This is the hard part: stick the female trap adapter on one end of the pipe, the curved joint at the other end (if you bought one), and start playing!
  • Optional: Some people like to remove the end of the female trap adapter and dip it in beeswax, to make a more natural and comfortable mouthpiece. Feel free. I haven't done this to any of my plastic 'didj's.
  • Really Optional: If you're really ambitious, you can paint your 'didj to make it look nicer. I have yet to do this, but have seen some for sale for $50 or so that look real nice (they don't play any better than your $5 'didj, though ;-)

If you'd like one that's more portable, you can buy ABS adapter joints (don't know what they're called offhand), cut your ABS pipe into whatever size pieces works for you (you need some kind of ABS pipe cutting tool), and assemble with the joints (don't glue!) Works fine; I have one cut into 1 foot pieces which is easily disassembled and fits into my back pack. Great for business trips, company picnics, campfires, trips to the in-laws, etc. ;-)
