Editorial Comment - 1

Greetings Brothers and Sisters In Arms,

I am writing this letter to you in the hope that the role of the Paladin in Norrath is laid out to you in greater detail so that you may understand our ways.

I have been a Paladin for a great number of years now and feel that some may have lost the Way.

One must understand the role a Paladin plays and the Beliefs They possess when presented with a problem or foe They must defeat. Ours is not an easy task. We bring Justice and Honour to our foes with blade and hammer. The use of ranged weapons or spells cast from a distance is an insult to Our Abilities and Our Testament to the Destruction of Evil in Norrath. Our Skills are granted to Us by the Righteousness of our Task and the Deity that grants us that Right. As you look upon a battle and watch the Wizards spurt fire from their fingers, and the Warriors loose their arrows, know that, while they are important elements of Our Battles, We carry the day by destroying the Evil with our own hands.

We do not fight with two blades. This would lessen the Power of Our Truthfulness. Every time we strike with Our blade, We are Cleansing the Land. To fight with two blades would lessen our Resolve. Our Holy Brothers, Clerics, refuse to use blades, rather fighting with blunted weapons, to lessen damage and live to their vow of refusing to draw blood. We too, fight with blunted weapons, but, on the whole, prefer to deal damage with Our Blades as the drawing of blood ensures Our Evil Foes Destruction.

Remember friends, Paladins have existed throughout the Ages. As we arrived in Norrath, We did not expect to change Our Ways, nor should We.

We must not be proud, nor be humble. Pride is a weakness, causing many to die needlessly, many to falter. Humility would lessen Our Resolve, for it takes away Our Confidence. We must not Judge. Evil will always present itself, do not look for it, for it will find you. When Evil has presented itself, slay it and Cleanse the Land! Remember, the Heart contains a Creatures Evil. Although a Creature may appear Evil on the outside, look for it Truthfully from the Heart.

Through our Service and Truthfulness, the Gods have granted us the use of Spells and Healing. To cast offensively would not be part of Our True Being. When We are faced with Undead, they Cringe at Our Holiness and Fear our Wrath. We rid the Land of Undead through the use of Our Holiness and Destructive Power. Remember, Undead Creatures have no Hearts. Their very existence is an insult to Our Task.

The Gods have also granted us the Exclusive Use of Holy Swords, for, if any other than a Paladin were to lay their hands upon a Holy Sword, their minds would be swallowed up by its power. Use these Swords with the utmost of care. Earn your blades. I encourage you to enter the Depths and earn your Holy Sword as you were meant to.

I hope I have left thee with more Resolve. Remember, We are not meant to go about our Tasks easily. Ours is the Road of Truthfulness. If it were to come easily, We would not become Stronger through the Trials we face.

Help thy Fellow Paladins, and Go in the Light,

Varrus

PS: I hope you enjoyed this letter. I DO feel that Paladins should be granted a "type" of Direct Damage spell. But it would not deal damage. Some sort of Taunting spell that would enrage the animal to attack you would be within our beliefs. Getting creatures to attack you from range is more of a gameplay issue than anything else. Why couldn't we yell at a creature to get them to attack us? Maybe a spell called Enrage, that would have the same range as the majority of Direct Damage spells and act like a Taunt times 5 or 10? Undead Creatures that were "blue" should certainly attack ANY Paladin that was within sight.
