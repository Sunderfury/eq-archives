Rolan's Player Guide

By Rolan of COR

I play a level 23 human paladin by the name of Rolan on the Tarrew Marr server. He's a follower of Mithaniel Marr, the LightBringer. So I suppose I have a few things to say about what it takes to be a Paladin.

Creating your character:

I chose to play a human, but race selection is a personal choice. Remember when creating your character that it doesn't pay to have any real weak areas, but most definitely put most of your extra points into strength. Wearing full plate is extremely heavy. I am usually at around 70/90 even before I start adventuring, and I am in Banded armor, not even plate yet.

Skills:

Basically do not neglect any of your skills. You don't know what the future holds, so why be unprepared? The debate over whether 1h or 2h is better for a paladin is a moot point if you are a master in all weaponry. Just use the one that helps you the most at diffrent times in your growth as a paladin. I personally started out with 2h slash, for the increased damage, allowing me to solo more I think when friends weren't around. I am leaning more towards 1h slash now, at level 23, as I have a good shield and need to be the damage taker in groups (I'm not a tank, I don't fire large bullets nor have treads). Thats the crux of the 1h vs. 2h difference I think. 1h weapons combined with Bash makes you a good damage taker in a group. But a paladin equipped with a good 2h handed sword can solo better I think. Use the weapon that is best suited to the type of adventuring you are going to be doing, solo or grouped.

Neglecting 1h blunt is also a mistake, since you'd be suprised how many times you are going to need to pull your body out of the depths of some nasty dungeon. Your Hammer of Wrath or Striking is going to be your only weapon, so you better know how to use it. Anybody that says Hammer of Wrath is a waste of money hasn't had to use it to get their body out of a nightmare like Guk or Najena.

Taunt- Max it out or you won't have many friends. If you can't keep monsters off of spell casters, they are going to find a new damage taker.

Bash- Extremely useful when fighting spellcasters to interrupt them. Don't neglect it. Obviously requires you are good at 1h slash or blunt.

Additionally, there are a couple of skills as a paladin that you have to put a lot of points in at first. You don't need to train Conjuration and Evocation till level 15, but when you do plan on splitting, 20 to 40 skill points between them. Otherwise you will have a difficult time with Ward Undead or the Hammer of Wrath summoning.

 

Good weapons for a paladin:

If you are a paladin, you have to have a magic weapon above level 12 or so. Most higher level undead can't be hit by normal weapons.

2h slash- Get your hands on a fine steel 2 handed sword for use till level 12 or so, then I hope you got some money saved up. You can either get a combine 2 handed sword for around 110 pp from a gypsie, or save a bit more and get your hands on a Shiny Brass Halberd from nother player. At around level 16 you have entered the level range where adventuring to attain magic weapons becomes possible.

1h slash- A Combine longsword costs around 55 pp. Its a good magic weapon for someone around level 12 to 16. Beyond that I'd advise more adventuring to discover some other magic 1h slash weapons.

1h blunt- You can get the Hammer of Wrath spell at level 15. Again, you have to have saved up some skill points to put into conjuration, or you won't be able to summon a hammer. It is an ok magic weapon, and is best used as a backup to defend yourself while recovering your corpse.

2h blunt- There is no easy way for a paladin to train in this weapon skill that I have found. The lowest level weapon that I've seen that a paladin can wield is found on a roughy level 23 monster. So I've neglected this skill, maybe in the future I'll train it.

One other point worth mentioning. There are sites out there that will give you large lists of magic items, and based on those a paladin should use 1h or 2h slash as his primary weapon skill. The thing is those sites have barely scratched the surface I think. Is there a Holy Hammer out there? Can anyone tell me definitively that it does not exist? Is the Fiery Avenger 1h or 2h? What about the other Holy Swords? All those questions don't matter if you are a skilled armsman.

Armor:

If you can find a good source of raw-hide armor in your size, get yourself a full set and then save to buy Banded Armor from a player smith. While saving you'll probably come accross some ringmail armor as loot. Its heavy but better than leather based armor. I wouldn't buy it though. If you don't know much about the different armor types, they are roughly in this order of usefullness for paladins. Cloth, patchwork, Raw-hide, Leather, Ringmail, Chainmail, Bronze plate, Banded (debateable), regular plate, magic plate. The reason I put Banded as higher than bronze plate is that Banded armor is between chainmail and plate in AC, but is the same weight as chainmail. Bronze plate is 25% heavier than regular plate. If you are incredibly strong you can get away with wearing it, but as for me I prefer to wear banded until I can afford regular plate, or magic plate/chainmail.

Thats about all the advice I can give. I hope this helps any paladins out there that are just starting out. =)
