Guide for the Dwarven Paladin

This document is intended for the edification of dwarven Paladins of Norrath. It is possible that the information in this document may be of use to the lesser races, but this is purely accidental.

Getting Starting

  • Put 5 points into agility! There is a large AC penalty for characters with agility lower than 75.
  • Put the rest of your points into strength.

Skills

All skills that become available after level 1 require a practice point to be spent to get them started. The starting value of those skills will be the level at which they became available.

Level 1
  • Archery - Caps at 75. Only good for pulling monsters to you.
  • One Hand Slashing - This skill is of less use to a Paladin than to fighters who can learn to use two weapons. You should still keep this skill near maximum for the day you get a Ghoulbane.
  • Two Hand Slashing - This will be your most effective melee skill for most of your life.
  • One Hand Blunt - You might use this if you don't have a magic weapon by level 15, and you need to use the summoned hammer. Who knows, you might some day get a Holy Water Sprinkler.
  • Two Hand Blunt - I'll tell you to practice this skill, but I'll bet you don't.
  • Bind Wounds - Not worth bothering, as you're going to get healing magic at level 9. YMMV
  • Lay Hands - Make a hot-key. Don't be afraid to use Lay Hands on group members (especially healers).
  • Sense Heading - As a Dwarf you have an innate sense of direction (start with a 50 skill). Make a hot-key and when you reach level 10, get into the habit of pressing the button during combat when you're pressing your Taunt button.
  • Taunt - If you don't learn and use this skill, you won't get invited back into groups.
  • Swimming - You will need this skill, so work on it when you are waiting for the boat.
Level 6
  • Bash - Use this when you are practicing your 1H Slash. When you get a Ghoulbane, you'll be glad you kept up with this skill. It is quite useful when fighting casters, as you can interrupt them as soon as they begin casting.
Level 9
  • Abjure - Cast your buffs.
  • Alter - You will be casting healing often enough to make this rise quickly.
  • Channel - Every spell you cast will drive this skill up.
  • Conjure - If you've already got a magic weapon, ignore this skill.
  • Divinate - Practice this skill up using Flash of Light and Sense the Dead.
  • Evoke - You will want to drop a few pracs here when you reach level 15 to improve your Ward Undead casting.
Level 10
  • Dodge - Spend one practice point then get beat on.
Level 12
  • Meditate - At level 35 you will no longer need to directly use this skill. Instead, you will recover mana at the same rate when sitting.
Level 17
  • Parry - Spend one practice point then get beat on.
Level 20
  • Double Attack - Drop as many points as you have into this skill.
Level 30
  • Riposte - Spend one practice point then get beat on.
Level 40
  • Disarm - If the monster has a fast non-magical weapon (FS dagger, FS rapier, etc...), use this skill to slow its attack rate.

Spells

Always have your best healing spell and buffs memorized. When you are injured, and your HP buff is about to expire, wait for the buff to end (or cancel it) before recasting. You'll get some free healing out of it.

Rank 1 (Level 9)
  • Minor Healing - 10 HPs.
  • Flash of Light - A most underrated spell. Can be used to make a monster run away from you. Just flash them, then step back and they will run along their predefined path. If you are on that path, or come too close to the monster, it will re-engage in melee. Doesn't last very long, but can keep one monster off of you long enough to defeat another (or flee). Even at high levels, this spell can be used as a taunt.
  • Courage - HP and AC.
  • Cure Poison - Can take multiple castings to cure a poison. I don't recall ever using this spell.
  • Spook the Dead - This can save lives when you have bitten off more undead than you can chew.
  • Yaulp - +10 STR, +6 AC for 8 seconds. I stopped using this once I had more spells than spell slots.
Rank 2 (Level 15)
  • Light Healing - 30 HPs.
  • Holy Armour - AC is your friend.
  • Lull - Very useful for pulling from camps. You can lull multiple monsters if you cast and pull quickly.
  • Cure Disease - You'll need this to cure the rabies you get from mummies.
  • Ward Undead - Finally, an effective spell for use in combat! 30+ damage maximum to undead.
  • Sense the Dead - If you're hunting skeletons for bone chips, this could be useful. Otherwise, the only reason to buy it is to practice Divination so that Invisibility won't fizzle so much.
  • Hammer of Wrath - If you don't have a magic weapon by 15, you might want to buy this spell.
Rank 3 (Level 22)
  • Reckless Strength - Useful when you know you are about to go into a fight. Also, this will remove the effects of strength debuffs.
  • Center - When you are injured, and your buff is about to expire, wait for the buff to end (or cancel it) before recasting. You'll get some free healing out of it.
  • Invigor - Restores fatigue. Needed when you are underwater as even with magical help breathing, you can't recover fatigue underwater.
  • Endure Poison - For when you know you're going to fight a mob with poison.
  • Root - This was my favorite spell until I got Stun.
  • Halo of Light - Don't see the use for this, though the lesser races may use it.
  • Invis. to Undead - Keep an eye out for the message "You are starting to appear". It is warning you that your invisibility is about to end. Attacking, looting, casting, or picking something up will end the spell.
 

Rank 4 (Level 30)

  • Spirit Armour - AC is your friend.
  • Hammer of Striking - Don't bother.
  • Healing - 80 HPs.
  • Stun - Extremely useful. With Stun you can turn a tough fight with a caster into a cake walk. With multiple stunners, a hard hitting monster can be killed without ever laying a hand on you. Acts as a taunt, even when it fails. Recovers very slowly, so a lone paladin will have trouble keeping a caster from casting.
  • Soothe - Longer lasting lull.
  • Expulse Undead - 94 damage maximum to undead.
  • Symbol of Transal - A new line of HP buffs. Carry lots of cat's eye agates (2-3gp).
Rank 5 (Level 39)
  • Yaulp II - +20 STR, +8 AC for 8 seconds.
  • Daring - HP and AC.
  • Counteract Poison - Cure poison with one casting.
  • Greater Healing - 200+ HPs. When you get this, you can actually contribute effectively by healing in combat.
  • Cancel Magic - Useful for removing damage shields, charms, etc...
  • Endure Disease - +25 disease resistance. For when you know you're going to fight a mob with disease.
  • Symbol of Ryltan - +HPs. Carry lots of bloodstones (6-7gp).
Rank 6 (Level 49)
  • Revive - Only use this when you can't find a xp ressurect.
  • Valour - HP and AC.
  • Guard - AC is your friend.
  • Holy Might - A new stun spell with 60 point direct damage. Cycle between this and Stun for best results.
  • Calm - Longer lasting lull.
  • Dismiss Undead - 162 damage maximum to undead.
  • Symbol of Pinzarn - +HPs. Carry lots of jasper.

Weapons

Always use the best weapon you can afford!

  1. Get a rusty two-handed sword and get it sharpened. You may need to run to Steamfont to get some sharpening stones if you can't find someone to sharpen it for you.
  2. Get a fine steel two-hander as soon as possible.
  3. The next step up is a shiny brass halberd. Some people like the bone bladed claymore, but the -8 to agility hits dwarves hard in a characteristic in which we are already deficient.
  4. The blackened iron bastard sword from Runnyeye is a next step up.
  5. Giagantic Zweihander from Karg Icebear in Everfrost
  6. The tainted steel bastard sword from undead in Kithikor is better than Ghoulbane against non-undead.
  7. Ghoulbane
  8. Soulfire

Armor

  1. Kill orc pawns in Greater Faydark for cloth armor.
  2. You can get patchwork by turning in Crushbone belts and Legionnaire pads.
  3. Get a scarab helm and boots from the dwarf at the entrance to the Kaladim Warriors guild. The components for both are easy to get.
  4. You can buy leather armor, but if you can, skip it and go straight to banded.
  5. You can buy or learn to make your own banded armor, but if you can, skip it and go straight to bronze. Becoming a blacksmith is very time consuming but will be neccessary later if you want to make your own Armor of Ro.
  6. Get bronze from Unrest as soon as possible. The weight won't bother you.
  7. After that, the different pieces are too many to mention them all, but here are a few (ranking with "better" equipment to the right):
    • Head: Blackened Iron Crown, Ro, Executioners Hood, Skull shaped barbute
    • Face: Grotesque Mask, Platinum Ruby Veil
    • Ears: Golden Amber earring, Diamondine earring, Electrum Saphire earring
    • Neck: Spiked Collar, Gold Black Pearl Choker
    • Shoulders: Bloodstained Mantle, Crested Spaulders, Carnal Pauldrons
    • Chest: Bloodstained Tunic, Charred Guardian BP, Black Enamel BP, Ro, Mithril BP
    • Back: Cloak of the Ice Bear, Lizardscale Cloak
    • Arms: Barbed Armplates, Ro
    • Wrist: Ro, Runed Mithril Bracer, Serpentine Bracer
    • Hands: Dark Mail Gauntlets, Ro
    • Fingers: Silver Fire Emerald ring, Platinum Fire Emerald ring
    • Waist: Thick Leather Apron, Thick Banded Belt, Flowing Black Silk Sash
    • Legs: Barbed Leggings, Ro, Silver-plated Leggings
    • Feet: Ro, Dwarven Work Boots

Where to Go

  • Butcherblock and Greater Faydark (bone chips) [1 to 7-8]

    The orc pawns in Greater Faydark drop cloth armor. The goblins in Butcherblock drop containers.

    Save all of the bone chips you get to turn in at the Kaladim Paladin guild. You can often buy bone chips for around 2sp from merchants near newbie areas. If you do any traveling, check the stores for bone chips. If the four chips for the quest cost you 8sp, you will actually make money on average from the quest, as well as earn experience and faction improvements.

  • Orc Hill and Crushbone (Crushbone belts and Legionnaire pads) [7-8 to 16-17]

    Save all of the belts and pads you get to turn in to Canloe Nusback at the Kaladim Warrior guild. If you can buy the belts from other players for 2 or 3gp each, it will be worth it. Turn them in four at a time for better rewards, usually patchwork armor and rusty two-handed weapons. Once you've done these quests a few times, you'll get better deals from dwarven merchants.

    Start at orc hill, and run to the guards at the orc lift when you are in danger. Don't wait too long, as these guards are not quick to respond to the presence of enemies.

    Move inside Crushbone when you can, and get bound in the tunnel on the Greater Faydark side. Stay away from the train tracks near the zone, and hunt to the left of the entrance to start. Collect everything the orcs drop, and sell it at a merchant. Crushbone can be profitable in both money and experience. Avoid the castle interior when it is full of orcs and you are alone, until you turn 15, when you can use lull to pull an orc or two from a small crowd.

  • Unrest (bronze and bloodstained armor) [12-17 to 25-35]

    At lower levels you can solo in Unrest, and if you stick to the hedge maze you won't ever be bothered by trains. There are three spawn points inside the maze that spawn bugs, TDs, jacks, and ghouls.

    When people complain about the trains in Unrest, I know they

    1. don't have a good group with a smart puller, and
    2. aren't resting in the right place.

    The place to rest and pull to in Unrest is against the wall on the left side of the house, near the porch, between the two trees. If you watch the pathing of the yard trash in Unrest, you will see that they don't come anywhere near the wall between the trees. You can rest there without worrying about trains or yard trash jumping you while meditating.

    Learn how to pull well in Unrest and you find it easier to get a group.

    One thing to remember about pulling though the porch door is don't jump the rail or get too far ahead of the mobs. If you do, they will go back in the house and exit using the back door, pulling the contents of the back room with them.

    When you start pulling from the second floor, always come down the front stairs. There is a bug that can cause the monsters in the house to come out of the house after you without you pulling them. This problem seems to come and go, but when you've got autopull, you'll know it.

    Pulling the bar can pull some of the monsters from the tower as well (and vice versa).

    If you are in a good group, you don't need to be afraid of hags. Just get everyone to melee with her, and she will be unable to cast. A magic resistance buff will help a lot.

  • Cazic Thule, Solusek A, and Splitpaw [25 to 35]
  • Solusek B and Lower Guk (dead side) [33-35 to 45]
