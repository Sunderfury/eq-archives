Editorial Comment - 6

Paladins and Moral Issues - Varrus Aurelius

Greetings all,

I do not like to "rant" much. I love the game and I appreciate all of the things Verant and team goes through every day.  BUT, if I hear from Brad or Gordon once more about how Paladin Quests are moral dilemmas, I WILL SCREAM!!!!!

Unless Verant feels it is acceptable for Paladins to be running around in Steel Armour and wielding Fine Steel 2 Handed Swords, there is a serious issue here.  The name of the game is EverQUEST.  What other choices do we truly have in playing this game?  Aside from being completely worthless at higher levels using an FS blade, or a Ykesha if we can beat back the Rangers looking for one, what choice do we have? NONE.

We *have* to get our Quest Armour to keep pace with the other members in our party.  We *have* to get a weapon that works well and hopefully puts out enough damage to make us feel helpful.  And most importantly, we HAVE to Quest.  That is why most of us purchased this game.  That's right, I CHOSE to be a Paladin, so I would like to Quest as a Paladin, fight as a Paladin and play like a Paladin.

Now the THREE major examples we have to look at are Ghoulbane, Armour of Ro and Fiery Avenger. 

Ghoulbane - To my knowledge, no one has found a Quest that relates to the blade. A Paladin killed the Shin Lord one day and got Ghoulbane, saw the stats, told all his friends and it became one of the the most valuable items in the game.  Suddenly, we realize that Frogloks are not necessarily evil.  They are neutral to good at best.  They hate Trolls, and have allied themselves with the Minotaurs in Lower Guk to destroy their undead counterparts who threaten to overrun their once great Kingdom.  The Shin Lord, one of the Frogloks greatest Paladins, wields the famed Ghoulbane. 

Verant's position : Will you kill a neutral to good creature who would never attack you unless provoked? 

Paladin's viewpoint : Yes, we do not have to kill him.  Yes, an FS Longsword does respectable damage.  Even 2 FS Longswords do respectable damage, of course Paladins do not get dual wield (nor should we).  Ghoulbane is the *only* blade that makes a Paladin worth his/her salt after level 30.   Is it acceptable for a Paladin to wield a Fine Steel Longsword, or a Blackened Iron Bastard Sword until level 40+, at which time an assault on Sir Lucan is possible?  

Solution : Make a Quest, let the Shin Lord give us the blade upon handing in items.  Stop the unnecessary "moral" tactics to cover up bad writing and lack of originality.  The hidden saga of the Frogloks battles and history are great!  Why stop there?  Give us the Quest, give us an opportunity to become part of the story.  OR, give us another blade that requires some half intelligent Quest that allows us to gain a decent weapon without infringing on a "neutral or good" race.

Some would argue that Ghoulbane or your weapon does not "make" or "break" you.  Paladins and ShadowKnights are not given the ability to Dual Wield for obvious reasons.  This makes it necessary for our classes to have a decent one handed slashing weapon.  Many see Paladins as Sword and Shield.  With the removal of Fiery Avenger and Ghoulbane, due to "moral" issues (read: bad writing or ideas).  One of our main weapons would be SoulFire.  That is fine, but it excludes 90% of Paladins from getting it (must be 40+).  Do not give us a good blade, make us work for it.  Give us moral dilemmas, but give us the opportunity to have a choice.  To have it or not at all is not a choice.  I have heard recently that there may be an alternate way of getting Fairy Dust through a Quest in Ak'Anon.  That would be great!  But, we are all dubious to KOS from killing Clockworks in Solusek's Eye for Melatite.  Why do all of the other classes just go to a Merchant and buy their gems?  

Armour of Ro - The Quests for the Molds are quite respectable.  Aside from half being insanely difficult, the Quests are quite fun and challenging.  Again, the ONLY Quest Armour Paladins are capable of getting are linked to a neutral/evil God and require the destruction of neutral/good beings.  How many Paladins worship Ro?  Why would we want Armour linked to a borderline Evil God?  What other choice do we have?  Steel Armour or Planes Armour when we reach 46(when it drops).  That brings us to Ronium.  Kill Clockworks and Fairies.  I don't want to.  No Ro Armour for you.  I will buy it?  No good, you are still condoning the death of innocent/neutral or good creatures. 

Verant's position : Will you kill creatures of a PC race (Gnome Clockworks) or Fairies to obtain a small amount of what you require to make your Armour of Ro?

Paladin's viewpoint : Completely unacceptable.  I don't want to kill Fairies.  Thanks to the Goblins in Solusek's Eye, I was able to purchase full Steel Armour at around level 33+.  Can I be expected to wear this armour until I reach 46+ and try for Planes Armour?  What other items are available that have the AC of Steel and stat buffs of other types?  None. 

Fiery Avenger - The Quest was great fun and I am glad we all came together to work it out!  It was a lot of fun and makes everyone happy thinking about wielding that great blade!  BUT, NO Paladin can receive FA without condoning or participating in the slaughter of Frogloks. 

There are a few more Quests that contain more questionable practices (Test of Truth, Deep Six Cutlass) but I think you get the idea.  Verant smiles smugly and tells us these are moral dilemmas we must decide upon.  I say to Verant, what is left of your game if Paladins refused to do the evil things you want them to? Nothing, why would we play a game that is made to destroy our ideals?  We need the option to complete them a different way in the way you have created them.  A word needs to be said of the other class Quests.  On the whole, these Quests have been great!  Rogue Armour is a little boring, Bard Armour is a little too easy and Warrior Armour was amazing when you released it.  Yes, one of my Companions is a Warrior and we had a GREAT time getting his Crafted Armour.  It left me very excited to work on my own.  

Does this sound like every Quest can and will conflict with some Paladin's views or beliefs?  No.  A little creativity will go a LONG way.  As mentioned previously, MAKE the Shin Lord give us a Quest.  Tell us he is a sick froggie, his leg is gimp and he can't fight anymore.  Retrieve this and this and this and we will give you Ghoulbane to assist all froggy kind in our Crusade!  At least getting three NO DROP items would be more difficult than standing in a mossy room and killing a frog when he spawns.  Armour of Ro?  Umm, it is not difficult to add an NPC to Qeynos, Felwithe, Erudin and Freeport, etc. and have him give the Armour out.  The Blacksmithing component has been removed.  Have a Dwarf standing outside of Groflah's in Freeport.  Hand him the bar of Ronium and the Mold.  He gives you the Armour piece.  And look, it is called the Armour of Marr!  I, a PALADIN, have proved myself worthy to my God, Mithaniel Marr and he has gifted me with his blessings upon my armour.  Is it too difficult to have different names on the same item?  Make Erud Armour, Armour of Prexus, etc etc.  Leave the name Ronium as a Fire God would be suitable for smelting metal. Leave Lord Searfire and the Cauldron component, it is fine.  hehe See?  I solved about half of the problems right there (with fixing the Fairy and Melatite drops on something else or on a Merchant).  And I can even fix that.  Put Melatite for sale in Ak'Anon or Solusek's Eye.  Remove the Fairy Dust component and make it Undead Froglok tongues.  Wouldn't putting some components or acheivements on Undead creatures make a lot of sense?

The Future

What saddens me in all of this, is the complete dependence of material items.  In UO (that was a long time ago) the characters had a personality, or were more reliant on their skills and reputation to make relationships and successful adventuring work.  Now, to clear the air, EQ is a far superior game to UO.  I am glad it is here and it truly is exciting to think about the future.  EQ brought us that future and pleased us all!  But the addition of SOOPR STAT Equipment lends EQ a major flaw.  The removal of "personality" or ability.  In EQ, if you do not have the equipment, you do not measure up to the task.  Sure, you can roleplay and make friends and co-operate and go on Planes raids and help a little.  BUT, you CANNOT assist or be a large force in a group without good equipment.   It is a shame and I hope the day comes when equipment plays less of a role, and the character is more important.  A little UO story that my Companions would surely back up. :-)  I played a Paladin in UO.  Yes, I did not have one offensive spell in my Spell book, did not use Archery(nor do I as a human in EQ) and fought with a Halberd or Sword/Shield.  I was at a SEVERE disadvantage in PvP but worked quite well in PvE situations.  The secret was, I still made a difference, be that interrupting or scaring casters, or taking the hits while my Companions blasted everything they could. :-)  I also made an Archer, right before OSI improved it.  I fought with a Longsword in close melee and used my bow 90% of the time.  Although I was quite useless, I added flavour and fun for my group.  It was quite amusing when a month later, OSI put in the Archery fixes and it became on of the best skills in the game.  But alas, I had retired Connor and was focusing solely on my Paladin.  In EQ, in a group of 6, you do not have room for flavour.  If you are not a contributing member, you are endangering the life span of your group.  I am level 39 right now.  They keep looking at me funny when I put away Ghoulbane and fight with a Shiny Brass Shield and Fine Steel Longsword.  I will NOT use the Mistmoore Shield OR the Shield of the Slain Unicorn.  And I cannot use Ghoulbane because I would have to slaughter what amounts to an ally (Frogloks kill Trolls).

I think a little more clarification on EQ and UO needs to be made.  I think UO gave us much more leeway and choice.  I could choose to be a Paladin, run around with a slow halberd and still be effective.  I like to think of UO as having a better "character" base.  I prefer a class based system (EQ), and Verant delivered a better, faster and more enjoyable game.  What does it lack?  The depth of character.  You are only as good as your equipment.  We can only dream of what the future will hold.  Could you imagine a world with the beauty and magic of EQ, and the character and personality of UO?  Verant! Please let EQ2 be the answer!  MAKE our Quests have alternative solutions, make equipment be an improvement, not a necessity, make our Quests challenging and difficult! Make them have a storyline, a logical outcome and solution.  I know hundreds of Paladins on this site who could give ideas, Quests and dilemmas that would keep us coming back for more!

I am sure you are wondering what my position is in all of this.  I will tell you.  I kill Fairies, I kill Clockworks, I even kill the cute little Gnomes when they aggro on me.  I wield Ghoulbane and get excited every time it procs.  Would I have EVER done those things if Verant had *truly* given me a choice? No.  Now, in history, or RL, we would be faced with real choices, in EQ we are faced with one option, do it and get the equipment or get nothing and do not participate in Paladin Quests. Of course, what is there to EQ for a Paladin without Armour of Ro, Ghoulbane or Fiery Avenger?  Am I less of a Paladin for these practices?  I would argue that in the context of EQ, I am more.  

 

Fare thee well,

Varrus Aurelius
