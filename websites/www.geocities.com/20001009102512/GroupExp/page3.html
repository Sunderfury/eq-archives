Ok, so if I'm that Halfling Warrior, does this mean my group is ripping me off?

At first glance, you might think the answer is yes, and you would be partly correct. It is true that you are receiving a small share of the experience being awarded to the group, and therefore you are leveling more slowly than you would if you went solo. However, how many Halfling Warriors do you know that can solo to level 60? Let's see how this group looks graphically:

Look at Player 2 - that's the Troll ShadowKnight. Looks like he's making off like a bandit, doesn't it? And there's our Halfling Warrior, Player 1, way down there at the bottom, hardly getting anything for his trouble... or is he? Let's take another look, this time looking at the progress each player is making toward his next level:

When you look at the same numbers again as a percentage of xp each member requires to level, the entire group is on a level playing field now. Some of you may have noticed that there seem to be group members missing from the above graph. They are still there, but they aren't visible because some Race/Class combinations present in the group use the same multipliers, and so their curves overlap. But the main thing to take from this graph is this: theoretically, if you put 6 people in a group that each just reached the same level, they will all level again at the same time as long as they remain grouped the entire time. In other words, within a group, each member advances at the same rate relative to their individual xp curve, all other things being equal.
