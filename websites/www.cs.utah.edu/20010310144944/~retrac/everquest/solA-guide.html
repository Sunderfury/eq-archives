Chiangli's Guide to Solusek's Eye (Sol A)

Sol A.  It's been called a monk's paradise, and for good reason.  I spent much of levels 30-40 down amongst the goblin and gnome denizens of this dungeon, picking up fast experience and enough cash to buy two Tink Bags, a FBSS, and other equipment upgrades.  How can you not love a dungeon with amazing loot plus not one but TWO merchants (including one, the goblin merchant, who sells bandages and water - no food)?  I learned a lot about how to handle this dungeon from fellow monks, mostly at Monkly Business, and this guide is my attempt to give something back to the community.  The guide is written assuming you know how to use feign, flop past MOBs, and feign off casters.

Let's start with a map of the entire dungeon.  This map is an annotated version of the one available at EQAtlas, the best EQ map website out there (in my opinion).  On the map, I have added indications of where many of the static and wandering spawns are located.  Pay special attention to the wanderers' paths: some of the best loot in Sol A drops from uncommon wandering spawns (Singe, Kindle, and the CWG Model EXG).  Understanding the wanderers' pathing is also important for handling the barroom effectively. 

Map Key:

The MOB level ranges are from the bestiary at Allakhazam's, another awesome EQ website.
G indicates a goblin (warrior, shaman, or wizard), which come in six varieties:
Young goblin: levels 18-20, mostly found near entrance to Lavastorm
Cinder goblin: levels 20-22
Flame goblin: levels 22-26 (Foreman is 27)
Fire goblin: levels 24-26 (Bartender is 25)
Inferno goblin: levels 26-28 (Torturer is 28, Captain is 35)
Solusek goblin: levels 28-30 (King is 31) - Solusek Mages are Wizards, not Mages
N indicates a gnome: miners are warriors, curators are clerics, and conjurers are magicians.  The only ones that are really annoying to a monk of the appropriate level are the conjurers, because of the damned damage shields.
C indicates a clockwork guardian.  They are named "CWG Model XX", where the "XX" tells you their approximate level (the first letter) and whether they have a special attack (the second letter).  There are also two special ones, Models SX and EXG.
Model Cx: levels 28-30
Model Mx: levels 30-32
Model Xx: levels 30-34
Model xA: regular warrior
Model xB: force module (knockback) - do not fight with back to lava!
Model xC: frost module (knockback) - do not fight with back to lava!
Model SX: spider-like clockwork, level 31
Model EXG: 100% magic resistant, levels 30-35, toughest CWG

Note that the only goblin above level 32 is the Goblin Captain, so you can safely assume that a successful feign will mem wipe any goblins that you have pissed off.  The same cannot be said for the gnomes and clockworks, so be careful -- this may be the first time you have experienced a MOB rushing back to you as soon as you stand up, even if they are well outside of aggro range!

Speaking of aggro ranges, none of the MOBs in Sol A have particularly large aggro ranges, or at least they did not for me (level 30+).  This fact, combined with the fact that both the goblins and gnomes/clockworks are social creatures, makes it relatively easy to pull and fight MOBs to locations where they will not run.  You read that right -- you can survive in Sol A as a monk, despite not having snare, which is normally considered a must-have for dungeon crawls.  I point out locations where I like to pull MOBs that have this "free root" effect in my discussions below.

Finally, as a monk, you should get used to timing respawns.  When I was hunting in Sol A, the respawn time was 19 minutes, but I've read that it changes depending on how many people are in the dungeon.  Time it yourself, and use this information to plan your hunting!  In the long run, when you're pulling Karnor's Castle, say, getting into the habit of timing respawns and adjusting your pulls accordingly is the difference between life and death, so start getting into the habit now.

The rest of this guide is organized as follows.  First I present strategies for handling certain particular rooms: the foreman/predator area, the bar, the goblin high shaman area, and the reckless efreeti pit.  I then present my strategy for optimally "farming"  a series of named spawns, which is really only useful to those monks who have gotten up to around level 40.

The Foreman/Predator Area:
The foreman spawns in a room with three goblins.  The best way to get there is via the secret passages, which are free of MOBs (see below).  The goblins in this room can be young, cinder, or flame, and thus range from levels 18-27.  Pull to the spot marked on the map in the hallway outside the foreman room and feign split them.  That spot is close enough to the goblins in the room with the stairs that your MOB will NOT run, but not so close that they will join in.  After killing the foreman (or placeholder), run up the stairs to find the predator (or PH), who wanders from the ledge to the room with two goblins.  Fight it on the ledge.  It will run to the 2-goblin room when injured, but you can often finish it before it gets there.
The Goblin Barroom:
The bar is a favorite hangout of monks.  The good news is that static MOBs in the area do not aggro on you if you are behind the bar.  However, the two wandering goblins will aggro if you are fighting behind the bar!  The goblins in this area can be fire or inferno goblins (levels 24-28), and can be warriors, shaman, or wizards.  You need to adapt your strategy depending on whether there are any shaman in the area and what kinds of MOBs are wandering.  The drunkard is a mage (read: nasty fire shield) with mediocre loot, so I ignored it.

Basic strategy: Tag the bartender (or placeholder) and fight in the corner marked on the map.  If he gets up on the bar, back away until it jumps down, or you risk aggroing the patrons.

Handling nearby shaman: Shaman heal their nearby friends, even if they won't join the fight.  If the shaman is in an adjoining room, just fight in the opposite corner.  If, however, the shaman is a bar patron, you will need to kill it first.  Do so by aggroing both patrons, pulling to the marked spot, and feign splitting.  Repeat until you get only the shaman and kill it.  Then go back to killing the bartender.

Handling wanderers: If a wanderer passes the door of the bar while you are fighting, it will aggro.  The wanderer that paths down by the goblin merchant only comes by every few minutes, so if you time it you can ignore it.  However, the one that paths to either side of the bar area passes by often, so it interferes a lot.  If it is a warrior, you can just feign when it aggros and resume fighting when it wanders off (several times per fight).  If it is a shaman or (worse) wizard, you need to kill it first (unless are high enough level that you do not care about adds).  This is tricky, given the tight spacing of MOBs in the area.  Since the aggro ranges are level dependent, I'll let you devise your own strategy for taking them out (experiment).

Goblin High Shaman and Lynada the Exile:
This area is tricky.  The goblins are tough (inferno and Solusek), Lynada is a mage (damage shield), and the high shaman is a healer.  After much trial and error and several deaths, I was finally able to solo the area at 40th level.  Attention human monks: The high shaman's Glowing Stone Band imparts infravision!

The key is pulling to the spot marked on the map, where there are no wandering MOBs, you are close to the backdoor to Sol B, and you are out of range of the high shaman's healing.  Single pull the wanderer and two goblins in the outer rooms.  This leaves three MOBs: a Solusek of some kind, Lynada, and the high shaman (or placeholders).  If Lynada is up, sit in the outer room and listen for her casting damage shields: they last five minutes.  I broke the room by hitting her pet, running, and feigning.  The pet will path back to the outer room, while the others will return to the room indicated.  Then, when I timed it so that I knew Lynada's shield was down, I hit the pet with a shuriken and ran to the marked spot.  At 40th level, only Lynada came.  She was easy to take down without her shield and with my MR gear on (MR of 67).  Then it was just a matter of feign pulling the other two, and killing the respawns until I got my ring.  If you bring lots of pumice, you do not need to be quite so careful about timing the shields.

Note: The high shaman WILL run, and at SOW speed, so get ready to chase it down, or move it to near another goblin (but not TOO near) before you fight!

Reckless Efreeti:

The Reckless Efreeti is one of the most cool looking MOBs that I've seen.  He spawns in a pit with two blazing elementals.  Conveniently, the elementals are KOS to all other denizens of Sol A, while the efreeti is indifferent... a fact you can exploit as follows.  Go down the ramp via the secret door and aggro an elemental (or two).  Run back to the goblin area marked and feign.  If the elemental is close, the goblins in the room forget you and attack it.  You may need to bring some more goblins to the fight (e.g., from up in the Captain area) if they are wimpy.  Repeat until all elementals are dead (the placeholder for the efreeti is a third elemental).  Warning: For some reason, it is common for a MOB to not de-aggro on you when you feign here, so you may need to repeat the feign.  I'm not sure why this is.

When only the efreeti is left, you have several choices.  If you brought pumice, dispel his shield and kick his butt (he's a level 27 wimp).  If you have no pumice, hit him with a flying kick and feign.  Repeat until you hit him but do not see a "You are burned!" message -- this means his damage shield has worn off, so get up and kick his butt.  You may need to bind wounds several times before the shield drops.
Ok, so that's how to handle a couple of particularly tricky and interesting areas.  At some point I may add a write-up on how to take down the King area, too, although the loot there is not all that interesting (good XP for those in their mid-to-upper 30s, though, and rarely camped).  What follows are my suggested paths for speed farming Sol A, should you be there when it is empty (something that was common before the XP patch, but is rare now).  Be considerate to lower level groups hunting in the dungeon!  First, do not train them as you run between areas.  Second, defer to groups/individuals who can get XP at a spawn... don't be a jerk.
Farming Sol A:

The following section describes my preferred path for farming several spawns that drop very salable loot as the common drops: the fire goblin bartender, the flame globlin foreman, the kobold predator, Singe, the CWG EXG, the Reckless Efreeti, and Kindle (ok, Kindle's common drop sucks, but his rare is worth 3-4kpp).  These spawns earned me almost 20kpp over ten levels.  To do the entire cycle in 20-minutes, you need to be about 40th level.  If you are higher than that, you could add Gabbie Mardoodle, the Lava Elemental, the Goblin King, the Goblin High Shaman, or more.
Phase 1: My cycle begins and ends in the bar.  See the guide above for how to kill the bartender.  Then, turn on Sneak and head out the bar, watching out for wanderers.

To exit the castle, especially when I was only level 30 or so, I jumped out of the window in the small room, which requires you to duck.  Doing so gets you fairly far away from the two guards at the entrance, which is important at a lower level since they are often infernos..

Take the secret passage out of the castle, which avoids two goblins, then turn right and run to the gnome area.  If you see a goblin at or past the first clockwork, it is the Singe placeholder, so kill it.

Travel through the gnome area and take the secret passages to the foreman area, ganking the foreman and kobold predator (or placeholders) as described above.  The secret passages are particularly useful for lower level monks, since they let you get from the castle to the foreman while passing only one goblin!


Phase 2: After finishing off the foreman and predator (or placeholders), run back through the foreman room into the secret passages.  Sneak/feign through the room up north and head towards gnome country.  Singe paths along here, and so try to pick him off.  To make my life easier later on, I often killed the solo goblin in the room just before the "X".  Pull deep into gnome territory so that you can finish runners off before they get help.  You will need to fight shaman near other goblins, since they run at SOW speed.  A potential complication is that Singe casts a fire-based DoT, which can aggro nearby clockworks if you are too close, and they will kill steal you.  Any goblin wanderer you see along Singe's path is his placeholder.

The other key spawn to kill in this area is the CWG Model EXG or its placeholder.  It is the only wandering clockwork in this area.  Fight it where indicated on the map to keep it away from potential curators (clerics) and to give you plenty of time to kill it before it can get to help when running.  You need to be at least level 35 to be able to take down the EXG very efficiently, even higher if it spawns at a higher level.  Her shield sells fast for 500pp+!
Phase 3: In this phase, we gank the Reckless Efreeti and Kindle.  The guide above describes the best way to get the Efreeti.

Finding Kindle can be tough.   He does not go all the way to one end or the other of his path every cycle.  If you are high level, you can run along his path to find him, ignoring the green gobs beating on you.  If not, clear goblins near one end of this path or the other, and pull any wanderers you see along his path to one of the two marked spots.

Kindle has a nasty damage shield.  If you have Pumice, you're set.  If not, things get tough, since (a) he will wander off when you feign and (b) he will not let you bind wounds in peace.  Bring pumice, or hope his shield drops at a convenient time.
Well, that's all I have to say for now, but I hope it helps.  Monks can get insane XP and plats fighting in Sol A throughout their 30s, starting with the lower level goblins and working their way up through the gnomes and clockworks (the only spawns that give XP by the upper 30s).  It's also great fun.  I hope this helps!
