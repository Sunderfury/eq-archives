Earth Time - 3 / 31 / 00

Hiya Darkpact,

I just wanted to thank you for saying many things that are unspoken but often thought about by players. You are dead on the mark about role-playing. I once commented in OOC that I would love to roleplay but this game forces me not to. Most of the quests are given by npc statues who want very strange things from you and will give you a nickel in return. Reminds me of the Knights of Nih, who want a shrubery. The players have been sucked up into pure capitalist gain like Homer Simpson. "Must have.... drool....Giagantic Zweidlhander!....drool." The history of the game, which I have yet to find except in the silly cut-scene im forced to escape out of each time I log in, is sparse and quickly thought up. No Tolkein here.
Now on to loot, which seems to be what the whole game is centered around, that and power. Power is even more addictive than loot. However, Verant has made certain that you will gain power, and you will gain it painfully slowly. You might as well be watching the hour hand of a clock which moves faster than your level bar. As a result, the immediate gratifacation comes from getting that Ilyandr's hoop, or Langseax of the Wolves. With my Shaman Telor on Veeshan i did the langseax quest about 40 times trying for it even though I couldnt even weild it. You do get the one handed version each time though, and i found that it was giving me great experience for each step of the quest. side note: This is the best Barbarian quest i have done, its fun giving the shirt to Frostbite and then having him lead you to the criminal all the way across WK plains. Sadly, the game is mostly: use your highest character, camp for hours and days, drop stuff on ground for your other characters or sell it for high prices, repeat.
I think more no drop quests/items should be created. My shaman armor quests were fun because i had to do each part myself (well i did loot a griffon feather from someone elses kill). Then i had to turn them in myself and get the reward. It was close to roleplaying cause its just me the NPC and my items. Oddly enough however the shaman armor pieces are NOT no drop! I see them for sale all the time at very high prices. I honestly dont know what Verant is thinking sometimes.
What if everyone had their own stuff? I have a mage and he cant wear diddly on his chest. Its robes or nada. I'd like to see more of that, and more no drop stuff. This would keep someone, even myself, from camping an item they cant even use and thus removing the profit from doing so. Auctioning and trading are fun, but its gone way too far. When i go to the Freeport bank, its crazy what you can buy and even crazier the prices. If you have a high level character, you can just go to Freeport and buy everything you need for your new character. Strange but true. And I not sure its all that bad because of the rarity of money and items in the game, staring a new character is a long process. I guess Im of 2 minds about twinking.
I've been playing about a year now and to fight the boredom i create a new character. Like you I tend to not like the game as I gain in power. leveling becomes tedious, loot rare to impossible, and players turn into something worse than a day-trader on Wallstreet. The funny thing that most people dont realize is that having the best stuff doesnt raise your power all that much. A newbie will still get killed frequently even with that shiney blue stuff.
Have you heard of MiddleEarth? Its was supposed to be a Tolkein based ultima online like game, centered on role playing. I couldnt wait to play it till I read that they sacked their whole programming dept. and are starting over. Who knows when and if it will be out now. This game had permanant death and magic would be very rare in the game as in Lord of the Rings. Also you could play an Orc if you wanted and not too many elves would be allowed. I think the link is on EQvaults page if you are interested.

-Telor, and Sareb on Veeshan
