May 14, 2003  --  Today's Patch Message

Ooh! Lots of interesting changes in this patch! To start with, the updated version of the Plane of Hate is now live on all servers! Verant claims they kept the old zone charm while improving the geometry and some encounters such as Innoruuk (no longer a joke to fight against, they say!). Veeshan's Peak has also seen some changes, making it possible to enjoy with small raid forces. The gate restriction has been removed from the zone as well (previously, one could not gate out). A new and highly anticipated dungeon zone, Veksar, is now open on Stormhammer. The entrance to this ancient Iksar city can be found in the Lake of Ill Omen (and I do mean, in the lake!). Hopefully it will hit all servers very soon.

Some cleric highlights from official patch report:

Divine Intervention Fix - Players have been reporting that Divine Intervention was not updating the hit points for the character every time when cast, causing characters to die even though the spell seemed to have saved them. We have been unable to reproduce the issue specifically, but we have made some changes that should fix the problem. Please use /bug with details of the incident if you continue to see this problem.

Healing Messages now include the name of the healer - In order to make it clearer who has healed you, we have changed the message that you receive when healed. It will now include the name of the character casting the healing spell: "Bob has healed you for 300 points of damage”.

Mark of Kings upgrade - In order to make this spell more appealing then its lower level counterpart, Mark of Karn, we have: increased the duration from 3.5 to 4 minutes, reduced the casting time from 3 to 2 seconds, reduced the mana cost from 170 to 75 and made the spell slightly less resistible.

Mana from Buffs - We have increased the maximum amount of mana that can be gained through the use of buff spells.

April 16, 2003  --  Your Own EQ Army

Some may have noticed that the official Everquest website has been "teasing" us over the past 24 hours. Today an announcement has been made for yet another addition to the EQ library of games. Sony plans to release Lords of Everquest later this year. LoE is a strategy game that appears to be quite similiar to Warcraft -- using armies to build and conquer. The game will allow solo play as well as online interaction. As usual, this site will not stray from the course of EQ1, but thought this info was worth mentioning!
