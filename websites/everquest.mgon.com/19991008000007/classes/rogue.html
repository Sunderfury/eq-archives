Rogue

Mykil's View
A lone warrior is making the dangerous trek through a thick forest on the way to his guild hall. Bedecked in a suit of leather armor and brandishing a combine long sword and kite shield, most fauna that would even consider challenging him decide to stay in the relative safety of the forest. The corpses of monsters that attacked and decided not to flee mark the dirt near where his boots have trodden. Suddenly, the flutter of a cape and the rustling of bushes alerts the warrior to danger, and he turns to face a bush that is shaking without the aid of the wind. He advances on the bush until he is close enough to touch the leaves, and in one swoop of his sword he cleaves the entire hedge off of its roots, finding nothing. Then, an unseen assailant from behind rams a long dagger through the back of his leather armor, and pain is known as it pierces skin, and severs the brave warrior's backbone in two. The assailant flees back into the darkness from whence he came, and the last things the warrior feels are the claws of some unseen creature stripping the skin off of his head, piece by piece.

Such are the ways of the rogue. Dweller of the shadows, a traveler must always be on the alert, for a rogue could be just behind you, hiding behind that tree. Even in town, the guards cannot protect you from what they cannot perceive. Masters of sneaking, rogues can slip through the gates under the watchful eyes of any guards, and get close enough to you to either stick a dagger through your back, or poison your next meal. Some don't even need to get close, as being masters of thrown weapons and archery, all they need is a clear shot, and you are done for. When in a large crowded area, count two or three rogues for every one you might chance to notice, and keep your purse under close guard. This isn't to say all rogues are "evil." The followers of Bristlebane, for example, steal and kill for the fun and sport of it. I wouldn't trust them, but they can be valuable allies or dangerous enemies. It all depends on which they find to be more fun. The followers of Innoruuk and Cazic-Thule, on the other hand, are not to be trusted. They hate everyone and everything, and do their works for the sole purpose of inspiring pain and repression. The only ones they treat with any remote sort of respect are other followers, and their common deity is the only thing that inspires this loyalty. Never trust these villains, as they will kill you quicker than they would kill their own (which has happened in the past mind you, and with an unsettling frequency I might add).

All that involves the discrete is the domain of the rogue. Backstabbing, sneaking, hiding, pickpocketing, lockpicking, and a score of others are trademarks of the rogue, with poisoning being the staple of the assassin (which no good rogue should be without). Rogues are, by nature, wanderers. In order to do any business at all, their reputations must be improved. They are not as badly off as necromancers, but are surely not too far away from the same social state either. Still, finding merchants who will do business with you is a task, and at times finding a merchant with food to sell you is a trying task. But that is a small price to pay, and improving social status is but a small task as your powers advance in ranks. Besides, necromancers can do it and their arts are even more vile than yours by the standards of the populace. Just remember, when traveling, try to be as discrete as possible when leaving any public place, and you just might evade the rogue who has its sights on your purse and your life. . . . .but then you can never be too sure of that, can you?

Bottom Line
Living as a rogue can be hard at first. Living as a rogue can be easy later, but you know the saying "life is what you make of it?" That applies here. Depending on how you play, the overall life of a rogue can be easy or hard. Both ways are rewarding, as both offer rich roleplay and gameplay opportunities. Roleplaying a good character will (hopefully) incur good feelings on the parts of good NPCs and players. Good treasure hunters, bounty hunters, scouts, and other good character roleplaying can lead to a good and easy life, as the populace will respect you and your skills (despite their feelings about them). This is the most unhindered path to riches and fame, which is what rogues in lore generally tend to work towards. If you are daring, and willing to take the bumps and bruises of a harder yet even more fun path, the rogue makes an excellent evil character. With a good lockpicking and sneaking rating, you can go anywhere and take anything before anyone knows your whereabouts. For maximum efficiency at being evil, try one of the evil races.

The beautiful and elegant yet unfortunately destitute of feeling dark elves make an excellent choice, as their ability to see at night makes is almost second to none. The perfect rogues they do not make, but the perfect evil rogues are one of their many callings. You can, if you wish, of course roleplay a good dark elf, for an interesting twist on a common theme, and see what happens. You can learn weaponry skills comparable to those of the warrior, making any warrior think twice before ending your career. You can move quicker than the monk by sneaking and taking advantage of the shadows, effectively being able to get behind the monk and end its life. You can easily go one - on - one with either of these classes. Mages are harder to dispose of. You can't evade that which cannot be missed, and spells tend to fall into that category. But with a little tact, strategy, and good old sneaking and hiding, you can get behind that mage and end its life before it gets through releasing any of those spells. Rogues are the masters of the discrete alongside survival (which is necessary because of their social status), and therefore must operate in such ways. The shadows are you home, your knife is your friend, and your cunning is your lifeline. Treat all well and all will lead you down the road of success. Neglect any of these things, and expect to be lynched by a rather irate group of peasants you might have swindled.

The skills of a rogue
The following does not constitute a complete list of skills for this class. As we learn more skills of higher levels will be added.

Skill Name Effect
   
Pick Locks Enables rogue to open locked doors and items (unsure of whether lockpick is necessary)
Sense Traps Detect presence (and location but unsure) of traps
Disarm Traps Disarm traps
Sneak Can move without being noticed (rogue invisibility) but cannot interact with environment in anyway besides movement
Hide Conceals location and presence, movement reveals location and presence and ends effect
Pick Pockets Relieve target of some wealth
Backstab Powerful rogue attack, requires rogue to be behind target, piercing must be primary weapon skill
Safe Fall Ability to fall from high ledges with minimum damage
Poison Skills Ability to poison weapons and certain other items ("Ugh. Must've been that apple I ate.")
Disarm Relieve target of armed weapon
Instill Doubt "Instill doubt" in the mind of foe, causing it to run or at least think twice before hitting you (high level skill)
