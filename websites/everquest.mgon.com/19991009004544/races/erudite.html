Erudite

Mykil's View
The first splitting of the human race was, alas, not meant to be the last. In the beginning, all humans roamed the frozen wastes of northern Antonica, surviving by their wits and their weapon arms. Frost giants were a constant threat, but by far not the least of their troubles. Few knew of mysteries that lurked beneath the ice and snow, and none knew exactly what they were, for none among them were foolhardy enough to venture too far away from Halas, the barbarian city. Barbarians always knew the difference between stupidity and honor, and venturing into uncharted regions of the frozen lands in search of adventures that might not even exist was foolishness. The few who had ventured out had almost never returned, and those that did merely returned with stories of more ice, snow, and monsters beyond the ken of those the barbarians generally knew. And so, for many ages, barbarians remained isolated in their city, venturing out only so far away from the safety of their walls, honing their fighting skills in the names of their respective clans and tribes.

Yet some of these "barbarians" grew restless. They tired of constantly fighting and defending the city from frost giants, sometimes at the cost of their very lives, when nothing was to be gained. They decided that they would prefer to best the frozen wastes in search of new lands and new opportunities. Some of the clan chieftains objected, but eventually these "barbarians" were not to be denied. And despite the predictions of these same chieftains that objected that those who left would die horribly in the wastes, the newly born "human" race survived the journey to see better climes, and to their credit built two great port cities: Qeynos and Freeport. As time passed, these "humans" lost some of the strength that was inherited from their barbarian ancestors and, while still remaining relatively strong, gained some intelligence and wisdom from the new things that they learned during their time away from their northern relatives. Yet some humans grew uncontented with their situations again. As word of the grandeur of the human cities spread, creatures from far off lands, many with magical abilities, visited the human lands to forge alliances and show off their powers. Some humans grew uncontented with the meagre increases of magical knowledge that were to be gained simply by watching these visitors, and so another split was made. These humans sailed to the island of Odus, where they founded Erudin, home of the erudites. They dedicated their lives to the study of magic, and eventually forfeited nearly all of their abilities with weapons in exchange for nearly unsurpassed magical prowess among the mortal races. And thus, the high men, or "erudites" as they prefer to be called, were born.

Erudites have come to be renowned as magic users of all types. Their magical abilities matched only by the high elves of distant Faydwer, the erudites dedicated their lives to the discovery, study, and categorization of all types of magic. They played a great part in the formation of the magical displines of clergy, wizardry, enchanting, summoning, and the forbidden magic, necromancy, as well as identifying different magics as being offensive, defensive, or among the other various types of spells. Erudites have also spent much of their time around artifacts of various levels of power, learning about the characteristics of such objects and their respective applications. Their libraries are chock full of information on such things, recorded from various different times by the hands of various great teachers and researchers in their society. Patterning their city out of white marble, its beauty is only enhanced by the magic employed by its inhabitants as part of their everyday lives. Respected for the art and beauty of their spellcasting, erudites always draw the attention of some people in a crowd who enjoy their colorful displays of power. The only art that is frowned upon is necromancy. Necromancers are the outcasts of erudite society, but do not mock them, lest ye find yeself as one of their undead servants. Erudites of other disciplines are not to be taken lightly either, as their magics will make you regret it.

Bottom Line
An erudite will stand well as any type of pure magic caster. Be it a magician, wizard, enchanter, cleric, or even a loathed necromancer, erudites excel in all things magical, and even make decent paladins. This great power comes at a great price, as erudites must be limited to inhabiting the rear lines of a battle. Confined to casting their spells from the relative safety of distance, if an erudite gets to close to any marauding creature, its life is very short. Erudites are not renowned for their melee or endurance qualities, and therefore would not last long in a direct physical confrontation. Unable to wear metal armor of any kind, erudites are limited to cloth and sometimes leather protection in general, and are only capable of carrying staves or other blunt weapons, which though powerful, still do not deal much harm in the hands of even a high level erudite. An erudite character has many roleplaying options available to it. In constant search of knowledge, erudites can go on quests for artifacts of mythical power, or tomes of ancient mystical lore to be studied. Some can be on a search for new lands, with new creatures to be observed and recorded in their archives, and new areas to stretch their influence to. Others, depending on their calling, have different quests. Clerics can go on vendettas against the undead, even challenging to wade into the murky depths of the holes such creatures inhabit, melting their carcasses with its holy light. Erudites can also be heretical, choosing to go the route of the necromancer, which can lead to great power as well as great disdain by other, more respectable, classes, despite their intentions. Your path is up to you, but whatever path that might be, as an erudite, the path is always to further magical enlightenment. Yours is to study what you see, and record what you study, and contribute your findings to the libraries to further the intellectual glory of your homeland, Erudin.
