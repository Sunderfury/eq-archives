Warrior

Mykil's View
Great armies cannot survive without them. They are the ones who first civilized the land we now inhabit. This order still holds the law of war next to their hearts: to civilize is to prove dominance over. Warriors, in all their glory, are a pride and joy of many towns and cities. As guards and watchment they protect the citizens of Norrath from the evil things that lurk beyond the gates. They ensure that the peace is maintained in the cities, and destroy without mercy whatever seeks to encroach upon their land. This is the class that bears the closest resemblance to their ancestors who were created and carved out their cities. Very few races have totally abandoned this order, and the majority of fighters in the world belong to this brotherhood.

Modern day warriors take pride in their mastery of weaponry. Able to use any weapon and armor, a fully armed warrior can be the nightmare of any foe foolish enough to face it. These people don't believe in what they see. They have to feel what they do to make sure it is done, and they are adept at this. Magic is too abstract for them to understand. Those that are found to have an aptness for the arcane and magical, either through act or through occurence, are quickly sought out by the various warrior-magi brotherhoods. Some paladins, rangers, shadow knights, and even some shamans were all originally warriors, but were found to have a natural affinity for magic, and thereby inducted into whatever order affected them most. The corrupted became shadow knights. The pure became paladins and the nature minded became rangers. Shamans are usually marked at birth, but should a warrior exhibit any direct connection to the spirits that govern their savage societies, they are quickly trained in the ways of the spirits to supplement their fighting skills.

Many brotherhoods for warriors exist in Norrath. There is no one big "union" of warriors available to be called by whoever needs them. Exclusive town militia groups and guilds exist. Companies of mercenaries, hunters, and bounty hunters are also known to be around, if one knows where to find them. Although fighting styles may vary, all of them encourage and train their warriors to spill as much blood with as many weapons in as little time as possible. Some warriors are loners, having alienated themselves from mainstream warriors or having been banished from a respectable order for some crime committed. But warriors are everywhere. If you can't find any, ask someone if a war is going on somewhere. The battlefield is the home of all warriors, young and old. Find a battlefield, and you will find warriors carrying out the ancient arts used by their ancestors in ancient times.

Bottom Line
The ways of life for a warrior can be varied. Militia men, guards, nomads, hunters, knights, and many others are basically all warriors. Their extreme stamina and abilities with weapons and armors enable them to be very versatile and extremely adaptable. Should they cross into a region where a specific type of armor is needed that they possess, they can slip it right on and continute on their way. If monster that reacts only to specific types of weapons (piercing skeletons can be a tad useless, since nothing is their to pierce), the warrior need simply equip another weapon (maces smash them skulls real good) and be on his or her way. To be a successful warrior, you must remember three things. First, your weapons are your friends. Second, mages are not weaklings. Never insult a mage, as they can all heal you and strengthen you to survive a critical battle, or they can turn you into a block of ice or a burnt cinder without the possibility of recourse. Third, keep your rage in check. If you lose control of your rage, you lose half the battle to the enemy, and then it is only a matter of time, unless your deity is with you, before you lose your life or flee.

Hand - to - Hand Ability in unarmed combate, cannot be mastered by any class except monks
