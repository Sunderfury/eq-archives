Wizard

Mykil's View
Magic has always existed. Since the beginning of time, it has permeated everything in existence with its ever present power, bathing it in a special irredescence that only a few people could perceive. As the centuries passed, these people came together, drawn by a hunger for knowledge of a common power, which at that point had no definition. These people, in the beginning, were good and wise, and the discovery of these secrets went smoothly. Definitions were found for the power, catalogued, and subsequently studied in order to find ways into the deeper characteristics of what they craved to know so much about. Different paths yielded different results, and nearly all were fruitful. At that time, magic was still one entity. The people had not grown powerful with it enough (or fearful of it enough) to assign magic to "houses." But that was the next step.

The wizard is one who studies the knowledge and lore of the destructive magics. Skilled in all the arts of causing magical damage, it is ill advised to anger a wizard, and even more so to get yourself permanently hated by one of their power. If anything, when faced with death at either the hands of a warrior with two bastard swords or a full powered wizard, run for the warrior. Being cleaved into several pieces while conscious is a very painful experience let me assure you. But the warrior is more inclined to be quick about it. A wizard is more inclined to burn off your skin first, which is a slower, and more painful, process.

Magicians are the only magic users who can stand against a wizard in direct combat and have a real non-luck oriented chance of winning, and that chance is slim. The ability of a magician to raise a fire elemental from the earth, whose strength and stamina and other vital statistics are not predetermined, to keep a wizard busy long enough is always in question, and whether it can be raised in time is even more questionable. In terms of raw destructive power, the wizard outdoes any other magic using class, and direct combat with one is not the road to long life. While lacking in healing spells, the wizard has a wide array of other spells available to make up for them. High powered wizards can teleport to far off places at a whim, effectively being able to evade situations in the rare cases when they get overwhelming. As they climb the ranks, their offensive power will never cease to improve. Some spells gain potency (as I understand it), and new spells, including vast and powerful area effect spells, become available. Wizards are renowned for these, and these are the spells that have made many a wizard legendary. But what happens when a wizard tires of its powers and wants more? The last one who actually went about satisfying its desires, a man whose name is rarely uttered, disappeared long ago.

Bottom Line
The wizard character is excellent for you powermongers out there. If positioned well, a lone wizard, even at low levels, can inflict the damage of a small group on a creature. It gets three direct offensive spells at the start, one being a radius spell, which are both facts no other magic user can boast to have. With all of this raw power, it is easy for wizards to advance quickly in early levels, being able to take out groups of creatures with its radius spells. The only limitations on a wizard are the characteristic limits that are imposed on other magic users - low stamina and can't wear armor beyond the cloth types - as well as an all too apparent lack of healing spells. No one is impressing anyone with the healing powers of the wizard. A wizard can serve as a makeshift enchanter in a scrunch, but only makeshift. It has some spells of this sort which make him useful as a backup character, but the only place where a wizard really shines is far beyond the front lines, at maximum range, raining firey (or icy, or whatever as it depends on its mood. :) ) death upon the enemy(s). As a wizard, you are the warrior of the magic users before the hybrids. Master of all forms of purely offensive magic, you can inflict devastating damage in the least amount of time. Don't be afraid to bombard the battlefield with your power (unless you stand of chance of hurting your friends). It is your job to inspire awe in the minds of the enemy of your power, and while they are transfixed by the display, it is your job to end their lives with your torrents of destruction.
