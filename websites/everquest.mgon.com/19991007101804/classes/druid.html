Druid

Mykil's View
The druid specializes in natural magics. In the outdoors, a confrontation with a druid can be deadly, as their power is drawn from the very land that both he/she and you, if you haven't noticed by now, are standing on. They can have their woodland creature pets attack you, or they can directly call upon the forces of nature to smite you. If you attack an animal while in the presence of a druid, and especially if the animal happens to be that particular druid's pet (although it probably makes no difference, god forbid anyway), the druid will more than likely make you pay with either your life, or a good chunk of your health before you manage to get away.

Druids are masters of nature where water flows, air blows, and the sun shines. However, if removed from their element - say they went dungeon or cave exploring with a group - their effectiveness as part of the group would be limited. Some of their spells are still of great value, but when removed from their natural habitat (the above ground where grass can grow, etc.) their total value is reduced drastically. Such is the weakness of the druid.

I personally feel that a person with a druid character would have an excellent array of roleplaying choices before him/her. You could play a guardian protector, exacting revenge on those who attack animals you proclaim as under your protection. You could serve as an animal tamer due to your ability to charm animals. The choices are endless. Also, if you wish to go solo, stick with this class for a while and once you are able to summon up food and drink, you are pretty much set as long as you remain above ground. Join a group if you want to explore a dungeon, because your effectiveness is reduced.

Bottom Line
The druid is an excellent class choice despite its weaknesses underground. If you wish to roleplay a dungeon explorer or spelunker or other underground roaming type, unless you are very adventurous (as I am so I might still try this), this character is not for you. If you want a character type rich in roleplay potential above ground and, after a while, can go truly recluse if you chose, give the druid a shot.
