Halfling

Mykil's View
Halflings are a strange race. Not quite so mysterious in the exact nature of their origins as half elves, halflings are believed to be one of the original races of Norrath. This race is the smallest in all of Norrath. Lacking the girth of dwarves and their gnomish relatives, this small race is often hard to see from the viewpoint of a taller race, such as barbarians. Their lack of physical size does not hamper this little race, as they are extremely dextrous, and can be successful in all of their small endeavors.

The actual history of the halfling goes back far beyond recorded history. The only proof of these eras is ancient lore and folk tales passed down from generation to generation in halfling families, and in general they never suspected these "house tales" to be true. Yet many wisemen, including some halflings among them, have suspected for quite a long time that these stories were more than mere myths and folk tales. Tales of ancient halflings, or "hobbits" - jolly little creatures who walked without shoes and prided themselves on their ability to enjoy stability, be relatively unadventurous and lazy, sometimes having a natural hiding ability, and hating change - are very often found in halfling libraries, and to a lesser extent those of Erudin and private owners. One of the more popular tales, written by a long dead writer known only as "Tollkeen" whose very existence is held in serious doubt, told of an unusually adventurous hobbit, who made a name for himself as a "burglar." Although the concept of "Tollkeen" is regarded by some as a hoax, the fact that modern day halflings are very much like the adventurous hobbits in his stories cannot be denied or disproven. They are all a very adventurous and jolly people, and some prefer to walk without shoes for reason of personal comfort. Even more, many halflings are proficient thieves, much like the "burglar" in his famous story. Yet the debates continue, and the exact origins of halflings are still held in doubt.

One thing is for certain, and that is no matter what their origins might be, halflings are a strong people. Although small and not quite as big as the other smaller races, they are still relatively strong, and faster as well. Being hard to see, a skilled halfling can easily dodge most blows in its general direction unless they are aimed with the utmost of accuracy. Their size allows them to go relatively unnoticed by all but the most observant of people who are accustomed to examining the entirety of their environment, not limiting their scrutiny to their line of sight. This, paired with their speed, has lead many a halfling to the life of the thief. Always enjoying a good laugh and suited to life as one of Bristlebane's followers, this is not totally surprising. They also make good druids and clerics, as they are a very wise people, and are knowledgable in the ways of magic. Even warriors, who cannot use any magic themselves whatsoever, understand what magic is and does on a deeper level than most other warriors. An enlightened and strong people, halflings are not to be overlooked. They might be small enough to be squashed flat under the foot of a clumsy troll or ogre, but their size is not an indicator of their power. If you underestimate one and are foolish enough to do battle with one, you might find yourself bested by your tiny nemesis. If confronted by a group of the little people, I must say, you certainly have a load of small problems on your hands.

Bottom Line
This tiny race is not to be ignored by the wise would be adventurer. Small and easy to overlook, halflings can venture into places that bigger races can't access relatively easily. They are not quite as stout as dwarves or gnomes, and live in the forests, but are a very formidable people despite their outside appearance. They make dependable warriors, clerics, and druids, and excel as theives, as their small size and speed gives them the advantage of being able to get in and out of a place quickly and without being noticed. Larger races cannot claim to be able to do this, unless by night, and even then they are more easily noticed. Halflings are a great race to roleplay, especially if you like being a fun loving person. Although their natures would make them seem suited to the service of Bristlebane, no matter what their deity, they are still the jolly little people of Norrath. A halfling is seldom evil, but such things have been told of in the old tales, and can very well happen. Time is like a river. If the stories hold truth, anything told in them can definitely happen again, and without warning. Naturally curious, it is not surprising to find halflings everywhere. A very amable race, they are liked all over Norrath, and are even tolerated by the more inhospitable races because of their ever present sense of fun and merriment. As a halfling, prepare to have a very fun life. Your opinion on life is positive, and you enjoy fun in its many forms, always willing to take the extra step for a little bit of amusement. You generally bring joviality with you wherever you go like a brother, as you will always get a smile out of someone who finds amusement at your diminutive yet stocky appearance. Use this to your advantage, as it is harder to kill an enemy that isn't busy laughing at you than killing one that is.
