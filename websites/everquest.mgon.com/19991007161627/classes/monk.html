Monk

Mykil's View
As you may have noticed by now, all of the other classes tend to specialize in at least one sort of weapon. Warriors specialize in the arts of nearly every weapon conceived. If its a weapon, give it to a warrior, and he/she will show you how its used. Rogues' forte' is the use of piercing weapons, so watch your back when near one, lest you be stabbed with one of the vile things Magic users tend to prefer staves to defend themselves, although a couple of the more physical types, such as shamans, can use axes. The hybrids, such as paladins, rangers, and shadow knights, are nearly as adept in the arts of melee as warriors, though frighteningly powerful in the arts of magic as they gain strength. Now, some might argue this point I am about to make, but there are no classes in this game that do not specialize in at least one weapon. The class that is NOT the exception to this rule, is none other than the monk.

The body of the monk is a weapon in and of itself. With a naturally occuring armor rating that increases as the monk gains levels, this class literally, and in most cases definitely, is not meant to wear armor. It can wear leather armors, but think about what you spend your money on before you make your purchase. Would you rather spend your gold on a cheap piece of armor that probably isn't that good? Or would you rather, as an adventurer, spend that gold on a fighting skill?

As a weapon, the monk is its own weapon. Being the only class that

can truly master hand to hand combat, and the only class besides a necromancer with Feign Death available, a strong monk's body can be as deadly - nay, deadlier - than the strongest blade ever forged, and when you think about it, those blades can be pretty strong. That is another area where the monk excels: it can operate without the added weight of a weapon, leaving more room on its person to haul loot across the countryside. This means more money made, more money saved, and more money to spend boning up on fighting skills and rations. And later, if you stick with it, the monk gets special attacks that other classes only dream about, like the Dragon Kick. And another thing to keep in mind about those special attacks: they count as magic. So once you gain a (rumored) certain level when your hand - to - hand attacks become magical (if the rumor is true), you can go out and hurt those pesky magic weapon only monsters. :)

Bottom Line
The monk is an excellent character to play. Although its hit points are lower, and it can only be human, it is arguably as powerful and effective in melee combat as the warrior. With its mastery of hand - to - hand combat, and the ability to use special attacks on par with some of the more powerful magics later on, the monk is a deadly foe to face. It is an awesome melee class and at least one should be in every group, as its strength and agility and stamina (fabled to be second only to the warrior in most cases) allow it to last for a long time. Its lack of need for heavy armors and weapons - or anything of that sort at all really for that matter - allow it to act unencumbered in battle and in travel. Roleplay is an excellent factor in this character as well, as being a very religious lot, monks can claim to be on a spiritual quest for perfection of body, mind, and essence. One could roleplay being a traveling martial artist, training aspiring monks to be better fighters and practicing their own arts on monsters along the way. If you feel like walking around in full combine plate armor, brandishing that gigantic combine bastard sword, you will not like the monk's apparent lack of need for material tools of war. Martial artist advocates, and people who like the idea of a powerful character with low expenses that is fun to roleplay (without much in the way of weapons and armor, remember that) will find playing a monk to be a wholely enjoyable experience.

The combat moves of a monk
The following does not constitute a complete list of skills for this class. As we learn more skills of higher levels will be added.
Skill Name Effect
   
Hand - to - Hand Basic attack form; not as potent as many weapons in the beginning, but hits connect more often; Rumor is that at a high level, monk punches become magical and can take out magic weapon only creatures
Kick Basic beginning monk kick; same as above, but no rumors regarding this becoming magical
Round Kick First special attack of the monk; gained at 5th level; does about as much damage as your punches at this point, but punches occur faster
Tiger Claw Low striking punch; available at level 10; deals nearly the same damage as a round kick but recycles faster
Dodge Once basics are trained, it activates automatically in combat; if successful, oponent misses
Block Similar to dodge; gained at level 12
Dragon Punch Special attack
Eagle Strike Special attack
Flying Kick Devastating attack that may be trained at level 30; can hit for as much as 70 hp in one attack alone
