Barbarian

Mykil's View
They were the first of the human races to walk the earth. Forced to survive in the wilds of nature, their abilities adapted to meed the demands of living in the loveless frozen wasteland that is the North. Some left, in search of warmer, and more hospitable climes. Some got through, and other died in the trek, but most stayed in the ramshackle town of Halas, now the capital of the frozen lands of men. The barbarians haven't conqured the environment, and never will, but have mastered its rules of survival. And that is what barbarians excel at: survival.

The barbarian race is the simplest of the of the three human races. Having never left the frozen north until now, these people are the strongest of the humans. They excel as warriors, and even their mages can swing a club with relative proficiency, although this paired with their low inteligence only allows them to gain the skills to be simple tribal shamans and witchdoctors. Having survived in the frozen wastelands so long, they are a clever lot as well despite their low intelligence, so finding a rogue among them is not entirely out of the question. In fact, it is quite common.

Barbarians can be a nomadic people. They are found all over the world, as some explore vast areas and travel greatly, returning home when they have amassed fortunes in money, jewels, and weaponry. Weaponry is prized above all, as one can't kill a creature with money, and good weapons are needed to drive off the frost giants that inhabit the north and constantly threaten their race. Some of them worship specific deities for their powers, but most of them worship tribal spirits of what they consider to be awesome power. Simple healing is something not taken for granted in barbarian societies. Simplistic and strong, barbarians have survived centuries of hardships in the north. Do not underestimate or anger one, for if you do, you will see first hand why barbarians are such efficient warriors.

Bottom Line
Warriors are excellent classes to play, because of their versatility, and barbarians make excellent warriors for the same reasons. As said, even shamans can serve as warriors in a pinch, and rogues are known fight well when they are barbarians. All have excellent roleplay possibilities, due to the roleplay versatility of the barbarian. Being a tribal people, barbarians are much like what we would call natives. They can be nomadic, moving from place to place in search of game and challenges. They could be explorers of towns and dungeons for their barbarian brethren, or on a quest of manhood for recognition in the respective tribe. The possibilities are vast. Being a big race, they have the ability to slam opponents, and possibly stun them. Their only set back is, without the aid of light or special vision, their night vision is totally hindered. Shamans get spells that fix this, but without these, barbarians are blind in the dark. But don't let that discourage you. Barbarians make excellent characters, as their simplicity makes them easy to roleplay and their strength and stamina make them survivors. So pick up your weapon, go out into the wilderness, and prove yourself to your tribe and bring honor to your people.
