Necromancer

Mykil's View
The necromancer is a departure from standard magic users. In general, magic users are regarded as people who command the elements, perform illusions, can summon animals, heal people without the use of a bandage (or a stretcher and other high tech medical tools), and attack people using pure energy. Very few fantasy role-playing environments conceived during modern times have entertained the necromancer class. The only known rpgs to have used this class in real abundance are old (and some new) D&D and AD&D board games, and even moreso, pencil and paper RPGs. In Norrath, necromancers have their own societies, and are a powerful force to contend with, and are one of the few, if not the only, considered "purely evil" classes in the game.

Necromancy is, quite simply, the art of divining the dead with new life. The very word means "divination throught the invokation of the dead." The true nature of necromancy is much more complex, spanning all that is and involves death. That being the case, the necromancers have the power to inflict diseases and poisons, to the ability to use exert almost vampiric power over their victims, to the ability to finally raise the dead from the grave, granting them with a new single minded zeal to do their new master's bidding, despite the results of their actions. A surprising point that must be made is that a necromancer can exhibit almost clerical powers. Although most of these come in the form of siphoning spells where the afflicted's stamina is sapped and temporarily added to that of the caster, the skilled necromancer has the ability to undo his diseases and poisons if necessary.

The power to raise the dead is a very valuable skill to have. The fledgling necromancer can attack and defeat creatures above his level with his skeletal and ghoulish pets. Unfortunately, this power comes at a terrible price. As the necromancer, alongside the magician (who gets its first summons at rank 2, and you can start right in during rank 1!) , can rise to power extremely fast through the use of his pets, the townsfolk tend to fear and shun them. Such a quick rise in strength must have been done through a pact with evil forces for all they know. That, paired with the fact that raising what has been lain to rest to live again is a rather unnatural feat. This corruption of nature frightens the populace even more, and makes the life of a necromancer potentially a tough life indeed. To do business, a necromancer must improve his/her standing. To survive, either do the same, or work up the ability to become invisible when it is available.

Bottom Line
Although the populace generally hates the necromancer, you don't have to. The necromancer is perfect for the dedicated "evil" roleplayer. Imagine raising an army of skeletons and ghouls with other necromancers, and then setting them loose on the unsuspecting country dwellers. Or you could go totally recluse, summoning skeletons to guard whatever corner of the world you mark as yours. Disease is your brother, poison is your sister, and the dead are your sons, and all are available for you to use in your quest for death. Then again, although you are considered to be evil right off by the populace, you don't have to be. You can use your powers to help those in need (although you don't have much in the way of spells of that type, you could send your skeletons off to aid an NPC), and destroy evil creatures. Like other magic casters, you are weak when you start out, but don't let that put a bad taste to the necromancer in your mouth. It is one of the most versatile classes in the game. You are the magician of death. Do not be afraid to dabble in death. Embrace it!
