Finding That Perfect Character For You
For all of you who have made it into the phase four beta or weren't quite as fortuneate and are waiting for the final to be released there is no doubt that you have thought to yourself, "Self, what race and class am I going to play?" You know you don't want to have to try out every combination before you find a race/class that is "you". This is where this editorial comes into play.

My name is Mentho and I am the editorial/story editor for Everquest Center. Any comments/ideas/requests/criticizm/brownies/hugs/money can be sent to briana@silverlink.net. This editorial will hopefully help you figure out what class/race combination you want to play when you finally get to play everquest. Although this editorial is focused around helping you choose your first character, if you're an experienced player you could use this information to help you make a better choice on a second character. That said let's get on with this article.

In The Beginning
Trying to pick a good character without ever having played the game or haveing seen it played is like trying to pick a pair of matching socks out of a drawer in the dark. You usually know what type of socks you have but there's no way of knowing for sure if you're getting the right ones. Hopefully this editorial should group your socks togeather so you'll have no trouble finding the perfect pair.

I can remember when I first started to think about what class I was going to play. I figured the best place to start was one of the major Everquest websites. I went to EQSS and EQVault and quickly fell in love with their class guides. I'd sit in front of the computer screen for hours reading the guides and trying to decide which class to play. There was only one problem: Each class guide was bias, they all said that their perticular class was the best. I'd read a class guide for the monk which said that in higher levels the monk had no equal in fighting ability and then I read another that said the rogue was never be messed with. I began to think that it was all a conspiracy to confuse me and I'd never figure it out. Thankfully, I was wrong.

A Golden Needle In a Haystack - Don't Poke Yourself
My many dedicated hours to searching for the perfect character finally led me to some unbias information. Raw statistics, immensely hard to find during the beta test of any game, but I found them because of the NDA (non disclosure agreement). Any raw statistics had to be obtained through 989 and then organized into some fashion that was user-friendly.

It seemed like a dream come true. It was a list of all the races and their classes with statistics. It not only had the raw statistics but it showed a class selection index for each class under each race. The class selection index was a number what measured how "good" the class was in relation to the race. So it seemed easy enough, find the class with the highest selection index and abracadabra, you've got the best race and class, right? WRONG!
(The information I am refering to can be found here)

It took me a while but I realized that it wasn't quite so easy. I had wrongly choosen to be a Ogre Warrior because it had a selection index of 114.3 which was far above any other selection index in the game. There were a couple of problems with this; 1) Ogres are EVIL. They like to munch on the small insignificant humies. This fact destroyed any posibility of me every grouping up with anything else that's not evil. It is true that Chrisma will get higher if Ogres kill evil things and they will eventually be accepted into society but they are never treated quite the same. The second reason? I'm not an Ogre type of person. I like to be honorable, well groomed, charming and I like to have a sense of humor. Can you imagine an ogre doing stand-up comedy?!?

-"Der wuz once dis man and he did walk in a bar ... Ouch..."
-Silence from the crowd.
-"Whut? You no laugh? You no LAUGH?!?! Now Thug-Thug angry. Now Thug-Thug bash heads!"
-Next day in the news: "Crowd at comedy house killed by ogre"

I hope you can see my point. I recognized the raw information as a good source but I knew I'd need more to base my opinion on. So my quest continued.

The Board, The Journal And The Decision
The next stop on my neverending journey was to a message board. I again thought to myself, "Self, why not ask people that are actually playing the game?" I posted a message simply asked "What is the best race/class combination in everquest?" I got one reply that simply answered "There is none." I sent the writer of my reply an e-mail because it confused me more than I ever thought I could have been. I asked him why there was no best race/class combination. He replied and said that it all depended on personal taste. The question he posed to be was this, "If you lived in Norrath what do you see yourself as being?" The answer to this didn't seem very clear to me so I went back to the class guides I had been looking at earlier.

I read them again in a new light, "Which one sounds like me?". My decision was a toss up between the Paladin and the Monk. To come to this conclusion I had to think about which one I could roleplay the best and I also had to take into account the advantages and disadvantages of each race/class. I didn't want to play a character that was heavily dependent on magic because that's not my style. I like to take problems head on rather than from a distance, like with spells. The paladin was a "tank" type character in battle and yet still very honorable. He also used some spells to help himself out, healing and the like. The monk didn't require any weapons and not very much armor to operate and was also a very honorable character.

This decision burned in my mind for a long time until I found a journal about a Paladin. The journal was long and chalked full of screen shots. I began reading from day one in the journal. After a couple of "days" of reading I decided the Paladin wasn't the one for me. Levels were very hard to gain and there seemed to be a lot of platinum required to operate a paladin because of all the weapons and armor a paladin needed to buy. I also read that the vast majority of players were paladins, and it has never been my style to conform to what everyone else is doing. Because the monk didn't require any armor or weapons and because not as many people were playing a monk I decided he would be the best choice for me.

How This Relates To You
By now you must be thinking. "Great Story... *yawn* But what does this have to do with me?" Even though you may not want to be a paladin or a monk you can use the information in the editorial/guide to help you in your decision. There are a few things you should have taken from this editorial/guide.

Don't choose you character simply because you think that it is better than any other character in the game because it's NOT! 989 Studios has done an EXCELLENT job of balanceing the game so that no one character can be better than another. It is true that some characters will work better alone than others, but most of the time those characters aren't as great an asset to a group wereas the weaker solo classes will find groups paying them to join-up.

The key to choosing your character is to find yourself in that character. Are you the type of person who used to be sent to the principles office almost every day when you were in grade school because of beating up on your peers? If that sounds like you, you're probably an ogre or a troll. Be a paladin with that type of personality and I can assure you that you won't enjoy it as much as you could be.

I am not saying that you shouldn't look at the raw data and use that to help you make your decision; the raw data is sometimes one of the best sources for completeing your decision. If you really want to be a wizard but you don't know wether to be a Human Wizard or a Erudite Wizard look at the statistics. If you like to have nothing to do with fighting and you just want to cast spells be a Erudite Wizard if you like to be slightly more "rounded" be a Human Wizard. However this is not always the case. For instance... A Barbarian Warrior can't/shouldn't be played the same as a Human Warrior. Barbarians are less accepted into society whereas humans ARE society thus a Barbarian would prefer to travel alone and a Human would prefer to travel with other players.

El Fin
In conclusion I'd just like to state that it's more important to be happy with your character than to try and have a character that is the most powerful thing you can find. If you're happy with you're character you'll have more fun, and the point of the game is to have fun "Right? Of course right!" With that final thought I'll leave you to your quest for "the perfect character". Farewell, and I hope to see you all in the fair lands of Norrath.
