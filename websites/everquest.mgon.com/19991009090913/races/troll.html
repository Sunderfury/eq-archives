Trolls

Mykil's View
There are cities of great grace and beauty in Norrath. There are cities of order where everything has its proper place. There are cities where the development of knowledge is encouraged towards the betterment of the magical arts. There are cities where the study of warfare and weaponry is a basic necessity of the people required by their superiors. There are cities that show off the splendour that marks advanced culture and high society in the impeccable construction methods used from makeing the walls that protect it, to the wells that supply the townspeople with water. Grobb is most certainly not among these, and its inhabitants are nothing like the people that live in these palaces of society.

Trolls lead simple lives. They kill some things by smashing their skulls in, go back to town, eat what they killed, rest a bit to regenerate, grunt and snort at the other trolls in their native language, and then go out again to repeat the process. Some trolls have grown beyond this simple pattern and ventured out into the wilderness in search of different things to kill, and others have grown tired of this simple pasttime, having gone in search of treasures consisting of pretty "flashy thingies." They are simple folk, but they are a tad offensive in their appearance as well as having a rather bad disposition towards some races. They are second only to ogres in terms of musclebound might, and one troll has been known to be able to take down several lesser creatures with out much trouble.

Their cognitive abilities are unfortunately not of much repute. Like barbarians, who to their credit are smarter yet not quite smart enough to escape much of their simplistic ways, trolls have shamans who can invoke the powers of tribal spirits. A troll shadow knight is also relatively common, as being only warriors with a few of the more basic necromancer tricks, trolls don't have to strain themselves figuring out all the workings behind the more advanced magics. Despite all this, their are only three things trolls are known for. The first is their ability to smash anything almost as flat as an ogre could. The second is the offensive smell that constantly comes from their persons and towns. The third is their amazing ability to regenerate lost health. While not as strong as an ogre, trolls can last longer in a battle as long as they can run away from the enemy long enough to regenerate some. Trolls are monsters, due to their greenish appearance and smell and social graces. Only a few races see past that. They are a society. Unfortunately, most trolls are too busy smashing things to really take the time to think, without hurting themselves, about changing this status.

Bottom Line
Trolls are never going to be great thinker characters. Gradually, one could roleplay small increases in knowledge, maybe to the point of the troll actually being able to think on the level of more civilized races, but at least in the beginning, a smart troll is a troll that doesn't exist. Due to their low intelligence, trolls can be easy to roleplay. They can be basic trolls out bashing things to increase their strength and food supply. One could roleplay a single minded quintessential "monster" troll in which the only thing on the troll's mind is killing anything that isn't a troll, or on a lesser scale anything trolls hate in general. If you wanted to get more complex, which one can do even with a troll (just make sure the troll stays stupid for a long while before showing any really great increase in intelligence, if you go that route) if done right. A troll could wander beyond the areas of Grobb and Oggok, in search of bigger things to kill and glory in their towns, treasure, and perhaps more food. A troll could also be a simple nomad, wandering without any idea of where it was going simply because it lacked any sense of knowing anyplace it really wanted to go. Or it could be a nomad in the sense of it following the herds. Trolls could even go on mystical type quests, like trying to find the spirits of his tribe somewhere in the world. Despite their simplicity, trolls have a wide range of roleplay options available. But never forget your roots. Even you will sometimes feel the urge to go out, club something, and then drag it back to Grobb and eat it. Primal urges are hard to overcome.
