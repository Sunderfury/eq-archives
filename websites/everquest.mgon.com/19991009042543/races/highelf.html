High Elf

Mykil's View
The Great Mother Tunare, given sway over the aboveground of Norrath in the fateful pact between her, Brell Serelis, and Prexus, blessed - and cursed - the world with her creations: the elves. In the beginning, the elves were all relatively the same. Like the humans, they started out as a single group, working to survive in this strange new world. But as time passed, two new races sprang from the ranks of this still very young group. The dark elves, created from the bodies of an elven king and queen stolen away from their people by Innoruuk, Prince of Hatred, as revenge upon Tunare and the other for not including him, secluded themselves in the caves where he deposited them.

The other elven race took slightly longer to develop, but developed over time nonetheless. As time passed, some elves took to more mental pursuits than their other elven, now know as wood elves or sylvans, brethren. While wood elves maintained a balance between their physical and mental discipline, a group of elves all but forsaked the physical fighting skills in favor of knowledge of the more abstract magical fighting skills. This group, over a period of time, became known as the high elves. More fair and with sharper features than their sylvan brethren, high elves live alongside the wood elves in their tree cities of Faydwer.

Not as intelligent but wiser than erudites, high elves are the only race that matches the erudite in magical power. Just as adept in the arcane as erudites and being an older race, their experience surpasses that of their human contemporaries, and they too are rumored to have records of magical studies in libraries that are believed to be more expansive in some cases than those of glorious Erudin on the continent of Odus. Considered of royal stock of elvenkind by the wood elves and several other races because of their high wisdom and higher levels of mental refinement than many other peoples, the high elves are respected just as much, if not moreso, than erudites for their capabilities. Indeed, trifling with a highly skilled high elven wizard or magician is not the path to long life, unless you have gotten close enough to inflict mortal wounds before any spells can be fired, which is a rare occurence.

Bottom Line
An elegant and beautiful race, high elves are an excellent choice for an aspiring master spell caster. Ill suited to the life of a warrior, high elves rival the erudites in magical ability, trading off some intelligence for higher wisdom. Being a "high race," high elves are not an inherently evil race. They can go evil, as stranger things have been known to happen, but a high elf, being a child of nature (despite their inability to be druids), cannot go against nature and practice the dark art of necromancy. Only the dark elves, out of all the elven races, are capable of practicing the dark arts, as their souls were twisted and cut off from nature from their dark creator, Innoruuk. Roleplay wise, they are not quite unlike the erudite, but not as zealous, and certainly incapable of the same type of "heresy," as the erudites call the actions of their darker, necromantic, brethren. They can go on knowledge finding quests, but more often in the case of high elves, one might consider lore as opposed to direct knowledge of power, which is the driving force behind the erudites searches. High elves share the curiosity of their sylvan kin, and often venture to human lands to learn what they can about the skill young race and to make their powers known. They are slightly better suited to the battlefield than erudites in general, as their agility is naturally higher making it easier for them to avoid hits in battle, although direct fighting is ill advised. As a high elf, you command respect. People admire you for you natural grace and beauty inherent to your race, and they will think twice before making a decision to confront you. As a proficient spell caster, you must constantly watch out for enemies so you can destroy them before they reach you. Once your power is witnessed, your enemies will either flee, or continute towards a similar fate as their then dead comrades. But finish them quickly.
