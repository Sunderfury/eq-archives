Half Elves

Mykil's View
Nobody really knows how half elves came about. Whether they were put on the world of Norrath as part of some grand scheme, or were simply the result of casual interspecies unions between humans and woodland elves is one of the greater mysteries of Norrath. It is rather clear how they come about. A human and elven mate, and from the union comes a child that shares the strengths and weaknesses of the two. The match is widely believed by scholars, elven and human, to have been preordained, simply because the traits of both races fit together so nicely in the final outcome.

Half Elves more closely resemble their woodland elf parents. At first glance, their sharpened features and characteristically pointed ears would fool anyone who didn't scrutinize further. Upon closer inspection, half elves look much more human than woodland elves, yet their fairness is still relatively the same. Although sharper than those of a human, the woodland elf features on a half elf are considerably less pronounced. The ears are slightly less pointed, and their features are softer. On the whole, the features of the half elf are more subtle than those of a sylvan, yet more sharpened than those of a human. The blend of attributes is similar.

In temperment, they are less refined than the sylvans. More eager to rush into battle with their human strength and agility, they can make very good warriors types. Half Elven heros of the battlefield have appeared relatively frequently, as their agility to avoid hits while being strong enough to inflict mortal blows (with practice building strength) is a legend in some lore. Both sides of their characteristics make them effective warriors. Paladin half elves appear just as frequently. Half Elves are also infamous as rogues and bards. Having enhanced agility, their ability to pick pockets clean without being noticed is nearly as famed as that of their distant dark elven brethren. Their natural connection with nature on their elven side make them ideal druids and rangers, and this occurs just as frequently as the others. Half Elves have no true calling. Their past blurred, you will rarely find a half elven who knows exactly where it came from and exactly what its purpose is. Even they themselves have trouble framing their purposes simply because they shine at whatever it is they want to do that is available to them. They are, because of this, possibly the vaguest race of Norrath, even moreso than the dark elves. Nothing much really known about their history and reason for being in Norrath, elves and humans alike strive to discover the real reason for this cross to have come about despite fact. Where did they come from? What is their purpose? And whither will they go when their true purpose is fullfilled?

Bottom Line
In general, the half elf is good for any role available to it. Their strength and stamina, which are from their human sides, enable them to serve much better as warriors than their wood elf cousins. On the flip side, their greatly better agility and slightly enhanced dexterity enable them to serve as excellent rogues and bards, being able to ply their trades and avoiding the weapons that will so often be seen coming down over their skulls in their lines of work. The only pure magic that a half elf can master is that of nature, and with their wisdom and intelligence, they work well in that profession as well. They have no known serious weaknesses inherent to their race, and the blend of human and elven characteristics has bred out any serious flaws from both. Roleplay wise, because of the small amount of history about them, they are perhaps the most diverse race. You will find half elves everywhere, owing to their being the race with the most starting location options available. Half Elves can be nomadic, moving from place to place at a whim, or very adventureous, going where the adventures are found. They can be explorers, being curious about their origins themselves, they naturally share the desires of their human and elven parents to figure out their reason for being. On the other hand, they can stick to being very local characters, staying in one place until they learn everything they possibly can, or defending it from monsters like a kind of "charge." Still others might not care about their origins, being the type out for material gain, such as treasure hunters, or the type out for a spot of fun, like rogues and bards who follow Bristlebane. The possibilities of this race are nearly endless no matter what race is picked. But one thing is for sure, being the representative of not one but two races. Many people are watching you, and you have to live, or try to live, up to both their expectations. Being a half elf is a hard life, as their is a double standard to maintain. But whatever the outcome, you will always be welcome in the homes of either of your parents.
