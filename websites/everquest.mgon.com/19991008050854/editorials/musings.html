RPGing with the Fairer sex (well maybe) - Kwan
OK, well firstly, I don't want any remarks about me doing this preamble, I'm putting it up for Rianna, and I repeat I am not female (I'll hunt down and slay anyone who says different). Anyway, as usual, everything in the editorial is the opinion of it author, If you would like to comment or berate her, feel free. Send your feedback to: hawk@evqcenter.com

Musings of a "Girl Gamer"
I admit it. I was going crazy waiting for EverQuest to begin. No game since Warcraft II has inspired me to make as big a flurry of system upgrades and frantic Internet searched for the slightest breath of rumor as this one. Although Online Role Playing Games (OLRPGs for brevity) have burned me in the past, I still anticipated this ones release with excruciating anxiety.

Why did I find myself checking my email every four hours for the mystic "second email" that would announce the beginning steps for the open 4th phase of beta? Why did I torment myself like this? The answer is simple. I see in Everquest many of the things that I have hoped for ages in an OLRPG.

I realize that my opinion of what makes a good OLRPG differs from that of some. I would hope that most Role Players would tend to agree that Everquest promises to deliver most, if not all, of the things we have dreamed for since we first plunked a quarter in the slot, picked up a gamepad, clicked a mouse or rolled the gaming dice.

Since my early arcade days I have lusted for eye candy. Great graphics beat great gameplay almost everytime. The exception was the Atari 2600. I still hold a soft spot in my heart for the clunky 4 bit graphics of "Combat". I could probably still fire up my old 2600 console and play the Combat games until I finally had to stop because of bugged out eyes, numb thumbs, and a severe case of "Gamers" Butt" (that numb, tingly feeling in your backside after 5-6 hours of consecutive play on the hard family room floor). EverQuest is definitely heavy on the eye candy.

I admit that I was completely addicted to Ultima Online. The first time I logged in, I had such dreams of what it could be. I imagined myself as part of the world. I created a female healer, and promptly died fighting a pig. I looked at the black "You Are Dead" screen in dismay. A PIG killed me?!? Disappointed by the stupidity of the death, I learned also on that first day the enormous concentration of jerks in the game. I saw my newbie corpse looted of everything by a "Lord Shadow" He only laughed at me (as I quickly took the penalty to instant resurrect to protect my few possessions from his grasping scummy hands). He was a fast looter...too fast. I was unable to save anything except my clothes, and a pair of thigh boots I had conveniently found by the place I appeared in Britannia. I politely asked, then begged for my meager possessions back. He just laughed and said "U lam3r. U should learn the rulz. This stuff is mine now Biatch haha." I dared to mention that he was NOT role-playing his character of a "Noble Lord" very well. He just turned around and left. This of course left a bad taste in my mouth, but I thought it was an isolated incident. I was of course, wrong. I wish I had kept a record of the number of times I died. Some were to my own stupidity such as attacking a pig with my newbie healer with no combat skills and a dagger, or entering a PK infested dungeon in full gold plate. Some were the to frustrating Lag Monster. Far too many deaths were to gangs of non Role Playing Quake Kidz.

Now I won't say that the Quakers aren't allowed their "fun". On the contrary, they are as entitled as I to enjoyment. However, their right to wildly swing their fists ends where my nose begins. I do not now, nor have I ever wanted to participate in PvP within the Role Playing games. If I want to "waste" other players, I do it in one of the games designed for such activity. If I have a bloodthirsty Amazon desire to see the color of my opponent's guts, I'll frag 'em in Quake. The reason is simple: those games were designed to be a humongous frag fest. Equipment is easy to come by (just walk to the nearest spawn point, and Voila! A rocket launcher). All players in Quake have equal abilities as far as their characters go. An OLRPG, however, is quite different. Getting items such as weapons and armor takes gold. Getting gold, (unless you are a bug abuser ) takes time. Nobody likes their time to be wasted. Getting Pked at the Britain crossroads in a set of chain you labored for 4 real time hours in the tailorshop for, even if you have another set safe in the bank, not only sucks the big one, it's a waste of time on top of it. The total time wasted is not only the time it takes to run back to town or to find an elusive wandering healer, but also to regain enough wealth through more tailoring, lumberjacking and mining to replace the goods lost. This may be why so many turned to the way of the PK. No "monster" holds more loot than other players. For some, the challenge of beating other players is the biggest thrill in the game. For me, it's not. I don't know whether my feminine point of view makes me different, or if it is a personal ethic. I never cared for Pking in OLRPGS. All I have to do is think of how crushed I was when I was looted of almost everything I had on my newbie healer character. I cannot do that to another person.

Sure, there ARE role playing evil PKs, but they are so few in my real experience. Believe me, I experienced PKs over...and over...and over. The only PK death that did not leave me feeling violated was one that I actually sought after a fashion. I had decided to meet the roleplaying Drow of Catskills. I roleplayed a Paladin who had come to reclaim the Crypts of Yew for the light. I died valiantly (some may say foolishly) trying to do just that...alone! But it was the best time I ever had dying!

Most PKs in UO (and other OLRPGs) kill you silently, or with a Psudo-Ebonic taunt such as the ever popular "Suk it Biatttttcccccchh!" or the eloquently simple "U sux!" I never understood why the youngsters whose mommies and daddies can afford a computer fast enough to play UO, plus the $10 a month would have not been taught proper English by their parents. Many things in this world continue to amaze me

The difference lies in whether you are "killing" the character or the player. Actions intended to harass, anger or hurt the player behind the monitor, rather than the one onscreen are the ones the PK switch are intended to prevent. Recently, in my insanity induced by waiting for EQ, I reactivated my UO account. Dusting off my now master healer character, I set out to roleplay my healer gal to the hilt. What I encountered in my first hour back amongst the Britannians made me regret reopening my account.

As I was walking to the bank from the tailor shop, I ran into a noisome twit we shall call "Mr Legume". This player apparently had named his character after the last HBO movie he'd seen. From half a screen away, he was "miner shuffling" his stack of 136 hides to the provisioner. Walking a few steps ahead of the hides, he would pick them up and drop them a few steps ahead of him. This way, he could move an obscenely large amount of material relatively quickly. Problem is, he became quite paranoid while doing this. "bite me" he yelled at me in lower case halfway across his screen. Wanting to know if he was perhaps a sadomasochist in need of assistance, I walked up to him. "go away you whore. these are mine. I see u trying to take em." Of course, this amount of hides would take me a few hours to gather, but I had no intention of taking his hides. After all, they wouldn't fit in my pack either, and I would only have to miner shuffle them too. None too impressed with his grammatical skills, I decided to teach the boy some manners. "There is no need to be rude. I wasn't anywhere near you when you said 'bite me'." He just laughed and started making rude and obscene comments and gestures with his bowing motion to a male passerby. He also continued the verbal onslaught to the point where I was forced to page a GM for harassment. Sure, I could have walked away, but he would only renew his totally inappropriate behavior with the next female character who came within half a screen of him.

In EQ, there is a blessing known as the Holy Ignore Feature. With this feature, the inbred gits we all hate are divinely rendered blissfully silent, at least to those who choose to use the option. No more need to put up with trash talking 12 year olds with an expensive toy and a phone line.

Now that the phase 4 beta has started, I have been immersing myself in the the game. Only once have I met a rude person. I saw a mage in trouble with an orc pawn. Even if the /con (consider) of these beasts show you have the upper hand, they can pull a few tricks on you and have you seeing the yellow "You have been slain by an Orc Pawn" and "LOADING. Please Wait" at the bottom of your screen. So, being helpful, and seeing that even if I did the rest of the damage on the orc, the mage would get all the experience and be able to loot the corpse, I threw in a few sword whacks. Seeing my female form, he called me a slut for this, even though I was only trying to help him. He was pretty badly hurt when I found him. I suppose I should have just let him die.

My hope is that the interface of EverQuest will allow for true roleplaying. Being able to TRUELY ignore those who choose to harass, annoy and be all around asses will make this much easier than in UO. The PK switch may make this game less attractive to those who like to have fun at the expense of others, while still allowing those who truly wish to roleplay killers and thieves to do so...with much needed penalties for living on the dark side. Although there are some features of UO I will miss (such as the ability to afford and actually wear plate!) I think I might join the ever growing group of EverQuest converts. See you in EQ!

Rianna, The Hawk
