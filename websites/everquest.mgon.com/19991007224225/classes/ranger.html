Ranger

Mykil's View
Much like the clerics have always protected life, purity, and the holy light that gives them power, the druids have protected their sacred realms, the great outdoors, from the onslaught of evil. Poachers, hunters, and anyone who would kill animals of nature are the enemies of druids. If you accidentally (or not accidentally) kill a druid's pet wolf, running would be a wise choice indeed. If that druid were a ranger, don't waste your time running. Rather, get on your knees and pray to your deity for a good afterlife. The arrows of the skilled archer ranger (a great many are archers) would find the back of your neck before you could make ten yards.

Rangers are warriors of myth and legends whose unique powers are second to none. The meld of warrior and druid, they wield both areas of their power with deadly proficiency, making them a force to think twice about contending with. Even one on one battles with a lower level ranger can be deadly to YOU. The tracking power that the rangers possess has grown inherent to their nature over ages of practice. The exact art of learning tracking forgotten, rangers are born with a natural sense of how to track quarry, making them the only remaining class with this skill. Life in the forest is as natural to them as it is to their druid friends, nay moreso, for they can track down anything they wish to track. The magic of a ranger is not as abundant as that of a druid. In a one on one magic duel with a druid, the druid stands a better chance of winning. Physical warfare is the ranger's calling, much like the warrior, but the true power of a ranger is much deeper, and much more felt than that of the warrior.

A ranger is much more versatile than a druid. People might argue this point, claiming that a ranger has less magical power than a druid, and therefore can't be the more flexible class. One thing to remember: rangers are to druids as paladins are to clerics. Rangers and paladins both share some (though less) of the magical powers of their pure magic using cousins, but they also have comparable prowess with weapons to that of a warrior, making them ideal front line fighters, especially with their enhancement powers to back them up. One other thing is that while druid powers are not the most useful underground, a ranger can survive underground very well, and with its highly potent tracking skill, no quarry is too hard to find, and no dungeon too hard to explore.

Bottom Line
The ranger is one of those classes that really gives you a good sense of what the game is all about: society. Groups form around rangers, as the tracking skill is available to no other class and it is so highly sought after. Tracking is a hunter's dream, and the fact that no normal hunter can out hunt a ranger is a fact not taken lightly. Rangers are also infamous in lore for being able to disappear into forests for long periods of time, and with a ranger's ability to track and call on some of the powers of nature for aid, a ranger can go solo if wished. However, when in town, bask in the requests of people to join their parties, knowing that you can make the difference between them going out almost right away or having to wait for another ranger to come along (if one does, and one might). Roleplaying with a ranger is also a difficult task (like with all the other classes if you haven't noticed by now) because you have too many choices. Rangers make good woodsmen, guides, hunters, fighters, healers (to a limited degree), druids (with an unusual level of expertise in weapons), explorers, scouts, hermits, and a score of other things which would take too long to mention here. It is by no means THE perfect class. Remember, as part druid, its effectiveness is somewhat reduced in caves like its druid cousins, whose powers are almost useless when not outdoors. Don't let that discourage you. The ranger is a powerful class, and therefore is one to be feared. Do not be mistaken. If enemies realize that they are on the receiving ends of your harsher powers, they just might run away, and you can smile to yourself, turn on tracking, and exact your wrath upon whoever was foolish enough to incur it.
