Dwarf

Razi's View
The dwarf is renowned in fantasy and lore for being the short, arrogant, sarcastic race with an attitude and they certainly live up to it in Norrath. Despite their small size, they are certainly a very tough race. With a very high Stamina and Dexterity base stat, they are certainly not a race to be underestimated. Their stockiness and stature could also mean that they could drink most other races under the table! They are the 4th strongest race, behind the Troll, Ogre and Barbarian.

They aren't the friendliest, nor most welcoming race in existance - accounted for by their low Charisma rating. Preferring to make sarcastic comments rather than pleasant conversation, some prices may be higher in general to most dwarves than other races. They also suffer from poor Agility, and are not the most intelligent of the races. They more than make up for their lack of intellect with their muscle. Their size has it's good and bad points, as is the same with all of the short races. Their home town, Kaladim, can be troublesome for the larger races due to the buildings and doorways being built for a smaller body, yet the Dwarves have no trouble in the larger doorways in other towns, although they may need to jump a little.

The area around Kaladim is fairly good for the very low levels. The surrounding area is home to smaller creatures such as snakes, bats and goblin whelps. Occasional higher level creatures do wander through the lands, allowing for a little more excitment and keeping you on your toes. One of the enjoyable features of the dwarven race is the famous "Running Barrel Roll" - a spectacular forward dive, activated by running forwards and jumping.

Bottom Line
The dwarf is a short, yet remarkably tough, ale drinking and insult giving person. A high stat base for Strength, Stamina and Dexterity certainly illustrate the melee style that dwarves can be known for, or for the light-fingered rogue. Wisdom is relatively high, which allows for some degree of magical ability (Cleric and Paladin mainly) but they are let down for their foul mouth and short temper, suffering a loss in the Charisma stakes. They are one of the small races, so they may have difficulty in reaching some areas that larger races can easily get to. If you are of the axe-swinging, loud mouth, ale-swilling frame of mind, then the dwarf is ideal. If you aren't, then just make sure that you don't go challenging any dwarves to arm-wrestles or drinking competitions! As the races are balanced well, it is a good idea to go for a race and class combination that you feel would suit your style of play most, rather than which combo is statiscally the best.
