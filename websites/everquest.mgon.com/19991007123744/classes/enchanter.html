Enchanter

Mykil's View
Of all the spell casting classes, none can exert the level of control over his/her environment than the Enchanter. A valuable asset to a group, an enchanter literally has the ability to control the environment...to an extent. Able to serve in the capacity of a directly offensive magic user in a party at early levels, at later levels he/she is better used on the sidelines, casting spells that enhance the strengths of the party and damper those of the enemy.

Even at early levels, the enchanter has a score of useful spells available. Some are offensive, but most at geared towards what the Enchanter does: enchantments. The enchanter can decide the outcome of a battle before it even starts through the proper use of spells and some strategy mixed in. Although alteration is his/her forte', be sure to pay proper attention to the other magic skills available. An enchanter unversed in even one of the major magic skills is not living up to its full potential.

I think the enchanter class would be perfect for people who like to play supporting roles. This character will not fare well alone (unless extreme strategy is woven into how it is played), as, like other spellcasters, endurance levels are low. However, even moreso is the fact that it has less offensive spells, making it not a character to have on the front lines. The enchanter belongs behind the front lines, at maximum spell range, where it can cast its strength spells in relative safety. Occasionally, it can zap the few monsters running from the group which are hanging by 1 hp.

Bottom Line
The enchanter class should not be shunned for its lack of directly offensive abilities. Its roleplay value is phenomenal, to say the least. In a limited capacity, an enchanter can be like a druid, offering protective spells while traveling through dangerous grounds. Unlike the druid, the enchanter can operate in full capacity away from nature, whereas the druid is not a good choice to be taken to the The Underfoot. If you want to be the famed hero who drove the bastard sword into the head of the emerald drake that has been terrorizing the countryside, play a warrior. If you want to be the magic user who single handedly brought down a score of frost giants with one fell swoop of the hand, play a wizard or mage. If you want to be the one known to have given these people the strength and stamina in those situations to do those things, play the enchanter. Just because you can't directly fight doesn't mean you can't be a hero. And, with some careful planning as to how and when you cast your spells, you might even be able to do all three. :)
