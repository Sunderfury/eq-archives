Paladin

Mykil's View
Clerics have always been known to wage the war against the undead. Advocates of natural light and life and healing, it is easy to see why they oppose the onslaught of dark forces with such zeal. For years, these wars were waged solely clerics against necromancers, necromancers against clerics, and back and forth until now, where the two are recognized as the natural enemies that they in fact are. Yet while the established ways were to fight magic with magic, time has always known radicals. These young ones, as they are called, with their new ideas conceived an idea that they thought would tip the balance in their war against cleric. Believe it or not, the older clerics, stoic conservatives in their own rights, who had been fighting for years and years with their old ways, were moved by the words of the young ones. And so, the brotherhood of knights known as Paladins was born.

Paladins are an even meld between warrior and cleric. By sacrificing some of a cleric's magical might, paladins are able to serve as fighters alongside their warrior cousins. This being the case, paladins are among the few magic using classes that can actually use heavy armors and weapons. If walking around in a full suit of combine plate armor and using magic is your thing, the paladin is one of your choices. Paladins come into their magical powers much later and much less so in their begginings than normal clerics. But being a holy order of knights, bestowed with special powers of light, they have a few tricks of their own up their sleeves to tip the balances.

The paladin in Norrath is not the quintessential holy knight of ancient lore and modern fantasy books. They have too many options to be so limited. Choosing to play a paladin does not bind you to playing the role of a knight of life on a quest to kill all necromancers and their evil pets. The nature of the peoples of Norrath give these roles much more flavor and variety. You could roleplay a knight who learned a few tricks from an old and wizened cleric. Or you could be a guide and protector. As a half cleric, you have some of the spells that are inherent to your nature, and can heal those in need and cast protection spells in battle. The possibilities are not as limited as the stories might lead you to believe. If you wanted to, you could even play an evil paladin or a rogue paladin if you wish, working for the forces of evil and terrorizing the countryside. The only thing a paladin lacks to my knowledge is a wide array of offensive spells, but the fact that paladins are warriors and do very well in melee battle makes up for this lack.

Bottom Line
A paladin is by no means a pure magic user. If anything, at least in the beginning he has less magical power than a low level cleric, being able to cast no spells as opposed to the list that the cleric gets at rank 1. Per rank, the paladin gets fewer spells per rank than most other magic users, and ranks come much less often. For those who want a whole list of magic spells and the ability to melee well, the shaman is a good choice for you. But shamans are not as good in melee as the paladin. A paladin's battle effectiveness is comparable to that of the warrior, and that is only until it reaches rank 1 at level 9, and that is when its magical powers mature enough to cast its first magic spells. This is when the paladin begins to really exceed the warrior in terms of power, when its spells come out, and he is able to begin to fulfill his role as a cleric as well as a knight, fighting with magic and arms of war. Roleplaying possibilities are excellent, and once you reach a certain level of power, you will find even powerful denizens of the undead fleeing from your powers of radiance.
