Yep, -2 with each hand off your total to hit due to the dual wielding. Also maximum of one off-hand attack until the improved two weapon feat is taken- at which point you can get 2 attacks with the offhand weapon.

BTW it is almost useless to take the dual wielding feats if you are a monk. They get flurry of blows which has the same minuses (-2), but you don't have to take up your feat spots taking the others. Although you could add an extra attack that way. All your attacks that round would be at -4.

Thanks for the info -- a friend over IRC sent me an email when he found out why -- it is basically what you said (another -2 from dual weilding), except that he showed where it is really a series of plusses and minusses which total -2...

Thanks for the answer, though -- couldn't find it anywhere. It would seem the problem with the book is the lack of examples -- I wouldn't know half of my negatives if I hadn't read the Normal section for skills I don't possess!

Here's what he wrote (oh, he gives me a base of +2 instead of +1 because my ranger made level 2):

OK, here goes.

A ranger receives the Amibidexterity and Two Weapon Fighting feats for free
if they wear light or no armor. A chain shirt is light, so your character
receives these automatically. In other words, as long as you intend on
wearing this armor, or lighter, don't waste your feats on Ambi or Two Weapon
Fighting.

Here then, is the modifier breakdown.

Main Hand
Base: +2 obvious
Modifier: +2 obvious
Hand: -4 (it would be -6 except for the fact that that your off-hand weapon
is Light, giving a +2 bonus)
Feat: +2 Two Weapon Fighting (automatically received)

Total : +2

Off Hand
Base: +2
Mod: +2
Hand: -8 (would be -10 but for the fact that the weapon is Light)
Feat: +6 (+4 Ambidexterity, +2 Two Weapon Fighting)

Total : +2

Final Note: The description of Ambidexterity in the Character Generator is
wrong. It says to ignore all penalties for using an offhand weapon. All
Ambidexterity actually does is to add a +4 modifier to your off-hand weapon.

If you want to read the Class Features: Weapon and Armor Proficiencies,
where some of this is explained, it's on page 45 of the PHB. The other
relevant chart is on p.125.
