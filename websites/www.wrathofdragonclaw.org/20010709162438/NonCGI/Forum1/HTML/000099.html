HOOOO KAYYYYY
Here goes.... I have been getting queries about Gen Con specifics. They are just too long to do in tells and I hate to repeat info with Email so here are the nuts and bolts.

When....
AOTGers will be arriving on Friday July 27th. The campsite is officially available to us at 3:00 pm that afternoon. Gen Con itself will open on Thrusday August 3rd. Some go in to into it every day others (like me) one day for a few hours to see what is there. Others ignore the actual con altogether. We all have to be out of the campground by 3:00 pm on Sunday afternoon August 5th. (This is serious.. don't hang out at the con all day on Sunday unless you are already packed up from the camp site).

Where.....
The campground is Bong State Rec Area in Wisconsin. It is located roughly half way between Chicago and Milwakee. More specifically it is 17 miles west of Kenosha Wisconsin on route 142. If you are coming from chicago or milwakee take 142 west from I 94.
There is a ranger station you have to stop at when you enter the park. Tell them you are with Jim Pearce/Alliance of the Griffin (not sure which name Vandy has reserved under).
They will give you directions to our group campsites whch are D and F (see below).

How Much.....
It costs $5 per day for your car, or $25 for a year pass to Wisconsin parks. You pay the rangers for this. If you are going to be there for 5 days or longer get the year pass (obviously). It costs a further $4 per night (payable to Vandy (Jim Pearce))per person. Please pay him right away since "Can I catch ya later?" has caused lots of problems in the past. People forget (giving them the benefit of the doubt). This is not profit. We have prepaid about $650. We are just trying to break even.

Rules.....
Damned few. See the above post about drinking, being loud at night, and belligerance.
Outside of that just play nice and have fun. Also as a word of warning. Do not drive fast in the park. I think the rangers see speeders and mobs who drop loot. At least they camp them that way.

Exceptions to this year
This year we got bumped on our first two nights. That means that on Fri July 27th and Sat July 28th we are at a different group campsite than we will be for the rest of the time.
"Why not just stay at the alternate site?" you ask? Cuz they are not as good nor are they large enough to support everyone that will be there by the second weekend. Therefor we will have what we call a bug-out. That means on Sunday the 29th we will move to our regular sites (D and F). Some of us do not mind this at all. I hate it and may not arrive myeslf until Sunday. But im getting old and lazy. Once I set up my tent im on break till I leave.

General Info.
For interesting facts on AOTG's history check the GEN CON 2000 section of the web site. It tells how we have been camping together at Gen Con since 1979. Not a typo, this guild really is 22 years old. It started as an AD&D gaming organization. Our membership in attendance will range in age from the teens to the 50s and pretty much all ages in between. We will have members coming from all over and not all play EQ. Gaming, eating, drinking, side excursions to various local landmarks (Town Fryer), fireside tales (please.... some new people show up....I know alll of our tales by heart now), swatting misquitos and perhaps even some vollyball and tennis (if we are not too lazy).
Anyway it is time well spent. You will see lots of alter egos to who you have been guild chatting with. (Palant looks exaclty like his Ogre only thinner).

Rai... Let's talk food.
You will eat like a king. Every morning we all go in a caravan to a place called the Town Fryer. It is not expensive and has WONDERFUL breakfasts. They know us there and have the place set up for us. (including the Chalula hot sauce on all tables). We let them know when we arrive so that they can put on extra morning staff and be ready for us.
It is great.
Right after breakfast we head over to the Pick and Save (a huge supermarket).
We pick up the day's ice and decide what will be for dinner (hardly ever do lunch after that huge breakfast). Plus there are often intense games going on under a pavilion tent so we are too busy to be bothered with lunch. Here we pickup what we will cook at the campsite later (either over the fire or on someone's smoky joe grill). This varies. Sometimes you just pickup something for yourself like brats to put on a sick over the fire or you and some other random camper decide to go in together and fix soemthing as a team.
Frequently there is a massive camp cook where we alll chip in and have some real good food. We do have some gourmet chefs there. We have had (excuse my spelling here)
CocoVaugn with palenta, Veal Mirangi, Whole smoked turkeys, Pots of chili. This year it looks like we will be bar-b-Qing a pig. And, especially if the weather is bad, we decide to drive into town somewhere and eat at a restaurant.
If you want to use someone elses utensils to cook, the cost is only that you have to wash them. In general, traveling in, I suggest you buy some things after you arrive. A lawn chair and a cooler would be a good idea. There is a Kmart right next to the Pick and save and is a stop made at least once by eveybody for something they forgot or did not want to pack.
Lets talk transportation.
Let us know on this message board what time you are coming into Milwakee airport and your flight number. We will have you picked up and brought to the campsite. I can't make that promise about Ohare. It is a lot further from the campsite and a LOT more of a pain in the ass to get in and out of. Transportation once you are there is not a problem either since so many of us drive. We always carpool to where ever we go. Lots of people drive in and never get in their cars again till they go home.
Lets talk booze.
Please see above about booze. You can have booze at the sight and it is sold (pretty cheap too) at the Pick and Save. But as we noted above, if you are a mean or a loud drunk don't do it. If you are just silly and sloppy (like Omcduffy) drink up. We think that is funny.

If you have any more questions just post them, I'll get back to them right away.
Mcduffy
