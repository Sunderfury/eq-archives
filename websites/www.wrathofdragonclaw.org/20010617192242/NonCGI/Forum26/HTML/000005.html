I have some friends that want to play evil characters... I am used to happy good guys as players, so I am faced thinking up a decent storyline to grip bad guys... Any suggestions?

I am throwing about a few ideas. One idea is to start with a man against environment or man against nature idea initially... Take morals and good versus evil out of it by placing them in a situation with wild beasts where anyone (good or evil) must do their best to get out alive...

Another idea I am working on might be the genesis of the evilness of the characters... They start out fine and happy until they face this... A charismatic, demonic character tries to charm and sway the characters on the pretense of helping this demonic character to right a wrong, but it turns out that to fix this the demonic creature is requiring the party to sacrafice themselves... Basically, the demonic character is presented as charismatic and all at first but that it is really a trickster demon must be discovered by the group. It turns out the whole time the demon was being honest and truthful -- it IS trying to right a wrong, and that to correct this requires the group to perish is just a side effect. He is lawful evil all the way.

If you're having issues about asking your players to do evil acts, maybe you aren't the DM for this scenario. I can see how you want to warm up to the task by putting them against a "neutral" character; that kinda works whether or not the PCs are good or evil.

But think about the cool, new possibilities!

They're the scum of the earth, these new NPCs. Since birth, they have lied, cheated, killed to live. Now, they have grouped together to increase their chances of survival. What about that shipment of precious artificats that's coming to town? Do you think they would like to go after that? Maybe they're hired as mercenaries. Maybe they form their own service called Dirty Works, and they won't turn down a job as long as it pays.

But how much can you REALLY trust that evil guy next to you?

As a spoof, you can come up with a story line as you normally would. Take for example the standard "rescue the princess from the evil nasties" story line.

Now switch everything around. Their mission: ensure that the princess remains captive until the ransom is paid. Uh-oh...here come those goody-goody characters from Greyhawk!

I'd ask them how evil they want to play the characters, as well as how often they want to play them. If it's a lark, then perhaps my last idea would be fun for them (kinda like what they did with the Dungeon Keeper computer game). If they want a campaign based on these characters, then maybe forming "Dirty Works" won't be a bad idea. Also, you could throw in "good" jobs to play with them a bit and really test their role playing.

Just some ramblings...

I have always found it a bit disturbing when people play evil.
Now when I say evil I assume they are being truly Evil. Torturing, raping, hurting innocents. My advice here is be careful. If it stops being a role playing game and becomes some sort of twisted, sadistic, mental masturbation session I would drop those players like a hot rock. (I would also probably tell them I was moving to Antarctica and change my phone to a new unlisted number).

If they just wanna be the bad guys and be more like outlaws its no biggie.
You come then to the concept... are orcs really evil or just dislike humans as a race. Do even Dark elves see themselves as evil or (like Nazis and some religious groups) do they just have a fanatic and (to themselves) righteous hatred for other races and/or beliefs?

If I were an intelligent monster race I would certainly label elves, dwarves, and humans as evil if they crawled through my dungeon and killed my kin to take my stuff and call it treasure. I would call that robery and murder (whether the elves, dwarves, and humans called themselves lawful good or not).

In my current campaign evil and good are pretty fuzzy. It is more based on races being allied or not allied. This takes care of the whole good/evil thing. Generally evil is according to what side you are on. A human thought of as evil is problably a criminal and bad to his own kind. If this is the case Gort's suggestion above gets even better...
If enemy races hate your pcs because they are human, or human allied, and humans and their allies hate your pcs because they are criminals to their own kind it makes for very difficult situations.

Where do you rest. Where do you get your supplies. There are enemies at every turn.

Maybe they will like this outlaw lifestyle. Just don't moly-cadle them. Also you could come up with some pretty neat doublecrosses from any npc friends they make. Their friends would likely be criminal also, or feel justified in screwing them over cuz.. they are evil after all.
