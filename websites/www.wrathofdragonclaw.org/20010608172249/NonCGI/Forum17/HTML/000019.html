There was a time
That through his sable hued eyes
(the color of sunlight through the sweetest jar of honey)
He beheld her once as an Italian painter's vision.
She reclined languidly in supple satins
Housed within a protective frame of grace and beauty.

Unveiled secret gardens of mystery and love
Poured into such a vibrant landscape of ardent lips and fervent song
Silhouettes of alabaster lines and curves
Swathed in fountains of dark sienna tresses
And hope for days to come were awash in her eyes of stormy grey
Eyes that looked out toward him.
Timeless beauty she was
When time hung upon every word.

But Time's neglect began clouding the surface
Obscuring the once porcelain features.
The frame's corners gave way
Broken and lost...
The edges of the canvas were torn
Worn from mishandling
The painting weathered from days left alone,
Left out, left to the side, left...

The minutes turned to weeks and fettered away
Picking mercilessly at the canvas
Erosion strewed the colorful pigments like broken shards,
Of the once priceless portrait, upon the ground
In the back
Under the layers
Behind the boxes
In the old closet upstairs
(the kind of closet you put things in you don't want to keep
but don't have the heart to throw away)
