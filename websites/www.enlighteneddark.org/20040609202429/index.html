Everquest Guild Summit Update. Posted Tuesday, June 8, 2004 by Nazlug

Well, I have recovered from the long weekend trip to San Diego, and boy let me tell you, it was quite the trip indeed. What was it all about? SoE invited approximately 50 guild leaders from around the country, of long lasting and successful guilds. I am proud to say that our Guild is one of those 50 that were selected based on our proven record of being near the top serverwide for 5 years now. Wow! What a ride it has been. To every member of ED past and present, this is a salute to you, from Sony. /cheer!!

The People:

Thomas Taylor and Alan Crosby - You guys totally rock. They were our hosts for the weekend, and never once showed a frown or any annoyance at the myriad of questions that we kept asking. Thank you so much guys!

John Smedley - The big cheese of SoE(president). You would expect some uptight number crunching type guy, but Smed is just the opposite. He is a gamer like the rest of us, and he truly wants Everquest to be a better game. He listened to every rant and flame, and when it was an Everquest problem, he was quick to say "yup, we messed up there, and will try to not do that again." A first class individual all the way.

Craig Knapp - Associate producer of EQ. This guy is a gamer for sure. He listened to every suggestion, no matter how outrageous. (Yes, even among the top 50 invited there were some uh, how shall I say it, not too swift individuals... :p) He would respond and take notes constantly. A solid guy all around.

Ryan Robinson - Customer service Supervisor. When he came in with the police riot gear on.... (ok so he didnt really, but I could tell he was ready for battle!) He knew he was going to get some major heat from us. EVERYONE seems to have had customer service issues. He listened well, addressed everything asked, and also told us how things are changing. There are approximately 80 GM's for Everquest, and no Martha they are not all in India! About half are at San Diego, close to the Development Team. I think over the next few weeks we will all benefit from the improved system they are implementing.

The Real workers....

John T and Alan Van C - these are the two Developers I had the pleasure to talk to one on one for a long time. Let me tell you, they really love the game, and always are trying to do what they feel is best. I will outline below what issues we talked about, but I cant overstate how smart these two guys are, and they have a thorough knowledge of Everquest, believe you me. Let me say it again, these guys on the Development Team, Tom and crew on the art team, and Eric "Grumbuk" the programmer (hihi DX9! hows collision coming Eric?) are what makes EQ the game that we play. You guys absolutely rock, and the long hours you work, off the clock, are truly appreciated after seeing how much work it takes.

The Players - I met so many, and pretty much everyone rocked. I even started to come around on the Knights vs Warriors Tanking issue... Thanks to the Paladins of Norath representative. :p

Uber Guild Leaders - Most of these guys (no girls!?) were down to earth, and just loved the game. I felt right at home chatting with these guys and we came up with a lot of stuff, that will benefit all players, not just the top ones. People seem to forget that the players in guilds like Enlightened Dark didnt get lvl 65 with 400+ AA pts by magic... We worked through all those levels and problems that the casual guy faces too.

The Issues - We were all given a list and asked to state 10 items of importance, and check off if they were covered. Here is my list:

1 - Alii Customer Service Issue. His character had been eaten (deleted) by the SoE servers. After four days it was not restored. A quick conversation and he was taken care of. This is a HUGE problem in my mind, and Customer Service promised they addressing these exact type of issues and delays. It was also interesting to note that over 70% of their time is used resolving trade scams and the like. Thats right, these suckers who try to rip us off with trades are taking away our valueable time that needs used settling real problems, like Alii's. That was the reason given for the recent polls concerning secure trading... But with the latest poll about secure character trading, I am sure the almighty $$ is also playing no small part in this.... But if it results in improvements for us all, then by all means lets have secure trading, etc. But if they allow the secure sale of accounts, they going to unban all those banned accounts? hmm? Inquiring minds want to know.

2- Healing diversity and Tank diversity. At the uber level, there is one healer, that is clerics, and one tank, warriors. The real question is, should there be 3 of each, clerics, druids, shamans, and warriors, sk's, paladins? If you make the two sub classes stronger and able to fill this role you obviously have to help warriors and clerics. This is a hot topic, and no real answer was to be had. Will update as we hear more.

3- Experience and Rewards. New zones need to have better awards and experience then old zones, or people simply wont go there. Look at GoD and the Elemental Planes or even Valor. No one does experience in Barindu for example, because you fight easier mobs, and get more experience in early zones. This was addressed and will be a major topic for Omens of War expansion.

4 - Craft and Quest Rewards - For crafters, they working on some uber quests that can be done with 100/175/220+ skills. This will allow the novice crafter to get a decent item, and work towards that uber item at 200 lvl craft skills. A good idea to me. Quest rewards, same thing.

5 - Chat Functions. A new window functionality that allows you to change the color of specific peoples chat, or have their chat go to a seperate window. Great for raiding people, to send that MA chat to another window so you dont miss commands.

6 - Character Progression in lots of small steps. Ever try to earn an AA skill that requires like 15 AA and think you will never get there? Yeah, me too. They are trying to make more steps, but smaller ones, so we can see the progress we are making. Good stuff.

7- Wizard spells and Mage pets in Gates. Wizards need more then a Fire spell, and mage pets needs immunities to aoe's. Simple stuff. Mages will make a choice, like Earth pets are immune to Disease and Poison Aoe's for example, so they dont get ate up by the harsh Gate's aoe's.

8 - A clear definition of class roles. This is so hard, and broad now with the game being 5 years old, but they are trying to provide flexibility so a raid is not crippled by lacking one particular class, but also making sure more then one of each class has something to do. Enchanters for example, we need one enchanter only now. For Buffs. Yay, lots of fun for that chanter, while the other sits on the bench of the raid for a dps or healing class.

9- This was about class roles on raids, see #8 above for an example.

10 - Make stats matter! The stat cap needs raised, and raised high. Remember when you actually wore items for their stats? Yeah, it was a long time ago. With the current lvl of buffs, bard songs, etc it is pretty easy for someone to get their important stats to the cap with no real problem. Make these matter! They are raising the stat cap in Omens, which is cool.

Ok that was my top 10 list. Every single topic was addressed directly by a SoE person.

A few more ideas, these are posted on some other websites, but here are the ones I thought were most important.

- Increased debuffs on mobs, to help dot type classes such as necros.
- New types of charms.
- Changing the backflagging process to make it less painful.
- Making Item Augments removable. yay!!!
- Reviewing the Epic 2.0 quests. I saw the art for these. Awesome is all I can say! Chanters looked really cool, as did Shamans. These two stood out, but they all looked great.
- Ldon style or similar Solo content. Yup thats right, all you people who want to play Everquest solo ( uh this is a multiplayer game, right? but I wont rant.) will get your wish. Instanced content designed for the solo player, much like an ldon adventure. Now the experience will not be as good as a solid group, but in reality this will be nice for the times when you have an hour to kill, and dont have time to do the whole group thing. I think this is honestly the most important thing they are working on.

The list goes on and on, but.... I am tired of typing!! Will post more as it comes to mind. I think that Everquest's future looks very bright, with these guys at the helm. Great job SoE!

In closing... at the San Diego airport.

We arrived way early, due to security and all that. There were three of us. Ken, a college student, and writer for IGN, the second was Mindy, a notable player board moderator, who happens to hold a Doctorate, and of course myself, the old troll. We had the most entertaining political debate you could imagine over that 2 hour waiting period. Ken is a save the world kinda guy, who is very bright, and wildly conservative on some issues, and a giant liberal on others. Mindy was the opposite, these two were salt and pepper. He uses stats of the masses, she uses the plight of the individual. I of course being a simple man, did a lot of listening to the two, interjecting here or there, and just having a great time.

To hear comments like "Jimmy Carter was the worst President in the last 100 years" from Ken, only to have Mindy respond " no he wasnt he was the best! Look at his track record for peace, and his care for mankind. He is still well respected around the world, more then 20 years after he left office." With these two comments you can imagine how the rest went. To you two and of course my friend Leo (Moxxie? forgive the spelling, you know how it is!) from Veeshan, you are some great people, and players of Everquest, which is what the game is all about right? Playing with people you enjoy.

-Naz
