General Membership Information:

Enlightened Dark is an EverQuest guild on the Tarew Marr server. Our primary raid times are in the evenings, Eastern time-zone, though we occasionally raid targets of opportunity before/after our normal raid hours. We focus on end-game content and new EQ content in general. We've done it all, we've seen it all, we want to do and see more.

Joining ED:

1. Start by grouping with members or friends in the guild. Send an officer a /tell, asking about the current recruitment status. Next, email (officers@enlighteneddark.org) or PM (on these forums) details about yourself - character name/class, playtimes, AA, guild history, current expansion flags and a link to your Magelo profile.

2. While we appreciate the variety of players in EverQuest, we have certain expectations for those joining ED. You must own all expansions. You must be level 70, with more AAXP than Volx (the guild slacker). These requirements will change with new content, and for some classes, specific AA skills are expected. Occasionally, we will make allowances for lower AA totals, based on needs of the guild.

3. Be patient. ED will seldom invite someone after they've been on just one or two raids. We take our time getting to know potential members, just as we want you to get to know us. Due to raid-size restrictions or flags, you may be denied access to a certain raid. Finally, we do not allow recruits to raid with us while wearing another guildtag, and will not guildinvite you until you've been out of that guild for some time.

4. Our loot system is Merit-based. You will not understand it upon joining the guild, but as time goes on its purpose will become clear. New members should not expect loot, even if they're the only Paladin on a raid and a Paladin-only weapon drops. While our system is not DKP-based, loot and attendance records are posted for all members to view.

5. YOU MUST KNOW HOW TO LISTEN TO AND FOLLOW INSTRUCTIONS...this means if you are told to do something by the leader of a raid, you do it, no matter how odd it may seem to you. If you aren't sure, ask someone in tells. Finally, members are expected to have a certain level of situational awareness - knowing where they are relative to camp, where they need to be and where the mobs are.

6. Be on frequently. Enlightened Dark is an active guild, if you join our ranks you should be someone who plays a lot. We are a guild of EQ-addicts, but we understand that real-life interferes with gaming plans all too often, and try to work with members to keep the guild running as a well-oiled machine.

7. Members are expected to play their primary character as originally invited. If you decide to start or pursude an alt-character, you may do so on your free time provided you keep your main up-to-date. If a raid is called, you are expected to be there if you're online, on your primary character. Alts of mains are only flagged when the member is a high-attendance raider with substantial time in guild.

8. Your PC/connection must be able to perform in a raid-setting. A LD or severely lagged character does us no good on a raid. EQ is not an incredibly hardware-intensive game, so keeping up with the current system requirements should not impose a hardship.

9. You should know your class. While we certainly don't expect you to be familiar with our strategies or raid habits, you should know your class inside-out. If you aren't sure about a particular situation, ask another member of your class on the raid. You should become familiar quite quickly with the equipment and role requirements of your class on our raids.

10. If you join ED with loot as your concern, you will be disappointed. We raid hard and are rewarded in kind. Loot is used not only as a reward for the hard work of members, but also as a tool to help us win future raids.

-Yakita GL of Enlightened Dark
