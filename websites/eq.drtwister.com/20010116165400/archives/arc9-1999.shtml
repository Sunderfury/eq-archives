Download from the Elemental Plane of Time.
Posted: Friday, October 22, 1999 by Stormlord

Here it is in all its simplicity. You will have to use your brain on this one. Not everything handed you can be fresh from the oven. In other words you will have to bake this cake.

First you need a few programs. You will need.
  • EQInside.
  • A Hex editor.
  • A Memory Editor.

First use a Hex Editor on the file spdat.eff file in your EQ dir. Go over this file and get to know how it works. Now you can change things in this file and bring up EQInside and see how your changes will effect a spell when you cast it. For
example the lev1 cleric spell courage. It gives you +10 hp, ac, and protection from dot. Find Courage in the spdat.eff file and change the values and reload EQInside to see the changes and if you found the right bytes. For example change 0A (10) to FF (255). If you don’t know Hex then you have some studying to do.

Once you become familiar with how this file is laid out and works you can start the real "hacking".

Now the fun begins.

Load up Everquest and get into the game. Alt-Tab out and fire up the Mem Editor. If you don’t know how to Alt-Tab or your system won't let you then your SOL. (sorry Loons). Now EQ loads up about 80,000,000 bytes into memory. The spdat.eff file is in here word for word byte for byte. All you have to do is find it. Its not that hard if you take a few min to familiarize yourself with it. You can do a simple search or scroll down the memory till you find it. It does me no good to say the Mem addresses because every computer is different. But for me the spells started at 59700000. Now once you find where the spells are stored you can edit the values just like you did offline with the Hex editor and EQInside. Write down what you want to change and do it. Once this is done go back to EQ and cast away. MAKE SURE that any editing you do is done in OVERWRITE not INSERT. The reasons for this are obvious and if you don’t know why then your out of your league.
Now here are a few warnings.

THE FOLLOWING WILL NOT WORK!!!!!
  • Changing Casting times.
  • Changing manna costs.
  • Giving spells other abilities than what they already do. (you cant give courage a regeneration property).
  • Changing stats like str and dex or the magic resists (these will change but they will not be valid server side).
  • Changing the summoned items (like summon food to summon POF armor).
  • Changing the Damage of a spell. (sorry no 10000point fire bolts).


What does work are modifications like Courage as you all have seen with the pics I have posted. The key here is EQInside. With this program and a Hex editor you will be able to find all the values in the spdat.eff file. You don't want to be guessing and checking while in game or you will get booted and sent to the cheater file. One easy way to test if a change is legit is to simply Zone. If you still have your mods then your successfull.

You now know what it takes to "hack" Everquest. I will not post anymore information regarding this. Use the message boards and help each other out. Have fun.

New Producer Letter - Customer Support
An new Producer Letter has been posted on the official EverQuest site. This week's edition is somewhat different, instead of being written by EverQuest Producer Brad McQuaid, it is written by Verant's Customer Service Manager Jeff Butler. In it he talks about customer service, policy and GMs. Here's the complete letter:
10/20/99
Hello everyone,
Brad invited me to fill in this week, as the topic for this Producer’s Letter was customer service-related. I would like to take this opportunity to give everyone an overview of the policy enhancements we are planning for EverQuest Customer Service.

Our original policies were designed to preserve EverQuest’s high-fantasy online role-playing environment. Like EverQuest itself, many of these policies were innovative, having never before been pursued in massively multiplayer online gaming. Although some were skeptical, we were committed to our naming and guilding policies, and to controlling more serious problems like harassment and abuse. Our basic customer service philosophy could be summed up with this statement "One player should never be permitted to intentionally ruin the play experience of another". EverQuest was created and maintained with that simple concept in mind. We have learned a lot in the seven months since EverQuest launched. Armed with that experience, we are ready to commit to an even higher level of service.

We are referring to these changes as the "Play Nice Policies". It is our intent to further define our responsibilities as EverQuest customer service representatives, and to provide resolutions when players come into conflict over in-game issues. The policy changes will directly affect how and when we will mediate for players in dispute. When the policies are implemented, our staff will be taking action to diffuse situations before they become so frustrating that a player’s enjoyment of the game is compromised. One of the most serious concerns that we often hear from players is that Guides and GM’s cannot always provide help. In these situations we will now be able to assist.

Before I delve into the policy changes, there is a point I would like to stress. EverQuest players are encouraged to make every effort to interact positively and cooperate with their fellow gamers. Our firm commitment to this ideal is reflected in these policies. Players who wish to destroy the gaming experience of others are not welcome in EverQuest, and will be removed from the game without hesitation.

Play Nice Policies: A revision to the GM/Guide FAQ and Rules of Conduct.

Kill Stealing will now be regarded as disruption and will result in disciplinary action when witnessed by any GM or Guide. The GM or Guide will review these situations on an individual basis and issue a decision which is considered binding upon all parties involved. This may include a requirement to depart the immediate zone or spawn area. Kill Stealing is defined as attacking an NPC with the intent to deprive another player or group of the experience or loot from that NPC. This practice commonly occurs in popular spawn areas. In these instances, the player or group who was first on the scene will have the rights to the kill. Players are encouraged to cooperate in these situations!

Intentional Training of NPC’s and PvP Switch Avoidance will result in immediate disciplinary action when witnessed by a GM or Guide. We are aware that accidents often happen causing unintentional Trains, and will scrutinize each report of this activity closely.

Fraud in all transactions between players will result in disciplinary action when confirmed by a GM. An example of this activity would be offering to recover the possessions from the corpse of another player, and refusing to return that property to it’s owner. Secure Trade Window Circumvention involving the sale of items in-game between players will also be prohibited and punishable under this rule.

The definition of Harassment will be expanded to include any use of any functionality to harm or otherwise inconvenience another player. An example of this would be dispelling another player’s protective spells without his consent. As always, measures will be taken in game code to prevent these activities.

Yielding the Right of Way is an issue where players choose to camp spawn sites (usually for treasure) where the monsters do not provide experience. This problem is initially being addressed in a broad plan involving changes to popular spawn areas and the inclusion of new quests. If and when we are comfortable with the success of these changes, we will consider the implementation of the Yield rule. This will require higher-level players or groups to yield to lower-level players in any situation where the NPC’s being fought consider green to the higher-level players. Due to the controversial nature of this issue, we will be conducting a comprehensive poll on this specific subject.

In the PvP+ environment, several issues will now be included in the definition of harassment. Bind Point Camping, or the practice of killing a player multiple times at his or her bind point. Corpse Camping, in which players lie in wait (often invisible or hidden) to kill another player who is returning to loot their corpse after a death. Zone Border Camping, which occurs when players attack other players before they obtain control of their character after crossing a zone boundary.

Disciplinary issues involving guilds will also be addressed on a broader basis. Guilds whose members habitually violate any of the Rules of Conduct may be disbanded. In addition, Monopolizing numerous spawn areas with the intent to exclude other players will not be permitted. If investigated and verified by a GM, Monopolizing will result in the disbanding of the guild.
I will paraphrase from the current GM/Guide FAQ as a reminder for anyone who may not be familiar with our responsibilities as they relate to the enforcement of our customer service policies. Verant GM Administrators review records of disciplinary incidents on a daily basis. Action is taken based on the severity and nature of the offense, and/or the number of warnings issued to the customer. These actions may include but are not limited to, temporary suspension or permanent banning from the game. Be advised that you may not receive any notification or warnings, in-game or otherwise, prior to disciplinary action being taken against your account.
As an additional note, please be aware that the /report command is to be used in conjunction with the /petition function for players to inform us of violations of the Rules of Conduct in all issues related to verbal harassment or threats and offensive language. After executing the /report command in one of these situations, remember to follow up with a /petition so that the situation can be promptly addressed.

We will be using the polling function of the Chat Server to gather your opinions on these subjects, and determine what issues are most important to you as EverQuest players. Implementation of the Play Nice Policies, in whole or in part, will be made contingent upon that determination. You will see an official notice on the patch server message and at www.everquest.com prior to the enforcement of these policies. Until that time, the standard policies will remain in effect.

Although you’re in OUR world now, YOU are the characters whose lives and stories make EverQuest what it is today. You are the most important part of our world. We will continue to improve our policies and service to provide you with the best online gaming experience possible.

Thanks to everyone for taking the time to read this. From myself, the GM’s and Guides, and everyone in the EverQuest Customer Service Department, have fun and Play Nice!!!

–Jeff Butler
Lead GM EverQuest
Customer Service Manager
Verant Interactive, Inc

eBay - how to protect yourself from being scammed
Posted: Sunday, October 10, 1999 by Baggle

As I'm sure the vast majority of you know, EverQuest is a very hot topic on eBay. Hundreds of transactions are made over eBay each week regarding EverQuest, and millions of dollars have been made doing so. Of course, honest people aren't the only ones out there looking to make a quick buck by selling stuff over eBay. There are scammers as well. I suppose it had to come about sooner or later, but it's just too bad that it ever really did have to come about.

Recently, I was looking over eBay just to see how much things were going for these days. As I was looking around at EverQuest items, I saw a description for an item, "EverQuest account". The big was $200, so I checked it out. My mouth dropped when I looked at what was being bid on. An account with a level 45 and a level 46 character with a full set of rubicite, and all of the other trimmings. I immediately looked at when the auction would end, with thoughts of huge amounts of profit racing through my mind about how much I could resell this for if I bought it. Quite an investment. To my dismay, the auction had ended three hours before I looked at it, and it had sold for $200 to a bidder named tinydragon.

All hope was not lost, however. I still had the meager chance of hoping the highest bidder was extremely stupid, and would sell it to me for $300 or so, so I could still make a huge profit. So, I emailed him. Soon after, I received a response. He would not sell the account to me, as it was too good of a deal to pass up. I emailed him back, thanking him for responding, and wishing him luck. A few days later, I received another email from him. Appearantly, it was a scam. After the person sent off the money, he received an account with two characters on it. However, they were level 1, and had no items on them. The scamee also sent me an email saying that this person is selling more things regarding EverQuest on eBay, and to beware. So, I am passing this on to you. While I no longer have the email I received with his warning, telling me the scammer's name and item numbers, I can give you a few tips for making eBay a little safter.

1) If you are dealing with a lot of money(or even a little money), and don't want to be scammed, you can contact a third party to negotiate the items between the buyer and the seller. eBay has listings of the companies that do such things on their web page.

2) Ask for screenshots! In the screenshots, ask the person to do something specific. Type your name into the text box, for example, when they take the screenshot with the item/character/etc. While screenshots can be faked, it is a pain in the arse, and most people will not have the will to do so. To make it even safter, ask them to have the see-through text screen activated when they speak. It'd be much harder to fake that, than it would be to fake a solid color background.

3) Use Western Union. It can be expensive(around 10% of the amount you are sending), but if you are investing your money in a deal like this, with somebody you don't know, it is better to be safe and happy, than sorry, and uhh, sorry. If you are buying an account, and using Western Union, you can send the money to somebody, but not tell them the confirmation number of the money(the number they give to the office so they can pick up the money) until you receive the account information. There is a 1-800 number you can call, where you enter your phone number, and it will tell you if you have money waiting for you at Western Union or not. After you have confirmed that the money is waiting for you, you can give the person the account information, and they can then send you the confirmation number for the money. While this does have its flaws, it is one of the safest, and people are much more easily tracked this way, since you have to give a thumbprint, valid ID, and your address/phone when you make out a Western Union or pick one up.

4) The easiest, and possibly one of the most effective ways to not be scammed - get the other person's phone number, and call them! It is much, much easier to get a read off of somebody by talking to them over the phone than it is to get a read off of them via email. Also, unless the person is a professional at this sort of thing(and most aren't), then it's going to be much harder for them to bullshit you over the phone than it will be over the internet.

I hope some of you get some use out of this. If there is one thing I can't stand, it has to be scammers, liars, and all around a-holes. Keep your eyes open, and, as the person who was scammed said to me, "If it seems too good to be true, it probably is."

Confirmed! Alt+tab out of EverQuest!

Great for looking at web sites for maps(PLUG - AllaKhazam's Magical Realm) while you are actually playing, instead of trying to just memorize where you need to go, or printing the map out. Anyhow, on to the instructions.

1) While in EverQuest, click on the "Options" button in the upper left hand corner in the graphics mode with the game window in the middle of the screen.

2) Click on "Display", then, find the text that says "Same screens resolution" "ON/OFF". Make sure it is off.

3) Get back into the game mode of it, and press alt+tab.

4) You should now be at your desktop, with EverQuest minimized in the task bar. Press ctrl+alt+del ONCE.

5) Click outside of the "Close Program" window, on the desk top.

6) Press the "Cancel" button on the window.

Okay. You are now out of EverQuest and can do anything you want with your computer. Your mouse should be working, and you should have full control over your keyboard as well. When you want to get back into the game, do the following...

1) Click on the EverQuest box on the task bar.
2) Press F10 a few times. You should now be back in EverQuest.

Well that was simple. Like I said, it can get a little buggy when you run other programs behind it, then get back into EverQuest, but, it works.
