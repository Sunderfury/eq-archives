"What I've wondered is, whenever creating an server emulator is legal in the U.S. It is legal here I live [in Austria], but new team members might be from the U.S. We've received a few 'Cease and Desist' e-mails from the CEO of Verant, but we've ignored them all up to now. Friday, August 11, 2000

Color Hack
Posted: Friday, August 11, 2000 by Dr.Twister

From Everquest Team
Today's patch added the ability to configure the color of the text in your EQ client, but in order to do it, you have to tweak a bunch of numbers in an INI file.

Unless, that is, you use Internet Explorer, then you use EverCrest's new EQCLIENT.INI Configuration page, at http://www.evercrest.com/setcolor/

Simply point and click your way through each of the color settings you can change, and preview it against different backgrounds, set your full-screen and auto-run settings, then click a button and download your custom EQCLIENT.INI file.

Of course on the player side, The last time someone hacked the colors in a Multiplayer Game, some unique things began to happen in the world. So for those looking to fix what you see, this may be of some interest to you.
Since Verant has seen fit to change many of the default colors in the EQ ini file to easter egg pastels, I've prepared a replacement eqclient.ini file for those who want the Classic EQ look (I didn't like New Coke either :P)

Just put this file in your Everquest directory as a replacement for eqclient.ini. Please, if you aren't completely comfortable with doing this, don't mess with it. I don't want to hear about people who had to reinstall and repatch EQ after trying to fix their colors. :P


http://2amstudios.com/eq/eqclassicclient.ini

Of course then Caster's Realm has this to offer...
With the introduction of the new client configuration system, EQ Casters Realm have compiled a wizard which will assist you in creating your client ini files.  This takes all the hassle out of trying to work out how colours will look when you get in the game, and includes a preview function.

You can access the wizard at http://eq.castersrealm.com/colorwizard/.
