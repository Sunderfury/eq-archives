EQ Bug Chat
 January 13, 2000

<DrTwisTer> Thank you everyone for joining us this evening for what we hope to become a regular event here. You are all about to participate in the first ever public bug chat for a multiplayer game.
<DrTwisTer> We will be starting in just a few minutes, but first we would like to share a few words regarding the outlines and topic for tonight's chat, and then introduce the team from Verant who will be sharing with us.
<DrTwisTer> Our goal for this evening is to help Verant track and understand the problems that we encounter on a daily basis while playing EverQuest.
<DrTwisTer> This is NOT an opportunity to flame Verant, nor is it a chat that will answer specific steps to reproduce bugs.
<DrTwisTer> If you have experienced a problem in game repeatedly, or if something has recently happened to you that you can't explain, this is the ideal time to question why the problem occurs.
<DrTwisTer> We hope that with your help, and with Verant's interest in actively participating in chats like this, that the development and improvement of EverQuest will always continue.
<DrTwisTer> Now Soy, our moderator this evening, will explain what you need to know to participate.

<soy> Hello Everyone! Once again I would like to welcome you on behalf of the entire staff at The Dr.TwisTer Network.
<soy> My name is Soy, I will be tonight's moderator and would like to discuss the format and procedures for tonight's Bug Chat.
<soy> Those of you wishing to participate in tonight's chat, and would like to ask a question to one of the participants, send your messages to any of the users with + signs next to their names.
<soy> This includes : Chosen, Duriel, J, Night, Ravage, RevFluffy, Adambomb and Twiggy-2,
<soy> We have very limited time tonight. Please keep your questions simple, and try to stick to one topic.
<soy> So that we may fit as many of your questions into the evening.

<DrTwisTer> Thanks Soy!
<DrTwisTer> Now if we could ask the folks from Verant to quickly say Hi and let us know who they are.

<soy> Question: *Phar* The zoning bug .. the one that if your low on hp, (normally trained to zone .. possible dying while zoning) throws you out of the bounds of the map, in lavastorm this results in a death in lava

<EQ_Carwasher> <end>

<soy> Question: *Night* How long will it be until the Temple of Solusek Ro armor quests will be revamped? Most clerics I know are getting a bit irritated at having to camp rare drops on rare spawns, and you said you were looking into it... (cont.)

<soy> Question: *Kaiser* why do monsters "poof" meaning, when you fight them, they go to run, then all of a sudden they are gone. There are several places in eq that this happens

<EQ_Carwasher> That is a known bug that is being worked on. It has to do with the pathing algorithm used. No specific date on when it wll be fixed. <end>

<soy> Question: *Jarax* Can you Please Make an Inquiry about the Packetloss at Primetime. It doesnt matter if Modem or T3 the big lag spikes still sweep the servers

<EQ_Smed> I'll take this one
<EQ_Smed> Basically, the Packet Loss you see at Primetime is in almost every case the result of Peering Points of ISP's being flooded
<EQ_Smed> We keep a really close eye on our routers and those along the points of our ISP's, and they stay fairly clean
<EQ_Smed> the packet loss you can see is usually attributed to different ISP's simply not having enough bandwidth
<EQ_Smed> I know that isn't the popular answer
<EQ_Smed> but it is accurate
<EQ_Smed> and those of you that say "CERFNET SUCKS" or "UUNET SUCKS" the bottom line is that whatever ISP we use,
<EQ_Smed> when you have this many people on it can drag down different parts of the Net
<EQ_Smed> <end>
