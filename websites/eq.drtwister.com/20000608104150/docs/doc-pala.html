Thoughts of a Paladin
by A Disgruntled Champion

October 22, 1999

I have worked to get my smithing skill to a mere 43 and it cost me over 100pp already. I have been earning the cash to do so by doing quests for the various paladin guilds around the land to raise faction for the Ro armor. I beseeched my guildmembers to aid me in obtaining as many of the ingredients as possible. I ran for my life through the rathe mountains to find the paladin camp. I have just today seen what I have to look forward to *if* I put in quite a bit more work.

To say that what Verant is doing with the armor of Ro is a joke is a major understatement. A guildmember who collected the data was kind enough to post the stats for us today and I was nearly ready to quit the game. I have lost nearly all of my faith in the abilities of Brad and crew to correctly institute just about anything in this game. I really wish I could understand exactly what these guys were thinking when they decided to incept many of the things that they have. Common sense seems to have blown right out the window, now all they seem to care about is making everyone a shiny happy person who does nothing but look to group together when they log on because they were made to weak to do anything else.

If you want people to smith, make it a skill that is actually worth the work put into it. If you want people to group, make it so that the xp gains are at least close to the amount gained when going solo. If you want the money to leave the PC's hands incept things like NPC's who SOW or buff for a price. If you want people to do long, intricate, boring, heavily camped quests, make the prize worth the work. Verant creates a quest so hard to do that it has paladins near and far drooling to see what kind of prize so much work is called for. They make it so every paladin who wishes to obtain the armor become a master smith, yet they add no forges.

I personally intend to boycott the armor. It's crap, I don't care if it's pure white with a red cross on the BP. I am not playing this game to be a model, I'm playing to be a headstomper, to role-play a paladin. I expect that the god I serve looks upon me as one of his champions fulfilling his will. I don't expect my god to treat me like a red headed step child, beat me, and throw me in the corner for a time out. I am appalled at the view Verant takes when it comes to many classes. However, I cannot speak for them as I have only played one other character, a cleric, to level 6. I have played a paladin for years, since the days of D&D and the comparison is so far off I tend to look at my EQ Paladin as a squire.

When a person thinks of a paladin they normally think of a grand figure, smiting evil across the land, helping the weak, placing themselves in front of the doom of another even if it costs them their lives. When a person sees a paladin on EQ the thought is normally "Where is the warrior who's gear you seem to be holding squire?" I don't want to be the all powerful, I don't want to have abilities I shouldn't have, I don't want to hear Verant tell us anymore that the class is pretty good and they see no fault in it. I *do* wish to see Verant, just once, say "Wow, we really screwed up this time, any feedback you guys might have would be appreciated."

There are many good things about paladins even in EQ. I enjoy using my LoH on a perfect stranger about to see his god face to face and hearing the surprise from them when the fight is over. I enjoy random acts of kindness in the form of healing people as they rest, even if the healing I can do is pretty weak. I enjoy running up to someone fighting a mob maybe just a bit too tough for them and taunting the creature so the person can finish it off unharmed and healing them when the fight is over. Seems the only thing I can't enjoy is questing. Give me a valid quest Verant. GB you say? Some quest, here I am a paladin, killing another paladin for a weapon. Soulfire you say? Great, being KOS in one of the biggest cities in the land is always fun. Here I am a hero and I can't enter Freeport. I will just have to use the tunnels like the evil classes or a thief would.

It's not bad enough that Verant does not fix bugs in the game, like the PoF requiring so many people to live yet it crashes when they try to gate them all in. Dragons requiring hordes of players to kill, yet the zone can't handle the amount of people required. It's called poor planning, plain and simple. No matter how you try to sugarcoat it, someone did not do their job correctly and from the user end it doesn't seem like they had to deal with the repercussions. I wish that in my job I could do so poorly and in fact keep that job. It is time to stop dodging the bullet and own up to what is really going on.

If creative minds can't be found to create good, involving quests. If you can't find programmers who can code the quests that are thought of. If you can't supply the hardware to handle the people doing the quests than take the name "Everquest" off the box and replace it with "Evercamp". It's not going to be long before people start referring to this game as "Quake, with a sword instead of a gun." I know that no one is perfect but at least most of us don't try to present the aura of being perfect. I'm sure people could poke a lot of hole's in this argument. When the guys up at Verant can stop trying to pretend they are perfect and start listening to the people playing this game without the benefit of knowing things like melee formulas or just how important AGI may be in a fight, maybe than things will begin to be put right.

I won't continue my search for Ro, I won't kill a Paladin to get any weapon(that was just a brainless idea), I won't become KOS in Freeport, and because of it, I won't be a paladin with any of the benefits due to me. I won't because it's simply not right, it's not well thought out, it's not something a paladin would be asked to do by his god. Common sense dictates much of this and that is the scariest part about it, where is the common sense? I may have a harder time doing much in this game than others because I refuse to do certain things with my well played character, but at least I will never think of my paladin as a sell out. This is no insult to others who have attained these items, it is merely me telling Verant where they can shove their thoughtless "quests". I may continue to play I may not, that has yet to be determined, but the guys up at Verant could make the whole decision much easier.
