Posted: Monday, January 24, 2000 by Dr.TwisTer

Within his letter, he also explains how he attempted to get in touch with Verant to bring this to their attention, but was given a foot out the door for his actions. Take a look at what he has to say, and let me know what you think.

Now I was REAL interested in making sure that there wasn't anything weird being sent out. So I yanked out my packet sniffer on my laptop and decided to take a look. After a while I discover what looks like people's account names and eq chars showing up. In alarm I posted to the main message board on Eqvault where I know some of guys at verant read messages.

I got no response.

Hmm. Okay maybe they aren't taking me seriously. Perhaps I should double check my data, maybe I'm wrong. So this time I go back in and I intend to verify whether the information I'm seeing is accurate or not. "/t foobar Hello is your eq account name john123?" "foobar tells you, "Why do you want to know?" "/t foobar Well if it is john123 could you please petition verant and tell them that I correctly got your account name and that I'd like to speak with them. Thank you".

Hmm.

1- I thought there might be a bug.

4- I contacted verant.

Here is a letter that someone sent me. Apparently he just sent it to the "Big" people up at Verant and DrTwister. So I was thinking, why have something private? You guys have a right to know what we do.

The following information is based on a current situation that involves a few aspects of Verant Interactive, and the legality of packet sniffers, as well a twist of ignorant GMs. This is not a rant, and I hope it does not come off that way to you, this is simply a letter of factional information.

This all started through a program being created and released to the public, called ShowEQ, I am sure you have all heard of it. If not, the program runs on a network system (using *nix ) and packet sniffs incoming data from Verant game servers. This data is then ran through different filters, and outputted into a readable information sheet. What ShowEQ currently does is shows you all NPCs, including min/max hp and level inside the zone and a strew of other information. Not only is this giving players an advantage over another player, but also is against Verants set user agreement, and I quote Mr. Smeadly the CEO of Verant Interactive inside the recent chat room.

Smed: Sniffer programs are illegal

Including his appearance on the Dr.TwiSter network also claiming packet sniffing is illegal and if users are found using them, will be banned from EverQuest.

Thats all fine and dandy, but what about commercial web sites using the same type of packet sniffers to illegally gain a commercial advantage over another site? Lets face it, these fan sites arent just there to help the community, they are bringing in a lot of money, millions of dollars each month goes to these types of web sites.

Now, as it was mentioned before, two current web sites has been open about using packet sniffers to gain detailed information that otherwise couldnt be gathered by a normal player.

These two web sites in question is Stratics Network "Straticscom INC" owned by Ryan Findley, and Eqmaps, which are owned by Kyle, Kirkpatrick.

The Stratics network has been promoting and using a tool called "Eqitems" (http://eq.stratics.com/items/). The program works by the Stratics network promoting users to download the 3rd party application, to then login with your everquest account and packet sniff item information for them. This data is then sent back to Stratics affiliate site and processed.

Mr. Findley has taken notice of these emails sent to Verant and different web sites regarding the usage of his program, and released the following statement on the EQ portion of his network.

"The controversy involves a completely unrelated program called ShowEQ, the use of which has been officially banned by Verant Interactive. The utility that our Database utilizes is very different than the illegal ShowEQ program"

Right here is he admitting and aware that the program ShowEQ is illegal. Yet he is claiming his program is quite different, which I do not know how. ShowEQ sniffs Verants data packets and displays the information to the user. EQitems sniffs Verants data packets and is sent to the Stratics network, so people can then go to his web site in turn to gain more page views equaling more money for the stratics network. Giving them a commercial advantage.

He continues on

"We recognize the sensitive nature of such a utility and have been in contact with Verant Interactive concerning this issue. Throughout this correspondence, no representative of Verant has expressed any desire for Stratics to remove or alter the EQ Items in any way. "

Very interesting, the stratics network has been around for awhile, and been promoting this tool also for awhile, including emails being sent to Verant by myself, and many others, including Mr. Findley. Hence Verant is well aware of what Eqitems is and how it works.



Eqmaps is using the same type of method only with the usage of ShowEQ to update their NPC list, which in a recent news posting (still in their archive) Mr. Kirkpatrick (NiteRaven) posted a note saying the following.

"One other small tid bit of info. I got my hands on a raw output datasheet from ShowEQ. It shows the levels and HPs for a couple hundred NPCs in the game. Highest known level for an NPC, Ping Fuzzlecutter, level 69. For all those people who thought the gods were 53, they are 55 :) I plan on importing this into the NPC database, but if anyone would like to see that info sheet posted online write me an email."



Clearly you can all see, each of these web sites, more so Stratics have been using and promoting 3rd party application, to illegally gain a commercial advantage of other web sites that our not. Yet Verant has ignored all attempts to bring this to their attention, and "NO" action has taken place.

I asked her what she thought about Packet Sniffing, she said they "were illegal" I then asked her why Verant has taken no action against the Stratics Network or Eqmaps. Both times she ignored my question, and answered with "verant is not related in any way to the stratics network". This clearly wasnt my question, so I asked it once again in a more clear form. I want to stress, I was not rude in any way, I was simply a player trying to find an answer to my question. GM-Camii ignored my request, and instead of answering my question or telling me whom to ask, she kicked me out of the chat room, only for myself to be confronted with "Your account has been suspended".
