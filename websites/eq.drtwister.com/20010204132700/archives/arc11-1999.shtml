Monday, December 20, 1999

Trick for No SoW
Posted: Monday, December 20, 1999 by Dr.TwisTer

If you are trying to find a short cut on your journey, this may be of some help.
If you do not have SoW and need to cross a large open area quickly (like West Karanas, for example), you can run diagonally. To do this, press your forward and either left or right key simultaneously while holding the right mouse key down (or the Control key). This will let you 'skate' on a diagonal path. You can outdistance most monsters this way. It is not as fast as SoW, but it does cut the time down on those long runs. Also, if you use the Control key method, you can respond to tells, con MOBs, etc while skating. I once trained some bandits from one end of West Karanas to the Quenos Hills zone using this method.

Pet Experience Remarks
Posted: Thursday, December 2, 1999 by Dr.TwisTer

Well this seems to be the answer everyone is giving me regarding yesterdays post about animal experience. This is what is posted on the Everquest page. Link Here

Q: When I party, how much experience does a pet take from my group.

A: When you group, the pet takes a minimal to zero experience share from your party. This is different from soloing - when you solo, your pet does take a percent of the experience that you would ordinarily have gotten.

On average, a pet will take about a .5 to 1 (out of 100) percent party experience share. From personal summoning experience, a pet is always worth having in a group if you are in an environment that is pet friendly.

And from player experience....
Actually, One of the Verant staff members posted something a while ago that DID clear up the group thing. He said "When grouped, A pet will take somewhere between .5% and 1% of the experience. When soloing however, its a different matter" And thats where it stopped. I think it was on alt.games.everquest somebody did a test with pets and seeing how many rats he had to kill to get to level 2. It turned out that without a pet, it took 10. With a pet that did under 50% of the damage to each rat, It still took 10. But if the pet did over 50% then it took him 20." This pretty much clears up that don't let your pet get the last hit in myth :o)

Dwarf Level Spot
Posted: Wednesday, December 1, 1999 by Dr.TwisTer

Don't like dwarfs? This would be a fun leveling spot for you.
Here is a spot i used to get from 29-32 in just a few days. (I got through hell level in one day here)

The sister island has dwarf NPC's that spawn every 8 minutes.

There are the 2 male dwarfs and a female dwarf.

The female I have seen a level 25 magician kill with great caution. The 2 males are tougher and should be looked at from 28+ (preferable 29 for pet classes)

There is a dwarf merchant on this island too, in a tent, do not attack him!

Pet classes do really well here, followed by wizards. I had a monk friend who was same level who said he could not solo these dwarfs easily. I have seen a shaman solo these dwarfs, with lots of downtime.

Anyways it is easy to gain a level a day here with next to no competition.

pet classes can then move on to the dwarfs npc's on butcher block docks. (These hit hard and I have not killed them, as I gave up my character before i moved on to them.)

Dwarf faction is lost through killing the above.
