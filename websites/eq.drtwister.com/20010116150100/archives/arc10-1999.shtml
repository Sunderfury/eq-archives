A Monk's Thought
Posted: Saturday, November 20, 1999 by Dr.TwisTer

If you would like to talk about this subject, you may want to visit the EverQuest Monks Forum. Otherwise, the following letter is getting a lot of support and press. Take a look and share your opinions.
I would like to provide the community with information so they can understand the nature of Verant's changes to FD. The changes are significant, a nerf, and their news message was misleading.

"If you were within sight, they would immediately come after you." - No, they came back based on aggro radius. If you saw them wander away to a safe distance, you could get up without training anyone.

"If not, they would come after you at some later point. This might happen within a few minutes, or could happen even a few hours later" This was not random, but the result of game mechanics understood by experienced feigners. A feigned upon mob has you in its aggro list. If someone else re-aggros the mob to a lesser degree than the original feigner, it will return to the feigner. Feigners knew this! If the mobs were in front of them in a room, they knew no one would disturb them and all was fine. If the mobs were scattered around the dungeon by a train wandering home, they knew to zone.

".... Four times means about 20 percent ...." - These numbers seem totally inaccurate. I had to feign 11 times on a green guard and 10 times on a knight while testing in Freeport. If ANY of your feign attempts fails (xxxx has fallen to the ground) then the mob comes running back. NO OTHER SKILL takes 4 or more successful skill checks in a row to perform a single act. If you have multiple mobs, it is almost impossible to fool them all. If even one is not fooled, it will build a train as it runs to you - quite possibly from so far out of sight that you don't even know you are killing other players.

Feign used to be predictable. If you were successful against a mob that can be fooled (some couldn't be - this was a matter of experience and feigners knew which mobs to avoid), it would never come back unless re-aggroed by another player. Verant implies otherwise, but it is NOW that the skill is totally unpredictable, with mobs that are far out of sight deep in the dungeon randomly returning, based on percentages apparently much worse than those Verant gave and which are useless against multiple mobs. Stand up, and a feigner doesn't know if he is killing people far out of sight, or has safely cleared aggro.

Before a player had to re-aggro a mob in such a way that the feigner was still higher on its aggro list (such as by coming very close, but doing no damage), and in this case it was usually only the feigner that was in danger. Now every time a feigner stands up mobs can come training from everywhere, and if he feigns again because he sees them coming, they will attack anyone nearby or in their path home.

Feign pulling is still possible, although harder. Feign twinking/leveling others is still possible, and should actually be easier. Feigning to avoid death or trains now results in far more trains. What are these "evolving gameplay issues" that Verant hoped to address? In what way was this supposed to make this a better game?

I ask that the EQ community understand this is not just a nerf to SKs, Necromancers, and Monks, but to anyone that has ever grouped with them or even played in a dungeon, and invite you to provide /feedback in game to voice your opinion.

Thank you for listening,
Yeti (Level 50 monk, Karana server)

The Site
Posted: Friday, November 19, 1999 by Dr.TwisTer

Let me start off by saying I apologize for the state that I have let the site get into recently. Please, don't get me wrong here, I do not feel that it was the doing of any of the reporters. Each one of the staff members contributed a great deal of time and energy to developing daily news content for you to read. I appreciate everything they offered and will continue to stay in touch with each one of them.

Although I admit, it is ultimately my responsibility to make things operate smoothly and continually strive to bring fresh new information to the players. This I have failed and I am sorry.

I have been able to use the excuse for some time that I do not play EQ and that is the reason for not being active around the site. This should not be an excuse and will hopefully be put aside in the future. I did log into EQ just the other night, created a character, and was amazed at some of the changes that I experienced. Some of the issues that I have problems with are still there, but for the most part, I saw some great development over the course of the past year.

With all of this said and done, let me give you some thoughts for the future of this site.

Most of you understand the philosophy of my network. Most of you get the concepts, and understand the ideas behind what I am trying to do. Granted, this is an extremely fine line that I have often crossed back and forth over, however I do feel that I have traveled this line pretty well. If you don't understand what this site is about, that is ok, I encourage you to visit more often, and open up your mind to new ideas, ask around, and you should figure it out. What I want to try and avoid from now on is crossing that line with the public. We are here for you, and we want to make sure that you benefit the most from what our news promotes.

We will continue to post bugs, problems, complaints, or issues that arise within the game. Our main goal is to get the bugs fixed, and the problems resolved. This is all focused around supporting Verant with information so that they may present us with a better all around game.

I would request a few things in the process. First, if you send me information to post, I would also encourage that you attach a copy to Verant. Second, the information that is posted is for public knowledge, to help Verant track down and fix problems, and to improve game play. Be warned, if you use any of the bugs or exploits to your benefit, we are not held responsible for your actions and you will most likely get banned. For those who are unaware of the tracking system in EQ, they can track ANYTHING you do. They can tell how many people took 10 steps to the North, turned east, and jumped. There is no way to hide from most bugs or exploits, so if you are attempting to screw the game, the game will have the last laugh. We are not about screwing over the game, we are about improving the game and making it more enjoyable.

In the near future you will begin to see key members of Verant's development team communicating with the players and this site through our message forum and main news page. If there are issues that you would like addressed, please post your concerns or email them to me.

In the next few weeks, we will be establishing a development team chat with focus on bugs and current items that are in testing. This will be the first chat of its kind and Verant has agreed to work out arrangements to support this type of player communication on a regular basis if the first chat is successful. This active approach by Verant is the first true attempt by any company to work directly with the players to actively improve their game.

There will obviously still be a struggle with looking at all of the information that you send me. I have access to EQ, but do not play it as often as I should for someone who has a site dedicated to the game. I will attempt to resolve this issue over the course of the next few days. At this time, I do not want to bring on any additional help, however I will not rule it out in the future. Please begin to send all EQ related information to eqnews@drtwister.com - or email me personally at drtwister@drtwister.com

Thank you all for your support, patience and understanding.

Dr.TwisTer

Announcing, *drum roll* the Dr.TwisTer Network SETI@home team!
Posted: Tuesday, November 2, 1999 by Baggle

I've been thinking about doing this for a while, and today I finally put in the five minutes of work needed to do this. I created the Dr.TwisTer Network SETI@Home team!

For those of you who aren't familiar with SETI@home, let me bring you up to speed. First of all, you are probably saying, "SETI? Isn't that Search For Extra-Terrestrial Intelligence?" Well, yes. It is. SETI is a semi-government funded program(if you can consider $100,000 a year funding)that collects radio waves from space on huge radio-telescopes. The data is then processed, and the computer determines if the radio wave could possibly be from an intelligent life form from anywhere in the galaxy.

As you might have guessed by now, deciphering all of this data takes an immense amount of time, and that's where SETI@home comes in. SETI@home is a small download(700k), that, when run, connects to the SETI@home server, collects a 340k clump of raw data, called a work unit, and uses your computer(and millions of other people's computers) to decipher the data. After you decipher it, your computer connects to the SETI@home main server, gives it your data, and gets a new work unit.

SETI@home functions mainly as a screensaver and just something to do while your computer is doing nothing, but it adds a level of competition. There are many different teams, in several different categories(small business, club, large business, gov. agency, school, university, etc) competing to be number one. Places are determined by the total sum of the CPU time of the group(what it goes by on the site for #1), and total work units processed(so you can brag to your friends about how fast your computer is, and then they can make fun of you for being such a geek).

Now that you're up to speed, go download SETI@home for Windows 9x/NT or the text based version for Linux, Unix, MacOS, BeOS, and many others.

After you have SETI@home, you will need to join the Dr.TwisTer Network SETI@Home team. To join, follow this link. It will ask you for a password, which you won't have, so just click on the "Don't have a password?" link and they will send one to the email address you used to make your account with.
