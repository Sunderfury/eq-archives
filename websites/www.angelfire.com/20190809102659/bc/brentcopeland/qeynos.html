Aenia and Behroe::
 In North Queynos there's a blue house which holds a daughter and her father. Only thing is they never leave. Why? Well first because the father hasn't gotten his shop up yet and more importantly because his daughter, Aenia, is not allowed to leave the house. This is because he doesn't want her around such rapscallions as those lowly bards, especially one certain one named Behroe Dlexon, who works nights at the docks compiling the watch report. Well it turns out that Aenia and Behroe are in love and if you go see him he tells his tale of woe. He is upset because Aenia's father will kill him if he goes near her. The lovesick fool has written a ballad that he will ask you to sing to her for him. You need to /say to Aenia the entire sonnet. Make sure you don't make a typo or it won't work. Aenia will swoon and gave you a love letter to take back to Behroe (note that Behroe only spawns at night - you might have to start the "watch reports" quest to spawn him). Take the love letter back to Behroe and he gives you a nice low-level item. (Usually a Shaw of the Wind Spirit). For completing the quest you get the following faction: LeagueofAntonicanBards, KnightsofTruth and GuardsofQeynos get better; RingofScale and MayongMistmoore get worse. (Submitted by Doriath and Amnion)

The Assassin::
 This quest is sometimes given to you after you complete the Nitrates quest in Surefall Glades. Return to surefall and give the note to Gerael for status and few coins. Gerael returns the note to you and asks you to give it to Captain Tillim in Qeynos. He asks you to also to warn the target (Gash Flockwaller) that [They are Trying to kill him]. Go to Qeynos and give the note to Captain Tillim. You are rewarded with some faction and told that Gash stays in the Lion's head INN. Going to the Lion's head inn. Gash Flockwaller should be in a back room during the day. Talking to him and repeating the key phrase as [they are trying to kill you] gives you a clue about the unkempt druids - that they carry [black wood chips]. Somewhere in this the Assassin will attack - his name is Raffel Minnmorn. At this point the quest may be broken. One time I beat the assassin with Gash's assistance - as a result the body dissappeared and I couldn't loot it; the second time I got a ranger/druid mob together and we splatted him, but since I hadn't gotten the wood chip comment yet all that I got from him was a rusty dagger. Raffel con's black to a 10th level ranger, but he has a slew of druid spells and shouldn't be solo'd unless you are pretty strong or lucky. So the ending is still unclear. (Submitted by Endager The Ranger)

Bard Reports::
 Bard Reports: The Bard Guild's needs your help in keeping an eye on the town, if you wish to accept their assignment. Talk to Juseanus and he will give you two scrolls to deliver. Take one to the North gate and another to the docks. You may be given scrolls to return as well. Each delivery is worth a little bit of experience. Also, the following factions will improve: LeagueofAntonicanBards, KnightsofTruth, GuardsofQeynos. RingofScale and MayongMistmoore go down. Because you can do this quickly and get a faction hit for each delivery, this is a good way to improve faction.

 There's a shop called "The Herb Jar." If you sit there for a while,the person in the middle of the room behind the podium says to one of her co-workers, "We're out of bat fur again?" Ask her about bat fur. I am told that you get little if no reward for this quest other than some faction changes.

The Bayle List::
 A paladin in the Temple of Thunder is investigating corruption among the guards of Qeynos. He asks you to go to Surefall Glade, find Frenway Marthank in the Druids' Guildtree, and ask him for "The Bayle List." However, when you do, Frenway is in a state of depression. His son -- who happened to be carrying the very list you're looking for -- was killed and devoured by the great grizzly bear Mammoth, who lives deep in the caves of Surefall Glade. As a Druid, Frenway is forbidden to harm the bears of the Glade, but he wants vengeance. If you can find and slay Mammoth, you will get the list, and Frenway will reward you if you return with Mammoth's hide. However, there are more than a few people in Surefall Glade who will strike you down for attacking the bears, so be very careful. (Submitted by Shendar)

Blank Scrolls::
 The Wizard's Guild needs blank scroll sheets. Talk to Kazlo and he will ask you to go to Surefall Glade to see if the druids have any blank scroll sheets. There may be other ways to get them, but Den Magason (wanders around the dock area) will sell you blank scroll sheets for 2 gold a sheet. You will not get your money back when you turn them in, but you will get some xp, faction and some minor coin. (Submitted by Doriath and Lysan)

Blessed Oil Quest::
 This starts in Western Karana by talking to Brother Estle, located on the road just as you enter the plains. Talk to him and he will tell you that he is waiting to get some blessed oil, perhaps to slick down the other monk he is always fighting. Go to the Temple of Life and talk to one of the priests, who will give you the oil to deliver to the good monk. Take it back to him for some money, experience, faction with the Priests of Life and occasionally the Prayer bead quest.

Camlend's Scythe Quest::
 The quest is given by Camlend Serbold at Qeynos' Temple of Life. You are supposed to bring him the Opal Scythe (found on Lord Grimrot in SK). The catch is that Lord Grimrot spawns in two versions- a weak one (level 25 or so) without the scythe, and a BIG level 35 with the scythe. Both have harm touch and all the other appropriate SK abilities. The big version can solo a cyclops quite easily, and has no problems making mincemeat out of anything less than a full 35'ish group. The reward for turning in the scythe to Camlend is actually far less than the value of the scythe, so it isn't really worth doing. Note: some people have claimed this is how you get the oaken scimitar, but many others have disputed that claim, so I wouldn't recommend trying this.

Clothspinner Sisters::
You will occasionally see an NPC running around outside the gates whose last name is Clothspinner. Knowledge of this quest is still developing, but here are the basics. Talk to her and hear her tale of woe. She will tell you that she misses her warrior sister, and will give you a letter to deliver to her sister somewhere in the Karanas. The sister is located in East Karana in a cave. Deliver the note to her, and she will thank you and give you a monogrammed cloth to take back to her sister. She will then be rewarded for talking to you by being attacked by a guard and killed. Bet that makes you feel good. If you kill the guard and the sister lives, she also gives you a veil to give to the sister. When you give the original sister the cloth she will send you with a note to the Temple of Life. If you give her the veil, you get a decent item as well. There you may or may not be rewarded, depending it seems upon your alignment and religion.

Cure for Lempeck Hargrim::
This is the quest for the Shining Star of Light, a 1HB cleric/paladin weapon. The quest starts with Astaed Wemore, the same cleric in the Temple of Life who gives the blessed oil quest. Apparantly Lempic Hargrim is sick and needs a special cure. He will tell you to go to Rivervale and find Kizzie Mintopp, and tell her that "I am from temple of life". She will then tell you to find some guy called Honeybugger outside in Misty Thicket and find out how to get honeycombs, because she needs them to make Honey Jum. Bring her the honeycombs and she makes the Honey Jum for you. You then need to give it back to the cleric who gave you the quest. He will make the Jum into a potion for Lempeck Hargrim of West Karana. Take the Potion to Lempeck, and receive a rusty scyth (Lore item version). I have been told that if your faction is not high enough with the Prieses of Life (probably at least amiable) he will attack and kill you, so be careful. Take the scyth back to the Aeston Wemore, and you get the shining star. Quickest method to complete the quest without all the talking: 1). Give Kizzie Mintopp 3 honeycombs and 5 gp for the Honey Jum. 2). Take Honey Jum to Cleric in temple of life (the one who gives the quest) and get Potion in return. 3). Take Potion to Lempeck Hargrim of West Karana and get LORE ITEM RUSTY SCYTH - NO DROP in return. His house is near the river bank, between the fields. 4). Take Rusty Scyth back to cleric in temple of life for the shining star of light. You also receive the following faction changes: Priests of Life, Knights of Thunder, Guards of Qeynos and Antonius Bayle get better, Blood Sabres gets worse. (Submitted by CloudKraken and Vertigo)

Diseased Animals::
Priestess Caulria in the temple in North Qeynos (the one in leather near the back wall) gives the quest, which requires you to look into the occurrence of rabid wolves in the area. Rabid wolves and bears are found in the Qeynos Hills. Return a 'diseased wolf pelt' to her for faction bonuses, experience and a 'cure disease' spell that she casts on you to purge you of that annoying PlagueRatDisease and Rabies. There are also rabid grizzly bears in the Hills, and if you return one of the pelts to her, you get a spell scroll in addition to the faction bonuses. You do not get a cure for this quest, so make sure you keep a diseased wolf pelt to give to her after the bear pelts, as rabies is annoying and takes an age to wear off. Sometimes, fairly rarely, you get a 'fleshy orb' on one of the rabid creatures. Give this to her, you'll get faction bonuses, some cash, and she'll give you back a lore version and ask you to take it to someone in Freeport (Lorme Trethadore or something like that)(Note there is a Lorne Tredore in the academy, but this doesn't seem to be the right person). Not sure what you get for that yet. (Submitted by Delakar)

The Donations::
One of the Knights of Thunder gives you a donation box and asks you to find six residents of Qeynos to donate to the temple. Here's a list of where to get the six donations: 1) Lanhern Firepride - Only at night when she is in Fish's bar. Give her an ogre swill for one; 2) Hansl Bigoon - Either at his house, or the office of the people; 3) Kane Bayle - Guard House; 4) Enil Runik - Temple of Life; 5) Guard Leopold - Qeynos Hills, wanders; 6) Guard Cheslin - Qeynos Hills, wanders - Frequently dead. The quest chest also requires your 'PK Note' and the 'PK Note' of another. There are other people who respond to "donation", but don't seem to cough one up: The cloth armor salesperson in front of the Lion Mane Inn and Tavern (she says to go away) in South Qeynos. The two gents who make bags in North Qeynos. A bard in the Bard's guild (says he donates to his own cause) in South Qeynos. Also in South Qeynos, a merchant by the Grounds of Fate who only makes three things, axes I believe. He laughs because of the taxes being so high. Oh, so do the bag makers. (Submitted by Verite, Olen and Malqanar)

Drosco the Zombie::
The keepers of Bertoxxulos' shrine are annoyed by a zombie named Drosco who is wandering the aqueducts and threatening their security. Talk to one of the Necromancer Guild Masters and he will ask you to eliminate this pesky zombie. Kill Drosco and return the letter and amulet you find on him for experience, faction, money and an occasional spell, as well as another quest. These factions change when you kill Droscoe: BloodSabers and Corrupt Guards of Queynos go down and Order of Thunder goes up. When you turn in the items, the faction change is the exact opposite. (Submitted by Bathory)

Faren's Tacklebox::
On the docks in Qeynos (not the boat dock, the other ones south of there) is a fisherman named Faren. Guard Beren will tell you that Trumpy, a dwarf, keeps pestering his poor brother Faren. Corshin thinks Trumpy is a friend of Commander Bayle. Fish with him for awhile & eventually Trumpy Irontoe will walk up and begin to taunt him. Faren will then say: "Hey! My [tacklebox]! You rotten dwarf!" or something similar. If you ask him about the tacklebox, he will explain that Trumpy just knocked it into the bay, he can't swim and could you please get it. Jump in (you have practiced swimming, right?) and look around. What fooled me the 1st time is the tacklebox doesn't look like a tacklebox, of course, it looks like dropped loot. Get it and return it to Faren for some exp, good faction bonuses some copper and a Tunic - which is a lore item. Fisherman Convert::
A guild trainer upstairs from the Deepwater Knights guild in Erudin will ask you to convert a fisherman in Qeynos. She tells you that the fisherman will break his pole and give it to you to return back to her for fulfilling the quest. This fisherman is located in the pond of N. Qeynos. Dive into the pond and go into the sewers. In the sewers, there is a fisherman near the "main open area" of the aquaducts (he is not there all the time). Talk to him and he will tell you that he is willing to go back to worshipping the God of Prexus if you will give him your guild tunic (I assume you must still have your guild tunic to fulfill this quest). Give him the tunic and he will give you his broken fishing pole. Then take the broken pole back to the guild trainer and she will give you experience, faction standing and an item, possibly an orange ring (Submitted by Buju)

Frostbite's Fish::
Talk to Enic Ruklin at the Temple of Life and he will say he has to deal with a [problem]. It turns out a pesky barbarian and his dog Frostbite visit Qeynos on occassion. Frostbite has a tendency to eat the sacred Koalindl fish from the Temple. Enic must find Frostbite and retrieve the corpse of the fish. Frostbite can be found at the Barbarian fishing village in the Westren Karanas. Watch out! Ulrich McMannus is very fond of the mutt and will insta-kill you if he sees you harming Frostbite. Also, frostbite does not always have a fish on him when you kill him and killing him causes severe faction drops with several barbarian factions. Deliver the fish corpse and you will get some decent gold and sometimes a nice item as well (Possibly prayer beads). A note on this. You can also complete this quest by killing Koalindl fish yourself, taking the severe faction hit, getting killed by the guard, and turning in the fish corpse to Ruklin. If you're not worried about the faction loss you will take and don't care about dying (i.e. are below level 4), this is a good way to make some money. (Submitted by Esmeraude)

Gnasher's Head::
This quest starts in Surefall Glade. Talk to Larsk Juton who has some sort of feud with a gnoll named Gnasher. Too lazy to do it himself I guess, he wants you to kill his enemy for him. This gnoll occasionally appears in Qeynos Hills and is around level 10 or so. Kill him and bring his head back to Lars and you will get some cash. Sometimes when you kill Gnasher Furrgutt, he is carrying a note from the bartender at Fish's Ale which you can turn in to the authorities. First, give the note to Larsk Juton (the same person you give Gnasher's head to). Note that you canot shortcut this by giving the note to Captain Tillian directly. He will give you some experience and a faction adjustment, and advise you to take the note to Captain Tillian in the Arena in Qeynos (the same one you give Gnoll Fangs to). Captain Tillian will give you more experience, more faction adjustments, and a Medal of Merit (a Lore Item which gives you +5 Charisma) for your good deeds. He will then summon the Executioner, who will proceed to Fish's Ale to carry out the sentence of death pronounced on the unwise bartender. Follow the executioner and watch the fun. Another note. If you are carrying gnoll fangs for Captain Tillian, give them to him and go collect the blackburrow stout first, since the executioner will kill your stout connection and he won't respawn for a while. (Submitted by Shendar)

Gnoll Fangs::
The factions have now changed on this. You may have noticed that on occasion you get a gnoll fang after killing a gnoll. You have probably been throwing them away since merchants disdain buying those dirty gnoll teeth. But wait! There is a guard Captain (Captain Tillin) located on the top floor of the arena in Qeynos who seems to have a pathological hatred of gnolls (don't we all). Take him your collection of fangs and give them to him one at a time for some experience and a moonstone. Then take your collection of moonstones to Fish's Ale (a pub down by the docks) and go into the back room, you'll find a bartender named Mcneal Jocub. Give the moonstones to the bartender, and for each stone, you'll recieve a bottle of Blackburrow Stout, experience and more faction adjustments. The value of a bottle of stout is in the range of 7 sp. Giving a gnoll tooth to Captain Tillin raises your faction with GuardsofQeynos. Giving a moonstone to Mcneal Jocub raises your faction with Karana Residents, Guards of Qeynos, Priests of Life, Knights of Thunder. Thus each gnoll fang is worth some experience, good faction, and just short of a gold. Well worth the trip to town. (Submitted by Edmond the Tall of Rivervale)

Nevertheless, he will cast the spell on you if you give him a donation of 10 gp, and you will get some xp and faction adjustments. (Submitted by Shendar)

Letter to Brother Hayle::
: After you complete the Prayer Beads Quest, The High Priestess might then ask you to take a LETTER to Brother Hayle asking him to come home immediately. Brother Hayle is in the prison of the Splitpaw Gnoll Den, a dungeon in the center of South Karana. Be sure to have a friend outside the bars or a gate spell when you go in, because the bars will close behind you and the switch to raise them is too far off to hit from inside the cell. Giving the letter to Brother Hayle results in some experience and some faction adjustment, and he then gives you a key with a strange name "T.O.L. 2020", and tells you to take it to Tyokan, a vendor in the temple shop back in Qeynos, for a reward. He is a fairly rare spawn. Giving the key to the vendor and you may receive a decent spell scroll (I've been told as high as level 29). (Submitted by Aoiryuu of Mithaniel Marr)

Menkas Tabolet::
