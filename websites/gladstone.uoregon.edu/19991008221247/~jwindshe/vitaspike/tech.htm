Is Your Soil Deficient?

   Trace elements are normally found in minute amounts in the soil. Naturally, if a tree has all of these metals that it needs, the injection of Vita-Spike into the tree trunk will not improve its health. However, the U.S. Dept.of Agriculture has published information showing that both the east and west coasts are deficient of these metals in the soil, with Florida and California extremely deficient. Also, the areas around the Great Lakes, and along the St. Lawrence Seaway are deficient, as are the states along the Gulf Coast.

   The entire Southwestern states have a different problem: Even though the trace elements are available in the soil, the tree roots have difficulty absorbing them, because the soil is so highly alkaline that it causes a condition called "lockup", in which the chemicals in the soil combine with trace elements to form compounds that roots can not absorb. There are also deficient areas scattered throughout the non-deficient regions.
